Corydalis sibirica var. impatiens

Corydalis sibirica var. impatiens, commonly known as Siberian Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant features slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
