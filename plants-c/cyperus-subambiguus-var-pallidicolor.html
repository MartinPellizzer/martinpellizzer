Cyperus subambiguus var. pallidicolor

Cyperus subambiguus var. pallidicolor, commonly known as the pale-colored flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring shallow water or moist soil. Its pale-colored flowers are often arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
