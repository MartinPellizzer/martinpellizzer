Chrysanthemum hispanicum

Chrysanthemum hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical structure and vibrant blooms contribute to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
