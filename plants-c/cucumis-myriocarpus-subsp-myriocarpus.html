Cucumis myriocarpus subsp. myriocarpus

Cucumis myriocarpus subsp.

myriocarpus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, elongated fruits that resemble horns, which are typically green when mature.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the fruit contains small, edible seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
