Capparis breynia var. uniflora

Capparis breynia var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically solitary or in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
