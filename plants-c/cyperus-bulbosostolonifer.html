Cyperus bulbosostolonifer

Cyperus bulbosostolonifer, commonly known as the bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread through underground stems called stolons. The plant produces dense clusters of small, grass-like leaves and bears spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its morphology makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
