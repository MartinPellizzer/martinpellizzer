Chrysopsis depressa

Chrysopsis depressa, commonly known as the low chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the late spring to early summer.
