Centaurea × schlosseri

Centaurea × schlosseri, commonly known as the Schlosseri's cornflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
