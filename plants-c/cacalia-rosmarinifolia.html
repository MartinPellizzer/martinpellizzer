Cacalia rosmarinifolia

Cacalia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cacalia rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild fragrance.
