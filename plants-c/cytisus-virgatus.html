Cytisus virgatus

Cytisus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thorn or yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus virgatus is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
