Cirsium × variabile

Cirsium × variabile, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. This perennial species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its morphology varies significantly, leading to its common name, as it exhibits a range of leaf shapes and flower structures.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
