Chloraea litoralis

Chloraea litoralis, commonly known as the sea dittany, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

Chloraea litoralis is often found in maritime climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance to coastal ecosystems.
