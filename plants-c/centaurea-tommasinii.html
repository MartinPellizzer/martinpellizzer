Centaurea tommasinii

Centaurea tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers with bright yellow centers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea tommasinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
