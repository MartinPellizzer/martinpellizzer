Crassina multiflora

Crassina multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered crassina, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Crassina multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
