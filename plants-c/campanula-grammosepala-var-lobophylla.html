Campanula grammosepala var. lobophylla

Campanula grammosepala var. lobophylla, commonly known as the lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, often with lobed margins, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
