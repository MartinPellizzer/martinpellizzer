Crataegus rosei

Crataegus rosei, commonly known as the rose hip bush, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, oval leaves, and showy pink or white flowers that appear in spring.

Its fruit, known as a rose hip, is a red, hairy capsule that contains small seeds and is rich in vitamin C. The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
