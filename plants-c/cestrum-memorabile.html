Cestrum memorabile

Cestrum memorabile, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, simple leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Cestrum memorabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique blooming habit.
