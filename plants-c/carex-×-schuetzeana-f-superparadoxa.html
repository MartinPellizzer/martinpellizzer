Carex × schuetzeana f. superparadoxa

Carex × schuetzeana f. superparadoxa, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant within the Carex genus, characterized by its tall, robust stature and dense foliage.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated and bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet to moderately moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental sedge is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
