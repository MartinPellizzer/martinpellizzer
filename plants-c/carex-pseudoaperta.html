Carex pseudoaperta

Carex pseudoaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgewort, is a species of sedge native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Carex pseudoaperta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
