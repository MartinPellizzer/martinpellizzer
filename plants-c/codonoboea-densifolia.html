Codonoboea densifolia

Codonoboea densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
