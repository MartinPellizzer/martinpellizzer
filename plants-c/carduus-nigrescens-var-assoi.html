Carduus nigrescens var. assoi

Carduus nigrescens var. assoi, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has been used historically for its medicinal properties, including as a remedy for digestive issues.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
