Coreopsis leucorrhiza

Coreopsis leucorrhiza, commonly known as whitehead coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 feet tall, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis leucorrhiza is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
