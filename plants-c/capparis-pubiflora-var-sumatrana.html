Capparis pubiflora var. sumatrana

Capparis pubiflora var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and has small, often spiny leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
