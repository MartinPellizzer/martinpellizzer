Cirsium × jonasii

Cirsium × jonasii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cirsium arvense (Canada thistle) and Cirsium vulgare (common thistle), resulting in a plant with characteristics of both parents. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America. It has spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of many small florets.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in many areas.
