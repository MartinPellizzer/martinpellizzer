Cereus gladiator var. geometrizans

Cereus gladiator var. geometrizans, commonly known as the Geometrical Cereus, is a striking cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive, geometrically arranged spines that give it its common name. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a dramatic floral display. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
