Carex hirta var. fuscescentes

Carex hirta var. fuscescentes, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers, which are arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
