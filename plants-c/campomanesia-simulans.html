Campomanesia simulans

Campomanesia simulans, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, apple-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
