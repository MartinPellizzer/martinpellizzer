Ceropegia namaquana

Ceropegia namaquana, commonly known as the Namibian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Ceropegia namaquana is adapted to survive in drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
