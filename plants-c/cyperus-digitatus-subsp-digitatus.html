Cyperus digitatus subsp. digitatus

Cyperus digitatus subsp.

digitatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
