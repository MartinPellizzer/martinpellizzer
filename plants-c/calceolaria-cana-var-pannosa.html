Calceolaria cana var. pannosa

Calceolaria cana var. pannosa, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is typically found in high-altitude regions with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
