Curarea cuatrecasasii

Curarea cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's curare, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds. Curarea cuatrecasasii is notable for its use in traditional poison arrows due to the presence of curare alkaloids.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in ethnobotany and its potential pharmacological applications.
