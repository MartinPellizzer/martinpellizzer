Carex plesiocephala

Carex plesiocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wet or moist habitats, this plant thrives in environments with consistent moisture, such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Carex plesiocephala plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
