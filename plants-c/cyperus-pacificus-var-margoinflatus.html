Cyperus pacificus var. margoinflatus

Cyperus pacificus var. margoinflatus, commonly known as the Pacific flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various water conditions and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
