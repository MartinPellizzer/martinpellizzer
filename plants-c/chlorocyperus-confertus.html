Chlorocyperus confertus

Chlorocyperus confertus, commonly known as the dense flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, often forming a mat-like structure that helps stabilize soil in wet areas. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Chlorocyperus confertus plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and preventing erosion.
