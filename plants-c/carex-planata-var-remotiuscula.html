Carex planata var. remotiuscula

Carex planata var. remotiuscula, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a variant of the swamp sedge species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, typically growing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full to partial sun and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
