Centaurea boissieri var. rouyi

Centaurea boissieri var. rouyi, commonly known as the Rouyi centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in dense, conical clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
