Callista transparens

Callista transparens, commonly known as the transparent bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, with a unique translucent quality to their petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Callista transparens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
