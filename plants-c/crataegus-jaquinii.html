Crataegus jaquinii

Crataegus jaquinii, commonly known as the jaquin's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
