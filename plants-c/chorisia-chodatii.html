Chorisia chodatii

Chorisia chodatii, commonly known as the African trumpet tree, is a striking member of the Bignoniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a distinctive, hollow, branching trunk that gives it a unique appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Chorisia chodatii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
