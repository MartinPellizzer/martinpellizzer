Carex gravida f. laxifolia

Carex gravida f. laxifolia, commonly known as the looseleaf sedgeland.

This plant is a variant of Carex gravida, characterized by its broader, more loosely arranged leaves compared to the typical form. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
