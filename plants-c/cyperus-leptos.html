Cyperus leptos

Cyperus leptos, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it a common species in marshy environments. Cyperus leptos has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize wetlands.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
