Convolvulus capensis var. plicatus

Convolvulus capensis var. plicatus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
