Coccineorchis

Coccineorchis, commonly known as the red orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it often forms dense clumps.

Coccineorchis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
