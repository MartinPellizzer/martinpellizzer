Cavendishia pterocarpa

Cavendishia pterocarpa, commonly known as the giant pterocarp, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach several meters in length, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit, known as a pterocarp, is a woody pod that contains seeds enclosed in a fibrous layer.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, Cavendishia pterocarpa is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
