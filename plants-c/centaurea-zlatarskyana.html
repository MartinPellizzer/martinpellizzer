Centaurea zlatarskyana

Centaurea zlatarskyana, commonly known as the Zlatarsky's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea zlatarskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
