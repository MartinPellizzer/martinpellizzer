Carapichea ligularis

Carapichea ligularis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and light-colored. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red pod that splits open when mature.
