Croton triumfettoides

Croton triumfettoides, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Croton triumfettoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
