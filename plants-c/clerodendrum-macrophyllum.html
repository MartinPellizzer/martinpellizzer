Clerodendrum macrophyllum

Clerodendrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
