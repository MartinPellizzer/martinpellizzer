Cupania fluminensis

Cupania fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hog plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, yellow or orange fruits. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor, making it valuable in local cuisines.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
