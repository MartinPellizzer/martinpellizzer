Ceropegia matthewiana

Ceropegia matthewiana, commonly known as the Matthew's African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers with a unique, intricate structure that resembles a bird's beak. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
