Chrysophyllum floribundum

Chrysophyllum floribundum, commonly known as the West African cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed, making it a valuable fruit in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
