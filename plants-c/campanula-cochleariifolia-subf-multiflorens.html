Campanula cochleariifolia subf. multiflorens

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

multiflorens, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically woolly and gray-green in appearance.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
