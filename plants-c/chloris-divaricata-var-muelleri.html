Chloris divaricata var. muelleri

Chloris divaricata var. muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
