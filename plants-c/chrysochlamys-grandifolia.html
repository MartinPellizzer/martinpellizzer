Chrysochlamys grandifolia

Chrysochlamys grandifolia, commonly known as the golden chrysochlamys, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often glossy, with a dark green upper surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
