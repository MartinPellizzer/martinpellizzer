Carex sempervirens subsp. tristis

Carex sempervirens subsp.

tristis, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
