Cactus × jenkinsonii

Cactus × jenkinsonii, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cereus jamensis and Cereus jenkinsonii.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

This hybrid is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
