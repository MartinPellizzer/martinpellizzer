Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp. davisii

Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp.

davisii, commonly known as Davis's narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, elliptical nutlets as fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
