Cryptocoryne minima

Cryptocoryne minima, commonly known as the dwarf cryptocoryne, is a compact aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in a rosette formation, typically reaching a height of less than 10 centimeters. The plant is well-adapted to low-light conditions and can thrive in a variety of water temperatures. Its rhizome system allows it to spread slowly and form dense underwater carpets.

Cryptocoryne minima is popular in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
