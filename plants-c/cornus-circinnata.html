Cornus circinnata

Cornus circinnata, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that turn a striking reddish color in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with smooth edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

Cornus circinnata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
