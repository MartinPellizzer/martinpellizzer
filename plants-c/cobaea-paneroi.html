Cobaea paneroi

Cobaea paneroi, commonly known as the Pacific moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

The flowers typically bloom in late summer and early autumn, making it a striking addition to gardens in temperate climates.
