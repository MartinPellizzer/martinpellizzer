Clematis peterae var. trichocarpa

Clematis peterae var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the trichocarpous clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
