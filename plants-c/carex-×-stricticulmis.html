Carex × stricticulmis

Carex × stricticulmis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge.

It is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, clustered growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and natural landscaping.
