Coreopsis mutica var. microcephala

Coreopsis mutica var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf coreopsis, is a compact perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in native plant gardens.
