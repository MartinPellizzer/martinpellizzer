Coffea resinosa var. thouarsii

Coffea resinosa var. thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar coffee tree, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
