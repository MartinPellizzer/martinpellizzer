Chrysopsis latisquamea

Chrysopsis latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, overlapping scales, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Chrysopsis latisquamea is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its long-lasting, drought-resistant qualities.
