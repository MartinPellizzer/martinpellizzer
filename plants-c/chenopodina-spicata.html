Chenopodina spicata

Chenopodina spicata, commonly known as the spicate goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish to reddish in color. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.
