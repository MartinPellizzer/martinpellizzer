Cerasus duclouxii

Cerasus duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux cherry, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, dark red fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The leaves are ovate and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
