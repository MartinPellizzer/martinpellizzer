Carduncellus tingitanus var. corymbosus

Carduncellus tingitanus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the Moroccan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, grayish-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking appearance.
