Carex maculata var. neurochlamys

Carex maculata var. neurochlamys, commonly known as the spotted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a distinctive reddish or brownish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
