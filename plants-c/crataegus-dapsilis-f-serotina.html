Crataegus dapsilis f. serotina

Crataegus dapsilis f. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom later in the growing season. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
