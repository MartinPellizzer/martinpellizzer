Crataegus mexicana var. microsperma

Crataegus mexicana var. microsperma, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, red to orange berry that matures in the fall, containing several seeds. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
