Carex hochstetteriana

Carex hochstetteriana, commonly known as Hochstetter's sedge, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Hochstetter's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
