Corylus tubulosa

Corylus tubulosa, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family桦树科 (Betulaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tubular fruits that mature into edible nuts. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The nuts are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
