Campanula persicifolia var. cupularis

Campanula persicifolia var. cupularis, commonly known as the cupular bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
