Centropogon ferrugineus var. costaricanus

Centropogon ferrugineus var. costaricanus, commonly known as the Costa Rican fireflower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, rusty-hued pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
