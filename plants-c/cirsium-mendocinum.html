Cirsium mendocinum

Cirsium mendocinum, commonly known as Mendo thistle, is a perennial plant native to California.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Cirsium mendocinum is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
