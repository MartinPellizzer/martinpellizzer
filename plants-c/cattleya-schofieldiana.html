Cattleya schofieldiana

Cattleya schofieldiana, commonly known as the Schofield's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence usually bears several blooms, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, especially in the evening, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
