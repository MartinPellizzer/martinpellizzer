Cassia chaetostegia

Cassia chaetostegia, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia chaetostegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
