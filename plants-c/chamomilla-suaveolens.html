Chamomilla suaveolens

Chamomilla suaveolens, commonly known as wild chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which are responsible for its calming properties.

Chamomilla suaveolens thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
