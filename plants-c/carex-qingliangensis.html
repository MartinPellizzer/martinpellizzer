Carex qingliangensis

Carex qingliangensis, commonly known as the Qingliang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its flowering occurs in late summer, producing small, greenish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex qingliangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
