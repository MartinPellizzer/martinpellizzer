Centaurea dianthoides

Centaurea dianthoides, commonly known as the pink centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers have five petals and are arranged in dense heads.

Centaurea dianthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
