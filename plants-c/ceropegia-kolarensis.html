Ceropegia kolarensis

Ceropegia kolarensis, commonly known as the Cape mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia kolarensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to trail from hanging baskets or shelves.
