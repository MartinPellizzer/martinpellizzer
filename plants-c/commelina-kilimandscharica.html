Commelina kilimandscharica

Commelina kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro arum, is a striking tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces striking blue-violet flowers with prominent white spathes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, contributing to its persistence in natural habitats.
