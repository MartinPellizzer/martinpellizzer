Cassia adiantifolia var. pteridophylla

Cassia adiantifolia var. pteridophylla, commonly known as the fern-like cassia, is a variant of the cassia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
