Callista longicollis

Callista longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
