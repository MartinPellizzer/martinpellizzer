Carex × tourletii

Carex × tourletii, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually dark green and grassy in appearance, while the flowers are small and clustered.

Carex × tourletii is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
