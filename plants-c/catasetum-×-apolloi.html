Catasetum × apolloi

Catasetum × apolloi, commonly known as the Apollo orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, with each bloom featuring a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The botanical interest of Catasetum × apolloi lies in its complex hybrid origin and its role in orchid horticulture.
