Centaurea ramosissima

Centaurea ramosissima, commonly known as the French centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or purple flowers, which are surrounded by narrow, blue-green bracts. The plant has a bushy growth habit with numerous branches and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Centaurea ramosissima is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
