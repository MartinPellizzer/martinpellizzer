Convallaria majalis var. manshurica

Convallaria majalis var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian lily, is a rare variant of the lily family native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers and lanceolate, dark green leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
