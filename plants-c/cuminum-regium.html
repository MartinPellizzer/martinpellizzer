Cuminum regium

Cuminum regium, commonly known as the royal cumin, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are used in various cuisines for their aromatic and spicy flavor. Cuminum regium is often cultivated for its medicinal properties as well as its culinary applications.

Its growth habit is typically annual, with a preference for well-drained soil and full sunlight.
