Calamus pilosellus

Calamus pilosellus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands.

Calamus pilosellus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
