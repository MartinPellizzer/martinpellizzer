Carex emmonsii var. distincta

Carex emmonsii var. distincta, commonly known as the Emmons' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctively textured surface.

The flowering spikes are usually brownish or reddish, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
