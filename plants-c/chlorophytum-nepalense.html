Chlorophytum nepalense

Chlorophytum nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal spider plant, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a lighter midrib, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Chlorophytum nepalense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
