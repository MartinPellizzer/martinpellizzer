Celastrus buxifolius var. laxiflorus

Celastrus buxifolius var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the broadleaf bindweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elliptical, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
