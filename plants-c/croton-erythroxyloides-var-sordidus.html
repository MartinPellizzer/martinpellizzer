Croton erythroxyloides var. sordidus

Croton erythroxyloides var. sordidus, commonly known as the dark croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, spiky orange or red flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
