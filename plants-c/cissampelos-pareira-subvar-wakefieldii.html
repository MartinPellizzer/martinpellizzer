Cissampelos pareira subvar. wakefieldii

Cissampelos pareira subvar. wakefieldii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and aromatic foliage.
