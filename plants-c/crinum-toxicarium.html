Crinum toxicarium

Crinum toxicarium, commonly known as the toxic crinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It is known for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

Crinum toxicarium thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
