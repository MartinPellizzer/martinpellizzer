Cynthia amplexicaulis

Cynthia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping cypress, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive foliage, which consists of small, scale-like leaves that are tightly clustered around the stems. The plant produces inconspicuous, small flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
