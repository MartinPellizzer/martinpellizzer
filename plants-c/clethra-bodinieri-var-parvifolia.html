Clethra bodinieri var. parvifolia

Clethra bodinieri var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, elliptical leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical variety, giving it a more compact appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
