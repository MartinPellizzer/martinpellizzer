Cassia divaricata

Cassia divaricata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia divaricata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
