Crocus banaticus var. niveus

Crocus banaticus var. niveus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, moist conditions.
