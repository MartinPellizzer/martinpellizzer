Crocus syriacus

Crocus syriacus, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
