Carex aristata var. hirtiformis

Carex aristata var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces tall, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, and it has a clump-forming growth habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
