Cattleya labiata

Cattleya labiata, commonly known as the fragrant cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya labiata is prized for its strong fragrance and is a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and hybridization.
