Callianthe inaequalis

Callianthe inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

Callianthe inaequalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
