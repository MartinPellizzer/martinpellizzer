Commersonia multiloba

Commersonia multiloba, commonly known as the coast wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring. It is well adapted to saline and wind-prone environments.

Commersonia multiloba is part of the Proteaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
