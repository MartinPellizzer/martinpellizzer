Cyanus depressus

Cyanus depressus, commonly known as the downy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and gray-green, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Cyanus depressus is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
