Crepis virens subvar. uniflora

Crepis virens subvar. uniflora, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head per stem.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
