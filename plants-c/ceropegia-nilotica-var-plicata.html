Ceropegia nilotica var. plicata

Ceropegia nilotica var. plicata, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, somewhat triangular stems and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.

The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
