Centaurea pallescens var. australis

Centaurea pallescens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and typically blue or purple, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
