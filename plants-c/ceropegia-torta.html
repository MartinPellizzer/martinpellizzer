Ceropegia torta

Ceropegia torta, commonly known as the string of turtles, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with fleshy, tubular leaves that are often adorned with small, waxy bumps. The plant produces delicate, white or pale purple flowers that are highly specialized, resembling tiny, folded petals. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
