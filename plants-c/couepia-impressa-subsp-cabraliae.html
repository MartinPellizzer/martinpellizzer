Couepia impressa subsp. cabraliae

Couepia impressa subsp.

cabraliae, commonly known as the Cabral's couepia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local insect and bird populations.
