Cydonia oblonga var. pyriformis

Cydonia oblonga var. pyriformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or pyriform quince, is a variant of the common quince with a distinctively shaped fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, which gives the variety its name, and is characterized by its thick, aromatic skin and sweet, aromatic flesh. It blooms with fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by the development of its unique fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in culinary applications.
