Crotalaria goreensis subsp. macrostipula

Crotalaria goreensis subsp.

macrostipula, commonly known as the large-stippled crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
