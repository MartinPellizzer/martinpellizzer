Christia

Christia, commonly known as the "cottonwood" or "kapok" plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and are characterized by their showy, often pink or red, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with several small leaflets arranged along a central stem. Christia species produce long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils make it an important component of many ecosystems.
