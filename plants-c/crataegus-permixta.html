Crataegus permixta

Crataegus permixta, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus permixta is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, though it can be invasive in some regions.
