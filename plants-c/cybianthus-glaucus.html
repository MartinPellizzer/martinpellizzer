Cybianthus glaucus

Cybianthus glaucus, commonly known as the bluebells or sky-blue bell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Cybianthus glaucus is known for its early spring blooming period, adding a splash of color to the landscape before the summer heat arrives.
