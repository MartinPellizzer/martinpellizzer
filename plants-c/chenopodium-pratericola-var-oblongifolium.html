Chenopodium pratericola var. oblongifolium

Chenopodium pratericola var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the prairie goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, oblong to elliptic leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It is native to North America and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
