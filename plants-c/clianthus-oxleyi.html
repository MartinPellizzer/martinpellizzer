Clianthus oxleyi

Clianthus oxleyi, commonly known as the New Zealand waratah, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red, showy bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Clianthus oxleyi is a symbol of New Zealand's unique flora and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
