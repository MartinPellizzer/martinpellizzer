Coelogyne unicallosa

Coelogyne unicallosa, commonly known as the single-ridged coelogyne, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters on branched inflorescences. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers typically display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Coelogyne unicallosa prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
