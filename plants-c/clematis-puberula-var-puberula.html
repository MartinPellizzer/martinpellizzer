Clematis puberula var. puberula

Clematis puberula var. puberula, commonly known as the downy clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of small tendrils, and its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis puberula var. puberula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
