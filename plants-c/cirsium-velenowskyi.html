Cirsium velenowskyi

Cirsium velenowskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple-tinged flower heads that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing the composite flower structure typical of the family.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
