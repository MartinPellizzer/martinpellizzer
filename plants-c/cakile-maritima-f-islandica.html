Cakile maritima f. islandica

Cakile maritima f. islandica, commonly known as the island sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the North Atlantic.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has a low-growing habit with a sprawling stem that produces numerous flowering shoots.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas along coastlines.
