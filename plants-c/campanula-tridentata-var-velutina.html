Campanula tridentata var. velutina

Campanula tridentata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its specific epithet "velutina.". The plant produces bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
