Cephaelis balansae

Cephaelis balansae, commonly known as the golden flame or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique floral structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
