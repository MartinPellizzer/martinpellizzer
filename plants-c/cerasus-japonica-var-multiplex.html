Cerasus japonica var. multiplex

Cerasus japonica var. multiplex, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yoshino cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and flowering.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
