Centropogon ovalifolius var. sneidernii

Centropogon ovalifolius var. sneidernii, commonly known as the sneidernii variant of the oval-leaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
