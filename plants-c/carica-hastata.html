Carica hastata

Carica hastata, commonly known as the Mexican papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and often have a rough texture, while the fruit contains a fleshy, edible pulp surrounding the seeds.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its nutritious and flavorful fruit.
