Camellia gracilipes

Camellia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, red or pink flowers with a subtle fragrance, making it a popular ornamental species. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Camellia gracilipes is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
