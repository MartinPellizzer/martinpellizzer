Chamaecrista leiophylla

Chamaecrista leiophylla, commonly known as the silky crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features delicate, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
