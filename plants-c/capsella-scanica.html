Capsella scanica

Capsella scanica, commonly known as the field chickweed, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves and produces small white or pink flowers with four petals. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Capsella scanica is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
