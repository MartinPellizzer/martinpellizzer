Clermontia kohalae var. robusta

Clermontia kohalae var. robusta, commonly known as the Kona mountain ginger, is a rare and endemic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the winter months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high elevations.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered.
