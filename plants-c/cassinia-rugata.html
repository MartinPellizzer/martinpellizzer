Cassinia rugata

Cassinia rugata, commonly known as the roughhead cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and rough, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, clustered flowers are often surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Cassinia rugata is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
