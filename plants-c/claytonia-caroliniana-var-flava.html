Claytonia caroliniana var. flava

Claytonia caroliniana var. flava, commonly known as the yellow springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Its flowers have five petals that are often slightly notched at the tips, and the plant is adapted to moist, woodland environments.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
