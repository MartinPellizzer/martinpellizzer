Colutea cruenta

Colutea cruenta, commonly known as red balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Colutea cruenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
