Copernicia miraguama

Copernicia miraguama, commonly known as the Miraguama palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits resembling small coconuts. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Copernicia miraguama is also valued for its resilience to humid and shaded environments.
