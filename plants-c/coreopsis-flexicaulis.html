Coreopsis flexicaulis

Coreopsis flexicaulis, commonly known as the slender coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow to orange petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, linear leaves and fluffy, daisy-like blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically beneficial plant for pollinators.
