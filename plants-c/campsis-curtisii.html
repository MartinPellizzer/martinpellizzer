Campsis curtisii

Campsis curtisii, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Campsis curtisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
