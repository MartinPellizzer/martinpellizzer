Catonia cordifolia

Catonia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf catonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa.

Catonia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
