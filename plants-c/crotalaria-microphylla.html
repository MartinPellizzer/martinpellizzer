Crotalaria microphylla

Crotalaria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved cowpea or false flax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its seeds are often used as a protein source in some parts of the world.

The plant also contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
