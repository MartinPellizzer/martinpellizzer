Campanula glomerata var. elliptica

Campanula glomerata var. elliptica, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of dark green, elliptical-shaped leaves that are densely packed at the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or alpine garden settings.
