Celastrus dispermus

Celastrus dispermus, commonly known as the two-seeded staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, two-seeded capsules. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to climb by means of its tendrils.

Celastrus dispermus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
