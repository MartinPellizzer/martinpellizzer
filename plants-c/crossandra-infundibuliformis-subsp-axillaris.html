Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp. axillaris

Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the axillary crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The subspecies axillaris is distinguished by its flowers emerging from the leaf axils, giving it a unique appearance.
