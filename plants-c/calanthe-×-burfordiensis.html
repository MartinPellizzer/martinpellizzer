Calanthe × burfordiensis

Calanthe × burfordiensis, commonly known as the Burford hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Calanthe triplicata and Calanthe borealis.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
