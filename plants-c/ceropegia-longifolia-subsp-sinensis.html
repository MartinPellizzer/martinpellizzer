Ceropegia longifolia subsp. sinensis

Ceropegia longifolia subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese milkplant, is a succulent perennial plant native to southern China. It features fleshy, trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and decorative displays.
