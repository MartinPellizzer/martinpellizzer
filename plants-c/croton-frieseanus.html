Croton frieseanus

Croton frieseanus, commonly known as the Friese's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that exhibit a wide range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
