Cistus × hybridus

Cistus × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspicatus. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
