Clibadium subauriculatum

Clibadium subauriculatum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped inflorescence that resembles a small bowl, typically adorned with yellow or orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Clibadium subauriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
