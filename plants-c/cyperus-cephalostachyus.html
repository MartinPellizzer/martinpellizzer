Cyperus cephalostachyus

Cyperus cephalostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Cyperus cephalostachyus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.
