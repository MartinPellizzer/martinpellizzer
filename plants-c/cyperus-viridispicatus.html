Cyperus viridispicatus

Cyperus viridispicatus, commonly known as the green spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or pale brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Cyperus viridispicatus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
