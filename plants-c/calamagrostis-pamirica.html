Calamagrostis pamirica

Calamagrostis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
