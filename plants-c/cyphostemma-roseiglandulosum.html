Cyphostemma roseiglandulosum

Cyphostemma roseiglandulosum, commonly known as the pink glandular cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its unique feature is the presence of pinkish glands on the leaves, which give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.
