Carex chermezonii

Carex chermezonii, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex chermezonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
