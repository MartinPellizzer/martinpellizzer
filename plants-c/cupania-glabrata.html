Cupania glabrata

Cupania glabrata, commonly known as the smoothcup plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Cupania glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
