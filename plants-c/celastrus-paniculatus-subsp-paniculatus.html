Celastrus paniculatus subsp. paniculatus

Celastrus paniculatus subsp.

paniculatus, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers.

The plant bears woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
