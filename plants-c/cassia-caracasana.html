Cassia caracasana

Cassia caracasana, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia caracasana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
