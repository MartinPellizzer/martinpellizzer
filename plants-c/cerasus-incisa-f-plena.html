Cerasus incisa f. plena

Cerasus incisa f. plena, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or Yoshino cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus incisa species.

It is characterized by its profuse display of double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 4 to 8 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and turn yellow in autumn.

The botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks worldwide.
