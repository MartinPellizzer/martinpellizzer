Cistus monspeliensis var. flavescens

Cistus monspeliensis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
