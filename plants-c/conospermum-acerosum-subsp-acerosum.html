Conospermum acerosum subsp. acerosum

Conospermum acerosum subsp.

acerosum, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to the south-west of Western Australia. It is characterized by its grey-green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in spring.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands. It is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native fauna.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
