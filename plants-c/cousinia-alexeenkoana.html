Cousinia alexeenkoana

Cousinia alexeenkoana, commonly known as the Alexandrov's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia alexeenkoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
