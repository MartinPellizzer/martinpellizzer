Cornus taiwaniensis

Cornus taiwaniensis, commonly known as the Taiwan dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its showy white or pink bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface.

Cornus taiwaniensis is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
