Callicarpa clemensiorum

Callicarpa clemensiorum, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its berries are edible for birds and some mammals but are considered toxic to humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
