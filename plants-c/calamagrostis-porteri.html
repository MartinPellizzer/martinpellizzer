Calamagrostis porteri

Calamagrostis porteri, commonly known as the Porters reedgrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Calamagrostis porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
