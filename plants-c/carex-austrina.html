Carex austrina

Carex austrina, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Carex austrina is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its use in restoration projects.
