Clerodendrum palmatolobatum

Clerodendrum palmatolobatum, commonly known as the broad-lobed chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange-red berries. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
