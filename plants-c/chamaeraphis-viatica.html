Chamaeraphis viatica

Chamaeraphis viatica, commonly known as the sea bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in salty, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Chamaeraphis viatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes.
