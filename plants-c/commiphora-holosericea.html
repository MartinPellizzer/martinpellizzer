Commiphora holosericea

Commiphora holosericea, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small, deciduous tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.
