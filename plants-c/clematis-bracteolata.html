Clematis bracteolata

Clematis bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolata clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
