Chascolytrum latifolium

Chascolytrum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chascolytrum, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Native to South America, particularly the Andean regions, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, and it has adapted to survive in high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
