Cerastium boissieri var. strictiforme

Cerastium boissieri var. strictiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats, with leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in early summer, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained conditions.
