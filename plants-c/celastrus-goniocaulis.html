Celastrus goniocaulis

Celastrus goniocaulis, commonly known as the warty staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in small, warty protuberances. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
