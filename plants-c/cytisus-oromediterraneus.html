Cytisus oromediterraneus

Cytisus oromediterraneus, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring. The plant has slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are trifoliate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus oromediterraneus is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
