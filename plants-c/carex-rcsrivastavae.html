Carex rcsrivastavae

Carex rcsrivastavae, commonly known as the R. Chaudhary Carex, is a rare sedge species native to the wetlands of northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
