Carex kawakamii

Carex kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Carex kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
