Canna patens var. limbata

Canna patens var. limbata, commonly known as the broadleaf canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, providing a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic, tropical look in their landscapes.
