Convolvulus linkii

Convolvulus linkii, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its helical stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of violet or blue, which typically open in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus linkii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
