Cleisostoma subviolaceum

Cleisostoma subviolaceum, commonly known as the violet-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant typically has short, leathery leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
