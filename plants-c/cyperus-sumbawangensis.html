Cyperus sumbawangensis

Cyperus sumbawangensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense spikes of tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus sumbawangensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
