Chamaecytisus mollis

Chamaecytisus mollis, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to western Europe.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense thickets. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Chamaecytisus mollis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
