Conophytum sellatum

Conophytum sellatum, commonly known as the "Rainbow Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum sellatum is prized for its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves.
