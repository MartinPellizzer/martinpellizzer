Carex lunelliana

Carex lunelliana, commonly known as the moonlight sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, Carex lunelliana is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
