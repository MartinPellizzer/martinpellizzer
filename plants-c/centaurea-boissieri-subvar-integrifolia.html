Centaurea boissieri subvar. integrifolia

Centaurea boissieri subvar. integrifolia, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple composite flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
