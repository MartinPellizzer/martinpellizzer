Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. venturii

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

venturii, commonly known as gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, greenish flowers.

The plant has serrated leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various habitats across the continent.
