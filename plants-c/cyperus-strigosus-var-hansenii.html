Cyperus strigosus var. hansenii

Cyperus strigosus var. hansenii, commonly known as the Hansen's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of wetland habitats, contributing to its ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
