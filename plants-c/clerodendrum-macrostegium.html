Clerodendrum macrostegium

Clerodendrum macrostegium, commonly known as the large-leaved glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
