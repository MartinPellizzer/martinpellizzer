Calamintha officinalis

Calamintha officinalis, commonly known as hedge hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is known for its fragrant, mint-like aroma and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Calamintha officinalis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
