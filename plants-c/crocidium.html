Crocidium

Crocidium, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Native to Australia, these plants thrive in dry, sunny conditions and are often found in rocky or sandy soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
