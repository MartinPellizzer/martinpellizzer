Caragana bongardiana

Caragana bongardiana, commonly known as the Bongard shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 10-20 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and cold climates.

Caragana bongardiana is often used for erosion control and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
