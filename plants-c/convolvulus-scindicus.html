Convolvulus scindicus

Convolvulus scindicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
