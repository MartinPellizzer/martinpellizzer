Cyperus amentaceus

Cyperus amentaceus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, composed of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Cyperus amentaceus has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, making it monoecious.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
