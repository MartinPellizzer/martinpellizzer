Cucumis sativus var. turcicus

Cucumis sativus var. turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It typically has a slender, elongated shape with a smooth, waxy skin that is usually pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and develops edible, crisp fruits that are often smaller than standard cucumbers. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications across various cuisines.
