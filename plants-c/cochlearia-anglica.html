Cochlearia anglica

Cochlearia anglica, commonly known as the British cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Cochlearia anglica is often found growing in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
