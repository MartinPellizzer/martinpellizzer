Clusia obovata

Clusia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed glorybark, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Clusia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
