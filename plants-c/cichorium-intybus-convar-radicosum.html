Cichorium intybus convar. radicosum

Cichorium intybus convar. radicosum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot and basal leaves that are often deeply lobed or toothed. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in salads, while the roots are roasted and used as a coffee substitute.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential benefits for digestion and liver health.
