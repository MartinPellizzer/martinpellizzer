Cyanotis axillaris

Cyanotis axillaris, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Cyanotis axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
