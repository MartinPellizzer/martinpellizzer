Carex sagei

Carex sagei, commonly known as Sagebrush Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions and alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex sagei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
