Corone quinquevulnera

Corone quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound crown, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its name refers to the five wounds or marks on its stem, which are believed to resemble the wounds of Christ in Christian iconography.

Corone quinquevulnera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.
