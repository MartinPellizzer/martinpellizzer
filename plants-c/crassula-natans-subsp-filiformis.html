Crassula natans subsp. filiformis

Crassula natans subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the thread-leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features small, thread-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in a rosette formation.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
