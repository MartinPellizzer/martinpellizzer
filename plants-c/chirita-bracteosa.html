Chirita bracteosa

Chirita bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa gesneriad, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse and indoor cultivation.
