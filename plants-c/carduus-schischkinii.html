Carduus schischkinii

Carduus schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are followed by prickly, spherical fruits. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
