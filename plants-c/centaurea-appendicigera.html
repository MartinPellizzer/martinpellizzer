Centaurea appendicigera

Centaurea appendicigera, commonly known as the appendiculate centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features slender, erect stems and produces bright yellow to orange flower heads.

Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
