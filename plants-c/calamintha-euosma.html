Calamintha euosma

Calamintha euosma, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Calamintha euosma is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in herbal remedies.
