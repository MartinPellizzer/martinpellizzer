Cogswellia suksdorfii

Cogswellia suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, it is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or orange blooms that appear at the top of the stem during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
