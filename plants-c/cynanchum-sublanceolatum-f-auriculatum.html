Cynanchum sublanceolatum f. auriculatum

Cynanchum sublanceolatum f. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate milkweed, is a variant of the milkweed plant characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves.

This plant belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are typically lanceolate with prominent auriculate lobes at the base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Cynanchum.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
