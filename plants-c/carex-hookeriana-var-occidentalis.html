Carex hookeriana var. occidentalis

Carex hookeriana var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and stiff, with a dense, clumping growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
