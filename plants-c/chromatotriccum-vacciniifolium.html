Chromatotriccum vacciniifolium

Chromatotriccum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Chromatotriccum vacciniifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
