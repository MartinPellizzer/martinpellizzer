Chondrorhyncha aurantiaca

Chondrorhyncha aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange coralroot, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, coral-like appearance, with fleshy, orange-red roots that resemble a coral structure. The plant lacks chlorophyll and is entirely dependent on mycorrhizal fungi for its nutrients, making it a myco-heterotroph. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in a spike and lacking the showy petals seen in many other orchids.

Due to its specialized habitat and conservation status, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.
