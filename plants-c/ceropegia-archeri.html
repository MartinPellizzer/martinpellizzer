Ceropegia archeri

Ceropegia archeri, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and small, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or grayish hue. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia archeri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
