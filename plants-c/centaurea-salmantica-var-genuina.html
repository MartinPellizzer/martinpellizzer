Centaurea salmantica var. genuina

Centaurea salmantica var. genuina, commonly known as the Salamanca centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed heads that are distinctive in appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
