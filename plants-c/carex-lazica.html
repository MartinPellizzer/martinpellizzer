Carex lazica

Carex lazica, commonly known as the Lazica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex lazica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
