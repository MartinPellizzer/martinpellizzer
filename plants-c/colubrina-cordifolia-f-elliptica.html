Colubrina cordifolia f. elliptica

Colubrina cordifolia f. elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange to red berries. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage and berries make it a valuable plant for wildlife, particularly birds.
