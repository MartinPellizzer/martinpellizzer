Cinnamomum camphora subvar. hosyo

Cinnamomum camphora subvar. hosyo, commonly known as the Japanese camphor tree, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, pale yellow bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. Its leaves and bark contain camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in pharmacology and aromatherapy.
