Canarium kinabaluense

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by oval, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
