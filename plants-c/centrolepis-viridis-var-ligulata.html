Centrolepis viridis var. ligulata

Centrolepis viridis var. ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centrolepis, is a unique plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
