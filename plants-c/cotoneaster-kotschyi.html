Cotoneaster kotschyi

Cotoneaster kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of red berries that persist through winter. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers are typically white or pink and appear in spring, followed by the development of its showy fruits.

Cotoneaster kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
