Cassia tula

Cassia tula, commonly known as golden shower tree or Indian laburnum, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia tula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
