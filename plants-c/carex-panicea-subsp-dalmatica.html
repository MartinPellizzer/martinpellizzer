Carex panicea subsp. dalmatica

Carex panicea subsp.

dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant features grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, triangular flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Dalmatian sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
