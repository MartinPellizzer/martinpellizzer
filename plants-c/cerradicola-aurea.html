Cerradicola aurea

Cerradicola aurea, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble misty clouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in dense forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cerradicola aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
