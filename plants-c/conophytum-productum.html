Conophytum productum

Conophytum productum, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.

Conophytum productum is also known for its ability to form dense colonies, enhancing its ornamental value in dry landscapes.
