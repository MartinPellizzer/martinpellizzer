Calamus ruptilis var. acaulescens

Calamus ruptilis var. acaulescens, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in wet, humid environments such as marshes and swamps, often forming dense stands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens and wetland restoration projects.
