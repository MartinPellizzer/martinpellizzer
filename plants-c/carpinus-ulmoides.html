Carpinus ulmoides

Carpinus ulmoides, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The American hornbeam is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
