Croton trilobatus

Croton trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
