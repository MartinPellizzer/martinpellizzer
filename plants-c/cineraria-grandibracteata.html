Cineraria grandibracteata

Cineraria grandibracteata, commonly known as the grandibracteate cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria grandibracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
