Cornus ulotricha

Cornus ulotricha, commonly known as the hairy cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Cornus ulotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
