Campanula rumeliana subsp. chalcidica

Campanula rumeliana subsp.

chalcidica, commonly known as the Chalcidic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
