Chrysanthemum burnatii

Chrysanthemum burnatii, commonly known as the Burnatii chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays a range of flower colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
