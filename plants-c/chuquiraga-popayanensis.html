Chuquiraga popayanensis

Chuquiraga popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan chuchuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.
