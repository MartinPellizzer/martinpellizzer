Cyphostemma adenocarpum

Cyphostemma adenocarpum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
