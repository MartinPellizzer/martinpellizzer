It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Candollea fissiloba is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
