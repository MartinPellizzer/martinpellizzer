Croton pycnocephalus var. echinulatus

Croton pycnocephalus var. echinulatus, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
