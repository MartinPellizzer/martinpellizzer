Crotonogyne parvifolia

Crotonogyne parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often with yellow or greenish hues.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
