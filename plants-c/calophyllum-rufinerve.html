Calophyllum rufinerve

Calophyllum rufinerve, commonly known as the red-veined calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
