Cyperus iria f. chrysomelinus

Cyperus iria f. chrysomelinus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a variant of the Cyperus iria species, characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flowers contribute to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist soil.
