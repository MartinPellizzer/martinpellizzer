Cuphea sessilifolia var. strigosa

Cuphea sessilifolia var. strigosa, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
