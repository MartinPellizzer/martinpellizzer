Chamaecrista hedysaroides

Chamaecrista hedysaroides, commonly known as the silverleaf cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often silvery or grayish in appearance, providing a striking contrast to its bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
