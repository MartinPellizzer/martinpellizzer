Cassia glauca var. suffruticosa

Cassia glauca var. suffruticosa, commonly known as golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry regions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The seeds of Cassia glauca var. suffruticosa are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind.
