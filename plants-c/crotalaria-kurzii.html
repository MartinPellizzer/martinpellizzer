Crotalaria kurzii

Crotalaria kurzii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crotalaria kurzii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and ecological benefits.
