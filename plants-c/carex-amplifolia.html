Carex amplifolia

Carex amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marsh environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
