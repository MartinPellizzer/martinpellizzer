Clematis × henryi

Clematis × henryi, commonly known as Henry's clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis × henryi is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
