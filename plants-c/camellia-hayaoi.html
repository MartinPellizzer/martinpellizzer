Camellia hayaoi

Camellia hayaoi, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Camellia hayaoi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden areas.
