Corchorus aestuans

Corchorus aestuans, commonly known as swamp cotton or water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist or swampy soils. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used historically for textile production and medicinal purposes.

Corchorus aestuans plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
