Cerastium nervosum

Cerastium nervosum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium nervosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological landscaping.
