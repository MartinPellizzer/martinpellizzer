Cardamine macrostachya

Cardamine macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, similar to other members of the Brassicaceae family. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in dense clusters atop the stems.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
