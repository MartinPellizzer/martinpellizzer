Cremnophila nutans

Cremnophila nutans, commonly known as the nodding flameflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of vibrant orange-red, nodding flowers that resemble flames. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Cremnophila nutans is valued in horticulture for its bold, showy blooms and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
