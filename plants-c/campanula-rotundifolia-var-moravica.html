Campanula rotundifolia var. moravica

Campanula rotundifolia var. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.

The variety moravica is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flower size compared to the typical form of Campanula rotundifolia.
