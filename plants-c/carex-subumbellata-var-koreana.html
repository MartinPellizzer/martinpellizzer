Carex subumbellata var. koreana

Carex subumbellata var. koreana, commonly known as Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Korean sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
