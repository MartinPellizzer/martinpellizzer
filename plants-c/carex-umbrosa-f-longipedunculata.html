Carex umbrosa f. longipedunculata

Carex umbrosa f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled shadow sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of long peduncles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
