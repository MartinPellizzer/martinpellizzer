Crepidomanes campanulatum

Crepidomanes campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Crepidomanes, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from blue to violet, which are a key feature in its botanical identification. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
