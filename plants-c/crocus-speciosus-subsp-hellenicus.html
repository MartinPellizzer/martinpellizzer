Crocus speciosus subsp. hellenicus

Crocus speciosus subsp.

hellenicus, commonly known as the Hellenic crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and dry soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and floral arrangements.
