Culcitium cocuyanum

Culcitium cocuyanum, commonly known as the cocuyanum plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
