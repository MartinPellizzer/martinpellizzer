Chassalia gracilis

Chassalia gracilis, commonly known as slender chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a distinctive member of its genus.

Chassalia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
