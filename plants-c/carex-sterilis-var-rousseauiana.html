Carex sterilis var. rousseauiana

Carex sterilis var. rousseauiana, commonly known as Rousseau's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
