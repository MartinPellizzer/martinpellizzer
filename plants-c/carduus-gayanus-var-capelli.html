Carduus gayanus var. capelli

Carduus gayanus var. capelli, commonly known as the Capelli thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with coarse, prickly hairs. The plant produces large, purple to pinkish composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often with spiny margins, and are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and wastelands.
