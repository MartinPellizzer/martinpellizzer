Ceratostylis triloba

Ceratostylis triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
