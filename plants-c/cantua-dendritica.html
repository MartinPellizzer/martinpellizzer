Cantua dendritica

Cantua dendritica, commonly known as the tree heath, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate.

Cantua dendritica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
