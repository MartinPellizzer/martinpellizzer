Campanula rotundifolia var. laxiflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, broad leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
