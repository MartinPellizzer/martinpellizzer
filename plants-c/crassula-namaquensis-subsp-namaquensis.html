Crassula namaquensis subsp. namaquensis

Crassula namaquensis subsp.

namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua Crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light exposure.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
