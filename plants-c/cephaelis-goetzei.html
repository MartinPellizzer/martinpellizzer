Cephaelis goetzei

Cephaelis goetzei, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy yellow or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Cephaelis goetzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
