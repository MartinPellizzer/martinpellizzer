Chrysanthemum trapezuntinum

Chrysanthemum trapezuntinum, commonly known as the Crimean chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and Crimea.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces numerous yellow to orange flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
