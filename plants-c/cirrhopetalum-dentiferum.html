Cirrhopetalum dentiferum

Cirrhopetalum dentiferum, commonly known as the toothed petal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without being parasitic.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
