Cyperus pennatiformis var. pennatiformis

Cyperus pennatiformis var. pennatiformis, commonly known as the grass-like sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its ability to form dense clumps. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ornamental gardening.
