Cyperus guanipensis

Cyperus guanipensis, commonly known as the Guanipensian flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in parts of South America. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its morphology features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus guanipensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
