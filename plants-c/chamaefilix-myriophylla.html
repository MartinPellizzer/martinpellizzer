Chamaefilix myriophylla

Chamaefilix myriophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved rock rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as rocky outcrops and gravelly plains.

Chamaefilix myriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
