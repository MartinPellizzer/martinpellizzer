Caraipa llanorum

Caraipa llanorum, commonly known as the Llanorum thornbush, is a shrub native to the grasslands of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Caraipa llanorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
