Convolvulus inconspicuus

Convolvulus inconspicuus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing or ascending.

The flowers are usually pale blue to white, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
