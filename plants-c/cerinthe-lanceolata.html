Cerinthe lanceolata

Cerinthe lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf cerinthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Cerinthe lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
