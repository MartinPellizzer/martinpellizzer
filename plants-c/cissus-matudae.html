Cissus matudae

Cissus matudae, commonly known as the Matudae devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus matudae is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
