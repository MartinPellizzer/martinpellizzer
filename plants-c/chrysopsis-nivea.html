Chrysopsis nivea

Chrysopsis nivea, commonly known as the snowflake dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white ray florets and a yellow disk flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

Chrysopsis nivea is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in spring.
