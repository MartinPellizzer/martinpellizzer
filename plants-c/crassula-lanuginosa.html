Crassula lanuginosa

Crassula lanuginosa, commonly known as the Wooly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Crassula lanuginosa thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for low-maintenance gardening.
