Calamagrostis curta

Calamagrostis curta, commonly known as dwarf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a decorative plant.
