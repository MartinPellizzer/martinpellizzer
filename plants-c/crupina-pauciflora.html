Crupina pauciflora

Crupina pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered crupina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch near the base, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Crupina pauciflora is often found in coastal areas and can be invasive in some regions outside its native range.
