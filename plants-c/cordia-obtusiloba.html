Cordia obtusiloba

Cordia obtusiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cordia obtusiloba is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
