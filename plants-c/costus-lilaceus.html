Costus lilaceus

Costus lilaceus, commonly known as the lilac costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its ornamental, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white, pink, and lilac, are clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stems.

Costus lilaceus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
