Crataegus turcicus

Crataegus turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and features thorny branches, which are characteristic of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
