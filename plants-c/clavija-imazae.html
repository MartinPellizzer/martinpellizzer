Clavija imazae

Clavija imazae, commonly known as the Mexican mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
