Cinnamomum longipedicellatum

Cinnamomum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long pedicellate cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that contain a single seed, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are also valued for their essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.
