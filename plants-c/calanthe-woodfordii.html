Calanthe woodfordii

Calanthe woodfordii, commonly known as Woodford's calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe woodfordii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
