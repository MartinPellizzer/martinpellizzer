Cleome brachycarpa var. filicaulis

Cleome brachycarpa var. filicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
