Cassia humboldtiana

Cassia humboldtiana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds.

Cassia humboldtiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
