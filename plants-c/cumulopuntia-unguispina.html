Cumulopuntia unguispina

Cumulopuntia unguispina, commonly known as the red-tipped cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the tips. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
