Coleus variifolius

Coleus variifolius, commonly known as the variegated coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, velvety leaves that display a variety of patterns, including shades of green, red, and white. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically appearing in shades of purple or pink.

Coleus variifolius is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
