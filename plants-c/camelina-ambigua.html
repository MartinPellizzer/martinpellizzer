Camelina ambigua

Camelina ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous camelina, is an annual herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family members. Camelina ambigua is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal practices and have potential applications in modern agriculture.
