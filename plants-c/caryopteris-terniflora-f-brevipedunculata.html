Caryopteris terniflora f. brevipedunculata

Caryopteris terniflora f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the compact blue mistflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, creating a dense, mist-like appearance. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
