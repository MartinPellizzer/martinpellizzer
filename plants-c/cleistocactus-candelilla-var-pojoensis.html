Cleistocactus candelilla var. pojoensis

Cleistocactus candelilla var. pojoensis, commonly known as the Pojoensis candle cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often branch into a candle-like appearance, covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
