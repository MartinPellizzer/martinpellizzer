Coelogyne saccolabium

Coelogyne saccolabium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and fleshy, club-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette around the pseudobulbs.

Coelogyne saccolabium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
