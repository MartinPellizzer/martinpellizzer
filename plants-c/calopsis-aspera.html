Calopsis aspera

Calopsis aspera, commonly known as the rough calopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
