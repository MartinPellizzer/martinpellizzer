Carex glauca var. erythrostachys

Carex glauca var. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a clumping growth habit. The plant produces cylindrical spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers, which are borne on tall, reddish stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
