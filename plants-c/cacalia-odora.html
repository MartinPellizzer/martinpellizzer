Cacalia odora

Cacalia odora, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor.

Cacalia odora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
