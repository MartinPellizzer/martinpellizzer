Cerinthe gymnandra var. iberica

Cerinthe gymnandra var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet tubular flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a resilient plant in Mediterranean climates.
