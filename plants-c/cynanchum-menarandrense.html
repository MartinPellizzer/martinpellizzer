Cynanchum menarandrense

Cynanchum menarandrense, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed vine, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive corona, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Cynanchum menarandrense is often found in moist, shaded environments in forested areas.
