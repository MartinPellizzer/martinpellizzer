Clematis suaveolens

Clematis suaveolens, commonly known as sweet clematis, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
