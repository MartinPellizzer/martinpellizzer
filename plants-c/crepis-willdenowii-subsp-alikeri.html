Crepis willdenowii subsp. alikeri

Crepis willdenowii subsp.

alikeri, commonly known as the alikeri thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a single head, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
