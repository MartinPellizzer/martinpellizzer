Caulopsis

Caulopsis, commonly known as the "milkweed of the desert," is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive corolla shape. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common trait among members of its family.

Caulopsis is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
