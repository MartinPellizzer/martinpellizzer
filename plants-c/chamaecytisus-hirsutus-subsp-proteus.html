Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. proteus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

proteus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and coastal gardens.
