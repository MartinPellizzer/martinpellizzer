Cuphea pulcherrima

Cuphea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower or red hot poker, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, vibrantly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last from spring through fall.
