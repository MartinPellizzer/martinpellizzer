Cnidoscolus albibracteatus

Cnidoscolus albibracteatus, commonly known as the white bract cnidoscolus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, spiny fruits. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's unique botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable in local herbal practices.
