Chloris polydactyla subsp. multiradiata

Chloris polydactyla subsp.

multiradiata, commonly known as the multiradiate grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
