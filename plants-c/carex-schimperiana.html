Carex schimperiana

Carex schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex schimperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
