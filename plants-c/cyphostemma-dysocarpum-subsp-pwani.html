Cyphostemma dysocarpum subsp. pwani

Cyphostemma dysocarpum subsp.

pwani, commonly known as the Kwale moonvine, is a climbing plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
