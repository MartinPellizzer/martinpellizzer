Celosia spathulata

Celosia spathulata, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange and pink. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Celosia spathulata is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
