Cyathea jacobsii

Cyathea jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance.

Cyathea jacobsii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
