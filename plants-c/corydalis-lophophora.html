Corydalis lophophora

Corydalis lophophora, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Corydalis lophophora is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
