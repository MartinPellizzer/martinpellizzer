Carex paniculata subsp. squarrosa

Carex paniculata subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are often grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
