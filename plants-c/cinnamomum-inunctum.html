Cinnamomum inunctum

Cinnamomum inunctum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum inunctum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.
