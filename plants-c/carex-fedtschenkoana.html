Carex fedtschenkoana

Carex fedtschenkoana, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
