Cormus domestica var. upsaliensis

Cormus domestica var. upsaliensis, commonly known as the Upsaliensis variety of the common cormus, is a lesser-known botanical variant of the common cormus.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including a more compact growth habit and distinct leaf patterns compared to the standard variety. This variant is native to specific regions in northern Europe, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its botanical significance lies in its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to different ecological niches.

Researchers are increasingly studying it for its possible applications in horticulture and conservation efforts.
