Commelina africana var. diffusa

Commelina africana var. diffusa, commonly known as the African spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various insects and its potential use in traditional medicine.
