Crocosmia aurea subsp. aurea

Crocosmia aurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the orange lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features striking orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense, sword-shaped inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly folded along the midrib. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
