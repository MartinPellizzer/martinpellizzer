Calamagrostis arundinacea var. heterogluma

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. heterogluma, commonly known as the silver reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscape design.
