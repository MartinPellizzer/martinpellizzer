Carex rosea var. staminata

Carex rosea var. staminata, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive pinkish hue, particularly on the inflorescence. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands or wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
