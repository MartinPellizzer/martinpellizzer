Cirsium undulatum

Cirsium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's structure and appearance make it well adapted to its environment, allowing it to thrive in challenging conditions.
