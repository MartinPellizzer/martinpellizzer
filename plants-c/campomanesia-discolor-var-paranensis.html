Campomanesia discolor var. paranensis

Campomanesia discolor var. paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is commonly used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
