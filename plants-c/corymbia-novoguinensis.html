Corymbia novoguinensis

Corymbia novoguinensis, commonly known as the New Zealand red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in a range of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
