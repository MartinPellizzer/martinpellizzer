Carica × heilbornii var. pentagona

Carica × heilbornii var. pentagona, commonly known as the pentagona papaya, is a hybrid plant within the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal shape of the fruit, which sets it apart from other papaya varieties. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous shrub with large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is fleshy, sweet, and edible, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
