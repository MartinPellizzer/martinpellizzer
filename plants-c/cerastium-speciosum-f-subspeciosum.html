Cerastium speciosum f. subspeciosum

Cerastium speciosum f. subspeciosum, commonly known as the showy mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
