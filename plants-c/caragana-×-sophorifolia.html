Caragana × sophorifolia

Caragana × sophorifolia, commonly known as the hybrid caragana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 leaflets, and its yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for reclamation of degraded soils.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
