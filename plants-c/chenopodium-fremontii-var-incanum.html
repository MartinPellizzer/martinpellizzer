Chenopodium fremontii var. incanum

Chenopodium fremontii var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage plant for wildlife.
