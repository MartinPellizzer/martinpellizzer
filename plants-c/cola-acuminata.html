Cola acuminata

Cola acuminata, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to western and central Africa, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsules containing seeds. The fruit is often used to extract caffeine and other alkaloids, which are found in the seeds.

This plant plays an important role in both traditional medicine and commercial caffeine production.
