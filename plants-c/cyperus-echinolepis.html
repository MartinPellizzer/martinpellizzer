Cyperus echinolepis

Cyperus echinolepis, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its slender, triangular stems are covered in fine, bristly hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus echinolepis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
