Clinopodium barbatum

Clinopodium barbatum, commonly known as hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Clinopodium barbatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
