Chloris pectinata var. fallax

Chloris pectinata var. fallax, commonly known as the silver grass or fallax chloris, is a variant of the common chloris grass.

It is a perennial grass native to Australia, characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic value.
