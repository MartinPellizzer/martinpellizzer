Commelina nudiflora var. werneana

Commelina nudiflora var. werneana, commonly known as the naked-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with three petals and three sepals, which are typically clustered in a spike at the top of the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Commelina nudiflora var. werneana is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils, making it an important species in ecological studies.
