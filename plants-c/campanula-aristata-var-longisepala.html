Campanula aristata var. longisepala

Campanula aristata var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in rocky or alpine habitats. Its sepals are notably elongated, giving it the specific epithet "longisepala.".

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
