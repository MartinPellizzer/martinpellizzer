Crocodilium peregrinum

Crocodilium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering crocodile plant, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that resemble the scales of a crocodile. The plant produces colorful, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unusual appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare houseplants.
