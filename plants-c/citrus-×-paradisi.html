Citrus × paradisi

Citrus × paradisi, commonly known as the grapefruit.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit resulting from the cross between the sweet orange and the pomelo. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically round, with a thick, yellow-orange rind and a segmented, juicy interior.

The botanical features of Citrus × paradisi make it well-suited for subtropical climates and widely cultivated for its fruit.
