Cyperus steudelianus

Cyperus steudelianus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Cyperus steudelianus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
