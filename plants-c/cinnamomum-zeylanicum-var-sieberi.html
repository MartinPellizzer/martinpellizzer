Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. sieberi

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. sieberi, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested and used as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its commercial value.
