Coleosanthus parvulus

Coleosanthus parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf sea holly, is a small flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions.

Coleosanthus parvulus is a hardy plant that requires minimal water once established, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
