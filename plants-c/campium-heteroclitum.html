Campium heteroclitum

Campium heteroclitum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.

This plant is often found in moist environments and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
