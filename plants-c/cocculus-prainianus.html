Cocculus prainianus

Cocculus prainianus, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its botanical features include a unique leaf structure with three to five leaflets and a climbing habit that allows it to grow along trees and shrubs.

The species is often noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
