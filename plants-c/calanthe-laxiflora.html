Calanthe laxiflora

Calanthe laxiflora, commonly known as the lax-flowered calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Calanthe laxiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
