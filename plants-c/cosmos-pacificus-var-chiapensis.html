Cosmos pacificus var. chiapensis

Cosmos pacificus var. chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan cosmos, is a striking flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to lavender petals and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
