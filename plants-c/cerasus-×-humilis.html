Cerasus × humilis

Cerasus × humilis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a hybrid species of the cherry genus.

It is characterized by its small stature, typically reaching heights of less than 3 meters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of other cherry species. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
