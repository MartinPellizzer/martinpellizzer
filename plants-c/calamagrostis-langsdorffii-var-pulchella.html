Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. pulchella

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pinkish inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
