Carex benthamiana

Carex benthamiana, commonly known as Bentham's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex benthamiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
