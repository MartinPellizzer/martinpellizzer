Corneria spathoides

Corneria spathoides, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, come in a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.
