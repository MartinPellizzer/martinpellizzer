Cynanchum foetidum

Cynanchum foetidum, commonly known as stinkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Cynanchum foetidum is characterized by its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
