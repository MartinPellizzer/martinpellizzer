Crataegus nigra f. pappii

Crataegus nigra f. pappii, commonly known as the hairy black hawthorn, is a variant of the black hawthorn characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and fruits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, typically found in woodland edges and moist soils. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and often bear a dense covering of fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
