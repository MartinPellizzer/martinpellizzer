Crataegus inducta

Crataegus inducta, commonly known as the inductive hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Crataegus inducta is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
