Craspedia glauca var. gracilis

Craspedia glauca var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender glaucous everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers that are often borne in dense, spherical clusters. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray bloom, which gives the plant its "glaucous" appearance. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
