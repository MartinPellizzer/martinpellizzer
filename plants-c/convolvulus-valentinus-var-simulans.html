Convolvulus valentinus var. simulans

Convolvulus valentinus var. simulans, commonly known as the Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
