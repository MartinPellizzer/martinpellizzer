Circaea cordata

Circaea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
