Coronaria

Coronaria, commonly known as the crown flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically feature a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plants are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in meadows and grasslands. Many species within the genus are valued for their ornamental appeal and are used in gardens and floral arrangements.

Botanically, they exhibit a variety of growth forms, including annuals, perennials, and shrubs.
