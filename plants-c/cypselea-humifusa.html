Cypselea humifusa

Cypselea humifusa, commonly known as the creeping cypress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a mat-forming habit with small, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
