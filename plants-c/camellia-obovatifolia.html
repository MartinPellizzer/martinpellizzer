Camellia obovatifolia

Camellia obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical features include compound inflorescences and a preference for acidic, well-drained soil.

Camellia obovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
