Cestrum parqui var. spurium

Cestrum parqui var. spurium, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine or sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant is renowned for its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, sweet scent.
