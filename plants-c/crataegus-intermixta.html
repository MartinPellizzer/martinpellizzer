Crataegus intermixta

Crataegus intermixta, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata, which contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability.

Its presence in various habitats makes it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
