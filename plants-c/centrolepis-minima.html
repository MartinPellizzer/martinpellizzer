Centrolepis minima

Centrolepis minima, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella plant, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and its unique, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Centrolepis minima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact size.
