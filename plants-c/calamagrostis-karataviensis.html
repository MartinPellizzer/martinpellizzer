Calamagrostis karataviensis

Calamagrostis karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatav Valley reed, is a tall perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
