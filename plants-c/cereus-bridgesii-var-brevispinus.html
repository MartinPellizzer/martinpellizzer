Cereus bridgesii var. brevispinus

Cereus bridgesii var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and sparse, short spines that give it its common name. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
