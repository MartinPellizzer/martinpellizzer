Ceropegia tubiformis

Ceropegia tubiformis, commonly known as the hooded milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, hood-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves and has a unique inflorescence structure that resembles a cluster of small bells. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia tubiformis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
