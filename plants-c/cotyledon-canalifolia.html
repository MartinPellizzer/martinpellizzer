Cotyledon canalifolia

Cotyledon canalifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating to store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its growth habit is compact, often forming a rosette shape.

Cotyledon canalifolia is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
