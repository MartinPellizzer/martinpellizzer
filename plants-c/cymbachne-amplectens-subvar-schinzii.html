Cymbachne amplectens subvar. schinzii

Cymbachne amplectens subvar. schinzii, commonly known as the Schinz's flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

This subvariety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
