Calytrix ecalycata subsp. brevis

Calytrix ecalycata subsp.

brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters.

The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stems. This species is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal exposure.
