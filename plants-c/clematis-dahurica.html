Clematis dahurica

Clematis dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with palmate leaves that are typically 5-7 lobed. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, often with a white throat, during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively hardy, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
