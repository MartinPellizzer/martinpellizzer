Centrosema dasyanthum

Centrosema dasyanthum, commonly known as the hairy centrosema, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
