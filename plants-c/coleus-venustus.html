Coleus venustus

Coleus venustus, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that comes in a variety of colors, including shades of green, red, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured surface that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaf patterns.

The plant prefers partial to full shade and well-draining soil to flourish.
