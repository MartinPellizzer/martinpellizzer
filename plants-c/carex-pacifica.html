Carex pacifica

Carex pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, dark green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spike-like structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forested areas.

Carex pacifica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
