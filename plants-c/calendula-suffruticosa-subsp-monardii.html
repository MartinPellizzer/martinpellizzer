Calendula suffruticosa subsp. monardii

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

monardii, commonly known as the American goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
