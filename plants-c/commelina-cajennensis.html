Commelina cajennensis

Commelina cajennensis, commonly known as the Jamaican arrowroot or the purple arum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple spatulate leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often surrounded by a showy bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Commelina cajennensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
