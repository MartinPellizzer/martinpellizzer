Campanula punctata f. impunctata

Campanula punctata f. impunctata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a cultivar of the campanula genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
