Cephaelis elata

Cephaelis elata, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branching.

Cephaelis elata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
