Croton pycnophyllus

Croton pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
