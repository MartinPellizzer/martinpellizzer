Clinopodium domingense

Clinopodium domingense, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Clinopodium domingense is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Mexican cuisine and herbal remedies.
