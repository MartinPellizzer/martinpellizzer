Centaurea molesworthiae

Centaurea molesworthiae, commonly known as the Molesworth's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits known as achenes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
