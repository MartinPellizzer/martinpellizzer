Carex sychnocephala

Carex sychnocephala, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is native to parts of North America. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex sychnocephala plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
