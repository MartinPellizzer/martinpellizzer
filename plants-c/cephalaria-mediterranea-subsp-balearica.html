Cephalaria mediterranea subsp. balearica

Cephalaria mediterranea subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in coastal and inland habitats across the region.
