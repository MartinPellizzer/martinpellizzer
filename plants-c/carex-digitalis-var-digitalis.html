Carex digitalis var. digitalis

Carex digitalis var. digitalis, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically dark green and arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
