Centaurea hypoleuca

Centaurea hypoleuca, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Centaurea hypoleuca is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
