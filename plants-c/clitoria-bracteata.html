Clitoria bracteata

Clitoria bracteata, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple pea-like flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its trifoliate leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
