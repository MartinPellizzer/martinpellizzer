Carduus viridiflorus

Carduus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or greenish flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in a compact cluster.

Carduus viridiflorus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
