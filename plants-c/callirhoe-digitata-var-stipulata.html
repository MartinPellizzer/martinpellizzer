Callirhoe digitata var. stipulata

Callirhoe digitata var. stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens and native plant landscapes.
