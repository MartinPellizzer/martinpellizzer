Cakile harperi

Cakile harperi, commonly known as the coastal rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb with yellow flowers and is often found growing in sandy or rocky coastal areas. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lobed or divided, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cakile harperi is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
