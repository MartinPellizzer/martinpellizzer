Calceolaria adenanthera subsp. bracteata

Calceolaria adenanthera subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate slipper orchid, is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant produces showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. Native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador, it thrives in high-altitude, humid environments.

It is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
