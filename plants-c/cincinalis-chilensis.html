Cincinalis chilensis

Cincinalis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean cypress, is a species of coniferous tree native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches, giving it a smooth, almost bark-like appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain the plant's seeds.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
