Crataegus apposita

Crataegus apposita, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.

The plant is often found in open woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
