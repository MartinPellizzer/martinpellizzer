Clinopodium brevifolium

Clinopodium brevifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and they emit a strong minty aroma when crushed.

Clinopodium brevifolium is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
