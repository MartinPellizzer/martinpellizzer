Chysis aurea var. bractescens

Chysis aurea var. bractescens, commonly known as the golden chysis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a showy appearance. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
