Calanthe triantherifera

Calanthe triantherifera, commonly known as the golden calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
