Citrosma lanceolata

Citrosma lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf citrosma, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
