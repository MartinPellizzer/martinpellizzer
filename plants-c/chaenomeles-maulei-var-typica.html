Chaenomeles maulei var. typica

Chaenomeles maulei var. typica, commonly known as the Maule cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its ornamental, red or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
