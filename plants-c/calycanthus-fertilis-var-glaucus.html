Calycanthus fertilis var. glaucus

Calycanthus fertilis var. glaucus, commonly known as the blueflower sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a glossy appearance. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, reddish-bark that becomes more rugged with age.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
