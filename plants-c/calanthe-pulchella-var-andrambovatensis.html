Calanthe pulchella var. andrambovatensis

Calanthe pulchella var. andrambovatensis, commonly known as the Andrambovatensis Calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This variety is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in the wild.
