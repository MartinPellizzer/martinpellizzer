Cymophora accedens

Cymophora accedens, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces smooth, oval-shaped fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cymophora accedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
