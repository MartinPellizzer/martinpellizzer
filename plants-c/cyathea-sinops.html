Cyathea sinops

Cyathea sinops, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyathea sinops plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
