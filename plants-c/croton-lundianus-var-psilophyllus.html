Croton lundianus var. psilophyllus

Croton lundianus var. psilophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
