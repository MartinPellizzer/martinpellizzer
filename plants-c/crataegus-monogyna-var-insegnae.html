Crataegus monogyna var. insegnae

Crataegus monogyna var. insegnae, commonly known as the white thorn.

It is a variant of the common hawthorn, characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various birds and small mammals.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows across Europe.
