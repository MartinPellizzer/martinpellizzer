Cinna purshii

Cinna purshii, commonly known as the Pursh's cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Cinna purshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
