Chamaecrista botryoides

Chamaecrista botryoides, commonly known as the hairy bean or yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Chamaecrista botryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species within the Fabaceae family.
