Cissus rheifolia

Cissus rheifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a vining plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fig-like fruits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its stems are succulent and can climb using tendrils, allowing it to reach light sources.

The leaves are typically glossy and emit a mild fragrance when crushed.
