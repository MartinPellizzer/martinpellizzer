Carex breviculmis var. leucochlora

Carex breviculmis var. leucochlora, commonly known as the white-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its propagation.

This variety is distinguished by its pale or white-colored stems, which set it apart from other Carex species.
