Clematis filifera

Clematis filifera, commonly known as the silver leaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis filifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be trained to grow on trellises or fences.
