Calamus hirsutus subsp. korthalsii

Calamus hirsutus subsp.

korthalsii, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae). It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

This subspecies is characterized by its long, rhizomatous growth and large, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
