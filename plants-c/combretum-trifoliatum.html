Combretum trifoliatum

Combretum trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf combretum, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
