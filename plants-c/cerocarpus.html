Cerocarpus

Cerocarpus, commonly known as the curlleaf mountain mahogany, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

It consists of several species native to North America, particularly in the western United States and Canada. The plants are shrubs or small trees, characterized by their compound leaves and yellow flowers. They are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.
