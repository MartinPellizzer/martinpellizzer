Carex pilulifera var. novae-angliae

Carex pilulifera var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
