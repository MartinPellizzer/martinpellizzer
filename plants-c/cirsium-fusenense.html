Cirsium fusenense

Cirsium fusenense, commonly known as the Fussen thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract various pollinators.

Cirsium fusenense is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
