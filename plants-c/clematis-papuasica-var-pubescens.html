Clematis papuasica var. pubescens

Clematis papuasica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Papuan Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to lilac, often with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are hairy or pubescent, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardens in temperate climates.
