Cuscuta leptantha var. typica

Cuscuta leptantha var. typica, commonly known as the slender dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, thread-like stem that wraps tightly around its host. The plant is native to North America and is often found on various flowering plants such as grasses and legumes. It reproduces through seeds and can form dense infestations on its host, potentially weakening or damaging the host plant.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth patterns of its host, making it an ecologically significant but often problematic species.
