Campylotropis splendens

Campylotropis splendens, commonly known as the goldenhead acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and have a distinctive golden hue when young. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
