Cantua buxifolia var. lanceolata

Cantua buxifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf woollyhead, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, woolly, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
