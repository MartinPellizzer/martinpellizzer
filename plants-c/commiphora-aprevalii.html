Commiphora aprevalii

Commiphora aprevalii, commonly known as the Kgalagadi gum tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a reddish-brown resin, which is exuded from fissures in the bark, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open savannas.

The tree is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of its ecosystem.
