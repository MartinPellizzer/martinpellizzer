Caltha intermedia

Caltha intermedia, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Caltha intermedia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
