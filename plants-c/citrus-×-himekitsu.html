Citrus × himekitsu

Citrus × himekitsu, commonly known as the Japanese sweet orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species originating from Japan, characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel. The fruit is typically sweet with a low acidity, making it popular for both fresh consumption and culinary use. Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species like the common orange and mandarin.

Its genetic makeup combines traits from its parent species, resulting in a unique flavor profile and adaptability to various climates.
