Clematis smilacifolia var. andersonii

Clematis smilacifolia var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a reddish or bronze tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, such as trellises or fences, for optimal display.
