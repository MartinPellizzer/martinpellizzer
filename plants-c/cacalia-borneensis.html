Cacalia borneensis

Cacalia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Cacalia borneensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
