Crucifera ruellii

Crucifera ruellii, commonly known as the Ruellia crucifera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
