Ceanothus neumannii

Ceanothus neumannii, commonly known as Neumann's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are evergreen, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Ceanothus neumannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
