Calamus guineensis

Calamus guineensis, commonly known as the African sweet flag, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is native to tropical Africa.

Calamus guineensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
