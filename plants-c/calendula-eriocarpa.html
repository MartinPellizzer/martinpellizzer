Calendula eriocarpa

Calendula eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
