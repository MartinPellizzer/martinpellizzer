Claytonia virginica var. simsii

Claytonia virginica var. simsii, commonly known as the pink mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or pink. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
