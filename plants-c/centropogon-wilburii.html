Centropogon wilburii

Centropogon wilburii, commonly known as the Wilbur's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Centropogon wilburii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
