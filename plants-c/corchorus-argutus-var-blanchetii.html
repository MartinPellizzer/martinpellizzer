Corchorus argutus var. blanchetii

Corchorus argutus var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanquet's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and palmate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fibrous roots are valued for their strength and have been used in traditional textile production.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
