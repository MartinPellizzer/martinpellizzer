Clematis dioica var. normalis

Clematis dioica var. normalis, commonly known as the common clematis, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its dioecious nature, meaning male and female flowers are found on separate plants. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis dioica var. normalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
