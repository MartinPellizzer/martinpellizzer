Cattleya labiata var. candida

Cattleya labiata var. candida, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its elegant, white flowers with a faint pinkish tinge.

It originates from the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces fragrant blooms that typically appear in late winter or early spring. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
