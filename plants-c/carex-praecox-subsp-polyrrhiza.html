Carex praecox subsp. polyrrhiza

Carex praecox subsp.

polyrrhiza, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
