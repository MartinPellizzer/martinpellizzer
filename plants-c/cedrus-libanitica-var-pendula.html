Cedrus libanitica var. pendula

Cedrus libanitica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar of Lebanon, is a unique variety of the cedar of Lebanon.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. This evergreen conifer is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient architecture.
