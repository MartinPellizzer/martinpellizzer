Crotalaria pycnocephala

Crotalaria pycnocephala, commonly known as the dense-headed crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
