Calea cuneifolia var. paraguensis

Calea cuneifolia var. paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan candle plant, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescences that emerge from a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant's foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some South American cultures.
