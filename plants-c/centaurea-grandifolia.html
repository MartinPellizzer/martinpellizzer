Centaurea grandifolia

Centaurea grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and rocky areas.

Centaurea grandifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
