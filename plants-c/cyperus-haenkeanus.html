Cyperus haenkeanus

Cyperus haenkeanus, commonly known as the yellow papyrus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swamps, ponds, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus haenkeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
