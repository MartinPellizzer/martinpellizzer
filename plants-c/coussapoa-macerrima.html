Coussapoa macerrima

Coussapoa macerrima, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid or the red orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral displays, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Coussapoa macerrima is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
