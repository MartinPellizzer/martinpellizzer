Clinopodium bolivianum

Clinopodium bolivianum, commonly known as Bolivian mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Clinopodium bolivianum is valued for its fragrant leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
