Calvaria bojeriana

Calvaria bojeriana, commonly known as the African helmet plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, helmet-like structure at the top of its stems, which is a modified inflorescence called a cyathium. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered within the cup-shaped structure. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Calvaria bojeriana is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
