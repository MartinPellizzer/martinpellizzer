Calamus vestitus

Calamus vestitus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is typically found in wetland or swampy habitats.

Calamus vestitus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
