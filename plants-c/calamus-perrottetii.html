Calamus perrottetii

Calamus perrottetii, commonly known as the Perrottet's arrowroot, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that are used as a starchy food source. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several meters in length, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
