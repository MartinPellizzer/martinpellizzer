Chloris alba var. aristulata

Chloris alba var. aristulata, commonly known as silver grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Australia.

It features fine, silver-white blades that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, pale green spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

This variety is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
