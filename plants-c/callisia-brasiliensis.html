Callisia brasiliensis

Callisia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian carpet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, mat-forming foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often borne in clusters. Its leaves are fleshy and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to spread rapidly and form lush, green carpets.
