Calamintha alpina var. marginata

Calamintha alpina var. marginata, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often edged with white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
