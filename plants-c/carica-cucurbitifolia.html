Carica cucurbitifolia

Carica cucurbitifolia, commonly known as the horned cucumber or horned gourd, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like fruits that resemble a miniature hornet's nest. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically harvested when green and can be cooked or used in traditional remedies.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and edible parts.
