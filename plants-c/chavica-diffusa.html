Chavica diffusa

Chavica diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
