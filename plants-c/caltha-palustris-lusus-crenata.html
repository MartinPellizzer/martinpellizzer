Caltha palustris lusus crenata

Caltha palustris lusus crenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly notched or serrated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
