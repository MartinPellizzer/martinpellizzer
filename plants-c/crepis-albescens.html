Crepis albescens

Crepis albescens, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

Crepis albescens is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
