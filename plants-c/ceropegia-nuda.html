Ceropegia nuda

Ceropegia nuda, commonly known as the naked ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia nuda is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
