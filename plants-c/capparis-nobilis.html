Capparis nobilis

Capparis nobilis, commonly known as the noble caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is noted for its aromatic foliage.

Capparis nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
