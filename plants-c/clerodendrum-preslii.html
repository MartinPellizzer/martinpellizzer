Clerodendrum preslii

Clerodendrum preslii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant produces small, round, bright red berries that are visually appealing but not typically edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum preslii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
