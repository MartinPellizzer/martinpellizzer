Cucumis melo var. bitarianus

Cucumis melo var. bitarianus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and slightly ribbed fruit, typically green in color with a smooth skin. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its fruit is often used in salads and has a mild, crisp flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is closely related to other melon species.
