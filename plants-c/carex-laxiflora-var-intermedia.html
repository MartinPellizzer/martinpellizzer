Carex laxiflora var. intermedia

Carex laxiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical shape.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
