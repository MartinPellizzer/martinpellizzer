Calamagrostis autumnalis

Calamagrostis autumnalis, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple-tinted leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and wet meadows. Its feathery plumes add ornamental value, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
