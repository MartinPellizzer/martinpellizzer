Coriaria arborea var. arborea

Coriaria arborea var. arborea, commonly known as the tree coriander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, spherical berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
