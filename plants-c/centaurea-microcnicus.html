Centaurea microcnicus

Centaurea microcnicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Centaurea microcnicus is often used in dry gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
