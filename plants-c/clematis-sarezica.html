Clematis sarezica

Clematis sarezica, commonly known as the Sarez clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer. The plant produces small, dry fruits known as follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Clematis sarezica is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
