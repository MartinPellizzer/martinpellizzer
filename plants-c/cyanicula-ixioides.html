Cyanicula ixioides

Cyanicula ixioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats or low shrubs. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyanicula ixioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
