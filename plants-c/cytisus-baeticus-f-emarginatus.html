Cytisus baeticus f. emarginatus

Cytisus baeticus f. emarginatus, commonly known as the Baeticus broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variety is distinguished by its emarginated leaflets, giving it a unique appearance among other cytisus species.
