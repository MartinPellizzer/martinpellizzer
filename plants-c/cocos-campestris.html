Cocos campestris

Cocos campestris, commonly known as the field coconut or wild coconut, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia. The plant produces a large, fibrous fruit that contains a single, edible kernel. Its leaves are large, feather-like, and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and for the use of its fibrous material in traditional crafts.
