Cinnamomum racemosum

Cinnamomum racemosum, commonly known as the wild cinnamon or mountain cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
