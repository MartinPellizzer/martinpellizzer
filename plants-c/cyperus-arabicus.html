Cyperus arabicus

Cyperus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or saline environments. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus arabicus is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
