Cyperus longus var. speciosus

Cyperus longus var. speciosus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Cyperus longus var. speciosus is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
