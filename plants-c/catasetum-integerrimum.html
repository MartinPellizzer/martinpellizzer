Catasetum integerrimum

Catasetum integerrimum, commonly known as the Elegant Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flower spikes that bear showy, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
