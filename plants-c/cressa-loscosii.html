Cressa loscosii

Cressa loscosii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, linear, and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp grasslands or along stream banks.

Cressa loscosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
