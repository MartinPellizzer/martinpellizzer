Cynanchum medium

Cynanchum medium, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
