Chrysobalanus pallidus

Chrysobalanus pallidus, commonly known as the pale chrysobalanus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalancaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
