Capparis brachybotrya var. angustifolia

Capparis brachybotrya var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, spherical fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
