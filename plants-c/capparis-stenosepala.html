Capparis stenosepala

Capparis stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Capparis stenosepala is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
