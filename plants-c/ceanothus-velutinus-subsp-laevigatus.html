Ceanothus velutinus subsp. laevigatus

Ceanothus velutinus subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the silky ceanothus, is a shrub native to California, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its silky appearance. It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The subspecies laevigatus is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy leaves compared to other varieties of Ceanothus velutinus.
