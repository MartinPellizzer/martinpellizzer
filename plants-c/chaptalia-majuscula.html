Chaptalia majuscula

Chaptalia majuscula, commonly known as the giant chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
