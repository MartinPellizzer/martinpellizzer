Carex tomentosa f. nigrescens

Carex tomentosa f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-hairy sedge, is a variant of the tomentosa sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that often have a silvery-gray underside. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous brown flowers.

This subspecies is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
