Calamus diffusus

Calamus diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in wet, marshy environments. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in various culinary applications.

The plant also plays a role in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
