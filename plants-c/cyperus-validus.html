Cyperus validus

Cyperus validus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Cyperus validus has small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
