Cereus remolinensis

Cereus remolinensis, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that bear clusters of vibrant flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, producing bright red, pink, or white flowers that last for several weeks. Its leaves are modified into spiny stems, and it thrives in well-drained soil with indirect sunlight.

Cereus remolinensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
