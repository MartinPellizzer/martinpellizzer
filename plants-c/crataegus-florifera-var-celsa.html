Crataegus florifera var. celsa

Crataegus florifera var. celsa, commonly known as the Sierra plum or California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, reddish-purple berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
