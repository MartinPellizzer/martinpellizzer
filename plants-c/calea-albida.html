Calea albida

Calea albida, commonly known as the white calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are broad and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Calea albida is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
