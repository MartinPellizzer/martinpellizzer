Chelonopsis giraldii

Chelonopsis giraldii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, supported by long, flexible petioles. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, and they bloom during the day. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique structure allows it to provide habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
