Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tetragona-aurea

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tetragona-aurea, commonly known as the golden Japanese cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its golden-yellow foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in a compact, pyramidal shape. The leaves are scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, giving the tree a dense appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and ability to retain its color throughout the year.
