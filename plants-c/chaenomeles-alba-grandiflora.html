Chaenomeles alba grandiflora

Chaenomeles alba grandiflora, commonly known as the white flowering Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
