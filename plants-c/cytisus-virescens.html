Cytisus virescens

Cytisus virescens, commonly known as the greenbark broom, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
