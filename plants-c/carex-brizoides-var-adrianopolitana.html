Carex brizoides var. adrianopolitana

Carex brizoides var. adrianopolitana, commonly known as Adrianopolitan sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream edges.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
