Cytisus diffusus var. adpressipilosus

Cytisus diffusus var. adpressipilosus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a flowering shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, yellow pea-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and blooms.
