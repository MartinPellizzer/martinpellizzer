Chrysosplenium griffithii

Chrysosplenium griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's golden spleenwort, is a species of aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Spleenworts.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving streams, ponds, and wetlands, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellowish and appear in clusters.

Chrysosplenium griffithii is valued for its ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
