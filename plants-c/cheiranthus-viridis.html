Cheiranthus viridis

Cheiranthus viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found growing on walls or in cracks. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
