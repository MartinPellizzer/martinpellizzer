Carex trichophylla

Carex trichophylla, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Carex trichophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat creation for wildlife.
