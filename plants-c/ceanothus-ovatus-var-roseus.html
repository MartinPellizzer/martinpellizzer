Ceanothus ovatus var. roseus

Ceanothus ovatus var. roseus, commonly known as the Roseleaf Ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically leathery and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
