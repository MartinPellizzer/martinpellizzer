Cymbidium chloranthum subsp. palawanense

Cymbidium chloranthum subsp.

palawanense, commonly known as the Palawan cymbidium, is a rare orchid species native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
