Cyperus cuspidatus f. exiguus

Cyperus cuspidatus f. exiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its compact size and ability to thrive in aquatic environments make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
