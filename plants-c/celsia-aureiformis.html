Celsia aureiformis

Celsia aureiformis, commonly known as the golden leaf celsia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which is unique among its genus. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are often shaped in a distinctive, almost shield-like form, contributing to its visual interest.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, Celsia aureiformis is considered a conservation concern.
