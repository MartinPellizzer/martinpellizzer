Carex abbreviata

Carex abbreviata, commonly known as the short-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces capsule fruits that disperse seeds.

Carex abbreviata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
