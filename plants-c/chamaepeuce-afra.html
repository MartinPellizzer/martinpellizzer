Chamaepeuce afra

Chamaepeuce afra, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Chamaepeuce afra is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
