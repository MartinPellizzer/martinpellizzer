Carex raphidocarpa

Carex raphidocarpa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, stiff, and often hairy stems that are typically reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex raphidocarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
