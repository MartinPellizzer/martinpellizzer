Cleistocactus hyalacanthus subsp. tarijensis

Cleistocactus hyalacanthus subsp.

tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to produce striking white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night and closing by morning.

This subspecies is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
