Corymbia foelscheana

Corymbia foelscheana, commonly known as the Red River gum, is a striking Australian native tree characterized by its distinctive red-brown to pinkish bark.

It typically grows in coastal and riverine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The tree produces showy, crimson-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
