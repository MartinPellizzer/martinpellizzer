Carex nigromarginata var. elliptica

Carex nigromarginata var. elliptica, commonly known as black-margin sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive black or dark purple margin. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
