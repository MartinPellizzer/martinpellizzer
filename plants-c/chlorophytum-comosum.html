Chlorophytum comosum

Chlorophytum comosum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces long, arching stems with small, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant is characterized by its ability to produce offsets, which are small plantlets that grow from the base of the parent plant. These offsets can be easily separated and propagated, making the spider plant a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and its air-purifying qualities further enhance its appeal as a decorative and functional plant.
