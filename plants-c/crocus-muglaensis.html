Crocus muglaensis

Crocus muglaensis, commonly known as the Mugla crocus, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
