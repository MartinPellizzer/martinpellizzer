Cuphea hookeriana f. polysperma

Cuphea hookeriana f. polysperma, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a unique variant of the Cuphea genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, tubular bracts that surround the small, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of tiny, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is often grown as a container plant or in shaded garden settings due to its preference for moist, well-drained soil.
