Cyrtopodium withneri

Cyrtopodium withneri, commonly known as the Withner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, showcasing a striking contrast between its dark green foliage and vibrant floral displays. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cyrtopodium withneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
