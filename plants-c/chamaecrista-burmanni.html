Chamaecrista burmanni

Chamaecrista burmanni, commonly known as burmann's ticktree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Chamaecrista burmanni is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
