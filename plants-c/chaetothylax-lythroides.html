Chaetothylax lythroides

Chaetothylax lythroides, commonly known as the red-flowered bluebell, is a striking member of the Bignoniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
