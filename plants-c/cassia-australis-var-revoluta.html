Cassia australis var. revoluta

Cassia australis var. revoluta, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
