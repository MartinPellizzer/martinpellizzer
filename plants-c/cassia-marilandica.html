Cassia marilandica

Cassia marilandica, commonly known as wild cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with several oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central stem.

Cassia marilandica is also known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
