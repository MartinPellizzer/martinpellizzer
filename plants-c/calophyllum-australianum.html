Calophyllum australianum

Calophyllum australianum, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians. Its bark is smooth and dark, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
