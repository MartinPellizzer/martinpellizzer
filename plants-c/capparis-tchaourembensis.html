Capparis tchaourembensis

Capparis tchaourembensis, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy and edible, though they are typically sour and used in local cuisines.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
