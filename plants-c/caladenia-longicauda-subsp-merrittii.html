Caladenia longicauda subsp. merrittii

Caladenia longicauda subsp.

merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia. It features a single, slender stem that produces one or two showy, hooded flowers with a distinctive spur.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a red or purple lip.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
