Carex pseudocyperus f. pallidior

Carex pseudocyperus f. pallidior, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the sedgeland sedge native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
