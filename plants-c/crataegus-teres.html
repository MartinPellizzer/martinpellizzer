Crataegus teres

Crataegus teres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often narrow leaves that are smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Crataegus teres typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in moist soils along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
