Coleus glabriflorus

Coleus glabriflorus, commonly known as the smoothflower coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its striking appearance.
