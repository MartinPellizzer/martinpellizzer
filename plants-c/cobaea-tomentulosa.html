Cobaea tomentulosa

Cobaea tomentulosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a dense, woolly covering that gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and garden displays.
