Cineraria scapiflora

Cineraria scapiflora, commonly known as the Cape flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Cineraria scapiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
