Cuscuta coronata

Cuscuta coronata, commonly known as the crown vine, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a rapid growth rate, often covering its host quickly. The plant's stems are thin, thread-like, and covered in tiny, hooked structures that allow it to attach to its host. It is commonly found in warm, humid environments and can infest a variety of plants, including crops and ornamental species.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to the host plant's growth and health.
