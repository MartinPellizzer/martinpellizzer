Cyperus spongiosovaginatus

Cyperus spongiosovaginatus, commonly known as the spongy-vaginal flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath at the base.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
