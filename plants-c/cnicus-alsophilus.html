Cnicus alsophilus

Cnicus alsophilus, commonly known as the Alsop's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a spiny texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Cnicus alsophilus is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
