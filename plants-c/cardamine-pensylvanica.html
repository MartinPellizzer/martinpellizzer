Cardamine pensylvanica

Cardamine pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a glossy surface. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its edible leaves, though it can be difficult to distinguish from similar species.
