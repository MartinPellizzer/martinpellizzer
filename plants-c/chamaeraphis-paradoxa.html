Chamaeraphis paradoxa

Chamaeraphis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
