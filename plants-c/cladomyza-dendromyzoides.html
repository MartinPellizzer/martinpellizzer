Cladomyza dendromyzoides

Cladomyza dendromyzoides, commonly known as the tree-like moss, is a unique member of the moss family, characterized by its branching, tree-like structure.

It forms dense, cushion-like colonies that can grow on soil or rock surfaces, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant's stems are wiry and covered with small, leaf-like structures that aid in water retention. It reproduces via spores released from specialized capsules, a typical method among mosses.

Despite its appearance, it lacks true roots and vascular tissues, distinguishing it from more complex plants.
