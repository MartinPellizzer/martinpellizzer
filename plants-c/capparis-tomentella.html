Capparis tomentella

Capparis tomentella, commonly known as the woolly caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces spherical, waxy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Capparis tomentella is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
