Cardamine dentata f. micrantha

Cardamine dentata f. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water shield, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It typically grows in wetlands, ponds, and slow-moving streams, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils.

This fern-like plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
