Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp. cinereus

Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

cinereus, commonly known as silver bush broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale gray-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, thornless stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms that add color to gardens.
