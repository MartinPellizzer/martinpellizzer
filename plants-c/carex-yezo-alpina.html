Carex yezo-alpina

Carex yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Yezo alpine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Botanically, it is classified as a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
