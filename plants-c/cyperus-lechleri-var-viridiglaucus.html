Cyperus lechleri var. viridiglaucus

Cyperus lechleri var. viridiglaucus, commonly known as the green glaucous flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in ecological restoration projects.
