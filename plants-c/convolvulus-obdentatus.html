Convolvulus obdentatus

Convolvulus obdentatus, commonly known as the toothed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly. Its leaves are heart-shaped with finely toothed edges, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in gardens and disturbed areas.
