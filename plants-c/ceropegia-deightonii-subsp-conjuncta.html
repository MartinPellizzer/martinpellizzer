Ceropegia deightonii subsp. conjuncta

Ceropegia deightonii subsp.

conjuncta, commonly known as the jointed African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple.

The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to store water in its stems and roots, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
