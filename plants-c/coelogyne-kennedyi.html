Coelogyne kennedyi

Coelogyne kennedyi, commonly known as the Kennedy's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing blossoms in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coelogyne kennedyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
