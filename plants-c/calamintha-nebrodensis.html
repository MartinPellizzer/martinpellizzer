Calamintha nebrodensis

Calamintha nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in summer.

Calamintha nebrodensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
