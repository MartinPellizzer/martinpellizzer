Celsia brevicaulis

Celsia brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed celsia, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, spiny flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Celsia brevicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
