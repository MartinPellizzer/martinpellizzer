Clematis theobromina

Clematis theobromina, commonly known as the chocolate flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often woody and twining. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
