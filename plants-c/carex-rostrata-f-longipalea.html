Carex rostrata f. longipalea

Carex rostrata f. longipalea, commonly known as the long-pale sedge, is a variant of the rostrata sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are generally longer and more slender compared to the typical form, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

The foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, textured appearance.
