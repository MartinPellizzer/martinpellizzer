Casearia graveolens

Casearia graveolens, commonly known as the stinking casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
