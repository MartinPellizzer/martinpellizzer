Cinnamomum litsaeifolium var. dentricupullatum

Cinnamomum litsaeifolium var. dentricupullatum, commonly known as the toothed cupleaf cinnamon, is a rare and unique variant of the cinnamon tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring deeply serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
