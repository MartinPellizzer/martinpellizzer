Centaurea deusta subsp. leucolepis

Centaurea deusta subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its floral structure includes both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.
