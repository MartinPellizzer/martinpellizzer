Carex acuta var. pseudaquatilis

Carex acuta var. pseudaquatilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
