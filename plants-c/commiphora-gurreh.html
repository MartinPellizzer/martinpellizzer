Commiphora gurreh

Commiphora gurreh, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes other resin-producing plants such as frankincense and myrrh. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Commiphora gurreh is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
