Carex luzulifolia var. strobilantha

Carex luzulifolia var. strobilantha, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a dense, tufted growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
