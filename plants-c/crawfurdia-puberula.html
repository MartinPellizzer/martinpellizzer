Crawfurdia puberula

Crawfurdia puberula, commonly known as the woolly crawfurdia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, pubescent surface.

Crawfurdia puberula is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
