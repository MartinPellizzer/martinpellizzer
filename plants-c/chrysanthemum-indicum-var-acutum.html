Chrysanthemum indicum var. acutum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. acutum, commonly known as the narrowleaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, white, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
