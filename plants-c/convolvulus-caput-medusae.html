Convolvulus caput-medusae

Convolvulus caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head," is a striking flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence that resembles the flowing hair of the mythological Medusa. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Convolvulus caput-medusae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
