Centaurea vochinensis var. flocculosa

Centaurea vochinensis var. flocculosa, commonly known as the flocculose centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fluffy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Centaurea.
