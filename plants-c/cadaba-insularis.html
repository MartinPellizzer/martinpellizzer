Cadaba insularis

Cadaba insularis, commonly known as the Socotra caper, is a unique shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, egg-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, green capsules containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study.
