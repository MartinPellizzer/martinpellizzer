Cinnamomum chago

Cinnamomum chago, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant produces small, clustered flowers and dark, glossy berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are aromatic and have a strong, spicy scent, similar to cinnamon.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
