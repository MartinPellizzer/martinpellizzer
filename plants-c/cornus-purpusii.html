Cornus purpusii

Cornus purpusii, commonly known as the Japanese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, white bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value.

Cornus purpusii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
