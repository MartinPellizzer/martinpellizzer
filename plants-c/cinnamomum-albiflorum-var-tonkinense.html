Cinnamomum albiflorum var. tonkinense

Cinnamomum albiflorum var. tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin cinnamon or white-flowered cinnamon, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal properties.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and aesthetic appeal.
