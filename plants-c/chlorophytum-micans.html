Chlorophytum micans

Chlorophytum micans, commonly known as the Bonnie Blue or the African violet plant, is a monocot belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which are typically borne on upright spikes. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Chlorophytum micans is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
