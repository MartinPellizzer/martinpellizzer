Cerasus × tamaclivorum

Cerasus × tamaclivorum, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus × yedoensis and Prunus mandshurica.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy spring blooms. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant colors in autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
