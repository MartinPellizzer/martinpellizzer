Cistus villosus var. incanus

Cistus villosus var. incanus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Cistus villosus var. incanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
