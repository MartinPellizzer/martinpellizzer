Cistus villosus var. creticus

Cistus villosus var. creticus, commonly known as the Cretan rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
