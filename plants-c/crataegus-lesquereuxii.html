Crataegus lesquereuxii

Crataegus lesquereuxii, commonly known as the Lesquereux hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
