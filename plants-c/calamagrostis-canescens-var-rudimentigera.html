Calamagrostis canescens var. rudimentigera

Calamagrostis canescens var. rudimentigera, commonly known as the rough bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces fluffy, blue-gray panicles during the summer months. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
