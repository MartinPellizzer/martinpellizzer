Cyperus drakensbergensis

Cyperus drakensbergensis, commonly known as the Drakensberg sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its cylindrical stems are often clustered, and it produces small, three-ranked spikelets that are characteristic of the genus.

Cyperus drakensbergensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
