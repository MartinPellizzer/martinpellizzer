Coursetia hidalgoana

Coursetia hidalgoana, commonly known as Hidalgo's coursetia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
