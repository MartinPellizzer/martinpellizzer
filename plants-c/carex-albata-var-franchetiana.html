Carex albata var. franchetiana

Carex albata var. franchetiana, commonly known as the white sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wetland biodiversity.
