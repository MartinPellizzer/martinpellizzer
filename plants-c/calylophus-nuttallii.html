Calylophus nuttallii

Calylophus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Calylophus nuttallii is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
