Chromolaena verticillata

Chromolaena verticillata, commonly known as flame bush or Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
