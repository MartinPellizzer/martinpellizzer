Cirsium corbariense f. eriophorum

Cirsium corbariense f. eriophorum, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a horticultural form of the common thistle.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spiny, purple flower heads. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The hairy texture of its leaves provides a unique visual appeal, making it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.

It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
