Cineraria anomala

Cineraria anomala, commonly known as the Canary Islands cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it is considered invasive in some regions.
