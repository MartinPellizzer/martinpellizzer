Crepis setigera

Crepis setigera, commonly known as the bentgrass thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often bent stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly bitter taste.

Crepis setigera thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
