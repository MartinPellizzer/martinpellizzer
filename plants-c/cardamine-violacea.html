Cardamine violacea

Cardamine violacea, commonly known as violetleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue and small, violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often used in salads, though they are less commonly consumed than other watercress varieties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
