Coreopsis grandiflora f. demareei

Coreopsis grandiflora f. demareei, commonly known as the Demaree's coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange ray flowers with a prominent central disk. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
