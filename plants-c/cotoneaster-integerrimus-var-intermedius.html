Cotoneaster integerrimus var. intermedius

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a range of climates, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
