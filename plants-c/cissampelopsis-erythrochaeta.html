Cissampelopsis erythrochaeta

Cissampelopsis erythrochaeta, commonly known as the red-hair vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by elliptical, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
