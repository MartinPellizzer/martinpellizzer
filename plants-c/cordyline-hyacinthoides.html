Cordyline hyacinthoides

Cordyline hyacinthoides, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or red flowers in the summer months, which are followed by dark blue berries.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
