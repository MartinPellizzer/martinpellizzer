Carex globistylosa

Carex globistylosa, commonly known as the globulous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex globistylosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
