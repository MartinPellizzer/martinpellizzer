Clausena cambodiana

Clausena cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright orange-red inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish fruits that are roughly spherical in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
