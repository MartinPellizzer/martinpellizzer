Carex ornithopodioides f. supina

Carex ornithopodioides f. supina, commonly known as the spreading sedge, is a variant of the ornithopodioides sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.
