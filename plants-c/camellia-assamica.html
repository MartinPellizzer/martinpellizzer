Camellia assamica

Camellia assamica, commonly known as the Assam tea plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce Assam black tea, renowned for its strong flavor and brisk aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained acidic soils.

Camellia assamica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
