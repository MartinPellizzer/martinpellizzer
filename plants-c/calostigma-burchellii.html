Calostigma burchellii

Calostigma burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Calostigma burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
