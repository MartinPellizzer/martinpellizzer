Croton stigmatosus var. eurybioides

Croton stigmatosus var. eurybioides, commonly known as the Eurybioides croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with reddish or purple veins. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish color, and it is known for its ornamental value.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
