Ceropegia secamonoides

Ceropegia secamonoides, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, tubular flowers. The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
