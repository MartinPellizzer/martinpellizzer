Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum subsp. zeylanicum

Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum subsp.

zeylanicum, commonly known as the golden jasmine vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia. It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The vine is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to warm climates.
