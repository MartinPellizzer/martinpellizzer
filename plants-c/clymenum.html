Clymenum

Clymenum, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Clymenum has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in wet soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
