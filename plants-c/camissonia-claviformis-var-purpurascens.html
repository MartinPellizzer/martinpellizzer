Camissonia claviformis var. purpurascens

Camissonia claviformis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often turn a striking purplish hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its unique coloration and habitat preferences make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
