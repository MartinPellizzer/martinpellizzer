Croton sipaliwinensis

Croton sipaliwinensis, commonly known as the Sipaliwini croton, is a shrub native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Guyana and Suriname.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and sometimes toxic species. Croton sipaliwinensis typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
