Calcearia koresii

Calcearia koresii, commonly known as the Koresii sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, creating a striking visual display.

Calcearia koresii is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
