Carex fraudulans

Carex fraudulans, commonly known as the false sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-brown flowers in dense spikes. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex fraudulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
