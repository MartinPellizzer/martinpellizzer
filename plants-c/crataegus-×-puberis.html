Crataegus × puberis

Crataegus × puberis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus × puberis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
