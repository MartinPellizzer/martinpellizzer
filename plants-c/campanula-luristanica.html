Campanula luristanica

Campanula luristanica, commonly known as the Luristan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
