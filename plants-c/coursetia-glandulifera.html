Coursetia glandulifera

Coursetia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically woody and can grow up to several feet in height.

Coursetia glandulifera is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
