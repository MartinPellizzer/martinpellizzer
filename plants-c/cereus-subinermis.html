Cereus subinermis

Cereus subinermis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a spiny, columnar plant that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its stems are covered in sharp, pale spines, and it is drought-tolerant, thriving in well-drained soil.

The species is popular in horticulture for its striking floral display and nocturnal blooming habit.
