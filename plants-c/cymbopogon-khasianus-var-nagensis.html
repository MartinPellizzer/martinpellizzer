Cymbopogon khasianus var. nagensis

Cymbopogon khasianus var. nagensis, commonly known as Nagin grass, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along riverbanks. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
