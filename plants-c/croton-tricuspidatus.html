Croton tricuspidatus

Croton tricuspidatus, commonly known as the three-angled croton, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its striking foliage, it is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic value.
