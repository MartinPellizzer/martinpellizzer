Cajanus aromaticus

Cajanus aromaticus, commonly known as the sweet pea bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
