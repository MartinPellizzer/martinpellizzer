Coccothrinax argentea

Coccothrinax argentea, commonly known as the silver palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches a height of about 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. It has a slender trunk with a smooth, pale surface and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, black berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and limestone soils.
