Citrus × nobilis subf. mitsuharu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

mitsuharu, commonly known as the Satsuma mandarin, is a citrus hybrid originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruits with thin, loose rinds.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is highly valued for its cold tolerance compared to other citrus varieties.

The fruit is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing into juices and marmalades.
