Cornus alba var. albomarginata

Cornus alba var. albomarginata, commonly known as the White-marginied Dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white-edged leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to landscaped areas.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental hedges and borders.
