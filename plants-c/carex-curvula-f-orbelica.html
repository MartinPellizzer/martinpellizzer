Carex curvula f. orbelica

Carex curvula f. orbelica, commonly known as the Orbelica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelike cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
