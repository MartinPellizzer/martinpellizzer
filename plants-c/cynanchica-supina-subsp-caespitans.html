Cynanchica supina subsp. caespitans

Cynanchica supina subsp.

caespitans, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
