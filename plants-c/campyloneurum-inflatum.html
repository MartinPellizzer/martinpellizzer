Campyloneurum inflatum

Campyloneurum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, inflated, and often brightly colored fronds that can reach up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically divided into lance-shaped segments with a distinctive, curled appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
