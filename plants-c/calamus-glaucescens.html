Calamus glaucescens

Calamus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage, making it a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
