Cracca sphaerosperma

Cracca sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking spherical blue or purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tightly packed florets. The plant has a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cracca sphaerosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
