Cortaderia dioeca

Cortaderia dioeca, commonly known as the silver reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow, silver-blue leaves that are arranged in a dense, clumping form. The plant produces large, feathery plumes that can reach several meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its dramatic visual impact.

Due to its invasive potential, it requires careful management in certain regions.
