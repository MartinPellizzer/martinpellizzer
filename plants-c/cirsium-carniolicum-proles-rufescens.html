Cirsium carniolicum proles rufescens

Cirsium carniolicum proles rufescens, commonly known as the red-hairy thistle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its stems are covered in dense, reddish-brown hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
