Clematis wightiana var. incisodentata

Clematis wightiana var. incisodentata, commonly known as the serrated toothed clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cascading foliage and floral display.
