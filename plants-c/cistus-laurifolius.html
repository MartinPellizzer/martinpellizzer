Cistus laurifolius

Cistus laurifolius, commonly known as the laurel heath, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus laurifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
