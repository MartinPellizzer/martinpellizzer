Carex adelostoma

Carex adelostoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex adelostoma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
