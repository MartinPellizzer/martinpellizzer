Croton kongensis

Croton kongensis, commonly known as the Congo croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and can vary in shape from elliptical to ovate.

Croton kongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
