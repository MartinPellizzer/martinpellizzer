Cyclophorus grandissimus

Cyclophorus grandissimus, commonly known as the giant cyclophorus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Cyclophoraceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, with its leaves expanding in a helical pattern as it matures. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for researchers studying plant morphology and adaptation.

Due to its rarity, it is often found in protected habitats and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
