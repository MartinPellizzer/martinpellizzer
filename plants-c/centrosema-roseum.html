Centrosema roseum

Centrosema roseum, commonly known as the pink leadplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Centrosema roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
