Conyza roylei

Conyza roylei, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellowish to white flowers bloom in clusters, often in late summer or early autumn.

Conyza roylei is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in environments with low water availability.
