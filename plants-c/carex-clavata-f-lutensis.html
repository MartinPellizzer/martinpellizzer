Carex clavata f. lutensis

Carex clavata f. lutensis, commonly known as the Lutensian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
