Callirhoe involucrata

Callirhoe involucrata, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its upright stems and showy pink to red flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous small seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry climates.

The species is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
