Campanula trachelium var. dasycarpa

Campanula trachelium var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
