Clinopodium cuifengense

Clinopodium cuifengense, commonly known as the Cuifeng mint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong mint-like fragrance, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Clinopodium cuifengense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
