Cacalia semperviva

Cacalia semperviva, commonly known as everlasting or sea daisies, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, tough, and long-lasting seeds that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cacalia semperviva is also valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in coastal and maritime climates.
