Campanula trachelium subsp. dasycarpa

Campanula trachelium subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
