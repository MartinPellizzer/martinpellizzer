Carex chamissonis

Carex chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

Carex chamissonis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
