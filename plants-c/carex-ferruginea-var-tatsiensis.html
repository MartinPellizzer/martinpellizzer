Carex ferruginea var. tatsiensis

Carex ferruginea var. tatsiensis, commonly known as the Tatsienlu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a reddish-brown hue, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
