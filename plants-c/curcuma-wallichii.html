Curcuma wallichii

Curcuma wallichii, commonly known as the golden ginger or Indian saffron, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, tuberous rhizomes. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences with yellow to orange flowers. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to turmeric.

The botanical features of Curcuma wallichii make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
