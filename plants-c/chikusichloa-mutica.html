Chikusichloa mutica

Chikusichloa mutica, commonly known as velvetleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become invasive in certain environments.
