Conyza arborea

Conyza arborea, commonly known as tree fleabane, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Conyza arborea is also known for its medicinal properties in some traditional healing practices.
