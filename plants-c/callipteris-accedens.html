Callipteris accedens

Callipteris accedens, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Callipteris accedens is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
