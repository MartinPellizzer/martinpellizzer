Carex ramentaceofructus

Carex ramentaceofructus, commonly known as the fructifying sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-ranked spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex ramentaceofructus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
