Cyrtochilum rhodoneurum

Cyrtochilum rhodoneurum, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often pink or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators. Cyrtochilum rhodoneurum is notable for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized petals and sepals adapted for pollination by insects.
