Collabium

Collabium, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the daisy family. The leaves are usually alternate, often with a rough or hairy texture, and the stems are typically upright. Collabium species are found primarily in temperate regions and are known for their ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

These plants are often used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
