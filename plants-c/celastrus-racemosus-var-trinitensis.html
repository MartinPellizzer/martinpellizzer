Celastrus racemosus var. trinitensis

Celastrus racemosus var. trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in racemose inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, smooth seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
