Cupressus stricta

Cupressus stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar growth habit and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically dark green and arranged in crossed pairs along the shoots. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in landscaping for its formal shape and drought tolerance.
