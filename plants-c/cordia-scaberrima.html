Cordia scaberrima

Cordia scaberrima, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its rough, often fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish or greenish color, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Cordia scaberrima is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland tropical regions.
