Crataegus exclusa

Crataegus exclusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Crataegus exclusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
