Curcuma antinaia

Curcuma antinaia, commonly known as the black turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids and other bioactive compounds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces fragrant, yellowish flowers during the flowering season.

The botanical features of Curcuma antinaia make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and ornamental purposes.
