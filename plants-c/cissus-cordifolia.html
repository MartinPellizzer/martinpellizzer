Cissus cordifolia

Cissus cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cordifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
