Cupressus bakeri subsp. typica

Cupressus bakeri subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Baker's cedar, is a subspecies of the genus Cupressus native to California. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland habitats.

Due to its limited range and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
