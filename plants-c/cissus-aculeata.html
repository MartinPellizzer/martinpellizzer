Cissus aculeata

Cissus aculeata, commonly known as the hedge bindweed, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its stems are often armed with tiny, hooked spines, which aid in its climbing habit.

Cissus aculeata is also known for its ability to produce edible fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
