Cleome deflexa

Cleome deflexa, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome deflexa is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from summer to fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
