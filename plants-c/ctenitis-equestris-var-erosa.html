Ctenitis equestris var. erosa

Ctenitis equestris var. erosa, commonly known as the erosa rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually divided into small, lance-shaped segments, contributing to its intricate appearance.

The botanical features of this variant make it a unique and sought-after specimen in horticultural collections.
