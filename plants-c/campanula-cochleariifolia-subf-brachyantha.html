Campanula cochleariifolia subf. brachyantha

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
