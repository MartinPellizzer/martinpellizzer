Cyperus platycaulis

Cyperus platycaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually inconspicuous.

Cyperus platycaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
