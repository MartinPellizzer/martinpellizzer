Cytisus hirsutus subsp. bisflorens

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

bisflorens, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
