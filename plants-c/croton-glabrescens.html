Croton glabrescens

Croton glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown, and its showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
