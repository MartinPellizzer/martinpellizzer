Cayaponia laxa

Cayaponia laxa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical conditions. It is often found in shaded areas near streams or in forest understories.

Cayaponia laxa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
