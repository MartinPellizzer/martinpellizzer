Cirsium pannonicum var. legionense

Cirsium pannonicum var. legionense, commonly known as the Pannonian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple-pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its rigid stems and dense foliage make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
