Convolvulus nolaniflorus

Convolvulus nolaniflorus, commonly known as the Nolan's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Convolvulaceae family.
