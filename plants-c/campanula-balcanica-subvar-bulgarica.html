Campanula balcanica subvar. bulgarica

Campanula balcanica subvar. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
