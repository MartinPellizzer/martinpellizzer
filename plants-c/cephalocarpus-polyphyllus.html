Cephalocarpus polyphyllus

Cephalocarpus polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leaved headcone, is a unique member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, cap-like fruit that resembles a helmet, hence the genus name "Cephalocarpus.". The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a leathery texture.

The species is notable for its unusual floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
