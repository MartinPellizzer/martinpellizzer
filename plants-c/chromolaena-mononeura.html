Chromolaena mononeura

Chromolaena mononeura, commonly known as the stinging bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.

The plant contains irritant chemicals, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
