Citrosma foliosa

Citrosma foliosa, commonly known as the leafy citrosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 meters and has simple, alternate leaves that are often fragrant. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, though they are not commonly cultivated for consumption.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
