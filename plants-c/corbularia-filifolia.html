Corbularia filifolia

Corbularia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf corbularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Corbularia filifolia is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to brackish water conditions.
