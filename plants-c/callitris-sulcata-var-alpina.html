Callitris sulcata var. alpina

Callitris sulcata var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine cypress, is a subspecies of the Australian cypress known for its distinctive botanical features.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like foliage. The leaves are typically gray-green in color and tightly packed, providing a dense canopy. Its cones are small and woody, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety thrives in high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptation to cold and arid conditions.
