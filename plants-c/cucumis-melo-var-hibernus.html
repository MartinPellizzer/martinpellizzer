Cucumis melo var. hibernus

Cucumis melo var. hibernus, commonly known as the winter melon, is a variant of the melon plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pale green, elongated fruit with a smooth, slightly ribbed surface. The plant produces flowers that are typically yellow and distinct, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, often in the fall, and is valued for its mild, slightly sweet flavor.

It is commonly used in soups, stews, and as a refreshing snack in various cuisines.
