Clibadium funkiae

Clibadium funkiae, commonly known as the Funk's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking white spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread rapidly, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.

Clibadium funkiae is also noted for its ability to purify indoor air, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
