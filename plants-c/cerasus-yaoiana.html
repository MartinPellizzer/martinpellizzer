Cerasus yaoiana

Cerasus yaoiana, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with a preference for well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, ripens in summer and is valued for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
