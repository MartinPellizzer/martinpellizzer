Centaurea tanaitica

Centaurea tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
