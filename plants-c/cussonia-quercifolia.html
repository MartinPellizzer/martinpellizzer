Cussonia quercifolia

Cussonia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved cussonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered together. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique foliage and architectural form make it a standout in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
