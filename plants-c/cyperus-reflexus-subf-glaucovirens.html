Cyperus reflexus subf. glaucovirens

Cyperus reflexus subf.

glaucovirens, commonly known as the silverleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, typically arranged in a spike-like structure.

This species is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
