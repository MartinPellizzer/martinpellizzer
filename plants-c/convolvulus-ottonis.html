Convolvulus ottonis

Convolvulus ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that often form mats on the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus ottonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
