Clusiella pendula

Clusiella pendula, commonly known as the weeping flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Clusiella pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
