Cussonia capuroniana var. bracteolata

Cussonia capuroniana var. bracteolata, commonly known as the African bracted cussonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, segmented stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The leaves are large, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
