Callaion

Callaion, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has vibrant violet flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks, surrounded by waxy, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense populations in still or slow-moving water bodies. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the water surface, though it can become invasive in non-native environments.

Callaion is often used in ornamental ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
