Capparis ovalis

Capparis ovalis, commonly known as the oval caper, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh climates.
