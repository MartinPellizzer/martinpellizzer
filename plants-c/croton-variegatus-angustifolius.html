Croton variegatus angustifolius

Croton variegatus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, with leaves displaying shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are woody and branching.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
