Cenchrus glaucifolius

Cenchrus glaucifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
