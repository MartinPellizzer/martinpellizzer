Clethra alnifolia f. scabra

Clethra alnifolia f. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf sweet pepperbush, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clustered panicles during late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or streamside habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
