Carapa longipetala

Carapa longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes other valuable timber species. The plant is characterized by its large, simple leaves with long petioles and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Carapa longipetala is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and commercial purposes.
