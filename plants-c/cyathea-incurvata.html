Cyathea incurvata

Cyathea incurvata, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that curve downward. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports its growth, allowing it to reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Cyathea incurvata plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
