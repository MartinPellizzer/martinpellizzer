Carex caucasica var. abbreviata

Carex caucasica var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with a three-parted perianth that is often green or brown in color.
