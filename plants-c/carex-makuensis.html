Carex makuensis

Carex makuensis, commonly known as Maku sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex makuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
