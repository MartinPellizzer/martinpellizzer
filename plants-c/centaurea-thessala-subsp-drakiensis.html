Centaurea thessala subsp. drakiensis

Centaurea thessala subsp.

drakiensis, commonly known as the Drakonikos Centaurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
