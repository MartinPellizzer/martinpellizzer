Cassia micans

Cassia micans, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. Cassia micans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
