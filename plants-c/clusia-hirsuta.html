Clusia hirsuta

Clusia hirsuta, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and often have a yellow or white margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by green, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clusia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
