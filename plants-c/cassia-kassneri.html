Cassia kassneri

Cassia kassneri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia kassneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
