Carex condensata var. flava

Carex condensata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bright yellow to golden green in color. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by three-sided, boat-shaped berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
