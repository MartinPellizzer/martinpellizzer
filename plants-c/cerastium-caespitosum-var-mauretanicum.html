Cerastium caespitosum var. mauretanicum

Cerastium caespitosum var. mauretanicum, commonly known as the Mauretanian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
