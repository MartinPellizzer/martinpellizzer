Chuquiraga revoluta

Chuquiraga revoluta, commonly known as the Andean burr plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like structures that enclose its small yellow flowers. The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits, and is often found growing in rocky or arid environments. Its unique morphology and adaptability to high-altitude conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
