Chascanum obovatum subsp. glaucum

Chascanum obovatum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the blue-flowered chascanum, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and frost-resistant nature.
