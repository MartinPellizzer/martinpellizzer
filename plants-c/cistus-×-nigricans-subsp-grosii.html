Cistus × nigricans subsp. grosii

Cistus × nigricans subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the dark rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and flowers that range from pink to purple.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
