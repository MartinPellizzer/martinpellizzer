Calliandra portoricensis var. multijuga

Calliandra portoricensis var. multijuga, commonly known as the Puerto Rican calliandra, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and thorn-like structures on its stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and supporting biodiversity.
