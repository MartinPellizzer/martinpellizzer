Cyperus solutus

Cyperus solutus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and waterways. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, often forming part of the vegetation in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
