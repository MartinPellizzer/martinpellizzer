Covola

Covola, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Covola thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
