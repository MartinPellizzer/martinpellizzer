Centaurea intercedens

Centaurea intercedens, commonly known as the interceding centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

Centaurea intercedens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
