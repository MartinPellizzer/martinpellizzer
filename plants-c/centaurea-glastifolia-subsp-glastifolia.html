Centaurea glastifolia subsp. glastifolia

Centaurea glastifolia subsp.

glastifolia, commonly known as the Glastifolia thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. Its bracts are often glandular and may exude a milky sap.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
