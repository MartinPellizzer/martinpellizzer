Cirsium libanoticum subsp. lycaonicum

Cirsium libanoticum subsp.

lycaonicum, commonly known as the Lycaonic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features robust, spiny leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-gray underside.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
