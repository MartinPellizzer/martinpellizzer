Cerastium banaticum

Cerastium banaticum, commonly known as the Banat chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium banaticum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons, making it a persistent ground cover in its native habitat.
