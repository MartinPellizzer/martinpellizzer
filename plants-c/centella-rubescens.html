Centella rubescens

Centella rubescens, commonly known as red-centella or red nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Centella rubescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
