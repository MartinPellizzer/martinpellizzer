Cannabis lupulus

Cannabis lupulus, commonly known as hops, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its cone-shaped, glandular flowers that contain essential oils and bitter compounds. The plant's rhizomes and roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Hops are widely cultivated for use in brewing beer, where they contribute bitterness, aroma, and stability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cannabaceae family, which also includes cannabis sativa and cannabis indica.
