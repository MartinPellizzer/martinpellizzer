Cibirhiza dhofarensis

Cibirhiza dhofarensis, commonly known as the Dhofar acacia, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Cibirhiza dhofarensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
