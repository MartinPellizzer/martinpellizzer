Cucurbita vacua

Cucurbita vacua, commonly known as the wild pumpkin or prickly gourd, is a species native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squashes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and elongated, spiny fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The fruit is typically used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
