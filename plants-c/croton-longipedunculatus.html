Croton longipedunculatus

Croton longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in long, drooping inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Croton longipedunculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
