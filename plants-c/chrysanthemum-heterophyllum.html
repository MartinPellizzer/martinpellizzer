Chrysanthemum heterophyllum

Chrysanthemum heterophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan and China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in the fall and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
