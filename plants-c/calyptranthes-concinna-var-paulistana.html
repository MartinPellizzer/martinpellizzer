Calyptranthes concinna var. paulistana

Calyptranthes concinna var. paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian starflower, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in spring and are known for their star-like appearance, which gives the plant its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
