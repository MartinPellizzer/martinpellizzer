Carex decora

Carex decora, commonly known as the Decorative Sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex decora has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers in the summer.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects and as an ornamental plant in water gardens.
