Cheiranthus knowlesii

Cheiranthus knowlesii, commonly known as the Knowles's mustards, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
