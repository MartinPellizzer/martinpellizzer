Cerastium decalvans subsp. adamovicii

Cerastium decalvans subsp.

adamovicii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh winter conditions.
