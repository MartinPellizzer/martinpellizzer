Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. acutum

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. acutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
