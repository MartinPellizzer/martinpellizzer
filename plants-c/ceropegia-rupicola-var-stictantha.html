Ceropegia rupicola var. stictantha

Ceropegia rupicola var. stictantha, commonly known as the rock ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rockeries and container gardening.
