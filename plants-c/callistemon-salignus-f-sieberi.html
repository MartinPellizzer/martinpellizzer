Callistemon salignus f. sieberi

Callistemon salignus f. sieberi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy, with a dense, upright growth habit. The plant is notable for its striking, crimson-red bottlebrush flowers that bloom in winter and early spring, attracting birds and pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a favored plant in both landscape design and cultivation.
