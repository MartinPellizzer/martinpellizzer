Ceiba glaziovii var. glabriflora

Ceiba glaziovii var. glabriflora, commonly known as the white ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk that can reach heights of up to 40 meters. The tree produces showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a silky, fibrous coating.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for numerous species.
