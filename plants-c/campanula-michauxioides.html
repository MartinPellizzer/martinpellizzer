Campanula michauxioides

Campanula michauxioides, commonly known as the Michaux's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Campanula michauxioides is often found in rich, loamy soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
