Crassula magellanica

Crassula magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
