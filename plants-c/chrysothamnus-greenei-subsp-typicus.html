Chrysothamnus greenei subsp. typicus

Chrysothamnus greenei subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its yellow-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in dense, brush-like clusters. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
