Cepa

Cepa, commonly known as leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to onions and garlic.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with a bulbous base that is the primary edible part. The leaves are long, cylindrical, and hollow, forming a dense rosette around the base. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its mild, sweet flavor and is often used in various culinary dishes.
