Cynometra acutiflora

Cynometra acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Cynometra acutiflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber and ecological importance.
