Chimantaea lanocaulis

Chimantaea lanocaulis, commonly known as the woolly stem plant, is a unique species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly stems that provide insulation against the cold, high-altitude environment. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Chimantaea lanocaulis is considered a rare and endangered species.
