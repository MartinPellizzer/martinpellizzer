Centaurea cassia subsp. dumanii

Centaurea cassia subsp.

dumanii, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
