Carlina oligocephala subsp. pallescens

Carlina oligocephala subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale-headed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its pale yellow to white flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, and the leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
