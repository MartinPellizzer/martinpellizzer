Callista grandis

Callista grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant white or pink hues. The plant features broad, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Callista grandis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its fragrant flowers.
