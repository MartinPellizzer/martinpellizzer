Cryptocoryne ciliata

Cryptocoryne ciliata, commonly known as the velvet cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats that help stabilize sediment.

Cryptocoryne ciliata is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
