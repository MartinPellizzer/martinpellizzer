Crassula pulchella

Crassula pulchella, commonly known as the silver rose or frost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
