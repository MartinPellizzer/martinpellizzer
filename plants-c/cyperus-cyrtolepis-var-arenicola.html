Cyperus cyrtolepis var. arenicola

Cyperus cyrtolepis var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
