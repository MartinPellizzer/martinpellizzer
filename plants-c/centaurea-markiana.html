Centaurea markiana

Centaurea markiana, commonly known as the Markian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Centaurea markiana is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
