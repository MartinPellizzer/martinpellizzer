Crataegus laurentiana

Crataegus laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
