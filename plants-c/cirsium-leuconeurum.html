Cirsium leuconeurum

Cirsium leuconeurum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Cirsium leuconeurum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
