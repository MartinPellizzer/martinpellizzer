Craspedodictyum quinatum

Craspedodictyum quinatum, commonly known as the Quinatum Cacti, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
