Cantharospermum volubile

Cantharospermum volubile, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
