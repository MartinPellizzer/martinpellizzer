Calamintha alpina var. orontia

Calamintha alpina var. orontia, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for digestive and respiratory ailments.
