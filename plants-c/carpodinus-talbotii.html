Carpodinus talbotii

Carpodinus talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ground-covering habit.
