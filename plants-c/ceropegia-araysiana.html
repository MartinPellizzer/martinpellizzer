Ceropegia araysiana

Ceropegia araysiana, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or the snake plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
