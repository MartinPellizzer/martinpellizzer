Centaurea ferulacea

Centaurea ferulacea, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Centaurea ferulacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
