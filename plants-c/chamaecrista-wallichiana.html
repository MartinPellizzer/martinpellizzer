Chamaecrista wallichiana

Chamaecrista wallichiana, commonly known as the Indian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its seeds are often used as a source of protein and can be ground into flour.
