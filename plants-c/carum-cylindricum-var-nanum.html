Carum cylindricum var. nanum

Carum cylindricum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf cylindrical caraway, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, cylindrical, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with hairy, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and it blooms during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental and practical uses.
