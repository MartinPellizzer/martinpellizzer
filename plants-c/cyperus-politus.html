Cyperus politus

Cyperus politus, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and dark green in color. Its flowering spikes are cylindrical and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together.

Cyperus politus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
