Convolvulus longipes

Convolvulus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a hairy texture.

Convolvulus longipes is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
