Crinum bancanum

Crinum bancanum, commonly known as the African lily or white crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Crinum bancanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
