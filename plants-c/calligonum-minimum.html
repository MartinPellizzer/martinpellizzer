Calligonum minimum

Calligonum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf boxthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Calligonum minimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
