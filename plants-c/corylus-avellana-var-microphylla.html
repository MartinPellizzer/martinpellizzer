Corylus avellana var. microphylla

Corylus avellana var. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature hazelnut, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a smooth, reddish-brown husk, which ripen in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and unique foliage.
