Cicerbita astorensis

Cicerbita astorensis, commonly known as the Astaran bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent, often edible leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and serrated, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

Cicerbita astorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
