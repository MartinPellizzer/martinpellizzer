Canistrum fragrans

Canistrum fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant canister, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, particularly in the evening. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often armed with spines.

Canistrum fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
