Crotalaria virgulta

Crotalaria virgulta, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Crotalaria virgulta is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in various environments.
