Centaurea pseudophilostizus

Centaurea pseudophilostizus, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
