Carica × pentagona

Carica × pentagona, commonly known as the pentagona papaya, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Carica papaya* and *Carica pubescens*.

It is characterized by its large, pentagonal-shaped fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn orange-yellow when mature. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy flesh and is commonly consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
