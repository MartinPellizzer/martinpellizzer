Centaurea fragilis var. integrifolia

Centaurea fragilis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
