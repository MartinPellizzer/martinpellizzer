Coursetia caribaea var. tomentosa

Coursetia caribaea var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Caribbean coursetia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are densely covered in fine, white pubescence, giving the plant a fluffy appearance. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical areas.
