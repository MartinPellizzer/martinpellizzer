Calamagrostis columbiensis

Calamagrostis columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
