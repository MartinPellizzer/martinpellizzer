Cissus gibertii

Cissus gibertii, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a climbing or trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cissus gibertii is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
