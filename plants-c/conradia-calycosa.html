Conradia calycosa

Conradia calycosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
