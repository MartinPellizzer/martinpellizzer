Cistus salviifolius var. cymosus

Cistus salviifolius var. cymosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies.
