Carex glauca var. arrecta

Carex glauca var. arrecta, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
