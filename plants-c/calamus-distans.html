Calamus distans

Calamus distans, commonly known as the distant sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Calamus distans produces small, clustered flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous nature.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
