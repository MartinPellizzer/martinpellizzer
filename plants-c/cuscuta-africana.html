Cuscuta africana

Cuscuta africana, commonly known as the African dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to Africa and can be found growing on a variety of host species, including crops like maize and beans. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, allowing it to spread widely.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
