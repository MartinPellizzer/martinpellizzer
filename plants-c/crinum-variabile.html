Crinum variabile

Crinum variabile, commonly known as the variable amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Crinum variabile is known for its long blooming period, typically from summer to fall, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
