Cordia campanulata

Cordia campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
