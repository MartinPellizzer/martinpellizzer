Cenchrus elegans

Cenchrus elegans, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with silky hairs.

Cenchrus elegans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
