Cirsium poeltii

Cirsium poeltii, commonly known as the Poelt's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with well-drained soil. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in coarse, prickly hairs, while its flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets.

Cirsium poeltii is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
