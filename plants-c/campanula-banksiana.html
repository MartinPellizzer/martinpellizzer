Campanula banksiana

Campanula banksiana, commonly known as the Banksian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula banksiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blossoms.
