Convolvulus cirrhosus

Convolvulus cirrhosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Convolvulus cirrhosus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
