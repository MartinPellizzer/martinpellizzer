Cirsium sommieri

Cirsium sommieri, commonly known as the summer thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium sommieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
