Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. howardii

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

howardii, commonly known as the Howard's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its structure consists of multiple stems with dense foliage, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
