Cirsium rosani

Cirsium rosani, commonly known as the Rosani thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, erect stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and serving as a habitat for various small invertebrates.
