Colona scabra

Colona scabra, commonly known as the rough colona, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
