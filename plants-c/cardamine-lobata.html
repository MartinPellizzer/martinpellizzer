Cardamine lobata

Cardamine lobata, commonly known as narrowleaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, lobed leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and lilies.
