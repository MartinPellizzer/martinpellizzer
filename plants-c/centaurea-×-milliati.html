Centaurea × milliati

Centaurea × milliati, commonly known as the Blueweed or Milletflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to self-seed, contributing to its naturalization in certain regions.
