Cheiranthus arkansanus

Cheiranthus arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas poppy, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
