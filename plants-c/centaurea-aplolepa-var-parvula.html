Centaurea aplolepa var. parvula

Centaurea aplolepa var. parvula, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with erect stems and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with marginal spines and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is an invasive species in many regions outside its native range, posing challenges to agricultural and natural ecosystems.
