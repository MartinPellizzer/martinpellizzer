Crocus filis-maculatis

Crocus filis-maculatis, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with distinctive purple or brown markings. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The spotted crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
