Campanula lusitanica f. racemosopaniculata

Campanula lusitanica f. racemosopaniculata, commonly known as the racemosopaniculata form of the Lusitanian bellflower, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive inflorescence structure.

This form exhibits a racemoso-paniculate arrangement, meaning its flowers are arranged in a combination of racemose and paniculate patterns, creating a more complex and branched flowering structure. The plant typically features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is native to specific regions of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral morphology and its role in the study of plant variation within the Campanulaceae family.
