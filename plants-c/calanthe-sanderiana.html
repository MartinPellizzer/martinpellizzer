Calanthe sanderiana

Calanthe sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
