Cornus drummondii

Cornus drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive white midrib.

Cornus drummondii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
