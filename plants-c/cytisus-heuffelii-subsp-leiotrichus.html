Cytisus heuffelii subsp. leiotrichus

Cytisus heuffelii subsp.

leiotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a distinctive silver or grayish underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
