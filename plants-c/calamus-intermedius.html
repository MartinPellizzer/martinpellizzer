Calamus intermedius

Calamus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that are often used as a food source. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are typical of the ginger family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a distinct, aromatic scent.

Calamus intermedius is often cultivated for its edible rhizomes and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
