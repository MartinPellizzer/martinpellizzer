Cyathea humbertiana

Cyathea humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea humbertiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
