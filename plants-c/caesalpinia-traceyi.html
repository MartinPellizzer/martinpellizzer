Caesalpinia traceyi

Caesalpinia traceyi, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its fruits are elongated pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
