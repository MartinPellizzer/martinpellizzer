Chamaecytisus aggregatus

Chamaecytisus aggregatus, commonly known as the sweet pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, thornless stems that often grow along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Chamaecytisus aggregatus is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
