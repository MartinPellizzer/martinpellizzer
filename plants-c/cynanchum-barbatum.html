Cynanchum barbatum

Cynanchum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Russia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Cynanchum barbatum has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
