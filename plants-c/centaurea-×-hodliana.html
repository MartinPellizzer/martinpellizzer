Centaurea × hodliana

Centaurea × hodliana, commonly known as the Hodliana knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grayish in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
