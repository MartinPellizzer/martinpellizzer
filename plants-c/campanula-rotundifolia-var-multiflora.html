Campanula rotundifolia var. multiflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its ecological benefits to pollinators.
