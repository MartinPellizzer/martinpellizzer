Calea acaulis

Calea acaulis, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Maya, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, white, velvety flowers that open at night. The plant is often found growing in shaded, humid environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are broad and have a unique, waxy texture, while its flowers emit a mild fragrance.

Calea acaulis is known for its spiritual significance and has been used in traditional rituals and medicine.
