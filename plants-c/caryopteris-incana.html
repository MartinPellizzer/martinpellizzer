Caryopteris incana

Caryopteris incana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue-to-lavender flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Caryopteris incana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
