Convolvulus pseudocantabrica

Convolvulus pseudocantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Mediterranean climate.
