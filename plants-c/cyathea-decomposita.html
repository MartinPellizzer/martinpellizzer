Cyathea decomposita

Cyathea decomposita, commonly known as the decomposed fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its fronds are sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
