Chlorospatha sucumbensis

Chlorospatha sucumbensis, commonly known as the fading leaf vine, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that gradually lose their color as the plant matures, a phenomenon linked to its unique photosynthetic adaptations. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom only during the early morning hours, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots are known to secrete a mild, sweet-smelling resin that has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Chlorospatha sucumbensis is now classified as critically endangered.
