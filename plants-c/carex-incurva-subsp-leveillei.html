Carex incurva subsp. leveillei

Carex incurva subsp.

leveillei, commonly known as the bent spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
