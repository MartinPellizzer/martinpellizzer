Callianthemum farreri

Callianthemum farreri, commonly known as the Farri's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and often hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Callianthemum farreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
