Ceropegia viridis

Ceropegia viridis, commonly known as the Green Milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and small, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, attracting specific pollinators like moths and flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ceropegia viridis is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
