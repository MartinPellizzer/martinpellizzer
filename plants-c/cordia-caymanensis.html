Cordia caymanensis

Cordia caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman Islands cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Cayman Islands.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in the region.
