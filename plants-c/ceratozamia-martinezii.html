Ceratozamia martinezii

Ceratozamia martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's cycad, is a rare and ancient gymnosperm native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a unique subject of botanical study.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
