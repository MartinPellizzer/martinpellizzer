Cyathea leoniae

Cyathea leoniae, commonly known as the Leonia hakea, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest environments, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the structural support of the plant.

Cyathea leoniae plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native species.
