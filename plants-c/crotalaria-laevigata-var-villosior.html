Crotalaria laevigata var. villosior

Crotalaria laevigata var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It features palmate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which is a key identifying trait of the variety. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Crotalaria genus. It is native to regions of South America and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain environments.
