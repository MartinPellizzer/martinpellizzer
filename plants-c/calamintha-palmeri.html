Calamintha palmeri

Calamintha palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calamintha palmeri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
