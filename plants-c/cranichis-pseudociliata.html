Cranichis pseudociliata

Cranichis pseudociliata, commonly known as the false beard orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its unique morphology includes a pseudobulb and a single, erect stem with a cluster of flowers at the top.

Cranichis pseudociliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
