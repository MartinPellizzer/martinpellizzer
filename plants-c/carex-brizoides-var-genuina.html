Carex brizoides var. genuina

Carex brizoides var. genuina, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and triangular stems. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
