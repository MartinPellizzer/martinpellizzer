Carex caricina var. major

Carex caricina var. major, commonly known as the large marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
