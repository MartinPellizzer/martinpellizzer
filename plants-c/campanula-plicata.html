Campanula plicata

Campanula plicata, commonly known as the pleated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pleated, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula plicata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
