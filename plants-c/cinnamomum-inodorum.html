Cinnamomum inodorum

Cinnamomum inodorum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces berries that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, while its bark has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
