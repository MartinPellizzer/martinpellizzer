Celastrus zeyheri

Celastrus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, and the plant is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Celastrus zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
