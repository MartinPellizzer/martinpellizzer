Convolvulus pauciflorus var. chilensis

Convolvulus pauciflorus var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is adapted to thrive in arid and coastal climates, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
