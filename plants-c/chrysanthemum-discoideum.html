Chrysanthemum discoideum

Chrysanthemum discoideum, commonly known as the star chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like flowers with white petals that have a star-shaped pattern. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the center, surrounded by ray florets.

Chrysanthemum discoideum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
