Cassia ignorata

Cassia ignorata, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are arranged in racemes, typical of the genus Cassia. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets.

Cassia ignorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
