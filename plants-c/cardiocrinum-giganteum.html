Cardiocrinum giganteum

Cardiocrinum giganteum, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, moist soils.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
