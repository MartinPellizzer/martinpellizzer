Corchorus discolor

Corchorus discolor, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and textile production.

Corchorus discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
