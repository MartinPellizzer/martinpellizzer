Carex enokii

Carex enokii, commonly known as the Enoki sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features dense, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular seed heads.

Carex enokii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils in shaded environments.
