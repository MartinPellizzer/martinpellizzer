Cynodon amabilis

Cynodon amabilis, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky-textured foliage that gives it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form a dense ground cover.

Cynodon amabilis is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
