Cereus geometrizans

Cereus geometrizans, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching, spiny stems and clusters of bright, showy flowers that typically bloom during the holiday season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, spiny structures, and it produces yellow or white flowers with prominent stamens.

Cereus geometrizans is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
