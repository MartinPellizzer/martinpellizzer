Centaurea × brandzae

Centaurea × brandzae, commonly known as the hybrid gladiolus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Centaurea × brandzae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
