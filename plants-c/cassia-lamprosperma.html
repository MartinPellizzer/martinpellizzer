Cassia lamprosperma

Cassia lamprosperma, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia lamprosperma is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
