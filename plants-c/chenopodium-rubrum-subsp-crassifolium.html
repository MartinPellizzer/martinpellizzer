Chenopodium rubrum subsp. crassifolium

Chenopodium rubrum subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as red sandspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, ovate to rhomboid leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny, inflated seed cases. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and roadside areas.
