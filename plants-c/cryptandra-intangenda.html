Cryptandra intangenda

Cryptandra intangenda, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Cryptandra intangenda is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
