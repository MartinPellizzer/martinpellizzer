Clematis florida var. bicolor

Clematis florida var. bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese vine or white-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
