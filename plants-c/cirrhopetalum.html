Cirrhopetalum

Cirrhopetalum, commonly known as the silky petal orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are characterized by their delicate, often fragrant flowers with long, silky petals that give the genus its name. Cirrhopetalum species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological roles in their native habitats.
