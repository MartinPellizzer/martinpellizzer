Cassia hispidula

Cassia hispidula, commonly known as the rough cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia hispidula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
