Cytinus hypocistis var. lutea

Cytinus hypocistis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow broomcup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and fleshy, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, often appearing as a rosette, and the stems are usually short and unbranched.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
