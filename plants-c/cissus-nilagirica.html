Cissus nilagirica

Cissus nilagirica, commonly known as the Nilagiri Cissus, is a climbing plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Cissus nilagirica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
