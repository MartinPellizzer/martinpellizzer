Centaurea alluvialis

Centaurea alluvialis, commonly known as the alluvial knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in riverbanks and coastal areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

Centaurea alluvialis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
