Centaurea acmophylla

Centaurea acmophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea acmophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
