Cruciata ciliata f. hirsutissima

Cruciata ciliata f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy cruciata, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, triangular leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
