Clematis mauritiana var. strigillosa

Clematis mauritiana var. strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its foliage is often covered in fine, short hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
