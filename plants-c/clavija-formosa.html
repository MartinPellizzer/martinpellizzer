Clavija formosa

Clavija formosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Clavija formosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
