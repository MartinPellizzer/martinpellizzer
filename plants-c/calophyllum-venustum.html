Calophyllum venustum

Calophyllum venustum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for landscaping purposes.
