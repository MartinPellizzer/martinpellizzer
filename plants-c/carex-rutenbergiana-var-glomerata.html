Carex rutenbergiana var. glomerata

Carex rutenbergiana var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
