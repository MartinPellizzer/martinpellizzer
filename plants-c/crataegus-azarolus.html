Crataegus azarolus

Crataegus azarolus, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The yellow hawthorn is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
