Cissampelos zairensis

Cissampelos zairensis, commonly known as the Zaire climbing vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
