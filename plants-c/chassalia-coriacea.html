Chassalia coriacea

Chassalia coriacea, commonly known as the rough chassalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features rough, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Chassalia coriacea is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
