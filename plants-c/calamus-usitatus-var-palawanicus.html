Calamus usitatus var. palawanicus

Calamus usitatus var. palawanicus, commonly known as the Palauan calamus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the Caroline Islands and Palau. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species.

This variety is often found in coastal and lowland areas, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
