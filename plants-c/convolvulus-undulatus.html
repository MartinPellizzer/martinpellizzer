Convolvulus undulatus

Convolvulus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that features tendrils for climbing, often forming dense mats or vines. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and twining.

Convolvulus undulatus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.
