Chrysalidocarpus glaucescens

Chrysalidocarpus glaucescens, commonly known as the sugar cane palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, grayish trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-sided, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching material.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
