Capparis zippeliana var. novobritannica

Capparis zippeliana var. novobritannica, commonly known as the New Britain caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in its native habitats.
