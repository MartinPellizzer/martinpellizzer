Cordia ulei

Cordia ulei, commonly known as African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored wood and distinctive, fragrant leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, tropical regions.

Cordia ulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
