Cirsium hainanense

Cirsium hainanense, commonly known as Hainan thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its flowers are usually visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Cirsium hainanense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
