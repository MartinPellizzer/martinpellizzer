Crocanthemum hirsutissimum

Crocanthemum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy crocanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
