Carex festucacea var. brevior

Carex festucacea var. brevior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
