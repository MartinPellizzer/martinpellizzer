Campanula nervosa

Campanula nervosa, commonly known as the nervous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct veining pattern, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Campanula nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
