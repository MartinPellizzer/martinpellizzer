Calyptridium quadripetalum

Calyptridium quadripetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled calyptridium, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its flowers are small, with four petals arranged symmetrically, and it produces achenes as its fruit type.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
