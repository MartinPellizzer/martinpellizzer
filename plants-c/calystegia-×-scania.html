Calystegia × scania

Calystegia × scania, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a hybrid species belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and has a strong, vine-like stem that can climb or trail along the ground. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Calystegia × scania is valued for its ornamental appeal but can be invasive in some regions.
