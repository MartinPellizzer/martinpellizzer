Croton astroites var. genuinus

Croton astroites var. genuinus, commonly known as the Hawaiian croton, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, which are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts aim to protect its natural habitat.
