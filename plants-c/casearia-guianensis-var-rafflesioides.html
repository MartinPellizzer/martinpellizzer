Casearia guianensis var. rafflesioides

Casearia guianensis var. rafflesioides, commonly known as the giant casearia, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are highly ornamental. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its showy foliage and unique fruit.

Due to its size and appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
