Celastrus alatus

Celastrus alatus, commonly known as the winged elder or American winged vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its stems are distinctive, featuring wing-like ridges that give it its common name.

Celastrus alatus is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
