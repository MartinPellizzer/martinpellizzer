Carex fraterna

Carex fraterna, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Carex fraterna has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
