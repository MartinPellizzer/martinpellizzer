Chrozophora obliqua var. hierosolymitana

Chrozophora obliqua var. hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
