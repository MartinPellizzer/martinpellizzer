Chazaliella obovoidea subsp. villosistipula

Chazaliella obovoidea subsp.

villosistipula, commonly known as the hairy-stippled chazaliella, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stipules. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
