Cistus cinereus

Cistus cinereus, commonly known as the grey cistus, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is drought-tolerant and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are also used in the production of essential oils and perfumes.
