Chamaesyce mendezii

Chamaesyce mendezii, commonly known as the Mexican sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Chamaesyce mendezii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
