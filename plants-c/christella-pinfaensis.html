Christella pinfaensis

Christella pinfaensis, commonly known as the Pinfaensian Christella, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring season. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Christella pinfaensis is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
