Cryptocarya micrantha

Cryptocarya micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
