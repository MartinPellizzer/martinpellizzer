Clematis terniflora f. denticulata

Clematis terniflora f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, which give it its specific epithet "denticulata.". Its showy, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
