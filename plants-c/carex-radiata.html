Carex radiata

Carex radiata, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex radiata is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
