Cassia nictitans var. hebecarpa

Cassia nictitans var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow-orange pods that contain numerous flat, elliptical seeds. The plant produces feathery, bipinnate leaves and is known for its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of this variety are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
