Chlorospatha sizemoreae

Chlorospatha sizemoreae, commonly known as the Sizemore's green fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rich, loamy soils along stream banks or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are small and inconspicuous, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
