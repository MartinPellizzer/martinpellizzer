Campanula aaronsohnii

Campanula aaronsohnii, commonly known as Aaronsohn's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-filled capsules for reproduction.

Campanula aaronsohnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
