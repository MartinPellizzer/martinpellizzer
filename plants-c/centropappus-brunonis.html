Centropappus brunonis

Centropappus brunonis, commonly known as the bluebunch silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the summer months. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, a common adaptation in grass species. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
