Conyza pencana

Conyza pencana, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza pencana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various habitats, including roadsides and fields.
