Carex foliata

Carex foliata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in dense, grass-like clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex foliata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
