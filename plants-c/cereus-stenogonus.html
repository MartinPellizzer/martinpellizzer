Cereus stenogonus

Cereus stenogonus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

Cereus stenogonus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
