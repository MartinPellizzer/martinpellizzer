Cyathea vernicosa

Cyathea vernicosa, commonly known as the velvet tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea vernicosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in some regions.
