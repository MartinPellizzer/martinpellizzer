Centaurea triumfettii var. slovenica

Centaurea triumfettii var. slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow centers, typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant has slender, branched stems and linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the family Asteraceae.
