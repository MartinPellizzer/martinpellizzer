Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. bourgaei

Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. bourgaei, commonly known as the fragrant cushion flower, is a hybrid form of the genus Chaenorhinum, characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage is aromatic, with leaves resembling those of oregano, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact form and delicate floral display.
