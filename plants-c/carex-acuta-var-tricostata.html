Carex acuta var. tricostata

Carex acuta var. tricostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers, which are usually borne on upright stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
