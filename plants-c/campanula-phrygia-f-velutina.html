Campanula phrygia f. velutina

Campanula phrygia f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture due to dense pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
