Colchicum gonarei

Colchicum gonarei, commonly known as the Gonarei colchicum, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers during the spring season. Its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids, which are used in medicinal applications but require careful handling.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject in both horticulture and pharmacology.
