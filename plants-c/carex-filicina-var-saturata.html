Carex filicina var. saturata

Carex filicina var. saturata, commonly known as the purple filica sedge.

This variant is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage, which adds a unique visual appeal to gardens and natural habitats. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, known for its grass-like appearance and ecological importance in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
