Correa glabra var. leucoclada

Correa glabra var. leucoclada, commonly known as the white correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and foliage.
