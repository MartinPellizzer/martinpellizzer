Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp. imintalense

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp.

imintalense, commonly known as the red-leafed horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive reddish hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
