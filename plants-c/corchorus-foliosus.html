Corchorus foliosus

Corchorus foliosus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Corchorus foliosus is typically found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.
