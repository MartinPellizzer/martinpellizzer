Combretum platypetalum subsp. baumii

Combretum platypetalum subsp.

baumii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, flame-like orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive glandular base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
