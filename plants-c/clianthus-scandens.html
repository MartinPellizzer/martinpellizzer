Clianthus scandens

Clianthus scandens, commonly known as the red mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a climbing shrub that produces striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces around the world.
