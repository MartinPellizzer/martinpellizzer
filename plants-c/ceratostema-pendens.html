Ceratostema pendens

Ceratostema pendens, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ceratostema pendens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and delicate appearance.
