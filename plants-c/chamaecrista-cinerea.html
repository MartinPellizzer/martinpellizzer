Chamaecrista cinerea

Chamaecrista cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are pinnately compound with a silvery-gray appearance. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

Chamaecrista cinerea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
