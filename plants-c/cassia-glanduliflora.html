Cassia glanduliflora

Cassia glanduliflora, commonly known as the glandular cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
