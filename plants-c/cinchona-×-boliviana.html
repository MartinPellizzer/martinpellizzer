Cinchona × boliviana

Cinchona × boliviana, commonly known as the hybrid cinchona, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of various cinchona species.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. This plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red flowers. It produces dark purple to black berries containing numerous seeds.

Cinchona × boliviana is valued for its historical role in the production of quinine, used in the treatment of malaria.
