Clematis uncinata f. retusa

Clematis uncinata f. retusa, commonly known as the smooth clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced, almost hairless foliage compared to other forms of Clematis uncinata.
