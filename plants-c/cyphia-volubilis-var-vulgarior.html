Cyphia volubilis var. vulgarior

Cyphia volubilis var. vulgarior, commonly known as the common climbing cyphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or trees.
