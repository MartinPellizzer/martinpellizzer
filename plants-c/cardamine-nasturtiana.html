Cardamine nasturtiana

Cardamine nasturtiana, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard.
