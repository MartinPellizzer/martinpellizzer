Cyperus schmitzianus

Cyperus schmitzianus, commonly known as the Schmitz's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically found in wet or marshy habitats. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus schmitzianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
