Croton regelii

Croton regelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Croton regelii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
