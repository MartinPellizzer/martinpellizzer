Croton perdicipes var. genuinus

Croton perdicipes var. genuinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
