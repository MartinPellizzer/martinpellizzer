Carbenia benedicta

Carbenia benedicta, commonly known as the Benedict's carbenia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Carbenia benedicta is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
