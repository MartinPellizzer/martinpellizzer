Clematis chinensis var. fujisanensis

Clematis chinensis var. fujisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
