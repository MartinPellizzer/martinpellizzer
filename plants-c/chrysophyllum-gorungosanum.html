Chrysophyllum gorungosanum

Chrysophyllum gorungosanum, commonly known as the African cherry or gorungosa, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor, encased in a hard, smooth shell. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
