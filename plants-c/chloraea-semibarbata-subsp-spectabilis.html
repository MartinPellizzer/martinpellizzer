Chloraea semibarbata subsp. spectabilis

Chloraea semibarbata subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the Spectacular Chloraea, is a striking flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally waterlogged areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
