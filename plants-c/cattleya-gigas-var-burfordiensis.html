Cattleya gigas var. burfordiensis

Cattleya gigas var. burfordiensis, commonly known as the Burford's giant orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with vibrant purple and white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include thick, fleshy roots and broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
