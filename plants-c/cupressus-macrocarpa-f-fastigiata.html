Cupressus macrocarpa f. fastigiata

Cupressus macrocarpa f. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lacebark, is a cultivar of the Australian brushwood tree.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are scale-like and closely arranged, giving the plant a compact appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for a variety of landscaping applications.
