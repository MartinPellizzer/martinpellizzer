Carex hostiana var. laurentiana

Carex hostiana var. laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers in the summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
