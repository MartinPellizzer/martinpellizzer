Chamaecrista grantii

Chamaecrista grantii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
