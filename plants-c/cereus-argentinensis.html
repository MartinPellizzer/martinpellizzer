Cereus argentinensis

Cereus argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine candleplant, is a flowering cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are fragrant and typically open only during the evening. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Cereus argentinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
