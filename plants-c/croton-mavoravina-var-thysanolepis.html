Croton mavoravina var. thysanolepis

Croton mavoravina var. thysanolepis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
