Crepis dakotana

Crepis dakotana, commonly known as the Dakota hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crepis dakotana is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including prairies and roadsides.
