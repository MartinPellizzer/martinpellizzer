Convallaria multiflora var. major

Convallaria multiflora var. major, commonly known as lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lily of the valley is also valued for its historical use in perfumes and as a symbol of humility and purity.
