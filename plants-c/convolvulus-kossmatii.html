Convolvulus kossmatii

Convolvulus kossmatii, commonly known as the Kossmat's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Convolvulus kossmatii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
