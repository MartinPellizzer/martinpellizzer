Cymbidium angolense

Cymbidium angolense, commonly known as the Angola orchid, is a striking orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium angolense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
