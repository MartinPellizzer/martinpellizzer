Cerasus × subhirtella f. tama-clivorum

Cerasus × subhirtella f. tama-clivorum, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a hybrid species of Prunus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded or spreading form. Its leaves are oval and have a glossy surface, turning yellow in autumn.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and landscapes.
