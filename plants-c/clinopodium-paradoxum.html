Clinopodium paradoxum

Clinopodium paradoxum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, often forming dense mats. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are fragrant when crushed, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
