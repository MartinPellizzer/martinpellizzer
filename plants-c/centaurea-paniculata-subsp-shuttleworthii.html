Centaurea paniculata subsp. shuttleworthii

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

shuttleworthii, commonly known as Shuttleworth's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and dense, spherical clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
