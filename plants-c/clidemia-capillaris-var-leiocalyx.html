Clidemia capillaris var. leiocalyx

Clidemia capillaris var. leiocalyx, commonly known as the hairy shieldflower, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
