Callicarpa eriophylla

Callicarpa eriophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa eriophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
