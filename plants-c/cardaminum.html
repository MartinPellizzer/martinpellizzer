Cardaminum

Cardaminum, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruits are slender siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Cardaminum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.
