Cruckshanksia oblonga

Cruckshanksia oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf cruckshanksia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is increasingly cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
