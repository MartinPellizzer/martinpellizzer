Carex maackii

Carex maackii, commonly known as Maack's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Carex maackii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
