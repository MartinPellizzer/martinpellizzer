Cyperus nigripes

Cyperus nigripes, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Cyperus nigripes has small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed, dark brown to black seed capsules.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
