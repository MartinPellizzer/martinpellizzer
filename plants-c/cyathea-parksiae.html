Cyathea parksiae

Cyathea parksiae, commonly known as the Parksia tree fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds. The fronds are typically arched and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Cyathea parksiae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
