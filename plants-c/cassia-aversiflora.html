Cassia aversiflora

Cassia aversiflora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. Cassia aversiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
