Calceolaria cana

Calceolaria cana, commonly known as the slender slipper orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that resemble small slippers, typically in shades of yellow and orange. Calceolaria cana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
