Carex × gotlandica

Carex × gotlandica, commonly known as the Gotland sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to parts of Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
