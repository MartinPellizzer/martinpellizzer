Curcuma bakeriana

Curcuma bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's turmeric, is a species of ginger plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, orange-yellow rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter.

Curcuma bakeriana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of curcuminoids in its rhizomes.
