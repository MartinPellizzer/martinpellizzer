Carduus fastigiatus

Carduus fastigiatus, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
