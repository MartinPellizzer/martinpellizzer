Cyperus pilosus var. pseudobabakensis

Cyperus pilosus var. pseudobabakensis, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
