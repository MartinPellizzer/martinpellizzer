Combretum relictum

Combretum relictum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
