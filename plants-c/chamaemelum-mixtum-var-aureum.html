Chamaemelum mixtum var. aureum

Chamaemelum mixtum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and aromatic, with a slightly bitter scent.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
