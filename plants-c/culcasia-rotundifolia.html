Culcasia rotundifolia

Culcasia rotundifolia, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its roots are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

The plant also plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various species.
