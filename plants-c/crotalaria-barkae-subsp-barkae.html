Crotalaria barkae subsp. barkae

Crotalaria barkae subsp.

barkae, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, ridged pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The bark and leaves contain alkaloids, making it both medicinally significant and potentially toxic.
