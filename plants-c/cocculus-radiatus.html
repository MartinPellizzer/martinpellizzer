Cocculus radiatus

Cocculus radiatus, commonly known as the star-shaped morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces distinctive berries that are typically orange or yellow when mature. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.

The botanical features of Cocculus radiatus make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
