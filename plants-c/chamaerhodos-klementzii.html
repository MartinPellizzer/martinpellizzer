Chamaerhodos klementzii

Chamaerhodos klementzii, commonly known as the Klementz's redroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
