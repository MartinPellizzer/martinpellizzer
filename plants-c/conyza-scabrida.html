Conyza scabrida

Conyza scabrida, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Conyza scabrida produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
