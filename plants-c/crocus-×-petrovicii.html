Crocus × petrovicii

Crocus × petrovicii, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or lilac-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10–20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during unfavorable conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.
