Carya ovalis var. mexicana

Carya ovalis var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican hickory, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing sweet, starchy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and has historical uses in traditional crafts and food preparation.
