Chrysophyllum sericeum var. obtusifolium

Chrysophyllum sericeum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf cherimoya, is a variant of the cherimoya plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that are broad and ovate with a rounded tip. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, spiny fruit known as the cherimoya. Its botanical features make it a unique ornamental and edible plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
