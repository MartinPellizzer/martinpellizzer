Cyperus bifolius

Cyperus bifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of grass-like plants.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are often arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive arrangement of veins.

Cyperus bifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
