Centaurea tardiflora

Centaurea tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom late in the growing season, typically from late summer to autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
