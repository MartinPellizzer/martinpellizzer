Ceanothus ferrugineus

Ceanothus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland areas, often in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive bluish-gray color.

Ceanothus ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
