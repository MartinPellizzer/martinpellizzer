Cojoba catenata

Cojoba catenata, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a shallow root system, allowing it to tolerate salt spray and wind exposure.

Cojoba catenata is also known for its ability to bloom year-round under favorable conditions.
