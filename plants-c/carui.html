Carui

Carui, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are used in traditional medicine, while its seeds are a source of food.

The lotus is also symbolically significant in many cultures, representing purity and enlightenment.
