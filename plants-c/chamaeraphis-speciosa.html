Chamaeraphis speciosa

Chamaeraphis speciosa, commonly known as the showy glassworts, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh coastal environment. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Chamaeraphis speciosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
