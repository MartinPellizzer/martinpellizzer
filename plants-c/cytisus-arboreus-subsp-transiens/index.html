Cytisus Arboreus Subsp. Transiens

Cytisus arboreus subsp. transiens is a rare and fascinating plant native to parts of southern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. This subspecies is distinguished by its delicate, yellow flowers and slender, branching structure. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodland edges. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
