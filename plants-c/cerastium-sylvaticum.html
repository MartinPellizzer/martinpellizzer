Cerastium sylvaticum

Cerastium sylvaticum, commonly known as woodland mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

Cerastium sylvaticum thrives in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
