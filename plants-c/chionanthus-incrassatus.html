Chionanthus incrassatus

Chionanthus incrassatus, commonly known as the Chinese Fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fringed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in cultivation, it is often sought after by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
