Camissonia brevipes

Camissonia brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous form. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Camissonia brevipes is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is known for its resilience in dry environments.
