Carex distans subsp. binervis

Carex distans subsp.

binervis, commonly known as the two-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The subspecies binervis is distinguished by its two distinct veins on the leaf blades, giving it its common name.
