Crocus antherotes

Crocus antherotes, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a purple or brown center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters.

The Persian crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
