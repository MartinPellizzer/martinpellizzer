Cestrum scandens var. terminale

Cestrum scandens var. terminale, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous climbing shrub that produces showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nocturnal beauty and ornamental value.
