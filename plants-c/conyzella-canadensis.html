Conyzella canadensis

Conyzella canadensis, commonly known as Canadian ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. It is known for producing allergenic pollen, contributing to seasonal allergies in humans.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host for various insects.
