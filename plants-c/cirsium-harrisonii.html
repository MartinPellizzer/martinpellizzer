Cirsium harrisonii

Cirsium harrisonii, commonly known as Harrison's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
