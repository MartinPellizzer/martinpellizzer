Carex verna f. globuliflora

Carex verna f. globuliflora, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are arranged in dense, globular clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
