Ceroxylon andicola var. occidentale

Ceroxylon andicola var. occidentale, commonly known as the western Andean wax palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its long, slender leaves and a smooth, waxy trunk. The plant thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, where it adapts to cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
