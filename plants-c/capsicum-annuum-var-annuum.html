Capsicum annuum var. annuum

Capsicum annuum var. annuum, commonly known as bell pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its fleshy, bell-shaped fruits that can range in color from green to red, yellow, orange, and even purple. The plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated worldwide for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and the plant produces a variety of fruit sizes and shapes depending on the cultivar. Capsicum annuum var. annuum is a dicot with alternate, ovate leaves and is often grown in warm climates for optimal growth.
