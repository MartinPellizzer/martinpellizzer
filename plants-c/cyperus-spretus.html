Cyperus spretus

Cyperus spretus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. Cyperus spretus is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, with rhizomes that help it spread and survive in flooded conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
