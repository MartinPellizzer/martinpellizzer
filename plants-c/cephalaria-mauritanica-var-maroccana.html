Cephalaria mauritanica var. maroccana

Cephalaria mauritanica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal uses.
