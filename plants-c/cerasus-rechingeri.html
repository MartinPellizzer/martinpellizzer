Cerasus rechingeri

Cerasus rechingeri, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Rechinger's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
