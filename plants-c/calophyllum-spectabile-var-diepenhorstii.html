Calophyllum spectabile var. diepenhorstii

Calophyllum spectabile var. diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's White Mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local communities.
