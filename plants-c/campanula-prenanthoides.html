Campanula prenanthoides

Campanula prenanthoides, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula prenanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
