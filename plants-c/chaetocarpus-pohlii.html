Chaetocarpus pohlii

Chaetocarpus pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Chaetocarpus pohlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
