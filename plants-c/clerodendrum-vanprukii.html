Clerodendrum vanprukii

Clerodendrum vanprukii, commonly known as the Vanprukii flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. These flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
