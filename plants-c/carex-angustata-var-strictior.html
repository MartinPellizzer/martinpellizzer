Carex angustata var. strictior

Carex angustata var. strictior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wetland habitats.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.
