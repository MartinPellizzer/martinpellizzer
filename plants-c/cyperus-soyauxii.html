Cyperus soyauxii

Cyperus soyauxii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ditches, and pond margins. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Cyperus soyauxii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
