Cinnamomum lowii

Cinnamomum lowii, commonly known as the Hainan cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which are rich in essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while the bark is reddish-brown and fragrant.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
