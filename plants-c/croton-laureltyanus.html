Croton laureltyanus

Croton laureltyanus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and diverse leaf shapes. The plant typically has smooth, glossy leaves that are often alternate and vary in color from green to reddish. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
