Cerastium octandrum var. octandrum

Cerastium octandrum var. octandrum, commonly known as the eight-stamen chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers with eight stamens, which are a distinctive botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as capsules, containing numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are opposite, linear to ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance and ecological role.
