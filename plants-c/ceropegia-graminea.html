Ceropegia graminea

Ceropegia graminea, commonly known as the carpet vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia graminea is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its cascading growth habit.
