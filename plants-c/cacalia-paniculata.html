Cacalia paniculata

Cacalia paniculata, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in panicles. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia paniculata is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
