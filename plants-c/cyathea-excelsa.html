Cyathea excelsa

Cyathea excelsa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea excelsa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
