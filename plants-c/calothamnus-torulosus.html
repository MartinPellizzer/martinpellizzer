Calothamnus torulosus

Calothamnus torulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.

Calothamnus torulosus is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and is well adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.
