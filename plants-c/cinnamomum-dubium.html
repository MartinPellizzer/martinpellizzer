Cinnamomum dubium

Cinnamomum dubium, commonly known as the African cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and emit a strong cinnamon scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its aromatic bark, which has been traditionally used in perfumery and as a spice.
