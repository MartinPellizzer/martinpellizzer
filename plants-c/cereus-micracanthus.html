Cereus micracanthus

Cereus micracanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom once a year, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

Cereus micracanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
