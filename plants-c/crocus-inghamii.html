Crocus inghamii

Crocus inghamii, commonly known as the Ingham's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
