Cyperus tremulus var. opulentus

Cyperus tremulus var. opulentus, commonly known as the velvet marsh grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant in moist soils.
