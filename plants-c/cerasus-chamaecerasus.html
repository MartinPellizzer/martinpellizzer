Cerasus chamaecerasus

Cerasus chamaecerasus, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscapes and botanical collections.
