Christisonia tricolor var. grandiflora

Christisonia tricolor var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Three-color Flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking tricolored flowers, typically featuring blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces small, fibrous seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant coloration and compact growth habit.
