Cistus monspeliensis var. densifolius

Cistus monspeliensis var. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and resilience to drought make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
