Chlorocyperus riograndensis

Chlorocyperus riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along riverbanks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
