Cordia guianensis

Cordia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese cordia, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
