Copernicia rubra

Copernicia rubra, commonly known as the red wax palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a unique red-colored wax, which has been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various purposes. Its growth habit is typically slow, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The red wax palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
