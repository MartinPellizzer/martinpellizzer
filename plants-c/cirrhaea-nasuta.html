Cirrhaea nasuta

Cirrhaea nasuta, commonly known as the slender swamp willow, is a shrub-like plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, which are a key feature of its reproductive structure. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its adaptability to wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
