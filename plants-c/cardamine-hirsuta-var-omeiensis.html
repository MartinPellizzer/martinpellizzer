Cardamine hirsuta var. omeiensis

Cardamine hirsuta var. omeiensis, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the broadleaf watercress native to specific regions in China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, and it is known for its peppery flavor when harvested young.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
