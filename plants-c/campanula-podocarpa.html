Campanula podocarpa

Campanula podocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula podocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.
