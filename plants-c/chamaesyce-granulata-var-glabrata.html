Chamaesyce granulata var. glabrata

Chamaesyce granulata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species often found in desert and semi-desert regions.
