Cotoneaster integerrimus subsp. niger

Cotoneaster integerrimus subsp.

niger, commonly known as black cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
