Cirrhopetalum compressum

Cirrhopetalum compressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and compressed, giving the plant its specific epithet "compressum.". The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
