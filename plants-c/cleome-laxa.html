Cleome laxa

Cleome laxa, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome laxa is often grown for its ornamental value and is easy to cultivate in gardens.
