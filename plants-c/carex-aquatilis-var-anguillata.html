Carex aquatilis var. anguillata

Carex aquatilis var. anguillata, commonly known as the ribbon sedge, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
