Carex pseudololiacea f. sejuncta

Carex pseudololiacea f. sejuncta, commonly known as the sparse-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its unique foliage structure.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often spaced apart, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other sedges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This form is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
