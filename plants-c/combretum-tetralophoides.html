Combretum tetralophoides

Combretum tetralophoides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
