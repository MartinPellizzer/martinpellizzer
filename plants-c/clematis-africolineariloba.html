Clematis africolineariloba

Clematis africolineariloba, commonly known as the African linear-lobed clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is less common than other clematis species.
