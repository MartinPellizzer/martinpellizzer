Calamagrostis rigescens

Calamagrostis rigescens, commonly known as stiff reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant features tall, stiff, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, feathery flowers that bloom in late summer.
