Cotoneaster × sabrina

Cotoneaster × sabrina, commonly known as the hybrid cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a cross between Cotoneaster horizontalis and Cotoneaster horizontalis var. macrophylla, resulting in a plant with attractive, often showy flowers. The species is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, red or orange berries that persist into winter. It is native to China and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its winter interest and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
