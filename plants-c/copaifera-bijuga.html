Copaifera bijuga

Copaifera bijuga, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces a valuable resin, known as copaiba balsam, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. Its bark and wood are also utilized in various industrial applications.

Copaifera bijuga plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for numerous species.
