Capparis leucophylla var. microphylla

Capparis leucophylla var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf flowering bush, is a compact shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
