Camellia laotica

Camellia laotica, commonly known as the Laos camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, spicy fragrance.

Camellia laotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
