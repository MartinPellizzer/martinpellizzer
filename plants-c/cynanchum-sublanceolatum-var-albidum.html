Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. albidum

Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. albidum, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which are typical of the milkweed family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its latex is milky and can be toxic, making it a plant to handle with care.
