Campanula circassica

Campanula circassica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula circassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
