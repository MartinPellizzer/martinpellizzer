Chamaemelum sevanense

Chamaemelum sevanense, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has aromatic, hairy leaves. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chamaemelum sevanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
