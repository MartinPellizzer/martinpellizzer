Caesalpinia andreana

Caesalpinia andreana, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of legume native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, bright orange-red inflorescences that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Caesalpinia andreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
