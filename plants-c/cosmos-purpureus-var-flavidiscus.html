Cosmos purpureus var. flavidiscus

Cosmos purpureus var. flavidiscus, commonly known as the yellow cosmos, is a striking annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow petals and a prominent, dark central disk. Its blooms appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
