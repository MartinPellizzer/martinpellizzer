Carex brunnea f. simplex

Carex brunnea f. simplex, commonly known as the simple brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually brown or purplish in hue, which are its reproductive structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
