Crotalaria confertiflora

Crotalaria confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typically surrounded by showy, leaf-like bracts. Its fruit is a pod containing several elliptical, flat seeds.

Crotalaria confertiflora is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
