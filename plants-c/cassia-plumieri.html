Cassia plumieri

Cassia plumieri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they have small, feather-like leaflets arranged in pairs.

Cassia plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
