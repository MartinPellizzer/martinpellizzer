Calea crassifolia

Calea crassifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
