Candollea rupestris

Candollea rupestris, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features spiny stems and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Candollea rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
