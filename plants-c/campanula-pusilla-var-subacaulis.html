Campanula pusilla var. subacaulis

Campanula pusilla var. subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, often hidden stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
