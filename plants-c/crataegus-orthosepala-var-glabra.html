Crataegus orthosepala var. glabra

Crataegus orthosepala var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
