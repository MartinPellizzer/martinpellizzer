Caesalpinia paraguariensis

Caesalpinia paraguariensis, commonly known as Guariroba, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological and medicinal significance.
