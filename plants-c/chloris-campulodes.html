Chloris campulodes

Chloris campulodes, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
