Calceolaria germainii

Calceolaria germainii, commonly known as the Germain's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped.

Calceolaria germainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
