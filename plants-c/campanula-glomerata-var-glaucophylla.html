Campanula glomerata var. glaucophylla

Campanula glomerata var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophyll bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a powdery blue-gray waxy coating, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
