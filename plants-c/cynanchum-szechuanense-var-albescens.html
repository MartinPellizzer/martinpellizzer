Cynanchum szechuanense var. albescens

Cynanchum szechuanense var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among members of its family. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
