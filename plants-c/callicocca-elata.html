Callicocca elata

Callicocca elata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Callicocca elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
