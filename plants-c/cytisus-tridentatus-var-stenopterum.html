Cytisus tridentatus var. stenopterum

Cytisus tridentatus var. stenopterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and narrow, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings.
