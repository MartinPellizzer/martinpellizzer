Calothamnus cupularis

Calothamnus cupularis, commonly known as the hooded bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its natural habitat.

Calothamnus cupularis is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
