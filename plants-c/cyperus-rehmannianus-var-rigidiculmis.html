Cyperus rehmannianus var. rigidiculmis

Cyperus rehmannianus var. rigidiculmis, commonly known as the rigid-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are stiff and rigid, contributing to its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
