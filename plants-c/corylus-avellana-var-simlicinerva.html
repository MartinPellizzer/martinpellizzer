Corylus avellana var. simlicinerva

Corylus avellana var. simlicinerva, commonly known as the smooth-shelled hazelnut, is a variety of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous husks that encase the edible nuts, distinguishing it from other hazel varieties. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, typically turning yellow in autumn.

It produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, contributing to its role in wind pollination.
