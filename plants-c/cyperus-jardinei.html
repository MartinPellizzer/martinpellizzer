Cyperus jardinei

Cyperus jardinei, commonly known as the Jardine's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its small, three-lobed flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces three-nutred fruits.

Cyperus jardinei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
