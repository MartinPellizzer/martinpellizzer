Centaurea adamovicii

Centaurea adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovici's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea adamovicii is known for its hardy nature and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.
