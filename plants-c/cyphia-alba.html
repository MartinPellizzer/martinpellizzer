Cyphia alba

Cyphia alba, commonly known as the white cyphia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the harsh coastal environment.

Cyphia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
