Camellia japonica var. conferta

Camellia japonica var. conferta, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia japonica var. conferta is also valued for its winter blooming period, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
