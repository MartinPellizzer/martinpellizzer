Cyperus wissmannii

Cyperus wissmannii, commonly known as the Wissmann's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its morphology features grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences with small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus wissmannii plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
