Cirsium steirolepis

Cirsium steirolepis, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its robust root system allows it to survive in harsh, arid environments.

Cirsium steirolepis is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
