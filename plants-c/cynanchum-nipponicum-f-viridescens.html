Cynanchum nipponicum f. viridescens

Cynanchum nipponicum f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered milkvetch, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and stems contain milky sap, a feature common to many species in the Asclepiadaceae family.

This plant is often found in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
