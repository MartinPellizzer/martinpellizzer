Chenopodium pseudoborbasii var. austromoravicum

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii var. austromoravicum, commonly known as the Austrian moravian goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
