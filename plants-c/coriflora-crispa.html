Coriflora crispa

Coriflora crispa, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with wavy, crinkled margins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Coriflora crispa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
