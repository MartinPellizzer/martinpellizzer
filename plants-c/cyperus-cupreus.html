Cyperus cupreus

Cyperus cupreus, commonly known as the copper rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its slender, reddish-brown stems are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Cyperus cupreus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water in wetland ecosystems.
