Cyathea × elliottii

Cyathea × elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's tree fern, is a hybrid species within the Cyatheaceae family.

It is a large, evergreen tree fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can reach several meters in length.

Cyathea × elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
