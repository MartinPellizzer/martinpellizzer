Cacalia ochroleuca

Cacalia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow flowering bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cacalia ochroleuca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
