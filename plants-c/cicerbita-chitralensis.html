Cicerbita chitralensis

Cicerbita chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Cicerbita chitralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
