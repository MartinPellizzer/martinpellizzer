Cola bracteata

Cola bracteata, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain stimulant-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The tree's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
