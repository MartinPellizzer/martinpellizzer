Centaurea alba subsp. formanekii

Centaurea alba subsp.

formanekii, commonly known as the white knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, and produces small, fluffy, pale blue to white flowers.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration and compact growth habit compared to other Centaurea species.
