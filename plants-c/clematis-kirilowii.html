Clematis kirilowii

Clematis kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved form. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis kirilowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
