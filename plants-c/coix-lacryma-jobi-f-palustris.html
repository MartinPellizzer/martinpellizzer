Coix lacryma-jobi f. palustris

Coix lacryma-jobi f. palustris, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to saturated soils and thrives in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its seeds, enclosed in a fleshy, teardrop-shaped structure, are an important food source for various wildlife.

Coix lacryma-jobi f. palustris plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
