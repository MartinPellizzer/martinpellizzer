Centaurea jacea subsp. grandiflora

Centaurea jacea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking, conical flower heads that range in color from pink to red, with a central yellow disk.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and sturdy, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
