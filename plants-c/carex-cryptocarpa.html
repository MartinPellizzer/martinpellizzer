Carex cryptocarpa

Carex cryptocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces distinctive, three-sided berries.

Carex cryptocarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
