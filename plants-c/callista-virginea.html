Callista virginea

Callista virginea, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
