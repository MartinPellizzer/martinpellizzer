Carpinus kweichowensis

Carpinus kweichowensis, commonly known as the Yunnan hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
