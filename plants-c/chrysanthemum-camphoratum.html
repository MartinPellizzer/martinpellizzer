Chrysanthemum camphoratum

Chrysanthemum camphoratum, commonly known as the camphor chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a camphor-like scent, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both horticulture and herbal practices.
