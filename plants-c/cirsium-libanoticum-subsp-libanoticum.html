Cirsium libanoticum subsp. libanoticum

Cirsium libanoticum subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Lebanon and Syria. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
