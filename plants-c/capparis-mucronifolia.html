Capparis mucronifolia

Capparis mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-pointed caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that contain several seeds.

Capparis mucronifolia is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
