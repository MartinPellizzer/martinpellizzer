Crataegus induta

Crataegus induta, commonly known as the velvet crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy, pink to white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Crataegus induta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
