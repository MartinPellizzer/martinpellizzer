Campanula leskovii

Campanula leskovii, commonly known as the Leskovii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula leskovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
