Calamus hallierianus

Calamus hallierianus, commonly known as the Hallier's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
