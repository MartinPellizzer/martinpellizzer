Cosmos longipetiolatus

Cosmos longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled cosmos, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has slender, elongated petioles that give it its common name, and it typically grows as an annual herb. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are branching and often hairy.

Cosmos longipetiolatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
