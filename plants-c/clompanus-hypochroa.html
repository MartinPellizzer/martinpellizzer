Clompanus hypochroa

Clompanus hypochroa, commonly known as the pale-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Clompanus hypochroa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
