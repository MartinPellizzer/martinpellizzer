Cistus lippii

Cistus lippii, commonly known as the Greek rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Cistus lippii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
