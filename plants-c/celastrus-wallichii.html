Celastrus wallichii

Celastrus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features woody stems that twine around supports and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
