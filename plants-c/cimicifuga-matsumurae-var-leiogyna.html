Cimicifuga matsumurae var. leiogyna

Cimicifuga matsumurae var. leiogyna, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bugbane, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white, lacecap flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland environments and has compound leaves with serrated margins. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The varietal name 'leiogyna' refers to its smooth, hairless stems, distinguishing it from other varieties of Cimicifuga.
