Cyclostemon grandifolius

Cyclostemon grandifolius, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and stout, unbranched trunk.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
