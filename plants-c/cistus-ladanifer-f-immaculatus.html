Cistus ladanifer f. immaculatus

Cistus ladanifer f. immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features silver-green foliage and produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that release a distinctive fragrance when crushed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in garden settings.
