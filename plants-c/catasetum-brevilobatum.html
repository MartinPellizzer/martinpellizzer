Catasetum brevilobatum

Catasetum brevilobatum, commonly known as the short-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique labellum that often has a specialized structure for pollination. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories.

Catasetum brevilobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
