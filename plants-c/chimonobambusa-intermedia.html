Chimonobambusa intermedia

Chimonobambusa intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rapid growth and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of sustainable materials.
