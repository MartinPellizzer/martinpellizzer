Chamaecrista cinerascens

Chamaecrista cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often tinged with gray or ash-like hues, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Chamaecrista cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
