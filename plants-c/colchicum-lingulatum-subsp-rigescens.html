Colchicum lingulatum subsp. rigescens

Colchicum lingulatum subsp.

rigescens, commonly known as the rigid tongue colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink during late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
