Coelogyne ophiopogonoides var. merapiensis

Coelogyne ophiopogonoides var. merapiensis, commonly known as the Merapi orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. The plant produces fragrant, lanceolate flowers in shades of yellow and green, often in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
