Croton hjalmarsonii

Croton hjalmarsonii, commonly known as the Hjalmarson's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a vibrant array of colors, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
