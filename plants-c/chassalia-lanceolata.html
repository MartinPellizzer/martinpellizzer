Chassalia lanceolata

Chassalia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Chassalia lanceolata produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
