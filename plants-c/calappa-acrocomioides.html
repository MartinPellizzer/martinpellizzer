Calappa acrocomioides

Calappa acrocomioides, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Calappa acrocomioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
