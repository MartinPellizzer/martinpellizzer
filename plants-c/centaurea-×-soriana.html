Centaurea × soriana

Centaurea × soriana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea stoebe (sow thistle), resulting in a species with diverse morphological traits. The plant typically features bright blue to purple flower heads surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other regions, often thriving in disturbed soils and gardens.

Centaurea × soriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
