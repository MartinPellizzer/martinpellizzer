Columbaria fissa

Columbaria fissa, commonly known as the split-stemmed columbaria, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a pale yellow or greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Columbaria fissa is considered a rare and endangered species.
