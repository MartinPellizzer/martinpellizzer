Crataegus × aberrans

Crataegus × aberrans, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus sanguinea, exhibiting a mix of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically has thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
