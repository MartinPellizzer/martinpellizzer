Cocos nucifera var. maldivica

Cocos nucifera var. maldivica, commonly known as the Maldivian coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm native to the Maldives and surrounding tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tall stature and produces coconuts with a distinct flavor and texture compared to the common coconut. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained sandy soils, making it well-suited for coastal areas. Its leaves are large, feather-like, and provide shade, while its trunk is smooth and often used for thatching.

The fruit, which contains both water and flesh, is a key resource for the local communities in the region.
