Chusua pulchella

Chusua pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful chusquea, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Chusua pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
