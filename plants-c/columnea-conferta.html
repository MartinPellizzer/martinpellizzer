Columnea conferta

Columnea conferta, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces bright red bracts that resemble the scales of a pineapple, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow and attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.

Columnea conferta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
