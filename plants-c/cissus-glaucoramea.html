Cissus glaucoramea

Cissus glaucoramea, commonly known as the silver leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscapes.
