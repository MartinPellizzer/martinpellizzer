Croton spica

Croton spica, commonly known as the spike croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Croton spica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
