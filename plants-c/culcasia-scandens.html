Culcasia scandens

Culcasia scandens, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend supports. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.

Culcasia scandens is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
