Cyphostemma oxyphyllum

Cyphostemma oxyphyllum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, berry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
