Calea ternifolia var. hypoleuca

Calea ternifolia var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
