Crocus olivieri subsp. balansae

Crocus olivieri subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant blooms in early spring, typically between February and April, and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and habitat preferences compared to the typical form of Crocus olivieri.
