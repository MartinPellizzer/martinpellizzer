Cissus araneosa

Cissus araneosa, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus araneosa is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens and hanging baskets.
