Cissus nitida

Cissus nitida, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, diamond-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus nitida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
