Campanula mairei

Campanula mairei, commonly known as the Maire bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula mairei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
