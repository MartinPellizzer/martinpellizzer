Chenopodium candolleanum subsp. candolleanum

Chenopodium candolleanum subsp.

candolleanum, commonly known as Indian spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in open fields and along riverbanks. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
