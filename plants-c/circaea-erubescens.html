Circaea erubescens

Circaea erubescens, commonly known as red stem cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that attract birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
