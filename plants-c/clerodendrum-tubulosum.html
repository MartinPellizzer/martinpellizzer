Clerodendrum tubulosum

Clerodendrum tubulosum, commonly known as the tubular clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
