Cistus laevipes

Cistus laevipes, commonly known as the smooth rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairless leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Cistus laevipes is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
