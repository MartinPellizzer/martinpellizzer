Calothamnus pachystachyus

Calothamnus pachystachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Calothamnus pachystachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
