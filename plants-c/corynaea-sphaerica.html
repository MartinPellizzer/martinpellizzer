Corynaea sphaerica

Corynaea sphaerica, commonly known as the globe willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique form and ecological importance, it is a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
