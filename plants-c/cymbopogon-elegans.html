Cymbopogon elegans

Cymbopogon elegans, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Cymbopogon elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
