Conyza obscura

Conyza obscura, commonly known as white dandelion, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Conyza obscura is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
