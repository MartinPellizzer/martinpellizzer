Coleosanthus thyrsiflorus

Coleosanthus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a star, with small, white or pale blue flowers clustered tightly together. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional ceremonies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
