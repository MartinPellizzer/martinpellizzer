Cestrum purpureum

Cestrum purpureum, commonly known as purple cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
