Chaubardiella subquadrata, commonly known as the quadrangular rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features small, four-angled stems and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.
