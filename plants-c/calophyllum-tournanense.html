Calophyllum tournanense

Calophyllum tournanense, commonly known as the Tournan's candlewood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Calophyllaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often found in moist, lowland forest areas and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
