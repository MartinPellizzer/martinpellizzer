Chamelum luteum

Chamelum luteum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and wasps.

Chamelum luteum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in certain regions.
