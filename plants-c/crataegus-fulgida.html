Crataegus fulgida

Crataegus fulgida, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces thorned branches. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus fulgida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
