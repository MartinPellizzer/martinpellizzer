Coussarea cornifolia

Coussarea cornifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed coussarea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often reddish or purplish on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Coussarea cornifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
