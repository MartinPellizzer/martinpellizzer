Cryptocoryne thwaitesii

Cryptocoryne thwaitesii, commonly known as the Singapore cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, sword-shaped leaves that are often marked with subtle white or pale veins. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in the understory of dense vegetation in its natural habitat. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its hardiness and ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.

Cryptocoryne thwaitesii is also valued for its role in maintaining water quality and providing shelter for aquatic life.
