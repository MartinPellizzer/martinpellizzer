Carex glaucescens var. androgyna

Carex glaucescens var. androgyna, commonly known as the gray-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
