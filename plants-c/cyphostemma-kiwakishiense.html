Cyphostemma kiwakishiense

Cyphostemma kiwakishiense, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often waxy and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
