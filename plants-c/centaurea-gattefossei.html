Centaurea gattefossei

Centaurea gattefossei, commonly known as the Corsican star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its growth habit is upright, with a compact form that allows it to thrive in sunny conditions.

Centaurea gattefossei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
