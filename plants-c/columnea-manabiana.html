Columnea manabiana

Columnea manabiana, commonly known as the Manabí flame or Manabí bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Columnea manabiana is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
