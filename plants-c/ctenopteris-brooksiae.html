Ctenopteris brooksiae

Ctenopteris brooksiae, commonly known as the Brooks' sea holly, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often waxy leaves that are arranged in a dense, feather-like pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Ctenopteris brooksiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
