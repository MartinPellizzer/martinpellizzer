Celsia maroccana

Celsia maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan celsia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a contrast to the vibrant flowers.
