Crataegus punicea

Crataegus punicea, commonly known as the red thornapple or scarlet hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny branches and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Crataegus punicea is often found in woodlands, along streambanks, and in open fields.
