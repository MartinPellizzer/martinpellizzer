Clematis serratifolia f. wilfordii

Clematis serratifolia f. wilfordii, commonly known as the Wilford's serrate clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features finely serrated, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, with blue-violet petals and a prominent yellow stamen. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or as a climbing plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
