Cynoglossum caeruleum

Cynoglossum caeruleum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Cynoglossum caeruleum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
