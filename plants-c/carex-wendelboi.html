Carex wendelboi

Carex wendelboi, commonly known as the Wendelbo's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex wendelboi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
