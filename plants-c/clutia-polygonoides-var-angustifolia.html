Clutia polygonoides var. angustifolia

Clutia polygonoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
