Calluna vulgaris subf. lilacina

Calluna vulgaris subf.

lilacina, commonly known as the purple heath, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its compact, dense foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while the flowers range from pink to deep purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
