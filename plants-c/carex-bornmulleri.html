Carex bornmulleri

Carex bornmulleri, commonly known as the Bornmüller’s sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly rough to the touch, with a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence, which is characteristic of the sedge family.

Carex bornmulleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
