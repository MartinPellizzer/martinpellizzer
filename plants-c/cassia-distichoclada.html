Cassia distichoclada

Cassia distichoclada, commonly known as the two-ranked cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with two rows of small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in long, drooping inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
