Cryptandra apetala var. apetala

Cryptandra apetala var. apetala, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cryptandra apetala var. apetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and landscapes.
