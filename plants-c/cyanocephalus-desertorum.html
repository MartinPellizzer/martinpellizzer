Cyanocephalus desertorum

Cyanocephalus desertorum, commonly known as the desert bluehead, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Cyanocephalus desertorum is also notable for its ability to produce a toxic alkaloid, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
