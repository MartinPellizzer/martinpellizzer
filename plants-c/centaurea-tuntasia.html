Centaurea tuntasia

Centaurea tuntasia, commonly known as the Tuntasia bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea tuntasia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
