Callicoma billardierei

Callicoma billardierei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The golden wattle is an important species in Australian flora and is recognized for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
