Celastrus cuneifolius

Celastrus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive ridged appearance.

Celastrus cuneifolius is often found in moist, wooded areas and is part of the Celastraceae family.
