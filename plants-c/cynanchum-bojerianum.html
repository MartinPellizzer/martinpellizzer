Cynanchum bojerianum

Cynanchum bojerianum, commonly known as Bojer's milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cynanchum bojerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
