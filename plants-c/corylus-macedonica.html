Corylus macedonica

Corylus macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its serrated leaves and edible hazelnuts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
