Carex macrolepis

Carex macrolepis, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex macrolepis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
