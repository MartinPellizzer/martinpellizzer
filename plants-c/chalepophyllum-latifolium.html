Chalepophyllum latifolium

Chalepophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chalepophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
