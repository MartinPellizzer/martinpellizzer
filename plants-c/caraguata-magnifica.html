Caraguata magnifica

Caraguata magnifica, commonly known as the giant caraguata, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
