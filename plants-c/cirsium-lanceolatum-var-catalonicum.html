Cirsium lanceolatum var. catalonicum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. catalonicum, commonly known as the Catalan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
