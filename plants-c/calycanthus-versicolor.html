Calycanthus versicolor

Calycanthus versicolor, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green appearance.

Calycanthus versicolor thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
