Cirrhopetalum remotifolium

Cirrhopetalum remotifolium, commonly known as the slender mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
