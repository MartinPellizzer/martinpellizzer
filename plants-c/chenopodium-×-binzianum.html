Chenopodium × binzianum

Chenopodium × binzianum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces dry, three-angled fruits that contain one or two seeds. Lambsquarters is commonly found in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

It is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
