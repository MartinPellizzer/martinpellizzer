Carex rivalis

Carex rivalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex rivalis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
