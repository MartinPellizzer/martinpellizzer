Campanula rotundifolia var. uniflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. uniflora, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are round or heart-shaped, with a hairy surface, and produces solitary, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
