Cnicus jucundus

Cnicus jucundus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that features bright yellow flowers with dark centers, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Cnicus jucundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
