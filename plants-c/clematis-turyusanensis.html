Clematis turyusanensis

Clematis turyusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, palmately lobed leaves and has a woody stem that supports its vine-like growth. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

Clematis turyusanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
