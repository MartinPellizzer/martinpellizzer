Chimonobambusa zhizhuzhu

Chimonobambusa zhizhuzhu, commonly known as the Zhizhuzhu bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, with tall, erect culms that can reach up to several meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
