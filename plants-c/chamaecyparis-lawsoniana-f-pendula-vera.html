Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. pendula-vera

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. pendula-vera, commonly known as the weeping Lawson cypress, is a unique cultivar of the Lawson cypress.

It features a distinctive weeping habit, with long, cascading branches that create a soft, feathery appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are bluish-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its graceful form and year-round visual interest.
