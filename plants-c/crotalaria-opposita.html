Crotalaria opposita

Crotalaria opposita, commonly known as the opposite leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive opposite leaf arrangement and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Crotalaria opposita is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
