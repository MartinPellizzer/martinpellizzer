Calcicola parvifolia

Calcicola parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calcicola, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and water-conserving mechanisms.

Calcicola parvifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
