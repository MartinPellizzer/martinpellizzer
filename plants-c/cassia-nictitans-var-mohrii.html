Cassia nictitans var. mohrii

Cassia nictitans var. mohrii, commonly known as the Mohri's cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal form, Mohri's cassia, is distinguished by specific morphological traits that set it apart from other cassia species.
