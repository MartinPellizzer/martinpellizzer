Crinum brevifolium var. bracteatum

Crinum brevifolium var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate crinum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Its unique floral structure and growth habit make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
