Campylanthus salsoloides

Campylanthus salsoloides, commonly known as sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Campylanthus salsoloides is adapted to withstand drought and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.
