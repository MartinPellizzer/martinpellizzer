Callista hasseltii

Callista hasseltii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded areas. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Callista hasseltii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
