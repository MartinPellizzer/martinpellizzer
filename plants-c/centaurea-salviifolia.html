Centaurea salviifolia

Centaurea salviifolia, commonly known as the leafy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea salviifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
