Coleocephalocereus purpureus

Coleocephalocereus purpureus, commonly known as the purple cactus or the red-tipped cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height, often with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
