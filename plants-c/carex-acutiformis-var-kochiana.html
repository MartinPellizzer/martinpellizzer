Carex acutiformis var. kochiana

Carex acutiformis var. kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's sharp-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
