Celastrus micranthus

Celastrus micranthus, commonly known as the small-fruited staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
