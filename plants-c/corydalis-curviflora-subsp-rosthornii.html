Corydalis curviflora subsp. rosthornii

Corydalis curviflora subsp.

rosthornii, commonly known as the Alpine Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
