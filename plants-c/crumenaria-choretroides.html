Crumenaria choretroides

Crumenaria choretroides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Crumenaria choretroides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
