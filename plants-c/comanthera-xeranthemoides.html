Comanthera xeranthemoides

Comanthera xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well-adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Comanthera xeranthemoides is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
