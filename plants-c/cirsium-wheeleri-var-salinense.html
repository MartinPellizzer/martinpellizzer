Cirsium wheeleri var. salinense

Cirsium wheeleri var. salinense, commonly known as the saltbrush thistle, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy or disturbed soils.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
