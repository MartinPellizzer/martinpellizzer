Carex schwackeana

Carex schwackeana, commonly known as Schwacke's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Native to North America, particularly the eastern United States, it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. This plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
