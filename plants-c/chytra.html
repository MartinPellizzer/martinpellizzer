Chytra

Chytra, commonly known as water molds, are aquatic fungi belonging to the phylum Chytridiomycota.

They are characterized by their ability to produce motile spores called zoospores, which aid in their dispersal in water. Chytra typically inhabit freshwater environments and can be found on decaying plant matter or in soil. Their life cycle involves both asexual and sexual reproduction, with the sexual stage producing thick-walled spores.

These organisms play a role in decomposing organic material in aquatic ecosystems.
