Cheiridopsis angustifolia

Cheiridopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, yellow, or white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in coastal gardens where it can withstand salt spray and strong winds.
