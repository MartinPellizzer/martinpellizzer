Cnicus pubigerus

Cnicus pubigerus, commonly known as the rough sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of basal leaves and produces a cluster of small flowers at the top of the stem.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
