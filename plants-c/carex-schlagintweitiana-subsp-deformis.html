Carex schlagintweitiana subsp. deformis

Carex schlagintweitiana subsp.

deformis, commonly known as the twisted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a distinctive, twisted appearance of its leaves.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
