Cranichis lehmanniana

Cranichis lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
