Coleus bojeri

Coleus bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a textured surface and a wide range of colors including red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in warm, humid environments.
