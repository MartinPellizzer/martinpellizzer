Caamembeca grandifolia

Caamembeca grandifolia, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring a dense, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a leathery texture.

Caamembeca grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
