Chelone obliqua var. erwiniae

Chelone obliqua var. erwiniae, commonly known as the nodding honeysuckle, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique floral structure makes it a valuable plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
