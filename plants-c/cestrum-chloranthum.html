Cestrum chloranthum

Cestrum chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly yellow or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Cestrum chloranthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
