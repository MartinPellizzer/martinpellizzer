Chrysophyllum oliviforme var. pallescens

Chrysophyllum oliviforme var. pallescens, commonly known as the African olive or white-leafed olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often pale green or white-veined. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are similar to olives. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
