Cerasus orientalis

Cerasus orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or oriental cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cerasus orientalis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
