Croton subgratissimus

Croton subgratissimus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
