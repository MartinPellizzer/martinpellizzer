Campanula beltranii

Campanula beltranii, commonly known as the Beltran's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Campanula beltranii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
