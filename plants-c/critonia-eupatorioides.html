Critonia eupatorioides

Critonia eupatorioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Critonia eupatorioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
