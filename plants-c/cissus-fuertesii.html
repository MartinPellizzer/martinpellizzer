Cissus fuertesii

Cissus fuertesii, commonly known as the Trinidad nightshade, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
