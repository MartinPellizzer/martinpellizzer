Cassutha chrysocoma

Cassutha chrysocoma, commonly known as golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Cassutha chrysocoma is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
