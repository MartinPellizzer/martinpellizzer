Clematis vaniotii

Clematis vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniotii Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis vaniotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
