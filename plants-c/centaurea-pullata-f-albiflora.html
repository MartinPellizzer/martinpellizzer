Centaurea pullata f. albiflora

Centaurea pullata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflowered centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, giving it its distinctive appearance. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
