Clinostigma gracile

Clinostigma gracile, commonly known as the slender clinostigma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clinostigma gracile is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
