Commelina rosea

Commelina rosea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking red petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often surrounded by three bracts resembling petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Commelina rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
