Cleome massae

Cleome massae, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces showy, spire-like clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers with five petals, resembling spider legs, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Cleome massae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
