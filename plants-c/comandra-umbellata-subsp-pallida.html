Comandra umbellata subsp. pallida

Comandra umbellata subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale comandra, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its subspecies pallida is distinguished by its pale coloration and reduced floral structure compared to other varieties.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems, where it plays a minor role in local biodiversity.
