Capparis sepiaria var. stuhlmannii

Capparis sepiaria var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's caper, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Capparaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
