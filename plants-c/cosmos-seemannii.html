Cosmos seemannii

Cosmos seemannii, commonly known as the Mexican starflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is an annual herb with upright stems and palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cosmos seemannii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
