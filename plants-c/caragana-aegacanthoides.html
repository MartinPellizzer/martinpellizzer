Caragana aegacanthoides

Caragana aegacanthoides, commonly known as the sandbar bean or shrub vetch, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Caragana aegacanthoides is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
