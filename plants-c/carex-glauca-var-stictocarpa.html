Carex glauca var. stictocarpa

Carex glauca var. stictocarpa, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark blue-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
