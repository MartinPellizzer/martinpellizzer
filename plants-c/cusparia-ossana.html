Cusparia ossana

Cusparia ossana, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cusparia ossana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
