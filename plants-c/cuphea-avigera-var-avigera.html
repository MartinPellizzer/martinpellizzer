Cuphea avigera var. avigera

Cuphea avigera var. avigera, commonly known as the flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea avigera var. avigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
