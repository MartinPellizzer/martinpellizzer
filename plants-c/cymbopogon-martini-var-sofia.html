Cymbopogon martini var. sofia

Cymbopogon martini var. sofia, commonly known as Sofia citron grass, is a variant of the citron grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a distinct citrus aroma. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and fragrance industries. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers, and it is typically propagated through stem cuttings.

The variety is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to various soil types.
