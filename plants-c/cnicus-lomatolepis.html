Cnicus lomatolepis

Cnicus lomatolepis, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Cnicus lomatolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
