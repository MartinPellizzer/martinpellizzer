Clavarioidia clavarioides f. cristata

Clavarioidia clavarioides f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate coral fungus, is a unique fungal species characterized by its branching, coral-like structure.

It belongs to the family Clavariaceae and is known for its delicate, gelatinous texture. The plant exhibits a distinctive cristate or wavy pattern on its surface, which is a key identifying feature. It typically grows on decaying wood in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, it is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
