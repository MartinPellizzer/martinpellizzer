Caesalpinia tinctoria var. horrida

Caesalpinia tinctoria var. horrida, commonly known as the horrida tick tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a serrated margin and its distinctive, bright orange to red floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally for its dyeing properties.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
