Carex woodii

Carex woodii, commonly known as Wood's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Carex woodii has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
