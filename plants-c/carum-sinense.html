Carum sinense

Carum sinense, commonly known as Chinese parsley or Chinese caraway, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are aromatic and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Carum sinense thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its ornamental and practical value.
