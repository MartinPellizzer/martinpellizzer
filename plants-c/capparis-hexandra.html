Capparis hexandra

Capparis hexandra, commonly known as the Indian sour plum or wild mango, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible, though they have a sour taste and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
