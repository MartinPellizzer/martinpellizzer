Carpinus caroliniana var. tropicalis

Carpinus caroliniana var. tropicalis, commonly known as the tropical hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive bark and seasonal foliage changes.
