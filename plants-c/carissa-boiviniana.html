Carissa boiviniana

Carissa boiviniana, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Carissa boiviniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
