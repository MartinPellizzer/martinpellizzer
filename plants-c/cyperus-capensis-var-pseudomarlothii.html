Cyperus capensis var. pseudomarlothii

Cyperus capensis var. pseudomarlothii, commonly known as the Cape flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. It is often found in wetlands and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
