Cypripedium × burtonii

Cypripedium × burtonii, commonly known as the Burtt's lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, white to pale pink floral spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
