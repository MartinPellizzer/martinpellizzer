Commelina robynsii

Commelina robynsii, commonly known as Robyn's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed spatulate leaf. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
