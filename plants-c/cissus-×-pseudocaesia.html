Cissus × pseudocaesia

Cissus × pseudocaesia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Vitaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-veined leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus × pseudocaesia is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
