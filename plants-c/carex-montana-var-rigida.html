Carex montana var. rigida

Carex montana var. rigida, commonly known as rigid marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright flowering stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually green to brown in color.

Carex montana var. rigida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
