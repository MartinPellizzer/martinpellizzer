Cystorchis macrophysa

Cystorchis macrophysa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Cystorchis macrophysa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
