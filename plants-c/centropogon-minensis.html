Centropogon minensis

Centropogon minensis, commonly known as the Minas grass.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Centropogon minensis is noted for its small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature.
