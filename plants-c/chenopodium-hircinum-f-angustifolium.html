Chenopodium hircinum f. angustifolium

Chenopodium hircinum f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower foliage compared to the typical form of Chenopodium hircinum.
