Campylotropis tenuiramea

Campylotropis tenuiramea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed campylotropis, is a species of legume native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, rounded seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
