Cinnamomum cupulatum

Cinnamomum cupulatum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or culinary cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is valued for its aromatic bark, which is harvested and dried to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. The bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
