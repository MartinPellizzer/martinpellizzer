Corymbia leichhardtii

Corymbia leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's bloodwood, is a species of flowering tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears reddish or brownish in color. The plant produces white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray appearance.

This tree is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
