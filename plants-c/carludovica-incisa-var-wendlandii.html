Carludovica incisa var. wendlandii

Carludovica incisa var. wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's giant cardon, is a striking cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a tall, columnar stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its spines are typically yellow or brown, providing both protection and a unique aesthetic.

This variety is notable for its size and resilience in arid environments.
