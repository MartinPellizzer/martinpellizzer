Centaurea nigrofimbria

Centaurea nigrofimbria, commonly known as the black-bordered knapweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers with dark purple markings around the edges. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Centaurea nigrofimbria is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
