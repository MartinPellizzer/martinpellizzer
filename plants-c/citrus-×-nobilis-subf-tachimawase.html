Citrus × nobilis subf. tachimawase

Citrus × nobilis subf.

tachimawase, commonly known as Japanese citrus or tachimawase mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth skin that ranges in color from yellow to orange.

The fruit contains a high proportion of juice and a mild, sweet flavor, making it popular for consumption fresh or in culinary applications. Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Citrus reticulata and Citrus sinensis, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. This subsp.

is typically grown in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
