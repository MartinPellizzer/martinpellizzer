Carex bonariensis

Carex bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex bonariensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
