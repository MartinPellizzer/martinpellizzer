Chrysophyllum gardneri

Chrysophyllum gardneri, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are yellow when ripe, with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
