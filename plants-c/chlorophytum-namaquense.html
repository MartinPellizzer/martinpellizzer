Chlorophytum namaquense

Chlorophytum namaquense, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial evergreen plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are succulent, storing water to help it survive in arid environments.

Chlorophytum namaquense is also noted for its air-purifying properties, improving indoor air quality.
