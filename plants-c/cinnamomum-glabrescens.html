Cinnamomum glabrescens

Cinnamomum glabrescens, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, spicy aroma.

The bark, when dried, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
