Croton kelantanicus

Croton kelantanicus, commonly known as the Kelantan croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and may exhibit a range of hues from green to red.

Croton kelantanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
