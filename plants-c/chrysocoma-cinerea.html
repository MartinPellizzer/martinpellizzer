Chrysocoma cinerea

Chrysocoma cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.
