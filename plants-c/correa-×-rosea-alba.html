Correa × rosea-alba

Correa × rosea-alba, commonly known as the white correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and bees.
