Clusia rubescens

Clusia rubescens, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Clusia rubescens is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
