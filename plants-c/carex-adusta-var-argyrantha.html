Carex adusta var. argyrantha

Carex adusta var. argyrantha, commonly known as silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The leaves are typically bluish-green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
