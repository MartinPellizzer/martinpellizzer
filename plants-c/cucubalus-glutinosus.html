Cucubalus glutinosus

Cucubalus glutinosus, commonly known as sticky cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines for its unique flavor and texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
