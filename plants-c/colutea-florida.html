Colutea florida

Colutea florida, commonly known as the sweetroot or sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it features delicate, fern-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is known for its aromatic, sweet-smelling roots, which have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Colutea florida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
