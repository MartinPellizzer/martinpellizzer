Chenopodium acuminatum var. vachellii

Chenopodium acuminatum var. vachellii, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
