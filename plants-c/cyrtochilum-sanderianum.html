Cyrtochilum sanderianum

Cyrtochilum sanderianum, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. Cyrtochilum sanderianum thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
