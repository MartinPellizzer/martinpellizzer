Cassinopsis ciliata

Cassinopsis ciliata, commonly known as the silky cassinopsis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Cassinopsis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
