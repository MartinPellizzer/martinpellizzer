Caucalis aglochis

Caucalis aglochis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.
