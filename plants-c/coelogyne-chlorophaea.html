Coelogyne chlorophaea

Coelogyne chlorophaea, commonly known as the green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne chlorophaea is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for its unique coloration.
