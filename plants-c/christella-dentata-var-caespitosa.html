Christella dentata var. caespitosa

Christella dentata var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered Christella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features upright, slender stems that often form dense clusters, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in late spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have finely toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
