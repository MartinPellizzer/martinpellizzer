Columnea lindleyana

Columnea lindleyana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and dark green color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Columnea lindleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
