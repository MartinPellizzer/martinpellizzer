Campanula malacitana var. gibraltarica

Campanula malacitana var. gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
