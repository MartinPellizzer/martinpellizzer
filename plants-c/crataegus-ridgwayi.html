Crataegus ridgwayi

Crataegus ridgwayi, commonly known as the Ridgway hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus ridgwayi is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along streambanks in its native range.
