Callista reptans

Callista reptans, commonly known as the creeping callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the arid conditions of its natural habitat.

Callista reptans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
