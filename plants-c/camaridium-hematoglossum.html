Camaridium hematoglossum

Camaridium hematoglossum, commonly known as the crimson tongue fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are deep red in color, with a glossy texture that reflects light like polished metal. The plant produces delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to prevent leaf scorch.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
