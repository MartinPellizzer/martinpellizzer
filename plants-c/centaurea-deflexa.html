Centaurea deflexa

Centaurea deflexa, commonly known as the bentleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Centaurea deflexa is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
