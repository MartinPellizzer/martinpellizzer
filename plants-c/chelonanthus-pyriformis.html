Chelonanthus pyriformis

Chelonanthus pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pear-shaped fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red or orange when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and pale grey. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental features and historical use in traditional medicine.
