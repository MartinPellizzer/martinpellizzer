Centaurea schmidtiana

Centaurea schmidtiana, commonly known as Schmidt's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, showy flower head at the end of each stem.

Centaurea schmidtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
