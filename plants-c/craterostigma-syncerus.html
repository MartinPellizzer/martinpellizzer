Craterostigma syncerus

Craterostigma syncerus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
