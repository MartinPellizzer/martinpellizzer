Cotyledon alpestris

Cotyledon alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine Houseplant, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with striking red or purple markings. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Cotyledon alpestris thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
