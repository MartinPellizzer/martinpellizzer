Cornus kousa var. yaeyamensis

Cornus kousa var. yaeyamensis, commonly known as the Yaeyama Cornus, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Kousa dogwood.

It is native to the Yaeyama Islands of Okinawa, Japan, and is characterized by its unique, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
