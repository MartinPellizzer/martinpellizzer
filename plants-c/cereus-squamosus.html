Cereus squamosus

Cereus squamosus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a cactus species characterized by its short, spiny stems and clusters of bright, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly the vibrant colors of its blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is also referred to as the night-blooming cereus.
