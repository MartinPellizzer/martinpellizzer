Cuphea spectabilis

Cuphea spectabilis, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features whorled leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Cuphea spectabilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
