Chenopodium triangulare var. convolvulinum

Chenopodium triangulare var. convolvulinum, commonly known as wild spinach or goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers that grow in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions. It produces small, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines and can be cultivated as a leafy green vegetable.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
