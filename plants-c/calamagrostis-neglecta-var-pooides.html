Calamagrostis neglecta var. pooides

Calamagrostis neglecta var. pooides, commonly known as the slender bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
