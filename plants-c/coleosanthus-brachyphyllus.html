Coleosanthus brachyphyllus

Coleosanthus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, forming a dense mat that helps prevent soil erosion.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
