Cinnamomum subsericeum

Cinnamomum subsericeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by egg-shaped berries containing a single seed. Its bark is aromatic and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for ornamental and commercial purposes.
