Convolvulus polyrrhizos

Convolvulus polyrrhizos, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Convolvulus polyrrhizos is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
