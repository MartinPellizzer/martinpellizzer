Cereus ghiesbreghtii

Cereus ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions and requires minimal water.

Its structure and growth habits make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
