Clerodendrum brunfelsiiflorum

Clerodendrum brunfelsiiflorum, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often woody and branched. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
