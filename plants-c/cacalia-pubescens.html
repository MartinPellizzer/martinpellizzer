Cacalia pubescens

Cacalia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small bells. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it its common name, and it produces vibrant blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Cacalia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
