Crepis primulifolia

Crepis primulifolia, commonly known as the primrose hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis primulifolia is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
