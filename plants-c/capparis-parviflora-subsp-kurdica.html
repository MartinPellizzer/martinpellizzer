Capparis parviflora subsp. kurdica

Capparis parviflora subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish dwarf caper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its small, white flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Capparaceae family.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually small and oval, and it produces small, edible fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
