Celtis polyclada

Celtis polyclada, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible berries, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Celtis polyclada is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and shade tree in urban landscapes.
