Cocculus linnaeanus

Cocculus linnaeanus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often twisted stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and historical uses, it has been the subject of both botanical study and traditional medicine.
