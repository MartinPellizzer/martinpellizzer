Calanthe ligo

Calanthe ligo, commonly known as the silver-veined calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom of hair. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with dark purple veins. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Calanthe ligo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
