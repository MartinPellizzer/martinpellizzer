Cyperus jamaicensis

Cyperus jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus jamaicensis is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
