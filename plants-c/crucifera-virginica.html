Crucifera virginica

Crucifera virginica, commonly known as the Virginia mustard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its four-petaled flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
