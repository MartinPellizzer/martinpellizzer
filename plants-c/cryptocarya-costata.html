Cryptocarya costata

Cryptocarya costata, commonly known as the rough-leaved sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southeastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. The plant is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed.

Cryptocarya costata is often found in dry, open forests and is valued for its aromatic timber and ecological significance.
