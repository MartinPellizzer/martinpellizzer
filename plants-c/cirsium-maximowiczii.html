Cirsium maximowiczii

Cirsium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium maximowiczii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
