Cyphostemma richardsiae

Cyphostemma richardsiae, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and woodlands.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
