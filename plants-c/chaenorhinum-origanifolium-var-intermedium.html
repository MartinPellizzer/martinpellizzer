Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. intermedium

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate oregano bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and somewhat resemble those of oregano, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
