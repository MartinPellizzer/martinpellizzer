Calliandra chilensis

Calliandra chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
