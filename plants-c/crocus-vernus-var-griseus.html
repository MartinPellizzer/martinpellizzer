Crocus vernus var. griseus

Crocus vernus var. griseus, commonly known as the silver crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue, often with a distinctive silvery or purple tinge. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it one of the first flowers to appear after winter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.
