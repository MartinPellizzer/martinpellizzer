Cytisus monspessulanus var. cinerascens

Cytisus monspessulanus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the grey acacia, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and trifoliate, with a distinctive greyish-blue hue on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environments.
