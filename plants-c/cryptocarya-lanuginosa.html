Cryptocarya lanuginosa

Cryptocarya lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
