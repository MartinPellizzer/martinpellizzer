Ceropegia priogonium

Ceropegia priogonium, commonly known as the Cape mountain ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
