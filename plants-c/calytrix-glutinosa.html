Calytrix glutinosa

Calytrix glutinosa, commonly known as sticky calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Calytrix glutinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
