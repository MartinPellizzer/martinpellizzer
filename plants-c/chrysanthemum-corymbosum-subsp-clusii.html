Chrysanthemum corymbosum subsp. clusii

Chrysanthemum corymbosum subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the New England aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a common species in native plant gardens.

The subspecies clusii is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
