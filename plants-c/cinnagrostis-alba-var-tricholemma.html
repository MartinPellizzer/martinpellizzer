Cinnagrostis alba var. tricholemma

Cinnagrostis alba var. tricholemma, commonly known as the white cinnagrostis, is a variant of the cinnagrostis genus within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or dune environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a hairy texture that distinguishes the tricholemma variety.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators.
