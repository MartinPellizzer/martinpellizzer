Camphoromoea ovalifolia

Camphoromoea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf camphor tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Camphoromoea ovalifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
