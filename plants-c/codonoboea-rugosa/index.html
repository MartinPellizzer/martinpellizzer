Codonoboea Rugosa

Codonoboea rugosa is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is known for its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique species for future generations.
