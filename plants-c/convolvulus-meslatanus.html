Convolvulus meslatanus

Convolvulus meslatanus, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing perennial that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a hairy stem, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus meslatanus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively through runners.
