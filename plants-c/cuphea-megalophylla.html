Cuphea megalophylla

Cuphea megalophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cuphea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea megalophylla is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to landscapes.
