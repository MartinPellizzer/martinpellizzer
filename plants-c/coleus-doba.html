Coleus doba

Coleus doba, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that range from deep red to purple and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly velvety texture.

Coleus doba thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
