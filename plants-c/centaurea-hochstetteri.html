Centaurea hochstetteri

Centaurea hochstetteri, commonly known as Hochstetter's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in dense, composite flower heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats like rocky soils and open woodlands.

Centaurea hochstetteri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
