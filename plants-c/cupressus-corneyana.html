Cupressus corneyana

Cupressus corneyana, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family, Cupressaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its elegant appearance.
