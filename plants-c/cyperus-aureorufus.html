Cyperus aureorufus

Cyperus aureorufus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, golden-brown hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it a common ornamental and ecological plant in various landscapes.
