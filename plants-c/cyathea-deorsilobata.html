Cyathea deorsilobata

Cyathea deorsilobata, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cyathea deorsilobata plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
