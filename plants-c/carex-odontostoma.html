Carex odontostoma

Carex odontostoma, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Carex odontostoma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
