Campanula rotundifolia subsp. hellenica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
