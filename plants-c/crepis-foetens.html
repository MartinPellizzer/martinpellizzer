Crepis foetens

Crepis foetens, commonly known as the stinking sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, often with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer.
