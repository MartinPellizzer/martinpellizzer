Celtis hottlei

Celtis hottlei, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. Celtis hottlei is often found in savannas and along riverbanks, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
