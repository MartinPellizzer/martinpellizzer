Cirsium ehrenbergii

Cirsium ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pink flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. Cirsium ehrenbergii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
