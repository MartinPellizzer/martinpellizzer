Carex capillacea var. aomorensis

Carex capillacea var. aomorensis, commonly known as the Aomorensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny brown flowers. Native to specific regions in East Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it exhibits a clumping growth habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
