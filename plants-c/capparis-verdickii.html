Capparis verdickii

Capparis verdickii, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, fruit-like structures that resemble small horns or cucumbers. The plant belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
