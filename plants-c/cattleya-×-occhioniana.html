Cattleya × occhioniana

Cattleya × occhioniana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native high-altitude environment.

Cattleya × occhioniana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
