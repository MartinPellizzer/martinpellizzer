Cyperus pseudopetiolatus

Cyperus pseudopetiolatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cyperus pseudopetiolatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in inflorescences.
