Cynoglossum penicillatum

Cynoglossum penicillatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum penicillatum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
