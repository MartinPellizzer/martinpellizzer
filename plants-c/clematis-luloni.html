Clematis luloni

Clematis luloni, commonly known as the Lulu clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Clematis luloni is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
