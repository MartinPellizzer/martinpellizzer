Crataegus neocanbyi

Crataegus neocanbyi, commonly known as the Neocanby hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered astringent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
