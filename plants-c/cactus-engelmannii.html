Cactus engelmannii

Cactus engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The Engelmann cactus is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
