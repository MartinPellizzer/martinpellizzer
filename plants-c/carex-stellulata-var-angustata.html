Carex stellulata var. angustata

Carex stellulata var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
