Crepis dumicola

Crepis dumicola, commonly known as the rock mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Crepis dumicola is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, scree slopes, and rocky meadows.
