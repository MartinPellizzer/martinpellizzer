Cryptosorus khasianus

Cryptosorus khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi cryptosorus, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understorey of tropical and subtropical forests. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cryptosorus khasianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
