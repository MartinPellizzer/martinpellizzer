Colchicum decaisnei

Colchicum decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne's colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ, and it produces narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually deep purple or violet, with a distinctive spurred shape, and it is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of colchicine.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
