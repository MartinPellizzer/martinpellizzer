Convolvulus arvensis f. decemvulnerus

Convolvulus arvensis f. decemvulnerus, commonly known as the ten-pierced field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

This form is distinguished by its unique leaf and stem characteristics, making it a notable variant within the species.
