Cheirolophus arbutifolius

Cheirolophus arbutifolius, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are typically thick and leathery, with a reddish or purplish hue, and its flowers range in color from white to pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
