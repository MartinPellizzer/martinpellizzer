Callicarpa hispida

Callicarpa hispida, commonly known as rough beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through the winter, making it a notable ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant flowers and is often used in traditional medicine.
