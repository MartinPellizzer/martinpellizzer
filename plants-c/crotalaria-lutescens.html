Crotalaria lutescens

Crotalaria lutescens, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and woodland edges.

Crotalaria lutescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
