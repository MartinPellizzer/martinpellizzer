Campanula rhomboidalis var. uniflora

Campanula rhomboidalis var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
