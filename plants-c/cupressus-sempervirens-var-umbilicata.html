Cupressus sempervirens var. umbilicata

Cupressus sempervirens var. umbilicata, commonly known as the Mediterranean cypress or umbrella cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a dense, rounded shape with short, scale-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by blue-gray cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its unique form and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
