Convolvulus fulcratus var. deltoideus

Convolvulus fulcratus var. deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
