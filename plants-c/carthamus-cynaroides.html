Carthamus cynaroides

Carthamus cynaroides, commonly known as the Persian saffron or safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which resemble those of the sunflower, and its feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking, daisy-like blooms with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its botanical features include a taproot system and fibrous roots, which help it thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is widely cultivated for its seeds and the vibrant red-orange petals used in traditional medicine and dye production.
