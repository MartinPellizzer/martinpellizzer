Carex franklinii var. misandroides

Carex franklinii var. misandroides, commonly known as Franklin's sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and typically grows in moist, acidic soils in wetlands or along stream edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes. It is often found in shaded areas and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
