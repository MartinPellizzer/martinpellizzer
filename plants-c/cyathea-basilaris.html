Cyathea basilaris

Cyathea basilaris, commonly known as the lowland tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense mat, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Cyathea basilaris is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
