Cassinia decipiens

Cassinia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela.

Cassinia decipiens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
