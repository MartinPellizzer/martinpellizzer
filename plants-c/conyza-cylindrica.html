Conyza cylindrica

Conyza cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical weed, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by cylindrical, hairy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Conyza cylindrica is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
