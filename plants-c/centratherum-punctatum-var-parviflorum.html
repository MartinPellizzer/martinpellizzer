Centratherum punctatum var. parviflorum

Centratherum punctatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or wildflower meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native plant collections.
