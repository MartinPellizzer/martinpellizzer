Cinnamomum pseudoloureiroi

Cinnamomum pseudoloureiroi, commonly known as the Guangxi cinnamon, is a member of the Lauraceae family native to southern China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark red berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its unique fragrance and is often cultivated for essential oil production.
