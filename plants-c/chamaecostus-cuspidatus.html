Chamaecostus cuspidatus

Chamaecostus cuspidatus, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
