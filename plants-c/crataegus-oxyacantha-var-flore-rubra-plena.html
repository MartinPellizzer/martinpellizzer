Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-rubra-plena

Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-rubra-plena, commonly known as the red flowering hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.
