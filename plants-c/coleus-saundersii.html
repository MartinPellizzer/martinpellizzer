Coleus saundersii

Coleus saundersii, commonly known as the red-leaf coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves of Coleus saundersii are typically a deep red or maroon, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns of white or green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
