Cousinia squarrosa var. behboudiana

Cousinia squarrosa var. behboudiana, commonly known as the Afghan cottonthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
