Cimicifuga bifida

Cimicifuga bifida, commonly known as twinleaf bugbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cimicifuga bifida is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
