Clinopodium glabellum

Clinopodium glabellum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

The species is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
