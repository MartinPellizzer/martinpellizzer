Catapodium halleri

Catapodium halleri, commonly known as the Haller's catapodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
