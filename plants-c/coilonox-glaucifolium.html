Coilonox glaucifolium

Coilonox glaucifolium, commonly known as the blueleaf coilonox, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are coated in a waxy, bluish-white cuticle, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, depending on the species. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems that often form dense clumps.

Coilonox glaucifolium is adapted to harsh, arid environments, exhibiting features such as reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
