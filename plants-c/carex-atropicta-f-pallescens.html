Carex atropicta f. pallescens

Carex atropicta f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
