Ceropegia schinziata

Ceropegia schinziata, commonly known as the Schinzi's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Ceropegia schinziata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
