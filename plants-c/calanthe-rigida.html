Calanthe rigida

Calanthe rigida, commonly known as the rigid calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive lip.

Calanthe rigida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
