Centaurea aspera proles pseudosphaerocephala

Centaurea aspera proles pseudosphaerocephala, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, yellow to reddish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces achenes as its seeds.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
