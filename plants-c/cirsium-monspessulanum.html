Cirsium monspessulanum

Cirsium monspessulanum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant is known for its defensive spines, which help protect it from herbivores.
