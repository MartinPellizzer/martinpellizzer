Cirsium tymphaeum

Cirsium tymphaeum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are spiny and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

The flower heads are purple to pink in color, consisting of both ray and disk flowers, and are visited by various pollinators.
