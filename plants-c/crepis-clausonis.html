Crepis clausonis

Crepis clausonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Crepis clausonis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy cultivation.
