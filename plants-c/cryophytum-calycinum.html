Cryophytum calycinum

Cryophytum calycinum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. Its scientific name reflects its affinity for cold climates, although it is more commonly found in warmer, dry regions.

Cryophytum calycinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought.
