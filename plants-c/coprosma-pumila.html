Coprosma pumila

Coprosma pumila, commonly known as the dwarf coprosma, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Coprosma pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
