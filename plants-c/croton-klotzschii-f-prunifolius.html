Croton klotzschii f. prunifolius

Croton klotzschii f. prunifolius, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from deep red to orange and yellow, depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique leaf coloration, it is a popular choice for adding visual interest to tropical and subtropical settings.
