Clinopodium pachyphyllum

Clinopodium pachyphyllum, commonly known as broad-leaf mint or thick-leaved wood mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Clinopodium pachyphyllum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
