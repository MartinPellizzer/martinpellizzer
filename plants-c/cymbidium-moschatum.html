Cymbidium moschatum

Cymbidium moschatum, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium moschatum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
