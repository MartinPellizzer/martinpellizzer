Celosia angustifolia

Celosia angustifolia, commonly known as flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a comb or a flame. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and grows as an annual herb. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Celosia angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
