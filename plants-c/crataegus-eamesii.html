Crataegus eamesii

Crataegus eamesii, commonly known as the Eames hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Crataegus eamesii is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
