Crotalaria ononoides var. grandiflora

Crotalaria ononoides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crotalaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent purple veins. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces elongated, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native habitats.
