Chaetocarpus acutifolius

Chaetocarpus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
