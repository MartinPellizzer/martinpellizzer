Coleus niamniamensis

Coleus niamniamensis, commonly known as the Niamniamensis coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually clustered in whorls along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
