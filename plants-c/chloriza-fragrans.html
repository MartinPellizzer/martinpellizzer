Chloriza fragrans

Chloriza fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers emit a sweet aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
