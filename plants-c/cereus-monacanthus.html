Cereus monacanthus

Cereus monacanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is known for producing large, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
