Conophytum subglobosum

Conophytum subglobosum, commonly known as the "Globular Stone Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
