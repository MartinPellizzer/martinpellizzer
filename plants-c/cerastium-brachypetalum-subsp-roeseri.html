Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. roeseri

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

roeseri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its compact form and delicate flowers make it a notable species in high-altitude flora.
