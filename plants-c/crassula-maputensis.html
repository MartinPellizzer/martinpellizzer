Crassula maputensis

Crassula maputensis, commonly known as the Maputuland crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
