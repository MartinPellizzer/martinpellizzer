Cenchrus purpurascens

Cenchrus purpurascens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking purple to reddish tinge on the inflorescence. The plant produces spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle, which is a typical feature of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
