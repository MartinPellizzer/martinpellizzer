Chrysanthemum vulgare subsp. boreale

Chrysanthemum vulgare subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and glandular. Its flowers are small, yellow to greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, blooming from late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
