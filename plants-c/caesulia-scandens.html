Caesulia scandens

Caesulia scandens, commonly known as the climbing legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its twining stems that climb along supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Caesulia scandens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
