Carex frankii

Carex frankii, commonly known as Frank's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
