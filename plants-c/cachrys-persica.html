Cachrys persica

Cachrys persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, single flowers with five petals that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its beauty and fragrance.
