Crataegus monogyna subsp. ruscinonensis

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Serbian hawthorn, is a subspecies native to southeastern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers in spring.

The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral and fruit morphology compared to other hawthorn species.
