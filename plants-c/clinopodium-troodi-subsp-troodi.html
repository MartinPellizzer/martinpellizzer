Clinopodium troodi subsp. troodi

Clinopodium troodi subsp.

troodi, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and many medicinal uses. Clinopodium troodi subsp.

troodi is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
