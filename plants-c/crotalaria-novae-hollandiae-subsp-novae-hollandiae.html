Crotalaria novae-hollandiae subsp. novae-hollandiae

Crotalaria novae-hollandiae subsp.

novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in ecological and agricultural systems.
