Croton pycnocephalus var. thellungianus

Croton pycnocephalus var. thellungianus, commonly known as the Teller's croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
