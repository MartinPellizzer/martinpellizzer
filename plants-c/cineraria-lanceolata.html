Cineraria lanceolata

Cineraria lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Cineraria lanceolata prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
