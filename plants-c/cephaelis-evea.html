Cephaelis evea

Cephaelis evea, commonly known as the golden flame or everlasting.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. It is typically found in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.
