Catha fruticosa

Catha fruticosa, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves that are usually elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, three-lobed fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its leaves contain cathinone and cathine, which are stimulant compounds responsible for its psychoactive effects.
