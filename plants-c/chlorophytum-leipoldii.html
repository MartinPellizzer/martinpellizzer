Chlorophytum leipoldii

Chlorophytum leipoldii, commonly known as the leopard lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular plant among gardeners.
