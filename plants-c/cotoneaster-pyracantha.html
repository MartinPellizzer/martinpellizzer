Cotoneaster pyracantha

Cotoneaster pyracantha, commonly known as the firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

However, its berries can be toxic to humans and some animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
