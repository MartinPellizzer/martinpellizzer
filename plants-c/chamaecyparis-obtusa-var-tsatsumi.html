Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tsatsumi

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tsatsumi, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Tsatsumi cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features a pyramidal shape with dense, scale-like leaves that are a deep green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the cypress family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for garden use.
