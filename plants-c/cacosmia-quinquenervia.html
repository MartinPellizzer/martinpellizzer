Cacosmia quinquenervia

Cacosmia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant exhibits a succulent structure, allowing it to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and bloom during the warmer months.

Cacosmia quinquenervia is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
