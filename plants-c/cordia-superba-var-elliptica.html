Cordia superba var. elliptica

Cordia superba var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to different climates.
