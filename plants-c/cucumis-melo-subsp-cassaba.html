Cucumis melo subsp. cassaba

Cucumis melo subsp.

cassaba, commonly known as the casaba melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its round, yellowish-green rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant is a trailing vine that produces small, yellow flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature yellow color, indicating readiness for consumption.
