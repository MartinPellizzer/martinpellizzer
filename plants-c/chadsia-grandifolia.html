Chadsia grandifolia

Chadsia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered chadsia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
