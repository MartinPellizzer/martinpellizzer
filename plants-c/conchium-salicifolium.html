Conchium salicifolium

Conchium salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved conchium, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Conchium salicifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
