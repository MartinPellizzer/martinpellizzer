Commiphora glaucescens

Commiphora glaucescens, commonly known as the white myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in perfumery and medicine.
