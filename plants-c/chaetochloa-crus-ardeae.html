Chaetochloa crus-ardeae

Chaetochloa crus-ardeae, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
