Cobaea macrostoma

Cobaea macrostoma, commonly known as the giant cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and can grow up to 6 feet tall. Its root system is extensive, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Cobaea macrostoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
