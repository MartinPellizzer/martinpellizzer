Cassia moschata

Cassia moschata, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-rich pods. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Cassia moschata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic bark used in traditional medicine.
