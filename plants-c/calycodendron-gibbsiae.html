Calycodendron gibbsiae

Calycodendron gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' candlewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in terminal umbels, which bloom in spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Calycodendron gibbsiae is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
