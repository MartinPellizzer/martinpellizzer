Crataegus albanthera

Crataegus albanthera, commonly known as the Albanian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Crataegus albanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
