Carex hornschuchiana var. fulva

Carex hornschuchiana var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms clumps and has a distinctive yellowish hue, especially in the inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
