Cineraria × hybrida

Cineraria × hybrida, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is native to South Africa but has been widely cultivated around the world for its decorative value.

Cineraria × hybrida is known for its ability to thrive in coastal climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
