Campanula napuligera var. hirsuta

Campanula napuligera var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a similar hairy coating, contributing to its overall texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
