Crocus vallicola var. suworowianus

Crocus vallicola var. suworowianus, commonly known as the Suworowianus crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, early-flowering habit. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a distinctive purple or violet eye. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
