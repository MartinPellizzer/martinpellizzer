Crataegus watsoniana var. inermis

Crataegus watsoniana var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth thornless hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
