Chadara velutina

Chadara velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Chadara velutina is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as along riverbanks and in agricultural fields.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
