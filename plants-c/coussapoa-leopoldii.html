Coussapoa leopoldii

Coussapoa leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in rocky or well-drained soil. It has adapted to the harsh climate with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
