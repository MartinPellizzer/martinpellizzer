Ceratocarpus utriculosus

Ceratocarpus utriculosus, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, spherical fruits known as utricles, which are characteristic of the genus. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
