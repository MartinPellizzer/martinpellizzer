Columnea brevipila

Columnea brevipila, commonly known as the short-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or indoor settings.

Columnea brevipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
