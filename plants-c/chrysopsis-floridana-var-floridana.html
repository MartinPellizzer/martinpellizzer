Chrysopsis floridana var. floridana

Chrysopsis floridana var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, cylindrical flower heads that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
