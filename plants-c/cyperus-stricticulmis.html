Cyperus stricticulmis

Cyperus stricticulmis, commonly known as the rigid flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes.

Cyperus stricticulmis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
