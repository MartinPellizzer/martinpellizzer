Clerodendrum ternifolium

Clerodendrum ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, three-leafed leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant's name is derived from its distinctive leaf structure and its association with the genus Clerodendrum.
