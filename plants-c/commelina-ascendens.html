Commelina ascendens

Commelina ascendens, commonly known as the climbing dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed bract. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Commelina ascendens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
