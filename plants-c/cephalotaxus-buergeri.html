Cephalotaxus buergeri

Cephalotaxus buergeri, commonly known as Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features a distinctive, twisted trunk and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for various birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
