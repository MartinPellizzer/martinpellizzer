Cephaelis acuminata

Cephaelis acuminata, commonly known as the yellow flame or flame vine, is a flowering plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
