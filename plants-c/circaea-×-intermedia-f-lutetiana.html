Circaea × intermedia f. lutetiana

Circaea × intermedia f. lutetiana, commonly known as the Paris daisy or white wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a distinct, slightly fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
