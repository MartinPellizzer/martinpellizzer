Capparis ovata subsp. myrtifolia

Capparis ovata subsp.

myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
