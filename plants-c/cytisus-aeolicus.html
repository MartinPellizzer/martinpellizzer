Cytisus aeolicus

Cytisus aeolicus, commonly known as Aeolic broom, is a shrub native to the island of Lesbos in Greece.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.

Cytisus aeolicus is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
