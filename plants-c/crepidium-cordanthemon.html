Crepidium cordanthemon

Crepidium cordanthemon, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, and it produces elongated, pendulous flower spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
