Crocus vernus var. phaeton

Crocus vernus var. phaeton, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep gold, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The variety is notable for its vibrant, sunlit appearance, which resembles the color of the sun.
