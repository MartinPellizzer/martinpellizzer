Cistus monspeliensis var. major

Cistus monspeliensis var. major, commonly known as the Greater Rock Rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
