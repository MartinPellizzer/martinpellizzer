Cerasus hirtipes

Cerasus hirtipes, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, though edible, is small and not typically cultivated for consumption.
