Chassalia densiflora

Chassalia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered chassalia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Chassalia densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
