Chloothamnus elatoides

Chloothamnus elatoides, commonly known as silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, and its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Silverleaf goldenrod is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
