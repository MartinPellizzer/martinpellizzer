Canavalia monodon

Canavalia monodon, commonly known as the horse gram or bean plant, is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is well adapted to grow in poor soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used as a food source in various cuisines.
