Colubrina macrocarpa var. lanulosa

Colubrina macrocarpa var. lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
