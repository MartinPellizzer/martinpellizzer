Cerastium grandiflorum subsp. grandiflorum

Cerastium grandiflorum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
