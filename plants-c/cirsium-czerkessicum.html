Cirsium czerkessicum

Cirsium czerkessicum, commonly known as the Caucasian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or violet flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and a distinctive basal rosette. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Cirsium czerkessicum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
