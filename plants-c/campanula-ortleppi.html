Campanula ortleppi

Campanula ortleppi, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Campanula ortleppi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
