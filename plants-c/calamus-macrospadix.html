Calamus macrospadix

Calamus macrospadix, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The botanical features of Calamus macrospadix make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
