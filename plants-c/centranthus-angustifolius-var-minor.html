Centranthus angustifolius var. minor

Centranthus angustifolius var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf redroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
