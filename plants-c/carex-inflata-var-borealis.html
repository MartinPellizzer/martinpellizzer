Carex inflata var. borealis

Carex inflata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern inflated sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to wetland habitats. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
