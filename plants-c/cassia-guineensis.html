Cassia guineensis

Cassia guineensis, commonly known as African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia guineensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
