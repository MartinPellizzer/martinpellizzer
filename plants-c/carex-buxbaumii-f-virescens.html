Carex buxbaumii f. virescens

Carex buxbaumii f. virescens, commonly known as the green-headed sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
