Celosia mucronata

Celosia mucronata, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant is herbaceous, with branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Celosia mucronata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
