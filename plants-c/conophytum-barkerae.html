Conophytum barkerae

Conophytum barkerae, commonly known as the "Barker's conophytum," is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, flattened leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
