Craspedia major

Craspedia major, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow florets surrounded by a ring of bright yellow ray florets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Craspedia major thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and coastal areas.
