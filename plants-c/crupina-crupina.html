Crupina crupina

Crupina crupina, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing tolerance to saline conditions. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems branching from the base.

Crupina crupina is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought.
