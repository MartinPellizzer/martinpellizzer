Cocos odorata

Cocos odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
