Colchicum greuterocymbium

Colchicum greuterocymbium, commonly known as the Greuter's corymbose colchicum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Colchicaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, particularly in countries like Nepal and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant produces a single, tall flowering stem.

Colchicum greuterocymbium is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
