Carex breviculmis f. longearistata

Carex breviculmis f. longearistata, commonly known as the long-awned sedge, is a variant of the narrow-leaf sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are adorned with long, bristle-like awns. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
