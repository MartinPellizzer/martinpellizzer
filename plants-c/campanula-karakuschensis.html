Campanula karakuschensis

Campanula karakuschensis, commonly known as the Karakush Mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Campanula karakuschensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
