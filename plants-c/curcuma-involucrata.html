Curcuma involucrata

Curcuma involucrata, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The flowers are typically borne in inflorescences that resemble a spike, adding ornamental value to the plant.
