Carex scoparia var. tessellata

Carex scoparia var. tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
