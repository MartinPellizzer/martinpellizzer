Combretum grotei

Combretum grotei, commonly known as the Natal combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The bark and leaves of Combretum grotei have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
