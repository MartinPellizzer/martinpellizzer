Cyathea ridleyi

Cyathea ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, unbranched stem, while the fronds are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Cyathea ridleyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
