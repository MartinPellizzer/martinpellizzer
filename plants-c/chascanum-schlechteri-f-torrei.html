Chascanum schlechteri f. torrei

Chascanum schlechteri f. torrei, commonly known as the Torre's chascanum, is a hybrid plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but have a mild, tangy flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
