Cissus tweedieana

Cissus tweedieana, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions as a houseplant or garden vine.
