Columnea floribunda

Columnea floribunda, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Columnea floribunda thrives in warm, moist conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
