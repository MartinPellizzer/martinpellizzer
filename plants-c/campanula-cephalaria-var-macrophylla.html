Campanula cephalaria var. macrophylla

Campanula cephalaria var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
