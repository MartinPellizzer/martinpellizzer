Cotoneaster cochleatus

Cotoneaster cochleatus, commonly known as the spiral cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Cotoneaster cochleatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular plant in horticultural settings.
