Crataegus darvasica

Crataegus darvasica, commonly known as the Darvas thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
