Cyperus denudatus var. sphaerospermus

Cyperus denudatus var. sphaerospermus, commonly known as the globular flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its unique feature is the spherical or globular appearance of its fruiting structures, which give it its specific epithet.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
