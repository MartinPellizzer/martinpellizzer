Calcearia taliensis

Calcearia taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets, typical of the sunflower family. Calcearia taliensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
