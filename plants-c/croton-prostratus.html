Croton prostratus

Croton prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate croton, is a low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
