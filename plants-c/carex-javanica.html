Carex javanica

Carex javanica, commonly known as the Java sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex javanica is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
