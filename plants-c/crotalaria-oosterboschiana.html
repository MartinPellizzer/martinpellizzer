Crotalaria oosterboschiana

Crotalaria oosterboschiana, commonly known as the Oosterbosch cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, showing adaptability to well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
