Convolvulus chondrilloides

Convolvulus chondrilloides, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features tendrils that allow it to climb or trail along the ground, and its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals. The leaves are alternate and often have a lobed or heart-shaped appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
