Citrullus lanatus var. caffer

Citrullus lanatus var. caffer, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a cultivar of the watermelon species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, green rind and juicy, red or yellow flesh. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces fruit that can weigh several kilograms.

The variety is valued for its high water content and nutritional benefits, including vitamins A and C.
