Carex distans var. hostiana

Carex distans var. hostiana, commonly known as the Hostian sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
