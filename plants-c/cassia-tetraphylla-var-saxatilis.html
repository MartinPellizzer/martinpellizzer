Cassia tetraphylla var. saxatilis

Cassia tetraphylla var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cassia, is a shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
