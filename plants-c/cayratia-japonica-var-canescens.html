Cayratia japonica var. canescens

Cayratia japonica var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf cayratia, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive climbing plant.
