Canavalia albiflora

Canavalia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown as a forage crop or for its ornamental value.

Canavalia albiflora also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
