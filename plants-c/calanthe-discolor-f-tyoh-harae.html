Calanthe discolor f. tyoh-harae

Calanthe discolor f. tyoh-harae, commonly known as the Tyoh-harae calanthe, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers that display a unique combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, spurred orchid flowers that are highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and delicate nature, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in its native habitat.
