Celastrus feddei

Celastrus feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
