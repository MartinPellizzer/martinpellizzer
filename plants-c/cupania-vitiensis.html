Cupania vitiensis

Cupania vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Cupania, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
