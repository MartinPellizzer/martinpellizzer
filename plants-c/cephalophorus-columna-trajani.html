Cephalophorus columna-trajani

Cephalophorus columna-trajani, commonly known as the Trajan's column plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tall inflorescence that resembles a column, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
