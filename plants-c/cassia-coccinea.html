Cassia coccinea

Cassia coccinea, commonly known as the bloodtwig or scarlet cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, with 8 to 16 pairs of elliptical leaflets.

Cassia coccinea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
