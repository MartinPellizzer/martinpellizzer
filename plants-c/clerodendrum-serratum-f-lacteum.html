Clerodendrum serratum f. lacteum

Clerodendrum serratum f. lacteum, commonly known as the milky clerodendrum, is a variant of the species characterized by its distinctive milky sap.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically clustered in panicles. The milky sap, which is a prominent feature, is exuded when the stems or leaves are broken.

This botanical trait contributes to its classification within the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.
