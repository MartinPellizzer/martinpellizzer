Carex chlorostachys

Carex chlorostachys, commonly known as the blue-flowered sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Carex chlorostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
