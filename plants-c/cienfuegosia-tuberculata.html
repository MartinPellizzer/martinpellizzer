Cienfuegosia tuberculata

Cienfuegosia tuberculata, commonly known as the Cuban flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
