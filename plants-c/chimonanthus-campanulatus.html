Chimonanthus campanulatus

Chimonanthus campanulatus, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, preceding the leaves. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
