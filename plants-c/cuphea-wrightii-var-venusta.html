Cuphea wrightii var. venusta

Cuphea wrightii var. venusta, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its showy blooms and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
