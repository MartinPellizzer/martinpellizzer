Carex communis var. gynandra

Carex communis var. gynandra, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along streambanks, and in shaded woodland environments.

Carex communis var. gynandra is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
