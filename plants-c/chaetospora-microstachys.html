Chaetospora microstachys

Chaetospora microstachys, commonly known as the slender spore moss, is a member of the family Dicranaceae and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant has small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps in retaining moisture. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems.

This moss plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its ecosystem.
