Centaurea calcitrapa

Centaurea calcitrapa, commonly known as the limestone cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its slender stems and small, spiny leaves are well-suited for survival in harsh conditions.

Centaurea calcitrapa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
