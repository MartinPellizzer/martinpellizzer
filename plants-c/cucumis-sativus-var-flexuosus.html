Cucumis sativus var. flexuosus

Cucumis sativus var. flexuosus, commonly known as the warty cucumber, is a variant of the cucumber plant characterized by its distinctive warty skin.

It is an annual vine that produces elongated, green fruits with a rough, bumpy surface. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in specialty gardening and culinary applications.
