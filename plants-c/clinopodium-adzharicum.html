Clinopodium adzharicum

Clinopodium adzharicum, commonly known as Caucasian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Clinopodium adzharicum produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
