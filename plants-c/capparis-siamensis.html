Capparis siamensis

Capparis siamensis, commonly known as the Siam crabapple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by orange or red fruits resembling small apples. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.
