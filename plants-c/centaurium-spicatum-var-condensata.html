Centaurium spicatum var. condensata

Centaurium spicatum var. condensata, commonly known as clustered mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
