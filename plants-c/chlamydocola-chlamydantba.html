Chlamydocola chlamydantba

Chlamydocola chlamydantba, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
