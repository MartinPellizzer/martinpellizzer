Chenopodium × tkalcsicsii

Chenopodium × tkalcsicsii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a naturally occurring hybrid species resulting from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berteroianum.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and typically has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are small and wind-dispersed. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its hybrid origin and adaptability.
