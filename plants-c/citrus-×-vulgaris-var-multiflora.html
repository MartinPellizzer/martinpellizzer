Citrus × vulgaris var. multiflora

Citrus × vulgaris var. multiflora, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus species characterized by its fragrant, sweet fruit.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and thornless branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that give way to round, orange-colored fruits. The fruit has a thin, leathery rind and juicy, segmented pulp with a high vitamin C content.

It is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
