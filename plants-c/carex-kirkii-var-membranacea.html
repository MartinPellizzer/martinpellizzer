Carex kirkii var. membranacea

Carex kirkii var. membranacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
