Carduus crispus subvar. euvalentinus

Carduus crispus subvar. euvalentinus, commonly known as the European curly thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
