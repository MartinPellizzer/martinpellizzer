Chaetoseris hispida

Chaetoseris hispida, commonly known as the hispid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species in its native environment.
