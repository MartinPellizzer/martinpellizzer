Crataegus cestrica

Crataegus cestrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus cestrica typically grows in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
