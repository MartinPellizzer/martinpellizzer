Chrysothamnus zionis

Chrysothamnus zionis, commonly known as Zion's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in Utah and Arizona.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has silver-green leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Zion's goldenbush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
