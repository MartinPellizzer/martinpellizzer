Cracca cephalantha

Cracca cephalantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
