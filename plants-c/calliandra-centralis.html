Calliandra centralis

Calliandra centralis, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm, arid climates. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calliandra centralis is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to provide shade in tropical environments.
