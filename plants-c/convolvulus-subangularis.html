Convolvulus subangularis

Convolvulus subangularis, commonly known as the angular bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along the edges of fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It has a sprawling growth habit and can form dense mats, often becoming invasive in certain regions.

The genus Convolvulus is characterized by its winding stems and showy flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects.
