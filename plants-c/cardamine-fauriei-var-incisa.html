Cardamine fauriei var. incisa

Cardamine fauriei var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
