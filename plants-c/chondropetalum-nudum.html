Chondropetalum nudum

Chondropetalum nudum, commonly known as the naked chondropetalum, is a rare and unique fern species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
