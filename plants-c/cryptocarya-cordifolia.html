Cryptocarya cordifolia

Cryptocarya cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cryptocarya, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Australia and New Guinea.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it often grows as an understory plant in humid, shaded environments.

Cryptocarya cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
