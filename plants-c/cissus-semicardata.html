Cissus semicardata

Cissus semicardata, commonly known as the heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus semicardata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
