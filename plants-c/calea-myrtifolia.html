Calea myrtifolia

Calea myrtifolia, commonly known as the Mexican heather or sacred plant of the Maya, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale blue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, clustered inflorescences.

Calea myrtifolia is also noted for its traditional use in herbal medicine by indigenous communities.
