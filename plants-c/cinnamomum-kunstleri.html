Cinnamomum kunstleri

Cinnamomum kunstleri, commonly known as the Malayan cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
