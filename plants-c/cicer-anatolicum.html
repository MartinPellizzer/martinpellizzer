Cicer anatolicum

Cicer anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian chickpea, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Cicer anatolicum is an important crop for both human consumption and animal feed.
