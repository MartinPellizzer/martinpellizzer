Citrus × tachibana

Citrus × tachibana, commonly known as the tachibana orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species native to Japan, characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruits. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often blooming in spring, followed by the development of orange-colored fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
