Chamaepeuce atropurpurea

Chamaepeuce atropurpurea, commonly known as purple fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and reaches heights of up to 2 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a purple-tinged edge and produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent white fringes. It blooms from July to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical name reflects its dark purple coloration and the fringed appearance of its petals.
