Cissus antarctica var. pubescens

Cissus antarctica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Antarctic Cissus, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative climbing plant.

Its unique foliage and adaptability to various climates make it an interesting addition to botanical collections.
