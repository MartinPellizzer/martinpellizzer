Croton chaetodus

Croton chaetodus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins and hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red fruits. Croton chaetodus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
