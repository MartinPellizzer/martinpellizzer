Caesarea ovata

Caesarea ovata, commonly known as the golden dewdrop or the evergreen hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, providing an attractive appearance throughout the year.

Caesarea ovata is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
