Clematoclethra actinidioides var. integrifolia

Clematoclethra actinidioides var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese arrow bamboo, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in late spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its elegant appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
