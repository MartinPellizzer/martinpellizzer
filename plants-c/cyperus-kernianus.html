Cyperus kernianus

Cyperus kernianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus kernianus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
