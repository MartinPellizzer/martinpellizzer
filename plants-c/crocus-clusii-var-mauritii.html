Crocus clusii var. mauritii

Crocus clusii var. mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape. This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
