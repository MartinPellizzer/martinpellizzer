Chrysophyllum bakhuizenii

Chrysophyllum bakhuizenii, commonly known as the African cherry or bakhuizen's cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
