Corylus pontica var. glandulifera

Corylus pontica var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, typical of the hazelnut genus. Its edible nuts are enclosed in a spiny husk and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
