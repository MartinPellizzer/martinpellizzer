Carya ovata subsp. australis

Carya ovata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia. It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet-tasting nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
