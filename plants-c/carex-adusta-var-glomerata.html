Carex adusta var. glomerata

Carex adusta var. glomerata, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial sedge characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit.

It features dark purple to reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that are typically a striking purple or reddish color, adding visual interest to wetland or bog environments. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in heathlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
