Clitoria quadalupensis

Clitoria quadalupensis, commonly known as the purple orchid bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling the human clitoris, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
