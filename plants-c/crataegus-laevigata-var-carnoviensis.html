Crataegus laevigata var. carnoviensis

Crataegus laevigata var. carnoviensis, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
