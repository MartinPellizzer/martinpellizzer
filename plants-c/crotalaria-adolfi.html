Crotalaria adolfi

Crotalaria adolfi, commonly known as the African crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers in raceme clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Crotalaria adolfi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
