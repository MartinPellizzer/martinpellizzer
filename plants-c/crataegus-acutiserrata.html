Crataegus acutiserrata

Crataegus acutiserrata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
