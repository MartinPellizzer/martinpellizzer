Cyperus nigritanus

Cyperus nigritanus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus nigritanus often grows in wet or moist soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and aquatic environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies mainly in its ecological role and adaptability.
