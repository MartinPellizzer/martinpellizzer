Crepidium bancanoides

Crepidium bancanoides, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its rhizome system allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
