Cussonia fosbergiana

Cussonia fosbergiana, commonly known as the Fosberg's candle bush, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are large, elliptical, and have a waxy texture.

Cussonia fosbergiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
