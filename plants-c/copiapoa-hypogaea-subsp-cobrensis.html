Copiapoa hypogaea subsp. cobrensis

Copiapoa hypogaea subsp.

cobrensis, commonly known as the Cobrescens cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its ribbed stem and dense, white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
