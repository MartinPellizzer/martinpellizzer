Cyperus tenuiculmis var. guineensis

Cyperus tenuiculmis var. guineensis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
