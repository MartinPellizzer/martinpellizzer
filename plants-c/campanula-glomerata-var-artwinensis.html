Campanula glomerata var. artwinensis

Campanula glomerata var. artwinensis, commonly known as the Artwin clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
