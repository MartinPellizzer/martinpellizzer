Cyclamen repandum subsp. rhodense

Cyclamen repandum subsp.

rhodense, commonly known as the pink cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, tuberous roots and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish tinge.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a distinctive upward-facing bloom. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. Cyclamen repandum subsp.

rhodense is well-suited for rock gardens and shaded borders due to its compact growth habit.
