Campanula petrophila var. exappendiculata

Campanula petrophila var. exappendiculata, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, providing protection against strong winds and intense sunlight.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
