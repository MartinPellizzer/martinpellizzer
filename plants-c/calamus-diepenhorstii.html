Calamus diepenhorstii

Calamus diepenhorstii, commonly known as Diepenhorst's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
