Clitoria mariana

Clitoria mariana, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a climbing legume native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to red pea-like flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Clitoria mariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
