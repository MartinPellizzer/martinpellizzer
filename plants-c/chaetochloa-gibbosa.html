Chaetochloa gibbosa

Chaetochloa gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae (grass) family. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in areas with seasonal flooding.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
