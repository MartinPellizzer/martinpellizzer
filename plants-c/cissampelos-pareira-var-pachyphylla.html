Cissampelos pareira var. pachyphylla

Cissampelos pareira var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
