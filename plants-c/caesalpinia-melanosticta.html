Caesalpinia melanosticta

Caesalpinia melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained crown flower, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Caesalpinia melanosticta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
