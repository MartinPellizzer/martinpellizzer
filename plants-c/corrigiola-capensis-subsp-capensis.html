Corrigiola capensis subsp. capensis

Corrigiola capensis subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape sandplume, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to South Africa. It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
