Cyperus parciflorus

Cyperus parciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flower flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

Cyperus parciflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
