Corythea multiflora

Corythea multiflora, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Corythea multiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
