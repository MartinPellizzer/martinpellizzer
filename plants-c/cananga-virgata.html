Cananga virgata

Cananga virgata, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or ylang-ylang, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that emit a strong, sweet aroma. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped or elliptic. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Cananga virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in perfumery due to its essential oils.
