Cineraria farfara

Cineraria farfara, commonly known as common cineraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cineraria farfara is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
