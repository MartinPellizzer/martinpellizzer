Clematis integrifolia var. grandiflora

Clematis integrifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb supports with its strong, twisting stems.

Clematis integrifolia var. grandiflora is relatively hardy and can tolerate mild frost, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
