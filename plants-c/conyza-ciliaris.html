Conyza ciliaris

Conyza ciliaris, commonly known as sandmat, is an annual flowering plant native to Africa, Australia, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant often forms dense stands in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
