Chrozophora subplicata

Chrozophora subplicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
