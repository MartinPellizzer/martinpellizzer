Cryptocarya depressa

Cryptocarya depressa, commonly known as the low cryptocarya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and bay leaves. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow-green in color, followed by oval-shaped berries.

Cryptocarya depressa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
