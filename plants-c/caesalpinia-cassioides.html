Caesalpinia cassioides

Caesalpinia cassioides, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, flame-colored flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
