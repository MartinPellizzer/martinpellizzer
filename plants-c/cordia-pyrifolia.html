Cordia pyrifolia

Cordia pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or pyriphyllo, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cordia pyrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
