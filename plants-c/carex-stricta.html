Carex stricta

Carex stricta, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense, upright clumps, forming tussocks that help stabilize soil. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saturated or seasonally flooded soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex stricta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
