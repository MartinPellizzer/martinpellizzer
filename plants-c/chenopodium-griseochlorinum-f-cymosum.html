Chenopodium griseochlorinum f. cymosum

Chenopodium griseochlorinum f. cymosum, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a metallic sheen. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in cymes, which are clusters of flowers with a single terminal flower. The species is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
