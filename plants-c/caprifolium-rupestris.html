Caprifolium rupestris

Caprifolium rupestris, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to rocky and coastal environments.

It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh, windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal cliffs and seaside gardens.

Caprifolium rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging landscapes.
