Cyclopogon pubescens

Cyclopogon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cyclopogon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that are typically clustered in a spike at the center of the leaf rosette. Its hairy texture, from which it gets its common name, is a distinctive feature that helps in identifying the species.

Cyclopogon pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
