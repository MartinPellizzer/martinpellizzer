Carex hydrophila

Carex hydrophila, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex hydrophila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
