Carpesium gigas

Carpesium gigas, commonly known as the giant cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a prickly husk. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
