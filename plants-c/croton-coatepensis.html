Croton coatepensis

Croton coatepensis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Croton coatepensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
