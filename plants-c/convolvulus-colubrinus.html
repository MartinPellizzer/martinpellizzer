Convolvulus colubrinus

Convolvulus colubrinus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to autumn.

Convolvulus colubrinus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
