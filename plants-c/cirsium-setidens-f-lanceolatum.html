Cirsium setidens f. lanceolatum

Cirsium setidens f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lance-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, upright stems and rough, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

Its foliage and flowers are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
