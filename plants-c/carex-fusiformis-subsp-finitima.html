Carex fusiformis subsp. finitima

Carex fusiformis subsp.

finitima, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically found in clusters at the top of the plant. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
