Campanula lamondiae

Campanula lamondiae, commonly known as Lamond's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high alpine regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

Campanula lamondiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
