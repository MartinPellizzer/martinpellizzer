Crataegus callista

Crataegus callista, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus callista is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, where it adapts to dry, sunny conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
