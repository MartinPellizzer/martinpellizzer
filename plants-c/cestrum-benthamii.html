Cestrum benthamii

Cestrum benthamii, commonly known as the night-flowering trumpet bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or red. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Cestrum benthamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
