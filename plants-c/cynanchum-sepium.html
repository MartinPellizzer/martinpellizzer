Cynanchum sepium

Cynanchum sepium, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, and its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Cynanchum sepium has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
