Commiphora corrugata

Commiphora corrugata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces a resinous gum, often referred to as frankincense, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and religious practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The plant's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
