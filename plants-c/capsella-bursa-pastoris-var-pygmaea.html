Capsella bursa-pastoris var. pygmaea

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf shepherd's purse, is a small annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive triangular seed pods, which resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it a low-growing species. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and fields.

Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural settings.
