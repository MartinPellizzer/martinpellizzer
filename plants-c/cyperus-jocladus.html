Cyperus jocladus

Cyperus jocladus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus jocladus thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
