Cineraria atropurpurea

Cineraria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
