Carex longiseta

Carex longiseta, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spadix-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Carex longiseta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
