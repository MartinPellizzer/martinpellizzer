Cherleria verna

Cherleria verna, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Cherleria verna thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
