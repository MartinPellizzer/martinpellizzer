Cladium triglomeratum

Cladium triglomeratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Cladium triglomeratum plays an important ecological role in marsh ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to thrive in saturated soils makes it a key component of wetland vegetation.
