Carpesium cernuum var. queenslandica

Carpesium cernuum var. queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland sow thistle, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp areas such as riverbanks and wetlands.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Australian herbal remedies.
