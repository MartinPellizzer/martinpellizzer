Conophytum membranaceum

Conophytum membranaceum, commonly known as the "Swartland moonstone," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
