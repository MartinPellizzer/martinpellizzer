Cirsium babanum

Cirsium babanum, commonly known as Baban thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium babanum is adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
