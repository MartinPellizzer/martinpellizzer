Cynanchum indicum

Cynanchum indicum, commonly known as Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive crown of lobes.

It is often found in wastelands, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
