Cassia unijuga

Cassia unijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. These flowers are followed by long, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Cassia unijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
