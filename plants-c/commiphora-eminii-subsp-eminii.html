Commiphora eminii subsp. eminii

Commiphora eminii subsp.

eminii, commonly known as the Ethiopian myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used for medicinal and incense purposes.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is valued for its unique chemical composition, including compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
