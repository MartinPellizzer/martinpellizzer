Clematis chinensis var. laeviachenium

Clematis chinensis var. laeviachenium, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
