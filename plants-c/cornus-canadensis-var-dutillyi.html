Cornus canadensis var. dutillyi

Cornus canadensis var. dutillyi, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage consists of elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn.
