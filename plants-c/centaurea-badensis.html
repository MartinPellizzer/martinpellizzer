Centaurea badensis

Centaurea badensis, commonly known as the Baden centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces striking blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, Centaurea badensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
