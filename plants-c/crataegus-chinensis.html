Crataegus chinensis

Crataegus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
