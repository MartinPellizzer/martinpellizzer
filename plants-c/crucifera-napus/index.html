Crucifera Napus

Crucifera napus is a member of the Brassicaceae family known for its agricultural importance. This plant is commonly cultivated for its oil-rich seeds used in food and industrial products. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant's flowers are typically yellow with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Its versatility has made it a key crop in global agriculture for centuries.
