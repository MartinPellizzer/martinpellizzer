Cochlearia linnaei

Cochlearia linnaei, commonly known as Linnaeus' cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and North America. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single umbel.

Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
