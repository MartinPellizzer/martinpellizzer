Carex × chevalieri

Carex × chevalieri, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex echinata, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and blade-like, with a prominent midrib.

Carex × chevalieri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
