Collaea bullata

Collaea bullata, commonly known as the silver scurfpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Collaea bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
