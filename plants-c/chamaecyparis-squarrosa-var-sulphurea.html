Chamaecyparis squarrosa var. sulphurea

Chamaecyparis squarrosa var. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its bluish-green stems. This evergreen conifer is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant has a compact, pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that give it a textured appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
