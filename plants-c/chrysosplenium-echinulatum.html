Chrysosplenium echinulatum

Chrysosplenium echinulatum, commonly known as the hairy chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
