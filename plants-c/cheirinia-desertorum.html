Cheirinia desertorum

Cheirinia desertorum, commonly known as the desert lousewort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils.

Cheirinia desertorum plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
