Cryptocarya oblongifolia

Cryptocarya oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly in the regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical or oblong-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, and it is often found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

The fruit is a small, smooth, spherical drupe that matures to a dark purple color.
