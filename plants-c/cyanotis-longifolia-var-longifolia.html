Cyanotis longifolia var. longifolia

Cyanotis longifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
