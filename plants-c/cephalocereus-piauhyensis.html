Cephalocereus piauhyensis

Cephalocereus piauhyensis, commonly known as the Piauhy cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically fragrant. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
