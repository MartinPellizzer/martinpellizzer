Cerastium bosniacum

Cerastium bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are oval and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Cerastium bosniacum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
