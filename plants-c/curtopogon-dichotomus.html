Curtopogon dichotomus

Curtopogon dichotomus, commonly known as the forked grass or zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with flowers arranged in a distinctive zigzag pattern. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Curtopogon dichotomus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
