Candollea squamellosa

Candollea squamellosa, commonly known as the spiny broomstick cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
