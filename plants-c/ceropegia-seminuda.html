Ceropegia seminuda

Ceropegia seminuda, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, fleshy stems that trail or climb, often adorned with small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its delicate appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
