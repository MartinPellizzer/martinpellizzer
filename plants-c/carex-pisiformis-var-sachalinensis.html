Carex pisiformis var. sachalinensis

Carex pisiformis var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
