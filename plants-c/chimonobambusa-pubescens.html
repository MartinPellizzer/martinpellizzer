Chimonobambusa pubescens

Chimonobambusa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Chimonobambusa pubescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
