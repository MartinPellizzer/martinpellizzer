Centaurea nigrescens subsp. smolinensis

Centaurea nigrescens subsp.

smolinensis, commonly known as the Smolin's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, conical clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
