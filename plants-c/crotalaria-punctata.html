Crotalaria punctata

Crotalaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers with distinctive black or purple spots. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria punctata is also known for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
