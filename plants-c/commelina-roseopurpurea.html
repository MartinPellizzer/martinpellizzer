Commelina roseopurpurea

Commelina roseopurpurea, commonly known as the purple rose commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have three blue or purple petals with a white or pink throat.

Commelina roseopurpurea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
