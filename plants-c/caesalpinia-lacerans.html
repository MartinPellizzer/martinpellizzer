Caesalpinia lacerans

Caesalpinia lacerans, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that split open when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia lacerans is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
