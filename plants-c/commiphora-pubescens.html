Commiphora pubescens

Commiphora pubescens, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small, evergreen shrub native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
