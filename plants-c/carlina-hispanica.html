Carlina hispanica

Carlina hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually spiny and grayish-green, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Carlina hispanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
