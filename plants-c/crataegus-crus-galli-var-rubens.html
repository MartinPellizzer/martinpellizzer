Crataegus crus-galli var. rubens

Crataegus crus-galli var. rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purplish stems, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic with serrated edges, and it is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
