Carex aematorhyncha

Carex aematorhyncha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender stalks.

Carex aematorhyncha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
