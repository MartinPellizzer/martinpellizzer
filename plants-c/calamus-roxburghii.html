Calamus roxburghii

Calamus roxburghii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine.

Calamus roxburghii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy or marshy areas.
