Citrullus lanatus var. caffrorum

Citrullus lanatus var. caffrorum, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a botanically distinct variant of the watermelon species.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged rind and vibrant red flesh, which is typically sweeter and juicier than the standard watermelon. The plant is a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is particularly valued for its high water content and nutritional value, making it a staple in many African cuisines.
