Cola stigmatosa

Cola stigmatosa, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, egg-shaped fruits that contain a stimulant alkaloid called caffeine. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

The tree thrives in humid, shady conditions and is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
