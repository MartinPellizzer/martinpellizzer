Cochlearia polymorpha

Cochlearia polymorpha, commonly known as the variable cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. It has a low-growing habit with fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cochlearia polymorpha is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is often found in mountainous and tundra habitats.
