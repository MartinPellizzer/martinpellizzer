Cereus royenii

Cereus royenii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to the dry forests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple stem and its small, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stem for survival in arid environments.
