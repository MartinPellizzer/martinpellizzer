Cleome recta

Cleome recta, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome recta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of growth.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.
