Cucurbita umbellata

Cucurbita umbellata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Central America and northern South America. Its fruit, which is typically green and cylindrical, can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
