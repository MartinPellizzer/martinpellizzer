Centrostachys angustifolia

Centrostachys angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf centrostachys, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, producing pale blue to purple blooms.

Centrostachys angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
