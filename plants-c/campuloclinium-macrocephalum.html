Campuloclinium macrocephalum

Campuloclinium macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
