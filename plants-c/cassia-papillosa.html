Cassia papillosa

Cassia papillosa, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, silky yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia papillosa is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
