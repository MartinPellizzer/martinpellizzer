Calceolaria elata

Calceolaria elata, commonly known as the skyflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups, often in shades of violet or pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Calceolaria elata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
