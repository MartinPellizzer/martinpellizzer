Calla aethiopica

Calla aethiopica, commonly known as the African calla, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, narrow leaf blade that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, with a waxy surface that helps it thrive in water. The plant produces a distinctive spathe, a showy, white or cream-colored leaf-like structure that encloses the small, yellowish-green spadix, which contains the actual flowers. It is often cultivated in ponds and wetlands for its ornamental value and ability to help control algae by consuming nutrients.

Calla aethiopica is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions, making it a versatile addition to aquatic gardens.
