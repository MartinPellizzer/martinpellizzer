Coreopsis tinctoria var. atkinsoniana

Coreopsis tinctoria var. atkinsoniana, commonly known as Atkinson's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow to orange-yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
