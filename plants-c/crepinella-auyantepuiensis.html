Crepinella auyantepuiensis

Crepinella auyantepuiensis, commonly known as the Angel's Tears, is a rare flowering plant native to the tepuis of Venezuela.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
