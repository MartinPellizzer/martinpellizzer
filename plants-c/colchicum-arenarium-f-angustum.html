Colchicum arenarium f. angustum

Colchicum arenarium f. angustum, commonly known as the narrowleaf autumn crocus, is a botanical variant of the autumn crocus native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces a distinctive corm, which serves as its storage organ and is crucial for its survival and propagation. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
