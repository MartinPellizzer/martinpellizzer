Coprosma rugosa

Coprosma rugosa, commonly known as rough coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage with a rough texture. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge.
