Crocus vernus var. pallidus

Crocus vernus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The pale crocus is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of renewal and hope.
