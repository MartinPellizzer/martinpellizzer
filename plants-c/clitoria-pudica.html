Clitoria pudica

Clitoria pudica, commonly known as the "pudica bean" or "everlasting pea," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces delicate, butterfly-like blue or violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often grown as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod that can remain attached to the plant for an extended period.

Clitoria pudica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
