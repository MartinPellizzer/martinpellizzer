Cryptanthus lutherianus

Cryptanthus lutherianus, commonly known as the Luther's winterbloom, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, colorful blooms in shades of pink, red, and white during the cooler months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette.

Cryptanthus lutherianus is well-suited for rock gardens and containers, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.
