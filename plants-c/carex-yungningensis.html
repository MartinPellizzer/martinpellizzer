Carex yungningensis

Carex yungningensis, commonly known as the Yungning sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
