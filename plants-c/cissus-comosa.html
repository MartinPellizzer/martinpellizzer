Cissus comosa

Cissus comosa, commonly known as the sand dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that resemble sand dollars, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Cissus comosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
