Cynanchum unguiculatum

Cynanchum unguiculatum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
