Cattleya guttata var. pernambucensis

Cattleya guttata var. pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco spotted orchid, is a striking orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its vibrant colors and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
