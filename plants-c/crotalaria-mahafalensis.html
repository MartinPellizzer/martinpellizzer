Crotalaria mahafalensis

Crotalaria mahafalensis, commonly known as Mahafal false nectarine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent black veins. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Crotalaria mahafalensis is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native region.
