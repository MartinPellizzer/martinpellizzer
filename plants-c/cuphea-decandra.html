Cuphea decandra

Cuphea decandra, commonly known as the flame flower or red cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea decandra is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 feet tall, often used for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
