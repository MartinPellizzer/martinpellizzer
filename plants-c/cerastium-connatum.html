Cerastium connatum

Cerastium connatum, commonly known as the hairy chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with soft, pubescent hairs.

Cerastium connatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and rocky soils.
