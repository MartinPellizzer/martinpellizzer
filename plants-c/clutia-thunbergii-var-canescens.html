Clutia thunbergii var. canescens

Clutia thunbergii var. canescens, commonly known as white clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal panicles, often blooming during the rainy season.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
