Cyperus pseudovegetus var. megalanthus

Cyperus pseudovegetus var. megalanthus, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Cyperus.
