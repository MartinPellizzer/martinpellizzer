Calocephalus birchii

Calocephalus birchii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. Calocephalus birchii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
