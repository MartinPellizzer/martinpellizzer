Cassia demissa

Cassia demissa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish or brownish color. Cassia demissa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
