Cestrum parqui var. longiflorum

Cestrum parqui var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically at night. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
