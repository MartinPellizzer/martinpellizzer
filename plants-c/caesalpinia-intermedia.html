Caesalpinia intermedia

Caesalpinia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
