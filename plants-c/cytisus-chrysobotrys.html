Cytisus chrysobotrys

Cytisus chrysobotrys, commonly known as the golden broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Cytisus chrysobotrys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
