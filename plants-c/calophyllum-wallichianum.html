Calophyllum wallichianum

Calophyllum wallichianum, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen, is a tropical tree belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia, and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
