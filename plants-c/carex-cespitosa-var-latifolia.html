Carex cespitosa var. latifolia

Carex cespitosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are broader than typical sedges. The plant forms dense clumps and thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Broadleaf sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
