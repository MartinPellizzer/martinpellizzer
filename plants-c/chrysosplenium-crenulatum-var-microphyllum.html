Chrysosplenium crenulatum var. microphyllum

Chrysosplenium crenulatum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved golden spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, crenate leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
