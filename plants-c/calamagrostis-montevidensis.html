Calamagrostis montevidensis

Calamagrostis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense reed, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver- or gray-green in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Calamagrostis montevidensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
