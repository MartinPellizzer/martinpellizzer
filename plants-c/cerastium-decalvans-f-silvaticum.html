Cerastium decalvans f. silvaticum

Cerastium decalvans f. silvaticum, commonly known as the forest mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.
