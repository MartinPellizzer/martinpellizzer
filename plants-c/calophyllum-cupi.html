Calophyllum cupi

Calophyllum cupi, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by yellowish or orange-red fruits containing sticky, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
