Carex amicta

Carex amicta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Carex amicta typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
