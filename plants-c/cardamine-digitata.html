Cardamine digitata

Cardamine digitata, commonly known as the forked buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves, with the leaflets often divided into fine, thread-like segments. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a cluster, producing a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Cardamine digitata is often found in rich, loamy soils and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
