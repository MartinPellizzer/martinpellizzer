Crassula pharnaceoides subsp. rhodesica

Crassula pharnaceoides subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense rosette, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among succulent enthusiasts.
