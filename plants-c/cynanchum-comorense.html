Cynanchum comorense

Cynanchum comorense, commonly known as Comorenese milkweed, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
