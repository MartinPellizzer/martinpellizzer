Cosmos calvus

Cosmos calvus, commonly known as the white cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cosmos calvus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
