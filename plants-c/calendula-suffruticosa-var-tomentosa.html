Calendula suffruticosa var. tomentosa

Calendula suffruticosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly calendula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy silver-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular addition to gardens.
