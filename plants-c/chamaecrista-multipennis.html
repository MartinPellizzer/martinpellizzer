Chamaecrista multipennis

Chamaecrista multipennis, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and ecological importance.
