Capparis cardiophylla

Capparis cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions with its succulent leaves.

Capparis cardiophylla is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
