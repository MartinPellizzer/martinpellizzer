Carex insularis

Carex insularis, commonly known as the island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain island regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex insularis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
