Clarorivinia grandifolia

Clarorivinia grandifolia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of golden-yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clarorivinia grandifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
