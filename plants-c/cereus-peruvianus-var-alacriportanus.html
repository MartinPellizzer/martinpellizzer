Cereus peruvianus var. alacriportanus

Cereus peruvianus var. alacriportanus, commonly known as the Peruvian torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, nocturnal flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It has sharp, cylindrical spines and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

This variety is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in dry climates.
