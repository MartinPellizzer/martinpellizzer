Campulosus falcatus

Campulosus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, sickle-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
