Croton ankarensis var. ankarafantsikae

Croton ankarensis var. ankarafantsikae, commonly known as the Ankarafantsikana croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
