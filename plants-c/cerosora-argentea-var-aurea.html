Cerosora argentea var. aurea

Cerosora argentea var. aurea, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a variant of the silverleaf willow species.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
