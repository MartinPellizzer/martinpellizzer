Cirsium maruyamanum

Cirsium maruyamanum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium maruyamanum is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
