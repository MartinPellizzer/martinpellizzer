Crataegus chitaensis

Crataegus chitaensis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
