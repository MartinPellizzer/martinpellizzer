Centaurea salmasensis

Centaurea salmasensis, commonly known as the Salmas thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Centaurea salmasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
