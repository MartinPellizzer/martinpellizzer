Centaurea atacorensis

Centaurea atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Andes region of Peru.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features striking blue to purple composite flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a circular pattern. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Centaurea atacorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native environment.
