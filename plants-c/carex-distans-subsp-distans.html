Carex distans subsp. distans

Carex distans subsp.

distans, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants.

This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
