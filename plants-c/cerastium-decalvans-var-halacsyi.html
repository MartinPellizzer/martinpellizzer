Cerastium decalvans var. halacsyi

Cerastium decalvans var. halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
