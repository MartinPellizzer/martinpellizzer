Cereus bicolor

Cereus bicolor, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a cactus species that produces striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has a spiny, columnar stem with ribbed surfaces and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its unique blooming habit and ornamental value.
