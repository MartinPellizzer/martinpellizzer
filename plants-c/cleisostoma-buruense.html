Cleisostoma buruense

Cleisostoma buruense, commonly known as the Buru moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet scent. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, hood-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cleisostoma buruense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral characteristics.
