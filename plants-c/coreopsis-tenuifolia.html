Coreopsis tenuifolia

Coreopsis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis tenuifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
