Camellia sonthaiensis

Camellia sonthaiensis, commonly known as the Thai camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant produces waxy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Camellia sonthaiensis prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
