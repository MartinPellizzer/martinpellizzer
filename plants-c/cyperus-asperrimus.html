Cyperus asperrimus

Cyperus asperrimus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus asperrimus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
