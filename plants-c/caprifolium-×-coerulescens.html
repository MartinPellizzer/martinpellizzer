Caprifolium × coerulescens

Caprifolium × coerulescens, commonly known as the hybrid blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Caprifolium (European honeysuckle) and Lonicera (honeysuckle).

It is known for its striking blue or purple flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Caprifolium × coerulescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
