Convolvulus schultesii

Convolvulus schultesii, commonly known as the Schultesi's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
