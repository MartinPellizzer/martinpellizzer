Cordia ignota

Cordia ignota, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cordia ignota is often valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
