Cerasus glandulosa f. alba

Cerasus glandulosa f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its white petals, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically sour in taste. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
