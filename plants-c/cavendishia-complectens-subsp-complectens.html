Cavendishia complectens subsp. complectens

Cavendishia complectens subsp.

complectens, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
