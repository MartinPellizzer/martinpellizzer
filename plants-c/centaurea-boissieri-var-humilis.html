Centaurea boissieri var. humilis

Centaurea boissieri var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf centaurea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flower heads that resemble the iconic centaur mythological figure. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
