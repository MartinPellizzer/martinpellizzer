Crataegus scabra

Crataegus scabra, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along stream banks.

Crataegus scabra is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
