Corylus tubulosa var. rubra

Corylus tubulosa var. rubra, commonly known as the red-hair hazelnut, is a variant of the hazelnut species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown pubescent stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, cup-shaped involucres that enclose its yellowish to reddish-brown nuts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
