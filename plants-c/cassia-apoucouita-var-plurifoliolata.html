Cassia apoucouita var. plurifoliolata

Cassia apoucouita var. plurifoliolata, commonly known as the many-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it a lush, feathery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
