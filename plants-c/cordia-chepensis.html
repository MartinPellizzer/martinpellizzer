Cordia chepensis

Cordia chepensis, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Cordia chepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
