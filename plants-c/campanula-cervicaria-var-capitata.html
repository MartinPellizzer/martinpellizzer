Campanula cervicaria var. capitata

Campanula cervicaria var. capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
