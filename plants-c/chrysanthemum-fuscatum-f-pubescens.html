Chrysanthemum fuscatum f. pubescens

Chrysanthemum fuscatum f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its hairy leaves provide a unique texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.
