Camara maximiliani

Camara maximiliani, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Camara maximiliani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
