Cordisepalum phalanthopetalum

Cordisepalum phalanthopetalum, commonly known as the heart-sepal orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features elongated, heart-shaped sepals that are often brightly colored, contributing to its ornamental value. Its petals are elongated and resemble fingers, giving it a striking appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately preserved species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
