Cypripedium × rubicundum

Cypripedium × rubicundum, commonly known as the red-lip lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from its parent species, Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium micranthum.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive red-lipped pouch, which serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower per stem.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
