Convolvulus secundus

Convolvulus secundus, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

The roots of Convolvulus secundus are fibrous and can become invasive in certain environments.
