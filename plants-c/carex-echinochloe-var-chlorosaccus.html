Carex echinochloe var. chlorosaccus

Carex echinochloe var. chlorosaccus, commonly known as the green-sac carex, is a variant of the echinochloe carex species.

It is a perennial sedge characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

The leaves are usually bright green and have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.
