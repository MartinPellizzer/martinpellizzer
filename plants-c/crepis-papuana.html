Crepis papuana

Crepis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc, and the leaves are linear and often hairy.

Crepis papuana is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
