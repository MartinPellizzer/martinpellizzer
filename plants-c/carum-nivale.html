Carum nivale

Carum nivale, commonly known as snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding white flowers that bloom in early spring, even through snow. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and along streambanks.

Carum nivale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
