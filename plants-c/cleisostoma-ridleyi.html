Cleisostoma ridleyi

Cleisostoma ridleyi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Cleisostoma ridleyi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
