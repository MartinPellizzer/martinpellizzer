Caroxylon parviflorum

Caroxylon parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the winter season. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Caroxylon parviflorum is well-suited for cultivation in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.
