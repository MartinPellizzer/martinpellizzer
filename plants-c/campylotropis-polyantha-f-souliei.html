Campylotropis polyantha f. souliei

Campylotropis polyantha f. souliei, commonly known as the Soulie's sweetpea vine, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative vine.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
