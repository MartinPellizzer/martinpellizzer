Calandrinia axilliflora var. foliosa

Calandrinia axilliflora var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a compact growth habit that helps it conserve water.
