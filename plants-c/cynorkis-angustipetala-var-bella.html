Cynorkis angustipetala var. bella

Cynorkis angustipetala var. bella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cycad, is a unique cycad species characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a thick, fibrous layer. Its reproductive structures include male and female cones, which are typically borne on separate plants.

This variant is notable for its distinct morphology and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
