Cheirolophus falcisectus

Cheirolophus falcisectus, commonly known as the falcate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique leaf structure and striking flowers make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
