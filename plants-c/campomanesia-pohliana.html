Campomanesia pohliana

Campomanesia pohliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
