Carex brenneri

Carex brenneri, commonly known as the Brenner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces triangular, three-sided seeds.

Carex brenneri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
