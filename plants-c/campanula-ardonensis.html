Campanula ardonensis

Campanula ardonensis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula ardonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
