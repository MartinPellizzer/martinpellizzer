Clematis hilarii var. triloba

Clematis hilarii var. triloba, commonly known as the trilobed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically blue or purple and are borne in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. This plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or support structures.

It is also noted for its moderate growth habit and relatively low maintenance requirements.
