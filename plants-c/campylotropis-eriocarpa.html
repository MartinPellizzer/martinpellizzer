Campylotropis eriocarpa

Campylotropis eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy stemmed bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are arranged in dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
