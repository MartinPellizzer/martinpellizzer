Cosmos marginatus

Cosmos marginatus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with red centers, typically blooming from summer to fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are gray-green in color, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for butterfly gardens.

Cosmos marginatus is easy to grow from seed and is often used as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
