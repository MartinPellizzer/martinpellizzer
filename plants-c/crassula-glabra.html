Crassula glabra

Crassula glabra, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in a compact, shrub-like form with small, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the warmer months.

Crassula glabra is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
