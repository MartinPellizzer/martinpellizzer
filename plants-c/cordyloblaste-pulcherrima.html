Cordyloblaste pulcherrima

Cordyloblaste pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that encase clusters of small, white flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cordyloblaste pulcherrima thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
