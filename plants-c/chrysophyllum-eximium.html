Chrysophyllum eximium

Chrysophyllum eximium, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a creamy, sweet flesh. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
