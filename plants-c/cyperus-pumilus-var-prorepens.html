Cyperus pumilus var. prorepens

Cyperus pumilus var. prorepens, commonly known as the dwarf sedgeland sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
