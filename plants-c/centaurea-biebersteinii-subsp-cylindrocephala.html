Centaurea biebersteinii subsp. cylindrocephala

Centaurea biebersteinii subsp.

cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, cylindrical flower heads that are typically reddish-brown in color.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact, bushy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
