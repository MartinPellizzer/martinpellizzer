Cichorium arnoseris

Cichorium arnoseris, commonly known as Armenian chicory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces blue or purple flowers arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern, typical of the Cichorium genus. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The leaves and roots are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
