Chionanthus bumelioides

Chionanthus bumelioides, commonly known as the whiteflower steeplebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Chionanthus bumelioides plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
