Campanula rapunculoides var. pallida

Campanula rapunculoides var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pale blue to white bell-shaped flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture. This species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its delicate appearance and easy cultivation make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
