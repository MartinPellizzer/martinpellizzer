Carex trinervis var. laxa

Carex trinervis var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly tapered, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, upright flowering stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
