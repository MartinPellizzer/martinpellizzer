Clerodendrum sumatranum var. fairchildii

Clerodendrum sumatranum var. fairchildii, commonly known as the Fairchild's glorybower, is a striking tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that range in color from deep red to orange, often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, surrounded by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
