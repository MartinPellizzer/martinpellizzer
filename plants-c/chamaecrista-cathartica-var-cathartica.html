Chamaecrista cathartica var. cathartica

Chamaecrista cathartica var. cathartica, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellow coloration. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
