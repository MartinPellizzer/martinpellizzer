Carpodinus chylorrhiza

Carpodinus chylorrhiza, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are adapted for water, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
