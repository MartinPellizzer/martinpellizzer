Cactus kunthii

Cactus kunthii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its structure consists of a thick, segmented stem with areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge.

Cactus kunthii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
