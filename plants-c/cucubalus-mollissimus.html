Cucubalus mollissimus

Cucubalus mollissimus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval, woody pods that contain several seeds. The seeds are encased in a fibrous, aromatic pulp, which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
