Cestrum parqui var. macrocalyx

Cestrum parqui var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
