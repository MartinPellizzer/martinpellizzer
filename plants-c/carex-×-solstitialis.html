Carex × solstitialis

Carex × solstitialis, commonly known as the summer sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant produces distinctive, three-sided seed heads.

Carex × solstitialis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
