Calamus pseudotenuis subsp. karnatakensis

Calamus pseudotenuis subsp.

karnatakensis, commonly known as the Karnatak slender reed, is a member of the Aroid family, Araceae. It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, which are enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to the tropical regions of southern Asia, particularly India, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
