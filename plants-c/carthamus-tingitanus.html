Carthamus tingitanus

Carthamus tingitanus, commonly known as the Moroccan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its scientific name reflects its historical association with the ancient city of Tingis, now Tangier.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
