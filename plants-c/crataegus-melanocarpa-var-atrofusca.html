Crataegus melanocarpa var. atrofusca

Crataegus melanocarpa var. atrofusca, commonly known as the black hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are clustered in dense, spherical formations. The plant has thorny branches and ovate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
