Citronella sarmentosa

Citronella sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping citronella, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces a yellowish-orange fruit that contains aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in natural insect repellents.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for use as a ground cover in warm climates.
