Clematis viticella subsp. campaniflora

Clematis viticella subsp.

campaniflora, commonly known as the Campanuliflora Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. It is known for its vigorous growth and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
