Cyperus lapidicola

Cyperus lapidicola, commonly known as the stone rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus lapidicola is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
