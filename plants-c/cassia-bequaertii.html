Cassia bequaertii

Cassia bequaertii, commonly known as the Hawaiian cassia, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cassia bequaertii is often found in coastal and lowland areas, showcasing its adaptability to the tropical climate of Hawaii.
