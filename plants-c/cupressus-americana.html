Cupressus americana

Cupressus americana, commonly known as the American cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, providing some resistance to fire and insects.

The American cedar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
