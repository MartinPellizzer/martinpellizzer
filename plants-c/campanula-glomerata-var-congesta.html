Campanula glomerata var. congesta

Campanula glomerata var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant blooms.
