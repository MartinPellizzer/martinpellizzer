Chrysalidocarpus arenarum

Chrysalidocarpus arenarum, commonly known as the desert pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are pollinated by wind and insects. Its fibrous roots help it anchor in sandy soils, and its leaves are often used by local communities for thatching and weaving.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
