Coleus harmandii

Coleus harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
