Crataegus monogyna var. panachaica

Crataegus monogyna var. panachaica, commonly known as the panachaea hawthorn, is a variety of the common hawthorn native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
