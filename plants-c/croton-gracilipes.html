Croton gracilipes

Croton gracilipes, commonly known as the slender croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is popular in horticulture but requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
