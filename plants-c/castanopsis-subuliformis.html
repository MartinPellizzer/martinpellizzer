Castanopsis subuliformis

Castanopsis subuliformis, commonly known as the needle-leaf tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. Its cones are small and woody, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.
