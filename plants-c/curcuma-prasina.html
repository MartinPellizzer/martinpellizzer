Curcuma prasina

Curcuma prasina, commonly known as green turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its thick, aromatic underground stems. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that are typically hidden within leaf sheaths. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, valued for their mild flavor and potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
