Croton anomalus

Croton anomalus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking foliage.
