Cracosna carinata

Cracosna carinata, commonly known as the mountain pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to high-altitude regions of South America. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
