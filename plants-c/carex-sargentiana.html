Carex sargentiana

Carex sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its appearance features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered inflorescences.

Carex sargentiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
