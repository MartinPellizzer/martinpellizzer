Cossinia ternata

Cossinia ternata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and has adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive, dandelion-like appearance.

Cossinia ternata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
