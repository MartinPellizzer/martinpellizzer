Chrysothamnus pulchellus var. elatior

Chrysothamnus pulchellus var. elatior, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
