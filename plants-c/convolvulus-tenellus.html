Convolvulus tenellus

Convolvulus tenellus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots and ability to thrive in well-drained soils make it adaptable to various environments.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and persistent seed production.
