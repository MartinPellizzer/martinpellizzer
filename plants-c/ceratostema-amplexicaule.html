Ceratostema amplexicaule

Ceratostema amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping ceratostema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often clasping the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
