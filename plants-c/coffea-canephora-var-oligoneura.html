Coffea canephora var. oligoneura

Coffea canephora var. oligoneura, commonly known as the Congo coffee or West African coffee, is a variant of the robusta coffee species.

It is native to Central and West Africa, where it thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries containing two seeds each. It is known for its high caffeine content and is often used in commercial coffee blends.

This variety is valued for its strong flavor profile and adaptability to various growing conditions.
