Carex valesiaca

Carex valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex valesiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
