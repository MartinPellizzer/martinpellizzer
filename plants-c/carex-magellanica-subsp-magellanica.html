Carex magellanica subsp. magellanica

Carex magellanica subsp.

magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
