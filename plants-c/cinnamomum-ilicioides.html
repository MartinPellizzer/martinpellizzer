Cinnamomum ilicioides

Cinnamomum ilicioides, commonly known as the Indian laurel or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum ilicioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and commercial uses in perfumery and herbal remedies.
