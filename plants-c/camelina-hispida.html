Camelina hispida

Camelina hispida, commonly known as rough camelina, is an annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence.

Camelina hispida is adapted to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
