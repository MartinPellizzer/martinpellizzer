Campanula bononiensis subsp. lychnitis

Campanula bononiensis subsp.

lychnitis, commonly known as the Bononiensis bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
