Conospermum polycephalum

Conospermum polycephalum, commonly known as the Sydney flame, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring.
