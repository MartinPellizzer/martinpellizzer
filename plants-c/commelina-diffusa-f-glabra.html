Commelina diffusa f. glabra

Commelina diffusa f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth commelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.
