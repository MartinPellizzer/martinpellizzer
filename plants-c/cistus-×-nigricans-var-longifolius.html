Cistus × nigricans var. longifolius

Cistus × nigricans var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species often used in xeriscaping.
