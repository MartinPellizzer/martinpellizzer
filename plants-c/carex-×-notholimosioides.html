Carex × notholimosioides

Carex × notholimosioides, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex notholimosa and Carex limosae, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.
