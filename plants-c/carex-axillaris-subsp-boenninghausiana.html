Carex axillaris subsp. boenninghausiana

Carex axillaris subsp.

boenninghausiana, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
