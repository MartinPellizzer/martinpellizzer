Cyrtochilum vierlingii

Cyrtochilum vierlingii, commonly known as the Vierling's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like labellum that resembles a slipper, a feature common in many orchid species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Cyrtochilum vierlingii has a fibrous root system and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

It is a relatively rare and specialized orchid, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
