Carex leucochlora var. longearistata

Carex leucochlora var. longearistata, commonly known as the white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its white to pale green inflorescences, which are often adorned with silky, thread-like hairs. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
