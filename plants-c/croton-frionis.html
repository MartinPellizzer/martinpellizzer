Croton frionis

Croton frionis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates for its foliage.
