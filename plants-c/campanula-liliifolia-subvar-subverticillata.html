Campanula liliifolia subvar. subverticillata

Campanula liliifolia subvar. subverticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
