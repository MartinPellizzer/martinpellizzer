Carpesium divaricatum var. divaricatum

Carpesium divaricatum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its leaf shape and flowering pattern.
