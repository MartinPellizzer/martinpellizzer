Carex abscondita var. glauca

Carex abscondita var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a variant of the Carex abscondita species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
