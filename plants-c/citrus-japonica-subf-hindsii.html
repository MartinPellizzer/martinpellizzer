Citrus japonica subf. hindsii

Citrus japonica subf.

hindsii, commonly known as the Hindsii citrus, is a unique hybrid plant originating from Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, round leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

This sub-species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
