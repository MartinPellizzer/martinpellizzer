Calathea trifasciata

Calathea trifasciata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display alternating bands of dark green and white, creating a zebra-like pattern. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Calathea trifasciata requires consistent moisture and high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.
