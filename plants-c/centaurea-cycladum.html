Centaurea cycladum

Centaurea cycladum, commonly known as the Cycladic centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround its flowering heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Centaurea cycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
