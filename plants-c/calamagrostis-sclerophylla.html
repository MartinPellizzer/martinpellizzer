Calamagrostis sclerophylla

Calamagrostis sclerophylla, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions. Its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

Calamagrostis sclerophylla is valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
