Cistus × laxus f. pedroi

Cistus × laxus f. pedroi, commonly known as the Pedro's rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, arid conditions.
