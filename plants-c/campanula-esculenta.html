Campanula esculenta

Campanula esculenta, commonly known as the edible bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible parts, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional cuisines.
