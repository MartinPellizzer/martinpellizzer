Cerasus spachiana var. koshiensis

Cerasus spachiana var. koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a botanical variant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in temperate and alpine environments. The plant produces small, sweet, and edible fruits that are highly valued in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink, blooming in early spring.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to cold and rocky soils.
