Chuquiraga rotundifolia

Chuquiraga rotundifolia, commonly known as the Andean burr plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. It is characterized by its unique fruit, which consists of a spiny, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant has been used traditionally in local medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
