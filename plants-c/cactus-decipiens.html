Cactus decipiens

Cactus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure is adapted to store water and withstand extreme temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in rocky soils and desert environments.
