Croton mucronatus

Croton mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-pointed croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with deep lobes and vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy foliage.

Croton mucronatus is relatively low-maintenance but requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to flourish.
