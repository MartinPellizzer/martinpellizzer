Calligonum kzyl-kumi

Calligonum kzyl-kumi, commonly known as the Kyzylkum spurge, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various desert species.
