Cupressus nabiana

Cupressus nabiana, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit. The plant has scale-like leaves that are densely packed on the shoots, giving it a distinct appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
