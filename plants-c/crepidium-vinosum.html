Crepidium vinosum

Crepidium vinosum, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, erect stems with elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically violet or purple in color, arranged in a raceme.

Crepidium vinosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
