Cinnamomum aromaticum var. longifolium

Cinnamomum aromaticum var. longifolium, commonly known as cinnamon wood or Chinese cinnamon, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties in both horticulture and commerce.
