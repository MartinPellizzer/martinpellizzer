Convolvulus blanchetii f. coeruleus

Convolvulus blanchetii f. coeruleus, commonly known as the sky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a variant of the species Convolvulus blanchetii, characterized by its striking sky-blue flowers. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
