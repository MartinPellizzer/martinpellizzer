Cynoglossum longipedicellatum

Cynoglossum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive, bell-like shape, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
