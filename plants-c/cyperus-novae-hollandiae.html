Cyperus novae-hollandiae

Cyperus novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in regions with high humidity and well-drained soils.

Cyperus novae-hollandiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
