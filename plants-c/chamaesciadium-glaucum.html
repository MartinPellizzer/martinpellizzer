Chamaesciadium glaucum

Chamaesciadium glaucum, commonly known as the blue star, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, star-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
