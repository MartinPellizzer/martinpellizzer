Caltha natans var. asarifolia

Caltha natans var. asarifolia, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are typically divided into thread-like segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
