Campanula linifolia f. vestita

Campanula linifolia f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name, and its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
