Crataegus wattiana var. incisa

Crataegus wattiana var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often sharply toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
