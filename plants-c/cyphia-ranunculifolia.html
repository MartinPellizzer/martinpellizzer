Cyphia ranunculifolia

Cyphia ranunculifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cyphia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green leaves that are often adorned with white or pale veins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring. Cyphia ranunculifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
