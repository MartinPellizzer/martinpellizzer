Cichorium endivia var. latifolium

Cichorium endivia var. latifolium, commonly known as Belgian endive, is a cultivated variety of the chicory plant characterized by its broad, pale leaves.

It is typically grown in cool climates and is often harvested before the leaves become too bitter. The plant's leaves are used in salads, offering a slightly bitter, nutty flavor. It is rich in inulin, a prebiotic fiber that supports digestive health.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes used in gourmet cooking.
