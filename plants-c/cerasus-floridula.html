Cerasus floridula

Cerasus floridula, commonly known as the Florida cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red fruits that are edible but often tart. It typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, preferring moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
