Cheilanthes spectabilis

Cheilanthes spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cliff brake, is a species of fern native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, evergreen rhizomes and its distinctive, elongated, serrated leaves that often display a vibrant green to reddish hue. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial plant that can tolerate drought conditions due to its ability to store moisture in its rhizomes.

The showy cliff brake is often used in xeriscaping and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
