Coleus scutellarioides var. crispipilus

Coleus scutellarioides var. crispipilus, commonly known as the curly-haired coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green to maroon hues and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and tubular, typically arranged in spikes, and are usually pale purple or white.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
