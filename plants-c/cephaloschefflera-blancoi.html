Cephaloschefflera blancoi

Cephaloschefflera blancoi, commonly known as the white-veined sycamore, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive white venation pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.
