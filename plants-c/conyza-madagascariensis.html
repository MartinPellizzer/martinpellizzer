Conyza madagascariensis

Conyza madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is an annual herb native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
