Chamaecrista axilliflora

Chamaecrista axilliflora, commonly known as the slender bird's-bill, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
