Carex seticulmis

Carex seticulmis, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex seticulmis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
