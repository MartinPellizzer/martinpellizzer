Crataegus flava lobata

Crataegus flava lobata, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments, and its bark is often rugged and fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
