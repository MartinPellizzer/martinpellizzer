Chamaecrista nictitans subsp. nictitans

Chamaecrista nictitans subsp.

nictitans, commonly known as the cowpea vine or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is a legume with compound leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its erect or climbing habit and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
