Commiphora rehmanniana

Commiphora rehmanniana, commonly known as Indian frankincense or guggul, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested to make frankincense, a substance used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its chemical composition and traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.
