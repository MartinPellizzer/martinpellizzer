Calla

Calla, commonly known as the Calla Lily, is a striking plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its spathe, a large, petal-like leaf that encloses the small, true flowers. The plant typically grows in wet environments such as marshes and ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and orange, adding vibrant visual appeal.

Calla lilies are often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as houseplants.
