Chamaeiris angustifolia

Chamaeiris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf chamaeiris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying shades of pink or white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
