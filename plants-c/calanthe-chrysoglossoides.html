Calanthe chrysoglossoides

Calanthe chrysoglossoides, commonly known as the golden-tongued calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a pair of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces a spike of showy, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive golden lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
