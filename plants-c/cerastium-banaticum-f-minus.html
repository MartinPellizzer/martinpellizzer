Cerastium banaticum f. minus

Cerastium banaticum f. minus, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Cerastium genus, with a unique morphology that sets it apart from its relatives.
