Cerastium vagans var. calvum

Cerastium vagans var. calvum, commonly known as the hairless mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine, short hairs, though the variety calvum is hairless, giving it a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
