Cathetus fasciculata

Cathetus fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cathetus fasciculata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
