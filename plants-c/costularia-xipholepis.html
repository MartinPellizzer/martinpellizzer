Costularia xipholepis

Costularia xipholepis, commonly known as the swordleaf costularia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
