Chenopodium bolivianum

Chenopodium bolivianum, commonly known as Bolivian goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Bolivia, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Chenopodium bolivianum is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in minerals.
