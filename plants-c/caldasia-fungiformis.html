Caldasia fungiformis

Caldasia fungiformis, commonly known as the fleshy caldasia, is a unique aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems and leaves that resemble a sponge, which help it absorb nutrients from the water. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its structure allows it to store water and nutrients, making it well-adapted to fluctuating aquatic conditions.

Caldasia fungiformis is often found in shaded, muddy substrates where it can anchor its roots and grow vigorously.
