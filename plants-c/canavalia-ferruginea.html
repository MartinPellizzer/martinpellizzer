Canavalia ferruginea

Canavalia ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored bean or red bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive reddish-brown pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the soil.

Canavalia ferruginea is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
