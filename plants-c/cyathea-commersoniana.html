Cyathea commersoniana

Cyathea commersoniana, commonly known as the Commerson's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Cyathea commersoniana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
