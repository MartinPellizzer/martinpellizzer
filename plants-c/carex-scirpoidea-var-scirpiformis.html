Carex scirpoidea var. scirpiformis

Carex scirpoidea var. scirpiformis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to aquatic habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
