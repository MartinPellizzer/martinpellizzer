Carpesium Divaricatum Var. Matsuei

Carpesium divaricatum var. matsuei is a unique variant of the common cocklebur plant known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance. This plant is native to parts of East Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its spiny foliage and yellow flowers make it easily identifiable, though it can be mistaken for other similar species. The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals. Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and has gained attention for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
