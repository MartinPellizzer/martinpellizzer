Citrus × nobilis var. chrysocarpa

Citrus × nobilis var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden orange, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange fruit.

The plant produces oval-shaped fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a high concentration of aromatic oils. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. The flowers are white and fragrant, typical of the Rutaceae family.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
