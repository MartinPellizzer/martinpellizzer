Cucumis melo var. firany

Cucumis melo var. firany, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its thick, aromatic rind and sweet, juicy flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and Central Asia. The fruit typically has a pale green or yellowish exterior with a textured surface, and it matures in the summer season. It is valued for its flavor and is often used in fresh consumption or as a dessert fruit.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
