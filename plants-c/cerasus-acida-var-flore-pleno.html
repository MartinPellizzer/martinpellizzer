Cerasus acida var. flore-pleno

Cerasus acida var. flore-pleno, commonly known as the double-flowered sour cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that give it a more ornamental appearance compared to single-flowered varieties. The plant typically produces small, tart cherries that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
