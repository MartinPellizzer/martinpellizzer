Centaurea caucasica

Centaurea caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads.

Centaurea caucasica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
