Cyathea williamsii

Cyathea williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in moist soil, while its fronds can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea williamsii plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to the understory vegetation.
