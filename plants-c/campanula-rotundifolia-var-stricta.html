Campanula rotundifolia var. stricta

Campanula rotundifolia var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
