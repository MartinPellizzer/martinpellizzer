Cassia curvistyla

Cassia curvistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate and pinnate, with slender, lance-shaped leaflets.
