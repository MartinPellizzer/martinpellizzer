Cinna arundinacea

Cinna arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands.

Cinna arundinacea is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance.
