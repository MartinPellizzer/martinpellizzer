Cissus hookeri

Cissus hookeri, commonly known as the hooked cutleaf grape.

It is a climbing plant native to South Africa, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and versatile plant for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
