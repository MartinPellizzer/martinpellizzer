Coleus sallyae

Coleus sallyae, commonly known as Sally's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

While it produces small, tubular flowers, its primary ornamental value lies in its striking leaf patterns.
