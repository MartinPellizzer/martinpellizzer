Carex × duereriana

Carex × duereriana, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, water-tolerant plant.
