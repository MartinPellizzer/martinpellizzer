Cestrum caloneurum

Cestrum caloneurum, commonly known as the sweet scent nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
