Campanula turbinata f. lilacina

Campanula turbinata f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered bellflower, is a horticultural form of the species Campanula turbinata.

It is characterized by its pale lilac to violet-blue flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
