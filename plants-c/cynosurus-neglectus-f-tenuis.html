Cynosurus neglectus f. tenuis

Cynosurus neglectus f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is relatively low-maintenance, making it a common sight in natural habitats.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
