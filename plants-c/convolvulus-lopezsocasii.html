Convolvulus lopezsocasii

Convolvulus lopezsocasii, commonly known as the Lopez de Soca convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the Convolvulaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
