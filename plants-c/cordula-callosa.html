Cordula callosa

Cordula callosa, commonly known as the rough cordula, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Cordula callosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
