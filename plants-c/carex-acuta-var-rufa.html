Carex acuta var. rufa

Carex acuta var. rufa, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically reddish in color. This variety is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
