Carex hirta f. paludosa

Carex hirta f. paludosa, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
