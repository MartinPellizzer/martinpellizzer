Campanopsis undulata f. albiflora

Campanopsis undulata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a rare and striking variant of the Campanopsis genus.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, contrasting with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant is native to specific regions in China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique coloration.
