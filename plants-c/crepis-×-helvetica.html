Crepis × helvetica

Crepis × helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss mouse-ear.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Crepis, characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy stems. The plant typically produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Crepis × helvetica is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
