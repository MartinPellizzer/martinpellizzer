Centaurea cruenta

Centaurea cruenta, commonly known as the bloody centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Centaurea cruenta is an annual or biennial herb, known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
