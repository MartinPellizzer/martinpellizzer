Campanula speciosa subsp. affinis

Campanula speciosa subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Campanula speciosa subsp.

affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
