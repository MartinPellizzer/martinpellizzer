Carex macroglossa var. tsukudensis

Carex macroglossa var. tsukudensis, commonly known as the Japanese broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape designs.
