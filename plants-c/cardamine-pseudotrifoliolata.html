Cardamine pseudotrifoliolata

Cardamine pseudotrifoliolata, commonly known as the false three-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three rounded lobes, often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
