Cryptophragmium hirsutum

Cryptophragmium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cryptophragmium, is a unique member of the Lecanoraceae family within the lichenized fungi.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy growth on the thallus, which gives it its common name. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with a green alga, contributing to its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions. It typically grows on bark, soil, or rock surfaces in temperate regions.

Its structure and composition make it an interesting subject for studies on lichen biology and ecological adaptation.
