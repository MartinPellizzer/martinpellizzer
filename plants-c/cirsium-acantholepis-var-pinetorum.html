Cirsium acantholepis var. pinetorum

Cirsium acantholepis var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the pine forests of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.
