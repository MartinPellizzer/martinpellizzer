Cytisus grandiflorus subsp. barbarus

Cytisus grandiflorus subsp.

barbarus, commonly known as the Barbary broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems covered in small, glandular hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
