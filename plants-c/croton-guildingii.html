Croton guildingii

Croton guildingii, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from yellow to red and orange hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton guildingii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves and adaptability to various growing conditions.
