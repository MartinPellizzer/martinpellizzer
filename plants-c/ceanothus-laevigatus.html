Ceanothus laevigatus

Ceanothus laevigatus, commonly known as the silky ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often shrub-like in growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny, dry environments.

Ceanothus laevigatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
