Crossoglossa elliptica

Crossoglossa elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crossoglossa, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in a spike-like inflorescence.

The genus Crossoglossa is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a specialized labellum.
