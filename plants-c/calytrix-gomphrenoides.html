Calytrix gomphrenoides

Calytrix gomphrenoides, commonly known as the red-flowered calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Calytrix gomphrenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
