Ceratocephalus debilis

Ceratocephalus debilis, commonly known as the slender sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called burrs, which contain seeds and aid in seed dispersal. It typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Ceratocephalus debilis is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.
