Caralluma australis

Caralluma australis, commonly known as the coastal prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spine-tipped pads and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along coastal regions.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
