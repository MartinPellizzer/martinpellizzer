Coelocline oxypetala

Coelocline oxypetala, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or pink petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
