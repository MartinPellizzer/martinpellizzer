Cattleya amethystoglossa var. alba

Cattleya amethystoglossa var. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a pale pink lip, which contrast beautifully against its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It produces fragrant blooms that can reach up to 8 inches in diameter, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

This variety is valued for its elegance and is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and botanical study.
