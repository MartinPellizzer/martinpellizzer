Cymbopogon finitimus

Cymbopogon finitimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications. Its leaves contain compounds such as citral and geraniol, contributing to its distinct citrus scent.

Cymbopogon finitimus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
