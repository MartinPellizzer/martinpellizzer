Clausena lenis

Clausena lenis, commonly known as the Indian mume or sweet-scented flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. Its botanical features include a single stem, alternate leaves, and a distinctive, showy floral display.

Clausena lenis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
