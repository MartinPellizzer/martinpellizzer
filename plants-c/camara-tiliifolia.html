Camara tiliifolia

Camara tiliifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, especially in mangrove swamps. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries.

Camara tiliifolia plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
