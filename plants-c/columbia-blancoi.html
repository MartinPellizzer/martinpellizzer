Columbia blancoi

Columbia blancoi, commonly known as the white columbia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and robust, woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
