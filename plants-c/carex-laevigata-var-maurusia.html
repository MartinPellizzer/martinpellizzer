Carex laevigata var. maurusia

Carex laevigata var. maurusia, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces cylindrical, three-sided seed heads.

Carex laevigata var. maurusia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
