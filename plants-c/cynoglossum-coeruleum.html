Cynoglossum coeruleum

Cynoglossum coeruleum, commonly known as blue hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or wildflower meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in natural habitats.
