Carex forrestii

Carex forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-sided seed heads.

Carex forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
