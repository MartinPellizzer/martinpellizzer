Coussapoa grandiceps

Coussapoa grandiceps, commonly known as the giant orchid or the Brazilian orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a complex inflorescence with numerous floral parts.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions for successful cultivation.
