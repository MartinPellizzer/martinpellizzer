Citrus combara var. micrantha

Citrus combara var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf mandarin, is a compact citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, sweet, and seedless fruits that are typically harvested in the winter months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and bears fragrant white flowers during the spring. It thrives in warm climates with well-draining soil and requires regular pruning to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in both home gardens and commercial orchards.
