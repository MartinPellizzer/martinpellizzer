Cereus × smithii

Cereus × smithii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cereus peruvianus and Schlumbergera truncata.

It is a flowering cactus native to Brazil, characterized by its segmented stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cereus × smithii is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
