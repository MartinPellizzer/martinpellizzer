Campanula rotundifolia subsp. intercedens

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

intercedens, commonly known as the round-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
