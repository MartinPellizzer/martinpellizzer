Carica gossypiifolia f. glabrata

Carica gossypiifolia f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-hairless papaya, is a variant of the papaya species characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces large, fleshy, orange-colored fruits that are edible and rich in nutrients. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic properties.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural potential in warm climates.
