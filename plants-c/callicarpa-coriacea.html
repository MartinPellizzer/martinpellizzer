Callicarpa coriacea

Callicarpa coriacea, commonly known as the rough beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, violet-blue berries that mature in the fall. The plant has oval, glossy leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
