Cyperus pachystylus

Cyperus pachystylus, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in three rows. Cyperus pachystylus typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the top of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.
