Camellia nitidissima var. phaeopubisperma

Camellia nitidissima var. phaeopubisperma, commonly known as the red-flowered camellia, is a species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from deep red to pink in color. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
