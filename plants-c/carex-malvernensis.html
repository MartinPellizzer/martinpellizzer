Carex malvernensis

Carex malvernensis, commonly known as Malvern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the UK.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.
