Cochleanthes bidentata

Cochleanthes bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with two prominent teeth on the lip, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cochleanthes bidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
