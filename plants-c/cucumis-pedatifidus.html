Cucumis pedatifidus

Cucumis pedatifidus, commonly known as the broadleaf cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a small, elongated cucumber with a mild flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
