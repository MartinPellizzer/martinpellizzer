Clinopodium serpyllifolium

Clinopodium serpyllifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma when crushed.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.
