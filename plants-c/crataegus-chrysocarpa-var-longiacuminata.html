Crataegus chrysocarpa var. longiacuminata

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. longiacuminata, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange berries, which are typically covered in a thin, waxy coating. The plant features thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique fruit coloration and its role in local ecosystems.
