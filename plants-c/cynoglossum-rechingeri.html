Cynoglossum rechingeri

Cynoglossum rechingeri, commonly known as the Persian hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in a raceme. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cynoglossum rechingeri is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
