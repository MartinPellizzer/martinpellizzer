Cytisus fontanesii subsp. plumosus

Cytisus fontanesii subsp.

plumosus, commonly known as the plumosus broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
