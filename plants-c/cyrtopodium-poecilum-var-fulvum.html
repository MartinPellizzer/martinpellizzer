Cyrtopodium poecilum var. fulvum

Cyrtopodium poecilum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with yellowish-orange blooms. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
