Chloranthus japonicus

Chloranthus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese spurge or Japanese chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It produces non-woody stems and has a short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
