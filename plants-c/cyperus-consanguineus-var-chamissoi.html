Cyperus consanguineus var. chamissoi

Cyperus consanguineus var. chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually borne in dense clusters, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
