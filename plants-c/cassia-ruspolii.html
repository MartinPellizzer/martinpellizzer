Cassia ruspolii

Cassia ruspolii, commonly known as the African cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with several pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Cassia ruspolii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value in gardens.
