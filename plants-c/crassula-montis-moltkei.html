Crassula montis-moltkei

Crassula montis-moltkei, commonly known as the Moltke's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
