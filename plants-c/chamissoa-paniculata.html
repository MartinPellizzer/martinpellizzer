Chamissoa paniculata

Chamissoa paniculata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.
