Campylocentrum palominoi

Campylocentrum palominoi, commonly known as the Palomino orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
