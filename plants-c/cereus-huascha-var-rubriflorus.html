Cereus huascha var. rubriflorus

Cereus huascha var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces striking, large, showy flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically bright red or pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Its unique blooming habit and ornamental value make it a notable plant in botanical collections.
