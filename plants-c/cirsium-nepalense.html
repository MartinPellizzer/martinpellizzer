Cirsium nepalense

Cirsium nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually spiny and serrated, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Cirsium nepalense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
