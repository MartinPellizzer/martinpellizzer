Cerastium candidissimum

Cerastium candidissimum, commonly known as white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
