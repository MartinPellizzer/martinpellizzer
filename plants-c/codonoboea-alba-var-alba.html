Codonoboea alba var. alba

Codonoboea alba var. alba, commonly known as the white codonoboea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
