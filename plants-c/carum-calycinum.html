Carum calycinum

Carum calycinum, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor to dishes.

Carum calycinum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
