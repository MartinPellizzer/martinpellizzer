Cheiridopsis peersii

Cheiridopsis peersii, commonly known as the Cape Town dandelion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Cheiridopsis peersii is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
