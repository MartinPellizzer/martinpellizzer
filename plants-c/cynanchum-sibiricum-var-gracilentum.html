Cynanchum sibiricum var. gracilentum

Cynanchum sibiricum var. gracilentum, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
