Campanula patula var. calycina

Campanula patula var. calycina, commonly known as the California bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
