Carex crassiflora

Carex crassiflora, commonly known as broad-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex crassiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and wetland habitats.
