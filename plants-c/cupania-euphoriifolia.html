Cupania euphoriifolia

Cupania euphoriifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian grape or Brazilian passionvine, is a woody vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately arranged with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Cupania euphoriifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
