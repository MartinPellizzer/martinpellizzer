Cytisus austriacus var. urumoffii

Cytisus austriacus var. urumoffii, commonly known as the Austrian broom variant, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are typical of the genus Cytisus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
