Clematis alpina var. triternata

Clematis alpina var. triternata, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a perennial climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that can climb and twine around supports, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are palmately divided into three leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
