Calamagrostis alajica

Calamagrostis alajica, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Calamagrostis alajica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
