Cliffortia lanata

Cliffortia lanata, commonly known as the woolly cliffortia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
