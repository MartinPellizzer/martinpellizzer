Centaurea csatoi

Centaurea csatoi, commonly known as the Csáto's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 centimeters, with upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea csatoi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
