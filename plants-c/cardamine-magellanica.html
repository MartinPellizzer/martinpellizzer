Cardamine magellanica

Cardamine magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma.

The small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.
