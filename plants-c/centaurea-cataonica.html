Centaurea cataonica

Centaurea cataonica, commonly known as the Anatolian starthistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head surrounded by pale yellow to white bracts.

Centaurea cataonica is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
