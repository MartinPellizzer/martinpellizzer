Cynoglossum montanum var. parviflorum

Cynoglossum montanum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
