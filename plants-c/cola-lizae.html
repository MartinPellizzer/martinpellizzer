Cola lizae

Cola lizae, commonly known as the "Liza's cola," is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, and it has palmately lobed leaves. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.
