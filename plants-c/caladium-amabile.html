Caladium amabile

Caladium amabile, commonly known as the white-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with vivid white veins on a deep green background. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Caladium amabile thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal.
