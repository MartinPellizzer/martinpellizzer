Chromolaena decumbens

Chromolaena decumbens, commonly known as spreading chromolaena, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
