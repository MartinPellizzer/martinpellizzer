Cyrtopodium virescens

Cyrtopodium virescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendant, greenish-white flowers with a pleasant fragrance, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cyrtopodium virescens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.
