Clerodendrum buchananii

Clerodendrum buchananii, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
