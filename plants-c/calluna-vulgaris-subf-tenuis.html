Calluna vulgaris subf. tenuis

Calluna vulgaris subf.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

This subsp. typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. It plays an important role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in horticulture.
