Cordia leonis

Cordia leonis, commonly known as the lion's heart tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled white or pink flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
