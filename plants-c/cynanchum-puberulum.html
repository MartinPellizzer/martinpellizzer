Cynanchum puberulum

Cynanchum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their toxic sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in umbels, and produce a milky latex when damaged.

Cynanchum puberulum is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
