Carex fuirenoides var. cirrhulosa

Carex fuirenoides var. cirrhulosa, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
