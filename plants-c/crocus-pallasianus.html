Crocus pallasianus

Crocus pallasianus, commonly known as the Pallas' crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a white or yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The bulbs of this plant are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, allowing it to bloom in early spring.
