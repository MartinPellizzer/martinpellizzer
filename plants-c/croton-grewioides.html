Croton grewioides

Croton grewioides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
