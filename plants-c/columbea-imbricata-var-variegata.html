Columbea imbricata var. variegata

Columbea imbricata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bluebell, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The leaves are lance-shaped and often display a variegated pattern of green and white, adding to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for.
