Circaea canadensis var. virginiana

Circaea canadensis var. virginiana, commonly known as Virginia white Campion, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white, five-petaled flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has opposite, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native ecosystems.
