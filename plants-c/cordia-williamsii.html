Cordia williamsii

Cordia williamsii, commonly known as Williams' cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Cordia williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
