Centaurea microlonchoides

Centaurea microlonchoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small stars. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
