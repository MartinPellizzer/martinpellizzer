Centaurea phrygia f. subcapitata

Centaurea phrygia f. subcapitata, commonly known as the narrowhead cornflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in compact, rounded clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
