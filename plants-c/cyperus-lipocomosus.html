Cyperus lipocomosus

Cyperus lipocomosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus lipocomosus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
