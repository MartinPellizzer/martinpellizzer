Cota ormenioides

Cota ormenioides, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Cota ormenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
