Caragana wenhsienensis

Caragana wenhsienensis, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reforestation. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
