Casearia sylvestris subsp. myricoides

Casearia sylvestris subsp.

myricoides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, waxy, yellow berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
