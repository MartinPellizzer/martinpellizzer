Cephalocereus robinii

Cephalocereus robinii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
