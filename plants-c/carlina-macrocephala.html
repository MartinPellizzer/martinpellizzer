Carlina macrocephala

Carlina macrocephala, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Carlina macrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
