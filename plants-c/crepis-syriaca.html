Crepis syriaca

Crepis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Crepis syriaca is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
