Crataegus insignis

Crataegus insignis, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Crataegus insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
