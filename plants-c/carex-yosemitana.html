Carex yosemitana

Carex yosemitana, commonly known as Yosemite sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Sierra Nevada region of California.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and streambanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in the summer.

Yosemite sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
