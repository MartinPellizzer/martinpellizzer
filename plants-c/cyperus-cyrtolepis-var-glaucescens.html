Cyperus cyrtolepis var. glaucescens

Cyperus cyrtolepis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed cyperus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
