Cheiranthus decumbens

Cheiranthus decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie flat on the ground, producing small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils. It blooms from spring to early summer and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
