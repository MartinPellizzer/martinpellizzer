Cheilanthes ceterachoides

Cheilanthes ceterachoides, commonly known as the rock spurge or narrow-leaved rock fern, is a terrestrial fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and features dark green, leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

This species is well adapted to harsh conditions and is often found in limestone or sandstone soils.
