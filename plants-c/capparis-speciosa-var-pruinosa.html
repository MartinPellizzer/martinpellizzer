Capparis speciosa var. pruinosa

Capparis speciosa var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant has a shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and tropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
