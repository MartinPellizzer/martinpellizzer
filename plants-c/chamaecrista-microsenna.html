Chamaecrista microsenna

Chamaecrista microsenna, commonly known as the smallleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seeds are encased in distinctive, twisted pods that split open when mature.

Chamaecrista microsenna is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
