Corybas pictus

Corybas pictus, commonly known as the painted hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that is often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a single, showy flower that blooms from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, growing close to the ground.

Corybas pictus is a rare and endangered species, found primarily in specific habitats within the North Island of New Zealand.
