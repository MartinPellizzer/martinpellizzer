Cirsium leducii

Cirsium leducii, commonly known as the eastern thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium leducii is known for its prickly stems and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
