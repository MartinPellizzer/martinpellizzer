Cyanea recta

Cyanea recta, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Cyanea recta has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of Hawaiian flora.
