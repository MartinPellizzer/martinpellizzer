Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. daghestanicus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. daghestanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in mountainous areas and has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered with a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its berries are edible but contain small seeds, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
