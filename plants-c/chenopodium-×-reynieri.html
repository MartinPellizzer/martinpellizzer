Chenopodium × reynieri

Chenopodium × reynieri, commonly known as hybrid goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri. This plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive bluish or purplish hue. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Chenopodium × reynieri is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as a forage plant for livestock.
