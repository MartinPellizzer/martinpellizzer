Carex acrophila

Carex acrophila, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous or high-altitude regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

The flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that are characteristic of the sedge family.
