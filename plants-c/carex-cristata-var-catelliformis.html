Carex cristata var. catelliformis

Carex cristata var. catelliformis, commonly known as the catelliform bracted sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, narrow, triangular leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, emerging from a rosette of bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
