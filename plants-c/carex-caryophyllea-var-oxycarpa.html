Carex caryophyllea var. oxycarpa

Carex caryophyllea var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowering spikes that typically bloom in early summer. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
