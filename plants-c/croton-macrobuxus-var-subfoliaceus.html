Croton macrobuxus var. subfoliaceus

Croton macrobuxus var. subfoliaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
