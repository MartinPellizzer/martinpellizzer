Centaurea candida

Centaurea candida, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Centaurea candida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
