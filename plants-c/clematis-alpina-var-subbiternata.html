Clematis alpina var. subbiternata

Clematis alpina var. subbiternata, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
