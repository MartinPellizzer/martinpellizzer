Conioselinum scopulorum

Conioselinum scopulorum, commonly known as rock parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. Native to rocky and arid regions, this plant is adapted to harsh environments with poor soil. The plant's aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
