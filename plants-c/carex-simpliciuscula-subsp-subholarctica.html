Carex simpliciuscula subsp. subholarctica

Carex simpliciuscula subsp.

subholarctica, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tundra and moist meadows.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in low-nutrient environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
