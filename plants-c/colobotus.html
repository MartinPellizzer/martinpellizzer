Colobotus

Colobotus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of other plants. The plant's appearance is often mistaken for a piece of decaying vegetation due to its pale, leafless structure. It is found in moist, shaded environments across various regions of the United States.

Despite its lack of visible leaves, it plays a role in the ecological balance by influencing its host's growth and nutrient availability.
