Cytisus perezii

Cytisus perezii, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
