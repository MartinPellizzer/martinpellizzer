Crataegus oxyacantha f. villosior

Crataegus oxyacantha f. villosior, commonly known as the woolly hawthorn, is a hawthorn species characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. The flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
