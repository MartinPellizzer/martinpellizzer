Cassia spectabilis

Cassia spectabilis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that appear in late spring or early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Cassia spectabilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various pollinators.
