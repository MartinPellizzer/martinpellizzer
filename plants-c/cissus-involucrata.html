Cissus involucrata

Cissus involucrata, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, heart-shaped leaves and small, white, berry-like flowers that resemble snowballs. The plant produces clusters of bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
