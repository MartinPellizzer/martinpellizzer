Calamagrostis laguroides

Calamagrostis laguroides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis laguroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
