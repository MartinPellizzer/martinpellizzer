Cyperus fuscus var. nigricans

Cyperus fuscus var. nigricans, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark brown to black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pale brown or greenish.

Cyperus fuscus var. nigricans plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
