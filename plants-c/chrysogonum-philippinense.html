Chrysogonum philippinense

Chrysogonum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
