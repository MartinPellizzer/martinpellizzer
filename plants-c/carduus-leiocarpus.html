Carduus leiocarpus

Carduus leiocarpus, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to two meters.

The white or pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
