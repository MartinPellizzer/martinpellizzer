Carex subdita

Carex subdita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex subdita plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
