Cerasus avium f. acuminatissima

Cerasus avium f. acuminatissima, commonly known as the acutely pointed cherry, is a botanical variant of the bird cherry species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and distinctive, sharply pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.

This form is noted for its compact growth habit and is favored in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
