Calea harleyi

Calea harleyi, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are velvety and have a distinct, often fragrant aroma.

Calea harleyi is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
