Cornus mas f. andrzejowskii

Cornus mas f. andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Andrzejowski dogwood, is a botanical variant of the Cornus mas species.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that appear in late spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is native to parts of Europe and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with small white flowers followed by red berries.

This form is valued for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
