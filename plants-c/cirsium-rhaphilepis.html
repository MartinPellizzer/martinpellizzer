Cirsium rhaphilepis

Cirsium rhaphilepis, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium rhaphilepis is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
