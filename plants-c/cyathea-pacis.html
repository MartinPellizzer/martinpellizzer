Cyathea pacis

Cyathea pacis, commonly known as the Pacific tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in coastal or mountainous areas. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's upright growth.

Cyathea pacis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
