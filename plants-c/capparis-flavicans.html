Capparis flavicans

Capparis flavicans, commonly known as the yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline conditions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Capparis flavicans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
