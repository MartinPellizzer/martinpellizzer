Cerasus mahaleb var. simonkaii

Cerasus mahaleb var. simonkaii, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Simonkaii cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, dark red to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
