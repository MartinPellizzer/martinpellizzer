Campanula scheuchzeri var. glabra

Campanula scheuchzeri var. glabra, commonly known as the glabrous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous (hairless) appearance compared to other varieties of Campanula scheuchzeri.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
