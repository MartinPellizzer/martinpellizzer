Caladenia leptochila subsp. dentata

Caladenia leptochila subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia. It features a slender, hairy stem that bears a single, lance-shaped flower with a distinctive, hooded labellum.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

This subspecies is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often striking floral morphology.
