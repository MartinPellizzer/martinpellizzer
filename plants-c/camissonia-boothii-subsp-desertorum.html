Camissonia boothii subsp. desertorum

Camissonia boothii subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Camissonia boothii subsp.

desertorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
