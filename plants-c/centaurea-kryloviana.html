Centaurea kryloviana

Centaurea kryloviana, commonly known as Krylov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea kryloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
