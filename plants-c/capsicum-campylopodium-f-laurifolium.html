Capsicum campylopodium f. laurifolium

Capsicum campylopodium f. laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chili pepper, is a botanical variant of the *Capsicum campylopodium* species.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that give it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in both horticultural and taxonomic studies.
