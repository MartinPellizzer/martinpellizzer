Corvisartia indica

Corvisartia indica, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-colored inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
