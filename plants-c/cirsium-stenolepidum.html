Cirsium stenolepidum

Cirsium stenolepidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinct, waxy texture.

Cirsium stenolepidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
