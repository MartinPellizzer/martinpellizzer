Ceraia goldfinchii

Ceraia goldfinchii, commonly known as the golden bower vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are often followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a lush, climbing plant.
