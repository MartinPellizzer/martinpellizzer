Cissus stipulaceoides

Cissus stipulaceoides, commonly known as the African arrowroot or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
