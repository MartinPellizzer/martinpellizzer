Ceropegia multiflora f. puberula

Ceropegia multiflora f. puberula, commonly known as the velvet leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This succulent is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
