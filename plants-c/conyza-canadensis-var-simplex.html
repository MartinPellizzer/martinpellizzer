Conyza canadensis var. simplex

Conyza canadensis var. simplex, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also serves as a food source for various insects and birds.
