Chasmone barbata

Chasmone barbata, commonly known as the hairy chasmone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Chasmone barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
