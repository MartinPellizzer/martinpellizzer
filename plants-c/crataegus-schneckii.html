Crataegus schneckii

Crataegus schneckii, commonly known as the Schneck's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
