Carya carolinae-septentrionalis

Carya carolinae-septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus *Carya* in the family Juglandaceae, which includes other hickory and walnut species. The tree is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets and produces edible nuts encased in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick, deeply furrowed, and often has a scaly texture.
