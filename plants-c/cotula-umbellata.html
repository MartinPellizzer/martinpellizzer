Cotula umbellata

Cotula umbellata, commonly known as the star daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers arranged in a flat, umbrella-like cluster. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Cotula umbellata has hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits called achenes.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
