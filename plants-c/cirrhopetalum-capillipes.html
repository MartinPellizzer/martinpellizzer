Cirrhopetalum capillipes

Cirrhopetalum capillipes, commonly known as the feathered petal orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated petals that resemble fine feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

The botanical features of Cirrhopetalum capillipes make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
