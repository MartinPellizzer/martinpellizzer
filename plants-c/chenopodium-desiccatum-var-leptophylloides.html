Chenopodium desiccatum var. leptophylloides

Chenopodium desiccatum var. leptophylloides, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to deltate in shape, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, smooth shell. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
