Crataegus quitensis

Crataegus quitensis, commonly known as the Andean hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Crataegus quitensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various birds and small mammals.
