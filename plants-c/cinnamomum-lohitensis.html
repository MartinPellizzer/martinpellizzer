Cinnamomum lohitensis

Cinnamomum lohitensis, commonly known as the Lohit cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has limited commercial cultivation due to its specific habitat requirements.
