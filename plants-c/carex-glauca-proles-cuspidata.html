Carex glauca proles cuspidata

Carex glauca proles cuspidata, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
