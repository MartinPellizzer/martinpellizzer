Chaenactis stevioides

Chaenactis stevioides, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in mountainous areas.
