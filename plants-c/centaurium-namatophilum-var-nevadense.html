Centaurium namatophilum var. nevadense

Centaurium namatophilum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its small, bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks at high elevations. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and adaptations to alpine environments.
