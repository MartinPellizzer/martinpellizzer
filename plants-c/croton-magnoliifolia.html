Croton magnoliifolia

Croton magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that resemble those of magnolia species, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
