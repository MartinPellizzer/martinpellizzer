Calyptranthes malabarica

Calyptranthes malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a pleasant aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the rainy season.

Calyptranthes malabarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
