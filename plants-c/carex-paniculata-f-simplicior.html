Carex paniculata f. simplicior

Carex paniculata f. simplicior, commonly known as the simple panicled sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on slender spikes. This variety is notable for its simplified inflorescence compared to the typical form of Carex paniculata. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
