Crepis foetida subsp. vulgaris

Crepis foetida subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as stinkcup, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, spiky, yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, grass-like leaves and produces achene fruits enclosed in a pappus. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its strong, unpleasant odor is a notable feature, especially when the plant is crushed.
