Crataegus cordifolia var. borealis

Crataegus cordifolia var. borealis, commonly known as the northern hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall. This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
