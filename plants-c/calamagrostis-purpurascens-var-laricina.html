Calamagrostis purpurascens var. laricina

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. laricina, commonly known as the bluejoint reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often display a bluish-green hue. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
