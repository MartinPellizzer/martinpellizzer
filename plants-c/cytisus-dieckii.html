Cytisus dieckii

Cytisus dieckii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre broom, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Cytisus dieckii is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
