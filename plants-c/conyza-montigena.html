Conyza montigena

Conyza montigena, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza montigena is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
