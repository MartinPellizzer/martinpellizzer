Cyperus multifolius

Cyperus multifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus multifolius thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
