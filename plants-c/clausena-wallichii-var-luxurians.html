Clausena wallichii var. luxurians

Clausena wallichii var. luxurians, commonly known as the glossy tamarind or Indian tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
