Croton rhamnifolius var. boliviensis

Croton rhamnifolius var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian croton, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to yellow, red, or even purple. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking foliage. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
