Collania miniata

Collania miniata, commonly known as the red-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of tiny flowers that attract pollinators.

Collania miniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful display.
