Cistus × glaucus

Cistus × glaucus, commonly known as the grey rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cistus × glaucus is often used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
