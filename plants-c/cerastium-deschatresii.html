Cerastium deschatresii

Cerastium deschatresii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of soft, white pubescence.

Cerastium deschatresii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
