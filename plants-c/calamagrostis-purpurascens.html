Calamagrostis purpurascens

Calamagrostis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking purple-tinted foliage that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, feathery flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
