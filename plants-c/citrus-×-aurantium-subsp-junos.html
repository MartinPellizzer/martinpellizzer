Citrus × aurantium subsp. junos

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

junos, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or sudachi, is a citrus species native to Japan. It is a small evergreen tree with fragrant, glossy green leaves and oval, yellow-orange fruits.

The fruit has a thin, loose rind and a segmented, juicy pulp that is highly aromatic. It is often used in culinary applications, particularly in Japanese cuisine, and is also valued for its essential oils.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
