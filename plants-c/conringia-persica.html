Conringia persica

Conringia persica, commonly known as Persian rocket, is an annual flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with a peppery aroma. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a spicy flavor similar to mustard.

Conringia persica thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
