Clavimyrtus lineata var. varingifolia

Clavimyrtus lineata var. varingifolia, commonly known as the variable coralwood, is a unique shrub native to the humid forests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a humid, subtropical climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
