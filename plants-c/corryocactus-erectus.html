Corryocactus erectus

Corryocactus erectus, commonly known as the upright corryocactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a common adaptation for water conservation in arid environments.

Corryocactus erectus is well-suited to full sun and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
