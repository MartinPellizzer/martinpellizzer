Commelina rorigera

Commelina rorigera, commonly known as the small blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of grass-like plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
