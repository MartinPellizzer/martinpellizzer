Coriaria sinica

Coriaria sinica, commonly known as Chinese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Coriaria sinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
