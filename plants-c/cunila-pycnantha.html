Cunila pycnantha

Cunila pycnantha, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and fern-like, pinnately compound leaves that emit a distinctive licorice-like fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which give it the species name "pycnantha," meaning "dense fruit.". Its flowers are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk.

Cunila pycnantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
