Camellia rhytidophylla

Camellia rhytidophylla, commonly known as the wrinkled-leaf camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive wrinkled texture. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Camellia rhytidophylla prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
