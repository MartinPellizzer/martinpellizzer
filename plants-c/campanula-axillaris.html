Campanula axillaris

Campanula axillaris, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula axillaris is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
