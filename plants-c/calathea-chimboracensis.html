Calathea chimboracensis

Calathea chimboracensis, commonly known as the Chimborazo calathea, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and intricate leaf patterns, which often feature a combination of green, white, and pink hues. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture in its native habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
