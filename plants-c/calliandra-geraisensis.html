Calliandra geraisensis

Calliandra geraisensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
