Calamagrostis neglecta var. borealis

Calamagrostis neglecta var. borealis, commonly known as northern bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with silver-white markings. This variety thrives in wet or moist soils, typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets that mature to a purplish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
