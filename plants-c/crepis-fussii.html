Crepis fussii

Crepis fussii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has slender stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

Crepis fussii is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.
