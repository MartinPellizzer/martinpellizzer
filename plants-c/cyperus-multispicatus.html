Cyperus multispicatus

Cyperus multispicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedge plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus multispicatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
