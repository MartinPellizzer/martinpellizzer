Carphobolus lucidus

Carphobolus lucidus, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carphobolus lucidus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
