Cracca acaciifolia

Cracca acaciifolia, commonly known as the acacia-leaf cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which resemble those of acacia species, and its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in savanna and thicket habitats, where it contributes to the local flora.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
