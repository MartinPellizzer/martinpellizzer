Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp. rhodesicum

Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp.

rhodesicum, commonly known as the Rhodes milkvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
