Calamintha suavis

Calamintha suavis, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal remedies.

Calamintha suavis is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
