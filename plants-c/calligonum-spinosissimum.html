Calligonum spinosissimum

Calligonum spinosissimum, commonly known as the spiny calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Calligonum spinosissimum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing desertification in its native habitat.
