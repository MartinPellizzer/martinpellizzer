Casalea hederacea

Casalea hederacea, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas, such as along stream banks or in woodland undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common and hardy ground cover in humid climates.
