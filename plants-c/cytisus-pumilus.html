Cytisus pumilus

Cytisus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Cytisus pumilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
