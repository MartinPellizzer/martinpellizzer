Centaurium erythraea subsp. austriacum

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian green gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its bright green to yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and mountainous regions, preferring cool and temperate climates. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is less commonly cultivated than its more colorful relatives but is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
