Cordyline banksii f. erythrorrhachis

Cordyline banksii f. erythrorrhachis, commonly known as the red-stemmed cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, arching leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge along the midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both garden beds and containers.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
