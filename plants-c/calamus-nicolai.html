Calamus nicolai

Calamus nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolo's canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. Calamus nicolai produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of hard, fibrous fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
