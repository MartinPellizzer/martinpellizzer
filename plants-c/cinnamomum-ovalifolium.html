Cinnamomum ovalifolium

Cinnamomum ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its contribution to the cinnamon spice trade and its ecological role in tropical forests.
