Cassebeeria angustata

Cassebeeria angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
