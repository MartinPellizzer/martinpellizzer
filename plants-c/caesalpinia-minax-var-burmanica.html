Caesalpinia minax var. burmanica

Caesalpinia minax var. burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese crown flower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves and bright yellow to orange-red inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
