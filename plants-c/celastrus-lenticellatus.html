Celastrus lenticellatus

Celastrus lenticellatus, commonly known as the smooth twiner, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The bark and leaves of Celastrus lenticellatus have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
