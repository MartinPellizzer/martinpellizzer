Cucumis sativus var. hardwickii

Cucumis sativus var. hardwickii, commonly known as the Hardwick cucumber, is a cultivar of the cucumber plant native to India.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vining growth habit, requiring support for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and ovate, serrated leaves.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often used in pickling and fresh consumption.
