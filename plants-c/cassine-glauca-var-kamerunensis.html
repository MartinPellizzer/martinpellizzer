Cassine glauca var. kamerunensis

Cassine glauca var. kamerunensis, commonly known as the Kamerun cassine, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
