Citrus × longispina

Citrus × longispina, commonly known as the longspine lime, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between the citron (Citrus medica) and the pomelo (Citrus maxima).

It is characterized by its spiny, rough-textured leaves and small, sour-green fruits that are typically used for culinary purposes. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued for its ornamental features and its fruit, which contains high levels of citric acid and is often used in traditional medicine.
