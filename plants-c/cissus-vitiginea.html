Cissus vitiginea

Cissus vitiginea, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to trail or climb surfaces, making it popular in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus vitiginea is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various light conditions.
