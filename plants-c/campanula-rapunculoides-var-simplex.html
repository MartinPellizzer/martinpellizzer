Campanula rapunculoides var. simplex

Campanula rapunculoides var. simplex, commonly known as the simple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
