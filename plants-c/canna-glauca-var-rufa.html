Canna glauca var. rufa

Canna glauca var. rufa, commonly known as the red-leaf canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a reddish tinge, especially on the underside. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with bright yellow petals and red stamens, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to add color and texture to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
