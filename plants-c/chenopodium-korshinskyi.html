Chenopodium korshinskyi

Chenopodium korshinskyi, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in low-nutrient conditions.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used as a leafy vegetable in local cuisines.
