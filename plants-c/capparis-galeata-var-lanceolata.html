Capparis galeata var. lanceolata

Capparis galeata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect stems covered in thorny branches and possesses lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, acidic fruits resembling small green olives. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, rocky environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
