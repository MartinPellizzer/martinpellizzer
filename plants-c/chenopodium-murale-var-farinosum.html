Chenopodium murale var. farinosum

Chenopodium murale var. farinosum, commonly known as Bluntleaf Goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are typically branching and somewhat prostrate. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in wastelands, roadsides, and along fences.

The species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
