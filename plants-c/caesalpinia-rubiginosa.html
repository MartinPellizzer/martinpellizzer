Caesalpinia rubiginosa

Caesalpinia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red torchwood or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, dark seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its striking appearance and seasonal flowering make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
