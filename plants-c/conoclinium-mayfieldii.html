Conoclinium mayfieldii

Conoclinium mayfieldii, commonly known as Mayfield's blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native ornamental plants.

Conoclinium mayfieldii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
