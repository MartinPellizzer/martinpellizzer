Clematis dioica subvar. nutans

Clematis dioica subvar. nutans, commonly known as the nodding clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The flowers are usually white or pale blue and have a distinct, nodding appearance. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
