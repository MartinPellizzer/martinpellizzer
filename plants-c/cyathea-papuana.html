Cyathea papuana

Cyathea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and storing nutrients.

Cyathea papuana plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and shelter to various species of wildlife.
