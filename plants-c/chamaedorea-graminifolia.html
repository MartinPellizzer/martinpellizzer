Chamaedorea graminifolia

Chamaedorea graminifolia, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It features slender, upright stems that resemble bamboo, often growing in dense clusters. The plant has long, narrow, and leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette pattern. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Chamaedorea graminifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and elegant appearance.
