Cyathea dissimilis

Cyathea dissimilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome that supports its growth and allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in structure and appearance.

Cyathea dissimilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
