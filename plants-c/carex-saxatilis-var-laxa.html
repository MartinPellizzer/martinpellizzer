Carex saxatilis var. laxa

Carex saxatilis var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or rocky habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
