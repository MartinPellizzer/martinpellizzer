Carex magellanica var. irrigua

Carex magellanica var. irrigua, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the sedge family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
