Croton integrifolius

Croton integrifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds.

Croton integrifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.
