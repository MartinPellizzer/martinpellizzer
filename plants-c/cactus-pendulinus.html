Cactus pendulinus

Cactus pendulinus, commonly known as the pendant cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like stems that hang downward, often reaching lengths of several feet. The plant features spiny areoles and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cactus pendulinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
