Cryptocoryne regina

Cryptocoryne regina, commonly known as the royal cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking green or reddish color. The plant thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions and prefers soft, slightly acidic water. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense underwater foliage, making it a popular choice for aquariums.

Cryptocoryne regina is also valued for its ability to help maintain water clarity and provide habitat for aquatic life.
