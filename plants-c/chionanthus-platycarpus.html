Chionanthus platycarpus

Chionanthus platycarpus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
