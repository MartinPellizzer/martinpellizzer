Curcuma prakasha

Curcuma prakasha, commonly known as the golden ginger or sacred turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Indonesia. The plant features rhizomes that are rich in curcumin, a compound known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

Curcuma prakasha is often cultivated for both ornamental and traditional medicinal purposes.
