Crotalaria prostrata var. jinpingensis

Crotalaria prostrata var. jinpingensis, commonly known as the Jinping false flax, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
