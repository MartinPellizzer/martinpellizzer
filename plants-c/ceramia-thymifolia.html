Ceramia thymifolia

Ceramia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved ceramia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ceramia thymifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
