Cachrys ambigua

Cachrys ambigua, commonly known as the broadleaf cowbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a stout, unbranched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, with three sepals and no petals.
