Cristaria loasifolia

Cristaria loasifolia, commonly known as the crested beauty, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often colorful bracts that form a crown-like structure around the flower head. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Cristaria loasifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
