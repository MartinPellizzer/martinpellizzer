Caladenia gemmata

Caladenia gemmata, commonly known as the Gemmata Spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southwestern Western Australia.

It typically grows in heathland and sandplain environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears a single, intricate flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a narrow, elongated labellum.

Caladenia gemmata is a protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the threats it faces in its natural habitat.
