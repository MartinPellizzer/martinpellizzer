Cynanchum tylophoroides

Cynanchum tylophoroides, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health purposes.
