Codonocalyx montevidensis

Codonocalyx montevidensis, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns resembling a heart, which are typical of the Passifloraceae family. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and showy floral structures with both stamens and carpels.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
