Chionanthus virginicus var. maritimus

Chionanthus virginicus var. maritimus, commonly known as the coastal chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils along the coastlines. The plant produces fragrant, white, pendant flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in dune habitats.

This variety is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
