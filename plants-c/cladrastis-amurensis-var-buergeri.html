Cladrastis amurensis var. buergeri

Cladrastis amurensis var. buergeri, commonly known as the Amur Fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural landscapes.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively slow growth rate.
