Cirsium quercetorum var. citrinum

Cirsium quercetorum var. citrinum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
