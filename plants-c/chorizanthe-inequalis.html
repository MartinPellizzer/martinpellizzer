Chorizanthe inequalis

Chorizanthe inequalis, commonly known as the unequal choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces seeds in small capsules, which are dispersed by wind.

Chorizanthe inequalis is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
