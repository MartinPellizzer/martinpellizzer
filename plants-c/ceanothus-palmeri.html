Ceanothus palmeri

Ceanothus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.

Ceanothus palmeri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
