Cirsium edule var. edule

Cirsium edule var. edule, commonly known as the edible thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its edible parts include the young leaves, which are tender and less bitter when cooked.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
