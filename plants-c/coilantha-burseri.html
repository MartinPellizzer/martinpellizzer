Coilantha burseri

Coilantha burseri, commonly known as the African sand dollar tree, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elongated leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small yellow flowers followed by fruiting bodies that resemble sand dollars, giving it its common name.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.
