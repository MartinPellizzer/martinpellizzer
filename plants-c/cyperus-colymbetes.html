Cyperus colymbetes

Cyperus colymbetes, commonly known as the floating flatsedge, is a wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and is often found in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in environments with standing water, making it a common sight in marshes and ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing wetland soils.
