Calcearia fornicata

Calcearia fornicata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble small bells, typically ranging in color from white to pale blue. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Calcearia fornicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
