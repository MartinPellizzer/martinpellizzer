Cryptospora omissa

Cryptospora omissa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying entirely on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by specific insects. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of botanical interest and conservation concern.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is listed as endangered in several states.
