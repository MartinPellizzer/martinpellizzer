Cattleya trianae var. hillii

Cattleya trianae var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and sometimes include a mix of colors. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
