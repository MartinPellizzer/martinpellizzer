Carex caulorrhiza var. kirigaminensis

Carex caulorrhiza var. kirigaminensis, commonly known as the Kirigamine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish, arranged in spikelets.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.
