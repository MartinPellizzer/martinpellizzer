Camissonia scapoidea subsp. utahensis

Camissonia scapoidea subsp.

utahensis, commonly known as the Utah filaree, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to June. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
