Croton repandus

Croton repandus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Croton repandus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
