Cryptocarya kavaiensis

Cryptocarya kavaiensis, commonly known as the Kava kava tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It produces drupes that contain a single seed, and it is often found in coastal or lowland forest habitats.

The plant has cultural and medicinal significance in some Pacific Island communities.
