Cucurbita pepo var. draconina

Cucurbita pepo var. draconina, commonly known as the Mexican tiger squash, is a variant of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped, and often spiky exterior, which resembles a dragon's skin, hence its name. The plant is a vine that produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, edible rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in traditional cuisines.
