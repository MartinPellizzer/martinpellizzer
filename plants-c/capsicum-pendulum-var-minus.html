Capsicum pendulum var. minus

Capsicum pendulum var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf chili pepper, is a compact variety of the bird's eye chili plant.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and produces small, fiery red or orange fruits. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild to hot flavor in culinary applications.
