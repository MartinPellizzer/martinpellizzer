Chimaphila maculata var. dasystemma

Chimaphila maculata var. dasystemma, commonly known as the spotted pipsissewa, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its preference for acidic, well-drained soils. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
