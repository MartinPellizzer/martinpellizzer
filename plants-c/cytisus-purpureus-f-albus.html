Cytisus purpureus f. albus

Cytisus purpureus f. albus, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a cultivar of the broom plant characterized by its striking white flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, compound leaves and showy, clustered blooms.

Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
