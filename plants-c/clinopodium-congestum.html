Clinopodium congestum

Clinopodium congestum, commonly known as autumn calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Clinopodium congestum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
