Campanula stevenii

Campanula stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy layer, giving it a soft texture.

Campanula stevenii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
