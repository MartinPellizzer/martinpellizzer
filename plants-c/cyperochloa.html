Cyperochloa

Cyperochloa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. These plants typically grow in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperochloa species are often found in temperate regions and are valued for their ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Their structure and growth habits make them important components of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
