Centranthus macrosiphon var. andalusii

Centranthus macrosiphon var. andalusii, commonly known as the Andalusian red campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
