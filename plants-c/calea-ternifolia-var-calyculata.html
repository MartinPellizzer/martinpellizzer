Calea ternifolia var. calyculata

Calea ternifolia var. calyculata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small, intricate flowers, often appearing at night. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

Calea ternifolia var. calyculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
