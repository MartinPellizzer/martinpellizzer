Coix lacryma-jobi var. major

Coix lacryma-jobi var. major, commonly known as Chinese Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like clusters of pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, tear-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including starch, protein, and minerals, and have been utilized for centuries in Chinese herbal practices.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both agricultural and medicinal contexts.
