Crocus chrysanthus f. lilacinus

Crocus chrysanthus f. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac crocus, is a distinctive hybrid within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms with a central stamen and three petals. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
