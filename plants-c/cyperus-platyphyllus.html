Cyperus platyphyllus

Cyperus platyphyllus, commonly known as broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus platyphyllus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
