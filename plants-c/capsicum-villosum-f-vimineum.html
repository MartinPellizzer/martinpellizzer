Capsicum villosum f. vimineum

Capsicum villosum f. vimineum, commonly known as the hairy pepper or creeping pepper, is a unique variant of the pepper plant characterized by its trailing growth habit.

It features small, elongated fruits that range in color from green to red, depending on maturity. The plant is notable for its dense, fine hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

This variety is relatively frost-sensitive and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
