Calliandra bahiana var. erythematosa

Calliandra bahiana var. erythematosa, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
