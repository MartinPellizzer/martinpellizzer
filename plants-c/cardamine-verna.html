Cardamine verna

Cardamine verna, commonly known as spring celandine, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cardamine verna is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, humid climates.
