Capsella bursa-pastoris albertina

Capsella bursa-pastoris albertina, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive heart-shaped seed pods. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
