Cirsium japonicum f. arakii

Cirsium japonicum f. arakii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle or Arakian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, glandular-stemmed structure and large, purple to pinkish-blue composite flowers. The plant features serrated, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Cirsium genus, particularly in its ornamental and ecological roles.
