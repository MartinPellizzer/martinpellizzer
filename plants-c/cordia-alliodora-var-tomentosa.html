Cordia alliodora var. tomentosa

Cordia alliodora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silky cordia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
