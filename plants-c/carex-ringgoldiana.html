Carex ringgoldiana

Carex ringgoldiana, commonly known as Ringgold sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex ringgoldiana is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
