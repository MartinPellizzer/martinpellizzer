Capsella bursa-pastoris proles humilis

Capsella bursa-pastoris proles humilis, commonly known as the common groundsel, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive heart-shaped, papery seed pods. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
