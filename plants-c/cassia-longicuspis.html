Cassia longicuspis

Cassia longicuspis, commonly known as the long-fruited cassia, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Cassia longicuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
