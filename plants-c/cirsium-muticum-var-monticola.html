Cirsium muticum var. monticola

Cirsium muticum var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is often found in open habitats like meadows and slopes at high elevations.
