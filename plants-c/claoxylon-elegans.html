Claoxylon elegans

Claoxylon elegans, commonly known as the elegant claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
