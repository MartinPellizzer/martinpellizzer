Campanula sibirica var. caucasica

Campanula sibirica var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
