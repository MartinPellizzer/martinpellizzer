Calliandra alba

Calliandra alba, commonly known as white calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates. It also has some medicinal uses and is known to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.

Calliandra alba is well-suited for gardens and landscapes in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.
