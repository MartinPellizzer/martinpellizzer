Colysis saxicola

Colysis saxicola, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or desert regions.

Colysis saxicola is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
