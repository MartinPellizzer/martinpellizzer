Chusua roborovskii

Chusua roborovskii, commonly known as the Roborovskii saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
