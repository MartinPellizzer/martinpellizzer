Cytisus tinctorius

Cytisus tinctorius, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, often thorny stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus tinctorius is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
