Cyperus schraderianus

Cyperus schraderianus, commonly known as the Schrader's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant's morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
