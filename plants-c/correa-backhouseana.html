Correa backhouseana

Correa backhouseana, commonly known as the Christmas Correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces striking clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and seasonal blooming, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
