Combretum anfractuosum var. macrostachyum

Combretum anfractuosum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the broad-stalked combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
