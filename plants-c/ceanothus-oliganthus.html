Ceanothus oliganthus

Ceanothus oliganthus, commonly known as the silverleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland regions. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
