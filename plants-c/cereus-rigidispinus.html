Cereus rigidispinus

Cereus rigidispinus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are usually short-lived, lasting only a single night.

Cereus rigidispinus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
