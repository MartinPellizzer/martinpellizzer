Carex rubra

Carex rubra, commonly known as redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Carex rubra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
