Callista acrobatica

Callista acrobatica, commonly known as the climbing star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Callista acrobatica is also valued for its ability to climb trellises or other structures, making it a popular choice for vertical gardens.
