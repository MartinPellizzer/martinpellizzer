Cola boxiana

Cola boxiana, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by hard, woody fruits containing elliptical seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild stimulant effect.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
