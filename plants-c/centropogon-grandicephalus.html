Centropogon grandicephalus

Centropogon grandicephalus, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Centropogon grandicephalus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
