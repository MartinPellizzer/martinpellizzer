Cryptomeria japonica var. araucarioides

Cryptomeria japonica var. araucarioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Australian cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its distinctive, narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape.

It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber quality. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters in its natural habitat. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain two to three seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
