Corispermum americanum var. americanum

Corispermum americanum var. americanum, commonly known as the American sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
