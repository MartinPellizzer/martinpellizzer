Cirsium odoratum

Cirsium odoratum, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, rough, and hairy stems, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink, red, or white, and they bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium odoratum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in floral arrangements.
