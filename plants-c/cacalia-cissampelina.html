Cacalia cissampelina

Cacalia cissampelina, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative ground cover.
