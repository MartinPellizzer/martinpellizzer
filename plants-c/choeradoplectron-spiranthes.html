Choeradoplectron spiranthes

Choeradoplectron spiranthes, commonly known as the spiral spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of flowers along a single, wiry stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small, greenish-white with a subtle fragrance.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for various pollinators.
