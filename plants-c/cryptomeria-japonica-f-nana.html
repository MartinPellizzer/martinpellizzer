Cryptomeria japonica f. nana

Cryptomeria japonica f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cedar, is a compact, slow-growing variant of the Japanese cedar.

It features a dense, pyramidal shape with short, stiff needles that are a vivid green. This form is naturally occurring and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal pruning.

The plant is resistant to most pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for horticultural use.
