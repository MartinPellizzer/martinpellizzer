Clinopodium abyssinicum var. abyssinicum

Clinopodium abyssinicum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane grasslands and forests. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for their pungent aroma and medicinal properties.

Clinopodium abyssinicum var. abyssinicum is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
