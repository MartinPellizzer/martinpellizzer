Chamaecrista pinetorum

Chamaecrista pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and pine flatwoods. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for erosion control and landscaping purposes.
