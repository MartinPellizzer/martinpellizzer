Calceolaria palustris subsp. megacarpa

Calceolaria palustris subsp.

megacarpa, commonly known as the swamp slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
