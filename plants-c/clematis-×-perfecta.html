Clematis × perfecta

Clematis × perfecta, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can climb and twine around supports. It blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

The botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from different Clematis genera.
