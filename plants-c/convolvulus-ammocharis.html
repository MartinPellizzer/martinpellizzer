Convolvulus ammocharis

Convolvulus ammocharis, commonly known as the Canary Island morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
