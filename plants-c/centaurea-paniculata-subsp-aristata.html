Centaurea paniculata subsp. aristata

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the aristate globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which are surrounded by bracts.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is known for its showy inflorescences, which can reach up to 20 cm in diameter.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
