Crataegus pruinosa var. philadelphica

Crataegus pruinosa var. philadelphica, commonly known as the Philadelphica hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
