Chenopodium foetidum subsp. tibetanum

Chenopodium foetidum subsp.

tibetanum, commonly known as Tibetan goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sometimes pubescent surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

It is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often aromatic foliage.
