Campanula rotundifolia var. lancifolia

Campanula rotundifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
