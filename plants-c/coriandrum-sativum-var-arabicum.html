Coriandrum sativum var. arabicum

Coriandrum sativum var. arabicum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, dry fruits containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in cuisines around the world for their warm, earthy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
