Coelogyne clemensii var. angustifolia

Coelogyne clemensii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clemens coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that often display a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence is usually a single, upright spike bearing several flowers, which bloom during the rainy season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
