Crataegus wattiana

Crataegus wattiana, commonly known as the Watt's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
