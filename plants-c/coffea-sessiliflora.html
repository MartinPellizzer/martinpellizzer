Coffea sessiliflora

Coffea sessiliflora, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee tree, is a species native to Ethiopia and grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, small elliptical leaves, and white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial shade, making it a valuable crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
