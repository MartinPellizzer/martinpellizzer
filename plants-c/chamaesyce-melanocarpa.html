Chamaesyce melanocarpa

Chamaesyce melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with a dark color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife.
