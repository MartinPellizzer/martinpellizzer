Campanula amana

Campanula amana, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanula amana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
