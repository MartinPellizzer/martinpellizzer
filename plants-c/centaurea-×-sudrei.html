Centaurea × sudrei

Centaurea × sudrei, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the daisy family.

Centaurea × sudrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
