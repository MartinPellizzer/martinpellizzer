Cinnamomum tenuipile

Cinnamomum tenuipile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cinnamon species like Cinnamomum verum.
