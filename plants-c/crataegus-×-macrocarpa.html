Crataegus × macrocarpa

Crataegus × macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces large, red to orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils across eastern North America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
