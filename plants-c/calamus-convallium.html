Calamus convallium

Calamus convallium, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, containing compounds that have been historically used for their medicinal properties.

Calamus convallium thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and in swampy regions.
