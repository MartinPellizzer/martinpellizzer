Ceropegia clavigera subsp. ingeae

Ceropegia clavigera subsp.

ingeae, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a waxy, velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
