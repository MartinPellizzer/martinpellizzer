Cyperus imbricatus

Cyperus imbricatus, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in two rows. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or purple in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus imbricatus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate both standing water and periodic flooding.
