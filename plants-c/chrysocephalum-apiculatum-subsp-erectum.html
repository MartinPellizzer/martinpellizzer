Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. erectum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

erectum, commonly known as the upright everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that are surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets, creating a striking visual effect. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is particularly noted for its resilience and ability to withstand dry conditions.
