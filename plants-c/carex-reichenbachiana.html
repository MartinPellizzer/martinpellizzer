Carex reichenbachiana

Carex reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, three-sided spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex reichenbachiana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
