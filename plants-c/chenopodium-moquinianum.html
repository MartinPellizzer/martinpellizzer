Chenopodium moquinianum

Chenopodium moquinianum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
