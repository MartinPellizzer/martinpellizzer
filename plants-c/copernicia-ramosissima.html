Copernicia ramosissima

Copernicia ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or copernicia palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Copernicia ramosissima is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
