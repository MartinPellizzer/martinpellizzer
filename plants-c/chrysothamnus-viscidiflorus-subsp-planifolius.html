Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus subsp. planifolius

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus subsp.

planifolius, commonly known as the flatleaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically flat and lance-shaped.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
