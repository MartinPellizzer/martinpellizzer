Cucurbita pomiformis

Cucurbita pomiformis, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, smooth, bottle-shaped fruits. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it flowers with both male and female blossoms.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature for culinary use or when mature for ornamental purposes.
