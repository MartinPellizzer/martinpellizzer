Chamaepeuce firma

Chamaepeuce firma, commonly known as the common yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
