Capparis securidacea

Capparis securidacea, commonly known as the sour fig or mountain sour fig, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, sprawling shrub structure with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically sour in taste. Capparis securidacea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
