Cestrum racemosum

Cestrum racemosum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant nocturnal fragrance.
