Camellia japonica f. kagamontana

Camellia japonica f. kagamontana, commonly known as the Japanese camellia variegata, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves with a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive double petal structure. It is native to Japan and thrives in humid, partially shaded environments.

Camellia japonica f. kagamontana is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
