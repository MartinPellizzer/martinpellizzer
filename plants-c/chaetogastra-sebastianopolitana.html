Chaetogastra sebastianopolitana

Chaetogastra sebastianopolitana, commonly known as the Sebastianopolis thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
