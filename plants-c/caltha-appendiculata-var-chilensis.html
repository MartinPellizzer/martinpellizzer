Caltha appendiculata var. chilensis

Caltha appendiculata var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
