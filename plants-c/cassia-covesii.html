Cassia covesii

Cassia covesii, commonly known as the Mexican cassia, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Cassia covesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
