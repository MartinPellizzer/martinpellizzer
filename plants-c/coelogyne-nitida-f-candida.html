Coelogyne nitida f. candida

Coelogyne nitida f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and arching, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a unique, spurred lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

This orchid is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
