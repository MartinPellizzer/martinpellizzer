Calypso madagascariensis

Calypso madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
