Clematis parvifolia

Clematis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of several meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a finely toothed margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in early summer.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
