Calathea chantrieri

Calathea chantrieri, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical foliage plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often feature intricate patterns and variegation. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and a waxy surface. It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The plant's unique leaf movements, where the leaves fold shut at night, add to its ornamental appeal.
