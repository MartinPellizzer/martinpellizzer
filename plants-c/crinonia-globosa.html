Crinonia globosa

Crinonia globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a globe, surrounded by spiky, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crinonia globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
