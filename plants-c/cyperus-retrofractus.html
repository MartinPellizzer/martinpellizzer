Cyperus retrofractus

Cyperus retrofractus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetland environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish tinge. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus retrofractus is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments.
