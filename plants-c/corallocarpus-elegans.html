Corallocarpus elegans

Corallocarpus elegans, commonly known as the elegant coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking coral-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
