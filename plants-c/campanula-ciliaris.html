Campanula ciliaris

Campanula ciliaris, commonly known as the bellflower or hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have hairy surfaces, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula ciliaris is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
