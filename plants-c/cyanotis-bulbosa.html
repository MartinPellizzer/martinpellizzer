Cyanotis bulbosa

Cyanotis bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a bulbous root system that allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Cyanotis bulbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
