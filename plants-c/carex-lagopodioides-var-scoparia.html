Carex lagopodioides var. scoparia

Carex lagopodioides var. scoparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
