Camphoromyrtus pluriflora

Camphoromyrtus pluriflora, commonly known as the broadleaf camphor tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrance and durability, making it a significant resource in traditional and modern industries.

Camphoromyrtus pluriflora also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
