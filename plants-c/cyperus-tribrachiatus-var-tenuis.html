Cyperus tribrachiatus var. tenuis

Cyperus tribrachiatus var. tenuis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often difficult to distinguish from other sedges. Its reproductive structures consist of spikelets with three to six flower spikes, which are wind-pollinated.

This variant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological adaptability and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
