Cestrum campanulatum

Cestrum campanulatum, commonly known as the white bellflower or night bloomer, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, elliptical seeds.
