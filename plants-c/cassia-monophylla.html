Cassia monophylla

Cassia monophylla, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous racemes of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, glossy leaflets. The plant produces small, triangular pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
