Clomenocoma porophylloides

Clomenocoma porophylloides, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
