Cynanchum duclouxii

Cynanchum duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
