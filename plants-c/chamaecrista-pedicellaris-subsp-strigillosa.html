Chamaecrista pedicellaris subsp. strigillosa

Chamaecrista pedicellaris subsp.

strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
