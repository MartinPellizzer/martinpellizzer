Carex tunimanensis

Carex tunimanensis, commonly known as the Tunimanensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex tunimanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
