Cardamine runcinata

Cardamine runcinata, commonly known as narrow-leaved watercress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are elongated and have a narrow, ribbon-like shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters.

Cardamine runcinata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to other watercress species.
