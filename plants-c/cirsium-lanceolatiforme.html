Cirsium lanceolatiforme

Cirsium lanceolatiforme, commonly known as the lanceolate thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its sharply lobed, lance-shaped leaves and spiny, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
