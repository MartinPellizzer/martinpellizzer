Cattleya × porphyritis

Cattleya × porphyritis, commonly known as the Porphyritic Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to purple, with intricate patterns and a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant features fleshy, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
