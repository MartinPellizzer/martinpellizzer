Calophyllum aureobrunnescens

Calophyllum aureobrunnescens, commonly known as the golden-brown calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow to golden-brown bark that becomes darker with age. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright orange or red fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
