Cyperus spectabilis

Cyperus spectabilis, commonly known as the purple gallows grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, often with purple or reddish bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal flowering.
