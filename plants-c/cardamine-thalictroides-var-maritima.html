Cardamine thalictroides var. maritima

Cardamine thalictroides var. maritima, commonly known as sea buttercup, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and dunes. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually divided into three parts. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize coastal soils.

This variety is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy plant in harsh maritime environments.
