Circaea racemosa var. lutetiana

Circaea racemosa var. lutetiana, commonly known as the European white bead, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and slightly hairy, with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
