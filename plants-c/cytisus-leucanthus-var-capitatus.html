Cytisus leucanthus var. capitatus

Cytisus leucanthus var. capitatus, commonly known as the yellow scurvygrass, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
