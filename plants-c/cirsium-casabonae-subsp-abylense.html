Cirsium casabonae subsp. abylense

Cirsium casabonae subsp.

abylense, commonly known as the Algerian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its floral structure consists of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Compositae family.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
