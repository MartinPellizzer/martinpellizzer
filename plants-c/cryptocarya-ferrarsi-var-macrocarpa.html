Cryptocarya ferrarsi var. macrocarpa

Cryptocarya ferrarsi var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and unique floral structures.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical berries. Its fruit is notable for its large size and fleshy texture, which attracts various bird species.

This variant is distinguished by its enlarged fruits compared to the typical form of Cryptocarya ferrarsi.
