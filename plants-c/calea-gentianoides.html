Calea gentianoides

Calea gentianoides, commonly known as the "Mexican ghost flower," is a striking perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and grows up to 1 meter tall, often in shaded, moist environments. Its unique nocturnal blooming habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Calea gentianoides is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
