Clinanthus luteus

Clinanthus luteus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Clinanthus luteus has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
