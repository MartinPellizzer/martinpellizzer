Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum var. fasciculosum

Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum var. fasciculosum, commonly known as the clustered goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in dense clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
