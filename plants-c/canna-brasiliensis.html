Canna brasiliensis

Canna brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian canna, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Canna brasiliensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to adverse conditions.
