Cynanchum sarcostemma

Cynanchum sarcostemma, commonly known as the heartleaf milkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals.

Cynanchum sarcostemma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
