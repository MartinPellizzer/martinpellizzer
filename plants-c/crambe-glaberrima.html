Crambe glaberrima

Crambe glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth crambe, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces yellow flowers in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The smooth crambe is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
