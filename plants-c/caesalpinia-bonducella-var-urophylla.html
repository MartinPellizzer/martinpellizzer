Caesalpinia bonducella var. urophylla

Caesalpinia bonducella var. urophylla, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking floral display.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
