Centropogon willdenowianus subsp. cylindricus

Centropogon willdenowianus subsp.

cylindricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a terminal spike, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
