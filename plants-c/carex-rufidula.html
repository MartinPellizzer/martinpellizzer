Carex rufidula

Carex rufidula, commonly known as red-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green with a reddish tinge on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Carex rufidula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
