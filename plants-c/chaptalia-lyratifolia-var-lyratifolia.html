Chaptalia lyratifolia var. lyratifolia

Chaptalia lyratifolia var. lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyratifolia chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinct lyre-shaped base, giving it its species name.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators.
