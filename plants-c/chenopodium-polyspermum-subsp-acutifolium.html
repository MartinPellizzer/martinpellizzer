Chenopodium polyspermum subsp. acutifolium

Chenopodium polyspermum subsp.

acutifolium, commonly known as sharpleaf goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to harsh conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
