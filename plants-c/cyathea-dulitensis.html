Cyathea dulitensis

Cyathea dulitensis, commonly known as the Dulitens Island tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the island of Dulitens in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth pattern, with a central trunk covered in a mass of old fronds that form a dense, fibrous sheath. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its striking appearance.

Cyathea dulitensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
