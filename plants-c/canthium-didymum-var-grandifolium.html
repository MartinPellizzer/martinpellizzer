Canthium didymum var. grandifolium

Canthium didymum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved spurge, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, somewhat unpleasant odor.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
