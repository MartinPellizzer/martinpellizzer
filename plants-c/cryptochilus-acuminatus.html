Cryptochilus acuminatus

Cryptochilus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender leaching orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
