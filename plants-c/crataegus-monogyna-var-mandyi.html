Crataegus monogyna var. mandyi

Crataegus monogyna var. mandyi, commonly known as the Common Hawthorn or its variant Mandys Hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that ripen in autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
