Cyclostemon cuspidatus

Cyclostemon cuspidatus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing plant in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
