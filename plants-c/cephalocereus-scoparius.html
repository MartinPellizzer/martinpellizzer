Cephalocereus scoparius

Cephalocereus scoparius, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink spines and a short, swollen stem that resembles a head. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
