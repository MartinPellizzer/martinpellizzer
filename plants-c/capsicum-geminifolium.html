Capsicum geminifolium

Capsicum geminifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaf chili, is a species of Capsicum native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its spicy fruits.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and culinary applications.
