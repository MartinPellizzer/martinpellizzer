Campanula nuristanica

Campanula nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Campanula nuristanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
