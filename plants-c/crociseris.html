Crociseris

Crociseris, commonly known as the crocus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant is also known for its edible corms, which are used in various culinary traditions.
