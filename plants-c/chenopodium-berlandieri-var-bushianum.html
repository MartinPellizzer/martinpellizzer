Chenopodium berlandieri var. bushianum

Chenopodium berlandieri var. bushianum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the Great Plains region.

It is an annual plant characterized by its spiny, ball-shaped seed heads that resemble small burs. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branching. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fence rows.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread rapidly, making it both a weed and a source of food for certain wildlife.
