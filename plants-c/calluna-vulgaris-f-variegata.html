Calluna vulgaris f. variegata

Calluna vulgaris f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated heather, is a cultivar of the common heather plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow margins on its narrow, evergreen leaves. This plant is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in the late summer and early autumn.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and as a ground cover in woodland settings.
