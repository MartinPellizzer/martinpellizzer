Caladenia elliptica

Caladenia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, spider-like flowers.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in regions such as New South Wales and Queensland. The plant produces a single or few flowers with narrow, elliptical petals and a distinctive, elongated labellum. It blooms during the late spring to early summer season, often in response to seasonal rainfall.

Caladenia elliptica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
