Chailletia nigrescens

Chailletia nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening chailletia, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage and striking, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Chailletia nigrescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
