Calanthe salmoniviridis

Calanthe salmoniviridis, commonly known as the salmon-colored calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking salmon-pink flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soil. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy flowers, which bloom during the spring and early summer.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
