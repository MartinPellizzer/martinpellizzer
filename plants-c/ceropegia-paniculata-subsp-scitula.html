Ceropegia paniculata subsp. scitula

Ceropegia paniculata subsp.

scitula, commonly known as the Cape mountain ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is often found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a striking addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
