Carex elongata var. guebhardii

Carex elongata var. guebhardii, commonly known as the Guebhard's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
