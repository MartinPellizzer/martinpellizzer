Cerastium fontanum var. kajanense

Cerastium fontanum var. kajanense, commonly known as the Kajanense mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
