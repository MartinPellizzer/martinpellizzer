Ceratostylis puncticulata

Ceratostylis puncticulata, commonly known as the dot-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, hairy spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
