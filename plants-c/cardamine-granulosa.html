Cardamine granulosa

Cardamine granulosa, commonly known as the granular watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in a fine, granular texture.

The small white or greenish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods.
