Carex nasuensis

Carex nasuensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brownish in color. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Carex nasuensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens and restoration projects.
