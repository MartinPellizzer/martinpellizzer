Cybianthus magnus subsp. magnus

Cybianthus magnus subsp.

magnus, commonly known as the giant cybianthus, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, often reaching up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely arranged around the stem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
