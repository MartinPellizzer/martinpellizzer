Conophyllum compactum

Conophyllum compactum, commonly known as the narrowleaf conophyllum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Conophyllum compactum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
