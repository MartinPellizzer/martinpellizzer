Coelachne subulifolia

Coelachne subulifolia, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains of seeds.

Coelachne subulifolia is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-resistant species.
