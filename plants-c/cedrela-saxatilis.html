Cedrela saxatilis

Cedrela saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued in the furniture and construction industries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in catkins.

Cedrela saxatilis is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
