Cycas pluma

Cycas pluma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and its pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and feather-like. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones appearing on separate plants. Cycas pluma is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
