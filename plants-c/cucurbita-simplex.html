Cucurbita simplex

Cucurbita simplex, commonly known as the little leaf gourd, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces small, pale green, lobed leaves and pale yellow flowers. The plant bears small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are still tender and immature. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinct, aromatic scent.

Cucurbita simplex is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is valued in traditional cuisines.
