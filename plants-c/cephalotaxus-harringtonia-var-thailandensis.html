Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. thailandensis

Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. thailandensis, commonly known as the Thai yew, is a rare evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive, pendulous cones. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
