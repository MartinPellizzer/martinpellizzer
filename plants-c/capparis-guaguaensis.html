Capparis guaguaensis

Capparis guaguaensis, commonly known as the Guagua Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
