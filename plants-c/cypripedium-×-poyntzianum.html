Cypripedium × poyntzianum

Cypripedium × poyntzianum, commonly known as the Poyntziana lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it produces a single, fragrant flower per stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
