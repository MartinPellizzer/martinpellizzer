Clavija umbrosa

Clavija umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy clavija, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring. Clavija umbrosa prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
