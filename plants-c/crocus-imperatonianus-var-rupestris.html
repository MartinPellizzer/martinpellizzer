Crocus imperatonianus var. rupestris

Crocus imperatonianus var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock crocus, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking violet to blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky crevices, thriving in cool, sunny conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
