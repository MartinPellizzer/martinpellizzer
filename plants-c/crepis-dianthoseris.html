Crepis dianthoseris

Crepis dianthoseris, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense tufts. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
