Casuarina huegeliana

Casuarina huegeliana, commonly known as the coastal casuarina, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered in fascicles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cylindrical flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy soils and tolerating salt spray.

Casuarina huegeliana plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
