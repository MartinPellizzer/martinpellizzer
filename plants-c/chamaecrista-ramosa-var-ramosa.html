Chamaecrista ramosa var. ramosa

Chamaecrista ramosa var. ramosa, commonly known as the slender ticktree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, making it visually striking. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its roots.

This adaptability makes it a useful plant in ecological restoration and soil enrichment.
