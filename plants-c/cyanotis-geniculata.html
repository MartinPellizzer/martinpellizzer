Cyanotis geniculata

Cyanotis geniculata, commonly known as the spiral ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in moist, shaded environments.

Cyanotis geniculata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens and boggy landscapes.
