Cnidoscolus appendiculatus

Cnidoscolus appendiculatus, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, spiny fruits. Its roots contain a toxic sap that has been used historically by indigenous peoples for its hallucinogenic properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal applications.
