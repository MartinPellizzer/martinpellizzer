Campanula americana var. illinoensis

Campanula americana var. illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies and wetlands.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.
