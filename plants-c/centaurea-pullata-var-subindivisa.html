Centaurea pullata var. subindivisa

Centaurea pullata var. subindivisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture. This variety is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed areas, contributing to its ecological significance.
