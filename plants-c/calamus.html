Calamus

Calamus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, clayey soils. Its tall, grass-like leaves are often used in ornamental gardening.

Calamus species are known for their distinctive, fragrant scent and their ability to purify water in their natural habitats.
