Cladrastis wilsonii

Cladrastis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
