Citharexylum megacanthum

Citharexylum megacanthum, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 8 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The sweet pepper tree thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.
