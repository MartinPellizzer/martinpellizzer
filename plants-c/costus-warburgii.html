Costus warburgii

Costus warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's costus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
