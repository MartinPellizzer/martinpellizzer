Cucurbita pepo var. schneebergeri

Cucurbita pepo var. schneebergeri, commonly known as the white-fruited gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its pale, often white or cream-colored fruits, which are typically elongated and cylindrical. The plant is an annual vine that produces palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a decorative element in gardens.
