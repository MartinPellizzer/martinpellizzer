Coryphantha scheeri var. uncinata

Coryphantha scheeri var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are often curved or hooked, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
