Crassularia sediformis

Crassularia sediformis, commonly known as the stonecrop or ice plant, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, adding ornamental value. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Crassularia sediformis thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in hot, dry climates.
