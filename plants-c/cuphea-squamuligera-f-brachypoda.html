Cuphea squamuligera f. brachypoda

Cuphea squamuligera f. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stalked cuphea, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
