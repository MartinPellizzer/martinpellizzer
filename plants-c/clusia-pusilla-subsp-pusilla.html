Clusia pusilla subsp. pusilla

Clusia pusilla subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf glorybower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America. It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments. Clusia pusilla subsp.

pusilla is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to salt spray.
