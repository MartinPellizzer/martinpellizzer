Colicodendron valerabellum

Colicodendron valerabellum, commonly known as the Valerian Tree, is a unique botanical species characterized by its striking violet and white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color. The plant contains bioactive compounds similar to valerian root, which have been studied for their potential calming effects.

Due to its rare habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
