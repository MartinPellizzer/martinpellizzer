Carex brasiliensis var. gracilis

Carex brasiliensis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
