Calamus castaneus var. griffithianus

Calamus castaneus var. griffithianus, commonly known as the Griffith's sedge, is a variant of the genus Calamus, which belongs to the family Arecaceae.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by spadices that produce small, edible fruits.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
