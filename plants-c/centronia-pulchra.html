Centronia pulchra

Centronia pulchra, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, indicating its adaptation to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Centronia pulchra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
