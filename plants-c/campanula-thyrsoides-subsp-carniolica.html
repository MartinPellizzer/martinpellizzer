Campanula thyrsoides subsp. carniolica

Campanula thyrsoides subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
