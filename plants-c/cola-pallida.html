Cola pallida

Cola pallida, commonly known as white cola, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its pale green to white leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.
