Celmisia verbascifolia subsp. verbascifolia

Celmisia verbascifolia subsp.

verbascifolia, commonly known as the alpine daisy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or scree habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
