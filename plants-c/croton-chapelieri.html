Croton chapelieri

Croton chapelieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Croton chapelieri thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.
