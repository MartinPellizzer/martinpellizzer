Calophyllum tonkinense

Calophyllum tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a fiery appearance. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is known for its aromatic, waxy leaves. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody fruit, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
