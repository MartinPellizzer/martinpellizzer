Clematis terniflora f. koreana

Clematis terniflora f. koreana, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a compact, flowering vine native to Korea.

It features delicate, fragrant white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in garden landscapes for its cascading growth habit.
