Colea hispidissima

Colea hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy colea, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to violet, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Colea hispidissima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
