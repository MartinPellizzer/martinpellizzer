Calamintha taquetii

Calamintha taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Calamintha taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.
