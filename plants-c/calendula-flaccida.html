Calendula flaccida

Calendula flaccida, commonly known as the drooping calender, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Calendula flaccida is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
