Carex psychroluta

Carex psychroluta, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Carex psychroluta plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
