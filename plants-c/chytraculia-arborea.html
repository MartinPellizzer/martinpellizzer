Chytraculia arborea

Chytraculia arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its fruit is a hard, spiky capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
