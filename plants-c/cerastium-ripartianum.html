Cerastium ripartianum

Cerastium ripartianum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its extensive root system allows it to spread readily, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
