Cyperus marojejyensis

Cyperus marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or marshy areas.

Cyperus marojejyensis is of particular interest due to its endemic status and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
