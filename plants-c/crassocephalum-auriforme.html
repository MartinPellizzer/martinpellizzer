Crassocephalum auriforme

Crassocephalum auriforme, commonly known as the golden crownleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive golden or yellowish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
