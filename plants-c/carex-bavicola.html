Carex bavicola

Carex bavicola, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marshy habitats, it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
