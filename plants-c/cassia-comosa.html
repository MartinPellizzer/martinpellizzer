Cassia comosa

Cassia comosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia comosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
