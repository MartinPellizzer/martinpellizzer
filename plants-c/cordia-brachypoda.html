Cordia brachypoda

Cordia brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cordia brachypoda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
