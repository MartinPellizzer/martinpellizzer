Celosia tomentosa

Celosia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky bracts that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Celosia tomentosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
