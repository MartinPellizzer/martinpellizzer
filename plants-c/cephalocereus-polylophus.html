Cephalocereus polylophus

Cephalocereus polylophus, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
