Carex distachya var. phyllostachioidea

Carex distachya var. phyllostachioidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
