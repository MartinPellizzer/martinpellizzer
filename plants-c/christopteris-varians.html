Christopteris varians

Christopteris varians, commonly known as the variable rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features small, basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers.

Christopteris varians is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
