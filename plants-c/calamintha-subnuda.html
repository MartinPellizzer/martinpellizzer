Calamintha subnuda

Calamintha subnuda, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, narrow, and often slightly hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Calamintha subnuda is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
