Cimicifuga obtusiloba

Cimicifuga obtusiloba, commonly known as blunt-leaf bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, compound leaves that are oval to ovate in shape, with a serrated margin. Its showy, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes and bloom in late summer.

Cimicifuga obtusiloba is often found in moist, rich soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
