Centaurea litochorea

Centaurea litochorea, commonly known as the Cyprus star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some areas, it is sometimes cultivated or considered a weed.
