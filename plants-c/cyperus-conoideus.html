Cyperus conoideus

Cyperus conoideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus conoideus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
