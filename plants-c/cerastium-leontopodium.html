Cerastium leontopodium

Cerastium leontopodium, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, often in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Cerastium leontopodium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
