Cereus jasmineus

Cereus jasmineus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. Its flowers emit a sweet, jasmine-like scent, which attracts pollinators such as moths.

Cereus jasmineus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
