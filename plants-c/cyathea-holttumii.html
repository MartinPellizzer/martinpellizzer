Cyathea holttumii

Cyathea holttumii, commonly known as the Holttum's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea holttumii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
