Capsella bursa-pastoris altissima

Capsella bursa-pastoris altissima, commonly known as the common chickweed or greater mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, white or pink flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually oval or kidney-shaped with notched margins. Its fruit is a dry, segmented pod that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
