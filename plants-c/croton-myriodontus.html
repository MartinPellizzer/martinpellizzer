Croton myriodontus

Croton myriodontus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
