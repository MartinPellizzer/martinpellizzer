Cassinopsis ilicifolia

Cassinopsis ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cassinopsis, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cassinopsis ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
