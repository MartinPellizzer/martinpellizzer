Cyperus strigosus var. multiflorus

Cyperus strigosus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or reddish in color, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
