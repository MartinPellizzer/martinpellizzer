Capsella bursa-pastoris laxa

Capsella bursa-pastoris laxa, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herbaceous plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, heart-shaped seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
