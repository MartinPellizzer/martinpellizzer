Cladium pulchrum

Cladium pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation.

Cladium pulchrum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
