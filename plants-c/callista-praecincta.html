Callista praecincta

Callista praecincta, commonly known as the golden bell, is a striking flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects.

Callista praecincta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
