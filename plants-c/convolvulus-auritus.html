Convolvulus auritus

Convolvulus auritus, commonly known as the ear of the dog, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its scientific name, *Convolvulus auritus*, refers to its ear-like appearance and the way its stems coil around objects.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
