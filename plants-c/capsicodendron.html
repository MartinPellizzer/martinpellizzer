Capsicodendron

Capsicodendron, commonly known as the "ghost pepper tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its ornamental red berries and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive, spicy fruit.

Capsicodendron species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and, in some cases, for their medicinal properties.
