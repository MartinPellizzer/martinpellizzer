Carpinus monbeigiana var. weisiensis

Carpinus monbeigiana var. weisiensis, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its hard, dense wood is valued for its durability and has been used in traditional craftsmanship.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
