Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. gracilis

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. gracilis, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress dwarf form, is a compact, evergreen conifer with a narrow, columnar shape.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a fine, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is native to the western United States and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.

Its slow growth rate and dense foliage make it suitable for use as a hedge or specimen plant.
