Cardamine oregana

Cardamine oregana, commonly known as Oregon toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant has distinctive, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and have a basal rosette structure. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring.

Oregon toothwort thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
