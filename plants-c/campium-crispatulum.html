Campium crispatulum

Campium crispatulum, commonly known as the hairy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Campium crispatulum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
