Campnosperma philippinense

Campnosperma philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is deep and helps in soil stabilization, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with well-drained soils.
