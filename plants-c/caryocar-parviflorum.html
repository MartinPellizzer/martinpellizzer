Caryocar parviflorum

Caryocar parviflorum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or the "pequi" tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit with a hard, woody shell enclosing a single seed. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for both humans and wildlife.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food provider in tropical and subtropical regions.
