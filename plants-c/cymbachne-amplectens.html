Cymbachne amplectens

Cymbachne amplectens, commonly known as the climbing grass or velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and dense, mat-forming foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters.

Cymbachne amplectens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
