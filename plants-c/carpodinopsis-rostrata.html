Carpodinopsis rostrata

Carpodinopsis rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
