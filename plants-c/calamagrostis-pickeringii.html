Calamagrostis pickeringii

Calamagrostis pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's reed, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, silver-tomentose leaves that are often described as having a frosted appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy florets that are typically golden to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pickeringii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
