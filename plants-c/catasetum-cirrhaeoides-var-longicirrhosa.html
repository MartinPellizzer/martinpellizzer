Catasetum cirrhaeoides var. longicirrhosa

Catasetum cirrhaeoides var. longicirrhosa, commonly known as the long-haired Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a unique structure called a "labellum," which is specialized for pollination by male bees. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

The variety longicirrhosa is distinguished by its elongated, hairy sepals and petals.
