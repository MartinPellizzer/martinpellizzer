Capsicum cumanense

Capsicum cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanense chili, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces small, elongated fruits with a mild to moderate heat level. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and relatively small, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines for its flavor and culinary versatility.
