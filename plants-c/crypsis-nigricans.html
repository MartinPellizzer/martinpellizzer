Crypsis nigricans

Crypsis nigricans, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dark purple to black berries and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pale green flowers.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing solanine and other alkaloids.
