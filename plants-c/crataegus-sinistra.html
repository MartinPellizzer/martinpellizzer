Crataegus sinistra

Crataegus sinistra, commonly known as the left-handed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus sinistra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
