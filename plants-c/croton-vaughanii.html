Croton vaughanii

Croton vaughanii, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red and orange. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking species. Croton vaughanii typically grows as an understory plant in humid, shaded environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
