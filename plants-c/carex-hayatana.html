Carex hayatana

Carex hayatana, commonly known as the Turkish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex hayatana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
