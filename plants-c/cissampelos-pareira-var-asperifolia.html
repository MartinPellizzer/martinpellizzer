Cissampelos pareira var. asperifolia

Cissampelos pareira var. asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf moonvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety surface. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its stems are often armed with short, stiff spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
