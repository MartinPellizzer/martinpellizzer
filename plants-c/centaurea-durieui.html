Centaurea durieui

Centaurea durieui, commonly known as the Durieu's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces bright blue to purple flowers.

Centaurea durieui is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
