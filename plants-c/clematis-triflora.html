Clematis triflora

Clematis triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with five petals. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
