Centaurea hermonis

Centaurea hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are often branched.

Centaurea hermonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
