Clematis austrotaiwanensis

Clematis austrotaiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

Clematis austrotaiwanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
