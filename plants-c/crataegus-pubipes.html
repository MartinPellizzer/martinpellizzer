Crataegus pubipes

Crataegus pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
