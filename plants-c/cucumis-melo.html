Cucumis melo

Cucumis melo, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Asia.

It is a vine that produces a large, lobed, orange-fleshed fruit with a distinctive netted rind. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. The fruit is typically harvested when the rind is fully colored and the sugars are mature.

It is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is commonly used in fresh consumption or in culinary applications.
