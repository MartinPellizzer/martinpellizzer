Capparis artensis

Capparis artensis, commonly known as the Articulated Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces round, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a thick, fleshy pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis artensis is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
