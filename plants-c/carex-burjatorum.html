Carex burjatorum

Carex burjatorum, commonly known as the Burjat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually green to brownish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex burjatorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
