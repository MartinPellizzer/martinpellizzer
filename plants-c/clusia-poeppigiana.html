Clusia poeppigiana

Clusia poeppigiana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Clusia poeppigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
