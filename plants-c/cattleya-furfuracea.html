Cattleya furfuracea

Cattleya furfuracea, commonly known as the velvety cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety petals and a distinctive, fragrant flower that blooms in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Cattleya furfuracea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
