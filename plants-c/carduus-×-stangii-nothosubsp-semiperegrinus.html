Carduus × stangii nothosubsp. semiperegrinus

Carduus × stangii nothosubsp.

semiperegrinus, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments. Its morphology includes a robust stem and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk florets.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variability.
