Coccoloba nodosa

Coccoloba nodosa, commonly known as the sea grape, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of the Atlantic coast.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments, making it a hardy species. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
