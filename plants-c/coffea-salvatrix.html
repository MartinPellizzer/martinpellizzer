Coffea salvatrix

Coffea salvatrix, commonly known as the Salvator coffee plant, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are often fragrant.

This species is valued for its unique flavor profile and is cultivated in specific regions for specialty coffee production.
