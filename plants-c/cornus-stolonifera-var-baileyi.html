Cornus stolonifera var. baileyi

Cornus stolonifera var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
