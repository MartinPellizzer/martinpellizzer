Campylocentrum brachycarpum

Campylocentrum brachycarpum, commonly known as the dwarf starfruit, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

The fruit, which is edible when mature, has a unique star-like shape and a slightly sour taste.
