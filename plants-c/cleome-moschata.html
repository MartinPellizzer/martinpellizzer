Cleome moschata

Cleome moschata, commonly known as sweet scabious, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall, featuring erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Cleome moschata is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
