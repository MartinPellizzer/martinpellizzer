Convolvulus roseatus

Convolvulus roseatus, commonly known as the rose-colored morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and ability to climb or trail along structures.
