Chamaesyce polycarpa var. simulans

Chamaesyce polycarpa var. simulans, commonly known as the redstem sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often turn reddish in color, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in the spring, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and sandy soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
