Cremaspora confluens

Cremaspora confluens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, often multi-stemmed structure and small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Cremaspora confluens is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
