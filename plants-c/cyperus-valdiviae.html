Cyperus valdiviae

Cyperus valdiviae, commonly known as the Valdivia flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Cyperus valdiviae produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
