Convolvulus zimmermannii

Convolvulus zimmermannii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial vine that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Convolvulus zimmermannii is often found in rocky areas and along dry streambeds.
