Cissus micrantha var. tripartita

Cissus micrantha var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
