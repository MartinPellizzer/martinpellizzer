Colocasia esculenta var. globulifera

Colocasia esculenta var. globulifera, commonly known as the elephant ear or giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by round, green berries. The leaves are often used as a decorative element in gardens due to their striking appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value.
