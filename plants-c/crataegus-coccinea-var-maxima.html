Crataegus coccinea var. maxima

Crataegus coccinea var. maxima, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries and distinctive, sharply toothed leaves. The plant produces showy pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the bright red fruits in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
