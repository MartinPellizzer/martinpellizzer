Cerastium pulchellum

Cerastium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, rounded leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
