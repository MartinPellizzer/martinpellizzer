Campomanesia guazumifolia var. rubiginosa

Campomanesia guazumifolia var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-berried satinash, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. The berries are highly aromatic and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
