Crepis albida subsp. longicaulis

Crepis albida subsp.

longicaulis, commonly known as the white bentgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or nutrient-rich environments.
