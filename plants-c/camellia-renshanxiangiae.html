Camellia renshanxiangiae

Camellia renshanxiangiae, commonly known as the Renshanxiangiae camellia, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Camellia genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, specifically in the Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region. This plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in the winter months. The flowers typically exhibit a range of colors, from deep red to pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for study and preservation.
