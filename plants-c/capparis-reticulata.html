Capparis reticulata

Capparis reticulata, commonly known as the Indian fig, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is characterized by its unique reticulate (net-like) leaf venation and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
