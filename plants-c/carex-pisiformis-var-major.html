Carex pisiformis var. major

Carex pisiformis var. major, commonly known as the large sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
