Camellia cordifolia var. glabrisepala

Camellia cordifolia var. glabrisepala, commonly known as the smooth-sepal camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, double flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in winter and early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The varietal name 'glabrisepala' refers to its smooth, hairless sepals, a distinguishing feature of this cultivar.
