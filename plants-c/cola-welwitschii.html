Cola welwitschii

Cola welwitschii, commonly known as the African kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces hard, capsule-like fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used as the kola nut. Cola welwitschii is notable for its high caffeine content and historical use in traditional ceremonies and as a stimulant.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
