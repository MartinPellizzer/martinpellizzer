Cytisus hirsutus var. vulgaris

Cytisus hirsutus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common broom, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and wastelands.

Cytisus hirsutus var. vulgaris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
