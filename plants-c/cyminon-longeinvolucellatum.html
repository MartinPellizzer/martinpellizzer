Cyminon longeinvolucellatum

Cyminon longeinvolucellatum, commonly known as the long-involucel cumin.

This plant is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels.

Its seeds, which are harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes, have a distinct, pungent aroma.
