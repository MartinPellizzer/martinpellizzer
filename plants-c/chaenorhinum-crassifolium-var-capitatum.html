Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. capitatum

Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. capitatum, commonly known as the clustered horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It is an annual herb that produces capsules containing numerous small, dark seeds.
