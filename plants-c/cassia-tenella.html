Cassia tenella

Cassia tenella, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
