Clinopodium coccineum

Clinopodium coccineum, commonly known as red stem mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale pink to purple, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Clinopodium coccineum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
