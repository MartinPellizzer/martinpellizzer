Cryptocarya peumus var. laxiflora

Cryptocarya peumus var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle or velvetleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
