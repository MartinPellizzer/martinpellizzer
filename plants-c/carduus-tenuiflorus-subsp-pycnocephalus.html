Carduus tenuiflorus subsp. pycnocephalus

Carduus tenuiflorus subsp.

pycnocephalus, commonly known as the dense-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves with a rough, serrated edge.

The plant produces dense, spherical clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
