Cnemidaria spectabilis var. colombiensis

Cnemidaria spectabilis var. colombiensis, commonly known as the Colombian flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant in greenhouses.
