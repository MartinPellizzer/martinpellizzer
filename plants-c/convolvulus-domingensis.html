Convolvulus domingensis

Convolvulus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar spiral-shaped flowers.

Convolvulus domingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
