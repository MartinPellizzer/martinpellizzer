Cyperus papyrus subsp. papyrus

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

papyrus, commonly known as the sacred papyrus plant, is a monocot native to the Nile River valley. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 4 meters in height.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense stands in wetland environments.

Historically, it was used to make papyrus paper in ancient Egypt, highlighting its cultural and botanical significance.
