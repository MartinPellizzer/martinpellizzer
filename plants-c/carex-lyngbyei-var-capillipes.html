Carex lyngbyei var. capillipes

Carex lyngbyei var. capillipes, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial sedge native to northern regions of North America.

It thrives in wet to moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers that mature into dark brown seeds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
