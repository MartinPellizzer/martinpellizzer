Cabralea jussiaeana

Cabralea jussiaeana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or cabralea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a pointed tip.

Cabralea jussiaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
