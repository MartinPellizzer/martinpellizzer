Chaetostoma fastigiatum

Chaetostoma fastigiatum, commonly known as the zigzag catfish, is a species of catfish native to the rivers of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-like patterns on its body, which provide camouflage in its aquatic environment. The plant-like appearance of its scales and body shape has led to its classification within the family Loricariidae, which includes many armored catfish species. It typically inhabits slow-moving freshwater streams and is often found in rocky or muddy substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular species among aquarium enthusiasts.
