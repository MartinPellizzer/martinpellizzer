Croton amazonicus

Croton amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazon croton, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright red to deep green and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. Its leaves are typically large and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Croton amazonicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant appearance.
