Cervaria laevis

Cervaria laevis, commonly known as the smooth cervaria, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with smooth margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
