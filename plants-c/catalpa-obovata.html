Catalpa obovata

Catalpa obovata, commonly known as the American catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Catalpa obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in landscaping.
