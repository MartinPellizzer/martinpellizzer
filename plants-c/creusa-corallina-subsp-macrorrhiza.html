Creusa corallina subsp. macrorrhiza

Creusa corallina subsp.

macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-lobed creusa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to retain moisture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
