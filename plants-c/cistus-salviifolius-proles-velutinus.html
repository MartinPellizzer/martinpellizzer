Cistus salviifolius proles velutinus

Cistus salviifolius proles velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, grey-green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
