Cyanus flosculosus

Cyanus flosculosus, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Cyanus flosculosus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
