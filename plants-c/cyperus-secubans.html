Cyperus secubans

Cyperus secubans, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus secubans is adapted to saturated soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
