Cynanchum ikema

Cynanchum ikema, commonly known as the Japanese milkweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
