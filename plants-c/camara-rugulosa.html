Camara rugulosa

Camara rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and cautionary significance.

Camara rugulosa thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
