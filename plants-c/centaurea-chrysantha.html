Centaurea chrysantha

Centaurea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bract-like structures that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Centaurea chrysantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
