Calanthe orthocentron

Calanthe orthocentron, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs. Calanthe orthocentron thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
