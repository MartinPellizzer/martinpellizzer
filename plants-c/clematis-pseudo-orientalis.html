Clematis pseudo-orientalis

Clematis pseudo-orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis pseudo-orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
