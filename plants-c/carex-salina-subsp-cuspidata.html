Carex salina subsp. cuspidata

Carex salina subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal wetlands. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in brackish or saltwater environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
