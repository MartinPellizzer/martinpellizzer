Crocus lageniflorus subvar. penicillatus

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. penicillatus, commonly known as the penicillate crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in early spring.

The subvariety penicillatus is distinguished by its elongated, slender petals and a unique, almost sword-like shape of its flower.
