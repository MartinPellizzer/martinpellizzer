Calandrinia diffusa

Calandrinia diffusa, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, vibrant red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

Calandrinia diffusa is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and colorful blooms.
