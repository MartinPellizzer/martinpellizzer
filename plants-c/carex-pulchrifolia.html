Carex pulchrifolia

Carex pulchrifolia, commonly known as the beautiful leaf sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Carex pulchrifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

It is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
