Cestrum flavinervium

Cestrum flavinervium, commonly known as the golden nerve cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a prominent yellow midrib. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the evening and are pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
