Codonopsis viridiflora

Codonopsis viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from green to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Codonopsis viridiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
