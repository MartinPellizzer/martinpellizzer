Catevala cordifolia

Catevala cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cat's claw, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooked tendrils used for climbing. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Catevala cordifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
