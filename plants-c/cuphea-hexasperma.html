Cuphea hexasperma

Cuphea hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both landscapes and containers.
