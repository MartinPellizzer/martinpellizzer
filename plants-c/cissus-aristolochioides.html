Cissus aristolochioides

Cissus aristolochioides, commonly known as the African nettle or climbing cissus, is a vine native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its stems are typically climbing and can be used for ornamental purposes.

Cissus aristolochioides is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in humid environments.
