Calea kirkbridei

Calea kirkbridei, commonly known as the Mexican ghost flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, white, star-shaped flowers that appear at night, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant produces a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts specific pollinators such as moths and flies. Its leaves are broad and hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Calea kirkbridei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
