Cochlearia danica f. colorata

Cochlearia danica f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in coastal landscapes.
