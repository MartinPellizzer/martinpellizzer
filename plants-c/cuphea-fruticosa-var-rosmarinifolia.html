Cuphea fruticosa var. rosmarinifolia

Cuphea fruticosa var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the fragrant rosemary bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, dry climates.
