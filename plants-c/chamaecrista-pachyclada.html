Chamaecrista pachyclada

Chamaecrista pachyclada, commonly known as the broad-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant flowers and foliage.
