Cylindropuntia acanthocarpa var. coloradensis

Cylindropuntia acanthocarpa var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid environments.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
