Carduus olympicus subsp. hypoleucus

Carduus olympicus subsp.

hypoleucus, commonly known as the Olympic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with stout, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
