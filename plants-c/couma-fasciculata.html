Couma fasciculata

Couma fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered couma, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical, smooth seeds.

Couma fasciculata is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
