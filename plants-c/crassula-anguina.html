Crassula anguina

Crassula anguina, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright leaves that are often ribbed and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers on slender stalks during the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
