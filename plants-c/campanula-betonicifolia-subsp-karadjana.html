Campanula betonicifolia subsp. karadjana

Campanula betonicifolia subsp.

karadjana, commonly known as Karadjana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
