Centropogon cornutus var. intermedius

Centropogon cornutus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate horned grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence with bracts that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
