Campylia cana

Campylia cana, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant features simple, opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are pink to white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Campylia cana produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
