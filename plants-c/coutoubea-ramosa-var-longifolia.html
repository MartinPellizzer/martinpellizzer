Coutoubea ramosa var. longifolia

Coutoubea ramosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered corymbia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in corymbs, which bloom in spring. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a woody stem structure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
