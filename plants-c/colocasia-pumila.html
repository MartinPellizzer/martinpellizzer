Colocasia pumila

Colocasia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf elephant ear, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to spread via rhizomes, it is often cultivated in gardens and wetlands.
