Cacalia achyrotricha

Cacalia achyrotricha, commonly known as the white thistle or slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a sprawling or ascending growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
