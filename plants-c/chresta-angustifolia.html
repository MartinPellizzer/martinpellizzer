Chresta angustifolia

Chresta angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chresta, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticultural trade.
