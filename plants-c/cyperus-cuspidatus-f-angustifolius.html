Cyperus cuspidatus f. angustifolius

Cyperus cuspidatus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower foliage compared to the typical form of Cyperus cuspidatus.
