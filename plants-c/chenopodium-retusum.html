Chenopodium retusum

Chenopodium retusum, commonly known as rough goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium retusum is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common weed in many regions.
