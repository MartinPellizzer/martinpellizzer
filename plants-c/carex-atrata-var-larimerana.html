Carex atrata var. larimerana

Carex atrata var. larimerana, commonly known as the Larimer Lake sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Colorado.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
