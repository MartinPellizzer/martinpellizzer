Centaurea sakarensis

Centaurea sakarensis, commonly known as the Sakar Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea sakarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
