Centaurea coutinhoi

Centaurea coutinhoi, commonly known as the Coutinho's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
