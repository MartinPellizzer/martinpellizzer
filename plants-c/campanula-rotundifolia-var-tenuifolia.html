Campanula rotundifolia var. tenuifolia

Campanula rotundifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are rounded and often hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
