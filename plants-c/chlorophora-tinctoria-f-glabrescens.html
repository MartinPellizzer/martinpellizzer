Chlorophora tinctoria f. glabrescens

Chlorophora tinctoria f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and wood, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
