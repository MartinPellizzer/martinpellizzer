Croton fuscus var. genuinus

Croton fuscus var. genuinus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated, foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
