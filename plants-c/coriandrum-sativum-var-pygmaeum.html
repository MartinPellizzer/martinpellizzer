Coriandrum sativum var. pygmaeum

Coriandrum sativum var. pygmaeum, commonly known as dwarf cumin, is a smaller variant of the common cumin plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller leaves compared to the standard variety. The plant produces small, spherical seeds that are used in culinary applications similar to regular cumin. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its aromatic qualities and is often grown in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
