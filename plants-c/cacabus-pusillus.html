Cacabus pusillus

Cacabus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are scale-like and its flowers are inconspicuous, typically green or yellowish.

Cacabus pusillus plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
