Crotalaria puberula

Crotalaria puberula, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, and its leaves are usually ovate with hairy surfaces. Crotalaria puberula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
