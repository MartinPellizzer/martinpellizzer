Campanula polymorpha f. gracilis

Campanula polymorpha f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks in temperate regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
