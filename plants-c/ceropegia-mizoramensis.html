Ceropegia mizoramensis

Ceropegia mizoramensis, commonly known as the Mizoram milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique corolla shape. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
