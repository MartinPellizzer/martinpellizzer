Carex peraltae

Carex peraltae, commonly known as the Peralta sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or riparian zones. Its leaves are usually dark green and narrow, with a prominent midrib.

Carex peraltae plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
