Crepidium bisepalum

Crepidium bisepalum, commonly known as the two-sepal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in rich, organic soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
