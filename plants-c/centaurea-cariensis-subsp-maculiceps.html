Centaurea cariensis subsp. maculiceps

Centaurea cariensis subsp.

maculiceps, commonly known as the spotted centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and bristly, glandular leaves.

The species typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as coastal areas and steppe zones. Its flowers are arranged in compact, cylindrical heads, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive purple markings on the bracts and its relatively compact growth habit.
