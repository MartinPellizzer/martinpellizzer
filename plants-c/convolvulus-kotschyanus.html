Convolvulus kotschyanus

Convolvulus kotschyanus, commonly known as the Kotschyan's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, with vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
