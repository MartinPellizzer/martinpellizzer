Chascanum pumilum

Chascanum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf chascanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens.
