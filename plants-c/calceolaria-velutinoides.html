Calceolaria velutinoides

Calceolaria velutinoides, commonly known as the velvet slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are velvety and hairy, contributing to its common name.

Calceolaria velutinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral structure.
