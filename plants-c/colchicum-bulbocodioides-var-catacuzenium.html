Colchicum bulbocodioides var. catacuzenium

Colchicum bulbocodioides var. catacuzenium, commonly known as the Catacuzenium colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a bulbous rootstock and produces striking, showy flowers with purple petals and a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are broad, lanceolate, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cool, moist conditions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
