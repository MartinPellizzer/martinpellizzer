Capsicum annuum var. varians

Capsicum annuum var. varians, commonly known as the chili pepper or bell pepper, is a widely cultivated species within the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its diverse range of fruit shapes, colors, and capsaicin levels, which vary depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow or white flowers.

Its fruits develop from the flowers and can range from sweet to extremely hot, depending on the cultivar. The botanical variability of Capsicum annuum var. varians makes it a valuable crop for both culinary and medicinal uses.
