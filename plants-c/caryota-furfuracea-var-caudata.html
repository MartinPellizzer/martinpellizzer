Caryota furfuracea var. caudata

Caryota furfuracea var. caudata, commonly known as the horse's tail palm, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its long, slender, feather-like leaves that resemble a horse's tail.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and can be harvested for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
