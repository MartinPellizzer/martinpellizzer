Cheilanthes mathewsii

Cheilanthes mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' rockcap, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dark green, leathery fronds. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with limited moisture availability.
