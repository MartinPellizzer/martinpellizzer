Cyperus linnaeanus

Cyperus linnaeanus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus linnaeanus is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along waterways due to its preference for moist soil.

Its ability to thrive in aquatic environments makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
