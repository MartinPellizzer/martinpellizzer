Costus cylindricus var. acreanus

Costus cylindricus var. acreanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf costus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological landscapes.
