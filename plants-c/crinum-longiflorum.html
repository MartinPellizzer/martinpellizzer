Crinum longiflorum

Crinum longiflorum, commonly known as the fragrant crane's-bill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spike at the top of the plant. The leaves are long, strap-like, and grass-like, often emerging from the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening.
