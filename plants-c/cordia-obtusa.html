Cordia obtusa

Cordia obtusa, commonly known as the smooth cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible and contain several seeds, making it an important food source for wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
