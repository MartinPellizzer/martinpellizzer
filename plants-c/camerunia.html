Camerunia

Camerunia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and thick, fleshy leaves. The plants typically grow as small shrubs or herbs, often with a compact growth habit. Camerunia species are popular houseplants due to their ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
