Cordyline neocaledonica

Cordyline neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
