Cyperus erythrorhizos var. infirmus

Cyperus erythrorhizos var. infirmus, commonly known as red-rooted flatsedge, is a wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its roots are reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
