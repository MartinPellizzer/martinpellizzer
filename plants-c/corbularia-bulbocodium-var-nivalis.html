Corbularia bulbocodium var. nivalis

Corbularia bulbocodium var. nivalis, commonly known as the snow lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the surrounding vegetation emerges. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
