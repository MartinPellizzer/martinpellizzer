Croton longiradiatus

Croton longiradiatus, commonly known as the long-rayed croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with long, radiating lobes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Due to its unique morphology, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
