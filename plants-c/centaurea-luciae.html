Centaurea luciae

Centaurea luciae, commonly known as the Luciae thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish-red flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a cylindrical stem covered in stiff hairs and a basal rosette of leaves.

Centaurea luciae is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
