Ceropegia crassifolia

Ceropegia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, often with a unique, tubular shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
