Calyptochloa gracillima subsp. ipsviciensis

Calyptochloa gracillima subsp.

ipsviciensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
