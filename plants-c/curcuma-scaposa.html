Curcuma scaposa

Curcuma scaposa, commonly known as the purple ginger or Malabar turmeric, is a rhizomatous perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a flowering spike that emerges from the base of the plant.

Curcuma scaposa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
