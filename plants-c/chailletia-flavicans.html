Chailletia flavicans

Chailletia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered chailletia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
