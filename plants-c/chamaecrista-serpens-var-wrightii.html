Chamaecrista serpens var. wrightii

Chamaecrista serpens var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
