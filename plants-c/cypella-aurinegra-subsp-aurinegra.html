Cypella aurinegra subsp. aurinegra

Cypella aurinegra subsp.

aurinegra, commonly known as the black-flowered cypella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
