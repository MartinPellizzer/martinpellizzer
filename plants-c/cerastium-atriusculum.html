Cerastium atriusculum

Cerastium atriusculum, commonly known as the black sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium atriusculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet habitats.
