Cladocolea nitida

Cladocolea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cladocolea nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
