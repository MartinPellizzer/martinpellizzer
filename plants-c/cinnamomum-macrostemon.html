Cinnamomum macrostemon

Cinnamomum macrostemon, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or large-fruited cinnamon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for culinary purposes.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been cultivated for centuries in China and other parts of Asia.
