Camellia sinensis var. kucha

Camellia sinensis var. kucha, commonly known as Kucha tea, is a variant of the Camellia sinensis plant native to China.

It is characterized by its small, tightly rolled leaves and a distinct, slightly smoky flavor profile. The plant thrives in subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is typically harvested during the spring and summer seasons.

Kucha tea is valued for its unique taste and is often used in traditional Chinese tea ceremonies.
