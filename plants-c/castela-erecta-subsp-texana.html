Castela erecta subsp. texana

Castela erecta subsp.

texana, commonly known as Texas castela, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as limestone soils and rocky habitats.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
