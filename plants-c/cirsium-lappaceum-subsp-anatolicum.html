Cirsium lappaceum subsp. anatolicum

Cirsium lappaceum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and parts of southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct genetic and morphological features compared to other members of the Cirsium genus.
