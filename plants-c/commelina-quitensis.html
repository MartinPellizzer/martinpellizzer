Commelina quitensis

Commelina quitensis, commonly known as the Andean spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Commelina family, which includes many species with showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers feature a distinctive three-lobed structure.

Commelina quitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
