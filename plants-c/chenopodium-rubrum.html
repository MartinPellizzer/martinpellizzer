Chenopodium rubrum

Chenopodium rubrum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.

Chenopodium rubrum is also used in traditional medicine and as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.
