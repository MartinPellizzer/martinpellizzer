Costus conicus

Costus conicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved costus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces striking orange-red flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
