Chrysocephalum gilesii

Chrysocephalum gilesii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, tough, and long-lasting flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its foliage is silvery-gray, adding a striking contrast to its vibrant yellow blooms.
