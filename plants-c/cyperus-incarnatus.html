Cyperus incarnatus

Cyperus incarnatus, commonly known as the pink flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often tinged with pink or reddish hues, especially near the base.

Cyperus incarnatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.
