Croton heptalon

Croton heptalon, commonly known as the seven-lobed croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with seven lobes, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
