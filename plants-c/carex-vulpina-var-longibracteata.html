Carex vulpina var. longibracteata

Carex vulpina var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth surface.

The varietal form, longibracteata, is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flowering spikes.
