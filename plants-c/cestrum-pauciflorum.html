Cestrum pauciflorum

Cestrum pauciflorum, commonly known as the night-blooming cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
