Calathea nitidifolia

Calathea nitidifolia, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are often described as having a waxy texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Calathea nitidifolia requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
