Cyphostemma adenocaule var. eglandulosopubescens

Cyphostemma adenocaule var. eglandulosopubescens, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and bear opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and succulent-like leaves.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
