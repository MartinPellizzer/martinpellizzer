Crepis velenovskyi

Crepis velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem with small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. It has fibrous roots and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
