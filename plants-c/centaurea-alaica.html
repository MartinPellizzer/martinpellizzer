Centaurea alaica

Centaurea alaica, commonly known as the Crimean centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Crimea and parts of southern Ukraine.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

Centaurea alaica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
