Cymopterus acaulis var. fendleri

Cymopterus acaulis var. fendleri, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a compact, low-growing habit, featuring dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical structure reflects its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or sandy soils.
