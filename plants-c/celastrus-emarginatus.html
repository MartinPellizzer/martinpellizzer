Celastrus emarginatus

Celastrus emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a distinctive notched or emarginate base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
