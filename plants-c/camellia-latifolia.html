Camellia latifolia

Camellia latifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a flowering evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of tea leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia latifolia is an important species in both horticulture and the tea industry.
