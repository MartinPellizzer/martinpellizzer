Crocus mersinensis

Crocus mersinensis, commonly known as the Mersin crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a purple or brown center. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
