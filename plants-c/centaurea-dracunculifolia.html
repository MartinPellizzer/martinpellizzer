Centaurea dracunculifolia

Centaurea dracunculifolia, commonly known as the dragon's tail centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea dracunculifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
