Cyperus riparius

Cyperus riparius, commonly known as the marsh grass or water clubrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus riparius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
