Campium undulatum

Campium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with wavy edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often growing in full sun.

Campium undulatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
