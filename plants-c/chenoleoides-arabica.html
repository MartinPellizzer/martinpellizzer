Chenoleoides arabica

Chenoleoides arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub-like plant with small, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. The plant is closely related to the well-known coffee genus Coffea and shares similar botanical characteristics. Its fruits are small berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production.

Due to its limited geographic distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
