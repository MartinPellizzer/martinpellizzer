Caccinia crassifolia f. linearifolia

Caccinia crassifolia f. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed caccinia, is a variant of the broader-leafed caccinia species.

It is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which appear during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The foliage is aromatic and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its potential medicinal properties.
