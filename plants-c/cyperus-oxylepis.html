Cyperus oxylepis

Cyperus oxylepis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus oxylepis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
