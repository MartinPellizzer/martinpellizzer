Campanula napuligera subf. latifrons

Campanula napuligera subf.

latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
