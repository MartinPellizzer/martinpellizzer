Cucumis melo f. typicus

Cucumis melo f. typicus, commonly known as the common muskmelon, is a cultivated variant of the muskmelon species.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit typically has a smooth, green rind with a distinctive netted pattern and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It is widely grown for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
