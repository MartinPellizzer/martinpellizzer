Capsella sabulosa

Capsella sabulosa, commonly known as sand rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The seeds are enclosed in slender pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
