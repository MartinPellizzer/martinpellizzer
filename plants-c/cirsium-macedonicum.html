Cirsium macedonicum

Cirsium macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
