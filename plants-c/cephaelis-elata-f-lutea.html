Cephaelis elata f. lutea

Cephaelis elata f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
