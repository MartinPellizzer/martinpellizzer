Cynanchum bicolor

Cynanchum bicolor, commonly known as the purple swallowwort, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, but it can be invasive in certain regions.
