Caladenia concinna

Caladenia concinna, commonly known as the pink spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its striking pink and white floral display.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. The plant features a single, slender stem that supports a single, intricate flower with a hooded labellum. It is a terrestrial orchid that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Caladenia concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens due to its unique and elegant appearance.
