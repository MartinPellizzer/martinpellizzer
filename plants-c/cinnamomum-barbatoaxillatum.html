Cinnamomum barbatoaxillatum

Cinnamomum barbatoaxillatum, commonly known as the hairy axillary cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.
