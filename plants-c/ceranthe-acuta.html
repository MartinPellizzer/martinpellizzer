Ceranthe acuta

Ceranthe acuta, commonly known as the acute ceranthe, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
