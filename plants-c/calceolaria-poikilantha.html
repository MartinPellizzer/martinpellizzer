Calceolaria poikilantha

Calceolaria poikilantha, commonly known as the variable slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Calceolaria poikilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unusual blooms.
