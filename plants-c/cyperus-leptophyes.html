Cyperus leptophyes

Cyperus leptophyes, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. Cyperus leptophyes is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
