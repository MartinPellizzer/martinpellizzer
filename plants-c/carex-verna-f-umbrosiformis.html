Carex verna f. umbrosiformis

Carex verna f. umbrosiformis, commonly known as the shade-forming marsh sedge, is a variant of the marsh sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, adapted for wind pollination.
