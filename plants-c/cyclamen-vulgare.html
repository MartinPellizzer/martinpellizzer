Cyclamen vulgare

Cyclamen vulgare, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tuberous root system that allows it to survive harsh winters. The plant produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred petal at the base.

Cyclamen vulgare is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
