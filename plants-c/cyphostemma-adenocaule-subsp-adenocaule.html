Cyphostemma adenocaule subsp. adenocaule

Cyphostemma adenocaule subsp.

adenocaule, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It features woody stems with thorn-like projections and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
