Corsia purpurata

Corsia purpurata, commonly known as the purple corsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Corsia purpurata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
