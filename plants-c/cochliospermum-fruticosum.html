Cochliospermum fruticosum

Cochliospermum fruticosum, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in panicle inflorescences, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and construction.
