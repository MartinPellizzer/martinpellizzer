Crenulluma flava

Crenulluma flava, commonly known as the golden crenulate, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that display a striking yellow hue with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in low-light conditions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Crenulluma flava is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
