Calceolaria hirtiflora

Calceolaria hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its hairy stems and leaves help it retain moisture in its arid environment.

Calceolaria hirtiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
