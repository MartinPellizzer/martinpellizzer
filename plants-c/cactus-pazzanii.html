Cactus pazzanii

Cactus pazzanii, commonly known as the Pazzani's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently. The plant features prominent spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its succulent stems are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
