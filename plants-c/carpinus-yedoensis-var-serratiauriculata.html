Carpinus yedoensis var. serratiauriculata

Carpinus yedoensis var. serratiauriculata, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, red fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
