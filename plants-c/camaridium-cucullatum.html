Camaridium cucullatum

Camaridium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and are often surrounded by a hood-like structure, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
