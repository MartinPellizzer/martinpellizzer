Cirsium incisum

Cirsium incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cirsium incisum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
