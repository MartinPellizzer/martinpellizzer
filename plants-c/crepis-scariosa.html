Crepis scariosa

Crepis scariosa, commonly known as the scarious hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough, scabrous texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
