Corydalis balfouriana

Corydalis balfouriana, commonly known as the Himalayan Corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Corydalis balfouriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
