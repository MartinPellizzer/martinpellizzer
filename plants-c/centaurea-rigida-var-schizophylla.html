Centaurea rigida var. schizophylla

Centaurea rigida var. schizophylla, commonly known as the rigid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
