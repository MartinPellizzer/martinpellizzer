Carex otomana

Carex otomana, commonly known as the Otoman sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Carex otomana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
