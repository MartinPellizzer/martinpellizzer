Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. antillanum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. antillanum, commonly known as the Caribbean cinnamon or West Indian cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to produce a spice similar to true cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and aromatic purposes.
