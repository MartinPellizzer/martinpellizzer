Carex usta

Carex usta, commonly known as stiff sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets of brown flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves and is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
