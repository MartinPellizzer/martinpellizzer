Cnicus cynara

Cnicus cynara, commonly known as the sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

Cnicus cynara is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
