Casearia grewiifolia var. cinerea

Casearia grewiifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
