Crepis crocea subsp. crocea

Crepis crocea subsp.

crocea, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that rise from the center.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
