Cladium laxiflorum

Cladium laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered sawgrass, is a species of flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Cladium laxiflorum is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
