Carex broteroana

Carex broteroana, commonly known as the Brotero sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ability to thrive in moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in marshy areas and can play a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
