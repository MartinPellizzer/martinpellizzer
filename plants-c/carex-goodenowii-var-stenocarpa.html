Carex goodenowii var. stenocarpa

Carex goodenowii var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-capsule sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often subtended by bracts.

The variety stenocarpa is distinguished by its narrow, elongated fruiting capsules, which contribute to its unique botanical identity.
