Calliandra hookeriana

Calliandra hookeriana, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of small, narrow leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Calliandra hookeriana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
