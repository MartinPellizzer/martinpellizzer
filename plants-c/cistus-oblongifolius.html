Cistus oblongifolius

Cistus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grey-green, oblong leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cistus oblongifolius is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
