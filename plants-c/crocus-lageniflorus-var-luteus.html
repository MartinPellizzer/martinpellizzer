Crocus lageniflorus var. luteus

Crocus lageniflorus var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, saucer-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow, with a prominent central stamen. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful, early-season display.
