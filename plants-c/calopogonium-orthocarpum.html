Calopogonium orthocarpum

Calopogonium orthocarpum, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, hairy pods containing small, edible seeds. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
