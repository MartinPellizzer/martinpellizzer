Calliandra paterna

Calliandra paterna, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra paterna is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
