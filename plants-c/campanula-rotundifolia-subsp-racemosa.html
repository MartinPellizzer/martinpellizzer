Campanula rotundifolia subsp. racemosa

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
