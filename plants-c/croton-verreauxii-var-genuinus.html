Croton verreauxii var. genuinus

Croton verreauxii var. genuinus, commonly known as the White Croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful leaves that can range from deep green to red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
