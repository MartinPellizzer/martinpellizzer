Cerasus × sieboldii albaplena

Cerasus × sieboldii albaplena, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, white petals that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This species is also valued for its fragrance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in landscape design.
