Chrysocoma divaricata

Chrysocoma divaricata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
