Capparis ferruginea

Capparis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, often thriving in saline conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, capsaicin-like berries. Its leaves are succulent and have a slightly prickly texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
