Colocasia odora

Colocasia odora, commonly known as the fragrant elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking purple and white spadices, which are its showy flower spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's name reflects its aromatic qualities, particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
