Crotalaria peduncularis

Crotalaria peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria peduncularis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
