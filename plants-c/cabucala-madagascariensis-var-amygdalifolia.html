Cabucala madagascariensis var. amygdalifolia

Cabucala madagascariensis var. amygdalifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar almond tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible fruits that resemble almonds in appearance. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Anacardiaceae family.
