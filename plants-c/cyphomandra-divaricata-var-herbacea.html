Cyphomandra divaricata var. herbacea

Cyphomandra divaricata var. herbacea, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. The fruit contains a soft, creamy pulp with a sweet and sour flavor, and it is often used in culinary applications.
