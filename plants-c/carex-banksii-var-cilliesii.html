Carex banksii var. cilliesii

Carex banksii var. cilliesii, commonly known as the Cillies' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
