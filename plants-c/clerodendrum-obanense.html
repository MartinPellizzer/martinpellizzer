Clerodendrum obanense

Clerodendrum obanense, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum obanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
