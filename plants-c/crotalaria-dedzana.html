Crotalaria dedzana

Crotalaria dedzana, commonly known as the Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height.

Crotalaria dedzana is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
