Cleome macrophylla

Cleome macrophylla, commonly known as the giant cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces clusters of five to seven petals, with prominent stamens that add to its visual appeal. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome macrophylla is a hardy annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.
