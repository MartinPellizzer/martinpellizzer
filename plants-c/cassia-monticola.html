Cassia monticola

Cassia monticola, commonly known as the mountain cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Cassia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
