Campanula jacquinii

Campanula jacquinii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Campanula jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
