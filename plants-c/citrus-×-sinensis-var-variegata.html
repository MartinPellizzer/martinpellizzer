Citrus × sinensis var. variegata

Citrus × sinensis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its unique foliage and fruit.

It exhibits variegated leaf patterns, often featuring white or yellow markings, which distinguish it from standard lemons. The plant is a small tree or shrub, typically growing to a height of 3–5 meters, with fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is similar to the common lemon but may have a slightly different flavor and appearance due to its hybrid nature.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
