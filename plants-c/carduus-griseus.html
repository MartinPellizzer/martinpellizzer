Carduus griseus

Carduus griseus, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus griseus is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
