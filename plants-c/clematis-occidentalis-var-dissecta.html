Clematis occidentalis var. dissecta

Clematis occidentalis var. dissecta, commonly known as the western clematis or dissected clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often appear dissected. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for climbing structures and adding color to garden landscapes.
