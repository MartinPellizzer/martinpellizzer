Cissus psoraliifolia

Cissus psoraliifolia, commonly known as the sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
