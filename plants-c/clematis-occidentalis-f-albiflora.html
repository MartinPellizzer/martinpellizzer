Clematis occidentalis f. albiflora

Clematis occidentalis f. albiflora, commonly known as the western clematis with white flowers, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, white blooms. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that turn white as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
