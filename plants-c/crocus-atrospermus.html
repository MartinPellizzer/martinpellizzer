Crocus atrospermus

Crocus atrospermus, commonly known as the black bulbous crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a dark purple center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, thick stem that supports its showy, cup-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
