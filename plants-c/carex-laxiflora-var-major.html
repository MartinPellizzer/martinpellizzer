Carex laxiflora var. major

Carex laxiflora var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually terminal, containing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
