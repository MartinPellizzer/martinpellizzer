Calophyllum bifurcatum

Calophyllum bifurcatum, commonly known as the splinter tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
