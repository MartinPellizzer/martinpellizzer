Colymbada scabiosa subsp. angustifrons

Colymbada scabiosa subsp.

angustifrons, commonly known as narrowleaf scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose spikes, and range in color from white to pink or purple.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
