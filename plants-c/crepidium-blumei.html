Crepidium blumei

Crepidium blumei, commonly known as the Blume's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, upright stem. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Crepidium blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
