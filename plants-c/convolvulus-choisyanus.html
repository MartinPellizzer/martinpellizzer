Convolvulus choisyanus

Convolvulus choisyanus, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and can also be found growing wild in rocky or dry environments.
