Cissampelos insignis

Cissampelos insignis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
