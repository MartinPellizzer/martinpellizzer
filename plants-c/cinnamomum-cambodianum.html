Cinnamomum cambodianum

Cinnamomum cambodianum, commonly known as Cambodian cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain seeds, which are used for propagation. Its bark and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
