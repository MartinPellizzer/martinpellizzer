Crataegus oxyacantha f. koernickei

Crataegus oxyacantha f. koernickei, commonly known as the Koernicke's hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in the fall.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
