Conoria

Conoria, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a unique plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Conoria typically blooms in the spring, producing clusters of small, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and conservation efforts in dry landscapes.
