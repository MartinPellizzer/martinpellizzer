Cliffortia nitidula subsp. angolensis

Cliffortia nitidula subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
