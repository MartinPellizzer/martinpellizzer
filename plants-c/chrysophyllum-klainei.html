Chrysophyllum klainei

Chrysophyllum klainei, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, white pulp.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
