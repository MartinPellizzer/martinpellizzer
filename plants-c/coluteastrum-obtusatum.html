Coluteastrum obtusatum

Coluteastrum obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved cress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and small white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.
