Carex diehlii

Carex diehlii, commonly known as the Diehl's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in the summer.

Carex diehlii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
