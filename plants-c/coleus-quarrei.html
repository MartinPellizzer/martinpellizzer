Coleus quarrei

Coleus quarrei, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
