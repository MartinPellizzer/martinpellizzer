Cissus sesquipedalis

Cissus sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
