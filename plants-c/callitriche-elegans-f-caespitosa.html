Callitriche elegans f. caespitosa

Callitriche elegans f. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted water starwort, is a compact, herbaceous plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It features small, floating or submerged leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The flowers are tiny, usually white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its delicate appearance. This variety is characterized by its growth habit, forming dense, mat-like colonies that help stabilize aquatic sediments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
