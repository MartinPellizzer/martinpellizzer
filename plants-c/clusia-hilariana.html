Clusia hilariana

Clusia hilariana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia hilariana is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
