Croton rotundifolius

Croton rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are round or oval in shape, often with a deep green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that give way to orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its striking foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
