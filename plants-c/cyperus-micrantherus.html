Cyperus micrantherus

Cyperus micrantherus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Cyperus micrantherus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
