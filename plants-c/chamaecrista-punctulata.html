Chamaecrista punctulata

Chamaecrista punctulata, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the plant is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
