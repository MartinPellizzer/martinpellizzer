Calamus pachystachys

Calamus pachystachys, commonly known as the broad-stemmed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its stout, creeping rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in cooking.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also appreciated in horticulture.
