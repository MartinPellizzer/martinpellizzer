Cryophytum guerichianum

Cryophytum guerichianum, commonly known as the Antarctic hair-crop, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is one of the few flowering plants that can survive in the harsh conditions of the continent, including freezing temperatures and strong winds. The plant has a low-growing, cushion-like structure to minimize exposure to wind and retain heat. Its small, white flowers bloom briefly during the summer months when conditions are most favorable.

Cryophytum guerichianum plays a unique role in the fragile Antarctic ecosystem, contributing to the limited plant life in this remote region.
