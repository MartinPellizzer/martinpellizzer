Cistus tricuspis

Cistus tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is known for its ability to withstand drought and is commonly used in xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
