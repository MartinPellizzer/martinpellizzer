Convolvulus maroccanus

Convolvulus maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

It is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
