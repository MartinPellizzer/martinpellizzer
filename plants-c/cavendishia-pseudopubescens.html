Cavendishia pseudopubescens

Cavendishia pseudopubescens, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
