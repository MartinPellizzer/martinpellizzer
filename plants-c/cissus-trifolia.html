Cissus trifolia

Cissus trifolia, commonly known as the bone vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, lemon-shaped fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus trifolia thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
