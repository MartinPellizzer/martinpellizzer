Convolvulus floridus

Convolvulus floridus, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, funnel-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often climbing or trailing along the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Convolvulus floridus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats and along roadsides.
