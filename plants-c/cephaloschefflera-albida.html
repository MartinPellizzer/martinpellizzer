Cephaloschefflera albida

Cephaloschefflera albida, commonly known as the white sycamore, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
