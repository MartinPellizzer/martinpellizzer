Chaenomeles japonica var. eburnea

Chaenomeles japonica var. eburnea, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, characterized by its ornamental value and distinctive foliage. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues during the growing season. In spring, it produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that precede the development of yellow-orange fruit.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe but is typically used in traditional Japanese cuisine and medicine.
