Cynoctonum dimidiatum

Cynoctonum dimidiatum, commonly known as the half-moon plant, is a unique species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care, it is widely appreciated by plant enthusiasts.
