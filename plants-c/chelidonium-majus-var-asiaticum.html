Chelidonium majus var. asiaticum

Chelidonium majus var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia.

It features a robust, branching root system and produces yellow, orange, or red-orange flowers in early summer. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive yellowish-green underside. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
