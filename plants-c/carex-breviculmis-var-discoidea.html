Carex breviculmis var. discoidea

Carex breviculmis var. discoidea, commonly known as the disk-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique, rounded inflorescence that resembles a disk.
