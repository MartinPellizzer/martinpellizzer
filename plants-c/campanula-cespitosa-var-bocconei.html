Campanula cespitosa var. bocconei

Campanula cespitosa var. bocconei, commonly known as the Bocconei bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features upright, clump-forming stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are basal, grass-like, and arranged in rosettes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
