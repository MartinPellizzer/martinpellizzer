Carex stenophylla var. rigescens

Carex stenophylla var. rigescens, commonly known as rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown to black seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
