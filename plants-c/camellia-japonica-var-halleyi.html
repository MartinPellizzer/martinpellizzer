Camellia japonica var. halleyi

Camellia japonica var. halleyi, commonly known as the Halley camellia, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese camellia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is native to southern China and is valued for its vibrant red and pink petals. It prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is also noted for its evergreen foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the year.
