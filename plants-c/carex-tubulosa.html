Carex tubulosa

Carex tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in early to mid-summer, typically in shades of green or brown. Carex tubulosa thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
