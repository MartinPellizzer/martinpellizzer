Catalpa bignonioides

Catalpa bignonioides, commonly known as the Southern catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain sticky, toxic seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Catalpa bignonioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
