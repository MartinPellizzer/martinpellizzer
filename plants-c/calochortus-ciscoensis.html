Calochortus ciscoensis

Calochortus ciscoensis, commonly known as the Cisco mariposa lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive green throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It produces basal leaves that are grass-like and can reach up to 15 inches in length.

Calochortus ciscoensis is a short-lived perennial, blooming in late spring to early summer, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
