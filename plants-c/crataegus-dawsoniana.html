Crataegus dawsoniana

Crataegus dawsoniana, commonly known as Dawson's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
