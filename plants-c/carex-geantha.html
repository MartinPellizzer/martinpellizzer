Carex geantha

Carex geantha, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex geantha plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
