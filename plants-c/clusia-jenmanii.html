Clusia jenmanii

Clusia jenmanii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that can store water, making it drought-tolerant. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Clusia jenmanii is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its resilience to poor growing conditions.
