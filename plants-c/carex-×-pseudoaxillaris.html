Carex × pseudoaxillaris

Carex × pseudoaxillaris, commonly known as the hybrid marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical spikes of small, usually green or brown flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. The plant has fibrous roots and forms clumps, often spreading through rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
