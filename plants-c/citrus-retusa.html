Citrus retusa

Citrus retusa, commonly known as the Japanese bitter orange, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, thornless bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that give way to small, round, green fruits with a bitter taste. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in the cultivation of other citrus species.
