Cerastium tetrandrum var. alsinoides

Cerastium tetrandrum var. alsinoides, commonly known as the Alsike mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
