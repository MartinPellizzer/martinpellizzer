Calamus impar

Calamus impar, commonly known as the odd-leaved sweet flag, is a member of the Acoraceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its sword-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Acoraceae family.
