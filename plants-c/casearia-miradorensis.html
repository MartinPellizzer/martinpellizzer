Casearia miradorensis

Casearia miradorensis, commonly known as the Mirador tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
