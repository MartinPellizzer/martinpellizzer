Cerastium commersonianum var. commersonianum

Cerastium commersonianum var. commersonianum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
