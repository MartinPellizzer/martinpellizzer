Casselia veronicifolia

Casselia veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf casselia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flattened seeds.

Casselia veronicifolia is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
