Chaetanthera chilensis var. involucrata

Chaetanthera chilensis var. involucrata, commonly known as the Chilean velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
