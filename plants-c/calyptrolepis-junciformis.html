Calyptrolepis junciformis

Calyptrolepis junciformis, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its flowers are often pink or red, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Calyptrolepis junciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
