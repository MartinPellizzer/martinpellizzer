Cyperus controversus var. subexalatus

Cyperus controversus var. subexalatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
