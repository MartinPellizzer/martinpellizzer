Cyathea microlepis

Cyathea microlepis, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically light green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Cyathea microlepis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
