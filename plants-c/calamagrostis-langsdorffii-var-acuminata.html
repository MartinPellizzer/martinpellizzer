Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. acuminata

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray panicles that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
