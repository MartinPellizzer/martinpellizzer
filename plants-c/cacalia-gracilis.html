Cacalia gracilis

Cacalia gracilis, commonly known as slender cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cacalia gracilis is often found in open woodlands and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
