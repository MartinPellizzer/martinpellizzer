Campanopsis graminifolia

Campanopsis graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, and they bloom in late summer.

Campanopsis graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
