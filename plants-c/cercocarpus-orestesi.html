Cercocarpus orestesi

Cercocarpus orestesi, commonly known as the Sierra sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and woodland areas. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
