Calophyllum tetrapterum var. obovale

Calophyllum tetrapterum var. obovale, commonly known as the oval-leaved white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
