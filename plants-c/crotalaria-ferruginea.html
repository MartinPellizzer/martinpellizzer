Crotalaria ferruginea

Crotalaria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins and palmate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It grows as an erect annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
