Columbaria rubella

Columbaria rubella, commonly known as the red columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Columbaria rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
