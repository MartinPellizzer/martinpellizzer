Chironia latifolia

Chironia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chironia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, which bloom during the warm season. Its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and colonize moist, well-drained soils.

Chironia latifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
