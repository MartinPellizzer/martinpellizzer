Cyrtandra × subsolana

Cyrtandra × subsolana, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra solana, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
