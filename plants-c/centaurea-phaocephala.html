Centaurea phaocephala

Centaurea phaocephala, commonly known as the pale-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stem.

Centaurea phaocephala is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
