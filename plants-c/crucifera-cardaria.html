Crucifera cardaria

Crucifera cardaria, commonly known as wild radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a peppery scent when crushed.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
