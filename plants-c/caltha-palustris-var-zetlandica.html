Caltha palustris var. zetlandica

Caltha palustris var. zetlandica, commonly known as Zetland marsh marigold, is a variant of the marsh marigold native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often near streams, ponds, or marshes. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky layer of hair.

The flowers are clustered in a cup-like arrangement, making it a distinctive and ornamental species in its natural environment.
