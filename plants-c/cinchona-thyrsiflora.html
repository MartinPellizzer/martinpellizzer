Cinchona thyrsiflora

Cinchona thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Andean quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds, and it is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the production of quinine.

This species plays an important role in both traditional medicine and ecological systems in its native habitat.
