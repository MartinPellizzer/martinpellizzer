Cynanchum phylladen

Cynanchum phylladen, commonly known as the leafy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
