Calliandra villosiflora

Calliandra villosiflora, commonly known as the hairy pod calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fluffy, yellow or orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
