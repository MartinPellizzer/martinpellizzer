Correa lawrenciana var. ferruginea

Correa lawrenciana var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a distinctive rusty red or orange-brown pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
