Cytisus rhodopnoa

Cytisus rhodopnoa, commonly known as the Greek firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive, orange-red berries that appear in late summer.
