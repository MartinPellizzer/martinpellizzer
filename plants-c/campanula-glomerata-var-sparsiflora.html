Campanula glomerata var. sparsiflora

Campanula glomerata var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsiflora bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
