Cavendishia pubescens var. typica

Cavendishia pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the velvety cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in a dense layer of pale, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
