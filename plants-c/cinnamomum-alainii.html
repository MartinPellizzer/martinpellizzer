Cinnamomum alainii

Cinnamomum alainii, commonly known as the Alain's cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
