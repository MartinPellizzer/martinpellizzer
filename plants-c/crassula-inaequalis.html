Crassula inaequalis

Crassula inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Crassula inaequalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
