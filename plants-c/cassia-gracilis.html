Cassia gracilis

Cassia gracilis, commonly known as slender cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia gracilis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
