Cercis canadensis var. chinensis

Cercis canadensis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese red maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to landscapes. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Due to its ornamental appeal and seasonal color changes, it is often cultivated in parks and residential areas.
