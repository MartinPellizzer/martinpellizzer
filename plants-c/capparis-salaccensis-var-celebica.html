Capparis salaccensis var. celebica

Capparis salaccensis var. celebica, commonly known as the Celebes caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and succulent leaves, which are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
