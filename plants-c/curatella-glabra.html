Curatella glabra

Curatella glabra, commonly known as white curatella, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly smooth. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Curatella glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
