Cyperus brunneovaginatus

Cyperus brunneovaginatus, commonly known as the brown sheath flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus brunneovaginatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
