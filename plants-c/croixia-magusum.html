Croixia magusum

Croixia magusum, commonly known as the sacred flame lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. Its unique coloration and rarity make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
