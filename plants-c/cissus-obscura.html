Cissus obscura

Cissus obscura, commonly known as the African grape or rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are typically consumed raw. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cissus obscura is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to trail from hanging baskets or shelves.
