Chionanthus lanceolatus

Chionanthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, white, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically 3 to 6 inches long. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and well-drained soil.

The narrow-leaf chionanthus is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive floral display.
