Cyperus chrysanthus

Cyperus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and often grows in wet or marshy environments.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental and wetland plant in many parts of the world.
