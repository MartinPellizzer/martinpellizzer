Chamaecrista negrensis var. negrensis

Chamaecrista negrensis var. negrensis, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
