Carex aperta f. concinnula

Carex aperta f. concinnula, commonly known as the slender flatleaf sedge, is a variant of the Carex aperta species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, compact rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
