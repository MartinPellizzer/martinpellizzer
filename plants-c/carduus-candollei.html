Carduus candollei

Carduus candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
