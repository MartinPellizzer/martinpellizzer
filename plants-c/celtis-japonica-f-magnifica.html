Celtis japonica f. magnifica

Celtis japonica f. magnifica, commonly known as the Japanese Hackberry, is a cultivar of the hackberry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter green on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark blue color. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to urban environments.
