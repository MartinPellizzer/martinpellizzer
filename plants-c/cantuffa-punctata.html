Cantuffa punctata

Cantuffa punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in small, raised spots, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Cantuffa punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
