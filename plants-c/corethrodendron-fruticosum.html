Corethrodendron fruticosum

Corethrodendron fruticosum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
