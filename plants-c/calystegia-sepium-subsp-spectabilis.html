Calystegia sepium subsp. spectabilis

Calystegia sepium subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in waste areas. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its showy blooms and vigorous growth habit.

The subspecies spectabilis is distinguished by its more prominent and showy flowers compared to other forms of Calystegia sepium.
