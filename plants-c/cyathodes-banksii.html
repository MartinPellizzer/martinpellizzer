Cyathodes banksii

Cyathodes banksii, commonly known as the Banks' cyathodes, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences called cyathodes, which are typical of the genus. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
