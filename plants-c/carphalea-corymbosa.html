Carphalea corymbosa

Carphalea corymbosa, commonly known as the Mexican coffee plant, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
