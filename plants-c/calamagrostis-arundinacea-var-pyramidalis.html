Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pyramidalis

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy white flowers in late summer, adding a striking visual contrast. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along waterways.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective erosion control plant in wetland restoration projects.
