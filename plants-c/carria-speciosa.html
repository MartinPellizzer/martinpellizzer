Carria speciosa

Carria speciosa, commonly known as the showy caryophyll.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Carria speciosa is typically found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions.
