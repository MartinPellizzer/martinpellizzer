Centaurea × valdesii-bermejoi

Centaurea × valdesii-bermejoi, commonly known as the hybrid centaurea, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, including meadows and rocky areas, and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seed heads that disperse easily by wind.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
