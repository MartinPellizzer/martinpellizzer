Chlorophytum palustre

Chlorophytum palustre, commonly known as the swamp spider plant, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas of South America.

It features long, arching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in marshy or swampy environments.

Chlorophytum palustre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
