Chenopodium album subsp. subficifolium

Chenopodium album subsp.

subficifolium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dry, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
