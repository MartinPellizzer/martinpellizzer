Calamus aquatilis

Calamus aquatilis, commonly known as water reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is a tall, rhizomatous grass that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Calamus aquatilis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
