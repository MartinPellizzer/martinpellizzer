Calophyllum excelsum

Calophyllum excelsum, commonly known as the white cedar or white sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
