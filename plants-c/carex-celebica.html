Carex celebica

Carex celebica, commonly known as the Celebes sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indonesian island of Celebes.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex celebica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
