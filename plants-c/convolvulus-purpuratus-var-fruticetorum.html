Convolvulus purpuratus var. fruticetorum

Convolvulus purpuratus var. fruticetorum, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a trailing or climbing annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
