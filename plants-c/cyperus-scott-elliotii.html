Cyperus scott-elliotii

Cyperus scott-elliotii, commonly known as the Scott-Elliot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus scott-elliotii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wetland wildlife.
