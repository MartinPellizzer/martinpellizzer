Cyanus ermenekensis

Cyanus ermenekensis, commonly known as the Armenian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
