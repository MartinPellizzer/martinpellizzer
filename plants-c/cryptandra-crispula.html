Cryptandra crispula

Cryptandra crispula, commonly known as the rough cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, glandular leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathland and scrub environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
