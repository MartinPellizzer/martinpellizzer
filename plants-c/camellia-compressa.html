Camellia compressa

Camellia compressa, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, double or semi-double flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Camellia compressa thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
