Calluna vulgaris subf. hermaphrodita

Calluna vulgaris subf.

hermaphrodita, commonly known as the purple heather, is a subshrub native to the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically evergreen, and it is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

The flowers are pollinated by a variety of insects, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
