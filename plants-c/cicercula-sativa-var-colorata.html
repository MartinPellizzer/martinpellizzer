Cicercula sativa var. colorata

Cicercula sativa var. colorata, commonly known as the colorful sedge, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
