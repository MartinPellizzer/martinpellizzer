Collinsia rattanii subsp. glandulosa

Collinsia rattanii subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular collinsia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
