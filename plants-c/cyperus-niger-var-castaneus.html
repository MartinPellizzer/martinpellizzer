Cyperus niger var. castaneus

Cyperus niger var. castaneus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its dark purple or blackish stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
