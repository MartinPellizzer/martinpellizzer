Cassia pilocarina

Cassia pilocarina, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing numerous seeds. Cassia pilocarina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves and flowers are rich in flavonoids and have been studied for their potential health benefits.
