Campanula pentagonophylla

Campanula pentagonophylla, commonly known as the five-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate, with five distinct lobes, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
