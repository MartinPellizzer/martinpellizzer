Campanula hyrcania

Campanula hyrcania, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula hyrcania is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
