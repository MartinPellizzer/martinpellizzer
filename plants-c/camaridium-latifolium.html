Camaridium latifolium

Camaridium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf camaridium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and native plant restoration projects.
