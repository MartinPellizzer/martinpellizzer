Circaea cordifolia

Circaea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf whitebead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
