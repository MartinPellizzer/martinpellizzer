Conopodium nodosum

Conopodium nodosum, commonly known as the nodding bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive bladders used for trapping small aquatic organisms.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches, where it can anchor itself with its root system. The plant has thin, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water resistance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of a single pistil and stamens, and it relies on water currents for pollination.

Conopodium nodosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms.
