Centaurea lainzii

Centaurea lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It features bright blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea lainzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
