Cerastium lithospermifolium var. humilius

Cerastium lithospermifolium var. humilius, commonly known as the low-growing stonecrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
