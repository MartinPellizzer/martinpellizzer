Cyphostemma duparquettii

Cyphostemma duparquettii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red fruits. Its botanical features make it suitable for tropical gardens and greenhouses, though it requires well-drained soil and moderate watering.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
