Citrus × florida

Citrus × florida, commonly known as the Mexican lime.

It is a hybrid citrus plant originating from natural crosses between the key lime (Citrus auracana) and the Persian lime (Citrus latifolia). The plant is characterized by its small, yellow, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking and beverages.
