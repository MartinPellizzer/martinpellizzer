Cochlearia officinalis subsp. groenlandica

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal and alpine ecosystems in its native habitats.
