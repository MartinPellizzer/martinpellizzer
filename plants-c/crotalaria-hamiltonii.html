Crotalaria hamiltonii

Crotalaria hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria hamiltonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
