Cololobus rupestris

Cololobus rupestris, commonly known as the rock colobus, is a species of primate found in the forests of East Africa.

It is a member of the Colobinae subfamily and is characterized by its distinctive black-and-white coat. The rock colobus has a specialized digestive system that allows it to efficiently process fibrous plant material. It primarily feeds on leaves, fruits, and flowers, with a preference for young, tender foliage.

This primate is often found in groups and is known for its social and vocal behaviors.
