Clematis aristata var. normalis

Clematis aristata var. normalis, commonly known as the Western virgins' bower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white to pale blue with a distinctive, showy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis aristata var. normalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or fences.
