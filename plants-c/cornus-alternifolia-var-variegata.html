Cornus alternifolia var. variegata

Cornus alternifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces white or pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
