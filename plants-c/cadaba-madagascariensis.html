Cadaba madagascariensis

Cadaba madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rose or Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom during the rainy season. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
