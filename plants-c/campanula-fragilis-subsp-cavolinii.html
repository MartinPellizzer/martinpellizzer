Campanula fragilis subsp. cavolinii

Campanula fragilis subsp.

cavolinii, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming blossoms.
