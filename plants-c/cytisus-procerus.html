Cytisus procerus

Cytisus procerus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a silky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
