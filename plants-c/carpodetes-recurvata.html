Carpodetes recurvata

Carpodetes recurvata, commonly known as the slender grass-of-parnassus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a unique, recurved shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
