Cistus palhinhae

Cistus palhinhae, commonly known as the Azores rockrose, is a shrub native to the Azores Islands in Portugal.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Cistus palhinhae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
