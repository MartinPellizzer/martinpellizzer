Cranzia densiflora

Cranzia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cranza, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Cranzia densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
