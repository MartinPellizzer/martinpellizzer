Cirsium caucasicum

Cirsium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium caucasicum is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
