Chimonobambusa gracilis

Chimonobambusa gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Chimonobambusa gracilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
