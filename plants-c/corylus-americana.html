Corylus americana

Corylus americana, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Corylus americana plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and has historical uses by indigenous peoples.
