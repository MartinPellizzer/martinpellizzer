Chaerophyllum trapezuntinum

Chaerophyllum trapezuntinum, commonly known as the Trapezuntine cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
