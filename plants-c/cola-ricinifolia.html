Cola ricinifolia

Cola ricinifolia, commonly known as African cola or kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, brown pods containing the aromatic seeds known as kola nuts. These nuts contain caffeine and are used in traditional ceremonies and as a stimulant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has economic significance in local communities.
