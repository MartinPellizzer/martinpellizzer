Centaurea seresiensis

Centaurea seresiensis, commonly known as the Seres Mountains centaury, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
