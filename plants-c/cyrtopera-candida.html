Cyrtopera candida

Cyrtopera candida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
