Chamaesyce concanensis

Chamaesyce concanensis, commonly known as the Indian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Chamaesyce concanensis is often found in coastal or arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
