Coelogyne pantlingii

Coelogyne pantlingii, commonly known as the Pink Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne pantlingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
