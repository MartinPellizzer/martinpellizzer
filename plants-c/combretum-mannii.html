Combretum mannii

Combretum mannii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn red or orange before falling in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and striking foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
