Cytisus pseudocajan

Cytisus pseudocajan, commonly known as the false acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes capable of nitrogen fixation.

Cytisus pseudocajan is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
