Coccinia roseiflora

Coccinia roseiflora, commonly known as the pink-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens or as a ground cover.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often containing several seeds.
