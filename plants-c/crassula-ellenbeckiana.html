Crassula ellenbeckiana

Crassula ellenbeckiana, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
