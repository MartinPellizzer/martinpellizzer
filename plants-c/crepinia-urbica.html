Crepinia urbica

Crepinia urbica, commonly known as the urban crepe myrtle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring lance-shaped leaves and a bushy habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
