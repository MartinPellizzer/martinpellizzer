Cassia nummulariifolia

Cassia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the copperleaf or golden shower tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, China, and Indonesia, and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with rounded leaflets, giving it a distinctive, almost coin-like appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

Cassia nummulariifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
