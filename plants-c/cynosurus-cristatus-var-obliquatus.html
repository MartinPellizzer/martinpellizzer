Cynosurus cristatus var. obliquatus

Cynosurus cristatus var. obliquatus, commonly known as the zigzag tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit.

It plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
