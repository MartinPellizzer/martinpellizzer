Cynthia montana

Cynthia montana, commonly known as the mountain cypress, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in areas with high altitude and moderate rainfall. Its botanical features include needle-like leaves and a conical shape, which help it adapt to its harsh mountainous environment.

Cynthia montana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
