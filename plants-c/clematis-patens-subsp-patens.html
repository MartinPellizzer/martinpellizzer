Clematis patens subsp. patens

Clematis patens subsp.

patens, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America. It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 feet long, often climbing or trailing along the ground.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
