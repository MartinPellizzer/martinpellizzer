Cyathea rudimentaris

Cyathea rudimentaris, commonly known as the rudimentary tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the soil, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea rudimentaris is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
