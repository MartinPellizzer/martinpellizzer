Crawfurdia lanceolata

Crawfurdia lanceolata, commonly known as the Natal spurge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a stout, branching stem covered with soft, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, lobed seed pods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Crawfurdia lanceolata is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
