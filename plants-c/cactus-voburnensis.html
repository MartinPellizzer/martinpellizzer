Cactus voburnensis

Cactus voburnensis, commonly known as the Voburna cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the cold climates of northern regions.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and spiny leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its slow growth rate and cold hardiness make it a challenging but rewarding plant for specialized gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
