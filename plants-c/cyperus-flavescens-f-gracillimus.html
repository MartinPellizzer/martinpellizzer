Cyperus flavescens f. gracillimus

Cyperus flavescens f. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish or brownish in hue. It thrives in wet or muddy environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
