Calanthe neglecta

Calanthe neglecta, commonly known as the neglected calanthe, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Calanthe neglecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
