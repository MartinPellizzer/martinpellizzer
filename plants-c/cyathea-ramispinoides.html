Cyathea ramispinoides

Cyathea ramispinoides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, which are used for both ornamental and traditional purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
