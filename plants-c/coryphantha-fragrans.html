Coryphantha fragrans

Coryphantha fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow or white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often fragrant. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Coryphantha fragrans is adapted to arid environments, showcasing typical succulent features for water storage.
