Cineraria ambigua

Cineraria ambigua, commonly known as the Canary Islands cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are often grayish or woolly, providing a contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Cineraria ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
