Cyathea flavovirens

Cyathea flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically bright green with a slight yellowish tinge, giving it its common name.

Cyathea flavovirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded environments.
