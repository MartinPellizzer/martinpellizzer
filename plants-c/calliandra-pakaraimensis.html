Calliandra pakaraimensis

Calliandra pakaraimensis, commonly known as the Pakaraima calliandra, is a shrub native to the Guyana Shield region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, pink to red flower clusters. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
