Carex magnifica

Carex magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that consist of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
