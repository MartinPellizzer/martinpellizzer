Crataegus leptophylla

Crataegus leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Crataegus leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
