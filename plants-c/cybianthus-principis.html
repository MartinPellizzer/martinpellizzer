Cybianthus principis

Cybianthus principis, commonly known as the royal candleplant, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the island of Mauritius and is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous tubular flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Cybianthus principis is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
