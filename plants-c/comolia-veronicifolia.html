Comolia veronicifolia

Comolia veronicifolia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes. It belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae and is known for its low-growing, perennial habit.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
