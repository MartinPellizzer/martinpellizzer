Carex varians

Carex varians, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters called spikes. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex varians is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
