Calea martinicensis

Calea martinicensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening at night and closing in the morning. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Calea martinicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
