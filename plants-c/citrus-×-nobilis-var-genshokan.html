Citrus × nobilis var. genshokan

Citrus × nobilis var. genshokan, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin, is a citrus hybrid originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, rounded fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is prized for its aromatic zest and juice.
