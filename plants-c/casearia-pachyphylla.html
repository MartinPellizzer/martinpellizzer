Casearia pachyphylla

Casearia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
