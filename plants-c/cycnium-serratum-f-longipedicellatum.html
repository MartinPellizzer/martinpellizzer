Cycnium serratum f. longipedicellatum

Cycnium serratum f. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked horse chestnut, is a variant of the horse chestnut plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
