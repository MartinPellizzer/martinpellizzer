Cyrtonema divergens

Cyrtonema divergens, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange to red calyces that resemble small lanterns, which persist throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by yellowish fruits. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.
