Coleus sahyadricus

Coleus sahyadricus, commonly known as the Western Ghats coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
