Cyphostemma simulans

Cyphostemma simulans, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
