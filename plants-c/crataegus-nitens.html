Crataegus nitens

Crataegus nitens, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Crataegus nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
