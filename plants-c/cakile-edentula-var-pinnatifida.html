Cakile edentula var. pinnatifida

Cakile edentula var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnatifid sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or gravelly soils.
