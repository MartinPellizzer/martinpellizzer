Corymbia plena

Corymbia plena, commonly known as the fully double red cottonwood, is a striking ornamental tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy inflorescences that are entirely composed of small, fluffy pink to red flowers, giving it a unique and dramatic appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and melaleuca. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces due to its vibrant display and relatively low maintenance requirements.
