Cyperus spicatocapitatus

Cyperus spicatocapitatus, commonly known as the spiculate flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along water bodies.

Cyperus spicatocapitatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
