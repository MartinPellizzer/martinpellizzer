Carex willdenowii

Carex willdenowii, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
