Caladium plowmanii

Caladium plowmanii, commonly known as the Florida caladium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with contrasting veins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Caladium plowmanii is typically propagated through rhizomes and is relatively disease-resistant.
