Coelachne pulchella var. gracillima

Coelachne pulchella var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pale flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
