Conanthus demissus

Conanthus demissus, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This plant is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
