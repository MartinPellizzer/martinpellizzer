Cryptantha pumila

Cryptantha pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cryptantha, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as gravelly soils and open slopes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet in color, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cryptantha pumila is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
