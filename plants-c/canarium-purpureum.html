Canarium purpureum

Canarium purpureum, commonly known as the purple candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit with a hard, woody shell enclosing a single seed. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and for making natural adhesives.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
