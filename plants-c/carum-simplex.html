Carum simplex

Carum simplex, commonly known as lesser cumin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its seeds are used in culinary applications, similar to other members of the Apiaceae family.

Carum simplex is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
