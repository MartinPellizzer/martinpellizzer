Centaurea ornata var. agustisquama

Centaurea ornata var. agustisquama, commonly known as the coastal centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
