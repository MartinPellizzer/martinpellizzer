Centrostachys moquinii

Centrostachys moquinii, commonly known as the mountain bluemink or yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centrostachys moquinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
