Coleus urticifolius

Coleus urticifolius, commonly known as the stinging nettleleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually dark green with a contrasting lighter underside, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in certain cultures.
