Citrus × sinensis subsp. hierochuntica

Citrus × sinensis subsp.

hierochuntica, commonly known as the Hierochuntic lemon, is a subspecies of the common lemon that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics. It is native to the Crimean Peninsula and is distinguished by its smaller, more elongated fruits compared to typical lemons.

The plant has thick, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically less acidic and has a sweeter flavor profile, making it unique among citrus varieties.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in specialty culinary applications.
