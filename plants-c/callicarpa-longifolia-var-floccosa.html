Callicarpa longifolia var. floccosa

Callicarpa longifolia var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are highly attractive to birds. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture on the upper surface. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking fruit and unique foliage, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
