Cremosperma cinnabarinum

Cremosperma cinnabarinum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red or reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The tree is also notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and valuable resource in its native habitat.
