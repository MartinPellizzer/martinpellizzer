Cyperus chrysanthus var. occidentalis

Cyperus chrysanthus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, yellowish flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to form dense colonies.
