Cardamine impatiens var. patulipes

Cardamine impatiens var. patulipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features alternate, broad, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its foliage is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines for its peppery flavor.
