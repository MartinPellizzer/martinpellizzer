Chrysophyllum marginatum var. salicifolium

Chrysophyllum marginatum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a yellowish margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into orange-yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional medicine.
