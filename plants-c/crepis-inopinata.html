Crepis inopinata

Crepis inopinata, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. It has small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pale yellow to white in color.

Crepis inopinata is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
