Clematis dioica var. australis

Clematis dioica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian native clematis, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
