Cornus canadensis f. dutillyi

Cornus canadensis f. dutillyi, commonly known as the dwarf cornel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the family Cornaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with smooth margins and a dark green upper surface.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
