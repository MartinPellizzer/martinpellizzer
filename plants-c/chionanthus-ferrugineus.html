Chionanthus ferrugineus

Chionanthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, rust-colored pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The rusty chionanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
