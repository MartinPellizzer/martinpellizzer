Campylospermum retinerve

Campylospermum retinerve, commonly known as the veined campylospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Campylospermum retinerve is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
