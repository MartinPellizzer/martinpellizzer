Calamagrostis × vassiljevii

Calamagrostis × vassiljevii, commonly known as the 'Morning Light' switchgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and habitat provision for wildlife.
