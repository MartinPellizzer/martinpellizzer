Cyathea steyermarkii

Cyathea steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can persist for several years, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea steyermarkii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
