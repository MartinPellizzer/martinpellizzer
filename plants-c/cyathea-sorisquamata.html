Cyathea sorisquamata

Cyathea sorisquamata, commonly known as the saddle-backed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

Cyathea sorisquamata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
