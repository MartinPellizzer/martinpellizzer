Calanthe testacea

Calanthe testacea, commonly known as the red-tipped calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its flowers are borne on tall, upright spikes and are often adorned with a distinctive yellow-green lip.

Calanthe testacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic beauty.
