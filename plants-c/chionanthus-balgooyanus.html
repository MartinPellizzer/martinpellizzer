Chionanthus balgooyanus

Chionanthus balgooyanus, commonly known as the Balgooyan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
