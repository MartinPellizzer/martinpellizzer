Crocus schneideri

Crocus schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in southern Europe and North Africa. This perennial plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
