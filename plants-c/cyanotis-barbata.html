Cyanotis barbata

Cyanotis barbata, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and a short, stocky stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cyanotis barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
