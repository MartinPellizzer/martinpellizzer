Cordia nelsonii

Cordia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cordia nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
