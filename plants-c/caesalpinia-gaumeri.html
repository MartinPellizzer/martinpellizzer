Caesalpinia gaumeri

Caesalpinia gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red and orange floral bracts that surround small yellow flowers, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, tropical environments.

Caesalpinia gaumeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
