Carex moorei

Carex moorei, commonly known as Moore's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Carex moorei typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in forest margins, stream banks, and wetlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
