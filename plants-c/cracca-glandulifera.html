Cracca glandulifera

Cracca glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a hairy stem and produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
