Cotoneaster melanotrichus

Cotoneaster melanotrichus, commonly known as the black-haired cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. Its berries are typically red or black, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Cotoneaster melanotrichus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
