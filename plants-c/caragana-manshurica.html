Caragana manshurica

Caragana manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian shrub or Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are often used for reclamation of degraded lands.

Caragana manshurica is valued for its hardiness and ability to prevent soil erosion.
