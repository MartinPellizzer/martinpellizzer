Cyperus laevigatus var. pictus

Cyperus laevigatus var. pictus, commonly known as the Variegated Umbrella Plant, is a striking ornamental grass native to Africa.

It features tall, arching stems with feathery, bluish-green foliage that has distinctive white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in summer, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that add visual interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
