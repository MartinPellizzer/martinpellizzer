Calamus insignis

Calamus insignis, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its jointed, herbaceous stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Calamus insignis is notable for its ancient lineage, having existed since the Paleozoic era.
