Caricina brizoidea

Caricina brizoidea, commonly known as the silky carina, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
