Cyperus holosericeus

Cyperus holosericeus, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted clusters of silver-white bristles at the tips of its flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.

Cyperus holosericeus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
