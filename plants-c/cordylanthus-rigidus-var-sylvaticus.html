Cordylanthus rigidus var. sylvaticus

Cordylanthus rigidus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining soil stability in arid environments.
