Chionanthus camptoneurus

Chionanthus camptoneurus, commonly known as the nodding chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble small candles, which bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The nodding chionanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
