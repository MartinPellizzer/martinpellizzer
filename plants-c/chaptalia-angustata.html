Chaptalia angustata

Chaptalia angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
