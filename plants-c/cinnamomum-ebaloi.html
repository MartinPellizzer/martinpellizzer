Cinnamomum ebaloi

Cinnamomum ebaloi, commonly known as the Bornean cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and ellipsoid berries that contain a single seed. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a leathery texture and emit a strong cinnamon-like scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
