Cerastium banaticum var. leontopodium

Cerastium banaticum var. leontopodium, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, covered in fine hairs, and often form a rosette at the base.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
