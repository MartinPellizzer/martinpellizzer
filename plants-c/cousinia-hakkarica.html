Cousinia hakkarica

Cousinia hakkarica, commonly known as the Kurdish cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves, with narrow, grass-like upper leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia hakkarica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
