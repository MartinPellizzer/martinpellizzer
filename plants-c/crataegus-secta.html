Crataegus secta

Crataegus secta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
