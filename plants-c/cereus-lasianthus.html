Cereus lasianthus

Cereus lasianthus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its flowers are usually short-lived, lasting only a single night.

Cereus lasianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
