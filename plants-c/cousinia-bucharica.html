Cousinia bucharica

Cousinia bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss, typical of xerophytic plants.

Cousinia bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
