Cyperus pseudoalatus

Cyperus pseudoalatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, often growing in wet or marshy environments. Cyperus pseudoalatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It is often found in wetlands, rice fields, and other moist habitats where it can thrive in standing water or saturated soil.
