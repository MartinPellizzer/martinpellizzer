Cynanchum physocarpum

Cynanchum physocarpum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, balloon-like fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
