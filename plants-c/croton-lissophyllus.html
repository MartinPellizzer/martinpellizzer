Croton lissophyllus

Croton lissophyllus, commonly known as the silver leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
