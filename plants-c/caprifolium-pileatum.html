Caprifolium pileatum

Caprifolium pileatum, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its stems are typically reddish-brown and can become woody with age.

Caprifolium pileatum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
