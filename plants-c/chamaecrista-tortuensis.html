Chamaecrista tortuensis

Chamaecrista tortuensis, commonly known as the zigzag cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a zigzag or twisted appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming during the wet season. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as savannas and forest edges.

Chamaecrista tortuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
