Chylismia claviformis var. typica

Chylismia claviformis var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chylismia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
