Coelogyne smithiana

Coelogyne smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces a single inflorescence with multiple flowers, each having three petals and a lip that is often marked with intricate patterns. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Coelogyne smithiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
