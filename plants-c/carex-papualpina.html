Carex papualpina

Carex papualpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Carex papualpina is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain wind-pollinated flowers, which is a common reproductive strategy among sedges.
