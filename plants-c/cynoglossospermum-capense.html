Cynoglossospermum capense

Cynoglossospermum capense, commonly known as the African silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
