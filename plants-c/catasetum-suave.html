Catasetum suave

Catasetum suave, commonly known as the sweet catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are known for their unique structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insects.

Catasetum suave is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
