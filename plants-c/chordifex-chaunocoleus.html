Chordifex chaunocoleus

Chordifex chaunocoleus, commonly known as the blue-green moss, is a species of liverwort characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or on rocky substrates. The plant's leaves are small, overlapping, and have a distinct blue-green coloration due to the presence of chlorophyll and carotenoids. It reproduces through spores released from specialized structures called capsules.

This non-vascular plant plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
