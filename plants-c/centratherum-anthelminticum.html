Centratherum anthelminticum

Centratherum anthelminticum, commonly known as the Indian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
