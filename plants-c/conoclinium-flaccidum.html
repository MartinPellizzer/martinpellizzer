Conoclinium flaccidum

Conoclinium flaccidum, commonly known as the Mexican mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Conoclinium flaccidum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
