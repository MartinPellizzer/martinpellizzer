Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. anthelminthicum

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

anthelminthicum, commonly known as wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and small, greenish flowers.

The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is traditionally used for its anthelmintic effects, helping to expel parasitic worms.

Its leaves and seeds are often harvested for use in herbal remedies and traditional medicine.
