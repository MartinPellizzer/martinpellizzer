Calligonum intertextum

Calligonum intertextum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and saline soils, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in unstable desert soils.

Calligonum intertextum plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
