Cyanus cyanomorphus

Cyanus cyanomorphus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
