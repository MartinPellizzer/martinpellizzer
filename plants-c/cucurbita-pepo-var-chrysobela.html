Cucurbita pepo var. chrysobela

Cucurbita pepo var. chrysobela, commonly known as the golden pumpkin, is a cultivated variant of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange-colored fruit, which is typically round or slightly oval in shape. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas. It produces large, lobed leaves and tendrils typical of the gourd family.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications due to its attractive appearance and versatile use.
