Crocus imperatonianus

Crocus imperatonianus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, showy spathe that encloses the true flowers. It typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

The corms of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like pigments.
