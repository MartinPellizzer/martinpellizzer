Cranfillia tovii

Cranfillia tovii, commonly known as the Tovii's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Native to a specific region in Mexico, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. It produces cones rather than flowers, typical of cycads, and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
