Calamagrostis wiluica

Calamagrostis wiluica, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to six feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis wiluica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
