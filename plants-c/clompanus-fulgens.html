Clompanus fulgens

Clompanus fulgens, commonly known as the fiery bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Clompanus fulgens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
