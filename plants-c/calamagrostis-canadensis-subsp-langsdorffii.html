Calamagrostis canadensis subsp. langsdorffii

Calamagrostis canadensis subsp.

langsdorffii, commonly known as the Canadian reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

This subspecies thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
