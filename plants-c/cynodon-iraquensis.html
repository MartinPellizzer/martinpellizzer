Cynodon iraquensis

Cynodon iraquensis, commonly known as the Persian grass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, bluish-green leaves that are often used for forage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for erosion control and pastureland.

Cynodon iraquensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
