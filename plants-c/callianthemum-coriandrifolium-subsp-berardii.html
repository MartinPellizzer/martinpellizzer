Callianthemum coriandrifolium subsp. berardii

Callianthemum coriandrifolium subsp.

berardii, commonly known as the Berard's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are pale yellow to white in color.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in pollinator support in its native range.
