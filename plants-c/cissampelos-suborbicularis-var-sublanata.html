Cissampelos suborbicularis var. sublanata

Cissampelos suborbicularis var. sublanata, commonly known as the velvety leaf moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name, with the underside often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by round, orange-red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
