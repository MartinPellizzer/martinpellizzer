Crataegus shallotte

Crataegus shallotte, commonly known as the French thornapple, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in woodland areas and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often characterized by its thorny stems.

Crataegus shallotte is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
