Cynanchum eggersii

Cynanchum eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its chemical composition includes various alkaloids, which may contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
