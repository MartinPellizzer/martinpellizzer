Calamintha menthifolia var. sylvatica

Calamintha menthifolia var. sylvatica, commonly known as woodland germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils in shaded to partially sunny conditions.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
