Coleus alpinus

Coleus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine coleus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Coleus alpinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate cooler climates.
