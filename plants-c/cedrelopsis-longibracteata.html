Cedrelopsis longibracteata

Cedrelopsis longibracteata, commonly known as the African cedar or African cypress, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and distinctive, flattened, scale-like bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cedrelopsis longibracteata is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
