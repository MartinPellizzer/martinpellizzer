Carex lasiocarpa var. latifolia

Carex lasiocarpa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved haircap moss, is a perennial sedge native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland or boggy areas.
