Clusia heterocolorata

Clusia heterocolorata, commonly known as the variegated glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking variegation of white and yellow, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its ability to withstand salt spray makes it well-suited for coastal environments.
