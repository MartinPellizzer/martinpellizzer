Campanula rotundifolia f. laciniata

Campanula rotundifolia f. laciniata, commonly known as the lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The foliage and flowers are both used in herbal remedies for their purported calming and medicinal properties.
