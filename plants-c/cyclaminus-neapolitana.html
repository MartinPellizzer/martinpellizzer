Cyclaminus neapolitana

Cyclaminus neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cyclaminus neapolitana is known for its short blooming period, typically in spring, and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
