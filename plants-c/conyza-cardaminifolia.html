Conyza cardaminifolia

Conyza cardaminifolia, commonly known as false dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Conyza cardaminifolia is often considered a weed but has ecological and potential medicinal significance.
