Crepinella montana

Crepinella montana, commonly known as the mountain crepinella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Crepinella montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
