Centaurea contracta

Centaurea contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has slender, erect stems and narrow, blue to purple leaves.

The small, bright blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
