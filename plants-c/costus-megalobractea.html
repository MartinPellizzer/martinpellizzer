Costus megalobractea

Costus megalobractea, commonly known as the giant bract costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, showy bracts that resemble petals, giving it a floral appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Costus megalobractea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
