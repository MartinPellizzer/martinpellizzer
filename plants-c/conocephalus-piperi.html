Conocephalus piperi

Conocephalus piperi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic to many animals, including mammals and birds.

Conocephalus piperi plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
