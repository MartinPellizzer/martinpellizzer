Cybianthus tamanus

Cybianthus tamanus, commonly known as the Tamanus lily, is a flowering plant native to the island of Taman.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Cybianthus tamanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
