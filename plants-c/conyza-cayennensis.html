Conyza cayennensis

Conyza cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
