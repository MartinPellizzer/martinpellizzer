Crocus vernus var. alpinus

Crocus vernus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It produces early spring blooms of pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive purple-pink stripe down the center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in mountainous areas where it withstands cold temperatures and short growing seasons.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
