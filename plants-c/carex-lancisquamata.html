Carex lancisquamata

Carex lancisquamata, commonly known as the narrow-sheath sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded areas.

Carex lancisquamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
