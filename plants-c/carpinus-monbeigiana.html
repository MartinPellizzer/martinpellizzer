Carpinus monbeigiana

Carpinus monbeigiana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
