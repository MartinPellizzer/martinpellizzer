Camellia septempetala var. rubra

Camellia septempetala var. rubra, commonly known as the red camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces woody, branched stems and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

The flowers bloom in winter and early spring, adding color to the landscape during colder months.
