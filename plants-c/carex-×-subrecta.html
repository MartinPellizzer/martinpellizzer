Carex × subrecta

Carex × subrecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and the edges of ponds.

Carex × subrecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
