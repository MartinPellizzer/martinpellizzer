Celastrus paniculatus var. venulosoides

Celastrus paniculatus var. venulosoides, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
