Cirsium heterolepis

Cirsium heterolepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a tall perennial herb native to North America.

It features distinctive purple to violet flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has spiny, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, cylindrical stem that is often reddish in color. It thrives in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical name reflects its unique combination of leaf and flower structures.
