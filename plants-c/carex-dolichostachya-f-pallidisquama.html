Carex dolichostachya f. pallidisquama

Carex dolichostachya f. pallidisquama, commonly known as the pale-squamate common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, three-lobed spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its distinctive foliage and is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
