Claytonia linaria

Claytonia linaria, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that split open when mature.

Claytonia linaria is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
