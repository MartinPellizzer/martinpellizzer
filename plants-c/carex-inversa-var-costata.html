Carex inversa var. costata

Carex inversa var. costata, commonly known as the ribbed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
