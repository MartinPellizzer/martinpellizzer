Cussonia

Cussonia, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyclanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. Cussonia species are often grown as ornamental plants due to their striking foliage.

They are also sometimes used in traditional medicine and for their starchy roots in some cultures.
