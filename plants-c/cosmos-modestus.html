Cosmos modestus

Cosmos modestus, commonly known as the dwarf cosmos, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from summer to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Cosmos modestus is easy to grow from seed and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
