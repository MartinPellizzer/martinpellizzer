Caralluma europaea var. gattefossei

Caralluma europaea var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in soft, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-shaped fruits. The fruit's outer husk turns from green to red or orange as it matures.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
