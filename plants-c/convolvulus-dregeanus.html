Convolvulus dregeanus

Convolvulus dregeanus, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has twining stems that climb or trail along the ground, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and hairy. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
