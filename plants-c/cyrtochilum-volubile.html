Cyrtochilum volubile

Cyrtochilum volubile, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, allowing it to climb trees or other structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. Cyrtochilum volubile thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
