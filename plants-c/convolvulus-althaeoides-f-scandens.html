Convolvulus althaeoides f. scandens

Convolvulus althaeoides f. scandens, commonly known as the creeping bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
