Cirsium chikabumiense

Cirsium chikabumiense, commonly known as the Chikabumi thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent serrations. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Cirsium chikabumiense is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.
