Campomanesia gomesiana

Campomanesia gomesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
