Cyperus glaucus

Cyperus glaucus, commonly known as blue flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
