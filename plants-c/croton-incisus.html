Croton incisus

Croton incisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are typically narrow and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Croton incisus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
