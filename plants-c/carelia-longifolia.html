Carelia longifolia

Carelia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf carelia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
