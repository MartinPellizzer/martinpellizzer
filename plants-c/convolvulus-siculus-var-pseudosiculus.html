Convolvulus siculus var. pseudosiculus

Convolvulus siculus var. pseudosiculus, commonly known as the Sicilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped, violet to pink flowers, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
