Cladium microstachyum

Cladium microstachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Cladium microstachyum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its adaptability to fluctuating water levels makes it a key component of many freshwater ecosystems.
