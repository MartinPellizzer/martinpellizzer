Combretum grandifolium subsp. katangense

Combretum grandifolium subsp.

katangense, commonly known as the Katanga flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and East Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange during the dry season.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
