Calyptranthes pallens var. williamsii

Calyptranthes pallens var. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' calyptranthes, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This variety is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
