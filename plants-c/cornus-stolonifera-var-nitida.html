Cornus stolonifera var. nitida

Cornus stolonifera var. nitida, commonly known as the silver thread dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a silvery-white underside that gives it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
