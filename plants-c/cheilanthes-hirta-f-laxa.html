Cheilanthes hirta f. laxa

Cheilanthes hirta f. laxa, commonly known as the laxa form of the hairy maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices, showcasing its ability to colonize harsh environments.
