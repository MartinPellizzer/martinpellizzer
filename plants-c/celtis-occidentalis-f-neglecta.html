Celtis occidentalis f. neglecta

Celtis occidentalis f. neglecta, commonly known as the American hackberry or a type of hackberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife.

It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
