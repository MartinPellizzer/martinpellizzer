Catharanthus ovalis

Catharanthus ovalis, commonly known as the oval Madagascar plume, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it produces elongated, plume-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
