Cassia megalantha

Cassia megalantha, commonly known as the giant senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.

Cassia megalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
