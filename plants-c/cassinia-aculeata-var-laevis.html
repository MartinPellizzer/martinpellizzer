Cassinia aculeata var. laevis

Cassinia aculeata var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are usually smooth and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by wind.
