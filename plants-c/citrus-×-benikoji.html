Citrus × benikoji

Citrus × benikoji, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originated in Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruits. The plant typically has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a smooth, thin rind that is easy to peel.
