Cacalia saracenica

Cacalia saracenica, commonly known as the Saracen's bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cacalia saracenica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
