Chamissoa altissima var. grandispicata

Chamissoa altissima var. grandispicata, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
