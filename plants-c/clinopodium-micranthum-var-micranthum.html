Clinopodium micranthum var. micranthum

Clinopodium micranthum var. micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf mintweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

The species is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats, making it a common ground cover in its native habitat.
