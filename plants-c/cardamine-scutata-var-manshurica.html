Cardamine scutata var. manshurica

Cardamine scutata var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian water shield, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features a rosette of pinnately divided, lanceolate leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are borne on slender stems and are arranged in a single umbel.

The species is notable for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
