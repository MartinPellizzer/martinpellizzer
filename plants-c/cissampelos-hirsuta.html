Cissampelos hirsuta

Cissampelos hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissampelos hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
