Crotalaria glauca var. genistifolia

Crotalaria glauca var. genistifolia, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
