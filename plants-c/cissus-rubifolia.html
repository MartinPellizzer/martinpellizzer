Cissus rubifolia

Cissus rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish or purple-colored leaves, which are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus rubifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
