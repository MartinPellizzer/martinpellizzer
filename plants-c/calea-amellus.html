Calea amellus

Calea amellus, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically violet or purple in color and emit a mild fragrance.

Calea amellus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
