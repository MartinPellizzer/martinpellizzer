Cinnamomum glanduliferum var. longipaniculatum

Cinnamomum glanduliferum var. longipaniculatum, commonly known as the long panicle cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into capsule fruits. The plant produces a fragrant bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
