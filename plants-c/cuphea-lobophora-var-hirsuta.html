Cuphea lobophora var. hirsuta

Cuphea lobophora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact size.

Cuphea lobophora var. hirsuta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
