Cichorium minimum

Cichorium minimum, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often used in salads or as a garnish. Its roots are also used to produce a bitter, caffeine-free beverage.

Cichorium minimum is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
