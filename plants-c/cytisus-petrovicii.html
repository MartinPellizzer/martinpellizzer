Cytisus petrovicii

Cytisus petrovicii, commonly known as the Serbian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus petrovicii is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
