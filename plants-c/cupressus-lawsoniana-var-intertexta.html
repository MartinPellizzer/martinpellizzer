Cupressus lawsoniana var. intertexta

Cupressus lawsoniana var. intertexta, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a variant of the Lawson cypress, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green foliage.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its attractive form and resilience. The plant features scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions, including pollution and compacted soils.
