Canarium pachyphyllum

Canarium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved candlenut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that provide resistance to drought and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by hard, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and sustainable agriculture.
