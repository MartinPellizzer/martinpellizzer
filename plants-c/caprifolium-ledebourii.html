Caprifolium ledebourii

Caprifolium ledebourii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Caprifolium ledebourii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
