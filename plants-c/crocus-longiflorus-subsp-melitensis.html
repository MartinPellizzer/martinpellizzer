Crocus longiflorus subsp. melitensis

Crocus longiflorus subsp.

melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive violet or purple stripe.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering crocuses. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This subspecies is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
