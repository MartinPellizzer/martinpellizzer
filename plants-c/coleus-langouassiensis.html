Coleus langouassiensis

Coleus langouassiensis, commonly known as the Langouassien coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a wide range of hues. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often with a distinctive blue or purple coloration.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
