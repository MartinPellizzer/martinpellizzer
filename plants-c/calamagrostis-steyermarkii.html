Calamagrostis steyermarkii

Calamagrostis steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender inflorescences that are often pink or purplish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Calamagrostis steyermarkii is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
