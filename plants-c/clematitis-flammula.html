Clematitis flammula

Clematitis flammula, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in woodland habitats. The plant features small, showy flowers with a distinctive red or orange hue, often with a purple or blue throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Clematitis flammula is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
