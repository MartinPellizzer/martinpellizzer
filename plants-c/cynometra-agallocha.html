Cynometra agallocha

Cynometra agallocha, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Ghana and Nigeria.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fuzzy fruits containing hard-shelled seeds. Its seeds are used to make shea butter, a valuable commodity in cosmetics and food industries.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and soil conservation.
