Cladium mariscus subsp. jamaicense

Cladium mariscus subsp.

jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica. It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant thrives in freshwater or brackish marshes and is adapted to periodically flooded environments. Its rhizomes form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Cladium mariscus subsp.

jamaicense plays an important ecological role in maintaining the structure and function of wetland ecosystems.
