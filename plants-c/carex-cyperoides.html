Carex cyperoides

Carex cyperoides, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Carex cyperoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
