Cheirolophus sventenii subsp. sventenii

Cheirolophus sventenii subsp.

sventenii, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
