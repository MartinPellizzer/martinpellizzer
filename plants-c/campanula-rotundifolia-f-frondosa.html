Campanula rotundifolia f. frondosa

Campanula rotundifolia f. frondosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant's foliage is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of this form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover.
