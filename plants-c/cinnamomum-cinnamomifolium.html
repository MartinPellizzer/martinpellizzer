Cinnamomum cinnamomifolium

Cinnamomum cinnamomifolium, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental and commercial value.
