Castanopsis trinervis

Castanopsis trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined chestnut.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical forests.

Castanopsis trinervis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
