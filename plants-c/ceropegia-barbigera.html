Ceropegia barbigera

Ceropegia barbigera, commonly known as the woolly ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial succulent that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features fleshy, branching stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and tubular, often with a distinctive hood-like structure, and are pollinated by specific insects.

Ceropegia barbigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
