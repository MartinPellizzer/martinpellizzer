Chamaedactylis maritima

Chamaedactylis maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces spiky, blue or purple flower heads that bloom in summer. Its robust root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

It is well adapted to saline and windy environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
