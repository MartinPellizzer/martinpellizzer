Carex kotoensis

Carex kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers.

Carex kotoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
