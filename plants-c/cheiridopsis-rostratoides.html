Cheiridopsis rostratoides

Cheiridopsis rostratoides, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it survive in arid conditions.

Cheiridopsis rostratoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
