Carex caricina

Carex caricina, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a terminal inflorescence. Carex caricina is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
