Cestrum gracile

Cestrum gracile, commonly known as the slender cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, while its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain several seeds.

Cestrum gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
