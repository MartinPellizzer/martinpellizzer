Cupressus fastigiata

Cupressus fastigiata, commonly known as the upright cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various climates.
