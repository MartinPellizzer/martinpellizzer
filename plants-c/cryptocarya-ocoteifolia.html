Cryptocarya ocoteifolia

Cryptocarya ocoteifolia, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya ocoteifolia is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
