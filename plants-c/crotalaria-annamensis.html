Crotalaria annamensis

Crotalaria annamensis, commonly known as the Annam Crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Crotalaria annamensis is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
