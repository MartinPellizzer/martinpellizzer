Cladium erythrocarpum

Cladium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and swampy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. Cladium erythrocarpum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its ability to thrive in saturated soils makes it a valuable indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
