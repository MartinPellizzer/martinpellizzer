Celastrus macrocarpus

Celastrus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited staff vine, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruits are distinctive, fleshy, and berry-like, containing several seeds.

Celastrus macrocarpus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
