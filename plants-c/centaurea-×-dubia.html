Centaurea × dubia

Centaurea × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea × dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
