Ceropegia pachyrrhiza

Ceropegia pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the fat-tailed spider plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Ceropegia pachyrrhiza prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
