Cyperus montis-sellae

Cyperus montis-sellae, commonly known as the mountain sella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Cyperus montis-sellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
