Columnea hiantiflora

Columnea hiantiflora, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly beautiful shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Columnea hiantiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
