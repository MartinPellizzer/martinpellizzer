Convolvulus lanuginosus var. angustifolia

Convolvulus lanuginosus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ascending stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is closely related to other convolvulus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
