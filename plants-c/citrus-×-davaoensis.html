Citrus × davaoensis

Citrus × davaoensis, commonly known as the Davao citrus or Davao orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically orange in color with a thick, aromatic rind. The plant is evergreen, with dark green, glossy leaves and produces flowers that are white with a yellowish tinge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in culinary and commercial applications.
