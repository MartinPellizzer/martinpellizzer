Carex sadleri

Carex sadleri, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

Carex sadleri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
