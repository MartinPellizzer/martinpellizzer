Cucubalus lugdunensis

Cucubalus lugdunensis, commonly known as the Lugdunense Cucurbit, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically elongated and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
