Carduus onopordioides subsp. onopordioides

Carduus onopordioides subsp.

onopordioides, commonly known as the crested dog's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust stem with distinctive, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the plant is known for its strong, sometimes foul odor.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
