Cerasus apetala f. multipetala

Cerasus apetala f. multipetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry with multiple petals, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
