Claytonia linearis var. dichotoma

Claytonia linearis var. dichotoma, commonly known as the forked springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often branch into two, giving it its varietal name "dichotoma.". The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
