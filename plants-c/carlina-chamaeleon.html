Carlina chamaeleon

Carlina chamaeleon, commonly known as the mountain carline, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its showy yellow flowers appear in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Carlina chamaeleon is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
