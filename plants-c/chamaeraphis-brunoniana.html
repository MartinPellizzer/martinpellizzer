Chamaeraphis brunoniana

Chamaeraphis brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's glassworts, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its small, green flowers are clustered in dense spikes. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Chamaeraphis brunoniana is also used in traditional medicine by local communities for its potential health benefits.
