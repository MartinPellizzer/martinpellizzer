Codiaeum wilsonii

Codiaeum wilsonii, commonly known as the flame tree or croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, yellow, and green, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

Codiaeum wilsonii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
