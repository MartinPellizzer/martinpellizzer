Carpodetus pullei

Carpodetus pullei, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
