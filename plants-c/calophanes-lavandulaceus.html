Calophanes lavandulaceus

Calophanes lavandulaceus, commonly known as the lavender-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble those of lavender. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
