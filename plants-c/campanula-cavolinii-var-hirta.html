Campanula cavolinii var. hirta

Campanula cavolinii var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name "hirta," meaning hairy in Latin. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula cavolinii var. hirta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
