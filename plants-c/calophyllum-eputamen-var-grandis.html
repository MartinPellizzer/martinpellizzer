Calophyllum eputamen var. grandis

Calophyllum eputamen var. grandis, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
