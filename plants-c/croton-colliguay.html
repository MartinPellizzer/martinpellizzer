Croton colliguay

Croton colliguay, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with serrated edges.

Croton colliguay is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.
