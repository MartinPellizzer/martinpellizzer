Campanula lingulata var. capitata

Campanula lingulata var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
