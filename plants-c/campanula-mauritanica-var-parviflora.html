Campanula mauritanica var. parviflora

Campanula mauritanica var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
