Clematis anemonoides

Clematis anemonoides, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that resemble anemones, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
