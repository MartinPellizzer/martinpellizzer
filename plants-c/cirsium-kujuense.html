Cirsium kujuense

Cirsium kujuense, commonly known as the Kuju thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene fruits.

Cirsium kujuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
