Crotalaria hyssopifolia

Crotalaria hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and palmate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in the morning, giving it its common name.

Crotalaria hyssopifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
