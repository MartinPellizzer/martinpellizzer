Carex haydenii

Carex haydenii, commonly known as Hayden's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Carex haydenii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
