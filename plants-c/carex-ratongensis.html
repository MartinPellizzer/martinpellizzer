Carex ratongensis

Carex ratongensis, commonly known as the Ratongensis sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to New Guinea and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. The plant has grass-like leaves that are often dark green and densely packed, forming a dense mat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex ratongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
