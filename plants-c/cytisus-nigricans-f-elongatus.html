Cytisus nigricans f. elongatus

Cytisus nigricans f. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a variant of the common broom characterized by its elongated stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, making it a popular choice in landscaping and restoration projects.
