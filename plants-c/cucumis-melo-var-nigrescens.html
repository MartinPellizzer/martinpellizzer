Cucumis melo var. nigrescens

Cucumis melo var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black horned melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, spiky rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is usually small to medium in size, with a distinct, rough texture on the exterior.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and flavor, often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
