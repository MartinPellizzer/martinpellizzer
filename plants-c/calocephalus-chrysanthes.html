Calocephalus chrysanthes

Calocephalus chrysanthes, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, yellowish-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Calocephalus chrysanthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
