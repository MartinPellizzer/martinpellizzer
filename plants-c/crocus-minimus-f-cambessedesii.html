Crocus minimus f. cambessedesii

Crocus minimus f. cambessedesii, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a small, bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, early spring blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically appearing before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
