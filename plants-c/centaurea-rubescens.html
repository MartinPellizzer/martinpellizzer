Centaurea rubescens

Centaurea rubescens, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer, and its distinctive, bristly seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Centaurea rubescens is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
