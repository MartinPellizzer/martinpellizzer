Campylocentrum aciculatum

Campylocentrum aciculatum, commonly known as the needleleaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
