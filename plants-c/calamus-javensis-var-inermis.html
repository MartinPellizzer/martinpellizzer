Calamus javensis var. inermis

Calamus javensis var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its long, flexible rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, tropical environments, often found in marshy areas or near water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
