Caltha confinis

Caltha confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and moist meadows in northern regions.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-rich soils and requires consistent moisture.

Caltha confinis is often found in riparian zones and is an important species for pollinators.
