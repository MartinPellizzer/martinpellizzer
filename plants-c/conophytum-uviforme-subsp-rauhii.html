Conophytum uviforme subsp. rauhii

Conophytum uviforme subsp.

rauhii, commonly known as the "stone plant" or "living stone," is a succulent native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, helping it survive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
