Cerastium vulgatum subsp. glabrescens

Cerastium vulgatum subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, which become less prominent as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is edible and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
