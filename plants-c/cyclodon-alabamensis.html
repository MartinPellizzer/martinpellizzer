Cyclodon alabamensis

Cyclodon alabamensis, commonly known as Alabama cyclone, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Cyclodon alabamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
