Campanula fenestrellata

Campanula fenestrellata, commonly known as the fenestrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Campanula fenestrellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
