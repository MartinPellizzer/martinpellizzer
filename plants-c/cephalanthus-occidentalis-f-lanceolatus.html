Cephalanthus occidentalis f. lanceolatus

Cephalanthus occidentalis f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf sweetshrub, is a variant of the sweetshrub species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and small, orange-red berries that appear in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
