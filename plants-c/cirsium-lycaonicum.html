Cirsium lycaonicum

Cirsium lycaonicum, commonly known as the Lycaonia thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of many tiny florets arranged in a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Cirsium lycaonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
