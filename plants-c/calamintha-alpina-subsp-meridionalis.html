Calamintha alpina subsp. meridionalis

Calamintha alpina subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, rounded fruits.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
