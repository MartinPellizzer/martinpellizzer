Calliandra angelica

Calliandra angelica, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow in a variety of soil types.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
