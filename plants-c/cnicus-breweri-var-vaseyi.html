Cnicus breweri var. vaseyi

Cnicus breweri var. vaseyi, commonly known as the San Benito dandelion, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
