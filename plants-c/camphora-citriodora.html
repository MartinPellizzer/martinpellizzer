Camphora citriodora

Camphora citriodora, commonly known as lemon-scented camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-scented leaves and pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits with a rough, leathery skin. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Camphora citriodora is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
