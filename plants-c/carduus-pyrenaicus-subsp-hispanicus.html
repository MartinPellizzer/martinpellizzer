Carduus pyrenaicus subsp. hispanicus

Carduus pyrenaicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
