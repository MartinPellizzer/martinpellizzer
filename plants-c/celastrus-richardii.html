Celastrus richardii

Celastrus richardii, commonly known as the Richard's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory flora in its native habitat.
