Carex lanceolata var. alashanica

Carex lanceolata var. alashanica, commonly known as the Alashan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in environments with low moisture availability.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in desert and steppe ecosystems.
