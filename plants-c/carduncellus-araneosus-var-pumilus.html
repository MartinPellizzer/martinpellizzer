Carduncellus araneosus var. pumilus

Carduncellus araneosus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf spider-thorn, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, spiny leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a compact and defensive structure. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
