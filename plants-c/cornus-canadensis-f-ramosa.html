Cornus canadensis f. ramosa

Cornus canadensis f. ramosa, commonly known as the slender cornel.

It is a variant of the Canadian cornel, characterized by its more branched growth habit. This plant is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth margin.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds.
