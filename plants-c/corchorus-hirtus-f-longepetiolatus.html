Corchorus hirtus f. longepetiolatus

Corchorus hirtus f. longepetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled prickly goosefoot, is a variant of the prickly goosefoot species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, spiny seed capsules.

This plant is native to regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
