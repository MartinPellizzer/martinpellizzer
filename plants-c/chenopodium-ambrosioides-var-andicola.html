Chenopodium ambrosioides var. andicola

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. andicola, commonly known as Andean wormseed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. It is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution in mountainous areas.
