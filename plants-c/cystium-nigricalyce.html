Cystium nigricalyce

Cystium nigricalyce, commonly known as the black calyx cystium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Cystium nigricalyce is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal environments.
