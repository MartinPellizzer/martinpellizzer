Capsicum crassiflorum

Capsicum crassiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered chili pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces fruits that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on maturity. It is typically an annual herb that thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful for culinary purposes.
