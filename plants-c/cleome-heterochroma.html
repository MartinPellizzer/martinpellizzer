Cleome heterochroma

Cleome heterochroma, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences that consist of small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive central tuft of stamens.

Cleome heterochroma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
