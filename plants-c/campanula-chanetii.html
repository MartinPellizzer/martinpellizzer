Campanula chanetii

Campanula chanetii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of down, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Campanula chanetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
