Claytonia mucronulata

Claytonia mucronulata, commonly known as the Sierra sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 2 to 6 inches. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a dense inflorescence at the stem tip.

Claytonia mucronulata thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
