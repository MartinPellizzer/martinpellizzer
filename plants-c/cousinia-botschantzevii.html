Cousinia botschantzevii

Cousinia botschantzevii, commonly known as the Armenian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia botschantzevii is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
