Clidemia campestris var. pauciflora

Clidemia campestris var. pauciflora, commonly known as the low-flowered marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
