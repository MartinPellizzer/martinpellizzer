Crassula mongolica

Crassula mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian Crassula, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Mongolia and northern China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the growing season.

Crassula mongolica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
