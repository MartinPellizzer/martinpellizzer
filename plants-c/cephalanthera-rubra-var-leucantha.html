Cephalanthera rubra var. leucantha

Cephalanthera rubra var. leucantha, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in dense, hair-like clusters, giving it its common name. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a slender rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
