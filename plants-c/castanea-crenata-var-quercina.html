Castanea crenata var. quercina

Castanea crenata var. quercina, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves and distinctive chestnut fruits. The tree produces spiny capsules that contain edible nuts, which are encased in a thick, hairy husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
