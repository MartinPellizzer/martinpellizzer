Crataegus rotundifolia var. bicknellii

Crataegus rotundifolia var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
