Chamaefilix septentrionalis

Chamaefilix septentrionalis, commonly known as northern rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called a bloom. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats such as cliffs and woodland edges.
