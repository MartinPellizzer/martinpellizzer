Campylotropis filipes

Campylotropis filipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hookvine, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and streamside areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
