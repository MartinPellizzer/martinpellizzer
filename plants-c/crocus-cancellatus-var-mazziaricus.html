Crocus cancellatus var. mazziaricus

Crocus cancellatus var. mazziaricus, commonly known as the Mazziaric crocus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging in early spring before the flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
