Cupressus pisifera var. plumosa

Cupressus pisifera var. plumosa, commonly known as the feathery lacey cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cypress native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in dense, flat sprays, creating a soft, cloud-like effect. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its unique aesthetic and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and adaptability to various soil conditions.
