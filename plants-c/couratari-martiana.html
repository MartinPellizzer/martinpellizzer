Couratari martiana

Couratari martiana, commonly known as the Amazonian rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its latex, which has been historically used in the production of natural rubber.
