Croton capitis-york var. pilosus

Croton capitis-york var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant originates from the Caribbean and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, variegated foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. The leaves are typically green with yellow or orange veins, adding a tropical flair to gardens and indoor spaces.

It is important to note that the plant is toxic if ingested, so it should be handled with care.
