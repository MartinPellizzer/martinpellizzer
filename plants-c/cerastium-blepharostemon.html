Cerastium blepharostemon

Cerastium blepharostemon, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.
