Corypha rotundifolia

Corypha rotundifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface. Its inflorescences are large and branched, producing both male and female flowers.

Corypha rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
