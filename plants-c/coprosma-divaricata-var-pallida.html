Coprosma divaricata var. pallida

Coprosma divaricata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically pale green to whitish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some birds. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
