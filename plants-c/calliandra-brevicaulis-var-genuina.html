Calliandra brevicaulis var. genuina

Calliandra brevicaulis var. genuina, commonly known as the dwarf coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. This species is well-suited for warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

It has moderate water requirements and thrives in well-drained soil.
