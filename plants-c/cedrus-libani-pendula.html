Cedrus libani pendula

Cedrus libani pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar of Lebanon, is a unique variant of the cedar tree native to the mountains of Lebanon.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop downward, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The tree has scale-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in dense clusters. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its striking visual appeal.
