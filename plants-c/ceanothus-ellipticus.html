Ceanothus ellipticus

Ceanothus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ceanothus, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ceanothus ellipticus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
