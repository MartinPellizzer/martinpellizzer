Cleome dumosa

Cleome dumosa, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the summer. Cleome dumosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

It attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
