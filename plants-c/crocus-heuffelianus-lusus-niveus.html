Crocus heuffelianus lusus niveus

Crocus heuffelianus lusus niveus, commonly known as the snow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces showy, early spring flowers with white petals and a yellow center, often blooming before the leaves emerge. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. Its bulbs are adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
