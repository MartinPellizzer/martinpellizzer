Calanthe masuca var. luteo-oculta

Calanthe masuca var. luteo-oculta, commonly known as the hidden yellow calanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy lips, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses or botanical gardens.
