Cyrtomium macrophyllum var. tukusicola

Cyrtomium macrophyllum var. tukusicola, commonly known as the Tukusic fern, is a fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
