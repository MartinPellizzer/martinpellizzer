Clematis okinawensis

Clematis okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and moist woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
