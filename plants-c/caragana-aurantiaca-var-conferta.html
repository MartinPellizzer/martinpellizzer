Caragana aurantiaca var. conferta

Caragana aurantiaca var. conferta, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, orange-red pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
