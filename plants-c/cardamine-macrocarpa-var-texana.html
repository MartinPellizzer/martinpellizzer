Cardamine macrocarpa var. texana

Cardamine macrocarpa var. texana, commonly known as Texas watercress, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
