Cansenia tomentosa

Cansenia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly candelabra, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or white.

Cansenia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and resilience.
