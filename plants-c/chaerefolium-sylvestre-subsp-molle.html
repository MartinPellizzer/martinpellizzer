Chaerefolium sylvestre subsp. molle

Chaerefolium sylvestre subsp.

molle, commonly known as the softleaf bird's-beak, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive beak-like shape. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
