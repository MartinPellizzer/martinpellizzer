Cybianthus cuspidatus

Cybianthus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Cybianthus cuspidatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
