Coleus preussii

Coleus preussii, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a low, compact rosette. Its foliage is often variegated, with patterns of green, white, and purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Coleus preussii prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
