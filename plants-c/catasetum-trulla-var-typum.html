Catasetum trulla var. typum

Catasetum trulla var. typum, commonly known as the Trulla Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid produces a single spike bearing multiple flowers, which are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators.
