Carex remyi

Carex remyi, commonly known as Remy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to North America, particularly the eastern United States, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in three rows, and its flowering structures are typically green or reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
