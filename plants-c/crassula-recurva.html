Crassula recurva

Crassula recurva, commonly known as the rock rose or stone rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula recurva is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.
