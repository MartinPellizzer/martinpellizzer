Calystegia fulcrata var. berryi

Calystegia fulcrata var. berryi, commonly known as the California morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
