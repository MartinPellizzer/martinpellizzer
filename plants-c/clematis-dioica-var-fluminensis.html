Clematis dioica var. fluminensis

Clematis dioica var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian virgin's bower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires support as it climbs.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
