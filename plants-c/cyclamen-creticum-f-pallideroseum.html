Cyclamen creticum f. pallideroseum

Cyclamen creticum f. pallideroseum, commonly known as the pale rose cyclamen, is a hybrid form of the cyclamen plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and pale pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded rockeries. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
