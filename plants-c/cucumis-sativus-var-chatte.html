Cucumis sativus var. chatte

Cucumis sativus var. chatte, commonly known as the Indian cucumber or snake cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces elongated, cylindrical fruits with a smooth, thin skin. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow and are pollinated by insects.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
