Chamaecereus grandiflorus

Chamaecereus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
