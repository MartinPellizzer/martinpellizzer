Convolvulus kentrocaulos

Convolvulus kentrocaulos, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
