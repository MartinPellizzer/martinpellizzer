Cirsium setosum var. subalatum

Cirsium setosum var. subalatum, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its height ranges from 30 to 100 centimeters, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scrublands.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
