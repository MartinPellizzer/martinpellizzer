Cornus lixianensis

Cornus lixianensis, commonly known as the Lixian dogwood, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn red or orange in the fall.

Cornus lixianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
