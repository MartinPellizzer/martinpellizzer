Chylismia aurantiaca

Chylismia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in well-drained soils. Chylismia aurantiaca is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
