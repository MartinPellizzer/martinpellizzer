Cirsium × petrakii

Cirsium × petrakii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is often found in alpine environments. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Cirsium × petrakii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
