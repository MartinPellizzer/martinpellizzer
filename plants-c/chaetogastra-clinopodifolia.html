Chaetogastra clinopodifolia

Chaetogastra clinopodifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic effects.
