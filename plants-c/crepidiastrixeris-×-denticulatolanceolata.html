Crepidiastrixeris × denticulatolanceolata

Crepidiastrixeris × denticulatolanceolata, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two distinct fern species.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent plants, including finely serrated, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its fronds are often delicate and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
