Croton comatus

Croton comatus, commonly known as the horse's tail croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and hairy leaves that resemble a horse's tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
