Cuscuta insquamata var. argentina

Cuscuta insquamata var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nourishment.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host to extract nutrients. The plant is native to South America and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas readily.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
