Cardenanthus tunariensis

Cardenanthus tunariensis, commonly known as the Tunari sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
