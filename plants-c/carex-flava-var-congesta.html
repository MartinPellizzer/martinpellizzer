Carex flava var. congesta

Carex flava var. congesta, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex flava var. congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
