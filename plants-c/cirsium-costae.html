Cirsium costae

Cirsium costae, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple to pink, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Cirsium costae is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
