Calamagrostis anomala

Calamagrostis anomala, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, blue-gray flowers in the summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis anomala is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
