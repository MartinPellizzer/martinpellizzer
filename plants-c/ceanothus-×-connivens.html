Ceanothus × connivens

Ceanothus × connivens, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with dark green leaves that turn red or bronze in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers in early spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and natural habitats.
