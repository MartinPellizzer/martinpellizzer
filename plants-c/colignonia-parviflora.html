Colignonia parviflora

Colignonia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered colignonia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, typically found in temperate regions.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
