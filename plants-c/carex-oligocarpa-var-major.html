Carex oligocarpa var. major

Carex oligocarpa var. major, commonly known as the large-fruited sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
