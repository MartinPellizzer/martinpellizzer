Chrysophyllum viride

Chrysophyllum viride, commonly known as the green star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically oval or egg-shaped, with a yellow or green skin and a creamy, sweet pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in traditional medicine.
