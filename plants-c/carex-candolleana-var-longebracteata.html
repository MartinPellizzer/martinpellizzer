Carex candolleana var. longebracteata

Carex candolleana var. longebracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often elongated and covered with bracts, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
