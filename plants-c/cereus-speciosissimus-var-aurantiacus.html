Cereus speciosissimus var. aurantiacus

Cereus speciosissimus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the Orange Blossom Cactus, is a striking cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often opening at night. These flowers are followed by small, red fruits that contain edible seeds.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert or semi-desert environments.
