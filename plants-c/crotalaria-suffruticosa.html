Crotalaria suffruticosa

Crotalaria suffruticosa, commonly known as the tropical spurred crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive spurred flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds. Crotalaria suffruticosa is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
