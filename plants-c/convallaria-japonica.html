Convallaria japonica

Convallaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often growing in woodland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
