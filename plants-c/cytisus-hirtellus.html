Cytisus hirtellus

Cytisus hirtellus, commonly known as the hirtellus broom, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically small, hairy, and compound, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cytisus hirtellus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.
