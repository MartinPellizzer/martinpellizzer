Cyperus vegetus

Cyperus vegetus, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus vegetus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
