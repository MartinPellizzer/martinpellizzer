Cucumis variabilis

Cucumis variabilis, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces yellow flowers and elongated, spiny fruits that can vary in size and shape. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
