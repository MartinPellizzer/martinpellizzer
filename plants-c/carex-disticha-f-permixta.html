Carex disticha f. permixta

Carex disticha f. permixta, commonly known as the mixed sedge, is a variant of the common sedge that exhibits a unique combination of green and silver-green foliage.

This ornamental plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like leaves that form a compact, mound-shaped growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens or shaded borders. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in late spring, which are followed by distinctive, triangular seed heads.

Its foliage color variation adds visual interest, making it a popular choice for moisture-loving garden settings.
