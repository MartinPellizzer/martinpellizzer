Chytranthus mannii

Chytranthus mannii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chytranthus mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, dandelion-like appearance.
