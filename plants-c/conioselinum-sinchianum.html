Conioselinum sinchianum

Conioselinum sinchianum, commonly known as the Sinchian wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
