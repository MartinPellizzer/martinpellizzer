Convolvulus reptans

Convolvulus reptans, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a trailing vine that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in gardens and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and invasive in certain environments.
