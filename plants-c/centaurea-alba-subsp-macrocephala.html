Centaurea alba subsp. macrocephala

Centaurea alba subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the white knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fluffy white or pale pink flower heads.

The plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
