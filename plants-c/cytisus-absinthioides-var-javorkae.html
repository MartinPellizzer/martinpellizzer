Cytisus absinthioides var. javorkae

Cytisus absinthioides var. javorkae, commonly known as the Javorka broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant has slender, woody stems with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
