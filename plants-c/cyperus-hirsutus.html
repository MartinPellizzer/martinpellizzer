Cyperus hirsutus

Cyperus hirsutus, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Cyperus hirsutus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
