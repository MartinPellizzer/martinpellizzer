Calliandra densifolia

Calliandra densifolia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescences that produce showy, pink or red flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra densifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
