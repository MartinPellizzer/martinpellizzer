Cyathea obtusa var. kohautiana

Cyathea obtusa var. kohautiana, commonly known as the Kohaut's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage in its natural environment.
