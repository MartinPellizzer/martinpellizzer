Clematis lasiandra

Clematis lasiandra, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, sweet aroma.

Clematis lasiandra thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
