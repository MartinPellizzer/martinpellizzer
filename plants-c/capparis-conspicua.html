Capparis conspicua

Capparis conspicua, commonly known as the showy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Capparis conspicua is typically found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
