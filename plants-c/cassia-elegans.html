Cassia elegans

Cassia elegans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Cassia elegans is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
