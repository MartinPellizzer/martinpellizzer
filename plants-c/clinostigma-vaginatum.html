Clinostigma vaginatum

Clinostigma vaginatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Clinostigma vaginatum is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

It is also recognized for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it both a common weed and a subject of botanical study.
