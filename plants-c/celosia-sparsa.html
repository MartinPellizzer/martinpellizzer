Celosia sparsa

Celosia sparsa, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble fireworks, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, conical inflorescences.

Celosia sparsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
