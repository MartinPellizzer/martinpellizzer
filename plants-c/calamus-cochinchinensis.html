Calamus cochinchinensis

Calamus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sweet flag, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by wind.

The rhizomes of Calamus cochinchinensis are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
