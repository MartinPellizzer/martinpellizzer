Coelogyne speciosa var. incarnata

Coelogyne speciosa var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
