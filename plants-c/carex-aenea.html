Carex aenea

Carex aenea, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
