Carex × descendens

Carex × descendens, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
