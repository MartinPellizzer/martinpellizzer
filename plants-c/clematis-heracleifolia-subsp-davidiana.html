Clematis heracleifolia subsp. davidiana

Clematis heracleifolia subsp.

davidiana, commonly known as the David's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
