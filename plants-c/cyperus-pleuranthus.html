Cyperus pleuranthus

Cyperus pleuranthus, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Cyperus pleuranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
