Carex nigra subsp. intricata

Carex nigra subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

This subspecies is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
