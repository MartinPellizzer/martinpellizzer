Cousinia esfandiarii

Cousinia esfandiarii, commonly known as the Persian feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Cousinia esfandiarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
