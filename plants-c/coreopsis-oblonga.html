Coreopsis oblonga

Coreopsis oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Coreopsis oblonga is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and wildflower landscapes.
