Cryptocoryne sinensis

Cryptocoryne sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese broad-leaf cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with light and dark green patterns. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in the understory of shaded wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Cryptocoryne sinensis is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
