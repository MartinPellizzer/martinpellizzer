Callicarpa longifolia var. longissima

Callicarpa longifolia var. longissima, commonly known as the long-fruited beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by vibrant purple to red berries in the fall. These berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
