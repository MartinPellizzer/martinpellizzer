Canariothamnus rupicola

Canariothamnus rupicola, commonly known as the rock canarythamnus, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
