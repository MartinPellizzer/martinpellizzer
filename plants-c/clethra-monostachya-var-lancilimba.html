Clethra monostachya var. lancilimba

Clethra monostachya var. lancilimba, commonly known as the lanceleaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 3 to 6 inches long, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
