Croton occidentalis var. setosus

Croton occidentalis var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is notable for the presence of fine, hairy trichomes, which give it its common name.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking leaf patterns.
