Carex pumila var. bichenoviana

Carex pumila var. bichenoviana, commonly known as the Bichenovian sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Russia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a muted green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
