Capsicum angulosum var. macrocarpum

Capsicum angulosum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited bird pepper, is a variant of the bird pepper plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, angular fruits that typically range in color from yellow to orange when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, leading to the development of its distinctive fruits. The leaves are ovate with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to medium heat level, making it popular in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
