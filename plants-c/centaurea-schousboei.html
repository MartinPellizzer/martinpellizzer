Centaurea schousboei

Centaurea schousboei, commonly known as the Schousboe's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea schousboei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
