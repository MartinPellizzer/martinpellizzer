Carex oxyandra var. oxyandra

Carex oxyandra var. oxyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
