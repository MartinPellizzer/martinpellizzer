Cereus pentlandii var. violaceoroseus

Cereus pentlandii var. violaceoroseus, commonly known as the Purple Rose Cactus, is a striking species native to South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems that often develop a reddish tinge. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming at night. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
