Crotalaria sagittalis var. parviflora

Crotalaria sagittalis var. parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are used for both forage and ornamental purposes.
