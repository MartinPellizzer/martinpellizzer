Cassia sunsub

Cassia sunsub, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
