Carex inversa f. major

Carex inversa f. major, commonly known as the giant inverted sedge, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its unique growth habit and foliage.

This plant features tall, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a distinctive upward-curving shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
