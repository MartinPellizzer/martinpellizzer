Caesalpinia urophylla

Caesalpinia urophylla, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange inflorescences that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Caesalpinia urophylla is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
