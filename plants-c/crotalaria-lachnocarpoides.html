Crotalaria lachnocarpoides

Crotalaria lachnocarpoides, commonly known as the narrow-fruit cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, spherical seeds enclosed in narrow, elongated pods.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it an adaptable species in various ecosystems.
