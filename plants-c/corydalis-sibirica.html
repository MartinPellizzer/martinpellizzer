Corydalis sibirica

Corydalis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
