Crataegus oxyacantha var. obovatocuneatis

Crataegus oxyacantha var. obovatocuneatis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its obovate to cuneate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed at the base. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
