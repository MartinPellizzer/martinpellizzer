Coryphantha neomexicana

Coryphantha neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico hedgehog cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often appreciated by cactus enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
