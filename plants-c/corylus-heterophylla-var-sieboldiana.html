Corylus heterophylla var. sieboldiana

Corylus heterophylla var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its alternate, serrate leaves that vary in shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into edible nuts encased in a spiky husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
