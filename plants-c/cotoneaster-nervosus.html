Cotoneaster nervosus

Cotoneaster nervosus, commonly known as the veined cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster nervosus is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
