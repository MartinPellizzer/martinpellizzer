Centaurea sipylea

Centaurea sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and bright blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea sipylea is a hardy species that thrives in sunny conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
