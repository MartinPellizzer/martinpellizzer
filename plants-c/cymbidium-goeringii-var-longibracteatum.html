Cymbidium goeringii var. longibracteatum

Cymbidium goeringii var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is typically grown as a terrestrial orchid in shaded, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice in horticultural collections.
