Calea sillaensis

Calea sillaensis, commonly known as the Mexican ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiny bracts that resemble ghostly faces. The plant is known for its unique morphology, with its bright yellow flowers surrounded by intricate, spiky structures. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Calea sillaensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in local herbal remedies.
