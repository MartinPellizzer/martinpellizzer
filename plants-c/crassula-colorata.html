Crassula colorata

Crassula colorata, commonly known as the "Snowball Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its low maintenance and compact size.

Its unique appearance and ability to withstand drought make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
