Crataegus chrysocarpa var. vernonensis

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. vernonensis, commonly known as the Vernon's golden hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange fruits that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and moist soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
