Cyperus cyperinus var. maximus

Cyperus cyperinus var. maximus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, often forming dense mats.

The leaves are narrow, sword-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in humid conditions.
