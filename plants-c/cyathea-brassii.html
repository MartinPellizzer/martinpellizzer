Cyathea brassii

Cyathea brassii, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used for both ornamental and traditional purposes.

Cyathea brassii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
