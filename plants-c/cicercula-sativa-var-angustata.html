Cicercula sativa var. angustata

Cicercula sativa var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or brown, arranged in dense spikes. It is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
