Cynanchum ledermannii

Cynanchum ledermannii, commonly known as Ledermann's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Cynanchum ledermannii is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock.
