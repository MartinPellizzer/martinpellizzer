Clerodendrum sinuatum var. aureum

Clerodendrum sinuatum var. aureum, commonly known as golden flame or golden glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are less showy than the colorful bracts.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
