Cerasus transarisanensis

Cerasus transarisanensis, commonly known as the Transarisan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The Transarisan cherry is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for hybridization with other cherry species.
