Calendula boissieri

Calendula boissieri, commonly known as the Algerian marigold, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Calendula boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
