Carex trichocarpa var. turbinata

Carex trichocarpa var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Clubmoss Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
