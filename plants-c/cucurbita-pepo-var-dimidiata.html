Cucurbita pepo var. dimidiata

Cucurbita pepo var. dimidiata, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive ridged, elongated fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and trailing.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
