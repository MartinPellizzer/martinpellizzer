Carlina subfusca

Carlina subfusca, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in soft, grayish-green hairy foliage. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, producing a mix of white to pale purple petals.

Carlina subfusca is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
