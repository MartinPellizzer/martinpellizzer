Cissus officinalis

Cissus officinalis, commonly known as the stonecrop or Indian rubber plant, is a member of the family Vitaceae.

It is a climbing or trailing plant that produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant is native to Africa and parts of Asia, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is known for its ability to form aerial roots, which help it attach to surfaces as it grows.

Cissus officinalis is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
