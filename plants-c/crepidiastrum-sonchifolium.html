Crepidiastrum sonchifolium

Crepidiastrum sonchifolium, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
