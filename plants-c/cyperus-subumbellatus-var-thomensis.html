Cyperus subumbellatus var. thomensis

Cyperus subumbellatus var. thomensis, commonly known as the Tomense flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
