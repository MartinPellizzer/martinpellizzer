Chrysanthemum × indo-japonense

Chrysanthemum × indo-japonense, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and a wide range of flower colors, including white, yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and produces daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disc. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in traditional floral arrangements.
