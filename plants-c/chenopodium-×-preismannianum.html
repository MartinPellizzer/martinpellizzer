Chenopodium × preismannianum

Chenopodium × preismannianum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients and have a mild, spinach-like flavor. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary versatility.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and the plant is well-suited for both home gardens and agricultural production.
