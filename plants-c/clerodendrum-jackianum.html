Clerodendrum jackianum

Clerodendrum jackianum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored, typically red or orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.
