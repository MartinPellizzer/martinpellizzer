Cerinthodes davuricum

Cerinthodes davuricum, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cerinthodes davuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
