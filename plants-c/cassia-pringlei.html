Cassia pringlei

Cassia pringlei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia pringlei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
