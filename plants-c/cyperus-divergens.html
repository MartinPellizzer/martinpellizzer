Cyperus divergens

Cyperus divergens, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to various regions of the Americas and is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are usually found at the base of the plant.

Cyperus divergens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
