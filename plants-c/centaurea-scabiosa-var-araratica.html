Centaurea scabiosa var. araratica

Centaurea scabiosa var. araratica, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center, resembling a crocus. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are often used in dried flower arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the region of Armenia.
