Chirita subulatisepala

Chirita subulatisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
