Cardamine × fockei

Cardamine × fockei, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cardamine pratensis (common buttercup) and Cardamine hirsuta (hairy bittercress). The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features trifoliate leaves with lobed segments and produces small, yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
