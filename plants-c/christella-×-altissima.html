Christella × altissima

Christella × altissima, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen shrub, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Christella monogyna and Christella tetrandra.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage that remains evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Christella × altissima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
