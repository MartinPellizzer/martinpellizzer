Carum atrosanguineum

Carum atrosanguineum, commonly known as black blood caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

The seeds are small, dark, and used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
