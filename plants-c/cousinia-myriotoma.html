Cousinia myriotoma

Cousinia myriotoma, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it a hardy perennial.

Cousinia myriotoma plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
