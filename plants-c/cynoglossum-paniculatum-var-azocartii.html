Cynoglossum paniculatum var. azocartii

Cynoglossum paniculatum var. azocartii, commonly known as the Azores bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores archipelago.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
