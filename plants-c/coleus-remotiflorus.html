Coleus remotiflorus

Coleus remotiflorus, commonly known as the distant-flowered coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
