Campanula kerneri

Campanula kerneri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula kerneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
