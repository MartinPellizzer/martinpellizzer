Croton lundianus var. serratus

Croton lundianus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage with serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, with a range of colors including green, yellow, and red.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, giving way to woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.
