Campanula ciliata var. pontica

Campanula ciliata var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with hairy margins, giving the plant its scientific name "ciliata," which refers to the fringed edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
