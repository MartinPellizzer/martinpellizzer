Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. chailaricus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. chailaricus, commonly known as the Chailaric cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
