Chamaepeuce casabonae

Chamaepeuce casabonae, commonly known as the Casabonana or Casabonnet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The Casabonana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
