Cistus sessiliflorus

Cistus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
