Commelina subscabrifolia

Commelina subscabrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes plants known for their striking floral structures. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue-violet flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
