Cephalaria fanourii

Cephalaria fanourii, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Cephalaria fanourii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
