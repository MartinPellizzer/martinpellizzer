Chiranthodendron pentadactylon

Chiranthodendron pentadactylon, commonly known as the white-flowered chironje, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
