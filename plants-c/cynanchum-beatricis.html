Cynanchum beatricis

Cynanchum beatricis, commonly known as the blessed milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
