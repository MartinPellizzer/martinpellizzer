Chrysanthemum praeteritum

Chrysanthemum praeteritum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in the fall.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.
