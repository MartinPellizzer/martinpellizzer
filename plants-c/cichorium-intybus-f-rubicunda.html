Cichorium intybus f. rubicunda

Cichorium intybus f. rubicunda, commonly known as the red leaf chicory, is a cultivated variant of the common chicory.

It is characterized by its striking red-tinged leaves, which are often more vibrant than those of the standard variety. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. It is typically grown for its ornamental value and can also be used in salads or as a vegetable.

The leaves are usually harvested young to ensure a tender texture and mild flavor.
