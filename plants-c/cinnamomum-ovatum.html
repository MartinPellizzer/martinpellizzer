Cinnamomum ovatum

Cinnamomum ovatum, commonly known as the mountain cinnamon or ovate cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family, Lauraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and China. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical in shape and have a fragrant, aromatic scent. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

The bark and leaves of Cinnamomum ovatum are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
