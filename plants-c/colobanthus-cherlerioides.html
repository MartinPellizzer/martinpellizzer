Colobanthus cherlerioides

Colobanthus cherlerioides, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in harsh, cold climates with minimal soil and nutrients. The species is characterized by its tiny, white flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. It plays a vital role in the fragile ecosystems of the polar regions, serving as a food source for local invertebrates.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a key indicator species of cold, dry environments.
