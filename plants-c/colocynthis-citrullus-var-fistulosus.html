Colocynthis citrullus var. fistulosus

Colocynthis citrullus var. fistulosus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, thin, spiny fruit that resembles a horn, hence its common name. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The fruit is typically harvested when it is still green and can be used in various culinary applications.
