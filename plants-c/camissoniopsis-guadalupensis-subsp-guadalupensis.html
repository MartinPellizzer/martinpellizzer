Camissoniopsis guadalupensis subsp. guadalupensis

Camissoniopsis guadalupensis subsp.

guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It has hairy, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments.
