Chorizanthe wheeleri

Chorizanthe wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in late spring or early summer.
