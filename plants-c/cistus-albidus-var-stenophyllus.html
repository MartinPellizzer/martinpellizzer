Cistus albidus var. stenophyllus

Cistus albidus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grey-green, narrow, and leathery leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
