Carpodinus violacea

Carpodinus violacea, commonly known as the violet-eyed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-colored sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of purple and white hues.

The botanical features of Carpodinus violacea make it a visually appealing and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
