Carduus litigiosus var. horridissimus

Carduus litigiosus var. horridissimus, commonly known as the rough hawkthorn, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny, woody achene that disperses seeds via wind and animals.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.
