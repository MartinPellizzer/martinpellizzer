Consolea macracantha subsp. nashii

Consolea macracantha subsp.

nashii, commonly known as the Nash's giant nightshade, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes.

The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

The leaves are typically large, broad, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its striking appearance.
