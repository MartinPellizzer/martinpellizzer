Cyathea thurstonii

Cyathea thurstonii, commonly known as the Thurston's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature typical of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea thurstonii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
