Clusia longistyla

Clusia longistyla, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its evergreen foliage and showy inflorescences.

Clusia longistyla is adaptable to a variety of soil types and thrives in humid, warm climates.
