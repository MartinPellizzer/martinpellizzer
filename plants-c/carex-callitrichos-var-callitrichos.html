Carex callitrichos var. callitrichos

Carex callitrichos var. callitrichos, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, a common feature in the Cyperaceae family.
