Cucullaria citrifolia

Cucullaria citrifolia, commonly known as the lemon thorn or sour thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green and have a sour taste. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cucullaria citrifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
