Citrus reticulata subsp. unshiu

Citrus reticulata subsp.

unshiu, commonly known as the unshiu mandarin, is a citrus species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
