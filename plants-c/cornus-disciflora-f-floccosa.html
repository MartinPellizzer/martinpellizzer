Cornus disciflora f. floccosa

Cornus disciflora f. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly dogwood, is a rare and ornamental variant of the common dogwood.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white bracts that resemble clouds, giving it a unique and striking appearance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
