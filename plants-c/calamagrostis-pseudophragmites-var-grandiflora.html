Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. grandiflora

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces tall, showy panicles of small, white or pale pink flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to moist or wet soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
