Cyperus serratangulus

Cyperus serratangulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in floodplains. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, giving it its scientific name.

Cyperus serratangulus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
