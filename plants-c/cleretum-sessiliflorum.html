Cleretum sessiliflorum

Cleretum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is relatively low maintenance once established.
