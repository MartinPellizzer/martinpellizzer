Crataegus russanovii f. rubella

Crataegus russanovii f. rubella, commonly known as the Russian hawthorn or red-fruited hawthorn, is a hybrid form of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an ornamental feature, often used in landscaping. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
