Crucifera hutchinsia

Crucifera hutchinsia, commonly known as the Hutchins' mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Native to Australia, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
