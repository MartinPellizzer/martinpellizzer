Campanula rotundifolia f. linifolia

Campanula rotundifolia f. linifolia, commonly known as the wooly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are rounded and have a woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
