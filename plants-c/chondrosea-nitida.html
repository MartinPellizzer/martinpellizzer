Chondrosea nitida

Chondrosea nitida, commonly known as the smooth sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Chondrosea nitida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
