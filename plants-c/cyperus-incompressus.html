Cyperus incompressus

Cyperus incompressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus incompressus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
