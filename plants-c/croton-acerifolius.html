Croton acerifolius

Croton acerifolius, commonly known as the maple-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble maple leaves, often with a vibrant green or variegated coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton acerifolius is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
