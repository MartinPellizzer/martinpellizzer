Clerodendrum quadrangulatum var. reclinans

Clerodendrum quadrangulatum var. reclinans, commonly known as the hanging clerodendrum, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. Its stems are woody and can be used for propagation through cuttings.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
