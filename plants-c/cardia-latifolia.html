Cardia latifolia

Cardia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Cardia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
