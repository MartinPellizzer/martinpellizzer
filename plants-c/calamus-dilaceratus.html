Calamus dilaceratus

Calamus dilaceratus, commonly known as the broken cane reed, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus dilaceratus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
