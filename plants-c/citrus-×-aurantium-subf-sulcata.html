Citrus × aurantium subf. sulcata

Citrus × aurantium subf.

sulcata, commonly known as the rough lemon, is a hybrid citrus species characterized by its distinctive, rough, and fissured rind. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is usually sour and has a high vitamin C content, making it valuable in traditional remedies. The subsp.

sulcata is particularly noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
