Carex ceylanica

Carex ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Sri Lanka, India, and parts of China. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex ceylanica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
