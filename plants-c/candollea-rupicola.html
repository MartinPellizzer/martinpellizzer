Candollea rupicola

Candollea rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Candollea rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
