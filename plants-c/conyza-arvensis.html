Conyza arvensis

Conyza arvensis, commonly known as common weed or horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Conyza arvensis is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse environments.
