Carex ligata var. nexa

Carex ligata var. nexa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, usually brownish or greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
