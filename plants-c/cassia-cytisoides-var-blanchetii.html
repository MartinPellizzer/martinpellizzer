Cassia cytisoides var. blanchetii

Cassia cytisoides var. blanchetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
