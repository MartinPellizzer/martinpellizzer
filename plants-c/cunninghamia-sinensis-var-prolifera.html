Cunninghamia sinensis var. prolifera

Cunninghamia sinensis var. prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese fir var. prolifera, is a variant of the Chinese fir, native to southern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit with a dense, pyramidal shape and produces small, cylindrical cones. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
