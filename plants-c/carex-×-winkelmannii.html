Carex × winkelmannii

Carex × winkelmannii, commonly known as the Winkelman's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Carex × winkelmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
