Cassia tetraphylla var. longifolia

Cassia tetraphylla var. longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and striking foliage make it a valuable addition to landscapes and gardens.
