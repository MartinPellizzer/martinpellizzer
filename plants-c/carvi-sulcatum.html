Carvi sulcatum

Carvi sulcatum, commonly known as the wrinkled caraway, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Carvi sulcatum is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
