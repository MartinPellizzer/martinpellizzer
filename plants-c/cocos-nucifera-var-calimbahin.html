Cocos nucifera var. calimbahin

Cocos nucifera var. calimbahin, commonly known as the Calimbahin coconut, is a variety of the coconut palm native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The fruit of this variety is typically smaller than the common coconut, with a thick husk and a high oil content. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical uses.
