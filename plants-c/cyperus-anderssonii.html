Cyperus anderssonii

Cyperus anderssonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.

Cyperus anderssonii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
