Carex deflexa

Carex deflexa, commonly known as the flexed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Carex deflexa plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
