Clinopodium kallaricum

Clinopodium kallaricum, commonly known as Indian catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach a height of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a mild minty scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.
