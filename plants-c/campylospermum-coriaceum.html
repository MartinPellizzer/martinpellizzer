Campylospermum coriaceum

Campylospermum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved campylospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental qualities.
