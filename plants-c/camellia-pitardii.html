Camellia pitardii

Camellia pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, large, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. Camellia pitardii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
