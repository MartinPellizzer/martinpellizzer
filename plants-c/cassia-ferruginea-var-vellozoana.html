Cassia ferruginea var. vellozoana

Cassia ferruginea var. vellozoana, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
