Carex billingsii

Carex billingsii, commonly known as the Billings sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Carex billingsii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
