Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. linifolia

Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf corethrogyne, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing form with thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
