Caprifolium chinense

Caprifolium chinense, commonly known as Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellow. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fruit, a red, fleshy berry, is edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Caprifolium chinense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
