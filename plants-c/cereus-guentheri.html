Cereus guentheri

Cereus guentheri, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers, which bloom in winter, are often pink or white and have a star-like appearance.

Cereus guentheri is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
