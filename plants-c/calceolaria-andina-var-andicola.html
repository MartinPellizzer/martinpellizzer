Calceolaria andina var. andicola

Calceolaria andina var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Calceolaria andina var. andicola is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional Andean culture.
