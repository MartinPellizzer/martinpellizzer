Carex luctuosa f. mucronata

Carex luctuosa f. mucronata, commonly known as the weeping sedge, is a variant of the species Carex luctuosa, characterized by its drooping, grass-like foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that often curl downward, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikes.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
