Cerastium pospichalii

Cerastium pospichalii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers with five petals that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits.

Cerastium pospichalii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
