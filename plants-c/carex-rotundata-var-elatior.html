Carex rotundata var. elatior

Carex rotundata var. elatior, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically broad and rounded, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
