Carex tonsa

Carex tonsa, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown, arranged in spikelets along the upper stem. Carex tonsa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
