Carex washingtoniana

Carex washingtoniana, commonly known as Washington sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color.

Washington sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
