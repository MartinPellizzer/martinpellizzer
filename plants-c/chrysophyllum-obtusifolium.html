Chrysophyllum obtusifolium

Chrysophyllum obtusifolium, commonly known as the African cherry or red cherry tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
