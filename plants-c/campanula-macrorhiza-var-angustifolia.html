Campanula macrorhiza var. angustifolia

Campanula macrorhiza var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, narrow, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
