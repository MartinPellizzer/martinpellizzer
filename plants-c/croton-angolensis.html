Croton angolensis

Croton angolensis, commonly known as the Angola croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, often succulent stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lobed or entire, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Croton angolensis is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
