Campanopsis marginata

Campanopsis marginata, commonly known as the Marginated Bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanopsis marginata is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, making it a notable presence in its native habitat.
