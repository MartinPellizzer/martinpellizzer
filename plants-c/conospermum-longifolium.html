Conospermum longifolium

Conospermum longifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Conospermum longifolium is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
