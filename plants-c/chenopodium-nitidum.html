Chenopodium nitidum

Chenopodium nitidum, commonly known as smooth lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically triangular and covered in a thin, powdery coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium nitidum is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
