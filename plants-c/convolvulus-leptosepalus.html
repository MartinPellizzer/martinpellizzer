Convolvulus leptosepalus

Convolvulus leptosepalus, commonly known as the slender-sepal convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Convolvulus leptosepalus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
