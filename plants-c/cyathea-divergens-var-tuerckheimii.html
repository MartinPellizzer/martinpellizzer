Cyathea divergens var. tuerckheimii

Cyathea divergens var. tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to shaded, humid environments.
