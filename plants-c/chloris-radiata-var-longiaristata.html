Chloris radiata var. longiaristata

Chloris radiata var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-awned lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
