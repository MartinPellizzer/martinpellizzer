Chloris geminata

Chloris geminata, commonly known as twin spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is a hardy species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
