Calamagrostis farcta

Calamagrostis farcta, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and striking purple to pinkish flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

Calamagrostis farcta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
