Calamagrostis longiaristata f. pilosa

Calamagrostis longiaristata f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy calamagrostis, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, pale to golden flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its graceful appearance and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife and stabilizing soil.
