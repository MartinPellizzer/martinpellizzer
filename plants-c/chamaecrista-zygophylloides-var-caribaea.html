Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. caribaea

Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. The plant is notable for its unique, woody, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in sandy or well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
