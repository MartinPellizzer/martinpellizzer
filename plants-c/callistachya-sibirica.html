Callistachya sibirica

Callistachya sibirica, commonly known as Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

This hardy species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
