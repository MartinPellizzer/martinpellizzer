Cerbera maculata

Cerbera maculata, commonly known as the spotted cerbera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and distinctive red or orange spots on the underside. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds.

The plant is known for its ornamental value but requires careful handling due to the toxicity of its parts.
