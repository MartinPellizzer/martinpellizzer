Carex alpina var. gracilenta

Carex alpina var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in rocky or mountainous environments. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in meadows and tundra habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
