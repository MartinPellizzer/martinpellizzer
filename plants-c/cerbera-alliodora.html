Cerbera alliodora

Cerbera alliodora, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable regions.
