Cuphea patula

Cuphea patula, commonly known as the sticky cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Cuphea patula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
