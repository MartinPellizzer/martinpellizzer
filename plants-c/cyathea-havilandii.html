Cyathea havilandii

Cyathea havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and produces new shoots, contributing to its slow but steady growth pattern.

Cyathea havilandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
