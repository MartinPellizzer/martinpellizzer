Carex vulpinoidea f. annectens

Carex vulpinoidea f. annectens, commonly known as the foxglove sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
