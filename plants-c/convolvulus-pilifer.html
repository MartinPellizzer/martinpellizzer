Convolvulus pilifer

Convolvulus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Convolvulus pilifer is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
