Ceropegia incarnata subsp. tentaculata

Ceropegia incarnata subsp.

tentaculata, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the Asclepiadaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
