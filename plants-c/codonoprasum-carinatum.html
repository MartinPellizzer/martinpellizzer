Codonoprasum carinatum

Codonoprasum carinatum, commonly known as the mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and aromatic, with a strong, peppery scent.

Codonoprasum carinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
