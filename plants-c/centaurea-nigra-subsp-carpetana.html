Centaurea nigra subsp. carpetana

Centaurea nigra subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the Carpathian bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features slender, upright stems and produces small, dark violet to black flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in gardens, though it requires well-drained conditions to flourish.
