Chelone laevigata

Chelone laevigata, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, making it an important component of native ecosystems.
