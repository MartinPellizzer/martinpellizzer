Carex pilulifera f. pallida

Carex pilulifera f. pallida, commonly known as the pale carex, is a perennial sedge native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its pale green to bluish-gray foliage distinguishes it from other carex species.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
