Cienfuegosia incana

Cienfuegosia incana, commonly known as the silky cienfuegosia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
