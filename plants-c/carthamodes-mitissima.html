Carthamodes mitissima

Carthamodes mitissima, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by tubular disc flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, including marshes and stream banks.

Carthamodes mitissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
