Capparis scortechinii

Capparis scortechinii, commonly known as the Mediterranean caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, thorny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's most notable characteristic is its edible, salt-tolerant fruit, which is used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-resistant.

Capparis scortechinii is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
