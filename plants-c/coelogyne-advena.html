Coelogyne advena

Coelogyne advena, commonly known as the "Lucky Orchid," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered with a thick, leathery sheath.

Coelogyne advena is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
