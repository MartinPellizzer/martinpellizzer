Clematis andersonii

Clematis andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically three-lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in early spring, which are fragrant and attract pollinators. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native species supporting local ecosystems and its ornamental value in gardens.
