Cucumis melo var. cassabar

Cucumis melo var. cassabar, commonly known as the Casablanca melon, is a cultivar of the melon family characterized by its large, round fruit with a thick, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

It originates from the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its high water content and mild, honey-like flavor. The plant is a vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It requires regular watering and is often grown in trellises to support its sprawling growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when the rind turns from green to a pale yellow or white, indicating maturity.
