Capparis welwitschii

Capparis welwitschii, commonly known as the wild quince, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, which resembles a quince, is typically yellow or orange and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
