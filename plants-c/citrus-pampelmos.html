Citrus pampelmos

Citrus pampelmos, commonly known as the pampelmosa, is a tropical citrus fruit native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, yellow-orange rind and juicy, segmented pulp. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and unique flavor profile.
