Carex pulicaris var. montana

Carex pulicaris var. montana, commonly known as mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It has narrow, upright leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
