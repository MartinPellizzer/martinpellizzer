Chrysanthellum americanum var. integrifolium

Chrysanthellum americanum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the American chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves with smooth, unbroken margins. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
