Coix chinensis

Coix chinensis, commonly known as Chinese Job's Tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces hard, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its rhizomes are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.

Coix chinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in wetland areas.
