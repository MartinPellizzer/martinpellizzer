Cucumis hardwickii

Cucumis hardwickii, commonly known as the hardwick cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in warm, arid climates. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, cucumber-like flavor. Its vines are trailing and can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for trellising.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms, a characteristic common in many cucurbit species.
