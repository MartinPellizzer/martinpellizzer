Capparidastrum macrophyllum

Capparidastrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae, which includes various species with distinctive yellow or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
