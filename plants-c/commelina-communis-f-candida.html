Commelina communis f. candida

Commelina communis f. candida, commonly known as the white-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking floral displays. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green veining pattern, and its flowers are typically small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The white-veined spiderwort is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
