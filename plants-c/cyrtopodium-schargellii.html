Cyrtopodium schargellii

Cyrtopodium schargellii, commonly known as the Schargell's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, allowing it to thrive in seasonal environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Cyrtopodium schargellii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
