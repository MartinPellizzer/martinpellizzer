Cyclopogon variegatus

Cyclopogon variegatus, commonly known as the variegated cyclopogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its leaves are long and pinnately compound, often displaying a variegated color pattern.

Cyclopogon variegatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
