Cryptocarya iridescens

Cryptocarya iridescens, commonly known as the shining ash or iridescent laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a striking iridescence when viewed in different light conditions. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, black berries.

Cryptocarya iridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
