Chenopodium pallasianum

Chenopodium pallasianum, commonly known as the Pallas's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves with a rough, sometimes hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are three-angled, dry capsules that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
