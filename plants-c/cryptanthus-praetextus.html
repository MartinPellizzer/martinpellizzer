Cryptanthus praetextus

Cryptanthus praetextus, commonly known as the "Cape Cod" or "Cape Cod carnation," is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with hairy, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cryptanthus praetextus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
