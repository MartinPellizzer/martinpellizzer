Crotalaria angulicaulis

Crotalaria angulicaulis, commonly known as the angled crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features distinctive angular leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, angular pod, contributing to its common name.
