Centropogon scabiosus

Centropogon scabiosus, commonly known as the rough centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
