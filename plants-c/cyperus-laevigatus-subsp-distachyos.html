Cyperus laevigatus subsp. distachyos

Cyperus laevigatus subsp.

distachyos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, with each spikelet containing two stamens, giving it the species name "distachyos.".

It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.
