Carex tenorei

Carex tenorei, commonly known as the Tenore's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the island of Socotra, which is located in the Arabian Sea. This plant is characterized by its unique, grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Carex tenorei typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its floral structure consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
