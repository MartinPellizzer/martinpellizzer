Clerodendrum ternifolium var. mexiae

Clerodendrum ternifolium var. mexiae, commonly known as Mexican flame bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
