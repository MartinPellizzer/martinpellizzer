Chaerophyllum coloratum

Chaerophyllum coloratum, commonly known as the purple carrot or purple root.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically appearing in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
