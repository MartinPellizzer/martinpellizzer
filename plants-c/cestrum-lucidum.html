Cestrum lucidum

Cestrum lucidum, commonly known as the shining trumpet bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, often fragrant scent. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cestrum lucidum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
