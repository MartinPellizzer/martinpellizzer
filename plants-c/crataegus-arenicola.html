Crataegus arenicola

Crataegus arenicola, commonly known as the sand hawthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus arenicola is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
