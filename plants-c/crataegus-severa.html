Crataegus severa

Crataegus severa, commonly known as the sharpthorn hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Crataegus severa typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various species.
