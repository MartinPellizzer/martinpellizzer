Callista amabilis

Callista amabilis, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Its fruit, which is the edible part, matures into a variety of peppers, including bell peppers and chili peppers.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
