Cissus enneaphylla

Cissus enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Cissus enneaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
