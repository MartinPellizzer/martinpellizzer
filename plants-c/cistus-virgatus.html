Cistus virgatus

Cistus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thorn or white rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus virgatus is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
