Cerastium vulgatum var. glandulosum

Cerastium vulgatum var. glandulosum, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are often glandular, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
