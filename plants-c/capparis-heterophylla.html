Capparis heterophylla

Capparis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid conditions.
