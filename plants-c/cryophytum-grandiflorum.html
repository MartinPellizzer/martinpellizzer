Cryophytum grandiflorum

Cryophytum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant frostflower, is a rare and striking plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, helping to retain moisture in the arid mountain climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
