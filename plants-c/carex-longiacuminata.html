Carex longiacuminata

Carex longiacuminata, commonly known as the long-awned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex longiacuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
