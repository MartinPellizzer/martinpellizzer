Cyclolepis genistoides

Cyclolepis genistoides, commonly known as the African broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant has slender, often thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

Cyclolepis genistoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with low rainfall.
