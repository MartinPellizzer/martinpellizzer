Carex petitiana

Carex petitiana, commonly known as the Petit's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
