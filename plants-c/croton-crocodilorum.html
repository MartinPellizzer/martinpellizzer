Croton crocodilorum

Croton crocodilorum, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often brightly colored leaves that resemble the skin of a crocodile. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Croton crocodilorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
