Cydonia maulei

Cydonia maulei, commonly known as the Maule quince, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous, spiny stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by yellow, apple-like fruits that ripen in autumn. Cydonia maulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species within the Rosaceae family.
