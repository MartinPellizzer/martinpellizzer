Cyperus buckleyi

Cyperus buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Australia and is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cyperus buckleyi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and tolerance to waterlogged conditions.
