Conyza megapotamica

Conyza megapotamica, commonly known as the South American ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
