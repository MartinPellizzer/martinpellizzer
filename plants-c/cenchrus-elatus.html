Cenchrus elatus

Cenchrus elatus, commonly known as the rigid grass or speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
