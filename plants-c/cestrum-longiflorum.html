Cestrum longiflorum

Cestrum longiflorum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that open at night and remain open until dawn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Cestrum longiflorum is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal beauty.
