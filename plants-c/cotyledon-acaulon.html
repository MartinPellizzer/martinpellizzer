Cotyledon acaulon

Cotyledon acaulon, commonly known as the "Fairy Fan" or "Ghost Plant," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a unique rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
