Chaetachme meyeri

Chaetachme meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's hairvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Meyer's hairvetch is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
