Crataegus oxyacantha var. brevispina

Crataegus oxyacantha var. brevispina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.
