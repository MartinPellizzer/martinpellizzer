Colutea humilis

Colutea humilis, commonly known as the lesser cress, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, branched stems and its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
