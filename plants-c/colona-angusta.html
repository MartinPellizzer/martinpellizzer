Colona angusta

Colona angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf colona, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
