Cotula matricarioides

Cotula matricarioides, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a low-growing stem with numerous small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cotula matricarioides is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
