Cyclosorus obstructus

Cyclosorus obstructus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a rare aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
