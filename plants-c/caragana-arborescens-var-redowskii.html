Caragana arborescens var. redowskii

Caragana arborescens var. redowskii, commonly known as the Redowskii's shrub pea, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often grow close to the ground, forming dense thickets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This hardy species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
