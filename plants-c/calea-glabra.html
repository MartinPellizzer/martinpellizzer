Calea glabra

Calea glabra, commonly known as the white-eyed plant or angel's tears, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of tiny white eyes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea glabra is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal medicine.
