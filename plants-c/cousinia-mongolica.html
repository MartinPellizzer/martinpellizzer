Cousinia mongolica

Cousinia mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

Cousinia mongolica is often found in steppes and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
