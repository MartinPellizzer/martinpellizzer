Chimonanthus nitens var. salicifolius

Chimonanthus nitens var. salicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, bright green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers in late winter or early spring, before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique blooming period and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to winter gardens.
