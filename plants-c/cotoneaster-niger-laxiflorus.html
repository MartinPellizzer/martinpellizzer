Cotoneaster niger laxiflorus

Cotoneaster niger laxiflorus, commonly known as the black cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, thorny branches. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, often blooming in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter interest.
