Comastoma polycladum

Comastoma polycladum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
