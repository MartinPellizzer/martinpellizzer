Campanula telephioides

Campanula telephioides, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Campanula telephioides is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
