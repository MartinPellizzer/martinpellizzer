Cassia taubertiana

Cassia taubertiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that provide a lush, green canopy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant flowers.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
