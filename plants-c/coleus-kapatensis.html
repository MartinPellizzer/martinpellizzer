Coleus kapatensis

Coleus kapatensis, commonly known as the Kapatensis coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often brightly colored, ranging from red, purple, and yellow to green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
