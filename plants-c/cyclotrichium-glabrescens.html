Cyclotrichium glabrescens

Cyclotrichium glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are smooth and narrowly lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Cyclotrichium glabrescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
