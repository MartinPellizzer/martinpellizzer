Campanula racemosa var. paniculiformis

Campanula racemosa var. paniculiformis, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is closely related to other campanula species, sharing similar floral structures and growth habits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
