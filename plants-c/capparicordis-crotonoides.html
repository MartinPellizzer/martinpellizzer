Capparicordis crotonoides

Capparicordis crotonoides, commonly known as the African caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and its flowers are yellow with four petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
