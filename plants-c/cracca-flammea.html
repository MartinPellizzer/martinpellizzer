Cracca flammea

Cracca flammea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cracca flammea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
