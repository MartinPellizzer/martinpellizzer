Cerasus caproniana ranunculiflora

Cerasus caproniana ranunculiflora, commonly known as the ranunculiflora cherry, is a species of Prunus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer.
