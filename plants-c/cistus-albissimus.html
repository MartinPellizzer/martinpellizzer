Cistus albissimus

Cistus albissimus, commonly known as white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus albissimus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
