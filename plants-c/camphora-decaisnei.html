Camphora decaisnei

Camphora decaisnei, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant, resinous wood. The plant is known for its ability to produce camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural preservative. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Camphora decaisnei plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
