Chondrodendron cretosum

Chondrodendron cretosum, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the presence of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
