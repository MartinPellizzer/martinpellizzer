Carex frigida var. huebneri

Carex frigida var. huebneri, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in cool, moist environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh, cold climates.
