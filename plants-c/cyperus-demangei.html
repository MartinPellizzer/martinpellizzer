Cyperus demangei

Cyperus demangei, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus demangei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
