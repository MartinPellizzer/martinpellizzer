Cicendia poeppigii

Cicendia poeppigii, commonly known as the mountain bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cicendia poeppigii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its habitat.
