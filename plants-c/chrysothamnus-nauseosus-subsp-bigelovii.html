Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. bigelovii

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

bigelovii, commonly known as Bigelow's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
