Crinum corantynum

Crinum corantynum, commonly known as the Corantyn lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllis family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, often with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Crinum corantynum is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
