Cuphea fluviatilis

Cuphea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cuphea fluviatilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
