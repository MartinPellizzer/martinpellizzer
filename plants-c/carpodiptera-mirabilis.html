Carpodiptera mirabilis

Carpodiptera mirabilis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, often with intricate patterns resembling lace. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake.

The species is also notable for its unique pollination mechanism, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators.
