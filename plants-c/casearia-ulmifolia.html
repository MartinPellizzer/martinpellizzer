Casearia ulmifolia

Casearia ulmifolia, commonly known as the Indian maple or small-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for landscaping purposes.
