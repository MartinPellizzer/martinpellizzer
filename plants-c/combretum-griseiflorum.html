Combretum griseiflorum

Combretum griseiflorum, commonly known as the grey-flowered combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish-green leaves and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and moist savannas.

Combretum griseiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
