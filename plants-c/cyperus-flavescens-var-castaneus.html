Cyperus flavescens var. castaneus

Cyperus flavescens var. castaneus, commonly known as the red rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, although it is often classified within the Cyperaceae family due to its grass-like appearance. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish-brown in color, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often pale green or brown.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet or moist environments.
