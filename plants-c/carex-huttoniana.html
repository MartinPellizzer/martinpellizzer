Carex huttoniana

Carex huttoniana, commonly known as Hutton's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, preferring moist, acidic soils. It produces small, clustered spikelets that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Hutton's sedge is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is often found in areas with high biodiversity.
