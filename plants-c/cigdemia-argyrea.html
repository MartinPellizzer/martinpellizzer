Cigdemia argyrea

Cigdemia argyrea, commonly known as silverleaf tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its feathery, needle-like foliage that turns a striking silver-white color in the fall. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Cigdemia argyrea is often found in swamps and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, acidic conditions.
