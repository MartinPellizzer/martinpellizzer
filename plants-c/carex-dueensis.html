Carex dueensis

Carex dueensis, commonly known as the due's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, which are usually borne on slender stalks. Carex dueensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
