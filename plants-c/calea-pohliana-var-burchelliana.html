Calea pohliana var. burchelliana

Calea pohliana var. burchelliana, commonly known as the flame flower or calea, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red bracts that resemble flames, which are not true flowers but specialized leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique appearance and medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and bracts are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
