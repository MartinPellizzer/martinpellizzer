Carex sparganioides var. cephaloidea

Carex sparganioides var. cephaloidea, commonly known as the purple stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its purple-tinged stems and inflorescence that consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
