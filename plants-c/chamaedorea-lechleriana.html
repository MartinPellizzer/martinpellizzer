Chamaedorea lechleriana

Chamaedorea lechleriana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a compact, upright palm native to Mexico and Central America.

It features slender, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. The plant has a short trunk and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
