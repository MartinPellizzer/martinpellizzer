Clusia parvicapsula

Clusia parvicapsula, commonly known as the dwarf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Clusia parvicapsula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
