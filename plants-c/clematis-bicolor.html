Clematis bicolor

Clematis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored clematis, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature white petals with a deep violet or purple center. The plant has compound leaves that are divided into three to five narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Clematis bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
