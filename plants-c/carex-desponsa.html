Carex desponsa

Carex desponsa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
