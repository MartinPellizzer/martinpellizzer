Carex paradoxa f. sparsiflora

Carex paradoxa f. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence is usually compact, with flowers arranged in a loose, open cluster.

The foliage is often a dark green, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
