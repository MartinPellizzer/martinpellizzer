Centaurea jacea

Centaurea jacea, commonly known as the Common Centaury, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with bright yellow centers, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. The plant grows up to 60 cm tall, with a branched stem and a basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Centaurea jacea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
