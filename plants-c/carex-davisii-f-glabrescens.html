Carex davisii f. glabrescens

Carex davisii f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous sedges, is a variant of the Carex davisii species, which belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
