Chamaecyparis glauca

Chamaecyparis glauca, commonly known as the blue cedar or Monterey cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to California.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens and landscapes. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

Chamaecyparis glauca is also valued for its aromatic wood and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
