Ceratophyllum muricatum subsp. australe

Ceratophyllum muricatum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian hornwort, is a species of aquatic plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. It is notable for its ability to provide habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
