Cercocarpus eximius

Cercocarpus eximius, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits called achenes, which are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Cercocarpus eximius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
