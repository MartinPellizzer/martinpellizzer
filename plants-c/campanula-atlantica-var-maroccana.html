Campanula atlantica var. maroccana

Campanula atlantica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
