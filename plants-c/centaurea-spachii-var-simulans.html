Centaurea spachii var. simulans

Centaurea spachii var. simulans, commonly known as the false crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and distinctive yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
