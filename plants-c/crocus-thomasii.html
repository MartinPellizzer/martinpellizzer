Crocus thomasii

Crocus thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are relatively small but robust.

Crocus thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
