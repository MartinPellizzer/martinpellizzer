Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. seminudus

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

seminudus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The subspecies seminudus is distinguished by its more reduced leaf structure compared to other subspecies of Calothamnus.
