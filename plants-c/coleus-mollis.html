Coleus mollis

Coleus mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that come in a variety of colors, including shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Coleus mollis is often cultivated for its foliage rather than its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous.
