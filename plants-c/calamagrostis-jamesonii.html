Calamagrostis jamesonii

Calamagrostis jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, arching culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are usually pale green to brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
