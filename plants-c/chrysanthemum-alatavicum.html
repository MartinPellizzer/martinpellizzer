Chrysanthemum alatavicum

Chrysanthemum alatavicum, commonly known as the Alatav Valley chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
