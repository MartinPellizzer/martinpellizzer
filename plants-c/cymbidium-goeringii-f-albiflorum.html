Cymbidium goeringii f. albiflorum

Cymbidium goeringii f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species belonging to the Cymbidium genus.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, strap-like leaves. This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the winter months.
