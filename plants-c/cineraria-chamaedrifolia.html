Cineraria chamaedrifolia

Cineraria chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features silver-gray, woolly foliage. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Cineraria chamaedrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
