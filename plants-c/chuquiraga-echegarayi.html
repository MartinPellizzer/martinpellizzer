Chuquiraga echegarayi

Chuquiraga echegarayi, commonly known as the Andean chuchundra, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chuquiraga echegarayi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
