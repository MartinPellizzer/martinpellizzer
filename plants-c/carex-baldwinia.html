Carex baldwinia

Carex baldwinia, commonly known as Baldwin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
