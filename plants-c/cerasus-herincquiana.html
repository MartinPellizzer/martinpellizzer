Cerasus herincquiana

Cerasus herincquiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus japonica, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cerasus herincquiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
