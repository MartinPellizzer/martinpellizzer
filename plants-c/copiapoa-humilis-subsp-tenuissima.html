Copiapoa humilis subsp. tenuissima

Copiapoa humilis subsp.

tenuissima, commonly known as the slender copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to northern Chile. It is a low-growing plant with a compact, globular stem that often develops a fluted appearance as it matures.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental value.
