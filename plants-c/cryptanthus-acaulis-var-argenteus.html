Cryptanthus acaulis var. argenteus

Cryptanthus acaulis var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver cryptanthus, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically in late summer. The plant has a rosette growth habit with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

This plant is popular in rock gardens and as a container specimen due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
