Centaurea monticola

Centaurea monticola, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Centaurea monticola is often found in alpine and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
