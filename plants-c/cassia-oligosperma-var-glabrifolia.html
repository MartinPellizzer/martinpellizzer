Cassia oligosperma var. glabrifolia

Cassia oligosperma var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the glabrous cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically green and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
