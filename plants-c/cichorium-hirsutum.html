Cichorium hirsutum

Cichorium hirsutum, commonly known as French endive, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are often used in salads. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bitter-tasting tubular fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its edible leaves and roots.

The plant contains compounds that contribute to its distinctive flavor and potential health benefits.
