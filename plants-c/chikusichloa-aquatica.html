Chikusichloa aquatica

Chikusichloa aquatica, commonly known as watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands, where it forms dense mats on the surface or submerged vegetation. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often floating or semi-submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing water surfaces.

Chikusichloa aquatica is also used in ornamental gardening and traditional medicine in some cultures.
