Carpha novae-zelandiae

Carpha novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, thriving in areas with consistently moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Carpha novae-zelandiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water garden settings.
