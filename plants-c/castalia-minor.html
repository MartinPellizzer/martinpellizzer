Castalia minor

Castalia minor, commonly known as the lesser castalia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color. Its small, white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Castalia minor is often found in rich, loamy soils and is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots.
