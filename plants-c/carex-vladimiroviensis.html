Carex vladimiroviensis

Carex vladimiroviensis, commonly known as the Vladimirov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
