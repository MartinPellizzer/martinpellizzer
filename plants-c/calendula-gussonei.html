Calendula gussonei

Calendula gussonei, commonly known as the Italian calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange petals and sturdy, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are known for their medicinal properties.

Calendula gussonei is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
