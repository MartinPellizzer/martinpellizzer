Cynanchum boldinghii

Cynanchum boldinghii, commonly known as Bolding's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
