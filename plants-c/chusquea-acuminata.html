Chusquea acuminata

Chusquea acuminata, commonly known as the needle-like bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its structural similarity to bamboo, it is sometimes used in traditional construction and crafts in its native regions.
