Citrus × tachibana subf. otachibana

Citrus × tachibana subf.

otachibana, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or sudachi, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan. It produces small, round fruits with a thin, fragrant peel and a segmented, juicy pulp.

The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and citrus fruits. The subsp.

otachibana is distinguished by its more compact growth and unique fruit characteristics compared to other citrus species.
