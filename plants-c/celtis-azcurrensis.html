Celtis azcurrensis

Celtis azcurrensis, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry to mesic habitats, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Celtis azcurrensis is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
