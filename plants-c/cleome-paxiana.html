Cleome paxiana

Cleome paxiana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Cleome paxiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
