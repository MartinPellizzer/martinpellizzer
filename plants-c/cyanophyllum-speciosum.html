Cyanophyllum speciosum

Cyanophyllum speciosum, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cyanophyllum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
