Canavalia oxyphylla

Canavalia oxyphylla, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Canavalia oxyphylla also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
