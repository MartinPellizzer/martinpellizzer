Chrysanthemum japonense

Chrysanthemum japonense, commonly known as Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the fall and are often used in traditional Japanese gardens.

The botanical name reflects its origin and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
