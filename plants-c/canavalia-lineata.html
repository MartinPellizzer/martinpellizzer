Canavalia lineata

Canavalia lineata, commonly known as the Everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that remain attached to the plant for an extended period. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. Canavalia lineata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
