Celastrus humilis

Celastrus humilis, commonly known as the low ceiba, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Celastrus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
