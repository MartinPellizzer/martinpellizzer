Cucumis melo var. arundel

Cucumis melo var. arundel, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruit, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces pale green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its fruit is typically harvested when still immature, with a crisp texture and a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
