Calamintha officinalis subsp. subnuda

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

subnuda, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features slender, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive mint-like fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to violet, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
