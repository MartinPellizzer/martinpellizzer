Caladenia interjacens

Caladenia interjacens, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is an elegant flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Caladenia interjacens is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
