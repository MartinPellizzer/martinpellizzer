Citrus aurantium

Citrus aurantium, commonly known as bitter orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, which is typically bitter and not eaten fresh, contains a thick, segmented rind used in perfumery and medicine. The plant is valued for its essential oils extracted from its peel, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional remedies.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
