Chelonanthera ventricosa

Chelonanthera ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows as a climbing vine with tendrils that help it attach to supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
