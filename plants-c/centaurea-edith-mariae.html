Centaurea edith-mariae

Centaurea edith-mariae, commonly known as Edith-Maria's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its striking purple-blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea edith-mariae is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
