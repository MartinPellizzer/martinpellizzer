Calathea rufibarba

Calathea rufibarba, commonly known as the Red-Barked Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage changes color in response to light, a phenomenon called nyctinasty.

Calathea rufibarba is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
