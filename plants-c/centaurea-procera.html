Centaurea procera

Centaurea procera, commonly known as the tall knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Centaurea procera is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
