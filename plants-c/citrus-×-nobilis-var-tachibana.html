Citrus × nobilis var. tachibana

Citrus × nobilis var. tachibana, commonly known as the Tachibana mandarin, is a citrus fruit native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, round, and thick-skinned fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The Tachibana mandarin is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese cuisine and medicine.
