Cordia viridis

Cordia viridis, commonly known as the green cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in coastal and dry habitats.

Cordia viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
