Cola flavescens var. hirsuta

Cola flavescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cola or yellow cola, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and less-studied species.
