Carex macloviana subsp. pachystachya

Carex macloviana subsp.

pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and dense, grass-like foliage.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
