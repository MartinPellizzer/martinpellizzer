Centropogon cordatus

Centropogon cordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.
