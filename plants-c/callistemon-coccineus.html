Callistemon coccineus

Callistemon coccineus, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flower spikes, which are actually clusters of stamens that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
