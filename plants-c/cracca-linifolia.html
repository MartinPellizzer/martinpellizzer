Cracca linifolia

Cracca linifolia, commonly known as the silky cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cracca linifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
