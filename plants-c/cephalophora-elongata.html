Cephalophora elongata

Cephalophora elongata, commonly known as the slender sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize sand dunes, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Cephalophora elongata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
