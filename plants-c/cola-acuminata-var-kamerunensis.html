Cola acuminata var. kamerunensis

Cola acuminata var. kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon cola or African cola, is a species of the Sterculiaceae family native to western Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits. The plant produces caffeine-containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it an important plant in its native region.
