Cytisus striatus subsp. striatus

Cytisus striatus subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically narrow with a distinctive striated appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
