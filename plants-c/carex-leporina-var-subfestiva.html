Carex leporina var. subfestiva

Carex leporina var. subfestiva, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
