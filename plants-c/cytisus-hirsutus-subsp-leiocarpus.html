Cytisus hirsutus subsp. leiocarpus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

leiocarpus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
