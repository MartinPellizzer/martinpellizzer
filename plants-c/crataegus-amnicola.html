Crataegus amnicola

Crataegus amnicola, commonly known as the Amniscous hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
