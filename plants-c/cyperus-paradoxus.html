Cyperus paradoxus

Cyperus paradoxus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Cyperus paradoxus is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it a common ornamental and wetland plant in many regions.
