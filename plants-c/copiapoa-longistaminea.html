Copiapoa longistaminea

Copiapoa longistaminea, commonly known as the long-stemmed copiapoa, is a cacti species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces pale green to grayish-green spines and has a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. It blooms with yellow flowers that appear at the base of the stem during the cooler months.

This species is adapted to extreme arid conditions and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
