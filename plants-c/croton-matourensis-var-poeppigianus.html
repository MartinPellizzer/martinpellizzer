Croton matourensis var. poeppigianus

Croton matourensis var. poeppigianus, commonly known as the Poepig's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from bright green to deep red and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is typically compact, with a dense, bushy form that makes it suitable for ornamental use in shaded garden settings.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its unique leaf patterns.
