Carex pilulifera f. fuscidula

Carex pilulifera f. fuscidula, commonly known as the purple false spike-rush, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America, typically found in wetland or moist soil environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a purple or reddish hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to brown in color.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic qualities in garden settings.
