Cassia atroviridis

Cassia atroviridis, commonly known as black-veined cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with dark green veins and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia atroviridis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
