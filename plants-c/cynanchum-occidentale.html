Cynanchum occidentale

Cynanchum occidentale, commonly known as western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Cynanchum occidentale is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
