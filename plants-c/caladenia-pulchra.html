Caladenia pulchra

Caladenia pulchra, commonly known as the pink spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in woodland areas, preferring well-drained conditions. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single, showy pink or purple flower that resembles a spider in appearance. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly thynnid wasps, which are attracted by its nectar.

Caladenia pulchra is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
