Coronilla minima var. floribunda

Coronilla minima var. floribunda, commonly known as the small-flowered crown vetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, often covering rocky or dry soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for soil stabilization and as a ornamental plant in dry gardens.
