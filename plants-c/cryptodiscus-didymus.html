Cryptodiscus didymus

Cryptodiscus didymus, commonly known as the double disc lily, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features a basal rosette of strap-shaped leaves and produces two large, disc-like flowers that open simultaneously.

Cryptodiscus didymus thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
