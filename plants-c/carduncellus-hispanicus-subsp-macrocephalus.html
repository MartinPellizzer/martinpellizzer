Carduncellus hispanicus subsp. macrocephalus

Carduncellus hispanicus subsp.

macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
