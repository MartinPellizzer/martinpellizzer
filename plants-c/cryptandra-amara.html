Cryptandra amara

Cryptandra amara, commonly known as the bitter cryptandra, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Cryptandra amara is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.
