Croton sambiranensis

Croton sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with varied leaf shapes and patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
