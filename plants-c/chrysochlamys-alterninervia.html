Chrysochlamys alterninervia

Chrysochlamys alterninervia, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement, and its flowers are yellowish-green, clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky coating, aiding in seed dispersal by birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which attach to the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Chrysochlamys alterninervia plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
