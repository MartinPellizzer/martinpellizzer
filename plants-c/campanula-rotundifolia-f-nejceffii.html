Campanula rotundifolia f. nejceffii

Campanula rotundifolia f. nejceffii, commonly known as the Nejceff's bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The leaves are round or heart-shaped, with a velvety texture, and the plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden conditions.
