Cleome foliosa var. lutea

Cleome foliosa var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cleome foliosa var. lutea is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
