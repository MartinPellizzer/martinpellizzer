Centaurea × lieblingii

Centaurea × lieblingii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Centaurea × lieblingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in naturalized areas.
