Carex paupercula var. irrigua

Carex paupercula var. irrigua, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. This variety is native to moist, temperate regions and thrives in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wet soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
