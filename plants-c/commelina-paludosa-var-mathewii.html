Commelina paludosa var. mathewii

Commelina paludosa var. mathewii, commonly known as the Mathew's spikerush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
