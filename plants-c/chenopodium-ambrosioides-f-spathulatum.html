Chenopodium ambrosioides f. spathulatum

Chenopodium ambrosioides f. spathulatum, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the species characterized by its broader, more spatulate leaves compared to the typical form.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually green with a slightly rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

This form is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive foliage.
