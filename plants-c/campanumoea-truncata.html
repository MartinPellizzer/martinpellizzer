Campanumoea truncata

Campanumoea truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Campanumoea truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
