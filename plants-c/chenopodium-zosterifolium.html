Chenopodium zosterifolium

Chenopodium zosterifolium, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, spinach-like flavor.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
