Celtis orthacanthos

Celtis orthacanthos, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Celtis orthacanthos is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
