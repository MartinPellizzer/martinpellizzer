Chamaecerasus niagerilli

Chamaecerasus niagerilli, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

The shrub is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant floral display.
