Citrus × nobilis var. kikaijimense

Citrus × nobilis var. kikaijimense, commonly known as the Kikaijima mandarin, is a hybrid citrus variety originating from the Kikaijima Islands in Japan.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in both fresh consumption and traditional Japanese cuisine.
