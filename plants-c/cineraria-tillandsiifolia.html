Cineraria tillandsiifolia

Cineraria tillandsiifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
