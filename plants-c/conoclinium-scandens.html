Conoclinium scandens

Conoclinium scandens, commonly known as sweet pepper vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of orange and yellow. The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
