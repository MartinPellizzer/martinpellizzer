Commelina scabra

Commelina scabra, commonly known as rough commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in the Commelinaceae family.
