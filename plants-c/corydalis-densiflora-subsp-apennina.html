Corydalis densiflora subsp. apennina

Corydalis densiflora subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine denseflower corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
