Campanula elatines var. garganica

Campanula elatines var. garganica, commonly known as the Garganica bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is a hardy perennial that is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
