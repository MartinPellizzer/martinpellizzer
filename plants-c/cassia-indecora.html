Cassia indecora

Cassia indecora, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia indecora is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
