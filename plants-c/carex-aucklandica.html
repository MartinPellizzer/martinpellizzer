Carex aucklandica

Carex aucklandica, commonly known as the Auckland rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous.

Carex aucklandica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
