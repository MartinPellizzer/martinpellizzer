Calytrix oldfieldii

Calytrix oldfieldii, commonly known as the Oldfield's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually red or pink, and it produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Calytrix oldfieldii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
