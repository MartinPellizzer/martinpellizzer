Campanula latisepala var. dubia

Campanula latisepala var. dubia, commonly known as the broad-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the stems are typically covered in fine pubescence. This variety is noted for its robust nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
