Cynanchum sibiricum var. triangularilobatum

Cynanchum sibiricum var. triangularilobatum, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and typically have five petals, with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

The plant produces small, dry fruits called follicles, which contain several seeds.
