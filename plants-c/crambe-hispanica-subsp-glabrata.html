Crambe hispanica subsp. glabrata

Crambe hispanica subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
