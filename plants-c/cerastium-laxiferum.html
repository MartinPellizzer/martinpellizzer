Cerastium laxiferum

Cerastium laxiferum, commonly known as the slender chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and hairy, often prostrate stems. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cerastium laxiferum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through stolons, forming dense mats.
