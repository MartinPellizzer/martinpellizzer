Carex moritzii var. laxior

Carex moritzii var. laxior, commonly known as the looseleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
