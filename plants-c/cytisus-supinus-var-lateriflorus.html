Cytisus supinus var. lateriflorus

Cytisus supinus var. lateriflorus, commonly known as the creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features small, oval leaves with a hairy texture and produces legume pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
