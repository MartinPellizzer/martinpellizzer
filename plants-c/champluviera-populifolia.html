Champluviera populifolia

Champluviera populifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
