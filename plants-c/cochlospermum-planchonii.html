Cochlospermum planchonii

Cochlospermum planchonii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Cochlospermum planchonii is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in urban and rural landscapes.
