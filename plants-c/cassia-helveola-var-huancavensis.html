Cassia helveola var. huancavensis

Cassia helveola var. huancavensis, commonly known as the Huancavelica cassia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds coated in a sticky, aromatic resin. It thrives in dry to semi-arid climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
