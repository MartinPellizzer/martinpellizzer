Cassia crassiramea

Cassia crassiramea, commonly known as the broad-leaf cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often broad and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia crassiramea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
