Chionanthus grandifolius

Chionanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically medium to dark green. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and have a distinctive fringe-like appearance around the petals.

Chionanthus grandifolius thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
