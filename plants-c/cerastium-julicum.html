Cerastium julicum

Cerastium julicum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Cerastium julicum thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.
