Crocus biflorus f. lineatus

Crocus biflorus f. lineatus, commonly known as the lined crocus, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, early spring blooms with petals that range from white to pale yellow, often adorned with intricate purple or brown markings. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral patterns and early blooming habit make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
