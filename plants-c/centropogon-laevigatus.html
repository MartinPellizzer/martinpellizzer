Centropogon laevigatus

Centropogon laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in its native habitat.
