Calamagrostis azorica

Calamagrostis azorica, commonly known as the Azorean sweet grass, is a species of grass native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calamagrostis azorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
