Croton engelmannii var. albinoides

Croton engelmannii var. albinoides, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a white or pale underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed, spiny capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in desert landscapes.
