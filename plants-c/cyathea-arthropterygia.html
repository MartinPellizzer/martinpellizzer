Cyathea arthropterygia

Cyathea arthropterygia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while its fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea arthropterygia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in tropical ecosystems.
