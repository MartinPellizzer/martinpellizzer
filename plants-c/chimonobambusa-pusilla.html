Chimonobambusa pusilla

Chimonobambusa pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a small, clump-forming bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect culms that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green and covered in a fine, silky texture. It produces small, pale green inflorescences that are clustered at the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
