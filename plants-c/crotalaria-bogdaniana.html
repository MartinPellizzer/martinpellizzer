Crotalaria bogdaniana

Crotalaria bogdaniana, commonly known as the Bogdan's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal that attracts pollinators.

Crotalaria bogdaniana is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.
