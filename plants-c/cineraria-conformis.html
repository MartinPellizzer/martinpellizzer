Cineraria conformis

Cineraria conformis, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Cineraria conformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
