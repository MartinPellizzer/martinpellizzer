Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. elata

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. elata, commonly known as tall reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value.

It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.
