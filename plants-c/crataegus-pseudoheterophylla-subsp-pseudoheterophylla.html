Crataegus pseudoheterophylla subsp. pseudoheterophylla

Crataegus pseudoheterophylla subsp.

pseudoheterophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which vary in shape and size depending on light conditions, a trait known as heterophylly.

The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have serrated margins.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.
