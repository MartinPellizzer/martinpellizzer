Calamintha alpina var. cuneata

Calamintha alpina var. cuneata, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
