Cimicifuga racemosa f. dissecta

Cimicifuga racemosa f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected bugbane, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, white, lace-like flowers. The foliage is typically deeply lobed, giving the plant its "dissected" appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.
