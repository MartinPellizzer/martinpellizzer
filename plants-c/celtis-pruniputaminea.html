Celtis pruniputaminea

Celtis pruniputaminea, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Celtis pruniputaminea is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate urban environments.
