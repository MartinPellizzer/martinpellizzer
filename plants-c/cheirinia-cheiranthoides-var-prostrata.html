Cheirinia cheiranthoides var. prostrata

Cheirinia cheiranthoides var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate cheirinia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to coastal or dry garden settings.
