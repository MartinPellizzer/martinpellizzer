Calamagrostis rivalis

Calamagrostis rivalis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis rivalis plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
