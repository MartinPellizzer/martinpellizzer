Cyperus bipontini

Cyperus bipontini, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus bipontini is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
