Cortusa semenovii

Cortusa semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Cortusa semenovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
