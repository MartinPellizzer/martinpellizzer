Citrus × nobilis subf. tankan

Citrus × nobilis subf.

tankan, commonly known as the Tahitian lime or kaffir lime. This subsp.

is characterized by its small, aromatic fruits with a distinctive bumpy, green rind. The leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, typical of the citrus family. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
