Cucumis melo subsp. microcarpus

Cucumis melo subsp.

microcarpus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivated variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, elongated fruit that typically measures between 10 to 20 centimeters in length.

The plant is an annual herb with trailing stems and hairy leaves, and it produces yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. The fruit has a crisp, juicy texture and is often used in fresh salads or as a snack.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants such as cucumbers and squash.
