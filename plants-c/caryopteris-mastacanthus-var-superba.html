Caryopteris mastacanthus var. superba

Caryopteris mastacanthus var. superba, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its profuse display of sky-blue, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery-blue pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
