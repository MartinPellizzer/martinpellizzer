Chiropetalum astroplethes

Chiropetalum astroplethes, commonly known as the star-leaf aster.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive star-shaped appearance, giving the plant its common name.

Its flowers bloom in the fall, producing small yellow to white florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
