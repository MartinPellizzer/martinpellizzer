Cathaya argyrophylla subsp. sutchuenensis

Cathaya argyrophylla subsp.

sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan silver fir, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China. It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white bark and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its branches.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is a member of the Pinaceae family and is notable for its slow growth and long lifespan.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
