Cassia javanica var. agnes

Cassia javanica var. agnes, commonly known as golden shower tree or *Saraca asiatica*, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three elliptical leaflets, and the bark is smooth and light brown in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
