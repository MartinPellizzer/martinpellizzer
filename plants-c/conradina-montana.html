Conradina montana

Conradina montana, commonly known as mountain inkberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient addition to native plant gardens.
