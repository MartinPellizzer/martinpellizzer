Coburgia humilis

Coburgia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf poinsettia, is a low-growing shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal color change.

Coburgia humilis is a popular choice for indoor gardening and holiday decorations.
