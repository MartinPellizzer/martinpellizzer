Cassia acosmifolia var. oropedii

Cassia acosmifolia var. oropedii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
