Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as the pot marigold, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces bright orange to yellow flowers with prominent central disk florets. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Calendula officinalis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
