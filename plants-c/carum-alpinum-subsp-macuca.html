Carum alpinum subsp. macuca

Carum alpinum subsp.

macuca, commonly known as Alpine caraway, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky and mountainous environments, thriving at high elevations.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers in umbels. Its fruits are small, dry, and contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in some cuisines.
