Cirsium suzukii

Cirsium suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium suzukii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
