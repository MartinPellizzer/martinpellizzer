Conophytum vitreopapillum

Conophytum vitreopapillum, commonly known as the glassy rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
