Calceolaria

Calceolaria, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Calceolariaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. Calceolaria species are native to South America, particularly the Andes region, where they thrive in cool, moist environments. They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and unique appearance.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to the plant's overall aesthetic appeal.
