Cyperus polyphyllus

Cyperus polyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Cyperus polyphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
