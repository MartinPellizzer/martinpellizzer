Cirsium virginianum f. revolutum

Cirsium virginianum f. revolutum, commonly known as the Virginia thistle or prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, and the leaves are lance-shaped with distinctive revolute margins.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
