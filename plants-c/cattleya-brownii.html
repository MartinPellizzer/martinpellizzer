Cattleya brownii

Cattleya brownii, commonly known as the Brown's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cattleya brownii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms in horticultural settings.
