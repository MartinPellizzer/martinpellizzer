Crepinia andropogon

Crepinia andropogon, commonly known as false switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Crepiniandropogon is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
