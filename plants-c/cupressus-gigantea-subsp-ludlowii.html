Cupressus gigantea subsp. ludlowii

Cupressus gigantea subsp.

ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's giant cypress, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, narrow pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in cones, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy evergreen suitable for coastal and inland landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round structure in gardens and natural settings.
