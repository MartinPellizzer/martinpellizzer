Chrysothamnus elatior

Chrysothamnus elatior, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-green foliage and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Chrysothamnus elatior is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.
