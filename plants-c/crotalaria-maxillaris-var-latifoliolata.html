Crotalaria maxillaris var. latifoliolata

Crotalaria maxillaris var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, often with two lobes on each petal. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.
