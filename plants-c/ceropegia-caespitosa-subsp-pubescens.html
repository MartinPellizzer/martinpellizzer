Ceropegia caespitosa subsp. pubescens

Ceropegia caespitosa subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant features dense pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and its leaves are generally opposite and ovate. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions of its native range.
