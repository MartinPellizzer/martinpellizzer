Crataegus exornata

Crataegus exornata, commonly known as the ornate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Crataegus exornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
