Carex behringensis var. pallidior

Carex behringensis var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale carex, is a sedge species native to the wetlands and marshes of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in areas with standing water or saturated ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually light green to pale blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, inconspicuous brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
