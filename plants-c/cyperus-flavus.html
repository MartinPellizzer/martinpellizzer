Cyperus flavus

Cyperus flavus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus flavus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
