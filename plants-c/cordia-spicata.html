Cordia spicata

Cordia spicata, commonly known as the spiny cordia, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Cordia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
