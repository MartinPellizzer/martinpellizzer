Cynorkis laggiarae

Cynorkis laggiarae, commonly known as the Laggiara's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in height. Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water. Its cones, which are either male or female, play a crucial role in its reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Cynorkis laggiarae is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
