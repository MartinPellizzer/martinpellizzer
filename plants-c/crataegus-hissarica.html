Crataegus hissarica

Crataegus hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features thorny branches. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crataegus hissarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
