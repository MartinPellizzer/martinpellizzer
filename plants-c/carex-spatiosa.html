Carex spatiosa

Carex spatiosa, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex spatiosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
