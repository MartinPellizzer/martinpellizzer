Calanthe arcuata

Calanthe arcuata, commonly known as the arched calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Calanthe arcuata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
