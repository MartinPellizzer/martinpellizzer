Caraipa psilocarpa

Caraipa psilocarpa, commonly known as the Caribbean psilocybin mushroom, is a species of psychedelic mushroom native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the genus Psilocybe and is characterized by its small, cap-like structure with a distinctive coloration. The fruiting bodies typically appear in humid, tropical environments, often growing on decaying wood or leaf litter. This species contains the psychoactive compounds psilocybin and psilocin, which are responsible for its hallucinogenic effects.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in both mycological research and ethnobotanical studies.
