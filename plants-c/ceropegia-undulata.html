Ceropegia undulata

Ceropegia undulata, commonly known as the African violet or string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features cascading stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
