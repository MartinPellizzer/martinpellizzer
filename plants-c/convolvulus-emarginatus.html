Convolvulus emarginatus

Convolvulus emarginatus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Convolvulus emarginatus is adapted to withstand saline conditions and is commonly used in coastal landscaping.
