Cotula australis

Cotula australis, commonly known as the Australian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Cotula australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
