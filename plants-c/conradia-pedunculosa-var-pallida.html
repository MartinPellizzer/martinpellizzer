Conradia pedunculosa var. pallida

Conradia pedunculosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale conradia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying its ecological adaptations.
