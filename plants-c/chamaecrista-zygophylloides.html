Chamaecrista zygophylloides

Chamaecrista zygophylloides, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
