Chenopodium album subsp. karoi

Chenopodium album subsp.

karoi, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its succulent, triangular leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant produces small, round, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
