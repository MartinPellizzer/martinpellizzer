Coccothrinax salvatoris subsp. loricata

Coccothrinax salvatoris subsp.

loricata, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean. It is a small, slow-growing palm characterized by its slender trunk and dense, feather-like foliage.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by various wildlife species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for thatching.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and limestone soils, making it resilient to salt spray and wind.
