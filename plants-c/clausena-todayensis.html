Clausena todayensis

Clausena todayensis, commonly known as the African yellow-wood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or yellow fruits that are edible.

Clausena todayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
