Chenopodium auricomum f. subglabrum

Chenopodium auricomum f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name "subglabrum.". The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in disturbed soils, often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It is a common weed in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.

The species is also valued in some traditional medicinal practices for its purported health benefits.
