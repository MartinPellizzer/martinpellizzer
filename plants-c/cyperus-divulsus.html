Cyperus divulsus

Cyperus divulsus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it often thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along waterways. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
