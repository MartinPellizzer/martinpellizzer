Cassia renigera

Cassia renigera, commonly known as black cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, cylindrical pods containing dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds.

Cassia renigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
