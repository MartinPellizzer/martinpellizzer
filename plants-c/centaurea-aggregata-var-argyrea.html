Centaurea aggregata var. argyrea

Centaurea aggregata var. argyrea, commonly known as silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with distinctive silver or white markings. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
