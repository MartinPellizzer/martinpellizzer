Colubrina ovalifolia

Colubrina ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are a striking feature of the plant.

Colubrina ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
