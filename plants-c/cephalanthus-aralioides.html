Cephalanthus aralioides

Cephalanthus aralioides, commonly known as the swamp sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries are red and attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
