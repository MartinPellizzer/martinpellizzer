Cerastium persicum

Cerastium persicum, commonly known as Persian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Cerastium persicum is often found in meadows, rocky areas, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
