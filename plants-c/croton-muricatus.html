Croton muricatus

Croton muricatus, commonly known as the spiked croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
