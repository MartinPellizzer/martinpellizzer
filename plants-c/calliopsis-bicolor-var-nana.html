Calliopsis bicolor var. nana

Calliopsis bicolor var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed Mary, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It produces striking blue and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its small size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
