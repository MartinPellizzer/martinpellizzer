Camellia japonica f. polypetala

Camellia japonica f. polypetala, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with polypetaloid form, is a hybrid cultivar characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features large, showy flowers with petals that are elongated and ribbon-like, giving it a unique, almost lace-like appearance. The plant is native to China and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding color to the early part of the growing season.

The leaves are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.
