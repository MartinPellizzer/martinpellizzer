Cardamine hirsuta var. pensylvanica

Cardamine hirsuta var. pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
