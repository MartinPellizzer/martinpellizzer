Caladium nymphaeifolium

Caladium nymphaeifolium, commonly known as the water caladium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of white, pink, and green. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, while its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellow.

It is cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
