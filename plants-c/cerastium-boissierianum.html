Cerastium boissierianum

Cerastium boissierianum, commonly known as the Boissier's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite or whorled. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
