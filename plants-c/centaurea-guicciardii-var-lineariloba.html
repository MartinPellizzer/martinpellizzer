Centaurea guicciardii var. lineariloba

Centaurea guicciardii var. lineariloba, commonly known as the slender stem centaury, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its linear, lance-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers make it a distinctive species.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
