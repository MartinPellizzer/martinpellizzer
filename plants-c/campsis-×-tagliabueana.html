Campsis × tagliabueana

Campsis × tagliabueana, commonly known as the orange flame trumpetvine, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release bright red, winged seeds.
