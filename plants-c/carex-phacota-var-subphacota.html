Carex phacota var. subphacota

Carex phacota var. subphacota, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months, often with a pale green or brownish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
