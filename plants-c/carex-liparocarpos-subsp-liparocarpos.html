Carex liparocarpos subsp. liparocarpos

Carex liparocarpos subsp.

liparocarpos, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a valuable indicator species for assessing ecosystem health in cold climates.
