Celastrus kusanoi

Celastrus kusanoi, commonly known as the Japanese staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Celastrus kusanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
