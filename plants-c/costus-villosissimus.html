Costus villosissimus

Costus villosissimus, commonly known as the woolly costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences and typically have a yellowish or greenish hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
