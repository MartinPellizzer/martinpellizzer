Crocus fussianus

Crocus fussianus, commonly known as the Italian crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a violet or purple center. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus fussianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
