Crinum distichum

Crinum distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked crinum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like leaves that are arranged in two rows, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Crinum distichum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
