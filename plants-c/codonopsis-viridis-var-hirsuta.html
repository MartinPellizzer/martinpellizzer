Codonopsis viridis var. hirsuta

Codonopsis viridis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy green bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, often with a slight reddish tinge. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
