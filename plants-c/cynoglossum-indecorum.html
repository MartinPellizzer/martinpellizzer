Cynoglossum indecorum

Cynoglossum indecorum, commonly known as the purple fig, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or violet hues. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
