Cardamine sylvatica var. regeliana

Cardamine sylvatica var. regeliana, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
