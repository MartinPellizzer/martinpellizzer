Cucubalus baccifer f. atropurpureus

Cucubalus baccifer f. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-fruited hog plum, is a variant of the hog plum species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purplish-red fruits, which are typically small and round, with a thick, leathery skin. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and can be consumed raw or cooked.
