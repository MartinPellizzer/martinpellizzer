Collinsonia elsholtzioides

Collinsonia elsholtzioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its flowering display.
