Convolvulus holosericeus subsp. holosericeus

Convolvulus holosericeus subsp.

holosericeus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its twining stems and showy blooms.
