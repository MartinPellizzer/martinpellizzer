Clinopodium lanceolatum

Clinopodium lanceolatum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as stream banks and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its lance-shaped leaves are opposite and often have a serrated edge, while its small, tubular flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

The plant is known for its strong mint-like fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
