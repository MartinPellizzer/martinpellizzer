Commelina nudiflora

Commelina nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, which are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands, forests, and along stream banks.
