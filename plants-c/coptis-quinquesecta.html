Coptis quinquesecta

Coptis quinquesecta, commonly known as the western goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids that have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
