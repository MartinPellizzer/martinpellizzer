Courrantia

Courrantia, commonly known as the redroot or redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. Courrantia is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
