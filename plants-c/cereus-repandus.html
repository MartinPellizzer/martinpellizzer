Cereus repandus

Cereus repandus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that are often branched and bear sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cereus repandus is a popular ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and adaptability to indoor environments.
