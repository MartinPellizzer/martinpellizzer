Carex vulpina

Carex vulpina, commonly known as the fox sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Carex vulpina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
