Clusia tetrastigma

Clusia tetrastigma, commonly known as the four-stemmed glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Clusia tetrastigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
