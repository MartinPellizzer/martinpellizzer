Cattleya virginalis

Cattleya virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs that support its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cattleya virginalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
