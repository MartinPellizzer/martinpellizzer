Combretum tetragonocarpum var. mokimii

Combretum tetragonocarpum var. mokimii, commonly known as the Mokimbi combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
