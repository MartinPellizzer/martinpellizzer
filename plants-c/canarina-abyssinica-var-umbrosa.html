Canarina abyssinica var. umbrosa

Canarina abyssinica var. umbrosa, commonly known as the Abyssinian canary, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted to attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are thick and waxy, allowing it to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
