Chaerefolium cerefolium subsp. trichospermum

Chaerefolium cerefolium subsp.

trichospermum, commonly known as the white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive white to pale green underside.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common component of the flora in temperate regions.
