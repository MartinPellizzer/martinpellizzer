Crataegus laevigata subsp. palmstruchii

Crataegus laevigata subsp.

palmstruchii, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
