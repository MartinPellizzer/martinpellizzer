Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. montanus

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the mountain goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
