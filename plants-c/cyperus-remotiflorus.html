Cyperus remotiflorus

Cyperus remotiflorus, commonly known as the distant-flower sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus remotiflorus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
