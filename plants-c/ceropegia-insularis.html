Ceropegia insularis

Ceropegia insularis, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a succulent species native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, tubular stems that often curl into a star-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators, such as certain moths. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia insularis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
