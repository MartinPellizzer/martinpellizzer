Celastrus oppositus

Celastrus oppositus, commonly known as the oppositeleaf staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant belongs to the Celastraceae family and is often found climbing or trailing along shrubs and trees. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
