Cornus bretschneideri var. gracilis

Cornus bretschneideri var. gracilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It features slender, arching stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red or purple berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
