Centaurea glaberrima subsp. divergens

Centaurea glaberrima subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the divergent bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its striking blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branching.
