Cotyledon lurida

Cotyledon lurida, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Cotyledon lurida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
