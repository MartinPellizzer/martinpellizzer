Cytisus pannonicus var. prodanii

Cytisus pannonicus var. prodanii, commonly known as the Pannonian broom, is a shrub native to the Pannonian region of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are often clustered in racemes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
