Canthium setosum

Canthium setosum, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in complex inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium setosum produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
