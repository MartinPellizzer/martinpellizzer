Chenopodium berlandieri f. dentatum

Chenopodium berlandieri f. dentatum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit capsules that contain seeds with a distinctive hooked shape. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to North America. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Buffalo bur is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
