Chenopodium candicans

Chenopodium candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Chenopodium candicans thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
