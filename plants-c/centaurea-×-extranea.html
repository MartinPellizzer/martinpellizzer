Centaurea × extranea

Centaurea × extranea, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and bright blue to purple flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Centaurea × extranea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
