Caryota rumphiana var. philippinensis

Caryota rumphiana var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine fishtail palm, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Philippines.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are deeply divided, resembling the tail of a fish, hence its common name. These leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
