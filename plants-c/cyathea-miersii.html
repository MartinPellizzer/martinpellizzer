Cyathea miersii

Cyathea miersii, commonly known as the Miers' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is evergreen, with a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea miersii plays a significant ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
