Cryptolappa hirsuta

Cryptolappa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cryptolappa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
