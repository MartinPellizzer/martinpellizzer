Cerasus jamasakura f. pendula

Cerasus jamasakura f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that drape downward, creating a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers in early spring, which bloom before the leaves emerge. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic beauty and seasonal display.
