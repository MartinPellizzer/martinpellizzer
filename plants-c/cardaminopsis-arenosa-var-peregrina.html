Cardaminopsis arenosa var. peregrina

Cardaminopsis arenosa var. peregrina, commonly known as the wandering rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in crevices and on dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This variety is distinguished by its variable distribution and morphological traits, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
