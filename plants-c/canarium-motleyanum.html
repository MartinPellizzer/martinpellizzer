Canarium motleyanum

Canarium motleyanum, commonly known as the Motley's candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
