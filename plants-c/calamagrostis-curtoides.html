Calamagrostis curtoides

Calamagrostis curtoides, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis curtoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
