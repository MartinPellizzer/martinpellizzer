Cymbidium trinerve

Cymbidium trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color. Its flowers are often used in traditional floral arrangements due to their elegance and longevity.

Cymbidium trinerve is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for both horticultural and cultural purposes.
