Cirsium orizabense

Cirsium orizabense, commonly known as the Orizaba thistle, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium orizabense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
