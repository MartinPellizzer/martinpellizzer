Clematis simsii var. chrysocarpa

Clematis simsii var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique feature is the development of small, yellowish fruits that resemble miniature citrus fruits, giving it its common name.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
