Comarostaphylis lucida

Comarostaphylis lucida, commonly known as the shining sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and can withstand periods of drought due to its succulent leaves. Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Comarostaphylis lucida plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
