Centaurea carolipauana

Centaurea carolipauana, commonly known as the Carolinian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a noxious weed.
