Chenopodium urbicum subsp. sinicum

Chenopodium urbicum subsp.

sinicum, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy stems that can store water.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and urban environments, making it a common weed in gardens and along roadsides.
