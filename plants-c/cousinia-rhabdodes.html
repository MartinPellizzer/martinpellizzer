Cousinia rhabdodes

Cousinia rhabdodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system for water absorption. It often grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Cousinia rhabdodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
