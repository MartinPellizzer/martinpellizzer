Chrysophyllum lujae

Chrysophyllum lujae, commonly known as the Lujan's starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid climates. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Chrysophyllum lujae is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
