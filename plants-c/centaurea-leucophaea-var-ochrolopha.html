Centaurea leucophaea var. ochrolopha

Centaurea leucophaea var. ochrolopha, commonly known as the yellow centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to September. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
