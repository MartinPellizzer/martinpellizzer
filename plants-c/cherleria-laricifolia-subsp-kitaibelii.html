Cherleria laricifolia subsp. kitaibelii

Cherleria laricifolia subsp.

kitaibelii, commonly known as the Alpine birchleaf spirea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, serrated leaves that resemble those of birch trees.

The plant produces clusters of pink to white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of high-altitude flora.
