Crassocephalum longirameum

Crassocephalum longirameum, commonly known as the long-stemmed false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and hairy foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
