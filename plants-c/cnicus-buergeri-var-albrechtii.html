Cnicus buergeri var. albrechtii

Cnicus buergeri var. albrechtii, commonly known as the Albrecht's sneakerweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lobed and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
