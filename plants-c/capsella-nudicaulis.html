Capsella nudicaulis

Capsella nudicaulis, commonly known as mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.
