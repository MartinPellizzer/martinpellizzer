Cacalia sagittata

Cacalia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, which gives it its common name, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in the eastern and central United States.
