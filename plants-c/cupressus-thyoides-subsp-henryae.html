Cupressus thyoides subsp. henryae

Cupressus thyoides subsp.

henryae, commonly known as the Henry's incense-cedar, is a subspecies of the western red cedar. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties. Native to the western United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of durable timber.
