Croton squamigerus

Croton squamigerus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often brightly colored foliage, which can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a garden plant in warm climates.
