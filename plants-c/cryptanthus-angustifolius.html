Cryptanthus angustifolius

Cryptanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved winterbloom, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which bloom during the cooler months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Cryptanthus angustifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
