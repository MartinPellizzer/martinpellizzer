Chrysopsis angustifolia

Chrysopsis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
