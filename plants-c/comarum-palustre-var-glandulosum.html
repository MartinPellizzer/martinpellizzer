Comarum palustre var. glandulosum

Comarum palustre var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetland environments.

It features bright green, rounded leaves that are often covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and marshes.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and healthy wetland ecosystems.
