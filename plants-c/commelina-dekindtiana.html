Commelina dekindtiana

Commelina dekindtiana, commonly known as the white arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers with a distinctive spathe and spadix. The plant is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The species is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
