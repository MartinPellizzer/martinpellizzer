Croton ramboi

Croton ramboi, commonly known as the Red-veined Croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Croton ramboi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.
