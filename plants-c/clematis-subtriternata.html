Clematis subtriternata

Clematis subtriternata, commonly known as the white clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, white or pale blue flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
