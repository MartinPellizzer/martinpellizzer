Cnicus andersonii

Cnicus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's sneakerweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically alternate and lance-shaped. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
