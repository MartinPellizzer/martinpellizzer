Cassia bicapsularis f. pilosa

Cassia bicapsularis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its fruit is a two-valved capsule, and the species is known for its ornamental value.

The hairy foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
