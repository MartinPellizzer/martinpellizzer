Cuphea impatientifolia

Cuphea impatientifolia, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that resemble tiny firecrackers, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage.
