Cyperus tegetiformis

Cyperus tegetiformis, commonly known as the swamp arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater wetlands.

Cyperus tegetiformis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
