Caiophora anemonoides

Caiophora anemonoides, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan and Korea.

It features showy, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along its stems. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Caiophora anemonoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
