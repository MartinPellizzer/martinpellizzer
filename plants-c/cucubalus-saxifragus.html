Cucubalus saxifragus

Cucubalus saxifragus, commonly known as the rock fig, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, fig-like flavor.

Cucubalus saxifragus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
