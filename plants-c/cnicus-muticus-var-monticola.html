Cnicus muticus var. monticola

Cnicus muticus var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain sneaker, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
