Cissus javellensis var. rotundata

Cissus javellensis var. rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it popular in both indoor and outdoor settings.
