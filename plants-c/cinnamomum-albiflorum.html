Cinnamomum albiflorum

Cinnamomum albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oval, dark brown to black berries that contain a single seed. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
