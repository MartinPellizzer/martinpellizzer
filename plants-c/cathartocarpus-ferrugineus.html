Cathartocarpus ferrugineus

Cathartocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced in dry conditions.

The rusty fringed grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
