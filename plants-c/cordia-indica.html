Cordia indica

Cordia indica, commonly known as the Indian snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces fleshy, yellowish or orange berries that are edible but have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cordia indica is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
