Croton pavonis

Croton pavonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Croton pavonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
