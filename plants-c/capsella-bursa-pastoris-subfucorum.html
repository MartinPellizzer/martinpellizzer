Capsella bursa-pastoris subfucorum

Capsella bursa-pastoris subfucorum, commonly known as the common shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods, which resemble a purse or wallet, hence its common name. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.
