Cactus cruciformis

Cactus cruciformis, commonly known as the cross cactus, is a species native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped spines, which give it its common name. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Cactus cruciformis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
