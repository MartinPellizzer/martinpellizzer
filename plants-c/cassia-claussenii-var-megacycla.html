Cassia claussenii var. megacycla

Cassia claussenii var. megacycla, commonly known as the giant cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
