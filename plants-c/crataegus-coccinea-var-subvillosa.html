Crataegus coccinea var. subvillosa

Crataegus coccinea var. subvillosa, commonly known as the red thornapple or scarlet hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny branches that provide structural support. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for birds.
