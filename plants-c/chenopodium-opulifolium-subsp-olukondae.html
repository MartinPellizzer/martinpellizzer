Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. olukondae

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

olukondae, commonly known as the Olukonda goosefoot, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides or in dry wastelands.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
