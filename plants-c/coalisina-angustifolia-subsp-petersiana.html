Coalisina angustifolia subsp. petersiana

Coalisina angustifolia subsp.

petersiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf coalisina, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
