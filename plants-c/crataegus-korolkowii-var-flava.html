Crataegus korolkowii var. flava

Crataegus korolkowii var. flava, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
