Cassupa juruana

Cassupa juruana, commonly known as the Juruana tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in the ecosystem. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in racemes, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

Cassupa juruana plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and maintaining soil fertility in its native habitat.
