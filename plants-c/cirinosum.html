Cirinosum

Cirinosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Cirinosum is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
