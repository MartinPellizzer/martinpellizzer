Chenopodium angustifolium

Chenopodium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a sprawling growth habit. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green color. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, and it produces tiny, dry fruits known as achenes.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a food source for certain wildlife species.
