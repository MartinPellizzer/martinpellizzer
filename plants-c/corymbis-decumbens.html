Corymbis decumbens

Corymbis decumbens, commonly known as the creeping corymbium, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically spreads along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Corymbis decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
