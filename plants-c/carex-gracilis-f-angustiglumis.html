Carex gracilis f. angustiglumis

Carex gracilis f. angustiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-sheath sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge native to North America.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
