Capsicum annuum var. frutescens

Capsicum annuum var. frutescens, commonly known as the bird pepper or tabasco pepper, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, upright growth habit and branched structure. The leaves are typically narrow and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and tubular, typical of the nightshade family, and it produces small, often red or orange, capsulate fruits.

This variety is valued for its spicy flavor and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
