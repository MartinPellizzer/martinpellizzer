Cyperus pilosus var. muticus

Cyperus pilosus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the stems and nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
