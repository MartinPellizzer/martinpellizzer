Carlina grandiflora

Carlina grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and tall, branching stems that bear yellowish-green flowers. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carlina grandiflora is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
