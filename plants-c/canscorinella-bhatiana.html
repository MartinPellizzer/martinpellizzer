Canscorinella bhatiana

Canscorinella bhatiana, commonly known as the Bhatiana dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and produce seeds with white pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
