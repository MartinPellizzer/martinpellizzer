Cuphea linarioides var. pseudothymoides

Cuphea linarioides var. pseudothymoides, commonly known as false thyme.

It is a flowering plant native to the southern United States, characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate flowers and fragrant leaves.
