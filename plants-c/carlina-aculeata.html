Carlina aculeata

Carlina aculeata, commonly known as the pricklyweed or rough Carlina, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces showy, blue to purple flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina aculeata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
