Cynanchum sinaloense

Cynanchum sinaloense, commonly known as Sinaloan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with distinctive, hooded structures called galeae. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Cynanchum sinaloense is also noted for its milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus.
