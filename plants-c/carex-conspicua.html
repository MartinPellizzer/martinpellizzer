Carex conspicua

Carex conspicua, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex conspicua thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
