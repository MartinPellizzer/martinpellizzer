Clematis dioica subsp. campestris

Clematis dioica subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field clematis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often found growing along roadsides or in disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively hardy, making it a common ornamental in gardens.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
