Carex lyngbyei var. gigas

Carex lyngbyei var. gigas, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The leaves are typically wide and flat, contributing to its distinctive appearance in its natural habitat.
