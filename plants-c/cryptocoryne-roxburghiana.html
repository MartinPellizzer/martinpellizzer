Cryptocoryne roxburghiana

Cryptocoryne roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions.

Cryptocoryne roxburghiana is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
