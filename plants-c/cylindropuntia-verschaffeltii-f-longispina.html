Cylindropuntia verschaffeltii f. longispina

Cylindropuntia verschaffeltii f. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
