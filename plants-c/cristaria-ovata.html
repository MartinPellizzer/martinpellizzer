Cristaria ovata

Cristaria ovata, commonly known as the oval false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint. The plant produces tall, unbranched flowering stems that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Cristaria ovata is often found in woodland areas and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
