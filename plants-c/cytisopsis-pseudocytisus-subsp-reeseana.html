Cytisopsis pseudocytisus subsp. reeseana

Cytisopsis pseudocytisus subsp.

reeseana, commonly known as the Reese's cytisopsis, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
