Convolvulus septenatus

Convolvulus septenatus, commonly known as the seven-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically features seven leaflets on its compound leaves, giving it its common name. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Convolvulus septenatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
