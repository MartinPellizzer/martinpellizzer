Carex chillanensis

Carex chillanensis, commonly known as the Chillan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex chillanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
