Cyperus flavidus

Cyperus flavidus, commonly known as the yellow flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct yellowish hue on the underside.

Cyperus flavidus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
