Citrus × ovata

Citrus × ovata, commonly known as the kumquat.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, oval-shaped leaves and small, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is edible, with a thin, sweet peel and a segmented, tart pulp inside. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is a hybrid between the mandarin orange and the pomelo, exhibiting traits from both parent species.
