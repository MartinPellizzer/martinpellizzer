Cedrus libanensis

Cedrus libanensis, commonly known as the Lebanon cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. Its cones are typically woody and contain small, winged seeds.

The Lebanon cedar is known for its slow growth and longevity, often living for several centuries.
