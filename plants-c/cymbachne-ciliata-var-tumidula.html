Cymbachne ciliata var. tumidula

Cymbachne ciliata var. tumidula, commonly known as the broad-leafed cymbachne, is a grass-like plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often hairy along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

The species is noted for its adaptability to humid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
