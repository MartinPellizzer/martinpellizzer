Cenchrus roseus

Cenchrus roseus, commonly known as the pink sand grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish florets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

Cenchrus roseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
