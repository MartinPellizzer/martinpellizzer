Coreopsis philadelphica

Coreopsis philadelphica, commonly known as the Philadelphia coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
