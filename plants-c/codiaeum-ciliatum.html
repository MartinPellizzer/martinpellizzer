Codiaeum ciliatum

Codiaeum ciliatum, commonly known as the flame leaf or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated foliage, which can range from deep red to yellow and green. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is popular as a ornamental species due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
