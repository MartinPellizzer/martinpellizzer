Callista macfarlanei

Callista macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.

Callista macfarlanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
