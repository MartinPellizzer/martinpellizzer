Convolvulus pentaphyllus var. serpens

Convolvulus pentaphyllus var. serpens, commonly known as the serpentine bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, five-lobed leaves that give it a serrated appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its rapid growth and spreading nature make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
