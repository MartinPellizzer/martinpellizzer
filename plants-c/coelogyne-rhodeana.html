Coelogyne rhodeana

Coelogyne rhodeana, commonly known as the Red-tipped Cymbidium, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, often in winter or early spring.

Coelogyne rhodeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
