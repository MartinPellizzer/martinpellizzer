Cynanchum amplexicaule f. castaneum

Cynanchum amplexicaule f. castaneum, commonly known as the red-veined milkweed, is a variant of the milkweed genus characterized by its striking foliage.

This plant is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a distinctive reddish vein pattern, which sets it apart from other milkweed species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
