Corybas moluccanus

Corybas moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that resembles a small cup or bowl, which is a distinctive feature of the Corybas genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually dull-colored, ranging from greenish-brown to reddish, and are pollinated by flies and beetles.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
