Cereus grandiflorus var. mexicanus

Cereus grandiflorus var. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
