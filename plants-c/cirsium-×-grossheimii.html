Cirsium × grossheimii

Cirsium × grossheimii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a sterile hybrid between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium undulatum, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
