Crataegus monogyna var. bicolor

Crataegus monogyna var. bicolor, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white and pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves, which are typical features of the Crataegus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
