Costus ridleyi

Costus ridleyi, commonly known as the yellow costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth texture.

Costus ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
