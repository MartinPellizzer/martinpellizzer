Clusia congestiflora

Clusia congestiflora, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Clusia congestiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying properties.
