Cyperus warmingii

Cyperus warmingii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus warmingii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
