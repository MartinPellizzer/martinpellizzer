Centaurea antennata

Centaurea antennata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with distinctive horn-like bracts at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea antennata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
