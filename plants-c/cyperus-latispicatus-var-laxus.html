Cyperus latispicatus var. laxus

Cyperus latispicatus var. laxus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This species is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
