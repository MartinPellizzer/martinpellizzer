Cyclanthus

Cyclanthus, commonly known as the "false arum," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyclanthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often showy inflorescences that resemble a crown or a flower cluster. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, warm climates. Cyclanthus species usually have heart-shaped or arrow-shaped leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

The genus includes around 150 species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
