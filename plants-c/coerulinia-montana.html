Coerulinia montana

Coerulinia montana, commonly known as the mountain coerula, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its botanical name reflects its habitat and the vivid color of its blooms.

Coerulinia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
