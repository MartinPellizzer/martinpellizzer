Cavendishia barnebyi

Cavendishia barnebyi, commonly known as the Barneby's Cavendish, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it a typical example of a cactus. Its botanical significance lies in its role in the study of cactus evolution and taxonomy.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
