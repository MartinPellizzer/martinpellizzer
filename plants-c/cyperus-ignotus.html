Cyperus ignotus

Cyperus ignotus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually upright, with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy.

Cyperus ignotus produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
