Cotula calcarea

Cotula calcarea, commonly known as limestone dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color, while its stems are slender and branching.

Cotula calcarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
