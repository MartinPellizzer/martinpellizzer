Citrosma paniculata

Citrosma paniculata, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry with a distinctive star-shaped appearance when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
