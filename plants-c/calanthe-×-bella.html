Calanthe × bella

Calanthe × bella, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Calanthe triplicata and Calanthe mitella, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The species features compact, evergreen foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a horticultural hybrid that combines traits from its parent species.
