Chrysophyllum multipetalum

Chrysophyllum multipetalum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
