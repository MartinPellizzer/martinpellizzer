Cynoglossum caespitosum

Cynoglossum caespitosum, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynoglossum caespitosum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its ornamental value in gardens.
