Cereus atroviridis

Cereus atroviridis, commonly known as the black widow cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces large, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, opening only at night. Its spines are short and sparse, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming behavior.
