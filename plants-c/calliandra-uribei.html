Calliandra uribei

Calliandra uribei, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bract-like flowers. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Calliandra uribei thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
