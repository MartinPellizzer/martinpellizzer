Cannabis sativa var. monoica

Cannabis sativa var. monoica, commonly known as hemp, is a variant of the cannabis plant characterized by its low tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) content and high cannabidiol (CBD) levels.

It is primarily cultivated for industrial purposes such as fiber production, seed oil, and nutritional supplements. The plant typically grows tall and slender, with broad leaves and can reach heights of up to 5 meters in optimal conditions. Its flowering period is relatively short, usually lasting around 8 to 10 weeks, depending on environmental factors.

The botanical features of Cannabis sativa var. monoica make it distinct from its psychoactive counterparts, emphasizing its utility in sustainable agriculture and medicine.
