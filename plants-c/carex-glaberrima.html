Carex glaberrima

Carex glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
