Citrus medica var. nana

Citrus medica var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf citron, is a compact variety of the citron tree.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically less than 5 cm in diameter. The plant has thick, waxy leaves and produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruit with a high concentration of essential oils. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its use in traditional medicine.

The dwarf citron is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines.
