Centranthus longiflorus subsp. atlanticus

Centranthus longiflorus subsp.

atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic redhead, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and erosion control.
