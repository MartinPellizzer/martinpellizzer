Convolvulus pseudomina

Convolvulus pseudomina, commonly known as the false bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
