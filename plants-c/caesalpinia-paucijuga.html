Caesalpinia paucijuga

Caesalpinia paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, making it visually striking. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Caesalpinia paucijuga is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
