Cassia virginis

Cassia virginis, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Cassia virginis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
