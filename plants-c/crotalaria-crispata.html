Crotalaria crispata

Crotalaria crispata, commonly known as the curled crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Crotalaria crispata is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in soil enrichment.
