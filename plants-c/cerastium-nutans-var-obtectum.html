Cerastium nutans var. obtectum

Cerastium nutans var. obtectum, commonly known as the bentwillow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
