Clematis inversa

Clematis inversa, commonly known as the upside-down clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

Clematis inversa thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
