Cardamine stylosa

Cardamine stylosa, commonly known as the clustered water-cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

Cardamine stylosa is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
