Crucifera irio

Crucifera irio, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, rounded fruits called silique, which contain several seeds. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Crucifera irio is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant flower colors.
