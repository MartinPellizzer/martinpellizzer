Chamaesyce radioloides

Chamaesyce radioloides, commonly known as the radioloid sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.
