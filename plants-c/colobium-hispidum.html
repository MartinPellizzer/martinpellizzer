Colobium hispidum

Colobium hispidum, commonly known as the roughleaf colobium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Colobium hispidum is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
