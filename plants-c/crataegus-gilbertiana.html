Crataegus gilbertiana

Crataegus gilbertiana, commonly known as Gilbert's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Gilbert's hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
