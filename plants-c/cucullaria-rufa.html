Cucullaria rufa

Cucullaria rufa, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Cucullaria rufa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
