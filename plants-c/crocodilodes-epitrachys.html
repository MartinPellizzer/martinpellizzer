Crocodilodes epitrachys

Crocodilodes epitrachys, commonly known as the hairy crocodile plant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
