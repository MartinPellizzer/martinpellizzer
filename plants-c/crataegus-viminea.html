Crataegus viminea

Crataegus viminea, commonly known as the weeping hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus viminea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
