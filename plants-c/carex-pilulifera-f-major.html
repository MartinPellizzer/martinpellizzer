Carex pilulifera f. major

Carex pilulifera f. major, commonly known as the giant button sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
