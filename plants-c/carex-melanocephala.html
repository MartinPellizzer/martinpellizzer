Carex melanocephala

Carex melanocephala, commonly known as black-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex melanocephala plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
