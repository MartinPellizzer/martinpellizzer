Costus congestus

Costus congestus, commonly known as the clustered costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically in shades of yellow or orange, and are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Costus congestus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
