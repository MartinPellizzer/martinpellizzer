Cytisus caramanicus

Cytisus caramanicus, commonly known as the Caramanica broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
