Chrysolarix amabilis

Chrysolarix amabilis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow and white flowers that resemble stars, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often grows in association with other epiphytic plants.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
