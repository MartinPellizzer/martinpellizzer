Cryptocarya kajewskii

Cryptocarya kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewski's cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, elliptic-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, glossy berries. Cryptocarya kajewskii is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
