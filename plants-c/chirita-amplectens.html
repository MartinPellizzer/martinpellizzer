Chirita amplectens

Chirita amplectens, commonly known as the clinging wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
