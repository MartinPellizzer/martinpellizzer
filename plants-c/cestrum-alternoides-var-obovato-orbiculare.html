Cestrum alternoides var. obovato-orbiculare

Cestrum alternoides var. obovato-orbiculare, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, ovate to orbicular leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
