Croton ramillatus var. magniglandulifer

Croton ramillatus var. magniglandulifer, commonly known as the large glandular croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
