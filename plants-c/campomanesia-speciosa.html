Campomanesia speciosa

Campomanesia speciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or cupuacu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it both nutritious and economically valuable.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts within its native habitat.
