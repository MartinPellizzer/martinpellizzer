Carex kryloviana

Carex kryloviana, commonly known as the Krylovian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of spikes of small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex kryloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
