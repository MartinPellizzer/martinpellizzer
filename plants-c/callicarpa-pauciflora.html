Callicarpa pauciflora

Callicarpa pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, violet-purple berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Callicarpa pauciflora typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
