Chloris cucullata

Chloris cucullata, commonly known as the hooded grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Chloris cucullata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
