Crataegus monogyna fastigiata

Crataegus monogyna fastigiata, commonly known as the upright hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in garden settings.
