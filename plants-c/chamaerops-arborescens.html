Chamaerops arborescens

Chamaerops arborescens, commonly known as the tree chamaerops, is a species of palm native to the Canary Islands.

It is a robust, evergreen plant that typically grows as a solitary tree with a straight, unbranched trunk. The plant features large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the trunk. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to dates.

Chamaerops arborescens is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
