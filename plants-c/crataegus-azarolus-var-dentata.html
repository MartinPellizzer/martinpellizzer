Crataegus azarolus var. dentata

Crataegus azarolus var. dentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
