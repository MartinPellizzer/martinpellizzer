Cleome procumbens

Cleome procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Cleome procumbens is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
