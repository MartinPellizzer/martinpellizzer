Chamaefilix ramosa

Chamaefilix ramosa, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy and versatile plant for various garden settings.
