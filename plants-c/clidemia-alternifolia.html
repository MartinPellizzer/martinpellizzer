Clidemia alternifolia

Clidemia alternifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silver-gray hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemia alternifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
