Chenopodium berlandieri var. glaucoviride

Chenopodium berlandieri var. glaucoviride, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the western regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and small greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in dry, open areas. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
