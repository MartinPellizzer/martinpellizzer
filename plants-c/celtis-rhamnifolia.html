Celtis rhamnifolia

Celtis rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Celtis rhamnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
