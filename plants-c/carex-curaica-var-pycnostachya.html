Carex curaica var. pycnostachya

Carex curaica var. pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
