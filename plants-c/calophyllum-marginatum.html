Calophyllum marginatum

Calophyllum marginatum, commonly known as the Margaritifera or the Margarita tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Calophyllaceae.

It is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces fruit that resembles a coconut in appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
