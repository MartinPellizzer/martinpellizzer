Cirrhopetalum punctatum

Cirrhopetalum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
