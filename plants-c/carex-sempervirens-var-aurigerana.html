Carex sempervirens var. aurigerana

Carex sempervirens var. aurigerana, commonly known as golden sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by distinctive, cylindrical seed heads.
