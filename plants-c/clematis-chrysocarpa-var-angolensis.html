Clematis chrysocarpa var. angolensis

Clematis chrysocarpa var. angolensis, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a flowering vine native to southern Africa.

It produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fruit is a small, yellow, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
