Campanula turczaninovii

Campanula turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula turczaninovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
