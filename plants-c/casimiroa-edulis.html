Casimiroa edulis

Casimiroa edulis, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive for its star-shaped cross-section when sliced, and it is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible, tangy fruit that is popular in many cuisines around the world.
