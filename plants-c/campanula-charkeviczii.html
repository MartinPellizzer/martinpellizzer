Campanula charkeviczii

Campanula charkeviczii, commonly known as the Charkevicz bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula charkeviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
