Cyperus pentadactylus

Cyperus pentadactylus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Cyperus pentadactylus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
