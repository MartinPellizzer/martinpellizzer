Citrullus caffer

Citrullus caffer, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, round, green fruit with a red or yellow flesh. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vigorous vine growth habit. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh, though it can also be used in traditional remedies.
