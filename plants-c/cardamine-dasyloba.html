Cardamine dasyloba

Cardamine dasyloba, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The small white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by pods that contain several seeds.
