Clematis flammula proles fragrans

Clematis flammula proles fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
