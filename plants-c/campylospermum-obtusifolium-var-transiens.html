Campylospermum obtusifolium var. transiens

Campylospermum obtusifolium var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Broadleaf Campylospermum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the cooler months.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
