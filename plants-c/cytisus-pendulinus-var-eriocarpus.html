Cytisus pendulinus var. eriocarpus

Cytisus pendulinus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give the plant its common name. Its fruit is a slender, hairy pod that splits open to release seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or coastal landscapes.
