Curtia montevidensis

Curtia montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth habit and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in wetland and marshy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various species.

Its fibrous stalks are sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
