Carex hormathodes

Carex hormathodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
