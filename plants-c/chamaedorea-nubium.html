Chamaedorea nubium

Chamaedorea nubium, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features a slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
