Carex strictior f. angustata

Carex strictior f. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex strictior.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a robust rhizomatous root system and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
