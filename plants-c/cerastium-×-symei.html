Cerastium × symei

Cerastium × symei, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its creeping habit and ability to form dense mats, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Cerastium × symei is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
