Clusia sellowiana

Clusia sellowiana, commonly known as the yellow curlleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright yellow-orange berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Clusia sellowiana is also valued for its air-purifying properties and resilience to environmental stress.
