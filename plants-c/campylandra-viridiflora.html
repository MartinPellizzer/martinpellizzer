Campylandra viridiflora

Campylandra viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered campylandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, waxy texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Campylandra viridiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
