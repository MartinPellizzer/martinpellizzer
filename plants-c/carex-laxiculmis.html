Carex laxiculmis

Carex laxiculmis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex laxiculmis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
