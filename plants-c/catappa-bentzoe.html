Catappa bentzoe

Catappa bentzoe, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
