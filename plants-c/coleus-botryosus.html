Coleus botryosus

Coleus botryosus, commonly known as the woolly coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a white or yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with striking red or purple veins. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coleus botryosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
