Cirsium pseudocanum

Cirsium pseudocanum, commonly known as the Canadian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to North America, this plant is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies. Its roots can persist in the soil for many years, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.

The plant has been studied for its ecological impact and potential medicinal properties.
