Clerodendrum boivinii

Clerodendrum boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with a tubular shape and a pleasant fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
