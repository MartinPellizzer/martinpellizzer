Carthamus glaucus

Carthamus glaucus, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and is part of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Carthamus glaucus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique color and hardy nature.
