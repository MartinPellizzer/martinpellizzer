Coleus × tryonii

Coleus × tryonii, commonly known as the hybrid coleus, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers are small and tubular, usually borne in clusters at the base of the plant.
