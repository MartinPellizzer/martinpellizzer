Carex capituliformis

Carex capituliformis, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads known as capitula, which are typically borne on short, leafy stalks. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream edges.

Carex capituliformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
