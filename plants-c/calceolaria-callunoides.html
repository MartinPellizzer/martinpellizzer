Calceolaria callunoides

Calceolaria callunoides, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of South America.

It features striking blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells, typical of the Calceolaria genus. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
