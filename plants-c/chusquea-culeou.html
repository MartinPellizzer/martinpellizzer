Chusquea culeou

Chusquea culeou, commonly known as the Culeo bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Chusquea genus in the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, clumping growth habit, forming thickets in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas.

The Culeo bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts by indigenous communities.
