Corylus rostrata var. sieboldiana

Corylus rostrata var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features distinctive, elongated nuts with a prominent beak-like structure, which is a key botanical characteristic. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are catkins, typical of the hazelnut genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible nuts.

The leaves turn vibrant yellow in autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
