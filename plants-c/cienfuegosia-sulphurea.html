Cienfuegosia sulphurea

Cienfuegosia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the island of Cienfuegos in Cuba.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cienfuegosia sulphurea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
