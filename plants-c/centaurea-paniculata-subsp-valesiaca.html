Centaurea paniculata subsp. valesiaca

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
