Campanula rapunculoides var. subsimplex

Campanula rapunculoides var. subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. This variety is typically found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
