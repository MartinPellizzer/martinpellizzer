Cyperus digitatus var. bruntii

Cyperus digitatus var. bruntii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
