Clematis cordifolia

Clematis cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically 3 to 5 inches long, with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis cordifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
