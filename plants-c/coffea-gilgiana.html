Coffea gilgiana

Coffea gilgiana, commonly known as the Gilgian coffee plant, is a species native to Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing one to three seeds. These seeds, when processed, can be used to make a unique type of coffee.

It is less commonly cultivated than other coffee species but is of interest to botanists and coffee enthusiasts alike.
