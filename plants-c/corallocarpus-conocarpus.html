Corallocarpus conocarpus

Corallocarpus conocarpus, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or wild apple, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that resemble apples. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its decorative foliage.
