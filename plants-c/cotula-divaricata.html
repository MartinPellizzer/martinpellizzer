Cotula divaricata

Cotula divaricata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has a compact, spreading habit with hairy leaves and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Cotula divaricata is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
