Capsicum lanceifolium

Capsicum lanceifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chili or wild pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by elongated, narrow fruits that can range in color from green to red.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
