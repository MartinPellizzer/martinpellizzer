Cyathea acrostichoides

Cyathea acrostichoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a thick, woody trunk that grows slowly over many years. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea acrostichoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
