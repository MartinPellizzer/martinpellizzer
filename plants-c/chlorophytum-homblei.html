Chlorophytum homblei

Chlorophytum homblei, commonly known as the Hombli spider plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum homblei is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
