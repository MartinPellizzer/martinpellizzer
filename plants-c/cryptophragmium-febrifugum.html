Cryptophragmium febrifugum

Cryptophragmium febrifugum, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense, compact inflorescence. It is characterized by its unique, aromatic scent and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cryptophragmium febrifugum is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional remedies for fever and inflammation.
