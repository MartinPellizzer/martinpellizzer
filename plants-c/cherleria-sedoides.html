Cherleria sedoides

Cherleria sedoides, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and limited natural propagation, Cherleria sedoides is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
