Copiapoa fusca

Copiapoa fusca, commonly known as the dark copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract various pollinators. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to the arid environment.

Copiapoa fusca is also valued for its slow growth and unique appearance, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
