Conyza tridentata

Conyza tridentata, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers.

Conyza tridentata is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
