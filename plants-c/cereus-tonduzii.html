Cereus tonduzii

Cereus tonduzii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Cereus tonduzii is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
