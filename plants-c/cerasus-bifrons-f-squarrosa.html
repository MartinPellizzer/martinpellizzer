Cerasus bifrons f. squarrosa

Cerasus bifrons f. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, dark red berries.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
