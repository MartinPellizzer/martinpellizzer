Corymbia leptoloma

Corymbia leptoloma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
