Canavalia vitiensis

Canavalia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Canavalia vitiensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.
