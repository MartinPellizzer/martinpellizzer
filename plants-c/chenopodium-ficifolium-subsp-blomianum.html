Chenopodium ficifolium subsp. blomianum

Chenopodium ficifolium subsp.

blomianum, commonly known as the blomianum goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of South America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are usually sprawling or ascending, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
