Cratylia intermedia

Cratylia intermedia, commonly known as the purple bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its protein-rich seeds.

The flowers are typically purple or pink, adding ornamental value to the plant.
