Cassia decrescens

Cassia decrescens, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods containing seeds. Cassia decrescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
