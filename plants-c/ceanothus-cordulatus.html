Ceanothus cordulatus

Ceanothus cordulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ceanothus, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland regions, often in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in early spring. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Ceanothus cordulatus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecosystem biodiversity.
