Chrysoprenanthes pendula

Chrysoprenanthes pendula, commonly known as the hanging chrysoprenanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
