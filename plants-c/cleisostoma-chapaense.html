Cleisostoma chapaense

Cleisostoma chapaense, commonly known as the Chapa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the rainy season.

Cleisostoma chapaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
