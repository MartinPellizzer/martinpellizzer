Cucumis leiospermus

Cucumis leiospermus, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces bright yellow flowers and elongated, ridged fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is edible and often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, slightly sweet flavor.
