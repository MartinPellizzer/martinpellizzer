Crassula expansa subsp. filicaulis

Crassula expansa subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, densely packed rosettes of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its slow growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
