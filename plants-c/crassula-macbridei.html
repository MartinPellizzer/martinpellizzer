Crassula macbridei

Crassula macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and striking foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
