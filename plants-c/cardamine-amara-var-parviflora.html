Cardamine amara var. parviflora

Cardamine amara var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bittercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are borne on slender, upright stems and are clustered in racemes.

The species is characterized by its peppery, edible leaves and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
