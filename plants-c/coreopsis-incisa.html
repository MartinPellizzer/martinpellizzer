Coreopsis incisa

Coreopsis incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms from late spring to early fall.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental value and ecological benefits.
