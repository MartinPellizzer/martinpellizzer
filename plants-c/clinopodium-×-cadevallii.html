Clinopodium × cadevallii

Clinopodium × cadevallii, commonly known as the hybrid mint or garden mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Clinopodium vulgare and Clinopodium pol bearum, characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies due to its menthol-like scent and potential medicinal properties.

Clinopodium × cadevallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
