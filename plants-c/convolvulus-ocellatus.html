Convolvulus ocellatus

Convolvulus ocellatus, commonly known as the spotted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive spotted pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
