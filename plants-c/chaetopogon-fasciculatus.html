Chaetopogon fasciculatus

Chaetopogon fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a hairy texture.

It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
