Chaerophyllum angelicifolium var. temuloides

Chaerophyllum angelicifolium var. temuloides, commonly known as the angelica-leaved chaerophyllum, is a member of theApiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It typically features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which attract various pollinators. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
