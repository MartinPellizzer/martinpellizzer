Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. boecheri

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. boecheri, commonly known as the Boechera chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves with a grayish-green hue. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
