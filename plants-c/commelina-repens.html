Commelina repens

Commelina repens, commonly known as the running spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
