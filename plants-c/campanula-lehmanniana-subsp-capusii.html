Campanula lehmanniana subsp. capusii

Campanula lehmanniana subsp.

capusii, commonly known as the Lehmann's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
