Centaurea affinis subsp. tartarea

Centaurea affinis subsp.

tartarea, commonly known as the Carpathian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in composite heads. Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower displays.
