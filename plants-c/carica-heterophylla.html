Carica heterophylla

Carica heterophylla, commonly known as the variable papaya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caricaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from lobed to entire. The plant produces large, showy flowers and is known for its edible fruit, which is similar to the common papaya but has a more distinct flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to grow in a variety of environmental conditions.
