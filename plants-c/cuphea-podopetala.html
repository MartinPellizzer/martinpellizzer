Cuphea podopetala

Cuphea podopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Cuphea podopetala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
