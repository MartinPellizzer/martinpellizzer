Crassula miriamiae

Crassula miriamiae, commonly known as the Miriam's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth form. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or gray tint. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring or early summer.

Crassula miriamiae is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
