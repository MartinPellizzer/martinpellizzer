Carex vesiculosa var. paniculata

Carex vesiculosa var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
