Campanula rotundifolia var. solstitialis

Campanula rotundifolia var. solstitialis, commonly known as the Solstitial Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically around the summer solstice, hence its common name. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
