Catasetum negrense

Catasetum negrense, commonly known as the Negrense Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can range from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces a unique structure called a "labellum," which is modified to aid in pollination by specific wasps. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
