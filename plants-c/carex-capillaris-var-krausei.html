Carex capillaris var. krausei

Carex capillaris var. krausei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
