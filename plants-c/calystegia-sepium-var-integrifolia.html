Calystegia sepium var. integrifolia

Calystegia sepium var. integrifolia, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are sprawling and can climb or trail along the ground.

It is known for its strong, persistent growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
