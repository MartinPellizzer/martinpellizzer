Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis

Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its clustered growth pattern and spiny leaves. The plant features a short, thick trunk with multiple stems emerging from the base, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into clustered, edible fruits.
