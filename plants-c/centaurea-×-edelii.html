Centaurea × edelii

Centaurea × edelii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (red cornflower), resulting in a plant with vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from blue to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea × edelii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping and cut flower arrangements.
