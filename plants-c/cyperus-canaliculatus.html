Cyperus canaliculatus

Cyperus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus canaliculatus is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
