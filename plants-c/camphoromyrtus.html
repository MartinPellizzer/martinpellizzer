Camphoromyrtus

Camphoromyrtus, commonly known as the camphor laurel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and comprises several species, including Camphoromyrtus laevigatus and Camphoromyrtus tetragonus. These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. They are often found in coastal and rainforest habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

The genus is notable for its ecological importance and use in traditional medicine.
