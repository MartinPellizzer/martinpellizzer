Ceropegia baldratii

Ceropegia baldratii, commonly known as the African starfish or starry ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive tubular flowers with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for succulent gardens.

Ceropegia baldratii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers.
