Commiphora omundomba

Commiphora omundomba, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes other resin-producing plants like frankincense and myrrh. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces a fragrant, yellowish resin that has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in dry, tropical, and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
