Cavendishia acuminata

Cavendishia acuminata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral bracts. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often blooming in a dense inflorescence that resembles a golden cluster.

Cavendishia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses for its unique appearance.
