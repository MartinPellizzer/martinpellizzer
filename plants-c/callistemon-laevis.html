Callistemon laevis

Callistemon laevis, commonly known as the smooth bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink cylindrical flower spikes, which are produced in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has smooth, grey-green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
