Conoclinium rigidum

Conoclinium rigidum, commonly known as rigid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
