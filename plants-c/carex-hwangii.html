Carex hwangii

Carex hwangii, commonly known as the Hwang's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

Carex hwangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
