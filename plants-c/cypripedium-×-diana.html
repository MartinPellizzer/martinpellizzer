Cypripedium × diana

Cypripedium × diana, commonly known as the Lady's Slipper orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique adaptation for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with rich, humus-filled soil. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Cypripedium × diana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
