Caiophora clavata

Caiophora clavata, commonly known as the club-shaped caiophora, is a unique species belonging to the family Caiophoraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive club-like or clavate shape, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are simple and inconspicuous.

The botanical features of Caiophora clavata make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
