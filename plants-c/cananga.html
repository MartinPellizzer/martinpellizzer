Cananga

Cananga, commonly known as the torch tree or frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant produces large, palm-like leaves and has a woody, evergreen structure. Cananga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are used to make perfume and are popular in religious ceremonies.
