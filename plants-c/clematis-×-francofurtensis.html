Clematis × francofurtensis

Clematis × francofurtensis, commonly known as the Francofurtensis Clematis, is a hybrid vine native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, typically blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively hardy nature.
