Carex leucochlora var. candolleana

Carex leucochlora var. candolleana, commonly known as the white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white or pale-colored hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
