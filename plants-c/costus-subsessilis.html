Costus subsessilis

Costus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved costus, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and well-drained soils. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinctive aromatic scent.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
