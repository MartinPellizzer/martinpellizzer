Collaea grewiifolia

Collaea grewiifolia, commonly known as the silver mat flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silvery-white, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Collaea grewiifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
