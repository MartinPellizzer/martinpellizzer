Corybas aduncus

Corybas aduncus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helmet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the southeastern regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Corybas aduncus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for various pollinators.
