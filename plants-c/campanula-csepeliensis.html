Campanula csepeliensis

Campanula csepeliensis, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, herbaceous stems.

Campanula csepeliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
