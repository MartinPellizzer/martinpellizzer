Cuscuta ceratophora

Cuscuta ceratophora, commonly known as the horned dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on various species of grasses and shrubs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and natural vegetation.
