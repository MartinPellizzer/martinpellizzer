Capparis pachyphylla

Capparis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by edible, green buds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater.

Capparis pachyphylla is well adapted to harsh, semi-desert conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
