Centaurea × mihaliki

Centaurea × mihaliki, commonly known as the Mihaliki bluemink, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea × mihaliki is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
