Cerasus avium var. variegata

Cerasus avium var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry, is a cultivar of the bird cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
