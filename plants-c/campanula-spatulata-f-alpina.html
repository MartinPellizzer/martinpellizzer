Campanula spatulata f. alpina

Campanula spatulata f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of broad, spatulate leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to high-altitude gardens.
