Cistocarpium utriculatum

Cistocarpium utriculatum, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, capsule-like fruits that resemble bladders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitats.
