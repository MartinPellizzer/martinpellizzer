Chloranthus hupehensis

Chloranthus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chloranthus hupehensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
