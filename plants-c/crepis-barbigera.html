Crepis barbigera

Crepis barbigera, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers that resemble a cluster of small blooms. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
