Cleome xanthopetala

Cleome xanthopetala, commonly known as yellow cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features branching stems covered with fine hairs and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome xanthopetala is also valued for its drought tolerance and ease of cultivation.
