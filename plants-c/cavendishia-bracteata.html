Cavendishia bracteata

Cavendishia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and often form a dense network in the soil.

Cavendishia bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
