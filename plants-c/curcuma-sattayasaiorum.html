Curcuma sattayasaiorum

Curcuma sattayasaiorum, commonly known as the golden ginger or Indian saffron, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves and striking, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to turmeric.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing close relatives with ginger and cardamom.
