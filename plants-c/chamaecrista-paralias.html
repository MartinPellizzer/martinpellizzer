Chamaecrista paralias

Chamaecrista paralias, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in distinctive, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Chamaecrista paralias is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in coastal environments.
