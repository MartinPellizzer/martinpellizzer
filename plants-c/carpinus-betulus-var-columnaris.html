Carpinus betulus var. columnaris

Carpinus betulus var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar hornbeam, is a cultivar of the hornbeam tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, providing seasonal interest. The plant has smooth, grey bark that becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value. It is a hardy, deciduous tree that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact form, it is often used in urban landscaping for its space-saving qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
