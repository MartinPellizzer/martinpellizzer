Commelina graminifolia

Commelina graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Commelina graminifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
