Coffea montana

Coffea montana, commonly known as the Ethiopian mountain coffee, is a species native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. Its botanical characteristics include dark green, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit.

Coffea montana is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often grown in specialized coffee regions.
