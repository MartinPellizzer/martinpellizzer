Cracca secunda

Cracca secunda, commonly known as the common cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Cracca secunda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
