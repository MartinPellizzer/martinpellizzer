Carex obtusisquama

Carex obtusisquama, commonly known as the broad-scaled sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, flat leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems. Carex obtusisquama prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
