Clematis aureolata

Clematis aureolata, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of twining stems and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a finely toothed edge.

Clematis aureolata thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
