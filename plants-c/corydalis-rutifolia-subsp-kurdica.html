Corydalis rutifolia subsp. kurdica

Corydalis rutifolia subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish cowslip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-yellow flowers with two prominent upper petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface. Corydalis rutifolia subsp.

kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
