Croton ankarensis

Croton ankarensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its foliage.
