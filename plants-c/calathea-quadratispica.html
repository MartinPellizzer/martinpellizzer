Calathea quadratispica

Calathea quadratispica, commonly known as the Quadrifida Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant's foliage is often adorned with a waxy coating that gives it a unique, almost metallic sheen. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea quadratispica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
