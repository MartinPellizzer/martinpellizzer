Calamintha clinopodium var. discolor

Calamintha clinopodium var. discolor, commonly known as purple mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
