Capnoides sempervirens

Capnoides sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen shrub or mountain holly, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, humid climates.

Capnoides sempervirens is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
