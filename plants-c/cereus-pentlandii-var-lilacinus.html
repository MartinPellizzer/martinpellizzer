Cereus pentlandii var. lilacinus

Cereus pentlandii var. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or lilac in color, opening at night. These flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are known for their intense scent.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick stems that store water and reduce transpiration.
