Cassine latifolia

Cassine latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cassine latifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
