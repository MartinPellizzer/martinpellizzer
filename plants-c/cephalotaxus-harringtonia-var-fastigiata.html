Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. fastigiata

Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It features a columnar or narrowly pyramidal shape, making it an ideal choice for small spaces or as a hedge. The plant has dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its cones are small and reddish-brown, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
