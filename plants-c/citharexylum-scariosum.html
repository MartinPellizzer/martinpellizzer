Citharexylum scariosum

Citharexylum scariosum, commonly known as the paperbark hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish or brownish inner layer. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Citharexylum scariosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
