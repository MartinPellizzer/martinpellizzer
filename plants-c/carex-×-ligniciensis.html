Carex × ligniciensis

Carex × ligniciensis, commonly known as the swamp sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered flower heads. This species typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex × ligniciensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
