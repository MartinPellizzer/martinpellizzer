Combretum quadrangulare

Combretum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
