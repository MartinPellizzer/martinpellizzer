Chenopodium nesodendron

Chenopodium nesodendron, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium nesodendron is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
