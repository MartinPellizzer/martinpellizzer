Colletia ulicina

Colletia ulicina, commonly known as the desert candelabra, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It features a branched, shrub-like structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Colletia ulicina is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
