Cistus salicifolius var. fugaceus

Cistus salicifolius var. fugaceus, commonly known as the quick-fading rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
