Cyathea melanotricha

Cyathea melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyathea melanotricha is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
