Carex subspathacea

Carex subspathacea, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist or wet environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, humid climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
