Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. dentatum

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

dentatum, commonly known as the toothed mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy stems.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated margins, giving it the common name "toothed.". Its flowers are pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
