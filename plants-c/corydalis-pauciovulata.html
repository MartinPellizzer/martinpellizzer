Corydalis pauciovulata

Corydalis pauciovulata, commonly known as the few-fruited larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to pink, and it has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Corydalis pauciovulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
