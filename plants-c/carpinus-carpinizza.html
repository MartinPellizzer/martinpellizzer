Carpinus carpinizza

Carpinus carpinizza, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, almost smooth bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, spherical berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Carpinus carpinizza is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in supporting local wildlife.
