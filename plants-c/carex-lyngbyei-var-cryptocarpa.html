Carex lyngbyei var. cryptocarpa

Carex lyngbyei var. cryptocarpa, commonly known as the cryptic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
