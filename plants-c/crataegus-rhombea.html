Crataegus rhombea

Crataegus rhombea, commonly known as the diamondleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus rhombea typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
