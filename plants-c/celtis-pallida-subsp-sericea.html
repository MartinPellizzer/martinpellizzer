Celtis pallida subsp. sericea

Celtis pallida subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered with a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy and adaptable species. Celtis pallida subsp.

sericea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
