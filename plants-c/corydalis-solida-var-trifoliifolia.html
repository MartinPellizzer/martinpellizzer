Corydalis solida var. trifoliifolia

Corydalis solida var. trifoliifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved Corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers with two upper petals and two lower ones. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and foliage.
