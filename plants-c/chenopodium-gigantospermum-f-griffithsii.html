Chenopodium gigantospermum f. griffithsii

Chenopodium gigantospermum f. griffithsii, commonly known as the giant seedsame, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often edible seeds that are a notable feature of the plant. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh climates.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential agricultural value.
