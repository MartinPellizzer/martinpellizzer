Crataegus mollis var. ellwangeriana

Crataegus mollis var. ellwangeriana, commonly known as the Ellwangeriana hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a pungent aroma, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
