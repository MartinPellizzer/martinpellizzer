Ceramia auricularis

Ceramia auricularis, commonly known as the auricle fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Ceramia auricularis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
