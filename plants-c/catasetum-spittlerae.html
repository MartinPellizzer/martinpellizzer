Catasetum spittlerae

Catasetum spittlerae, commonly known as the Spittler's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can range from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence is a dense spike of flowers, and it produces a unique, elongated pollination structure called a "staminode.".

Catasetum spittlerae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
