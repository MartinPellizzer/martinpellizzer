Cyperus niigatensis

Cyperus niigatensis, commonly known as the Niigata sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Cyperus niigatensis is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
