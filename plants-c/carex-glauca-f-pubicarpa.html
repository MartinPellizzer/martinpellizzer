Carex glauca f. pubicarpa

Carex glauca f. pubicarpa, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex glauca.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its blue-green foliage and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
