Caesalpinia robusta

Caesalpinia robusta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and bright orange to red floral spikes that appear in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers. Caesalpinia robusta thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to local wildlife.
