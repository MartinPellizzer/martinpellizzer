Castanea crenata f. gigantea

Castanea crenata f. gigantea, commonly known as the Japanese giant chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and the production of sizable, edible nuts. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 20 meters. Its botanical features include smooth, grey bark and clusters of yellowish-green catkins.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
