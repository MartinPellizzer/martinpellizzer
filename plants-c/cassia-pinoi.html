Cassia pinoi

Cassia pinoi, commonly known as the Pino's cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia pinoi is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
