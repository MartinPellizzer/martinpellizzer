Cymbopetalum lanugipetalum

Cymbopetalum lanugipetalum, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

Cymbopetalum lanugipetalum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
