Camelina anomala

Camelina anomala, commonly known as yellow cameline, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Camelina anomala is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
