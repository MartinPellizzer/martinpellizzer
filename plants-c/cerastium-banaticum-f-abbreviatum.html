Cerastium banaticum f. abbreviatum

Cerastium banaticum f. abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its abbreviated stem structure is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
