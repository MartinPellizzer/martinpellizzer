Conyza sicula

Conyza sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by narrow, bract-like leaves.

Conyza sicula is an annual herb that is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
