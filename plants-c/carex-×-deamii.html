Carex × deamii

Carex × deamii, commonly known as the Deam's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Carex × deamii is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
