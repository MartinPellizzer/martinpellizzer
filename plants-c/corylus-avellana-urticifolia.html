Corylus avellana urticifolia

Corylus avellana urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often stinging leaves that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
