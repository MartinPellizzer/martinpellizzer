Cyathea alsophila

Cyathea alsophila, commonly known as the silver tree, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick, fibrous layer of old leaf bases. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea alsophila thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
