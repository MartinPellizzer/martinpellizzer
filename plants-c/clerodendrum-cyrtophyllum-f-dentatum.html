Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum f. dentatum

Clerodendrum cyrtophyllum f. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed clerodendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated or toothed margin, giving it its specific epithet "dentatum.". Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
