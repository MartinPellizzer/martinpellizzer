Cereus hildmannianus subsp. xanthocarpus

Cereus hildmannianus subsp.

xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited torch cactus, is a columnar cactus native to southern Brazil. It features a tall, spiny stem with bright green, ribbed surfaces and produces yellow flowers that bloom at night.

The plant's fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
