Chionanthus peglerae

Chionanthus peglerae, commonly known as the Pegler's fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its foliage. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chionanthus peglerae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
