Centaurea cithaeronea

Centaurea cithaeronea, commonly known as the Greek star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
