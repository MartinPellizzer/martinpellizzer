Celastrus obovatus

Celastrus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
