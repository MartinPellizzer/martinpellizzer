Carex pseudocyperus var. americana

Carex pseudocyperus var. americana, commonly known as the American flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
