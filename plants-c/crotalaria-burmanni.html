Crotalaria burmanni

Crotalaria burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain elongated, kidney-shaped seeds. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Crotalaria burmanni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
