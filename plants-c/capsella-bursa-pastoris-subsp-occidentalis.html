Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp. occidentalis

Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America. It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained environments.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
