Carex brevicuspis

Carex brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-bristled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the seeds.

Carex brevicuspis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
