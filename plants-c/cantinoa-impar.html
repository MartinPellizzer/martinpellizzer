Cantinoa impar

Cantinoa impar, commonly known as the odd-leaved cantinoa, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, asymmetrical leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
