Crepis marschallii

Crepis marschallii, commonly known as the marsh hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. Crepis marschallii is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and other damp environments where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
