Clibadium havanense

Clibadium havanense, commonly known as the Cuban sumpweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Clibadium havanense thrives in moist, sandy soils and is typically found in coastal areas.
