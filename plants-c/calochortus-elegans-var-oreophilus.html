Calochortus elegans var. oreophilus

Calochortus elegans var. oreophilus, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and often feature a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall flowering stem with multiple blooms.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
