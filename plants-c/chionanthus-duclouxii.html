Chionanthus duclouxii

Chionanthus duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's fringeflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture.
