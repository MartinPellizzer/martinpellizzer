Conyza argutidens

Conyza argutidens, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, while its flowers are yellow with white centers.

Conyza argutidens is known for its adaptability and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
