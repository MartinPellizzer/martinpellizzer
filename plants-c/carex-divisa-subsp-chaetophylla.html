Carex divisa subsp. chaetophylla

Carex divisa subsp.

chaetophylla, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.
