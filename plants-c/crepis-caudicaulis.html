Crepis caudicaulis

Crepis caudicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Crepis caudicaulis is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
