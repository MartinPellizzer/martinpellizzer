Carex garberi subsp. bifaria

Carex garberi subsp.

bifaria, commonly known as the Garber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and streambanks, and is often found in shaded areas. Carex garberi subsp.

bifaria is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
