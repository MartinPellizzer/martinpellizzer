Cerasus microcarpa f. brevipedunculata

Cerasus microcarpa f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub with small, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its short peduncles, giving it a dense appearance. The plant typically produces small, bright red fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
