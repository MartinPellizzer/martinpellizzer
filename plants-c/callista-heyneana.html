Callista heyneana

Callista heyneana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in long, drooping clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
