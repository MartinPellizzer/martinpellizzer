Cordia chabrensis

Cordia chabrensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
