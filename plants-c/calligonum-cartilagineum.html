Calligonum cartilagineum

Calligonum cartilagineum, commonly known as the cartilaginous goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Calligonum cartilagineum is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.
