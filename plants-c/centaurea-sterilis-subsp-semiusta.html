Centaurea sterilis subsp. semiusta

Centaurea sterilis subsp.

semiusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
