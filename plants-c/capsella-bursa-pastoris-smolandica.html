Capsella bursa-pastoris smolandica

Capsella bursa-pastoris smolandica, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and produces heart-shaped, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically oval or kidney-shaped. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
