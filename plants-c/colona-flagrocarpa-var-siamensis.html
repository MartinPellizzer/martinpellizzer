Colona flagrocarpa var. siamensis

Colona flagrocarpa var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam flagrocarpa, is a rare and unique plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often display a vibrant green color with distinctive patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
