Carex obtusa

Carex obtusa, commonly known as broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex obtusa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
