Cyperus fulvescens

Cyperus fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often tinged with yellow or reddish hues.

Cyperus fulvescens is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
