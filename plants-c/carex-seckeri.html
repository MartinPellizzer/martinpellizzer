Carex seckeri

Carex seckeri, commonly known as the Secker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Carex seckeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
