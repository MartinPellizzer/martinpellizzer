Carex brownii var. dissociata

Carex brownii var. dissociata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of flowering spikes. Native to the wetlands and marshy areas of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
