Calamagrostis atropurpurea

Calamagrostis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to reddish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its dense growth habit makes it an effective erosion control plant.

It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
