Ceropegia cornuta

Ceropegia cornuta, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like structures on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Ceropegia cornuta is a perennial species that is well adapted to drought conditions.
