Cocos graminifolia

Cocos graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy coconut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that resemble those of grasses. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by drupes containing a single seed. It is typically found in coastal areas and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, saline environments.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
