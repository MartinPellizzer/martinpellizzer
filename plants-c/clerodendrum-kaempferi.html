Clerodendrum kaempferi

Clerodendrum kaempferi, commonly known as the red flame or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, with five lobes and a prominent corona. It blooms throughout the year, especially during the warmer months, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
