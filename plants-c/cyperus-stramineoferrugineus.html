Cyperus stramineoferrugineus

Cyperus stramineoferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty sandflat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a fine, rusty-colored bloom, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Cyperus stramineoferrugineus is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
