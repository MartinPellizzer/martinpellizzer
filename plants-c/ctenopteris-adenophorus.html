Ctenopteris adenophorus

Ctenopteris adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Ctenopteris adenophorus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
