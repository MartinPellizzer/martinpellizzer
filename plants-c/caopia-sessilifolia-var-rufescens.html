Caopia sessilifolia var. rufescens

Caopia sessilifolia var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaved caopia, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive reddish-brown foliage. The leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without stalks, and are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow or orange, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
