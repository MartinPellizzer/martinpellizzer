Cyperus kuebensis

Cyperus kuebensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus kuebensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
