Corybas imperatorius

Corybas imperatorius, commonly known as the imperial ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flower that resembles a ghostly face, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically a deep purple or violet, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a human face.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Corybas imperatorius is considered a conservation concern.
