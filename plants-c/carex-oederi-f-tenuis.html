Carex oederi f. tenuis

Carex oederi f. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a ground cover in shady, damp areas.
