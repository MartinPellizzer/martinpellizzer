Cereus quadricostatus

Cereus quadricostatus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
