Cynophalla tarapotensis

Cynophalla tarapotensis, commonly known as the Tarapoto cynophalla, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered, often with a yellow or white hue.

Due to its unique habitat and potential pharmacological applications, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
