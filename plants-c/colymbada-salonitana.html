Colymbada salonitana

Colymbada salonitana, commonly known as the Balkan marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and thrives in marshy or pond environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often short and sturdy.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
