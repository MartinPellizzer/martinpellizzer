Callista subulata

Callista subulata, commonly known as the slender callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Callista subulata is also noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to grow in full sun.
