Campanula cespitosa subsp. pubescens

Campanula cespitosa subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive pubescent appearance.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its overall textured foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
