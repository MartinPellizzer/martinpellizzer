Couepia martini

Couepia martini, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Couepia martini is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
