Carex boissieri

Carex boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex boissieri produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
