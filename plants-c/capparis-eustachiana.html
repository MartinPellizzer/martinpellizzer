Capparis eustachiana

Capparis eustachiana, commonly known as the Eustachian caper, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often followed by edible, green capers, which are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are succulent and have a slightly sour taste, contributing to its historical use in traditional medicine.

Capparis eustachiana is also valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
