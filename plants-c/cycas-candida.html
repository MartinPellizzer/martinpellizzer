Cycas candida

Cycas candida, commonly known as the white cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Cycas candida is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
