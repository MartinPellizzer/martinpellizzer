Cirsium penicillatum subsp. hermonis

Cirsium penicillatum subsp.

hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silver-green leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
