Cytisus pentaphyllus

Cytisus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the purple flame acacia, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
