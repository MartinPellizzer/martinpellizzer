Cicuta occidentalis f. frondosa

Cicuta occidentalis f. frondosa, commonly known as the western water hemlock, is a variant of the water hemlock species native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of theApiaceae family. It is highly toxic, containing cicutoxin, which can be lethal to humans and animals.
