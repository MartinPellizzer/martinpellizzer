Craterispermum robbrechtianum

Craterispermum robbrechtianum, commonly known as the Robbrecht's craterpod, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration.

The seed pods, which give the plant its scientific name, are inflated and split open to release seeds.
