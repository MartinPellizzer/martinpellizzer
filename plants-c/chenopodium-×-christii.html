Chenopodium × christii

Chenopodium × christii, commonly known as the New Mexico goosefoot, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium × christii is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional Native American cuisine.
