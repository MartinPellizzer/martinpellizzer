Campanula preissii

Campanula preissii, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula preissii is valued for its striking appearance and adaptability to various garden environments.
