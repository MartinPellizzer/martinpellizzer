Cucurbita pepo var. georgica

Cucurbita pepo var. georgica, commonly known as the prickly pear or caltrop, is a species of flowering plant in the gourd family.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit and is native to the Americas. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into the distinctive, rough, spiky fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a tough, inedible outer layer.
