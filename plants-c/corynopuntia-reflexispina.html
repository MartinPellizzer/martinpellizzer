Corynopuntia reflexispina

Corynopuntia reflexispina, commonly known as the hooked spine cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Corynopuntia reflexispina is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a common plant in desert ecosystems.
