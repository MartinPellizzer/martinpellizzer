Cystacanthus insignis

Cystacanthus insignis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Cystacanthus insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
