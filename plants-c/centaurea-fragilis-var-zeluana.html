Centaurea fragilis var. zeluana

Centaurea fragilis var. zeluana, commonly known as the Zeluana star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely leafed stems and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its growth habit is prostrate to upright, and it can form dense colonies.

The variety zeluana is distinguished by its unique floral structure and smaller size compared to other varieties of Centaurea fragilis.
