Calamintha enriquei

Calamintha enriquei, commonly known as Enrique's germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Calamintha enriquei is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic effects.
