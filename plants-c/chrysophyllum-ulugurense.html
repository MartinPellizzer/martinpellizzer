Chrysophyllum ulugurense

Chrysophyllum ulugurense, commonly known as the Uluguru cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a sweet and tart flavor. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
