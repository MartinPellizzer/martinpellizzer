Cepa flexuosa

Cepa flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, flexible leaves that can bend and twist, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with six petals, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, making it useful in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
