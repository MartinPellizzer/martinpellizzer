Caconapea stricta

Caconapea stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poinsettia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant is evergreen and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.

Caconapea stricta is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
