Cynometra pierreana

Cynometra pierreana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered threatened in some regions.
