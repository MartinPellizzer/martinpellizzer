Centaurea vulnerariifolia

Centaurea vulnerariifolia, commonly known as the wounded leaf centaurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spiny leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
