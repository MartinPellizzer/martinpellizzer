Carex acuta var. moenchiana

Carex acuta var. moenchiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
