Ceraia confundens

Ceraia confundens, commonly known as the false orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Ceraia confundens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
