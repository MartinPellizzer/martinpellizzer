Coreosma dombeyana

Coreosma dombeyana, commonly known as the golden flamebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Coreosma dombeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
