Crotonogyne argentea

Crotonogyne argentea, commonly known as silver croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of silver, white, and green hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
