Cereus brevistylus

Cereus brevistylus, commonly known as the short-styled cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are often fragrant. Its spines are short and sparse, and it is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments.

The plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique flowering habits.
