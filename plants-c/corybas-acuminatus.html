Corybas acuminatus

Corybas acuminatus, commonly known as the acuminated hooded orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that is typically dark purple or violet in color. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinct, narrow tube. Corybas acuminatus thrives in damp, shaded environments, often growing in forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family, particularly in New Zealand's flora.
