Cassia tortuosa

Cassia tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, twisted branches that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. Cassia tortuosa is often grown for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
