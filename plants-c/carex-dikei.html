Carex dikei

Carex dikei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex dikei is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
