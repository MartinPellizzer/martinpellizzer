Cerasus × makinoana

Cerasus × makinoana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × yedoensis, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and has ovate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a small, tart cherry that is edible but less commonly consumed than other cherry varieties.

Cerasus × makinoana is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
