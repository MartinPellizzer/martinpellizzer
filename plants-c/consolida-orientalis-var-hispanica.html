Consolida orientalis var. hispanica

Consolida orientalis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and cut flower arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
