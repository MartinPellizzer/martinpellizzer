Coreopsis coriacea

Coreopsis coriacea, commonly known as rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a white center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
