Carex marahuacana

Carex marahuacana, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex marahuacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
