Crossoglossa caulescens

Crossoglossa caulescens, commonly known as the slender cross-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals, which gives it its scientific name. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Crossoglossa caulescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
