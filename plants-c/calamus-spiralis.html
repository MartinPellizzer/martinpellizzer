Calamus spiralis

Calamus spiralis, commonly known as spiral reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender, spirally twisted stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, which are leaf-like bracts. Calamus spiralis is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique spiral growth pattern makes it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
