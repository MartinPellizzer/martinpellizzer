Cordyline thomsonii

Cordyline thomsonii, commonly known as the Thompson's druid palm, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, crowned with a dense, feathery cluster of dark green, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both gardens and landscapes.
