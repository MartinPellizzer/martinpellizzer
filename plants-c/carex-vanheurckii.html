Carex vanheurckii

Carex vanheurckii, commonly known as the Vanheurck's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Carex vanheurckii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.
