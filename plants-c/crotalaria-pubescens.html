Crotalaria pubescens

Crotalaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Crotalaria pubescens is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
