Capparis retusa f. obovata

Capparis retusa f. obovata, commonly known as the obovate caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture, while the fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature. This variety is native to regions in southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
