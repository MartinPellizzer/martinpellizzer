Cupressus nucifera

Cupressus nucifera, commonly known as the Indian tea tree or white cedar, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and dense, feathery foliage that forms a crown of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing near water sources.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
