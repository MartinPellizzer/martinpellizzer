Cerasus bigarella francofurtensis

Cerasus bigarella francofurtensis, commonly known as the Francofurt cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are typically edible. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the fruiting season in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

The Francofurt cherry is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
