Calestania lancifolia

Calestania lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf calestania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calestania lancifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
