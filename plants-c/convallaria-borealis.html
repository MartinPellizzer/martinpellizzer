Convallaria borealis

Convallaria borealis, commonly known as the northern lily or white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
