Crotalaria atusia

Crotalaria atusia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive spurred structure, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant produces small, hairy pods containing several seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
