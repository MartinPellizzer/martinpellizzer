Cassia itzana

Cassia itzana, commonly known as Mexican cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia itzana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in folk remedies for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
