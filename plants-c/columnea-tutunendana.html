Columnea tutunendana

Columnea tutunendana, commonly known as the red-tipped columnea, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that are often adorned with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy texture.

Columnea tutunendana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
