Cuviera bolo

Cuviera bolo, commonly known as the Bolo plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to yellow.

The Bolo plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
