Cleome brasiliensis

Cleome brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian spider flower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome brasiliensis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
