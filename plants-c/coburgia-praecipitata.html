Coburgia praecipitata

Coburgia praecipitata, commonly known as the Crimson Cup, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, cup-shaped flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Coburgia praecipitata is a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical collections due to its vibrant appearance and unique structure.
