Callianthemum kernerianum

Callianthemum kernerianum, commonly known as the Kerner's crownflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
