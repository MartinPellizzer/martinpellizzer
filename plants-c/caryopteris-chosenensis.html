Caryopteris chosenensis

Caryopteris chosenensis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance further enhance its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
