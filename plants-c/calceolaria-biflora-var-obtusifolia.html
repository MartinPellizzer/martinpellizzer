Calceolaria biflora var. obtusifolia

Calceolaria biflora var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like labellum, which resembles a slipper and is often brightly colored. The plant produces two flowers per stem, with showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a velvety texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Calceolaria biflora var. obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
