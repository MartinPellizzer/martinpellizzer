Caesalpinia leiostachya

Caesalpinia leiostachya, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Caesalpinia leiostachya is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
