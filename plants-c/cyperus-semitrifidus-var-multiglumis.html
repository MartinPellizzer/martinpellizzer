Cyperus semitrifidus var. multiglumis

Cyperus semitrifidus var. multiglumis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, three-ranked flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
