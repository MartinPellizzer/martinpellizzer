Cactus pondii

Cactus pondii, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cactus pondii thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for xeriscaping.
