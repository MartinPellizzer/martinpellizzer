Cinnagrostis polygama var. filifolia

Cinnagrostis polygama var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and pale blue-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it a suitable choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
