Cirsium × haussmannii

Cirsium × haussmannii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pink flower heads. The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the Compositae family, and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. Cirsium × haussmannii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, where it can spread rapidly through its seeds.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
