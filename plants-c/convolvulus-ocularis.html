Convolvulus ocularis

Convolvulus ocularis, commonly known as the eye of the gourd, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Convolvulus ocularis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
