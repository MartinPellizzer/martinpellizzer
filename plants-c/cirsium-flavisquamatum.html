Cirsium flavisquamatum

Cirsium flavisquamatum, commonly known as the yellow-squamate thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
