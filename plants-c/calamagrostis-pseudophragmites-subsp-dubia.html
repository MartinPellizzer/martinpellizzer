Calamagrostis pseudophragmites subsp. dubia

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the slender reed, is a grass species native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This subspecies thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, along stream banks, and in other aquatic habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in hue. Calamagrostis pseudophragmites subsp.

dubia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
