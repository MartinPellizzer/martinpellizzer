Cuphea spruceana

Cuphea spruceana, commonly known as the spruce's cuphea, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. Cuphea spruceana thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
