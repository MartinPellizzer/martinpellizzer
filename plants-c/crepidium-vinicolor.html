Crepidium vinicolor

Crepidium vinicolor, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Crepidium vinicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
