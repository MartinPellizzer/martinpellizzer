Chenolea salsuginosa

Chenolea salsuginosa, commonly known as the saltwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other regions with high soil salinity. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or white, and it reproduces through seeds.

Chenolea salsuginosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in saline habitats.
