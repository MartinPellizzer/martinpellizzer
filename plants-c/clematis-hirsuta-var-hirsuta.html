Clematis hirsuta var. hirsuta

Clematis hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

This species is well-suited for trellises or arbors and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
