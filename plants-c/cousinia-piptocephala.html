Cousinia piptocephala

Cousinia piptocephala, commonly known as the Kashmir copper grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in unstable mountain soils.

Cousinia piptocephala plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
