Calliandra chulumania

Calliandra chulumania, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracteate inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and is also used in some regions for fodder and soil improvement.
