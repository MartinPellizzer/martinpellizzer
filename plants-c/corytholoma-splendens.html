Corytholoma splendens

Corytholoma splendens, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped fronds that resemble small cups, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Corytholoma splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
