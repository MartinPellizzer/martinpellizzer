Cupheanthus microphyllus

Cupheanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
