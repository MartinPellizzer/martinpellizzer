Clarkia amoena var. pacifica

Clarkia amoena var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the variety. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its coastal habitat.

The species is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
