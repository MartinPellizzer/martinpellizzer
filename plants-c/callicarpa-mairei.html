Callicarpa mairei

Callicarpa mairei, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of violet to blue-purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
