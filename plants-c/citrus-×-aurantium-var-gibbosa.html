Citrus × aurantium var. gibbosa

Citrus × aurantium var. gibbosa, commonly known as the pomelo, is a citrus tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit that can weigh up to 10 pounds. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for its sweet, less acidic juice.

The plant is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
