Centaurium venustum f. tupae

Centaurium venustum f. tupae, commonly known as the Tupai Centaury, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant of the Centaurium genus.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and features small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and delicate nature, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
