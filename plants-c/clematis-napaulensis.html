Clematis napaulensis

Clematis napaulensis, commonly known as the Indian Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine with woody stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic and consist of five sepals and five petals, with numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

Clematis napaulensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soil.
