Calamus heteroideus var. procerus

Calamus heteroideus var. procerus, commonly known as the slender umbrella palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
