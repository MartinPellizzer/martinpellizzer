Chondrilla urumoffii

Chondrilla urumoffii, commonly known as the Urumoff's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
