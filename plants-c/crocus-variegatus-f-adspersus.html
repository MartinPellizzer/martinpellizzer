Crocus variegatus f. adspersus

Crocus variegatus f. adspersus, commonly known as the speckled crocus, is a botanical variant of the variegated crocus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers that typically feature a mix of yellow, orange, and purple hues. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens for its vibrant, early-season flowers.
