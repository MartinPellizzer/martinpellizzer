Chamaecrista concinna

Chamaecrista concinna, commonly known as the partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and dry environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Chamaecrista concinna is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
