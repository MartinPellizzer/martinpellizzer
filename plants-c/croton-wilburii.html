Croton wilburii

Croton wilburii, commonly known as the Wilbur's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors ranging from green to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer regions.
