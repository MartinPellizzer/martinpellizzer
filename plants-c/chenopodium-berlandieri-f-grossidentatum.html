Chenopodium berlandieri f. grossidentatum

Chenopodium berlandieri f. grossidentatum, commonly known as the buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical structures called heads. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough, sandpapery texture.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
