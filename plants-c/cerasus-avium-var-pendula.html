Cerasus avium var. pendula

Cerasus avium var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a variant of the Prunus avium species.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and seasonal beauty.
