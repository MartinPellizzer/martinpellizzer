Chromolaena hypericifolia

Chromolaena hypericifolia, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 5 meters in height. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Chromolaena hypericifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
