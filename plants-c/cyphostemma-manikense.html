Cyphostemma manikense

Cyphostemma manikense, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
