Calamus melanacanthos

Calamus melanacanthos, commonly known as black-spined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
