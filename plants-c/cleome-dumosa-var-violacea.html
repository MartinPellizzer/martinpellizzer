Cleome dumosa var. violacea

Cleome dumosa var. violacea, commonly known as purple cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
