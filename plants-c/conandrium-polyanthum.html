Conandrium polyanthum

Conandrium polyanthum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, bushy appearance.

Conandrium polyanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
