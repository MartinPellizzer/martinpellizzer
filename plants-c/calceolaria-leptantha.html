Calceolaria leptantha

Calceolaria leptantha, commonly known as the slender slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique calyces that resemble small slippers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom during the cooler months.

Calceolaria leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
