Cymbopogon flexuosus var. arunachalis

Cymbopogon flexuosus var. arunachalis, commonly known as the Arunachal lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic and fragrant. The plant produces inflorescences of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically clustered in dense spikes. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

This variety is well adapted to moist, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic uses.
