Carex × mixta

Carex × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex appressa and Carex breviculmis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex × mixta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
