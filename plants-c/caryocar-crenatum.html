Caryocar crenatum

Caryocar crenatum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or yellow chestnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique, hard-shelled fruit that contains a single edible seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable use, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
