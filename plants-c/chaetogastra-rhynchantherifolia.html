Chaetogastra rhynchantherifolia

Chaetogastra rhynchantherifolia, commonly known as the fragrant spirea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly serrated edge.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to various garden settings.
