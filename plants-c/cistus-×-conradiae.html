Cistus × conradiae

Cistus × conradiae, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes several species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are leathery and fragrant, often emitting a resinous scent when crushed.
