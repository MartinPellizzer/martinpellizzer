Cornus sylvestris

Cornus sylvestris, commonly known as the common dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Cornus sylvestris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
