Carex vulpina f. bracteata

Carex vulpina f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its distinctive bract-like structures.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions of North America. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in consistently damp soil. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are densely packed.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
