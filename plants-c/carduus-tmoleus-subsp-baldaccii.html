Carduus tmoleus subsp. baldaccii

Carduus tmoleus subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a striking purple to pink flower head that blooms in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth compared to other Carduus species.
