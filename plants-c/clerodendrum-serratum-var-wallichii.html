Clerodendrum serratum var. wallichii

Clerodendrum serratum var. wallichii, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
