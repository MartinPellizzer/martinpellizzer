Clethra alnifolia

Clethra alnifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Clethra alnifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
