Cordyline terminalis var. baileyi

Cordyline terminalis var. baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters at the top of the stem during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
