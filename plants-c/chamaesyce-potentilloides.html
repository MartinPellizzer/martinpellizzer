Chamaesyce potentilloides

Chamaesyce potentilloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as fields and woodland edges. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring.

Chamaesyce potentilloides is valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
