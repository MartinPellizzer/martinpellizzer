Caryochloa caudata

Caryochloa caudata, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, particularly when in bloom. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often adorned with soft, hairy structures. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
