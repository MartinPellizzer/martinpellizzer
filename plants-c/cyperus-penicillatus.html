Cyperus penicillatus

Cyperus penicillatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, along waterways, and in disturbed soils.

Cyperus penicillatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
