Canarium hirtellum

Canarium hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
