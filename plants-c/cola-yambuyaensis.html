Cola yambuyaensis

Cola yambuyaensis, commonly known as the Yambuya nut tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts within its native habitat.
