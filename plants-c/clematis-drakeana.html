Clematis drakeana

Clematis drakeana, commonly known as the Drake's clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in diameter, with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis drakeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
