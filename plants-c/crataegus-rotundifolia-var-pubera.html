Crataegus rotundifolia var. pubera

Crataegus rotundifolia var. pubera, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but generally considered tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

The downy hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
