Cerastium mutabile var. bialynickii

Cerastium mutabile var. bialynickii, commonly known as the variable mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite, with a rounded or ovate shape. It produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its variability in leaf shape and flower color, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
