Calligonum santoanum

Calligonum santoanum, commonly known as the Santoanum goosefoot, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its woody stems and thick leaves make it well-adapted to drought conditions.

Calligonum santoanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
