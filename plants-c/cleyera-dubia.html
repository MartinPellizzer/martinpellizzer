Cleyera dubia

Cleyera dubia, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in shaded environments.
