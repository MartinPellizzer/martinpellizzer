Chartolepis tournefortii

Chartolepis tournefortii, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are typically green with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, often blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
