Campanula persicifolia f. kirschlegeri

Campanula persicifolia f. kirschlegeri, commonly known as the Kirschleger's bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
