Cayratia carnosa

Cayratia carnosa, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Cayratia carnosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and climbing habit.
