Cassia adenensis

Cassia adenensis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Cassia adenensis is an important species in Australian flora and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
