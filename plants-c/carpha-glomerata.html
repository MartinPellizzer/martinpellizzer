Carpha glomerata

Carpha glomerata, commonly known as clustered greenflag, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Carpha glomerata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
