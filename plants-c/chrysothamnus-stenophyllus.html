Chrysothamnus stenophyllus

Chrysothamnus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
