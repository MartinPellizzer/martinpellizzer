Caesalpinia selloi

Caesalpinia selloi, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright red, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its striking appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
