Coelogyne cootesii

Coelogyne cootesii, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the forests of Sri Lanka and parts of India.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne cootesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
