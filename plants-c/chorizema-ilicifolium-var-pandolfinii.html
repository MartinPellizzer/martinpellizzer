Chorizema ilicifolium var. pandolfinii

Chorizema ilicifolium var. pandolfinii, commonly known as the Pandolfini's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that resemble those of the holly. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically found at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
