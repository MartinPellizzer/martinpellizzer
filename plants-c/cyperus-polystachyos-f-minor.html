Cyperus polystachyos f. minor

Cyperus polystachyos f. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus polystachyos f. minor is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
