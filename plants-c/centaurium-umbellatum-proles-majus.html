Centaurium umbellatum proles majus

Centaurium umbellatum proles majus, commonly known as the greater white centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
