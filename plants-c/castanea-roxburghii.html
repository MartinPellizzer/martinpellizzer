Castanea roxburghii

Castanea roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are typically harvested in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across China, Korea, and Japan.

Castanea roxburghii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional agriculture.
