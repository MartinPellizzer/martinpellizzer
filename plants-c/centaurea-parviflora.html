Centaurea parviflora

Centaurea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Centaurea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
