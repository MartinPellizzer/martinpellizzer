Chesniella depressa

Chesniella depressa, commonly known as the dwarf chesniella, is a small flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Chesniella depressa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden environments with proper care.
