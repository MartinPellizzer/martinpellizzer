Calamintha nepeta var. athonica

Calamintha nepeta var. athonica, commonly known as Greek calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are aromatic, often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
