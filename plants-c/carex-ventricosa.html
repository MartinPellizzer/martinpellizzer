Carex ventricosa

Carex ventricosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically broad and flat, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces spikelets that are usually clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers. Carex ventricosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
