Cavendishia lindauiana

Cavendishia lindauiana, commonly known as the Lindau's Cavendish, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features rosettes of grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. Its flowers are usually red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Cavendishia lindauiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
