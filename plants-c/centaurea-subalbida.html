Centaurea subalbida

Centaurea subalbida, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Centaurea subalbida is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
