Calliandra turbinata

Calliandra turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Calliandra turbinata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
