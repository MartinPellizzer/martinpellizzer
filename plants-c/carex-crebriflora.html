Carex crebriflora

Carex crebriflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often with a distinctive coloration.

Carex crebriflora is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
