Croton guineensis

Croton guineensis, commonly known as African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its thick, rough bark and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

Croton guineensis thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in savannas and woodland areas.
