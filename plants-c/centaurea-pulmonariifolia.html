Centaurea pulmonariifolia

Centaurea pulmonariifolia, commonly known as the lungwort centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive spotted or veined appearance.

Centaurea pulmonariifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
