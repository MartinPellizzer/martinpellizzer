Convolvulus multibracteatus

Convolvulus multibracteatus, commonly known as the many-bract convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers can be white, pink, or purple in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
