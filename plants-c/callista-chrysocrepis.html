Callista chrysocrepis

Callista chrysocrepis, commonly known as the golden crepe flower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, crepe-paper-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
