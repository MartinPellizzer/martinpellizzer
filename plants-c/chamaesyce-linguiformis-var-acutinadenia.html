Chamaesyce linguiformis var. acutinadenia

Chamaesyce linguiformis var. acutinadenia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its succulent leaves help it store water, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
