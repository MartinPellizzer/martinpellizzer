Crataegus villosa

Crataegus villosa, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
