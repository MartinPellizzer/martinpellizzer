Cytisus leiocarpus var. bosniacus

Cytisus leiocarpus var. bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkans.

It features slender, arching branches with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
