Chionanthus monticola

Chionanthus monticola, commonly known as the mountain chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chionanthus monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
