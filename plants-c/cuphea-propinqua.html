Cuphea propinqua

Cuphea propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf wax plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring compact, rounded foliage. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for container gardening or as a bedding plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season with proper care.
