Camaridium multicaule

Camaridium multicaule, commonly known as the clustered camaridium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Camaridium multicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
