Carex harperi

Carex harperi, commonly known as the Harperi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered at the top of the plant.

Carex harperi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
