Columnea jacquiniana

Columnea jacquiniana, commonly known as the scarlet flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Columnea jacquiniana prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity to flourish.
