Corynephorus divaricatus subsp. articulatus

Corynephorus divaricatus subsp.

articulatus, commonly known as the jointed hairgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive jointed stems.

The leaves are narrow and usually shorter than the flowering stems, which are often erect. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, giving it a unique appearance.

This plant is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
