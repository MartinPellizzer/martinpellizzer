Cucumis anguria subsp. longipes

Cucumis anguria subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

The plant produces elongated, spiky fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with lobed margins, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is valued for both its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
