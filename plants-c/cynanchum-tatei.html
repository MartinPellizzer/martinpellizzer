Cynanchum tatei

Cynanchum tatei, commonly known as Japanese milkweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It features opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels.

Cynanchum tatei is often used in traditional medicine but requires caution due to its poisonous properties.
