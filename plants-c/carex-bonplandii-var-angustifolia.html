Carex bonplandii var. angustifolia

Carex bonplandii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds.

Carex bonplandii var. angustifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
