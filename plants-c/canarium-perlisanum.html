Canarium perlisanum

Canarium perlisanum, commonly known as the Philippine canarium, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic uses, it is of interest in botanical and conservation studies.
