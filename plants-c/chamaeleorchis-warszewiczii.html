Chamaeleorchis warszewiczii

Chamaeleorchis warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a unique structure typical of orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid or semi-arid habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural range.
