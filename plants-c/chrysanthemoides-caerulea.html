Chrysanthemoides caerulea

Chrysanthemoides caerulea, commonly known as the blue chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
