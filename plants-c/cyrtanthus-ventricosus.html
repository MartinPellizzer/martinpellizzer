Cyrtanthus ventricosus

Cyrtanthus ventricosus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tubular, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Cyrtanthus ventricosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
