Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. abyssinicus

Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with slender, hairy stems that twine around supports. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking flowers and unique foliage.
