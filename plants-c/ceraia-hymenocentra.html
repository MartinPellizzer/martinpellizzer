Ceraia hymenocentra

Ceraia hymenocentra, commonly known as the African moon snail, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Raphitomidae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy shell that is typically pale or cream-colored with a faint spiral pattern. The shell is elongated and conical, with a low spire and a wide, rounded aperture. The body of the snail is soft and muscular, adapted for burrowing in sandy substrates.

It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical marine environments across the Indo-Pacific region.
