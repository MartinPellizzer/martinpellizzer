Crotalaria spinosa subsp. aculeata

Crotalaria spinosa subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the spiny crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It is known for its spiny calyces and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is also noted for its potential use in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
