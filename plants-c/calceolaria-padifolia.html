Calceolaria padifolia

Calceolaria padifolia, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Calceolaria padifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
