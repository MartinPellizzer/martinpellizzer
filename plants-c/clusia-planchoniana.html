Clusia planchoniana

Clusia planchoniana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Clusia planchoniana is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
