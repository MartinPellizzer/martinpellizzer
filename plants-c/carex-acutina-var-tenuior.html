Carex acutina var. tenuior

Carex acutina var. tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and may produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
