Cirsium phulchokiense

Cirsium phulchokiense, commonly known as the Indian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
