Crocus heuffelianus lusus pictus

Crocus heuffelianus lusus pictus, commonly known as the painted crocus, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, satiny flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, with a distinctive painted pattern on the petals. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are adapted for storing nutrients and surviving harsh winters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
