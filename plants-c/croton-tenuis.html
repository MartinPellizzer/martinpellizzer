Croton tenuis

Croton tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct venation pattern. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton tenuis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
