Carex nigra var. juncea

Carex nigra var. juncea, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It features dark green, strap-like leaves that are typically narrow and stiff, with a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions, making it a common species in marshy environments. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscaping projects.
