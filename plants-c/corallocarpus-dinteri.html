Corallocarpus dinteri

Corallocarpus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's corallocarpus, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has adapted to the extreme arid conditions by developing deep root systems to access groundwater. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying desert flora.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and climate change.
