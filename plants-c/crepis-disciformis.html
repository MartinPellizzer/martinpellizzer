Crepis disciformis

Crepis disciformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has a short stem and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
