Celastrus paniculatus subsp. angladeanus

Celastrus paniculatus subsp.

angladeanus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
