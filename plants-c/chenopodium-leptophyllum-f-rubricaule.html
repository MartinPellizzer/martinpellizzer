Chenopodium leptophyllum f. rubricaule

Chenopodium leptophyllum f. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique coloration and drought tolerance.
