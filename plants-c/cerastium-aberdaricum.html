Cerastium aberdaricum

Cerastium aberdaricum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Cerastium aberdaricum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
