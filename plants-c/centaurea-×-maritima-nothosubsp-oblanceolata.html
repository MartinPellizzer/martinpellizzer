Centaurea × maritima nothosubsp.

oblanceolata, commonly known as the coastal centaury, is a hybrid plant species that exhibits characteristics of both Centaurea maritima and Centaurea cyanus. It is native to coastal regions of Europe and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright blue or purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
