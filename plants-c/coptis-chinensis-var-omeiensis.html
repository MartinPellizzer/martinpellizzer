Coptis chinensis var. omeiensis

Coptis chinensis var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and erect stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven lobes, and its flowers bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids used in traditional Chinese medicine.
