Cyathea × marcescens

Cyathea × marcescens, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a tall, robust fern that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with the trunk developing a fibrous, bark-like covering as it matures. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Cyathea × marcescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
