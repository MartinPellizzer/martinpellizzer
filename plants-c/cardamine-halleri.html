Cardamine halleri

Cardamine halleri, commonly known as lesser celandine, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cardamine halleri is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
