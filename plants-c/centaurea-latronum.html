Centaurea latronum

Centaurea latronum, commonly known as the thief's thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its name is derived from the Latin word "latro," meaning thief, possibly due to its historical use in treating wounds.

Centaurea latronum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
