Clematis perulata

Clematis perulata, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

Clematis perulata prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is relatively easy to care for in moderate climates.
