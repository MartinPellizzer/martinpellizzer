Centaurea albida

Centaurea albida, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bright white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive central yellow disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
