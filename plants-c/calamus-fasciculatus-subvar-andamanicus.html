Calamus fasciculatus subvar. andamanicus

Calamus fasciculatus subvar. andamanicus, commonly known as the Andaman calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a robust, climbing palm characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. This variety is native to the Andaman Islands and thrives in tropical wet climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Its rhizomes are often harvested for traditional uses, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
