Coelogyne concolor

Coelogyne concolor, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with a single or few leaves, and its roots are typically epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Coelogyne concolor is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
