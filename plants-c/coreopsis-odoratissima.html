Coreopsis odoratissima

Coreopsis odoratissima, commonly known as the sweetcore or fragrant coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, fragrant center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis odoratissima is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
