Callicarpa globiflora

Callicarpa globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spherical inflorescences that resemble small globes, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Callicarpa globiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
