Colobachne pallasii

Colobachne pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually greenish or purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Colobachne pallasii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
