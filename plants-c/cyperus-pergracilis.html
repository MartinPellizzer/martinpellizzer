Cyperus pergracilis

Cyperus pergracilis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy environments. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
