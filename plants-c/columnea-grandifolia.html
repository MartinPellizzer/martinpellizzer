Columnea grandifolia

Columnea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub or vine, with opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowering season occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Columnea grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
