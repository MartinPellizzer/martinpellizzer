Campanula arctica subsp. mentiens

Campanula arctica subsp.

mentiens, commonly known as Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is well adapted to low temperatures and has a long flowering period during the short Arctic summer.
