Crepis capillaris f. bischoffii

Crepis capillaris f. bischoffii, commonly known as the slender hawk's-beard, is a horticultural form of the common hawk's-beard.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and yellow composite flowers. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often cultivated for its fine foliage and early blooming habit. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to many common garden pests.
