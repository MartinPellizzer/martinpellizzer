Cyanocephalus coriaceus

Cyanocephalus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
