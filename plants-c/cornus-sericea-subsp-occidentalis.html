Cornus sericea subsp. occidentalis

Cornus sericea subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces red or yellow berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland edges.
