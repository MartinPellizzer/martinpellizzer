Crocus vernus var. obovatus

Crocus vernus var. obovatus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a purple or reddish tinge at the base. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulb that stores nutrients for the plant's growth. It typically blooms in early spring, making it one of the first flowers to appear after winter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
