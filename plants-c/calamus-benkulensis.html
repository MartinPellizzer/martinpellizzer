Calamus benkulensis

Calamus benkulensis, commonly known as the Benkulensis rattan, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms and other tropical plants. The plant is characterized by its long, flexible stems that are often used for making furniture and crafts. Its leaves are typically large, elongated, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Calamus benkulensis thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense rainforests.
