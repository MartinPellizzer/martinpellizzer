Chenopodium album subsp. hastatum

Chenopodium album subsp.

hastatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered with fine, prickly hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is a short-lived annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and widespread distribution.
