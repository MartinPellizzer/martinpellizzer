Coryanthes rutkisii

Coryanthes rutkisii, commonly known as the giant coryanthes, is a striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is renowned for its large, intricate flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features a distinctive, fleshy, red-and-black striped column that serves as a perch for its pollinators. Its flowers are specialized to attract specific flies, which play a crucial role in its pollination process.

The botanical structure of Coryanthes rutkisii is adapted to its unique ecological niche, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
