Cheiranthus × cheiri var. corinthius

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. corinthius, commonly known as the Corinthian wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
