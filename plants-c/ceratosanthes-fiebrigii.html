Ceratosanthes fiebrigii

Ceratosanthes fiebrigii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
