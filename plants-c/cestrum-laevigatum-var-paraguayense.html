Cestrum laevigatum var. paraguayense

Cestrum laevigatum var. paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay yellow bell, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
