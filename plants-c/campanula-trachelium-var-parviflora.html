Campanula trachelium var. parviflora

Campanula trachelium var. parviflora, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
