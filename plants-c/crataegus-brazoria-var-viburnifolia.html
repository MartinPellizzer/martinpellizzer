Crataegus brazoria var. viburnifolia

Crataegus brazoria var. viburnifolia, commonly known as the coastal hawthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in coastal and sandy soils. It is often found in dune environments and is adapted to salt spray and wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
