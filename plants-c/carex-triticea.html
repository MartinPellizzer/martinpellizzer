Carex triticea

Carex triticea, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or green in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex triticea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
