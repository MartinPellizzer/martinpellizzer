Carex hypochlora

Carex hypochlora, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a pale green coloration that distinguishes it from other sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
