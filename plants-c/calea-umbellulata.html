Calea umbellulata

Calea umbellulata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pink flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

The unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
