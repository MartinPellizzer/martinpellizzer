Cyperus uniflorus var. dicarpus

Cyperus uniflorus var. dicarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, which is a notable feature in its reproductive biology.
