Centaurea resupinata subsp. spachii

Centaurea resupinata subsp.

spachii, commonly known as the creeping cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, often branched stems that lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with red or purple central discs, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a hardy, adaptable plant.
