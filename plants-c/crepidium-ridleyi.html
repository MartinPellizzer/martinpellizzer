Crepidium ridleyi

Crepidium ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native humid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in shaded forest understories.

Crepidium ridleyi is often found growing on trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic growth habit.
