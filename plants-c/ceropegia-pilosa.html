Ceropegia pilosa

Ceropegia pilosa, commonly known as the silver leaf string plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems with small, pale green leaves that have a silvery appearance due to a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded shape. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and easy care make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
