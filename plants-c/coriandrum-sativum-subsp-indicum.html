Coriandrum sativum subsp. indicum

Coriandrum sativum subsp.

indicum, commonly known as Indian cumin, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to South Asia. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and feathery, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into spherical, dried seed pods containing the aromatic seeds used in cooking. These seeds are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
