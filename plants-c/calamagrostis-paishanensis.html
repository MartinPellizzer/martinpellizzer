Calamagrostis paishanensis

Calamagrostis paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan reed, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
