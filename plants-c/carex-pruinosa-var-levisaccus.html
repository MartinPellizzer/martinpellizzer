Carex pruinosa var. levisaccus

Carex pruinosa var. levisaccus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The leaves are typically smooth and glabrous, with a waxy coating that gives the plant its name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
