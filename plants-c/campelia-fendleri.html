Campelia fendleri

Campelia fendleri, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Campelia fendleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
