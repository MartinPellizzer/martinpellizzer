Carduus centifolius

Carduus centifolius, commonly known as the centipede thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and a tall, branched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Carduus centifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
