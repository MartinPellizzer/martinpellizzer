Celosia margaritacea

Celosia margaritacea, commonly known as the "plumeflower," is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feathery bracts that resemble plumes or fireworks. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink, red, or yellow, and they bloom in dense, showy clusters.

Celosia margaritacea is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
