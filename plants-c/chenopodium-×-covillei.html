Chenopodium × covillei

Chenopodium × covillei, commonly known as the New Mexico goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of whitish powder, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium × covillei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
