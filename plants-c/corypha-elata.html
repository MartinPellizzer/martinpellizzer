Corypha elata

Corypha elata, commonly known as the giant panda palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach impressive sizes, often spanning several meters in diameter. The plant produces a unique, elongated fruit that is encased in a spiny, fibrous husk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in botanical collections.
