Cirsium pumilum f. candidum

Cirsium pumilum f. candidum, commonly known as the white prickly lettuce, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of ray florets, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
