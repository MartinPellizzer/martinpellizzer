Cassia cathartica var. acutifolia

Cassia cathartica var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acutely leafed senna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
