Calycodendron pubiflorum

Calycodendron pubiflorum, commonly known as the Cape rosewood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy underside, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
