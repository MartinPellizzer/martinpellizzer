Calytrix puberula

Calytrix puberula, commonly known as the silky bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, soft, grey-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Calytrix puberula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
