Caryophyllus deltoides

Caryophyllus deltoides, commonly known as the deltoid carnation, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
