Centaurea sicula

Centaurea sicula, commonly known as Sicilian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea sicula is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
