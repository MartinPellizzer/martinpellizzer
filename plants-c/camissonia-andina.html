Camissonia andina

Camissonia andina, commonly known as the Andean camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round seeds in pods.

Camissonia andina is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions and is often found in association with other alpine flora.
