Cattleya intermedia var. pallida

Cattleya intermedia var. pallida, commonly known as the pale cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive red or pink lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
