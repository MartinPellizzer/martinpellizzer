Candollea repens

Candollea repens, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous stem that allows it to spread horizontally, forming dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive frond structure that resembles a fern.

Candollea repens is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
