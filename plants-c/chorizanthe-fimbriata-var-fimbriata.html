Chorizanthe fimbriata var. fimbriata

Chorizanthe fimbriata var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features erect, branching stems and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers clustered in globular inflorescences. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, fringed appearance along the edges.

The species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
