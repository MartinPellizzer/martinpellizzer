Cousinia waldheimiana

Cousinia waldheimiana, commonly known as the Waldheim's tussockgrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Cousinia waldheimiana is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
