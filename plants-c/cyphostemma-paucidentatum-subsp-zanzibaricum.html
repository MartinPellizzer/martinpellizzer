Cyphostemma paucidentatum subsp. zanzibaricum

Cyphostemma paucidentatum subsp.

zanzibaricum, commonly known as the Zanzibar moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
