Croton triacros

Croton triacros, commonly known as the three-toothed croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have three prominent lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
