Croton oreades var. craspedadenius

Croton oreades var. craspedadenius, commonly known as the mountain croton, is a perennial shrub native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
