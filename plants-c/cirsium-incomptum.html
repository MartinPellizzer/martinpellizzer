Cirsium incomptum

Cirsium incomptum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its defensive adaptations. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense, spherical clusters, typical of the genus Cirsium.

The species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
