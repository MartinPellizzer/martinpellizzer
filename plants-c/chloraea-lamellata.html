Chloraea lamellata

Chloraea lamellata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Chloraea lamellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
