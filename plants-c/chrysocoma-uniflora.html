Chrysocoma uniflora

Chrysocoma uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Chrysocoma uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
