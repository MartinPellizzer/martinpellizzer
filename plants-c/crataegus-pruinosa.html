Crataegus pruinosa

Crataegus pruinosa, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to North America.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a grayish-white underside.

Crataegus pruinosa is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
