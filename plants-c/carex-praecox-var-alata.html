Carex praecox var. alata

Carex praecox var. alata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their triangular stems and spikelet inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

The slender sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
