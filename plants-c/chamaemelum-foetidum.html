Chamaemelum foetidum

Chamaemelum foetidum, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent that can be unpleasant.

Stinkweed is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
