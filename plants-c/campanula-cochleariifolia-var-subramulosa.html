Campanula cochleariifolia var. subramulosa

Campanula cochleariifolia var. subramulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
