Croton campestris var. subacutus

Croton campestris var. subacutus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features lance-shaped, glossy green leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its striking foliage makes it a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
