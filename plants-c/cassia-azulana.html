Cassia azulana

Cassia azulana, commonly known as blue cassia, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cassia azulana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
