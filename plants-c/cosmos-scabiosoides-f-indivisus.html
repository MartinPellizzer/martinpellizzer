Cosmos scabiosoides f. indivisus

Cosmos scabiosoides f. indivisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf cosmos, is a variant of the cosmos genus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant a delicate, grass-like appearance. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
