Crataegus bastardii

Crataegus bastardii, commonly known as the bastard hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red or orange pome that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
