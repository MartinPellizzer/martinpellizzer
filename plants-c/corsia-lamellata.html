Corsia lamellata

Corsia lamellata, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often folded or pleated, giving it its scientific name "lamellata," which means "having laminae or plates.". The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Corsia lamellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
