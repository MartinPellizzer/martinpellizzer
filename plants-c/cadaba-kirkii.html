Cadaba kirkii

Cadaba kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's cadabra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cadaba kirkii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique appearance.
