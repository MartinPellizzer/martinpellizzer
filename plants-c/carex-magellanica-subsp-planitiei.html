Carex magellanica subsp. planitiei

Carex magellanica subsp.

planitiei, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
