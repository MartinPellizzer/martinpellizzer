Cyperus camphoratus var. boliviensis

Cyperus camphoratus var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
