Calytrix truncatifolia

Calytrix truncatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Calytrix truncatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
