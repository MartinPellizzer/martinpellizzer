Cyrtostachys renda

Cyrtostachys renda, commonly known as the white sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

However, it requires a warm, humid climate to thrive.
