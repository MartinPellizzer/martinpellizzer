Cassia savannensis

Cassia savannensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia savannensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
