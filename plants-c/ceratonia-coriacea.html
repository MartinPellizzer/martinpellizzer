Ceratonia coriacea

Ceratonia coriacea, commonly known as the African carob tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces a sweet, sticky resin known as carob gum, which has various industrial and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The flowers are typically pale yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
