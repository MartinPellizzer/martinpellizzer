Carpopogon atropurpureus

Carpopogon atropurpureus, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple to reddish-purple foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to form dense clumps.
