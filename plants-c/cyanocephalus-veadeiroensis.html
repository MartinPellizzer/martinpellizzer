Cyanocephalus veadeiroensis

Cyanocephalus veadeiroensis, commonly known as the Veadeiro's bluehead, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has adapted to its harsh environment with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources. Its leaves are typically narrow and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cyanocephalus veadeiroensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
