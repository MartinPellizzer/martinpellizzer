Cnidoscolus hypoleucus

Cnidoscolus hypoleucus, commonly known as the "arrow leaf" or "pupunha," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring palmately compound leaves with three to five lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are spiny capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to grow in various soil types.
