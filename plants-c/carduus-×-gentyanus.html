Carduus × gentyanus

Carduus × gentyanus, commonly known as the Gentyan thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistles. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various parts of Europe and western Asia. It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to thrive in diverse environments.

The botanical features of Carduus × gentyanus make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage in agricultural settings.
