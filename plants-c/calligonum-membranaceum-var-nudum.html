Calligonum membranaceum var. nudum

Calligonum membranaceum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its resilience makes it an important species for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
