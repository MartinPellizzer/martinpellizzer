Coreopsis cyclocarpa

Coreopsis cyclocarpa, commonly known as the wheelcup coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections.
