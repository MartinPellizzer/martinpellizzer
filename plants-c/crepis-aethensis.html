Crepis aethensis

Crepis aethensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
