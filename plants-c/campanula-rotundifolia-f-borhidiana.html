Campanula rotundifolia f. borhidiana

Campanula rotundifolia f. borhidiana, commonly known as the Borhidian bellflower, is a distinctive variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant is perennial, with a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
