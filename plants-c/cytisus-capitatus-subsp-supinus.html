Cytisus capitatus subsp. supinus

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

supinus, commonly known as the Alpine broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.
