Chamaeriphes thebaica

Chamaeriphes thebaica, commonly known as the Theban thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features upright stems with spiny leaves and produces purple or pink flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a spherical head.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
