Crinum macowanii subsp. kalahariense

Crinum macowanii subsp.

kalahariense, commonly known as the Kalahari Crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
