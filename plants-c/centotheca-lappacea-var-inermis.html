Centotheca lappacea var. inermis

Centotheca lappacea var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth centotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
