Caragana przewalskii

Caragana przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's caragana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and mountainous regions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Caragana przewalskii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
