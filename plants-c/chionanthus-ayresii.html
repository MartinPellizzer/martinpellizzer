Chionanthus ayresii

Chionanthus ayresii, commonly known as the whiteflower fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens in the summer.

Chionanthus ayresii thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
