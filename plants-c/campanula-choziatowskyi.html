Campanula choziatowskyi

Campanula choziatowskyi, commonly known as the Choziatowski bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula choziatowskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
