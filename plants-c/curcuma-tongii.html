Curcuma tongii

Curcuma tongii, commonly known as the Tongshan turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric species. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive ginger-like aroma.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other turmeric species.
