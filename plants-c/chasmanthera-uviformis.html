Chasmanthera uviformis

Chasmanthera uviformis, commonly known as the uvula plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a prominent, fleshy appendage resembling a uvula. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its unique floral display and adaptability to various climates.
