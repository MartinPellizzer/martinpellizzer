Clerodendrum aculeatum f. mucronatum

Clerodendrum aculeatum f. mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
