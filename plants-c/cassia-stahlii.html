Cassia stahlii

Cassia stahlii, commonly known as the African cassia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Cassia stahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
