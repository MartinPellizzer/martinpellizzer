Carex xanthosperma

Carex xanthosperma, commonly known as yellow sand sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-brown spikelets that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex xanthosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
