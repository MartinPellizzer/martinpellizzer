Cyperus fraternus

Cyperus fraternus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus fraternus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
