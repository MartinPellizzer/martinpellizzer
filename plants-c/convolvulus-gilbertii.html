Convolvulus gilbertii

Convolvulus gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Convolvulus gilbertii is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
