Carex polymorpha var. californica

Carex polymorpha var. californica, commonly known as the California sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a narrow, cylindrical shape. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
