Cyperus kurzii

Cyperus kurzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually narrow and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus kurzii is characterized by its small, clustered flower heads and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.
