Chrysopsis breweri var. multibracteata

Chrysopsis breweri var. multibracteata, commonly known as the multibracteate goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds.
