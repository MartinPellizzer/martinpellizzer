Cyperus paramattensis

Cyperus paramattensis, commonly known as the Paramatta flats sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions, including Queensland and New South Wales. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often reddish in color.

Cyperus paramattensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
