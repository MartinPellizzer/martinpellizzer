Calanthe proboscidea

Calanthe proboscidea, commonly known as the proboscideous calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble a proboscis, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The orchid produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts.
