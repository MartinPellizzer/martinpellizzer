Carduus arabicus subsp. marmoratus

Carduus arabicus subsp.

marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern of white and green.

The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for gardening purposes.
