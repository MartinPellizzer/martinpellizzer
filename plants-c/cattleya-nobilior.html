Cattleya nobilior

Cattleya nobilior, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with vibrant colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya nobilior is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
