Calophyllum wightianum

Calophyllum wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's torch tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing orange-red seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal and disturbed soils.
