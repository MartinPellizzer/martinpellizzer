Clarkia gracilis subsp. albicaulis

Clarkia gracilis subsp.

albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of showy, pink to white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale or white coloration, which sets it apart from other Clarkia species. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
