Cotoneaster nummularius var. ovalifolius

Cotoneaster nummularius var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to central and southern Europe.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

This variety is also valued for its use in hedging and as a specimen plant in gardens.
