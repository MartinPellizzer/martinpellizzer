Carex wightiana

Carex wightiana, commonly known as Wight's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones and swampy areas.

Carex wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
