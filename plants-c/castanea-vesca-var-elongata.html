Castanea vesca var. elongata

Castanea vesca var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated horse chestnut.

It is a variant of the horse chestnut tree, characterized by its elongated fruits and distinctive foliage. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and have a smooth texture.

The flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain edible seeds.
