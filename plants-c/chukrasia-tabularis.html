Chukrasia tabularis

Chukrasia tabularis, commonly known as the Indian flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral displays in spring, which give it its common name. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
