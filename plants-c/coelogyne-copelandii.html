Coelogyne copelandii

Coelogyne copelandii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral displays, with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coelogyne copelandii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
