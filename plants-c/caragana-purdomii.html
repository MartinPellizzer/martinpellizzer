Caragana purdomii

Caragana purdomii, commonly known as the Tibetan caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Caragana purdomii plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
