Callichilia monopodialis

Callichilia monopodialis, commonly known as the single-stemmed orchid, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its single, upright stem that bears a cluster of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Callichilia monopodialis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
