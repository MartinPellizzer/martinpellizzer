Campanula × truedingeri

Campanula × truedingeri, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula × truedingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
