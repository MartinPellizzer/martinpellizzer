Centaurea uniflora var. helvetica

Centaurea uniflora var. helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss singleflower centaury, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces a single, striking blue to purple flower at the tip of each stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats.
