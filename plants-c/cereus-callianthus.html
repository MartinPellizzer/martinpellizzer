Cereus callianthus

Cereus callianthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in spiny areoles and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. These flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
