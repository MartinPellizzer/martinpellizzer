Cyathea glaucophylla

Cyathea glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically a deep green with a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

Cyathea glaucophylla is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
