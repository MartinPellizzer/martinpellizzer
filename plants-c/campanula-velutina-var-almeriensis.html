Campanula velutina var. almeriensis

Campanula velutina var. almeriensis, commonly known as the Almeria bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Spain.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
