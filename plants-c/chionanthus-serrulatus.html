Chionanthus serrulatus

Chionanthus serrulatus, commonly known as the fragrant chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges, contributing to its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
