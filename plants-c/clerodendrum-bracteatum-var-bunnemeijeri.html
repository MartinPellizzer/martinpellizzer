Clerodendrum bracteatum var. bunnemeijeri

Clerodendrum bracteatum var. bunnemeijeri, commonly known as the Bunnemeijer's Clerodendrum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that range in color from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
