Campe americana

Campe americana, commonly known as the American camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The tree is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and opposite, simple, elliptical leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its camphor-rich wood, which has been used historically in medicinal and aromatic applications.
