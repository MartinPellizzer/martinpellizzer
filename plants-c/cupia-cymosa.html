Cupia cymosa

Cupia cymosa, commonly known as the clustered cupia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Cupia cymosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-maintenance gardens.
