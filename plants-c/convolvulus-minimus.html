Convolvulus minimus

Convolvulus minimus, commonly known as lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a nuisance in agricultural settings.
