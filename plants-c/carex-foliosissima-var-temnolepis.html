Carex foliosissima var. temnolepis

Carex foliosissima var. temnolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
