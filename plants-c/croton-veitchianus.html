Croton veitchianus

Croton veitchianus, commonly known as the Veitch's croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that display vibrant colors ranging from bright green to deep red. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
