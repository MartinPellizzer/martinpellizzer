Chionanthus roxburghii

Chionanthus roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its showy flowers and graceful form.
