Campanula trachelium subsp. mauritanica

Campanula trachelium subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
