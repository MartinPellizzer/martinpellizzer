Carex hololasius

Carex hololasius, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a distinctive basal rosette structure. Its flowering stems bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence.

Carex hololasius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
