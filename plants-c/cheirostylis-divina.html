Cheirostylis divina

Cheirostylis divina, commonly known as the divine orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
