Cereus bridgesii var. longispinus

Cereus bridgesii var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in long, sharp spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and xeriscaping enthusiasts.
