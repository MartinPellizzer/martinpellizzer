Cocos nucifera var. alangan

Cocos nucifera var. alangan, commonly known as the Alangan coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and long, pinnate leaves. The fruit, or coconut, is typically smaller compared to the common coconut and has a thinner husk. This variety is well adapted to coastal and tropical environments, thriving in sandy soils.

It is valued for its edible nuts and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
