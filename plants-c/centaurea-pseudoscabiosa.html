Centaurea pseudoscabiosa

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa, commonly known as the false scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
