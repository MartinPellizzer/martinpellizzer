Cichorium intybus subsp. spicatum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often used in salads or as a garnish.

The plant produces tall, spire-like flower heads composed of small blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

Its leaves and roots are also used in the production of coffee substitutes and herbal teas.
