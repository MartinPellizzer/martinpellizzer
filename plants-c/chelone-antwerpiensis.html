Chelone antwerpiensis

Chelone antwerpiensis, commonly known as the Antwerp bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in moist, woodland habitats. It is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Chelone antwerpiensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
