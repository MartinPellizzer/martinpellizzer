Croton anosiravensis

Croton anosiravensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and can vary in shape, often featuring a waxy surface.

Croton anosiravensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
