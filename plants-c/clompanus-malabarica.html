Clompanus malabarica

Clompanus malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
