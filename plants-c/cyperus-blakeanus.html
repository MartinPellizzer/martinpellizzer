Cyperus blakeanus

Cyperus blakeanus, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes.

Cyperus blakeanus is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
