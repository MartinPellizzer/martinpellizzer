Celosia patula

Celosia patula, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Celosia patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
