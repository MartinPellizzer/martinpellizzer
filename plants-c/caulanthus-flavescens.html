Caulanthus flavescens

Caulanthus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow stalked crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Caulanthus flavescens typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows.
