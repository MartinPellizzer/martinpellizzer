Carex forficula var. melinacra

Carex forficula var. melinacra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
