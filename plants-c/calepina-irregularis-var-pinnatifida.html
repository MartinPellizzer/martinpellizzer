Calepina irregularis var. pinnatifida

Calepina irregularis var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calepina, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
