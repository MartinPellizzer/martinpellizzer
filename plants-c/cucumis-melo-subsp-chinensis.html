Cucumis melo subsp. chinensis

Cucumis melo subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, juicy, pale yellow to white flesh.

The plant is typically herbaceous, with trailing stems and palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often cultivated for its edible flesh, which is used in various culinary applications.
