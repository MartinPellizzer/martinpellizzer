Cortusa matthioli subsp. moravica

Cortusa matthioli subsp.

moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
