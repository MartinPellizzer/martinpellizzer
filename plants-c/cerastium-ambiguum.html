Cerastium ambiguum

Cerastium ambiguum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Cerastium ambiguum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of North America.
