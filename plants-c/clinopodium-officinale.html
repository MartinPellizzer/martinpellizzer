Clinopodium officinale

Clinopodium officinale, commonly known as oregano, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Clinopodium officinale is widely cultivated for its flavor and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.
