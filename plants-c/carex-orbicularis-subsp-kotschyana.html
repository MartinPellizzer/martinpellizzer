Carex orbicularis subsp. kotschyana

Carex orbicularis subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along water edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanic research.
