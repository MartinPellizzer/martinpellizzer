Cardamine impatiens proles elongata

Cardamine impatiens proles elongata, commonly known as the elongated watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a slightly succulent texture.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible leaves in certain culinary traditions.
