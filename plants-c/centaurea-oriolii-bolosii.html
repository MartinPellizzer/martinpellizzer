Centaurea oriolii-bolosii

Centaurea oriolii-bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, providing a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
