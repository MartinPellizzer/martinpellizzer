Camphora oldhamii

Camphora oldhamii, commonly known as Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves that emit a strong camphor scent. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which have medicinal and industrial applications.
