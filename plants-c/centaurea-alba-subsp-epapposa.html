Centaurea alba subsp. epapposa

Centaurea alba subsp.

epapposa, commonly known as the white centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
