Ceiba erianthos

Ceiba erianthos, commonly known as the woolly ceiba, is a towering rainforest tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a swollen, trunk-like base that stores water. The tree produces showy, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in soft, silky fibers.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for numerous species.
