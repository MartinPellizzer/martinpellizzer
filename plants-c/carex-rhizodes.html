Carex rhizodes

Carex rhizodes, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has rhizomes that help it spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
