Clusia ellipticifolia

Clusia ellipticifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybark, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia ellipticifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
