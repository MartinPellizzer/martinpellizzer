Calamagrostis porteri var. perplexa

Calamagrostis porteri var. perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing reed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscape design.
