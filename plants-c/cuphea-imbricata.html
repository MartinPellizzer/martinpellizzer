Cuphea imbricata

Cuphea imbricata, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, red or orange flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea imbricata is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
