Cordia macrodonta

Cordia macrodonta, commonly known as the large-leaf cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
