Calamagrostis neglecta var. latifolia

Calamagrostis neglecta var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf calamagrostis, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and wide, bluish-green leaves that are often described as having a silky texture. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks. Its feathery plumes add ornamental value, especially in the fall.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
