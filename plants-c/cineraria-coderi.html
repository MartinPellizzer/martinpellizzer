Cineraria coderi

Cineraria coderi, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery or grayish appearance.

Cineraria coderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
