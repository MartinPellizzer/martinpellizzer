Cyananthus microphyllus

Cyananthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are small and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a pointed tip.

Cyananthus microphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
