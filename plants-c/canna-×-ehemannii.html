Canna × ehemannii

Canna × ehemannii, commonly known as the red banana, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Canna indica and Canna flaccida.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purple tinge along the veins. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna × ehemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.
