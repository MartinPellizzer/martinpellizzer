Clematis japonica f. purpureofusca

Clematis japonica f. purpureofusca, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis with purple stems, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, grayish-white hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
