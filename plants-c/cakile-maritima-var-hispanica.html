Cakile maritima var. hispanica

Cakile maritima var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean coast.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky coastal environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is often found in maritime ecosystems.
