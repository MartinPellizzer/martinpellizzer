Cheirostylis philippinensis

Cheirostylis philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine cheirostylis, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
