Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. teretifolius

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
