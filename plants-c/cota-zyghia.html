Cota zyghia

Cota zyghia, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Cota zyghia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
