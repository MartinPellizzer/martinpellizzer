Centaurea affinis var. lacerata

Centaurea affinis var. lacerata, commonly known as the fringed cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with frayed petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can be slightly lobed.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
