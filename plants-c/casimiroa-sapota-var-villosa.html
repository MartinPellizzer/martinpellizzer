Casimiroa sapota var. villosa

Casimiroa sapota var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that eventually develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
