Clinopodium mirum

Clinopodium mirum, commonly known as the miraculous summer savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a pungent, savory flavor to dishes.

Clinopodium mirum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
