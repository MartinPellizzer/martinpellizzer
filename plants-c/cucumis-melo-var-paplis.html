Cucumis melo var. paplis

Cucumis melo var. paplis, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon that belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruit with a thin, crisp skin that is typically light green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit. Its fruits are often used in salads and as a snack due to their refreshing taste and high water content.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for cultivation in warm climates.
