Cestrum tenuiflorum var. glabrescens

Cestrum tenuiflorum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a mild, sour taste. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its nocturnal fragrance and ornamental value.

This variety is generally resistant to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance addition to gardens.
