Carex yabei

Carex yabei, commonly known as the Yabei sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex yabei produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
