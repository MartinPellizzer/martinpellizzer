Cassia morelensis

Cassia morelensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble showers of light. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia morelensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.
