Carex bella

Carex bella, commonly known as the silvergrass or white sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Carex bella is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
