Celtis julianae var. calvescens

Celtis julianae var. calvescens, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It features smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age and elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
