Cyphia tomentosa

Cyphia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cyphia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Cyphia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
