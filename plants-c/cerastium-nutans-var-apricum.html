Cerastium nutans var. apricum

Cerastium nutans var. apricum, commonly known as the creeping snowberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
