Crocus spruneri

Crocus spruneri, commonly known as the Almond Crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant, almond-scented aroma. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
