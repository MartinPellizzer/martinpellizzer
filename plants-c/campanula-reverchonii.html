Campanula reverchonii

Campanula reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
