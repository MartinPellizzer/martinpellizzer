Carex × salisiana

Carex × salisiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex × salisiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
