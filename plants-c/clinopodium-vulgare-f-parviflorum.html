Clinopodium vulgare f. parviflorum

Clinopodium vulgare f. parviflorum, commonly known as narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clustered, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its distinct scent and potential medicinal uses.

It is often found in the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
