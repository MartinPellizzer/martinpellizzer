Copaiba rigida

Copaiba rigida, commonly known as Brazilian frankincense, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces a valuable resin, known for its aromatic and medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Copaiba rigida plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
