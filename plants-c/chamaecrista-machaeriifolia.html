Chamaecrista machaeriifolia

Chamaecrista machaeriifolia, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
