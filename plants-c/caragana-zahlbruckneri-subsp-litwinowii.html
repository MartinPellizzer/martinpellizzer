Caragana zahlbruckneri subsp. litwinowii

Caragana zahlbruckneri subsp.

litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a useful plant for landscaping in dry regions.
