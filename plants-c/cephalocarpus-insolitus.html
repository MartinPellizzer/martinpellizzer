Cephalocarpus insolitus

Cephalocarpus insolitus, commonly known as the strange-headed grass, is a unique member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-like inflorescence that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Due to its rare and unusual appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
