Celastrus laoticus

Celastrus laoticus, commonly known as the Laotian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
