Celastrus punctatus var. microphyllus

Celastrus punctatus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it its varietal name "microphyllus.". The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
