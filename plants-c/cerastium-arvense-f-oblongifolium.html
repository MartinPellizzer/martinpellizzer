Cerastium arvense f. oblongifolium

Cerastium arvense f. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has succulent, oblong leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Caryophyllaceae family.
