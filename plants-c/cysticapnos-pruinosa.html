Cysticapnos pruinosa

Cysticapnos pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from a dense layer of fine, waxy hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and striking seasonal color changes.
