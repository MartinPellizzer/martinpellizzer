Centaurea tamanianiae

Centaurea tamanianiae, commonly known as the Tamanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracts that surround its flower heads.

Centaurea tamanianiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
