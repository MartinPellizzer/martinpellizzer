Crepis aculeolata

Crepis aculeolata, commonly known as the prickly hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Crepis aculeolata is known for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
