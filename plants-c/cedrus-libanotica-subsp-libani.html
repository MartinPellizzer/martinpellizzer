Cedrus libanotica subsp. libani

Cedrus libanotica subsp.

libani, commonly known as the Lebanon cedar, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features a conical shape with horizontal branches and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature into woody structures containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient architecture and religious symbolism.
