Centaurea tagana var. africana

Centaurea tagana var. africana, commonly known as the African cornflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
