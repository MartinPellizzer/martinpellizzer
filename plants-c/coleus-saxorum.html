Coleus saxorum

Coleus saxorum, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from red to purple and green, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
