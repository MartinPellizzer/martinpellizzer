Campylocentrum neglectum var. angustifolium

Campylocentrum neglectum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mountain fig, is a shrub native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and have a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the family Euphorbiaceae.
