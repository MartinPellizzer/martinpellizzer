Carex capricornis var. latifolia

Carex capricornis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and pond edges.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
