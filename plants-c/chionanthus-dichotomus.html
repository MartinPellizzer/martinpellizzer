Chionanthus dichotomus

Chionanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the dichotomous fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite leaf arrangement, with simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
