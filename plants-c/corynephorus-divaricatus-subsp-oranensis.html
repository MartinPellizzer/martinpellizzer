Corynephorus divaricatus subsp. oranensis

Corynephorus divaricatus subsp.

oranensis, commonly known as the Oranese hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscaping in suitable climates.
