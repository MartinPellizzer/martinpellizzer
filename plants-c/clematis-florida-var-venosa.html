Clematis florida var. venosa

Clematis florida var. venosa, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive venation pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
