Crataegus sanguinea var. glabra

Crataegus sanguinea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

The smooth hawthorn is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow construction.
