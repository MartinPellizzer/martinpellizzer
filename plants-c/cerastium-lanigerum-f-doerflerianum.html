Cerastium lanigerum f. doerflerianum

Cerastium lanigerum f. doerflerianum, commonly known as the Doerfler's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a woolly texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
