Cassia edulis

Cassia edulis, commonly known as the giant senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia edulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
