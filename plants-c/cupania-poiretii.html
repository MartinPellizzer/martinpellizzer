Cupania poiretii

Cupania poiretii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible for humans and animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
