Croton francoanus

Croton francoanus, commonly known as the Franco's croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental plants. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton francoanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structures.
