Carex fecunda var. atropurpurea

Carex fecunda var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple hue along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management and wildlife habitat.
