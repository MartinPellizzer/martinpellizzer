Centrosema hastatum

Centrosema hastatum, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Centrosema hastatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
