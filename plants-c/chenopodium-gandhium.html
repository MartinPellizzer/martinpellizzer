Chenopodium gandhium

Chenopodium gandhium, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, green berries that turn orange-red when mature. The berries are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, semi-shade environments.
