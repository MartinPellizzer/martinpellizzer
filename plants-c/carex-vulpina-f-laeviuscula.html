Carex vulpina f. laeviuscula

Carex vulpina f. laeviuscula, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly curved. The plant produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
