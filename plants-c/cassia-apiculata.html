Cassia apiculata

Cassia apiculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia apiculata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
