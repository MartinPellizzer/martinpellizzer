Celtis koraiensis var. holophylla

Celtis koraiensis var. holophylla, commonly known as the Korean hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
