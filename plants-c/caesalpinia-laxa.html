Caesalpinia laxa

Caesalpinia laxa, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

Caesalpinia laxa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
