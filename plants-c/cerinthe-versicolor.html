Cerinthe versicolor

Cerinthe versicolor, commonly known as the variable cerinthe, is a member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
