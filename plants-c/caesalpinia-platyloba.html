Caesalpinia platyloba

Caesalpinia platyloba, commonly known as the flatpod senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
