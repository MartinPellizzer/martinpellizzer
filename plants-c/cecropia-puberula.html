Cecropia puberula

Cecropia puberula, commonly known as the velvet ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Cecropia puberula plays an important ecological role in providing shelter and food for various animals.
