Conchium gibbosum

Conchium gibbosum, commonly known as the cushion lichen, is a foliose lichen characterized by its leaf-like thallus that forms dense, cushion-shaped growths.

It typically grows on soil, rock, or wood in temperate and boreal regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The thallus is usually green to yellowish in color, with a smooth or slightly wrinkled surface. It consists of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner, which together carry out photosynthesis and nutrient absorption.

This lichen is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
