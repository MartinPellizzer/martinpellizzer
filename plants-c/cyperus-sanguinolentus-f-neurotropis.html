Cyperus sanguinolentus f. neurotropis

Cyperus sanguinolentus f. neurotropis, commonly known as the neurotropic flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
