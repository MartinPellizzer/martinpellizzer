Clethra euosmoda

Clethra euosmoda, commonly known as the white alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Clethra euosmoda is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its role in stabilizing soil along riverbanks.
