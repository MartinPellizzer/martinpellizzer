Chrysophyllum immersum

Chrysophyllum immersum, commonly known as the water apple or wild persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically a smooth, yellow or orange berry that ripens in clusters.
