Crepis turczaninowii

Crepis turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are hairy and form a rosette, while the flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in late summer.

Crepis turczaninowii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
