Cytisus pterocladus

Cytisus pterocladus, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, often thorny stems. The plant has small, pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cytisus pterocladus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
