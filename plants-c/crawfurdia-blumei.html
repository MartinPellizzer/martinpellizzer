Crawfurdia blumei

Crawfurdia blumei, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence composed of several yellow flowers clustered together. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Crawfurdia blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
