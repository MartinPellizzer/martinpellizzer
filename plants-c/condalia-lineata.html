Condalia lineata

Condalia lineata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its latex, which is milky and can be toxic if ingested.
