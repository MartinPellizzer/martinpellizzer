Cyrtocarpa edulis

Cyrtocarpa edulis, commonly known as the edible cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its reproductive structures, including cones, are typical of cycads, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

The plant is notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for centuries in its natural habitat.
