Calamus amischus

Calamus amischus, commonly known as the slender reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Calamus amischus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
