Conophytum truncatum subsp. viridicatum

Conophytum truncatum subsp.

viridicatum, commonly known as the green-flowered conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water.

The plant produces small, bright green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
