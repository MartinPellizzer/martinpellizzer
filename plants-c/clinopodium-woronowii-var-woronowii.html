Clinopodium woronowii var. woronowii

Clinopodium woronowii var. woronowii, commonly known as Russian sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a minty scent, and its flowering spikes produce showy, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
