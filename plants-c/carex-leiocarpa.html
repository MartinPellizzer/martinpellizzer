Carex leiocarpa

Carex leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

Carex leiocarpa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
