Callicarpa japonica f. latifolia

Callicarpa japonica f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese beautyberry, is a cultivar of the beautyberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval leaves that are broader than those of the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
