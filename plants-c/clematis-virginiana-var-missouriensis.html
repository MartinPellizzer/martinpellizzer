Clematis virginiana var. missouriensis

Clematis virginiana var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the central United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a bluish-green hue and hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
