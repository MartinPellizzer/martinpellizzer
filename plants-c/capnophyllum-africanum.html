Capnophyllum africanum

Capnophyllum africanum, commonly known as the African candleplant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, candle-like stems that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Capnophyllum africanum is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
