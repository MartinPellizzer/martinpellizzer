Ceropegia dammanniana

Ceropegia dammanniana, commonly known as the Madagascar moonvine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it thrives in warm, humid conditions with well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to climb.
