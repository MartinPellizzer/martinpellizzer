Carex canescens var. bracteata

Carex canescens var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
