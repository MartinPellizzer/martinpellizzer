Corypha australis

Corypha australis, commonly known as the Australian fan palm, is a tropical plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Corypha australis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
