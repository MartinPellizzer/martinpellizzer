Cosmos atrosanguineus

Cosmos atrosanguineus, commonly known as black sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Cosmos atrosanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
