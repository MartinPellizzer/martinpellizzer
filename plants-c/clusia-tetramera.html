Clusia tetramera

Clusia tetramera, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia tetramera is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution.
