Calamagrostis orizabae

Calamagrostis orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green blades that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Calamagrostis orizabae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
