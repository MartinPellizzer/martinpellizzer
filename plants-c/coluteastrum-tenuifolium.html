Coluteastrum tenuifolium

Coluteastrum tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved colutea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It typically grows in dry, grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
