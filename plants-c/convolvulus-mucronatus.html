Convolvulus mucronatus

Convolvulus mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dry seeds.
