Cyperus ligularis var. spicatocapitatus

Cyperus ligularis var. spicatocapitatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense, spike-like clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
