Cornus stolonifera var. splendens

Cornus stolonifera var. splendens, commonly known as the silver leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features striking silver-white variegated foliage that contrasts beautifully with its deep green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
