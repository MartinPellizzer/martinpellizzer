Cypripedium × lemoinierianum

Cypripedium × lemoinierianum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium micranthum.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pouch-like flowers that are typically pink or white with a contrasting lip. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a thick, fleshy rhizome and broad, leathery leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in orchid cultivation.
