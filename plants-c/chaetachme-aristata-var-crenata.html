Chaetachme aristata var. crenata

Chaetachme aristata var. crenata, commonly known as the crested chaetachme, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often bear a distinctive crest of small, upward-facing leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.
