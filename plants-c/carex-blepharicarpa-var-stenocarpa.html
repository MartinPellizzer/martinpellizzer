Carex blepharicarpa var. stenocarpa

Carex blepharicarpa var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-capsule sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

The variety stenocarpa is distinguished by its narrower, more elongated capsules compared to the typical form.
