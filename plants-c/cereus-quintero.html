Cereus quintero

Cereus quintero, commonly known as the Quintero's candleplant, is a flowering cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
