Cassia santanderensis var. parvianthera

Cassia santanderensis var. parvianthera, commonly known as the dwarf anthera cassia, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
