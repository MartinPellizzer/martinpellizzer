Castalia odorata var. latifolia

Castalia odorata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved castalia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
