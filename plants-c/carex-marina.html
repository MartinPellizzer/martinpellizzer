Carex marina

Carex marina, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in brackish or saltwater environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and cylindrical in shape. Its flowering spike is usually short and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex marina plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
