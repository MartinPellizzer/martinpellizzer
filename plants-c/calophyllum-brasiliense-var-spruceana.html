Calophyllum brasiliense var. spruceana

Calophyllum brasiliense var. spruceana, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown to black bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local communities.
