Cautleya gracilis var. hispida

Cautleya gracilis var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy caudiciform orchid, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, caudiciform stem that often appears as a thickened underground rhizome. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
