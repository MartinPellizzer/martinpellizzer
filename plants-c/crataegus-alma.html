Crataegus alma

Crataegus alma, commonly known as the Alma hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally not consumed by humans. Crataegus alma typically grows in woodlands and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
