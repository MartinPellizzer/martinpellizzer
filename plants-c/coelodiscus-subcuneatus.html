Coelodiscus subcuneatus

Coelodiscus subcuneatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, cylindrical fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Coelodiscus subcuneatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
