Clinopodium barosmum

Clinopodium barosmum, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, pink or white flowers are arranged in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium barosmum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
