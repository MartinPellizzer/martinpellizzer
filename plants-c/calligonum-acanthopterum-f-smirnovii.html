Calligonum acanthopterum f. smirnovii

Calligonum acanthopterum f. smirnovii, commonly known as the Smirnov's spiny saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and high temperatures, which makes it suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
