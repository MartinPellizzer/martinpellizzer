Crataegus polyacantha

Crataegus polyacantha, commonly known as the manythorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus polyacantha is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
