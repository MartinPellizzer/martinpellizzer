Chlorocyperus iria

Chlorocyperus iria, commonly known as the blue grass sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or bluish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Chlorocyperus iria plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
