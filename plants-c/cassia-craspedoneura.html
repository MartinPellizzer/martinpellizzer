Cassia craspedoneura

Cassia craspedoneura, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color. Cassia craspedoneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
