Clusia hoffmannseggiana

Clusia hoffmannseggiana, commonly known as Hoffmannsegg's glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is well adapted to coastal and humid environments, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Clusia hoffmannseggiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and persistent flowers.
