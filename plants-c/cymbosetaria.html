Cymbosetaria

Cymbosetaria, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It consists of several species that are typically small, herbaceous plants with simple, alternate leaves. The flowers are usually small and consist of four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family. These plants are often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
