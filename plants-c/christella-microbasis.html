Christella microbasis

Christella microbasis, commonly known as the narrowleaf Christella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters at the base of the plant.

It is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.
