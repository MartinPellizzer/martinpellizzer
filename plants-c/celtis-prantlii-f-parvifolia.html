Celtis prantlii f. parvifolia

Celtis prantlii f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hackberry, is a flowering plant in the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
