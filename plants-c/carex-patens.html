Carex patens

Carex patens, commonly known as the spreading sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, followed by three-angled berries.

Carex patens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
