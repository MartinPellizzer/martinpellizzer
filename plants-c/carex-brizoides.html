Carex brizoides

Carex brizoides, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils, such as those found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex brizoides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
