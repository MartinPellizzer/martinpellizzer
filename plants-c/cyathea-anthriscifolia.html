Cyathea anthriscifolia

Cyathea anthriscifolia, commonly known as the fern with the appearance of a broad-leaved plant, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a distinctive, somewhat triangular shape.

Cyathea anthriscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
