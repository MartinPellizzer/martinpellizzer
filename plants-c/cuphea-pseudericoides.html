Cuphea pseudericoides

Cuphea pseudericoides, commonly known as the false firecracker, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique flower structure.
