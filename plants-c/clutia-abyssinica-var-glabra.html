Clutia abyssinica var. glabra

Clutia abyssinica var. glabra, commonly known as African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
