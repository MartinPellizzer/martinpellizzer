Callisia navicularis

Callisia navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Callisia navicularis is a perennial species that is relatively easy to care for in gardens with appropriate conditions.
