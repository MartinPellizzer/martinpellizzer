Crantzia tigrina

Crantzia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger's eye plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, hairy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble the pattern of a tiger's eye. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crantzia tigrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
