Cistus × florentinus f. adriaticus

Cistus × florentinus f. adriaticus, commonly known as the Adriatic rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for coastal and arid environments. Its leaves are typically grey-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are often clustered in terminal umbels.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging landscapes.
