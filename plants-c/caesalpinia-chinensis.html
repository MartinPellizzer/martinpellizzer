Caesalpinia chinensis

Caesalpinia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets, and its distinctive bright red or orange floral spikes that appear at the base of the branches. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and public spaces.
