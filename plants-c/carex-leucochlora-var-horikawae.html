Carex leucochlora var. horikawae

Carex leucochlora var. horikawae, commonly known as the Japanese white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
