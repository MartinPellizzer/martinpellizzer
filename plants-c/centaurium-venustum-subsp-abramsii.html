Centaurium venustum subsp. abramsii

Centaurium venustum subsp.

abramsii, commonly known as Abram's centaury, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and capsule-like.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
