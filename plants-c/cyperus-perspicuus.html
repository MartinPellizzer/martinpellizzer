Cyperus perspicuus

Cyperus perspicuus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus perspicuus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
