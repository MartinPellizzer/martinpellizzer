Cousinia curviloba

Cousinia curviloba, commonly known as the zigzag gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often curling, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cousinia curviloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
