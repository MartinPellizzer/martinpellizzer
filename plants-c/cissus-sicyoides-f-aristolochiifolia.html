Cissus sicyoides f. aristolochiifolia

Cissus sicyoides f. aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the aristolochiifolia cissus, is a unique variant of the genus Cissus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the genus Aristolochia, giving it its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to surfaces and grow vertically. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and sometimes pink, making it an ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
