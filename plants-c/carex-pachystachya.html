Carex pachystachya

Carex pachystachya, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, cylindrical spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet to moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex pachystachya plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
