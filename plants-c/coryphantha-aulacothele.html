Coryphantha aulacothele

Coryphantha aulacothele, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cactus, is a species native to the deserts of North America.

It is a small, spineless cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of pale green or yellow flowers. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the arid environment. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, edible fruits that are a food source for local wildlife.

This plant is well-suited to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water to thrive.
