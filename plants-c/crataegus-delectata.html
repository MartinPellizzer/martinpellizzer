Crataegus delectata

Crataegus delectata, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a species of hawthorn native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features thorny branches, simple ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
