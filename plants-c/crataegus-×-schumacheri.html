Crataegus × schumacheri

Crataegus × schumacheri, commonly known as the Schumacher hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–10 meters. The plant features thorned branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
