Cassia zygophylloides

Cassia zygophylloides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cassia zygophylloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
