Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. aristulata

Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. aristulata, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
