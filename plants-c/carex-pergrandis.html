Carex pergrandis

Carex pergrandis, commonly known as the large flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Carex pergrandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
