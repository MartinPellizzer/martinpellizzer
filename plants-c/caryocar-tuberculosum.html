Caryocar tuberculosum

Caryocar tuberculosum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or the baleia, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
