Crataegus sieboldii

Crataegus sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
