Cyrtochilum × monacranthum

Cyrtochilum × monacranthum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Cyrtochilum species.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, typically pink, red, or orange, with a distinctive labellum. It thrives in warm, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
