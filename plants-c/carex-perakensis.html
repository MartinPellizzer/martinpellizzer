Carex perakensis

Carex perakensis, commonly known as the Perak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex perakensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
