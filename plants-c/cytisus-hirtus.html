Cytisus hirtus

Cytisus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features woody stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and hairy, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Cytisus hirtus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
