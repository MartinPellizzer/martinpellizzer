Celtis laevigata var. apposita

Celtis laevigata var. apposita, commonly known as the smoothleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by yellowish berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The leaves are typically 5-9 lobed, with a smooth margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
