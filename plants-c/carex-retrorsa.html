Carex retrorsa

Carex retrorsa, commonly known as the reverse beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carex retrorsa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
