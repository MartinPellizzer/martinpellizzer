Caragana czetyrkinii

Caragana czetyrkinii, commonly known as the four-leaf caragana, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of four to six leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
