Calyptrogyne kalbreyeri

Calyptrogyne kalbreyeri, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped stems and dense clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it a notable species in coastal ecosystems. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Calyptrogyne kalbreyeri plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
