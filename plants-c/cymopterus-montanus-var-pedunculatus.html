Cymopterus montanus var. pedunculatus

Cymopterus montanus var. pedunculatus, commonly known as the mountain eveningflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers open in the late afternoon, hence its common name, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
