Cactus phyllanthus

Cactus phyllanthus, commonly known as the desert pear, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible berry that contains a high concentration of nutrients.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique adaptations, it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in desert communities.
