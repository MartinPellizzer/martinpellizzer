Convolvulus villosus

Convolvulus villosus, commonly known as hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
