Campanula parryi var. idahoensis

Campanula parryi var. idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Idaho and Montana.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 7,000 feet. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and may have a slight bluish tinge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
