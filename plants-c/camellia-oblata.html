Camellia oblata

Camellia oblata, commonly known as the mountain camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and red.

Camellia oblata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
