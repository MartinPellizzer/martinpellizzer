Cnicus iowensis

Cnicus iowensis, commonly known as the Iowa thistle, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are yellow, with ray florets that bloom in the summer.

Cnicus iowensis is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining native plant communities.
