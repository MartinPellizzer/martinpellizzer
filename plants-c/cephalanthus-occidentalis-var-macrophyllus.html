Cephalanthus occidentalis var. macrophyllus

Cephalanthus occidentalis var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, spherical flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface and a serrated margin. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
