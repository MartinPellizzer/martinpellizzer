Cytisus austriacus f. obtusifolius

Cytisus austriacus f. obtusifolius, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
