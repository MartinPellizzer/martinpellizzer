Crataegus compacta

Crataegus compacta, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus compacta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
