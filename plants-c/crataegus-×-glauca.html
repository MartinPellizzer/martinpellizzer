Crataegus × glauca

Crataegus × glauca, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments.
