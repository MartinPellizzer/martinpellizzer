Citrus × aurantium var. proper

Citrus × aurantium var. proper, commonly known as the common orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia and widely cultivated for its edible fruit. The plant is a small evergreen tree with dark green, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, typically orange in color, contains a segmented juicy pulp surrounded by a thick, aromatic peel.

The plant is valued for its culinary uses, medicinal properties, and ornamental appeal.
