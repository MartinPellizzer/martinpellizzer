Chamaesyce mauritiana

Chamaesyce mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

Chamaesyce mauritiana is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.
