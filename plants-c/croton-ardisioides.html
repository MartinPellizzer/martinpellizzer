Croton ardisioides

Croton ardisioides, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and silver. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red or orange berries.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant leaf coloration and adaptability to various growing conditions.
