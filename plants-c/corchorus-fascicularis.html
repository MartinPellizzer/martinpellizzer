Corchorus fascicularis

Corchorus fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kochia, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Corchorus fascicularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
