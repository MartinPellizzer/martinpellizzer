Carduus verdii

Carduus verdii, commonly known as the green thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple or pink flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Carduus verdii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
