Cyperus pohlianus

Cyperus pohlianus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the top of the stems. Cyperus pohlianus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
