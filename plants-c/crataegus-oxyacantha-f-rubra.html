Crataegus oxyacantha f. rubra

Crataegus oxyacantha f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features thorny branches, small ovate leaves, and produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
