Cassia castiglionia

Cassia castiglionia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Cassia castiglionia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
