Croixia semaram

Croixia semaram, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
