Carpodinus baumannii

Carpodinus baumannii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a star-like appearance, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
