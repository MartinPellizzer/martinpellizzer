Cyclamen coum var. ibericum

Cyclamen coum var. ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces showy, bowl-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas. It has a corm-like tuberous root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Cyclamen coum var. ibericum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
