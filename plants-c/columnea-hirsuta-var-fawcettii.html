Columnea hirsuta var. fawcettii

Columnea hirsuta var. fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's hairy columnea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or shaded gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among horticultural enthusiasts.
