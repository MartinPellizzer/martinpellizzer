Columnea capitata

Columnea capitata, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Columnea capitata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its striking floral display.
