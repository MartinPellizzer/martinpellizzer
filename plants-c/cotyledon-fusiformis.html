Cotyledon fusiformis

Cotyledon fusiformis, commonly known as the football plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or football-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant has a short, thick stem and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

The leaves can change color in response to light, often displaying striking red or purple hues.
