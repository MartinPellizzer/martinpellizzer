Carex ericetorum var. alpicola

Carex ericetorum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous areas, often in rocky or peaty habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
