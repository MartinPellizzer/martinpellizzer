Colutea armena

Colutea armena, commonly known as Armenian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or woodland areas. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Colutea armena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
