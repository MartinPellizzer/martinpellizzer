Cassinia fulvida var. montana

Cassinia fulvida var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cassinia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Cassinia fulvida var. montana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
