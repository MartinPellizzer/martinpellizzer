Ceratostema lobbii

Ceratostema lobbii, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ceratostema lobbii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
