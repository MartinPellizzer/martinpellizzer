Capparis laotica

Capparis laotica, commonly known as the Laotian caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various species known for their distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with prominent petals and stamens, and it produces small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
