Cyperus diffusus f. turgidulus

Cyperus diffusus f. turgidulus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a variant of the Cyperus genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowering spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a common indicator of aquatic habitats.
