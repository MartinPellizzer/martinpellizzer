Carex adumana

Carex adumana, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, Carex adumana typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
