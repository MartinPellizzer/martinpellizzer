Codia obcordata

Codia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf codia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Codia obcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
