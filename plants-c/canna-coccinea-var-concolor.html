Canna coccinea var. concolor

Canna coccinea var. concolor, commonly known as the red ginger or scarlet canna, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, brightly colored leaves and showy, spadix-like flowers.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is native to Central and South America. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, while its inflorescence consists of a red or orange spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Canna coccinea var. concolor is relatively low maintenance, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
