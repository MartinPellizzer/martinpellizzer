Cyrtochilum lucescens

Cyrtochilum lucescens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Cyrtochilum lucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
