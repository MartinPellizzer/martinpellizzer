Chaenomeles japonica var. tricolor

Chaenomeles japonica var. tricolor, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces edible fruits, though they are typically tart and not commonly consumed fresh. Its foliage is dark green and provides good autumn color, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
