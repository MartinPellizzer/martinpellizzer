Crucifera odorata

Crucifera odorata, commonly known as the fragrant crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Crucifera odorata is native to certain regions of Asia and is valued for its aromatic properties.
