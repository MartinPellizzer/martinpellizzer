Calothamnus montanus

Calothamnus montanus, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often displaying a striking contrast with its silver-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Calothamnus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.
