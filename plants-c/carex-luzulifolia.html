Carex luzulifolia

Carex luzulifolia, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, blue-gray leaves that give it a striking appearance.

Carex luzulifolia produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often pollinated by insects.
