Crassula corallina subsp. corallina

Crassula corallina subsp.

corallina, commonly known as the coral pine or red crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
