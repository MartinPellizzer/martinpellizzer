Chamaesyce celastroides var. laehiensis

Chamaesyce celastroides var. laehiensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
