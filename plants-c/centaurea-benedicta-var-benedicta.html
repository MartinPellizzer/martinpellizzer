Centaurea benedicta var. benedicta

Centaurea benedicta var. benedicta, commonly known as the blessed thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
