Citrosma amazonum

Citrosma amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian citron tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
