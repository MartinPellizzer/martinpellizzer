Clerodendrum longiflorum var. pubescens

Clerodendrum longiflorum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that typically display shades of pink to red. The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its name. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a tendency to spread through vegetative propagation.
