Cochlearia amana

Cochlearia amana, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Cochlearia amana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine habitats.
