Cercocarpus traskiae

Cercocarpus traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cercocarpus traskiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
