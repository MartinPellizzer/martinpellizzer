Chloroides petraeum

Chloroides petraeum, commonly known as the rock liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically on rocky substrates. The plant has a distinctive structure with leafy shoots that are often clustered closely together. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are borne on short, erect stalks.

Chloroides petraeum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
