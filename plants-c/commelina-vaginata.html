Commelina vaginata

Commelina vaginata, commonly known as the purple arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps retain moisture in humid conditions.

Commelina vaginata is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
