Centaurea nigrescens

Centaurea nigrescens, commonly known as the black centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea nigrescens is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
