Claudia ciliata

Claudia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Claudia ciliata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
