Carex paleacea f. neopaleacea

Carex paleacea f. neopaleacea, commonly known as the pale sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
