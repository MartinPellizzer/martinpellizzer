Carex nubigena var. albata

Carex nubigena var. albata, commonly known as the white-flowered sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus, characterized by its distinctive white inflorescence.

This plant is a perennial sedge that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue. The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears white or pale yellow flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
