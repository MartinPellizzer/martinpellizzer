Codonopsis

Codonopsis, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or "dang shen," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

It consists of several species, with Codonopsis pilosula being the most well-known. These plants are native to East Asia, particularly China, and are characterized by their tuberous roots. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Codonopsis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including enhancing vitality and supporting the immune system.
