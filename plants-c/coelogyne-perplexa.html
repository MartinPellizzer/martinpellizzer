Coelogyne perplexa

Coelogyne perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern on a raceme. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette formation, aiding in water retention.

Coelogyne perplexa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
