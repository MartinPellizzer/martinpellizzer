Citrus × pumila

Citrus × pumila, commonly known as the dwarf lemon.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its rounded leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. Its fruit is highly aromatic and contains a high concentration of citric acid, making it valuable for culinary and medicinal uses.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
