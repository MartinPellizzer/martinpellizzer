Cleisocratera elegans

Cleisocratera elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
