Commelina dielsii

Commelina dielsii, commonly known as the blue water hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its roots are often submerged, and it can form dense mats on the water surface.

Commelina dielsii is also known for its ability to absorb heavy metals from the water, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
