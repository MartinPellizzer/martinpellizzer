Cirsium tempskyanum

Cirsium tempskyanum, commonly known as the Tempskyan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
