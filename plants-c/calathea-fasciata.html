Calathea fasciata

Calathea fasciata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves with contrasting patterns of green, white, and silver, which are often described as resembling a zebra's stripes. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Calathea fasciata requires indirect light, consistent moisture, and well-draining soil to flourish.
