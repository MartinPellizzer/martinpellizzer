Cyathea monosticha

Cyathea monosticha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a smooth, grayish trunk that lacks a true stem, and its fronds are usually arranged in a spiral pattern. Cyathea monosticha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to the structure of its native ecosystems.
