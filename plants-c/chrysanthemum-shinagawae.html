Chrysanthemum shinagawae

Chrysanthemum shinagawae, commonly known as the Shikoku chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in traditional Japanese gardens.
