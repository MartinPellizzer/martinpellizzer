Carex chinensis var. chinensis

Carex chinensis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
