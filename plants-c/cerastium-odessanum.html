Cerastium odessanum

Cerastium odessanum, commonly known as the Odessa chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, and it often forms dense mats on the ground.

Cerastium odessanum is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a common sight in alpine and coastal environments.
