Carex tribuloides var. turbata

Carex tribuloides var. turbata, commonly known as the Turbata sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
