Caltha himalayanus

Caltha himalayanus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, erect stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
