Campanula linifolia var. hirta

Campanula linifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that are softly hairy, particularly along the veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
