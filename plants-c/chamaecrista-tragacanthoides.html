Chamaecrista tragacanthoides

Chamaecrista tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring compound leaves with 8–12 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
