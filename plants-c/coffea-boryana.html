Coffea boryana

Coffea boryana, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and white, tubular flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. It thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is often used in specialty coffee blends.
