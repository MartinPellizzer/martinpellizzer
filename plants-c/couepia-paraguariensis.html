Couepia paraguariensis

Couepia paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan coffee tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elliptical, reddish-brown fruits containing a single seed. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
