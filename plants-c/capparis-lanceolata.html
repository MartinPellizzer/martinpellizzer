Capparis lanceolata

Capparis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Capparis lanceolata is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their unique floral structures.
