Chamaesaracha nana

Chamaesaracha nana, commonly known as the dwarf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep roots and minimal water requirements.

Chamaesaracha nana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
