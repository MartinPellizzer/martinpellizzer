Crepidorhopalon chironioides

Crepidorhopalon chironioides, commonly known as the golden-eyed orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble small, intricate eyes. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, enhancing its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
