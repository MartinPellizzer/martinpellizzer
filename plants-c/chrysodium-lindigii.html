Chrysodium lindigii

Chrysodium lindigii, commonly known as the Lindig's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in open, dry habitats.

Chrysodium lindigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
