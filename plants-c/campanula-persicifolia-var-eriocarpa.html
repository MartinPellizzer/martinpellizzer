Campanula persicifolia var. eriocarpa

Campanula persicifolia var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine, velvety down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
