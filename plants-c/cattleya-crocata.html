Cattleya crocata

Cattleya crocata, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya crocata is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
