Cephalophyllum aureorubrum

Cephalophyllum aureorubrum, commonly known as the golden red-headed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, flattened, disc-like stems. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
