Calamus prasinus

Calamus prasinus, commonly known as the green clubmoss, is a member of the family Micropteridaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as strobili, are borne on the upper parts of the stems.

Calamus prasinus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
