Calamagrostis subspicata

Calamagrostis subspicata, commonly known as the silvergrass or tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
