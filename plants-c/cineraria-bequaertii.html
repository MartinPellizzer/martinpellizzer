Cineraria bequaertii

Cineraria bequaertii, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a silvery appearance.

Cineraria bequaertii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
