Carex madagascariensis

Carex madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan sedge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
