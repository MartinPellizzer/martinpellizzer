Carex stricta var. decora

Carex stricta var. decora, commonly known as the showy sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, glossy appearance, and it thrives in full to partial sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
