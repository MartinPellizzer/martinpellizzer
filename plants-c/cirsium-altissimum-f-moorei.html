Cirsium altissimum f. moorei

Cirsium altissimum f. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's thistle, is a variant of the tall thistle species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
