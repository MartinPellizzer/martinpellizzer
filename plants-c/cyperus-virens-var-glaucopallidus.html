Cyperus virens var. glaucopallidus

Cyperus virens var. glaucopallidus, commonly known as the pale bluish flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
