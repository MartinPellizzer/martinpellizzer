Calea angustifolia

Calea angustifolia, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue or violet flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
