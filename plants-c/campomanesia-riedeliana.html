Campomanesia riedeliana

Campomanesia riedeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a waxy coating similar to that of the wax apple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
