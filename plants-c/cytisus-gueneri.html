Cytisus gueneri

Cytisus gueneri, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus gueneri is adapted to mild, Mediterranean climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
