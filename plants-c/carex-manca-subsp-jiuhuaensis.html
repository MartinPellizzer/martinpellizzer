Carex manca subsp. jiuhuaensis

Carex manca subsp.

jiuhuaensis, commonly known as the Jiuhua sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
