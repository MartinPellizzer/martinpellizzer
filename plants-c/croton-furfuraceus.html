Croton furfuraceus

Croton furfuraceus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
