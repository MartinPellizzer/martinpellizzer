Chenopodium coronopus

Chenopodium coronopus, commonly known as common knotgrass, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Chenopodium coronopus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
