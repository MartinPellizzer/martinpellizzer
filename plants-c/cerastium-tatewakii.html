Cerastium tatewakii

Cerastium tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, rounded leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
