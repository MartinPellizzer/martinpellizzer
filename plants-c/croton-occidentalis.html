Croton occidentalis

Croton occidentalis, commonly known as the western croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, yellow, and orange, which are due to the presence of various pigments such as anthocyanins and carotenoids. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Croton occidentalis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
