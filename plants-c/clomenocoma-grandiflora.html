Clomenocoma grandiflora

Clomenocoma grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flameflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Clomenocoma grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
