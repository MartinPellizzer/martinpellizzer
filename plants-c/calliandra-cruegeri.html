Calliandra cruegeri

Calliandra cruegeri, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Calliandra cruegeri is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy flowers and drought tolerance.
