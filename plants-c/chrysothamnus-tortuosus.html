Chrysothamnus tortuosus

Chrysothamnus tortuosus, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chrysothamnus tortuosus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
