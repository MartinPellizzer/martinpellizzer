Convolvulus ternatus

Convolvulus ternatus, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar vine-like structures.

Convolvulus ternatus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
