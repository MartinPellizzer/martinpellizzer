Crassula diffusa

Crassula diffusa, commonly known as the fairy floss plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in response to sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula diffusa is popular as a houseplant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.
