Carex elachycarpa

Carex elachycarpa, commonly known as the slender-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its fruiting structures, called achenes, are small and three-angled, often with a short beak.

Carex elachycarpa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
