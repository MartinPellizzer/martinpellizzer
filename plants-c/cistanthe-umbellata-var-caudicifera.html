Cistanthe umbellata var. caudicifera

Cistanthe umbellata var. caudicifera, commonly known as the desert cistanthe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often grown in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry climates.
