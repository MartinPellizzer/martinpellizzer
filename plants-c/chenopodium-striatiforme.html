Chenopodium striatiforme

Chenopodium striatiforme, commonly known as the striped goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with distinct, wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used as a food source by birds and small mammals. Chenopodium striatiforme thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

It is also known for its tolerance to poor soil conditions and drought.
