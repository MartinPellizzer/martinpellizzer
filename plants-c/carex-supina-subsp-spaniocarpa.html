Carex supina subsp. spaniocarpa

Carex supina subsp.

spaniocarpa, commonly known as the spreading-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and the subsp. spaniocarpa is distinguished by its spreading, elongated fruits.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
