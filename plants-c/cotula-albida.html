Cotula albida

Cotula albida, commonly known as white groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant features a fibrous root system and hairy stems, and its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. Cotula albida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
