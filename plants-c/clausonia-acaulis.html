Clausonia acaulis

Clausonia acaulis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, short-stemmed inflorescence.

Clausonia acaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
