Cynanchum ambovombense

Cynanchum ambovombense, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. It is often found in moist, forested areas and is adapted to thrive in the tropical climate of its native region.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
