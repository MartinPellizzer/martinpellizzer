Clusia multiflora subsp. scariosepala

Clusia multiflora subsp.

scariosepala, commonly known as the scarious-sepaled clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas.

The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually green or yellow, and it produces elliptical, yellow or orange fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
