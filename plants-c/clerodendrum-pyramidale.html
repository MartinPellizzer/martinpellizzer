Clerodendrum pyramidale

Clerodendrum pyramidale, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
