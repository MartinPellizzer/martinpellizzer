Carduus stenolepis

Carduus stenolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value as a food source for certain insects.
