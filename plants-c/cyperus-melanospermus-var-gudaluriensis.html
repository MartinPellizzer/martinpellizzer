Cyperus melanospermus var. gudaluriensis

Cyperus melanospermus var. gudaluriensis, commonly known as the Gudaluri sedge, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are characteristic of the genus Cyperus.
