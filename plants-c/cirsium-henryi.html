Cirsium henryi

Cirsium henryi, commonly known as Henry's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
