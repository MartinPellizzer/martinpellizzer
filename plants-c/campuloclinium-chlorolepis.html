Campuloclinium chlorolepis

Campuloclinium chlorolepis, commonly known as the greenleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense terminal panicles, and are typically pink to purple in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local pollinators.
