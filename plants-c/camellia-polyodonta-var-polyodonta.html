Camellia polyodonta var. polyodonta

Camellia polyodonta var. polyodonta, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partially shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
