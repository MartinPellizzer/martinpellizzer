Chionothrix hyposericea

Chionothrix hyposericea, commonly known as the snowbell plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
