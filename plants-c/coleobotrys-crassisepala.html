Coleobotrys crassisepala

Coleobotrys crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. Its sepals are notably broad and fleshy, contributing to its common name.

Coleobotrys crassisepala is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
