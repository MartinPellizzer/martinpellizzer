Cyperus fuscus

Cyperus fuscus, commonly known as the brown flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, often forming dense mats near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus fuscus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments.
