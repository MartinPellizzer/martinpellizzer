Cassia lindleyana

Cassia lindleyana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a glossy surface and has a rapid growth rate. Cassia lindleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.
