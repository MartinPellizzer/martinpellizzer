Crotalaria acapulcensis

Crotalaria acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco spurred cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain several seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
