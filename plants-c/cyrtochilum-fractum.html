Cyrtochilum fractum

Cyrtochilum fractum, commonly known as the broken-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in a raceme. Cyrtochilum fractum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
