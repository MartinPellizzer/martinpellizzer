Cardamomum pininga

Cardamomum pininga, commonly known as the Pininga cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces aromatic, spindle-shaped pods containing dark, peppery seeds used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
