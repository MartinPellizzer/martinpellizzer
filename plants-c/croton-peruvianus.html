Croton peruvianus

Croton peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or even purple. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
