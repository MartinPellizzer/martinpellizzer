Crupina vulgaris var. typica

Crupina vulgaris var. typica, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its hardy nature allows it to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

It is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize dunes.
