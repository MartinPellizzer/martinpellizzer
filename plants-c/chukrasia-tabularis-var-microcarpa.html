Chukrasia tabularis var. microcarpa

Chukrasia tabularis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-colored inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide dense foliage. Its small, round fruits are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
