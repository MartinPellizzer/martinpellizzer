Cuviera latior

Cuviera latior, commonly known as the broad-leafed cuviera, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cuviera latior is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
