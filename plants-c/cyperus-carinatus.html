Cyperus carinatus

Cyperus carinatus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus carinatus is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist conditions.
