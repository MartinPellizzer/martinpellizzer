Calatola columbiana

Calatola columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a distinct spur.

Calatola columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
