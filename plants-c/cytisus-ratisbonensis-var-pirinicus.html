Cytisus ratisbonensis var. pirinicus

Cytisus ratisbonensis var. pirinicus, commonly known as the Pirin broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
