Camelina sativa var. sublinicola

Camelina sativa var. sublinicola, commonly known as the narrow-leafed camelina, is a variety of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. It is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in food and industrial applications.

The variety is also valued for its adaptability and potential as a sustainable crop in agricultural systems.
