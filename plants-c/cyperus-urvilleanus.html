Cyperus urvilleanus

Cyperus urvilleanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus urvilleanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
