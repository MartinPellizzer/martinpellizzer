Chenopodium chaldoranicum

Chenopodium chaldoranicum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a source of edible leaves.
