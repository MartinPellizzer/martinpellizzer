Coleus erythraeae

Coleus erythraeae, commonly known as the red-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its smaller, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance, it is also popular in horticultural displays and botanical collections.
