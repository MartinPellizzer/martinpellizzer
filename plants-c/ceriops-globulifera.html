Ceriops globulifera

Ceriops globulifera, commonly known as the mangrove buttonwood, is a tropical tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Rhizophoraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, woody fruits that contain a single seed. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often forming dense mangrove forests. Its roots help stabilize shorelines and provide habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
