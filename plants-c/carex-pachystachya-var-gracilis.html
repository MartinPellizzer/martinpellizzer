Carex pachystachya var. gracilis

Carex pachystachya var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
