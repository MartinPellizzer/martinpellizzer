Calea caroliniana

Calea caroliniana, commonly known as the "Mexican widow's tears," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy flowers that resemble tears, which appear in clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
