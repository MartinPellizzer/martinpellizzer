Crataegus flava var. pubescens

Crataegus flava var. pubescens, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and orange-red fruits, which are typical of the Crataegus genus. The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture, especially on the underside, giving it the "pubescens" variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
