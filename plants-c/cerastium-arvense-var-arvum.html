Cerastium arvense var. arvum

Cerastium arvense var. arvum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features prostrate stems that produce small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are spoon-shaped, hairy, and arranged in pairs along the stems, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
