Crocus sulphureus

Crocus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season insects.
