Calea sublantanoides

Calea sublantanoides, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea sublantanoides is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its calming effects.
