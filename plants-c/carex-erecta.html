Carex erecta

Carex erecta, commonly known as upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex erecta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
