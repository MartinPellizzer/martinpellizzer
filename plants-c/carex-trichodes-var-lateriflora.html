Carex trichodes var. lateriflora

Carex trichodes var. lateriflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variant is distinguished by its laterally facing flowers, which give it its specific epithet "lateriflora.". It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along streambanks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
