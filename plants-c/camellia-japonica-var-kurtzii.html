Camellia japonica var. kurtzii

Camellia japonica var. kurtzii, commonly known as the Kurtz's camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter flowering.
