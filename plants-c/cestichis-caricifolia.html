Cestichis caricifolia

Cestichis caricifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-tasting fruits. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
