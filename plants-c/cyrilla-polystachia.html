Cyrilla polystachia

Cyrilla polystachia, commonly known as the Florida rosewood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and swamps. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Cyrilla polystachia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
