Campanula latisepala f. alba

Campanula latisepala f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It has oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
