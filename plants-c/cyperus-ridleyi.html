Cyperus ridleyi

Cyperus ridleyi, commonly known as the ridley flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swampy areas and seasonally flooded soils. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, leafless stalks, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus ridleyi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
