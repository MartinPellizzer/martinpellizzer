Caesalpinia laxiflora var. pubescens

Caesalpinia laxiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crown flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a distinctive crown-like arrangement. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
