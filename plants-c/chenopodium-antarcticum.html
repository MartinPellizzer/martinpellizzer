Chenopodium antarcticum

Chenopodium antarcticum, commonly known as Antarctic goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing herb that thrives in harsh environments such as Antarctica, South America, and southern Africa. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Chenopodium antarcticum is adapted to survive extreme cold and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.
