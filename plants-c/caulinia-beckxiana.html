Caulinia beckxiana

Caulinia beckxiana, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, violet flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are edible but can be mildly toxic.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
