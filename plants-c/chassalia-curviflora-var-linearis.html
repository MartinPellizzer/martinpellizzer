Chassalia curviflora var. linearis

Chassalia curviflora var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chassalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.
