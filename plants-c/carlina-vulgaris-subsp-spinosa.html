Carlina vulgaris subsp. spinosa

Carlina vulgaris subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the spiny carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and large, showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with coarse teeth and may have a fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
