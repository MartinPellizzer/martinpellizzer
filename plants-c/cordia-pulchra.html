Cordia pulchra

Cordia pulchra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-colored bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, round fruits containing several seeds. Cordia pulchra thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinct scent.
