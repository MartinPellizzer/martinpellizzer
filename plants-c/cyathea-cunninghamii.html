Cyathea cunninghamii

Cyathea cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea cunninghamii plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
