Calamus capillosus

Calamus capillosus, commonly known as the feathered arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in clusters. Calamus capillosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
