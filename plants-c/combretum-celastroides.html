Combretum celastroides

Combretum celastroides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late spring or early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue, which adds to its ornamental value.
