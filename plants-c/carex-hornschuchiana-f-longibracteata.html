Carex hornschuchiana f. longibracteata

Carex hornschuchiana f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial sedge characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetlands, bog gardens, or rain gardens. It produces showy, spike-like inflorescences in late spring to early summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
