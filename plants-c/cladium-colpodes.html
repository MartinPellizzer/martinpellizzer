Cladium colpodes

Cladium colpodes, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like blades that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often lined with sharp, serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cladium colpodes plays an important ecological role in marsh ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
