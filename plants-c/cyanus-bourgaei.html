Cyanus bourgaei

Cyanus bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgae's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Cyanus bourgaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
