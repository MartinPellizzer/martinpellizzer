Calliandra pacara

Calliandra pacara, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Calliandra pacara is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
