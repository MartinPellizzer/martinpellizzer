Cestrum depauperatum

Cestrum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
