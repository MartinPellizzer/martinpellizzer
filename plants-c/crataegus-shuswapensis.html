Crataegus shuswapensis

Crataegus shuswapensis, commonly known as the Shuswap hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
