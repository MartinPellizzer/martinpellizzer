Cissus heterotricha

Cissus heterotricha, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a member of the Vitaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
