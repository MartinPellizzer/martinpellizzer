Calophyllum rugosum

Calophyllum rugosum, commonly known as the rough-skin candlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its thick, bark-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
