Chamaecrista greggii var. macdougaliana

Chamaecrista greggii var. macdougaliana, commonly known as Gregg's crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-to-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in desert regions.
