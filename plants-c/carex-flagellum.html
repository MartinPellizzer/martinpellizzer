Carex flagellum

Carex flagellum, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are pollinated by wind.

Carex flagellum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
