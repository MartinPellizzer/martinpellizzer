Cassia tuberculata

Cassia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia tuberculata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
