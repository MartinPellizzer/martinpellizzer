Crassula corymbulosa

Crassula corymbulosa, commonly known as the Christmas Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
