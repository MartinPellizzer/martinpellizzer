Convolvulus sagittatus

Convolvulus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
