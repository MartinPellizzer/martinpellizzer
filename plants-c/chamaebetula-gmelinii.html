Chamaebetula gmelinii

Chamaebetula gmelinii, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small shrub belonging to the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in boreal and subarctic regions, often growing in moist, acidic soils.

Chamaebetula gmelinii is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream edges.
