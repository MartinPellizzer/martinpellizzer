Ceropegia bosseri var. razafindratsirana

Ceropegia bosseri var. razafindratsirana, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive tubular corolla. The plant has thin, leafy stems that can trail or climb along the ground or other structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This species is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories.
