Cosmos sulphureus var. hirsuticaulis

Cosmos sulphureus var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed sulphur cosmos, is a striking annual herb native to Mexico.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive sulphur-like hue, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, supporting its showy inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
