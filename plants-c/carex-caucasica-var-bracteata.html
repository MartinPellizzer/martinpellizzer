Carex caucasica var. bracteata

Carex caucasica var. bracteata, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

The varietal form bracteata is distinguished by its bract-like structures that modify the inflorescence.
