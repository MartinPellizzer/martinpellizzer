Cucurbita venosa

Cucurbita venosa, commonly known as the veined gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply veined leaves. The plant produces elongated, pale green fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes trailing or climbing vines that require support for optimal development.
