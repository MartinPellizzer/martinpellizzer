Cassia bicapsularis var. augusti

Cassia bicapsularis var. augusti, commonly known as August's cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with slender, pointed leaflets and small yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The plant produces two-lobed seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
