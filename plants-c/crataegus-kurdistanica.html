Crataegus kurdistanica

Crataegus kurdistanica, commonly known as Kurdish hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally not consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
