Cerastium endressianum

Cerastium endressianum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Cerastium endressianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
