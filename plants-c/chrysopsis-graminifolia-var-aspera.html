Chrysopsis graminifolia var. aspera

Chrysopsis graminifolia var. aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its stems are erect and often hairy, while the leaves are linear and slightly rough to the touch.

It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.
