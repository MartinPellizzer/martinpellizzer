Calanthe stevensiana

Calanthe stevensiana, commonly known as the Stevens' calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with a spike of 5 to 10 flowers. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe stevensiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
