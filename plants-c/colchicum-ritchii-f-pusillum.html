Colchicum ritchii f. pusillum

Colchicum ritchii f. pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf autumn crocus, is a variant of the autumn crocus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically shorter and more compact compared to the standard species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming cycle, it is a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
