Carum nigrum

Carum nigrum, commonly known as black cumin, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are rich in essential oils and have a pungent, spicy aroma. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Its botanical features include finely divided leaves and umbel-like flower clusters.
