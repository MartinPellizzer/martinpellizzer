Cirsium leucocephalum subsp. hermonis

Cirsium leucocephalum subsp.

hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble a ball of cotton.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
