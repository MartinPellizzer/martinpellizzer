Convolvulus sericatus

Convolvulus sericatus, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, smooth, oval seeds that are often dispersed by wind. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
