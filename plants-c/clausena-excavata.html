Clausena excavata

Clausena excavata, commonly known as the African blackberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, black, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a rough, fissured bark and a pungent aroma, which are typical of the Rutaceae family.

Clausena excavata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
