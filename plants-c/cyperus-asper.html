Cyperus asper

Cyperus asper, commonly known as rough flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus asper is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
