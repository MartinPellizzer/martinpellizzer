Carex hudsonii f. reticulosa

Carex hudsonii f. reticulosa, commonly known as the Hudson's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct reticulate pattern on the upper surface, giving it its specific epithet.

The inflorescence consists of two distinct spikelets, with the lower one being more prominent and bearing the flowers.
