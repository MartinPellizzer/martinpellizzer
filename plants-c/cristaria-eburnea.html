Cristaria eburnea

Cristaria eburnea, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy, pale green appearance. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint fragrance. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Cristaria eburnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in water ecosystems.
