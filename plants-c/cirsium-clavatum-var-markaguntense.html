Cirsium clavatum var. markaguntense

Cirsium clavatum var. markaguntense, commonly known as the Markagunt thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
