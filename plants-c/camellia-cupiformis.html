Camellia cupiformis

Camellia cupiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped inflorescence, which consists of a central stem surrounded by clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and elliptical in shape.

Camellia cupiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
