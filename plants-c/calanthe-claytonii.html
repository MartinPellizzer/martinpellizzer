Calanthe claytonii

Calanthe claytonii, commonly known as the Clayton's calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe claytonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
