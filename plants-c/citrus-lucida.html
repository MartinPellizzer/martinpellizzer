Citrus lucida

Citrus lucida, commonly known as the golden orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a thin, leathery skin. The fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp that is high in vitamin C and has a tart, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
