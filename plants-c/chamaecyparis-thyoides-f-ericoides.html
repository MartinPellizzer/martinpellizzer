Chamaecyparis thyoides f. ericoides

Chamaecyparis thyoides f. ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoid Form of the Eastern Redcedar, is a compact, evergreen shrub with dense, needle-like foliage.

It exhibits a unique growth habit, forming a rounded or conical shape with a slow growth rate. The leaves are typically a deep green, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous cones. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
