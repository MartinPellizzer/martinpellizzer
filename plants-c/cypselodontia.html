Cypselodontia

Cypselodontia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, hollow stem that supports a flower head. The flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in a composite head, consisting of many small florets.
