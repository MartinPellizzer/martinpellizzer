Clematis × jackmanii var. alba

Clematis × jackmanii var. alba, commonly known as the white jackman clematis, is a hybrid vine native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It requires well-drained soil and a support structure for its climbing habit.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
