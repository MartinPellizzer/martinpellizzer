Calytrix ericoides

Calytrix ericoides, commonly known as the Heath Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, typical of the Ericaceae family.

Calytrix ericoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
