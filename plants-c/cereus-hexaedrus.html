Cereus hexaedrus

Cereus hexaedrus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its flowers are typically followed by small, red fruits that contain edible seeds.

Cereus hexaedrus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
