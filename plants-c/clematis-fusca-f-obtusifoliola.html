Clematis fusca f. obtusifoliola

Clematis fusca f. obtusifoliola, commonly known as the blunt-leaved clematis, is a compact, deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive, fragrant, white to pale blue flower cluster. The flowers are typically borne in late spring to early summer and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
