Curcuma rangsimae

Curcuma rangsimae, commonly known as the Thai turmeric or golden ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental rhizomes and vibrant, variegated leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive fragrance, often blooming in summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to common turmeric.

Curcuma rangsimae is valued for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.
