Cecropia urbaniana

Cecropia urbaniana, commonly known as the urban tree, is a fast-growing species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cecropiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, hollow stems. The plant is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions due to its adaptability. Its rapid growth makes it an important pioneer species in forest succession.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing its ecological role in nutrient cycling.
