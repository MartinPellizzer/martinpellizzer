Codonanthe picta

Codonanthe picta, commonly known as the pictured codonanthe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the showy bracts serve as a key ornamental feature.
