Carex cespitosa var. hyalinosquamis

Carex cespitosa var. hyalinosquamis, commonly known as the silver-squamate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branched stalks. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often used in wetland restoration projects and natural landscaping.
