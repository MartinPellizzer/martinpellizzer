Chesnea

Chesnea, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads, which are often surrounded by bright yellow ray florets. Chesnea species typically grow as annuals or perennials, with some varieties reaching heights of up to six feet.

These plants are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in gardens for their striking visual appeal.
