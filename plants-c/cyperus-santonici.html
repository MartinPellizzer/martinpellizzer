Cyperus santonici

Cyperus santonici, commonly known as the sacred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps with slender, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, nut-like seeds.

Cyperus santonici is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
