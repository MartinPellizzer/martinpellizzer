Cocculus mokiangensis

Cocculus mokiangensis, commonly known as the Mokiang bignay, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and palmately lobed leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical forests.
