Cyclosorus pterosporus

Cyclosorus pterosporus, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are divided into lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyclosorus pterosporus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through filtration.
