Commelina erecta f. albina

Commelina erecta f. albina, commonly known as the white commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking white flowers with a distinctive blue-violet calyx, which are borne on slender, erect stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is a popular choice for garden use due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
