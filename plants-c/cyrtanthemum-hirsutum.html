Cyrtanthemum hirsutum

Cyrtanthemum hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a hairy texture. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs that store nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cyrtanthemum hirsutum is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
