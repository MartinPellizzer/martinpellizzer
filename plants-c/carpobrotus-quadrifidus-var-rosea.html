Carpobrotus quadrifidus var. rosea

Carpobrotus quadrifidus var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The variety rosea is distinguished by its showy, colorful blooms compared to the typical green-flowered form.
