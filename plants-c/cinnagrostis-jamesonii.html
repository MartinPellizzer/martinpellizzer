Cinnagrostis jamesonii

Cinnagrostis jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow cones. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals.

Cinnagrostis jamesonii is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in areas with disturbed soil.
