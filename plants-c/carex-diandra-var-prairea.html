Carex diandra var. prairea

Carex diandra var. prairea, commonly known as prairie sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and prairies. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
