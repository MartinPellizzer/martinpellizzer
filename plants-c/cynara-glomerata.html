Cynara glomerata

Cynara glomerata, commonly known as the clustered artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces a compact, spherical flower head that resembles a small artichoke, which is typically green with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by hard, dry fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cynara glomerata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
