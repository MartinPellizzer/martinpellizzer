Cerastium moesiacum f. thacium

Cerastium moesiacum f. thacium, commonly known as the Thacium form of mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a ground cover plant in ecological and horticultural settings.
