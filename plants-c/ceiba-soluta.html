Ceiba soluta

Ceiba soluta, commonly known as the wild silk cotton tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense canopy. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
