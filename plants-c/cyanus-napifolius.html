Cyanus napifolius

Cyanus napifolius, commonly known as the Napifolius bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Cyanus napifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
