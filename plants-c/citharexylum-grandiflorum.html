Citharexylum grandiflorum

Citharexylum grandiflorum, commonly known as the broadleaf cithara or sweet cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The flowers are borne on slender pedicels and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.
