Cirsium eriophorum f. calabrum

Cirsium eriophorum f. calabrum, commonly known as the Calabrian thistle, is a botanical variant of the common thistle.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Italy. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its presence in such habitats makes it an important component of local flora.
