Capparis flexicaulis

Capparis flexicaulis, commonly known as the slender caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its woody stems and spiny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Capparis flexicaulis is often used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various regions.
