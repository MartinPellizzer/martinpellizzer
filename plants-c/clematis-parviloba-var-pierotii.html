Clematis parviloba var. pierotii

Clematis parviloba var. pierotii, commonly known as the dwarf clematis, is a compact, flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, delicate white flowers with a distinctive greenish tint, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
