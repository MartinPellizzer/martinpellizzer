Cephalaria kesruanica

Cephalaria kesruanica, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Cephalaria kesruanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
