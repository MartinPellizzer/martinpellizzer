Chrysothamnus filifolius

Chrysothamnus filifolius, commonly known as the golden sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silvery-green foliage, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
