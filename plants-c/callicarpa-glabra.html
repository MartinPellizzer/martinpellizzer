Callicarpa glabra

Callicarpa glabra, commonly known as the smooth beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface. It produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in umbels.

Callicarpa glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
