Cissus campestris

Cissus campestris, commonly known as the rock fig or mountain fig, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Cissus campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
