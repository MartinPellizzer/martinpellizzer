Cousinia campylacantha

Cousinia campylacantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved cousinia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, appearing in dense clusters during the summer months.

Cousinia campylacantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
