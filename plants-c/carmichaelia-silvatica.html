Carmichaelia silvatica

Carmichaelia silvatica, commonly known as the silver tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive pod fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Carmichaelia silvatica plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
