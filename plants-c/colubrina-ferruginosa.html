Colubrina ferruginosa

Colubrina ferruginosa, commonly known as the scarlet flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 15 feet tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Colubrina ferruginosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
