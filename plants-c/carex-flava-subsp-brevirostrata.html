Carex flava subsp. brevirostrata

Carex flava subsp.

brevirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved at the tips. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It is characterized by its distinctive, short, narrow bracts that give it its subspecies name.
