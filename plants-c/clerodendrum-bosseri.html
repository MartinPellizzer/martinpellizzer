Clerodendrum bosseri

Clerodendrum bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
