Cistus populifolius subsp. major

Cistus populifolius subsp.

major, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
