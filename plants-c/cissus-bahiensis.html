Cissus bahiensis

Cissus bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian stonecrop, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus bahiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb structures, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
