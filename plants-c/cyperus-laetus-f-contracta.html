Cyperus laetus f. contracta

Cyperus laetus f. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
