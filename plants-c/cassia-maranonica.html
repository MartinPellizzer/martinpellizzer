Cassia maranonica

Cassia maranonica, commonly known as the Maranon cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Cassia maranonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
