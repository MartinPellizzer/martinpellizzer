Crataegus schuettei var. schuettei

Crataegus schuettei var. schuettei, commonly known as the Schuettehawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet, featuring thorned branches and oval to elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
