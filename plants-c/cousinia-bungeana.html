Cousinia bungeana

Cousinia bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe regions.

Cousinia bungeana is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
