Ceropegia devecchii var. adelaidae

Ceropegia devecchii var. adelaidae, commonly known as the Adelaidas's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces tubular, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate. It is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
