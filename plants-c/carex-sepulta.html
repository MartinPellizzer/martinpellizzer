Carex sepulta

Carex sepulta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a distinct sheathing base. Carex sepulta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
