Chamaerhodiola scabrida

Chamaerhodiola scabrida, commonly known as the rough fireweed, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor, rocky soils.

Chamaerhodiola scabrida is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
