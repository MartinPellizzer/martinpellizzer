Cupressus nepalensis

Cupressus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. The tree has a tall, slender form with needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in crossed pairs. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The wood of Cupressus nepalensis is durable and has been used traditionally for construction and furniture.
