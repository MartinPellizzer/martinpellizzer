Chenopodium carthagenense

Chenopodium carthagenense, commonly known as Carthaginian goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Chenopodium carthagenense is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
