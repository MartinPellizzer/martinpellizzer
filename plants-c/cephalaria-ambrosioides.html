Cephalaria ambrosioides

Cephalaria ambrosioides, commonly known as the yellow cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria ambrosioides is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.
