Cyathea brunnescens

Cyathea brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of brown, fibrous tissue. The plant has large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea brunnescens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
