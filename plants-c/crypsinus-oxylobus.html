Crypsinus oxylobus

Crypsinus oxylobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crypts, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
