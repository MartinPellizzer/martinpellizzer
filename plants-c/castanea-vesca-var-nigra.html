Castanea vesca var. nigra

Castanea vesca var. nigra, commonly known as the black horned chestnut.

It is a variant of the common horned chestnut, characterized by its dark, almost black bark and smaller, more pointed leaves. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible nuts, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
