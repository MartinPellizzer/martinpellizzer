Camellia japonica var. curvatifolia

Camellia japonica var. curvatifolia, commonly known as the curlleaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, gracefully curved leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value and hardy nature.

This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
