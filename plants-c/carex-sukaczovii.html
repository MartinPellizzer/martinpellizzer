Carex sukaczovii

Carex sukaczovii, commonly known as Sukaczov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green to reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Carex sukaczovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
