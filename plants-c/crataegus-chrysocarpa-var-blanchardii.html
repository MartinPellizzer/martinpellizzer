Crataegus chrysocarpa var. blanchardii

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. blanchardii, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange berries that mature in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy green surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges or open areas.
