Celtis australis f. variegata

Celtis australis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated European hackberry, is a cultivar of the European hackberry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3 to 6 meters. It has smooth, grey bark and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

The variegation in its leaves is due to genetic mutations affecting chlorophyll distribution, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
