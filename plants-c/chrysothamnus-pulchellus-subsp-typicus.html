Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp. typicus

Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has silvery-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert areas.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
