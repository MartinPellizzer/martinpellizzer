Caragana leveillei

Caragana leveillei, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a reddish or bronze hue during the growing season. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Caragana leveillei is often used in reforestation and soil stabilization projects due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.
