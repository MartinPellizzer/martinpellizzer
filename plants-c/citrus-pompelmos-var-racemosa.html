Citrus pompelmos var. racemosa

Citrus pompelmos var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose citrus, is a variant of the pomelo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in racemes, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, green, inedible fruits that are often used for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a strong citrus scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and its role in traditional horticulture.
