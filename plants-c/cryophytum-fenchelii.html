Cryophytum fenchelii

Cryophytum fenchelii, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Europe and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, alpine environments, often found in rocky soils and tundra habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh, arid conditions.

Cryophytum fenchelii is an important species for studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
