Callisia ornata

Callisia ornata, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
