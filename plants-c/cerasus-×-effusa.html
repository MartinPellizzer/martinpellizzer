Cerasus × effusa

Cerasus × effusa, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry, is a hybrid species in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its showy, double flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, while the fruit, when mature, is a small, dark red drupe.

This ornamental tree is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
