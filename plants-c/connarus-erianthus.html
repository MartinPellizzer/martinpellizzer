Connarus erianthus

Connarus erianthus, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist lowland forests.
