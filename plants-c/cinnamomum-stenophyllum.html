Cinnamomum stenophyllum

Cinnamomum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or narrow-leafed camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic bark, which contain essential oils. The plant is part of the Lauraceae family, known for its aromatic and medicinal properties. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Its wood is valued for its durability and scent, while its leaves and bark have traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
