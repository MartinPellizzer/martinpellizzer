Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. pindigenum

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

pindigenum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It features small, white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for alpine environments and it is often found in mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and the Altai Mountains.
