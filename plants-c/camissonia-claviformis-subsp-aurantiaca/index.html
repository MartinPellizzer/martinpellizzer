Camissonia Claviformis Subsp. Aurantiaca

Camissonia claviformis subsp. aurantiaca is a rare and striking plant native to the western United States. It is known for its vivid orange flowers that bloom in early spring. This subspecies thrives in moist, shaded environments along stream banks and forest floors. Its unique appearance makes it a favorite among botanists and nature enthusiasts. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this delicate and endangered species.
