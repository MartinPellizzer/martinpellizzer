Coelogyne kingii

Coelogyne kingii, commonly known as the King's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of white to pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Coelogyne kingii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
