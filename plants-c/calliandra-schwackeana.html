Calliandra schwackeana

Calliandra schwackeana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their visual appeal. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
