Camaridium synsepalum

Camaridium synsepalum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are heart-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
