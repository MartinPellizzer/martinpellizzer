Calceolaria bergii

Calceolaria bergii, commonly known as the Berg's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Calceolaria bergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique floral structure.
