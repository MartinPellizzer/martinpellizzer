Candollea cuneiformis

Candollea cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
