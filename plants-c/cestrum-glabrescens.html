Cestrum glabrescens

Cestrum glabrescens, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Cestrum glabrescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
