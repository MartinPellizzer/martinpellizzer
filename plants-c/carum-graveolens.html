Carum graveolens

Carum graveolens, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are highly aromatic and widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are also edible and often used as a herb in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and agricultural fields.
