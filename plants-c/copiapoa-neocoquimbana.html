Copiapoa neocoquimbana

Copiapoa neocoquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbo copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically pale yellow or white. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
