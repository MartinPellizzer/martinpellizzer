Curcuma viridiflora

Curcuma viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other aromatic plants. The plant features rhizomatous stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its bright green flowers, which contrast with its yellow-orange rhizomes.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and for its potential medicinal properties.
