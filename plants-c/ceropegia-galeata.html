Ceropegia galeata

Ceropegia galeata, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Ceropegia galeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
