Cerradicola elliptica

Cerradicola elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers, when present, are usually small and may vary in color, adding ornamental value.

Despite its resilience, it requires careful cultivation to thrive outside its natural habitat.
