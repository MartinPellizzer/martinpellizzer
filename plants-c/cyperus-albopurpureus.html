Cyperus albopurpureus

Cyperus albopurpureus, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or silver along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching panicles. It thrives in moist or wet soil conditions, making it a common sight in wetlands and marshy areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
