Centaurea sirdjanica

Centaurea sirdjanica, commonly known as the Sirdjan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea sirdjanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
