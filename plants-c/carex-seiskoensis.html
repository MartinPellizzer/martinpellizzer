Carex seiskoensis

Carex seiskoensis, commonly known as the Seisko sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and spikes of small, typically greenish or brownish flowers. Native to specific regions in Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of its ecological community.
