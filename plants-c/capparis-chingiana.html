Capparis chingiana

Capparis chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese caper, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
