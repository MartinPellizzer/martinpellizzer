Camellia viridicalyx var. linearifolia

Camellia viridicalyx var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
