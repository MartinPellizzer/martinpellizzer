Crotalaria maritima

Crotalaria maritima, commonly known as sea cowpea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy soils along shorelines and dunes. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Crotalaria maritima is adapted to salty environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
