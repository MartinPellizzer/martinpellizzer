Cephaelis conephoroides

Cephaelis conephoroides, commonly known as the yellow flame or Indian flame, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cephaelis conephoroides is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
