Capsella lobulata

Capsella lobulata, commonly known as the heartweed or smallheart, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Capsella lobulata is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.
