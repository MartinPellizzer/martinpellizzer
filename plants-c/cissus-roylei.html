Cissus roylei

Cissus roylei, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can form a dense, trailing mass when grown in suitable conditions.

Cissus roylei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environments.
