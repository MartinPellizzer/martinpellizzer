Cinnamomum paratriplinerve

Cinnamomum paratriplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The leaves are elliptical, with a prominent central vein and three secondary veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark brown, ellipsoid fruits.

Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
