Carduus malyi

Carduus malyi, commonly known as the Malaya thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and a tall, upright stem that can reach up to two meters in height. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Carduus malyi is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
