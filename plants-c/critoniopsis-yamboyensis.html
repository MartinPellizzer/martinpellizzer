Critoniopsis yamboyensis

Critoniopsis yamboyensis, commonly known as the Yamboye cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, leaf-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
