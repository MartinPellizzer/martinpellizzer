Callista thymifolia

Callista thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in spring.
