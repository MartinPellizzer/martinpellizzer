Chthonia humifusa

Chthonia humifusa, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the understory of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity and rich, organic soil. Its reproductive structures are concealed beneath the soil surface, contributing to its difficulty in cultivation.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging species for botanists and horticulturists alike.
