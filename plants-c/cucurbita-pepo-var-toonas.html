Cucurbita pepo var. toonas

Cucurbita pepo var. toonas, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often ribbed fruit.

The plant typically produces yellow to orange-colored squash with a hard, thick rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. It is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the plant is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners and culinary enthusiasts alike.
