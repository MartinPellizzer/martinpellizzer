Cacalia scorpioides var. sororia

Cacalia scorpioides var. sororia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue petals and prominent yellow central disk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
