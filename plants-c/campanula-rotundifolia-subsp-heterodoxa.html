Campanula rotundifolia subsp. heterodoxa

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

heterodoxa, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features basal leaves that are round or oval with a wavy margin, and the stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
