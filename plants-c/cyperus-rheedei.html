Cyperus rheedei

Cyperus rheedei, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous.

Cyperus rheedei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
