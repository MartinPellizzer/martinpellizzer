Cordia patagonula

Cordia patagonula, commonly known as the Patagonian cordia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
