Corydalis hebephylla var. glabrescens

Corydalis hebephylla var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink or lilac in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and attractive floral display.
