Chenopodium spinacifolium

Chenopodium spinacifolium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features succulent, triangular leaves with serrated edges and a rough, prickly texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by triangular, spiky seed cases. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium spinacifolium is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
