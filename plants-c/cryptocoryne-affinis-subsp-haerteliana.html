Cryptocoryne affinis subsp. haerteliana

Cryptocoryne affinis subsp.

haerteliana, commonly known as the Java cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a lighter midrib.

This subspecies thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, making it an effective plant for stabilizing substrates in aquariums.

It is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for both freshwater and brackish water setups.
