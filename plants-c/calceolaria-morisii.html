Calceolaria morisii

Calceolaria morisii, commonly known as the Moris's slipper orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Calceolaria morisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
