Carex brizoides subsp. ohmuelleriana

Carex brizoides subsp.

ohmuelleriana, commonly known as the Ohmueller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
