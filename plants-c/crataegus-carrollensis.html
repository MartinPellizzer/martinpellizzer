Crataegus carrollensis

Crataegus carrollensis, commonly known as Carroll's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
