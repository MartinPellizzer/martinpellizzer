Chrysocycnis rhomboglossa

Chrysocycnis rhomboglossa, commonly known as the diamond-leaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
