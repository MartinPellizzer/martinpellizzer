Clematis dioica subsp. havanensis

Clematis dioica subsp.

havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban climbing vine, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to Cuba and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically blooms in spring, producing blue to purple flowers with five petals. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
