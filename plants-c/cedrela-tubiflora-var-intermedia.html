Cedrela tubiflora var. intermedia

Cedrela tubiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or red cedar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, aromatic foliage. Its heartwood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a prized timber species.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, and the fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature.
