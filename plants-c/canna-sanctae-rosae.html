Canna sanctae-rosae

Canna sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the sacred rose cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, often with a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a red center, resembling a rose in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna sanctae-rosae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
