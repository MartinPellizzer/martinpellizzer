Catasetum × mirabile

Catasetum × mirabile, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar floral structure, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that feature a specialized column and a distinctive, often colorful lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are highly specialized for pollination by specific wasps, showcasing an intricate evolutionary adaptation.

The genus Catasetum is known for its diverse morphology, with C. × mirabile being a notable example of hybrid vigor in orchid horticulture.
