Citrus hystrix var. balincolong

Citrus hystrix var. balincolong, commonly known as the Balinese finger lime, is a unique citrus variety native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, finger-like fruits that are covered in a rough, bumpy skin. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications for their distinctive flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species.

Its ornamental value is also notable, making it a popular choice for both gardens and landscapes.
