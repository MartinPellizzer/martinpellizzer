Corynephorus divaricatus

Corynephorus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
