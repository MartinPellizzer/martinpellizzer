Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp. auratum

Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp.

auratum, commonly known as the golden star apple, is a member of the Sapotaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that give way to yellow or orange fruits. The fruit has a smooth, waxy skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp, making it both ornamental and edible.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and edible yield.
