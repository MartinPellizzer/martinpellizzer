Carex indiciformis

Carex indiciformis, commonly known as the Indian sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months.

Carex indiciformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
