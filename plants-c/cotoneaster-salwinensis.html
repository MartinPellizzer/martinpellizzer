Cotoneaster salwinensis

Cotoneaster salwinensis, commonly known as Salwin cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Cotoneaster salwinensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
