Cyperus sertularinus var. discigerus

Cyperus sertularinus var. discigerus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, three-ranked spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
