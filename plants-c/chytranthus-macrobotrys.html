Chytranthus macrobotrys

Chytranthus macrobotrys, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
