Cavendishia osaensis

Cavendishia osaensis, commonly known as the Osaensis Cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the Osa Peninsula in Costa Rica.

This plant is characterized by its columnar stem and clusters of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's adaptations, including its succulent stems and nocturnal blooming, reflect its evolutionary response to the tropical rainforest environment.

Due to habitat loss, Cavendishia osaensis is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
