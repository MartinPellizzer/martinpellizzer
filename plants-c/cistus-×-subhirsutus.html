Cistus × subhirsutus

Cistus × subhirsutus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
