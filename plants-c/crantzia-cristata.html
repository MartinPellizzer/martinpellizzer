Crantzia cristata

Crantzia cristata, commonly known as the crested crantzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often tufted appearance, with small, white to pinkish flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in coastal areas. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Its compact form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
