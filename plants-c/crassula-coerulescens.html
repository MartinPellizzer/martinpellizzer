Crassula coerulescens

Crassula coerulescens, commonly known as the Blue Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
