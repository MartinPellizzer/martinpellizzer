Cerastium grandiflorum subsp. serpentini

Cerastium grandiflorum subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

The plant has hairy, spreading stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions with suitable habitat.
