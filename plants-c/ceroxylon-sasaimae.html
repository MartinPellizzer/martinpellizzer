Ceroxylon sasaimae

Ceroxylon sasaimae, commonly known as the Sasaima wax palm, is a rare and endangered species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves. The plant is notable for its unique wax-like coating on its leaves, which helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude habitat. It typically grows in cloud forests at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
