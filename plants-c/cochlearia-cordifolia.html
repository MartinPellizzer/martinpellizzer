Cochlearia cordifolia

Cochlearia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf scurvygrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features rosettes of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from April to June.

Native to the northern hemisphere, it thrives in moist, coastal habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
