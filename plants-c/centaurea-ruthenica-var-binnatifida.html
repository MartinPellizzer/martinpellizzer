Centaurea ruthenica var. binnatifida

Centaurea ruthenica var. binnatifida, commonly known as the Binnatifid Bluet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea ruthenica var. binnatifida is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
