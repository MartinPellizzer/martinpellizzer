Crotalaria calva

Crotalaria calva, commonly known as the woolly crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the pods are usually slender and hairy.

Crotalaria calva is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
