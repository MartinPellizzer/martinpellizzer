Cyperus scaettae

Cyperus scaettae, commonly known as the Scetta's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its cylindrical, grass-like stems are often covered with small, scale-like leaves.

Cyperus scaettae produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
