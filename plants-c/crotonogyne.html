Crotonogyne

Crotonogyne, commonly known as the golden croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Crotonogyne is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.
