Crataegus coccinea var. indentata

Crataegus coccinea var. indentata, commonly known as the red flowering dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and turn vibrant shades of red in the autumn. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
