Clidemia ramiflora var. colombiana

Clidemia ramiflora var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian flameleaf, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive reddish or bronze-colored underside. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
