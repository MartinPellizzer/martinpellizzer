Carex bonariensis var. remota

Carex bonariensis var. remota, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. This variety is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is valued in landscaping for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its ornamental appearance.
