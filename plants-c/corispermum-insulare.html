Corispermum insulare

Corispermum insulare, commonly known as the island sandspurry, is a small annual plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in the Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia. The plant has small, succulent leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry capsule that disperses seeds via wind.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.
