Coptosapelta janowskii

Coptosapelta janowskii, commonly known as the Janowsky's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Native to a limited geographic region, it is adapted to specific climatic and soil conditions.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
