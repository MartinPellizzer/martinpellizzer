Cymbidium purpureohiemale

Cymbidium purpureohiemale, commonly known as the purple-tinged winter orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, lance-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple in color, with a white or cream-colored lip, and bloom during the winter months. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.
