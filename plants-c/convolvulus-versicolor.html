Convolvulus versicolor

Convolvulus versicolor, commonly known as the variable bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pale blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
