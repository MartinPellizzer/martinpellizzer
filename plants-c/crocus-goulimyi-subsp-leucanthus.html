Crocus goulimyi subsp. leucanthus

Crocus goulimyi subsp.

leucanthus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
