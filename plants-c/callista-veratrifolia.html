Callista veratrifolia

Callista veratrifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are opposite in arrangement, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Callista veratrifolia is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
