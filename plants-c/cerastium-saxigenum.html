Cerastium saxigenum

Cerastium saxigenum, commonly known as the rock chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically has alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cerastium saxigenum plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and provides nectar for pollinators.
