Cladium gaudichaudii

Cladium gaudichaudii, commonly known as the swamp cordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and coastal areas, where it thrives in saline or brackish water conditions. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that are adapted for wind pollination.

Cladium gaudichaudii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic species.
