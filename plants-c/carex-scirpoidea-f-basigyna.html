Carex scirpoidea f. basigyna

Carex scirpoidea f. basigyna, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

This variety is notable for its distinctive broad, flat leaves and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
