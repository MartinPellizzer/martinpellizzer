Calamagrostis fasciculata

Calamagrostis fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are usually blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis fasciculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in wetland ecosystems.
