Cirsium calvescens

Cirsium calvescens, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright, spiny stems with broad, lobed leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are purple to pink, arranged in a spherical shape, and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium calvescens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
