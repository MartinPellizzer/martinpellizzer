Croton oreoborneicus

Croton oreoborneicus, commonly known as the mountain croton, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
