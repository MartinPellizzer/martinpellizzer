Campanula dilecta

Campanula dilecta, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Campanula dilecta is known for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
