Carex vulgaris var. juncea

Carex vulgaris var. juncea, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with staminate and pistillate spikelets arranged in a distinct sequence.
