Calamagrostis forsteri

Calamagrostis forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's reed, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray plumes during the summer months, which are visually striking and attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens and restoration projects.
