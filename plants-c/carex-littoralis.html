Carex littoralis

Carex littoralis, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to coastal areas of North America.

It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in wetlands and along shorelines. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex littoralis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
