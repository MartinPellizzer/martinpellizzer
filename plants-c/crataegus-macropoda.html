Crataegus macropoda

Crataegus macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Crataegus macropoda thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open fields.
