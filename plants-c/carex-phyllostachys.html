Carex phyllostachys

Carex phyllostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex phyllostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
