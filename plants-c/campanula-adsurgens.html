Campanula adsurgens

Campanula adsurgens, commonly known as the ascending bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Campanula adsurgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blossoms.
