Chamaesyce ingallsii

Chamaesyce ingallsii, commonly known as Ingalls' sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, making it visually distinctive.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
