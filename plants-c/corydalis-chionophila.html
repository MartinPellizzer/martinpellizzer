Corydalis chionophila

Corydalis chionophila, commonly known as the snowflake corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forested areas. The plant produces delicate, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Corydalis chionophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
