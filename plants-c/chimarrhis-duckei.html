Chimarrhis duckei

Chimarrhis duckei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique foliage and flowering habit.
