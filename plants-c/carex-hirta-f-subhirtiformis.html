Carex hirta f. subhirtiformis

Carex hirta f. subhirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a variant of the hairy sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy, especially along the midrib, giving it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.
