Clematopsis stanleyi

Clematopsis stanleyi, commonly known as Stanley's clematopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Clematopsis stanleyi is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and extended flowering period.
