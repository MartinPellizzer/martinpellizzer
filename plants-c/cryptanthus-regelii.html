Cryptanthus regelii

Cryptanthus regelii, commonly known as the "Bloomerang" or "Rainbow Plant," is a species of flowering plant in the family Haemodoraceae.

Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath the striking foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
