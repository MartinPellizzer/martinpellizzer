Clematis populifolia var. goudotiana

Clematis populifolia var. goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its species name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and support for its vigorous growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
