Carex umbrosa subsp. pseudosabynensis

Carex umbrosa subsp.

pseudosabynensis, commonly known as the shadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
