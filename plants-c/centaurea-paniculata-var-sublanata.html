Centaurea paniculata var. sublanata

Centaurea paniculata var. sublanata, commonly known as the velvethead thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in dense, grayish-white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
