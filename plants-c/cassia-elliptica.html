Cassia elliptica

Cassia elliptica, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
