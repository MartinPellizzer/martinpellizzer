Crassocephalum gracile

Crassocephalum gracile, commonly known as the slender-headed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils. The plant has opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Crassocephalum gracile is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
