Croton thurifer

Croton thurifer, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and colorful, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which are modified leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.

The plant is also culturally significant in some African communities.
