Crataegus magnifolia

Crataegus magnifolia, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring and vibrant red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically 3-5 lobed, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Crataegus magnifolia is also valued for its ecological role, supporting a variety of pollinators and wildlife.
