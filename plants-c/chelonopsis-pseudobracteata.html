Chelonopsis pseudobracteata

Chelonopsis pseudobracteata, commonly known as the false bract cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
