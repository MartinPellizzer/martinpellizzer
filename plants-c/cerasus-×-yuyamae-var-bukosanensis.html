Cerasus × yuyamae var. bukosanensis

Cerasus × yuyamae var. bukosanensis, commonly known as the Bukosan cherry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are white with a pinkish tinge.

The Bukosan cherry is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
