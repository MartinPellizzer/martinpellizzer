Chrysalidocarpus ankaizinensis

Chrysalidocarpus ankaizinensis, commonly known as the Ankaizini palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
