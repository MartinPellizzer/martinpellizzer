Campanula silifkeensis

Campanula silifkeensis, commonly known as the Silifke bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula silifkeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
