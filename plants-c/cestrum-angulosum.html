Cestrum angulosum

Cestrum angulosum, commonly known as the angular cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring angular, often spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the evening. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cestrum angulosum is known for its nocturnal blooming habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
