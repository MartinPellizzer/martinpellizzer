Conostylis lepidospermoides

Conostylis lepidospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved conostylis, is an aromatic shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathlands or open forests. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Conostylis lepidospermoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
