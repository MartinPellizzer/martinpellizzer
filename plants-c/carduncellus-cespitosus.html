Carduncellus cespitosus

Carduncellus cespitosus, commonly known as the hairy cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been historically used in cooking and medicine.
