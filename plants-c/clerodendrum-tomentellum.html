Clerodendrum tomentellum

Clerodendrum tomentellum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits that are covered in a thick, waxy coating. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
