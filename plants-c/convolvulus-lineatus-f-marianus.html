Convolvulus lineatus f. marianus

Convolvulus lineatus f. marianus, commonly known as the Marianus bindweed, is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is native to regions in the Mediterranean and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
