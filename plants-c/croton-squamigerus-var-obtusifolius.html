Croton squamigerus var. obtusifolius

Croton squamigerus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape and vary in color from green to reddish-brown. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage.
