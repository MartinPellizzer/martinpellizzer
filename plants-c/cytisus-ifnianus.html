Cytisus ifnianus

Cytisus ifnianus, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves are typical of the genus Cytisus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
