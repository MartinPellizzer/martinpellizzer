Callicarpa erioclona f. rivularis

Callicarpa erioclona f. rivularis, commonly known as the river beautyberry, is a variant of the beautyberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its clusters of small, violet-purple berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin and a slightly glossy surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near waterways.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
