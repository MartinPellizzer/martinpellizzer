Carex macloviformis

Carex macloviformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to moist or wet habitats, this plant thrives in environments with consistent moisture, such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
