Cerasus xueluoensis

Cerasus xueluoensis, commonly known as the Xueluo cherry, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The Xueluo cherry is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
