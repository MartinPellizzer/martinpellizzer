Cerasus vulgaris f. frutescens

Cerasus vulgaris f. frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Europe.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in summer. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
