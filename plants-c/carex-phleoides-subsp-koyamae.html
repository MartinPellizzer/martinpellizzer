Carex phleoides subsp. koyamae

Carex phleoides subsp.

koyamae, commonly known as Koyama's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
