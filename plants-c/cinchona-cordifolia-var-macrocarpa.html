Cinchona cordifolia var. macrocarpa

Cinchona cordifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its production of red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other cinchona species used in the production of quinine.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
