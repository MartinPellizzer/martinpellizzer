Centaurea maroccana

Centaurea maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Centaurea maroccana is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
