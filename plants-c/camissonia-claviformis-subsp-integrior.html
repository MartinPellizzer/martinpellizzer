Camissonia claviformis subsp. integrior

Camissonia claviformis subsp.

integrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf camissonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers.

The subspecies integrior is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves, which are lanceolate in shape. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
