Cestrum grande

Cestrum grande, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
