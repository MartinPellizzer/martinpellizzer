Carex fischeri var. basiandra

Carex fischeri var. basiandra, commonly known as the Fischer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a native plant in ecological restoration projects.
