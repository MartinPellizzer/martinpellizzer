Carex filipes var. oligostachys

Carex filipes var. oligostachys, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
