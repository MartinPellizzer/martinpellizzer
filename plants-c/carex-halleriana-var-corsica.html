Carex halleriana var. corsica

Carex halleriana var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces spikelets that are often tinged with reddish or brownish colors.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
