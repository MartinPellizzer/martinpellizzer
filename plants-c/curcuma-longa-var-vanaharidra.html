Curcuma longa var. vanaharidra

Curcuma longa var. vanaharidra, commonly known as the golden turmeric, is a variant of the turmeric plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow rhizomes, which are rich in curcumin, a compound known for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while its flowers are usually orange or red in color.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
