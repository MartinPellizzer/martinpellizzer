Carex phaeodon

Carex phaeodon, commonly known as the dark-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat creation.

Carex phaeodon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
