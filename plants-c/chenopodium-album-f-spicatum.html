Chenopodium album f. spicatum

Chenopodium album f. spicatum, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters along the upper stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields, gardens, and along roadsides.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
