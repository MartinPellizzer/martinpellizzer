Campylospermum anceps var. perrieri

Campylospermum anceps var. perrieri, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.
