Centrosema plumieri

Centrosema plumieri, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its deep root system helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.
