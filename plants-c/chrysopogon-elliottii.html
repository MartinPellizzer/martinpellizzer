Chrysopogon elliottii

Chrysopogon elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, pale-green inflorescences that are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Chrysopogon elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
