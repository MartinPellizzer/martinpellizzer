Ceanothus tomentosus var. olivaceus

Ceanothus tomentosus var. olivaceus, commonly known as the oliveleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to California.

It features dense, grayish-white foliage covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
