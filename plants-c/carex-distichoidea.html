Carex distichoidea

Carex distichoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex distichoidea plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
