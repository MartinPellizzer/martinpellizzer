Crassula columella

Crassula columella, commonly known as the columnar crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula columella is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
