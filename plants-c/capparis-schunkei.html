Capparis schunkei

Capparis schunkei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green, spiny calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Capparis schunkei include its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers.
