Cicer pinnatifidum subsp. judaicum

Cicer pinnatifidum subsp.

judaicum, commonly known as the Judean vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture and can be harvested for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The subspecies is notable for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
