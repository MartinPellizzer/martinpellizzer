Clompanus comosa

Clompanus comosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, bean-like seed pods. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Clompanus comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
