Cotyledon muirii

Cotyledon muirii, commonly known as the "Rainbow Cotyledon," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that come in a variety of vibrant colors, including pink, red, and purple. The plant belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Its unique leaf patterns and striking coloration make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Cotyledon muirii typically produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months.
