Cassia vernicosa

Cassia vernicosa, commonly known as golden shower tree or yellow cassia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
