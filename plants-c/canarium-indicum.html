Canarium indicum

Canarium indicum, commonly known as the Indian oil palm, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is notable for its fruit, which contains a valuable oil used in various industries. Its bark and leaves have also been used traditionally in medicinal applications.

Canarium indicum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
