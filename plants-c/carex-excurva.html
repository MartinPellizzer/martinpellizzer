Carex excurva

Carex excurva, commonly known as the bent-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex excurva plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
