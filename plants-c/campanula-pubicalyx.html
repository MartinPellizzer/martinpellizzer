Campanula pubicalyx

Campanula pubicalyx, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms during the summer months.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
