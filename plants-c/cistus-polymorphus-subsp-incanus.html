Cistus polymorphus subsp. incanus

Cistus polymorphus subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or coastal terrains.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
