Crocus paschei

Crocus paschei, commonly known as the Pasche crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean, including parts of Turkey and Cyprus. The plant produces showy, saffron-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky and dry environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
