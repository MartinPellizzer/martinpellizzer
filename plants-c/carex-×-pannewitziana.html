Carex × pannewitziana

Carex × pannewitziana, commonly known as the Pannewitziana sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, strap-shaped leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

Carex × pannewitziana is also known for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
