Cocculus oblongus

Cocculus oblongus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, green, warty fruits. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a rough texture.

Cocculus oblongus is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
