Colutea arborescens subsp. gallica

Colutea arborescens subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the red broom or red broom, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its upright, branching habit and bright red to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and woodland edges.
