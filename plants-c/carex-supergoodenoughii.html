Carex supergoodenoughii

Carex supergoodenoughii, commonly known as the supergoodenough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
