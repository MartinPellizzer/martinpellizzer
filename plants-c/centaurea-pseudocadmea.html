Centaurea pseudocadmea

Centaurea pseudocadmea, commonly known as the false cadmean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its growth habit is perennial, and it produces small, bract-like leaves that are densely covered with stiff hairs.

The flowers are usually arranged in a compact cluster, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
