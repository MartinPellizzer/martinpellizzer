Careya orbiculata

Careya orbiculata, commonly known as the broadleaf soursop or mountain soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits that resemble a cross between a pineapple and a mango. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in traditional medicine.
