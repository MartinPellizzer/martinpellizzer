Cleisostoma lanatum

Cleisostoma lanatum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "lanatum," meaning "woolly.". The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Cleisostoma lanatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage.
