Crambe orientalis subvar. glaberrima

Crambe orientalis subvar. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth crambe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
