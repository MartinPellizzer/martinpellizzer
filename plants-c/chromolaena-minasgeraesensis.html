Chromolaena minasgeraesensis

Chromolaena minasgeraesensis, commonly known as the Brazilian chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to several meters in height. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
