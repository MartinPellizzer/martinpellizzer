Carex subumbellata

Carex subumbellata, commonly known as the slender bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex subumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
