Callistemon viminalis subsp. rhododendron

Callistemon viminalis subsp.

rhododendron, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical red flower spikes that appear in spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is evergreen, making it suitable for various garden settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The subspecies is notable for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to different climates.
