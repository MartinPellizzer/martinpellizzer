Campanula brotheri

Campanula brotheri, commonly known as the Ethiopian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Campanula brotheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
