Centunculus indicus

Centunculus indicus, commonly known as the Indian carpetweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer.

Centunculus indicus is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect life.
