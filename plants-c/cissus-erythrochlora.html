Cissus erythrochlora

Cissus erythrochlora, commonly known as the red-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often display a striking red or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cissus erythrochlora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
