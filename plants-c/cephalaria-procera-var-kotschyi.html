Cephalaria procera var. kotschyi

Cephalaria procera var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kashmiri cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features a robust root system and has distinctive, hairy stems that often branch near the base. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
