Calceolaria microbefaria subsp. tatamana

Calceolaria microbefaria subsp.

tatamana, commonly known as the tatamana slipper orchid, is a member of the Calceolaria genus, characterized by its unique slipper-shaped flowers. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive pouch-like structure that aids in pollination by insects.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
