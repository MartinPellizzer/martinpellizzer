Cucumis conomon

Cucumis conomon, commonly known as the common cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces cucumbers with a smooth, green rind and a mild, refreshing flavor. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with tendrils that help it climb. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit develops from the female flower and is widely cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
