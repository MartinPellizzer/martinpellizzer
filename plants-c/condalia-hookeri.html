Condalia hookeri

Condalia hookeri, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.
