Carex benkei

Carex benkei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex benkei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
