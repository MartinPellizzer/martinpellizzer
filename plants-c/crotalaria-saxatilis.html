Crotalaria saxatilis

Crotalaria saxatilis, commonly known as the rock crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow, spurred flowers and compound leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria saxatilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
