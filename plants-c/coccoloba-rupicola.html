Coccoloba rupicola

Coccoloba rupicola, commonly known as the rock coccoloba, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows on rocky or coastal soils. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
