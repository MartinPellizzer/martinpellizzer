Clematis smilacifolia var. chrysocarpa

Clematis smilacifolia var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, followed by orange to yellow berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
