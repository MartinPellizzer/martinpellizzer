Croton trichocephalus var. rigidus

Croton trichocephalus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that are usually opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
