Calliandra pilosa

Calliandra pilosa, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with small, velvety, hairy stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fluffy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Calliandra pilosa is also valued in some areas for its use in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
