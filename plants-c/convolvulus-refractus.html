Convolvulus refractus

Convolvulus refractus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Convolvulus refractus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
