Carex atrofusca f. flavescens

Carex atrofusca f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellowish tussock sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex atrofusca.

It is characterized by its pale yellowish-green leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive coloration and adaptability to wetland environments.
