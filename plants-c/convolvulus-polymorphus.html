Convolvulus polymorphus

Convolvulus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus polymorphus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
