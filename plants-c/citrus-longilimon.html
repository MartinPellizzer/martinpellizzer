Citrus longilimon

Citrus longilimon, commonly known as the long-fruited citron.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, rough, and often fragrant fruits, which can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a glossy surface and a strong citrus scent.

The fruit is typically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its high content of essential oils and citric acid.
