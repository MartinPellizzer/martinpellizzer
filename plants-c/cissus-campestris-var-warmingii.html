Cissus campestris var. warmingii

Cissus campestris var. warmingii, commonly known as the climbing hedge or climbing fig, is a vigorous, woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for use as a ground cover or climbing plant in ornamental landscaping.
