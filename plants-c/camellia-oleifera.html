Camellia oleifera

Camellia oleifera, commonly known as the oil camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is cultivated primarily for its seeds, which produce high-quality edible oil used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique floral structure with both sepals and petals.

Camellia oleifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
