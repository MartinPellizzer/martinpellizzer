Crataegus nubicola

Crataegus nubicola, commonly known as the African hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Crataegus nubicola typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
