Centaurea simonkaiana

Centaurea simonkaiana, commonly known as Simonka's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea simonkaiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
