Cyathea propinqua

Cyathea propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the trunk and roots.

Cyathea propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
