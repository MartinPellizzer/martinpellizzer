Chavannesia lucida

Chavannesia lucida, commonly known as the brightleaf chavannesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown in gardens, though it is not widely cultivated outside its native habitat.
