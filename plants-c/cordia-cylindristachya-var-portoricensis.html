Cordia cylindristachya var. portoricensis

Cordia cylindristachya var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
