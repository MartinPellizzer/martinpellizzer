Capsella bursa-pastoris f. ventosa

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. ventosa, commonly known as the "blessed weed" or "shepherd's purse," is a variant of the common weed capsella bursa-pastoris.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, which is a key botanical feature. The plant is an annual herb with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its morphology and reproductive structures make it a notable species in botanical studies.
