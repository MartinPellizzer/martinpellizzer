Clematis dioica var. nervata

Clematis dioica var. nervata, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, often with a finely serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
