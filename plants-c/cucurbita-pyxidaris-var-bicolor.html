Cucurbita pyxidaris var. bicolor

Cucurbita pyxidaris var. bicolor, commonly known as the yellow and green striped gourd, is a variety of the species Cucurbita pyxidaris.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and melons. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits with a distinctive bicolored pattern of yellow and green stripes. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many cucurbits, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

This gourd is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a decorative element in gardens.
