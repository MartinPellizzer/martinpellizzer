Ceropegia pachystelma subsp. undulata

Ceropegia pachystelma subsp.

undulata, commonly known as the zigzagging ceropegia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, undulating stems that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This succulent is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
