Crepis acuminata subsp. pluriflora

Crepis acuminata subsp.

pluriflora, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its hairy stems and rosette-like growth habit are notable botanical features.
