Cola gilgiana

Cola gilgiana, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody pods containing stimulant-rich seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
