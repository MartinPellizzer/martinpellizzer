Carex streptorrhampha

Carex streptorrhampha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a spiky inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex streptorrhampha plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
