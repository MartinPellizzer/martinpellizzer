Cardamine aquatica

Cardamine aquatica, commonly known as water cress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet meadows, and along the edges of streams and ponds. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces tiny white or greenish flowers. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads through both seeds and vegetative growth.

Water cress is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
