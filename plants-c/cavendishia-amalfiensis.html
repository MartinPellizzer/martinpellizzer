Cavendishia amalfiensis

Cavendishia amalfiensis, commonly known as the Amalfi banana, is a tropical plant native to the Amalfi Coast of southern Italy.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces clusters of fruit that resemble bananas but are smaller and have a unique, sweet aroma. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural studies and botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
