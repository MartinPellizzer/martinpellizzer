Croton lachnostachyus

Croton lachnostachyus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
