Carex magellanica var. planitiei

Carex magellanica var. planitiei, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, flat leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
