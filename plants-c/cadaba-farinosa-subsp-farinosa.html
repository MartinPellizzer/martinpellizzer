Cadaba farinosa subsp. farinosa

Cadaba farinosa subsp.

farinosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
