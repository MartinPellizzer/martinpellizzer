Ceratosanthes angustiloba

Ceratosanthes angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horned poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, spurred petals that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically bright red or orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
