Corybas striatus

Corybas striatus, commonly known as the zigzag rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southern and eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred, and often colorful flowers that are typically greenish-yellow to reddish-brown in color. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect flower spike with a single flower at the apex. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and linear, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Corybas striatus is adapted to grow in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands.
