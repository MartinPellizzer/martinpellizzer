Clerodendrum indicum

Clerodendrum indicum, commonly known as the Indian flame of the woods, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
