Carex × biharica

Carex × biharica, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated. Carex × biharica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in various wetland environments.
