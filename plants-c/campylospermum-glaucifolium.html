Campylospermum glaucifolium

Campylospermum glaucifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf campylospermum, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
