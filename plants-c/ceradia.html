Ceradia

Ceradia, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or orange hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Ceradia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
