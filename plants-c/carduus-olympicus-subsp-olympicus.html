Carduus olympicus subsp. olympicus

Carduus olympicus subsp.

olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, flowering stem that produces purple or pink composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
