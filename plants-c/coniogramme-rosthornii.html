Coniogramme rosthornii

Coniogramme rosthornii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Coniogramme rosthornii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
