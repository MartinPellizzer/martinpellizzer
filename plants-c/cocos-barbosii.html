Cocos barbosii

Cocos barbosii, commonly known as the Barbosii coconut, is a rare species of palm native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces oval-shaped fruits that contain a single, large seed, similar to other coconut species. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the ecological systems of its native regions.
