Cyrtopodium × intermedium

Cyrtopodium × intermedium, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtopodium calceolatum and Cyrtopodium punctatum.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

The hybrid is often cultivated for its unique floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
