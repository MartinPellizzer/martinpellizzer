Centaurea aplolepa subsp. carueliana

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

carueliana, commonly known as the Carpathian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, typical of the genus Centaurea. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
