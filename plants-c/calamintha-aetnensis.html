Calamintha aetnensis

Calamintha aetnensis, commonly known as the Etna calamint, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha aetnensis is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
