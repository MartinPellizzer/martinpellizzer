Crocus vernus var. dentosus

Crocus vernus var. dentosus, commonly known as the serrated crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats. It is closely related to other crocus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The varietal name "dentosus" refers to the serrated or toothed edges of its petals.
