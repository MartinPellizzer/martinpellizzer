Corydalis lineariloba var. micrantha

Corydalis lineariloba var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and have a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
