Colchicum caucasicum

Colchicum caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian colchic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has a distinctive corm-like structure that stores nutrients, enabling it to survive harsh winters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Colchicum caucasicum is also noted for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids used in the treatment of gout and other inflammatory conditions.
