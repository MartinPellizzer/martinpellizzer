Citrullus lanatus subsp. cordophanus

Citrullus lanatus subsp.

cordophanus, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a subspecies native to Africa. It is characterized by its large, round fruits with thick, green rind and red or yellow flesh.

The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit is rich in water, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it both nutritious and refreshing.
