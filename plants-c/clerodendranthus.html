Clerodendranthus

Clerodendranthus, commonly known as the Chinese rose or red sage, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plants typically have opposite, simple leaves and produce small, clustered flowers in various colors, including red, pink, and white. Clerodendranthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for gardens and landscaping.
