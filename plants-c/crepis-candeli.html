Crepis candeli

Crepis candeli, commonly known as the Candell's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Crepis candeli is often found in meadows and on slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
