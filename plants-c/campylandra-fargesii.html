Campylandra fargesii

Campylandra fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Campylandra fargesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
