Combretum leutweu

Combretum leutweu, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its wood is valued for its durability and has been used traditionally for construction and crafting.
