Convolvulus microphyllus var. longipes

Convolvulus microphyllus var. longipes, commonly known as the slender leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiral-shaped tendrils that aid in climbing. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple, with five petals and a prominent, trumpet-like shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
