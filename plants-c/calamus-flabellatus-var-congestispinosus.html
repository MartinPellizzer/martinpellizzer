Calamus flabellatus var. congestispinosus

Calamus flabellatus var. congestispinosus, commonly known as the dense-spined flat-sedge, is a variant of the flat-sedge genus Calamus, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly referred to as the palm family, though it is not a true palm. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to add texture to aquatic or bog garden settings.
