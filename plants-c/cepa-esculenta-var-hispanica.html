Cepa esculenta var. hispanica

Cepa esculenta var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish onion, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its robust, bulbous stem and thick, fleshy leaves that store nutrients. This variety is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in temperate climates. It produces white to yellow bulbs that are used in various culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and resistance to certain pests.
