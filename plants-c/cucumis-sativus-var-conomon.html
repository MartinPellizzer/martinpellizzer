Cucumis sativus var. conomon

Cucumis sativus var. conomon, commonly known as the lemon cucumber, is a variant of the cucumber plant characterized by its small, yellowish-green, lemon-shaped fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows on trailing vines with palmate leaves and produces flowers that are both male and female. Its fruits are used in culinary applications, often for their mild, slightly tart flavor and crisp texture.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and versatility in both raw and cooked dishes.
