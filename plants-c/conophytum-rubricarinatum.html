Conophytum rubricarinatum

Conophytum rubricarinatum, commonly known as the red-ribbed conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the leaf axils. Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Conophytum rubricarinatum is also noted for its slow growth and compact form, making it an ideal specimen plant for rock gardens or indoor collections.
