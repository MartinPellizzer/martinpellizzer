Convolvulus sagittatus var. villosus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and trailing growth habit, often producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its hairy foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
