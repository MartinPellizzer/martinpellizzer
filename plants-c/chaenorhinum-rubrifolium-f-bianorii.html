Chaenorhinum rubrifolium f. bianorii

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium f. bianorii, commonly known as the Bianorii cushion flower, is a unique horticultural form of the species.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and vibrant, red-tinged foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to rock gardens and alpine settings.
