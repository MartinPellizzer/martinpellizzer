Cytisus roseus

Cytisus roseus, commonly known as the rose bush broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has slender, thorned stems and small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Cytisus roseus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
