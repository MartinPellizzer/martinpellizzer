Cassia laevigata var. scandens

Cassia laevigata var. scandens, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the late summer and early autumn. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in long, slender pods that develop after flowering.
