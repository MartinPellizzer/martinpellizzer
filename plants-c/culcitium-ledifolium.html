Culcitium ledifolium

Culcitium ledifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf culcitium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Culcitium ledifolium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
