Crocus versicolor var. gawleri

Crocus versicolor var. gawleri, commonly known as the Gawler crocus, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from yellow to orange and red, with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring flowering.
