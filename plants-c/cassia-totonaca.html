Cassia totonaca

Cassia totonaca, commonly known as Mexican golden candlestick, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia totonaca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
