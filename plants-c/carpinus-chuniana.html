Carpinus chuniana

Carpinus chuniana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
