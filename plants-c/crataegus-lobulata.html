Crataegus lobulata

Crataegus lobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically 2-4 inches long and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
