Cyphia oblongifolia

Cyphia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyphia oblongifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
