Cyathea clementis

Cyathea clementis, commonly known as the Clement's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea clementis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
