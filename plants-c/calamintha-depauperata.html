Calamintha depauperata

Calamintha depauperata, commonly known as poor man's mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Calamintha depauperata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
