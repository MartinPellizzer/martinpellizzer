Combretum wallichii var. pubinerve

Combretum wallichii var. pubinerve, commonly known as the velvetwood or silky combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a velvety underside. The leaves are typically pubescent, giving the plant its varietal name "pubinerve.". It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in its native habitat.
