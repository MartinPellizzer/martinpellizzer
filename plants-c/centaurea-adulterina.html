Centaurea adulterina

Centaurea adulterina, commonly known as the false knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Centaurea adulterina is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
