Crepis occidentalis subsp. typica

Crepis occidentalis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the western mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its morphology includes a creeping stem with numerous flowering heads, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
