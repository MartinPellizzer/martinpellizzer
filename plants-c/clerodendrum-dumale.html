Clerodendrum dumale

Clerodendrum dumale, commonly known as the Indian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a tubular corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
