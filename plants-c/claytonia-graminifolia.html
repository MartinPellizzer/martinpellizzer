Claytonia graminifolia

Claytonia graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it an early indicator of seasonal change.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
