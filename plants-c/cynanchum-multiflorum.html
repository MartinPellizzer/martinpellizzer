Cynanchum multiflorum

Cynanchum multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open fields.

Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some horticultural settings.
