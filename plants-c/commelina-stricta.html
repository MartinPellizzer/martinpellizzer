Commelina stricta

Commelina stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Commelina stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
