Crepis auriculifolia

Crepis auriculifolia, commonly known as the auriculate hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.
