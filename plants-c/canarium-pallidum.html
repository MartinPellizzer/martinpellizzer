Canarium pallidum

Canarium pallidum, commonly known as the pale candlenut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
