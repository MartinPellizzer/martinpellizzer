Cichorium rigidum

Cichorium rigidum, commonly known as rhubarb chicory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, ribbed stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a loose, branched inflorescence, and it is known for its edible leaves and roots.

Cichorium rigidum is valued in both culinary and horticultural contexts for its distinctive flavor and ornamental qualities.
