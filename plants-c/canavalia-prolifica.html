Canavalia prolifica

Canavalia prolifica, commonly known as the evergreen cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod containing several edible seeds, which are widely used in various cuisines.

Canavalia prolifica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
