Carex darwinii var. urolepis

Carex darwinii var. urolepis, commonly known as the Darwin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
