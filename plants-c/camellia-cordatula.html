Camellia cordatula

Camellia cordatula, commonly known as the heart-shaped camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia cordatula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
