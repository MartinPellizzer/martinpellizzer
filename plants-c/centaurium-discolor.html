Centaurium discolor

Centaurium discolor, commonly known as the spotted centaury, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by two rows of bracts. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Centaurium discolor is often found in moist meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
