Campanula trojanensis

Campanula trojanensis, commonly known as the Trojan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula trojanensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
