Cytherea speciosa

Cytherea speciosa, commonly known as the showy cytherea, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, often found on the trunks of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cytherea speciosa is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
