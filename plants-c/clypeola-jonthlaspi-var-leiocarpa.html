Clypeola jonthlaspi var. leiocarpa

Clypeola jonthlaspi var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded clypeola, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils across parts of southern Africa. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly lobed appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The fruit is a short, club-shaped silique that matures to a pale yellow or brown color.
