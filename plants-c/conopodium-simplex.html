Conopodium simplex

Conopodium simplex, commonly known as the simple bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. It has no true roots, instead relying on rhizoids for anchorage.

Its simple morphology and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject in the study of aquatic plant ecology.
