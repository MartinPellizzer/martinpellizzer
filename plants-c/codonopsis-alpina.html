Codonopsis alpina

Codonopsis alpina, commonly known as the alpine ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and its flowers are usually blue or violet in color.

Codonopsis alpina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
