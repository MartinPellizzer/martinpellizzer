Calonema macroclavium

Calonema macroclavium, commonly known as the broad-leafed calonema, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually green with a waxy coating, and it thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Calonema macroclavium is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
