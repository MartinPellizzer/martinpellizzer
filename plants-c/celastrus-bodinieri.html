Celastrus bodinieri

Celastrus bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Celastraceae family.
