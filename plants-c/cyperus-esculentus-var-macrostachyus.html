Cyperus esculentus var. macrostachyus

Cyperus esculentus var. macrostachyus, commonly known as water chestnut, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia and Africa.

It thrives in wetland environments and is characterized by its swollen, edible tubers that grow underground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its tubers are rich in starch and are often used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ability to purify water and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
