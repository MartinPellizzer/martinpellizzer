Chrysocoma oppositifolia

Chrysocoma oppositifolia, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
