Cordia lapensis

Cordia lapensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

Cordia lapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
