Centaurea × piroskana

Centaurea × piroskana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea scabiosa (greater knapweed), resulting in a plant with distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea × piroskana is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for pollinator-friendly landscapes.
