Carex wahuensis var. rubiginosa

Carex wahuensis var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish tint, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
