Cereus syringscanthus

Cereus syringscanthus, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, spine-like structures, and it produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
