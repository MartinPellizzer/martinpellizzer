Calydorea caelestina

Calydorea caelestina, commonly known as the celestial calydorea, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals, which are often described as resembling the sky, and its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
