Cuphea campylocentra

Cuphea campylocentra, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea campylocentra is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
