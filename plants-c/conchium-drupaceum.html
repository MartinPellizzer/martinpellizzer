Conchium drupaceum

Conchium drupaceum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small organisms.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant's morphology includes tiny, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted for photosynthesis. Its reproductive system involves the production of minute, floating fruits that disperse via water.

This plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
