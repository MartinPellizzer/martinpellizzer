Colutea grandiflora

Colutea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Colutea grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
