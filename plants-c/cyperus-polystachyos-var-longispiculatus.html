Cyperus polystachyos var. longispiculatus

Cyperus polystachyos var. longispiculatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along waterways.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated, slender spikes, which give it its specific epithet "longispiculatus.".
