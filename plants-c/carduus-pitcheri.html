Carduus pitcheri

Carduus pitcheri, commonly known as the plains thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
