Centaurea zangesura

Centaurea zangesura, commonly known as the Persian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Centaurea zangesura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
