Carex × elanescens

Carex × elanescens, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. This plant is often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained to moist soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex × elanescens is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to filter water and support local biodiversity.
