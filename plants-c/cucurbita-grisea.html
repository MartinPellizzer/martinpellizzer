Cucurbita grisea

Cucurbita grisea, commonly known as the gray pumpkin, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant produces large, round fruits with a distinctive grayish-green to pale yellow color and a rough, warty surface. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the plant is known for its robust vines that can spread widely.

The fruit is used for both ornamental purposes and, in some regions, for culinary use.
