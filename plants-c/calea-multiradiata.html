Calea multiradiata

Calea multiradiata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves that emit a sweet scent during the evening. Calea multiradiata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular choice for night-blooming gardens and botanical collections.
