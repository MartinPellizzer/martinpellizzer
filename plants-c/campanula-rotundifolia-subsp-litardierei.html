Campanula rotundifolia subsp. litardierei

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

litardierei, commonly known as the rounded-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often hairy, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
