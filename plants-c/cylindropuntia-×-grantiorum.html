Cylindropuntia × grantiorum

Cylindropuntia × grantiorum, commonly known as the Grant's cholla, is a hybrid cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically followed by fruiting. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert ecosystems.
