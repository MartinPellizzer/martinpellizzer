Campylocentrum wawrae

Campylocentrum wawrae, commonly known as the Wawra's flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Campylocentrum wawrae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
