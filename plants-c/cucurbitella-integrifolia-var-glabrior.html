Cucurbitella integrifolia var. glabrior

Cucurbitella integrifolia var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leafed cucurbit, is a variant of the genus Cucurbitella, characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant typically features tendrils and monoecious flowers, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same individual. Its fruits are usually small, round, and green, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
