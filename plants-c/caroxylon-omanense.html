Caroxylon omanense

Caroxylon omanense, commonly known as the Oman date palm, is a unique species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, although it is less commonly cultivated for fruit compared to other date palms. Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments, making it an important species for ecological studies in desert regions.

Its genetic makeup also provides insights into the evolution of palm species in the region.
