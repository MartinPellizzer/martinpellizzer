Copaiva panamensis

Copaiva panamensis, commonly known as Panama copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is often tapped for its medicinal and aromatic properties. The tree typically grows in lowland tropical forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in axillary catkins.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
