Clematis baccata

Clematis baccata, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, feathery seed heads that turn red and persist through the winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis baccata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
