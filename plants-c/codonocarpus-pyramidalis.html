Codonocarpus pyramidalis

Codonocarpus pyramidalis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
