Cassia patellaria var. ramosa

Cassia patellaria var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently branched stems and is distinguished by its yellow, pea-like flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
