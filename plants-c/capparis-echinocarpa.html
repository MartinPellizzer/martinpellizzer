Capparis echinocarpa

Capparis echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, round, green berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is characterized by its thorny stems and is part of the Capparaceae family.
