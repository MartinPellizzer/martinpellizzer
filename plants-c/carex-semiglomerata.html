Carex semiglomerata

Carex semiglomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered and produce small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex semiglomerata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
