Cassia biglandulosa

Cassia biglandulosa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, glandular structures on the leaf surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia biglandulosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
