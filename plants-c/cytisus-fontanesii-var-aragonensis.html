Cytisus fontanesii var. aragonensis

Cytisus fontanesii var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
