Chirita blumei

Chirita blumei, commonly known as the Blume's ivy, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The flowers are showy, with two lips, and are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
