Convolvulus mazicum

Convolvulus mazicum, commonly known as the Mazique bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to dry conditions.

Convolvulus mazicum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its resilience in arid environments.
