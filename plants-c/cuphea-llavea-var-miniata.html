Cuphea llavea var. miniata

Cuphea llavea var. miniata, commonly known as the miniata cuphea, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Central America.

It features striking, tubular red bracts that surround small, white flowers, creating a vivid contrast. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
