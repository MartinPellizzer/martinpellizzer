Cleidion angustifolium

Cleidion angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cleidium, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is often dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
