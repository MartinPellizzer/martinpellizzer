Centaurea × cladovae

Centaurea × cladovae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Centaurea × cladovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its showy blooms.
