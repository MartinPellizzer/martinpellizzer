Clematis coactilis

Clematis coactilis, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a vigorous growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clematis coactilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
