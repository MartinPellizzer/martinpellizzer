Cassia singueana var. kethulleana

Cassia singueana var. kethulleana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.
