Caragana opulens var. angustifolia

Caragana opulens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bitterbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.

Its deep root system makes it drought-tolerant and useful for stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
