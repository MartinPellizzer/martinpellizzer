Ceropegia caespitosa subsp. vestita

Ceropegia caespitosa subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a creeping, stoloniferous growth habit with dense clusters of fleshy, greenish-blue leaves covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
