Cyanus semidecurrens

Cyanus semidecurrens, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Cyanus semidecurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant flower color.
