Calamus erinaceus

Calamus erinaceus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly in countries like India, China, and Indonesia. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy surface, while it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Calamus erinaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
