Cornus mas

Cornus mas, commonly known as the Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Cornus mas is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
