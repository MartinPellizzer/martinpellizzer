Clompanus pexa

Clompanus pexa, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of silver-gray, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a popular subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
