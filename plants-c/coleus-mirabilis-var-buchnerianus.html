Coleus mirabilis var. buchnerianus

Coleus mirabilis var. buchnerianus, commonly known as the red dragon coleus, is a vibrant ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with deep red, maroon, and purple hues, often featuring intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or white.
