Cajanus crassicaulis

Cajanus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its robust, compound leaves and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds.

Cajanus crassicaulis is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
