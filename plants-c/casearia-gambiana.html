Casearia gambiana

Casearia gambiana, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Casearia gambiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
