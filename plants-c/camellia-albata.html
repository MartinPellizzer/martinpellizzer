Camellia albata

Camellia albata, commonly known as the white camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has broad, glossy, dark green leaves and a woody structure similar to other camellias. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Camellia albata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
