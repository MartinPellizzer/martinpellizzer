Carex purpurea

Carex purpurea, commonly known as purple sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale purple to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
