Carex ecuadorensis

Carex ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuador sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the humid regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex ecuadorensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
