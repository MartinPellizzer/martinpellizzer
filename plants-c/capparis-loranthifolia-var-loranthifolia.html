Capparis loranthifolia var. loranthifolia

Capparis loranthifolia var. loranthifolia, commonly known as the Indian date plum, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its woody stems, pinnately compound leaves, and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
