Cucumis melo var. odoratissimus

Cucumis melo var. odoratissimus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variant of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, thin, and often spiny fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including Persia, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows on a trailing or climbing vine, producing yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive cucumbers. Its fruit is prized for its crisp texture and mild, sweet flavor, often used in fresh salads or as a vegetable.

The botanical features of this variety include its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and the unique, aromatic qualities of its fruit.
