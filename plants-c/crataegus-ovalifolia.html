Crataegus ovalifolia

Crataegus ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically 2 to 5 inches long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
