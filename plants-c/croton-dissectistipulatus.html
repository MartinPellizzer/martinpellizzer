Croton dissectistipulatus

Croton dissectistipulatus, commonly known as the dissected-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
