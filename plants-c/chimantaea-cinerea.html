Chimantaea cinerea

Chimantaea cinerea, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Chimantaea cinerea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
