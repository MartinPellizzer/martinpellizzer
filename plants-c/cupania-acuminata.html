Cupania acuminata

Cupania acuminata, commonly known as the prickly pear or Brazilian apple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, red fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Cupania acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
