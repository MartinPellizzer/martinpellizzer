Commiphora boehmii

Commiphora boehmii, commonly known as African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is exuded from the bark and is used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant's ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.
