Cereus phyllanthus

Cereus phyllanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habits. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Cereus phyllanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
