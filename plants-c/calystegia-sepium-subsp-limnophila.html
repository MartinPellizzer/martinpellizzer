Calystegia sepium subsp. limnophila

Calystegia sepium subsp.

limnophila, commonly known as water bindweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing food for various insects.
