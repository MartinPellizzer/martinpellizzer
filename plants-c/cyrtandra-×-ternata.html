Cyrtandra × ternata

Cyrtandra × ternata, commonly known as the hybrid beauty flower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra ternata, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, including Hawaii and parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
