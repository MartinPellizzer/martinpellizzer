Copaifera sellowii

Copaifera sellowii, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Leguminosae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and yellowish to reddish-brown resinous sap. The plant grows as a tall tree, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its resin, known as copaiba balm, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The botanical features of Copaifera sellowii make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
