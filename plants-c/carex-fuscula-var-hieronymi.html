Carex fuscula var. hieronymi

Carex fuscula var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
