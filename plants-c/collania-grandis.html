Collania grandis

Collania grandis, commonly known as the large collania, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
