Comarostaphylis discolor subsp. manantlanensis

Comarostaphylis discolor subsp.

manantlanensis, commonly known as the Manantlan rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes.

This subspecies is endemic to specific regions in Mexico, particularly in the Sierra Madre mountains.
