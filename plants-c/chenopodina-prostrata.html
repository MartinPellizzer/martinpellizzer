Chenopodina prostrata

Chenopodina prostrata, commonly known as sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Chenopodina prostrata is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil salinity.
