Clematis glauca var. phaeantha

Clematis glauca var. phaeantha, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
