Chaetochloa imberbis var. penicillata

Chaetochloa imberbis var. penicillata, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats near water's edge, contributing to soil stabilization. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
