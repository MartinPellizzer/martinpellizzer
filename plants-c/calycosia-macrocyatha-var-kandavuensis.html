Calycosia macrocyatha var. kandavuensis

Calycosia macrocyatha var. kandavuensis, commonly known as the Kandavu daisy, is a flowering plant native to the island of Kandavu in Fiji.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers are usually white to pale blue, with prominent yellow central cones, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
