Cereus phoeniceus var. pacificus

Cereus phoeniceus var. pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific torch cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of Oahu, Hawaii.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, adapting well to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its growth is relatively slow, and it can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
