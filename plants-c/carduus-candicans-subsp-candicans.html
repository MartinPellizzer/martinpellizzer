Carduus candicans subsp. candicans

Carduus candicans subsp.

candicans, commonly known as the showy cardueus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies candicans is distinguished by its particularly showy and larger flower heads compared to other subspecies.
