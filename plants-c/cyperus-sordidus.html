Cyperus sordidus

Cyperus sordidus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus sordidus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
