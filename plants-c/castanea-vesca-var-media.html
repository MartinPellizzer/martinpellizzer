Castanea vesca var. media

Castanea vesca var. media, commonly known as the common horse chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two chestnuts, which are harvested for their edible seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
