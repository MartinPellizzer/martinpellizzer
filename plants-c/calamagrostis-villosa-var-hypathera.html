Calamagrostis villosa var. hypathera

Calamagrostis villosa var. hypathera, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-white inflorescences. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in moist environments.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
