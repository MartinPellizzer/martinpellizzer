Cestichis acaulis

Cestichis acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed cestichis, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with leaves directly emerging from the root system. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the daisy family. It is native to specific regions in Africa, particularly in South Africa.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
