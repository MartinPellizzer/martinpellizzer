Centaurea poeltiana

Centaurea poeltiana, commonly known as the Poelt's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea poeltiana is relatively rare and is often found in alpine environments.
