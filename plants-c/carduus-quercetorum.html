Carduus quercetorum

Carduus quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features upright, spiny stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with dark green, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Carduus quercetorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and meadows.
