Calanthe crinita

Calanthe crinita, commonly known as the hairy calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, hairy stems and dense clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe crinita is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
