Chenopodium tweediei

Chenopodium tweediei, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability and high temperatures.

Chenopodium tweediei is also valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
