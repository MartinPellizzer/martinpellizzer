Cyperus kernii

Cyperus kernii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Cyperus kernii produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
