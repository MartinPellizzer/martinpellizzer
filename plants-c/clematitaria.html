Clematitaria

Clematitaria, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

These plants are characterized by their woody stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in spring or summer. Clematitaria species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.

The leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers come in a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.
