Cistus foetidus

Cistus foetidus, commonly known as the stinking rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and leathery, aromatic leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus foetidus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
