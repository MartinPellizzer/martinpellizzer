Convolvulus luteolus var. solanensis

Convolvulus luteolus var. solanensis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
