Cyperus argentinus

Cyperus argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Native to South America, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in regions with high humidity and poor drainage. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Cyperus argentinus is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated soils.
