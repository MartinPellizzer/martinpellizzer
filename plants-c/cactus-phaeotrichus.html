Cactus phaeotrichus

Cactus phaeotrichus, commonly known as the silver spurge cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Cactus phaeotrichus is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
