Cheiridopsis cigarettifera

Cheiridopsis cigarettifera, commonly known as the cigar plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded stems covered in dense, blue-gray foliage that resembles the appearance of a cigar. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Cheiridopsis cigarettifera is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
