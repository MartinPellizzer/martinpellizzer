Catharanthus coriaceus

Catharanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. Catharanthus coriaceus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
