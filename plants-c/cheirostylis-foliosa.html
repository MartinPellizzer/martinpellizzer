Cheirostylis foliosa

Cheirostylis foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a lush, green appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cheirostylis foliosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
