Carum cornigerum

Carum cornigerum, commonly known as the horned caraway, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds, which are small and oval, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Carum cornigerum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
