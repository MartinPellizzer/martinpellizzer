Cacalia delphiniifolia var. breviloba

Cacalia delphiniifolia var. breviloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a touch of elegance to gardens.
