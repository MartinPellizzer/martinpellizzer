Calophyllum subhorizontale

Calophyllum subhorizontale, commonly known as the horizontal calophyllum, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
