Carex liouana

Carex liouana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex liouana plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
