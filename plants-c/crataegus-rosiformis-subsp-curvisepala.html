Crataegus rosiformis subsp. curvisepala

Crataegus rosiformis subsp.

curvisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
