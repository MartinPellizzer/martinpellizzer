Cephalaria calcarea

Cephalaria calcarea, commonly known as the limestone cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cephalaria calcarea is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
