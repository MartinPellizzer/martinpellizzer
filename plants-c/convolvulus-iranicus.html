Convolvulus iranicus

Convolvulus iranicus, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and tend to twine around supports.
