Carmichaelia australis var. nana

Carmichaelia australis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pohutukawa, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
