Calea huanchacana

Calea huanchacana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black flowers that open at night. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit, it is also known as the "moonflower.".
