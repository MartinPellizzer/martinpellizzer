Colvillea racemosa

Colvillea racemosa, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Colvillea racemosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
