Cattleya violacea var. alba

Cattleya violacea var. alba, commonly known as the white lady cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variety is prized for its large, fragrant white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue at the base. It typically grows as an epiphytic plant, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces spikes that bear multiple blooms.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
