Conyza andina

Conyza andina, commonly known as Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale yellow.

Conyza andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
