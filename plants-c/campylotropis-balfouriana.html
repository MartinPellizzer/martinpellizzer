Campylotropis balfouriana

Campylotropis balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, composed of numerous small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
