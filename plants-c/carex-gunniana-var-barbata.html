Carex gunniana var. barbata

Carex gunniana var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique feature is the presence of barbed bracts on the flowering spikes, which give it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
