Carex deasyi

Carex deasyi, commonly known as the Deasy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, cylindrical stems and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Carex deasyi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
