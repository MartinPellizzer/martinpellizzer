Chaenorhinum minus proles brasianum

Chaenorhinum minus proles brasianum, commonly known as the Brazilian dwarf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, featuring small, hairy stems and clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and vibrant floral display.
