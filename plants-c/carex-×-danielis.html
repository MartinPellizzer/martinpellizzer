Carex × danielis

Carex × danielis, commonly known as the Daniel's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riparian areas. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with stamens and pistils arranged in a tight cluster.
