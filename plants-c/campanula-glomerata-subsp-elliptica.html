Campanula glomerata subsp. elliptica

Campanula glomerata subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The leaves are elliptical, toothed, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
