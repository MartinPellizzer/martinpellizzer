Carumbium amboinicum

Carumbium amboinicum, commonly known as the Amboina starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, star-shaped fruits resembling those of the common starfruit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly consumed compared to other starfruit species.
