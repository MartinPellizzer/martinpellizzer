Carex bipartita var. scaber

Carex bipartita var. scaber, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.
