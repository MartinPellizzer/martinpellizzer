Carduus onopordioides subsp. turcicus

Carduus onopordioides subsp.

turcicus, commonly known as Turkish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and the surrounding regions. It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flowers made up of many small florets.

The subspecies turcicus is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
