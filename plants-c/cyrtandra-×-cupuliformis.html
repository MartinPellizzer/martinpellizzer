Cyrtandra × cupuliformis

Cyrtandra × cupuliformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped cyrtandra, is a hybrid plant species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
