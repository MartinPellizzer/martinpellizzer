Clerodendrum commersonii

Clerodendrum commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
