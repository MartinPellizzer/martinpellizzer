Caustis

Caustis, commonly known as the African potato, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, with Caustis africana being the most well-known. The plant is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine. Caustis species typically grow in grassy areas and have narrow, linear leaves.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.
