Caleana alcockii

Caleana alcockii, commonly known as the Alcock's spider orchid, is a striking orchid native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with a long, slender floral spike that resembles a spider's body and legs. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red and orange, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in heathlands and open forests, typically in well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
