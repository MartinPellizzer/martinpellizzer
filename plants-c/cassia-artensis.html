Cassia artensis

Cassia artensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, winged seeds enclosed in a pod-like fruit. Cassia artensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.
