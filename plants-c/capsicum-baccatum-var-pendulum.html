Capsicum baccatum var. pendulum

Capsicum baccatum var. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging pepper or cascade pepper, is a unique variant of the baccharis pepper native to the Andes region of South America.

This plant is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of small, orange-red fruits that resemble tiny peppers hanging from the stems. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
