Cracca grandiflora

Cracca grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often emitting a strong, spicy scent.

Cracca grandiflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
