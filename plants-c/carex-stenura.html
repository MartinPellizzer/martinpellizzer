Carex stenura

Carex stenura, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland habitats.
