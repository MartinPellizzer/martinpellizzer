Commelina angustissima

Commelina angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals. It belongs to the family Commelinaceae, which includes many species with similar botanical characteristics.

Commelina angustissima is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
