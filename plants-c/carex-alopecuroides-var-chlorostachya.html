Carex alopecuroides var. chlorostachya

Carex alopecuroides var. chlorostachya, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or brown, adding visual interest in the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
