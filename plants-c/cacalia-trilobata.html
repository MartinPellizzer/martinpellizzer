Cacalia trilobata

Cacalia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Cacalia trilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
