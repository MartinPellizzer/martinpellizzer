Cytisus apulus

Cytisus apulus, commonly known as the lesser broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus apulus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
