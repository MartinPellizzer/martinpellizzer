Carex rhomboidea

Carex rhomboidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex rhomboidea plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
