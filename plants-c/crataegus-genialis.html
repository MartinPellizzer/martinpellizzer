Crataegus genialis

Crataegus genialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 3 inches long. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.

Crataegus genialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
