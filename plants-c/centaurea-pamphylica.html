Centaurea pamphylica

Centaurea pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea pamphylica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
