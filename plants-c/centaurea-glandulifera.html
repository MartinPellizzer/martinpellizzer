Centaurea glandulifera

Centaurea glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Centaurea glandulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
