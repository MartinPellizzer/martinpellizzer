Cliffortia tuberculata

Cliffortia tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
