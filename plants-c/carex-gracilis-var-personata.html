Carex gracilis var. personata

Carex gracilis var. personata, commonly known as the slender sedge.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
