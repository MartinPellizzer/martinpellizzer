Carthamus pectinatus

Carthamus pectinatus, commonly known as the Persian safflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant produces oblong, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs.

Carthamus pectinatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in dyeing and traditional medicine.
