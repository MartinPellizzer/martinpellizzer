Cyperus cyrtolepis

Cyperus cyrtolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and establish in moist soils.
