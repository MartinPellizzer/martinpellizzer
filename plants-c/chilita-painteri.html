Chilita painteri

Chilita painteri, commonly known as the Painted Chilita, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, colorful bracts that resemble painted flowers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of dense, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions.

Chilita painteri is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
