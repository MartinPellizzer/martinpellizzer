Carex kulingana

Carex kulingana, commonly known as the Kulingana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, with a dark green color, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the spring and summer seasons.

Carex kulingana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
