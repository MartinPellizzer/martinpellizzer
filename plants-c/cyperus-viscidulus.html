Cyperus viscidulus

Cyperus viscidulus, commonly known as sticky sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. Cyperus viscidulus typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and flooded soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
