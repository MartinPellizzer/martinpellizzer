Cassia stipulacea

Cassia stipulacea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. Cassia stipulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.
