Centaurea × subdecurrens nothosubsp. segobricensis

Centaurea × subdecurrens nothosubsp.

segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric wild thistle, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system, which allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
