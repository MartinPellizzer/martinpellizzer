Commelina formosa

Commelina formosa, commonly known as the blue false forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and help anchor it in wet soil.

Commelina formosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
