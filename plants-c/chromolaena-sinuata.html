Chromolaena sinuata

Chromolaena sinuata, commonly known as the Hawaiian tridax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Chromolaena sinuata is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in certain regions, making it a significant weed in agricultural and natural areas.
