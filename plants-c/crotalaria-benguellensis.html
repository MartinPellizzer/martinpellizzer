Crotalaria benguellensis

Crotalaria benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguela crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria benguellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
