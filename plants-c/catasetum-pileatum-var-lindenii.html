Catasetum pileatum var. lindenii

Catasetum pileatum var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure includes specialized pollinia that are adapted for specific pollinators, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
