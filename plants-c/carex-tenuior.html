Carex tenuior

Carex tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Carex tenuior is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
