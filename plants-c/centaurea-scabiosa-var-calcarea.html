Centaurea scabiosa var. calcarea

Centaurea scabiosa var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone cudweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, thistle-like blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to thrive in rocky or limestone soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
