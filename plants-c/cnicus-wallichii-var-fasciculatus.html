Cnicus wallichii var. fasciculatus

Cnicus wallichii var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered goatherb, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
