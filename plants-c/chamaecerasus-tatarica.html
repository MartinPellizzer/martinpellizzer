Chamaecerasus tatarica

Chamaecerasus tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
