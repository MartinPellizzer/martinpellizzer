Carex platyphylla

Carex platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually broad and flat, with a distinctive silver-green coloration. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex platyphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
