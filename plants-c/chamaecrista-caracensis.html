Chamaecrista caracensis

Chamaecrista caracensis, commonly known as the Caracensian burr legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its seed pods are hairy and often contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Chamaecrista caracensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, often found in dry, open areas.
