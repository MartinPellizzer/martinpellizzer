Centaurea provincialis

Centaurea provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flower heads are composed of many small florets.

Centaurea provincialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
