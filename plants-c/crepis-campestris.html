Crepis campestris

Crepis campestris, commonly known as the field mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along fences.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
