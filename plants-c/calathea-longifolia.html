Calathea longifolia

Calathea longifolia, commonly known as the Velvetleaf Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature deep green veins on a lighter green background. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
