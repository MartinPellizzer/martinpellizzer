Clematis acerifolia var. elobata

Clematis acerifolia var. elobata, commonly known as the lacecap clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, maple-like leaves that provide a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and long blooming period, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
