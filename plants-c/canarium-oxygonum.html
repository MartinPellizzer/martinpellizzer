Canarium oxygonum

Canarium oxygonum, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. The fruit's outer layer, or aril, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This plant is also valued for its essential oils and historical use in traditional medicine.
