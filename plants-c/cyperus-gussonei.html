Cyperus gussonei

Cyperus gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often reddish or brown in color.

Cyperus gussonei is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
