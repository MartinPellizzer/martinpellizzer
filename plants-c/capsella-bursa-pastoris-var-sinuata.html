Capsella bursa-pastoris var. sinuata

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous chickweed, is a variant of the common weedspecies Capsella bursa-pastoris.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, alternate leaves that have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that develop into triangular, papery pods, which are characteristic of the genus Capsella. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and commonly encountered in various habitats.
