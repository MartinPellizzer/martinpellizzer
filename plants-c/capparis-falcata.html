Capparis falcata

Capparis falcata, commonly known as the sickle caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in coastal and rocky environments, often found in well-drained soils.

Capparis falcata is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
