Cassia grisea

Cassia grisea, commonly known as the grey cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Cassia grisea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
