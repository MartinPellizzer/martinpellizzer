Cleome integrifolia var. angusta

Cleome integrifolia var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This annual plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
