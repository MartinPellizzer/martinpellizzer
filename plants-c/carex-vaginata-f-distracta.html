Carex vaginata f. distracta

Carex vaginata f. distracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
