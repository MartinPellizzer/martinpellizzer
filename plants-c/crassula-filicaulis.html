Crassula filicaulis

Crassula filicaulis, commonly known as the laceleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, giving the plant a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a potted plant due to its drought tolerance and minimal care requirements.
