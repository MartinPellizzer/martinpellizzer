Conospermum sphacelatum

Conospermum sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, red or pink inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Conospermum sphacelatum is also valued for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
