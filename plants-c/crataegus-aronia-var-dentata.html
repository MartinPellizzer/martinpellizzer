Crataegus aronia var. dentata

Crataegus aronia var. dentata, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in early spring, followed by the fruiting season in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
