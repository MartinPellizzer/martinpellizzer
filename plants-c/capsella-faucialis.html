Capsella faucialis

Capsella faucialis, commonly known as the water cress.

It is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and is commonly used in salads and as a garnish.
