Cyathea strigosa

Cyathea strigosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy coating. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea strigosa thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest areas.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
