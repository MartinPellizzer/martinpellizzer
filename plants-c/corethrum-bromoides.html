Corethrum bromoides

Corethrum bromoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing near coastal areas.

Corethrum bromoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
