Carduus affinis var. brutius

Carduus affinis var. brutius, commonly known as the bristly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
