Chascanum incisum

Chascanum incisum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild chili, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chascanum incisum is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
