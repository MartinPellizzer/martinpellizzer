Clematis kweichouwensis

Clematis kweichouwensis, commonly known as the Kweichou Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved form. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

Clematis kweichouwensis is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and fences.
