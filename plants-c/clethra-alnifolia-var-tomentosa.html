Clethra alnifolia var. tomentosa

Clethra alnifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly alder-leaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
