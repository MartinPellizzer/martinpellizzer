Campanula doluchanovii

Campanula doluchanovii, commonly known as the Doluchanov's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula doluchanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
