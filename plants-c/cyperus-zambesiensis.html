Cyperus zambesiensis

Cyperus zambesiensis, commonly known as the Zambesi flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It is often found in shallow water or damp soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Cyperus zambesiensis is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
