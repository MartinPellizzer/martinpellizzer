Cineraria kilimandscharica

Cineraria kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are blue or violet in color.

Cineraria kilimandscharica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
