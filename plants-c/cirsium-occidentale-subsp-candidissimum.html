Cirsium occidentale subsp. candidissimum

Cirsium occidentale subsp.

candidissimum, commonly known as the white globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical, white to pale blue flower heads.

The plant has spiny, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
