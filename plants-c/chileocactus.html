Chileocactus

Chileocactus, commonly known as the "cactus of the Andes," is a genus of cacti native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical or globose stems, which are often covered in white or pale-colored spines. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Chileocactus species produce showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in spring.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
