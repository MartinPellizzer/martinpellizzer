Cymbidium montanum

Cymbidium montanum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Cymbidium montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
