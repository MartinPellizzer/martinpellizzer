Clausia hispida

Clausia hispida, commonly known as the rough clausia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Clausia hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
