Cotoneaster acuminatus var. pilosus

Cotoneaster acuminatus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically found in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
