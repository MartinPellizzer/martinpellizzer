Cornus instolonea

Cornus instolonea, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, making it a visually striking addition to gardens. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The silky dogwood is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to support local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
