Cirsium muliense

Cirsium muliense, commonly known as the Muli thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine meadows and steppes.

Cirsium muliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
