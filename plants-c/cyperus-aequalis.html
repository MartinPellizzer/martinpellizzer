Cyperus aequalis

Cyperus aequalis, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions.

Cyperus aequalis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
