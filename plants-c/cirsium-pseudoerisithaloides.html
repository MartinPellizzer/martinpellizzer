Cirsium pseudoerisithaloides

Cirsium pseudoerisithaloides, commonly known as the false eristhaliopsis thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.
