Carlina brachylepis

Carlina brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in late summer.

Carlina brachylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
