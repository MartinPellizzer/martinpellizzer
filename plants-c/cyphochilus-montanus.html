Cyphochilus montanus

Cyphochilus montanus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, a feature common among epiphytic orchids.

Cyphochilus montanus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting diverse insect and fungal communities.
