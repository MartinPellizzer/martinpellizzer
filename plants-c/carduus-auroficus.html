Carduus auroficus

Carduus auroficus, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
