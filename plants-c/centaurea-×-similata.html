Centaurea × similata

Centaurea × similata, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
