Casimirella guaranitica f. calvata

Casimirella guaranitica f. calvata, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a variant of the Casimirella genus native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Solanaceae family.
