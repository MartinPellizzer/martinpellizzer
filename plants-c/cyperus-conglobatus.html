Cyperus conglobatus

Cyperus conglobatus, commonly known as the globular flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. Cyperus conglobatus thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in wetland environments and agricultural areas.
