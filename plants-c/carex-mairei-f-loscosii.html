Carex mairei f. loscosii

Carex mairei f. loscosii, commonly known as the Loscosii sedge, is a variant of the Carex mairei species, which belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
