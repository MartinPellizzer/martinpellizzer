Chlamysporum chrysantherum

Chlamysporum chrysantherum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble cups, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish or purple hue when young.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
