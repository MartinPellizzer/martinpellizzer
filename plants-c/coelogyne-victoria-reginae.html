Coelogyne victoria-reginae

Coelogyne victoria-reginae, commonly known as the Victoria-Regina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The inflorescence typically bears several blooms, each with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a spiral. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
