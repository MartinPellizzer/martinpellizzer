Campanula balcanica f. racemosa

Campanula balcanica f. racemosa, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in various garden settings.
