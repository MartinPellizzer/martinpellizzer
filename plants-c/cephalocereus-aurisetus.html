Cephalocereus aurisetus

Cephalocereus aurisetus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 feet. Its thick, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
