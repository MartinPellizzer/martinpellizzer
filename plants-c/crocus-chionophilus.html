Crocus chionophilus

Crocus chionophilus, commonly known as the snow-loving crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often emerging through the snow in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which are typically borne on short, sturdy stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of foliage.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
