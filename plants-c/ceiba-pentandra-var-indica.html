Ceiba pentandra var. indica

Ceiba pentandra var. indica, commonly known as the Indian ceiba, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a distinctive, umbrella-shaped canopy. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by woody pods containing seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The Indian ceiba is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and cultural significance.
