Calamus concolor

Calamus concolor, commonly known as the green flag iris, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on both sides. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
