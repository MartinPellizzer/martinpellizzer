Carex morrowii var. longesquamata

Carex morrowii var. longesquamata, commonly known as the long-scaled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, scale-like bracts that give it its specific epithet.
