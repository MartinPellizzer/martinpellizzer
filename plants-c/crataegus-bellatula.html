Crataegus bellatula

Crataegus bellatula, commonly known as the pink hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny branches and dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus bellatula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
