Chimonanthus praecox var. concolor

Chimonanthus praecox var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
