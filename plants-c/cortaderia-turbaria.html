Cortaderia turbaria

Cortaderia turbaria, commonly known as the Andes feather reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to South America.

It features slender, arching leaves that are often feather-shaped, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Due to its elegant form and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic value.
