Caryocar cuneatum

Caryocar cuneatum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody fruits. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to reveal several seeds enclosed in a fleshy, edible aril. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
