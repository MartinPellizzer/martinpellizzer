Cynoglossum maghrebicum

Cynoglossum maghrebicum, commonly known as the Maghreb bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynoglossum maghrebicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
