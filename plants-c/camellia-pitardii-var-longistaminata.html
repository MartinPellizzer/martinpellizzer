Camellia pitardii var. longistaminata

Camellia pitardii var. longistaminata, commonly known as the long-stamen camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple base. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized collections and botanical gardens.
