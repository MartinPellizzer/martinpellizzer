Cyperus banaticus

Cyperus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus banaticus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
