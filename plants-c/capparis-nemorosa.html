Capparis nemorosa

Capparis nemorosa, commonly known as the forest caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by edible, greenish fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
