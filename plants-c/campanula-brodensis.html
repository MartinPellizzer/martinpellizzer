Campanula brodensis

Campanula brodensis, commonly known as the Bredensk bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Campanula brodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
