Corylus avellana var. gloiotricha

Corylus avellana var. gloiotricha, commonly known as the European hazel, is a variety of the hazel tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deciduous, compound leaves that are alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional agriculture and wildlife habitats.
