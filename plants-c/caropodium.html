Caropodium

Caropodium, commonly known as the common water flea, is a genus of small crustaceans belonging to the order Cladocera.

These organisms are typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and streams. They are characterized by their transparent, planktonic bodies and the presence of a carapace that covers their dorsal side. Caropodium species play a vital role in aquatic ecosystems as primary consumers and as a food source for larger organisms.

Their rapid reproduction and short life cycle contribute to their abundance in various water bodies.
