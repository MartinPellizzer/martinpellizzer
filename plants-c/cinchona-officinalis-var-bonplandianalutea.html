Cinchona officinalis var. bonplandianalutea

Cinchona officinalis var. bonplandianalutea, commonly known as the yellow bark cinchona, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is part of the Rubiaceae family. The plant produces red, elongated fruits that contain elliptical seeds. It is closely related to other cinchona species used in the production of quinine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional medicine.
