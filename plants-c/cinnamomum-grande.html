Cinnamomum grande

Cinnamomum grande, commonly known as the large cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia.

It is a tall evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty.

The species is closely related to other cinnamon trees, but it is distinguished by its larger size and unique foliage.
