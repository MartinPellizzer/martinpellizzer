Clavija grandis

Clavija grandis, commonly known as the giant clavija, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its growth habit is shrub-like, often reaching heights of several meters, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
