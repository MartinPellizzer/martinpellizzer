Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. ciliatus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirsutus" name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky areas.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
