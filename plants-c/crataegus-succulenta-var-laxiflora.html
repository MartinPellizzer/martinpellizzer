Crataegus succulenta var. laxiflora

Crataegus succulenta var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
