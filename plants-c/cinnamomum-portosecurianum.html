Cinnamomum portosecurianum

Cinnamomum portosecurianum, commonly known as the West African cinnamon tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The tree is valued for its potential economic and ecological importance in its native habitat.
