Coelogyne macgregorii

Coelogyne macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are thick and segmented, storing water and nutrients.

The flowers are often clustered in racemes and are highly valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal.
