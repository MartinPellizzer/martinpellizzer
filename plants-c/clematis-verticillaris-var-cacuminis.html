Clematis verticillaris var. cacuminis

Clematis verticillaris var. cacuminis, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in loose racemes. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
