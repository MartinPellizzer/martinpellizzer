Cactus fellneri

Cactus fellneri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and spiny, segmented stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its adaptations to desert environments include a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Cactus fellneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
