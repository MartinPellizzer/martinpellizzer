Clematis napoensis

Clematis napoensis, commonly known as the Napo clematis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, usually white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Clematis napoensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
