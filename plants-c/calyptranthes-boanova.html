Calyptranthes boanova

Calyptranthes boanova, commonly known as the Boana waxleaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
