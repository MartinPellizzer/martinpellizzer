Cerastium latifolium subsp. uniflorum

Cerastium latifolium subsp.

uniflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
