Commiphora quadricincta

Commiphora quadricincta, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellowish, sticky resinous exudates.

This resin has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
