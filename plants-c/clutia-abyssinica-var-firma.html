Clutia abyssinica var. firma

Clutia abyssinica var. firma, commonly known as African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
