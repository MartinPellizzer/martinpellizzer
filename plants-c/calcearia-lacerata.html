Calcearia lacerata

Calcearia lacerata, commonly known as the broken calcearia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its unique, pale yellow to white flowers that resemble small, delicate bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Calcearia lacerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
