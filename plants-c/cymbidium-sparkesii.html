Cymbidium sparkesii

Cymbidium sparkesii, commonly known as the Sparkes' orchid, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or brown lip. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Cymbidium sparkesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in traditional Chinese horticulture.
