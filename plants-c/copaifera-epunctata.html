Copaifera epunctata

Copaifera epunctata, commonly known as the punctate copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
