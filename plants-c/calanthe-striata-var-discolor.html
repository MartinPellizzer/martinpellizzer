Calanthe striata var. discolor

Calanthe striata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
