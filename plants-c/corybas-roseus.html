Corybas roseus

Corybas roseus, commonly known as the pink torch orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its striking, red or pink, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, such as thrips, which are attracted to the nectar-rich structures.

Corybas roseus is a perennial herb that produces its flowers from late summer to early autumn.
