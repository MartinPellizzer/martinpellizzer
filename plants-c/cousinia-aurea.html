Cousinia aurea

Cousinia aurea, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia aurea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
