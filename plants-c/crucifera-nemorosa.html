Crucifera nemorosa

Crucifera nemorosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the mustard family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Crucifera nemorosa is often found in temperate regions and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
