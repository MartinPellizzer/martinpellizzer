Cineraria lobata subsp. lobata

Cineraria lobata subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is well adapted to dry, coastal climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
