Cotoneaster fulvidus

Cotoneaster fulvidus, commonly known as the golden cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Cotoneaster fulvidus is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
