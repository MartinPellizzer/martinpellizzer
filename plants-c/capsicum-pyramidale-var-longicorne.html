Capsicum pyramidale var. longicorne

Capsicum pyramidale var. longicorne, commonly known as the longhorn pepper.

This variety is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruits that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a vibrant red, though it can also be found in yellow or orange hues depending on the maturity stage.
