Crataegus azarolus var. lutescens

Crataegus azarolus var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces thorny branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
