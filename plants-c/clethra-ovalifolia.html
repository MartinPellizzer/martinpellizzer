Clethra ovalifolia

Clethra ovalifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
