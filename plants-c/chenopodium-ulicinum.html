Chenopodium ulicinum

Chenopodium ulicinum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium ulicinum is also known for its edible leaves, which can be consumed in salads or cooked like spinach.
