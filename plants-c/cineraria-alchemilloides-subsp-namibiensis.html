Cineraria alchemilloides subsp. namibiensis

Cineraria alchemilloides subsp.

namibiensis, commonly known as the Namibian cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Namibia. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and coastal climate of its native habitat.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
