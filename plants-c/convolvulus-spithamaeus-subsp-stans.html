Convolvulus spithamaeus subsp. stans

Convolvulus spithamaeus subsp.

stans, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
