Cereus crispatus

Cereus crispatus, commonly known as the crested cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique appearance and flowering habits make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
