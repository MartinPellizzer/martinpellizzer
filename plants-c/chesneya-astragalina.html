Chesneya astragalina

Chesneya astragalina, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Chesneya astragalina is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
