Cleistanthus discolor

Cleistanthus discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered cleistanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically grouped in clusters, with the showy white bracts attracting pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
