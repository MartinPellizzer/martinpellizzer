Corydalis ambigua f. pectinata

Corydalis ambigua f. pectinata, commonly known as the pink corydalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant features slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
