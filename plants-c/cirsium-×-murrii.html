Cirsium × murrii

Cirsium × murrii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its morphology includes a robust stem and a rosette of basal leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Cirsium × murrii is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats where it can thrive in full sun.
