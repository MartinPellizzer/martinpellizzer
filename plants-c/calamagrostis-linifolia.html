Calamagrostis linifolia

Calamagrostis linifolia, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Calamagrostis linifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
