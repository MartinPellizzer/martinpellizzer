Cucurbita cerifera

Cucurbita cerifera, commonly known as the wax gourd or white pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, waxy, and often white or light green rind, which becomes thicker as the fruit matures. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems. Its fruit is typically oblong or spherical and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
