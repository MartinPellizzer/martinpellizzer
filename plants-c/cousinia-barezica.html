Cousinia barezica

Cousinia barezica, commonly known as the Afghan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia barezica is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
