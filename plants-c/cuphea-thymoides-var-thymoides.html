Cuphea thymoides var. thymoides

Cuphea thymoides var. thymoides, commonly known as the hummingbird flower or red hot poker, is a member of the Lythraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking, tubular red bracts that resemble flames, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
