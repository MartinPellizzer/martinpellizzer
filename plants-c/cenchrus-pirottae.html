Cenchrus pirottae

Cenchrus pirottae, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodland areas.

Cenchrus pirottae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
