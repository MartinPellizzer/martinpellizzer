Convolvulus trichocarpus

Convolvulus trichocarpus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Convolvulus trichocarpus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
