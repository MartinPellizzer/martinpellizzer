Carex norvegica var. inserrulata

Carex norvegica var. inserrulata, commonly known as the Norwegian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly rough to the touch. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
