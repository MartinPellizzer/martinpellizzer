Cirsium vlassovianum var. viridifolium

Cirsium vlassovianum var. viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, giving the plant its common name, and are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
