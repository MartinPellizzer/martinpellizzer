Crocus speciosus var. laxior

Crocus speciosus var. laxior, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pale yellow or pink. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This variety is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
