Crotalaria cunninghamii subsp. cunninghamii

Crotalaria cunninghamii subsp.

cunninghamii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred shape.

The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems, and its leaves are alternate and ovate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
