Campanula bellardii f. hoppeana

Campanula bellardii f. hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and visual charm.
