Cochlioda sanguinea

Cochlioda sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. Cochlioda sanguinea is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
