Crataegus monogyna f. insegnae

Crataegus monogyna f. insegnae, commonly known as the lesser hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to the typical species. The leaves are generally narrower and shorter, and the flowers are less showy. It produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds.

This form is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils.
