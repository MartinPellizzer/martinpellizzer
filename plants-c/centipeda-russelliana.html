Centipeda russelliana

Centipeda russelliana, commonly known as Russell's centipede plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
