Cryptocarya austrokweichouensis

Cryptocarya austrokweichouensis, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
