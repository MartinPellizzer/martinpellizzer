Ceanothus papillosus var. regius

Ceanothus papillosus var. regius, commonly known as the Sierra foothill ceanothus, is a shrub native to California.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has cultural significance among Native American communities.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
