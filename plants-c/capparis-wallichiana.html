Capparis wallichiana

Capparis wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan caper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Capparis wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
