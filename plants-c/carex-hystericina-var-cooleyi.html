Carex hystericina var. cooleyi

Carex hystericina var. cooleyi, commonly known as the Cooleys' sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, brownish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
