Camarotis falciformis

Camarotis falciformis, commonly known as the sickle-shaped fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, sickle-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to the substrate.

The leaves are covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures that aid in water retention and protection from herbivores.
