Cirsium drijeri

Cirsium drijeri, commonly known as the Drijer's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Cirsium drijeri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its habitat.
