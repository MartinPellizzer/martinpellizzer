Carduus tenuiflorus var. pycnocephalus

Carduus tenuiflorus var. pycnocephalus, commonly known as the dense-headed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a dense cluster of small, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats. Its flowering heads are surrounded by tightly packed bracts, giving it a compact, globular appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
