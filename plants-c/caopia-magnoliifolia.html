Caopia magnoliifolia

Caopia magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved caopia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of magnolia species, hence its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its white or pinkish petals. Caopia magnoliifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
