Cochlearia aestuaria

Cochlearia aestuaria, commonly known as the sea kale, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the British Isles and northern Europe.

It thrives in saline and maritime environments, often growing on rocky shores and salt marshes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh coastal conditions.

Cochlearia aestuaria is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
