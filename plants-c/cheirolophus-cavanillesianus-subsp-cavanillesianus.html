Cheirolophus cavanillesianus subsp. cavanillesianus

Cheirolophus cavanillesianus subsp.

cavanillesianus, commonly known as the Cavanilles' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy flowers in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
