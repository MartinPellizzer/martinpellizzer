Crataegus chapmanii var. plukenetii

Crataegus chapmanii var. plukenetii, commonly known as the Plukenet hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet, featuring thorny branches and oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.
