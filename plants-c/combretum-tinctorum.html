Combretum tinctorum

Combretum tinctorum, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
