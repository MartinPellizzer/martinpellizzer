Croton oliganthus

Croton oliganthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in yellow or greenish hues, and it produces orange or red berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
