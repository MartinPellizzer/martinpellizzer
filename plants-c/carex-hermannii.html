Carex hermannii

Carex hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. Hermann's sedge typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color.

This plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
