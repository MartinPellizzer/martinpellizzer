Clematis lasiantha subsp. pauciflora

Clematis lasiantha subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered clematis, is a perennial vine native to parts of Asia. It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The flowers are small, typically white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
