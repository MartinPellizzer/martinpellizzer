Carex fischeri subsp. fischeri

Carex fischeri subsp.

fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
