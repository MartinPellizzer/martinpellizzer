Cynara diversifolia

Cynara diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and purple, thistle-like flower heads. The plant produces edible buds that are similar to those of the common artichoke, though they are less widely cultivated. Cynara diversifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and culinary benefits.
