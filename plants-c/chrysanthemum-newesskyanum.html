Chrysanthemum newesskyanum

Chrysanthemum newesskyanum, commonly known as the Siberian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler temperatures.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
