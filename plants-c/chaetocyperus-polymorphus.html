Chaetocyperus polymorphus

Chaetocyperus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
