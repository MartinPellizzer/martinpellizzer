Circaea alpina f. imaicola

Circaea alpina f. imaicola, commonly known as the Imaicola form of the white bead-vine, is a variant of the alpine cinquefoil.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant produces small, bead-like fruits.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
