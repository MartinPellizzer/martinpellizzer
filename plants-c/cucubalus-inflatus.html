Cucubalus inflatus

Cucubalus inflatus, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, inflated, balloon-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
