Clethra lancilimba

Clethra lancilimba, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
