Camellia tenuiflora

Camellia tenuiflora, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often accompanied by a single, prominent sepal.

Camellia tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
