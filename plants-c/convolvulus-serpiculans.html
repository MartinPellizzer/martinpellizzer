Convolvulus serpiculans

Convolvulus serpiculans, commonly known as the serpentine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its name refers to the twisted, spiraling growth pattern of its vines.
