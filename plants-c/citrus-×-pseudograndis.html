Citrus × pseudograndis

Citrus × pseudograndis, commonly known as the rough lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between the citron and the mandarin. The tree is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, yellow-orange fruit. The fruit has a thick, bitter rind and a segmented, juicy pulp.

It is primarily cultivated for its aromatic peel rather than its fruit, which is often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
