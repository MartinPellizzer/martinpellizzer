Centaurea limbata subsp. hanryi

Centaurea limbata subsp.

hanryi, commonly known as Hanry's centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender stems and blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in open habitats like grasslands and slopes, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
