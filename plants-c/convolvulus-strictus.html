Convolvulus strictus

Convolvulus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a climbing herb with slender, twining stems that often ascend along supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
