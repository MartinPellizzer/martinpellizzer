Crateva moschata

Crateva moschata, commonly known as the musk cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant, orange-yellow fruits that resemble small cucumbers. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are edible when mature and have a mild, musky flavor.

Crateva moschata is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
