Coffea mangoroensis

Coffea mangoroensis, commonly known as the Mangoro coffee.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Madagascar. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the coffee beans used for brewing. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is considered a rare and valuable species in the Coffea genus.
