Cebatha carolina

Cebatha carolina, commonly known as the Carolina larkspur, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features tall, slender stems with clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Cebatha carolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
