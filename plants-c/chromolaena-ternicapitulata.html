Chromolaena ternicapitulata

Chromolaena ternicapitulata, commonly known as the three-headed chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in three-headed inflorescences, giving it its common name. It is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a notable presence in its native and introduced habitats.
