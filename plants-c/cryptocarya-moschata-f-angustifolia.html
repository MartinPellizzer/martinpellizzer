Cryptocarya moschata f. angustifolia

Cryptocarya moschata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon tree, is a rare botanical variant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits aromatic foliage, with a distinct scent reminiscent of cinnamon, and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, glossy berries.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
