Coelogyne incrassata var. incrassata

Coelogyne incrassata var. incrassata, commonly known as the broad-leafed coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
