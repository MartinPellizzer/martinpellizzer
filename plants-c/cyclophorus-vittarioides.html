Cyclophorus vittarioides

Cyclophorus vittarioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Cyclophorus vittarioides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.
