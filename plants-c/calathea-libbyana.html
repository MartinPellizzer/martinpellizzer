Calathea libbyana

Calathea libbyana, commonly known as the Libby's Calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate leaf patterns that feature a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Calathea libbyana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
