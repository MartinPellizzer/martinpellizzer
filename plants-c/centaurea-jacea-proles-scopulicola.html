Centaurea jacea proles scopulicola

Centaurea jacea proles scopulicola, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its small, daisy-like flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
