Campanula hispanica var. glabra

Campanula hispanica var. glabra, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft hair, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
