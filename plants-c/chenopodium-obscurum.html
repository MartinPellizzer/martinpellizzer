Chenopodium obscurum

Chenopodium obscurum, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and rough, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Chenopodium obscurum is also considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature.
