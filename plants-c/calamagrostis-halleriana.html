Calamagrostis halleriana

Calamagrostis halleriana, commonly known as the Haller's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.

Calamagrostis halleriana is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
