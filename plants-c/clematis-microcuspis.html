Clematis microcuspis

Clematis microcuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Clematis microcuspis prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
