Cyperus flavus subsp. redolens

Cyperus flavus subsp.

redolens, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other Cyperus species.
