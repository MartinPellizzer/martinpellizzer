Ceropegia copleyae

Ceropegia copleyae, commonly known as the Copley's African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and enthusiasts of rare succulents.
