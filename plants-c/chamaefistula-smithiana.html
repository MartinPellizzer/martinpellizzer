Chamaefistula smithiana

Chamaefistula smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Chamaefistula smithiana is valued in horticulture for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
