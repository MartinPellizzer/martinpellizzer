Cassia microphylla var. guineensis

Cassia microphylla var. guineensis, commonly known as African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, fragrant flowers in panicles. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and is often found in dry, open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.

The seeds are encased in pods and can be used to produce a yellow dye.
