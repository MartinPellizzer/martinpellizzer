Crataegus mollis var. dumetosa

Crataegus mollis var. dumetosa, commonly known as the shrubby hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring thorned branches and oval, serrated leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a dense, compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
