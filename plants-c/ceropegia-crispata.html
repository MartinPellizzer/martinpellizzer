Ceropegia crispata

Ceropegia crispata, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia crispata is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
