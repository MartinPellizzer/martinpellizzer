Coryphantha roederiana

Coryphantha roederiana, commonly known as the Roeder's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid environments.

Coryphantha roederiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
