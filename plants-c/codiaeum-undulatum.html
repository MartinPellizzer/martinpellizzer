Codiaeum undulatum

Codiaeum undulatum, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, waxy leaves that often display striking patterns of red, yellow, and green. The leaves are typically undulating, giving the plant its common name, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its colorful foliage.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
