Cassia trachycarpa subsp. macrophylla

Cassia trachycarpa subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
