Carex kotagirica

Carex kotagirica, commonly known as the Kotagiric sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly India, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Carex kotagirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
