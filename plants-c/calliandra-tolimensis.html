Calliandra tolimensis

Calliandra tolimensis, commonly known as the Toliman calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright red or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
