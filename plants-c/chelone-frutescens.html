Chelone frutescens

Chelone frutescens, commonly known as the spotted wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its attractive foliage and unique floral structure make it a valuable addition to native gardens.
