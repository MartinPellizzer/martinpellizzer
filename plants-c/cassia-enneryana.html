Cassia enneryana

Cassia enneryana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, seed-containing segments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia enneryana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
