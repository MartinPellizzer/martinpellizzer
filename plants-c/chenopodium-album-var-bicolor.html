Chenopodium album var. bicolor

Chenopodium album var. bicolor, commonly known as red-rooted lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and reddish or purplish on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine.
