Cactus cylindraceus

Cactus cylindraceus, commonly known as the slender cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in small, spine-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The plant's structure also provides shelter for various desert animals.
