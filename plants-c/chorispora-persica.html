Chorispora persica

Chorispora persica, commonly known as Persian sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.
