Cochlearia longifolia

Cochlearia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.
