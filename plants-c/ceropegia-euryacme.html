Ceropegia euryacme

Ceropegia euryacme, commonly known as the African violet or moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its unique, waxy flowers that resemble small bells, often with a distinct hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia euryacme is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
