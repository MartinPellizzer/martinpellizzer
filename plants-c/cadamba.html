Cadamba

Cadamba, commonly known as Indian cherry or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed, making it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes. Cadamba is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Its wood is lightweight and used in furniture and construction, while its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
