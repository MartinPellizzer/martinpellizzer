Callerya fordii

Callerya fordii, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It features long, pendulous racemes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically 9 to 15. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
