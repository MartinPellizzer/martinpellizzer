Carex longerostrata var. pallida

Carex longerostrata var. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces pale to white inflorescences, which consist of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
