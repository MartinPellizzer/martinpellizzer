Carpanthea pilosa

Carpanthea pilosa, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
