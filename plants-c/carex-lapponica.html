Carex lapponica

Carex lapponica, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions.

It thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. Its flowering spikes are usually short and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex lapponica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic wildlife.
