Crataegus rhipidophylla subsp. lindmanii

Crataegus rhipidophylla subsp.

lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and floral structure, making it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
