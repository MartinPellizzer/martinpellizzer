Cruciata coronata subsp. persica

Cruciata coronata subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Persian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
