Convolvulus bauhiniifolius

Convolvulus bauhiniifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
