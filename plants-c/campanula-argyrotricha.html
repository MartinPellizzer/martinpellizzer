Campanula argyrotricha

Campanula argyrotricha, commonly known as the silver-haired bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers with white to pale blue petals. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
