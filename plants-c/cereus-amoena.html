Cereus amoena

Cereus amoena, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

Cereus amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
