Castanopsis choboensis

Castanopsis choboensis, commonly known as the Chobo chestnut, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and produces small, spiky fruits that enclose edible nuts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
