Cryptocarpha lanata

Cryptocarpha lanata, commonly known as woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in the central and eastern United States.

Cryptocarpha lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
