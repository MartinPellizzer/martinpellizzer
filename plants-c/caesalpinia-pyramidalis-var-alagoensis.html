Caesalpinia pyramidalis var. alagoensis

Caesalpinia pyramidalis var. alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are surrounded by long, narrow bracts that give it its dramatic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The Alagoas flame tree is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
