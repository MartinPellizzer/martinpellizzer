Cogswellia geyeri

Cogswellia geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
