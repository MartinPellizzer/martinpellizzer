Cassia nemophila

Cassia nemophila, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
