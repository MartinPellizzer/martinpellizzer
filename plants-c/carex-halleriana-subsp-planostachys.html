Carex halleriana subsp. planostachys

Carex halleriana subsp.

planostachys, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often wind-pollinated.

The leaves are broad and flat, contributing to its distinct appearance among other sedges.
