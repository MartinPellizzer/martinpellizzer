Cytisus pygmaeus

Cytisus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant has slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are delicate and fern-like in appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
