Caltha palustris f. silvatica

Caltha palustris f. silvatica, commonly known as the forest marsh buttercup, is a variant of the marsh marigold.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist woodland and streamside habitats. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint along the edges.

It thrives in partial shade and is often found in shaded, wet environments such as bogs and forest floors.
