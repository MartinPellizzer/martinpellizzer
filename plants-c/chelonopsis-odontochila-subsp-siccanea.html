Chelonopsis odontochila subsp. siccanea

Chelonopsis odontochila subsp.

siccanea, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, hairy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils across its native range.
