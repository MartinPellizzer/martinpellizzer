Centaurea alba f. titani

Centaurea alba f. titani, commonly known as the white centaury or white knapweed, is a variant of the common centaury.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
