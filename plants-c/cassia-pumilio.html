Cassia pumilio

Cassia pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf senna, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features delicate, feather-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cassia pumilio is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
