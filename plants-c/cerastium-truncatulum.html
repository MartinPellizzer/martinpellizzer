Cerastium truncatulum

Cerastium truncatulum, commonly known as the cut-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often appearing before the leaves fully develop. Its leaves are rounded and truncate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
