Calamagrostis preslii

Calamagrostis preslii, commonly known as the Presl's reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces feathery, panicle-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for water garden and natural landscape designs.
