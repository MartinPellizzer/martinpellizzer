Cinchona × pavoniana

Cinchona × pavoniana, commonly known as the hybrid cinchona, is a cross between species of the genus Cinchona, which includes the source of quinine.

This plant is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Andes. It possesses lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically red or yellow, clustered in panicles.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the production of medicinal compounds.
