Correa calycina var. halmaturorum

Correa calycina var. halmaturorum, commonly known as the Halmaturong Correa, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers during spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
