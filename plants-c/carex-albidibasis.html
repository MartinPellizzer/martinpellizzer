Carex albidibasis

Carex albidibasis, commonly known as the white-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and prefers full to partial sunlight. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided seed capsules.

Carex albidibasis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
