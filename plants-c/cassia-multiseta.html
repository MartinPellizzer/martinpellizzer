Cassia multiseta

Cassia multiseta, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, spiny pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia multiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
