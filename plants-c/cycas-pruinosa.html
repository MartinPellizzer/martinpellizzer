Cycas pruinosa

Cycas pruinosa, commonly known as the pink cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the male cones being yellow and the female cones reddish-brown. Cycas pruinosa is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
