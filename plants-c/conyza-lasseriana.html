Conyza lasseriana

Conyza lasseriana, commonly known as the Mexican horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces numerous seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
