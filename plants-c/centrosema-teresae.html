Centrosema teresae

Centrosema teresae, commonly known as the small-flowered crownflower, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. It is a nitrogen-fixing plant, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
