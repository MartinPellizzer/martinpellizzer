Cladium latissimum

Cladium latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes, swamps, and other wetland habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Cladium latissimum plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wetland species.
