Chamaefistula astroites

Chamaefistula astroites, commonly known as the star-twisted fiddlewood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems that often have a twisted or gnarled appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chamaefistula astroites is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
