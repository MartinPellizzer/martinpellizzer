Campanula patula var. glauca

Campanula patula var. glauca, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray bloom, giving the plant its varietal name "glauca.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
