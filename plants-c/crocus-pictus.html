Crocus pictus

Crocus pictus, commonly known as the painted crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from deep purple to bright yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that typically appear in early spring, before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Crocus pictus is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
