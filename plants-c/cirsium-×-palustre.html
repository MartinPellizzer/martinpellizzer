Cirsium × palustre

Cirsium × palustre, commonly known as swamp thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to wetland environments in Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically perennial, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Cirsium × palustre plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
