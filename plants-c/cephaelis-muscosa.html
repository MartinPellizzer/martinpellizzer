Cephaelis muscosa

Cephaelis muscosa, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-white, fan-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cephaelis muscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
