Crataegus leucantha

Crataegus leucantha, commonly known as the white hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny branches and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Crataegus leucantha is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
