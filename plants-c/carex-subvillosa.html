Carex subvillosa

Carex subvillosa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex subvillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
