Callicarpa scandens

Callicarpa scandens, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that can twine around supports, reaching lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition into bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. These fruits are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.

Its adaptability to various soil types and partial shade makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
