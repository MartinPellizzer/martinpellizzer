Carica cnidoscoloides

Carica cnidoscoloides, commonly known as the chontaduro, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caricaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to papaya but have a distinct, sweet, and sometimes spicy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
