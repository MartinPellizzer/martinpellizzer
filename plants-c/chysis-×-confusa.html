Chysis × confusa

Chysis × confusa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Chysis fimbriata and Chysis formosana.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, a feature common in the Araceae family. It is native to regions of China and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Chysis × confusa is relatively low maintenance and thrives in humid, indirect light conditions.
