Coreopsis californica

Coreopsis californica, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across various environments.

Coreopsis californica is also known for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
