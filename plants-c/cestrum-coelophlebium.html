Cestrum coelophlebium

Cestrum coelophlebium, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive bell shape, which is adapted for pollination by night-flying moths.

The plant is also known for its mild toxicity, making it important to handle with care.
