Clematis calycina

Clematis calycina, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive violet or blue tint. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and vigorous climbing habit.
