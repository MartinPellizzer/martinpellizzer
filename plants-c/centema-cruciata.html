Centema cruciata

Centema cruciata, commonly known as the crossleaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. Its flowers have five petals, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Centema cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
