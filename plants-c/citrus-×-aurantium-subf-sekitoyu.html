Citrus × aurantium subf. sekitoyu

Citrus × aurantium subf.

sekitoyu, commonly known as the Japanese bitter orange, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and round, yellow-orange fruits. It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and a strong, citrusy aroma. Its fruit contains a high concentration of essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. The subsp.

sekitoyu is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique fruit characteristics compared to other citrus varieties.
