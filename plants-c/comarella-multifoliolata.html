Comarella multifoliolata

Comarella multifoliolata, commonly known as the many-leaved comarella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, palmate leaves that are often divided into multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
