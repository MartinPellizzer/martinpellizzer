Cereus pentlandii var. aurantiacus

Cereus pentlandii var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Night Blooming Cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically bright orange or yellow, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking appearance.
