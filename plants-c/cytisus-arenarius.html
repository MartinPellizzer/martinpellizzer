Cytisus arenarius

Cytisus arenarius, commonly known as the sand broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cytisus arenarius is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
