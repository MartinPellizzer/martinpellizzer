Catasetum purum

Catasetum purum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, often with a pink or purple hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers, each with a specialized structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

The botanical features of Catasetum purum make it a distinctive and visually striking member of the orchid family.
