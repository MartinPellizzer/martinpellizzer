Conyza brevipetiolata

Conyza brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and small, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
