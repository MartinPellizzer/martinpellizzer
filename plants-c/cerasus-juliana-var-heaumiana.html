Cerasus juliana var. heaumiana

Cerasus juliana var. heaumiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that ripen in early summer. The plant is deciduous, featuring attractive pink to white flowers in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
