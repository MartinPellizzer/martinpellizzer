Cyanus montanus subsp. maramarosiensis

Cyanus montanus subsp.

maramarosiensis, commonly known as the Maramarosi blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which consist of numerous small, ray-like florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
