Crataegus leimonia

Crataegus leimonia, commonly known as the leimonia hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
