Chysis aurea var. maculata

Chysis aurea var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted golden bell, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are often speckled with red or orange markings, giving it its varietal name "maculata.". The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
