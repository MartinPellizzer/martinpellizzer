Chusquea gigantea

Chusquea gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant forms dense clumps and has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Chusquea gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices by local communities.
