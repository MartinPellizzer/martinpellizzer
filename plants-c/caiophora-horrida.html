Caiophora horrida

Caiophora horrida, commonly known as the hairy caiophora, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often found in shaded, humid environments.
