Calluna vulgaris subf. aureovariegata

Calluna vulgaris subf.

aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden-variegated heather, is a variant of the common heather native to Europe. It is characterized by its striking leaves that display a golden-yellow margin, contrasting with the dark green central portion.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardy and decorative qualities.
