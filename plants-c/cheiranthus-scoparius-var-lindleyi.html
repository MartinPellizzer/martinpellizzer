Cheiranthus scoparius var. lindleyi

Cheiranthus scoparius var. lindleyi, commonly known as Lindley's winter flowering mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in winter, making it a notable species in its habitat.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
