Cyanea coriacea var. serratifolia

Cyanea coriacea var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, coral-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered an important native species in Hawaii's flora.
