Chenopodium × bernburgense

Chenopodium × bernburgense, commonly known as garden goosefoot, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, often lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are small and oval-shaped. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it often grows in disturbed soils.

Chenopodium × bernburgense is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
