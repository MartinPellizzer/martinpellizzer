Carex chinoi

Carex chinoi, commonly known as Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex chinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
