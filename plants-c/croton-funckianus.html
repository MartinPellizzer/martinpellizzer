Croton funckianus

Croton funckianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, and it produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds.

Croton funckianus is often cultivated for its decorative foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
