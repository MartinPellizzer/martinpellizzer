Carpanthea calendulacea

Carpanthea calendulacea, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
