Carduus membranaceus

Carduus membranaceus, commonly known as the Russian thistle or tarragon weed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and a distinctive purple or pink flower head that resembles a dandelion. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
