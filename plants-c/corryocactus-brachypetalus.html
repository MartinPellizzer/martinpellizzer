Corryocactus brachypetalus

Corryocactus brachypetalus, commonly known as the short-petaled corryocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
