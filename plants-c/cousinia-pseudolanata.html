Cousinia pseudolanata

Cousinia pseudolanata, commonly known as the Kashmiri ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia pseudolanata is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
