Convolvulus divaricatus

Convolvulus divaricatus, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers open in the morning and close at dusk.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
