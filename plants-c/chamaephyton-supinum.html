Chamaephyton supinum

Chamaephyton supinum, commonly known as the prostrate chamaephyte, is a low-growing plant characterized by its mat-forming growth habit.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has small, often succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually small and clustered. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
