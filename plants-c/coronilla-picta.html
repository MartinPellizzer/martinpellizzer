Coronilla picta

Coronilla picta, commonly known as the painted vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
