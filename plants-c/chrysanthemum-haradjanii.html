Chrysanthemum haradjanii

Chrysanthemum haradjanii, commonly known as the Persian chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
