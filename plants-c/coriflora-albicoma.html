Coriflora albicoma

Coriflora albicoma, commonly known as the white-haired bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
