Cyathea balanocarpa

Cyathea balanocarpa, commonly known as the Balanocarpus tree, is a species of tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its large, fern-like fronds. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests, preferring humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are bioluminescent, a unique feature that helps in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
