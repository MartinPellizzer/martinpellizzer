Convallaria glabra

Convallaria glabra, commonly known as lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers with a faintly pinkish hue. The plant is known for its fragrant, nectar-rich blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which make it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
