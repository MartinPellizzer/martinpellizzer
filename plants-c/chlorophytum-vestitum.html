Chlorophytum vestitum

Chlorophytum vestitum, commonly known as the shawl plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its strap-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscaping.
