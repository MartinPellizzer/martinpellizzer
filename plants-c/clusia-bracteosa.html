Clusia bracteosa

Clusia bracteosa, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Clusia bracteosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
