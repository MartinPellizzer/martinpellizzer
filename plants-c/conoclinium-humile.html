Conoclinium humile

Conoclinium humile, commonly known as hummingbird vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
