Carex firmula

Carex firmula, commonly known as the firm moss sedge.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has grass-like leaves that are generally narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex firmula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
