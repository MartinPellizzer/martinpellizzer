Copernicia glabrescens var. glabrescens

Copernicia glabrescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the golden cup palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in coastal areas and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray and wind.
