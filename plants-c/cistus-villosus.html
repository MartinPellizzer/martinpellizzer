Cistus villosus

Cistus villosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus villosus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
