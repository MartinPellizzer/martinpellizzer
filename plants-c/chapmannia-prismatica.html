Chapmannia prismatica

Chapmannia prismatica, commonly known as the prismatic grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
