Cissus vitiginea variegata

Cissus vitiginea variegata, commonly known as the variegated devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with striking variegation of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
