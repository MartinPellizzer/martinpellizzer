Cyperus breedlovei

Cyperus breedlovei, commonly known as the Breddle's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
