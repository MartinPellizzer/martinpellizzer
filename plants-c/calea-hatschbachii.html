Calea hatschbachii

Calea hatschbachii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink petals that resemble ghostly faces, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calea hatschbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
