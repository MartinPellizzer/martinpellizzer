Cassia xiphoidea

Cassia xiphoidea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Cassia xiphoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
