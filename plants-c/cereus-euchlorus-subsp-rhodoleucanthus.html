Cereus euchlorus subsp. rhodoleucanthus

Cereus euchlorus subsp.

rhodoleucanthus, commonly known as the White-Flowered Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has spiny stems for protection. It blooms at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The subspecies is noted for its distinctive floral coloration and is popular among cactus enthusiasts.
