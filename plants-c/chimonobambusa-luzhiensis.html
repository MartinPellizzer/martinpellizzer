Chimonobambusa luzhiensis

Chimonobambusa luzhiensis, commonly known as the Luzhi bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, culm-like structure and dense foliage. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its culms are typically hollow and exhibit a distinctive grayish-green color, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
