Clematis australis var. rutifolia

Clematis australis var. rutifolia, commonly known as the Australian native clematis, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a vigorous vine with slender, wiry stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
