Campanula velutina

Campanula velutina, commonly known as velvet bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula velutina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
