Crepinia theratophila

Crepinia theratophila, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, star-like clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Crepinia theratophila is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
