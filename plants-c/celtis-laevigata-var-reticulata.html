Celtis laevigata var. reticulata

Celtis laevigata var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like vein pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
