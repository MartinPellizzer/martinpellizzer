Cephalaria tatarica var. brevipalea

Cephalaria tatarica var. brevipalea, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in moist, shady environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
