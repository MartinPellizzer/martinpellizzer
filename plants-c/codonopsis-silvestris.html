Codonopsis silvestris

Codonopsis silvestris, commonly known as the wild ginseng or mountain ginseng, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The roots of Codonopsis silvestris are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
