Croton vulpinus

Croton vulpinus, commonly known as the "fireplant," is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spiny, orange or red capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's striking foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
