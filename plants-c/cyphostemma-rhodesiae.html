Cyphostemma rhodesiae

Cyphostemma rhodesiae, commonly known as the Rhodesian moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shrublands and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
