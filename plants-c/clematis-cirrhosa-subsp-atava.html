Clematis cirrhosa subsp. atava

Clematis cirrhosa subsp.

atava, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
