Campylotropis pinetorum subsp. albopubescens

Campylotropis pinetorum subsp.

albopubescens, commonly known as the white-hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with white-hairy undersides.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites.
