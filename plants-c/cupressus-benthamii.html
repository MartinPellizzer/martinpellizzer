Cupressus benthamii

Cupressus benthamii, commonly known as the Benthams' cypress, is a species of conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
