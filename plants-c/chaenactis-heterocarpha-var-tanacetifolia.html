Chaenactis heterocarpha var. tanacetifolia

Chaenactis heterocarpha var. tanacetifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually blue-gray in color, with a distinctive fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid conditions.
