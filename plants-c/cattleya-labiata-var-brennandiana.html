Cattleya labiata var. brennandiana

Cattleya labiata var. brennandiana, commonly known as the Brennand's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant mix of pink, white, and purple hues. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or other structures rather than in soil. It produces pendulous blooms that are highly prized in the orchid trade for their beauty and fragrance.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
