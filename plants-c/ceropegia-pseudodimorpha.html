Ceropegia pseudodimorpha

Ceropegia pseudodimorpha, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and succulent stems. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
