Chrysopsis adenolepis

Chrysopsis adenolepis, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
