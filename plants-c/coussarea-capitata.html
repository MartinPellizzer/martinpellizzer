Coussarea capitata

Coussarea capitata, commonly known as the red-flowered coussarea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense, terminal clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Coussarea capitata is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
