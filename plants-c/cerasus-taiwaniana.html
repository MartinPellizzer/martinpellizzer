Cerasus taiwaniana

Cerasus taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Cerasus taiwaniana prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
