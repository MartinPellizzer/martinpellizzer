Callicarpa integerrima

Callicarpa integerrima, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

The berries are a food source for birds and are also used in traditional medicine.
