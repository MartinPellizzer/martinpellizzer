Crotalaria umbellata

Crotalaria umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in umbellate clusters, giving it its common name.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and can also serve as a forage crop in certain regions.
