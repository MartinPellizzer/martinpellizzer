Cyperus neglectus

Cyperus neglectus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus neglectus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
