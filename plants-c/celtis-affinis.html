Celtis affinis

Celtis affinis, commonly known as the American hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Celtis affinis typically grows in a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in urban landscaping due to its tolerance of pollution and urban conditions.
