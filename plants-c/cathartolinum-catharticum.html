Cathartolinum catharticum

Cathartolinum catharticum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its cathartic properties.
