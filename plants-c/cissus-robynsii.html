Cissus robynsii

Cissus robynsii, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Cissus robynsii thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

It is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and green walls.
