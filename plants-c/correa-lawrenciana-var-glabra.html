Correa lawrenciana var. glabra

Correa lawrenciana var. glabra, commonly known as the Smooth Correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
