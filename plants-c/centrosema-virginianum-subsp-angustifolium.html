Centrosema virginianum subsp. angustifolium

Centrosema virginianum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States. It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in full sun and is often used in erosion control and wildlife habitat restoration.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility.
