Convolvulus pannosus

Convolvulus pannosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Convolvulus pannosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
