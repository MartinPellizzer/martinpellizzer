Cytisus scoparius f. albus

Cytisus scoparius f. albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a variant of the common broom plant.

It is characterized by its white flowers, which contrast with the typical yellow blooms of the species. This plant is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia, often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

The white broom is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
