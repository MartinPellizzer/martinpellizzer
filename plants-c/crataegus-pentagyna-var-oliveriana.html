Crataegus pentagyna var. oliveriana

Crataegus pentagyna var. oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically white or pink in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
