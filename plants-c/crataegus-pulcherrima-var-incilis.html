Crataegus pulcherrima var. incilis

Crataegus pulcherrima var. incilis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal changes.
