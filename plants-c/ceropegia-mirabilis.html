Ceropegia mirabilis

Ceropegia mirabilis, commonly known as the "Cape ivy," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia mirabilis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
