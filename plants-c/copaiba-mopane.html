Copaiba mopane

Copaiba mopane, commonly known as the mopane tree, is a medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, waxy leaves that turn red in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in many local communities.
