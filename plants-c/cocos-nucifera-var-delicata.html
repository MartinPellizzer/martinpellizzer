Cocos nucifera var. delicata

Cocos nucifera var. delicata, commonly known as the dwarf coconut palm, is a compact variety of the coconut tree characterized by its shorter stature and broader leaves.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces coconuts that are typically smaller than those of the standard coconut palm, with a sweeter, more tender flesh. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in small-scale coconut production.

The variety is valued for its resilience and adaptability to coastal environments.
