Cymbidium dayanum f. avoviride

Cymbidium dayanum f. avoviride, commonly known as the green-veined orchid, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Cymbidium genus.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that exhibit a distinctive greenish-white veining pattern. The plant produces tall, upright inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It is native to China and is highly valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

This cultivar requires well-draining soil and partial shade to thrive, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
