Capsella bursa-pastoris belgica

Capsella bursa-pastoris belgica, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate, and it produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe but has been naturalized in many parts of the world, including North America.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in gardens.
