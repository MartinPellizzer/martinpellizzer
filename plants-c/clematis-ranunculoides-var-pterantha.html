Clematis ranunculoides var. pterantha

Clematis ranunculoides var. pterantha, commonly known as the pterantha clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically appearing in spring. Its climbing habit is supported by tendrils that allow it to grow along structures.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
