Cylindrorebutia conoidea

Cylindrorebutia conoidea, commonly known as the conical rooted cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its root system is notably conical, which helps it anchor firmly in the soil and access moisture from deeper layers.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
