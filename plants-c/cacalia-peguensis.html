Cacalia peguensis

Cacalia peguensis, commonly known as the Pegean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cacalia peguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
