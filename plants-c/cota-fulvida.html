Cota fulvida

Cota fulvida, commonly known as the golden cota, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Cota fulvida is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and delicate appearance.
