Cinnamomum camphora var. glaucescens

Cinnamomum camphora var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, bluish-green underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in ecological systems.
