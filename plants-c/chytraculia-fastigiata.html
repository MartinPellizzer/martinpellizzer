Chytraculia fastigiata

Chytraculia fastigiata, commonly known as the slender bamboo fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically green and have a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
