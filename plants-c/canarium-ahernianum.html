Canarium ahernianum

Canarium ahernianum, commonly known as the Ahern's candlenut, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a hard, oily shell. The oil extracted from its seeds is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
