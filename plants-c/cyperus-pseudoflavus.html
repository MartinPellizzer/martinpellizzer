Cyperus pseudoflavus

Cyperus pseudoflavus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes.

Cyperus pseudoflavus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
