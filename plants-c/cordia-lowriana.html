Cordia lowriana

Cordia lowriana, commonly known as the New Zealand coral tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its clusters of bright red, coral-like flowers that appear in spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Cordia lowriana is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.
