Cleisostoma gemmatum

Cleisostoma gemmatum, commonly known as the golden cleisostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with dark red veins, which bloom during the wet season. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cleisostoma gemmatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
