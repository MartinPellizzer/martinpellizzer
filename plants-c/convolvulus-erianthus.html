Convolvulus erianthus

Convolvulus erianthus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Convolvulus erianthus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
