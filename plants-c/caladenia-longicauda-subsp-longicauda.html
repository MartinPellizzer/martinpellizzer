Caladenia longicauda subsp. longicauda

Caladenia longicauda subsp.

longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands.

The plant features a single, erect stem with a single flower that has a distinctive long, narrow spur. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a contrasting red or pink lip.

This subspecies is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
