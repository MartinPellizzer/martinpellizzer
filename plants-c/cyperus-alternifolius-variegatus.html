Cyperus alternifolius variegatus

Cyperus alternifolius variegatus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically borne on slender stalks. Its variegated leaf patterns, featuring white or yellow markings, make it a popular ornamental plant in water gardens and containers.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in bog gardens or as a background plant in aquatic landscapes.
