Chaetochloa lindenbergiana

Chaetochloa lindenbergiana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
