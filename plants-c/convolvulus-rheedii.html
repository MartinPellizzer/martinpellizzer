Convolvulus rheedii

Convolvulus rheedii, commonly known as the Rheed's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
