Capsicum leucocarpon

Capsicum leucocarpon, commonly known as the white bell pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to southern Africa. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. The leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the plant is characterized by its compact growth habit.

It is primarily cultivated for its unique fruit color and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
