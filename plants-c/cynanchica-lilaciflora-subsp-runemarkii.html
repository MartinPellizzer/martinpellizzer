Cynanchica lilaciflora subsp. runemarkii

Cynanchica lilaciflora subsp.

runemarkii, commonly known as the Runemark milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces showy, lilac-colored flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies in dryland ecosystems.
