Crypsis phalaroides

Crypsis phalaroides, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
