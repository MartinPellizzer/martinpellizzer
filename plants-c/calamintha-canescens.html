Calamintha canescens

Calamintha canescens, commonly known as white germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Calamintha canescens is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
