Cacalia lessingioides

Cacalia lessingioides, commonly known as the Lessing's cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are typically pale blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
