Centropogon intermedius

Centropogon intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate centropogon, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.
