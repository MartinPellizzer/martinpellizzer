Crotalaria variegata var. humpatensis

Crotalaria variegata var. humpatensis, commonly known as the humpatensis crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces vibrant yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
