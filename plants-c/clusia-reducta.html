Clusia reducta

Clusia reducta, commonly known as the Brazilian shieldleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often shield-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Clusia reducta is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
