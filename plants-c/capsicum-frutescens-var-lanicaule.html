Capsicum frutescens var. lanicaule

Capsicum frutescens var. lanicaule, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its small, pungent fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and narrow, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by small, round fruits that range in color from green to red when mature.

This variety is widely cultivated for its spicy flavor and is used in various cuisines around the world.
