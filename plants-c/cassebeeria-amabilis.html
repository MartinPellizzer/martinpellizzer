Cassebeeria amabilis

Cassebeeria amabilis, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
