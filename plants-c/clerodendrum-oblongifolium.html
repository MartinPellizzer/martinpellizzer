Clerodendrum oblongifolium

Clerodendrum oblongifolium, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warm seasons, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
