Circaea × taronensis

Circaea × taronensis, commonly known as the hybrid white campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
