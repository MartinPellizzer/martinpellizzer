Coleus termetophilus

Coleus termetophilus, commonly known as the termite-loving coleus, is a unique plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant is notable for its association with termites, as it thrives in termite-infested soils, benefiting from the nutrients released by these insects. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
