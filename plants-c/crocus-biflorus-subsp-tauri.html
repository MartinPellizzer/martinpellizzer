Crocus biflorus subsp. tauri

Crocus biflorus subsp.

tauri, commonly known as the Taurian crocus, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
