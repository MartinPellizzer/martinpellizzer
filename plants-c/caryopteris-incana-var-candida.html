Caryopteris incana var. candida

Caryopteris incana var. candida, commonly known as silverblue bush mint, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically silvery-blue in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
