Crataegus divergens

Crataegus divergens, commonly known as the spreading hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus divergens typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, streambanks, and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
