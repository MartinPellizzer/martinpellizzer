Ceratochloa haenkeana

Ceratochloa haenkeana, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, feathery foliage and delicate, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, high-altitude environments, often in mountainous areas with cool climates. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
