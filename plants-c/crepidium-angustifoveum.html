Crepidium angustifoveum

Crepidium angustifoveum, commonly known as the narrow-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. It is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral display.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
