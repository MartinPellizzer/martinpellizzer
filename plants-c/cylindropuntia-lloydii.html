Cylindropuntia lloydii

Cylindropuntia lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
