Cyperus pumilus f. gracilis

Cyperus pumilus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in ecological restoration projects.
