Christella augescens

Christella augescens, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers and its hairy, silver-gray leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Christella augescens is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
