Cadetia similis

Cadetia similis, commonly known as the similar bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Cadetia similis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
