Carex urbis-malorum

Carex urbis-malorum, commonly known as the urban malorum sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex urbis-malorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
