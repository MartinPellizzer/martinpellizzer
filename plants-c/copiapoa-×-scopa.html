Copiapoa × scopa

Copiapoa × scopa, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Copiapoa cerifera and Copiapoa scopa.

It is native to the Atacama Desert in Chile, where it thrives in arid and coastal conditions. The plant typically grows as a solitary, short-stemmed cactus with dense, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh environments.
