Citrullus lanatus f. amarus

Citrullus lanatus f. amarus, commonly known as the wild watermelon, is a botanical variant of the watermelon species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more rugged fruit compared to cultivated watermelons, with a thick, rough rind and a less sweet, more acidic flesh. This subspecies is native to Africa and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It possesses a high water content and contains various bioactive compounds, making it valuable for medicinal and nutritional purposes.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological resilience.
