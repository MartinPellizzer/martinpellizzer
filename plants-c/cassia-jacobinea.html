Cassia jacobinea

Cassia jacobinea, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia jacobinea is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
