Centaurea × cirlani

Centaurea × cirlani, commonly known as the Cirl Bank centaurea, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to southern England.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is known for its ecological importance, supporting a variety of pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
