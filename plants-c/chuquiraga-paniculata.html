Chuquiraga paniculata

Chuquiraga paniculata, commonly known as the Andean burflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its distinctive yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Chuquiraga paniculata is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses among local communities.
