Chenopodium glaucum var. salinum

Chenopodium glaucum var. salinum, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deep soil layers.

This species is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils.
