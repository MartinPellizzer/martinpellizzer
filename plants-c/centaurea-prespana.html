Centaurea prespana

Centaurea prespana, commonly known as the Prespa centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region, particularly the Prespa Lakes area.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by pale blue bracts.

Centaurea prespana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
