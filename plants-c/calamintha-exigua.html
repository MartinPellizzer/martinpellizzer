Calamintha exigua

Calamintha exigua, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its fragrant foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha exigua is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
