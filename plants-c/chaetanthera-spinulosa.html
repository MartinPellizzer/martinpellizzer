Chaetanthera spinulosa

Chaetanthera spinulosa, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
