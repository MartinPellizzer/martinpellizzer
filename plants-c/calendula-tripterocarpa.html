Calendula tripterocarpa

Calendula tripterocarpa, commonly known as the three-winged calendula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy, linear leaves. Its unique feature is the presence of three-winged bracts beneath the flower head, giving it its common name.

Calendula tripterocarpa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
