Cestrum sendtnerianum

Cestrum sendtnerianum, commonly known as night blooming jasmine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly hairy underside.

Cestrum sendtnerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
