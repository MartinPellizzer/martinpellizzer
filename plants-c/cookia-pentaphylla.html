Cookia pentaphylla

Cookia pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf cookia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name.

Cookia pentaphylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, feathery appearance.
