Coronidium flavum

Coronidium flavum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces long, slender flower stalks adorned with small, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
