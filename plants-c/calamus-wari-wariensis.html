Calamus wari-wariensis

Calamus wari-wariensis, commonly known as the Wari-Wari reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and plays a significant role in local ecosystems.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
