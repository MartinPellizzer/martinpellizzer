Cinnamomum perglabrum

Cinnamomum perglabrum, commonly known as the slender cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other cinnamon and laurel species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
