Corydalis tenuifolia

Corydalis tenuifolia, commonly known as slender corydalis, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with two upper petals and two lower ones, resembling a spurred shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Corydalis tenuifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is also used in traditional medicine.
