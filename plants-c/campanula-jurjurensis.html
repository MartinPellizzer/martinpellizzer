Campanula jurjurensis

Campanula jurjurensis, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its root system is fibrous and adapted to cold climates.

Campanula jurjurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
