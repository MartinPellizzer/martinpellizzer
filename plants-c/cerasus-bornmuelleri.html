Cerasus bornmuelleri

Cerasus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its small, sweet-tart red fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a dark green color, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
