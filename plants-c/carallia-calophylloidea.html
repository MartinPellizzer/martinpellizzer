Carallia calophylloidea

Carallia calophylloidea, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
