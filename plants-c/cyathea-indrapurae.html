Cyathea indrapurae

Cyathea indrapurae, commonly known as the Indrapura tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with its fronds emerging from the top of the trunk in a spiral arrangement. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland and montane rainforests.

The fronds are often used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in local communities.
