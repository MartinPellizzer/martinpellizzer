Colobium taraxacoides

Colobium taraxacoides, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and produces a single, elongated flower head that resembles a dandelion. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring, followed by the formation of a pappus-equipped seed capsule.

The species is characterized by its simple, unbranched stem and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
