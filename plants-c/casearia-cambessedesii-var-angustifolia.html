Casearia cambessedesii var. angustifolia

Casearia cambessedesii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
