Cytisus alpinus var. pendulus

Cytisus alpinus var. pendulus, commonly known as the hanging broom, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its pendulous, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems with small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
