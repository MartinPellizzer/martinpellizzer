Crepis claryi

Crepis claryi, commonly known as Clary's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by tufted achenes, which are adapted for wind dispersal.

Crepis claryi is a herbaceous perennial that exhibits a low-growing, mat-forming habit.
