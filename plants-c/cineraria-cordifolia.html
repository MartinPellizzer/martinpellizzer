Cineraria cordifolia

Cineraria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Cineraria cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
