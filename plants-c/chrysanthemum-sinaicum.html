Chrysanthemum sinaicum

Chrysanthemum sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it blooms during the cooler months of the year.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
