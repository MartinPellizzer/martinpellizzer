Coreopsis grandiflora var. harveyana

Coreopsis grandiflora var. harveyana, commonly known as Harvey's tickseed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk, and its foliage is typically green and lance-shaped. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Harvey's tickseed is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
