Coelogyne ochracea subsp. conferta

Coelogyne ochracea subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the orange orchid or the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or brown lip.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a thick, leathery sheath.

This subspecies is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
