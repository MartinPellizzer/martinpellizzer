Capparis flexuosa subsp. polyantha

Capparis flexuosa subsp.

polyantha, commonly known as the clustered caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces elliptical, fleshy fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
