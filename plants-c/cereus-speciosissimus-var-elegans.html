Cereus speciosissimus var. elegans

Cereus speciosissimus var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
