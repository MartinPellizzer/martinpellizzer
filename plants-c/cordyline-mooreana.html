Cordyline mooreana

Cordyline mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with a red or purple tinge on the underside, adding visual interest. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters at the base of the plant, which are followed by dark blue berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
