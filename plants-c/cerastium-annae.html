Cerastium annae

Cerastium annae, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are oval or ovate, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils.

Cerastium annae is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
