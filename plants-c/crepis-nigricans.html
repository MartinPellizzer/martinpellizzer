Crepis nigricans

Crepis nigricans, commonly known as the black-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its seeds are small, wind-dispersed, and often found in dry, disturbed soils.

Crepis nigricans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
