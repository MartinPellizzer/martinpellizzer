Chamarea caffra

Chamarea caffra, commonly known as the African firecracker, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking red and yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.

Chamarea caffra is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
