Cardamine chelidonioides

Cardamine chelidonioides, commonly known as the yellow celandine, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its delicate appearance and early blooming habit, it is often found in temperate regions across Asia and parts of Europe.
