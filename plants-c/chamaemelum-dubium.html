Chamaemelum dubium

Chamaemelum dubium, commonly known as the lesser tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
