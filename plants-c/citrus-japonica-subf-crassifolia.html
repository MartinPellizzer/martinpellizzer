Citrus japonica subf. crassifolia

Citrus japonica subf.

crassifolia, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or Japanese kumquat, is a member of the Rutaceae family. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when mature.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and produces fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit's skin is thick and leathery, making it distinct from other citrus species.
