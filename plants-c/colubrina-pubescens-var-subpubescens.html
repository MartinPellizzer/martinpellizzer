Colubrina pubescens var. subpubescens

Colubrina pubescens var. subpubescens, commonly known as the silverleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
