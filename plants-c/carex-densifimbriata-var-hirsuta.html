Carex densifimbriata var. hirsuta

Carex densifimbriata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The inflorescence features dense, hairy bracts that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
