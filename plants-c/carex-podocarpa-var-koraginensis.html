Carex podocarpa var. koraginensis

Carex podocarpa var. koraginensis, commonly known as the Koragin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This variety is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
