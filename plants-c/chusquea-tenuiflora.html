Chusquea tenuiflora

Chusquea tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered weeping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in moist, shaded areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth habit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
