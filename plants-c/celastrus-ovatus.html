Celastrus ovatus

Celastrus ovatus, commonly known as the ovalleaf staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
