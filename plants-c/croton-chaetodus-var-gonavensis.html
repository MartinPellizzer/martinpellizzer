Croton chaetodus var. gonavensis

Croton chaetodus var. gonavensis, commonly known as the Gonave croton, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Haiti and the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, or orange, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
