Crambe hispanica subvar. borjae

Crambe hispanica subvar. borjae, commonly known as the Borja crambe, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
