Clitoria mexicana

Clitoria mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is climbing and can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Clitoria mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
