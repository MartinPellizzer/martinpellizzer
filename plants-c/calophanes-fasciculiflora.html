Calophanes fasciculiflora

Calophanes fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with a slightly glossy texture.

Calophanes fasciculiflora thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
