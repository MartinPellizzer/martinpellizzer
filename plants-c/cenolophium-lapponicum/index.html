Cenolophium Lapponicum

Cenolophium lapponicum is a rare and fascinating plant native to the cold climates of northern Europe and Siberia. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and heathlands. This small, herbaceous plant is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its unique structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in its ecosystem.
