Cyperus rufinus

Cyperus rufinus, commonly known as the red flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are inconspicuous but adapted for wind pollination.

Cyperus rufinus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
