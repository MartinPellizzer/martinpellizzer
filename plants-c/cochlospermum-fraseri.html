Cochlospermum fraseri

Cochlospermum fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
