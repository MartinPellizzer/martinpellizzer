Calceolaria pubescens

Calceolaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its high-altitude environment. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
