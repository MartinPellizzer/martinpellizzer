Capsella kyphosa

Capsella kyphosa, commonly known as the bent-thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Capsella kyphosa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
