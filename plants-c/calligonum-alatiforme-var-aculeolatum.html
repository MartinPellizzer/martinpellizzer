Calligonum alatiforme var. aculeolatum

Calligonum alatiforme var. aculeolatum, commonly known as the spiny arrowweed, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its succulent stems help store water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
