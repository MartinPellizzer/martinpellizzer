Capnorea campanulata

Capnorea campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in summer.

Capnorea campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
