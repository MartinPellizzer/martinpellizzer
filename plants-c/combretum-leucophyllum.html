Combretum leucophyllum

Combretum leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaf combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
