Cleretum apetalum

Cleretum apetalum, commonly known as the naked flowered cleretum, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically green or white, lacking petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
