Ceanothus americanus

Ceanothus americanus, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus americanus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
