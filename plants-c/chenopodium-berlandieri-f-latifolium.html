Chenopodium berlandieri f. latifolium

Chenopodium berlandieri f. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf prickly gooseberry, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and spiny stems, which are typical of the Chenopodium genus. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to the central and southern United States, where it thrives in dry, disturbed soils.

This species is often found in prairies, roadsides, and along creek banks, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
