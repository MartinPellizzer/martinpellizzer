Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tetragona

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. tetragona, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Hinoki cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that ranges from dark green to bluish-green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that give way to spherical, reddish-brown cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and aromatic wood make it a valued specimen in both horticulture and forestry.
