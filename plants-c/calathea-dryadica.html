Calathea dryadica

Calathea dryadica, commonly known as the Dryad's Calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, patterned foliage that changes color in response to light conditions. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with intricate veining that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
