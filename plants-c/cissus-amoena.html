Cissus amoena

Cissus amoena, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb structures.

Cissus amoena is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
