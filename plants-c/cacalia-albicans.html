Cacalia albicans

Cacalia albicans, commonly known as the white wood violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and have a subtle fragrance.

Cacalia albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
