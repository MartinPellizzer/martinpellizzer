Cattleya trianae var. dodgsonii

Cattleya trianae var. dodgsonii, commonly known as the Dodgson's Cattleya, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the Andes of South America.

This variety is distinguished by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant yellow lip and a contrasting white or cream-colored dorsal sepal. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and bright, indirect light. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in challenging conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
