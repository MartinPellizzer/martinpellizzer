Cymbopogon hispidus

Cymbopogon hispidus, commonly known as hairy lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a citrusy aroma. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.

Cymbopogon hispidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
