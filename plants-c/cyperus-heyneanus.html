Cyperus heyneanus

Cyperus heyneanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces triangular, boat-shaped fruits.

Cyperus heyneanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
