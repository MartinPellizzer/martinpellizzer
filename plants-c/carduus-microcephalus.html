Carduus microcephalus

Carduus microcephalus, commonly known as the lesser cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, rough burs. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to various parts of North America and is considered an invasive species in some regions. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can grow in a wide range of climates.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for many years.
