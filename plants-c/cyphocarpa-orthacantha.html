Cyphocarpa orthacantha

Cyphocarpa orthacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical seed heads.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
