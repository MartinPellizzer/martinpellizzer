Cyperus regiomontanus var. pittieri

Cyperus regiomontanus var. pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
