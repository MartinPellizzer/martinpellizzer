Carex pallescens f. cylindrica

Carex pallescens f. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
