Chondrorhyncha eburnea

Chondrorhyncha eburnea, commonly known as the white coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
