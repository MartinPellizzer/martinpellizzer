Cyperus laxiflorus

Cyperus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus laxiflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
