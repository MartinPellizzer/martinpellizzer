Caladenia tonellii

Caladenia tonellii, commonly known as the Pink-tipped Spider-orchid, is a species of orchid native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking pink and white flowers that resemble spider-like structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. Caladenia tonellii is a perennial herb with a short-lived flowering period, usually occurring in late winter to early spring.

Its unique floral morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the orchid family.
