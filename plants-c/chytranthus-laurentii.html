Chytranthus laurentii

Chytranthus laurentii, commonly known as the laurentii chytranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Chytranthus laurentii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
