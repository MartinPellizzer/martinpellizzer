Chrysanthemum sinuatum

Chrysanthemum sinuatum, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.

Its botanical structure supports both ornamental and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
