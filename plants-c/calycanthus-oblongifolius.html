Calycanthus oblongifolius

Calycanthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. The plant produces fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in loose panicles and are noted for their strong, sweet scent.

Calycanthus oblongifolius is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
