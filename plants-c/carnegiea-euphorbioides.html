Carnegiea euphorbioides

Carnegiea euphorbioides, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble a spoon, which are coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
