Capparis fascicularis var. elaeagnoides

Capparis fascicularis var. elaeagnoides, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, coastal areas.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
