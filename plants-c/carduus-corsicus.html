Carduus corsicus

Carduus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, sturdy stem that bears a single, large, purple flower head. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which are typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
