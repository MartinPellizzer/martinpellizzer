Capparis urophylla

Capparis urophylla, commonly known as the small-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
