Cusparia pentandra

Cusparia pentandra, commonly known as the five-angled spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to dry environments.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices.
