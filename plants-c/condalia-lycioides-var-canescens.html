Condalia lycioides var. canescens

Condalia lycioides var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
