Colutea abyssinica

Colutea abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
