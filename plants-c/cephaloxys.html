Cephaloxys

Cephaloxys, commonly known as the crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, showy flower heads that resemble a crown, hence the common name. They are native to various regions in North and South America, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Cephaloxys species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce yellow or orange flowers.

The genus includes several species, such as Cephaloxys microphylla, which is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
