Cistus canariensis

Cistus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary rockrose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to coastal salt spray. Its silvery foliage and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in Mediterranean and subtropical regions.

Cistus canariensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
