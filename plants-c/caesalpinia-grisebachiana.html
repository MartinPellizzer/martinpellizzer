Caesalpinia grisebachiana

Caesalpinia grisebachiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Caesalpinia grisebachiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
