Coffea vatovavyensis

Coffea vatovavyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar coffee, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in the understory of humid forests. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
