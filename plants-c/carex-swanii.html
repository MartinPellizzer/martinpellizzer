Carex swanii

Carex swanii, commonly known as the Swan's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including Japan and China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, often arranged in spikelets. Carex swanii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
