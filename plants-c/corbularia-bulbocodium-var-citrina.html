Corbularia bulbocodium var. citrina

Corbularia bulbocodium var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow bulbocodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
