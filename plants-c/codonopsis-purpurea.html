Codonopsis purpurea

Codonopsis purpurea, commonly known as the purple ginseng or Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its root system is similar to ginseng, which contributes to its traditional medicinal use.

Codonopsis purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in herbal medicine for centuries.
