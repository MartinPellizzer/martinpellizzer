Convolvulus capensis var. bowieanus

Convolvulus capensis var. bowieanus, commonly known as the Bowie's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
