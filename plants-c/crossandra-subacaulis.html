Crossandra subacaulis

Crossandra subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
