Cyathea phegopteroides

Cyathea phegopteroides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a mass of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. Cyathea phegopteroides thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its slow growth rate makes it a long-lived plant, often reaching maturity over several decades.
