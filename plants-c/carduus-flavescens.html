Carduus flavescens

Carduus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and thistles.

Its flowers are usually visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
