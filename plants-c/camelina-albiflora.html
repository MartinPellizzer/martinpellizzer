Camelina albiflora

Camelina albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered camelina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Camelina albiflora is often found in disturbed soils and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.

It is also noted for its potential as a source of oilseed for both industrial and nutritional purposes.
