Cryptocarya riedeliana

Cryptocarya riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a leathery texture and can have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
