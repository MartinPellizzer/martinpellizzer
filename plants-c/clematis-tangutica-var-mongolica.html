Clematis tangutica var. mongolica

Clematis tangutica var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along rocky slopes or in forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and vigorous growth.
