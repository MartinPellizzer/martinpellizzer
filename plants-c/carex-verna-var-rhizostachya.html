Carex verna var. rhizostachya

Carex verna var. rhizostachya, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This variety is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
