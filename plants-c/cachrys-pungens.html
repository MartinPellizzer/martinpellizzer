Cachrys pungens

Cachrys pungens, commonly known as the pungent saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

Cachrys pungens is known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
