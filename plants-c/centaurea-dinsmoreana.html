Centaurea dinsmoreana

Centaurea dinsmoreana, commonly known as Dinsmore's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
