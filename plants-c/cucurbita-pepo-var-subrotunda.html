Cucurbita pepo var. subrotunda

Cucurbita pepo var. subrotunda, commonly known as the scallop pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and irregularly shaped fruits that are often used in culinary and ornamental applications. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruits are typically harvested when they reach a mature orange color, indicating readiness for use.
