Calliandra haematomma var. minutifolia

Calliandra haematomma var. minutifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bark and its compound leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces fluffy, pink or red flower clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
