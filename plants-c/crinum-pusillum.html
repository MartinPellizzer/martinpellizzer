Crinum pusillum

Crinum pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped blooms during the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-shaped leaves that are often evergreen, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crinum pusillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
