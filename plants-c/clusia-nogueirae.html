Clusia nogueirae

Clusia nogueirae, commonly known as the Brazilian shield-leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Clusia nogueirae is also noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance to dry periods.
