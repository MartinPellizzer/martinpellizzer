Codiaeum albicans

Codiaeum albicans, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and prominent veins. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its vibrant foliage.
