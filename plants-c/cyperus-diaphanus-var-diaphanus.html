Cyperus diaphanus var. diaphanus

Cyperus diaphanus var. diaphanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
