Convolvulus luteolus var. fulcratus

Convolvulus luteolus var. fulcratus, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that ascend along supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture. The flowers are small, funnel-shaped, and typically yellow or orange, blooming in early summer. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
