Crataegus abdita

Crataegus abdita, commonly known as the Abhorsen's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Crataegus abdita is adapted to harsh alpine environments, showcasing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
