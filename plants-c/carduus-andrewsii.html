Carduus andrewsii

Carduus andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Carduus andrewsii is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
