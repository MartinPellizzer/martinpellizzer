Colchicum caucasicum var. raddeanum

Colchicum caucasicum var. raddeanum, commonly known as the Caucasian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces toxic alkaloids, particularly colchicine, which has been historically used in medicine.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
