Carex perprava

Carex perprava, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex perprava plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
