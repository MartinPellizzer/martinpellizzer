Calamagrostis laxa var. transsilvanica

Calamagrostis laxa var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ability to form dense clumps and its graceful appearance.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
