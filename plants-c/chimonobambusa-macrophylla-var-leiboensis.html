Chimonobambusa macrophylla var. leiboensis

Chimonobambusa macrophylla var. leiboensis, commonly known as the Leibo bamboo, is a unique variant of the giant bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and robust culms that can grow to significant heights. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its botanical structure includes a sympodial growth pattern, which allows for the development of thick, sturdy stalks.

The plant's foliage contributes to its ornamental value and plays a role in its ecological niche within its native habitat.
