Correllia

Correllia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, clustered flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to various regions in Africa, Madagascar, and parts of Asia. Correllia species are known for their ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making them resilient to drought.

Their flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to their ecological significance.
