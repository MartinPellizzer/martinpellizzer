Cachrys decursiva

Cachrys decursiva, commonly known as the creeping snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often spread along the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Cachrys decursiva thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
