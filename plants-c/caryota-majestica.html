Caryota majestica

Caryota majestica, commonly known as the giant fishtail palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that resemble the tail of a fish, hence its common name. The plant has a tall, slender trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of over 3 meters, providing a dramatic visual impact.

Caryota majestica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
