Clematis vitalba f. crenata

Clematis vitalba f. crenata, commonly known as the crested traveler's joy, is a compact, deciduous climbing plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a distinctive white or pale pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It is known for its vigorous climbing habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
