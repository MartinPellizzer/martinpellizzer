Camara trifolia

Camara trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically has trifoliate leaves, with three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually purple or white in color.

Camara trifolia produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing various alkaloids.
