Claytonia pogonophora

Claytonia pogonophora, commonly known as the pinkroot or woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and meadow habitats. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.

Claytonia pogonophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
