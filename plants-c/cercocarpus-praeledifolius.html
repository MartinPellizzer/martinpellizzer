Cercocarpus praeledifolius

Cercocarpus praeledifolius, commonly known as the redstem curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, leathery leaves that curl at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
