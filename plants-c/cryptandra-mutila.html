Cryptandra mutila

Cryptandra mutila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptandra, is a small shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. Cryptandra mutila is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
