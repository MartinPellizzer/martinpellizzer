Commelina micrantha

Commelina micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

Commelina micrantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
