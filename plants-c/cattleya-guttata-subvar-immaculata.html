Cattleya guttata subvar. immaculata

Cattleya guttata subvar. immaculata, commonly known as the immaculate spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves and its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The flowers feature a distinctive red or purple lip with intricate patterns, which is a hallmark of the species. This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
