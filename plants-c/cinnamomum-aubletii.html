Cinnamomum aubletii

Cinnamomum aubletii, commonly known as the wild cinnamon or cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is closely related to other cinnamon species. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and aromatic purposes.
