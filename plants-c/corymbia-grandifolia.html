Corymbia grandifolia

Corymbia grandifolia, commonly known as the slender bloodwood, is a species of eucalyptus native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin flakes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces creamy-white flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of native pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Corymbia grandifolia is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
