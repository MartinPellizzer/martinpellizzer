Cola pachycarpa

Cola pachycarpa, commonly known as the thickshelled cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sterculiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the western and central parts. The plant is characterized by its large, thick-walled fruits that contain hard, pale seeds. The leaves are typically large and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
