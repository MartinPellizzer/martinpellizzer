Croton garckeanus lusus abortivus

Croton garckeanus lusus abortivus, commonly known as the abortive croton, is a species native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or orange. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
