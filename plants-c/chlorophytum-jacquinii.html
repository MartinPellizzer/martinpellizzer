Chlorophytum jacquinii

Chlorophytum jacquinii, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a hardy, evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with white or silver markings. This plant is well adapted to low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal choice for indoor environments. Its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions contributes to its popularity as a houseplant.

Chlorophytum jacquinii is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, enhancing its value as both an ornamental and functional plant.
