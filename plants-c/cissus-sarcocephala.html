Cissus sarcocephala

Cissus sarcocephala, commonly known as the African snowball vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, round, waxy leaves that resemble small balls. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
