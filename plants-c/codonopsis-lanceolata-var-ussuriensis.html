Codonopsis lanceolata var. ussuriensis

Codonopsis lanceolata var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces tubular roots that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
