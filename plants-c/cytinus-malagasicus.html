Cytinus malagasicus

Cytinus malagasicus, commonly known as the Madagascar carpet flower, is a unique member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that produces striking, bright red flowers with a distinctive, fleshy, club-shaped structure called a pseudobulb. The plant is native to Madagascar and thrives in dry, rocky habitats, often growing in full sun. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions.

Cytinus malagasicus is also notable for its short life cycle, which includes a period of dormancy during dry seasons.
