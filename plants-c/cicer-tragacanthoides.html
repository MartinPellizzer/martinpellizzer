Cicer tragacanthoides

Cicer tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which contribute to its resilience in harsh conditions.

Cicer tragacanthoides is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
