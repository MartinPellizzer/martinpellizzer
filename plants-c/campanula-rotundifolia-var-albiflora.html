Campanula rotundifolia var. albiflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, broad leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
