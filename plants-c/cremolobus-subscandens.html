Cremolobus subscandens

Cremolobus subscandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy texture.
