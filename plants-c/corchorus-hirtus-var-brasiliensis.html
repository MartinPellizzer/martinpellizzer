Corchorus hirtus var. brasiliensis

Corchorus hirtus var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian pricklyleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used for fiber production and medicinal purposes.

The species is valued for its adaptability and ecological importance in its native habitat.
