Carex goodenowii var. polygama

Carex goodenowii var. polygama, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This sedge is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
