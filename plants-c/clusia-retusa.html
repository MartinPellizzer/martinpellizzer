Clusia retusa

Clusia retusa, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia retusa is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively low maintenance.
