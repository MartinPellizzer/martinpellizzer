Carex rafflesiana var. scaberrima

Carex rafflesiana var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their specialized morphology. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.
