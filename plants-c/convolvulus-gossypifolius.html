Convolvulus gossypifolius

Convolvulus gossypifolius, commonly known as the cottonleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
