Croton caryophyllus

Croton caryophyllus, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from red to orange and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Its leaves are alternate and typically have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
