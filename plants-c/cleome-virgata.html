Cleome virgata

Cleome virgata, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Cleome virgata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
