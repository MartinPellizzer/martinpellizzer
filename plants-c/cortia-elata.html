Cortia elata

Cortia elata, commonly known as the tall cortia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Cortia elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
