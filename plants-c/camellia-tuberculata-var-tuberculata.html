Camellia tuberculata var. tuberculata

Camellia tuberculata var. tuberculata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
