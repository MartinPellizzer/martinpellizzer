Coelogyne perrinei

Coelogyne perrinei, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with long, narrow leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its blooming period usually occurs during the winter months, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Coelogyne perrinei is also noted for its relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
