Clematis tibetana var. dentata

Clematis tibetana var. dentata, commonly known as the Tibetan toothed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the high altitudes of Tibet.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters, often climbing over rocks or shrubs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
