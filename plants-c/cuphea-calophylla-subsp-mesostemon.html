Cuphea calophylla subsp. mesostemon

Cuphea calophylla subsp.

mesostemon, commonly known as the red-flowered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
