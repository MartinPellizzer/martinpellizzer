Cacao theobroma

Cacao theobroma, commonly known as the cacao tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Americas.

It produces pods containing the seeds used to make chocolate. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with high rainfall and partial shade. Its leaves are large, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

The flowers, which grow directly on the trunk and branches, are white or pale pink and are essential for fruit production.
