Chlorocyperus longus subsp. badius

Chlorocyperus longus subsp.

badius, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
