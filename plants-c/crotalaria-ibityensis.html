Crotalaria ibityensis

Crotalaria ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
