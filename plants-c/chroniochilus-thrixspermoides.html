Chroniochilus thrixspermoides

Chroniochilus thrixspermoides, commonly known as the feather-armed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, branched pseudobulbs and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers during the wet season. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
