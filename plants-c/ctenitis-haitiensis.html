Ctenitis haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian serrate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically Haiti, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features fronds with a serrated or toothed margin, which is a distinguishing characteristic. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both sterile and fertile fronds.

Ctenitis haitiensis is often found in tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
