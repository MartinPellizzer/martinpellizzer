Carex halleriana var. bracteosa

Carex halleriana var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
