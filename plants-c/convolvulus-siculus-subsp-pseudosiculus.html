Convolvulus siculus subsp. pseudosiculus

Convolvulus siculus subsp.

pseudosiculus, commonly known as the Sicilian bindweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The flowers are small, funnel-shaped, and usually pink or purple in color, blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

This subspecies is closely related to other convolvulus species and is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology and growth habit.
