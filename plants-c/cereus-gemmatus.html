Cereus gemmatus

Cereus gemmatus, commonly known as the gemmiferous cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. Its stems are covered in sparse, pale spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

Cereus gemmatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
