Capparis copelandii

Capparis copelandii, commonly known as the Cape York gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Capparis copelandii is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
