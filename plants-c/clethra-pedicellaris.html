Clethra pedicellaris

Clethra pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Clethra pedicellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
