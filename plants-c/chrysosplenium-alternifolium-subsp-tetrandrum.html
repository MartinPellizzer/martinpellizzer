Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp. tetrandrum

Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp.

tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-stamen chrysosplenium, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and have four stamens, giving it its subspecific name.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
