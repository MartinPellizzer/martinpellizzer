Carpodinus turbinata

Carpodinus turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in dry environments.

Carpodinus turbinata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall.
