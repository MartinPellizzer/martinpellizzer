Carex × anticostensis var. longidens

Carex × anticostensis var. longidens, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to aquatic and semi-aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
