Cucurbita pepo f. alba

Cucurbita pepo f. alba, commonly known as the white-fruited gourd, is a botanical variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its pale, waxy skin and elongated, cylindrical fruits that typically range from 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and melons. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a vegetable in various cuisines.
