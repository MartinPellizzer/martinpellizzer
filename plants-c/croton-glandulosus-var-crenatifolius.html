Croton glandulosus var. crenatifolius

Croton glandulosus var. crenatifolius, commonly known as the warty croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and glandular leaves, which are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
