Centaurea elbrusensis

Centaurea elbrusensis, commonly known as the Elbrus centaury, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, with a basal rosette of leaves.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
