Camellia reticulata f. albescens

Camellia reticulata f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered camellia, is a botanical variant of the camellia species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white blooms that contrast beautifully with its dark green, leathery foliage. The plant is an evergreen shrub that thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowers typically appear in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its elegant appearance and cold hardiness, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
