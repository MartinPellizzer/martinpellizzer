Coreopsis ambacensis

Coreopsis ambacensis, commonly known as the mountain coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
