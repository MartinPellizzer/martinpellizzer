Campanula akhverdovii

Campanula akhverdovii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula akhverdovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
