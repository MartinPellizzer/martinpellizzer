Cistus guttatus

Cistus guttatus, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cistus guttatus is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
