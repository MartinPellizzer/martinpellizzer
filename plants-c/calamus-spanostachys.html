Calamus spanostachys

Calamus spanostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect rhizomes and elongated, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus spanostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
