Careya pendula

Careya pendula, commonly known as the hanging careya, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found near rivers or in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
