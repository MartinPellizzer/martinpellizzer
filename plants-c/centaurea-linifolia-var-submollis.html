Centaurea linifolia var. submollis

Centaurea linifolia var. submollis, commonly known as the softleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a soft, delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
