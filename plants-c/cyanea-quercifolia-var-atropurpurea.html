Cyanea quercifolia var. atropurpurea

Cyanea quercifolia var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf hibiscus, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that display a deep purple hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its foliage is often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its beauty and cultural significance, it is cultivated in many regions with warm climates.
