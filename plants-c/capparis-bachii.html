Capparis bachii

Capparis bachii, commonly known as the Bachii Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
