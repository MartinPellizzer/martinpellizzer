Cyperus mundii var. mundii

Cyperus mundii var. mundii, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water edges, and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an effective pioneer species in disturbed habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
