Crossoglossa palaciosii

Crossoglossa palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios' cross-lobed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its arid environment. Its flowers are typically small, with a distinctive cross-shaped lip, and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
