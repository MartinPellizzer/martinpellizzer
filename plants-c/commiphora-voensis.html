Commiphora voensis

Commiphora voensis, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
