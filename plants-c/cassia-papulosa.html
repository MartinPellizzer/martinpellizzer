Cassia papulosa

Cassia papulosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough in appearance. Cassia papulosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and vibrant flowers make it a striking addition to any tropical or warm climate setting.
