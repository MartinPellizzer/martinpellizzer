Chrysosplenium rhabdospermum var. rhabdospermum

Chrysosplenium rhabdospermum var. rhabdospermum, commonly known as the golden spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs. Its flowers are clustered in cymes and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
