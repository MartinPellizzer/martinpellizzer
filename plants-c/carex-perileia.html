Carex perileia

Carex perileia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, with a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a cylindrical shape.

Carex perileia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
