Ceanothus × intermedius

Ceanothus × intermedius, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 feet and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ceanothus × intermedius is also known for its drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
