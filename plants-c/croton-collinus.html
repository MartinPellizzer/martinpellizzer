Croton collinus

Croton collinus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
