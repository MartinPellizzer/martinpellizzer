Codonopsis japonica

Codonopsis japonica, commonly known as Japanese Codonopsis, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color and bloom in late summer.

Codonopsis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
