Cytisus capitatus subsp. pannonicus

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian broom, is a shrub native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, hairy leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
