Calamagrostis hookeriana

Calamagrostis hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in restoration projects.
