Comptonanthus molluginoides

Comptonanthus molluginoides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
