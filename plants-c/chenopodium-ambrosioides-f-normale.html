Chenopodium ambrosioides f. normale

Chenopodium ambrosioides f. normale, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a source of traditional herbal remedies.
