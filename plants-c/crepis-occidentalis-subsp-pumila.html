Crepis occidentalis subsp. pumila

Crepis occidentalis subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the dwarf western mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically forms dense cushions of foliage close to the ground, with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit to conserve moisture.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
