Centropogon simulans

Centropogon simulans, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and purple-tinted flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, violet-colored florets.

Centropogon simulans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
