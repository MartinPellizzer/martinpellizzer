Convolvulus finlaysonianus

Convolvulus finlaysonianus, commonly known as the Finlayson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has tendrils that help it climb and attach to supports, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
