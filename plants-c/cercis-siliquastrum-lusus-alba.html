Cercis siliquastrum lusus alba

Cercis siliquastrum lusus alba, commonly known as the white Persian lilac, is a rare and cultivated variant of the Persian lilac.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grey, and it produces small, elongated pods that turn brown as they mature.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its attractive appearance.
