Corydalis cusickii var. hastata

Corydalis cusickii var. hastata, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is distinguished by its hairy stems and flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
