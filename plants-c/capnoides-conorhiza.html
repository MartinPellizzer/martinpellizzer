Capnoides conorhiza

Capnoides conorhiza, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and possesses opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing swamp ecosystems.

Capnoides conorhiza is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
