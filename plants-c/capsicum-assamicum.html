Capsicum assamicum

Capsicum assamicum, commonly known as the Assam chili or Indian bush pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly Assam, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, with a mild to moderate level of capsaicin. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and branched.

Capsicum assamicum is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
