Crepidomanes subclathratum

Crepidomanes subclathratum, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has pinnately divided leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
