Crataegus disjuncta

Crataegus disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique distribution pattern, being found in isolated populations across its range.
