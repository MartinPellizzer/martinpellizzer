Coleus hairulii

Coleus hairulii, commonly known as the Hairulii coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other species of Coleus. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly textured surface.

Coleus hairulii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
