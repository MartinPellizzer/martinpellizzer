Cuphea gracilis var. major

Cuphea gracilis var. major, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features linear, opposite leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.

Cuphea gracilis var. major prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
