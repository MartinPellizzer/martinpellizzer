Chloraea fonkii

Chloraea fonkii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Chloraea fonkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
