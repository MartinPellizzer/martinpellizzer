Cattleya alutacea var. velutina

Cattleya alutacea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It blooms once a year, often during the spring or early summer.

The variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
