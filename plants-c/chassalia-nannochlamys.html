Chassalia nannochlamys

Chassalia nannochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
