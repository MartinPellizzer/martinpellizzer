Campanula scheuchzeri subf. altior

Campanula scheuchzeri subf.

altior, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

This sub-species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
