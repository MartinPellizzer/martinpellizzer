Chordifex stenandrus

Chordifex stenandrus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant is typically found in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical seed heads.

Chordifex stenandrus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
