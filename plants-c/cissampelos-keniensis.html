Cissampelos keniensis

Cissampelos keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan climbing star, is a vine native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal research.
