Conospermum longifolium subsp. longifolium

Conospermum longifolium subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in fine, white or greyish hairs.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
