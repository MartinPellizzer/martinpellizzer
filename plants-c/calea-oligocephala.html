Calea oligocephala

Calea oligocephala, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Calea oligocephala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
