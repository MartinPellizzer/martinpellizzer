Clusia dukei

Clusia dukei, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by orange or red berries. Clusia dukei is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to urban conditions.
