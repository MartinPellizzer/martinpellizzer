Carduus kotovskyi

Carduus kotovskyi, commonly known as Kotovsky's thistle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
