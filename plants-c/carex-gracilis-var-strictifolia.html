Carex gracilis var. strictifolia

Carex gracilis var. strictifolia, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clump-forming habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
