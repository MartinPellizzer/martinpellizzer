Ceropegia longipes subsp. villetii

Ceropegia longipes subsp.

villetii, commonly known as the Cape mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
