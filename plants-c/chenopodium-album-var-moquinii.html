Chenopodium album var. moquinii

Chenopodium album var. moquinii, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, particularly near the base. The leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, with young leaves used in traditional cuisines.
