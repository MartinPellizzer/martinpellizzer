Clerodendrum viscosum

Clerodendrum viscosum, commonly known as sticky clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil.

The sticky secretions from its leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
