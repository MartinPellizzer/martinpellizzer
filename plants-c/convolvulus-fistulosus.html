Convolvulus fistulosus

Convolvulus fistulosus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are slender and twining.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
