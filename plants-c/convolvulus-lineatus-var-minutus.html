Convolvulus lineatus var. minutus

Convolvulus lineatus var. minutus, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The small, five-petaled flowers are typically pale blue to lavender in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
