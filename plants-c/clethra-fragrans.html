Clethra fragrans

Clethra fragrans, commonly known as fragrant clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the plant family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Clethra fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
