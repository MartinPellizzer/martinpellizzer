Campanopsis undulata

Campanopsis undulata, commonly known as the wavy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in rocky or clay soils, making it a resilient choice for horticultural applications.
