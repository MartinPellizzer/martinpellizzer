Calendula arvensis var. macroptera

Calendula arvensis var. macroptera, commonly known as the giant marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist environments and its historical use in traditional medicine.
