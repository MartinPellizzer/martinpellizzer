Coccoloba allenii

Coccoloba allenii, commonly known as the Allen's beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the cashew family, Anacardiaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, typically found in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are an important food source for various bird species.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
