Condylocarpon glabrum

Condylocarpon glabrum, commonly known as the smooth bladderwrack, is a marine algae belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery thallus that grows in dense mats on rocky substrates in intertidal zones. The plant has a simple, unbranched structure with a central axis and small, bladder-like air bladders that aid in buoyancy. It reproduces both sexually and asexually, with spores released into the water for dispersal.

This species plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
