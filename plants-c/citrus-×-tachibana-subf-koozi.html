Citrus × tachibana subf. koozi

Citrus × tachibana subf.

koozi, commonly known as the Japanese kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that change color from green to orange as they ripen.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
