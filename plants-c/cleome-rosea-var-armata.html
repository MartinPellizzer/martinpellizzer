Cleome rosea var. armata

Cleome rosea var. armata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is often used as a ground cover or in mixed borders.
