Cineraria spinulosa

Cineraria spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cineraria spinulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
