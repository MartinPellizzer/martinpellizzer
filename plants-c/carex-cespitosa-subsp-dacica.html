Carex cespitosa subsp. dacica

Carex cespitosa subsp.

dacica, commonly known as the Dacian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
