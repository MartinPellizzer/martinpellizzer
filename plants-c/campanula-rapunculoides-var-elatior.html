Campanula rapunculoides var. elatior

Campanula rapunculoides var. elatior, commonly known as the greater bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
