Calliandra haematomma var. locoensis

Calliandra haematomma var. locoensis, commonly known as the red-veined calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like, bipinnate leaves that are often adorned with bright red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a valuable addition to both landscape and ecological settings.
