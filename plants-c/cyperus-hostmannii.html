Cyperus hostmannii

Cyperus hostmannii, commonly known as the Hostmann's flatsedge, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along waterways. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain wetland habitats.
