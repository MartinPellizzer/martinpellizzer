Coelogyne quinquangularis

Coelogyne quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with five angular, fleshy segments, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Coelogyne quinquangularis is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate for orchid enthusiasts.
