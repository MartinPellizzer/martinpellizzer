Chromolaena sanctopaulensis

Chromolaena sanctopaulensis, commonly known as the sacred heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in certain habitats.
