Caperonia heteropetaloides var. major

Caperonia heteropetaloides var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered caper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
