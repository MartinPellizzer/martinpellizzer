Cardiospermum integerrimum

Cardiospermum integerrimum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically vine-like, with twining stems that can climb or trail along the ground.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
