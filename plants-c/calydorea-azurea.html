Calydorea azurea

Calydorea azurea, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique coloration and delicate structure make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
