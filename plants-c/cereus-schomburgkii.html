Cereus schomburgkii

Cereus schomburgkii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique structure, with a distinct "base" and a colorful "top" that is actually a different species grafted onto the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
