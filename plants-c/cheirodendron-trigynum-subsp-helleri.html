Cheirodendron trigynum subsp. helleri

Cheirodendron trigynum subsp.

helleri, commonly known as Heller's cheirodendron, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
