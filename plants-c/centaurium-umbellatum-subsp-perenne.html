Centaurium umbellatum subsp. perenne

Centaurium umbellatum subsp.

perenne, commonly known as the perennial mouse-ear, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features small, bright red flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
