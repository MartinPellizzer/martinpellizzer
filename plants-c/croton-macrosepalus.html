Croton macrosepalus

Croton macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal croton, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Croton.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a wide range of hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
