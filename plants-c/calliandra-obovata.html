Calliandra obovata

Calliandra obovata, commonly known as the sicklepod or red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has bipinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its stems are often thorny. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
