Callista sanguinolenta

Callista sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that resemble blood droplets, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The leaves are ovate and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
