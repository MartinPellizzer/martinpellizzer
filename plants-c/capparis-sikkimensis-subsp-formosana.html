Capparis sikkimensis subsp. formosana

Capparis sikkimensis subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan caper, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan and parts of China. It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing several seeds, and it is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
