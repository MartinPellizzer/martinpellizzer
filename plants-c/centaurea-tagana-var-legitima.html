Centaurea tagana var. legitima

Centaurea tagana var. legitima, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine, hairy tissue. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink composite flower heads, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
