Cissus villosiglandulosa

Cissus villosiglandulosa, commonly known as the velvet glandular cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, aiding in its climbing habit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
