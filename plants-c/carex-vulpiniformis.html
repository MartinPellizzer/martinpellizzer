Carex vulpiniformis

Carex vulpiniformis, commonly known as the foxglove sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish to brownish flowers. It is often found in regions with temperate climates, particularly in North America.

Carex vulpiniformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
