Calamus salakka

Calamus salakka, commonly known as salakka, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant with long, slender rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
