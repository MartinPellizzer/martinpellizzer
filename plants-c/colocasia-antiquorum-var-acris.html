Colocasia antiquorum var. acris

Colocasia antiquorum var. acris, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green or variegated coloration. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe.
