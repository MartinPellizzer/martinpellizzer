Centaurea ispahanica subsp. sirjanensis

Centaurea ispahanica subsp.

sirjanensis, commonly known as the Sirjan centaury, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
