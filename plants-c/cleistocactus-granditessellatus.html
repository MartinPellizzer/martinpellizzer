Cleistocactus granditessellatus

Cleistocactus granditessellatus, commonly known as the giant tessellated cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, making it a nocturnal flowering species. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
