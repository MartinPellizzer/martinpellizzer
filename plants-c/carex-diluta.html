Carex diluta

Carex diluta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

Carex diluta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
