Cytisus candicans var. eriocarpus

Cytisus candicans var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving the plant its common name. The fruit is a slender, cylindrical pod that splits open to release the seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
