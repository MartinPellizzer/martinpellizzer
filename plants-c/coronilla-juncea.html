Coronilla juncea

Coronilla juncea, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are also used as a forage crop for livestock.
