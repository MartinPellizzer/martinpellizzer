Cyperus taylorii

Cyperus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often obscured by the dense foliage. It is native to parts of Africa and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in moist garden environments.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
