Cyathea ascendens

Cyathea ascendens, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, pinnately compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea ascendens is a slow-growing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
