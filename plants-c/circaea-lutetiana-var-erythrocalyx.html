Circaea lutetiana var. erythrocalyx

Circaea lutetiana var. erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-calyx whitebeard, is a variant of the whitebeard plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are surrounded by a distinctive red calyx that gives it its common name. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
