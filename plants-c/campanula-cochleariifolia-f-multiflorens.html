Campanula cochleariifolia f. multiflorens

Campanula cochleariifolia f. multiflorens, commonly known as the multiblooming bellflower, is a cultivated form of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces numerous small, sky-blue to white flowers throughout the summer season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and abundant flowering display.
