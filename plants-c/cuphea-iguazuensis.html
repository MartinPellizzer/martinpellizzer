Cuphea iguazuensis

Cuphea iguazuensis, commonly known as the flame bean or fire bean, is a striking tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow tubular flowers that resemble small beans, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. Cuphea iguazuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
