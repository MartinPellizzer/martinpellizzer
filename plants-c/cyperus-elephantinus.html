Cyperus elephantinus

Cyperus elephantinus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas.

Cyperus elephantinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
