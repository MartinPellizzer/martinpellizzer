Calamintha glabella var. diversifolia

Calamintha glabella var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable smooth germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both garden and ecological landscapes.
