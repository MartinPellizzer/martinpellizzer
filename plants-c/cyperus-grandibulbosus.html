Cyperus grandibulbosus

Cyperus grandibulbosus, commonly known as the broadbulb sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. Cyperus grandibulbosus thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
