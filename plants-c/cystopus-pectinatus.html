Cystopus pectinatus

Cystopus pectinatus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves that resemble those of holly. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in saline environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cystopus pectinatus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
