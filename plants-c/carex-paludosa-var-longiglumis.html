Carex paludosa var. longiglumis

Carex paludosa var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically elongated and have distinctive bracts, which are often longer than the flowers themselves. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
