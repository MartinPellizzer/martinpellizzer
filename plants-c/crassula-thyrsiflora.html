Crassula thyrsiflora

Crassula thyrsiflora, commonly known as the candle bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces dense inflorescences of yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula thyrsiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth and attractive foliage.
