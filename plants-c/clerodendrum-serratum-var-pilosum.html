Clerodendrum serratum var. pilosum

Clerodendrum serratum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
