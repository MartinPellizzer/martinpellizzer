Campanula rotundifolia subf. reflexa

Campanula rotundifolia subf.

reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which are typically reflexed or curved backward.

The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
