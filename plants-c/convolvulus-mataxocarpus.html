Convolvulus mataxocarpus

Convolvulus mataxocarpus, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
