Commiphora atramentaria

Commiphora atramentaria, commonly known as the black gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
