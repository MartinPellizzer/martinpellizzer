Cactus pictus

Cactus pictus, commonly known as the "Fairy Cactus," is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny, finger-like stems that can grow up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
