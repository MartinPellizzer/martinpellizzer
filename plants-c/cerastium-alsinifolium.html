Cerastium alsinifolium

Cerastium alsinifolium, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rosette-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its basal leaves and short, creeping stems.
