Cymbachne guineensis subvar. gabonensis

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and West Africa.

It typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
