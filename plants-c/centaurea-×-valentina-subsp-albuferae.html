Centaurea × valentina subsp. albuferae

Centaurea × valentina subsp.

albuferae, commonly known as the Albufera centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a hybrid species within the genus Centaurea, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, daisy-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is also considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
