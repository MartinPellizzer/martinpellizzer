Ceropegia decidua subsp. decidua

Ceropegia decidua subsp.

decidua, commonly known as the Desert Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are adapted to store water.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
