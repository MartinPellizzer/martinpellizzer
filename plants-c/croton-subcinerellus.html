Croton subcinerellus

Croton subcinerellus, commonly known as the black croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and striking, colorful inflorescences that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
