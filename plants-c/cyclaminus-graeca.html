Cyclaminus graeca

Cyclaminus graeca, commonly known as the Greek cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Cyclaminus graeca has a tuberous root system that allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival during dry periods.

Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
