Capparis trichopoda

Capparis trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, reddish fruits. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat succulent texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in its native ecosystem.
