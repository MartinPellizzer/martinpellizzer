Croton wassi-kussae var. stockeri

Croton wassi-kussae var. stockeri, commonly known as the Somali croton, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed or notched, with a variety of colors ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Croton, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
