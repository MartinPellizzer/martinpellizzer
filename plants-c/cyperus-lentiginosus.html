Cyperus lentiginosus

Cyperus lentiginosus, commonly known as the spotted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikes of tiny, pale brown flowers that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus lentiginosus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various moisture conditions.
