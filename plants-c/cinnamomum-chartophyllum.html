Cinnamomum chartophyllum

Cinnamomum chartophyllum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
