Ceropegia meyeri-johannis var. angiensis

Ceropegia meyeri-johannis var. angiensis, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
