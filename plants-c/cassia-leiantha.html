Cassia leiantha

Cassia leiantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cassia leiantha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
