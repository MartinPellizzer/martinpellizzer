Chloraea bella

Chloraea bella, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Chloraea bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
