Carya ovata var. ellipsoidalis

Carya ovata var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the ellipsoidalis hickory, is a variant of the shagbark hickory.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, elongated nuts and thick, fissured bark. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a doubly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
