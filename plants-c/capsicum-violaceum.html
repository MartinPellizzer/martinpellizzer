Capsicum violaceum

Capsicum violaceum, commonly known as the purple bell pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped fruits that range in color from green to deep purple. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are followed by the distinctive purple fruits that ripen gradually throughout the growing season.
