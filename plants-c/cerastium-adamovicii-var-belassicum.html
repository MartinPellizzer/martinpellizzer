Cerastium adamovicii var. belassicum

Cerastium adamovicii var. belassicum, commonly known as the Belassic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it contributes to the vegetation structure of mountainous regions.
