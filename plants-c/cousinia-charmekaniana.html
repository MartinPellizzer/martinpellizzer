Cousinia charmekaniana

Cousinia charmekaniana, commonly known as the Karakoram fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage is usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cousinia charmekaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
