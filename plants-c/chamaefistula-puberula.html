Chamaefistula puberula

Chamaefistula puberula, commonly known as the silky buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped berries that turn dark purple as they mature. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
