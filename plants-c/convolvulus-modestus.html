Convolvulus modestus

Convolvulus modestus, commonly known as the modest morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
