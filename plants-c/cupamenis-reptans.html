Cupamenis reptans

Cupamenis reptans, commonly known as the creeping cupamens, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
