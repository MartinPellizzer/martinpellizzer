Cirsium ochrolepidium

Cirsium ochrolepidium, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a purple-tinged center.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
