Centaurea webbiana

Centaurea webbiana, commonly known as Webb's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Centaurea webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
