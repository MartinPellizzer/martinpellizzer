Cistus × cyprius f. immaculatus

Cistus × cyprius f. immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in coastal and arid landscapes.
