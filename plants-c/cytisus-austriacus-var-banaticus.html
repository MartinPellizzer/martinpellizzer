Cytisus austriacus var. banaticus

Cytisus austriacus var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat shrub or Austrian broom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
