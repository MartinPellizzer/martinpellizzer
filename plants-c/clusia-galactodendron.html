Clusia galactodendron

Clusia galactodendron, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, making it of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
