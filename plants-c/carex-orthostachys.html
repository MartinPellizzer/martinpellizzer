Carex orthostachys

Carex orthostachys, commonly known as the stiff spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are stiff and grass-like, with triangular cross-sections characteristic of sedges.

The plant produces tall, erect spikes of small, clustered flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.
