Campanula cymaea

Campanula cymaea, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Campanula cymaea is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
