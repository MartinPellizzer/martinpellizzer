Cissus andraeana

Cissus andraeana, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cissus andraeana is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
