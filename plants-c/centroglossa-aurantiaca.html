Centroglossa aurantiaca

Centroglossa aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-tongued orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive orange hue. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat.

The orchid produces fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
