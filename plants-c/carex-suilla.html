Carex suilla

Carex suilla, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
