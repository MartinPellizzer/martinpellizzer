Cinnamomum hentyi

Cinnamomum hentyi, commonly known as Henty's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, blue-black berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
