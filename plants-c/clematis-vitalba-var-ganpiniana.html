Clematis vitalba var. ganpiniana

Clematis vitalba var. ganpiniana, commonly known as the Ganpinian honeysuckle vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
