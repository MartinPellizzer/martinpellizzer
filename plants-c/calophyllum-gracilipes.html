Calophyllum gracilipes

Calophyllum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender beauty leaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruits that are woody and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
