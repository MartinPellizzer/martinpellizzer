Campanula cochleariifolia var. hauryi

Campanula cochleariifolia var. hauryi, commonly known as the Hauri's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
