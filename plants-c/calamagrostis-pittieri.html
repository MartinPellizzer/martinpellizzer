Calamagrostis pittieri

Calamagrostis pittieri, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a robust root system that helps stabilize soil.

Calamagrostis pittieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
