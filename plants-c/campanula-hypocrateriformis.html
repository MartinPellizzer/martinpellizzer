Campanula hypocrateriformis

Campanula hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula hypocrateriformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
