Centaurea sintenisii

Centaurea sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, leafy stems. This species typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures beneath its flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
