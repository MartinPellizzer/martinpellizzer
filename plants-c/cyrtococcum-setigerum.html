Cyrtococcum setigerum

Cyrtococcum setigerum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Cyrtococcum setigerum is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
