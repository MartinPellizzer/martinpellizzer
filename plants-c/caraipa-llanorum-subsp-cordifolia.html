Caraipa llanorum subsp. cordifolia

Caraipa llanorum subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the Cordate Caraipa, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of northern South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
