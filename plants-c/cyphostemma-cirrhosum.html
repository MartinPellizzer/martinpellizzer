Cyphostemma cirrhosum

Cyphostemma cirrhosum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture with a distinct, sometimes hairy surface.

Cyphostemma cirrhosum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
