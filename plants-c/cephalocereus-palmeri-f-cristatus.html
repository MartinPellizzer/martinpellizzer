Cephalocereus palmeri f. cristatus

Cephalocereus palmeri f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate organ pipe cactus, is a unique variant of the organ pipe cactus, characterized by its distinctive crown-like shape.

It is a flowering cactus native to the Sonoran Desert, primarily found in Arizona and northern Mexico. The plant features a tall, cylindrical stem with a dense, layered growth pattern that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and greenhouses.
