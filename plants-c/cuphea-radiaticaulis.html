Cuphea radiaticaulis

Cuphea radiaticaulis, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea radiaticaulis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to add color to gardens throughout the growing season.
