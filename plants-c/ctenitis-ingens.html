Ctenitis ingens

Ctenitis ingens, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like structure with a thick, fibrous trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its striking visual presence in forest ecosystems.
