Cirsium amplexifolium

Cirsium amplexifolium, commonly known as the clasping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves that clasps the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Cirsium amplexifolium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
