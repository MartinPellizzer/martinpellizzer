Ceropegia flanaganii var. fallax

Ceropegia flanaganii var. fallax, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features delicate, translucent leaves that give it a ghostly appearance, and its stems are often trailing or creeping. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among collectors.
