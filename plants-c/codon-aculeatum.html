Codon aculeatum

Codon aculeatum, commonly known as the spiny codon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

Codon aculeatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
