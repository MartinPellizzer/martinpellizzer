Carex × turfosa

Carex × turfosa, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically narrow and parallel-veined. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Carex × turfosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
