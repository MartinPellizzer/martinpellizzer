Cissus glaucophylla

Cissus glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that often have a waxy, silver appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor settings.
