Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filifera-pendula

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filifera-pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, fountain-like appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are bright green on top and whitish underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is often used in gardens for its unique form and year-round aesthetic appeal.
