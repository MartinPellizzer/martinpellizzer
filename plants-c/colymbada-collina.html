Colymbada collina

Colymbada collina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Colymbada collina is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters.
