Crotalaria barkae subsp. zimmermannii

Crotalaria barkae subsp.

zimmermannii, commonly known as the Zambian crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bark contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
