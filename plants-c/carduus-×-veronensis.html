Carduus × veronensis

Carduus × veronensis, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus marianus and Carduus benedictus.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistle flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is a perennial herb with a robust root system and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
