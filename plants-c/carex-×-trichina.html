Carex × trichina

Carex × trichina, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Carex × trichina is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
