Carduus spinosissimus var. elliottii

Carduus spinosissimus var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silver-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Elliott's thistle is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
