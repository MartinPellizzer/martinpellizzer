Crotalaria genistella

Crotalaria genistella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in open, disturbed areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria genistella is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
