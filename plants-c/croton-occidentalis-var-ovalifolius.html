Croton occidentalis var. ovalifolius

Croton occidentalis var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored foliage, which can range from green to red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically oval in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking leaf colors.
