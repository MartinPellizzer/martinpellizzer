Campanula himalayana

Campanula himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a fine layer of down.

Campanula himalayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
