Cirsium vulgare

Cirsium vulgare, commonly known as common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive prickly texture.

Cirsium vulgare is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
