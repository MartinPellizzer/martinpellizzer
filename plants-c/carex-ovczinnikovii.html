Carex ovczinnikovii

Carex ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Ovczinnikov's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce brownish flowers, and it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Carex ovczinnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
