Calamagrostis mexicana

Calamagrostis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often lined with fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
