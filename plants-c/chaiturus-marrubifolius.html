Chaiturus marrubifolius

Chaiturus marrubifolius, commonly known as the Spanish lungwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces and reddish veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
