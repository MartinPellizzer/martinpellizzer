Carex subviridis

Carex subviridis, commonly known as the green sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex subviridis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
