Crassula sylvatica var. diaphana

Crassula sylvatica var. diaphana, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and fleshy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
