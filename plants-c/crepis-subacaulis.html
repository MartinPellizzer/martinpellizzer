Crepis subacaulis

Crepis subacaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. It has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and produces small, yellow flowers in the summer.

Crepis subacaulis is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
