Chrysopsis camporum var. glandulissima

Chrysopsis camporum var. glandulissima, commonly known as the glandular chrysopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant is distinguished by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture and appearance. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, adding visual interest to its natural environment.
