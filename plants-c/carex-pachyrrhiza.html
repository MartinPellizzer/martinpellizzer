Carex pachyrrhiza

Carex pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Carex pachyrrhiza is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
