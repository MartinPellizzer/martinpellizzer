Condea americana

Condea americana, commonly known as the American coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads that bloom in summer. Condea americana thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

It also serves as a valuable food source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
