Cirsium pannonicum

Cirsium pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Cirsium pannonicum is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
