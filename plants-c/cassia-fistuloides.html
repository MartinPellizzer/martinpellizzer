Cassia fistuloides

Cassia fistuloides, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is often fissured and can be used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
