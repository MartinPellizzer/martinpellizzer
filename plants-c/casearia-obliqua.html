Casearia obliqua

Casearia obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elliptic to ovate berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its attractive foliage.
