Cotoneaster acuminatus var. simonsii

Cotoneaster acuminatus var. simonsii, commonly known as the Sierra cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
