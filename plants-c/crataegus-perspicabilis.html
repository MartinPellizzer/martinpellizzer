Crataegus perspicabilis

Crataegus perspicabilis, commonly known as the transparent hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
