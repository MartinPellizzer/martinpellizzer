Chlorophytum katangense

Chlorophytum katangense, commonly known as the Katanga spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to Central Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum katangense is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
