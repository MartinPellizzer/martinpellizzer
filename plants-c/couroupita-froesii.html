Couroupita froesii

Couroupita froesii, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain multiple seeds. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it relies on specific insects for pollination.

The fruit, known as a "capsule," opens to release its seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts.
