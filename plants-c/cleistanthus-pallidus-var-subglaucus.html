Cleistanthus pallidus var. subglaucus

Cleistanthus pallidus var. subglaucus, commonly known as the pale cleistanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually pale green and covered with a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
