Cheirostylis monteiroi

Cheirostylis monteiroi, commonly known as the Monteiro's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
