Camellia sinensis f. formosensis

Camellia sinensis f. formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan mountain tea, is a botanical variant of the tea plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are often used in the production of high-quality oolong and black teas.

This form is valued for its unique flavor profile and aromatic qualities.
