Cissus aphylla

Cissus aphylla, commonly known as the leafless cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring stems that are often covered in small, glandular hairs. Despite its name, it does produce small, inconspicuous leaves that are typically reduced or almost absent. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its stems are used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued as an ornamental plant in certain regions.
