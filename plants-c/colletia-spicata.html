Colletia spicata

Colletia spicata, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent stems that store water. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Colletia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
