Commelina chantransia

Commelina chantransia, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while the inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts.

Commelina chantransia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
