Carex bonariensis var. achalensis

Carex bonariensis var. achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
