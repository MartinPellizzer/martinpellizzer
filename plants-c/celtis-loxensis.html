Celtis loxensis

Celtis loxensis, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by spherical, smooth fruits that turn from green to dark blue as they mature.

Celtis loxensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
