Calceolaria longepetiolata

Calceolaria longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms during the spring and early summer.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
