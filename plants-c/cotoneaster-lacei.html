Cotoneaster lacei

Cotoneaster lacei, commonly known as the lace cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Cotoneaster lacei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
