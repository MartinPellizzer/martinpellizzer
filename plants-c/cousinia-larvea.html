Cousinia larvea

Cousinia larvea, commonly known as the Persian hare's-foot, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia larvea is adapted to withstand drought and has a low-growing habit for survival in harsh conditions.
