Colchicum multiflorum

Colchicum multiflorum, commonly known as the Meadow Colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant has a short, stout stem.

Colchicum multiflorum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
