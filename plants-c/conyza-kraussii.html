Conyza kraussii

Conyza kraussii, commonly known as the common cudweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small, fluffy white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It produces a large number of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Conyza kraussii is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
