Carex macrocephala subsp. anthericoides

Carex macrocephala subsp.

anthericoides, commonly known as the broad-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green, with a dense growth habit.

The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of clustered spikelets, which are often reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in coastal areas, bogs, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
