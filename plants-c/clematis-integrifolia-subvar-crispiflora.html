Clematis integrifolia subvar. crispiflora

Clematis integrifolia subvar. crispiflora, commonly known as the wavy-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive wavy edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are follicles, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
