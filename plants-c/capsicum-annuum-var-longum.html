Capsicum annuum var. longum

Capsicum annuum var. longum, commonly known as the chili pepper or long pepper, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruits that can range in color from green to red, yellow, or orange depending on maturity. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with white stamens, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

This variety is widely cultivated for its spicy fruits, which contain capsaicin, the compound responsible for its heat.
