Citrus × sinensis f. sekkan

Citrus × sinensis f. sekkan, commonly known as the Japanese satsuma mandarin.

This citrus variety is characterized by its small, rounded fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a high juice content. It is native to Japan and is closely related to the common mandarin. The fruit typically ripens in late autumn and is prized for its sweet flavor and easy-to-peel skin.

It is often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts due to its aesthetic appeal and mild taste.
