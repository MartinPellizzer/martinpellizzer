Chionanthus malabaricus

Chionanthus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
