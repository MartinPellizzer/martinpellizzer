Christella perpubescens

Christella perpubescens, commonly known as the hairy Christella, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually pink or red and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
