Cynanchum trifurcatum

Cynanchum trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-branched milkweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It is often found in woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant contains milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
