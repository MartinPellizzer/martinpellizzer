Canala macrophylla

Canala macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf canella, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like citrus. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Canala macrophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal uses.
