Cyperus adoensis

Cyperus adoensis, commonly known as the African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Cyperus adoensis produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often reddish or brown in color, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in certain ecosystems.
