Carex sempervirens var. angustata

Carex sempervirens var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
