Campsiandra comosa var. laurifolia

Campsiandra comosa var. laurifolia, commonly known as the hairy campsiandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a soft, hairy texture on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical gardens.
