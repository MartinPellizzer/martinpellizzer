Convolvulus cardiosepalus

Convolvulus cardiosepalus, commonly known as the heart-sepal convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae, and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
