Cosmos sulphureus var. typicus

Cosmos sulphureus var. typicus, commonly known as the sulphur cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange blooms, which appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces feathery, bipinnate leaves that are soft and hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
