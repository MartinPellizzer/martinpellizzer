Cyperus lipomonostachyus

Cyperus lipomonostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, often forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
