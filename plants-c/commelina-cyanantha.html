Commelina cyanantha

Commelina cyanantha, commonly known as blue commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, seed-bearing capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina cyanantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
