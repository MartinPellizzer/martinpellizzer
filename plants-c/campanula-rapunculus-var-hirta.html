Campanula rapunculus var. hirta

Campanula rapunculus var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, which gives it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil.
