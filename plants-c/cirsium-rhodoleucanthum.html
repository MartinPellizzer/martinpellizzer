Cirsium rhodoleucanthum

Cirsium rhodoleucanthum, commonly known as the pink and white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, pink and white flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to dry, rocky environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a distinctive member of its genus.
