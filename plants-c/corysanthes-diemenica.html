Corysanthes diemenica

Corysanthes diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian Christmas Tree, is an evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in winter, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in Tasmania's flora.
