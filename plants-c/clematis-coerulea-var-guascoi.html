Clematis coerulea var. guascoi

Clematis coerulea var. guascoi, commonly known as the Guasco clematis, is a compact, flowering vine native to South America.

It produces showy, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or supports. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, colorful vines.
