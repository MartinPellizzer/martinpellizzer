Chamaecrista strictula

Chamaecrista strictula, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Chamaecrista strictula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
