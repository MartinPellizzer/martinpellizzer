Crataegus virginica

Crataegus virginica, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 12 meters and features thorny branches, simple oval leaves, and showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate stipules and persistent calyx.
