Cassia amoena

Cassia amoena, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface.

Cassia amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
