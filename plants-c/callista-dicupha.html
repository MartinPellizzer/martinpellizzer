Callista dicupha

Callista dicupha, commonly known as the purple-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Callista dicupha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
