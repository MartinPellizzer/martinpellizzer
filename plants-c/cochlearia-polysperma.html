Cochlearia polysperma

Cochlearia polysperma, commonly known as the coastal mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This plant is characterized by its small, basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils.

Cochlearia polysperma is known for its ability to tolerate salt and wind, making it a resilient species in harsh maritime climates.
