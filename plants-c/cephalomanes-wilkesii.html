Cephalomanes wilkesii

Cephalomanes wilkesii, commonly known as the Wilkes' sandpiper crab, is a species of crab belonging to the family Ocypodidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated carapace and is typically found in sandy or muddy intertidal zones. The plant-like appearance of its exoskeleton can sometimes lead to confusion with certain marine plants. It plays a role in coastal ecosystems by contributing to sediment turnover and providing habitat for smaller organisms.

However, it is not a plant but a crustacean, and its botanical features are primarily structural rather than related to photosynthesis.
