Cracca obcordata

Cracca obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate to obcordate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cracca obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
