Campanula loeflingii var. matritensis

Campanula loeflingii var. matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
