Campe barbarea var. hirsuta

Campe barbarea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that appear in the spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
