Citrus × deliciosa

Citrus × deliciosa, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible both in their peel and pulp. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is typically grown for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. The fruit's peel contains a high amount of vitamin C and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
