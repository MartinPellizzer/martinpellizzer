Convolvulus violaceus

Convolvulus violaceus, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive heart-shaped leaf. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
