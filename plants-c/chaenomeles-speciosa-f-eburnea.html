Chaenomeles speciosa f. eburnea

Chaenomeles speciosa f. eburnea, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
