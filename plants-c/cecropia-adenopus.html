Cecropia adenopus

Cecropia adenopus, commonly known as the giant water willow, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble umbrellas. The plant thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, often forming dense stands along riverbanks and swamps. Its hollow stems are adapted for water transport and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cecropia adenopus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native environments.
