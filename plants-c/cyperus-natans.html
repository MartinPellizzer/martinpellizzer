Cyperus natans

Cyperus natans, commonly known as floating chaffflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant is typically found in shallow water bodies, where its rhizomes help anchor it to the substrate. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus natans is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
