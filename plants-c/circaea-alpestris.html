Circaea alpestris

Circaea alpestris, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, opposite leaves. This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Circaea alpestris is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and supports local insect populations.
