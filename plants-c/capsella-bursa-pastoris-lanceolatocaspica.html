Capsella bursa-pastoris lanceolatocaspica

Capsella bursa-pastoris lanceolatocaspica, commonly known as the heart-shaped weed or common chickweed, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. Small white or pink flowers appear in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
