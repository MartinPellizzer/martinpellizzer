Calliandra capillata

Calliandra capillata, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, hair-like projections, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra capillata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.
