Cardamine pratensis var. spasskajae

Cardamine pratensis var. spasskajae, commonly known as the Spasskaja water cress, is a variant of the broadleaf water cress.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, calcareous soils near streams and wet meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a delicate, peppery aroma, and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes and leaves are edible, often used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in various regions.
