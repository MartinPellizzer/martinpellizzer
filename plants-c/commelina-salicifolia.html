Commelina salicifolia

Commelina salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the morning. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
