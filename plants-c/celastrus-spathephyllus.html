Celastrus spathephyllus

Celastrus spathephyllus, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny, flat seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some areas.
