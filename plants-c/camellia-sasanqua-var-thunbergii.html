Camellia sasanqua var. thunbergii

Camellia sasanqua var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a shrub native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late autumn to early winter. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive double petal structure. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
