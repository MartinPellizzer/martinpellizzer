Calophyllum brasiliense var. burchellii

Calophyllum brasiliense var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's yellow cedar, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
