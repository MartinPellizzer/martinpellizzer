Cyperus strigosus var. gracilis

Cyperus strigosus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

This variant is distinguished by its slender, more delicate stems compared to the typical form of Cyperus strigosus.
