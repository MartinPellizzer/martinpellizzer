Chelidonium japonicum f. lanceolatum

Chelidonium japonicum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as Japanese greater celandine, is a variant of the greater celandine species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its yellow-orange sap and lanceolate-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive yellow hue.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
