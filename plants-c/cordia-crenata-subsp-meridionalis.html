Cordia crenata subsp. meridionalis

Cordia crenata subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with crenate margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
