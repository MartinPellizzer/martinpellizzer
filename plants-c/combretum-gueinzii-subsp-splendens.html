Combretum gueinzii subsp. splendens

Combretum gueinzii subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the splendour combretum, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
