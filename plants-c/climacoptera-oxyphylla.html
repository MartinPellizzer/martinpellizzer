Climacoptera oxyphylla

Climacoptera oxyphylla, commonly known as the mountain flamebush, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Climacoptera oxyphylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
