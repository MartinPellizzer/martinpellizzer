Callicarpa bodinieri var. rosthornii

Callicarpa bodinieri var. rosthornii, commonly known as the Rosethorn beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
