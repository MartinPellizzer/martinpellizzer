Coreopsis cardaminifolia var. angustiloba

Coreopsis cardaminifolia var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets, blooming from early summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
