Cyperus granatensis

Cyperus granatensis, commonly known as the granadilla sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many Cyperaceae species.

Cyperus granatensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typically blooming during the rainy season.
