Conophytum apertum

Conophytum apertum, commonly known as the "Twin-leafed Stone Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features two fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, typically in late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum apertum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its compact size and drought tolerance.
