Centaurea jacea proles dracunculifolia

Centaurea jacea proles dracunculifolia, commonly known as the dragon's tail centaurea, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in summer, often forming dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name refers to the shape of its flower heads, which resemble a dragon's tail.
