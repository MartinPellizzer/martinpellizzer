Cypripedium concolor var. godefroyae

Cypripedium concolor var. godefroyae, commonly known as the Godefroy's yellow lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow pouch-shaped flower with a distinctive greenish throat and intricate veining. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, often on rocky or humus-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it produces a single, elongated flowering stem each year.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, this variant is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
