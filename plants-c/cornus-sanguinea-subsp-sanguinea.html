Cornus sanguinea subsp. sanguinea

Cornus sanguinea subsp.

sanguinea, commonly known as red dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vivid in winter, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
