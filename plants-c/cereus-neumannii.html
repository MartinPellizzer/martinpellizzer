Cereus neumannii

Cereus neumannii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
