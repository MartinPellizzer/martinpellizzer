Clematis gebleriana var. integrifolia

Clematis gebleriana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with smooth, entire margins and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can reach lengths of up to 15 feet.

The flowers are typically blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
