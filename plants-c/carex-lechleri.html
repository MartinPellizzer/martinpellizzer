Carex lechleri

Carex lechleri, commonly known as the blood grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Carex lechleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
