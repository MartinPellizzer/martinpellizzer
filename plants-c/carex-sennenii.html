Carex sennenii

Carex sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Carex sennenii has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wet or boggy conditions.
