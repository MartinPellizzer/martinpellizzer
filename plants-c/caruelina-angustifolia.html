Caruelina angustifolia

Caruelina angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caruelina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
