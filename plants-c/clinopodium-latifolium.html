Clinopodium latifolium

Clinopodium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic and possibly medicinal properties.

Clinopodium latifolium plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
