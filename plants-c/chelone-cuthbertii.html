Chelone cuthbertii

Chelone cuthbertii, commonly known as Cuthbert's honeysuckle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features attractive, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are a food source for wildlife. It is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Chelone cuthbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
