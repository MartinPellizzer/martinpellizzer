Corylus avellana var. lenticularis

Corylus avellana var. lenticularis, commonly known as the lenticular hazelnut, is a variety of the hazelnut tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lens-shaped nuts that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its unique nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible nuts.
