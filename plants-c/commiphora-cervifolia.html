Commiphora cervifolia

Commiphora cervifolia, commonly known as the elephant tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces a fragrant resin that has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.

The resin of Commiphora cervifolia has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
