Callista aphrodite

Callista aphrodite, commonly known as the Venus's looking glass, is a striking aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble glass, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Callista aphrodite plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small fish and invertebrates.
