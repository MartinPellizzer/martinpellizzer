Crescentia spathulata

Crescentia spathulata, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a fruit that is typically a smooth, green capsule containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
