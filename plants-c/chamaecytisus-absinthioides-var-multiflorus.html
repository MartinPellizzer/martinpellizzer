Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. multiflorus

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. multiflorus, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or sweet everlasting shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to golden flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, leathery seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
