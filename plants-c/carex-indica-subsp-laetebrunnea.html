Carex indica subsp. laetebrunnea

Carex indica subsp.

laetebrunnea, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters, which are typically arranged in a cylindrical shape.

This subspecies is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green and may have a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
