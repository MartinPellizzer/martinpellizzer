Cactus webbianus

Cactus webbianus, commonly known as the webbed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to quickly absorb rainfall.

Cactus webbianus plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
