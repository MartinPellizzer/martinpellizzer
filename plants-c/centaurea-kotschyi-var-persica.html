Centaurea kotschyi var. persica

Centaurea kotschyi var. persica, commonly known as Persian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, bright red to pink flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
