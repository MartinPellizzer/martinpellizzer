Crypsinus albidopaleatus

Crypsinus albidopaleatus, commonly known as the white-pale sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a white center, which are adapted to attract pollinators in its harsh environment. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its stems are covered in a fine, white coating. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
