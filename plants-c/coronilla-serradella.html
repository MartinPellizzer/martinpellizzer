Coronilla serradella

Coronilla serradella, commonly known as serradella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in forage and soil improvement. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in agricultural settings.
