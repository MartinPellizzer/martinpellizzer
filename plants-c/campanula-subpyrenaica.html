Campanula subpyrenaica

Campanula subpyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often on slopes and cliffs. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and has a preference for full sun to partial shade.

Campanula subpyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
