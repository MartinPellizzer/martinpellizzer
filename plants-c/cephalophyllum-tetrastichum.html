Cephalophyllum tetrastichum

Cephalophyllum tetrastichum, commonly known as the four-line headplant, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its structure is adapted to store water and withstand extreme temperatures, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Despite its hardy nature, it is a rare and specialized species that requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
