Cerastium lanigerum var. albanicum

Cerastium lanigerum var. albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.
