Cereus × mallisonii

Cereus × mallisonii, commonly known as the Christmas Star Cactus, is a hybrid cactus species originating from crosses between Cereus peruvianus and Schlumbergera truncata.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that form a bushy, star-like appearance, especially when mature. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooming, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and homes.
