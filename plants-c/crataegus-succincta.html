Crataegus succincta

Crataegus succincta, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Crataegus succincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
