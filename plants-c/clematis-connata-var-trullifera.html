Clematis connata var. trullifera

Clematis connata var. trullifera, commonly known as the Trullifera Clematis, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its stems are typically covered with fine, rusty-colored hairs.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
