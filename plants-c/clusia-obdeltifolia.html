Clusia obdeltifolia

Clusia obdeltifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is adaptable to a range of light conditions.

Clusia obdeltifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate pollution, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
