Caesarea albiflora var. ramosa

Caesarea albiflora var. ramosa, commonly known as the white-flowered sea holly, is a coastal plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive silvery-blue foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscape designs.
