Crotalaria macrocarpa

Crotalaria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea or yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria macrocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
