Cleome atropurpurea

Cleome atropurpurea, commonly known as purple cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cleome atropurpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
