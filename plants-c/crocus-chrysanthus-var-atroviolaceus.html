Crocus chrysanthus var. atroviolaceus

Crocus chrysanthus var. atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to deep purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from February to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that help it spread vegetatively. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and early blooming habit.
