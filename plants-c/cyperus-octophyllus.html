Cyperus octophyllus

Cyperus octophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus octophyllus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
