Campanula linifolia var. jaubertiana

Campanula linifolia var. jaubertiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
