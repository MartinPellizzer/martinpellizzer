Cytisus polytrichus

Cytisus polytrichus, commonly known as the creeping yellow flowering shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit with many stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cytisus polytrichus is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
