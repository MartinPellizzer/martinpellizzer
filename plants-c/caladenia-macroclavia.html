Caladenia macroclavia

Caladenia macroclavia, commonly known as the large-clubbed spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and distinctive, spider-like flowers with a prominent, club-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open forests and woodlands. It blooms between September and November, producing pale yellow to greenish flowers with intricate patterns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in some regions.
