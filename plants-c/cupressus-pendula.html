Cupressus pendula

Cupressus pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, and it produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Cupressus pendula is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
