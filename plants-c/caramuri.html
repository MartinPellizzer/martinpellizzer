Caramuri

Caramuri, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Caramuri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
