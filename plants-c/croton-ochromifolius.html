Croton ochromifolius

Croton ochromifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a distinctive yellow or orange midrib. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
