Crassula laxa

Crassula laxa, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that resemble tiny pearls, arranged in a cascading fashion. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for dry environments.

Crassula laxa is often used in rock gardens or as a hanging plant due to its compact growth habit.
