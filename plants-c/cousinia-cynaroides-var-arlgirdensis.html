Cousinia cynaroides var. arlgirdensis

Cousinia cynaroides var. arlgirdensis, commonly known as the Arlgird daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with dense, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
