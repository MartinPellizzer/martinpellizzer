Copiapoa hypogaea subsp. hypogaea

Copiapoa hypogaea subsp.

hypogaea, commonly known as the Santa Rosalia, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots are adapted to absorb water from the extremely arid environment.

It is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is highly valued for its ornamental beauty.
