Coreopsis ambigua

Coreopsis ambigua, commonly known as the zigzag coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its common name derives from the zigzag pattern of its stems, which twist and turn as the plant grows.
