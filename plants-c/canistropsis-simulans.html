Canistropsis simulans

Canistropsis simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange.

Canistropsis simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
