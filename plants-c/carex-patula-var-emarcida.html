Carex patula var. emarcida

Carex patula var. emarcida, commonly known as the emerald sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
