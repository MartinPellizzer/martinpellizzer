Cistus × stenophyllus f. albiflorus

Cistus × stenophyllus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and aromatic leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
