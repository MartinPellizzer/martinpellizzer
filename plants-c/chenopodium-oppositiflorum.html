Chenopodium oppositiflorum

Chenopodium oppositiflorum, commonly known as pigweed, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in various environments.

Chenopodium oppositiflorum is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a wide range of soil types and climatic conditions.
