Clematis falcifolia

Clematis falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, with a distinctive narrowing at the tip. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
