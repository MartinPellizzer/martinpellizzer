Cupressus formosensis

Cupressus formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are scale-like and dark green, arranged in crossed pairs along the shoots. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
