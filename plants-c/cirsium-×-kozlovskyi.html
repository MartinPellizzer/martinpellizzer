Cirsium × kozlovskyi

Cirsium × kozlovskyi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
