Cyathea kessleriana

Cyathea kessleriana, commonly known as the Kessler's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea kessleriana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
