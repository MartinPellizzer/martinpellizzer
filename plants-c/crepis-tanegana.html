Crepis tanegana

Crepis tanegana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Crepis tanegana is generally herbaceous and can be found in various parts of southern Europe and North Africa.
