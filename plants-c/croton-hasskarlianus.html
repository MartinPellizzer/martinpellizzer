Croton hasskarlianus

Croton hasskarlianus, commonly known as the Khasi croton, is a tropical shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves ranging from yellow to red and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
