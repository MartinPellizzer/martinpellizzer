Carex spicata subsp. andresii

Carex spicata subsp.

andresii, commonly known as the Andes sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in high-altitude environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
