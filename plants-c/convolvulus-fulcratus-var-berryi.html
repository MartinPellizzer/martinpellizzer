Convolvulus fulcratus var. berryi

Convolvulus fulcratus var. berryi, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
