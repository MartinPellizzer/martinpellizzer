Crepidospermum rhoifolium

Crepidospermum rhoifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves that reflect light, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in the morning. Its stems are hairy and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb various structures.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
