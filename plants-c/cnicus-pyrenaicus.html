Cnicus pyrenaicus

Cnicus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish or purplish tint, while the flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer.

Cnicus pyrenaicus is a perennial plant that is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
