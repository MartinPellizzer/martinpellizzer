Crataegus monogyna filicifolia

Crataegus monogyna filicifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with a serrated edge, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but typically used in jams and jellies.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.
