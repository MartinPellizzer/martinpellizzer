Cheiranthus × cheiri var. maximus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. maximus, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to red, bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable early-flowering species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
