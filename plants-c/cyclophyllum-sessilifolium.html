Cyclophyllum sessilifolium

Cyclophyllum sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaved cyclophyllum, is a unique plant belonging to the family Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cyclical arrangement of leaves, which form a rosette pattern around the central stem. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in well-drained soils.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
