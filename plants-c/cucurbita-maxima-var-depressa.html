Cucurbita maxima var. depressa

Cucurbita maxima var. depressa, commonly known as the Mexican squash or hubbard squash, is a variety of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is a sprawling, annual vine that produces large, elongated fruits with a deep green to orange skin. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit can weigh several kilograms when mature.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is often grown for both decoration and harvest in gardens.
