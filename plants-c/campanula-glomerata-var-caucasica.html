Campanula glomerata var. caucasica

Campanula glomerata var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
