Croton caryocarpus

Croton caryocarpus, commonly known as the orange croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from bright yellow to deep red. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Croton caryocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf colors.
