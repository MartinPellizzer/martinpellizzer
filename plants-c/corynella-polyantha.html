Corynella polyantha

Corynella polyantha, commonly known as the white-flowered corynella, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Corynella polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal flowering.
