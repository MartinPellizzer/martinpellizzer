Crataegus azarolus var. heterophylla

Crataegus azarolus var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Spanish white thorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, orange, or yellow berries in autumn. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Crataegus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
