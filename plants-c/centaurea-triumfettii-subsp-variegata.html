Centaurea triumfettii subsp. variegata

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and yellow-orange centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, cylindrical seed heads.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
