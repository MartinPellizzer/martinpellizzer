Carex potosina

Carex potosina, commonly known as the Potosi sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a dense, mound-like formation, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the spring.

Carex potosina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
