Cucumis melo var. saidi

Cucumis melo var. saidi, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, striped fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant typically grows on a vine, producing large, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, broad lobed leaves, and a distinct, fragrant aroma.

This variety is often cultivated for its high water content and use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
