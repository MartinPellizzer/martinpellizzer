Cardiochilos williamsonii

Cardiochilos williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's heartleaf, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small perennial herb with distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
