Crataegus illuminata

Crataegus illuminata, commonly known as the shining hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus illuminata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
