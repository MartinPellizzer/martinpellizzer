Cyrtosperma giganteum

Cyrtosperma giganteum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces striking white to yellow flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
