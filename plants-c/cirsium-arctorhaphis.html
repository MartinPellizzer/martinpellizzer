Cirsium arctorhaphis

Cirsium arctorhaphis, commonly known as the European thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cirsium arctorhaphis is known for its defensive spines, which deter herbivores and aid in seed dispersal.
