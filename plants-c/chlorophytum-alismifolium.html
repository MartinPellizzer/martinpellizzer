Chlorophytum alismifolium

Chlorophytum alismifolium, commonly known as the water spider plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features long, grass-like leaves that are often used in water features due to their ability to thrive in moist environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it a popular choice for aquatic gardens.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.
