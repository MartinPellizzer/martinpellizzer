Cyperus badius var. inconspicuus

Cyperus badius var. inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing cluster, which is usually inconspicuous, giving the plant its scientific name.
