Chamaerops khasyana

Chamaerops khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly the Khasi Hills. This palm is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of black, spherical fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
