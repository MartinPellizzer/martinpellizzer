Convolvulus ruspolii var. pilosa

Convolvulus ruspolii var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
