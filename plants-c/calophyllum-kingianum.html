Calophyllum kingianum

Calophyllum kingianum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The tree also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
