Caltha hiranoi

Caltha hiranoi, commonly known as the Japanese marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream sides. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth margin and a waxy coating.

Caltha hiranoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
