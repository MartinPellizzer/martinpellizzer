Carex paraguayensis

Carex paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

Carex paraguayensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
