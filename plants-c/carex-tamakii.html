Carex tamakii

Carex tamakii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex tamakii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
