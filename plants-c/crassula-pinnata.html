Crassula pinnata

Crassula pinnata, commonly known as the Fairy Fingers or Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, finger-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the growing season. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight.

Crassula pinnata is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant due to its unique appearance.
