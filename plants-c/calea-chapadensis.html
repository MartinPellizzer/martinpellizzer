Calea chapadensis

Calea chapadensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea chapadensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
