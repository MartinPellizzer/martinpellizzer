Calceolaria melissifolia

Calceolaria melissifolia, commonly known as the honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and hairy, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

Calceolaria melissifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
