Cestrum forsythii

Cestrum forsythii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum forsythii is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
