Crotalaria malabarica

Crotalaria malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar Crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with distinctive spurred petals, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
