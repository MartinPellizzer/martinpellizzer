Crocus georgei

Crocus georgei, commonly known as the George crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often emerging in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which are typically borne on short, sturdy stems. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a corm as its underground storage organ.

Crocus georgei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering period.
