Centaurea paczoskyi

Centaurea paczoskyi, commonly known as the Paczosky's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea paczoskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
