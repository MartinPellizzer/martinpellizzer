Cucumis melo var. deliciosus

Cucumis melo var. deliciosus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanically distinct variety of melon.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender fruits with a smooth, thin rind. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine, with palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is usually harvested when still immature, providing a crisp, mild flavor.

This variety is prized for its use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
