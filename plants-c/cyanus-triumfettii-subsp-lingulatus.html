Cyanus triumfettii subsp. lingulatus

Cyanus triumfettii subsp.

lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crown flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery underside.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in mountainous areas.
