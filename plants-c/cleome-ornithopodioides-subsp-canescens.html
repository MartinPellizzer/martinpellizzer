Cleome ornithopodioides subsp. canescens

Cleome ornithopodioides subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the woolly cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly rough texture. Cleome ornithopodioides subsp.

canescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.
