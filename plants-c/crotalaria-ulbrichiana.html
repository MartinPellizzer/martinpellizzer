Crotalaria ulbrichiana

Crotalaria ulbrichiana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria ulbrichiana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
