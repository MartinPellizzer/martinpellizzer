Cucubalus pumilio

Cucubalus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a small, round, green gourd that matures to a pale yellow or orange color.

This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruit.
