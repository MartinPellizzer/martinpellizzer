Carex tuckermanii var. niagarensis

Carex tuckermanii var. niagarensis, commonly known as Tuckerman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
