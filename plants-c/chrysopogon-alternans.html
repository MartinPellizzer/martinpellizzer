Chrysopogon alternans

Chrysopogon alternans, commonly known as the alternate grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
