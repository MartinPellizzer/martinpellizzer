Calycera involucrata

Calycera involucrata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like calyx that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals that are often reflexed at the tips.

Calycera involucrata is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
