Cassia leptophylla

Cassia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Cassia leptophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
