Carex emmonsii var. colorata

Carex emmonsii var. colorata, commonly known as the emmonsii sedge with coloration, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features bright green to reddish-brown leaves that vary in color depending on the season and light exposure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
