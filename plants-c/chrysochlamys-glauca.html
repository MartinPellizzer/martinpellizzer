Chrysochlamys glauca

Chrysochlamys glauca, commonly known as the silver-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy, glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Chrysochlamys glauca is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
