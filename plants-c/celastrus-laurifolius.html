Celastrus laurifolius

Celastrus laurifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bindfruit, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
