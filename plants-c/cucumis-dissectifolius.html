Cucumis dissectifolius

Cucumis dissectifolius, commonly known as the dissected-leaf cucumber, is a rare and unusual species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are similar in shape to typical cucumbers but are less commonly cultivated. It thrives in hot, dry climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
