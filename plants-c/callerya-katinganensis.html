Callerya katinganensis

Callerya katinganensis, commonly known as the Katingan callerya, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited but significant ecological roles in its native habitat.
