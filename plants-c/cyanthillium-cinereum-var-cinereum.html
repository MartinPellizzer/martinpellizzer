Cyanthillium cinereum var. cinereum

Cyanthillium cinereum var. cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, bristly seed heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
