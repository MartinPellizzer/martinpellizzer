Chlorocyperus megapotamicus

Chlorocyperus megapotamicus, commonly known as the yellow flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Its yellowish-green coloration is a distinguishing feature, particularly in its flowering structures.
