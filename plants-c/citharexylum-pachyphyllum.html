Citharexylum pachyphyllum

Citharexylum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved cithara, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, with leaves that are broad and often glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
