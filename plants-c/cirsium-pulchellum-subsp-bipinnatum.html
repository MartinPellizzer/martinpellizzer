Cirsium pulchellum subsp. bipinnatum

Cirsium pulchellum subsp.

bipinnatum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
