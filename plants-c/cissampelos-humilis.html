Cissampelos humilis

Cissampelos humilis, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a climbing vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Cissampelos humilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
