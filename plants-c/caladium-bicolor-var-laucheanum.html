Caladium bicolor var. laucheanum

Caladium bicolor var. laucheanum, commonly known as the "Painted Leaf" caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that comes in shades of pink, red, white, and green. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have intricate patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is typically grown as an annual in cooler climates but can be perennial in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 11.
