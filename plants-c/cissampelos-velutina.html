Cissampelos velutina

Cissampelos velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf or velvet bower vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated fruit capsules. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, textured surface.

Cissampelos velutina is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
