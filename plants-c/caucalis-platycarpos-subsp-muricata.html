Caucalis platycarpos subsp. muricata

Caucalis platycarpos subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels, appearing in early spring. The subspecies muricata is distinguished by its rough, serrated leaf margins and hairy stems.

It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
