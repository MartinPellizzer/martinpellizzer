Cephalaria tchihatchewii

Cephalaria tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a tall, slender stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
