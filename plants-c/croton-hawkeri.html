Croton hawkeri

Croton hawkeri, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "False Croton," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from deep red to orange and yellow, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add bold color to their landscapes.
