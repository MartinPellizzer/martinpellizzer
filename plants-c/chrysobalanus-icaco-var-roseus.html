Chrysobalanus icaco var. roseus

Chrysobalanus icaco var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered cherite, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, rose-colored flowers. It produces edible, round fruits that are about the size of a cherry, containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
