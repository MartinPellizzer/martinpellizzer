Cordia diospyrifolia

Cordia diospyrifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits with a segmented interior. The fruit is edible and contains a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often consumed fresh.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
