Crataegus holmesiana

Crataegus holmesiana, commonly known as Holmes' hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its serrated leaves and distinctive floral structure.
