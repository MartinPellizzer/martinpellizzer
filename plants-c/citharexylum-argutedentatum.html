Citharexylum argutedentatum

Citharexylum argutedentatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The wood of Citharexylum argutedentatum is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
