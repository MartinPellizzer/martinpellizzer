Chuanminshen violaceum

Chuanminshen violaceum, commonly known as purple skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
