Cupania sapida

Cupania sapida, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by orange to red fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.
