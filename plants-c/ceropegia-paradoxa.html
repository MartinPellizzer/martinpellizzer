Ceropegia paradoxa

Ceropegia paradoxa, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, pale green stems that often appear almost translucent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
