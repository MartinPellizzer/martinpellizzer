Cirsium peckii

Cirsium peckii, commonly known as Peck's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. Cirsium peckii typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
