Campomanesia cerasoides

Campomanesia cerasoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
