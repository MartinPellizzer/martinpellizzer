Campanula rosanoi

Campanula rosanoi, commonly known as the Rosano's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula rosanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
