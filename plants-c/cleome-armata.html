Cleome armata

Cleome armata, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking, spider-like blooms that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cleome armata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
