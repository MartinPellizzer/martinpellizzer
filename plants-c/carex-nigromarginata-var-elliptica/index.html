Carex Nigromarginata Var. Elliptica

Black-margin sedge, scientifically known as Carex nigromarginata var. elliptica, is a striking plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of North America. It is characterized by its dark green leaves with distinct black margins that give it a unique and eye-catching appearance. This sedgeland plant thrives in moist to wet conditions, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its compact growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for water gardeners and ecological restoration projects. With its adaptability and aesthetic value, the Black-margin sedge plays an important role in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
