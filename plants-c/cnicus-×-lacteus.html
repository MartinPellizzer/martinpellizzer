Cnicus × lacteus

Cnicus × lacteus, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy secretions. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Cnicus × lacteus thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
