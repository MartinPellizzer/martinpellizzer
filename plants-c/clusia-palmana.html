Clusia palmana

Clusia palmana, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Clusia palmana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.
