Cyphostemma meyeri-johannis

Cyphostemma meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with thorn-like projections and produces small, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant's leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a distinctive heart-shaped base. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Cyphostemma meyeri-johannis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
