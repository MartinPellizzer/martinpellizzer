Campanula liliifolia var. setulosa

Campanula liliifolia var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, which gives the variety its name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
