Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. polygyna

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

polygyna, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its subspecific traits, including its leaf morphology and floral structure.
