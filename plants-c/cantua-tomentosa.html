Cantua tomentosa

Cantua tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cantua tomentosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
