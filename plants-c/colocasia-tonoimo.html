Colocasia tonoimo

Colocasia tonoimo, commonly known as the Tonoimo arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 50 cm in diameter. The plant produces striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are edible when cooked, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
