Clinopodium megalanthum f. leucanthum

Clinopodium megalanthum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered wood betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to the typical form of Clinopodium megalanthum.
