Cardaminopsis amurensis

Cardaminopsis amurensis, commonly known as the Amur watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found near streams and in wet meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and aquatic habitats.
