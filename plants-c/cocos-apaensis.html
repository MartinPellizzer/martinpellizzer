Cocos apaensis

Cocos apaensis, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and large, edible fruits that resemble dates. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its trunk is often swollen and covered with a fibrous, spongy layer that helps store water.

Cocos apaensis plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem and has cultural significance in the region.
