Coelanthus complicatus

Coelanthus complicatus, commonly known as the fragrant thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
