Chaetanthera linifolia var. albiflora

Chaetanthera linifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when mature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.
