Croton volubilis

Croton volubilis, commonly known as the creeping croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a wide range of hues, including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking foliage.
