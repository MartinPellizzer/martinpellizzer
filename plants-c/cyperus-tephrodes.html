Cyperus tephrodes

Cyperus tephrodes, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus tephrodes produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
