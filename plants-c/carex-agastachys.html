Carex agastachys

Carex agastachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex agastachys is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
