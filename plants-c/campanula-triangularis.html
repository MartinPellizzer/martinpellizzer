Campanula triangularis

Campanula triangularis, commonly known as the triangular bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.

Campanula triangularis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
