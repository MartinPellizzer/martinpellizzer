Carduus coloratus

Carduus coloratus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, globular head.

Carduus coloratus is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.
