Cautleya lutea var. gracilior

Cautleya lutea var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender yellow orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
