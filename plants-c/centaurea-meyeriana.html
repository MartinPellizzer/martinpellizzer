Centaurea meyeriana

Centaurea meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, seed-like fruits. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
