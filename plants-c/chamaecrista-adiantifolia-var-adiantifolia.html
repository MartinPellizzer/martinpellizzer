Chamaecrista adiantifolia var. adiantifolia

Chamaecrista adiantifolia var. adiantifolia, commonly known as the fern-like cassia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seed pods are elongated and often contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
