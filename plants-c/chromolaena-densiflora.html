Chromolaena densiflora

Chromolaena densiflora, commonly known as ironwood or wild hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It is known for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Chromolaena densiflora is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its potential as a medicinal plant.
