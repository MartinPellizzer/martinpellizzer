Cometes apiculata

Cometes apiculata, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers with white markings, which resemble a star. It produces small, toxic berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
