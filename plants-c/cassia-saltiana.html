Cassia saltiana

Cassia saltiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of light. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Cassia saltiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
