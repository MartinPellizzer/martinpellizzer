Cucumis melo subvar. elongatus

Cucumis melo subvar. elongatus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruit shape, typically measuring up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a trailing vine with lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit has a mild, sweet flavor with a crisp texture, making it suitable for fresh consumption.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
