Coleosanthus oblongifolius

Coleosanthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, thick leaves that are typically oval or oblong in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
