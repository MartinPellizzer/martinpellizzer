Calamus viridis

Calamus viridis, commonly known as green calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically green on both sides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
