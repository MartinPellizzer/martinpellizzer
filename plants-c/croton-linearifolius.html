Croton linearifolius

Croton linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiky capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
