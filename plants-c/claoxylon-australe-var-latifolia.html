Claoxylon australe var. latifolia

Claoxylon australe var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often glabrous and have a distinctive reddish or brownish midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in wetland or riparian habitats and is often found in swampy areas with high moisture levels.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential horticultural value.
