Crepis bulbosa

Crepis bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Crepis bulbosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
