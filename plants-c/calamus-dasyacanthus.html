Calamus dasyacanthus

Calamus dasyacanthus, commonly known as the hairy thorned reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often surrounded by bracts. Calamus dasyacanthus is typically found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
