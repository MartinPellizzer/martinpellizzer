Celtis chuanchowensis

Celtis chuanchowensis, commonly known as the Chuanchow hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
