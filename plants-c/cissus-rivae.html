Cissus rivae

Cissus rivae, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cissus rivae is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
