Carex festiva

Carex festiva, commonly known as the festive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually dark green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring.

Carex festiva is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
