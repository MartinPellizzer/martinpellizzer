Cirsium iowense var. crattyii

Cirsium iowense var. crattyii, commonly known as the Cratty's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silver-green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and woodland edges.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
