Crocodilodes cirsiifolia

Crocodilodes cirsiifolia, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners.
