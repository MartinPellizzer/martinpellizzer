Callicarpa acuminata var. acuminata

Callicarpa acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. These fruits are edible and often used in culinary applications, though they are more commonly appreciated for their ornamental value.

The plant is valued for its vibrant foliage and attractive fruit display, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
