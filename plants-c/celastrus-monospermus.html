Celastrus monospermus

Celastrus monospermus, commonly known as the single-seeded staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a single, hard-seeded fruit that is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and pharmacological research.
