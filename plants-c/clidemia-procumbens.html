Clidemia procumbens

Clidemia procumbens, commonly known as creeping bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm seasons. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Clidemia procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
