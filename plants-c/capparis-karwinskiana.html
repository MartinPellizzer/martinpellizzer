Capparis karwinskiana

Capparis karwinskiana, commonly known as the Karwinskian Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellowish flowers, which are followed by round, greenish fruits.

Its botanical features include a distinctive root system and a tendency to produce thorn-like structures on its stems, which help in its survival in arid environments.
