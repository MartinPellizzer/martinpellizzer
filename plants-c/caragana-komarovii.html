Caragana komarovii

Caragana komarovii, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in arid and semi-arid regions with poor soil conditions. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which improve soil fertility.

Caragana komarovii is often used in reclamation projects due to its hardiness and ability to prevent soil erosion.
