Convolvulus timorensis

Convolvulus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It is a herbaceous annual with climbing stems that produce tendrils for support. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
