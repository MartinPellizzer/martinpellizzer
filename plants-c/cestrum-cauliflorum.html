Cestrum cauliflorum

Cestrum cauliflorum, commonly known as the red-veined cestrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters along the stems, a feature known as cauliflorous growth. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with prominent red veins. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
