Calamagrostis sylvaticostricta

Calamagrostis sylvaticostricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green leaves and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its dense growth habit makes it an effective erosion control plant in wetland environments.

Calamagrostis sylvaticostricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
