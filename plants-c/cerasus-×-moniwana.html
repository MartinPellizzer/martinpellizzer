Cerasus × moniwana

Cerasus × moniwana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × yedoensis, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The tree typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and has a rounded, compact shape. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces small, reddish-purple fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

This species is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and public spaces.
