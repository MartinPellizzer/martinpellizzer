Caucalis longisepala

Caucalis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with elongated sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and blooms from late spring to early summer.

Caucalis longisepala is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
