Capparis mekongensis var. crispata

Capparis mekongensis var. crispata, commonly known as the Mekong Caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, fragrant odor. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant is known for its edible fruits, which are typically harvested when fully mature.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
