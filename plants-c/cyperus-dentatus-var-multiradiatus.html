Cyperus dentatus var. multiradiatus

Cyperus dentatus var. multiradiatus, commonly known as the radiate flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
