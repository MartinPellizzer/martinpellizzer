Cleomella grandiflora

Cleomella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers range from pink to purple and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleomella grandiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
