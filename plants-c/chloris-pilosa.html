Chloris pilosa

Chloris pilosa, commonly known as hairy barleygrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant produces small, pale green or brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Chloris pilosa is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, where it contributes to ecological diversity.
