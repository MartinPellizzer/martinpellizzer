Clerodendrum citrinum

Clerodendrum citrinum, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum citrinum is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to attract pollinators.
