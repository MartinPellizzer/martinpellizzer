Carex cespitosa var. retorta

Carex cespitosa var. retorta, commonly known as the curled sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly curled or twisted appearance, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
