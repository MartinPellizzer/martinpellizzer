Crypsinus likiangensis

Crypsinus likiangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
