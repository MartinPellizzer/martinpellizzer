Calamagrostis poiformis

Calamagrostis poiformis, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.

Calamagrostis poiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
