Calophyllum eputamen var. eputamen

Calophyllum eputamen var. eputamen, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its essential oil, derived from the heartwood, is valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also used in traditional medicine.
