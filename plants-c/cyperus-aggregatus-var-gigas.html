Cyperus aggregatus var. gigas

Cyperus aggregatus var. gigas, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense vegetation, which can impact aquatic ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
