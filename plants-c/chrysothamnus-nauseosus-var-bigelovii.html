Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. bigelovii

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. bigelovii, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with many branches and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
