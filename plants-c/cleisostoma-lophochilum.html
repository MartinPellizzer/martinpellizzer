Cleisostoma lophochilum

Cleisostoma lophochilum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble a torch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in fluctuating environmental conditions.

The flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators like bees and flies.
