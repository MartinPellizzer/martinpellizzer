Cosmos aurantiacus

Cosmos aurantiacus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are green on top and silvery underneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cosmos aurantiacus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
