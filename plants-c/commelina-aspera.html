Commelina aspera

Commelina aspera, commonly known as rough commelina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Commelinaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and distinctive blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are usually rough to the touch, giving it its common name.

Commelina aspera often grows in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
