Caryodaphne australis

Caryodaphne australis, commonly known as the Australian candlewood, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Caryodaphne australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
