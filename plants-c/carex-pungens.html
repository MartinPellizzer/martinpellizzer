Carex pungens

Carex pungens, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Carex pungens thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
