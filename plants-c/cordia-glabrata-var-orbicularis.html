Cordia glabrata var. orbicularis

Cordia glabrata var. orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
