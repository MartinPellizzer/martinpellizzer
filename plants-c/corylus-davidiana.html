Corylus davidiana

Corylus davidiana, commonly known as the Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the hazelnut family, Corylaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers that develop into edible nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential in horticulture and nut production.
