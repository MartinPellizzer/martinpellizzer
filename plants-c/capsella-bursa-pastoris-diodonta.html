Capsella bursa-pastoris diodonta

Capsella bursa-pastoris diodonta, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from early spring to late summer. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
