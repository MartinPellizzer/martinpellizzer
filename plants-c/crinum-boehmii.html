Crinum boehmii

Crinum boehmii, commonly known as the Boehm's crinum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Crinum boehmii is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes many bulbous plants known for their dramatic floral displays.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
