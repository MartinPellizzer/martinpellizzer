Cissus marlothii

Cissus marlothii, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
