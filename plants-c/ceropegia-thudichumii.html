Ceropegia thudichumii

Ceropegia thudichumii, commonly known as the Cape York ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
