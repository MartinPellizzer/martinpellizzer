Cissus ambongensis

Cissus ambongensis, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus ambongensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
