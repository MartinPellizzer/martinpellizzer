Campomanesia phaea var. lauroana

Campomanesia phaea var. lauroana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or caipira cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and fruit yield make it a popular species in horticulture and agroforestry.
