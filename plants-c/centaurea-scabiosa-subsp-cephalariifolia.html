Centaurea scabiosa subsp. cephalariifolia

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

cephalariifolia, commonly known as the hairyhead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, spiky purple or red flower heads.

The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom in summer.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
