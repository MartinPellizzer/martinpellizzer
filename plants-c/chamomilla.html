Chamomilla

Chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is an aromatic herb belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of a central yellow disc surrounded by white or pale yellow petals. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Chamomilla is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from the flowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for herbal teas and medicinal applications.
