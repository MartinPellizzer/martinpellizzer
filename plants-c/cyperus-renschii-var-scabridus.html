Cyperus renschii var. scabridus

Cyperus renschii var. scabridus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

The varietal name "scabridus" refers to its rough or scabrous leaf surface, which is a distinguishing feature of this plant.
