Copiapoa variispinata

Copiapoa variispinata, commonly known as the variable spiny cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection from intense sunlight and herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical research.
