Colchicum bulbocodium subsp. versicolor

Colchicum bulbocodium subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a distinctive bulbous root system that stores nutrients and helps it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
