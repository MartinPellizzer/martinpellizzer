Carex maolanensis

Carex maolanensis, commonly known as the Maolan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with potential conservation significance.
