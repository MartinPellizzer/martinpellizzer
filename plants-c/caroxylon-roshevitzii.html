Caroxylon roshevitzii

Caroxylon roshevitzii, commonly known as the Roshevitzii date palm, is a rare and ancient species native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible dates. Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique and culturally important species.
