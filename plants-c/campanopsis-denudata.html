Campanopsis denudata

Campanopsis denudata, commonly known as the naked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Campanopsis denudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
