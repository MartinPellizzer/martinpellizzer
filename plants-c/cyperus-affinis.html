Cyperus affinis

Cyperus affinis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Cyperus affinis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
