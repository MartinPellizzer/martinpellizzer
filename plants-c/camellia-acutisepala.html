Camellia acutisepala

Camellia acutisepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepaled camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers with a distinctive, sharp-pointed sepals that set it apart from other camellia species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia acutisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
