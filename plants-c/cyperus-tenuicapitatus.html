Cyperus tenuicapitatus

Cyperus tenuicapitatus, commonly known as slender-headed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear dense clusters of tiny, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus tenuicapitatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
