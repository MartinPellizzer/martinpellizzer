Cotoneaster kangdingensis

Cotoneaster kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
