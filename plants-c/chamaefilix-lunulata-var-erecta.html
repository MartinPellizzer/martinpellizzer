Chamaefilix lunulata var. erecta

Chamaefilix lunulata var. erecta, commonly known as the upright moonflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small white flowers that open at night. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique nocturnal blooming pattern and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to native plant gardens.
