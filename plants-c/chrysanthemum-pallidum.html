Chrysanthemum pallidum

Chrysanthemum pallidum, commonly known as the pale chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Chrysanthemum pallidum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
