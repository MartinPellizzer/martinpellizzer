Chrysanthemum dyris

Chrysanthemum dyris, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy, daisy-like blooms with white to pale yellow petals surrounding a central disc of smaller florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a herbaceous perennial.

Chrysanthemum dyris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
