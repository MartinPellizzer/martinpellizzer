Crocus reticulatus var. auritextus

Crocus reticulatus var. auritextus, commonly known as the auriculated crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, saucer-shaped flowers with a deep violet or purple hue, often adorned with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
