Cotoneaster × gracia

Cotoneaster × gracia, commonly known as the Grace cotoneaster, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

Cotoneaster × gracia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and winter berries.
