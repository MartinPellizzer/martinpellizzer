Cleistanthus travancorensis var. travancorensis

Cleistanthus travancorensis var. travancorensis, commonly known as the Travancore cleistanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and has elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
