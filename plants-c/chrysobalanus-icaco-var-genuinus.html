Chrysobalanus icaco var. genuinus

Chrysobalanus icaco var. genuinus, commonly known as the West Indian chestnut, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalaneceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange or red drupes containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ability to grow in disturbed sites make it a notable species in tropical horticulture.
