Cassia basifolia var. aristulata

Cassia basifolia var. aristulata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant has compound leaves with slender, pointed leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
