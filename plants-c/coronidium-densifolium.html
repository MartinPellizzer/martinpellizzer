Coronidium densifolium

Coronidium densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved coronidium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Coronidium densifolium is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
