Capsicum lundellii

Capsicum lundellii, commonly known as the desert chili or Lundell's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. It has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines for its mild to medium heat level and unique flavor.
