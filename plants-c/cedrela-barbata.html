Cedrela barbata

Cedrela barbata, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or yellow cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and fragrant, yellowish wood. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for reforestation and conservation efforts.
