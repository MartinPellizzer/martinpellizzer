Cameraria

Cameraria, commonly known as the leafminer moth, is a genus of small moths belonging to the family Lyonetiidae.

The larvae of these moths are known for feeding on the leaves of various plants, creating distinctive mines or tunnels within the leaf tissue. Cameraria species are often found in temperate regions and can be hosts to a variety of plants, including shrubs and trees. Their life cycle involves several stages, with the larvae being the most damaging to plant health.

These moths play a role in ecological systems, though they can be considered pests in agricultural and horticultural settings.
