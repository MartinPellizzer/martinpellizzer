Carissa brownii var. angustifolia

Carissa brownii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant bears small, round, yellow berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are narrow and glossy, with a distinctive waxy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
