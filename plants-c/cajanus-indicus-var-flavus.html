Cajanus indicus var. flavus

Cajanus indicus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a valuable crop in arid and semi-arid environments.
