Cynorkis ochroglossa

Cynorkis ochroglossa, commonly known as the golden-tongued orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive orange lip, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.

Botanically, it is characterized by its complex inflorescence and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its natural habitat.
