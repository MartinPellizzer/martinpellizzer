Cryptomeria elegans

Cryptomeria elegans, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

The tree produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs along the shoots. Its cones are woody and contain two to four seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cryptomeria elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes worldwide.
