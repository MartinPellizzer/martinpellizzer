Croton virgultosus

Croton virgultosus, commonly known as the Florida croton, is a tropical shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive yellow or orange coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.
