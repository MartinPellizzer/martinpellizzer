Chamaepentas greenwayi var. glabra

Chamaepentas greenwayi var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf greenway senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
