Calliandra izalcoensis

Calliandra izalcoensis, commonly known as the Izalco calliandra, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical shapes. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, fern-like leaflets.

Calliandra izalcoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
