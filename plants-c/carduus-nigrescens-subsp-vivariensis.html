Carduus nigrescens subsp. vivariensis

Carduus nigrescens subsp.

vivariensis, commonly known as the blackthorn sowthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces a flowering stem with several bracts. Its seeds are small, oval, and often have a pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
