Coleus murrayi

Coleus murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Coleus murrayi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
