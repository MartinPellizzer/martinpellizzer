Canna flavescens

Canna flavescens, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a pale green or yellowish tint. The plant produces showy, sword-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Canna flavescens is also valued for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for water gardens and pond edges.
