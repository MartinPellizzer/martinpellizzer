Colchicum robustum

Colchicum robustum, commonly known as the giant autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features a robust, tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winter conditions. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and often with a waxy coating.

Colchicum robustum is noted for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids like colchicine, which have been used in the treatment of gout and other inflammatory conditions.
