Cassia richardiana

Cassia richardiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant produces oval, ribbed pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Cassia richardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
