Carex opetiola

Carex opetiola, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often borne on tall, leafy inflorescences.

Carex opetiola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
