Cuphea cordata

Cuphea cordata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
