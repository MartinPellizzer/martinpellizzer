Cassia hispidula var. killipii

Cassia hispidula var. killipii, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, papery flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several oblong seeds covered in a fine, silky coating.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
