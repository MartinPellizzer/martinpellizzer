Christella papilio

Christella papilio, commonly known as the golden waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and emit a mild fragrance.
