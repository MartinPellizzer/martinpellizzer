Crotalaria inflexa

Crotalaria inflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has medicinal properties.

Crotalaria inflexa plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
