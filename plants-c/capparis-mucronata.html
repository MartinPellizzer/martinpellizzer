Capparis mucronata

Capparis mucronata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its fruit.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
