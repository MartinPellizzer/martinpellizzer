Chrysophyllum pumilum f. glabrescens

Chrysophyllum pumilum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glossy pumila cherry, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are sweet and acidic. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a fruit-bearing plant in warm climates.
