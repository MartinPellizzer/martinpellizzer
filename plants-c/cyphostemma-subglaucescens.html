Cyphostemma subglaucescens

Cyphostemma subglaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green cyphostemma, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically blue-green in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss.

Cyphostemma subglaucescens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
