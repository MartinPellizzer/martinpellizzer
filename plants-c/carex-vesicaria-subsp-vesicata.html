Carex vesicaria subsp. vesicata

Carex vesicaria subsp.

vesicata, commonly known as bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream sides. Its flowering stems bear tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
