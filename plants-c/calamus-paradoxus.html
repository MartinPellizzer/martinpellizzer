Calamus paradoxus

Calamus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and crafts. Its rhizomes are particularly valued for their aromatic properties and are used in perfumery.

Calamus paradoxus thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
