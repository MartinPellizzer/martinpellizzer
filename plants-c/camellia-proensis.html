Camellia proensis

Camellia proensis, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Camellia proensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
