Carex satsumensis

Carex satsumensis, commonly known as the Satsuma sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex satsumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
