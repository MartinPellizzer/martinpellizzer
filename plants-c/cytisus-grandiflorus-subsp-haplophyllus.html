Cytisus grandiflorus subsp. haplophyllus

Cytisus grandiflorus subsp.

haplophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive yellowish-green color and a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are yellow, pea-like, and appear in clusters, blooming in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
