Calamagrostis tibetica

Calamagrostis tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan reed, is a grass species native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Calamagrostis tibetica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
