Campanula hierosolymitana

Campanula hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The Jerusalem bellflower is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
