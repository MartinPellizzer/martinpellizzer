Cardamine oligosperma var. bracteata

Cardamine oligosperma var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is native to parts of Asia and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
