Cheiridopsis hallii

Cheiridopsis hallii, commonly known as the Hall's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the winter months. Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
