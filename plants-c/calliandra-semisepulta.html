Calliandra semisepulta

Calliandra semisepulta, commonly known as the partial-lobed calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of small, showy flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets.

Calliandra semisepulta is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
