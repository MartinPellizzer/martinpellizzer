Calamagrostis lanceolata f. sublaxa

Calamagrostis lanceolata f. sublaxa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
