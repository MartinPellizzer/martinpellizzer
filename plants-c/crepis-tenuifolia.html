Crepis tenuifolia

Crepis tenuifolia, commonly known as slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Crepis tenuifolia is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
