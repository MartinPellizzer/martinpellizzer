Cucumis asper

Cucumis asper, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny skin and elongated, cylindrical fruit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit develops from the female flowers.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, refreshing taste.
