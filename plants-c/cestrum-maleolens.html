Cestrum maleolens

Cestrum maleolens, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
