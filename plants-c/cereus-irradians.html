Cereus irradians

Cereus irradians, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, showy flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Cereus irradians is a popular choice for desert gardens due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
