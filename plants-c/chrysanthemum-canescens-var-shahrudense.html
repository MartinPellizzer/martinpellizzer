Chrysanthemum canescens var. shahrudense

Chrysanthemum canescens var. shahrudense, commonly known as the Shahrud chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow to white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
