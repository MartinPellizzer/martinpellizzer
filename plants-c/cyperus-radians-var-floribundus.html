Cyperus radians var. floribundus

Cyperus radians var. floribundus, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of wetland habitats, contributing to its ecological significance.
