Cacalia pumila

Cacalia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hawksbeard, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cacalia pumila is characterized by its short stature and distinctive foliage, making it a notable wildflower in its native range.
