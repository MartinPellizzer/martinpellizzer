Cylindropuntia ramosissima

Cylindropuntia ramosissima, commonly known as the cholla cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. Its adaptations include reduced leaves and a succulent stem structure to store water efficiently in arid environments.

The cholla cactus plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for desert wildlife.
