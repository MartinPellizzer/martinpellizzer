Cardamine hirsuta f. pensylvanica

Cardamine hirsuta f. pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are hairy and have a peppery taste, making it a culinary and medicinal plant.

It is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
