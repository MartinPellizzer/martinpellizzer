Cupressus arizonica f. typica

Cupressus arizonica f. typica, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies of the Arizona cypress native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its dense, pyramidal shape and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in landscaping.
