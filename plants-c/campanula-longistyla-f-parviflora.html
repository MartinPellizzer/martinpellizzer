Campanula longistyla f. parviflora

Campanula longistyla f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
