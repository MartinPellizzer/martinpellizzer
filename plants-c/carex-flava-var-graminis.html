Carex flava var. graminis

Carex flava var. graminis, commonly known as yellow sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish, containing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex flava var. graminis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
