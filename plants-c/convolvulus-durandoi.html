Convolvulus durandoi

Convolvulus durandoi, commonly known as the Durango morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth forms.

Convolvulus durandoi is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that can withstand drought conditions.
