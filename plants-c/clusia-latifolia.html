Clusia latifolia

Clusia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green on the top and white or silver on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Clusia latifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution, making it a hardy plant for urban environments.
