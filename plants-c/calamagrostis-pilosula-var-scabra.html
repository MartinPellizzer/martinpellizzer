Calamagrostis pilosula var. scabra

Calamagrostis pilosula var. scabra, commonly known as rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy texture and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its foliage is often blue-green in color, and it produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
