Campanula amorgina

Campanula amorgina, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the island of Santorini in Greece.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Campanula amorgina is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
