Copaifera langsdorffii var. langsdorffii

Copaifera langsdorffii var. langsdorffii, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The tree is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its most notable feature is the aromatic resin it exudes, which is used in traditional medicine and for making incense.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
