Cladium lavarum var. madagascariensis

Cladium lavarum var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. This variety thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of thatching material in local communities.
