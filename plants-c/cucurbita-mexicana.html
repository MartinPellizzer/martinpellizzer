Cucurbita mexicana

Cucurbita mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash. The plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and cylindrical, often hairy stems. Its fruits can vary in shape and color, ranging from orange to green, and are typically used in traditional cuisines.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
