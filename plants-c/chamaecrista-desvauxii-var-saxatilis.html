Chamaecrista desvauxii var. saxatilis

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock burr or desert burr, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with small, compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
