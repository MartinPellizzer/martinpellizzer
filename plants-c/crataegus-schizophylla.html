Crataegus schizophylla

Crataegus schizophylla, commonly known as the split-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into 5 to 9 lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus schizophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
