Carduus uniflorus

Carduus uniflorus, commonly known as the singleflower thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring spiny leaves and a tall, often branched stem. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, and they bloom from late summer to early autumn.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
