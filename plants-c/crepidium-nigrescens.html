Crepidium nigrescens

Crepidium nigrescens, commonly known as the blackened orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a dense rosette pattern.

The species is notable for its unique coloration, with the flowers darkening to a deep purple or blackish hue as they mature.
