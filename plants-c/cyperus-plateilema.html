Cyperus plateilema

Cyperus plateilema, commonly known as the paperplant, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.

Cyperus plateilema is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil and its attractive foliage.
