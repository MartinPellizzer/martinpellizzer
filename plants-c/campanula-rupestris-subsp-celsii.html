Campanula rupestris subsp. celsii

Campanula rupestris subsp.

celsii, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This subspecies thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
