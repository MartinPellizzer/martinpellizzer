Calliandra portoricensis var. major

Calliandra portoricensis var. major, commonly known as the Puerto Rican calliandra, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, and showy inflorescences. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
