Carduus nidulus

Carduus nidulus, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive spherical fruits. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of rough, prickly burs. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in temperate climates.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
