Carex fernandesiana

Carex fernandesiana, commonly known as the Fernandes' sedge, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
