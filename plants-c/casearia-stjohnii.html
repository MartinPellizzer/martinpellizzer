Casearia stjohnii

Casearia stjohnii, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
