Calamus feanus var. medogensis

Calamus feanus var. medogensis, commonly known as the Yunnan calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and its fruits are three-winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
