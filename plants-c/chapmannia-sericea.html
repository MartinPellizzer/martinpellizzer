Chapmannia sericea

Chapmannia sericea, commonly known as silky chapmannia, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silky-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Chapmannia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
