Cyperus trinervis var. flaccidus

Cyperus trinervis var. flaccidus, commonly known as the soft flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus trinervis var. flaccidus is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
