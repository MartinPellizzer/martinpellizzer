Crotalaria sessiliflora

Crotalaria sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas.

Crotalaria sessiliflora is also known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
