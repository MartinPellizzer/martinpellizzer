Ceropegia chlorozona

Ceropegia chlorozona, commonly known as the green-flowered ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces distinctive flowers that are often yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique structure resembling a tube or hood. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
