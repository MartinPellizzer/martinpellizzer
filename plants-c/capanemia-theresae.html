Capanemia theresae

Capanemia theresae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and captivating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often displaying shades of white, pink, and purple. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks without parasitizing them.

Due to habitat loss, Capanemia theresae is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
