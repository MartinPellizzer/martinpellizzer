Chrysopsis texana

Chrysopsis texana, commonly known as the Texas goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, showing adaptability to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
