Cytisus atratus

Cytisus atratus, commonly known as the black thorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds covered in a hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus atratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
