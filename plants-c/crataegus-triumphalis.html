Crataegus triumphalis

Crataegus triumphalis, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

It is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its seasonal beauty and hardy nature.
