Cistus salviifolius var. flavescens

Cistus salviifolius var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
