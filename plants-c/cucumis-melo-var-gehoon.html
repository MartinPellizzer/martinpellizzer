Cucumis melo var. gehoon

Cucumis melo var. gehoon, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary preparations across various cultures.
