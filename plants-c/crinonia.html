Crinonia

Crinonia, commonly known as the white-flowered crinum, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllis family.

These plants are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas. Crinonia species are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. They typically grow as herbaceous perennials, often with strap-like leaves and bulbous roots.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
