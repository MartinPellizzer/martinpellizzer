Campanula rotundifolia subsp. sudetica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is typically found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
