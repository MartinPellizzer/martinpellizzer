Crotalaria purshii var. polyphylla

Crotalaria purshii var. polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leafed cowpea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
