Cissus assamica var. pilosissima

Cissus assamica var. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy Indian gooseberry, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, tangy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
