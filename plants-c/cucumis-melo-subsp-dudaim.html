Cucumis melo subsp. dudaim

Cucumis melo subsp.

dudaim, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanically significant subspecies of the melon genus. It is characterized by its elongated, thin fruits with a smooth, pale green skin and a crisp, mild flavor.

The plant typically grows as an annual vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is closely related to other melon species and is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
