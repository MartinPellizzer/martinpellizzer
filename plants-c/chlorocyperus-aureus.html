Chlorocyperus aureus

Chlorocyperus aureus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically found in dense, grassy habitats.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
