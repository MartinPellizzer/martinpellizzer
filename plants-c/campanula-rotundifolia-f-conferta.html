Campanula rotundifolia f. conferta

Campanula rotundifolia f. conferta, commonly known as the compact bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
