Centaurea hyalina

Centaurea hyalina, commonly known as the glassy knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Centaurea hyalina thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

It is a perennial species that is relatively low maintenance and often used in drought-tolerant landscaping.
