Calendula incana

Calendula incana, commonly known as woolly marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its scientific name, *Calendula incana*, reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its association with the calendar due to its blooming pattern.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
