Copernicia cerifera

Copernicia cerifera, commonly known as the carnauba wax palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a thick, waxy substance known as carnauba wax, which is highly valued for its use in cosmetics and industry. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its economic importance, it is often cultivated for commercial wax production.
