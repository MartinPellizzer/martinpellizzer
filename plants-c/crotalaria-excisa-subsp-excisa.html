Crotalaria excisa subsp. excisa

Crotalaria excisa subsp.

excisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crotalaria, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It features upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod that splits open when mature.

This species is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
