Chrysothamnus pulchellus

Chrysothamnus pulchellus, commonly known as the golden rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has gray-green foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
