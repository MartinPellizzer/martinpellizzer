Citharexylum albicaule

Citharexylum albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed cithara, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Citharexylum albicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
