Carex rariflora f. erecta

Carex rariflora f. erecta, commonly known as the erect form of slender sedge.

This plant is a perennial herbaceous species characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. The leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth texture, while the stems are sturdy and often slightly hairy.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
