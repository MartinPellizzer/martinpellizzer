Carex suteri

Carex suteri, commonly known as the Suter's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Carex suteri produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
