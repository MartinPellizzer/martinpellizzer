Chrysothamnus moquianus

Chrysothamnus moquianus, commonly known as the Mojave goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that provides habitat and forage for desert wildlife. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soils make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
