Chamaecytisus dalmaticus

Chamaecytisus dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkans and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, giving it a delicate appearance.

Chamaecytisus dalmaticus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
