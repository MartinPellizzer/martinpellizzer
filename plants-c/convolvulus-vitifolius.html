Convolvulus vitifolius

Convolvulus vitifolius, commonly known as the grape-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm climates and can be found in various regions of the Mediterranean.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
