Chenopodium aridum

Chenopodium aridum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and stems, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.

Chenopodium aridum plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
