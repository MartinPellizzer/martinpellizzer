Copiapoa hypogaea var. laui

Copiapoa hypogaea var. laui, commonly known as the Laui cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and sparse, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and conservation efforts.
