Cytisus genistoides

Cytisus genistoides, commonly known as the creeping yellow shrub, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, prostrate or upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Cytisus genistoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
