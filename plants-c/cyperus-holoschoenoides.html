Cyperus holoschoenoides

Cyperus holoschoenoides, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, and has narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus holoschoenoides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
