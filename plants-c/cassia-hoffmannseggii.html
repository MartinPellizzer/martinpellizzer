Cassia hoffmannseggii

Cassia hoffmannseggii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and the bark is smooth and greyish in color.

Cassia hoffmannseggii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
