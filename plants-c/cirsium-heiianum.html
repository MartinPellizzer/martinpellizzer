Cirsium heiianum

Cirsium heiianum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Cirsium heiianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
