Coptophyllum capitatum

Coptophyllum capitatum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Coptophyllum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
