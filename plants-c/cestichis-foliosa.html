Cestichis foliosa

Cestichis foliosa, commonly known as the featherleaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that resemble feathers, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Cestichis foliosa is well adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a hardy plant for xerophytic gardens.
