Cerasus serotina var. montana

Cerasus serotina var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cherry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
