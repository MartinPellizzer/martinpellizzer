Corybas sexalatus

Corybas sexalatus, commonly known as the malehood orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often colorful flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. The plant produces a single, erect spike bearing several small, club-shaped flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest floors or on rocky outcrops.

Corybas sexalatus is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves the attraction of certain insects.
