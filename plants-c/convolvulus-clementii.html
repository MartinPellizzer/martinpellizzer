Convolvulus clementii

Convolvulus clementii, commonly known as the Mediterranean bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
