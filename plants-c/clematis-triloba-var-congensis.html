Clematis triloba var. congensis

Clematis triloba var. congensis, commonly known as the Congo clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and climbing nature, with stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
