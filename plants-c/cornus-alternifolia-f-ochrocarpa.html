Cornus alternifolia f. ochrocarpa

Cornus alternifolia f. ochrocarpa, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems that become more pronounced in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

The variety f. ochrocarpa is notable for its vibrant stem color and ornamental value in gardens.
