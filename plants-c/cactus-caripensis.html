Cactus caripensis

Cactus caripensis, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its landscape. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Overall, Cactus caripensis is well-adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually appealing plant.
