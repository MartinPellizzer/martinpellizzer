Centropogon gravidus

Centropogon gravidus, commonly known as the heavy-berry grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence on the underside.

The flowers are typically pale green to brown, and the plant is known for its ability to form dense mats, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
