Caladenia riparia

Caladenia riparia, commonly known as the River Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest floors. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive hooded labellum.

Caladenia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
