Carex oerstedii

Carex oerstedii, commonly known as Oersted's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Carex oerstedii is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
