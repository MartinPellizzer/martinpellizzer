Cucumis melo var. persicodorus

Cucumis melo var. persicodorus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruit with a smooth, pale green skin and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
