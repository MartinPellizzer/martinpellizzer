Calanthe baliensis

Calanthe baliensis, commonly known as the Bali Calanthe, is a rare orchid species native to the Indonesian island of Bali.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, and it produces lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Calanthe baliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
