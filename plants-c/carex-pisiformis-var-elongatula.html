Carex pisiformis var. elongatula

Carex pisiformis var. elongatula, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other wet habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
