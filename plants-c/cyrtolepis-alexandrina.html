Cyrtolepis alexandrina

Cyrtolepis alexandrina, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Cyrtolepis alexandrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
