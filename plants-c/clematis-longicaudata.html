Clematis longicaudata

Clematis longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep blue, depending on the cultivar. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis longicaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
