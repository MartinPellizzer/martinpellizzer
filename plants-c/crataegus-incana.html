Crataegus incana

Crataegus incana, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
