Cyperus fuscus f. virescens

Cyperus fuscus f. virescens, commonly known as the green sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its distinctively green foliage.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes that are often inconspicuous. It thrives in moist to aquatic conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
