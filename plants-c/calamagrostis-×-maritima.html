Calamagrostis × maritima

Calamagrostis × maritima, commonly known as saltmeadow cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes during the summer months. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to tolerate saltwater makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
