Carum appuanum subsp. palmatum

Carum appuanum subsp.

palmatum, commonly known as the palmate caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a slender, branched stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruits are small, dry, and ribbed, typical of the Carum genus.
