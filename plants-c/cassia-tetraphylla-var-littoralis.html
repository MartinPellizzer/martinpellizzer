Cassia tetraphylla var. littoralis

Cassia tetraphylla var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal cassia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near the shoreline, thriving in sandy or saline soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
