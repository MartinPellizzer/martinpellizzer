Coleus fragrantissimus

Coleus fragrantissimus, commonly known as the sweet coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
