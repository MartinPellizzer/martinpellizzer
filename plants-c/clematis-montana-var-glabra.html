Clematis montana var. glabra

Clematis montana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage and blooms.
