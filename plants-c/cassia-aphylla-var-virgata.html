Cassia aphylla var. virgata

Cassia aphylla var. virgata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear bright yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
