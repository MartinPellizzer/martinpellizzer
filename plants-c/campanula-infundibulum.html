Campanula infundibulum

Campanula infundibulum, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
