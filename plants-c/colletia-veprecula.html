Colletia veprecula

Colletia veprecula, commonly known as the desert cactus or the yellow cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Colletia veprecula is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
