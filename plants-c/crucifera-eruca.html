Crucifera eruca

Crucifera eruca, commonly known as wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is an annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has serrated, lobed leaves and produces a pungent odor due to the presence of glucosinolates. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
