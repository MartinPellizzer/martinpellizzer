Coleus forskohlii

Coleus forskohlii, commonly known as Indian coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
