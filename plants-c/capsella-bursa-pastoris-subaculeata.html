Capsella bursa-pastoris subaculeata

Capsella bursa-pastoris subaculeata, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive triangular seed pods. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and pod-like fruits.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
