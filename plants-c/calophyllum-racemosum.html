Calophyllum racemosum

Calophyllum racemosum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The tree has smooth, pale bark and produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in furniture and construction.
