Capparis heterogenea

Capparis heterogenea, commonly known as the variable caper, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and features small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Capparis heterogenea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
