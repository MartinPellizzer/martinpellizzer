Cacalia dichroa

Cacalia dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored petals, typically featuring a white base and a blue or purple upper part. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
