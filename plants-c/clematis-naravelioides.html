Clematis naravelioides

Clematis naravelioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters, allowing it to climb trees and other structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

Clematis naravelioides thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires support for optimal growth.
