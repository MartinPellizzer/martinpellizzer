Chusquea heterophylla var. squamosa

Chusquea heterophylla var. squamosa, commonly known as the scaly dwarf bamboo, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive scaly leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, scaly structures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
