Caprifolium simile

Caprifolium simile, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Caprifolium simile is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also provide food for birds and wildlife.
