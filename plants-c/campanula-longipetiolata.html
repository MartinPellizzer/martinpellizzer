Campanula longipetiolata

Campanula longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, resembling small bells, hence its common name. The plant has slender, hairy stems and leaves that are elongated with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula longipetiolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
