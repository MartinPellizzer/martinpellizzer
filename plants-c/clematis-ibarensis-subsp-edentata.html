Clematis ibarensis subsp. edentata

Clematis ibarensis subsp.

edentata, commonly known as the toothless clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, almost waxy texture.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
