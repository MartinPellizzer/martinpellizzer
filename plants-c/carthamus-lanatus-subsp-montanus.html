Carthamus lanatus subsp. montanus

Carthamus lanatus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the alpine safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
