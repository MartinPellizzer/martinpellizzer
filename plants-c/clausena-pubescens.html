Clausena pubescens

Clausena pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
