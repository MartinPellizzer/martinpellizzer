Carex arcta var. oregana

Carex arcta var. oregana, commonly known as the Oregon sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
