Chionanthus luzonicus

Chionanthus luzonicus, commonly known as the Luzon chionathus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
