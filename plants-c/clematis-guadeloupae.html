Clematis guadeloupae

Clematis guadeloupae, commonly known as the Guadeloupe clematis, is a flowering vine native to the Caribbean island of Guadeloupe.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis guadeloupae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
