Centaurea nigra subsp. microptilon

Centaurea nigra subsp.

microptilon, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, dark blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
