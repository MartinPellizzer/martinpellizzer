Centrolepis curta

Centrolepis curta, commonly known as the dwarf screw pine, is a unique succulent plant native to various parts of the world, including Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by the development of woody, cone-like structures. Centrolepis curta is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, exhibiting remarkable drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
