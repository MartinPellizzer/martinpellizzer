Cerastium glanduliferum var. lucorum

Cerastium glanduliferum var. lucorum, commonly known as white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during early summer, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
