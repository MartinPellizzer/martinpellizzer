Cephalanthus pilulifer

Cephalanthus pilulifer, commonly known as the redroot dogwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cephalanthus pilulifer thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
