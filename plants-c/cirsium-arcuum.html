Cirsium arcuum

Cirsium arcuum, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium arcuum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
