Carex straminea var. mirabilis

Carex straminea var. mirabilis, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that produce dense, golden-yellow spikes of catkins, making it a standout in gardens. This variety is a variant of Carex straminea, which is known for its ornamental value and adaptability. It prefers moist to wet soils and partial to full sun, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ability to thrive in wet conditions makes it an excellent choice for rain gardens or water features.
