Crotalaria lachnocarpa var. melanocalyx

Crotalaria lachnocarpa var. melanocalyx, commonly known as the blackseed crotalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive black or purple calyx. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
