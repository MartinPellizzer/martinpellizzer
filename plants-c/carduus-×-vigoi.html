Carduus × vigoi

Carduus × vigoi, commonly known as Italian chicory.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Carduus maximus and Cichorium intybus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. It is characterized by its rosette of blue-green leaves and a striking purple or red flowering head. The plant is often cultivated for its leafy greens, which are used in salads and cooked dishes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and agricultural systems.
