Catasetum catarinense

Catasetum catarinense, commonly known as the Catarinense orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest environments.

Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, which is typical for many orchid species.
