Cissus intermedia

Cissus intermedia, commonly known as the climbing hedgeplant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus intermedia is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls.
