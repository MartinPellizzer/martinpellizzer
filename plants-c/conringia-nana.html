Conringia nana

Conringia nana, commonly known as dwarf bladderwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy in aquatic or wetland habitats.

Conringia nana is an important component of its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various invertebrates.
