Cyperus hypochlorus var. densispicatus

Cyperus hypochlorus var. densispicatus, commonly known as the dense-spike flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically at the base of the plant. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
