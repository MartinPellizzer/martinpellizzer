Calyptranthes santalucia

Calyptranthes santalucia, commonly known as the Santa Lucia calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its limited range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
