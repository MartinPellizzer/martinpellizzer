Colchicum cupanii var. latifolium

Colchicum cupanii var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf meadow crocus, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces large, showy flowers with six petals and a distinctive, spurred petal at the base. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
