Cerastium imbricatum

Cerastium imbricatum, commonly known as the snow-in-summer, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. Cerastium imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet habitats.
