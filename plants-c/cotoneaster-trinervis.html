Cotoneaster trinervis

Cotoneaster trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster trinervis is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and other animals.
