Cyrtanthus vittatus

Cyrtanthus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pink with distinctive yellow or orange markings. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are often spotted or striped, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cyrtanthus vittatus is also valued for its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
