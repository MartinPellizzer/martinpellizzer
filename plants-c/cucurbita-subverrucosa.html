Cucurbita subverrucosa

Cucurbita subverrucosa, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its rugged, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical fruits with a rough, bumpy texture. Its fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
