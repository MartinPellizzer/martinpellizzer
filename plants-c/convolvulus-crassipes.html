Convolvulus crassipes

Convolvulus crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It has a trailing growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
