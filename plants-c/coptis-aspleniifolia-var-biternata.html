Coptis aspleniifolia var. biternata

Coptis aspleniifolia var. biternata, commonly known as the three-parted goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
