Cyathea cyclodonta

Cyathea cyclodonta, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, and it plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
