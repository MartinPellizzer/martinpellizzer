Chrysothamnus nauseosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which are adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects.
