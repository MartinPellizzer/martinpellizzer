Campanula daucoides

Campanula daucoides, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Campanula daucoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can also be found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is relatively low maintenance and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
