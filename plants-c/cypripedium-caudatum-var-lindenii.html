Cypripedium caudatum var. lindenii

Cypripedium caudatum var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a large, pouch-like lip that is typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its often harsh, high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single, elegant flower per stem.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticulture but is also threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
