Cinnamomum okinawense

Cinnamomum okinawense, commonly known as Okinawa cinnamon, is a rare evergreen tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, spindle-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
