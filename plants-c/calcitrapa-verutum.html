Calcitrapa verutum

Calcitrapa verutum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply lobed leaves and striking orange-red petals. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, hairy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
