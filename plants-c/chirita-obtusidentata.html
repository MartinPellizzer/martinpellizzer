Chirita obtusidentata

Chirita obtusidentata, commonly known as the blunt-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
