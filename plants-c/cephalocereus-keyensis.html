Cephalocereus keyensis

Cephalocereus keyensis, commonly known as the Key Mountain cactus, is a species native to the Florida Keys.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, slender stem and few spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
