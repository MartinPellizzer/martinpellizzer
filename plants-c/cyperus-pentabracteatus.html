Cyperus pentabracteatus

Cyperus pentabracteatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus pentabracteatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in suitable climates.
