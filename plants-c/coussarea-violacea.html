Coussarea violacea

Coussarea violacea, commonly known as the purple flame or purple coussarea, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while its stems are often covered in a waxy coating.

Coussarea violacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
