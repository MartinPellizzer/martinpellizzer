Clematis × reginae

Clematis × reginae, commonly known as the Queen Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbing plants.

Clematis × reginae is relatively easy to care for, though it benefits from regular pruning to encourage vigorous growth and flowering.
