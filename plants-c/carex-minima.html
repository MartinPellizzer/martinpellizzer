Carex minima

Carex minima, commonly known as the dwarf sedges, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex minima has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
