Cucumis melo var. pubescens

Cucumis melo var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough skin and typically yellow or orange flesh. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique texture and flavor, making it a popular choice in certain regions.
