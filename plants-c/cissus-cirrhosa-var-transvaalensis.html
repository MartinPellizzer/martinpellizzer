Cissus cirrhosa var. transvaalensis

Cissus cirrhosa var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus cirrhosa var. transvaalensis is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential medicinal properties.
