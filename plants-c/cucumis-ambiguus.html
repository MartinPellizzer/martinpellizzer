Cucumis ambiguus

Cucumis ambiguus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, green fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in many cucurbit species.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.
