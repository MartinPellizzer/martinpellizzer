Campanula cochleariifolia f. modesta

Campanula cochleariifolia f. modesta, commonly known as the modest bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that form a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant flowers and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
