Cucurbita maxima messanensis

Cucurbita maxima messanensis, commonly known as the Messina pumpkin, is a species of winter squash native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its large, round, orange-colored fruits with a smooth, thick rind. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
