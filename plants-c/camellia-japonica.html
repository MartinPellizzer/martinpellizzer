Camellia japonica

Camellia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The flowers are typically double or semi-double, with layers of pink, red, or white petals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia japonica is also valued for its evergreen foliage and ability to tolerate cold temperatures.
