Crataegus brainerdii

Crataegus brainerdii, commonly known as Brainerd's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
