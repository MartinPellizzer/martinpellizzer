Copernicia humicola

Copernicia humicola, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a flowering plant species native to the northeastern regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant is known for producing a type of wax, which has been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various purposes.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for numerous species and its contribution to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
