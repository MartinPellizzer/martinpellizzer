Carex harlandii subsp. harlandii

Carex harlandii subsp.

harlandii, commonly known as Harland's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and narrow, with a dense, spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. It produces wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
