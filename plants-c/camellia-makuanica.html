Camellia makuanica

Camellia makuanica, commonly known as the Makuan camellia, is a species native to China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a red or purple base. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Camellia makuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
