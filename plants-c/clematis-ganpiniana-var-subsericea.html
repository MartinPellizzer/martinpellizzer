Clematis ganpiniana var. subsericea

Clematis ganpiniana var. subsericea, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
