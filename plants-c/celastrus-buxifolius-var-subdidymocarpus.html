Celastrus buxifolius var. subdidymocarpus

Celastrus buxifolius var. subdidymocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed staff-vine, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

Its botanical features make it notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
