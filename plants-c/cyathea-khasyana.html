Cyathea khasyana

Cyathea khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi tree fern, is a tropical evergreen tree fern native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in humid and cool climates. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall trunk, while its fronds play a crucial role in photosynthesis.

Cyathea khasyana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
