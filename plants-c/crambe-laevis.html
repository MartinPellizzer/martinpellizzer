Crambe laevis

Crambe laevis, commonly known as the smooth crambe, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and serrated leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing round, dark brown seeds.

Crambe laevis is often grown as a forage crop and has potential uses in oil production.
