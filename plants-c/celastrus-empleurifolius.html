Celastrus empleurifolius

Celastrus empleurifolius, commonly known as the Mexican staff vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red berries. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often twining and covered in small, hooked tendrils. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed areas and along forest edges.

The berries are an important food source for various bird species.
