Caesalpinia antsiranensis

Caesalpinia antsiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar glory flower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bracteate flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forest or woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
