Chrysothemis melittifolia

Chrysothemis melittifolia, commonly known as the honeybee milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), which is known for its milky sap and specialized pollination mechanisms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
