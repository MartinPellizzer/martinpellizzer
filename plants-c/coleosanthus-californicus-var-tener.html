Coleosanthus californicus var. tener

Coleosanthus californicus var. tener, commonly known as the California tener.

This variant is a flowering plant native to California, characterized by its rosette growth habit and succulent leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
