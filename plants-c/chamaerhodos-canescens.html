Chamaerhodos canescens

Chamaerhodos canescens, commonly known as the gray feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable ornamental and erosion control plant.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to a variety of environments.
