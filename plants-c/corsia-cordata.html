Corsia cordata

Corsia cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Corsia cordata is known for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
