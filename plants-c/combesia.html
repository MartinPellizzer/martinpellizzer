Combesia

Combesia, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that often resemble a crown, with bright blue or purple petals. The plants typically grow in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments. Their leaves are usually silvery or grayish, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Combesia species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in gardens for their unique appearance.
