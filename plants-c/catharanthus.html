Catharanthus

Catharanthus, commonly known as the Madagascar periwinkle, is a genus of flowering plants native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes. Catharanthus species contain alkaloids that have been used in pharmaceuticals, including drugs used to treat cancer and hypertension.

Its leaves and stems are also used in traditional medicine.
