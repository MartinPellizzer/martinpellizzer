Chassalia corallifera

Chassalia corallifera, commonly known as coralflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Chassalia corallifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
