Celmisia repens

Celmisia repens, commonly known as the creeping celmisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with small, oval leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
