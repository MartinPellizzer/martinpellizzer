Commelina reptans

Commelina reptans, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces three-lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Commelina reptans is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a ornamental and sometimes invasive species.
