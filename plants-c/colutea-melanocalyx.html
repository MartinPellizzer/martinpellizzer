Colutea melanocalyx

Colutea melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leafed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable crop and an ornamental plant in gardens.
