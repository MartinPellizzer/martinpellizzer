Coronidium elatum

Coronidium elatum, commonly known as the crownless sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
