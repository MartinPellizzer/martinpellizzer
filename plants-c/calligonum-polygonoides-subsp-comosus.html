Calligonum polygonoides subsp. comosus

Calligonum polygonoides subsp.

comosus, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
