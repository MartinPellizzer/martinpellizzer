Carex × candrianii

Carex × candrianii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, emerging from leafy bracts. Carex × candrianii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
