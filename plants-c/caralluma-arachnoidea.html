Caralluma arachnoidea

Caralluma arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider flower or desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has succulent stems and leaves adapted to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Caralluma arachnoidea is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
