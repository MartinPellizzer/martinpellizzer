Conophytum praegratum

Conophytum praegratum, commonly known as the "Cape Hottentot" or "Tortoise Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. Its unique leaf structure and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Conophytum praegratum is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with minimal water.
