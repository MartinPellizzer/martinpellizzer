Conophytum uviforme var. occultum

Conophytum uviforme var. occultum, commonly known as the "Ghost Leaf" or "Ghost Plant," is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that resemble small, rounded stones, often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
