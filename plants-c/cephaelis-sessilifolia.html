Cephaelis sessilifolia

Cephaelis sessilifolia, commonly known as the purple flame or flame violet, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender pedicels, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments.

Cephaelis sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
