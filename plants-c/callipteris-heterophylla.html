Callipteris heterophylla

Callipteris heterophylla, commonly known as the Florida rosemary, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its foliage is often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.
