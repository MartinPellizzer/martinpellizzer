Crepis diffusa

Crepis diffusa, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has slender, hairy stems that help reduce water loss. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crepis diffusa produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
