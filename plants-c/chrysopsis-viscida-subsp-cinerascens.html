Chrysopsis viscida subsp. cinerascens

Chrysopsis viscida subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysopsis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
