Cyperus rehmannii

Cyperus rehmannii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Cyperus rehmannii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions.
