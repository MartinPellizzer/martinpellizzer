Convolvulus canescens

Convolvulus canescens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
