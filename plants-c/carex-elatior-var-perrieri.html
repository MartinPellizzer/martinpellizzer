Carex elatior var. perrieri

Carex elatior var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
