Calanthe davidii var. bungoana

Calanthe davidii var. bungoana, commonly known as the Bungoan calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically produces clusters of pale pink to white petals with a contrasting yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
