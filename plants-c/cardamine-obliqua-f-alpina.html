Cardamine obliqua f. alpina

Cardamine obliqua f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine water-cress, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically borne on slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and mountain meadows.

Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies due to their peppery flavor and medicinal properties.
