Centaurea alba var. cinerascens

Centaurea alba var. cinerascens, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are pale blue to white, with a distinctive, slender central stem and finely divided leaves.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
