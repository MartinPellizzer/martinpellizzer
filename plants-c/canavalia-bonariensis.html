Canavalia bonariensis

Canavalia bonariensis, commonly known as the bonariensis bean or the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Additionally, the seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
