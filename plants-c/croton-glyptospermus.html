Croton glyptospermus

Croton glyptospermus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, red, and orange. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique, asymmetrical leaves. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

Croton glyptospermus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
