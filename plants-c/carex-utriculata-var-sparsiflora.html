Carex utriculata var. sparsiflora

Carex utriculata var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually short and compact, producing small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive bulb-like structures, which are used for vegetative propagation.
