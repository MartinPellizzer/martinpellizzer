Chrysodium urvillei

Chrysodium urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or swampy areas.
