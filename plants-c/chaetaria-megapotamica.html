Chaetaria megapotamica

Chaetaria megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms, it is an important component of the Amazonian ecosystem.
