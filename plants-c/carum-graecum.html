Carum graecum

Carum graecum, commonly known as Greek parsley or sweet cicely, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Carum graecum is valued for its mild, anise-like flavor and its historical use in traditional medicine.
