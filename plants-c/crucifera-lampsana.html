Crucifera lampsana

Crucifera lampsana, commonly known as the Mediterranean watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, edible leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in wet meadows. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by water, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and nutritional content, making it a useful addition to salads and culinary dishes.
