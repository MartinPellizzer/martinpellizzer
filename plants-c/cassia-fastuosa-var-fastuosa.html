Cassia fastuosa var. fastuosa

Cassia fastuosa var. fastuosa, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy inflorescences that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its showy flowers, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical regions.
