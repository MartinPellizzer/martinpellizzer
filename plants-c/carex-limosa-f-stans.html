Carex limosa f. stans

Carex limosa f. stans, commonly known as the silky sedges, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common species in marshes and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
