Cracca californica

Cracca californica, commonly known as California goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
