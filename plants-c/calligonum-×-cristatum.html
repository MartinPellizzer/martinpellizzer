Calligonum × cristatum

Calligonum × cristatum, commonly known as the crested spurge, is a hybrid shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branched stems that form a dense, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by green, spherical fruits. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a unique appearance.

Calligonum × cristatum is adapted to harsh environments and is often used for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
