Campanula filicaulis var. antiatlantica

Campanula filicaulis var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is well adapted to maritime climates, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
