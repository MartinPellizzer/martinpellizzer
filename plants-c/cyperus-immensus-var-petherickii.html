Cyperus immensus var. petherickii

Cyperus immensus var. petherickii, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, reed-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually green or brown in color.

This variant is notable for its height and adaptability to various wetland environments.
