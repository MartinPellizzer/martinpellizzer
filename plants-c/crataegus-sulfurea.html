Crataegus sulfurea

Crataegus sulfurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display and autumn fruit color.
