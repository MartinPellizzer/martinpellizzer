Chenopodium suffruticosum

Chenopodium suffruticosum, commonly known as redroot chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Chenopodium suffruticosum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in its native environment.
