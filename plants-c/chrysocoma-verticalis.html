Chrysocoma verticalis

Chrysocoma verticalis, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The leaves and stems contain milky sap, which is responsible for its common name.
