Campanula lagranensis

Campanula lagranensis, commonly known as the Lagrange bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy layer.

Campanula lagranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
