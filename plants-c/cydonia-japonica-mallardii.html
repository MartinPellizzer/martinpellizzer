Cydonia japonica mallardii

Cydonia japonica mallardii, commonly known as the Japanese quince.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and orange-red fruits. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
