Cyclopogon garayanus

Cyclopogon garayanus, commonly known as the Yucatán grass, is a flowering plant native to the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Cyclopogon garayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
