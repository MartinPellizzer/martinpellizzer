Camellia mairei var. alba

Camellia mairei var. alba, commonly known as the white camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Camellia mairei var. alba is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
