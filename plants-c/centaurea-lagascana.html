Centaurea lagascana

Centaurea lagascana, commonly known as the Sicilian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

Centaurea lagascana is often found in open fields and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
