Cheiridopsis pearsonii

Cheiridopsis pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the winter months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
