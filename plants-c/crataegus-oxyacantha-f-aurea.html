Crataegus oxyacantha f. aurea

Crataegus oxyacantha f. aurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant characterized by its striking yellow foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, featuring thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy, thriving in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for birds and insects.
