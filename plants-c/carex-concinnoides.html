Carex concinnoides

Carex concinnoides, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

Carex concinnoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
