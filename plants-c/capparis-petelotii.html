Capparis petelotii

Capparis petelotii, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Capparis petelotii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
