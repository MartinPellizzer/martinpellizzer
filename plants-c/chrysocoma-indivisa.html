Chrysocoma indivisa

Chrysocoma indivisa, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating ailments related to the respiratory and digestive systems.
