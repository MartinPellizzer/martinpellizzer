Capparis breynia f. atropurpurea

Capparis breynia f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-fruited caper, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by fleshy, edible fruits that range in color from green to red. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
