Carex turbaria

Carex turbaria, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or boggy environments, thriving in acidic, nutrient-poor soils. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex turbaria plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
