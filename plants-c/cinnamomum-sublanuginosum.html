Cinnamomum sublanuginosum

Cinnamomum sublanuginosum, commonly known as the silky cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for flavoring.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
