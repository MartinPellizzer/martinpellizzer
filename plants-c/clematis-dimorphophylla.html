Clematis dimorphophylla

Clematis dimorphophylla, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically twining or trailing. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis dimorphophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
