Cyperus coriifolius

Cyperus coriifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically inconspicuous and borne on spikelets. Cyperus coriifolius thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
