Croton rhamnifolius var. antillanus

Croton rhamnifolius var. antillanus, commonly known as the West Indian croton, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from yellow to red, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with prominent veins and a waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant foliage.
