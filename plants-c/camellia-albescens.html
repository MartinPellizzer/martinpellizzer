Camellia albescens

Camellia albescens, commonly known as the white camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and long blooming period.

Camellia albescens prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
