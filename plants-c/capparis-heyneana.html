Capparis heyneana

Capparis heyneana, commonly known as the Indian Caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, arid environments.

Capparis heyneana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
