Collinsia tricolor

Collinsia tricolor, commonly known as the three-color checkerbloom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colored flowers that display three distinct shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often hairy.

Collinsia tricolor blooms in spring and early summer, producing clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
