Canna orientalis var. flava

Canna orientalis var. flava, commonly known as the golden canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, and its showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Cannaceae family and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and dramatic floral display in gardens and water features.

Its growth habit is herbaceous, with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread and form dense clumps.
