Calycanthus floridus var. purpureus

Calycanthus floridus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. Its ornamental value comes from both its aromatic flowers and its attractive foliage.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
