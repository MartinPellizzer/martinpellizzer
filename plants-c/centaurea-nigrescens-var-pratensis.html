Centaurea nigrescens var. pratensis

Centaurea nigrescens var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. Its flowers are surrounded by pale blue to white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
