Cymbopogon tortilis subsp. goeringii

Cymbopogon tortilis subsp.

goeringii, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a member of the Poaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and aromatic, essential oil-rich leaves.

Native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, this plant thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their distinctive citrus scent.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and practical applications.
