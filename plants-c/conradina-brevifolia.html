Conradina brevifolia

Conradina brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf sandverbena, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Conradina brevifolia is an important component of native plant communities and is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.
