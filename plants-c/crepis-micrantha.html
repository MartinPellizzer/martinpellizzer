Crepis micrantha

Crepis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a single head.

Crepis micrantha is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in disturbed environments.
