Chaetogastra reticulata

Chaetogastra reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive with adequate humidity. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
