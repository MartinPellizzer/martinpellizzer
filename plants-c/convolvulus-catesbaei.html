Convolvulus catesbaei

Convolvulus catesbaei, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
