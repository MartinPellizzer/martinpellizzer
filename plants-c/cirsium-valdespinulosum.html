Cirsium valdespinulosum

Cirsium valdespinulosum, commonly known as the Valdespinulose thistle, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, followed by bristly purple-brown seed heads.

Cirsium valdespinulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
