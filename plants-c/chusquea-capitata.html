Chusquea capitata

Chusquea capitata, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and long, slender culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the top of the culms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Chusquea capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
