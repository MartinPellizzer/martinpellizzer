Cyrtochilum graminoides

Cyrtochilum graminoides, commonly known as the grassy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the humid tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Cyrtochilum graminoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
