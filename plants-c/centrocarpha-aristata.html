Centrocarpha aristata

Centrocarpha aristata, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Centrocarpha aristata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.
