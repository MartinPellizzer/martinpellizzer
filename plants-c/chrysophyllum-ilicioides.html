Chrysophyllum ilicioides

Chrysophyllum ilicioides, commonly known as the African cherry or sour cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and biodiversity conservation.
