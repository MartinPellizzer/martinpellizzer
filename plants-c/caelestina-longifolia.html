Caelestina longifolia

Caelestina longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in loose racemes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Caelestina longifolia is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
