Crataegus panda

Crataegus panda, commonly known as the panda hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
