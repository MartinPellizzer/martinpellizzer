Cryptoloma pictum

Cryptoloma pictum, commonly known as the painted cryptoloma, is a rare and striking member of the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from deep purples to bright greens, creating a visually captivating display. Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and bell-shaped, typically appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Cryptoloma pictum is considered a conservation concern.
