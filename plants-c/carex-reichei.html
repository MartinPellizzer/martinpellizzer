Carex reichei

Carex reichei, commonly known as the Reiche's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, forming dense clumps with grass-like foliage. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex reichei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
