Cissus xerophila

Cissus xerophila, commonly known as the desert grape or stonecrop fig, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a ground cover or hanging plant. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it highly drought-tolerant.

Cissus xerophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry environments.
