Ceropegia flava

Ceropegia flava, commonly known as the yellow ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and opposite leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia flava is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
