Chrysocoma dentata

Chrysocoma dentata, commonly known as the African sarsaparilla, is a shrub native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats with partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in various African cultures for its purported health benefits.
