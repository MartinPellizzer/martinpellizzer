Cineraria pinnatifida

Cineraria pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
