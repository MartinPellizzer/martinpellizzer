Convolvulus cujanensis

Convolvulus cujanensis, commonly known as the Java morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces funnel-shaped, trumpet-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, giving it its common name.
