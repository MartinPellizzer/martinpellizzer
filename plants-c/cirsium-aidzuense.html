Cirsium aidzuense

Cirsium aidzuense, commonly known as the Aidzu thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and covered with stiff, prickly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium aidzuense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
