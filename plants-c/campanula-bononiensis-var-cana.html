Campanula bononiensis var. cana

Campanula bononiensis var. cana, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
