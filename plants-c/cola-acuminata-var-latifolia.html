Cola acuminata var. latifolia

Cola acuminata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cola, is a species native to western Africa.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown fruits that contain stimulant alkaloids, primarily caffeine. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
