Carex apoiensis

Carex apoiensis, commonly known as the Apoiensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex apoiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
