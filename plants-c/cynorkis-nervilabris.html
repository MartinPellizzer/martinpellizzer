Cynorkis nervilabris

Cynorkis nervilabris, commonly known as the narrow-veined orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Cynorkis nervilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
