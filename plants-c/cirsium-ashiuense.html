Cirsium ashiuense

Cirsium ashiuense, commonly known as the Ashiu thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a rosette growth form with thick, woolly leaves that help retain moisture.

Cirsium ashiuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
