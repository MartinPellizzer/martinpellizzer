Crataegus crus-galli

Crataegus crus-galli, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that resemble a goat's beard, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

The species is widely distributed across Europe, Asia, and North America, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
