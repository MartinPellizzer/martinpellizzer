Cereus hirschtianus

Cereus hirschtianus, commonly known as the Hirschtian's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces striking, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cereus hirschtianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
