Clematis henryi var. trifolia

Clematis henryi var. trifolia, commonly known as the trifoliate clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it its common name, and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its cascading growth habit.

Clematis henryi var. trifolia is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of growing conditions.
