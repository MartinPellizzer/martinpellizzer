Coscinium miosepalum

Coscinium miosepalum, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their mild toxicity. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a distinctive, aromatic odor.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
