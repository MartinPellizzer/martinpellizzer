Chamaecrista rovirosana

Chamaecrista rovirosana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which contribute to soil fertility.
