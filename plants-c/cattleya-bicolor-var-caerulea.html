Cattleya bicolor var. caerulea

Cattleya bicolor var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-tinged cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often bicolor flowers that feature a combination of white and blue hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
