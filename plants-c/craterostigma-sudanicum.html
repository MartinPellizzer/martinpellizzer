Craterostigma sudanicum

Craterostigma sudanicum, commonly known as the Sudanese rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant exhibits remarkable drought tolerance, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Craterostigma sudanicum is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
