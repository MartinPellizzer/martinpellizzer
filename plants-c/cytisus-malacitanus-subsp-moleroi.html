Cytisus malacitanus subsp. moleroi

Cytisus malacitanus subsp.

moleroi, commonly known as the Molero's broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant thrives in rocky and coastal habitats, often found in southern Spain. It has small, pod-like fruits that release seeds through mechanical means.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean ecosystems.
