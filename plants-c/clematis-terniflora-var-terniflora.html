Clematis terniflora var. terniflora

Clematis terniflora var. terniflora, commonly known as the sweet autumn clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.
