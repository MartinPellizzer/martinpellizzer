Cyclamen peloponnesiacum subsp. vividum

Cyclamen peloponnesiacum subsp.

vividum, commonly known as the vivid cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Peloponnese region of Greece. It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded rock crevices. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a splash of color during the colder months. Cyclamen peloponnesiacum subsp.

vividum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
