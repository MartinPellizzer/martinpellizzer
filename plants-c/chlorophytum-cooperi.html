Chlorophytum cooperi

Chlorophytum cooperi, commonly known as the snake plant, is a hardy, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with green and white or yellow markings. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal choice for indoor environments. Its air-purifying qualities also make it a popular choice for improving indoor air quality.

The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers on long, slender stalks, which are followed by clusters of black, berry-like fruits.
