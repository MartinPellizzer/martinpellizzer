Caltha alba

Caltha alba, commonly known as white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal leaves that are broadly kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and its most distinguishing feature is its cluster of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as marshes and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy flowering plants.

Caltha alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
