Chionolaena capitata

Chionolaena capitata, commonly known as the silverleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. The leaves are typically grayish-green with a velvety texture, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.
