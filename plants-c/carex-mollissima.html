Carex mollissima

Carex mollissima, commonly known as soft hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective plant for erosion control and wetland restoration.
