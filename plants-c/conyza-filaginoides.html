Conyza filaginoides

Conyza filaginoides, commonly known as silverleaf weed, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has hairy, silver-tomentose leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Conyza filaginoides is often found in disturbed soils and can become a weed in agricultural fields.
