Cyperus sesquiflorus f. globosus

Cyperus sesquiflorus f. globosus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a decorative element in aquatic gardens.
