Croton juigalpensis

Croton juigalpensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre croton, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant typically has alternate, palmate leaves with a rough texture and vibrant hues of green, red, and yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
