Calamus divaricatus var. contrarius

Calamus divaricatus var. contrarius, commonly known as the twisted calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, twisted rhizomes and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
