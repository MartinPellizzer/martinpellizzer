Crataegus persica

Crataegus persica, commonly known as the Persian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Crataegus persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
