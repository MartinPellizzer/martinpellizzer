Centaurea holtzii

Centaurea holtzii, commonly known as Holtz's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
