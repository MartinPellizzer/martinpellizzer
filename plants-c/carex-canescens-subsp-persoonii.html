Carex canescens subsp. persoonii

Carex canescens subsp.

persoonii, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

The subspecies persoonii is distinguished by its silver-gray foliage and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
