Cecropia santanderensis

Cecropia santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander ceiba, is a species of tree native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, often whitish bark. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
