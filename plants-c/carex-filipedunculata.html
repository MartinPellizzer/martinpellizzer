Carex filipedunculata

Carex filipedunculata, commonly known as the slender club-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Its botanical structure is characterized by a fibrous root system and a distinctive inflorescence that aids in its identification.
