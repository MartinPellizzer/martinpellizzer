Cuphea klugii

Cuphea klugii, commonly known as the "whispering bells" or "cuphea," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling tiny bells. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.

Cuphea klugii is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
