Calamus polycladus

Calamus polycladus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem system that stores nutrients and allows for vegetative propagation. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface.
