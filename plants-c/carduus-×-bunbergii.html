Carduus × bunbergii

Carduus × bunbergii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus × bunbergii is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
