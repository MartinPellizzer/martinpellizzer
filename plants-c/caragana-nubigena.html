Caragana nubigena

Caragana nubigena, commonly known as the cloud-like shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in harsh, arid environments with poor soil conditions.

Caragana nubigena is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in reforestation projects.
