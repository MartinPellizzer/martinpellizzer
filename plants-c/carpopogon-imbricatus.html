Carpopogon imbricatus

Carpopogon imbricatus, commonly known as the grassland panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Carpopogon imbricatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
