Centaurea meryonis

Centaurea meryonis, commonly known as the meadow centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, bract-like leaves and has a compact, upright growth habit.

Centaurea meryonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
