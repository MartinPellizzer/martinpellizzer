Chaiturus marrubiastrum

Chaiturus marrubiastrum, commonly known as the woolly marrubium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are often aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Chaiturus marrubiastrum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
