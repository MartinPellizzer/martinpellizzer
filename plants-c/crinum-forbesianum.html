Crinum forbesianum

Crinum forbesianum, commonly known as the Forbes' crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crinum forbesianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
