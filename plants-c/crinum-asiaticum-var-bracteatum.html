Crinum asiaticum var. bracteatum

Crinum asiaticum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Asian crinum or bracteate crinum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in umbels at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
