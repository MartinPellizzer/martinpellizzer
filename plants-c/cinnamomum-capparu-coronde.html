Cinnamomum capparu-coronde

Cinnamomum capparu-coronde, commonly known as the Andean cinnamon or Peruvian cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is highly valued for its medicinal and culinary properties. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils rich in cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its distinctive scent and therapeutic uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, subtropical climates.
