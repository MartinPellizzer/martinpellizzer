Cardamine komarovii

Cardamine komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of aquatic environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
