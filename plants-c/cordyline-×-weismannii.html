Cordyline × weismannii

Cordyline × weismannii, commonly known as the rainbow tree, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cordyline australis and Cordyline indivisa.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to New Zealand. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves displaying shades of red, orange, yellow, and green. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
