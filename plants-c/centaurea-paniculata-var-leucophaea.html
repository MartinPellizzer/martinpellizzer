Centaurea paniculata var. leucophaea

Centaurea paniculata var. leucophaea, commonly known as the white-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 60 to 120 centimeters. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, pale blue to white flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure.

This variety is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.
