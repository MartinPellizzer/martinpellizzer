Cleomella hemsleyana

Cleomella hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae, which includes various species of flowering plants often referred to as "spider flowers.". The plant is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate, and the plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

Cleomella hemsleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive floral display.
