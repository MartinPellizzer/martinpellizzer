Carex peruviana

Carex peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or wetland environments.

Carex peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
