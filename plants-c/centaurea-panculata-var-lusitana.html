Centaurea panculata var. lusitana

Centaurea panculata var. lusitana, commonly known as the Lusitanian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
