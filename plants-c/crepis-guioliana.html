Crepis guioliana

Crepis guioliana, commonly known as the Guionian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Crepis guioliana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
