Corydalis maackii

Corydalis maackii, commonly known as the Siberian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Corydalis maackii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
