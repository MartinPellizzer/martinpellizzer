Carex lepidopristis

Carex lepidopristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that consist of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex lepidopristis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
