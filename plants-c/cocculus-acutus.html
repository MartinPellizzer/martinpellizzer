Cocculus acutus

Cocculus acutus, commonly known as the sharp-nosed cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, elongated fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The botanical features of Cocculus acutus make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
