Crataegus subintegriloba

Crataegus subintegriloba, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and are often used in ornamental gardening.
