Cheilococca apocynifolia

Cheilococca apocynifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it both a valuable and potentially dangerous species in its ecosystem.
