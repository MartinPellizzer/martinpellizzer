Crataegus altaica var. latifolia

Crataegus altaica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval to ovate leaves that are broad and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe or forest-steppe regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
