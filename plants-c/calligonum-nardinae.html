Calligonum nardinae

Calligonum nardinae, commonly known as the Kashmir sandthorn, is a shrub native to the cold desert regions of Jammu and Kashmir in India.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and thorny stems, which are adapted to the harsh, arid environment. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Calligonum nardinae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
