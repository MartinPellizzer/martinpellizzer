Carduus undulatus var. ochrocentrus

Carduus undulatus var. ochrocentrus, commonly known as the curled thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
