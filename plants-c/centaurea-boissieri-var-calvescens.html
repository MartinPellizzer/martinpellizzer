Centaurea boissieri var. calvescens

Centaurea boissieri var. calvescens, commonly known as the Algerian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flower heads, which are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
