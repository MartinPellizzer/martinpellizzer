Calophyllum poilanei

Calophyllum poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
