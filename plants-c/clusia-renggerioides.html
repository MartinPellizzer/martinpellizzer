Clusia renggerioides

Clusia renggerioides, commonly known as the broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Clusia renggerioides is also known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
