Cucurbita maxima var. pluviceps

Cucurbita maxima var. pluviceps, commonly known as the swamp pumpkin, is a variant of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often warty fruits that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in wet, swampy environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow-orange flowers.

The fruit is typically used for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance, though it can also be edible when mature.
