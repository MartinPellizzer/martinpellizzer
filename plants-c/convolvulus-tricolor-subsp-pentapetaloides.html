Convolvulus tricolor subsp. pentapetaloides

Convolvulus tricolor subsp.

pentapetaloides, commonly known as the five-petaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding stems that often climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental planting.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
