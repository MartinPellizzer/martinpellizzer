Ceropegia archeriana

Ceropegia archeriana, commonly known as the Archer's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
