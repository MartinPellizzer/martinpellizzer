Conchochilus

Conchochilus, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, helmet-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates. Conchochilus species typically have slender, erect stems and produce small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Their botanical features make them popular among orchid enthusiasts for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
