Copaifera reticulata var. peruviana

Copaifera reticulata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated capsules containing aromatic resinous seeds. Its resin, known as copaiba balsam, is widely used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
