Columnea erythrocalyx

Columnea erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-lobed shrub, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Columnea erythrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and moisture conditions.
