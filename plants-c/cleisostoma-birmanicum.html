Cleisostoma birmanicum

Cleisostoma birmanicum, commonly known as the Bornean moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, night-blooming flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces pale yellow or white flowers with a strong scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cleisostoma birmanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal floral display.
