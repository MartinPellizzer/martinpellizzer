Crassula tetragona subsp. connivens

Crassula tetragona subsp.

connivens, commonly known as the Fairy Fan Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa. It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the tips.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
