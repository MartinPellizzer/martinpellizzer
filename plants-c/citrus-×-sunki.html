Citrus × sunki

Citrus × sunki, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn orange or yellow. The fruit's outer skin is edible, and it contains a segmented, juicy pulp inside. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its flavorful fruit.
