Cupressus hartwegii

Cupressus hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Cupressus hartwegii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

It is also sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to withstand harsh conditions.
