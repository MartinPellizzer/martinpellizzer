Crotalaria serpyllifolia

Crotalaria serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common spurred crotalaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow with a spurred upper petal. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria serpyllifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
