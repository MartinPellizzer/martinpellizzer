Carex korshinskyi

Carex korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex korshinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
