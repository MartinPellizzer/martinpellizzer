Cynanchum ekmanii

Cynanchum ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and often display a mix of white and pink hues.

Cynanchum ekmanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
