Calamagrostis pseudopurpurea

Calamagrostis pseudopurpurea, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pseudopurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
