Clusia tocuchensis

Clusia tocuchensis, commonly known as the Tocantins glory leaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and ornamental value. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns, providing visual interest. It produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Clusia tocuchensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
