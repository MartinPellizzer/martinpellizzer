Croton bathianus

Croton bathianus, commonly known as the Bathian croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored plants. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. Croton bathianus is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
