Cattleya × spectabilis

Cattleya × spectabilis, commonly known as the orchid with the showy, fragrant flowers, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Cattleya × spectabilis is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
