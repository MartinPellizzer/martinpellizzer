Cephalocrotonopsis

Cephalocrotonopsis, commonly known as the helmet-shaped croton, is a unique plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves that are alternate and often lobed. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is native to tropical regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
