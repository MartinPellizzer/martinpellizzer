Carex diversicolor

Carex diversicolor, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex diversicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal, especially due to its variegated foliage that adds visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes.
