Calamus graminosus

Calamus graminosus, commonly known as slender calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its rhizomes are also valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural practices.
