Cordia goudotii

Cordia goudotii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cordia goudotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
