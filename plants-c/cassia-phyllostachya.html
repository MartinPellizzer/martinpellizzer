Cassia phyllostachya

Cassia phyllostachya, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds with a distinctive aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Cassia phyllostachya is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability.
