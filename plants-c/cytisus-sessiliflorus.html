Cytisus sessiliflorus

Cytisus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

The plant is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
