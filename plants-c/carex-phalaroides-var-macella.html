Carex phalaroides var. macella

Carex phalaroides var. macella, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
