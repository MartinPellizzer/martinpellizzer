Cissus natalensis

Cissus natalensis, commonly known as Natal fig or African snowball, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along fences or trees.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
