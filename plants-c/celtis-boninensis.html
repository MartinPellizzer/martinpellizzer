Celtis boninensis

Celtis boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin hackberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Japan and the Ryukyu Islands.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round drupes that turn dark blue when mature.

Celtis boninensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
