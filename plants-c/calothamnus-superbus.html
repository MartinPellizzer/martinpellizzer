Calothamnus superbus

Calothamnus superbus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Calothamnus superbus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and floral arrangements.
