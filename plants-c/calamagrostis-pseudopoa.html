Calamagrostis pseudopoa

Calamagrostis pseudopoa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, blue-green, and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Calamagrostis pseudopoa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
