Coniogramme macrophylla

Coniogramme macrophylla, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features a robust, upright rhizome that supports its expansive fronds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, fan-like appearance.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
