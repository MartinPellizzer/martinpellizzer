Crepis chamaephylla

Crepis chamaephylla, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats on the ground, with small, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crepis chamaephylla is often found in grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed habitats, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
