Cordia scabrifolia

Cordia scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Cordia scabrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
