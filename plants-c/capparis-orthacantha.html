Capparis orthacantha

Capparis orthacantha, commonly known as the African mountain caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
