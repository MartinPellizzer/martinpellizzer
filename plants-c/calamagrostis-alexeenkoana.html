Calamagrostis alexeenkoana

Calamagrostis alexeenkoana, commonly known as the silvergrass or Russian silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes silvery in the sun.

Calamagrostis alexeenkoana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
