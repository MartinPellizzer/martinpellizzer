Cassia jaegeri

Cassia jaegeri, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered flowers. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

Cassia jaegeri is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
