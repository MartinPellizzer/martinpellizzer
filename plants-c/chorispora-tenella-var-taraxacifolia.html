Chorispora tenella var. taraxacifolia

Chorispora tenella var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the "dandelionleaf chorispora," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, and its leaves are often mistaken for dandelion leaves due to their similar appearance.

This variety is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
