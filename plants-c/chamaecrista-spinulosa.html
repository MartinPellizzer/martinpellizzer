Chamaecrista spinulosa

Chamaecrista spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Chamaecrista spinulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
