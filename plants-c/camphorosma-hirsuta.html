Camphorosma hirsuta

Camphorosma hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy camphorosma, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
