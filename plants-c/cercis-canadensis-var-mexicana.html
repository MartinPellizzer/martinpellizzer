Cercis canadensis var. mexicana

Cercis canadensis var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

The Mexican redbud is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of climates.
