Calamintha menthifolia var. adscendens

Calamintha menthifolia var. adscendens, commonly known as ascending catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often with a woolly texture. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late summer.

This plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
