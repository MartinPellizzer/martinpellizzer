Calophyllum apetalum

Calophyllum apetalum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a perfume. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense canopy, making it suitable for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in crafting and construction.
