Carex sadoensis

Carex sadoensis, commonly known as Sado sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in the summer.

Carex sadoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
