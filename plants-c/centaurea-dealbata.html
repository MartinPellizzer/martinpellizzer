Centaurea dealbata

Centaurea dealbata, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea dealbata is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas, slopes, and disturbed ground.
