Caesalpinia guanensis

Caesalpinia guanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with bright yellow flowers that resemble peacock feathers, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.

Caesalpinia guanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
