Cirsium × sudae

Cirsium × sudae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
