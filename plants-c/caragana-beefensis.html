Caragana beefensis

Caragana beefensis, commonly known as the Mongolian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana beefensis is often used in reclamation projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
