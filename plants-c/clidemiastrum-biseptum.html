Clidemiastrum biseptum

Clidemiastrum biseptum, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom throughout the year, giving it a continuous appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its soft, misty appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemiastrum biseptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
