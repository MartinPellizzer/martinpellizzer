Croton dolichostachyus

Croton dolichostachyus, commonly known as the long-stalked croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton dolichostachyus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
