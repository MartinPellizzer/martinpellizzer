Convolvulus volubilis

Convolvulus volubilis, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions.

Convolvulus volubilis is known for its vigorous growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
