Cyperus waterlotii

Cyperus waterlotii, commonly known as the water flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikes of brownish or purplish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus waterlotii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
