Chrysodium fasciculatum

Chrysodium fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chrysodium fasciculatum is relatively low-maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
