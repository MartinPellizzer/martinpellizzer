Clematis tozanensis

Clematis tozanensis, commonly known as the Tozanensis Clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Clematis tozanensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various growing conditions.
