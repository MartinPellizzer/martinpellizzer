Ceropegia leachii

Ceropegia leachii, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
