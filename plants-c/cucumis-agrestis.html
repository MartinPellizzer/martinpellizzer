Cucumis agrestis

Cucumis agrestis, commonly known as wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, spiny fruits that mature to a yellowish or orange color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

Cucumis agrestis is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
