Cornus microcarpa

Cornus microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Cornus microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
