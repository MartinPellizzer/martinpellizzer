Cinnamomum japonicum var. nervosum

Cinnamomum japonicum var. nervosum, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Japanese camphor tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, which gives it its varietal name "nervosum.". The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves and bark contain aromatic compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
