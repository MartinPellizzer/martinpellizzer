Cistus arrigens

Cistus arrigens, commonly known as the Portuguese rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Cistus arrigens is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and its ability to support local biodiversity.
