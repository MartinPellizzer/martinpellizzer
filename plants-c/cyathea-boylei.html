Cyathea boylei

Cyathea boylei, commonly known as the Boyle's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Cyathea boylei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
