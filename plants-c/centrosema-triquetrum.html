Centrosema triquetrum

Centrosema triquetrum, commonly known as the three-lobed centrosema, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Centrosema triquetrum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
