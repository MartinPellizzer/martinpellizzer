Calceolaria alicahuensis

Calceolaria alicahuensis, commonly known as the Andean hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers that resemble tiny bells. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white, and bloom during the cooler months.

Calceolaria alicahuensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
