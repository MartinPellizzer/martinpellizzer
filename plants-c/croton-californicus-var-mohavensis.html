Croton californicus var. mohavensis

Croton californicus var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
