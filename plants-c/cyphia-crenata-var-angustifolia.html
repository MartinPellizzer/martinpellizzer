Cyphia crenata var. angustifolia

Cyphia crenata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cyphia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
