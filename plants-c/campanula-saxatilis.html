Campanula saxatilis

Campanula saxatilis, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing on limestone or rocky substrates.

Campanula saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
