Capsicum mirabile var. grandiflorum

Capsicum mirabile var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flower pepper, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 inches in diameter, often in shades of yellow or orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to flourish.

While primarily grown for its ornamental value, it can also be cultivated for its edible fruits in suitable conditions.
