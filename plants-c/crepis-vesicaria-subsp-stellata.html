Crepis vesicaria subsp. stellata

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starless mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has a compact growth habit and is typically short-lived, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
