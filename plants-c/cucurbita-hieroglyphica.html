Cucurbita hieroglyphica

Cucurbita hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its large, warty, green fruit that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is typically used for ornamental purposes or as a container due to its unique texture and appearance.

The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
