Cistus berthelotianus var. leucophyllus

Cistus berthelotianus var. leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with silvery-white undersides, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
