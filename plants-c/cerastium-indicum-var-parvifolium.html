Cerastium indicum var. parvifolium

Cerastium indicum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical structure is adapted for efficient nutrient absorption in poor soils.
