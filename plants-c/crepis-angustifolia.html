Crepis angustifolia

Crepis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are yellow, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
