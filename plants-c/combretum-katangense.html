Combretum katangense

Combretum katangense, commonly known as the Katanga flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer. The tree has smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn red or orange before falling in the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
