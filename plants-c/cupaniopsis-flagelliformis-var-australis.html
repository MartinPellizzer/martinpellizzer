Cupaniopsis flagelliformis var. australis

Cupaniopsis flagelliformis var. australis, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
