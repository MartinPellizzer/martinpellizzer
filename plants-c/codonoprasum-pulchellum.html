Codonoprasum pulchellum

Codonoprasum pulchellum, commonly known as the purple carrot.

This plant is a biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and umbel-shaped inflorescences. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
