Cardaminopsis arenosa subsp. borbasii

Cardaminopsis arenosa subsp.

borbasii, commonly known as the Borbas's rock-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, adapting to harsh, dry conditions with its compact growth form. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.
