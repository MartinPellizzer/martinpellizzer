Cardamine argentina

Cardamine argentina, commonly known as the Argentine water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as streams, ponds, and marshes. The plant features delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Cardamine argentina plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
