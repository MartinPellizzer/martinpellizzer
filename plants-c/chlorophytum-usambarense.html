Chlorophytum usambarense

Chlorophytum usambarense, commonly known as the Tiger's Tail or Tiger's Tooth, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often striped with white or yellow markings, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value and ease of care have contributed to its widespread cultivation.
