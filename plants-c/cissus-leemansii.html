Cissus leemansii

Cissus leemansii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or grayish sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative climbing plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Cissus leemansii is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
