Convolvulus aureus

Convolvulus aureus, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Convolvulus aureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
