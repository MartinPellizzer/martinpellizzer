Calliandra hygrophila

Calliandra hygrophila, commonly known as the silky sericea tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that produce small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
