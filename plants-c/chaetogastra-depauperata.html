Chaetogastra depauperata

Chaetogastra depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-haired grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. It produces panicles of tiny, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Chaetogastra depauperata is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
