Conyza musra

Conyza musra, commonly known as musra weed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Africa and Asia. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Conyza musra is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
