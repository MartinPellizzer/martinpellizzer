Chulusium amphibium

Chulusium amphibium, commonly known as the water lily of the marshes, is a semi-aquatic plant native to wetland environments.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water below. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in muddy substrates.

It plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
