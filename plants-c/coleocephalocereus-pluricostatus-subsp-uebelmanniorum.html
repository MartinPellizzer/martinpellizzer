Coleocephalocereus pluricostatus subsp. uebelmanniorum

Coleocephalocereus pluricostatus subsp.

uebelmanniorum, commonly known as the Uebelmann's candelabra cactus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with multiple branches that form a candelabra-like structure.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
