Capsella bottnica

Capsella bottnica, commonly known as the Baltic knotweed, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant features small, white flowers with four petals and produces pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
