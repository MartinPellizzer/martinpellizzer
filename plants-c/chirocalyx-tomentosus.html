Chirocalyx tomentosus

Chirocalyx tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly calyx plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are covered in fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
