Cladothrix oblongifolia

Cladothrix oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cladothrix, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Cladothrix oblongifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and resilience to mild drought conditions.
