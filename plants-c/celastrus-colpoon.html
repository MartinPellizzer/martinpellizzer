Celastrus colpoon

Celastrus colpoon, commonly known as the Carolina moonseed, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red berries that are edible but often considered unpalatable. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported tonic properties.
