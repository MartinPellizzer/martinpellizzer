Chamaedorea woodsoniana

Chamaedorea woodsoniana, commonly known as the Woodson's bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems and feathery, compound leaves that give it a delicate, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Chamaedorea woodsoniana is a low-maintenance plant well-suited for indoor cultivation.
