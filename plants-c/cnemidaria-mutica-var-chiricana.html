Cnemidaria mutica var. chiricana

Cnemidaria mutica var. chiricana, commonly known as the Chiricahua prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions through its succulent structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has been used by indigenous peoples for both food and medicinal purposes.
