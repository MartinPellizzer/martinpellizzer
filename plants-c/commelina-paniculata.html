Commelina paniculata

Commelina paniculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with three petals and three sepals, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Commelina paniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
