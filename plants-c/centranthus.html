Centranthus

Centranthus, commonly known as red valerian, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

These plants are native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and they are known for their showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Centranthus species are typically perennial herbs that thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. They produce small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus includes several species, with Centranthus ruber being the most commonly cultivated for its ornamental value.
