Campanula cochleariifolia subf. aberrans

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

aberrans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender stems that are often hairy and bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to other forms of Campanula cochleariifolia.
