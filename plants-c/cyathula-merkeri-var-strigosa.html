Cyathula merkeri var. strigosa

Cyathula merkeri var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy cyathula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the genus Cyathula. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its unique foliage.
