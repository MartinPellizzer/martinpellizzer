Calystegia cymosa

Calystegia cymosa, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple, often opening in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. Its flowers are typically followed by spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Calystegia cymosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
