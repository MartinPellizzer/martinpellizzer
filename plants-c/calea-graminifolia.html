Calea graminifolia

Calea graminifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and they bloom during the summer months.

Calea graminifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
