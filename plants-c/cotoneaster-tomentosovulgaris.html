Cotoneaster tomentosovulgaris

Cotoneaster tomentosovulgaris, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate with a fuzzy, grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cotoneaster tomentosovulgaris is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.
