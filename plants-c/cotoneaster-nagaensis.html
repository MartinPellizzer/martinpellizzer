Cotoneaster nagaensis

Cotoneaster nagaensis, commonly known as the Naga cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves seasonally. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Cotoneaster nagaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
