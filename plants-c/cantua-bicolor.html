Cantua bicolor

Cantua bicolor, commonly known as the woolly thimblecush, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cantua bicolor is also cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
