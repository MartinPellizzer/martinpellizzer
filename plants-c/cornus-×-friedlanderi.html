Cornus × friedlanderi

Cornus × friedlanderi, commonly known as the Friedland viburnum, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pinkish bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, white, clustered flowers that are followed by red or purple berries in the fall.

Cornus × friedlanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant seasonal color changes.
