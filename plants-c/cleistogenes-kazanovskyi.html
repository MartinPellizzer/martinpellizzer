Cleistogenes kazanovskyi

Cleistogenes kazanovskyi, commonly known as the Kazanovsky's bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
