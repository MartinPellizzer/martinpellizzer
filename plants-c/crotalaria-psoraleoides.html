Crotalaria psoraleoides

Crotalaria psoraleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria psoraleoides is also known for its potential toxicity to livestock.
