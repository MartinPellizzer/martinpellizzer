Chassalia fontanesii

Chassalia fontanesii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chassalia fontanesii is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
