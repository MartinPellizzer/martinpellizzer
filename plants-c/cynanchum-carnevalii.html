Cynanchum carnevalii

Cynanchum carnevalii, commonly known as the Carnevalii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Mongolia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, green fruits that contain toxic seeds, a feature common among members of its family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
