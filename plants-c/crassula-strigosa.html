Crassula strigosa

Crassula strigosa, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula strigosa is often grown as a houseplant or in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
