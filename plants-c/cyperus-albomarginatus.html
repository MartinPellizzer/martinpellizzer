Cyperus albomarginatus

Cyperus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, pond edges, and other aquatic habitats.

Cyperus albomarginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
