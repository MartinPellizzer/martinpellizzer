Campanula dasyantha f. albiflora

Campanula dasyantha f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a variant of the campanula genus characterized by its striking white blooms.

This perennial plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically borne in clusters on slender stems. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
