Cassinia obovata

Cassinia obovata, commonly known as the golden corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are obovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Cassinia obovata is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
