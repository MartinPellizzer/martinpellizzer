Caragana nuristanica

Caragana nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan thorny bush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Caragana nuristanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
