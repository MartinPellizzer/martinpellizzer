Chamaepeuce cynaroides

Chamaepeuce cynaroides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright stems that bear showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
