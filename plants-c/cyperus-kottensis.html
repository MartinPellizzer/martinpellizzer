Cyperus kottensis

Cyperus kottensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Cyperus kottensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
