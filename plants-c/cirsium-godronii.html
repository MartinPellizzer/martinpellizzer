Cirsium godronii

Cirsium godronii, commonly known as Godron's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium godronii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
