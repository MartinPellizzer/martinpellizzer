Cephalaria flava subsp. setulifera

Cephalaria flava subsp.

setulifera, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy structures, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
