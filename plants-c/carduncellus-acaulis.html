Carduncellus acaulis

Carduncellus acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem carduncellus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that is covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are basal, elongated, and often have a whitish or grayish coating.

Carduncellus acaulis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and shrublands.
