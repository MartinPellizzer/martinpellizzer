Carex sorianoi

Carex sorianoi, commonly known as the Sorianoi sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Carex sorianoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
