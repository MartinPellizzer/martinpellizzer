Chamaecrista confertiformis

Chamaecrista confertiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and covered in hooked spines, which aid in seed dispersal.

The plant is adapted to sandy and disturbed soils, often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.
