Carex uncinata

Carex uncinata, commonly known as the hooked sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex uncinata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
