Centrosema variifolium

Centrosema variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf centrosema, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which vary in shape and size, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
