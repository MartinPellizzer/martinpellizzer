Chionanthus caudatus

Chionanthus caudatus, commonly known as the nodding whiteflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Chionanthus caudatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
