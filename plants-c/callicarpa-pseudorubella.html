Callicarpa pseudorubella

Callicarpa pseudorubella, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple to red berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
