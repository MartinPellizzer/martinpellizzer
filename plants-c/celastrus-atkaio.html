Celastrus atkaio

Celastrus atkaio, commonly known as the Atkaio bindweed, is a woody vine native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
