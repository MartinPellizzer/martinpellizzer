Carex siccata var. obscurior

Carex siccata var. obscurior, commonly known as the dark fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or greenish flowers. Its leaves are often dark green and have a distinctive, slightly rough texture.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
