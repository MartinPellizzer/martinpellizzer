Celastrus molinae

Celastrus molinae, commonly known as the Molina's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
