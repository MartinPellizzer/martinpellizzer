Carex oliveriana

Carex oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its foliage is grass-like with narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

Carex oliveriana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
