Carex furcata

Carex furcata, commonly known as the forked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Carex furcata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
