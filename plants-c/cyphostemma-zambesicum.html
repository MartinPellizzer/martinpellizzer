Cyphostemma zambesicum

Cyphostemma zambesicum, commonly known as the Zambesi moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
