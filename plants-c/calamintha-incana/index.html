Calamintha Incana

Calamintha incana is a hardy perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is known for its fragrant, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties. With its delicate appearance and aromatic qualities, Calamintha incana is a valuable addition to any garden.
