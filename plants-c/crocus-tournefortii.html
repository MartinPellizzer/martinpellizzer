Crocus tournefortii

Crocus tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, violet-blue flowers in the autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.
