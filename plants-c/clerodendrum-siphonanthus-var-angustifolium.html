Clerodendrum siphonanthus var. angustifolium

Clerodendrum siphonanthus var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure typical of the Rubiaceae family.
