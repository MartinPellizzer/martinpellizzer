Carex crinita var. angusta

Carex crinita var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscapes.
