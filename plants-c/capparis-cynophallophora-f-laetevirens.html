Capparis cynophallophora f. laetevirens

Capparis cynophallophora f. laetevirens, commonly known as the golden caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This variety is a natural hybrid of Capparis cynophallophora and Capparis spinosa, exhibiting distinct botanical characteristics.
