Cyperus bracteolatus

Cyperus bracteolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in three rows, and it produces small, three-sided spikelets that are often inconspicuous.

Cyperus bracteolatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
