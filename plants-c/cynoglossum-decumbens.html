Cynoglossum decumbens

Cynoglossum decumbens, commonly known as creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
