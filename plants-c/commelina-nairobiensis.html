Commelina nairobiensis

Commelina nairobiensis, commonly known as Nairobi grass, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are clustered in umbels.

Commelina nairobiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
