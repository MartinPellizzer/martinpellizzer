Cousinia nivea

Cousinia nivea, commonly known as the white mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia nivea is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
