Cerasus × nudiflora

Cerasus × nudiflora, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
