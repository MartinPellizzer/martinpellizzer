Crepis nigrescens

Crepis nigrescens, commonly known as the black-haired mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often darken with age, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
