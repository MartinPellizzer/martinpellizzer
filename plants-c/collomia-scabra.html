Collomia scabra

Collomia scabra, commonly known as the scabrous collomia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
