Caladium schomburgkii f. erythraeum

Caladium schomburgkii f. erythraeum, commonly known as the Red-veined Caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with vibrant red veins contrasting against a deep green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor spaces.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for decorative purposes.
