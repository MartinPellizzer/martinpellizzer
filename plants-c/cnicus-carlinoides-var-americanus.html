Cnicus carlinoides var. americanus

Cnicus carlinoides var. americanus, commonly known as the American hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and is native to North America. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
