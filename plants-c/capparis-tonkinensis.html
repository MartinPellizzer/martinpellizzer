Capparis tonkinensis

Capparis tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sour plum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, yellowish berries that contain a single seed and are used in traditional cuisines for their tart flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
