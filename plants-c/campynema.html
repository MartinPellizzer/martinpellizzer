Campynema

Campynema, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of a single species, Campynema glabrum, which is native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
