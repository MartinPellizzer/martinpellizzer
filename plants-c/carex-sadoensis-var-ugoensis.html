Carex sadoensis var. ugoensis

Carex sadoensis var. ugoensis, commonly known as the Sado Ugo sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
