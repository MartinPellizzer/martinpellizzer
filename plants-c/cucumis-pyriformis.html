Cucumis pyriformis

Cucumis pyriformis, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like shape. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
