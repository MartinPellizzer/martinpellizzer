Cyclophorus malacophyllus

Cyclophorus malacophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf cyclophorus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It possesses large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a tactilely appealing texture. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a tendency to form dense clusters near the ground.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
