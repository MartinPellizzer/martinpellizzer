Caladium brasiliense

Caladium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright red, orange, or yellow, with a contrasting green or white border. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Caladium brasiliense thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor decoration.
