Convolvulus sagittatus var. linearifolius

Convolvulus sagittatus var. linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are elongated in this variety. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pale blue to purple in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
