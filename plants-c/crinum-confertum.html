Crinum confertum

Crinum confertum, commonly known as the broad-leaved crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking umbellate inflorescences with fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Crinum confertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
