Citrus maxima var. uvacarpa

Citrus maxima var. uvacarpa, commonly known as the Natal orange, is a tropical citrus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, yellow-orange rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering.

This variety is valued for its fruit's flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
