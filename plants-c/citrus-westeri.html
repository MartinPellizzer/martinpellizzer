Citrus westeri

Citrus westeri, commonly known as the Wester's kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C. The tree is relatively easy to grow in warm climates and is often cultivated in home gardens.
