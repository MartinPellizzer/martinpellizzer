Cnicus benedictus

Cnicus benedictus, commonly known as blessed thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
