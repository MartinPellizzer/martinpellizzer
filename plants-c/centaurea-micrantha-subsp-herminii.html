Centaurea micrantha subsp. herminii

Centaurea micrantha subsp.

herminii, commonly known as Hermin's dwarf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
