Campanula baumgartenii subsp. baumgartenii

Campanula baumgartenii subsp.

baumgartenii, commonly known as the Baumann's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high elevations where conditions are cool and moist.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
