Clerodendrum sansibarense subsp. occidentale

Clerodendrum sansibarense subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the West African glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
