Cordia canescens

Cordia canescens, commonly known as the white cedar or white cordia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Cordia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
