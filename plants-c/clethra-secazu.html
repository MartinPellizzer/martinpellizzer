Clethra secazu

Clethra secazu, commonly known as the dryland clethra, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Clethra secazu is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
