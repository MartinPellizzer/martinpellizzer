Croton tatacuensis

Croton tatacuensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its showy, clustered inflorescences that range from yellow to red in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry and disturbed habitats, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental members.
