Cineraria geifolia

Cineraria geifolia, commonly known as the white cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silvery-green, fuzzy leaves and produces showy, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
