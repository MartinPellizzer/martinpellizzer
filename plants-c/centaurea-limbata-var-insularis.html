Centaurea limbata var. insularis

Centaurea limbata var. insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features spiny, branching stems and produces purple to pinkish flowers with distinctive ray florets. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions outside its native range.
