Chenopodina fruticulosa

Chenopodina fruticulosa, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a hardy shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and rich in nutrients, making it a valuable plant for both wildlife and human consumption.

Chenopodina fruticulosa is well adapted to harsh conditions, thriving in poor soils and high temperatures.
