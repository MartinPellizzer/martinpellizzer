Callista hispida

Callista hispida, commonly known as the rough callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Callista hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
