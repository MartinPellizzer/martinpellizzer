Castanea vesca var. aurata

Castanea vesca var. aurata, commonly known as the golden horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in umbel-like clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are large, palmately lobed, and provide a lush green canopy throughout the growing season. This variety is often cultivated for its showy blooms and is relatively resistant to some common pests and diseases.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
