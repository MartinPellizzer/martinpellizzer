Crataegus laetifica

Crataegus laetifica, commonly known as the joyful hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus laetifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
