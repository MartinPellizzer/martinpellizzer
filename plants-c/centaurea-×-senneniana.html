Centaurea × senneniana

Centaurea × senneniana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
