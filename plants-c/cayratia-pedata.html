Cayratia pedata

Cayratia pedata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, berry-like fruits. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cayratia pedata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
