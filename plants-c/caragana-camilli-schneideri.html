Caragana camilli-schneideri

Caragana camilli-schneideri, commonly known as the dwarf caragana, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
