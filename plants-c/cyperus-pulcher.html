Cyperus pulcher

Cyperus pulcher, commonly known as the handsome sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. Cyperus pulcher is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in moist garden environments.
