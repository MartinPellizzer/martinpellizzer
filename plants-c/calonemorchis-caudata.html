Calonemorchis caudata

Calonemorchis caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a distinctive elongated spur that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Calonemorchis caudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
