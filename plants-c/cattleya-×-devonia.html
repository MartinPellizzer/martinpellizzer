Cattleya × devonia

Cattleya × devonia, commonly known as the Devonian cattleya, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features large, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often displaying a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
