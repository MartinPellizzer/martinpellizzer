Carex teres var. spathulata

Carex teres var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management and wildlife habitat.
