Cochlearia grandiflora

Cochlearia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which are often bright yellow or white, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
