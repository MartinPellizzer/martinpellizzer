Cassuvium subracemosum

Cassuvium subracemosum, commonly known as the silverleaf cascuvia, is a perennial plant native to the humid tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in nutrient-poor soils.

Cassuvium subracemosum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and air-purifying properties.
