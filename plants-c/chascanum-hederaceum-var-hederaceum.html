Chascanum hederaceum var. hederaceum

Chascanum hederaceum var. hederaceum, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with a trailing habit, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It produces showy blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
