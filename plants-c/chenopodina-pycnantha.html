Chenopodina pycnantha

Chenopodina pycnantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf chenopod, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are followed by dry, hard fruits. It is well adapted to harsh environments, showing tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of the local flora in its native range.
