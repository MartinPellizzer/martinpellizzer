Circaea lutetiana var. villosa

Circaea lutetiana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped leaflet and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate various soil types and is often found in temperate regions.
