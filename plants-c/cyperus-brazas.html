Cyperus brazas

Cyperus brazas, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often inconspicuous. Cyperus brazas is adapted to aquatic conditions, with its roots capable of tolerating low oxygen levels in waterlogged soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
