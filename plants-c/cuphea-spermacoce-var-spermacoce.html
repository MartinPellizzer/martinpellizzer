Cuphea spermacoce var. spermacoce

Cuphea spermacoce var. spermacoce, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sunlight.
