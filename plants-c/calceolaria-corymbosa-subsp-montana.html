Calceolaria corymbosa subsp. montana

Calceolaria corymbosa subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain calceolaria, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small pouches.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas of mountain forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in shades of violet, purple, and white.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
