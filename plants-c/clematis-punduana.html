Clematis punduana

Clematis punduana, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil. Clematis punduana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb supports with its strong, twisting stems.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
