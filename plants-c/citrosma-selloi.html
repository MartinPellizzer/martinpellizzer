Citrosma selloi

Citrosma selloi, commonly known as the Malabar citron or Indian citron, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by oval-shaped fruits with a thick, leathery rind. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, citrus-like aroma.

Citrosma selloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
