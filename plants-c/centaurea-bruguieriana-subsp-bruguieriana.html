Centaurea bruguieriana subsp. bruguieriana

Centaurea bruguieriana subsp.

bruguieriana, commonly known as the Bruguier's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
