Callicarpa cana f. glabriuscula

Callicarpa cana f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-flowered beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces striking, bright purple berries that are a key feature of its ornamental value. These berries are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
