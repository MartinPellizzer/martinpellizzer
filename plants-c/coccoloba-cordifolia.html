Coccoloba cordifolia

Coccoloba cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often near coastal areas or in humid forests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

The botanical characteristics of Coccoloba cordifolia make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
