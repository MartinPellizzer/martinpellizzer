Clematis pickeringii

Clematis pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Clematis pickeringii is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
