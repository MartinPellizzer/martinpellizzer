Chamaecrista lomatopoda

Chamaecrista lomatopoda, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. Chamaecrista lomatopoda is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots and a low, sprawling growth habit.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
