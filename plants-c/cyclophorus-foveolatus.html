Cyclophorus foveolatus

Cyclophorus foveolatus, commonly known as the burr daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, rounded burrs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by the development of its unique seed structures. Cyclophorus foveolatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
