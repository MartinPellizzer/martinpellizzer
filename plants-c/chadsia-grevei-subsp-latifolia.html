Chadsia grevei subsp. latifolia

Chadsia grevei subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chadsia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
