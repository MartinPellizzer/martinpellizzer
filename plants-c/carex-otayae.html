Carex otayae

Carex otayae, commonly known as Otay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams. Its appearance includes grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or reddish in color.

Carex otayae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
