Convolvulus calvertii subsp. ruprechtii

Convolvulus calvertii subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
