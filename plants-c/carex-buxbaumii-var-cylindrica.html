Carex buxbaumii var. cylindrica

Carex buxbaumii var. cylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils, often in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
