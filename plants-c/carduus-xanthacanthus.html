Carduus xanthacanthus

Carduus xanthacanthus, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant features a tall, upright stem with deeply lobed, rough-textured foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, typically arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
