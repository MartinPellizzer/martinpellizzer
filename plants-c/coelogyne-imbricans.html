Coelogyne imbricans

Coelogyne imbricans, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and showy flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Coelogyne imbricans is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
