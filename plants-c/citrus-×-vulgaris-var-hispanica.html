Citrus × vulgaris var. hispanica

Citrus × vulgaris var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish lemon.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, aromatic peel. The leaves are glossy and dark green, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is primarily cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
