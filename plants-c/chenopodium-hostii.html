Chenopodium hostii

Chenopodium hostii, commonly known as the Navajo spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional Native American cuisine. Chenopodium hostii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high levels of vitamins and minerals.
