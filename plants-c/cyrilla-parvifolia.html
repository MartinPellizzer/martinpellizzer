Cyrilla parvifolia

Cyrilla parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cyrilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gland-dotted and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, winged fruits. Cyrilla parvifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
