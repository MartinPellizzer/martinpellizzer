Crataegus crus-galli f. nana

Crataegus crus-galli f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf dogwood or miniature common hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular addition to gardens and landscapes.
