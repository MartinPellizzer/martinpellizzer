Cytisus ponticus

Cytisus ponticus, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often thorned stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus ponticus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
