Casearia tomentosa

Casearia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
