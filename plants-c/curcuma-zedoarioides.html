Curcuma zedoarioides

Curcuma zedoarioides, commonly known as black ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Botanically, it is closely related to other turmeric species, sharing similar morphological features.
