Canarina eminii

Canarina eminii, commonly known as the Armenian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Armenia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five to seven lobes, and its distinctive red or orange-red flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Canarina eminii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in Armenian culture.
