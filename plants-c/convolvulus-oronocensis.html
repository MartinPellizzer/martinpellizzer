Convolvulus oronocensis

Convolvulus oronocensis, commonly known as the Oronoko morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, funnel-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Convolvulus oronocensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
