Crucifera rapa

Crucifera rapa, commonly known as field mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and four-petaled blooms.

It is an annual or biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the plant can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Field mustard is also known for its peppery taste, making it a useful plant in some culinary traditions.
