Cynara cardunculus var. scolymus

Cynara cardunculus var. scolymus, commonly known as the globe artichoke, is a cultivated variety of the cardoon plant.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and a dense, edible flower head that resembles a globe. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous stem and a system of underground rhizomes.

The flower head consists of tightly packed bracts that enclose the central flowering parts, making it a popular vegetable in many cuisines.
