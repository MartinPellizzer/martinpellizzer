Cupania glabra

Cupania glabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. Cupania glabra is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.

It is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
