Calesiam acidum

Calesiam acidum, commonly known as the sour leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly acidic taste. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
