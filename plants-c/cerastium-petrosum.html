Cerastium petrosum

Cerastium petrosum, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear or rock mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Cerastium petrosum is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
