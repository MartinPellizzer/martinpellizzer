Carex malazena

Carex malazena, commonly known as the Malazan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex malazena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
