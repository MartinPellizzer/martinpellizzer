Cleome amblyocarpa var. glandulosa

Cleome amblyocarpa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
