Cirsium silvaticum

Cirsium silvaticum, commonly known as the woodland thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are spiny and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium silvaticum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
