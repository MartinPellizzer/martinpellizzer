Campanula herminii var. atlantica

Campanula herminii var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
