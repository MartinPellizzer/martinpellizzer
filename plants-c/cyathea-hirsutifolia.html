Cyathea hirsutifolia

Cyathea hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive hairy appearance on its trunk and young fronds, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea hirsutifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
