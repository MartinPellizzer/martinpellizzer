Cuphea spicata

Cuphea spicata, commonly known as the spike cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea spicata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
