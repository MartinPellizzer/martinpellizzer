Cryptocarya maroniensis

Cryptocarya maroniensis, commonly known as the Maroni cryptomeria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
