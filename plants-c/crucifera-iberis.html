Crucifera iberis

Crucifera iberis, commonly known as Iberis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Iberis is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
