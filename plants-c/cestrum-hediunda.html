Cestrum hediunda

Cestrum hediunda, commonly known as the red-flowered cestrum, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
