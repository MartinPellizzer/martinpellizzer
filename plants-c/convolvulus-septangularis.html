Convolvulus septangularis

Convolvulus septangularis, commonly known as the angular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Convolvulus septangularis is a climbing plant that uses its tendrils to support itself as it grows.
