Crataegus intricata var. neobushii

Crataegus intricata var. neobushii, commonly known as the Neobush hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
