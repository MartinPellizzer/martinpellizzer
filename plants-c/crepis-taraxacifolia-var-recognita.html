Crepis taraxacifolia var. recognita

Crepis taraxacifolia var. recognita, commonly known as the recognized dandelion-like hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

The species is notable for its horticultural value and ability to colonize various habitats.
