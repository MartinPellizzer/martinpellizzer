Caylusea abyssinica

Caylusea abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian caylusea, is a unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, waxy leaves that are adapted to the arid and cool conditions of its habitat. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
