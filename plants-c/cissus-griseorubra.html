Cissus griseorubra

Cissus griseorubra, commonly known as the red-veined cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple veining, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can develop aerial roots, allowing it to climb and attach to surfaces.

Cissus griseorubra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
