Citrus tardiva

Citrus tardiva, commonly known as the late citrus or winter citrus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested in late winter or early spring. Its botanical features include smooth, glossy leaves and a branched structure that supports its ornamental value.

Citrus tardiva is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible fruits.
