Chrysopsis graminifolia var. latifolia

Chrysopsis graminifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and linear, and the inflorescence consists of small, clustered yellow flowers.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
