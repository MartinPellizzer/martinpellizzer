Celastrus pyrius

Celastrus pyrius, commonly known as the European bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and persistent leaves.
