Correa calycina var. calycina

Correa calycina var. calycina, commonly known as the common correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
