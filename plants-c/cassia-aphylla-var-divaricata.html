Cassia aphylla var. divaricata

Cassia aphylla var. divaricata, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
