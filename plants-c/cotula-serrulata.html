Cotula serrulata

Cotula serrulata, commonly known as the serrated cotula, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cotula serrulata produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
