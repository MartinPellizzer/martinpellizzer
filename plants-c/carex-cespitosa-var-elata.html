Carex cespitosa var. elata

Carex cespitosa var. elata, commonly known as tall sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic habitats.
