Capparis hierosolymitana

Capparis hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has thorned stems and pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
