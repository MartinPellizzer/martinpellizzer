Clematis montana var. batangensis

Clematis montana var. batangensis, commonly known as the Batangensis Clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a support structure for its vigorous growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.
