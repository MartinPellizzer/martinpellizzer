Cyathea funebris

Cyathea funebris, commonly known as the black tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered in a fibrous, dark brown to black outer layer. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive and dramatic appearance. Cyathea funebris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
