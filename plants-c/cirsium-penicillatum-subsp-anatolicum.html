Cirsium penicillatum subsp. anatolicum

Cirsium penicillatum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
