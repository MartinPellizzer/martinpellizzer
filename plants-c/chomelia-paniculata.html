Chomelia paniculata

Chomelia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, flat seeds.

Chomelia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
