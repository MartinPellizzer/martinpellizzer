Carex sachalinensis f. calvescens

Carex sachalinensis f. calvescens, commonly known as the Sachalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-parted flowers, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
