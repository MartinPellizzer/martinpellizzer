Chironia centaurium var. humilis

Chironia centaurium var. humilis, commonly known as lesser centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
