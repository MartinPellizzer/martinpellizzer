Coptis laciniata

Coptis laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing berberine, an alkaloid with medicinal properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Coptis laciniata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
