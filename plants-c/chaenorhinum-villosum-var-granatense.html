Chaenorhinum villosum var. granatense

Chaenorhinum villosum var. granatense, commonly known as the Granate evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
