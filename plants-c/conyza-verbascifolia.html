Conyza verbascifolia

Conyza verbascifolia, commonly known as yellow tarweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Conyza verbascifolia is known for its ability to adapt to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
