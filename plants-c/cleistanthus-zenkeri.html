Cleistanthus zenkeri

Cleistanthus zenkeri, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, dark bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Cleistanthus zenkeri plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
