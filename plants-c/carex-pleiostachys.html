Carex pleiostachys

Carex pleiostachys, commonly known as the many-stamened sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pleiostachys is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
