Cymbidium mackinnonii

Cymbidium mackinnonii, commonly known as the MacKinnon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Cymbidium mackinnonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
