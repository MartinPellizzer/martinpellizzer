Critoniopsis meridensis

Critoniopsis meridensis, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing adaptations to the island's unique climate. Its small, strap-like leaves and clustered inflorescences are key botanical features.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
