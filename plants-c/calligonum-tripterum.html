Calligonum tripterum

Calligonum tripterum, commonly known as the three-winged goosefoot, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-winged leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Calligonum tripterum is also known for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and is used in ecological restoration projects.
