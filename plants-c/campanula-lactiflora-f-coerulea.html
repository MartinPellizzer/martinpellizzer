Campanula lactiflora f. coerulea

Campanula lactiflora f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
