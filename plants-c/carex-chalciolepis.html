Carex chalciolepis

Carex chalciolepis, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, blue-green flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex chalciolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
