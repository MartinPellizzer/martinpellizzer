Calamagrostis aequivalvis

Calamagrostis aequivalvis, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet to moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis aequivalvis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
