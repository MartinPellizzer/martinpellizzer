Christensonella juergensii

Christensonella juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure and lack of a visible stem, which makes it distinct from many other orchid species. The plant typically grows on the bark of trees, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that aids in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
