Cocos syagrus

Cocos syagrus, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the pão de açúcar, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.
