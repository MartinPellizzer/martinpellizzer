Calceolaria rosmarinifolia

Calceolaria rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the lavender leaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Calceolaria rosmarinifolia is known for its unique flower structure, which resembles a small pouch, aiding in pollination by insects.
