Copernicia glabrescens var. ramosissima

Copernicia glabrescens var. ramosissima, commonly known as the ramose copernicia, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts.
