Cedrela glabra

Cedrela glabra, commonly known as white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
