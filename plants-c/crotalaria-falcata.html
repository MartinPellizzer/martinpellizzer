Crotalaria falcata

Crotalaria falcata, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in long, thin pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds to the wind.

Crotalaria falcata is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
