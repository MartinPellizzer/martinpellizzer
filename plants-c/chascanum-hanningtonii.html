Chascanum hanningtonii

Chascanum hanningtonii, commonly known as the Hannington's chascanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces yellow to orange composite flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
