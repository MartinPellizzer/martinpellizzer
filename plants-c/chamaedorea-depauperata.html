Chamaedorea depauperata

Chamaedorea depauperata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the poor man's bamboo, is a compact, evergreen fern native to Mexico and Central America.

It features feathery, dark green fronds that grow in a compact, upright form, making it ideal for indoor cultivation. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture. It is known for its low maintenance and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.

Chamaedorea depauperata is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its elegant, tropical appearance.
