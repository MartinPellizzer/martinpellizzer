Cistus polymorphus f. incanus

Cistus polymorphus f. incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
