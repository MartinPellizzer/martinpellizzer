Cephalophyllum gracile var. longisepalum

Cephalophyllum gracile var. longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal cephalophyllum, is a rare and delicate succulent native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
