Calytrix diversifolia

Calytrix diversifolia, commonly known as the variable calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

Calytrix diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
