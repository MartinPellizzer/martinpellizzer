Castanea sativa var. pendulifolia

Castanea sativa var. pendulifolia, commonly known as the weeping sweet chestnut, is a variant of the sweet chestnut tree characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East, typically growing in temperate climates. The plant produces large, spiny nuts enclosed in a prickly husk, which ripen in the autumn. Its ornamental value comes from its cascading foliage and showy flowers in the spring.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance.
