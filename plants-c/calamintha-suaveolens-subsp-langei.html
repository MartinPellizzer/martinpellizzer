Calamintha suaveolens subsp. langei

Calamintha suaveolens subsp.

langei, commonly known as sweet germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
