Cacalia poculifera

Cacalia poculifera, commonly known as the cup-shaped bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cacalia poculifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
