Cyrtochilum trulla

Cyrtochilum trulla, commonly known as the Trulla orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Cyrtochilum trulla thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.
