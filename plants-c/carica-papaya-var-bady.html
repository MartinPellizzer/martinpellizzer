Carica papaya var. bady

Carica papaya var. bady, commonly known as the Indian papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinct, fragrant fruit that ripens quickly. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with a short, sturdy stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms, and the fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored berry containing numerous black seeds.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for warm climates and easy cultivation.
