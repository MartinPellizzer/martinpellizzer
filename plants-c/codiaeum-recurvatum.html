Codiaeum recurvatum

Codiaeum recurvatum, commonly known as the flame maple or croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting patterns. The plant belongs to the Crotonaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a waxy texture and prominent veins.

Codiaeum recurvatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its eye-catching foliage.
