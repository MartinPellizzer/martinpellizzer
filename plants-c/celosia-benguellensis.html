Celosia benguellensis

Celosia benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguela cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a cockscomb. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Celosia benguellensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and drought tolerance.
