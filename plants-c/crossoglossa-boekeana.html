Crossoglossa boekeana

Crossoglossa boekeana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
