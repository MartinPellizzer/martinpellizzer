Cuphea bracteolosa

Cuphea bracteolosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea bracteolosa is also valued for its ability to produce seed pods that contain edible seeds, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.
