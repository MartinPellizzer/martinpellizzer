Cytisus arboreus var. tetuanensis

Cytisus arboreus var. tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuanese broom, is a shrub native to North Africa, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
