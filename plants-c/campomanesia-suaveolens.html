Campomanesia suaveolens

Campomanesia suaveolens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is rough and fissured.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruits and medicinal properties.
