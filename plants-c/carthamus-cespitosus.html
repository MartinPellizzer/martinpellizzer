Carthamus cespitosus

Carthamus cespitosus, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
