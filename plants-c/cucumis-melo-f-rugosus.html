Cucumis melo f. rugosus

Cucumis melo f. rugosus, commonly known as the rough melon, is a botanical variant of the melon species characterized by its textured, warty surface.

This variety belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and southern Asia. The plant typically produces large, elongated fruits with a pale green exterior and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It is cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a edible fruit in some regions.

The rough skin of the fruit is a distinguishing feature that sets it apart from other melon varieties.
