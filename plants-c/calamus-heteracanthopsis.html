Calamus heteracanthopsis

Calamus heteracanthopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves that are often a deep green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus heteracanthopsis is often grown for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
