Convolvulus erubescens var. albus

Convolvulus erubescens var. albus, commonly known as white morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides. Its rapid growth and prolific flowering make it both a weed and a decorative plant.

The species is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it is known for its ability to climb using its spiraling stems.
