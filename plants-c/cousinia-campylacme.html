Cousinia campylacme

Cousinia campylacme, commonly known as the narrowleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia campylacme produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
