Catasetum dilectum

Catasetum dilectum, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive yellow and green flowers, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific bees. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are short-lived but highly attractive, often featuring a striking color contrast between the petals and sepals.

Catasetum dilectum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
