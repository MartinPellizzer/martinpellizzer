Crotalaria stewartii

Crotalaria stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria stewartii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, some species within the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling this plant.
