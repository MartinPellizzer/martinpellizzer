Corymbia bloxsomei

Corymbia bloxsomei, commonly known as the Northern Territory gum, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in savanna woodlands and seasonally flooded areas. The tree has smooth, grey bark that peels in thin strips, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
