Ceanothus prostratus var. grandifolius

Ceanothus prostratus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved creeping ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
