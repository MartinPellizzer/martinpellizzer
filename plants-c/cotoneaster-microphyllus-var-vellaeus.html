Cotoneaster microphyllus var. vellaeus

Cotoneaster microphyllus var. vellaeus, commonly known as the velvet cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of soil types.
