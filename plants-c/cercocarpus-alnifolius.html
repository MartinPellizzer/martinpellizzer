Cercocarpus alnifolius

Cercocarpus alnifolius, commonly known as the Nuttall's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
