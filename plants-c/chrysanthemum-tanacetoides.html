Chrysanthemum tanacetoides

Chrysanthemum tanacetoides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy foliage and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting nature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
