Cephalophora glauca

Cephalophora glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Cephalophora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, typically from late August to November. The plant grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cephalophora glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
