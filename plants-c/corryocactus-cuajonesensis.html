Corryocactus cuajonesensis

Corryocactus cuajonesensis, commonly known as the Cuajones cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
