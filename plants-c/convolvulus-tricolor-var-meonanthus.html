Convolvulus tricolor var. meonanthus

Convolvulus tricolor var. meonanthus, commonly known as the meadow bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of blue, purple, and white, and are often spotted or streaked with contrasting colors. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
