Calceolaria connatifolia

Calceolaria connatifolia, commonly known as the fringed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular leaves and thrives in cool, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and unusual floral structure.

Calceolaria connatifolia is also noted for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
