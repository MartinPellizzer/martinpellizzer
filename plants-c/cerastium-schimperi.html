Cerastium schimperi

Cerastium schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
