Centaurea paniculata var. biformis

Centaurea paniculata var. biformis, commonly known as the two-branched bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
