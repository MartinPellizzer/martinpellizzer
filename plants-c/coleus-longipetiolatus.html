Coleus longipetiolatus

Coleus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with long petioles that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor settings.
