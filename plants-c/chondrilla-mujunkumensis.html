Chondrilla mujunkumensis

Chondrilla mujunkumensis, commonly known as the Mojunkum Desert Cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, featuring small, narrow leaves and a deep root system. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
