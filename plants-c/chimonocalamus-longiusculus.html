Chimonocalamus longiusculus

Chimonocalamus longiusculus, commonly known as the long-leaf reed, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
