Clelandia convallis

Clelandia convallis, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that attracts pollinators.

Clelandia convallis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
