Clematis puberula var. ganpiniana

Clematis puberula var. ganpiniana, commonly known as the Ganpinian Clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
