Cirrhopetalum rivesii

Cirrhopetalum rivesii, commonly known as the Rives' orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and showy floral structures. The plant typically features slender stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique, wavy petal pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
