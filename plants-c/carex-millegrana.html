Carex millegrana

Carex millegrana, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to moist, temperate regions, this plant thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Carex millegrana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
