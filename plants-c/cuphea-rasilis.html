Cuphea rasilis

Cuphea rasilis, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fluffy bracts that resemble tassels, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Cuphea rasilis is valued for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
