Crassula filiformis

Crassula filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, thread-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula filiformis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
