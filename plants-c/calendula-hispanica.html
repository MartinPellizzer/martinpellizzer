Calendula hispanica

Calendula hispanica, commonly known as Spanish marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are typically borne on sturdy, hairy stems. The plant produces ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Calendula hispanica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
