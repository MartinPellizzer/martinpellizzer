Calamus multinervis

Calamus multinervis, commonly known as the many-veined calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and erect, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its intricate vascular system and unique reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
