Callista johannis

Callista johannis, commonly known as the John's callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Callista johannis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
