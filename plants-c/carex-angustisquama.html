Carex angustisquama

Carex angustisquama, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
