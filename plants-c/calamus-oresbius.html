Calamus oresbius

Calamus oresbius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweet flag, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, though it is not a true palm. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are characterized by their fibrous structure and are sometimes harvested for their medicinal properties.

Calamus oresbius thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.
