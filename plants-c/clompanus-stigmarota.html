Clompanus stigmarota

Clompanus stigmarota, commonly known as the star-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped stems and bell-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize the soil.

Clompanus stigmarota is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
