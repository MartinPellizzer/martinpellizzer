Clematis argentea

Clematis argentea, commonly known as silver clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis argentea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
