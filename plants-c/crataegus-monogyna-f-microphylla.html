Crataegus monogyna f. microphylla

Crataegus monogyna f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hawthorn, is a compact shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiac benefits.
