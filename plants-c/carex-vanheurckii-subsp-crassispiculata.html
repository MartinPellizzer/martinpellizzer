Carex vanheurckii subsp. crassispiculata

Carex vanheurckii subsp.

crassispiculata, commonly known as the broad-bristled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, fens, and along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are clustered and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust, bristly inflorescence and is often found in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
