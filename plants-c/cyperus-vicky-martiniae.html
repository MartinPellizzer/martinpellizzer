Cyperus vicky-martiniae

Cyperus vicky-martiniae, commonly known as the Vicky-Martinia sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
