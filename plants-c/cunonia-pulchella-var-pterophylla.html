Cunonia pulchella var. pterophylla

Cunonia pulchella var. pterophylla, commonly known as the golden cunonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The botanical description highlights its ornamental value and its adaptation to arid environments.
