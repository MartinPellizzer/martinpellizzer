Ceropegia sudanica

Ceropegia sudanica, commonly known as the Sudanese milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Ceropegia sudanica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
