Cactus pyrrhocephalus

Cactus pyrrhocephalus, commonly known as the red-headed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by bees and other insects. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The plant's unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its ecosystem.
