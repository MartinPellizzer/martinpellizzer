Carissa madagascariensis

Carissa madagascariensis, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
