Clarisia biflora subsp. mexicana

Clarisia biflora subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
