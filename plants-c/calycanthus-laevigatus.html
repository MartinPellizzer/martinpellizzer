Calycanthus laevigatus

Calycanthus laevigatus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically reddish-purple and have a unique, spicy scent. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth texture.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
