Chlorophytum camporum

Chlorophytum camporum, commonly known as the African spear lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a white midrib, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chlorophytum camporum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and indoor spaces.
