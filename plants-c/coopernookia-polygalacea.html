Coopernookia polygalacea

Coopernookia polygalacea, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, mist-like inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Coopernookia polygalacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
