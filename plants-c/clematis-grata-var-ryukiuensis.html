Clematis grata var. ryukiuensis

Clematis grata var. ryukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu Clematis, is a compact, deciduous vine native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, berry-like fruits that mature to a dark red color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
