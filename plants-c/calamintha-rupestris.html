Calamintha rupestris

Calamintha rupestris, commonly known as rock mint, is a perennial herb native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in herbal remedies.

Calamintha rupestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
