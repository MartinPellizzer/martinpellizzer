Campylocentrum hispidulum

Campylocentrum hispidulum, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom throughout the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
