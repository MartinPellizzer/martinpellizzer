Carduus platypus var. granatensis

Carduus platypus var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
