Calligonum pallasia

Calligonum pallasia, commonly known as the Pallas' spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Calligonum pallasia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.
