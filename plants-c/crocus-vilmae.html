Crocus vilmae

Crocus vilmae, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus vilmae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
