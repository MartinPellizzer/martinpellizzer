Chupalon capitatum

Chupalon capitatum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a dense, terminal inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
