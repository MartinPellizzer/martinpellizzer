Chenopodium nitrariaceum

Chenopodium nitrariaceum, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to dry environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Chenopodium nitrariaceum is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
