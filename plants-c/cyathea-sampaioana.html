Cyathea sampaioana

Cyathea sampaioana, commonly known as the Sampaio's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded, moist forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk, which is covered in a layer of dead fronds.

Cyathea sampaioana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
