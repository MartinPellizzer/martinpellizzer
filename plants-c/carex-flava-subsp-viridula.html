Carex flava subsp. viridula

Carex flava subsp.

viridula, commonly known as the greenish yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
