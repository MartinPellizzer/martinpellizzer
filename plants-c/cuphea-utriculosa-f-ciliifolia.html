Cuphea utriculosa f. ciliifolia

Cuphea utriculosa f. ciliifolia, commonly known as the ciliate cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract hummingbirds.
