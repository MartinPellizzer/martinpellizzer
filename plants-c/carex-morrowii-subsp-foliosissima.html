Carex morrowii subsp. foliosissima

Carex morrowii subsp.

foliosissima, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green to blue-green in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
