Chondropetalum mucronatum

Chondropetalum mucronatum, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
