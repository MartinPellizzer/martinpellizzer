Cyperus phaeolepis

Cyperus phaeolepis, commonly known as the black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its three-ranked leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Cyperus phaeolepis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to wet conditions.
