Convolvulus americanus

Convolvulus americanus, commonly known as the American bindweed, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has a robust root system and can spread aggressively through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
