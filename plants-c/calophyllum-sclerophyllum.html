Calophyllum sclerophyllum

Calophyllum sclerophyllum, commonly known as the rough-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
