Cytisus arboreus var. catalaunicus

Cytisus arboreus var. catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, though it can be invasive in some regions.
