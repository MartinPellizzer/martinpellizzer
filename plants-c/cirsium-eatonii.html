Cirsium eatonii

Cirsium eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Compositae family.

Cirsium eatonii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
