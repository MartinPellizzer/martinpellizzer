Cousinia chrysochlora

Cousinia chrysochlora, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Cousinia chrysochlora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to prevent soil erosion.
