Crataegus tomentosa

Crataegus tomentosa, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish down. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
