Cochlearia brevicaulis

Cochlearia brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found on sandy or rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
