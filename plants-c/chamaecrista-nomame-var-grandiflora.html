Chamaecrista nomame var. grandiflora

Chamaecrista nomame var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
