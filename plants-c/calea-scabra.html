Calea scabra

Calea scabra, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture.

Calea scabra is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
