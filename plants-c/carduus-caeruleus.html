Carduus caeruleus

Carduus caeruleus, commonly known as blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, blue or purple flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Carduus caeruleus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
