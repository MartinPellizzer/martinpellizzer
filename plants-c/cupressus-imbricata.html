Cupressus imbricata

Cupressus imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and dark green, scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

Cupressus imbricata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
