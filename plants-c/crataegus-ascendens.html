Crataegus ascendens

Crataegus ascendens, commonly known as the ascending hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
