Cyperus mutisii var. fuscisquamatus

Cyperus mutisii var. fuscisquamatus, commonly known as the dark-scaled flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in shallow water or saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a dense layer of fine, dark-colored scales, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.
