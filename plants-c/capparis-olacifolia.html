Capparis olacifolia

Capparis olacifolia, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit, which resembles a fig, is edible and has a tart, sour flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Capparis olacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
