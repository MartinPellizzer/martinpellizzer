Cytisus benehoavensis

Cytisus benehoavensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian firetree, is a shrub native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is considered an important part of Hawaii's native flora.
