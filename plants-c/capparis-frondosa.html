Capparis frondosa

Capparis frondosa, commonly known as the fronded caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, capsaicin-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
