Convolvulus massonii

Convolvulus massonii, commonly known as the Cape spinach, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are similar in taste to spinach. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Convolvulus massonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
