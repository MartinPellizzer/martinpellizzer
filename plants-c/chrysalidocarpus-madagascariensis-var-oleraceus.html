Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis var. oleraceus

Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis var. oleraceus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in carbohydrates and have been a staple food source in many Pacific island cultures. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, smooth bark, and a dense canopy that provides shade.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
