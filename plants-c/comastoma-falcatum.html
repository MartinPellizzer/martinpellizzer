Comastoma falcatum

Comastoma falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tansy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

Comastoma falcatum is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
