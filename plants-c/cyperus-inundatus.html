Cyperus inundatus

Cyperus inundatus, commonly known as the water grass or swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist or aquatic conditions.

Cyperus inundatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
