Cinchona pavonii

Cinchona pavonii, commonly known as the pink quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Cinchona pavonii is notable for its historical use in the production of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.
