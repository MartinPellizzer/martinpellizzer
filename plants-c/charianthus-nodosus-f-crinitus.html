Charianthus nodosus f. crinitus

Charianthus nodosus f. crinitus, commonly known as the hairy nodding thistle, is a variant of the nodding thistle characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, typically growing in dry, rocky soils. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. Its hairy texture is a notable botanical trait, contributing to its unique appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
