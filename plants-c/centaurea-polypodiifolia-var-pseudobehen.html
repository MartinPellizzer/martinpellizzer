Centaurea polypodiifolia var. pseudobehen

Centaurea polypodiifolia var. pseudobehen, commonly known as the False Bristlecone Centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in dense, spiky clusters called capitula. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
