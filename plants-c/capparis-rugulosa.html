Capparis rugulosa

Capparis rugulosa, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and has woody stems covered in rough, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, tangy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Capparis rugulosa is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
