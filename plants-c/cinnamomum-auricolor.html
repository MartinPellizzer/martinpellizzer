Cinnamomum auricolor

Cinnamomum auricolor, commonly known as golden cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known species like cinnamon and laurel. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
