Coriandrum tordylioides

Coriandrum tordylioides, commonly known as the Turkish coriander or tordylium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a delicate, peppery scent.
