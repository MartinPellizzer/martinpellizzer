Cucumis diniae

Cucumis diniae, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to arid regions of North America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, green, elongated fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in hot, dry environments.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, cucumber-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in desert agriculture.
