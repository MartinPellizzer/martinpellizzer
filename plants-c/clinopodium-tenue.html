Clinopodium tenue

Clinopodium tenue, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium tenue is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
