Chonemorpha cristata

Chonemorpha cristata, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, spiny stems that form a dense, bushy structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and often shed during periods of drought.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
