Cyperus atronitens

Cyperus atronitens, commonly known as black rush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Cyperus atronitens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
