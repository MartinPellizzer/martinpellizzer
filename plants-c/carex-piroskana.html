Carex piroskana

Carex piroskana, commonly known as the Hungarian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex piroskana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
