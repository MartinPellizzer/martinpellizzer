Cereus backebergii

Cereus backebergii, commonly known as the Backberg Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and flowering habits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
