Carex aethostachya

Carex aethostachya, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a silvery-blue hue, particularly on the undersides of the leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex aethostachya is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
