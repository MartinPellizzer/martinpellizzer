Cyperus paramatta

Cyperus paramatta, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Cyperus paramatta thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
