Ceratoides pungens

Ceratoides pungens, commonly known as the spiny cypress, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like structures that resemble horns, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its drought-resistant properties. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and extreme temperatures.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
