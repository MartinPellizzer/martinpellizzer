Campanula rotundifolia var. angustiflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded or heart-shaped, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. The flowers are bell-shaped, blue to purple in color, and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
