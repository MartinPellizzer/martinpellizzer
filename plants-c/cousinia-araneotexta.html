Cousinia araneotexta

Cousinia araneotexta, commonly known as the spider-web cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, and often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia araneotexta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
