Cinnamomum trichophyllum

Cinnamomum trichophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its aromatic bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.
