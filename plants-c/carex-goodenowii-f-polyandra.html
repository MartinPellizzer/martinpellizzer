Carex goodenowii f. polyandra

Carex goodenowii f. polyandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a variant of the Carex genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense vegetation.

This sedgeland species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
