Crotalaria villosa

Crotalaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

Crotalaria villosa is known for its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
