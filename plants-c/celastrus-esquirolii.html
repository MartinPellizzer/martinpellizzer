Celastrus esquirolii

Celastrus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in cymes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
