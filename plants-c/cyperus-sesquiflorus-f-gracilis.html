Cyperus sesquiflorus f. gracilis

Cyperus sesquiflorus f. gracilis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is distinguished by its more delicate and refined appearance compared to other forms of Cyperus sesquiflorus.
