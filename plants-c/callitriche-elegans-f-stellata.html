Callitriche elegans f. stellata

Callitriche elegans f. stellata, commonly known as star-shaped water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features small, delicate, star-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can form dense mats.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important species for water quality and biodiversity in aquatic habitats.
