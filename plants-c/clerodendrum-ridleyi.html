Clerodendrum ridleyi

Clerodendrum ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
