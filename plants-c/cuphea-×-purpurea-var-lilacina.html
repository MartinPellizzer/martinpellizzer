Cuphea × purpurea var. lilacina

Cuphea × purpurea var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac candytuft, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

It produces vibrant pink to lilac-colored bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, creating a striking visual display. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
