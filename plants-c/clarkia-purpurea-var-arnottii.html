Clarkia purpurea var. arnottii

Clarkia purpurea var. arnottii, commonly known as the Arnott's Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
