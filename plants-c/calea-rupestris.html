Calea rupestris

Calea rupestris, commonly known as the rock calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, sometimes fragrant, aroma.

Calea rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
