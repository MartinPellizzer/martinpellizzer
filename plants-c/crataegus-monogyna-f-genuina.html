Crataegus monogyna f. genuina

Crataegus monogyna f. genuina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a valued plant in both garden and natural settings.
