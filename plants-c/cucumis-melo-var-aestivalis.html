Cucumis melo var. aestivalis

Cucumis melo var. aestivalis, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
