Campanula guinochetii

Campanula guinochetii, commonly known as the Guinochet's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula guinochetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
