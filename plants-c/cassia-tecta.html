Cassia tecta

Cassia tecta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 15 meters tall. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cassia tecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and its wood is used in traditional crafts.
