Calamagrostis aleutica

Calamagrostis aleutica, commonly known as the Alaska grass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is well adapted to cold climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, feather-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis aleutica plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
