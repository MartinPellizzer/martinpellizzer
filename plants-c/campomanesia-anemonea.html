Campomanesia anemonea

Campomanesia anemonea, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
