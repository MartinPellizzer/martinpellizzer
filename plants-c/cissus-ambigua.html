Cissus ambigua

Cissus ambigua, commonly known as the creeping grape or devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus ambigua is also valued as a decorative plant due to its vigorous growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
