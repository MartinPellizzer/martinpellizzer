Cirsium × ciliatiforme

Cirsium × ciliatiforme, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, and often fuzzy leaves, which provide a protective mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
