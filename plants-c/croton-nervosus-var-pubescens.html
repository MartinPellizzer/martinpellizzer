Croton nervosus var. pubescens

Croton nervosus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features palmate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The hairy croton is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its striking leaf patterns and adaptability to various growing conditions.
