Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. penninervis

Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. penninervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined sandbroom, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
