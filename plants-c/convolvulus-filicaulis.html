Convolvulus filicaulis

Convolvulus filicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant has a climbing habit, often twining around supports, and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
