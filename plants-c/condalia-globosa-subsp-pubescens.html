Condalia globosa subsp. pubescens

Condalia globosa subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy globe tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its spherical, woody fruit capsules that are covered in short, stiff hairs.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
