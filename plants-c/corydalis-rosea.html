Corydalis rosea

Corydalis rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, spurred flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Corydalis rosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
