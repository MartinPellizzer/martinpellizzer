Cryptanthus cinereus

Cryptanthus cinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf cryptanthus, is a perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and covered in a fine, silvery coating, which gives it its common name.

Cryptanthus cinereus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
