Centaurea montana var. semidecurrens

Centaurea montana var. semidecurrens, commonly known as the Alpine blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, blue to purple bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-centered composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapting to high-altitude environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
