Centaurea alberti

Centaurea alberti, commonly known as Albert's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea alberti is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
