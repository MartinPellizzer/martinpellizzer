Cytisus purpureus var. incarnatus-major

Cytisus purpureus var. incarnatus-major, commonly known as the purple broom or scarlet broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
