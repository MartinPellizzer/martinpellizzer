Crotalaria harmsiana var. congoensis

Crotalaria harmsiana var. congoensis, commonly known as the Congo senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces yellow or orange flowers in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open upon maturity, dispersing the seeds effectively.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
