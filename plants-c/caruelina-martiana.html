Caruelina martiana

Caruelina martiana, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Caruelina martiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
