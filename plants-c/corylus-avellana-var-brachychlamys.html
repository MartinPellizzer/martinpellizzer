Corylus avellana var. brachychlamys

Corylus avellana var. brachychlamys, commonly known as the Hungarian hazel, is a variety of hazelnut native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, oval leaves that are typically hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
