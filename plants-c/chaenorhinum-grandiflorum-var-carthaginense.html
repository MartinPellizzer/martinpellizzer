Chaenorhinum grandiflorum var. carthaginense

Chaenorhinum grandiflorum var. carthaginense, commonly known as the Carthaginian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to violet. The plant has slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This annual species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
