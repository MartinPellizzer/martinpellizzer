Cucurbita pepo convar. pomiformis

Cucurbita pepo convar. pomiformis, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct, elongated, and often ridged fruit shape, which resembles an acorn. The plant produces large, yellow to orange-colored fruits with a thick, edible rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typically with a hairy surface.

This variety is commonly cultivated for its nutritional value and is a popular choice in both home gardens and commercial farming.
