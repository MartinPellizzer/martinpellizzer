Calamus marginatus

Calamus marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has long, slender rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of materials for crafts. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinctive margin that is slightly curled or toothed.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spikes, and are adapted for wind pollination.
