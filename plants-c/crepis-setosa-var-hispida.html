Crepis setosa var. hispida

Crepis setosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence is a single, erect stem with several flower heads.

The species is characterized by its distinctive pubescent (hairy) surface and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
