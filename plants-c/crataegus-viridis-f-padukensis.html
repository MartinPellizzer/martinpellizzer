Crataegus viridis f. padukensis

Crataegus viridis f. padukensis, commonly known as the green hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red, persistent berry that ripens in autumn. This form is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role.
