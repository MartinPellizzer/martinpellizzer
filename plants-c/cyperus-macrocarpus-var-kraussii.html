Cyperus macrocarpus var. kraussii

Cyperus macrocarpus var. kraussii, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-angled, spike-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
