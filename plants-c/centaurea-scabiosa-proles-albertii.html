Centaurea scabiosa proles albertii

Centaurea scabiosa proles albertii, commonly known as Albert's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and prominent central veins. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
