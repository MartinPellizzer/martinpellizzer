Cestrum alternifolium

Cestrum alternifolium, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

Cestrum alternifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
