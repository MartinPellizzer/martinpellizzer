Cuphea lanceolata f. rosea

Cuphea lanceolata f. rosea, commonly known as the roseate lanceleaf cuphea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, rose-pink bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its blooming period extends from summer to fall, adding color to gardens throughout the growing season.
