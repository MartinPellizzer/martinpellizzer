Canthium microphyllum

Canthium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of Central and South America.

Canthium microphyllum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
