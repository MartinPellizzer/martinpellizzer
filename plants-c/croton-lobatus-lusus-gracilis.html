Croton lobatus lusus gracilis

Croton lobatus lusus gracilis, commonly known as the slender croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often palmate leaves that are lobed and vary in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
