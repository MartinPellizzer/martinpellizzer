Capsella segetum

Capsella segetum, commonly known as field pennycress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, lobed, and hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The seeds of Capsella segetum are small and have a distinctive shape, making them easily dispersed by wind.
