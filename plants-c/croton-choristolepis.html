Croton choristolepis

Croton choristolepis, commonly known as the red-flowered croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from yellow to red, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage.
