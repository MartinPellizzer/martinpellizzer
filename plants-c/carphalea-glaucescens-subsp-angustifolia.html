Carphalea glaucescens subsp. angustifolia

Carphalea glaucescens subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in loose spikes and bloom in the summer months.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaf and flower morphology.
