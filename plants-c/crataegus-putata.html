Crataegus putata

Crataegus putata, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a small shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the autumn.

Crataegus putata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
