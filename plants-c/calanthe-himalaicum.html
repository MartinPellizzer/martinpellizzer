Calanthe himalaicum

Calanthe himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high elevations. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Calanthe himalaicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
