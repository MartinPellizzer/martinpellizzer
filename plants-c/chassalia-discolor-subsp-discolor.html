Chassalia discolor subsp. discolor

Chassalia discolor subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white chassalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
