Catappa mauritiana

Catappa mauritiana, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and long, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, triangular, red or orange fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and practical benefits.
