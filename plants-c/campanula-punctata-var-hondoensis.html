Campanula punctata var. hondoensis

Campanula punctata var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, often with white markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
