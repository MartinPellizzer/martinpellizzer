Cassia telfairiana

Cassia telfairiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain numerous flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.

Cassia telfairiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
