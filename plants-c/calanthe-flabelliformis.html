Calanthe flabelliformis

Calanthe flabelliformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb structure.

Calanthe flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
