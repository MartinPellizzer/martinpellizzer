Crepis kerneri

Crepis kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in spring, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
