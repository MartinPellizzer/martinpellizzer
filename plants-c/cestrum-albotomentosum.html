Cestrum albotomentosum

Cestrum albotomentosum, commonly known as the white-hairy trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, white hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
