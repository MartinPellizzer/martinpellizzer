Connarus fasciculatus subsp. pachyneurus

Connarus fasciculatus subsp.

pachyneurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved connarus, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
