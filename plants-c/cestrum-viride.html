Cestrum viride

Cestrum viride, commonly known as the green cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its fragrant, night-blooming flowers.

Cestrum viride is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
