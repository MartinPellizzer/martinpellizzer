Crotalaria tetragona

Crotalaria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant produces seed pods that are initially green but turn brown as they mature, containing several seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
