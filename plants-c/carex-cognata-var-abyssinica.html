Carex cognata var. abyssinica

Carex cognata var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
