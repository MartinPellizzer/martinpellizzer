Cynanchum antandroy

Cynanchum antandroy, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to Madagascar, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces distinctive flowers with a corolla that is typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a follicle containing toxic latex, which is a common feature in the genus Cynanchum.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native region.
