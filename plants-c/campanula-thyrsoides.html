Campanula thyrsoides

Campanula thyrsoides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
