Carex aphylla

Carex aphylla, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex aphylla plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
