Carex debeauxii

Carex debeauxii, commonly known as Beaux's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a triangular cross-section. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Beaux's sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
