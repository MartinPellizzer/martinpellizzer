Crotalaria goetzei

Crotalaria goetzei, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive rattle-like structure at the base of the petals. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria goetzei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
