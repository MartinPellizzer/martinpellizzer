Crataegus monogyna subsp. calycina

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of many ecosystems.
