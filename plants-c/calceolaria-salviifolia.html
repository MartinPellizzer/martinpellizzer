Calceolaria salviifolia

Calceolaria salviifolia, commonly known as the salvia-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Calceolaria salviifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique and colorful blooms.
