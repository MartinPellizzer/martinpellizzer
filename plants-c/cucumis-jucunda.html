Cucumis jucunda

Cucumis jucunda, commonly known as the "sweet cucumber," is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a small, vining plant that produces small, round, yellow-green fruits with a sweet, mild flavor. Native to the Canary Islands, it thrives in warm, dry climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruit. The plant has palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects.
