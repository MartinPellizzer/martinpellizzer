Cattleya × nesyana

Cattleya × nesyana, commonly known as the Orchid Queen, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of pink, red, and white, and a distinctive fragrance. It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in various conditions.

Cattleya × nesyana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
