Citrus albida

Citrus albida, commonly known as the white citrus or white limes, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and has a compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
