Clematis tubulosa

Clematis tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, tubular-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Clematis tubulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
