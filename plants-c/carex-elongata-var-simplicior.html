Carex elongata var. simplicior

Carex elongata var. simplicior, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
