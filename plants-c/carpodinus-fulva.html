Carpodinus fulva

Carpodinus fulva, commonly known as the golden star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its erect, triangular stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Carpodinus fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and water gardens.
