Cyperus gondanus

Cyperus gondanus, commonly known as the African sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in wetlands or moist soils.

Cyperus gondanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and water gardens.
