Centaurea pulcherrima

Centaurea pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Cape Flats star" or "red star flower," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Centaurea pulcherrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
