Cistus laurifolius subsp. laurifolius

Cistus laurifolius subsp.

laurifolius, commonly known as the laurel leaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and silver-white underneath.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
