Centaurea collina subsp. serratulifolia

Centaurea collina subsp.

serratulifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, toothed edge, and its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
