Cephalocereus hermentianus

Cephalocereus hermentianus, commonly known as the Hermentia cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces bright red fruits that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
