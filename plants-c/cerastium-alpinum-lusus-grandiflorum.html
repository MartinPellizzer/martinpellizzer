Cerastium alpinum lusus grandiflorum

Cerastium alpinum lusus grandiflorum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
