Coriflora beadlei

Coriflora beadlei, commonly known as the beadleaf coralplant, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bead-like, fleshy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
