Conostalix

Conostalix, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants. Conostalix relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Its ecological role includes providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
