Cattleya maxima

Cattleya maxima, commonly known as the orchid of the Amazon, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors including pink, white, and purple.

Cattleya maxima is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in horticulture.
