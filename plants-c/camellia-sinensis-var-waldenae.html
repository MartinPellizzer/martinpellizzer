Camellia sinensis var. waldenae

Camellia sinensis var. waldenae, commonly known as the Walden's tea plant, is a rare variant of the tea plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and agricultural research.
