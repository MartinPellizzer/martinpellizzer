Caladenia saxatilis

Caladenia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, resupinate (upside-down) flower with a distinctive, often pink or purple hue. Its floral structure is characteristic of the orchid family, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the labellum being highly specialized.
