Croton tenuissimus

Croton tenuissimus, commonly known as the "Flossflower" or "Velvetleaf," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
