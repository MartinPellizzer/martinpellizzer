Choisya dumosa

Choisya dumosa, commonly known as Mexican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, coral-pink berries that appear in late summer and persist through winter. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its vibrant fruits. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Choisya dumosa is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ornamental appeal in both residential and public spaces.
