Cassia belemii var. paludicola

Cassia belemii var. paludicola, commonly known as the swamp cassia, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often waterlogged stems and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
