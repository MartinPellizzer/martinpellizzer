Chromolaena hirsuta

Chromolaena hirsuta, commonly known as hirsute chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges due to its adaptability.

Chromolaena hirsuta is also known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in certain regions.
