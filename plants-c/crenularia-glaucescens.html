Crenularia glaucescens

Crenularia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features erect, slender stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually glaucous, giving the plant its characteristic blue-green hue. The flowers are small, pinkish-white, and arranged in dense, terminal clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
