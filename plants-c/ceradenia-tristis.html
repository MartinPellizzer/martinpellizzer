Ceradenia tristis

Ceradenia tristis, commonly known as the weeping orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that hang like tears from its stems. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Ceradenia tristis is considered a rare and endangered species.
