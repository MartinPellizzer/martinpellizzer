Cassia chinensis

Cassia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a spice.

Cassia chinensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
