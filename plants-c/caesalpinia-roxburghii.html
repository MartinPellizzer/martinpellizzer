Caesalpinia roxburghii

Caesalpinia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom prominently during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically bisexual, with five petals. It produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia roxburghii is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
