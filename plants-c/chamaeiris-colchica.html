Chamaeiris colchica

Chamaeiris colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian colchic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high altitudes.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
