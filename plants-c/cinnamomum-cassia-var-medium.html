Cinnamomum cassia var. medium

Cinnamomum cassia var. medium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is harvested for its essential oils and medicinal properties. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red or orange berries containing winged seeds.

This variety is widely cultivated for its bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
