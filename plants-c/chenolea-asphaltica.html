Chenolea asphaltica

Chenolea asphaltica, commonly known as the asphaltic chenolea, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its unusual adaptation to extreme environments.

It thrives in areas with high levels of asphalt and heavy metals, showcasing a remarkable ability to tolerate and even utilize these substances for growth. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, which helps in reducing water loss in its harsh habitat. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to absorb nutrients from contaminated soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Chenolea asphaltica is considered a subject of scientific interest in the study of extremophile plants.
