Coreopsis boykiniana

Coreopsis boykiniana, commonly known as the Boykin's coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Coreopsis boykiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to support local pollinators.
