Cissus neilgherrensis

Cissus neilgherrensis, commonly known as the Himalayan money plant, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
