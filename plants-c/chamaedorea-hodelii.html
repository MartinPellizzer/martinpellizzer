Chamaedorea hodelii

Chamaedorea hodelii, commonly known as the Hodel's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular, dark blue-black fruits. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Chamaedorea hodelii is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is often used in interior landscaping.
