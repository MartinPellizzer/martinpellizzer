Cupressus lusitanica subsp. mexicana

Cupressus lusitanica subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican lacebark cypress, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scaly, reddish-brown bark.

The foliage consists of small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
