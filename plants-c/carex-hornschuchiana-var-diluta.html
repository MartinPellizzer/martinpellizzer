Carex hornschuchiana var. diluta

Carex hornschuchiana var. diluta, commonly known as the slender bulbous sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
