Clinopodium campanella

Clinopodium campanella, commonly known as blue mountain betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters. Clinopodium campanella typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a valuable plant for pollinators.
