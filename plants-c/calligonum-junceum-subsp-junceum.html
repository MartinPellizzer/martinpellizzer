Calligonum junceum subsp. junceum

Calligonum junceum subsp.

junceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf jointfoot, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its growth form and ecological role make it an important component of local plant communities in its range.
