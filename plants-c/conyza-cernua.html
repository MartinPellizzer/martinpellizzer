Conyza cernua

Conyza cernua, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white composite flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces numerous seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
