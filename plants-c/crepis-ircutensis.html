Crepis ircutensis

Crepis ircutensis, commonly known as the Irkutsk hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the cold regions of Siberia and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it has a low-growing habit.

Crepis ircutensis is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in cool climates.
