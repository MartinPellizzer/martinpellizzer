Centella tridentata var. dregeana

Centella tridentata var. dregeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf centella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
