Cistus × nigricans var. grosii

Cistus × nigricans var. grosii, commonly known as the dark-flowered rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of dark purple to almost black flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
