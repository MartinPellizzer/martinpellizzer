Crotalaria monteiroi var. galpinii

Crotalaria monteiroi var. galpinii, commonly known as Monteiro's crotalaria, is a flowering plant species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its botanical features include a bushy growth habit and the presence of nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots, which contribute to soil fertility.
