Carmichaelia petriei var. minor

Carmichaelia petriei var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered Carmichaelia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in the lower South Island and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
