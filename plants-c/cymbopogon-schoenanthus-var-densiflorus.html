Cymbopogon schoenanthus var. densiflorus

Cymbopogon schoenanthus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, fragrant leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic essential oils, which are derived from the leaves and used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
