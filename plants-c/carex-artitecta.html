Carex artitecta

Carex artitecta, commonly known as the architectural sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed, giving it a structured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a loose panicle.

Carex artitecta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist to wet soils.
