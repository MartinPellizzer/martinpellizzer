Chrysanthemum roxburghii

Chrysanthemum roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow to orange petals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Chrysanthemum roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
