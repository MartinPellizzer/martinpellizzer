Carex dissitispicula

Carex dissitispicula, commonly known as the spreading bracted sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in shades of brown or purple, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is native to parts of North America.

Carex dissitispicula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
