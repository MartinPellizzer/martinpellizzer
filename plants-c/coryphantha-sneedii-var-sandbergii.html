Coryphantha sneedii var. sandbergii

Coryphantha sneedii var. sandbergii, commonly known as the Sandberg's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with pale green to bluish-gray stems that are covered in spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It has adapted to arid environments with deep roots and succulent stems for water storage.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local desert ecosystem.
