Calamagrostis moupinensis

Calamagrostis moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-haired beneath. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Calamagrostis moupinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
