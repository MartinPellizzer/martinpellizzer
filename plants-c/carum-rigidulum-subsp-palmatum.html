Carum rigidulum subsp. palmatum

Carum rigidulum subsp.

palmatum, commonly known as Japanese parsley or mountain caraway, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its aromatic foliage and seeds are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.
