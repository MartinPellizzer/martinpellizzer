Cistus × olbiensis

Cistus × olbiensis, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a cross between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspicensis, resulting in a plant with distinctive features. The species produces fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Cistus × olbiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
