Crepis taraxacum

Crepis taraxacum, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
