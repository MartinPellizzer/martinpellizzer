Cardamine heterophylla var. macrostylis

Cardamine heterophylla var. macrostylis, commonly known as the broad-styled water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its flowers have distinctive, broad stamens that give it its varietal name, macrostylis.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
