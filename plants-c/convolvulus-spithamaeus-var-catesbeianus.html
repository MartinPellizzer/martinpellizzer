Convolvulus spithamaeus var. catesbeianus

Convolvulus spithamaeus var. catesbeianus, commonly known as the Catesby's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing and trailing plants.

This variant is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
