Cyperus tristis

Cyperus tristis, commonly known as the sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Cyperus tristis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
