Cordia globifera

Cordia globifera, commonly known as the globe tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, globe-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions, making it adaptable to coastal environments. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Cordia globifera is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
