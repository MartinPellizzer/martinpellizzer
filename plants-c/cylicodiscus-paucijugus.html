Cylicodiscus paucijugus

Cylicodiscus paucijugus, commonly known as the slender-leaf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and feather-like, pinnate leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its reproductive structures include large, cone-like inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
