Cyphostemma masukuense subsp. masukuense

Cyphostemma masukuense subsp.

masukuense, commonly known as the Namibian moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that mature in late summer.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
