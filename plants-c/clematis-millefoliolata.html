Clematis millefoliolata

Clematis millefoliolata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with finely divided, feathery foliage that resembles lace, giving it its scientific name "millefoliolata," which translates to "thousand leaflets.". The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis millefoliolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
