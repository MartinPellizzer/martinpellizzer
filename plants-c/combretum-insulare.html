Combretum insulare

Combretum insulare, commonly known as the island flame bush, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental species.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
