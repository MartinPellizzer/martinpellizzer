Campanula spatulata f. lepidota

Campanula spatulata f. lepidota, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features hairy, often velvety foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "lepidota," meaning scaly or covered in small scales. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
