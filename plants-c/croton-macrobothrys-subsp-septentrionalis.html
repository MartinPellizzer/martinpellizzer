Croton macrobothrys subsp. septentrionalis

Croton macrobothrys subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern croton, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscaped gardens.
