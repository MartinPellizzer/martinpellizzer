Codonopsis mollis

Codonopsis mollis, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. Its tuberous roots are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Codonopsis mollis thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
