Conophytum multicolor

Conophytum multicolor, commonly known as the "Rainbow Stone" or "Cape Plaster," is a member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a small, succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ability to survive prolonged droughts.

Conophytum multicolor is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
