Crataegus sanguinea

Crataegus sanguinea, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Crataegus sanguinea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
