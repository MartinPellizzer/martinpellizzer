Ceropegia zambesiaca

Ceropegia zambesiaca, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is popular as a houseplant and for rock garden displays.
