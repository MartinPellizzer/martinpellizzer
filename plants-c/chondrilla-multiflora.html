Chondrilla multiflora

Chondrilla multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered goatweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.

Chondrilla multiflora is also known for its ability to accumulate minerals, making it a potential indicator plant for certain soil types.
