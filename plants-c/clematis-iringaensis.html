Clematis iringaensis

Clematis iringaensis, commonly known as the East African Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Clematis iringaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
