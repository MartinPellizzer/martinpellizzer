Campanula rhomboidalis f. rubra

Campanula rhomboidalis f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright stems adorned with clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a striking red or pinkish-red in color. The foliage consists of oval, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
