Caryopteris incana var. superba

Caryopteris incana var. superba, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
