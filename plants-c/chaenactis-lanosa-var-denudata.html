Chaenactis lanosa var. denudata

Chaenactis lanosa var. denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and basal rosette growth habit. The plant features soft, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its small white to yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is typically found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
