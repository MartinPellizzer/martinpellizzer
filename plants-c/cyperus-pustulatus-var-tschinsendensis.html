Cyperus pustulatus var. tschinsendensis

Cyperus pustulatus var. tschinsendensis, commonly known as the African bulbous sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and upright, grass-like stems that produce small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, while the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens and bog landscapes.
