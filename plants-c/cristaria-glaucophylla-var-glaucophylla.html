Cristaria glaucophylla var. glaucophylla

Cristaria glaucophylla var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla variant of Cristaria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
