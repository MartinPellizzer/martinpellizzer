Crocus vernus var. purpureus

Crocus vernus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers with a distinctive, three-lobed petal structure. Its corms are typically small and white, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring display.
