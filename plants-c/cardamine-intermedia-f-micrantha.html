Cardamine intermedia f. micrantha

Cardamine intermedia f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Its botanical features include stipules and a distinct, elongated stem with a hairy surface.
