Campanula hercegovina subf. longicalycina

Campanula hercegovina subf.

longicalycina, commonly known as the long calyx bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features elongated calyces that are often mistaken for petals, giving it its common name. Native to the Balkan Peninsula, it thrives in woodland and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardeners interested in native flora.
