Carapa gogo

Carapa gogo, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Carapa gogo is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
