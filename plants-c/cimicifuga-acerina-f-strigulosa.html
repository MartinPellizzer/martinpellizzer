Cimicifuga acerina f. strigulosa

Cimicifuga acerina f. strigulosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bugbane, is a striking perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, lacecap flowers at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a dark green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
