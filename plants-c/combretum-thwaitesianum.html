Combretum thwaitesianum

Combretum thwaitesianum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
