Crambe paniculata

Crambe paniculata, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Crambe paniculata is often found in dry meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
