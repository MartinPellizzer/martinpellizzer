Clerodendrum philippinum f. subfertile

Clerodendrum philippinum f. subfertile, commonly known as the Philippine flame bush, is a variant of the Clerodendrum philippinum species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange bracts that resemble flowers, though it produces fewer actual flowers compared to the typical form. The plant is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly pubescent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in its native region.
