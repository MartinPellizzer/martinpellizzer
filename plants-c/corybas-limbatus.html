Corybas limbatus

Corybas limbatus, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-like flower that is typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. It produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is pollinated by flies.

Corybas limbatus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its distinctive morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
