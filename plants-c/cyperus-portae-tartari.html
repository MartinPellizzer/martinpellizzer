Cyperus portae-tartari

Cyperus portae-tartari, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus portae-tartari is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
