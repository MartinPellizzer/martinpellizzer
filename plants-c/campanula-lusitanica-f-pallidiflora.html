Campanula lusitanica f. pallidiflora

Campanula lusitanica f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bellflower, is a rare and attractive perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
