Cinnamomum oroi

Cinnamomum oroi, commonly known as the Orito cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, dark blue berries that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum oroi is also noted for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
