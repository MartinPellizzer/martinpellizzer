Chirita heucherifolia

Chirita heucherifolia, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slight purplish tinge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners who appreciate its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
