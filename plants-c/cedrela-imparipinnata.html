Cedrela imparipinnata

Cedrela imparipinnata, commonly known as the yellowheart tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are odd-pinnate and have a distinctive yellowish hue when mature. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its heartwood is highly valued for its durability and rich golden color, making it a sought-after timber species.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered a threatened species in many regions.
