Cereus rosaceus

Cereus rosaceus, commonly known as the rose cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths.

Cereus rosaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
