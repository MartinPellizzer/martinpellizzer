Caladenia × ornata

Caladenia × ornata, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with elongated, hairy petals. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually golden or yellowish in color, with a distinctive, narrow labellum that resembles a spider's web.

Caladenia × ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and delicate beauty.
