Claoxylon welwitschianum

Claoxylon welwitschianum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and long, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered an endangered species.
