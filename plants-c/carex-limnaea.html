Carex limnaea

Carex limnaea, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Carex limnaea plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
