Convolvulus galpinii

Convolvulus galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heliotropic flowers that follow the sun's movement. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of yellow to orange, often with a distinctive purple throat. Its vines can climb or trail, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
