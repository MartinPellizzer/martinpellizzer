Cyperus boreohemisphaericus

Cyperus boreohemisphaericus, commonly known as the northern hemisphere sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Cyperus boreohemisphaericus plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
