Carex oligocarya

Carex oligocarya, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex oligocarya produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
