Camellia rosthorniana

Camellia rosthorniana, commonly known as the Rosthorn's camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub that produces striking, double-flowered blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. Camellia rosthorniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is also noted for its cold hardiness, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
