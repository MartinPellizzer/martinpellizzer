Centaurea paniculata subsp. urgelensis

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

urgelensis, commonly known as the Urgelean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers, which are surrounded by pale blue to purple bracts. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
