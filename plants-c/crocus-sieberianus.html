Crocus sieberianus

Crocus sieberianus, commonly known as the Sieber's crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its corms are adapted for survival in cold climates, allowing it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
