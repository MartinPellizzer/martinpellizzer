Cirsium periacanthaceum

Cirsium periacanthaceum, commonly known as the thistle of the sea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cirsium periacanthaceum is often found in dune ecosystems, playing a role in stabilizing coastal environments.
