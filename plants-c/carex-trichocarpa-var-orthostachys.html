Carex trichocarpa var. orthostachys

Carex trichocarpa var. orthostachys, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet to mesic environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
