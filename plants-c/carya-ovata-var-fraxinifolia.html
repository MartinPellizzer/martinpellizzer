Carya ovata var. fraxinifolia

Carya ovata var. fraxinifolia, commonly known as the black hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its dark, deeply furrowed bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible, sweet, chestnut-like nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
