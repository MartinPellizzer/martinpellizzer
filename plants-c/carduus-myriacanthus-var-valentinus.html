Carduus myriacanthus var. valentinus

Carduus myriacanthus var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's thistle, is a variant of the common thistle with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust stem and dense foliage. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
