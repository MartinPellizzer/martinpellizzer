Clematis orientalis f. persoonii

Clematis orientalis f. persoonii, commonly known as the Persian clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. It is well-suited for growing in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
