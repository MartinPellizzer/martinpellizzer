Coronilla coronata f. multiflora

Coronilla coronata f. multiflora, commonly known as the multibloom crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
