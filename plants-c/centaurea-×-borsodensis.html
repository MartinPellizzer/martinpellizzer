Centaurea × borsodensis

Centaurea × borsodensis, commonly known as the Hungarian knapweed, is a hybrid species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are typically surrounded by pale yellow to white bracts. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in open fields. It has a bushy growth habit with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
