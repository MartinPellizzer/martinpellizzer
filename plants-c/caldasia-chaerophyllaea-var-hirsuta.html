Caldasia chaerophyllaea var. hirsuta

Caldasia chaerophyllaea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy caldasia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
