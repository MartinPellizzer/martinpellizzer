Campanula rhomboidalis var. calycina

Campanula rhomboidalis var. calycina, commonly known as the Calycina bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
