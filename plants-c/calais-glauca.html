Calais glauca

Calais glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Calais glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
