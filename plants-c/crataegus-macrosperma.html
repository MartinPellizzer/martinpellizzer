Crataegus macrosperma

Crataegus macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
