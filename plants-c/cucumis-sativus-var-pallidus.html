Cucumis sativus var. pallidus

Cucumis sativus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its lighter green coloration compared to typical cucumber varieties. The plant produces elongated, thin fruits with a mild flavor and reduced bitterness. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and use in specialty culinary applications.
