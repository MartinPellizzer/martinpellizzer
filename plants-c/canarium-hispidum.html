Canarium hispidum

Canarium hispidum, commonly known as the rough-pear tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces a fruit that is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
