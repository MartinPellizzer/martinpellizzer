Croton serratus var. genuinus

Croton serratus var. genuinus, commonly known as the serrated croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique leaf venation patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.
