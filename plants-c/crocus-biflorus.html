Crocus biflorus

Crocus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers consist of six petal-like segments, with two prominent stamens and a three-lobed stigma.

Crocus biflorus is often found in rocky soils and open woodland areas, thriving in well-drained conditions.
