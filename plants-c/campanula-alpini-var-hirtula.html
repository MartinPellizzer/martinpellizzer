Campanula alpini var. hirtula

Campanula alpini var. hirtula, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and lanceolate, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
