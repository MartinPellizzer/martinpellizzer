Calceolaria palustris subsp. pennatipartita

Calceolaria palustris subsp.

pennatipartita, commonly known as the marsh calceolaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in South America. It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
