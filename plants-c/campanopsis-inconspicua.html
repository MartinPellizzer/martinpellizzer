Campanopsis inconspicua

Campanopsis inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It is relatively rare and has limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanical studies.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance contribute to its importance in conservation efforts.
