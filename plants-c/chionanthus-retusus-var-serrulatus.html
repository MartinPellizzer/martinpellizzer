Chionanthus retusus var. serrulatus

Chionanthus retusus var. serrulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white, lantern-shaped flowers that appear in late spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its specific epithet "serrulatus.". It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
