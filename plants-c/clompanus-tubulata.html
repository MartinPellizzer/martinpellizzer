Clompanus tubulata

Clompanus tubulata, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
