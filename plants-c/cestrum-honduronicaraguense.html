Cestrum honduronicaraguense

Cestrum honduronicaraguense, commonly known as the Honduran bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually white or pale blue.

Cestrum honduronicaraguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
