Crinitaria cretica

Crinitaria cretica, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in bundles of five. It produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain its seeds.

This evergreen tree is adapted to the dry, coastal climates of its native habitat.
