Cyrtoceras floribundum

Cyrtoceras floribundum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Cyrtoceras floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
