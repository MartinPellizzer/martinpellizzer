Commelina communis f. ciliata

Commelina communis f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with hairy margins, which give it its specific epithet "ciliata.". It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance plant.
