Caralluma europaea

Caralluma europaea, commonly known as the European nightshade or European torchroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, which helps it retain moisture in dry climates.

Caralluma europaea is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
