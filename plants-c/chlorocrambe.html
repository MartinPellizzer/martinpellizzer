Chlorocrambe

Chlorocrambe, commonly known as the yellow bladderwrack, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its yellow flowers and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant is native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in saline and sandy soils. Its unique morphology includes small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Chlorocrambe plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small organisms.
