Crucifera latifolia

Crucifera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Its presence can indicate soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
