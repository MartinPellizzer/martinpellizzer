Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp. austriacus

Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp.

austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to the Alps of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
