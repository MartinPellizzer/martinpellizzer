Clematis melanonema

Clematis melanonema, commonly known as blackstem clematis, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, dark-colored hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis melanonema is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
