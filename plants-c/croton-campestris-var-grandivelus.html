Croton campestris var. grandivelus

Croton campestris var. grandivelus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of milky sap, which is common in the Croton genus.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
