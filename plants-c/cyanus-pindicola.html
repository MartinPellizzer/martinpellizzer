Cyanus pindicola

Cyanus pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Cyanus pindicola is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
