Ceratostylis spathulata

Ceratostylis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a distinctive, spurred lip.

Ceratostylis spathulata is notable for its ability to form dense colonies and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
