Caraipa grandifolia subsp. grandifolia

Caraipa grandifolia subsp.

grandifolia, commonly known as the Caraipa rose, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
