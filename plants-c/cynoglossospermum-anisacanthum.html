Cynoglossospermum anisacanthum

Cynoglossospermum anisacanthum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
