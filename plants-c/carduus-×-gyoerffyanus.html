Carduus × gyoerffyanus

Carduus × gyoerffyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus benedictus and Carduus maximus.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various parts of Europe and the Middle East. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The flowers are usually purple to pink in color and bloom in the summer months.
