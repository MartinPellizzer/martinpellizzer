Cassia artemisioides var. eremophila

Cassia artemisioides var. eremophila, commonly known as desert cassia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. It produces elongated seed pods that contain numerous small, winged seeds.

Cassia artemisioides var. eremophila is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
