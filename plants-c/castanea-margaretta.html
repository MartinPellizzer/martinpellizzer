Castanea margaretta

Castanea margaretta, commonly known as the Margaretta chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, similar to other chestnut species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The Margaretta chestnut is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in forest ecosystems.
