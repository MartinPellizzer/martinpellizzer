Carex sarawaketensis

Carex sarawaketensis, commonly known as the Sarawak sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Malaysian state of Sarawak. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
