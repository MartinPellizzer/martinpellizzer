Corylopsis cordata

Corylopsis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf corylopsis, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide early seasonal color.

Corylopsis cordata is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
