Carex × schmidtiana

Carex × schmidtiana, commonly known as the Schmidt's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × schmidtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
