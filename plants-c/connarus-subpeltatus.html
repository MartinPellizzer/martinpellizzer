Connarus subpeltatus

Connarus subpeltatus, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Due to its valuable timber, it is often threatened by overharvesting and habitat loss.
