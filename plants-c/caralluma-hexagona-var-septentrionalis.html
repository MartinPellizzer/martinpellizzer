Caralluma hexagona var. septentrionalis

Caralluma hexagona var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern Candelabra Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its branching structure, with multiple stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
