Cinnamomum pittieri

Cinnamomum pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon or *Cinnamomum pittieri*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
