Cirsium hispanicum

Cirsium hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Cirsium hispanicum is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
