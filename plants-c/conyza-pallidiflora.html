Conyza pallidiflora

Conyza pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Conyza pallidiflora is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds.
