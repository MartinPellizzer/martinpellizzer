Calamus kandariensis

Calamus kandariensis, commonly known as the Kandari reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clusters of edible, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for crafting purposes due to their durability and flexibility.

Calamus kandariensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forests or near water bodies.
