Capparis fascicularis

Capparis fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a rough texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
