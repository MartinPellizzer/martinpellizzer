Cardamine duraniensis

Cardamine duraniensis, commonly known as the Duran's watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.
