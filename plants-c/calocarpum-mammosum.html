Calocarpum mammosum

Calocarpum mammosum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and slightly soft.

The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous, starchy pulp, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
