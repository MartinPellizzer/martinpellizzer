Caesalpinia benguetensis

Caesalpinia benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
