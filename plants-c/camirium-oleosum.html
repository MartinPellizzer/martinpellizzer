Camirium oleosum

Camirium oleosum, commonly known as the golden oil plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and waxy, glossy leaves. The plant produces a valuable essential oil, which is used in aromatherapy and cosmetics. Camirium oleosum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically significant.
