Carex grallatoria var. grallatoria

Carex grallatoria var. grallatoria, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
