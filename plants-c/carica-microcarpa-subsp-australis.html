Carica microcarpa subsp. australis

Carica microcarpa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian papaw, is a tropical plant native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small papayas. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
