Cephalanthus chinensis

Cephalanthus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of small, violet-blue berries that mature in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn foliage.
