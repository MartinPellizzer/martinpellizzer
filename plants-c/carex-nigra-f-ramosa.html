Carex nigra f. ramosa

Carex nigra f. ramosa, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
