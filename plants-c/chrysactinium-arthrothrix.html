Chrysactinium arthrothrix

Chrysactinium arthrothrix, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for leaves, a feature that contributes to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
