Capparis spinosa subsp. aegyptia

Capparis spinosa subsp.

aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean and parts of North Africa. It features woody stems with thorny branches and produces small, white flowers that are followed by green, spherical fruits.

The plant is characterized by its pungent, edible leaves and fruits, which are used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves and a distinctive, tangy flavor that sets it apart from other caper species.
