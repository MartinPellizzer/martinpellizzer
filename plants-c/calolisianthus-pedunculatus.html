Calolisianthus pedunculatus

Calolisianthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical structure of Calolisianthus pedunculatus includes long, slender stems and broad, strap-like leaves.
