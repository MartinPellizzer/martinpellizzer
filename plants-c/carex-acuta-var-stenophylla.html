Carex acuta var. stenophylla

Carex acuta var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-stamened flowers.

Carex acuta var. stenophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
