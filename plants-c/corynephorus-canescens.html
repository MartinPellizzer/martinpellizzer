Corynephorus canescens

Corynephorus canescens, commonly known as the silver grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Corynephorus canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
