Cucumis melo var. endaurantius

Cucumis melo var. endaurantius, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruit, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for trellising or ground cultivation. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns from green to yellow, indicating maturity.
