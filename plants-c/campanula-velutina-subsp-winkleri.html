Campanula velutina subsp. winkleri

Campanula velutina subsp.

winkleri, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
