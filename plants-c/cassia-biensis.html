Cassia biensis

Cassia biensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically compound with multiple leaflets. Cassia biensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
