Calamintha umbrosa var. sachalinensis

Calamintha umbrosa var. sachalinensis, commonly known as Siberian germander, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members such as mint and lavender.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable garden plant.
