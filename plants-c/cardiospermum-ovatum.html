Cardiospermum ovatum

Cardiospermum ovatum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental elements in gardens.

Its growth habit includes trailing or climbing vines, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
