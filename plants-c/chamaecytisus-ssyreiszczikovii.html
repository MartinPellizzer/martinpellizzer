Chamaecytisus ssyreiszczikovii

Chamaecytisus ssyreiszczikovii, commonly known as the Ssyreiszczikov's rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, thorny stems that are often prostrate or scrambling, adapting to rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing some drought tolerance.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
