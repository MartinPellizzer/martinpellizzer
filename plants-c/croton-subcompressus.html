Croton subcompressus

Croton subcompressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
