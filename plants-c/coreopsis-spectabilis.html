Coreopsis spectabilis

Coreopsis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis spectabilis is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
