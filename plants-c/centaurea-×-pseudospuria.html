Centaurea × pseudospuria

Centaurea × pseudospuria, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (common cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (woollyhead), resulting in a plant with distinctive blue to purple flowers. The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and foliage that ranges from gray-green to blue-gray. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
