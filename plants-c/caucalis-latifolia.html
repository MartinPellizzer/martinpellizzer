Caucalis latifolia

Caucalis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf caucalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Caucalis latifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to partial shade.
