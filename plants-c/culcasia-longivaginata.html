Culcasia longivaginata

Culcasia longivaginata, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and swamps. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
