Capsicum ramosissimum

Capsicum ramosissimum, commonly known as the bird pepper or yellow bird pepper, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple branches and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild to moderate heat. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in herbal medicine in some regions.
