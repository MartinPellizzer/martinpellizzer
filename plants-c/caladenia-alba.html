Caladenia alba

Caladenia alba, commonly known as the White Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single, showy white flower that has a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly flies, which are attracted by its pale color and scent.

Caladenia alba is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its unique and elegant floral display.
