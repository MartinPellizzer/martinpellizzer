Commelina guaranitica

Commelina guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features bright blue or violet flowers with white markings, typically blooming in the early morning. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Commelina guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
