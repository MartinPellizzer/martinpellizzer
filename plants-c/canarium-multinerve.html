Canarium multinerve

Canarium multinerve, commonly known as the Indian cheese tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, cheese-like fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
