Cupressus arizonica subsp. revealiana

Cupressus arizonica subsp.

revealiana, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive, aromatic resinous scent.

The tree typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its cones are small and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
