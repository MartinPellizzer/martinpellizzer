Croton tenuilobus

Croton tenuilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
