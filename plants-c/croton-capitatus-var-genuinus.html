Croton capitatus var. genuinus

Croton capitatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Hawaiian croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or bronze coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
