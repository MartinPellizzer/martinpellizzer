Cymbalaria muralis subsp. pubescens

Cymbalaria muralis subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wallgecko or hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls, paths, and in urban environments.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ability to colonize various habitats, making it a common sight in many regions.
