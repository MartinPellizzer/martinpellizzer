Charlwoodia

Charlwoodia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in winter, giving it a striking appearance against the cold climate. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Charlwoodia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and provide habitat for wildlife.
