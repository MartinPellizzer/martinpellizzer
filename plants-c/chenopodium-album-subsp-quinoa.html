Chenopodium album subsp. quinoa

Chenopodium album subsp.

quinoa, commonly known as quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andes region of South America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant produces small, edible seeds that are rich in protein, fiber, and essential amino acids. Quinoa thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both traditional and modern agriculture.
