Callicarpa basitruncata

Callicarpa basitruncata, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant purple to red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
