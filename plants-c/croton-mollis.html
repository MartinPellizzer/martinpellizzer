Croton mollis

Croton mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red capsules containing numerous seeds. Croton mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
