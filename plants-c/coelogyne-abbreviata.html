Coelogyne abbreviata

Coelogyne abbreviata, commonly known as the abbreviated coelogyne, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Coelogyne abbreviata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
