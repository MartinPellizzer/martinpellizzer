Cirsium ligulare var. albanum

Cirsium ligulare var. albanum, commonly known as the Albanian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. Native to the Balkan Peninsula, this variant thrives in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant's distinctive features include its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to produce a large number of seeds.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides in its native range.
