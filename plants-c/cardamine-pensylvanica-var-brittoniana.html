Cardamine pensylvanica var. brittoniana

Cardamine pensylvanica var. brittoniana, commonly known as Britton's canadian toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has trifoliate leaves with a distinct, tooth-like appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five sepals and numerous stamens.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Edward Britton, reflecting its historical significance in botanical studies.
