Crataegus uniflora

Crataegus uniflora, commonly known as the singleflower hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
