Chaetotropis rioplatensis

Chaetotropis rioplatensis, commonly known as the Rio Platense burr.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit that resembles a burr, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
