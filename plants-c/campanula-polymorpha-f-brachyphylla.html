Campanula polymorpha f. brachyphylla

Campanula polymorpha f. brachyphylla, commonly known as the compact bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its dense appearance. This variety is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
