Cuphea squamuligera

Cuphea squamuligera, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cuphea squamuligera is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
