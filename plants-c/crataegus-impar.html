Crataegus impar

Crataegus impar, commonly known as the five-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically five-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often developing fissures with age.

Crataegus impar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
