Croton brachiatus

Croton brachiatus, commonly known as the branched croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton brachiatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and adaptability to various growing conditions.
