Cyperus monti var. stylosus

Cyperus monti var. stylosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
