Calamintha × planasii

Calamintha × planasii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, pale blue to violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the upper stem, and it blooms from June to August.

Calamintha × planasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and delicate floral display.
