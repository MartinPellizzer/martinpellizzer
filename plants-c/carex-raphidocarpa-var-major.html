Carex raphidocarpa var. major

Carex raphidocarpa var. major, commonly known as the giant beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive spikelets that are often reddish or brownish in color, and it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
