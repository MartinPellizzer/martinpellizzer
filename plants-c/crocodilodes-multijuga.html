Crocodilodes multijuga

Crocodilodes multijuga, commonly known as the multi-jugate crocodile plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crocodile-like appearance, with elongated, ridged leaves that resemble the scales of a reptile. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces fruit that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
