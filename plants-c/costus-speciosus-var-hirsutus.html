Costus speciosus var. hirsutus

Costus speciosus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
