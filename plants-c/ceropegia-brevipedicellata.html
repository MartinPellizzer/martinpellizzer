Ceropegia brevipedicellata

Ceropegia brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
