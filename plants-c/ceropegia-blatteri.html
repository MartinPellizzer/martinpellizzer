Ceropegia blatteri

Ceropegia blatteri, commonly known as the Blatter's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, green, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are actually modified stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia blatteri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
