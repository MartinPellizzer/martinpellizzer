Crataegus macracantha var. integriloba

Crataegus macracantha var. integriloba, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
