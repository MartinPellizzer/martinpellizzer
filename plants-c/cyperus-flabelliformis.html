Cyperus flabelliformis

Cyperus flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
