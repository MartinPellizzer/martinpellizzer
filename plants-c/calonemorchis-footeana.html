Calonemorchis footeana

Calonemorchis footeana, commonly known as the Foote's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.
