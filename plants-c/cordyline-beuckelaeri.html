Cordyline beuckelaeri

Cordyline beuckelaeri, commonly known as the red-veined cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with striking red or purple veins, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.

This plant is also valued for its ability to produce small, fragrant white flowers in the summer months.
