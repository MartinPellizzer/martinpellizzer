Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. angustifolium

Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
