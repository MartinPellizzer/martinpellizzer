Calanthe × porphyrea

Calanthe × porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyrean Calanthe, is a hybrid orchid that combines traits from its parent species, Calanthe sieboldii and Calanthe formosana.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers with intricate patterns. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits variability in flower color and form, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
