Convolvulus tuckeyanus

Convolvulus tuckeyanus, commonly known as Tuckey's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a hardy species that can thrive in poor soils.
