Clematis lugubris

Clematis lugubris, commonly known as the weeping clematis, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, climbing stems with thornless stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its attractive flowers and climbing habit, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
