Cephalaria longifolia

Cephalaria longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Cephalaria longifolia is typically found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
