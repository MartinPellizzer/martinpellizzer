Cineraria glandulifera

Cineraria glandulifera, commonly known as the sticky cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
