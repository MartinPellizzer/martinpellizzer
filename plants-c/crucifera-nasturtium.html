Crucifera nasturtium

Crucifera nasturtium, commonly known as garden nasturtium, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

It is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that come in shades of orange, yellow, and red, and its heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to South America but is now cultivated worldwide for its ornamental value and edible parts. Its flowers and leaves are often used in salads and herbal teas due to their peppery flavor.

The plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
