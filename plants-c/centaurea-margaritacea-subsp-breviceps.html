Centaurea margaritacea subsp. breviceps

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

breviceps, commonly known as the narrow-headed cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, linear leaves.

The subspecies breviceps is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescence compared to other subspecies of Centaurea margaritacea. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
