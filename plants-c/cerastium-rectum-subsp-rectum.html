Cerastium rectum subsp. rectum

Cerastium rectum subsp.

rectum, commonly known as the upright mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
