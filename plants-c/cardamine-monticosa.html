Cardamine monticosa

Cardamine monticosa, commonly known as alpine water-cress, is a small herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky slopes and forest floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces elongated, segmented fruits containing multiple seeds.

Cardamine monticosa is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
