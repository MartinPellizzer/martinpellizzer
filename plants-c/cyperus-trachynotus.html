Cyperus trachynotus

Cyperus trachynotus, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces distinctive, spiky inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing wetland soils.
