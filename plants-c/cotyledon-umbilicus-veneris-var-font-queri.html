Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. font-queri

Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. font-queri, commonly known as the "heartleaf cotyledon," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are often green with a bluish tint and can develop a reddish hue when exposed to sunlight. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by pale green, fleshy fruits.

This plant is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
