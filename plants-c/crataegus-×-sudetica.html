Crataegus × sudetica

Crataegus × sudetica, commonly known as the Bohemian hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates, though it may require protection from extreme weather conditions.
