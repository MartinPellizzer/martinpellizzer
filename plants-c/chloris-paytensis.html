Chloris paytensis

Chloris paytensis, commonly known as Payten's grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes numerous other grass species. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its morphology includes slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Chloris paytensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
