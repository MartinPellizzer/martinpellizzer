Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. hederiforme

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

hederiforme, commonly known as edible goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

This subspecies is notable for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and have a slightly tangy flavor.
