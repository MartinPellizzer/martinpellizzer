Chenopodium aureum

Chenopodium aureum, commonly known as golden goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oblong, bead-like seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium aureum is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
