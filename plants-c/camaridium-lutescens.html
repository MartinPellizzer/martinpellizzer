Camaridium lutescens

Camaridium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow camaridium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
