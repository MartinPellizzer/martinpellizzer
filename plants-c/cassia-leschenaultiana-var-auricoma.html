Cassia leschenaultiana var. auricoma

Cassia leschenaultiana var. auricoma, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
