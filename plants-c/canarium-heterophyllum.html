Canarium heterophyllum

Canarium heterophyllum, commonly known as the Philippine canarium, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
