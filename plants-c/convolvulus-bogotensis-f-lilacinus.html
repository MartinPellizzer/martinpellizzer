Convolvulus bogotensis f. lilacinus

Convolvulus bogotensis f. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lilac to deep purple. The plant produces slender, climbing stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
