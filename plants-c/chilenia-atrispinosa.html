Chilenia atrispinosa

Chilenia atrispinosa, commonly known as the Chilean thornbush, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Chilenia atrispinosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
