Coelogyne ochracea

Coelogyne ochracea, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulbous structure with long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, and its flowers are often arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Coelogyne ochracea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
