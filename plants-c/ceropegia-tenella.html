Ceropegia tenella

Ceropegia tenella, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
