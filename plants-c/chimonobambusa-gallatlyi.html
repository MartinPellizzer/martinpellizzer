Chimonobambusa gallatlyi

Chimonobambusa gallatlyi, commonly known as the Yunnan bamboo, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan province.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in horticulture and sustainable construction.
