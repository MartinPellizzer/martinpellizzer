Cenchrus aethiopicus

Cenchrus aethiopicus, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of grass. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

Cenchrus aethiopicus is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
