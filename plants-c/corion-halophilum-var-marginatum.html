Corion halophilum var. marginatum

Corion halophilum var. marginatum, commonly known as the salt marsh thistle, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations. The plant features a rosette of spiny, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish, arranged in a head with bracts that provide additional protection.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting specialized insect pollinators.
