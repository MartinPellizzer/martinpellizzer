Calycera lanata

Calycera lanata, commonly known as the woolly calycera, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
