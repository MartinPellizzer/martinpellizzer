Chamaecrista repens var. multijuga

Chamaecrista repens var. multijuga, commonly known as the creeping crown vetch, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
