Chrysoglossum cyrtopetalum

Chrysoglossum cyrtopetalum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high sunlight exposure. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in its natural habitat.
