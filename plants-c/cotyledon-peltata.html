Cotyledon peltata

Cotyledon peltata, commonly known as the peacock plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with striking, colorful patterns. The plant's leaves are typically arranged in a rosette formation, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the growing season.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
