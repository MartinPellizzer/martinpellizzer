Cuphea scabra

Cuphea scabra, commonly known as the rough cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Cuphea scabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
