Cucurbitula

Cucurbitula, commonly known as the "spiny apple," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cucurbitaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tendrils, lobed leaves, and distinct fruit shapes. The fruit typically has a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, fleshy interior. Cucurbitula species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They play an important role in traditional medicine and local agriculture.
