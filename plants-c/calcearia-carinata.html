Calcearia carinata

Calcearia carinata, commonly known as the horned sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, green, spiny bracts that surround its central flower head, giving it a unique appearance. Calcearia carinata typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and unusual floral structure.
