Convolvulus bracteatus

Convolvulus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other species of morning glories and bindweeds.

Convolvulus bracteatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and easy care requirements.
