Cyperus nubianus

Cyperus nubianus, commonly known as the Nubian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus nubianus is adapted to thrive in environments with limited water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
