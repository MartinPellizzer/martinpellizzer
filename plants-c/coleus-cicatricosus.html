Coleus cicatricosus

Coleus cicatricosus, commonly known as the scarred coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are often marked with dark markings or scars, giving it its common name.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.
