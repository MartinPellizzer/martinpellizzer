Corymborkis corymbosa

Corymborkis corymbosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
