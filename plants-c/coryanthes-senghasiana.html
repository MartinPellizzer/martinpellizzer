Coryanthes senghasiana

Coryanthes senghasiana, commonly known as the Senghasian coryanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that resemble a crown, with a unique structure that includes a central column and specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a distinctive lip that plays a key role in attracting pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
