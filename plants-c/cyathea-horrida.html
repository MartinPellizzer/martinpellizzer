Cyathea horrida

Cyathea horrida, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, scaly bark. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea horrida thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
