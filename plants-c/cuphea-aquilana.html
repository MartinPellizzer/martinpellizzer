Cuphea aquilana

Cuphea aquilana, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Cuphea aquilana thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
