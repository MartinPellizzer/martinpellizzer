Crotalaria luniemuensis

Crotalaria luniemuensis, commonly known as the moonflower crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its seeds are encased in a pod-like fruit, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
