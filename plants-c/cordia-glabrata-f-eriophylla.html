Cordia glabrata f. eriophylla

Cordia glabrata f. eriophylla, commonly known as the smooth cordia or a type of flowering plant, is a variant of the Cordia glabrata species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
