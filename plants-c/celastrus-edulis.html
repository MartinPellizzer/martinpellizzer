Celastrus edulis

Celastrus edulis, commonly known as the edible staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its fruit is edible when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

However, caution is advised as some species in the genus can be toxic.
