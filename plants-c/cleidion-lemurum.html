Cleidion lemurum

Cleidion lemurum, commonly known as the Madagascar ceiba, is a large tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and thick, flaky bark. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. It produces yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
