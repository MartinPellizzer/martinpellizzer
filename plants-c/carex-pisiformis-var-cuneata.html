Carex pisiformis var. cuneata

Carex pisiformis var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often clustered at the top of the plant.
