Clematis grandidentata

Clematis grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with large, serrated leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Clematis grandidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
