Cyperus pallescens

Cyperus pallescens, commonly known as the silver flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or purplish in color. Cyperus pallescens is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
