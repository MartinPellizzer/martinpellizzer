Coelogyne clarkei

Coelogyne clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Coelogyne clarkei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
