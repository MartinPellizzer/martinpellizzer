Calamagrostis pseudoepigeios

Calamagrostis pseudoepigeios, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis pseudoepigeios plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
