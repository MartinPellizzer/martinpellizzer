Carex × microstyla

Carex × microstyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, spike-like seed heads.
