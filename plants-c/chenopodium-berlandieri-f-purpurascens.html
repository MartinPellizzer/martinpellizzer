Chenopodium berlandieri f. purpurascens

Chenopodium berlandieri f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically purple-tinged, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, three-angled seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions.
