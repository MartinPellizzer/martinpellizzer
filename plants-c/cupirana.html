Cupirana

Cupirana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
