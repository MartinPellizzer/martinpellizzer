Carum vulgare

Carum vulgare, commonly known as wild cumin, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Its fruit is a globular, three-lobed seed that is harvested for its distinctive flavor.

Carum vulgare thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and wildflower habitats.
