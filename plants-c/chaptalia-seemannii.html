Chaptalia seemannii

Chaptalia seemannii, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, upright stems that often grow in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to its natural habitat and suitable for cultivation in similar environments.
