Cissus feminea

Cissus feminea, commonly known as the female fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it popular in ornamental horticulture.

Cissus feminea is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
