Caldesia parnassifolia var. dubia

Caldesia parnassifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the white-flowered caldesia, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white spathes that enclose a cluster of small, greenish-white spadices. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
