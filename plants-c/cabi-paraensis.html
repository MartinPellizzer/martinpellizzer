Cabi paraensis

Cabi paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Cabi paraensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
