Chlora sessilis

Chlora sessilis, commonly known as the sessile chloris, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
