Cerasus susquehanae

Cerasus susquehanae, commonly known as the Susquehanna cherry, is a species of Prunus native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along rivers and streams.
