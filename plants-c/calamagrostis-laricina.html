Calamagrostis laricina

Calamagrostis laricina, commonly known as the black-footed grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomes and its slender, arching blades that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis laricina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
