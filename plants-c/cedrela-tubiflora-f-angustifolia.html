Cedrela tubiflora f. angustifolia

Cedrela tubiflora f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahogany, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
