Croton pendulus

Croton pendulus, commonly known as weeping croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that bear clusters of small, bright green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spiky, orange-red capsules that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
