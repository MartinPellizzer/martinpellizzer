Campelia standleyi

Campelia standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its leaves are typically simple and alternate, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
