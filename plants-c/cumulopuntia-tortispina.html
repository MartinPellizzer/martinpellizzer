Cumulopuntia tortispina

Cumulopuntia tortispina, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its stems are often covered with small, white spines that provide protection from herbivores.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
