Cakile lanceolata var. edentula

Cakile lanceolata var. edentula, commonly known as the toothless bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in coastal or desert environments.
