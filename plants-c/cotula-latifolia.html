Cotula latifolia

Cotula latifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a yellow center. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is typically found in open, sunny habitats. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil.

Cotula latifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
