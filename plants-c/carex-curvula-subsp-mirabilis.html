Carex curvula subsp. mirabilis

Carex curvula subsp.

mirabilis, commonly known as the graceful sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
