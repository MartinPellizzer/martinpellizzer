Carex rara subsp. biwensis

Carex rara subsp.

biwensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
