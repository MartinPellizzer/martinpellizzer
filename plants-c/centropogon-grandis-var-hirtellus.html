Centropogon grandis var. hirtellus

Centropogon grandis var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The hairy centropogon is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
