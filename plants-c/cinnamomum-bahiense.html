Cinnamomum bahiense

Cinnamomum bahiense, commonly known as the Bahamian cinnamon or Bahamian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries containing one to three seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
