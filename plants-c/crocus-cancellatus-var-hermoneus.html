Crocus cancellatus var. hermoneus

Crocus cancellatus var. hermoneus, commonly known as the Hermoneus crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat and a finely veined pattern on its petals. The plant produces a single, showy bloom in early spring, typically between February and April. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
