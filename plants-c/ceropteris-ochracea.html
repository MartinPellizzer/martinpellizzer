Ceropteris ochracea

Ceropteris ochracea, commonly known as the orange brake fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow to orange fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with fertile sporangia located on the underside of the leaves.

Ceropteris ochracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
