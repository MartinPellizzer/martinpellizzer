Chordifex crispatus

Chordifex crispatus, commonly known as the curly-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curling leaves that float on the water surface. The plant has finely divided, thread-like leaves that provide a habitat for small aquatic organisms. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with floating seeds dispersing via water currents.

Chordifex crispatus is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
