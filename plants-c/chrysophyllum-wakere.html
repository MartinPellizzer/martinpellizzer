Chrysophyllum wakere

Chrysophyllum wakere, commonly known as the Wakere cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar to cherries, with a sweet and tart flavor. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
