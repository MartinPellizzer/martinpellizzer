Cirsium korsakoviense

Cirsium korsakoviense, commonly known as the Korsakov thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a compact, globular shape.

Cirsium korsakoviense is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
