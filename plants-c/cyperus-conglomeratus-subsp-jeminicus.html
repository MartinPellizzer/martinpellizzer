Cyperus conglomeratus subsp. jeminicus

Cyperus conglomeratus subsp.

jeminicus, commonly known as the Jemini sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.
