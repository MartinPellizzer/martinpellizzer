Campanula cochleromena

Campanula cochleromena, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
