Citrus × nobilis subf. unshiu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

unshiu, commonly known as the Ponkan or Japanese mandarin, is a hybrid citrus species originating from Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with thin, loose peels.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is known for its sweet flavor and high vitamin C content, making it popular in both culinary and medicinal uses.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
