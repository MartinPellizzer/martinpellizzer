Cactus paniculatus

Cactus paniculatus, commonly known as the desert candle cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that resembles a candle, covered in spines for protection against herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the soil after rare rainfall.

Cactus paniculatus is well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water and high temperatures.
