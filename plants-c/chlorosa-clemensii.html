Chlorosa clemensii

Chlorosa clemensii, commonly known as the Clemens' greenbark, is a rare and specialized plant found in specific microhabitats.

It is characterized by its unique chlorophyll distribution, which gives it a distinct greenish hue. The plant has adapted to low-light conditions, making it a shade-tolerant species. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid environment.

Due to habitat fragmentation, Chlorosa clemensii is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
