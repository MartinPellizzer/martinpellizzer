Circaea × ovata

Circaea × ovata, commonly known as the hybrid white campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
