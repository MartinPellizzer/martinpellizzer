Cestrum cristinae

Cestrum cristinae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in winter and early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
