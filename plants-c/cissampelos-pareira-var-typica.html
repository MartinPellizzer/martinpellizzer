Cissampelos pareira var. typica

Cissampelos pareira var. typica, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, yellow berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
