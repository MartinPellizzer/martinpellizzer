Carex lamprosandra

Carex lamprosandra, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale green sheen. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex lamprosandra produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually green or brown in color.
