Chodanthus

Chodanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf chodanthus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chodanthus is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water once established.
