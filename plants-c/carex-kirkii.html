Carex kirkii

Carex kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
