Crataegus beckerana

Crataegus beckerana, commonly known as the Becker's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in temperate regions, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, and it is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
