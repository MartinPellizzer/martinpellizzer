Callicarpa pentandra f. farinosa

Callicarpa pentandra f. farinosa, commonly known as the smooth-berried beautyberry, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking clusters of violet to purple berries in the fall, which are edible but have a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and berries, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
