Carex psychrophila

Carex psychrophila, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to cold climates.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Carex psychrophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
