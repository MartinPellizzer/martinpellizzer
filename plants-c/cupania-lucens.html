Cupania lucens

Cupania lucens, commonly known as the silverleaf cupania, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its silver-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a fuzzy texture.

Cupania lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
