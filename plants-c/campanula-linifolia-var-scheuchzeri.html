Campanula linifolia var. scheuchzeri

Campanula linifolia var. scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
