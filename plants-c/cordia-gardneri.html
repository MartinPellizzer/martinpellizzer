Cordia gardneri

Cordia gardneri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Cordia gardneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
