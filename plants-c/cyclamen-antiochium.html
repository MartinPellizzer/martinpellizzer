Cyclamen antiochium

Cyclamen antiochium, commonly known as the Antioch cyclamen, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers typically appear in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for winter bedding plants.

Cyclamen antiochium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as houseplants.
