Clematis brevicaudata var. filipes

Clematis brevicaudata var. filipes, commonly known as the slender-tailed clematis, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant growth habit.
