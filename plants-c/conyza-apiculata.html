Conyza apiculata

Conyza apiculata, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Conyza apiculata is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
