Cerasus tortuosa

Cerasus tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted cherry or Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting branches and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique growth pattern and ornamental value make it a distinctive addition to horticultural collections.
