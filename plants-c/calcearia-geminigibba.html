Calcearia geminigibba

Calcearia geminigibba, commonly known as the twin-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Calcearia geminigibba is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate and attractive floral display.
