Crocus chrysanthus var. albidus

Crocus chrysanthus var. albidus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

The flowers are also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
