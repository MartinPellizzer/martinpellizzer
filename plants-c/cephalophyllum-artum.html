Cephalophyllum artum

Cephalophyllum artum, commonly known as the artful head plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
