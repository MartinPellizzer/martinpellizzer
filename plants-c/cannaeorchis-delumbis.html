Cannaeorchis delumbis

Cannaeorchis delumbis, commonly known as the cloud orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, providing support for its flowering spike. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with intricate patterns.

Cannaeorchis delumbis is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded, well-drained soils.
