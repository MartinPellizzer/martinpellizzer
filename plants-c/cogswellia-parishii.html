Cogswellia parishii

Cogswellia parishii, commonly known as the desert rose.

It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa. This succulent plant is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
