Croton chilensis

Croton chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
