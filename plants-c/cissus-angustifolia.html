Cissus angustifolia

Cissus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in various light conditions.

Cissus angustifolia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
