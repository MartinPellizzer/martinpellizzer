Crocus caeruleus

Crocus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crocus caeruleus is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of saffron-like stigmas.
