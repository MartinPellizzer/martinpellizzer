Centaurea ragusina subsp. lungensis

Centaurea ragusina subsp.

lungensis, commonly known as the Lunga centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its environment.
