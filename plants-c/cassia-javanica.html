Cassia javanica

Cassia javanica, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often covering the entire plant in a striking display. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
