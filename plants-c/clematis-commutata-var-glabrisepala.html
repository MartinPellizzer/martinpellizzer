Clematis commutata var. glabrisepala

Clematis commutata var. glabrisepala, commonly known as the smooth-sepal clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, supporting its showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with smooth, glabrous sepals and five petals. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to climb trellises or fences with ease.
