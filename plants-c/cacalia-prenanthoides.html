Cacalia prenanthoides

Cacalia prenanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Cacalia prenanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
