Cerastium vulgatum lusus grandiflorum

Cerastium vulgatum lusus grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive five-lobed shape, which is characteristic of the genus Cerastium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
