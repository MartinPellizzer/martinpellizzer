Centaurea × porcii

Centaurea × porcii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea scabiosa (greater knapweed). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of blue to purple ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.
