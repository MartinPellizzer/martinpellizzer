Cirsium forsteri

Cirsium forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces purple to pinkish flower heads in the summer. Cirsium forsteri typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
