Castanea sphaerocarpa

Castanea sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which ripen in the autumn. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is valued for its hardy nature and resistance to pests and diseases.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious food.
