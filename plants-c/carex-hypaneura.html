Carex hypaneura

Carex hypaneura, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, three-ranked flowers.

Carex hypaneura plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
