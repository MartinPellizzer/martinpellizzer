Cyathea setchellii

Cyathea setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes produce new shoots, allowing it to form dense clumps over time.

Cyathea setchellii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
