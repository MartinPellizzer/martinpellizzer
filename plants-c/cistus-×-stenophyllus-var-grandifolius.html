Cistus × stenophyllus var. grandifolius

Cistus × stenophyllus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
