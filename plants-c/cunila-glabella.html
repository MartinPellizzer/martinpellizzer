Cunila glabella

Cunila glabella, commonly known as lesser betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting various pollinators.

Cunila glabella is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
