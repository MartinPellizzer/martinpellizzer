Crinita

Crinita, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Africa, particularly in southern and eastern areas. They are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant flowers that resemble spiders, hence their common name. Crinita species typically grow in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Their unique floral structures and ecological adaptations make them a subject of interest in botanical studies.
