Chrysanthemum macrocarpum subsp. maroccanum

Chrysanthemum macrocarpum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan chrysanthemum, is a species native to North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing numerous florets that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
