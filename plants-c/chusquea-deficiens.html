Chusquea deficiens

Chusquea deficiens, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine regions.

Chusquea deficiens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
