Capparis usiacurina

Capparis usiacurina, commonly known as the mountain caper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, mountainous terrain, thriving in cool and humid climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
