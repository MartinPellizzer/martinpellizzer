Carex personata

Carex personata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shady, damp areas and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Carex personata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
