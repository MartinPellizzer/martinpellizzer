Conohoria

Conohoria, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, edible berries.

The botanical name *Conohoria* reflects its unique nocturnal blooming behavior.
