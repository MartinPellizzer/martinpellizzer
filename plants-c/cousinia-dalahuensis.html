Cousinia dalahuensis

Cousinia dalahuensis, commonly known as the Dalahuan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a cluster.

Cousinia dalahuensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
