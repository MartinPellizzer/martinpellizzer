Cyperus leptophyllus var. deliciosus

Cyperus leptophyllus var. deliciosus, commonly known as the sweet sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, often arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
