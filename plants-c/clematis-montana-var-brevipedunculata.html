Clematis montana var. brevipedunculata

Clematis montana var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a climbing plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
