Calophyllum rupicola var. elatum

Calophyllum rupicola var. elatum, commonly known as the tall calophyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Calophyllaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, making it a significant component of the forest canopy in its native habitat.
