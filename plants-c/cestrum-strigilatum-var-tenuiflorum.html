Cestrum strigilatum var. tenuiflorum

Cestrum strigilatum var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nocturnal fragrance.
