Carduus personata subsp. albidus

Carduus personata subsp.

albidus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its spiny, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
