Callistachys cordifolia

Callistachys cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
