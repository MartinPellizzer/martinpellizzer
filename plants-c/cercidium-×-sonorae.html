Cercidium × sonorae

Cercidium × sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran creosotebush, is a hybrid shrub native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with deep roots and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a significant component of the desert ecosystem.
