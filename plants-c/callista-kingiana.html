Callista kingiana

Callista kingiana, commonly known as the King's Callista, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Callista kingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
