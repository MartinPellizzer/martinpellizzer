Chamaerops martiana

Chamaerops martiana, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is not as widely cultivated for this purpose as other date palms. It thrives in warm, coastal climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Chamaerops martiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
