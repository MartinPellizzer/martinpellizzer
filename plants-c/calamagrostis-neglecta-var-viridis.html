Calamagrostis neglecta var. viridis

Calamagrostis neglecta var. viridis, commonly known as green bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage that exhibits a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
