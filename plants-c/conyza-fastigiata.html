Conyza fastigiata

Conyza fastigiata, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and open woodlands. The plant has slender stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Conyza fastigiata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
