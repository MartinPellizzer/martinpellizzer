Cosmibuena grandiflora

Cosmibuena grandiflora, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem and broad, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The golden bell tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
