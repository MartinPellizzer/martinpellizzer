Calliandra imbricata

Calliandra imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that resemble the tail of a peacock. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often in a cascading pattern. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra imbricata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
