Capparidastrum pachaca subsp. oxysepalum

Capparidastrum pachaca subsp.

oxysepalum, commonly known as the white-sepal capparid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru. It belongs to the family Capparaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive floral structures and often succulent leaves.

The subspecies is characterized by its white, showy sepals that are prominently displayed before the petals open. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.
