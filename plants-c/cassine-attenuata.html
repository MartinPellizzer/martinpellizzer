Cassine attenuata

Cassine attenuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cassine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Cassine attenuata thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
