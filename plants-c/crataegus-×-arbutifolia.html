Crataegus × arbutifolia

Crataegus × arbutifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn or swamp hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that persist into winter. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, swamps, and riverbanks, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are usually oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a smooth upper surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
