Curcuma sixsensesensis

Curcuma sixsensesensis, commonly known as the Six Senses Turmeric, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange-yellow rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from a spike at the top of the plant.

The rhizomes are harvested for their medicinal and culinary properties, rich in curcuminoids.
