Cerasiocarpum penangense

Cerasiocarpum penangense, commonly known as the Penang cherry tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Malay Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
