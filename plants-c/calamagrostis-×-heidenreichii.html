Calamagrostis × heidenreichii

Calamagrostis × heidenreichii, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple to pinkish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that contrasts with the vibrant plumes.

Calamagrostis × heidenreichii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in damp conditions.
