Cymbidium × bennett-poei

Cymbidium × bennett-poei, commonly known as the Bennett-Poei orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern. It prefers cool, bright environments with good air circulation and moderate humidity.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
