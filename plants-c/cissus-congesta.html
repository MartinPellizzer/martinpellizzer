Cissus congesta

Cissus congesta, commonly known as the rock fig or stone fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus congesta thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocks or trees in its natural habitat.
