Carex lusitanica

Carex lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

Carex lusitanica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
