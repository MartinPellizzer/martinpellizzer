Carex ontakensis

Carex ontakensis, commonly known as the Ontake sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex ontakensis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
