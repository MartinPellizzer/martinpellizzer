Calypso speciosa

Calypso speciosa, commonly known as the showy calypso, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, colorful flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its unique structure includes a slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
