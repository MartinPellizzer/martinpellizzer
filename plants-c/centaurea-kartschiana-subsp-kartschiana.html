Centaurea kartschiana subsp. kartschiana

Centaurea kartschiana subsp.

kartschiana, commonly known as the Karstchian bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showing adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
