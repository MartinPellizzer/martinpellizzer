Cirrhopetalum roseum

Cirrhopetalum roseum, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
