Cestrum hortulanum

Cestrum hortulanum, commonly known as night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
