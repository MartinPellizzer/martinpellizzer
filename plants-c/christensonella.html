Christensonella

Christensonella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, pale green or white flowers that bloom directly from the stem. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its botanical classification places it in the Orchidaceae family, and it is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Christensonella is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
