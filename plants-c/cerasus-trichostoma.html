Cerasus trichostoma

Cerasus trichostoma, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, where it is celebrated for its ornamental value during the spring blossom season. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red fruits. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in traditional Japanese gardens.

The leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
