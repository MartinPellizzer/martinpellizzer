Carex riparia var. gracilescens

Carex riparia var. gracilescens, commonly known as slender river sedge, is a variant of the river sedge species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found along streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
