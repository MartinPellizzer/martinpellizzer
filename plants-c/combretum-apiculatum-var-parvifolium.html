Combretum apiculatum var. parvifolium

Combretum apiculatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that display bright yellow to orange hues, which are particularly striking during the flowering season. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
