Carica cubensis

Carica cubensis, commonly known as the papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange fruit. The plant produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit that is rich in vitamin C and contains enzymes like papain, which aid in digestion. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The papaya plant is also notable for its rapid growth cycle, often bearing fruit within a year of planting.
