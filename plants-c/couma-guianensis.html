Couma guianensis

Couma guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese couma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves are valued for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Couma guianensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
