Cicuta maculata var. bolanderi

Cicuta maculata var. bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander water hemlock, is a toxic member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant contains toxic compounds such as cicutoxin, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It thrives in wet, disturbed soils along streambanks and marshes.

Due to its high toxicity, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
