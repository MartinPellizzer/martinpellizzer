Carex spectabilis f. alpina

Carex spectabilis f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a variant of the spectabilis form of Carex spectabilis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky or wetland habitats.
