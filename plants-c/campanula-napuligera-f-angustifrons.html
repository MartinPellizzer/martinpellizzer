Campanula napuligera f. angustifrons

Campanula napuligera f. angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
