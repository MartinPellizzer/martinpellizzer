Coilantha campanulata

Coilantha campanulata, commonly known as the spiral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Coilantha campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
