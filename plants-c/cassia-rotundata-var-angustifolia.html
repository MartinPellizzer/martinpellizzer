Cassia rotundata var. angustifolia

Cassia rotundata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
