Citrus × limetta var. acris

Citrus × limetta var. acris, commonly known as the acris lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thin, yellowish rind.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin. The fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, making it popular for culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in perfumery and traditional herbal remedies.
