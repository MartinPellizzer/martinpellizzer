Carex bullata var. cylindracea

Carex bullata var. cylindracea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along streams.
