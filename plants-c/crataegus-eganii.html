Crataegus eganii

Crataegus eganii, commonly known as the Egan's hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Crataegus eganii is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
