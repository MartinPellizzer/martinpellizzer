Circaea lutetiana f. cordifolia

Circaea lutetiana f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved woodrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to cordate in shape, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
