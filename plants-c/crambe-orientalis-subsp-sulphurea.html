Crambe orientalis subsp. sulphurea

Crambe orientalis subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur canary, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of the canary flower, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, with a rough texture.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
