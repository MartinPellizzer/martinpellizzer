Croton luteovirens

Croton luteovirens, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of yellow, green, and white. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported health benefits.
