Carex saruensis

Carex saruensis, commonly known as the Sarawak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Borneo and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and swampy areas. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex saruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
