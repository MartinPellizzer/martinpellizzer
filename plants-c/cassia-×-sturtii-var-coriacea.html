Cassia × sturtii var. coriacea

Cassia × sturtii var. coriacea, commonly known as the Sturt's desert thorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety layer of hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. Its adaptability to harsh, dry environments makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The varietal name 'coriacea' refers to its leathery, tough foliage, which helps it conserve water.
