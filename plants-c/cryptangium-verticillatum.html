Cryptangium verticillatum

Cryptangium verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Cryptangium verticillatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
