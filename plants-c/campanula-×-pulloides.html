Campanula × pulloides

Campanula × pulloides, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive flowers and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
