Cuphea retrorsicapilla var. enneanthera

Cuphea retrorsicapilla var. enneanthera, commonly known as the nine-lobed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
