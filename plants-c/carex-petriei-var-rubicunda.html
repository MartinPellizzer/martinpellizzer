Carex petriei var. rubicunda

Carex petriei var. rubicunda, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
