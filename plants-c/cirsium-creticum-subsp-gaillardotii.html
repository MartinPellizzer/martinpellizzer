Cirsium creticum subsp. gaillardotii

Cirsium creticum subsp.

gaillardotii, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal areas or on slopes. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to other Cirsium species.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
