Canna ellipticifolia var. patens

Canna ellipticifolia var. patens, commonly known as the spreading cannal, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, providing a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
