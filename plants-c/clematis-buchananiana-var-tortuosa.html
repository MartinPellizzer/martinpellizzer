Clematis buchananiana var. tortuosa

Clematis buchananiana var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its long, twining stems.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
