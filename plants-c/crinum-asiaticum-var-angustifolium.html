Crinum asiaticum var. angustifolium

Crinum asiaticum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strap-shaped, narrow leaves and produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a strong fragrance. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a bulbous base that stores nutrients and water. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
