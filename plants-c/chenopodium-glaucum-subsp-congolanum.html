Chenopodium glaucum subsp. congolanum

Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

congolanum, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

congolanum is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines.
