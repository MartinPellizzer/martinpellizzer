Callicarpa giraldii var. lyi

Callicarpa giraldii var. lyi, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of purple berries that appear in the fall, which are edible but often tart. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, aromatic bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn color.
