Catasetum seccoi

Catasetum seccoi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored, spurred petals. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Catasetum seccoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
