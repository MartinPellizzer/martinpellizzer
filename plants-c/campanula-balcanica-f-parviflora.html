Campanula balcanica f. parviflora

Campanula balcanica f. parviflora, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
