Cenchrus setigerus

Cenchrus setigerus, commonly known as the speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cenchrus setigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
