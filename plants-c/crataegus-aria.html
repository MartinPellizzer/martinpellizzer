Crataegus aria

Crataegus aria, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody, often branched structure, making it a notable species in hedgerows and woodland edges.
