Calamus salicifolius var. leiophyllus

Calamus salicifolius var. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of edible, starchy tubers. Its leaves are elongated and smooth, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical gardens.

This variant is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
