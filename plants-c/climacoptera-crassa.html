Climacoptera crassa

Climacoptera crassa, commonly known as the mountain spurge, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, waxy texture to reduce water loss.
