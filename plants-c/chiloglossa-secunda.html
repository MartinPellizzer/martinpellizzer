Chiloglossa secunda

Chiloglossa secunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Chiloglossa secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique form.
