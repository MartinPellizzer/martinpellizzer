Cornus californica

Cornus californica, commonly known as red twig dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features striking red stems that remain visible throughout the winter, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green hue during the growing season.

Cornus californica thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
