Crotalaria virgulata subsp. longistyla

Crotalaria virgulata subsp.

longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent stamens.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are often arranged in racemes and are known for their showy, butterfly-like appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated stamens, which contribute to its unique botanical identity.
