Cactus micracanthus

Cactus micracanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and bright yellow spines that give it a striking appearance. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
