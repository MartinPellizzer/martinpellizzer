Chenopodium strictum f. atroviridiolivaceum

Chenopodium strictum f. atroviridiolivaceum, commonly known as the dark green goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot, characterized by its dark green foliage and compact growth habit.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, typically found in disturbed soils and arid environments. The leaves are usually ovate to deltoid in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.
