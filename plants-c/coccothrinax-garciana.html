Coccothrinax garciana

Coccothrinax garciana, commonly known as the Cuban royal palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clustered, orange-colored fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
