Centaurea spinosa

Centaurea spinosa, commonly known as the thorny centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Centaurea spinosa is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
