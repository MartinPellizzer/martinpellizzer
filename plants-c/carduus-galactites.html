Carduus galactites

Carduus galactites, commonly known as the Galactite thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
