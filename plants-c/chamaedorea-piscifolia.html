Chamaedorea piscifolia

Chamaedorea piscifolia, commonly known as the fish-tailed palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface. The plant produces long, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and have a feathery appearance. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Chamaedorea piscifolia prefers indirect light and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
