Caesia vittata var. chlorantha

Caesia vittata var. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-stemmed caesia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often greenish stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
