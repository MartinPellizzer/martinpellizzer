Chianthemum graecum

Chianthemum graecum, commonly known as Greek chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in horticulture.
