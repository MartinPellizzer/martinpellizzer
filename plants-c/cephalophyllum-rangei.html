Cephalophyllum rangei

Cephalophyllum rangei, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and unique succulent native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, balloon-like stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, a feature that aids in pollination by nocturnal insects. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Cephalophyllum rangei is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
