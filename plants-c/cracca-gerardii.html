Cracca gerardii

Cracca gerardii, commonly known as Gerard's cranesbill, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gerard's cranesbill is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
