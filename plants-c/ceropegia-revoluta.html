Ceropegia revoluta

Ceropegia revoluta, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or bluish hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ceropegia revoluta is also known for its ability to climb or trail, often using its stems to attach to surfaces.
