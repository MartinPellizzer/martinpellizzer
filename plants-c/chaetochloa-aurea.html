Chaetochloa aurea

Chaetochloa aurea, commonly known as golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, golden-yellow foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with slender, erect stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height. Its leaves are narrow and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chaetochloa aurea plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
