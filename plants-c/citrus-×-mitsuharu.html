Citrus × mitsuharu

Citrus × mitsuharu, commonly known as the Ichikawa mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruits that are typically harvested in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in both home gardens and commercial orchards.
