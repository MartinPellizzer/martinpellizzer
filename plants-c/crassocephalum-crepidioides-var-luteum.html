Crassocephalum crepidioides var. luteum

Crassocephalum crepidioides var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow cudweed or yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and coastal areas. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in the summer months.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental value in some regions.
