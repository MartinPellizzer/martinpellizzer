Clematis smilacifolia var. coriacea

Clematis smilacifolia var. coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a rough, textured surface, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its twining stems.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand mild frosts.
