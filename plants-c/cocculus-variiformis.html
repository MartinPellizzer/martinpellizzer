Cocculus variiformis

Cocculus variiformis, commonly known as the variable cocklebur, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
