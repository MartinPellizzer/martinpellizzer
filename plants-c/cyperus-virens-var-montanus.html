Cyperus virens var. montanus

Cyperus virens var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, which are typically greenish or reddish in hue.

This variant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
