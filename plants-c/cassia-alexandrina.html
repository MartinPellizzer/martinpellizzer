Cassia alexandrina

Cassia alexandrina, commonly known as sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, curved pods, which are characteristic of the genus Cassia. Its seeds are enclosed within these pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Cassia alexandrina is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
