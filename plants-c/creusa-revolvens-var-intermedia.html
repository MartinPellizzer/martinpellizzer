Creusa revolvens var. intermedia

Creusa revolvens var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
