Cephalotaxus fortunei var. globosa

Cephalotaxus fortunei var. globosa, commonly known as the globe yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical form and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
