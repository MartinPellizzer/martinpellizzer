Cosmidium engelmannii

Cosmidium engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann's water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Cosmidium engelmannii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
