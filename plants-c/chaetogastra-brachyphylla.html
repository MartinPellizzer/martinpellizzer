Chaetogastra brachyphylla

Chaetogastra brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense arrangement of bristle-like structures along its stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
