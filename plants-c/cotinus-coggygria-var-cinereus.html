Cotinus coggygria var. cinereus

Cotinus coggygria var. cinereus, commonly known as the smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pinkish-white flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
