Calycadenia mollis f. rosea

Calycadenia mollis f. rosea, commonly known as the pinkhead stickybush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, glandular-hairy stems and leaves, with small, pinkish to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in a sticky substance, which is a distinguishing botanical trait.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, showcasing a unique combination of structural and floral characteristics.
