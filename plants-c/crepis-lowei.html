Crepis lowei

Crepis lowei, commonly known as the low's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Crepis lowei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
