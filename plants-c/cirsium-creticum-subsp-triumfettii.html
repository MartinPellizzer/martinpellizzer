Cirsium creticum subsp. triumfettii

Cirsium creticum subsp.

triumfettii, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The plant features a spiny stem and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are purple to pink, arranged in a spherical head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its robust structure and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
