Chilenia fusca

Chilenia fusca, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to drought and damage, making it well adapted to its native environment.
