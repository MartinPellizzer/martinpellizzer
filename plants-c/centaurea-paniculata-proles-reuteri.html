Centaurea paniculata proles reuteri

Centaurea paniculata proles reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's spiked centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
