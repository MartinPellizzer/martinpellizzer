Crataegus monogyna f. punicea-plena

Crataegus monogyna f. punicea-plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its showy, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the autumn.

The fruit is a small, red pome that matures in the fall, providing food for birds and wildlife.
