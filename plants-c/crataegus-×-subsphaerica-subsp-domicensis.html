Crataegus × subsphaerica subsp. domicensis

Crataegus × subsphaerica subsp.

domicensis, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems and a rounded form.

The plant features small, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
