Clematis calyculata

Clematis calyculata, commonly known as the white clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hooked hairs. Clematis calyculata thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be trained to grow on supports or trellises.
