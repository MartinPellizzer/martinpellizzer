Catasetum × intermedium

Catasetum × intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate catasetum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces specialized structures called pollinia, which are adapted for epiphytic pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Catasetum × intermedium is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
