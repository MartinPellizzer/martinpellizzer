Cosentinia vellea subsp. vellea

Cosentinia vellea subsp.

vellea, commonly known as the common rock cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.
