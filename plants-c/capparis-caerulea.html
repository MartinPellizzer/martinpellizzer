Capparis caerulea

Capparis caerulea, commonly known as the blue caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and thorny stems, which are typical of the Capparaceae family. The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, often with a glossy appearance.

Capparis caerulea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.
