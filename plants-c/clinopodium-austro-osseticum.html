Clinopodium austro-osseticum

Clinopodium austro-osseticum, commonly known as the Caucasian oregano, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Clinopodium austro-osseticum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
