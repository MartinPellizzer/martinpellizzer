Cephaelis phaneroneura

Cephaelis phaneroneura, commonly known as the showy celandine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.
