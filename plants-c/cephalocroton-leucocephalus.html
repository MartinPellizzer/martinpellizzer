Cephalocroton leucocephalus

Cephalocroton leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that give way to orange-red berries. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
