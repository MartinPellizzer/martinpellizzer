Chlorophytum graptophyllum

Chlorophytum graptophyllum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a hardy, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually variegated with green and white or yellow markings. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal choice for indoor environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by narrow, grass-like seed capsules.

Chlorophytum graptophyllum is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
