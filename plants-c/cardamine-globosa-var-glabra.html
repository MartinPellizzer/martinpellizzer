Cardamine globosa var. glabra

Cardamine globosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered in a fine, smooth texture.

The species is notable for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
