Chionanthus retusus

Chionanthus retusus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Chionanthus retusus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
