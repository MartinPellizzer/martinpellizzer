Cistus hispanicus

Cistus hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Cistus hispanicus plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
