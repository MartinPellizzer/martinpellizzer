Centaurea joharchii

Centaurea joharchii, commonly known as the Afghan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its upright, branching structure. It produces small, bract-like leaves and has a long flowering period, often blooming from summer to autumn.

Centaurea joharchii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
