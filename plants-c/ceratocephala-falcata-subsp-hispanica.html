Ceratocephala falcata subsp. hispanica

Ceratocephala falcata subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish horned poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange flowers with prominent, horn-like sepals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
