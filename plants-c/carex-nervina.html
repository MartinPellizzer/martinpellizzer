Carex nervina

Carex nervina, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its scientific name.

Carex nervina produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
