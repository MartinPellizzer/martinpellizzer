Carex paradoxa f. ramosa

Carex paradoxa f. ramosa, commonly known as the ramose sedges, is a variant of the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

The ramose form is distinguished by its more branched inflorescence compared to the typical form.
