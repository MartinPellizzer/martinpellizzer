Carex longipes

Carex longipes, commonly known as the long-spurred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flowering spikes that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Carex longipes typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
