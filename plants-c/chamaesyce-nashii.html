Chamaesyce nashii

Chamaesyce nashii, commonly known as Nash's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with numerous stolons, and its leaves are fleshy, succulent, and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called capitula.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
