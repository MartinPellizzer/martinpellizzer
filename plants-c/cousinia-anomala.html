Cousinia anomala

Cousinia anomala, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in high-altitude grasslands. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Cousinia anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
