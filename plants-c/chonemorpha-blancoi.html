Chonemorpha blancoi

Chonemorpha blancoi, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
