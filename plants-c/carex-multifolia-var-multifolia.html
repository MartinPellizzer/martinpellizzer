Carex multifolia var. multifolia

Carex multifolia var. multifolia, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-stamened flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex multifolia var. multifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
