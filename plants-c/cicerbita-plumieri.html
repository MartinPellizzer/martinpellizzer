Cicerbita plumieri

Cicerbita plumieri, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cicerbita plumieri is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges.
