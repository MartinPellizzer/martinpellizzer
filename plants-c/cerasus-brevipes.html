Cerasus brevipes

Cerasus brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a species of Prunus native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 meters. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
