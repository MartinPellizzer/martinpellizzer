Cucurbita maxima var. neapolitana

Cucurbita maxima var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Neapolitan pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima, which belongs to the gourd family, Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, waxy skin that can range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is a vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of many Cucurbita species. It is native to Central and South America but has been widely cultivated in Europe, particularly in Italy, where it is used in traditional cuisine.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and ornamental appeal.
