Campium molle

Campium molle, commonly known as soft campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often sprawling.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, dry seeds when mature.
