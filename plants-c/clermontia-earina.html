Clermontia earina

Clermontia earina, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves. The plant produces a single, tall stem that bears a cluster of small, tubular, white flowers. It thrives in the high-elevation volcanic soils of Haleakalā National Park.

Due to its limited habitat and historical decline, it is considered an endangered species.
