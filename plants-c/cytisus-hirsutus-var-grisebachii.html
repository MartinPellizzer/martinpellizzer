Cytisus hirsutus var. grisebachii

Cytisus hirsutus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-haired broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems covered in a dense layer of grayish hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
