Cordiera coriacea

Cordiera coriacea, commonly known as the rough cordiera, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cordiera coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
