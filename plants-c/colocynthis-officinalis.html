Colocynthis officinalis

Colocynthis officinalis, commonly known as the wild cucumber or bitter apple, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, elongated fruits that resemble small cucumbers. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a fibrous, branching structure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit contains a toxic, yellowish pulp and is often used in traditional medicine despite its harmful properties.
