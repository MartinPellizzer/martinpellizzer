Centaurea ugamica

Centaurea ugamica, commonly known as the Ugam thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Centaurea ugamica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
