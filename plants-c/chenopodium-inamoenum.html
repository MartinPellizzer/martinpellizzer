Chenopodium inamoenum

Chenopodium inamoenum, commonly known as the slender goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves and clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor nutrient conditions. Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open landscapes.

Chenopodium inamoenum is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
