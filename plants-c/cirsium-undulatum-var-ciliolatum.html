Cirsium undulatum var. ciliolatum

Cirsium undulatum var. ciliolatum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The variety ciliolatum is distinguished by its more densely hairy leaves compared to other varieties of Cirsium undulatum.
