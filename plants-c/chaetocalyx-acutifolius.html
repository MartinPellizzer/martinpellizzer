Chaetocalyx acutifolius

Chaetocalyx acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a sharp, pointed appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.
