Callitriche autumnalis subsp. tenuior

Callitriche autumnalis subsp.

tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and ditches.

The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite, oval leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late summer to autumn.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
