Chrysothemis villosa

Chrysothemis villosa, commonly known as the woolly goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Chrysothemis villosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
