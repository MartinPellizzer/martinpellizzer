Carex neoguineensis

Carex neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex neoguineensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
