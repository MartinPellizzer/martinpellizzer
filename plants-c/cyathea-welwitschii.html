Cyathea welwitschii

Cyathea welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native regions.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a striking addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
