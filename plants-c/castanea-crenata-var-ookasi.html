Castanea crenata var. ookasi

Castanea crenata var. ookasi, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its lobed leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, spiny capsules that enclose the nuts, which are typically harvested in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Japanese culture.
