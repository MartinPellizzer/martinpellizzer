Chaerophyllum procumbens var. shortii

Chaerophyllum procumbens var. shortii, commonly known as the creeping parsley.

This variant is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
