Clausena heptaphylla

Clausena heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leafed Clausena, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round, and resemble cherries, containing a single seed.

While not widely cultivated for food, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
