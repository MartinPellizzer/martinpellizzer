Cactus centricirrhus

Cactus centricirrhus, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

The cactus also plays an important ecological role by supporting various pollinators and providing shelter for small desert animals.
