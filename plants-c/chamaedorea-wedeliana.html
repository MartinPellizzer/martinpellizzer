Chamaedorea wedeliana

Chamaedorea wedeliana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the feather palm, is a compact, upright palm native to Mexico and Central America.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and is adorned with feathery, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate watering requirements. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Chamaedorea wedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
