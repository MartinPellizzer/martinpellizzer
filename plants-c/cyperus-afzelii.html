Cyperus afzelii

Cyperus afzelii, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and floodplains, and thrives in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus afzelii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
