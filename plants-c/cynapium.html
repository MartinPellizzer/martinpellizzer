Cynapium

Cynapium, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cynapium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
