Coleus nigericus

Coleus nigericus, commonly known as black coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its vibrant, colorful bracts. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and features square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in whorls at the ends of the branches.

Coleus nigericus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
