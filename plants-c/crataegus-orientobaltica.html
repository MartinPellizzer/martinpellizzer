Crataegus orientobaltica

Crataegus orientobaltica, commonly known as the Eastern Baltic hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are a food source for various birds and wildlife.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
