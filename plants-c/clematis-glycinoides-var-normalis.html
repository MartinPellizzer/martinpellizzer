Clematis glycinoides var. normalis

Clematis glycinoides var. normalis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, often with a white center, during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.
