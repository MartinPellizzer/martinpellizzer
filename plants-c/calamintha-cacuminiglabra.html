Calamintha cacuminiglabra

Calamintha cacuminiglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a fragrant scent when crushed.

Calamintha cacuminiglabra is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
