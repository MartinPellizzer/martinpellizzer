Camelina sativa var. glabrata

Camelina sativa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth camelina, is a variant of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and reduced flowering structures compared to the typical variety. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types. It produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its high oil content, which is used in both culinary and industrial applications.

The glabrata variant is often cultivated for its resilience and low input requirements in agricultural settings.
