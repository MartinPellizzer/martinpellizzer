Crataegus × deltoxyacantha

Crataegus × deltoxyacantha, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
