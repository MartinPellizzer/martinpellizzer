Callista struthioliflora

Callista struthioliflora, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Callista struthioliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in ecological restoration projects.
