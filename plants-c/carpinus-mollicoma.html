Carpinus mollicoma

Carpinus mollicoma, commonly known as the silky carpin, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, alders, and hazels. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in early spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
