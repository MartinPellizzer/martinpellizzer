Chrysoxylon speciosum

Chrysoxylon speciosum, commonly known as the golden wood or golden sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and golden color. The plant has a straight trunk with smooth, grayish-brown bark that often exudes a resinous scent. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Chrysoxylon speciosum is also known for its small, yellowish flowers and spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.
