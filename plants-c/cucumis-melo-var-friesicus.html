Cucumis melo var. friesicus

Cucumis melo var. friesicus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivated variety of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruits with a thin, edible skin and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant is a vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow and are pollinated by insects, which is essential for fruit development.

This variety is often grown for its edible cucumbers, which are used in various culinary applications.
