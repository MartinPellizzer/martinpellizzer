Cereus duledevantii

Cereus duledevantii, commonly known as the Duledevantii cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
