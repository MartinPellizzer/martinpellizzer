Camphora griffithii var. lanceolata

Camphora griffithii var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor laurel native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and aromatic wood that contains camphor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations. Its leaves are opposite and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
