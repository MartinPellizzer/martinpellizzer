Clematis reticulata

Clematis reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.
