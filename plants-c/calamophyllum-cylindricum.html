Calamophyllum cylindricum

Calamophyllum cylindricum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a coastal ornamental plant.

Calamophyllum cylindricum is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
