Crotalaria singuliflora

Crotalaria singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flower crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive single flower per stem. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, posing a challenge to native plant communities.
