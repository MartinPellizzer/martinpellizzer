Chamaeraphis italica var. germanica

Chamaeraphis italica var. germanica, commonly known as the German sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near the shore. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

This species is notable for its ability to withstand saline and wind conditions, making it a characteristic component of maritime ecosystems.
