Cheilanthes nealleyi var. mexicana

Cheilanthes nealleyi var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican rock fern, is a species of fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant has thick, leathery fronds that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This variety is often found in limestone outcrops and is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience.
