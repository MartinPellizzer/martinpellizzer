Cissus smilacina

Cissus smilacina, commonly known as the creeping goosefoot, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Cissus smilacina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
