Cyanus nigrescens

Cyanus nigrescens, commonly known as the blackened blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh mountain environment.

Cyanus nigrescens is known for its resilience to cold and arid conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
