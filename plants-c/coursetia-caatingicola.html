Coursetia caatingicola

Coursetia caatingicola, commonly known as the Caatinga coursetia, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, featuring deep roots and a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration in arid environments.
