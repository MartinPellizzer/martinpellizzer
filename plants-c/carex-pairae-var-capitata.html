Carex pairae var. capitata

Carex pairae var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in dense, grass-like clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
