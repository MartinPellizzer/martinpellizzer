Cirsium sieboldii f. leucanthum

Cirsium sieboldii f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered thistle, is a horticultural form of the common thistle.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or blue hues of the species. The plant is a perennial herb with spiny leaves and a robust root system, typical of the Cirsium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display and unique foliage.
