Chamaenerion crassifolium var. kobstanicum

Chamaenerion crassifolium var. kobstanicum, commonly known as the Kobstan fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the summer, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a distinctive feature of its native habitat.
