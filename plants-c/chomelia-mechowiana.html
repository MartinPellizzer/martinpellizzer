Chomelia mechowiana

Chomelia mechowiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The flowers are small and yellow, clustered in spikes, and the plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
