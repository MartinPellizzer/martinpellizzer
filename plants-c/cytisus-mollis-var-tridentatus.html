Cytisus mollis var. tridentatus

Cytisus mollis var. tridentatus, commonly known as the thorned broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Fabaceae family.
