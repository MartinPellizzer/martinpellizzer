Chamaecrista depauperata

Chamaecrista depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
