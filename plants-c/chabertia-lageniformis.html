Chabertia lageniformis

Chabertia lageniformis, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like seed pods that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
