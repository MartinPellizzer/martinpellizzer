Crepis armena

Crepis armena, commonly known as Armenian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often spoon-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis armena is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
