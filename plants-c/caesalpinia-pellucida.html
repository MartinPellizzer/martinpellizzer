Caesalpinia pellucida

Caesalpinia pellucida, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
