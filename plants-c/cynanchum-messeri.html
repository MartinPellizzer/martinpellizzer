Cynanchum messeri

Cynanchum messeri, commonly known as the Desert Milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Cynanchum messeri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.
