Celtis hamiltonii

Celtis hamiltonii, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis hamiltonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
