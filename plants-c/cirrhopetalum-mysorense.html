Cirrhopetalum mysorense

Cirrhopetalum mysorense, commonly known as the Mysore bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
