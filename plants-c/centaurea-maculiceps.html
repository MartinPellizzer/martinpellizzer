Centaurea maculiceps

Centaurea maculiceps, commonly known as the spotted headspeed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers with a distinctive black spot at the base of each petal. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea maculiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
