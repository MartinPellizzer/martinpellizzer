Cissus verticillata subsp. laciniata

Cissus verticillata subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
