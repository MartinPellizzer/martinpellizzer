Carapa gummiflua

Carapa gummiflua, commonly known as the rubberwood tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is valued for its latex-producing capabilities, which can be tapped to yield natural rubber. The plant belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes other economically important trees. Its bark and seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
