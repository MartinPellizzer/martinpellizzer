Cranichis beckii

Cranichis beckii, commonly known as the Beck's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in cloud forests or alpine meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it has a short flowering period, making it a challenging species to study.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Cranichis beckii is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
