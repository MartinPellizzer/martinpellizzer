Citrus limetta var. vulgaris

Citrus limetta var. vulgaris, commonly known as the lemon.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a yellow-green rind and a high concentration of citric acid. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and opposite, while the flowers are fragrant and white. The fruit is typically harvested before it is fully mature to ensure optimal acidity and flavor.

It is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
