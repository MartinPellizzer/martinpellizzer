Cedrus atlantica f. argentea

Cedrus atlantica f. argentea, commonly known as the silver-blue cedar, is a unique cultivar of the Atlantic cedar.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The leaves are needle-like and closely spaced, contributing to its dense, compact growth habit. This variety is typically grown as a shrub or small tree, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design for its ornamental qualities.
