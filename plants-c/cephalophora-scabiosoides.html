Cephalophora scabiosoides

Cephalophora scabiosoides, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
