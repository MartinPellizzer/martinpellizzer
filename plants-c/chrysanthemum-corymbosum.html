Chrysanthemum corymbosum

Chrysanthemum corymbosum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink, purple, or white, and they attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
