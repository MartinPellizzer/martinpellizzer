Cibotium × heleniae

Cibotium × heleniae, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cibotium decurrens and Cibotium lanigerum.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific, including Hawaii and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
