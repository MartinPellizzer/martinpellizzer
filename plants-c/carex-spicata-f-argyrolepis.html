Carex spicata f. argyrolepis

Carex spicata f. argyrolepis, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white sheen on the leaf blades, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This ornamental plant is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
