Calydorea charruana

Calydorea charruana, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, giving it its common name. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil in its natural habitat.

Calydorea charruana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
