Coryphantha sneedii var. villardii

Coryphantha sneedii var. villardii, commonly known as the Villa's prickly pear, is a rare and succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It features a low-growing, cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
