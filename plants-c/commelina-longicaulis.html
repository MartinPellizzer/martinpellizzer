Commelina longicaulis

Commelina longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to several feet in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Commelina longicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
