Carex nematosperma

Carex nematosperma, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex nematosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
