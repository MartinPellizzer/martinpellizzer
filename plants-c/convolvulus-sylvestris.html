Convolvulus sylvestris

Convolvulus sylvestris, commonly known as wild morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
