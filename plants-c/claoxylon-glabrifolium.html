Claoxylon glabrifolium

Claoxylon glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, dark green, smooth leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durability and resistance to pests. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
