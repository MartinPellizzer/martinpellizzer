Canna tuerckheimii

Canna tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, strap-like leaves and showy, spadix-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often a deep green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful inflorescences.

Canna tuerckheimii thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
