Crotalaria athroophylla

Crotalaria athroophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant has hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
