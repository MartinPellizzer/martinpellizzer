Carex rochebrunei subsp. remotispicula

Carex rochebrunei subsp.

remotispicula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, typically green or brown flowers.

This subspecies is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Vietnam and Cambodia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
