Cieca auriculata

Cieca auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cieca, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate leaves, which have ear-like lobes at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
