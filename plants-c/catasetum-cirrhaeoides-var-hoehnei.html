Catasetum cirrhaeoides var. hoehnei

Catasetum cirrhaeoides var. hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces specialized pollinia, which are adapted for attachment to specific insect pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
