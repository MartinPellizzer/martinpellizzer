Cineraria tharsoidea

Cineraria tharsoidea, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Cineraria tharsoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
