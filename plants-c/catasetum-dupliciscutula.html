Catasetum dupliciscutula

Catasetum dupliciscutula, commonly known as the double-skin orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific wasps. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum dupliciscutula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
