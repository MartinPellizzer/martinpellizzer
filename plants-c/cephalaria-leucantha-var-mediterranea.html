Cephalaria leucantha var. mediterranea

Cephalaria leucantha var. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clustered, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
