Croton artibonitensis

Croton artibonitensis, commonly known as the Artibonite croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many members of the family.

The plant's sap is milky and can be toxic, which is a common trait among Euphorbiaceae species.
