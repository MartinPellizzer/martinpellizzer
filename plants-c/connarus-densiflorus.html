Connarus densiflorus

Connarus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower connarus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has a fibrous, woody trunk with a smooth, pale bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
