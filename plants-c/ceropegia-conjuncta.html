Ceropegia conjuncta

Ceropegia conjuncta, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia conjuncta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
