Callicarpa pentandra f. pentamera

Callicarpa pentandra f. pentamera, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a key feature of its botanical appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are medium green and turn red in autumn. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically pink or white, appearing in the summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and seasonal color changes.
