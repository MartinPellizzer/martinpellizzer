Cardamine engelmanniana

Cardamine engelmanniana, commonly known as Engelmann's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has small, alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are bright yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
