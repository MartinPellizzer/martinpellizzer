Capparis canescens

Capparis canescens, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, pungent scent. The plant's leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in traditional contexts.
