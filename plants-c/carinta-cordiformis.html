Carinta cordiformis

Carinta cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped fern, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fronds.

This plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. Its foliage consists of delicate, lobed leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Carinta cordiformis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
