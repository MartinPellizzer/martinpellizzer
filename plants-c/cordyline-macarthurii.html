Cordyline macarthurii

Cordyline macarthurii, commonly known as the MacArthur's cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters at the tips of its stems, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
