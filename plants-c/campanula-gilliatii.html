Campanula gilliatii

Campanula gilliatii, commonly known as the Gilliat's bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Campanula gilliatii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
