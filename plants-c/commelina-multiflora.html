Commelina multiflora

Commelina multiflora, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue or purple flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by a white or pale blue spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Commelina multiflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed.
