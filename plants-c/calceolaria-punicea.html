Calceolaria punicea

Calceolaria punicea, commonly known as the scarlet cup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, resembling small cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calceolaria punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
