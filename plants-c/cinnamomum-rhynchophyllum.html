Cinnamomum rhynchophyllum

Cinnamomum rhynchophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum rhynchophyllum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-10 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its bark is rich in essential oils, which are responsible for its distinctive cinnamon-like aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in medicine and as a spice.
