Cyperus conspicuus

Cyperus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus conspicuus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it forms dense vegetation.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
