Colchicum greuteri

Colchicum greuteri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-elevation habitats, such as meadows and open forests. The plant features a bulbous stem and produces striking violet or purple flowers in the spring. Its leaves are broad and basal, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Colchicum greuteri is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
