Croton baliospermus

Croton baliospermus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, rough bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
