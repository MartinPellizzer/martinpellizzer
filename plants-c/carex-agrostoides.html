Carex agrostoides

Carex agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spike-like flowers. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
