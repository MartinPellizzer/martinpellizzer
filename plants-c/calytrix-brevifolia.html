Calytrix brevifolia

Calytrix brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeycomb bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense, honeycomb-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Calytrix brevifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
