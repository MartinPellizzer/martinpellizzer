Clematis filifera var. incisa

Clematis filifera var. incisa, commonly known as the fragrant clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil. It is known for its vigorous growth and can reach lengths of up to 20 feet.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
