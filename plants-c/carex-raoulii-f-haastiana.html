Carex raoulii f. haastiana

Carex raoulii f. haastiana, commonly known as the Haast's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the spring and summer months.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment.
