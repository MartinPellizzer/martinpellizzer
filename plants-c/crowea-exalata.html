Crowea exalata

Crowea exalata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crowea exalata is also notable for being the floral emblem of the state of New South Wales.
