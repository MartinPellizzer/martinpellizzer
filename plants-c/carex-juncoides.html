Carex juncoides

Carex juncoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds.

Carex juncoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
