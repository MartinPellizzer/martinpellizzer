Canthium barbatum var. korrorense

Canthium barbatum var. korrorense, commonly known as the hairy spurge or korrorense spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the genus Canthium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
