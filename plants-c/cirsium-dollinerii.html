Cirsium dollinerii

Cirsium dollinerii, commonly known as Dolliner's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
