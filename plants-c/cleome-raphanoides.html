Cleome raphanoides

Cleome raphanoides, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, resembling spider-like structures, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have toothed margins.

Cleome raphanoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display and ease of growth.
