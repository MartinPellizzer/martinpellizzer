Calamus travancoricus

Calamus travancoricus, commonly known as the Travancore calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of southern India, particularly in the Kerala region. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
