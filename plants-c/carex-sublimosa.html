Carex sublimosa

Carex sublimosa, commonly known as the skyward sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex sublimosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
