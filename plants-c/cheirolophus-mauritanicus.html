Cheirolophus mauritanicus

Cheirolophus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
