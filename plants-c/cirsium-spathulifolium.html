Cirsium spathulifolium

Cirsium spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Cirsium spathulifolium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
