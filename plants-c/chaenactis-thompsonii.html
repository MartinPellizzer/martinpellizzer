Chaenactis thompsonii

Chaenactis thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's frost flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high elevation areas with limited soil nutrients.
