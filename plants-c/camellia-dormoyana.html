Camellia dormoyana

Camellia dormoyana, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Camellia dormoyana prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
