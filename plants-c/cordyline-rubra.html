Cordyline rubra

Cordyline rubra, commonly known as the red cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink, which appear at the top of the stem. Cordyline rubra is well-suited for warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor gardening.
