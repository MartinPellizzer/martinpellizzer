Carex luzulifolia var. ablta

Carex luzulifolia var. ablta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
