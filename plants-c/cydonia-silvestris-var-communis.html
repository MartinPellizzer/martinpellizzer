Cydonia silvestris var. communis

Cydonia silvestris var. communis, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces edible, sweet fruit that ripens in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a distinctive, rough bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by small, green fruits that mature to a pale yellow or red.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
