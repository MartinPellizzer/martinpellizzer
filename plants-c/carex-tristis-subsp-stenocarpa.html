Carex tristis subsp. stenocarpa

Carex tristis subsp.

stenocarpa, commonly known as narrow-capsule sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the top of the stems. It prefers moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its narrow, cylindrical capsules are a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
