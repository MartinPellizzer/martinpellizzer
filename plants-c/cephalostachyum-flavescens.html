Cephalostachyum flavescens

Cephalostachyum flavescens, commonly known as the golden spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscaping projects.
