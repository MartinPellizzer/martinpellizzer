Centaurea amara var. virens

Centaurea amara var. virens, commonly known as bitterweed or green centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Centaurea amara var. virens is known for its bitter taste and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
