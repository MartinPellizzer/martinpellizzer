Croton longinervius var. major

Croton longinervius var. major, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins.

It originates from the Philippines and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage. The leaves can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, displaying a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is notable for its unique leaf structure and is a popular choice in indoor and outdoor landscaping.
