Cactus hexasanthus

Cactus hexasanthus, commonly known as the six-flowered cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters of six, giving it its common name.

The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
