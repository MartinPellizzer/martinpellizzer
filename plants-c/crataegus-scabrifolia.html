Crataegus scabrifolia

Crataegus scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rugged, scabrous leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Crataegus scabrifolia plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
