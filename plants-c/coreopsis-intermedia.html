Coreopsis intermedia

Coreopsis intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.

Coreopsis intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
