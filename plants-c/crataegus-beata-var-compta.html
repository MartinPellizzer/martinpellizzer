Crataegus beata var. compta

Crataegus beata var. compta, commonly known as the Compta hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often bitter, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and seasonal interest.
