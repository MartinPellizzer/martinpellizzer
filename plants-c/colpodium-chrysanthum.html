Colpodium chrysanthum

Colpodium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Colpodium chrysanthum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy environments.
