Chamaecrista choriophylla var. latifolia

Chamaecrista choriophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow,蝶形花 (butterfly-shaped) flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil restoration and erosion control.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance contribute to its ecological and ornamental significance.
