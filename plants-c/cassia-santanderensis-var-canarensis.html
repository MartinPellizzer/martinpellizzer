Cassia santanderensis var. canarensis

Cassia santanderensis var. canarensis, commonly known as the Canarian cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats, showing adaptation to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
