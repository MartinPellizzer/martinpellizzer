Chelonopsis longipes

Chelonopsis longipes, commonly known as the long-footed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated stems and spiny appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
