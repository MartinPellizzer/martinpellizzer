Convolvulus sphaerophorus

Convolvulus sphaerophorus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
