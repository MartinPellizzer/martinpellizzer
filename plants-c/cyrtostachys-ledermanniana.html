Cyrtostachys ledermanniana

Cyrtostachys ledermanniana, commonly known as the Ledermann's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female plants. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important subject in botanical research.
