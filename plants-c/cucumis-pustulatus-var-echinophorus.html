Cucumis pustulatus var. echinophorus

Cucumis pustulatus var. echinophorus, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a rare and distinctive variety of the cucumber genus.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny skin and small, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to survive in harsh conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lobed leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and potential for cultivation in specific environments.
