Calliandra humilis var. gentryana

Calliandra humilis var. gentryana, commonly known as the Gentry's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, papery bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a delicate, fern-like pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
