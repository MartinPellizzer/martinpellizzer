Carduus illyricus

Carduus illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with spiny leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. The plant produces large, showy purple flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming from late summer to autumn. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette at the base, and the plant is known for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.

Carduus illyricus is often found in dry grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
