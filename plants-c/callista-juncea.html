Callista juncea

Callista juncea, commonly known as the slender callista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in a cylindrical shape, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Callista juncea is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
