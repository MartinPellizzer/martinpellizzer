Cissus palmatifida var. integrifolia

Cissus palmatifida var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cissus, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other varieties of the genus. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its stems are succulent and capable of rooting at nodes, aiding in its climbing habit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
