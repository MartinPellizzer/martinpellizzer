Cynanchum ophiticola

Cynanchum ophiticola, commonly known as the snake's head milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive central structure called a gynostemon.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
