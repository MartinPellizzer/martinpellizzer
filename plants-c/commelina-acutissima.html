Commelina acutissima

Commelina acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Commelina acutissima is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
