Carduus litigiosus subsp. litigiosus

Carduus litigiosus subsp.

litigiosus, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The plant features a robust, upright stem with numerous leafy branches, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flower heads are surrounded by bracts and are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
