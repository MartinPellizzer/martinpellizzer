Carex acuta var. pallida

Carex acuta var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
