Carex × rikuchiuensis

Carex × rikuchiuensis, commonly known as the Rikuchiu sedge, is a hybrid plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
