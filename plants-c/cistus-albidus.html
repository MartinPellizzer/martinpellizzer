Cistus albidus

Cistus albidus, commonly known as white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus albidus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
