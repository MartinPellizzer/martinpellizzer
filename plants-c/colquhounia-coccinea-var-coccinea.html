Colquhounia coccinea var. coccinea

Colquhounia coccinea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered false nettle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and forested areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
