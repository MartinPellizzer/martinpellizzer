Carlina biebersteinii var. fennica

Carlina biebersteinii var. fennica, commonly known as the Finnish cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
