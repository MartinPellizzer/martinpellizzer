Capparis macrocarpa

Capparis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant's fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often in areas with high salt exposure.

The leaves are typically succulent, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate arid conditions.
