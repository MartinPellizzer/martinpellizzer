Coronilla coccinea

Coronilla coccinea, commonly known as red vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a soil stabilizer.

Its vibrant color makes it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
