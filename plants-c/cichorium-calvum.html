Cichorium calvum

Cichorium calvum, commonly known as the naked chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces a distinctive, bitter-tasting root that is used in traditional herbal medicine. Cichorium calvum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
