Cuphea multicaulis

Cuphea multicaulis, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and compact, whorled arrangements of leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Cuphea multicaulis is well-suited for containers and garden beds, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
