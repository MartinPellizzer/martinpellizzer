Cymbopogon gratus

Cymbopogon gratus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, silver-gray blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their attractive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Cymbopogon gratus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
