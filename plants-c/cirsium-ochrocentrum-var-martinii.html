Cirsium ochrocentrum var. martinii

Cirsium ochrocentrum var. martinii, commonly known as the Martin's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
