Chrysosplenium baicalense var. lectus-cochleae

Chrysosplenium baicalense var. lectus-cochleae, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to specialized gardens.
