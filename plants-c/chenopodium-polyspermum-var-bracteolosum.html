Chenopodium polyspermum var. bracteolosum

Chenopodium polyspermum var. bracteolosum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tart flavor and are used in traditional cuisines.
