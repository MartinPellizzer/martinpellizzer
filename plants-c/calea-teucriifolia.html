Calea teucriifolia

Calea teucriifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are tubular and have a unique structure that facilitates pollination by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Calea teucriifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
