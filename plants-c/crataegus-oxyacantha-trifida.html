Crataegus oxyacantha trifida

Crataegus oxyacantha trifida, commonly known as the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
