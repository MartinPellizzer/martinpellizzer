Calorophus lateriflorus

Calorophus lateriflorus, commonly known as the sideflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their lateral arrangement, giving the plant its specific epithet "lateriflorus.".

Calorophus lateriflorus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
