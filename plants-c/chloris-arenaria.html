Chloris arenaria

Chloris arenaria, commonly known as sand lovegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Australia.

It thrives in sandy and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and arid ecosystems.
