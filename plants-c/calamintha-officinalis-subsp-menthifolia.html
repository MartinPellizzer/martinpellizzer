Calamintha officinalis subsp. menthifolia

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

menthifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
