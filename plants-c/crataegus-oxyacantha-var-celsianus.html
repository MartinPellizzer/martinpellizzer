Crataegus oxyacantha var. celsianus

Crataegus oxyacantha var. celsianus, commonly known as the Celsian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often sour.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.
