Centaurea saxicola var. cervantesi

Centaurea saxicola var. cervantesi, commonly known as the Cervantes' centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny, well-drained habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
