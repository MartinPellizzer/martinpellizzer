Cupressus tonkinensis

Cupressus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage that provides a striking visual contrast against its bark. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it has scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in local ecosystems.
