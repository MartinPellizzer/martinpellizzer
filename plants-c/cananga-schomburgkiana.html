Cananga schomburgkiana

Cananga schomburgkiana, commonly known as the white champaca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.
