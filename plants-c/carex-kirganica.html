Carex kirganica

Carex kirganica, commonly known as Kirgan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex kirganica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
