Cousinia hissarica

Cousinia hissarica, commonly known as the Persian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Cousinia hissarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
