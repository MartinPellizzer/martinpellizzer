Campanula propinqua var. grandiflora

Campanula propinqua var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
