Cichorium intybus subsp. foliosum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white vein pattern.

The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
