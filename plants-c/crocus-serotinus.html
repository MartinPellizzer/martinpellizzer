Crocus serotinus

Crocus serotinus, commonly known as the late crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is well adapted to mild climates.

Crocus serotinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in early pollination by bees and other insects.
