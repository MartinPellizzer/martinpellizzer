Cylindropus junciformis

Cylindropus junciformis, commonly known as the slender rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cylindropus junciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
