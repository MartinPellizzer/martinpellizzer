Cerbera parviflora

Cerbera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cerbera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a waxy texture.

Cerbera parviflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
