Calamina sehima

Calamina sehima, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a slightly rough texture.

Calamina sehima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
