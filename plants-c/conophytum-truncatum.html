Conophytum truncatum

Conophytum truncatum, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" or "Cape Harefoot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The leaves are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge, and they have a unique, pointed shape that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

This hardy plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
