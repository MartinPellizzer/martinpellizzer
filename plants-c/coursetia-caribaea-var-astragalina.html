Coursetia caribaea var. astragalina

Coursetia caribaea var. astragalina, commonly known as the Caribbean coursetia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of many species in its family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical environments.
