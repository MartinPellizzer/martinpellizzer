Cymbidium macrorhizon

Cymbidium macrorhizon, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant colors such as pink, red, and white.

Cymbidium macrorhizon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
