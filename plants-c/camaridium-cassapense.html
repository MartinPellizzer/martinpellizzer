Camaridium cassapense

Camaridium cassapense, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or purple. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits, making it a plant of caution.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
