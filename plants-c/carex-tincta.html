Carex tincta

Carex tincta, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Carex tincta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its striking blue-green foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
