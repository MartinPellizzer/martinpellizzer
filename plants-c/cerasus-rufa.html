Cerasus rufa

Cerasus rufa, commonly known as the red cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, red, tart fruits that are often used in cooking and baking. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young.

Cerasus rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
