Chionanthus bakeri

Chionanthus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
