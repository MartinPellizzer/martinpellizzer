Croton ferrugineus var. genuinus

Croton ferrugineus var. genuinus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique, branching structure. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a velvety texture that enhances its visual appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.
