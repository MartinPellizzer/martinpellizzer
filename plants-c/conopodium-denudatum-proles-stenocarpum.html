Conopodium denudatum proles stenocarpum

Conopodium denudatum proles stenocarpum, commonly known as the slender-fruited bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat preference, often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are typically reduced to thread-like structures, while the stems are often branched and floating.

The species is notable for its unique adaptation to nutrient-poor environments through its carnivorous behavior.
