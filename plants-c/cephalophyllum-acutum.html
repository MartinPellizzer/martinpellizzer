Cephalophyllum acutum

Cephalophyllum acutum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cephalophyllum, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. Its growth pattern is compact, with a low profile that helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in rocky or sandy soils.
