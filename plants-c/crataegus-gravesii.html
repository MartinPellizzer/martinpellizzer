Crataegus gravesii

Crataegus gravesii, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Crataegus gravesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native range.
