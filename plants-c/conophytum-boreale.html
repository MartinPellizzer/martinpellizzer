Conophytum boreale

Conophytum boreale, commonly known as the snowflake stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
