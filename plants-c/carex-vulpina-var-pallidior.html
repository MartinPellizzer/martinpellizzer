Carex vulpina var. pallidior

Carex vulpina var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and typically have a pale green to silver-gray coloration, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
