Crotalaria bakeriana

Crotalaria bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
