Capsicum villosum var. muticum

Capsicum villosum var. muticum, commonly known as the hairy wild pepper.

This variant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. It is a wild species native to Central and South America, often found in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically not cultivated for consumption.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other pepper species.
