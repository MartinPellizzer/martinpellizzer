Coreopsis drummondii var. wrightii

Coreopsis drummondii var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a yellow center and white petals, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
