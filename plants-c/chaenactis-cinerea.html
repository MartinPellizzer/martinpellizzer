Chaenactis cinerea

Chaenactis cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or arid regions. Its silver-gray leaves are covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
