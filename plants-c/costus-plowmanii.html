Costus plowmanii

Costus plowmanii, commonly known as the golden costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble spikes of flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Costus plowmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
