Crocus reticulatus f. pluriflorus

Crocus reticulatus f. pluriflorus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a botanical variety characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers.

It is a bulbous perennial that typically blooms in early spring, showcasing clusters of fragrant, petal-like bracts. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and early blooming habit.
