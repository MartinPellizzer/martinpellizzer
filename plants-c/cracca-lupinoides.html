Cracca lupinoides

Cracca lupinoides, commonly known as the yellow lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cracca lupinoides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
