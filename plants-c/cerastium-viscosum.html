Cerastium viscosum

Cerastium viscosum, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, often sticky stems and leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium viscosum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
