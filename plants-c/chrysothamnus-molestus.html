Chrysothamnus molestus

Chrysothamnus molestus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
