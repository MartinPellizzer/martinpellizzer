Convolvulus nodiflorus

Convolvulus nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has twining stems that coil around supports, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
