Cytisus hirsutus subsp. ciliatus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, often hairy stems.

The subspecies ciliatus is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and slightly hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
