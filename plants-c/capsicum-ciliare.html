Capsicum ciliare

Capsicum ciliare, commonly known as the hairy pepper or ciliates pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fiery red or yellow fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and the fruits are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.

Capsicum ciliare is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
