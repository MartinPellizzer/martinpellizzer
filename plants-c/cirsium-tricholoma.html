Cirsium tricholoma

Cirsium tricholoma, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a rosette of basal leaves.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
