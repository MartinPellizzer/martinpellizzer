Campanula longibracteata

Campanula longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
