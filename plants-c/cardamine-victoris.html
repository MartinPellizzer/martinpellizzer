Cardamine victoris

Cardamine victoris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils at high elevations. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
