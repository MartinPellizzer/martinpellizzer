Cyrtochilum diceratum

Cyrtochilum diceratum, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Cyrtochilum diceratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
