Cathayanthe

Cathayanthe, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cathayanthe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
