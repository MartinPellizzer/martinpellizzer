Cassia duckeana

Cassia duckeana, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, rectangular seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and grasslands.

Cassia duckeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
