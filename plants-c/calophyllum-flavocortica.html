Calophyllum flavocortica

Calophyllum flavocortica, commonly known as the yellow-cork calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark that peels off in thin layers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with prominent veins.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
