Crepidium grandifolium

Crepidium grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-white to yellowish flowers with prominent lips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and is often found in colonies.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
