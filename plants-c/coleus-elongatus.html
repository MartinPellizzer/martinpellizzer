Coleus elongatus

Coleus elongatus, commonly known as the elongated coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus elongatus prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
