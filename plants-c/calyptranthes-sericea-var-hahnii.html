Calyptranthes sericea var. hahnii

Calyptranthes sericea var. hahnii, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
