Chylodia sarmentosa

Chylodia sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping chylodia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
