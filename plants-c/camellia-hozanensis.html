Camellia hozanensis

Camellia hozanensis, commonly known as the Hozan camellia, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, double pink flowers in the spring. The plant belongs to the Theaceae family and is closely related to tea plants. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Camellia hozanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
