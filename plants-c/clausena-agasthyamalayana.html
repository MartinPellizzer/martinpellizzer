Clausena agasthyamalayana

Clausena agasthyamalayana, commonly known as the Nilgiri tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or red when ripe, and contains a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The plant is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and traditional herbal practices.
