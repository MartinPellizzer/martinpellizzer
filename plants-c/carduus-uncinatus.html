Carduus uncinatus

Carduus uncinatus, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and has been naturalized in many other regions. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
