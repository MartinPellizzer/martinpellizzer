Convolvulus longipedunculatus

Convolvulus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

This species is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
