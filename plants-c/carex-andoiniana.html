Carex andoiniana

Carex andoiniana, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex andoiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
