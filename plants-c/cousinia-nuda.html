Cousinia nuda

Cousinia nuda, commonly known as the naked cousinia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers make it a distinctive species in its native habitat.

Cousinia nuda is often found in steppes and mountainous areas where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
