Campanula garganica subsp. garganica

Campanula garganica subsp.

garganica, commonly known as the Gargano bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
