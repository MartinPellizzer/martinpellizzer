Conyza pumila

Conyza pumila, commonly known as Littleleaf Ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

Conyza pumila produces tiny white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
