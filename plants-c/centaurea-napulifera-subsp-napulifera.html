Centaurea napulifera subsp. napulifera

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

napulifera, commonly known as the Naples star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are adaptations to its arid environment.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular garden plant, though it can become invasive in some regions.
