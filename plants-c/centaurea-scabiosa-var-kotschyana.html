Centaurea scabiosa var. kotschyana

Centaurea scabiosa var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of purple to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
