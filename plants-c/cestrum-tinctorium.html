Cestrum tinctorium

Cestrum tinctorium, commonly known as the red-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its fragrant flowers and vibrant colors.

Cestrum tinctorium thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
