Cerastium scammaniae

Cerastium scammaniae, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
