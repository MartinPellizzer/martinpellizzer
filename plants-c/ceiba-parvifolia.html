Ceiba parvifolia

Ceiba parvifolia, commonly known as the white ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
