Celtis bainingensis

Celtis bainingensis, commonly known as the Baining fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cannaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ecological role and potential economic uses.
