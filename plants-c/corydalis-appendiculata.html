Corydalis appendiculata

Corydalis appendiculata, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Corydalis appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
