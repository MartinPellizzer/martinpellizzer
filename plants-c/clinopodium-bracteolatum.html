Clinopodium bracteolatum

Clinopodium bracteolatum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to mesic habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma when crushed.

It produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
