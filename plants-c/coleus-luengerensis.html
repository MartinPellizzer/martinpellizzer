Coleus luengerensis

Coleus luengerensis, commonly known as the Luengeren coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often ornamental, displaying a variety of colors such as red, purple, and green.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
