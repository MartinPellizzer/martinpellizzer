Cornus sanguinea f. atrosanguinea

Cornus sanguinea f. atrosanguinea, commonly known as black-stemmed dogwood, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to black stems, which contrast with its bright green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
