Cassia septemtrionalis

Cassia septemtrionalis, commonly known as the northern cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Cassia septemtrionalis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
