Cardamine Douglassii

Cardamine douglassii is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest floors or along rocky stream banks. This species is known for its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival. Its unique beauty and ecological importance make it a valuable subject for botanical study and preservation.
