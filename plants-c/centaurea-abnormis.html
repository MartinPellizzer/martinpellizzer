Centaurea abnormis

Centaurea abnormis, commonly known as the abnormal cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers and bristly, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its height can range from 30 to 100 centimeters, with a compact, upright growth habit.

Centaurea abnormis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.
