Crunocallis calcicola

Crunocallis calcicola, commonly known as the limestone crunocallis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Crunocallis calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
