Conanthus carnosus

Conanthus carnosus, commonly known as the wooly conanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Conanthus carnosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and delicate blooms.
