Camellia cylindracea

Camellia cylindracea, commonly known as the narrow-cylinder camellia, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, double-flowered blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Camellia cylindracea thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers are particularly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
