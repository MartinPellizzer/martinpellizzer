Coreopsis perfoliata

Coreopsis perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often with a striking red or orange center. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often fused around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to gardens.
