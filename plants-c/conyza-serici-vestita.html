Conyza serici-vestita

Conyza serici-vestita, commonly known as the silky cottonweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features soft, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a silvery appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind pollination.

Conyza serici-vestita is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
