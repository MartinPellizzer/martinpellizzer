Centaurea × kleineana

Centaurea × kleineana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea stoebe (sneakerweed). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring blue to purple composite flower heads with bright yellow centers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea × kleineana is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
