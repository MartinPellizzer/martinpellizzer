Cestrum weberbaueri

Cestrum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Cestrum weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
