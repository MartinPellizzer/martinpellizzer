Crepidium euanthum

Crepidium euanthum, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Crepidium euanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
