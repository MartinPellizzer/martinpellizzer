Charianthus brasiliensis

Charianthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it is not commonly found in horticultural trade outside of its native range.
