Crataegus lenneana

Crataegus lenneana, commonly known as the Lenné hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Crataegus lenneana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
