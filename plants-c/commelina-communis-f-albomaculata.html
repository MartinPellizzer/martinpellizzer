Commelina communis f. albomaculata

Commelina communis f. albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted spiderwort, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers with white spots, which are borne in clusters at the leaf axils. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

The white-spotted spiderwort is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
