Cyperus alternifolius var. obtusangulum

Cyperus alternifolius var. obtusangulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in saturated soils. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
