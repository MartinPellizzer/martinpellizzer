Crotalaria schimperi

Crotalaria schimperi, commonly known as the African cowpea or Schimper's horsegram, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, ridged pods and are used as a leguminous crop in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
