Capparis membranacea var. angustissima

Capparis membranacea var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf prickly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Indigenous Australian cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
