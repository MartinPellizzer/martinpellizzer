Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. dickinsii

Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. dickinsii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
