Campanula albiflora

Campanula albiflora, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Campanula albiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
