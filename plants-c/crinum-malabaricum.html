Crinum malabaricum

Crinum malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar arum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles an arum. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinct spathe and spadix structure.

Crinum malabaricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
