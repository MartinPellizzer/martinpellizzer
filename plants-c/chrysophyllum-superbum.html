Chrysophyllum superbum

Chrysophyllum superbum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their various therapeutic properties.
