Cirsium pubigerum var. glomeratum

Cirsium pubigerum var. glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small flowers, which are surrounded by bracts.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
