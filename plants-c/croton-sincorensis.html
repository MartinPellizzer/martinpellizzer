Croton sincorensis

Croton sincorensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which vary in shape from ovate to elliptic. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.
