Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea var. aurea

Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a compact, evergreen conifer with a distinctive rounded form.

Its foliage is dense and features golden-yellow needles that contrast beautifully with its dark bark. Native to Japan, this variety thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.

The plant is relatively slow-growing and requires minimal pruning to maintain its natural shape.
