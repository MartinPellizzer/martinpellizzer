Cotyledon meyeri

Cotyledon meyeri, commonly known as the "Lamb's Ear" plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, upright flower spikes in shades of pink, red, or orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Cotyledon meyeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
