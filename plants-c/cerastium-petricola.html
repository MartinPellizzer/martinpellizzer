Cerastium petricola

Cerastium petricola, commonly known as the rock chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

Cerastium petricola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes in mountainous regions.
