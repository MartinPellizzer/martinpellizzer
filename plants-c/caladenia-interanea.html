Caladenia interanea

Caladenia interanea, commonly known as the Western Plains Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a single, slender stem that produces one or two narrow, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, from late summer to early autumn. Its flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals.

Caladenia interanea is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heath.
