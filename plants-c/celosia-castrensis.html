Celosia castrensis

Celosia castrensis, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful, modified leaves. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in various climates.

Celosia castrensis is also known for its resilience, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
