Cyathoglottis crocea

Cyathoglottis crocea, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Cyathoglottis crocea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
