Cytisus catalaunicus

Cytisus catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Cytisus catalaunicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in coastal and mountainous regions.
