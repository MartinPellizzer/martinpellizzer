Calorophus clavatus

Calorophus clavatus, commonly known as the clustered firecup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are typically hairy and wind-dispersed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
