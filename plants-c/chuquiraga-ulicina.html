Chuquiraga ulicina

Chuquiraga ulicina, commonly known as the Andean burweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and prickly, bristly leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Chuquiraga ulicina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
