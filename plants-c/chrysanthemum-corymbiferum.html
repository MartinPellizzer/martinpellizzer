Chrysanthemum corymbiferum

Chrysanthemum corymbiferum, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features finely divided, feathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical structure includes a compact growth habit and a flowering head composed of ray and disc florets.
