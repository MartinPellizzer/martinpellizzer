Carex austrocaroliniana

Carex austrocaroliniana, commonly known as the Southern Carolina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown, depending on the season.
