Clematis simsii

Clematis simsii, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Clematis simsii is valued for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in suitable climates.
