Cistus ladanifer var. tangerinus

Cistus ladanifer var. tangerinus, commonly known as the tangerine cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous substance called labdanum, which has been historically used in perfumes and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky and arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
