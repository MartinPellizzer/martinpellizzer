Cassia torulosa

Cassia torulosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and the bark is smooth and pale in color.

Cassia torulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
