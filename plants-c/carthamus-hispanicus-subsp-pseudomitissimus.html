Carthamus hispanicus subsp. pseudomitissimus

Carthamus hispanicus subsp.

pseudomitissimus, commonly known as the Spanish safflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, particularly thriving in Mediterranean climates.

The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and robust, branched stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.
