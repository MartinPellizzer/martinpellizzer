Cirsium microspicatum

Cirsium microspicatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It typically grows in grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds for reproduction.
