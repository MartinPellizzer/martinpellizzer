Cyperus dilutus f. decolorans

Cyperus dilutus f. decolorans, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that often display striking variegation. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in water gardens and as a landscape plant.
