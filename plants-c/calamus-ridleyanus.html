Calamus ridleyanus

Calamus ridleyanus, commonly known as the Ridley's canes, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palms. Its roots and rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus ridleyanus thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
