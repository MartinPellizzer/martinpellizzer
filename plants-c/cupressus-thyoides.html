Cupressus thyoides

Cupressus thyoides, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It has scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin, fibrous strips.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
