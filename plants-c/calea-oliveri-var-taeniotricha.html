Calea oliveri var. taeniotricha

Calea oliveri var. taeniotricha, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral behavior, which is triggered by changes in light and temperature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Calea oliveri var. taeniotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its intriguing nocturnal flowering habit.
