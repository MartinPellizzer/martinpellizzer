Chenopodium holopterum

Chenopodium holopterum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, prickly hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

Chenopodium holopterum is adapted to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
