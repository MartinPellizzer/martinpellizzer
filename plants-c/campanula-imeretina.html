Campanula imeretina

Campanula imeretina, commonly known as the Imereti bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
