Calamus javensis subvar. intermedius

Calamus javensis subvar. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate calamus, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical wetlands.
