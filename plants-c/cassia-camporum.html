Cassia camporum

Cassia camporum, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cassia camporum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
