Chenopodium album var. centrorubrum

Chenopodium album var. centrorubrum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its alternate, ovate to deltoid leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in many regions.
