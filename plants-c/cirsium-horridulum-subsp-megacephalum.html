Cirsium horridulum subsp. megacephalum

Cirsium horridulum subsp.

megacephalum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple to pinkish-blue flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its floral structure consists of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its notably enlarged, spherical flower heads compared to other members of the species.
