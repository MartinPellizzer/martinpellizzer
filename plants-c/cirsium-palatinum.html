Cirsium palatinum

Cirsium palatinum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are arranged in a circular pattern, with tubular florets surrounded by bracts that form a prickly involucre.

Cirsium palatinum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
