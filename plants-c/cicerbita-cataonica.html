Cicerbita cataonica

Cicerbita cataonica, commonly known as the Balkan bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Cicerbita cataonica is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
