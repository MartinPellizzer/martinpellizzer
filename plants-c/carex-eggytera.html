Carex eggytera

Carex eggytera, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically broader and more robust than those of true grasses. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often clustered at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex eggytera plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
