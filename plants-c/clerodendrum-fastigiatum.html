Clerodendrum fastigiatum

Clerodendrum fastigiatum, commonly known as the purple flame or flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
