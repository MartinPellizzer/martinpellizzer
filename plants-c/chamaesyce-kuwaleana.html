Chamaesyce kuwaleana

Chamaesyce kuwaleana, commonly known as the Kuwalean sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Hawaii.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils, often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Chamaesyce kuwaleana plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is considered an important component of Hawaii's unique flora.
