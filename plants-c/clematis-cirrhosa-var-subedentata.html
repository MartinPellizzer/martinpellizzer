Clematis cirrhosa var. subedentata

Clematis cirrhosa var. subedentata, commonly known as the curly clematis, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that have serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
