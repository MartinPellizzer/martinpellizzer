Cassia cytisoides var. unijuga

Cassia cytisoides var. unijuga, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets arranged in pairs. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
