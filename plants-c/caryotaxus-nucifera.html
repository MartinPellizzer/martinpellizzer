Caryotaxus nucifera

Caryotaxus nucifera, commonly known as the coconut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The tree produces distinctive coconuts, which contain both water and a white, fleshy pulp. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize coastal soils.

The plant is highly adapted to saline environments and plays a significant role in coastal ecosystems.
