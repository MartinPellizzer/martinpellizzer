Celtis wallichii

Celtis wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, dark blue berries. Celtis wallichii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
