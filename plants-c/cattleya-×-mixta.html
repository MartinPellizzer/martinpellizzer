Cattleya × mixta

Cattleya × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs that support its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a distinctive fragrance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically arranged in two rows around the pseudobulbs.

Cattleya × mixta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is widely cultivated for floral arrangements and horticultural displays.
