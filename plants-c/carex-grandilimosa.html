Carex grandilimosa

Carex grandilimosa, commonly known as the large-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and tapering, with a bluish-green color. Its flowering spike is usually short and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex grandilimosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
