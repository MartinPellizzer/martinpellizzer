Cynorkis papillosa

Cynorkis papillosa, commonly known as the silky orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high rainfall. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or purple, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
