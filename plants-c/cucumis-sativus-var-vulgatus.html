Cucumis sativus var. vulgatus

Cucumis sativus var. vulgatus, commonly known as the common cucumber, is a widely cultivated variant of the cucumber plant.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces elongated, edible fruits that are typically green and contain numerous seeds. The leaves are broad and lobed, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

This variety is valued for its use in fresh consumption, pickling, and as a garnish in various cuisines.
