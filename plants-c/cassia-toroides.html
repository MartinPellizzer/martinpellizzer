Cassia toroides

Cassia toroides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia toroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
