Caesalpinia myabensis

Caesalpinia myabensis, commonly known as the Myabens or the Myabens tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright yellow to orange flower clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many leguminous species.

It is often found in dry to semi-deciduous forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
