Calamagrostis nagensis

Calamagrostis nagensis, commonly known as the Nagasaki reed, is a grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Calamagrostis nagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
