Centrocarpha chrysantha

Centrocarpha chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and a prominent, conical yellow to orange central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centrocarpha chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
