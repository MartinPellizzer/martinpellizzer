Crocus yaseminiae

Crocus yaseminiae, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces striking violet-blue petals with a yellow center, resembling the color of saffron. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils and require a cold winter dormancy period.

Crocus yaseminiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
