Chenopodium karoi

Chenopodium karoi, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source in various indigenous communities.

Chenopodium karoi also plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
