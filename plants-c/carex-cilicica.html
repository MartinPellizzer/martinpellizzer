Carex cilicica

Carex cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Carex cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
