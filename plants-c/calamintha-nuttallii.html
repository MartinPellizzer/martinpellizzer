Calamintha nuttallii

Calamintha nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in herbal remedies.
