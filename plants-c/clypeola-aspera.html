Clypeola aspera

Clypeola aspera, commonly known as the rough clypeola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Clypeola aspera is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
