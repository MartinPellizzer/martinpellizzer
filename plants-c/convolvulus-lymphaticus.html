Convolvulus lymphaticus

Convolvulus lymphaticus, commonly known as the lymphatic convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
