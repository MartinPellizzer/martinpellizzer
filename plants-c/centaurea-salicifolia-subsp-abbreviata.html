Centaurea salicifolia subsp. abbreviata

Centaurea salicifolia subsp.

abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed broomweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its growth habit is typically herbaceous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
