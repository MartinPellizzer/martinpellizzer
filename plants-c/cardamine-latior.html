Cardamine latior

Cardamine latior, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features alternate, trifoliate leaves that are broad and glossy, with a distinctive peppery aroma when crushed. Its small, white or greenish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
