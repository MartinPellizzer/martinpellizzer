Crotalaria rangei

Crotalaria rangei, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and palmately lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Crotalaria rangei typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
