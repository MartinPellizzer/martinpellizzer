Clematis fremontii var. riehlii

Clematis fremontii var. riehlii, commonly known as the Riehli's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, making it a vigorous climber. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability.
