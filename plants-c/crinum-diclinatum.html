Crinum diclinatum

Crinum diclinatum, commonly known as the yellow crinum, is a bulbous herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in a showy inflorescence. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is known for its strong, spicy fragrance.
