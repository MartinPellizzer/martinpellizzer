Commiphora betschuanica

Commiphora betschuanica, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in countries like Botswana and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces a resin that has been historically used for incense and medicinal purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters and is valued for its ecological and cultural significance in the region.
