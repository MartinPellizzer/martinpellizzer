Carex caryophyllea subsp. nervata

Carex caryophyllea subsp.

nervata, commonly known as the nervate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

The subspecies nervata is distinguished by its finely veined leaves and compact growth habit.
