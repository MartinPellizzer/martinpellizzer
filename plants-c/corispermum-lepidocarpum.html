Corispermum lepidocarpum

Corispermum lepidocarpum, commonly known as the smooth bladderwrack, is a small, annual plant belonging to the family Corispermaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in saline or coastal environments. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores of salt lakes and inland seas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
