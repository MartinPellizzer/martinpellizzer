Camellia cuspidata var. cuspidata

Camellia cuspidata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The flowers are borne on short pedicels and have a prominent central stamen, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in humid, cool environments.
