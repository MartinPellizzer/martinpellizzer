Coriandrum maculatum

Coriandrum maculatum, commonly known as spotted coriander, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
