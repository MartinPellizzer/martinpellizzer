Catasetum rionegrense

Catasetum rionegrense, commonly known as the Rio Negro Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with specialized pollination mechanisms, including a pollinium that adheres to the bodies of visiting insects.

Catasetum rionegrense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
