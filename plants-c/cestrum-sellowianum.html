Cestrum sellowianum

Cestrum sellowianum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which typically open at night. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically 5-lobed and emit a scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Cestrum sellowianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates.
