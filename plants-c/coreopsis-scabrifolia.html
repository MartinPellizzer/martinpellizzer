Coreopsis scabrifolia

Coreopsis scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, and it thrives in full sun.

Coreopsis scabrifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
