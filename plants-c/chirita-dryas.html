Chirita dryas

Chirita dryas, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Chirita dryas is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
