Capparis spinosa var. genuina

Capparis spinosa var. genuina, commonly known as the Barbary fig or sour orange, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are tart and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
