Carex gigas

Carex gigas, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Carex gigas thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
