Cleome albescens subsp. omanensis

Cleome albescens subsp.

omanensis, commonly known as the Oman white cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and branching.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
