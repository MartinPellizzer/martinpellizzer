Collomia linearis f. lateritia

Collomia linearis f. lateritia, commonly known as the desert phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
