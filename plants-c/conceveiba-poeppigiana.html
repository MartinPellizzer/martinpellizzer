Conceveiba poeppigiana

Conceveiba poeppigiana, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Conceveiba poeppigiana make it a popular specimen in horticulture and botanical gardens.
