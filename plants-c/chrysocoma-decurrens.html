Chrysocoma decurrens

Chrysocoma decurrens, commonly known as the African wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its legumes, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of dye.
