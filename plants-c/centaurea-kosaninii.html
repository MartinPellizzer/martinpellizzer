Centaurea kosaninii

Centaurea kosaninii, commonly known as Kosanin's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea kosaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
