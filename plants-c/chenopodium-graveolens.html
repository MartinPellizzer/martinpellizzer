Chenopodium graveolens

Chenopodium graveolens, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is often found in disturbed soils, such as gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also has historical uses in traditional medicine and cuisine.
