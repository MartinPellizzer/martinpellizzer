Circaea lutetiana var. canadensis

Circaea lutetiana var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian whitecampion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
