Centaurea × nyaradyana

Centaurea × nyaradyana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Centaurea × nyaradyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
