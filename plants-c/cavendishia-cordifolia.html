Cavendishia cordifolia

Cavendishia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with prominent veins and showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under appropriate conditions.
