Carex depressa var. transsilvanica

Carex depressa var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
