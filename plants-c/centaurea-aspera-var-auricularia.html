Centaurea aspera var. auricularia

Centaurea aspera var. auricularia, commonly known as the auricular centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are bright yellow, with a distinctive purple or reddish central disk, and are arranged in compact heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
