Coffea bourbonica

Coffea bourbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon coffee plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are processed into coffee beans. These beans are known for their distinct flavor profile, often described as having a sweet, floral, and slightly citrusy note.

Coffea bourbonica is prized for its aromatic and complex characteristics, making it a popular choice among coffee connoisseurs.
