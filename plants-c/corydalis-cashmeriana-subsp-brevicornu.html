Corydalis cashmeriana subsp. brevicornu

Corydalis cashmeriana subsp.

brevicornu, commonly known as the Kashmir corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers.

The flowers typically have two upper petals that are spurred, a distinctive feature of the genus Corydalis. The subspecies brevicornu is distinguished by its shorter corona compared to other subspecies.

Native to the Himalayan regions, it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats.
