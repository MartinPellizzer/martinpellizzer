Comesperma scandens

Comesperma scandens, commonly known as the climbing comesperma, is a woody vine native to Australia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
