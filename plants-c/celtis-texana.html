Celtis texana

Celtis texana, commonly known as the Texas hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, pale green berries. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and dry areas.

The Texas hackberry is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
