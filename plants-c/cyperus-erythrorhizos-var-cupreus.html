Cyperus erythrorhizos var. cupreus

Cyperus erythrorhizos var. cupreus, commonly known as the copper rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a reddish or coppery hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its unique coloration and adaptability make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
