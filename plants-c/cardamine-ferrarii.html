Cardamine ferrarii

Cardamine ferrarii, commonly known as Ferrari's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a single umbel.

Cardamine ferrarii is known for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
