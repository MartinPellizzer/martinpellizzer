Cordia bicolor

Cordia bicolor, commonly known as the yellow balsa or white mangrove, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and wetlands, often forming dense thickets.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
