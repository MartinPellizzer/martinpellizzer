Callisia fragrans

Callisia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can range in color from green to variegated with white or yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Callisia fragrans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
