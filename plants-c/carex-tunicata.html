Carex tunicata

Carex tunicata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex tunicata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.
