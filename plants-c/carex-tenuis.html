Carex tenuis

Carex tenuis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to moist environments such as wet meadows and marshes, it thrives in areas with consistent moisture. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
