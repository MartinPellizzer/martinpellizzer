Cuminum cyminum var. hispanicum

Cuminum cyminum var. hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish cumin, is a variant of the common cumin plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, spherical, yellowish-brown seeds that are rich in essential oils and aromatic compounds. These seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates with well-drained soils.
