Colobanthus muelleri var. strictus

Colobanthus muelleri var. strictus, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh conditions such as those found in Antarctica and subantarctic regions.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native habitats.
