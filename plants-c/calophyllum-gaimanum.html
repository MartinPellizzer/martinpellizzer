Calophyllum gaimanum

Calophyllum gaimanum, commonly known as the Gaiman's candlewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, resinous shell, and the tree is often found in lowland rainforests.

Calophyllum gaimanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
