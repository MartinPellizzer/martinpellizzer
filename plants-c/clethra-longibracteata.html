Clethra longibracteata

Clethra longibracteata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered panicles during the summer months. These flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and attractive autumn foliage.
