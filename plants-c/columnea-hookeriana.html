Columnea hookeriana

Columnea hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's flame or red flame plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Columnea hookeriana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
