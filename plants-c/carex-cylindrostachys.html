Carex cylindrostachys

Carex cylindrostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
