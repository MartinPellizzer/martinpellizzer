Comesperma breviflorum

Comesperma breviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlet, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal flowering.
