Cousinia flavispina

Cousinia flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Cousinia flavispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
