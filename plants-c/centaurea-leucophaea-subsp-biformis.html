Centaurea leucophaea subsp. biformis

Centaurea leucophaea subsp.

biformis, commonly known as the two-colored centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
