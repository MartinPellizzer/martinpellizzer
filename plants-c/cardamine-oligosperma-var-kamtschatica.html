Cardamine oligosperma var. kamtschatica

Cardamine oligosperma var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatic mountain buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are trifoliate, with lobed or palmate divisions, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
