Chrysocyathus villosus

Chrysocyathus villosus, commonly known as the hairy cyathus, is a unique member of the family Cyathaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped structure called a cyathium, which houses its reduced flowers. Its hairy stems and leaves contribute to its identification and help in water retention.

The botanical features of Chrysocyathus villosus make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecology.
