Cassia augusti

Cassia augusti, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets, and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain seeds. Cassia augusti thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and seasonal display of vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to ecological and horticultural settings.
