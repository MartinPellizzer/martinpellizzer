Cyclanthera quinqueloba

Cyclanthera quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant features palmate leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a long, slender, green gourd that matures into a yellowish color, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
