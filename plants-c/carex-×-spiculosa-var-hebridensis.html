Carex × spiculosa var. hebridensis

Carex × spiculosa var. hebridensis, commonly known as the Hebridean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in upland and coastal habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are generally dark green, and its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its aesthetic value in natural landscapes.
