Cassia patellaria var. longifolia

Cassia patellaria var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The long, slender leaves of this variety distinguish it from other cassia species.
