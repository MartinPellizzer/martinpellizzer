Chionodoxa forbesii

Chionodoxa forbesii, commonly known as the Persian snowflake, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of Turkey and Iran.

It produces clusters of delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and elegant appearance.
