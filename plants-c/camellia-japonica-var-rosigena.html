Camellia japonica var. rosigena

Camellia japonica var. rosigena, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with rose-like petals, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive rose-like appearance due to its layered petals. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.
