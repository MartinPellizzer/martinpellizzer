Calamagrostis nana subsp. ohminensis

Calamagrostis nana subsp.

ohminensis, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf reed, is a low-growing grass species native to Japan. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
