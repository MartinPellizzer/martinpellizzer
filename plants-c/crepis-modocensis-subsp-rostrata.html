Crepis modocensis subsp. rostrata

Crepis modocensis subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as Modoc thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native insects.
