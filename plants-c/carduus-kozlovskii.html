Carduus kozlovskii

Carduus kozlovskii, commonly known as Kozlovsky's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by bracts that form a spiny crown. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
