Callista perula

Callista perula, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five petals and intricate, lobe-like structures. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Callista perula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
