Campanula fragilis

Campanula fragilis, commonly known as the brittle bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Campanula fragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, delicate blooms.
