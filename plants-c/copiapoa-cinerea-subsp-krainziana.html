Copiapoa cinerea subsp. krainziana

Copiapoa cinerea subsp.

krainziana, commonly known as the Krainzian cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a grayish-white stem covered in short, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It is adapted to survive in arid conditions with minimal rainfall.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
