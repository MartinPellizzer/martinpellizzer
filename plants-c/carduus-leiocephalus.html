Carduus leiocephalus

Carduus leiocephalus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and fluffy, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
