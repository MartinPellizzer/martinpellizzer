Ceropegia superba

Ceropegia superba, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a unique type of flower known as a ceropegium, which is adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia superba is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms.
