Cucurbita moschata var. luffiformis

Cucurbita moschata var. luffiformis, commonly known as the squash gourd, is a variant of the winter squash species characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and other gourds. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is usually harvested when mature, featuring a thick, waxy skin and a sweet, nutty flavor.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and culinary use in various cuisines.
