Cattleya trianae var. reginae

Cattleya trianae var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen of the Andes, is a hybrid orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces thick, fleshy roots adapted to its cool, moist, and shaded habitat. Its inflorescence usually consists of 1 to 3 blooms per stem, which appear during the rainy season.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its elegance and fragrance.
