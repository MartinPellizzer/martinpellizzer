Cytisus calycinus

Cytisus calycinus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, compound, and trifoliate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cytisus calycinus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
