Cnemidostachys bidentata

Cnemidostachys bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
