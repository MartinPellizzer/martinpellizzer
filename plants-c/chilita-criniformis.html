Chilita criniformis

Chilita criniformis, commonly known as the feathered chilita, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
