Cyperus aristatus var. inflexus

Cyperus aristatus var. inflexus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often inconspicuous, and it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
