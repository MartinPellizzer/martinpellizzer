Carex gasparrinii

Carex gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.
