Centaurea magocsyana

Centaurea magocsyana, commonly known as the Magocsy's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
