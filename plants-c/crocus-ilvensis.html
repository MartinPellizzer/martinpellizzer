Crocus ilvensis

Crocus ilvensis, commonly known as the Ilven's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically borne on short, sturdy stems. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus ilvensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early-season color.
