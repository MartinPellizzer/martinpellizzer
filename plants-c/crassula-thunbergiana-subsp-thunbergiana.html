Crassula thunbergiana subsp. thunbergiana

Crassula thunbergiana subsp.

thunbergiana, commonly known as the stone rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color, with small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
