Cyperus niveus var. leucocephalus

Cyperus niveus var. leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
