Campomanesia rivularis

Campomanesia rivularis, commonly known as the river holly or water holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
