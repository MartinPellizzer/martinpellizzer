Cephaelis plagiantha

Cephaelis plagiantha, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Cephaelis plagiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
