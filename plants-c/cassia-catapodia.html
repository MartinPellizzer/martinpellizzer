Cassia catapodia

Cassia catapodia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia catapodia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
