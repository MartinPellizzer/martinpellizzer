Cyclanthus plumieri

Cyclanthus plumieri, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage with a pleasant, spicy scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly hairy underside.

Cyclanthus plumieri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
