Centaurea vatevii

Centaurea vatevii, commonly known as the Vatsevitch's cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia. The plant features erect, sparsely branched stems and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Centaurea vatevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
