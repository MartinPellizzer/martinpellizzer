Crinum erythrophyllum

Crinum erythrophyllum, commonly known as the red-flowered crinum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of flowers, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Crinum erythrophyllum is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
