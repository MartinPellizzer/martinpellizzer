Cephalorrhynchus subplumosus

Cephalorrhynchus subplumosus, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, bell-like flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
