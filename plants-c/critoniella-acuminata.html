Critoniella acuminata

Critoniella acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
