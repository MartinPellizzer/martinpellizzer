Carex × nicoloffii

Carex × nicoloffii, commonly known as the Nicoloff's sedge, is a hybrid plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex × nicoloffii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
