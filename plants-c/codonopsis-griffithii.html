Codonopsis griffithii

Codonopsis griffithii, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Codonopsis griffithii thrives in cool, moist environments and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
