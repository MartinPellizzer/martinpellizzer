Centaurea pullata subsp. pullata

Centaurea pullata subsp.

pullata, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for garden use.
