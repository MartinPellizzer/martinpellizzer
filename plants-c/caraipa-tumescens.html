Caraipa tumescens

Caraipa tumescens, commonly known as the Caraipa cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny foliage, which help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its structure allows it to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils, making it well adapted to its native ecosystem.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
