Comparettia sillarensis

Comparettia sillarensis, commonly known as the Sillares orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Comparettia sillarensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native ecosystems.
