Cypripedium concolor var. chlorophyllum

Cypripedium concolor var. chlorophyllum, commonly known as the green-flowered lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and oval, and it produces a single, elongated flower stem.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
