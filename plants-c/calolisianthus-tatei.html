Calolisianthus tatei

Calolisianthus tatei, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Calolisianthus tatei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
