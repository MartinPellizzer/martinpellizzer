Cactus cirrhifer

Cactus cirrhifer, commonly known as the woolly cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems covered in dense, white, woolly hair that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The cactus also plays a role in providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
