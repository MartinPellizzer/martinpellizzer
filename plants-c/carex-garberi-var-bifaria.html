Carex garberi var. bifaria

Carex garberi var. bifaria, commonly known as the Garber's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant is also important in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
