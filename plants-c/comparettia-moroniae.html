Comparettia moroniae

Comparettia moroniae, commonly known as the Moronia orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
