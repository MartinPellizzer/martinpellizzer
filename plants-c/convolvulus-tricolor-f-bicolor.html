Convolvulus tricolor f. bicolor

Convolvulus tricolor f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and strikingly colorful flowers that typically display two distinct colors, often pink and white. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens.
