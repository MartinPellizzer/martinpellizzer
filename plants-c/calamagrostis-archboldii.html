Calamagrostis archboldii

Calamagrostis archboldii, commonly known as the Archbold's reed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, are slender and often tinged with purplish hues.

Calamagrostis archboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
