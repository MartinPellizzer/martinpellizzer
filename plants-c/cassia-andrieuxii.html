Cassia andrieuxii

Cassia andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are arranged in panicles and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

Cassia andrieuxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
