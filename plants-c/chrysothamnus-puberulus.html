Chrysothamnus puberulus

Chrysothamnus puberulus, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
