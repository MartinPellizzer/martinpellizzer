Cruciata taurica subsp. persica

Cruciata taurica subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Caucasian crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers, which are typically pink or white.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
