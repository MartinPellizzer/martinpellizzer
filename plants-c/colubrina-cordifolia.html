Colubrina cordifolia

Colubrina cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, sunny habitats.

Colubrina cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
