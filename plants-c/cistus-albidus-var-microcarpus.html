Cistus albidus var. microcarpus

Cistus albidus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
