Cyphostemma biternatum

Cyphostemma biternatum, commonly known as the desert milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, storing water in its stems and having a deep root system.

Cyphostemma biternatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
