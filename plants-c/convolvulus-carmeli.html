Convolvulus carmeli

Convolvulus carmeli, commonly known as the Carmel bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
