Crepis striata

Crepis striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a distinctive ribbed structure.

Crepis striata is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
