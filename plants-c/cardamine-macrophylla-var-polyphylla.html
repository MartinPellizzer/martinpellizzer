Cardamine macrophylla var. polyphylla

Cardamine macrophylla var. polyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a valuable wild vegetable in certain cuisines.
