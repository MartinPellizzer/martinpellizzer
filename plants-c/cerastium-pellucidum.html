Cerastium pellucidum

Cerastium pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, succulent stems. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, translucent hairs, giving it a unique appearance.

Cerastium pellucidum is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
