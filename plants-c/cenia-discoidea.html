Cenia discoidea

Cenia discoidea, commonly known as the flat disk fern, is a rare and unique species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, disk-like fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are arranged in a unique, circular pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
