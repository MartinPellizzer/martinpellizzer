Chlora perfoliata var. pusilla

Chlora perfoliata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf perfoliate, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky habitats.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a hardy species well adapted to xeric environments. Its leaves have a tendency to perforate or almost fuse at the base, a trait that gives it its scientific name.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert and semi-desert regions.
