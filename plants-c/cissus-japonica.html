Cissus japonica

Cissus japonica, commonly known as Japanese grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
