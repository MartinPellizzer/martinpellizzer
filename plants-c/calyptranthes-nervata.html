Calyptranthes nervata

Calyptranthes nervata, commonly known as the veined calyptranthes, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that have distinct venation patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Calyptranthes nervata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
