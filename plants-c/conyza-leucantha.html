Conyza leucantha

Conyza leucantha, commonly known as whiteleaf ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

Conyza leucantha is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in the central and eastern regions of the United States.
