Crossostylis grandiflora

Crossostylis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crossostylis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, cross-shaped structure. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
