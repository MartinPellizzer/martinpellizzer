Curcuma fimbriata

Curcuma fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate fringed petals. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a waxy cuticle.

The rhizomes of Curcuma fimbriata are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
