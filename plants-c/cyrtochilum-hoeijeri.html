Cyrtochilum hoeijeri

Cyrtochilum hoeijeri, commonly known as the Hoijer's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, slipper-shaped flower that resembles a small pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Cyrtochilum hoeijeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate and unusual appearance.
