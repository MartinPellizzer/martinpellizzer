Crataegus wilmorensis

Crataegus wilmorensis, commonly known as the Wilmore hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally not palatable to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
