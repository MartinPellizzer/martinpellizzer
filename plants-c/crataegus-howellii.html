Crataegus howellii

Crataegus howellii, commonly known as Howell's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
