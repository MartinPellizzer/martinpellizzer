Crotalaria salicifolia

Crotalaria salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Crotalaria salicifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in agricultural settings.
