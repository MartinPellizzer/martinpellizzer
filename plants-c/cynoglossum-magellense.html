Cynoglossum magellense

Cynoglossum magellense, commonly known as the Magellanic hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Cynoglossum magellense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
