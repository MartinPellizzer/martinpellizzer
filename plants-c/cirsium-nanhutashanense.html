Cirsium nanhutashanense

Cirsium nanhutashanense, commonly known as the Nanhu-Tashan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium nanhutashanense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
