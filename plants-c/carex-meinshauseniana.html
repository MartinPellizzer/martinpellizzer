Carex meinshauseniana

Carex meinshauseniana, commonly known as the Meinhausean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
