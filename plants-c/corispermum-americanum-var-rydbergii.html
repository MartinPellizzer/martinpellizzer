Corispermum americanum var. rydbergii

Corispermum americanum var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
