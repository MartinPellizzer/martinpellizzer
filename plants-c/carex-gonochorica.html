Carex gonochorica

Carex gonochorica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, with separate male and female flowers on different stems.

Carex gonochorica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
