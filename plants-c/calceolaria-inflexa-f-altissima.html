Calceolaria inflexa f. altissima

Calceolaria inflexa f. altissima, commonly known as the tall calceolaria, is a hybrid form of the calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable ornamental species. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
