Cyphostigma diphyllum

Cyphostigma diphyllum, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and compact foliage. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often arranged in clusters.

Cyphostigma diphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
