Corydalis fumariifolia f. candida

Corydalis fumariifolia f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered fumariifolia corydalis, is a rare and striking variant of the species.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are arranged in clusters along the stems.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental qualities.
