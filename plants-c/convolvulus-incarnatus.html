Convolvulus incarnatus

Convolvulus incarnatus, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing vines and herbs. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread along surfaces, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening early in the day and closing by midday.
