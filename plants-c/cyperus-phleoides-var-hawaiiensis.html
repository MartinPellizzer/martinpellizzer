Cyperus phleoides var. hawaiiensis

Cyperus phleoides var. hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, grassy foliage and slender, erect stems that often form clumps. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
