Cinnamomum costaricanum

Cinnamomum costaricanum, commonly known as the Costa Rican cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy scent, while the bark is thick and fibrous.

This species is valued for its essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
