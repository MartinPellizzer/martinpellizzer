Centaurea spinosa var. tomentosa

Centaurea spinosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white tomentum. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
