Calycadenia truncata subsp. microcephala

Calycadenia truncata subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced flower head size compared to other members of the species.
