Coelachyrum oligobrachiatum

Coelachyrum oligobrachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 millimeters wide, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in wet or waterlogged conditions.

Coelachyrum oligobrachiatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
