Carex vulpinoidea var. glomerata

Carex vulpinoidea var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
