Conchidium nanum

Conchidium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf conchidium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are borne in clusters.

Conchidium nanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
