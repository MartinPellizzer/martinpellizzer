Capsella bursa-pastoris pinnata

Capsella bursa-pastoris pinnata, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling habit with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are heart-shaped or kidney-shaped. Its fruit is a small, triangular capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
