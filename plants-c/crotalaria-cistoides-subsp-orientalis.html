Crotalaria cistoides subsp. orientalis

Crotalaria cistoides subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and has a hairy stem, typical of many Crotalaria species. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.

This subspecies is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
