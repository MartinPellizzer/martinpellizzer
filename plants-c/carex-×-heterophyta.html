Carex × heterophyta

Carex × heterophyta, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, three-rowed berries.

Carex × heterophyta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
