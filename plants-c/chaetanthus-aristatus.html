Chaetanthus aristatus

Chaetanthus aristatus, commonly known as the spiked chaetanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that enclose its small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chaetanthus aristatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
