Cynorkis laeta var. angavoensis

Cynorkis laeta var. angavoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This variant is noted for its unique coloration and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
