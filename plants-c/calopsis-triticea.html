Calopsis triticea

Calopsis triticea, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along streambanks, and in disturbed areas.

Calopsis triticea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
