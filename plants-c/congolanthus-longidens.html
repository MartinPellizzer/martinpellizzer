Congolanthus longidens

Congolanthus longidens, commonly known as the Congo longleaf, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
