Commelina coreana

Commelina coreana, commonly known as the Korean spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, with three prominent white petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Commelina coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
