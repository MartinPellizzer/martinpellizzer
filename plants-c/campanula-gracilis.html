Campanula gracilis

Campanula gracilis, commonly known as slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
