Cattleya guttata var. lilacina

Cattleya guttata var. lilacina, commonly known as the Lilac Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-pink flowers with a distinctive white throat and prominent yellow lip. The plant produces pendulous blooms that are often fragrant, adding a unique sensory element to its appearance. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
