Carpesium cernuum var. pedunculosa

Carpesium cernuum var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish to reddish, and clustered in spherical heads, which bloom from late summer to early autumn.

The species is characterized by its drooping inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance in the landscape.
