Campanula dulcis

Campanula dulcis, commonly known as sweet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branching.

Campanula dulcis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
