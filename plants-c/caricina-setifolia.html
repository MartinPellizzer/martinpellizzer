Caricina setifolia

Caricina setifolia, commonly known as the slender bristled grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Caricina setifolia is often found in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils.
