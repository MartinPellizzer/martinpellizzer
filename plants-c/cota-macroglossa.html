Cota macroglossa

Cota macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue cota, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with elongated petals that resemble tongues, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Cota macroglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
