Ceropteris argentea var. aurea

Ceropteris argentea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a variant of the silver shield fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its large, leathery, and intricately divided fronds. The fronds are typically bright green with a distinctive golden or bronze hue along the midrib and veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
