Coccoloba venosa f. major

Coccoloba venosa f. major, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
