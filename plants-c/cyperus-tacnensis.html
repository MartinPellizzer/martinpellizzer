Cyperus tacnensis

Cyperus tacnensis, commonly known as the Andean flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its slender stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus tacnensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
