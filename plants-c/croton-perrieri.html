Croton perrieri

Croton perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from green to red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Croton perrieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
