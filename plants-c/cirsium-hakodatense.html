Cirsium hakodatense

Cirsium hakodatense, commonly known as the Hakodate thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
