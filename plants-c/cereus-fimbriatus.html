Cereus fimbriatus

Cereus fimbriatus, commonly known as the Christmas Star cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its branching, spiny stems and clusters of bright, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cereus fimbriatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
