Carex onoei subsp. capituliformis

Carex onoei subsp.

capituliformis, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
