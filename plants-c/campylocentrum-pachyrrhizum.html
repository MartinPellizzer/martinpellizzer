Campylocentrum pachyrrhizum

Campylocentrum pachyrrhizum, commonly known as the thick-rooted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, and it is often found growing in shaded, moist environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
