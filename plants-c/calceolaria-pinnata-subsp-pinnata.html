Calceolaria pinnata subsp. pinnata

Calceolaria pinnata subsp.

pinnata, commonly known as the fringed pincushion flower, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble small pincushions, with intricate, fringed petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. Calceolaria pinnata subsp.

pinnata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
