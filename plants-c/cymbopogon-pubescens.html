Cymbopogon pubescens

Cymbopogon pubescens, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and tall, slender inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cymbopogon pubescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
