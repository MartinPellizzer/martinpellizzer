Cladium arthrophyllum var. riparium

Cladium arthrophyllum var. riparium, commonly known as the river paper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, broad leaves that are often used for thatching or as a natural filter in water systems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of rivers and streams, thriving in nutrient-rich, muddy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are pollinated by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic wildlife.
