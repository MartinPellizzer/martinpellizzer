Clematis velutina

Clematis velutina, commonly known as velvet clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a bluish tinge. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, velvety hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
