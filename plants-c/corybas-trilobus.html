Corybas trilobus

Corybas trilobus, commonly known as the Three-lobed Helmet Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually dark purple or reddish-brown, with a prominent, hood-like lip.

Corybas trilobus is a perennial species that produces a single, showy flower per stem, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
