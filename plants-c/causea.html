Causea

Causea, commonly known as the Andean mountain potato, is a tuberous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in carbohydrates and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. Its growth is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Causea is an important crop for indigenous communities in South America.
