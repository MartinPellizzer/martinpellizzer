Carex blepharicarpa var. hirtifructus

Carex blepharicarpa var. hirtifructus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The variety hirtifructus is distinguished by its hairy fruits, which are a key identifying feature.
