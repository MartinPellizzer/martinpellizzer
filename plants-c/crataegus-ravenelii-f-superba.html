Crataegus ravenelii f. superba

Crataegus ravenelii f. superba, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a rare and ornamental variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. Its foliage consists of ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red berries in the fall.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
