Cyclamen macrophyllum

Cyclamen macrophyllum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bowl-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive periods of dormancy, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cyclamen macrophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly used in floral arrangements.
