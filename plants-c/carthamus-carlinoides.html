Carthamus carlinoides

Carthamus carlinoides, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its stems are usually erect and can grow up to 1 meter tall, often branching near the top.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
