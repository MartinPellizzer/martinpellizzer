Carolinella racemosa

Carolinella racemosa, commonly known as the Florida rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Carolinella racemosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and sandy environments.
