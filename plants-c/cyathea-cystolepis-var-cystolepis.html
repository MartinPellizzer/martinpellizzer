Cyathea cystolepis var. cystolepis

Cyathea cystolepis var. cystolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a leathery texture, and the plant prefers moist, shaded environments with high humidity.

It plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
