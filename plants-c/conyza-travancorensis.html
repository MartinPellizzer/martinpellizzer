Conyza travancorensis

Conyza travancorensis, commonly known as the Travancore weed, is a flowering plant native to India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and serrated.

Conyza travancorensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
