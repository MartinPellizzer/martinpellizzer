Croton urucurana var. albidus

Croton urucurana var. albidus, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves that often display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
