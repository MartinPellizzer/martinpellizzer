Castalia flava

Castalia flava, commonly known as the yellow castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and slightly hairy.

Castalia flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
