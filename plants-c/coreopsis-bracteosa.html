Coreopsis bracteosa

Coreopsis bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis bracteosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
