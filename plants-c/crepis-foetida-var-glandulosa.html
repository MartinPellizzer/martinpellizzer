Crepis foetida var. glandulosa

Crepis foetida var. glandulosa, commonly known as the stinking mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive odor, which is described as unpleasant or foul.

This variety is part of the genus Crepis, which includes several species with similar morphological features.
