Carpinus tanakaeana

Carpinus tanakaeana, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
