Clematis trinervis

Clematis trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis trinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be trained to climb trellises or walls.
