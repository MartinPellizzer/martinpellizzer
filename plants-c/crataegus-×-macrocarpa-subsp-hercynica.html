Crataegus × macrocarpa subsp. hercynica

Crataegus × macrocarpa subsp.

hercynica, commonly known as the Hercules hawthorn, is a hybrid plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters.

The plant features thorned branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, containing several small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
