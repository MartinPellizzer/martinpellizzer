Carex diluta f. subdistans

Carex diluta f. subdistans, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
