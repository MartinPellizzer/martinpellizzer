Carduus globifer

Carduus globifer, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny, green bracts. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
