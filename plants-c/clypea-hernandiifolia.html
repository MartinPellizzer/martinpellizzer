Clypea hernandiifolia

Clypea hernandiifolia, commonly known as the shieldleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which are thick and leathery, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Clypea hernandiifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and medicinal properties.
