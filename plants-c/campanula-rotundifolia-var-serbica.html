Campanula rotundifolia var. serbica

Campanula rotundifolia var. serbica, commonly known as the Serbian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans.

It features rosettes of rounded, dark green leaves and produces bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
