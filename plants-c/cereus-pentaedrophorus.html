Cereus pentaedrophorus

Cereus pentaedrophorus, commonly known as the five-edged cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom at night. Its spines are usually short and sparse, providing minimal protection.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
