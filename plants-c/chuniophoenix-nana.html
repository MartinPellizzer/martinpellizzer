Chuniophoenix nana

Chuniophoenix nana, commonly known as the dwarf date palm, is a small, compact palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of narrow, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into edible dates, although it is less productive than its larger relatives. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
