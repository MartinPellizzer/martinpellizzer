Carex gebleri

Carex gebleri, commonly known as the Gebleri sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

Carex gebleri is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
