Croton furcellatus

Croton furcellatus, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton furcellatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
