Curcuma supraneeana

Curcuma supraneeana, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and pink floral bracts. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive color gradient. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Curcuma supraneeana is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
