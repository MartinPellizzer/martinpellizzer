Capnoides pruinosa

Capnoides pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf capnoides, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white clusters, and bloom during the rainy season.

Capnoides pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
