Crotalaria lachnophora

Crotalaria lachnophora, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria lachnophora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
