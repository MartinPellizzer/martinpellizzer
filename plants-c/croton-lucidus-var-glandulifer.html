Croton lucidus var. glandulifer

Croton lucidus var. glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both ornamental purposes and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
