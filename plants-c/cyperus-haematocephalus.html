Cyperus haematocephalus

Cyperus haematocephalus, commonly known as bloodroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its dark red or purple flower heads, which are clustered at the end of its slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters around the base.

Cyperus haematocephalus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
