Chenopodium album var. boscianum

Chenopodium album var. boscianum, commonly known as yellow goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often considered a weed in cultivated areas.

The plant is notable for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
