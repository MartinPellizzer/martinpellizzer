Callisteris bridgesii

Callisteris bridgesii, commonly known as the bridgesii cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaf surfaces and the ability to store water in its succulent stems.

Callisteris bridgesii plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
