Cerbera thevetia

Cerbera thevetia, commonly known as yellow oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and woody, often branched stems. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially hazardous to animals and humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a waxy texture.

Cerbera thevetia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
