Clidemia pycnantha

Clidemia pycnantha, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Clidemia pycnantha plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
