Carpodiptera africana

Carpodiptera africana, commonly known as the African winged bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive winged pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, flattened pods containing edible seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.
