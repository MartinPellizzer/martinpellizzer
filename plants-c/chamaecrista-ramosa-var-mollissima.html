Chamaecrista ramosa var. mollissima

Chamaecrista ramosa var. mollissima, commonly known as silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, pinnate leaves with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in raceme clusters, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its seeds are encased in distinctive, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.
