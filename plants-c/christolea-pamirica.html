Christolea pamirica

Christolea pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to high altitudes and can be found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Christolea pamirica is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
