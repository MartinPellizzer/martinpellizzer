Cissus jackii

Cissus jackii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is well adapted to humid, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade.

Cissus jackii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
