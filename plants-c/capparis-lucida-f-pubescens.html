Capparis lucida f. pubescens

Capparis lucida f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a botanical variant of the caper plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a slightly bitter taste.

This variety is notable for its hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other caper species.
