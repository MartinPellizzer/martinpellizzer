Cynometra madagascariensis subsp. rivularis

Cynometra madagascariensis subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is typically found in moist, lowland areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
