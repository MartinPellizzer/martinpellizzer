Commelina flexuosa

Commelina flexuosa, commonly known as wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround the flower cluster. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Commelina flexuosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through stolons, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
