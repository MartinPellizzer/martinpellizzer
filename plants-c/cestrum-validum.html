Cestrum validum

Cestrum validum, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum validum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
