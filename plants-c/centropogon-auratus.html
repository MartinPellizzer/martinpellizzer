Centropogon auratus

Centropogon auratus, commonly known as the golden flame grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Centropogon auratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its bold, colorful display.
