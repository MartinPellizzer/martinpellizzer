Citrus × leiocarpa var. tumida

Citrus × leiocarpa var. tumida, commonly known as the swollen lemon.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy rind and juicy, aromatic pulp. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is usually round or oval, with a thick, yellowish-orange peel that contains a high concentration of essential oils.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its unique flavor and ornamental qualities.
