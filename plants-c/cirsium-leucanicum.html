Cirsium leucanicum

Cirsium leucanicum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium leucanicum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
