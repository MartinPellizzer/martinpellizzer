Chionochloa rubra subsp. cuprea

Chionochloa rubra subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as the red mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown coloration, which is most prominent in its inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky or gravelly slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are erect and dense.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
