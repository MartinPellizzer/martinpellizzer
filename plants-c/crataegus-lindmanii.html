Crataegus lindmanii

Crataegus lindmanii, commonly known as Lindman's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus lindmanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
