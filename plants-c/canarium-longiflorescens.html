Canarium longiflorescens

Canarium longiflorescens, commonly known as the long-flowered candlenut, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Burseraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in long, drooping inflorescences. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, oily shell. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability to various soil types.
