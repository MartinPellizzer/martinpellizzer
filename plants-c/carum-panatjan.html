Carum panatjan

Carum panatjan, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are used as a spice, valued for their aromatic and slightly bitter flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
