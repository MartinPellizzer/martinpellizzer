Carduus hallii

Carduus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple composite flowers, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush ecosystems. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Carduus hallii is generally non-invasive but can be considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
