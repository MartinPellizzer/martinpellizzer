Centaurea carbonellii

Centaurea carbonellii, commonly known as the Carbonell's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and scrublands. It produces bright blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
