Coleus marquesii

Coleus marquesii, commonly known as the Marquesas coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the Marquesas Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from red, purple, and green to variegated patterns. Its flowers are small and tubular, typically appearing in clusters, and are usually a pale blue or purple in color.

Coleus marquesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking leaf colors and compact growth habit.
