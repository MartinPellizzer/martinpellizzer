Cyclamen clusii

Cyclamen clusii, commonly known as the Clusius cyclamen, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Syria.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bowl-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Cyclamen clusii is typically grown as a tuberous plant, with its underground storage organs supporting its growth cycle.
