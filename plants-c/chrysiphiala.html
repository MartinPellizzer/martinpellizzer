Chrysiphiala

Chrysiphiala, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are typically clustered at the ends of the branches, creating a visually appealing display.

Chrysiphiala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
