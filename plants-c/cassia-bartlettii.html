Cassia bartlettii

Cassia bartlettii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its showy, yellow, racemose inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and seasonal color changes make it a distinctive addition to gardens and urban landscapes.
