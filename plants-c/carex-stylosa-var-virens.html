Carex stylosa var. virens

Carex stylosa var. virens, commonly known as greenstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are generally dark green and have a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex stylosa var. virens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
