Chrysocoma vulgaris

Chrysocoma vulgaris, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Chrysocoma vulgaris is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
