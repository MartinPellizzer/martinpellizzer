Cordyline terminalis var. sepiaria

Cordyline terminalis var. sepiaria, commonly known as the purple cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Oceania.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and vibrant, lance-shaped leaves that display a striking combination of deep purple and green hues. The leaves are often adorned with a waxy, almost metallic sheen, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its coloration.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted specimen.
