Cirsium heldreichii

Cirsium heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its height can range from 1 to 2 meters, making it a striking presence in its native landscape.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is generally avoided due to its defensive spines.
