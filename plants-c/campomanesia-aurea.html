Campomanesia aurea

Campomanesia aurea, commonly known as the golden guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Campomanesia aurea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
