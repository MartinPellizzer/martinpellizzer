Carex negrii

Carex negrii, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex negrii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
