Capsella cuneolata

Capsella cuneolata, commonly known as the heart-shaped sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in gardens or along roadsides.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
