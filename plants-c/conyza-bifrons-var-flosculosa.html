Conyza bifrons var. flosculosa

Conyza bifrons var. flosculosa, commonly known as the clustered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and grasslands. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

The flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
