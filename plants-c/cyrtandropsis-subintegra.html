Cyrtandropsis subintegra

Cyrtandropsis subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
