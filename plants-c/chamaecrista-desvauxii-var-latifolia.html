Chamaecrista desvauxii var. latifolia

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf crownflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
