Carex hymenolepis

Carex hymenolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
