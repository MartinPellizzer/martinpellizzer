Crucifera semperflorens

Crucifera semperflorens, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and floral design.
