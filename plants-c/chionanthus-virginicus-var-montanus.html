Chionanthus virginicus var. montanus

Chionanthus virginicus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
