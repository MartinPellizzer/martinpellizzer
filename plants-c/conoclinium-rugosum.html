Conoclinium rugosum

Conoclinium rugosum, commonly known as roughleaf honeysuckle, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically orange to red in color. These flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Its showy blooms and vigorous growth make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
