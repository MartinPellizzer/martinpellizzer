Cassia acosmifolia

Cassia acosmifolia, commonly known as the white cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Cassia acosmifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
