Carex pilosa f. gracilior

Carex pilosa f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, more delicate appearance compared to the typical form of Carex pilosa.
