Crocus suterianus

Crocus suterianus, commonly known as the Suter's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly the Pyrenees and the Alps. This perennial plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of early spring in its native habitats.
