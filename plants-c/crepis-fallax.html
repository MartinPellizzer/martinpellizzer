Crepis fallax

Crepis fallax, commonly known as the bentgrass of the Alps, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, basal leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small, yellowish flower heads.

Crepis fallax is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
