Chenopodium formosanum

Chenopodium formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its seeds are rich in nutrients.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
