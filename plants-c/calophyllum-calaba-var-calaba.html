Calophyllum calaba var. calaba

Calophyllum calaba var. calaba, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its durable timber, which is used in furniture and construction.

The species also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
