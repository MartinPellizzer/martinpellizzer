Centaurea nemoralis

Centaurea nemoralis, commonly known as the wood centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-tipped ray floret. Its botanical name reflects its association with woodland habitats, and it is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
