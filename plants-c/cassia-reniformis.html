Cassia reniformis

Cassia reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with kidney-shaped leaflets, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds, similar to those of the common bean. Cassia reniformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its seeds are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
