Caesalpinia falcaria var. stricta

Caesalpinia falcaria var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ticktree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
