Caladenia unita

Caladenia unita, commonly known as the slender helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that resemble a helmet in shape. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Caladenia unita is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is a common feature among orchid species.
