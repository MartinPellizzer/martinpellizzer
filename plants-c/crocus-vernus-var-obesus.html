Crocus vernus var. obesus

Crocus vernus var. obesus, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces large, showy flowers with violet petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
