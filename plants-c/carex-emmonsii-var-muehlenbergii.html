Carex emmonsii var. muehlenbergii

Carex emmonsii var. muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muehlenberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
