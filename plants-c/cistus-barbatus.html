Cistus barbatus

Cistus barbatus, commonly known as the Barbatus rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cistus barbatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
