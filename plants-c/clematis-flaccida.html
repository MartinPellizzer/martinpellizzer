Clematis flaccida

Clematis flaccida, commonly known as the trailing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, flexible stems that can trail or climb, often reaching lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Clematis flaccida thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained, moist soil for optimal growth.
