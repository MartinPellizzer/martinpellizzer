Crataegus × roubalii

Crataegus × roubalii, commonly known as the Roubalii hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant features thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the autumn, containing several small seeds.

Crataegus × roubalii is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
