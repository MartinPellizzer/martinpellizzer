Cyperus grabowskianus

Cyperus grabowskianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus grabowskianus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
