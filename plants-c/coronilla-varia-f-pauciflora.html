Coronilla varia f. pauciflora

Coronilla varia f. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered variable crownvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and soil restoration efforts.
