Campanula erinus f. coerulea

Campanula erinus f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue campanula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Campanula erinus f. coerulea is also valued for its drought tolerance and suitability for rock gardens or containers.
