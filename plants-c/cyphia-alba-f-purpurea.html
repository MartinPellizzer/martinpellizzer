Cyphia alba f. purpurea

Cyphia alba f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered Cyphia, is a variant of the Cyphia alba species native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique coloration and compact growth habit.
