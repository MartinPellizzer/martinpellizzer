Capparidastrum estrellae

Capparidastrum estrellae, commonly known as Estrella's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, specifically the island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is often found in limestone soils.

Its botanical features include yellowish-white flowers with four petals and a distinctive, pungent odor reminiscent of mustard.
