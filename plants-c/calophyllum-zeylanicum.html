Calophyllum zeylanicum

Calophyllum zeylanicum, commonly known as the yellow cedar or Indian cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it important in traditional crafts and medicine.

The plant also has ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
