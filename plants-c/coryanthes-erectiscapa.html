Coryanthes erectiscapa

Coryanthes erectiscapa, commonly known as the standing hooded orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This species is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble elaborate hoods. The flowers are typically bright red or orange with intricate patterns, designed to attract specific pollinators like wasps and flies. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous blooms, which can last for several months during the wet season.

Coryanthes erectiscapa is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
