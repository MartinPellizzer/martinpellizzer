Campomanesia schlechtendaliana

Campomanesia schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Schlechtendaliana cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
