Calamagrostis purpurea var. flexuosa

Calamagrostis purpurea var. flexuosa, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted foliage and fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and wet meadows. Its flexible, arching stems and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.

It plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
