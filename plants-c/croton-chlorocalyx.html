Croton chlorocalyx

Croton chlorocalyx, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
