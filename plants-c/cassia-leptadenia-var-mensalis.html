Cassia leptadenia var. mensalis

Cassia leptadenia var. mensalis, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
