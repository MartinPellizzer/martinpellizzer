Crepidium dresslerianum

Crepidium dresslerianum, commonly known as the Dressler's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
