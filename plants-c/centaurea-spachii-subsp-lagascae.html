Centaurea spachii subsp. lagascae

Centaurea spachii subsp.

lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasca star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble stars.

The plant produces bristly, spiny leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in some regions.
