Centaurea paniculata var. baetica

Centaurea paniculata var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetican globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking, globular flower heads composed of numerous small, bright red to orange-red ray florets surrounded by a yellow disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
