Croton rheedei

Croton rheedei, commonly known as the African croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant typically has opposite, palmate leaves that can vary in shape and color, ranging from green to red or yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant, though it may be toxic to some animals.
