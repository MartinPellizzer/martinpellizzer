Curcuma glaucophylla

Curcuma glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leafed turmeric, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces orange-yellow rhizomes that are similar in appearance to other Curcuma species. These rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
