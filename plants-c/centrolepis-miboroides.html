Centrolepis miboroides

Centrolepis miboroides, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female plants. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
