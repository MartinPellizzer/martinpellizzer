Chrysanthemum satsumense

Chrysanthemum satsumense, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Chrysanthemum satsumense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
