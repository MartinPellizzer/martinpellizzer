Cyathea subdubia

Cyathea subdubia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a short, stocky caudex and a crown of pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea subdubia is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems and a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
