Chaenomeles maulei var. tricolor

Chaenomeles maulei var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

The varietal name 'tricolor' refers to the distinct coloration of its leaves, which exhibit a combination of green, white, and red hues.
