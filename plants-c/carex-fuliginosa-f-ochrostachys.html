Carex fuliginosa f. ochrostachys

Carex fuliginosa f. ochrostachys, commonly known as the pale-stemmed sedges, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

The botanical features of this sedge make it well adapted to wetland ecosystems and suitable for ecological restoration projects.
