Cyperus teneriffae

Cyperus teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, exhibiting grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, clustered inflorescences.

Cyperus teneriffae is characterized by its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
