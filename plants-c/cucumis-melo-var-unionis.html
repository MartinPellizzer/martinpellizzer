Cucumis melo var. unionis

Cucumis melo var. unionis, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and usually ribbed fruit with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is often used in fresh consumption or as a decorative element in gardens.
