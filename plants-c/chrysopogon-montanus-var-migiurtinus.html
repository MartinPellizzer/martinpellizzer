Chrysopogon montanus var. migiurtinus

Chrysopogon montanus var. migiurtinus, commonly known as the Ethiopian grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
