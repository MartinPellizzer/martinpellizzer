Coelogyne subcalceata

Coelogyne subcalceata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered with a thick, leathery epidermis.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow with red or orange markings, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.
