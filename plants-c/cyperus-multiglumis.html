Cyperus multiglumis

Cyperus multiglumis, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wet soils effectively.

Cyperus multiglumis is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
