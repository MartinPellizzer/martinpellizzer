Correa affinis

Correa affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a spreading, shrub-like habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Correa affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
