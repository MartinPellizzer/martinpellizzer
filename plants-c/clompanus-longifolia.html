Clompanus longifolia

Clompanus longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf clompanus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
