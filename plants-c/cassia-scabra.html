Cassia scabra

Cassia scabra, commonly known as rough cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Cassia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
