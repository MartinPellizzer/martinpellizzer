Crataegus atrovirens

Crataegus atrovirens, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The berries are used in making jams, liqueurs, and traditional medicinal remedies.
