Clidemia quadrisulca

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly in the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to birds and other animals for dispersal.
