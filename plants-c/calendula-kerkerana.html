Calendula kerkerana

Calendula kerkerana, commonly known as the common marigold, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Calendula kerkerana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
