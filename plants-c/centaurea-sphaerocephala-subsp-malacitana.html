Centaurea sphaerocephala subsp. malacitana

Centaurea sphaerocephala subsp.

malacitana, commonly known as the Malaga centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
