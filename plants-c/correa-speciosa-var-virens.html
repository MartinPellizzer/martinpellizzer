Correa speciosa var. virens

Correa speciosa var. virens, commonly known as the green-flowered correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to greenish-white. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
