Cocos nucifera var. rutila

Cocos nucifera var. rutila, commonly known as the golden coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its unique golden-colored fruit husk.

This variety is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The fruit of Cocos nucifera var. rutila contains both edible flesh and water, making it valuable for culinary and commercial purposes. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal as well as its practical uses.

The plant is also known for its fast growth and ability to adapt to coastal environments.
