Centaurea procumbens subsp. jordaniana

Centaurea procumbens subsp.

jordaniana, commonly known as Jordanian star-thistle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground, often covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, spiny flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.
