Croton caudatiformis

Croton caudatiformis, commonly known as the long-tailed croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The leaves are usually a deep green with prominent veins and sometimes exhibit variegation. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules.

This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
