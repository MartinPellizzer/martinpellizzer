Crudia ripicola

Crudia ripicola, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
