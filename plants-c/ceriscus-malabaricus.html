Ceriscus malabaricus

Ceriscus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar ceris, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
