Chirita scaberrima

Chirita scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chirita scaberrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
