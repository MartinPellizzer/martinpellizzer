Crepis caespitosa

Crepis caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, forming dense, low-growing clumps. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pale green center.

Crepis caespitosa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
