Cerasus avium melanocarpa

Cerasus avium melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, edible black fruits. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a fruit that is typically 1 to 2 centimeters in diameter.

The black cherry is also known for its aromatic wood, which is used in various traditional and commercial applications.
