Carex canescens subsp. arctiformis

Carex canescens subsp.

arctiformis, commonly known as the arctic carex, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for arctic wildlife.
