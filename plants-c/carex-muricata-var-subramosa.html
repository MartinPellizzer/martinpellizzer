Carex muricata var. subramosa

Carex muricata var. subramosa, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering season occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable environments.
