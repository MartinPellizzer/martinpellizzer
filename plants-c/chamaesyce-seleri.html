Chamaesyce seleri

Chamaesyce seleri, commonly known as the coastal sandplant, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.
