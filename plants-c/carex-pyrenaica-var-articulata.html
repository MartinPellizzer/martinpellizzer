Carex pyrenaica var. articulata

Carex pyrenaica var. articulata, commonly known as the Pyrenean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
