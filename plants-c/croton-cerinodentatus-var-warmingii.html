Croton cerinodentatus var. warmingii

Croton cerinodentatus var. warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage with a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant foliage.
