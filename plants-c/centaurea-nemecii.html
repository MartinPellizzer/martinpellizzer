Centaurea nemecii

Centaurea nemecii, commonly known as the Czech thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces vibrant purple to red flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Centaurea nemecii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
