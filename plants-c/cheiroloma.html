Cheiroloma

Cheiroloma, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique morphology, with delicate, thread-like leaves and a short, compact stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cheiroloma is considered a conservation concern.
