Coelanthum verticillatum

Coelanthum verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorl, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Coelanthum verticillatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
