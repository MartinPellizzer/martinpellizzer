Carex doenitzii var. okuboi

Carex doenitzii var. okuboi, commonly known as the Okuboi sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, specifically the Okuboi area of Hokkaido. This sedge is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
