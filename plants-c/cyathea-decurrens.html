Cyathea decurrens

Cyathea decurrens, commonly known as the spreading tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea decurrens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
