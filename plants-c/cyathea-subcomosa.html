Cyathea subcomosa

Cyathea subcomosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and robust, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

Cyathea subcomosa plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
