Conophytum roodiae subsp. corrugatum

Conophytum roodiae subsp.

corrugatum, commonly known as the Roodia's conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, compact leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The leaves are typically green or bluish-green and have a textured, corrugated surface, which gives the subspecies its name. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
