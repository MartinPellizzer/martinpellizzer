Cyperus flavidus var. strictus

Cyperus flavidus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
