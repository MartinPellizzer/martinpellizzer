Centaurea atlantica subsp. calvescens

Centaurea atlantica subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the Atlantic centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces bright blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
