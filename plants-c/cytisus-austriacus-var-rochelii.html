Cytisus austriacus var. rochelii

Cytisus austriacus var. rochelii, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in gardens and natural landscapes.
