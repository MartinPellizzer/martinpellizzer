Calamagrostis longiaristata

Calamagrostis longiaristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters tall, with finely textured, silver-blue foliage. The plant produces fluffy, grayish-brown seed heads in late summer, which add ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.
