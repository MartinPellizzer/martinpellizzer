Carex dianae var. dianae

Carex dianae var. dianae, commonly known as the Dianae sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
