Camarea pulchella

Camarea pulchella, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring dense, pinkish-white floral spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Camarea pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
