Cyanostegia turczaninovii var. angustifolia

Cyanostegia turczaninovii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cyanostegia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are often hairy, providing some protection against desiccation.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
