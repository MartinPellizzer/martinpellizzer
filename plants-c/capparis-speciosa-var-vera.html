Capparis speciosa var. vera

Capparis speciosa var. vera, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's leaves are ovate and have a slightly succulent texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. The flowers are followed by round, green berries that mature to a dark purple color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
