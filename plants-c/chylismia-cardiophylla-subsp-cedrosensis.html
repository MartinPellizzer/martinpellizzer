Chylismia cardiophylla subsp. cedrosensis

Chylismia cardiophylla subsp.

cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros Island holly, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands of California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is considered a rare and endemic species.
