Cyperus obtusatus var. tenuis

Cyperus obtusatus var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
