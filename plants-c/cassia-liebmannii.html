Cassia liebmannii

Cassia liebmannii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cassia liebmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
