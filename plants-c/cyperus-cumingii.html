Cyperus cumingii

Cyperus cumingii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Cyperus cumingii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
