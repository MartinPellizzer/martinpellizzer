Corylus avellana var. rubra

Corylus avellana var. rubra, commonly known as the red hazel, is a variant of the common hazel that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and leaves that turn a vibrant orange-red in the fall. Its flowers are catkins, typical of the hazel genus, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
