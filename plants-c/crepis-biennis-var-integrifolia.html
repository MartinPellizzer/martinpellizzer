Crepis biennis var. integrifolia

Crepis biennis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and entire, giving the plant a distinctive, grass-like appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
