Cytisus eriocarpus var. absinthioides

Cytisus eriocarpus var. absinthioides, commonly known as the silverleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in silvery-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, leathery, and opposite, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.
