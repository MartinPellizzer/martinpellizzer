Crataegus rugosa

Crataegus rugosa, commonly known as the rough crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. Crataegus rugosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
