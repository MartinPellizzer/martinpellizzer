Chenopodium scabricaule f. megalospermum

Chenopodium scabricaule f. megalospermum, commonly known as the giant-seeded goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its large, fleshy seeds.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Chile. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique seed morphology and potential uses in traditional medicine or agriculture.
