Centaurea scabiosa subsp. alpina

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates.

The plant features spiny, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
