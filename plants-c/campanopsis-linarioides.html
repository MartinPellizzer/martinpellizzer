Campanopsis linarioides

Campanopsis linarioides, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
