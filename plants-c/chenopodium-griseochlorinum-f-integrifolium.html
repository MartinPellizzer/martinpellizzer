Chenopodium griseochlorinum f. integrifolium

Chenopodium griseochlorinum f. integrifolium, commonly known as the gray-green goosefoot, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish-blue waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The leaves are usually entire, meaning they lack teeth or lobes, a trait reflected in its subspecific name *integrifolium*. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

This plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid regions.
