Cercestis afzelii

Cercestis afzelii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
