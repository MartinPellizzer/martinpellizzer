Chironia linoides var. subulata

Chironia linoides var. subulata, commonly known as the slender leaf chironia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
