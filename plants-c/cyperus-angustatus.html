Cyperus angustatus

Cyperus angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Cyperus angustatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
