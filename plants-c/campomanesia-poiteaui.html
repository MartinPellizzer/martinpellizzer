Campomanesia poiteaui

Campomanesia poiteaui, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
