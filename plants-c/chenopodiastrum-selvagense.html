Chenopodiastrum selvagense

Chenopodiastrum selvagense, commonly known as wild gooseberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in open fields. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the fruit is a small, edible berry.

Chenopodiastrum selvagense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
