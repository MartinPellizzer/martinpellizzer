Casimirella rupestris

Casimirella rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Casimirella rupestris is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
