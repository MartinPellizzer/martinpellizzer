Combretum glutinosum

Combretum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often sticky bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including moist woodlands and riverine areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
