Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. gaditana

Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. gaditana, commonly known as the "Cradle of Venus," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
