Cassia javanica var. indochinensis

Cassia javanica var. indochinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typical of the legume family. These flowers are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
