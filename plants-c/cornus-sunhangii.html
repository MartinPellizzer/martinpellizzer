Cornus sunhangii

Cornus sunhangii, commonly known as the Sunhang dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish in color. Cornus sunhangii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
