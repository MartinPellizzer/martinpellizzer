Carex perangusta

Carex perangusta, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Carex perangusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
