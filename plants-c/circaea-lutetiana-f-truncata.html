Circaea lutetiana f. truncata

Circaea lutetiana f. truncata, commonly known as the truncated whitecampion, is a compact perennial herb native to the European region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, white flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, often with a truncate or slightly notched apex, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
