Carex multiflora var. microsperma

Carex multiflora var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological value.
