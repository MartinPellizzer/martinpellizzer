Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp. laburnifolia

Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp.

laburnifolia, commonly known as the golden chain bean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features vibrant yellow flowers arranged in cascading racemes, which are characteristic of the Crotalarieae tribe.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects.
