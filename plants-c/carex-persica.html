Carex persica

Carex persica, commonly known as Persian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ornamental gardening.
