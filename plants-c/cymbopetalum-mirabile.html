Cymbopetalum mirabile

Cymbopetalum mirabile, commonly known as the flame violet, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture.

Cymbopetalum mirabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, well-lit environments.
