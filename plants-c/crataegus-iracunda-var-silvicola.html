Crataegus iracunda var. silvicola

Crataegus iracunda var. silvicola, commonly known as the woodland hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
