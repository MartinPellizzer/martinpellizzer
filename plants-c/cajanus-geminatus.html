Cajanus geminatus

Cajanus geminatus, commonly known as the twin-stemmed pigeonpea, is a leguminous plant native to India.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Cajanus geminatus is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important crop in sustainable agricultural systems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
