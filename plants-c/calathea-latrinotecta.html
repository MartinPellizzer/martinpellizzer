Calathea latrinotecta

Calathea latrinotecta, commonly known as the Spotted Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricately patterned leaves that display a mix of green, silver, and white hues. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
