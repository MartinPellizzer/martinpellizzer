Crataegus aemula

Crataegus aemula, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features thorny branches, which are a distinctive botanical characteristic. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland or open areas.

Crataegus aemula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
