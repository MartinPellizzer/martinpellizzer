Chrysopogon wightianus

Chrysopogon wightianus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose leaves and slender, tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
