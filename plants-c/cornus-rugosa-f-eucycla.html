Cornus rugosa f. eucycla

Cornus rugosa f. eucycla, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus rugosa species, which is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
