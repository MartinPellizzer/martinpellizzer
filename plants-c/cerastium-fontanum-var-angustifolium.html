Cerastium fontanum var. angustifolium

Cerastium fontanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their delicate appearance.
