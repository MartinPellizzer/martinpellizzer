Crataegus curvisepala var. rigidula

Crataegus curvisepala var. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid-leaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its rigid, often spiny leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
