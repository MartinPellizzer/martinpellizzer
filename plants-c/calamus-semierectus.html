Calamus semierectus

Calamus semierectus, commonly known as the semi-erect calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family plants.

Calamus semierectus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
