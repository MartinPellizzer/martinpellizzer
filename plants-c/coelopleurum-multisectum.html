Coelopleurum multisectum

Coelopleurum multisectum, commonly known as the multisectioned cup fern, is a unique fern species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often divided into multiple segments, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Coelopleurum multisectum make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
