Crepidium undulatum

Crepidium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes mountains.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers have a distinctive wavy pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name.

It is often found in rocky or open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
