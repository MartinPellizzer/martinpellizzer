Citrus × nobilis subf. yatsushiro

Citrus × nobilis subf.

yatsushiro, commonly known as the Yatsushiro mandarin, is a citrus hybrid native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with thin, smooth skin.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
