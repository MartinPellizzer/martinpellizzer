Caesalpinia paniculata

Caesalpinia paniculata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
