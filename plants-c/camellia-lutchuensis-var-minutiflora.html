Camellia lutchuensis var. minutiflora

Camellia lutchuensis var. minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered camellia, is a species native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flowers. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.
