Citrus elongata

Citrus elongata, commonly known as the long citrus or long lemon, is a species of citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its aromatic properties.

Citrus elongata is also valued for its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
