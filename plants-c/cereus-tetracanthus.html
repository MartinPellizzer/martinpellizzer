Cereus tetracanthus

Cereus tetracanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
