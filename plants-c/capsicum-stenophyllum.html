Capsicum stenophyllum

Capsicum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellow or orange fruits that mature to a red color. It is often grown for its edible fruits, which have a mild to spicy flavor depending on the variety.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
