Chrysanthemum szovitsii

Chrysanthemum szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

Chrysanthemum szovitsii is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
