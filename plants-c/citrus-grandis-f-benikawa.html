Citrus grandis f. benikawa

Citrus grandis f. benikawa, commonly known as the Benikawa citrus, is a botanical variant of the loquat tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruit, which is typically smaller than the standard loquat. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is known for its sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular choice for both culinary and medicinal uses.
