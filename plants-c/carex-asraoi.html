Carex asraoi

Carex asraoi, commonly known as the Asraoi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. Native to specific regions in Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, Carex asraoi is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
