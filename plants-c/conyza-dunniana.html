Conyza dunniana

Conyza dunniana, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters tall, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Conyza dunniana is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
