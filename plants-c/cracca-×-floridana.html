Cracca × floridana

Cracca × floridana, commonly known as the Florida cracca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cracca americana and Cracca luteola.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are yellow and attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
