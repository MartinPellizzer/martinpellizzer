Cytisus smyrnaeus var. prostratus

Cytisus smyrnaeus var. prostratus, commonly known as prostrate broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and erosion control.
