Cordia gharaf var. quercifolia

Cordia gharaf var. quercifolia, commonly known as the African mahogany or African cherry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It features smooth, greyish bark and large, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local industries.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
