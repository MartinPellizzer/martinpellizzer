Camphoromoea subtriplinervia

Camphoromoea subtriplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black berries.

Camphoromoea subtriplinervia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
