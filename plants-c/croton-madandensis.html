Croton madandensis

Croton madandensis, commonly known as the Madang croton, is a tropical shrub native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
