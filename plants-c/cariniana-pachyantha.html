Cariniana pachyantha

Cariniana pachyantha, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a large evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and thick, leathery leaves that are elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also listed as an endangered species.
