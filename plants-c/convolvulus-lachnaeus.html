Convolvulus lachnaeus

Convolvulus lachnaeus, commonly known as the pale morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Convolvulus lachnaeus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny conditions.
