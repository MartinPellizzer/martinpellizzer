Cosmea scabiosae

Cosmea scabiosae, commonly known as the mountain glory or alpine bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are upright with a woolly texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Cosmea scabiosae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
