Corunastylis apostasioides

Corunastylis apostasioides, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or under dense vegetation. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while its stems are short and erect.

The species is noted for its delicate floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
