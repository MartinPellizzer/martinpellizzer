Ceropegia lophophora

Ceropegia lophophora, commonly known as the honeyplant or the starfish plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like flies and bees. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
