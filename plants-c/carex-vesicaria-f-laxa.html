Carex vesicaria f. laxa

Carex vesicaria f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf bulbous sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along stream banks or in shallow water. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in damp, nutrient-rich soils.

The foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, upright growth habit.
