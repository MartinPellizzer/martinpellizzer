Cyperus nodosus

Cyperus nodosus, commonly known as the nodding flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, clustered inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. Cyperus nodosus typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
