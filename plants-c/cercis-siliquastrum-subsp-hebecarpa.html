Cercis siliquastrum subsp. hebecarpa

Cercis siliquastrum subsp.

hebecarpa, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the autumn.

The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a slender, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
