Cyperus cyperinus var. bengalensis

Cyperus cyperinus var. bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
