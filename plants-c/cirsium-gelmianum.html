Cirsium gelmianum

Cirsium gelmianum, commonly known as the Gelmian thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its structure includes a thick, upright stem with bracts that protect the flower heads.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
