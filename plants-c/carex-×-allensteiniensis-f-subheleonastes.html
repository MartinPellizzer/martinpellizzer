Carex × allensteiniensis f. subheleonastes

Carex × allensteiniensis f. subheleonastes, commonly known as the golden sedges, is a hybrid sedge species characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive golden hues.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and the edges of ponds. The plant features triangular stems with smooth, waxy leaves that are often bright green or yellowish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky, cylindrical cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
