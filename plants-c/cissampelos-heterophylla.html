Cissampelos heterophylla

Cissampelos heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf moonvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be alternate, opposite, or whorled depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by bright orange or red berries. Its stems are hairy and often have a milky sap, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
