Cassia huntii var. correntina

Cassia huntii var. correntina, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small leaflets.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
