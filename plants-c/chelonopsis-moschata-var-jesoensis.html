Chelonopsis moschata var. jesoensis

Chelonopsis moschata var. jesoensis, commonly known as the Jesoensis cucumber, is a variant of the musk melon native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit with a pale yellow rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and ornamental appeal in gardens.
