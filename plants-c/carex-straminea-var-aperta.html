Carex straminea var. aperta

Carex straminea var. aperta, commonly known as the open sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical leaves. This variety is distinguished by its open, spreading growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
