Croton claussenianus var. latifolius

Croton claussenianus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
