Chenopodium congolanum

Chenopodium congolanum, commonly known as wild spinach, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium congolanum is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
