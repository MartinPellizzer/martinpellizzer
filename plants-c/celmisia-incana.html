Celmisia incana

Celmisia incana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in spring.

Celmisia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
