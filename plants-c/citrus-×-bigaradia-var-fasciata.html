Citrus × bigaradia var. fasciata

Citrus × bigaradia var. fasciata, commonly known as the variegated kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its unique foliage and fruit.

The leaves are typically variegated with white or yellow markings, which distinguish it from other citrus species. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and mild citrus aroma.
