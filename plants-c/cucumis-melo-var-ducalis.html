Cucumis melo var. ducalis

Cucumis melo var. ducalis, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically grows on a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow-orange flowers.

The fruit is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications due to its high water content and mild flavor.
