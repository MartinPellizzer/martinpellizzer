Cirrhaea tristis

Cirrhaea tristis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically oval in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring.

This plant is often found near water sources and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
