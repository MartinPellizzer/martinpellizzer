Ceraia hymenopetala

Ceraia hymenopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Ceraia hymenopetala is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
