Cinnamomum sumatranum

Cinnamomum sumatranum, commonly known as wild cinnamon or Java cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and dark blue to black berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
