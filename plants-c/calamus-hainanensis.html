Calamus hainanensis

Calamus hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the southern Chinese province of Hainan and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
