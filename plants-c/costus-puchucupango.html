Costus puchucupango

Costus puchucupango, commonly known as the golden costus, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow inflorescences that resemble torches, adding dramatic visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
