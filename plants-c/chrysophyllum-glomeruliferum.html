Chrysophyllum glomeruliferum

Chrysophyllum glomeruliferum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
