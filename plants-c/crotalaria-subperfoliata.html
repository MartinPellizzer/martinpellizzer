Crotalaria subperfoliata

Crotalaria subperfoliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, cylindrical pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria subperfoliata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
