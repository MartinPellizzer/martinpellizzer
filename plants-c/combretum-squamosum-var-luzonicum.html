Combretum squamosum var. luzonicum

Combretum squamosum var. luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, opposite leaves that are often covered in a distinctive whitish or greyish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, followed by small, woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and ecological benefits.
