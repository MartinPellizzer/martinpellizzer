Carex lageniformis

Carex lageniformis, commonly known as the bottle-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex lageniformis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
