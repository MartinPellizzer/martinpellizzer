Codonopsis pinifolia

Codonopsis pinifolia, commonly known as the pineapple orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its inflorescence consists of multiple clusters of small, colorful blooms.

Codonopsis pinifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
