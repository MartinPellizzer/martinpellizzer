Cynoglossum monophlebium

Cynoglossum monophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are often blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
