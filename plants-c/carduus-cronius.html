Carduus cronius

Carduus cronius, commonly known as the Cronius thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
