Caltha napalensis

Caltha napalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Caltha napalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
