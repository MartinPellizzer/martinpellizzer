Crassula mataikona

Crassula mataikona, commonly known as the Mataikona Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
