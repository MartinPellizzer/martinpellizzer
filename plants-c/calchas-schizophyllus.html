Calchas schizophyllus

Calchas schizophyllus, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fan-shaped fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

The plant's unique coloration and morphology make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
