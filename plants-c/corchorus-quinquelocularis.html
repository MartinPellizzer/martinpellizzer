Corchorus quinquelocularis

Corchorus quinquelocularis, commonly known as the five-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
