Centaurea inexpugnabilis

Centaurea inexpugnabilis, commonly known as the "inexpugnable centaurea," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea inexpugnabilis is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
