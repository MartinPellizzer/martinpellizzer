Cardamine californica var. rupicola

Cardamine californica var. rupicola, commonly known as the Sierra shield-fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into small, shield-like lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters, and it produces rounded, green pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and preference for high-altitude habitats.
