Carex madida

Carex madida, commonly known as the Madida sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, greenish-brown flowers in spikelets.

Carex madida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
