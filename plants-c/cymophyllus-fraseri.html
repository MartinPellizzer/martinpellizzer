Cymophyllus fraseri

Cymophyllus fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's cymophyll, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Cymophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
