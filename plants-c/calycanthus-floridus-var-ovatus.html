Calycanthus floridus var. ovatus

Calycanthus floridus var. ovatus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
