Cissus overlaetii

Cissus overlaetii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue leaves that are opposite and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus overlaetii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
