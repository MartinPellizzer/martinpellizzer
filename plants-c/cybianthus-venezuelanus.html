Cybianthus venezuelanus

Cybianthus venezuelanus, commonly known as the Venezuelan flamebush, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in dry to semi-arid environments.

Cybianthus venezuelanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
