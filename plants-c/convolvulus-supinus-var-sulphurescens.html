Convolvulus supinus var. sulphurescens

Convolvulus supinus var. sulphurescens, commonly known as the sulphur-flowered bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features tendrils that allow it to climb and twine around other plants or structures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and can be found in dry, sunny habitats.
