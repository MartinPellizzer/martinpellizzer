Carex miyabei

Carex miyabei, commonly known as Miyabe's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in wet or semi-aquatic habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers.
