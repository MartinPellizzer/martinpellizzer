Carex thwaitesii

Carex thwaitesii, commonly known as Thwaites' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to the northern hemisphere, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
