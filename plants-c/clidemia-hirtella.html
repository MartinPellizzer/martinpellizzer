Clidemia hirtella

Clidemia hirtella, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemia hirtella is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both vegetative propagation and seeds.
