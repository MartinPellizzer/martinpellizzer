Callicarpa apoensis

Callicarpa apoensis, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the showy red fruits in the fall.

Callicarpa apoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful berries and attractive foliage.
