Cassia labouriaeae

Cassia labouriaeae, commonly known as the Labouria cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open forests or along riverbanks. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
