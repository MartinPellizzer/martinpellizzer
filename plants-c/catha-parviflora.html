Catha parviflora

Catha parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered khat, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark green color.

Catha parviflora is known for containing cathinone and cathine, which are stimulant compounds used traditionally in social and cultural contexts.
