Cistus glomeratus

Cistus glomeratus, commonly known as rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus glomeratus is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
