Camaridium xylobiichilum

Camaridium xylobiichilum, commonly known as the redwood lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on the bark of coniferous trees in temperate forests. The lichen's thallus is crust-like, with a dense, leathery texture that allows it to retain moisture. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.

Its unique botanical structure reflects a complex mutualistic relationship between its fungal and photobiotic partners.
