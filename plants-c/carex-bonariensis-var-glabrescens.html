Carex bonariensis var. glabrescens

Carex bonariensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Glabrous Carex, is a perennial sedge native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
