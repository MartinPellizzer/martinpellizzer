Chromolaena ulei

Chromolaena ulei, commonly known as destruction weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fast-growing, invasive nature. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Chromolaena ulei is highly adaptable and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a challenging weed to control in agricultural and natural settings.
