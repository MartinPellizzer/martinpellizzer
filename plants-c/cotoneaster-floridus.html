Cotoneaster floridus

Cotoneaster floridus, commonly known as the Florida cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. The plant bears red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Cotoneaster floridus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
