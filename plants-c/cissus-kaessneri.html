Cissus kaessneri

Cissus kaessneri, commonly known as the stonecrop fig.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its trailing stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Cissus kaessneri is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its trailing growth habit.
