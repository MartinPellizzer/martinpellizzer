Cestrum mositicum

Cestrum mositicum, commonly known as the Mosquitia nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum mositicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
