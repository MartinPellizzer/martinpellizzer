Crepinia henricii

Crepinia henricii, commonly known as the Henry's crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often grown as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Crepinia henricii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
