Carex nigra subsp. broteroana

Carex nigra subsp.

broteroana, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, brownish flowers, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
