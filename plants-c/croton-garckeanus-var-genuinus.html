Croton garckeanus var. genuinus

Croton garckeanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with lobed segments, often displaying vibrant shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
