Cereus × roetteri

Cereus × roetteri, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting moths for pollination. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
