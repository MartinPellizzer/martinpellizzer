Cotoneaster franchetii

Cotoneaster franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and features dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Cotoneaster franchetii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
