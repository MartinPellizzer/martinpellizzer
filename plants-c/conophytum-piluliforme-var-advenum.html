Conophytum piluliforme var. advenum

Conophytum piluliforme var. advenum, commonly known as the "button plant" or "lily of the desert," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
