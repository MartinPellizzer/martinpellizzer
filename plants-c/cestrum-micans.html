Cestrum micans

Cestrum micans, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
