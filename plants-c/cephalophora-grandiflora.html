Cephalophora grandiflora

Cephalophora grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cephalophora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like inflorescences with yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Cephalophora grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
