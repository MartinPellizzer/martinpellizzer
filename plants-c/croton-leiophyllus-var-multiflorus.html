Croton leiophyllus var. multiflorus

Croton leiophyllus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated foliage, featuring combinations of green, yellow, and white patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
