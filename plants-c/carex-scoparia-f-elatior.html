Carex scoparia f. elatior

Carex scoparia f. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and pond edges.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
