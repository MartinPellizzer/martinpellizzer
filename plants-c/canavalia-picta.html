Canavalia picta

Canavalia picta, commonly known as the painted bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful pods that range from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Canavalia picta is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some areas.
