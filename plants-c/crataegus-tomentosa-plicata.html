Crataegus tomentosa plicata

Crataegus tomentosa plicata, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and oval, hairy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
