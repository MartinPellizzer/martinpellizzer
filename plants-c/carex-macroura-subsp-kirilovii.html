Carex macroura subsp. kirilovii

Carex macroura subsp.

kirilovii, commonly known as Kirilov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
