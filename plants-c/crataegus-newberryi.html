Crataegus newberryi

Crataegus newberryi, commonly known as Newberry hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often sour, and the leaves are ovate with serrated edges.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
