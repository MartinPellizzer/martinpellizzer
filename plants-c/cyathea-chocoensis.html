Cyathea chocoensis

Cyathea chocoensis, commonly known as the Choco fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can grow quite long, contributing to its structural support.

Cyathea chocoensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
