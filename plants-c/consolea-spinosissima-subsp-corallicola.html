Consolea spinosissima subsp. corallicola

Consolea spinosissima subsp.

corallicola, commonly known as the coral-like cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its branched stems that bear clusters of small, green, spiny leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species.
