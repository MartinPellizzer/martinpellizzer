Chelone digitalis

Chelone digitalis, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone digitalis is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
