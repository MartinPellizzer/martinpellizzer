Cyathea veitchiana

Cyathea veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height, with fronds reaching lengths of over 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Cyathea veitchiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance.
