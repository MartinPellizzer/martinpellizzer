Cucurbita maxima var. californica

Cucurbita maxima var. californica, commonly known as the California pumpkin, is a variety of the common squash species native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often ribbed fruit that can vary in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
