Callista pulchella

Callista pulchella, commonly known as the pretty callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has hairy, narrow leaves. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Callista pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
