Clinopodium matthewsii

Clinopodium matthewsii, commonly known as Matthews' mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and are arranged in dense spikes.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and emit a mint-like scent when crushed.
