Ceropegia cincta

Ceropegia cincta, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor or outdoor cultivation.

Ceropegia cincta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
