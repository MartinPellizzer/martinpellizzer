Combretum burttii

Combretum burttii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
