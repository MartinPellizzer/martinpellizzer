Conophytum limpidum

Conophytum limpidum, commonly known as the transparent conophytum, is a species of flowering plant in the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations to arid environments, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
