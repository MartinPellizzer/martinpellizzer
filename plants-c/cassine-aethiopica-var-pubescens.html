Cassine aethiopica var. pubescens

Cassine aethiopica var. pubescens, commonly known as the African sycamore or African maple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and riverine habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
