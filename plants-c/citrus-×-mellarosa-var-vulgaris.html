Citrus × mellarosa var. vulgaris

Citrus × mellarosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between the mandarin and the sweet orange.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented fruits that are typically easy to peel. The plant is a evergreen shrub or small tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its juice, pulp, and culinary uses.
