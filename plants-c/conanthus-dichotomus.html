Conanthus dichotomus

Conanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the dichotomous conanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
