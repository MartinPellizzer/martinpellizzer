Cuphea racemosa subsp. racemosa

Cuphea racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the whorled bearded cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink.

The plant produces clusters of small, hairy bracts that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
