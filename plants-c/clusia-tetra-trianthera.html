Clusia tetra-trianthera

Clusia tetra-trianthera, commonly known as the four-flowered clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers, which are typically borne in groups of four, giving it its specific name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
