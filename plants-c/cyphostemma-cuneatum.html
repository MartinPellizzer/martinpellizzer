Cyphostemma cuneatum

Cyphostemma cuneatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cyphostemma cuneatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
