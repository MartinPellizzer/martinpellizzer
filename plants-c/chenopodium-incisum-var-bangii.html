Chenopodium incisum var. bangii

Chenopodium incisum var. bangii, commonly known as Bang's lamb's quarters, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically appearing in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential forage plant.
