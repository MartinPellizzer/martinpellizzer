Clerodendrum molle var. glabrescens

Clerodendrum molle var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet chalice, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red, often with a velvety texture. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
