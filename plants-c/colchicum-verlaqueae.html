Colchicum verlaqueae

Colchicum verlaqueae, commonly known as the Verlaque autumn crocus, is a perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region of southwestern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Colchicum verlaqueae is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.
