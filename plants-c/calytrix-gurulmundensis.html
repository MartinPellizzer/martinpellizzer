Calytrix gurulmundensis

Calytrix gurulmundensis, commonly known as the Gurgurmund sandbush, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Calytrix gurulmundensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens with low water requirements.
