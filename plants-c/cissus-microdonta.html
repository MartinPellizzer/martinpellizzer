Cissus microdonta

Cissus microdonta, commonly known as the climbing goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Cissus microdonta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
