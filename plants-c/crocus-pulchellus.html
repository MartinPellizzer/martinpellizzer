Crocus pulchellus

Crocus pulchellus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of central Asia.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus pulchellus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking early-season blooms.
