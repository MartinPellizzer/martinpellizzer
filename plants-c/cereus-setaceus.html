Cereus setaceus

Cereus setaceus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
