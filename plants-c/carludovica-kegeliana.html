Carludovica kegeliana

Carludovica kegeliana, commonly known as the Kegel's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in botanical collections.
