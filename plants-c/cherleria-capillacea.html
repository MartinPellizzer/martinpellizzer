Cherleria capillacea

Cherleria capillacea, commonly known as the silky hairplant, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth and small, ovate leaves covered in fine, hair-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, contain spores that are dispersed by wind.

Cherleria capillacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its ecosystem.
