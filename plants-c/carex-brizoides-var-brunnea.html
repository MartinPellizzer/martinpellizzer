Carex brizoides var. brunnea

Carex brizoides var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its darker, more bronzed foliage compared to other Carex species.
