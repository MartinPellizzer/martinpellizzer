Conyza pedunculata

Conyza pedunculata, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. The plant has a bushy appearance with numerous branches and small, blue to purple flower heads. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Conyza pedunculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
