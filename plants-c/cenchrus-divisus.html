Cenchrus divisus

Cenchrus divisus, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to form dense mats, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
