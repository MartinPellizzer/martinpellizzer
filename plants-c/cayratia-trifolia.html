Cayratia trifolia

Cayratia trifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, blue-black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Cayratia trifolia is also known for its ability to emit a sweet, fragrant aroma when touched.
