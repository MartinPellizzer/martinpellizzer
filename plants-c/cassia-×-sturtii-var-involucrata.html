Cassia × sturtii var. involucrata

Cassia × sturtii var. involucrata, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, silky yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
