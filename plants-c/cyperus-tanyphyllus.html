Cyperus tanyphyllus

Cyperus tanyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Cyperus tanyphyllus is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
