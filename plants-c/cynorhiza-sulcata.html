Cynorhiza sulcata

Cynorhiza sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cynorhiza sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
