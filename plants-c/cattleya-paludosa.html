Cattleya paludosa

Cattleya paludosa, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in damp, shaded environments and has a distinctive appearance with broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a yellow or green lip. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cattleya paludosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
