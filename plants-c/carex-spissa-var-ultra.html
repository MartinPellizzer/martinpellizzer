Carex spissa var. ultra

Carex spissa var. ultra, commonly known as the narrow leaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
