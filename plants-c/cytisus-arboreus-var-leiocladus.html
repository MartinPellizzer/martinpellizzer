Cytisus arboreus var. leiocladus

Cytisus arboreus var. leiocladus, commonly known as the silverleaf broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, narrow, and also covered in fine white pubescence, contributing to its silvery foliage. It produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
