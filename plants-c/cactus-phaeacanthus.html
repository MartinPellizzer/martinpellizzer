Cactus phaeacanthus

Cactus phaeacanthus, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, columnar stem with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its surface, which helps in reducing water loss through transpiration. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to extreme drought conditions.

The plant's shallow root system enables it to quickly absorb rainwater when it becomes available.
