Chionanthus harmandii

Chionanthus harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's fringeflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often described as having a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Chionanthus harmandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
