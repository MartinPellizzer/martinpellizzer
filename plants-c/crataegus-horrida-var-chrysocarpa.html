Crataegus horrida var. chrysocarpa

Crataegus horrida var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits in the fall, which are edible but typically not consumed by humans.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open areas with partial shade.
