Cassia ochnacea var. latifolia

Cassia ochnacea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Additionally, it has been utilized in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
