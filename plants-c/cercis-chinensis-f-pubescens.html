Cercis chinensis f. pubescens

Cercis chinensis f. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a rounded, compact growth habit with a dense canopy of foliage. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
