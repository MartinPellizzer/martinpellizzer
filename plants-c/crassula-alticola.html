Crassula alticola

Crassula alticola, commonly known as the rock rose or mountain crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky outcrops and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
