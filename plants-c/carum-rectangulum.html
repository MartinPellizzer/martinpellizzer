Carum rectangulum

Carum rectangulum, commonly known as square caraway, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The seeds, which are small and oval, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Carum rectangulum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
