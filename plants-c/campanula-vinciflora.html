Campanula vinciflora

Campanula vinciflora, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula vinciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
