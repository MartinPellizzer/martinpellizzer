Crepidium clemensii

Crepidium clemensii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Crepidium clemensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
