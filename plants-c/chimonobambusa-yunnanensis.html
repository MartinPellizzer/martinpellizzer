Chimonobambusa yunnanensis

Chimonobambusa yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its culms are often used in traditional construction and crafts. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local biodiversity.
