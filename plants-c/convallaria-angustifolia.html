Convallaria angustifolia

Convallaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowers develop into red berries, which contain the seeds.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
