Carex spadicea var. kochiana

Carex spadicea var. kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and flowering structures called spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
