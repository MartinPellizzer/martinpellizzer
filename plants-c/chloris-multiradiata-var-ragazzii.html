Chloris multiradiata var. ragazzii

Chloris multiradiata var. ragazzii, commonly known as the Ragazzi's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
