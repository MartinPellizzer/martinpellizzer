Crocus caelestis

Crocus caelestis, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crocus caelestis is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread over time, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
