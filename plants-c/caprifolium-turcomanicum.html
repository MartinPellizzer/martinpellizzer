Caprifolium turcomanicum

Caprifolium turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

Caprifolium turcomanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
