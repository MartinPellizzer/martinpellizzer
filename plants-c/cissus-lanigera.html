Cissus lanigera

Cissus lanigera, commonly known as the velvety green vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
