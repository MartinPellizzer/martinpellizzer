Crataegus walokochiana var. joachmyii

Crataegus walokochiana var. joachmyii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and riverine vegetation. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and the plant is characterized by its thorny stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
