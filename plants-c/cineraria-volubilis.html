Cineraria volubilis

Cineraria volubilis, commonly known as the trailing cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant features fuzzy, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cineraria volubilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
