Celaenodendron mexicanum

Celaenodendron mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
