Chiloglottis diphylla

Chiloglottis diphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a rare and striking terrestrial orchid native to the subtropical rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a unique growth habit with two distinct leaf types. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.
