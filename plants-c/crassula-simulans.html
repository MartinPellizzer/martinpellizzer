Crassula simulans

Crassula simulans, commonly known as the "Baby Blue Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring or summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
