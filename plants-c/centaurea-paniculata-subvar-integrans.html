Centaurea paniculata subvar. integrans

Centaurea paniculata subvar. integrans, commonly known as the purple gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
