Crepis pulchra

Crepis pulchra, commonly known as the showy hoary grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are arranged in a composite head, consisting of many tiny florets, which attract various pollinators.

Crepis pulchra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and subtle blooms.
