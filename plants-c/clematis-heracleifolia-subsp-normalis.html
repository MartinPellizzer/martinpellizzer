Clematis heracleifolia subsp. normalis

Clematis heracleifolia subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the common clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe and western Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
