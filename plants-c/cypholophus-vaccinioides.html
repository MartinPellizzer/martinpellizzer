Cypholophus vaccinioides

Cypholophus vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically red or reddish-brown in color, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces tiny, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
