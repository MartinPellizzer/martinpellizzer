Copaifera rigida

Copaifera rigida, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a valuable resin used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. Copaifera rigida thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
