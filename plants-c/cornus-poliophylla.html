Cornus poliophylla

Cornus poliophylla, commonly known as the white cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

Cornus poliophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
