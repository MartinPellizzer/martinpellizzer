Cestrum plukenetii

Cestrum plukenetii, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.
