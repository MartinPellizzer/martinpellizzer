Caraipa laurifolia

Caraipa laurifolia, commonly known as the Caripito laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Caraipa laurifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
