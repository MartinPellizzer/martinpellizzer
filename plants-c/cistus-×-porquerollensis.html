Cistus × porquerollensis

Cistus × porquerollensis, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, known for its aromatic and often resinous foliage. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
