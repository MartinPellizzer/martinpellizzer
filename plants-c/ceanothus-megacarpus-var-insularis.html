Ceanothus megacarpus var. insularis

Ceanothus megacarpus var. insularis, commonly known as the island ceanothus, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mesic forests and rocky areas. It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
