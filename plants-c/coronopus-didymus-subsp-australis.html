Coronopus didymus subsp. australis

Coronopus didymus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the slender heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
