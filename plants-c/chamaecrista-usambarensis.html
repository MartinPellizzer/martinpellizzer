Chamaecrista usambarensis

Chamaecrista usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis Crownflower, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
