Chrysocoma microphylla

Chrysocoma microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Chrysocoma microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
