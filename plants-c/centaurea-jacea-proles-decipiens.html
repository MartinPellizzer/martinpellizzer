Centaurea jacea proles decipiens

Centaurea jacea proles decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
