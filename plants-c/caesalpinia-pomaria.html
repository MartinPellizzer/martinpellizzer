Caesalpinia pomaria

Caesalpinia pomaria, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright red or orange pod-like fruits. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
