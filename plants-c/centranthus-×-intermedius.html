Centranthus × intermedius

Centranthus × intermedius, commonly known as the hybrid red valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is a hybrid between Centranthus ruber and Centranthus ramosus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species.
