Cyperus canus

Cyperus canus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikelets are usually borne in clusters, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus canus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
