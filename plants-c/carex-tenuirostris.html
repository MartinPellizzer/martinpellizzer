Carex tenuirostris

Carex tenuirostris, commonly known as narrow-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally light to dark green in color. Its flowering structure consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex tenuirostris is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
