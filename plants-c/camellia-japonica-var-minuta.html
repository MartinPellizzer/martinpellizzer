Camellia japonica var. minuta

Camellia japonica var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide year-round foliage. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Due to its small size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
