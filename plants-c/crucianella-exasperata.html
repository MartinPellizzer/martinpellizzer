Crucianella exasperata

Crucianella exasperata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils near the shore. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, narrow, bean-like fruits. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh coastal environment.

Crucianella exasperata plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small coastal organisms.
