Commelina rhizocarpa

Commelina rhizocarpa, commonly known as the root-bearing spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
