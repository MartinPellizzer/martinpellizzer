Cynthia viridis

Cynthia viridis, commonly known as the green cynthia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in moist, grassy areas. The plant features slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Cynthia viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
