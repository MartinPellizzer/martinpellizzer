Convolvulus urosepalus

Convolvulus urosepalus, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
