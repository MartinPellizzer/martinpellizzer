Centaurium turneri

Centaurium turneri, commonly known as the Sierra centaury, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Centaurium turneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
