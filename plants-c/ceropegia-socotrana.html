Ceropegia socotrana

Ceropegia socotrana, commonly known as Socotra's moon vine, is a unique succulent native to the Socotra Archipelago.

It features distinctive, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as flies. The plant has fleshy, jointed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ceropegia socotrana is part of the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its specialized floral structures and ecological significance.
