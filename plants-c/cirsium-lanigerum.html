Cirsium lanigerum

Cirsium lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and prairies.

Cirsium lanigerum is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
