Cactus melaleucus

Cactus melaleucus, commonly known as the honey bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.
