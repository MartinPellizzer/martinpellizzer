Coelogyne ecallosa

Coelogyne ecallosa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and sword-shaped, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Coelogyne ecallosa is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
