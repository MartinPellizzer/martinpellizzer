Campanula cymbalaria

Campanula cymbalaria, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
