Cecropia sandersoniana

Cecropia sandersoniana, commonly known as the sanderson's cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cecropiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with certain ant species, which protect it from herbivores in exchange for shelter and food. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important component of secondary forest succession.

The tree is also valued for its wood and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
