Ceropegia paniculata subsp. kougabergensis

Ceropegia paniculata subsp.

kougabergensis, commonly known as the Kougaberg ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
