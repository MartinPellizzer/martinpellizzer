Campanula cissophylla var. brachycalyx

Campanula cissophylla var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
