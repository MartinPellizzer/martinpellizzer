Calamagrostis suksdorfii

Calamagrostis suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's reed, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with spikelets that are typically light green to golden in hue. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis suksdorfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
