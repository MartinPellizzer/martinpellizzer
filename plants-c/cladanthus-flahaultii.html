Cladanthus flahaultii

Cladanthus flahaultii, commonly known as the Flahault's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
