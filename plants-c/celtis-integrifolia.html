Celtis integrifolia

Celtis integrifolia, commonly known as the velvet tree or netleaf elms, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that turn dark purple when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Celtis integrifolia is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
