Cirrhopetalum blepharistes

Cirrhopetalum blepharistes, commonly known as the fringed petal orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with elongated petals that give it a unique, flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The orchid's name reflects the fringed edges of its petals, which are a striking feature in its floral display.
