Cleistocactus smaragdiflorus

Cleistocactus smaragdiflorus, commonly known as the emerald-flowered cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that form a dense, globular shape. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically hidden within the stem, a feature that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
