Chrysanthemum richterioides

Chrysanthemum richterioides, commonly known as the Richter's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
