Crataegus pinnatifida var. major

Crataegus pinnatifida var. major, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 11 leaflets, and its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
