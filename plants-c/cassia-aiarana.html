Cassia aiarana

Cassia aiarana, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cassia aiarana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
