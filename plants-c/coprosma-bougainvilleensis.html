Coprosma bougainvilleensis

Coprosma bougainvilleensis, commonly known as the Bougainville coprosma, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Bougainville.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal or lowland areas. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but it produces colorful, papery bracts that can range from red to pink or white.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
