Campanula rotundifolia subsp. romanica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

romanica, commonly known as the Roman bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural habitats.
