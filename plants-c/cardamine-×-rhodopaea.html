Cardamine × rhodopaea

Cardamine × rhodopaea, commonly known as the purple crowfoot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and has a short flowering period in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
