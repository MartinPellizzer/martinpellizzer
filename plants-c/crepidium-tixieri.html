Crepidium tixieri

Crepidium tixieri, commonly known as the Tixieri's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has specialized roots that form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to habitat destruction, Crepidium tixieri is considered endangered and is often found in protected conservation areas.
