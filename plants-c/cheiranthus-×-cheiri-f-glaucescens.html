Cheiranthus × cheiri f. glaucescens

Cheiranthus × cheiri f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and fuzzy, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often found in alpine or coastal environments due to its tolerance for harsh climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, colorful plants.
