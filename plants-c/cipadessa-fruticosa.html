Cipadessa fruticosa

Cipadessa fruticosa, commonly known as the white-flowered cipadessa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
