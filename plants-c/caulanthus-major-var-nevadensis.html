Caulanthus major var. nevadensis

Caulanthus major var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Valley onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
