Crocus concinnus

Crocus concinnus, commonly known as the slender crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm.

The slender crocus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
