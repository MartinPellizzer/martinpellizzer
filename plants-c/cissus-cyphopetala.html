Cissus cyphopetala

Cissus cyphopetala, commonly known as the African leafy vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
