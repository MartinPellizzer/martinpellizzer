Canarium ovatum

Canarium ovatum, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits. The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biodiesel. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Canarium ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
