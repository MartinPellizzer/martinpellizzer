Carex longipedunculata subsp. cuprea

Carex longipedunculata subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as coppery sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which are typically reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name.

The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually green with a reddish tinge on the underside.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, each containing small, three-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated.
