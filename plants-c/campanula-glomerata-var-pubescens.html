Campanula glomerata var. pubescens

Campanula glomerata var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically hairy or pubescent, giving the plant its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
