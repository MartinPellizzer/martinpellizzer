Convallaria majalis subspecioid prolificans

Convallaria majalis subspecioid prolificans, commonly known as the prolific lily of the valley, is a rare and botanically distinct variant of the traditional lily of the valley.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and genetic divergence from the more common subspecies.
