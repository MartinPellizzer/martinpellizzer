Calliandra formosa var. gracilis

Calliandra formosa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses nitrogen-fixing capabilities through its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
