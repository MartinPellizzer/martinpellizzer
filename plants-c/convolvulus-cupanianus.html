Convolvulus cupanianus

Convolvulus cupanianus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
