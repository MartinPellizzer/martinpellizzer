Canthium golungense

Canthium golungense, commonly known as the African spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Canthium golungense is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
