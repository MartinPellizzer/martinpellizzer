Comolia ovalifolia var. acutifolia

Comolia ovalifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved comolia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
