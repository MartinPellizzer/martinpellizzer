Corylus avellana var. schizochlamys

Corylus avellana var. schizochlamys, commonly known as the split-leaf hazel, is a variety of the hazel tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are followed by edible nuts in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

This variety is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in a range of soil conditions.
