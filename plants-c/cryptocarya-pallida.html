Cryptocarya pallida

Cryptocarya pallida, commonly known as the white-leafed cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine white waxy coating. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Cryptocarya pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
