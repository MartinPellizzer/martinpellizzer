Ceanothus oliganthus var. hirsutus

Ceanothus oliganthus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in native plant landscaping.
