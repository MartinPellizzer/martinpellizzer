Cleistanthus boivinianus

Cleistanthus boivinianus, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
