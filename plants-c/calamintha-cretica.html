Calamintha cretica

Calamintha cretica, commonly known as Greek pennyroyal, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features small, pale blue or purple flowers and has a strongly aromatic, menthol-like scent. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported antiseptic and digestive properties.

Calamintha cretica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
