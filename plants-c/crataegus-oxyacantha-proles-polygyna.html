Crataegus oxyacantha proles polygyna

Crataegus oxyacantha proles polygyna, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity in various habitats.
