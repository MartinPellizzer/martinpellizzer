Cnicus neomexicanus

Cnicus neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico pricklyweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cnicus neomexicanus is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
