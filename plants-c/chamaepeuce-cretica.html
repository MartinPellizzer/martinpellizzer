Chamaepeuce cretica

Chamaepeuce cretica, commonly known as Cretan rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
