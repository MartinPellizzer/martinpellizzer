Chlorophora tinctoria subsp. mora

Chlorophora tinctoria subsp.

mora, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and is valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also economically significant for timber production.
