Cousinia winkleriana

Cousinia winkleriana, commonly known as the Winkler's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by silver-gray foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Cousinia winkleriana is often found in rocky or sandy areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its region.
