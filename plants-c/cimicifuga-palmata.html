Cimicifuga palmata

Cimicifuga palmata, commonly known as the American bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, white, lace-like flowers arranged in panicles. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cimicifuga palmata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
