Capparis horrida

Capparis horrida, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

Capparis horrida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
