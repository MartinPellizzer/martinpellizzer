Carex austrolucorum

Carex austrolucorum, commonly known as the southern light sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Carex austrolucorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
