Cerasus jamasakura var. chikusiensis

Cerasus jamasakura var. chikusiensis, commonly known as the Chikusi cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.
