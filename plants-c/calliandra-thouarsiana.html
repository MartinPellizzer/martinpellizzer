Calliandra thouarsiana

Calliandra thouarsiana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, and they bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
