Cyathea hastulata

Cyathea hastulata, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea hastulata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
