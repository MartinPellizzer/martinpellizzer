Calamagrostis acuminata

Calamagrostis acuminata, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-gray foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis acuminata is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
