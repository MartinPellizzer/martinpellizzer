Calophyllum dongnaiense

Calophyllum dongnaiense, commonly known as the Dong Nai torch tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and dense, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
