Calliandra schottii

Calliandra schottii, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
