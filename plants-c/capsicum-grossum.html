Capsicum grossum

Capsicum grossum, commonly known as the giant capsicum or large bell pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, bell-shaped fruits that can grow up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow-orange flowers.

The fruit is edible, commonly used in cooking, and contains capsaicin, though it is generally less spicy than other chili varieties.
