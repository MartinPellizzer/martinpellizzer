Calceolaria scandens

Calceolaria scandens, commonly known as the climbing calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
