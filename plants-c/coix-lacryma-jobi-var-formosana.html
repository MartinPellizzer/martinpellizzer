Coix lacryma-jobi var. formosana

Coix lacryma-jobi var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwanese Job's tears, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, teardrop-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and rice paddies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
