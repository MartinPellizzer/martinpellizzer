Cirsium × breviscapum

Cirsium × breviscapum, commonly known as the short-horned thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
