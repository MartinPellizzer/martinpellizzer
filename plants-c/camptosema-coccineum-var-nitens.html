Camptosema coccineum var. nitens

Camptosema coccineum var. nitens, commonly known as the red-flowered camptosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Camptosema coccineum var. nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
