Chionochloa flavescens

Chionochloa flavescens, commonly known as the yellow mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
