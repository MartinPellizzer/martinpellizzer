Cerasus sargentii f. pubescens

Cerasus sargentii f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a variant of the Sargent cherry.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

The botanical features of this variety make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
