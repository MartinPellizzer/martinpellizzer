Crotalaria longibracteata

Crotalaria longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, elongated bracts that enclose its fruit, which is a pod containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria longibracteata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
