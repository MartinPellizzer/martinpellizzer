Clematis trichocarpa

Clematis trichocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep red. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Clematis trichocarpa prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for climbing structures.
