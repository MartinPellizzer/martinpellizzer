Carex haematolepis

Carex haematolepis, commonly known as the blood-stained sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex haematolepis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
