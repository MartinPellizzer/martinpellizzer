Carex retroflexa var. texensis

Carex retroflexa var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in prairie and wetland habitats, contributing to ecological diversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
