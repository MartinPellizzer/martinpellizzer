Chamaescilla dyeri

Chamaescilla dyeri, commonly known as the Tasmanian tea-tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
