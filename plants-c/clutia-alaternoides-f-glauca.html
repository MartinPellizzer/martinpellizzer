Clutia alaternoides f. glauca

Clutia alaternoides f. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous clutia, is a variant of the clutia plant characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in coastal and moist woodland habitats. The plant features small, simple leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, contributing to its unique appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The glaucous varietal form is notable for its enhanced frost resistance and ornamental value in gardens.
