Centaurea deusta subsp. deusta

Centaurea deusta subsp.

deusta, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking purple to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has spiny, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a characteristic species of Mediterranean flora.
