Croton argithamnoides

Croton argithamnoides, commonly known as the silver croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and white. The leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture, while the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Croton argithamnoides prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
