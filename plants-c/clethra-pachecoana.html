Clethra pachecoana

Clethra pachecoana, commonly known as the Pacheco's sweet pepper, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Clethraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white to pale yellow blossoms that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Clethra pachecoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
