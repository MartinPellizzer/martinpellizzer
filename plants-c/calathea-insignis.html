Calathea insignis

Calathea insignis, commonly known as the Velvet Leaf Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. It thrives in low light conditions and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its foliage changes color slightly in response to light exposure, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty.
