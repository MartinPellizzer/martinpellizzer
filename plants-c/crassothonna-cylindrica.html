Crassothonna cylindrica

Crassothonna cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical flax.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
