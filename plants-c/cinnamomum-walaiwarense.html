Cinnamomum walaiwarense

Cinnamomum walaiwarense, commonly known as the Thai cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a fragrant scent when crushed.

The bark, which contains essential oils, is the primary source of its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
