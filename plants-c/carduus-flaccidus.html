Carduus flaccidus

Carduus flaccidus, commonly known as the sickle thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
