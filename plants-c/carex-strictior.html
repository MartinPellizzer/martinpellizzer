Carex strictior

Carex strictior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for invertebrates and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
