Clutia alaternoides var. microphylla

Clutia alaternoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved clutia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
