Caesalpinia yucatanensis subsp. hondurensis

Caesalpinia yucatanensis subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America. It is characterized by its striking orange to red flower clusters that appear in late spring.

The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry to moist forest environments.
