Catasetum galeritum

Catasetum galeritum, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its striking, elongated green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, which are attracted by its unique structure and scent.

Catasetum galeritum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
