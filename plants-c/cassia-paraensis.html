Cassia paraensis

Cassia paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. Cassia paraensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
