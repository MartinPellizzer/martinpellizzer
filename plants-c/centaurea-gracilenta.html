Centaurea gracilenta

Centaurea gracilenta, commonly known as slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers make it a distinctive species within the Centaurea genus.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
