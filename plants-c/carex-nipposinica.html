Carex nipposinica

Carex nipposinica, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex nipposinica produces small, clustered inflorescences that are usually inconspicuous, with wind-pollinated flowers.
