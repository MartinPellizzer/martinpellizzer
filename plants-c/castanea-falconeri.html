Castanea falconeri

Castanea falconeri, commonly known as the Falkland Island chestnut, is a rare and unique species native to the Falkland Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and grasslands. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that enclose one or two edible nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.
