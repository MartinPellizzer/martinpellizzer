Commiphora acuminata

Commiphora acuminata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
