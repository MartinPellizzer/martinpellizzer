Carex pallescens var. luxuriosa

Carex pallescens var. luxuriosa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or semi-aquatic habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
