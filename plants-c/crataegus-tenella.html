Crataegus tenella

Crataegus tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus tenella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
