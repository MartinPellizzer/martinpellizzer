Calanthe kawakamii

Calanthe kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami's calanthe, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its blooms, which appear in late spring, feature a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, making it a visually appealing species.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
