Croton lindheimerianus var. tharpii

Croton lindheimerianus var. tharpii, commonly known as the Texas croton, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It features vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and eye-catching addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
