Carex firma var. subalpina

Carex firma var. subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous or alpine environments. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
