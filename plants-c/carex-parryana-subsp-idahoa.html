Carex parryana subsp. idahoa

Carex parryana subsp.

idahoa, commonly known as Parry's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and narrow, with a fibrous root system. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
