Crataegus hupehensis var. flavida

Crataegus hupehensis var. flavida, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, thorn-like structures on its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
