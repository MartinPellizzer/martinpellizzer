Cnemidostachys longifolia

Cnemidostachys longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and intricate, spider-like floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive elongated spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
