Cousinia larica

Cousinia larica, commonly known as the Karakoram gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces small, blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia larica is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
