Crataegus × eriocalyx

Crataegus × eriocalyx, commonly known as the common hawthorn or midland hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant is typically deciduous, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and gardens.

Crataegus × eriocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
