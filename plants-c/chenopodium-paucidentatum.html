Chenopodium paucidentatum

Chenopodium paucidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium paucidentatum is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil salinity.
