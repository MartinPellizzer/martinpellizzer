Cousinia malekiana

Cousinia malekiana, commonly known as the Afghan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Cousinia malekiana is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
