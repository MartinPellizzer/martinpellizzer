Caesalpinia ebano

Caesalpinia ebano, commonly known as blackwood or ebony tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The tree is notable for its dense, dark wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savannas and forest edges.

Caesalpinia ebano plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
