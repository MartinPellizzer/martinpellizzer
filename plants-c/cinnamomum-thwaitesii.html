Cinnamomum thwaitesii

Cinnamomum thwaitesii, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon or true cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, and it produces small, round berries containing winged seeds.

The tree is closely related to other cinnamon species but is distinguished by its unique bark and essential oil composition.
