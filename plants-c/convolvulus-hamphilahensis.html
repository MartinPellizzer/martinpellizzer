Convolvulus hamphilahensis

Convolvulus hamphilahensis, commonly known as the Hamphil's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit and showy flowers. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent spur. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on xerophytic adaptations and floral morphology.
