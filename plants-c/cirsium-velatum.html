Cirsium velatum

Cirsium velatum, commonly known as the velvet thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as fields and woodland edges. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, which give it its common name.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
