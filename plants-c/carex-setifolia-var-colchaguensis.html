Carex setifolia var. colchaguensis

Carex setifolia var. colchaguensis, commonly known as the Colchagua sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
