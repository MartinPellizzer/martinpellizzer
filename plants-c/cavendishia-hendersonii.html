Cavendishia hendersonii

Cavendishia hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's cavendishia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flower spike. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a unique and eye-catching appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
