Coleus palliolatus

Coleus palliolatus, commonly known as the variegated coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage, which often features shades of green, white, and red. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and trailing stems. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a textured surface that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Coleus palliolatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
