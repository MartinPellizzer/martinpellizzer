Caesalpinia tinctoria

Caesalpinia tinctoria, commonly known as the Brazilian wood or false mallow, is a leguminous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.

Additionally, it has historical significance in dye production due to its rich pigments.
