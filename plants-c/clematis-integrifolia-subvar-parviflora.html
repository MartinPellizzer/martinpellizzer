Clematis integrifolia subvar. parviflora

Clematis integrifolia subvar. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are small, typically white or pale blue, with five petals. The plant produces actinomorphic flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
