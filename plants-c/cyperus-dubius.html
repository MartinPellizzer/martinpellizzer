Cyperus dubius

Cyperus dubius, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This perennial plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters of grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus dubius is often used in aquatic landscaping and can serve as a natural water filter in wetland ecosystems.
