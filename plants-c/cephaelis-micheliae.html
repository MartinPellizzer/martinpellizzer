Cephaelis micheliae

Cephaelis micheliae, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding insects.

Cephaelis micheliae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
