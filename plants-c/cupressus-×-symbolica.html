Cupressus × symbolica

Cupressus × symbolica, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a hybrid conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for landscaping in arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
