Coleus villosus

Coleus villosus, commonly known as the hairy coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus villosus is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant for its striking visual appeal.
