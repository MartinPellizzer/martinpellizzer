Candollea glaberrima

Candollea glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous candollea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
