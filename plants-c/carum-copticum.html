Carum copticum

Carum copticum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and aromatic, finely divided leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oblong, seed-filled capsules. Its seeds are rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive spicy and aromatic flavor.

Carum copticum thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.
