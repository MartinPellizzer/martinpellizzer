Cola sphaerosperma

Cola sphaerosperma, commonly known as the African cola nut, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spherical fruits containing hard, black seeds. The seeds are the primary part used, containing caffeine and other alkaloids.

It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.
