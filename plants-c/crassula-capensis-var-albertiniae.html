Crassula capensis var. albertiniae

Crassula capensis var. albertiniae, commonly known as the Albertina Crassula, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and requiring minimal watering.

This variety is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
