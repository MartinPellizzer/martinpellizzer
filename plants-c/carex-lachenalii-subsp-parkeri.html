Carex lachenalii subsp. parkeri

Carex lachenalii subsp.

parkeri, commonly known as the Parkers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
