Carex × luteola

Carex × luteola, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

Carex × luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
