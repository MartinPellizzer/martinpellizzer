Cleome subcordata

Cleome subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cleome subcordata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
