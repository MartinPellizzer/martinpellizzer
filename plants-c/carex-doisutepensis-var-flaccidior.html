Carex doisutepensis var. flaccidior

Carex doisutepensis var. flaccidior, commonly known as the flaccidior sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to wetland habitats, with fibrous roots and a compact growth form.
