Croton rhamnifolius f. ovatus

Croton rhamnifolius f. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to shades of red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
