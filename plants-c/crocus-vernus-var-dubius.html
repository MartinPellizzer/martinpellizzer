Crocus vernus var. dubius

Crocus vernus var. dubius, commonly known as the Dutch crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

The species is closely related to other crocus varieties and is sometimes used in hybrid breeding programs.
