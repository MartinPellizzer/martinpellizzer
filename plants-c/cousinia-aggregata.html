Cousinia aggregata

Cousinia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Cousinia aggregata is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
