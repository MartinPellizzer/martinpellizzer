Cousinia pseudaffinis

Cousinia pseudaffinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia pseudaffinis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
