Coscinium fenestratum var. macrophyllum

Coscinium fenestratum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf black wood or wild eggplant, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. The fruit's skin is thick and leathery, containing a single large, dark red seed.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
