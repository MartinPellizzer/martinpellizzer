Chaerophyllum rigidum

Chaerophyllum rigidum, commonly known as rigid hogweed.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a tall, hollow stem that is often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The flowers are small, white or greenish, and arranged in an umbel, typical of the Apiaceae family.
