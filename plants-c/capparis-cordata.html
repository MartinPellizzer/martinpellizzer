Capparis cordata

Capparis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, edible seeds.

Capparis cordata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
