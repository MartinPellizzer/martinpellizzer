Cestrum sylvaticum

Cestrum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or yellow, often blooming in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, black seeds.

Cestrum sylvaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
