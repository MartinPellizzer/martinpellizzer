Campanula speciosa var. corbariensis

Campanula speciosa var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbarien bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
