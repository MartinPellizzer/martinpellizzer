Cocos sapida

Cocos sapida, commonly known as the sweet coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fruit known as the coconut, which contains both a liquid endosperm and a fibrous husk. The coconut's development is influenced by tropical climate conditions, requiring high temperatures and abundant rainfall.

Botanically, Cocos sapida belongs to the Arecaceae family and is valued for its economic and nutritional contributions.
