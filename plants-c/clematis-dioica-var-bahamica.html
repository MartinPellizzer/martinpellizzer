Clematis dioica var. bahamica

Clematis dioica var. bahamica, commonly known as the Bahamian Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
