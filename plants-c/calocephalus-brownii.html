Calocephalus brownii

Calocephalus brownii, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calocephalus brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
