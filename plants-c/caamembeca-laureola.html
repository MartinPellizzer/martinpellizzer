Caamembeca laureola

Caamembeca laureola, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
