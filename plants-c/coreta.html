Coreta

Coreta, commonly known as the "false indigo," is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade. Coreta has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
