Clematis viticella subsp. normalis

Clematis viticella subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the panicled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and its ability to climb using its twining stems.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is compound, with finely divided leaves that provide good shade.

It is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vigorous, ornamental vines.
