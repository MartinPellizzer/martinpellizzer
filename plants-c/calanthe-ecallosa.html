Calanthe ecallosa

Calanthe ecallosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Calanthe ecallosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
