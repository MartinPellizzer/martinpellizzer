Chrysanthemum atratum var. liburnicum

Chrysanthemum atratum var. liburnicum, commonly known as the Liburnian chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in the autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
