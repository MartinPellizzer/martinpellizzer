Cousinia alfredii

Cousinia alfredii, commonly known as the alfred's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia alfredii is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and seasonal blooms.
