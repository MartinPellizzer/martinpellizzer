Cotoneaster koidzumii

Cotoneaster koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright branches covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding ornamental value.

Cotoneaster koidzumii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
