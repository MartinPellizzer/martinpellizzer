Croton dichogamus

Croton dichogamus, commonly known as the African croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are either male or female, a feature known as dichogamy. Its leaves are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
