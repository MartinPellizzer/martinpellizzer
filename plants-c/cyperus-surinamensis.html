Cyperus surinamensis

Cyperus surinamensis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus surinamensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
