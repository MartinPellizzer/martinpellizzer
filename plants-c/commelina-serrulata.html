Commelina serrulata

Commelina serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes other species of spiderworts.

Commelina serrulata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
