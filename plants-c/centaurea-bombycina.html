Centaurea bombycina

Centaurea bombycina, commonly known as the silvery centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often surrounded by pale yellow bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Centaurea bombycina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
