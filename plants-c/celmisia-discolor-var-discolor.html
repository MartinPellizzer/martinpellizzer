Celmisia discolor var. discolor

Celmisia discolor var. discolor, commonly known as the silver-topped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
