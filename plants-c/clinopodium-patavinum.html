Clinopodium patavinum

Clinopodium patavinum, commonly known as Patavine Mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to violet in color and bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium patavinum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
