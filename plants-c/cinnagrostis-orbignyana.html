Cinnagrostis orbignyana

Cinnagrostis orbignyana, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, compact flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the botanist Alcide d'Orbigny.
