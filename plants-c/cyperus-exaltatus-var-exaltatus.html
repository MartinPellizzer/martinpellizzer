Cyperus exaltatus var. exaltatus

Cyperus exaltatus var. exaltatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus exaltatus var. exaltatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
