Caryopteris odorata

Caryopteris odorata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and have a slight fuzzy texture.

Caryopteris odorata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
