Campanula rotundifolia var. catalanica

Campanula rotundifolia var. catalanica, commonly known as the Catalan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
