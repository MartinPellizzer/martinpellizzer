Cousinia silyboides subsp. disfulensis

Cousinia silyboides subsp.

disfulensis, commonly known as the Afghan goatherb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears numerous small florets.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
