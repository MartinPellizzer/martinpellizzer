Cedrus libanotica

Cedrus libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in crossed pairs. Its cones are woody and contain two to three seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient architecture and religious symbolism.
