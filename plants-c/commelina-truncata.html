Commelina truncata

Commelina truncata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive truncate (notched) base, and it produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Commelina truncata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
