Cissus elegans

Cissus elegans, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plant displays.
