Ceropegia concinna

Ceropegia concinna, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia concinna is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
