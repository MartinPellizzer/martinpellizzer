Casearia subcuneata

Casearia subcuneata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral display.
