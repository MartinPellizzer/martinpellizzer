Centaurea battandieri

Centaurea battandieri, commonly known as the Balkan star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
