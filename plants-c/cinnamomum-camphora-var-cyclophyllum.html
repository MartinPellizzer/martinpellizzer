Cinnamomum camphora var. cyclophyllum

Cinnamomum camphora var. cyclophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, camphor-containing wood and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The varietal form cyclophyllum is distinguished by its unique leaf and fruit morphology compared to other camphor species.
