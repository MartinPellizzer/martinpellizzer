Carduus repandus

Carduus repandus, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its leaves are typically lobed with a rough, hairy texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and roadsides.
