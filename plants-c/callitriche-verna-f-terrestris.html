Callitriche verna f. terrestris

Callitriche verna f. terrestris, commonly known as the common waternymph, is a low-growing aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically forms dense mats on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny, white, and borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
