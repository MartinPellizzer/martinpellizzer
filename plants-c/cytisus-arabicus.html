Cytisus arabicus

Cytisus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of elliptical leaflets. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cytisus arabicus is also known for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
