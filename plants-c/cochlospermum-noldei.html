Cochlospermum noldei

Cochlospermum noldei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer or early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Cochlospermum noldei is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its ecological benefits to local wildlife.
