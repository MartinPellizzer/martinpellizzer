Centaurea lapsanifolia

Centaurea lapsanifolia, commonly known as the Lapsanifolia thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Centaurea lapsanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
