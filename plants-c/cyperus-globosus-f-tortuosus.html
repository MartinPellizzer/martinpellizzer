Cyperus globosus f. tortuosus

Cyperus globosus f. tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted globeflower, is a variant of the globeflower plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spherical flower heads that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence is densely packed with small, clustered flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a specimen plant in moist environments.
