Callista hercoglossa

Callista hercoglossa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Callista hercoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
