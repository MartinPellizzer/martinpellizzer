Cimicifuga podocarpa

Cimicifuga podocarpa, commonly known as the western black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga podocarpa is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
