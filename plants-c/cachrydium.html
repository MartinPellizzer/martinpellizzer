Cachrydium

Cachrydium, commonly known as the "scented cress," is a genus of flowering plants in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, herbaceous plants with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and are followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cachrydium species are native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils.

These plants are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value and for their historical use in herbal medicine.
