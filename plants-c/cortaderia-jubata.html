Cortaderia jubata

Cortaderia jubata, commonly known as the silver grass or pampas grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring long, feathery plumes that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves that are stiff and grass-like, with a fibrous texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or as a landscape accent.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a popular choice and a potential invasive species in some regions.
