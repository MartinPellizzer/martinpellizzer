Cassia africana

Cassia africana, commonly known as the African candle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in whorls of three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Cassia africana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
