Cassia martinicensis

Cassia martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with 8 to 12 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cassia martinicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
