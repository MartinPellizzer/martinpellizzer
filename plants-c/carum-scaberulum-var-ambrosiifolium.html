Carum scaberulum var. ambrosiifolium

Carum scaberulum var. ambrosiifolium, commonly known as Mexican tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a subtle, herbaceous scent. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their mild anise-like flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its aromatic properties.
