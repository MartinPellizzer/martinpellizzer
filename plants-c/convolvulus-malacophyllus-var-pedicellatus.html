Convolvulus malacophyllus var. pedicellatus

Convolvulus malacophyllus var. pedicellatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The variety pedicellatus is distinguished by its longer pedicels compared to the typical form of Convolvulus malacophyllus.
