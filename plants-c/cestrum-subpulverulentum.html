Cestrum subpulverulentum

Cestrum subpulverulentum, commonly known as the silver bell or silver cestrum, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has smooth, often silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cestrum subpulverulentum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.
