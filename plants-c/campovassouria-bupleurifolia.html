Campovassouria bupleurifolia

Campovassouria bupleurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, serrated leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
