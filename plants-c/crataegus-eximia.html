Crataegus eximia

Crataegus eximia, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon, where it grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible red berries. It has small, oval leaves and produces thorny branches, typical of many species in the genus Crataegus.

The Pacific crabapple is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
