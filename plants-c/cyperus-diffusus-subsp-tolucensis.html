Cyperus diffusus subsp. tolucensis

Cyperus diffusus subsp.

tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucan flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This subspecies is often found in regions of Central and South America, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
