Condylostylis venusta

Condylostylis venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil while absorbing nutrients efficiently.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
