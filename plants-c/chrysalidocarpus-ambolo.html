Chrysalidocarpus ambolo

Chrysalidocarpus ambolo, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

The Madagascar palm is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in dry environments.
