Calliandra haematostoma var. minutifolia

Calliandra haematostoma var. minutifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or pink inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, fluffy flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.

Due to its showy appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces.
