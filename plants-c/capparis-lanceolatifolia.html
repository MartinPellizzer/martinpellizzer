Capparis lanceolatifolia

Capparis lanceolatifolia, commonly known as the lanceolate caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which is characterized by its often spiny stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis lanceolatifolia is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
