Crataegus monogyna var. laciniata

Crataegus monogyna var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
