Cheirostylis moniliformis

Cheirostylis moniliformis, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a series of closely spaced, small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
