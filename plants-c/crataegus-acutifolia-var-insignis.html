Crataegus acutifolia var. insignis

Crataegus acutifolia var. insignis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, acuminate leaves that are typically hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
