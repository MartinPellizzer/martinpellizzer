Centaurea cadevallii

Centaurea cadevallii, commonly known as the Catalan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive purple center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea cadevallii is often found in open habitats like grasslands and woodland edges.
