Crataegus corusca var. hillii

Crataegus corusca var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and small white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a red to orange pome that ripens in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
