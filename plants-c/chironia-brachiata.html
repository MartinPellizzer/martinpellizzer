Chironia brachiata

Chironia brachiata, commonly known as the slender bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems and delicate, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or near streams. Chironia brachiata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
