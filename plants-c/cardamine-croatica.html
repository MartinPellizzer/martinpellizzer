Cardamine croatica

Cardamine croatica, commonly known as Croatian watercress, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in forest understories.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
