Crotalaria cannabinua

Crotalaria cannabinua, commonly known as the Indian hemp or wild cotton.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

The plant has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of fiber and in herbal medicine.
