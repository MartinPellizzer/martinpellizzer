Campanula robinsiae

Campanula robinsiae, commonly known as Robinsia bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine down.

Campanula robinsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
