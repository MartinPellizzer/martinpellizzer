Cornus oblonga f. pilosula

Cornus oblonga f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy-leaf dogwood, is a unique variant of the dogwood genus native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in shaded garden settings.
