Campanula rotundifolia proles bocconei

Campanula rotundifolia proles bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with a distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark blue or black berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
