Cheilanthes farinosa var. grisea

Cheilanthes farinosa var. grisea, commonly known as the gray fern, is a perennial, evergreen fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and grayish-green, leathery fronds that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, rocky gardens.
