Cousinia pamirica

Cousinia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia pamirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
