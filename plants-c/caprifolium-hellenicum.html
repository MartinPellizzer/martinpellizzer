Caprifolium hellenicum

Caprifolium hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually white or yellowish, with a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Caprifolium hellenicum is often found in rocky soils and open woodland areas, thriving in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
