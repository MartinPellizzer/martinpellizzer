Chrysophyllum lacourtianum

Chrysophyllum lacourtianum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a deciduous tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that mature to a bright red color, containing a single seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
