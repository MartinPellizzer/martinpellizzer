Chamaesciadium flabellifolium

Chamaesciadium flabellifolium, commonly known as the fan-leaved chamaesciadium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the end of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
