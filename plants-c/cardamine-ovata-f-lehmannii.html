Cardamine ovata f. lehmannii

Cardamine ovata f. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's watercress, is a variant of the broadleaf watercress.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its edible leaves and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
