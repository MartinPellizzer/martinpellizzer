Camunium chinense

Camunium chinense, commonly known as Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Camunium chinense is valued for its camphor-rich wood, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural preservative.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
