Carduus nutans subsp. perspinosus

Carduus nutans subsp.

perspinosus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust, upright growth habit.

The subspecies perspinosus is distinguished by its more densely spined leaves compared to the typical Carduus nutans.
