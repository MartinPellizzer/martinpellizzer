Clausia hispida var. lasiocarpa

Clausia hispida var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly clausia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are small, oval, and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
