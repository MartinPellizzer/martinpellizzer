Cinnamomum brasiliense

Cinnamomum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or cinnamomum of Brazil, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic berries that contain hard, elliptical seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils.

This species is valued for its unique fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
