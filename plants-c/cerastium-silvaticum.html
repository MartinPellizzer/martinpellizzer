Cerastium silvaticum

Cerastium silvaticum, commonly known as the woodland mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium silvaticum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
