Cephalaria vaillantii

Cephalaria vaillantii, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cephalaria vaillantii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
