Cylindropuntia weingartiana

Cylindropuntia weingartiana, commonly known as the Weingartian barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its spines are relatively short and can be either straight or curved, providing protection from herbivores.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in drought conditions.
