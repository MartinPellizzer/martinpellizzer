Carex vesiciformis

Carex vesiciformis, commonly known as the bladderleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is native to parts of North America. Its unique feature is the presence of bladder-like structures on the stems, which are thought to aid in water retention.

Carex vesiciformis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
