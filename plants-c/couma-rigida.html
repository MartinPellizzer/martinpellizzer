Couma rigida

Couma rigida, commonly known as the rigid couma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Couma rigida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
