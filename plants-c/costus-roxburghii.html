Costus roxburghii

Costus roxburghii, commonly known as the blackroot or Indian costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous perennial that produces tall, leafless stems with showy, spadix-like flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are thick and fleshy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Costus roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and horticulture.
