Cotula potentillina

Cotula potentillina, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Cotula potentillina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
