Campanopsis decipiens

Campanopsis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Campanopsis decipiens is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
