Cineraria cacalioides

Cineraria cacalioides, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, providing a contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Cineraria cacalioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
