Crataegus aestivalis var. maloides

Crataegus aestivalis var. maloides, commonly known as the Maloides hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
