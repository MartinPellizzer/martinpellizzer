Cochlospermum luetzeiburgii

Cochlospermum luetzeiburgii, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
