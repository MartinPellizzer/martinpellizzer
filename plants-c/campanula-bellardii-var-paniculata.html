Campanula bellardii var. paniculata

Campanula bellardii var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The variety is noted for its compact size and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
