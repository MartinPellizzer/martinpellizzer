Canavalia aurita

Canavalia aurita, commonly known as the ear bean or golden cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Canavalia aurita is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm climates with moderate rainfall.

It also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
