Clinopodium wardii

Clinopodium wardii, commonly known as Ward's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Clinopodium wardii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
