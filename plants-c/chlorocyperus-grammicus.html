Chlorocyperus grammicus

Chlorocyperus grammicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist habitats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, typical of the sedge family.

Chlorocyperus grammicus is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
