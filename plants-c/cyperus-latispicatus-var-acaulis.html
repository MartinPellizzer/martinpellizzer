Cyperus latispicatus var. acaulis

Cyperus latispicatus var. acaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-sided stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with standing water or high humidity. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced stem structure, giving it a more compact and rosette-like growth form.
