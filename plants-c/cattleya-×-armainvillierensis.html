Cattleya × armainvillierensis

Cattleya × armainvillierensis, commonly known as the Armainvillier's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns and a distinct lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It prefers well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
