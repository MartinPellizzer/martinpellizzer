Cornus mas f. variegata

Cornus mas f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cornel cherry, is a decorative shrub prized for its ornamental foliage.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, enhancing its visual appeal. It produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This cultivar thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
