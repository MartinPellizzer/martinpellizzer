Clematis ulbrichiana

Clematis ulbrichiana, commonly known as the Ulbrich's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
