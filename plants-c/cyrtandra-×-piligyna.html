Cyrtandra × piligyna

Cyrtandra × piligyna, commonly known as the hybrid Cyrtandra, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra sericea and Cyrtandra hirta. This plant is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The flowers are typically showy, with tubular corollas and two lips, often in shades of white or yellow.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value.
