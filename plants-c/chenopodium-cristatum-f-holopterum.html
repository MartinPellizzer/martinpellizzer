Chenopodium cristatum f. holopterum

Chenopodium cristatum f. holopterum, commonly known as the smooth lamb's quarters, is a variant of the common lamb's quarters.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and branched stems. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a slightly succulent texture. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, triangular seed pods.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly cultivated as a leaf vegetable in some regions.
