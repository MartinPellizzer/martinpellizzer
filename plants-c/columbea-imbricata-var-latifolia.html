Columbea imbricata var. latifolia

Columbea imbricata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf columbea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance. This variant is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
