Carex longicuspis

Carex longicuspis, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Carex longicuspis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
