Conopodium hermonis

Conopodium hermonis, commonly known as the Hermionis bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Conopodium hermonis is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
