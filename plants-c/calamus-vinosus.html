Calamus vinosus

Calamus vinosus, commonly known as red canes or red bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish-brown stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found growing in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in herbal medicine, though its primary value lies in its ornamental and practical uses.

Calamus vinosus is a hardy plant that thrives in humid conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
