Cayaponia petiolulata

Cayaponia petiolulata, commonly known as the Cayaponia vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing plant with slender, woody stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that is a small, fleshy berry.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
