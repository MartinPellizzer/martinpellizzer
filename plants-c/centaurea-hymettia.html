Centaurea hymettia

Centaurea hymettia, commonly known as the Hymettian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hymettus in Greece.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Centaurea hymettia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
