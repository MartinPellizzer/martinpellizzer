Cynorkis paradoxa

Cynorkis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a unique combination of colors, often featuring a deep purple or violet hue with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several blooms, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
