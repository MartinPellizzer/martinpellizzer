Crambe cordifolia subvar. sewerzowii

Crambe cordifolia subvar. sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian rapeseed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are encased in a thick, triangular pod, and the plant is known for its high oil content.

It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.
