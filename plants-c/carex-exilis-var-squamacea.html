Carex exilis var. squamacea

Carex exilis var. squamacea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. Native to moist, shady environments, this plant thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
