Conyza stricta var. pinnatifida

Conyza stricta var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other common weeds and ornamental plants.
