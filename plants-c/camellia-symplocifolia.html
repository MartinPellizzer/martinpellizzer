Camellia symplocifolia

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a glossy surface, and the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals.

Camellia symplocifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
