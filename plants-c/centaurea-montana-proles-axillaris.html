Centaurea montana proles axillaris

Centaurea montana proles axillaris, commonly known as the alpine centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
