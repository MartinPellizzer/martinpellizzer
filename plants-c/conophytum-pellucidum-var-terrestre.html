Conophytum pellucidum var. terrestre

Conophytum pellucidum var. terrestre, commonly known as the "window plant," is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often translucent, allowing light to pass through. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
