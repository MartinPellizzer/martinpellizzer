Chromolaena perforata

Chromolaena perforata, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curly leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences. Chromolaena perforata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
