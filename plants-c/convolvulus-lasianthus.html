Convolvulus lasianthus

Convolvulus lasianthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped blooms that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
