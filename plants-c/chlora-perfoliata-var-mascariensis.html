Chlora perfoliata var. mascariensis

Chlora perfoliata var. mascariensis, commonly known as the Mascarene chlora, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in coastal areas with sandy soils. It is adapted to tolerate salty air and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
