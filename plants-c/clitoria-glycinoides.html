Clitoria glycinoides

Clitoria glycinoides, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and has compound leaves with three leaflets. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
