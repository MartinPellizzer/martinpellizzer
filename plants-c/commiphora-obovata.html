Commiphora obovata

Commiphora obovata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and obovate leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for incense and medicinal purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
