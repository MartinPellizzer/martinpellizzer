Catasetum confusum

Catasetum confusum, commonly known as the confused orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often colorful and fragrant, with a distinctive labellum that plays a key role in attracting specific pollinators.

Catasetum confusum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
