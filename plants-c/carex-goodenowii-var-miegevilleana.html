Carex goodenowii var. miegevilleana

Carex goodenowii var. miegevilleana, commonly known as the Miegeville sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and unique morphological features compared to other Carex species.
