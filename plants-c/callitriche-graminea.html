Callitriche graminea

Callitriche graminea, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in groups of three, which is a distinctive feature.

Callitriche graminea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
