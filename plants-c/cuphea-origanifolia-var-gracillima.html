Cuphea origanifolia var. gracillima

Cuphea origanifolia var. gracillima, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs that give them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
