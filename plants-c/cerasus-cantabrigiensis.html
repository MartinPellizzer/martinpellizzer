Cerasus cantabrigiensis

Cerasus cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Harvard cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by its distinctive berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
