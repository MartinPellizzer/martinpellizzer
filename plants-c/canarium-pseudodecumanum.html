Canarium pseudodecumanum

Canarium pseudodecumanum, commonly known as the false decumbent canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
