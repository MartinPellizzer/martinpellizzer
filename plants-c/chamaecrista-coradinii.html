Chamaecrista coradinii

Chamaecrista coradinii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
