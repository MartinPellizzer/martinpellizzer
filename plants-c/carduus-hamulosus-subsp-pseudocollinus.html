Carduus hamulosus subsp. pseudocollinus

Carduus hamulosus subsp.

pseudocollinus, commonly known as the false buttonweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a taproot system and produces a rosette of spiny, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering heads are composite, consisting of both ray and disk flowers, with yellow petals and a central cluster of tubular florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, including lobed and toothed margins, which aid in its identification.
