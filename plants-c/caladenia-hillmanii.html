Caladenia hillmanii

Caladenia hillmanii, commonly known as the Hillman's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with a slender, erect stem and a single, elongated flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid regions of its habitat. Caladenia hillmanii is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the cooler months, usually between August and November.

Its striking appearance and limited distribution make it a notable and ecologically significant species within its native range.
