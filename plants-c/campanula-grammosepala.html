Campanula grammosepala

Campanula grammosepala, commonly known as the Grammosena bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This species is valued for its attractive flowers and hardy nature, though it is not commonly cultivated in home gardens.
