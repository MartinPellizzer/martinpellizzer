Cayaponia subsessilis

Cayaponia subsessilis, commonly known as the Cayaponia tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are yellow or orange in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
