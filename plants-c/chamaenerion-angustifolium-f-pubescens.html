Chamaenerion angustifolium f. pubescens

Chamaenerion angustifolium f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent fireweed, is a variant of the fireweed plant characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions, as well as parts of the northern United States and Canada. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as those found in tundra, wetlands, and along roadsides. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are pink or red, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
