Calamina imberbis

Calamina imberbis, commonly known as the smooth calamina, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the late spring and early summer. The plant's leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, Calamina imberbis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
