Catabrosa pumila

Catabrosa pumila, commonly known as the dwarf catabrosa, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
