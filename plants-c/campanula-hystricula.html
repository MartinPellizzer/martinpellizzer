Campanula hystricula

Campanula hystricula, commonly known as the hedgehog bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and southern Europe.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
