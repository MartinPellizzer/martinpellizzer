Campanula hawkinsiana

Campanula hawkinsiana, commonly known as the Hawkin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula hawkinsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
