Cosmos intercedens

Cosmos intercedens, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with feathery leaves and vibrant pink or white petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cosmos intercedens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
