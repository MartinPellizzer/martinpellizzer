Carex enervis

Carex enervis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the tips of the stems. Carex enervis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
