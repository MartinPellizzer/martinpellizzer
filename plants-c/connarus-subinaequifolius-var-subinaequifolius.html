Connarus subinaequifolius var. subinaequifolius

Connarus subinaequifolius var. subinaequifolius, commonly known as the slender connarus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
