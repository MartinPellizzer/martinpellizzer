Caltha scaposa

Caltha scaposa, commonly known as the scapose crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and numerous, with showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are slender and often bear the flowers singly or in clusters.

Caltha scaposa thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
