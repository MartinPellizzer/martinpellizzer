Capparis mayana

Capparis mayana, commonly known as the Mexican sour orange, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
