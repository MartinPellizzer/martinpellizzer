Campanula lorei

Campanula lorei, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine layer of down.

Campanula lorei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
