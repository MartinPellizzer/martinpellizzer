Chamaefistula latifolia

Chamaefistula latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf boxwood, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate pruning make it a versatile plant in garden settings.
