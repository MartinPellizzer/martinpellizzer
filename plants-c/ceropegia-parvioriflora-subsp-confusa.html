Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp. confusa

Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
