Clitoria selloi

Clitoria selloi, commonly known as the purple winged pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
