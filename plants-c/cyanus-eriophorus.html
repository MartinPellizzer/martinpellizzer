Cyanus eriophorus

Cyanus eriophorus, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

Cyanus eriophorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
