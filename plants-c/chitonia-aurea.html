Chitonia aurea

Chitonia aurea, commonly known as the golden chitons, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Chitoniidae.

It is characterized by its smooth, iridescent shell that displays a golden hue, often with a metallic sheen. The shell is typically oval in shape and can grow up to 5 centimeters in length. This marine gastropod is found in shallow tropical and subtropical waters, often on rocky substrates.

Its diet consists mainly of algae and other microscopic organisms, which it scrapes from surfaces using its radula.
