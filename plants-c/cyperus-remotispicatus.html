Cyperus remotispicatus

Cyperus remotispicatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Cyperus remotispicatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
