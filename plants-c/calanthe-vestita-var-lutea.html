Calanthe vestita var. lutea

Calanthe vestita var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow calanthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive lip petal. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
