Cytisus commutatus subsp. merinoi

Cytisus commutatus subsp.

merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
