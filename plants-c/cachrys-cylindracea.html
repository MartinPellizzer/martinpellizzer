Cachrys cylindracea

Cachrys cylindracea, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Cachrys cylindracea is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
