Crataegus illudens

Crataegus illudens, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to central and southern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Crataegus illudens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
