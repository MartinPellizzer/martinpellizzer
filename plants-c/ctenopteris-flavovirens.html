Ctenopteris flavovirens

Ctenopteris flavovirens, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically bright green with a yellowish or golden hue on the underside. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces spores on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Ctenopteris flavovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
