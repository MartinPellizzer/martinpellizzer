Cornucopiae perennans

Cornucopiae perennans, commonly known as the Everbloom Vine, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate woodlands of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, producing lush, broad leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is renowned for its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
