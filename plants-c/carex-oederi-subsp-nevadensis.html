Carex oederi subsp. nevadensis

Carex oederi subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are generally narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
