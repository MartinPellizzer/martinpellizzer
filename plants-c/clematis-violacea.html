Clematis violacea

Clematis violacea, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can climb trellises or fences with support.
