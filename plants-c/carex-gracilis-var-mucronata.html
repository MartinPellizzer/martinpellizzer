Carex gracilis var. mucronata

Carex gracilis var. mucronata, commonly known as the slender sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly hairy, while the flower heads are small and clustered.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
