Cynoglossum siculum

Cynoglossum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian mouse-ear, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cynoglossum siculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
