Crotalaria triquetra var. garanbiensis

Crotalaria triquetra var. garanbiensis, commonly known as the Garanbi crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a spurred structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
