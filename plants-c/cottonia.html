Cottonia

Cottonia, commonly known as the cottonwood plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that often have four petals and are typically pink or white in color. The leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Cottonia species are often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
