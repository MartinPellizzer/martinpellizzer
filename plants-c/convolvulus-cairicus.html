Convolvulus cairicus

Convolvulus cairicus, commonly known as the Kashmir convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and traditional medicine.
