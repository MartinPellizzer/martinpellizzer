Callicarpa areolata

Callicarpa areolata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple fruits that appear in the fall. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, with a glossy green surface.

Callicarpa areolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
