Carex fuscoatia

Carex fuscoatia, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Carex fuscoatia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
