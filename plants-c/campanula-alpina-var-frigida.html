Campanula alpina var. frigida

Campanula alpina var. frigida, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and other mountainous regions.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

Campanula alpina var. frigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
