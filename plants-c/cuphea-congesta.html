Cuphea congesta

Cuphea congesta, commonly known as the clustered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Cuphea congesta thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
