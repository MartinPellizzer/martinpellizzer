Crassula rattrayi

Crassula rattrayi, commonly known as the Rattray's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
