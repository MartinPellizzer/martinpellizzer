Cerris

Cerris, commonly known as the sweet chestnut, is a genus of flowering plants in the beech family, Fagaceae.

It includes several species, with *Cerris siliquastrum* being one of the most notable. The plant is characterized by its large, deciduous leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits called cupules. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and the Middle East.

Cerris trees are valued for their ornamental beauty and historical use in woodworking and as a food source.
