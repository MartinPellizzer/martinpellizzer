Crataegus microphylla

Crataegus microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus microphylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
