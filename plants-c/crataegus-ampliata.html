Crataegus ampliata

Crataegus ampliata, commonly known as the broadleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical features include pinnate stipules and a distinctive, often fragrant, floral scent.

Crataegus ampliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
