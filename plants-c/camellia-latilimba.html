Camellia latilimba

Camellia latilimba, commonly known as the broad-leaf camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Camellia latilimba thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value and is also valued for its hardy nature in cooler climates.
