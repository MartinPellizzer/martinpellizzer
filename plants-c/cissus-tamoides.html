Cissus tamoides

Cissus tamoides, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cissus tamoides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
