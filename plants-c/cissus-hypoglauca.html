Cissus hypoglauca

Cissus hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded areas.

Cissus hypoglauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
