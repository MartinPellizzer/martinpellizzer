Chionanthus adamsii

Chionanthus adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
