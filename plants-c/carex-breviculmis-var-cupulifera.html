Carex breviculmis var. cupulifera

Carex breviculmis var. cupulifera, commonly known as the cupular sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically short and compact, giving it a mat-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
