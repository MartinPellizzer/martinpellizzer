Convolvulus tricolor var. cupanianus

Convolvulus tricolor var. cupanianus, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored petals.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, white, and blue, depending on the variety. It is a climbing vine that thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
