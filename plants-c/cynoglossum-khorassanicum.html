Cynoglossum khorassanicum

Cynoglossum khorassanicum, commonly known as the Persian mouse ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
