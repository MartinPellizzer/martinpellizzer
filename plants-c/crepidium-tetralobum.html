Crepidium tetralobum

Crepidium tetralobum, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with four distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a short rhizome and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
