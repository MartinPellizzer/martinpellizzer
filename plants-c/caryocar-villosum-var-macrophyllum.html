Caryocar villosum var. macrophyllum

Caryocar villosum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf cupuacu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, hairy leaves and produces yellowish flowers that develop into edible, hairy fruits. The plant belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is known for its distinctive, elongated fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of the Amazonian ecosystem.
