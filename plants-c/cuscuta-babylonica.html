Cuscuta babylonica

Cuscuta babylonica, commonly known as the Babylonian dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, thread-like structure that allows it to attach to a wide range of host plants. The plant is native to various regions in Asia and has been introduced to other parts of the world. It is often found growing on crops such as soybeans and cotton, causing significant damage.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a challenging weed to control.
