Cleome viscosa var. parviflora

Cleome viscosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
