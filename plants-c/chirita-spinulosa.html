Chirita spinulosa

Chirita spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny chirita, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom from spring to early summer. Chirita spinulosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering display.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
