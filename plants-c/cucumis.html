Cucumis

Cucumis, commonly known as the cucumber plant, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces elongated, edible fruits. The plant has palmate leaves and yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Cucumis species are cultivated worldwide for their crisp, refreshing fruits used in various culinary applications.

The genus includes several economically important crops, such as cucumber, melon, and various types of squash.
