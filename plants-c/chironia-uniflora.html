Chironia uniflora

Chironia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower chironia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, solitary flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its small, elegant blooms.

Chironia uniflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
