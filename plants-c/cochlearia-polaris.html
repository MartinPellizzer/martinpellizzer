Cochlearia polaris

Cochlearia polaris, commonly known as the polar horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs.

Cochlearia polaris is known for its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer.
