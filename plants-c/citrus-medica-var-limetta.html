Citrus medica var. limetta

Citrus medica var. limetta, commonly known as the lima lemon, is a small citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow, smooth, and thin rind, which is typically harvested when it reaches a mature size. The fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, giving it a tart flavor, and its pulp is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is commonly used in aromatherapy.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
