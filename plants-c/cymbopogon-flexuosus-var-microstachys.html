Cymbopogon flexuosus var. microstachys

Cymbopogon flexuosus var. microstachys, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves that contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinct lemon scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it valuable in both traditional medicine and modern fragrance industries.
