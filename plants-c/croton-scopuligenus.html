Croton scopuligenus

Croton scopuligenus, commonly known as the rock croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
