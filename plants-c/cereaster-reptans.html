Cereaster reptans

Cereaster reptans, commonly known as the creeping candleplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, candle-like yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cereaster reptans thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
