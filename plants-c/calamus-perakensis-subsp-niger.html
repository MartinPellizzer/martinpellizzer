Calamus perakensis subsp. niger

Calamus perakensis subsp.

niger, commonly known as black calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae). It is a climbing herb characterized by its dark brown, segmented rhizomes and long, narrow leaves.

The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
