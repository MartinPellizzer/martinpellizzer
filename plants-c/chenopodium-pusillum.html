Chenopodium pusillum

Chenopodium pusillum, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are minute and dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas easily.

Chenopodium pusillum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for microorganisms.
