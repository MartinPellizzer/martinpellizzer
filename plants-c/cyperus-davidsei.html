Cyperus davidsei

Cyperus davidsei, commonly known as the David's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in three rows, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus davidsei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
