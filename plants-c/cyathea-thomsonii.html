Cyathea thomsonii

Cyathea thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan. Its rhizomes produce roots that help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Cyathea thomsonii plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
