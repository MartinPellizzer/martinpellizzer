Carex × fragosoana

Carex × fragosoana, commonly known as the fragosoan sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Carex × fragosoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
