Cissampelos pareiroides

Cissampelos pareiroides, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.
