Cyathea patens

Cyathea patens, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea patens plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
