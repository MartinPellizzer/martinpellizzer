Cassia oxyadena

Cassia oxyadena, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia oxyadena is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
