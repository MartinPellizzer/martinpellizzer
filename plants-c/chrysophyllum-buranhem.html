Chrysophyllum buranhem

Chrysophyllum buranhem, commonly known as the African cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to red in color when ripe. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
