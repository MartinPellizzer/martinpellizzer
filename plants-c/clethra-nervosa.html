Clethra nervosa

Clethra nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath family, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are elliptical with prominent veins, and its clusters of white or pale pink flowers appear in late summer.

Clethra nervosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
