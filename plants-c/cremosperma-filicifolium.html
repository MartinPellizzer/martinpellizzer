Cremosperma filicifolium

Cremosperma filicifolium, commonly known as the fern-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns, with elongated, feather-like pinnae. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
