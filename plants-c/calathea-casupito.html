Calathea casupito

Calathea casupito, commonly known as the flame plant or zigzag plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and have a zigzag or undulating edge, which gives the plant its common name. Its leaves exhibit a unique phenomenon called nyctinasty, where they fold at night and unfold during the day.

Calathea casupito thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil for optimal growth.
