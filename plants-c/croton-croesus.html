Croton croesus

Croton croesus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, which often features shades of yellow, green, and red. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
