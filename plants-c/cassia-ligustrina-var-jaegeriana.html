Cassia ligustrina var. jaegeriana

Cassia ligustrina var. jaegeriana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
