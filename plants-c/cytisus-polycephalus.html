Cytisus polycephalus

Cytisus polycephalus, commonly known as the yellow flowering broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval, leathery leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus polycephalus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
