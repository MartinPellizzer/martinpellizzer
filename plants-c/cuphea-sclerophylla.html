Cuphea sclerophylla

Cuphea sclerophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Cuphea sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
