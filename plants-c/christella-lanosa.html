Christella lanosa

Christella lanosa, commonly known as the woolly christella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and its stems are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

Christella lanosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
