Cenolophium fischeri subsp. lapponicum

Cenolophium fischeri subsp.

lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is well adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to avoid harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of high-altitude and tundra ecosystems.
