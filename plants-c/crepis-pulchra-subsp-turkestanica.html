Crepis pulchra subsp. turkestanica

Crepis pulchra subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
