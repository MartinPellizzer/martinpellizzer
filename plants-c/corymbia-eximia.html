Corymbia eximia

Corymbia eximia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators. The timber of this species is highly valued for its density and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

It is also an important plant for local wildlife, providing habitat and food sources.
