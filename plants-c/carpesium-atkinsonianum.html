Carpesium atkinsonianum

Carpesium atkinsonianum, commonly known as the Atkinson's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and basal rosette of dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, which are composed of many tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Carpesium atkinsonianum has a taproot system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
