Carex atrata var. japonoalpina

Carex atrata var. japonoalpina, commonly known as the Japanese alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its narrow, dark green leaves are often adorned with a reddish tint, especially in colder climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or alpine habitats.
