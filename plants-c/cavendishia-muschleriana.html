Cavendishia muschleriana

Cavendishia muschleriana, commonly known as the Muschler's Cavendish, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
