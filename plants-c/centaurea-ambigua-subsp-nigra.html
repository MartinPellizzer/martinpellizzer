Centaurea ambigua subsp. nigra

Centaurea ambigua subsp.

nigra, commonly known as blackhead thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust, upright stem with spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape, which bloom in summer. Its bracts are dark purple to black, giving it its common name.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of open, sunny habitats.
