Carex alata

Carex alata, commonly known as the arrow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of pale to dark brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Carex alata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
