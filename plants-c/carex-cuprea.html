Carex cuprea

Carex cuprea, commonly known as copper sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Copper sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
