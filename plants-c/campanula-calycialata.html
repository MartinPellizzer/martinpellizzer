Campanula calycialata

Campanula calycialata, commonly known as the bellflower of the alps, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Campanula calycialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
