Chaenorhinum thymiflorum

Chaenorhinum thymiflorum, commonly known as the thyme-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and leaves that are aromatic, resembling those of thyme. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
