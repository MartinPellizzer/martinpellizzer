Catha decolor

Catha decolor, commonly known as the African coffee plant, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its woody stem and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The leaves are traditionally chewed for their stimulant properties, containing compounds similar to caffeine.
