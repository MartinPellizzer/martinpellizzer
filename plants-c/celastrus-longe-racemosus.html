Celastrus longe-racemosus

Celastrus longe-racemosus, commonly known as the long-stemmed staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds.

It is often found in humid environments such as forests and along riverbanks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
