Chondrolaena phalaroides

Chondrolaena phalaroides, commonly known as the slender gothic grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, often along roadsides or in grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its competitive nature.
