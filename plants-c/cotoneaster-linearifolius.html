Cotoneaster linearifolius

Cotoneaster linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a bright red color, often containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cotoneaster linearifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
