Centaurea scabiosa var. glehnii

Centaurea scabiosa var. glehnii, commonly known as Glehn's Scotch thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
