Cyperus rubrotinctus

Cyperus rubrotinctus, commonly known as the red-stained flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Cyperus rubrotinctus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
