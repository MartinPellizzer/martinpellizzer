Calliandra seleri

Calliandra seleri, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
