Calliandra erubescens

Calliandra erubescens, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red bracts that surround clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Calliandra erubescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
