Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. scribneri

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. scribneri, commonly known as Scribner's reed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, silver-gray flowers in the summer months. It prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
