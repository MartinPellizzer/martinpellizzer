Carex triquetrifolia

Carex triquetrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial sedge native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

Carex triquetrifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
