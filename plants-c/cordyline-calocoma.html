Cordyline calocoma

Cordyline calocoma, commonly known as the red-veined cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that display a vibrant mix of green and red veins. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, making it suitable for use in gardens or as a landscape accent. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its striking appearance.

Cordyline calocoma prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
