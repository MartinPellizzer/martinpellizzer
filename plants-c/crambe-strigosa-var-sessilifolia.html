Crambe strigosa var. sessilifolia

Crambe strigosa var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved crambe, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its capsule fruits contain several seeds, which are often used in agricultural settings.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is sometimes cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.
