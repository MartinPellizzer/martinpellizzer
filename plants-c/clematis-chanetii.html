Clematis chanetii

Clematis chanetii, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Clematis chanetii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
