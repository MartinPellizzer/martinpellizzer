Clerodendrum longipetiolatum

Clerodendrum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a long petiole, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
