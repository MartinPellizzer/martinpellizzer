Cosmos bipinnatus var. parviflorus

Cosmos bipinnatus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf cosmos, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its delicate appearance and extended blooming period make it a favorite among gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, colorful addition to the landscape.
