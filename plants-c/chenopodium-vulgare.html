Chenopodium vulgare

Chenopodium vulgare, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium vulgare is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of nutrients such as iron and magnesium.
