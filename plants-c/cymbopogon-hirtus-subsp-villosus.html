Cymbopogon hirtus subsp. villosus

Cymbopogon hirtus subsp.

villosus, commonly known as hairy lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.
