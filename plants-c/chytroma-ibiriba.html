Chytroma ibiriba

Chytroma ibiriba, commonly known as the Ibiriba ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the humid lowland rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer with a ghostly blue hue under sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night. Its roots are highly specialized, capable of absorbing moisture from the air rather than soil.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Chytroma ibiriba remains a subject of scientific curiosity and conservation concern.
