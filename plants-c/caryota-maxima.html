Caryota maxima

Caryota maxima, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces large, yellow-orange flowers followed by edible fruits resembling small bananas. Its robust growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Caryota maxima thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
