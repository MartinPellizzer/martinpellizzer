Cassia malacotricha

Cassia malacotricha, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia malacotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
