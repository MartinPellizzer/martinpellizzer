Campyloneurum minus

Campyloneurum minus, commonly known as the lesser rock fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a delicate, creeping rhizome with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
