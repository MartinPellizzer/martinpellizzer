Cassinia aculeata subsp. nova-anglica

Cassinia aculeata subsp.

nova-anglica, commonly known as the New England cassinia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge beneath.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
