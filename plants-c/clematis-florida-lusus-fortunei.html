Clematis florida lusus fortunei

Clematis florida lusus fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to climb trellises and walls, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
