Citrus tanaka

Citrus tanaka, commonly known as the Tanaka lemon, is a lesser-known citrus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still slightly green. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has a bushy growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. Its fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

The Tanaka lemon is also appreciated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
