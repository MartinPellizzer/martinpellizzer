Cynanchica pestalozzae

Cynanchica pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pescalozza's milkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
