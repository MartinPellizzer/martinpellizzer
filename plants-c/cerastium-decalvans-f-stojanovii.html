Cerastium decalvans f. stojanovii

Cerastium decalvans f. stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine white pubescence. The flowers are pink to purple in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer. This plant is native to the Balkans and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of mountainous ecosystems.
