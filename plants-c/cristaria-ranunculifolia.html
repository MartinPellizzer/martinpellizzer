Cristaria ranunculifolia

Cristaria ranunculifolia, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, floating leaves that resemble clover leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and they appear in clusters near the water surface.

Cristaria ranunculifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
