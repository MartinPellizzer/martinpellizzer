Cupressus sphaerocarpa

Cupressus sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical shape and scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive, compact appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. Its leaves are dark green and arranged in crossed pairs, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often used in arid garden settings.
