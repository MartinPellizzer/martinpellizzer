Cocculus acuminatus

Cocculus acuminatus, commonly known as the spurred cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by green, spiny fruits that turn red when mature. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, palmate leaves, and a unique fruit structure with tuberculate ridges.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
