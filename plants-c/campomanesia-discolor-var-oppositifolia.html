Campomanesia discolor var. oppositifolia

Campomanesia discolor var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the white myrtle or white starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small star-shaped berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local use.
