Chorizanthe palmeri

Chorizanthe palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
