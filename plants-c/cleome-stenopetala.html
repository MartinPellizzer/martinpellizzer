Cleome stenopetala

Cleome stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petioled cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome stenopetala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
