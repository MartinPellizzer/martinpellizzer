Chenopodium pekeloi

Chenopodium pekeloi, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.

Chenopodium pekeloi plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and supports local wildlife.
