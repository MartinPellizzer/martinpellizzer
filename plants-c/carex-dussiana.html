Carex dussiana

Carex dussiana, commonly known as the Dussian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

Carex dussiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
