Convolvulus rottlerianus var. tenella

Convolvulus rottlerianus var. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pale yellow or white flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its twining stems that often trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
