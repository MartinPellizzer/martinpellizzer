Chavica sarmentosa

Chavica sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping chavica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Chavica sarmentosa is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
