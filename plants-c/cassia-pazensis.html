Cassia pazensis

Cassia pazensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Cassia pazensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that provide a lush, green canopy.
