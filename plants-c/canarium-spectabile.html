Canarium spectabile

Canarium spectabile, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiky fruits containing a single seed. The fruit, known as a "golden apple," is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
