Carex marcida f. alterna

Carex marcida f. alterna, commonly known as the alternate sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Carex marcida.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in moist, temperate environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
