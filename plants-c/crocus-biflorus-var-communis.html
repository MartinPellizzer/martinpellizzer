Crocus biflorus var. communis

Crocus biflorus var. communis, commonly known as the common crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces showy, early spring blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically emerging through snow-covered ground. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers are composed of six petals arranged in a cup-like shape, with a central stamen that is often brightly colored.

The common crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also known to have medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
