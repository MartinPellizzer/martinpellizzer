Conyzanthus graminifolius

Conyzanthus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved conyzanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming a dense inflorescence that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed habitats.
