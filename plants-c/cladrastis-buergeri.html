Cladrastis buergeri

Cladrastis buergeri, commonly known as Buerger's honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which appear in the spring before the leaves fully develop. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Cladrastis buergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
