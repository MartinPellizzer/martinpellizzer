Cerasus mahaleb var. monstrosa

Cerasus mahaleb var. monstrosa, commonly known as the twisted cherry or mock cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mahaleb species.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It is a hardy shrub or small tree, typically growing in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
