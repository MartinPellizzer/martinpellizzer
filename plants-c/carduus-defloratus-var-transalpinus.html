Carduus defloratus var. transalpinus

Carduus defloratus var. transalpinus, commonly known as the Transalpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth habit. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
