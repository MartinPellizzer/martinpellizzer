Campanula cochleariifolia subf. reflexa

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features attractive, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and are characterized by their lobed and serrated edges. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
