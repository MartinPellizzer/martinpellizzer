Camelina sagittata

Camelina sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf camelina, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Camelina sagittata is also known for its potential as a biofuel crop due to its high oil content.
