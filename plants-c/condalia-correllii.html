Condalia correllii

Condalia correllii, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
