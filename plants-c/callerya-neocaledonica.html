Callerya neocaledonica

Callerya neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia callerya, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia and is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Callerya neocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
