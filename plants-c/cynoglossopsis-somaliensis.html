Cynoglossopsis somaliensis

Cynoglossopsis somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali tongue-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, tongue-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in regions with low rainfall. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often blooming during the dry season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
