Couepia amaraliae

Couepia amaraliae, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, foliage that forms a thick canopy, often used in ornamental horticulture for its lush appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
