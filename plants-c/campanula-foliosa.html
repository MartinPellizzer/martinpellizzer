Campanula foliosa

Campanula foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Campanula foliosa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
