Cirsium pseudopannonicum

Cirsium pseudopannonicum, commonly known as the pseudopannonic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas. The plant features a robust stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are purple to violet, forming a spherical or cylindrical shape, and are typically visited by various pollinators.

The plant is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
