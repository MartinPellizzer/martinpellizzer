Carex festivelloides

Carex festivelloides, commonly known as the festive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
