Carludovica latifolia

Carludovica latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus characterized by its broad, fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Carludovica latifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
