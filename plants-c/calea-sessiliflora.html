Calea sessiliflora

Calea sessiliflora, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea sessiliflora is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
