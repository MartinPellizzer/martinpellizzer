Calamintha hydaspidis

Calamintha hydaspidis, commonly known as water thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces tiny, four-angled fruits.

Calamintha hydaspidis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
