Crataegus poloniensis

Crataegus poloniensis, commonly known as the Polish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in a variety of soil types, including woodland and hedgerow environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
