Cardamomum rufescens

Cardamomum rufescens, commonly known as the red cardamom or scarlet cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant produces small, red, aromatic capsules that contain the spice used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinct, fragrant aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
