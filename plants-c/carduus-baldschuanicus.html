Carduus baldschuanicus

Carduus baldschuanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple or pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in a whitish coating. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native habitat, though it can be invasive in other regions.
