Corysanthes incurva

Corysanthes incurva, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Corysanthes incurva thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
