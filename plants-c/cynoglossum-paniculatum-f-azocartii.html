Cynoglossum paniculatum f. azocartii

Cynoglossum paniculatum f. azocartii, commonly known as the Azocartii bellflower, is a botanical variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to regions in southern Europe and is often found in mountainous areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
