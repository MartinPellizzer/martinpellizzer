Coumarouna ferrea

Coumarouna ferrea, commonly known as Brazilian ironwood, is a member of the Rhamnaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin, while the flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters. The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns yellow when mature, containing a single seed.

This plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
