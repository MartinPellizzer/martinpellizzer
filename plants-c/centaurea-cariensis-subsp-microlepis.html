Centaurea cariensis subsp. microlepis

Centaurea cariensis subsp.

microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
