Cistus × ferreri

Cistus × ferreri, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Cistus × ferreri is also noted for its ability to contribute to soil stabilization in arid and coastal areas.
