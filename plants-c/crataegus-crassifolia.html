Crataegus crassifolia

Crataegus crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus crassifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
