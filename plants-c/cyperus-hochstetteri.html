Cyperus hochstetteri

Cyperus hochstetteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus hochstetteri is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
