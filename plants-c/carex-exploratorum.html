Carex exploratorum

Carex exploratorum, commonly known as the explorer sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters, which are typically green to brown in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex exploratorum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
