Cissus alata

Cissus alata, commonly known as the Devil's Ivy, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, translucent glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. Its stems can climb or trail, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange berries.

Cissus alata is also notable for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various soil types.
