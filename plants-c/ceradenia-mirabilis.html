Ceradenia mirabilis

Ceradenia mirabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Ceradenia mirabilis typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
