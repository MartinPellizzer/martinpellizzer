Citrus × aurantium subsp. natsudaidai

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

natsudaidai, commonly known as the Japanese bitter orange, is a citrus species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, bitter peel and a segmented, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other citrus plants but is distinguished by its unique botanical features and regional significance.
