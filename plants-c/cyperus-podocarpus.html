Cyperus podocarpus

Cyperus podocarpus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Cyperus podocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
