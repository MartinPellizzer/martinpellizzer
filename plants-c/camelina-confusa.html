Camelina confusa

Camelina confusa, commonly known as the Mexican camelina, is an annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Camelina confusa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
