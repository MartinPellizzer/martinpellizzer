Chamaecytisus paczoskii

Chamaecytisus paczoskii, commonly known as the Pacific buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various ecological conditions, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
