Chrysothamnus baileyi

Chrysothamnus baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its structure consists of upright stems with dense clusters of small, bright yellow composite flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
