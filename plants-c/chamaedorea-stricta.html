Chamaedorea stricta

Chamaedorea stricta, commonly known as the Parlor Palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feathery, pinnately compound leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements. It has a slow growth rate, making it ideal for small spaces such as offices or homes.

The leaves are typically a deep green, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries.
