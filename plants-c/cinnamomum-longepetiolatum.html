Cinnamomum longepetiolatum

Cinnamomum longepetiolatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
