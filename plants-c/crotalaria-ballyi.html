Crotalaria ballyi

Crotalaria ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure on the petals.

Crotalaria ballyi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright, showy blooms.
