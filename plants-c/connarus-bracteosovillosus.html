Connarus bracteosovillosus

Connarus bracteosovillosus, commonly known as the silky bract connarus, is a unique tropical plant characterized by its delicate, silky-haired bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
