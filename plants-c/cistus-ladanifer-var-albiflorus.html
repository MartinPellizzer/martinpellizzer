Cistus ladanifer var. albiflorus

Cistus ladanifer var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
