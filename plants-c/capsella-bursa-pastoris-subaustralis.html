Capsella bursa-pastoris subaustralis

Capsella bursa-pastoris subaustralis, commonly known as the Australian chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
