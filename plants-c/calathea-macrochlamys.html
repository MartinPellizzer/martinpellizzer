Calathea macrochlamys

Calathea macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often featuring intricate patterns and vibrant coloration. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
