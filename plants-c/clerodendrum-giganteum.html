Clerodendrum giganteum

Clerodendrum giganteum, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both horticulture and pharmacology.
