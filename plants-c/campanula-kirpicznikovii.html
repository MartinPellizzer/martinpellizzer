Campanula kirpicznikovii

Campanula kirpicznikovii, commonly known as the Kirpicznikov's bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Campanula kirpicznikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
