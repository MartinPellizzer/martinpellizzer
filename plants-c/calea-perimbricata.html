Calea perimbricata

Calea perimbricata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, white petals that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating that gives them a silvery appearance.

Calea perimbricata is also notable for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
