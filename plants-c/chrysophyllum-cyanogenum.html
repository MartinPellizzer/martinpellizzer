Chrysophyllum cyanogenum

Chrysophyllum cyanogenum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow star.

It is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
