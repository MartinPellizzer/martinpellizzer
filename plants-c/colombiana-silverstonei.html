Colombiana silverstonei

Colombiana silverstonei, commonly known as the silver stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, silver-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
