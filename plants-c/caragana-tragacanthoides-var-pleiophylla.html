Caragana tragacanthoides var. pleiophylla

Caragana tragacanthoides var. pleiophylla, commonly known as the yellow flowered caragana, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and medicinal research.
