Chamaecrista setosa var. subsetosa

Chamaecrista setosa var. subsetosa, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
