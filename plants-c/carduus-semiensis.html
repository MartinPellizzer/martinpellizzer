Carduus semiensis

Carduus semiensis, commonly known as the southern thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
