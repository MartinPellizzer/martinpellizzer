Caesalpiniodes triacanthum

Caesalpiniodes triacanthum, commonly known as the three-spined cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets and is distinguished by its spiny thorns along the stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and has some use in traditional medicine.
