Croton rzedowskii

Croton rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that range in color from bright green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton rzedowskii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
