Chenopodium crusoeanum

Chenopodium crusoeanum, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water.

Its edible leaves are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.
