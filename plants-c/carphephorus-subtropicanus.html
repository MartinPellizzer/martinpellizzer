Carphephorus subtropicanus

Carphephorus subtropicanus, commonly known as the subtropical sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Carphephorus subtropicanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
