Croton incanus

Croton incanus, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical landscapes.
