Cucurbita maxima virginica

Cucurbita maxima virginica, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a species native to North America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. This plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant orange, round fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the plant produces both male and female flowers on the same individual.

It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
