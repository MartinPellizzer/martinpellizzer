Crocus pseudoiranicus

Crocus pseudoiranicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The bulbs of this plant are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
