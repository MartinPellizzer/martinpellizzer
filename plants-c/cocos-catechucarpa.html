Cocos catechucarpa

Cocos catechucarpa, commonly known as the African coconut or West African coconut, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and large, fibrous fruits. The fruit produces a white, fleshy mesocarp that is rich in oils and nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible products and ornamental value.

The plant plays an important role in both local ecosystems and human economies in its native regions.
