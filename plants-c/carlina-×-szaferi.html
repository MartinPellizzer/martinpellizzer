Carlina × szaferi

Carlina × szaferi, commonly known as the Szafer's thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carlina acaulis and Carlina vulgaris.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Alps. The plant is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically grayish-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Carlina × szaferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
