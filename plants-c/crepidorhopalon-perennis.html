Crepidorhopalon perennis

Crepidorhopalon perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers remain open for extended periods, giving it the common name "everlasting.".

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
