Crataegus shicola

Crataegus shicola, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Crataegus shicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
