Calophyllum cuneifolium

Calophyllum cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
