Cercocarpus montanus var. traskiae

Cercocarpus montanus var. traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, papery, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky slopes and chaparral habitats.
