Centrocarpha grandiflora

Centrocarpha grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered centaury, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Centrocarpha grandiflora is often found in open meadows and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
