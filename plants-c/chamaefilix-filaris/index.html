Chamaefilix Filaris

Chamaefilix filaris is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and arid regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and mountainous landscapes. Known for its compact form and silvery foliage, this plant is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, adding a subtle touch of color to rocky gardens. Despite its modest appearance, Chamaefilix filaris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
