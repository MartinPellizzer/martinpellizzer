Carex acuta var. polystachia

Carex acuta var. polystachia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
