Cattleya × chloris

Cattleya × chloris, commonly known as the green cattleya, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya moschata.

It is characterized by its vibrant green and yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat of Central and South America. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
