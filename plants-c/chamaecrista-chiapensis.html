Chamaecrista chiapensis

Chamaecrista chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are bipinnate, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
