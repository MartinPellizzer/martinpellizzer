Clematis bartlettii

Clematis bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features climbing stems with thorns and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is typically found in moist, wooded areas. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
