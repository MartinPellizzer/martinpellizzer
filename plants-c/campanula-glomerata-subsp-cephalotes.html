Campanula glomerata subsp. cephalotes

Campanula glomerata subsp.

cephalotes, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
