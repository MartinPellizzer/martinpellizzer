Campium nigrum

Campium nigrum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale purple.

Campium nigrum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
