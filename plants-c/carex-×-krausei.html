Carex × krausei

Carex × krausei, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex divulsa and Carex scabrata, both of which are native to North America. The plant typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with narrow, grassy leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex × krausei is often found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
