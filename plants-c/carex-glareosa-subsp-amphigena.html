Carex glareosa subsp. amphigena

Carex glareosa subsp.

amphigena, commonly known as the yellow tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which help it thrive in waterlogged soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
