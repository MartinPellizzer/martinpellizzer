Cynanchum pyrotechnicum

Cynanchum pyrotechnicum, commonly known as the flamevine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

The flower structure is complex, featuring a crown of five petals and a distinctive, horn-like corona.
