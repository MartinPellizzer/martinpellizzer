Carduus nutans subsp. taygeteus

Carduus nutans subsp.

taygeteus, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears large, showy purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
