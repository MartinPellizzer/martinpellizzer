Convolvulus orixensis

Convolvulus orixensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive funnel-shaped, trumpet-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
