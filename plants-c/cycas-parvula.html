Cycas parvula

Cycas parvula, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, a feature common to cycads. Cycas parvula is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
