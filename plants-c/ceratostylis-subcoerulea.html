Ceratostylis subcoerulea

Ceratostylis subcoerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces delicate, greenish-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the flowers are small and clustered in a spike.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral characteristics.
