Carex blinii

Carex blinii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Carex blinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
