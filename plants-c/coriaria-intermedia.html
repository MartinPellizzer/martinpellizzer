Coriaria intermedia

Coriaria intermedia, commonly known as the European coral-tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Coriariaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
