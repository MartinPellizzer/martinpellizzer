Cleome speciosa

Cleome speciosa, commonly known as the fringed cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome speciosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display and ease of cultivation.
