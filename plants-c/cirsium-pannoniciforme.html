Cirsium pannoniciforme

Cirsium pannoniciforme, commonly known as the Pannonian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its robust growth and ecological adaptability, it plays a role in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
