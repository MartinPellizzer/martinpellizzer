Convolvulus genistoides

Convolvulus genistoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
