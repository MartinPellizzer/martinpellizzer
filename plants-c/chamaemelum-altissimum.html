Chamaemelum altissimum

Chamaemelum altissimum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and aromatic. The flowers are typically white to pale yellow, with a distinctive, sweet, honey-like fragrance.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its longevity and pleasant scent.
