Cunninghamia verticillata

Cunninghamia verticillata, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, tall trunk and conical crown, with needle-like leaves arranged in whorls of three or four. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber.

Cunninghamia verticillata plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its resilience to environmental stress.
