Caesalpinia nuga

Caesalpinia nuga, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian redwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange floral spikes. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Caesalpinia nuga is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in craftsmanship and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
