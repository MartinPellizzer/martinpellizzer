Cleome spinosa var. horrida

Cleome spinosa var. horrida, commonly known as the horrid cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in the Cleome genus.
