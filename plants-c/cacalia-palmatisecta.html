Cacalia palmatisecta

Cacalia palmatisecta, commonly known as the palmate-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes resembling fingers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a desirable plant for gardens and botanical collections.
