Commelina tricolor

Commelina tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its strikingly colored flowers, which typically display three different hues. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Commelina tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
