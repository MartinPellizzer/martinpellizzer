Canthium edule

Canthium edule, commonly known as the edible spurge, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain seeds, and the plant is often grown for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines.

Canthium edule is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
