Campanula polymorpha var. typica

Campanula polymorpha var. typica, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
