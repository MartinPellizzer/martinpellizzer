Croton lehmbachii

Croton lehmbachii, commonly known as the Borneo croton, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a waxy texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
