Commelina africana var. krebsiana

Commelina africana var. krebsiana, commonly known as the African spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by white or pale blue bracts. The plant has heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
