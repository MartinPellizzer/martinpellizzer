Crotalaria virgulata

Crotalaria virgulata, commonly known as the small cowpea or tickseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred shape. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a green manure crop.
