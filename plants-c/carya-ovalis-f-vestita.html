Carya ovalis f. vestita

Carya ovalis f. vestita, commonly known as the smooth-shelled hickory, is a variety of the hickory tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thin-shelled nuts that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and serrated margins. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The fruit matures in the fall and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
