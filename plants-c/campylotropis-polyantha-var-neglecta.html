Campylotropis polyantha var. neglecta

Campylotropis polyantha var. neglecta, commonly known as the Chinese peacock vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets that are often adorned with a distinctive purplish or reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its twining stems allow it to climb and spread along trellises or walls.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
