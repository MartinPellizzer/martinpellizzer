Calorophus asper

Calorophus asper, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive center of dark brown or black tubular florets. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Calorophus asper is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
