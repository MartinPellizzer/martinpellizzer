Coleus hybridus

Coleus hybridus, commonly known as the horsewhin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value rather than its edible properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Coleus hybridus is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens for its striking foliage.
