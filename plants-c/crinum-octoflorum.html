Crinum octoflorum

Crinum octoflorum, commonly known as the eight-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters of eight, giving it its common name. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help the plant survive in its natural habitat.

Crinum octoflorum is known for its strong fragrance, which can be both pleasant and overpowering.
