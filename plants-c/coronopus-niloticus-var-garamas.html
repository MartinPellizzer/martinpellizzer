Coronopus niloticus var. garamas

Coronopus niloticus var. garamas, commonly known as the Egyptian crown-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley and surrounding regions.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and for forage in livestock feed.
