Cylindropuntia rosea

Cylindropuntia rosea, commonly known as the rose prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in soft, pale green to pinkish spines. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is a fleshy, spiny structure that contains edible seeds.

Cylindropuntia rosea is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
