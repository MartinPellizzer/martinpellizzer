Convolvulus puniceus

Convolvulus puniceus, commonly known as the rock convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus puniceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
