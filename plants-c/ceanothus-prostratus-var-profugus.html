Ceanothus prostratus var. profugus

Ceanothus prostratus var. profugus, commonly known as the creeping ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
