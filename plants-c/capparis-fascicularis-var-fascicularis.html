Capparis fascicularis var. fascicularis

Capparis fascicularis var. fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or sandy soils. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
