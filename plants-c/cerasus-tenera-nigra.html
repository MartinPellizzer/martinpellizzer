Cerasus tenera nigra

Cerasus tenera nigra, commonly known as the black cherry or Prunus tenera nigra, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, dark red to black fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
