Crassocephalum rubens var. sarcobasis

Crassocephalum rubens var. sarcobasis, commonly known as the red-headed grass or red-headed panicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are clustered in panicles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
