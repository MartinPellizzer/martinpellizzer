Carduus nyassanus subsp. nyassanus

Carduus nyassanus subsp.

nyassanus, commonly known as the Nyassa thistle, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
