Cymbidium sandersonii

Cymbidium sandersonii, commonly known as the sanderson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces striking, often fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, with a unique spurred lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium sandersonii is a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and garden displays due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
