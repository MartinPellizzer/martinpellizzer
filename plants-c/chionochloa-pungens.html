Chionochloa pungens

Chionochloa pungens, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage that thrives in cold, mountainous environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures, making it a resilient species in alpine ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
