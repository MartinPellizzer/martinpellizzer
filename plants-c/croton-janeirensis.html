Croton janeirensis

Croton janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.

Croton janeirensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
