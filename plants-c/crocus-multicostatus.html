Crocus multicostatus

Crocus multicostatus, commonly known as the multilobe crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, multi-lobed corona. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that typically appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Crocus multicostatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
