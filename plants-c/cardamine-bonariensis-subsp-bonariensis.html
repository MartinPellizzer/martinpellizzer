Cardamine bonariensis subsp. bonariensis

Cardamine bonariensis subsp.

bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by water.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
