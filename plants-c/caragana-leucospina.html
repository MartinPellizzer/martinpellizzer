Caragana leucospina

Caragana leucospina, commonly known as the white-spined shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by pea-like pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often used for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Caragana leucospina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
