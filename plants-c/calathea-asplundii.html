Calathea asplundii

Calathea asplundii, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns of deep green and silver. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its sensitivity to temperature changes, it is often grown as a houseplant in temperate climates.
