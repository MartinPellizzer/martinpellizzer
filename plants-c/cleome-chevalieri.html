Cleome chevalieri

Cleome chevalieri, commonly known as the spider flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cleome chevalieri is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
