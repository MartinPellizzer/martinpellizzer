Cinnamodendron angustifolium

Cinnamodendron angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamodendron, is a member of the Myristicaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma similar to cinnamon, which is reflected in its genus name.

The tree produces drupes that are an important food source for various bird and mammal species in its natural habitat.
