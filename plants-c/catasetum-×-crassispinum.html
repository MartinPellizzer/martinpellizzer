Catasetum × crassispinum

Catasetum × crassispinum, commonly known as the large-spurred orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that often have a distinctive spurred shape. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in humid, shaded forest environments. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Catasetum × crassispinum requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
