Camptopus letestui

Camptopus letestui, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slight pubescence.

Camptopus letestui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
