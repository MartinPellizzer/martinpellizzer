Cirsium tashiroi var. hidaense

Cirsium tashiroi var. hidaense, commonly known as the Hida thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
