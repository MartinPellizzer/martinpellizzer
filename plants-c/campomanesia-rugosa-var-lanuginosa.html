Campomanesia rugosa var. lanuginosa

Campomanesia rugosa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
