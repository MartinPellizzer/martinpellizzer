Cyathea teysmannii

Cyathea teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with the trunk forming from the accumulation of old fronds, creating a distinctive appearance. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural stability.

Cyathea teysmannii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
