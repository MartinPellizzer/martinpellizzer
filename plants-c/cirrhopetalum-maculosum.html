Cirrhopetalum maculosum

Cirrhopetalum maculosum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple with distinctive spots. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
