Crataegus

Crataegus, commonly known as hawthorn, is a genus of shrubs and small trees belonging to the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, showy white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils. Crataegus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine for their cardiovascular benefits.

The genus includes over 200 species, many of which are cultivated for their floral display and autumn fruit.
