Castanea sativa var. fastigiata

Castanea sativa var. fastigiata, commonly known as the columnar horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features large, palmate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in clustered panicles during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny bur containing one or two chestnuts, which mature in the autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
