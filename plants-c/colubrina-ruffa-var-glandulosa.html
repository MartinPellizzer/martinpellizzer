Colubrina ruffa var. glandulosa

Colubrina ruffa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a distinctive botanical feature of this variety.
