Campylotropis decora

Campylotropis decora, commonly known as the decorative cowpea, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and agricultural settings.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
