Carex tetrastachya

Carex tetrastachya, commonly known as the giant sweetflag, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds.

Carex tetrastachya is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
