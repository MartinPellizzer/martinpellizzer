Carduus × theriotii nothosubsp.

theriotii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Carduus acanthoides and Carduus tenuifolius. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers, typical of many thistle species.

This nothosubspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its growth habit is perennial, with a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above them.
