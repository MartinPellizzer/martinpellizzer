Chrysanthemum balsamita f. tanacetoides

Chrysanthemum balsamita f. tanacetoides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features aromatic, finely divided leaves and produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It is known for its long-lasting, fragrant foliage that remains fresh and dry for extended periods, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to the genus Chrysanthemum and its resemblance to tanacetum species.
