Cyperus sesquiflorus subsp. appendiculatus

Cyperus sesquiflorus subsp.

appendiculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.
