Capriola ciliaris

Capriola ciliaris, commonly known as the fringe gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are often edged with white or pale blue markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Capriola ciliaris is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
