Cyperus mbitheanus

Cyperus mbitheanus, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

Cyperus mbitheanus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
