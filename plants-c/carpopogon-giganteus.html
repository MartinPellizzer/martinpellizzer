Carpopogon giganteus

Carpopogon giganteus, commonly known as the giant grass or giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carpopogon giganteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
