Cassia truncata

Cassia truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often truncate at the ends. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, typical of the Leguminosae family. Cassia truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
