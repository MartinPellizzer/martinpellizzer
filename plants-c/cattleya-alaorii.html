Cattleya alaorii

Cattleya alaorii, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms with a contrasting white lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cattleya alaorii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
