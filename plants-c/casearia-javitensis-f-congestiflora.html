Casearia javitensis f. congestiflora

Casearia javitensis f. congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white to yellowish, and often occur in dense clusters at the ends of branches.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
