Cyperus afrorobustus

Cyperus afrorobustus, commonly known as African bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus afrorobustus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both fresh and brackish water conditions.
