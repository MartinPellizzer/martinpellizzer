Carex distans subvar. trispicata

Carex distans subvar. trispicata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
