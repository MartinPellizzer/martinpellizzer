Casimiroa sapota f. salvadorensis

Casimiroa sapota f. salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both local consumption and commercial use.
