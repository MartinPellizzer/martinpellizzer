Crataegus maroccana

Crataegus maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
