Cacalia atriplicifolia var. angulata

Cacalia atriplicifolia var. angulata, commonly known as the angular leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are elongated and have a distinctive angular appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
