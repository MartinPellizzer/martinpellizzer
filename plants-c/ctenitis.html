Ctenitis

Ctenitis, commonly known as the hawksbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. Ctenitis species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They often have hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
