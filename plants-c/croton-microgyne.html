Croton microgyne

Croton microgyne, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicle inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Croton microgyne thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
