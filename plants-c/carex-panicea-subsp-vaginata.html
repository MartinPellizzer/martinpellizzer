Carex panicea subsp. vaginata

Carex panicea subsp.

vaginata, commonly known as the panicea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
