Catalpa × hybrida f. purpurea

Catalpa × hybrida f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. It produces long, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
