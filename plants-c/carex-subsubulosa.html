Carex subsubulosa

Carex subsubulosa, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex subsubulosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
