Coreopsis macrantha

Coreopsis macrantha, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Coreopsis macrantha is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
