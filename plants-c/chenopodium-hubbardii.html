Chenopodium hubbardii

Chenopodium hubbardii, commonly known as wild spinach, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are edible and have been used by Indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium hubbardii thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.
