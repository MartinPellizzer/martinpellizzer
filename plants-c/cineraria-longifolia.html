Cineraria longifolia

Cineraria longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
