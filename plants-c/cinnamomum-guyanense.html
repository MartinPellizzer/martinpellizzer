Cinnamomum guyanense

Cinnamomum guyanense, commonly known as the Guyana cinnamon or black cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which are used for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile in culinary applications.
