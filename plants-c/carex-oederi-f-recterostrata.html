Carex oederi f. recterostrata

Carex oederi f. recterostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and a more upright growth habit compared to other Carex species.
