Cirsium × breunium

Cirsium × breunium, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. This species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it often thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its morphology includes a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed.

Cirsium × breunium is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, where it can form dense stands.
