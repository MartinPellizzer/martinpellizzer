Crotalaria henrici

Crotalaria henrici, commonly known as the Henry's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features distinctive spurred petals and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in agricultural systems.
