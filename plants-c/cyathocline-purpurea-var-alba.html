Cyathocline purpurea var. alba

Cyathocline purpurea var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cyathocline, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and clusters of small, yellow stamens, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
