Cassia cristalinae

Cassia cristalinae, commonly known as the crystal cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of its family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Cassia cristalinae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
