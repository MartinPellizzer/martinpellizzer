Celmisia asteliifolia

Celmisia asteliifolia, commonly known as the silver hairbell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Celmisia asteliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
