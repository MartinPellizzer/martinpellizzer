Celtis brasiliensis

Celtis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, pale green fruits. Celtis brasiliensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine.
