Cyperus hydra

Cyperus hydra, commonly known as water flag, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus hydra thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas, contributing to its ecological significance.
