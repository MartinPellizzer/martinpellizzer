Crotalaria pseudodiloloensis

Crotalaria pseudodiloloensis, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant features showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
