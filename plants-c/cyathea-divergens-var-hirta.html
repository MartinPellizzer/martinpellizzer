Cyathea divergens var. hirta

Cyathea divergens var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
