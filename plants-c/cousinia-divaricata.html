Cousinia divaricata

Cousinia divaricata, commonly known as the alpine cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often silver- or gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
