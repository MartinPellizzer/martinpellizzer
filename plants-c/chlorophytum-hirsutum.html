Chlorophytum hirsutum

Chlorophytum hirsutum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow streaks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright blue or purple berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Chlorophytum hirsutum is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to purify the air and its ease of propagation through offsets.
