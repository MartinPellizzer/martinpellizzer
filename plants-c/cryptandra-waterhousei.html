Cryptandra waterhousei

Cryptandra waterhousei, commonly known as the waterhouse's cryptandra, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as gravel plains and dunes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
