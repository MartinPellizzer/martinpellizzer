Carex antoniensis

Carex antoniensis, commonly known as the Antoni's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense clusters of spikelets. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy coating.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are often borne in clusters at the top of the plant.
