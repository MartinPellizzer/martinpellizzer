Chrysobalanus prancei

Chrysobalanus prancei, commonly known as the Prance's chrysobalanus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalaneceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
