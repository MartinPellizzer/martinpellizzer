Cerastium utriense

Cerastium utriense, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cerastium utriense thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
