Cinnamomum cinnamomum

Cinnamomum cinnamomum, commonly known as cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China, India, and other parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is the source of the spice cinnamon. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic value.
