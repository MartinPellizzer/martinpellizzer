Croton nitrariifolius var. polymorphus

Croton nitrariifolius var. polymorphus, commonly known as the variable croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf morphology, which can vary significantly between individuals, often featuring a range of colors from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
