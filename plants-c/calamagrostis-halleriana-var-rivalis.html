Calamagrostis halleriana var. rivalis

Calamagrostis halleriana var. rivalis, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for erosion control and landscape design.
