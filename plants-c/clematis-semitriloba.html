Clematis semitriloba

Clematis semitriloba, commonly known as the trailing clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a trifoliate pattern. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil.

Clematis semitriloba is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
