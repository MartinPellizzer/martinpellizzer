Cyathea venezuelensis

Cyathea venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
