Comandra umbellata subsp. richardsiana

Comandra umbellata subsp.

richardsiana, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has deep root systems to access water.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
