Carex coreana

Carex coreana, commonly known as Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or wetlands.

Carex coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
