Cleistanthus polyneurus

Cleistanthus polyneurus, commonly known as the hairy cleistanthus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and dense foliage of elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
