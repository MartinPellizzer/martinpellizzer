Cancrinia rupestris

Cancrinia rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional African medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
