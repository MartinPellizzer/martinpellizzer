Clinopodium uhligii var. uhligii

Clinopodium uhligii var. uhligii, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in shades of blue or purple. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family, with a strong mint-like scent.
