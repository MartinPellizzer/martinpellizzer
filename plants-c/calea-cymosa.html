Calea cymosa

Calea cymosa, commonly known as the "flower of the morning," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open in the early morning. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows as a herbaceous perennial. Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calea cymosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
