Campanula waldsteiniana subsp. tommasiniana

Campanula waldsteiniana subsp.

tommasiniana, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
