Critonia guerrerensis

Critonia guerrerensis, commonly known as the Guerrerense cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing stems. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
