Chaetogastra herzogii

Chaetogastra herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's chaetogastra, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
