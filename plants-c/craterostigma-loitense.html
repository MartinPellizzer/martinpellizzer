Craterostigma loitense

Craterostigma loitense, commonly known as the Kenyan rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
