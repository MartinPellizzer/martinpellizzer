Campanula pusilla var. veronicifolia

Campanula pusilla var. veronicifolia, commonly known as the small bellflower or Veronica-like bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, flowering plant.
