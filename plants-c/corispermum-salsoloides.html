Corispermum salsoloides

Corispermum salsoloides, commonly known as saltbush or sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its ability to tolerate high salinity and drought makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
