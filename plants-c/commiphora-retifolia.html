Commiphora retifolia

Commiphora retifolia, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forest regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
