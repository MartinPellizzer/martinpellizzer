Cheirodendron marquesense

Cheirodendron marquesense, commonly known as the Marquesas hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Marquesas Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Cheirodendron marquesense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
