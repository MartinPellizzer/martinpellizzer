Centaurea erivanensis subsp. holargyrea

Centaurea erivanensis subsp.

holargyrea, commonly known as the Erivan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features spiny, upright stems and produces purple to pink flowers in conical clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
