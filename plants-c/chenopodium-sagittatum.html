Chenopodium sagittatum

Chenopodium sagittatum, commonly known as arrowleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are usually covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Chenopodium sagittatum is also known for its potential medicinal uses and is sometimes used as a food source for wildlife.
