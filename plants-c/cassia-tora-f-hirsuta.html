Cassia tora f. hirsuta

Cassia tora f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cassia tora f. hirsuta is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
