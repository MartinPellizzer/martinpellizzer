Carex aristata var. lanceisquama

Carex aristata var. lanceisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shady, damp conditions and is often found in woodland or marshy areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
