Carex preussii var. camerunensis

Carex preussii var. camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
