Cloiselia madagascariensis

Cloiselia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar clover, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in tropical climates.

Cloiselia madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
