Calanthe similis

Calanthe similis, commonly known as the similar calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves and blooms during the spring and early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions.
