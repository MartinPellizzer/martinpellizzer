Crepidium orbicans

Crepidium orbicans, commonly known as the orbiculate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers with a distinctive orbicular (rounded) shape. The plant typically has a single pseudobulb and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom during the rainy season.

Crepidium orbicans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
