Calliandra chapadae

Calliandra chapadae, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Calliandra chapadae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
