Callistemon salignus var. roseus

Callistemon salignus var. roseus, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink cylindrical flower spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract birds and bees, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
