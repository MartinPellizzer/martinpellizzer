Carex tribuloides var. tribuloides

Carex tribuloides var. tribuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
