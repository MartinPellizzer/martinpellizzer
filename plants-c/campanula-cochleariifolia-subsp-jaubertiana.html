Campanula cochleariifolia subsp. jaubertiana

Campanula cochleariifolia subsp.

jaubertiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
