Carduus atriplicifolius

Carduus atriplicifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with spiny margins. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Carduus atriplicifolius is often found in dry grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in disturbed soils.
