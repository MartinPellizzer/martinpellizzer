Cyperus kalli

Cyperus kalli, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often surrounded by bracts. Cyperus kalli typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits make it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
