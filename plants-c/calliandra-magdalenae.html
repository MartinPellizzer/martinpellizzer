Calliandra magdalenae

Calliandra magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red floral clusters that bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Calliandra magdalenae is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
