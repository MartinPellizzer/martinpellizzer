Coreopsis verticillata var. tenuifolia

Coreopsis verticillata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
