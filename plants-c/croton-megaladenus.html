Croton megaladenus

Croton megaladenus, commonly known as the giant croton, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, often with vibrant green or variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its size and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for tropical landscapes and indoor displays.
