Crypsinus oblongifolius

Crypsinus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf crypts, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a dense canopy that provides habitat for various small animals.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
