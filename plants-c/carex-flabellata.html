Carex flabellata

Carex flabellata, commonly known as the flat-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often flat and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Carex flabellata produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle-like structure.
