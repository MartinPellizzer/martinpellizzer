Crataegus viridis nothovar. lutensis

Crataegus viridis nothovar. lutensis, commonly known as the green hawthorn hybrid, is a botanical variant resulting from crossbreeding between different hawthorn species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This nothovar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in various garden settings.
