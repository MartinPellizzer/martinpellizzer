Carthamus glaucus subsp. glaucus

Carthamus glaucus subsp.

glaucus, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and prefers well-drained soils. It produces yellow-orange cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
