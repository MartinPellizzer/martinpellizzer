Cardamine angustata var. multifida

Cardamine angustata var. multifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to moist, shady environments in North America, this species thrives in stream banks and woodland floors. The plant produces small, elongated fruits known as siliques, which contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in areas with cool, humid climates and is valued for its edible leaves.
