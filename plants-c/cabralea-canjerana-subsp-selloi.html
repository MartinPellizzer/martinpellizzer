Cabralea canjerana subsp. selloi

Cabralea canjerana subsp.

selloi, commonly known as the Sello's cabralea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, mountainous environments.

The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
