Cyperus ankaizinensis

Cyperus ankaizinensis, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus ankaizinensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
