Caprifolium hirsutum

Caprifolium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are fragrant, tubular, and usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for humans and wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
