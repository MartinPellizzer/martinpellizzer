Crataegus salviifolia

Crataegus salviifolia, commonly known as the Salvia-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that resemble those of sage, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus salviifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
