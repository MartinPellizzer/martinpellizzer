Chibaca salutaris

Chibaca salutaris, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still, freshwater lakes. Its botanical name reflects its traditional use in Andean medicine for its purported healing properties.

Chibaca salutaris is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance among indigenous communities.
