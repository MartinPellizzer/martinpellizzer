Chrozophora verbascifolia

Chrozophora verbascifolia, commonly known as the silky weed or yellow weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas or along roadsides.

Chrozophora verbascifolia is an annual herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
