Coleus batemannii

Coleus batemannii, commonly known as Batemann's coleus, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

Native to tropical regions of Africa, it is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
