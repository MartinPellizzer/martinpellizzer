Coleus porcatus

Coleus porcatus, commonly known as the pig's ear plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver, white, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Coleus porcatus is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ease of care.
