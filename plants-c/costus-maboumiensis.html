Costus maboumiensis

Costus maboumiensis, commonly known as the Maboumi costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy inflorescences. The plant produces large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals, which are often mistaken for true flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Costus maboumiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
