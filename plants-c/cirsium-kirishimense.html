Cirsium kirishimense

Cirsium kirishimense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are covered in dense, bristly hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in East Asian countries.
