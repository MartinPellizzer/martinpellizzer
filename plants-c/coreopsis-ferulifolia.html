Coreopsis ferulifolia

Coreopsis ferulifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it prefers full sun to partial shade with well-drained soil.

Coreopsis ferulifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
