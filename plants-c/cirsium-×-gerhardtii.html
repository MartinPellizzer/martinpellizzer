Cirsium × gerhardtii

Cirsium × gerhardtii, commonly known as the Gerhardt's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a single terminal flower head.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
