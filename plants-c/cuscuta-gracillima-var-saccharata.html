Cuscuta gracillima var. saccharata

Cuscuta gracillima var. saccharata, commonly known as the sweetleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found growing on shrubs and trees. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it can form dense infestations on its hosts.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
