Cenchrus ekmanianus

Cenchrus ekmanianus, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Cenchrus ekmanianus is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
