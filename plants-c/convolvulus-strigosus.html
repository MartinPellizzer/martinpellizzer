Convolvulus strigosus

Convolvulus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Convolvulus strigosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive flowers and vigorous growth.
