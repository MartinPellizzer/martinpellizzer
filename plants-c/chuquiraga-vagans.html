Chuquiraga vagans

Chuquiraga vagans, commonly known as the Andean burweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Chuquiraga vagans is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
