Carduus × littoralis

Carduus × littoralis, commonly known as the coastal thistle, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Carduus and other thistle species.

It is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to salt-laden winds and can tolerate moderate levels of coastal salinity.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for insects and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
