Commelina divaricata

Commelina divaricata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by a white or pale blue spatulate bract. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Commelina divaricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
