Crocus biflorus subsp. weldenii

Crocus biflorus subsp.

weldenii, commonly known as the Welden's crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and early bloom, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking winter interest.
