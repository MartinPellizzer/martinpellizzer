Carex redowskiana

Carex redowskiana, commonly known as the Redowski's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Eastern Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Carex redowskiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
