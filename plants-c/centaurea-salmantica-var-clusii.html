Centaurea salmantica var. clusii

Centaurea salmantica var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusius's Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and vibrant purple to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a distinctive, upright growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
