Capparis zeyheri

Capparis zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Capparis zeyheri is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
