Campanula saxifraga

Campanula saxifraga, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence.

Campanula saxifraga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
