Caucalis nodosa var. peduncularis

Caucalis nodosa var. peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and succulent, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate and often clustered, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
