Croixia obovata

Croixia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Croixia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
