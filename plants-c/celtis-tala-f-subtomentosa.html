Celtis tala f. subtomentosa

Celtis tala f. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky hackberry, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, white or yellowish fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
