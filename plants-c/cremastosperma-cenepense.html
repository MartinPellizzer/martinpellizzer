Cremastosperma cenepense

Cremastosperma cenepense, commonly known as the Ceylon velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both agricultural and medicinal purposes.
