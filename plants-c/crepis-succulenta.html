Crepis succulenta

Crepis succulenta, commonly known as the succulent mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Crepis succulenta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
