Cestrum pallidum

Cestrum pallidum, commonly known as the pale trumpetflower, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
