Cirrhopetalum gamosepalum

Cirrhopetalum gamosepalum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
