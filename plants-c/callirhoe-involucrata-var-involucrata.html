Callirhoe involucrata var. involucrata

Callirhoe involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces seed pods that are inflated and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
