Cronquistianthus origanoides

Cronquistianthus origanoides, commonly known as the fragrant leaf plant, is a species native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a distinct herbal scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both container and garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and delicate floral display.
