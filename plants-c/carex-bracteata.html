Carex bracteata

Carex bracteata, commonly known as the bracted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wet meadows.

Carex bracteata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
