Callicarpa japonica f. kiiruninsularis

Callicarpa japonica f. kiiruninsularis, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to red berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its striking fruit display.
