Crocus visianicus

Crocus visianicus, commonly known as the Visianica crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean area. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

The Visianica crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
