Cyperus piptolepis var. pyrophytus

Cyperus piptolepis var. pyrophytus, commonly known as the flame sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant, flame-like appearance, making it a popular choice for water gardens and landscaping.
