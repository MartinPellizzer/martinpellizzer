Centaurea amara var. pannonica

Centaurea amara var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian bitterweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered with fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters, typical of the genus Centaurea. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its bitter taste.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
