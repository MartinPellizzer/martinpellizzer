Cacalia peltifolia

Cacalia peltifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It blooms during the summer months and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cacalia peltifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
