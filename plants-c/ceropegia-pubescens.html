Ceropegia pubescens

Ceropegia pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silver vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, velvety leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia pubescens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
