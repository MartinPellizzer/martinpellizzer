Carex verna f. laxiflora

Carex verna f. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender flowering sedge, is a variant of the early sedge species.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
