Chymococca empetroides

Chymococca empetroides, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
