Croton purdiei var. santurbanensis

Croton purdiei var. santurbanensis, commonly known as the Santurban croton, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
