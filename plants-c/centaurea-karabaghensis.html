Centaurea karabaghensis

Centaurea karabaghensis, commonly known as the Karabakh coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its morphology includes a robust stem and a compact growth habit, which helps it survive in its native range.

Centaurea karabaghensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
