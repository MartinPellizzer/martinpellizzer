Ceropegia adscendens

Ceropegia adscendens, commonly known as the climbing ceropegia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing plant that typically grows along the ground or on other structures, featuring thin, wiry stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia adscendens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
