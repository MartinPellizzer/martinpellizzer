Cephalaria boetica

Cephalaria boetica, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and erect.

Cephalaria boetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
