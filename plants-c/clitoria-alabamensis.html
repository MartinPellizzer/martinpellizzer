Clitoria alabamensis

Clitoria alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its bright purple or pink butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Clitoria alabamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
