Coleotrype goudotii var. boinensis

Coleotrype goudotii var. boinensis, commonly known as the Boinensian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
