Corylus ferox var. ferox

Corylus ferox var. ferox, commonly known as the fierce hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its sharp, bristly leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
