Cyathea yungensis

Cyathea yungensis, commonly known as the Yungens' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland rainforests. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind to propagate the species.

Cyathea yungensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
