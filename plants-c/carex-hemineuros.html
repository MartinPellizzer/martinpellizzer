Carex hemineuros

Carex hemineuros, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, brownish seeds.

Carex hemineuros plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
