Carex incurva var. pratensis

Carex incurva var. pratensis, commonly known as the bent grass sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, meadows, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
