Clomena peruviana var. pulvinata

Clomena peruviana var. pulvinata, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid or the pulvinate orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive growth habit, featuring a short rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a unique pulvinate (bulbous) structure at the base of the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
