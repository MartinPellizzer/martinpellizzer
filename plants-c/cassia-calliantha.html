Cassia calliantha

Cassia calliantha, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, winged pods that contain seeds. Cassia calliantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
