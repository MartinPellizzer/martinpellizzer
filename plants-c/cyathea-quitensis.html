Cyathea quitensis

Cyathea quitensis, commonly known as the Quitense tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests and montane areas. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

Cyathea quitensis plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
