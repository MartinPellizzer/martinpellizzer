Chamaesyce conjuncta

Chamaesyce conjuncta, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in groups of three, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
