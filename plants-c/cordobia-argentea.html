Cordobia argentea

Cordobia argentea, commonly known as silver cordoba, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles, and bloom during the warmer months.

Cordobia argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
