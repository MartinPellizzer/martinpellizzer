Chrysanthemum pseudachillea

Chrysanthemum pseudachillea, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disc florets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
