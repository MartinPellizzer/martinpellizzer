Clematis petersiana

Clematis petersiana, commonly known as the Petersiana Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems that twine around supports and can reach lengths of several meters. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

Clematis petersiana thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbing plants.
