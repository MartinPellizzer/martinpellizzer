Chirita eburnea

Chirita eburnea, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking foliage rather than its flowers.
