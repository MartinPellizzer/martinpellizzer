Cadetia finisterrae

Cadetia finisterrae, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous species with basal leaves and delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, coastal habitats and is adapted to the region's arid climate. It has a compact growth habit and is often found growing in crevices or on slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
