Citrus oblonga

Citrus oblonga, commonly known as the Mexican lime or mock orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and tart. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its flowers are fragrant and white, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
