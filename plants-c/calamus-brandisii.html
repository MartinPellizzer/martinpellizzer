Calamus brandisii

Calamus brandisii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

Calamus brandisii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
