Carex insularis var. recurvata

Carex insularis var. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved sedge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to specific ecological niches.
