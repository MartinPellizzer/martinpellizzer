Cirsium horridulum var. elliottii

Cirsium horridulum var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
