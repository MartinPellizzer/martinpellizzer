Centrolepis pallida

Centrolepis pallida, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a unique flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and produces small, pale green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often appear as if they are not fully developed, giving it a ghostly appearance.

Centrolepis pallida is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive look.
