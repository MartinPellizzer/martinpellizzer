Ceropegia yorubana

Ceropegia yorubana, commonly known as the African violet or string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
