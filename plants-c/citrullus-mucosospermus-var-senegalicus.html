Citrullus mucosospermus var. senegalicus

Citrullus mucosospermus var. senegalicus, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, fleshy, and juicy fruits. The plant produces pale green, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. The fruit has a thick rind and a sweet, water-rich pulp, making it a valuable crop for hydration and nutrition.

It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.
