Cordiera myrciifolia

Cordiera myrciifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cordiera myrciifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
