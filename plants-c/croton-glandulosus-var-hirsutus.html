Croton glandulosus var. hirsutus

Croton glandulosus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often prominently veined. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
