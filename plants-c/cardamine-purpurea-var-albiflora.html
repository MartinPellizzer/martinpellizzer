Cardamine purpurea var. albiflora

Cardamine purpurea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream sides and woodland areas. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
