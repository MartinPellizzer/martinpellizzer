Capparis longipes

Capparis longipes, commonly known as the longipes caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
