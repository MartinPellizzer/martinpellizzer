Calamintha × bellantiana

Calamintha × bellantiana, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a hybrid plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small, white, umbel-shaped flowers. The plant produces a licorice-scented seed pod that contains tiny, black seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
