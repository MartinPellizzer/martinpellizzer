Clinopodium arvense

Clinopodium arvense, commonly known as field mint or creeping thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has a spreading habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

Clinopodium arvense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.
