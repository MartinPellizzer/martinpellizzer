Casparia uniflora

Casparia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower casparia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, solitary flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Casparia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
