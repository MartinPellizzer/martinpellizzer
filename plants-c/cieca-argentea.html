Cieca argentea

Cieca argentea, commonly known as silverleaf cieca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cieca argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
