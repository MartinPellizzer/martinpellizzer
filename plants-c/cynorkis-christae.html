Cynorkis christae

Cynorkis christae, commonly known as the Christa's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to the humid lowland forests of Madagascar, it thrives in warm, moist environments with partial shade. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
