Crepis miyabei

Crepis miyabei, commonly known as the Miyabei mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
