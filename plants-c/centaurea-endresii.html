Centaurea endresii

Centaurea endresii, commonly known as Endres' cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea endresii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
