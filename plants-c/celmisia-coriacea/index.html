Celmisia Coriacea

Celmisia coriacea is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand. It is known for its distinctive rosette of waxy, leathery leaves that help it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a splash of color to its rocky habitat. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Celmisia coriacea exemplifies the adaptability and beauty of plant life in extreme climates.
