Cenchrus triflorus

Cenchrus triflorus, commonly known as three-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with three flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cenchrus triflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
