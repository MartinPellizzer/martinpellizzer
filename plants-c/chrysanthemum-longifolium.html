Chrysanthemum longifolium

Chrysanthemum longifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a cultivated species in horticulture.
