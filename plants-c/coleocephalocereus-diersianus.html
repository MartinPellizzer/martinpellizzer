Coleocephalocereus diersianus

Coleocephalocereus diersianus, commonly known as the Dieters' night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardens.
