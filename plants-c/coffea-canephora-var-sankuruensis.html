Coffea canephora var. sankuruensis

Coffea canephora var. sankuruensis, commonly known as the Kivu coffee.

It is a variant of the robusta coffee species, native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. This plant is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves and produces red, fleshy berries containing two seeds. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and consistent rainfall.

The beans are typically used for their strong flavor and higher caffeine content in commercial coffee blends.
