Cyanthillium moluccense

Cyanthillium moluccense, commonly known as the Moluccan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyanthillium moluccense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
