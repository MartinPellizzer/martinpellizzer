Clematis orientalis var. microcuspis

Clematis orientalis var. microcuspis, commonly known as the Chinese vine or small-cusped clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have finely serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
