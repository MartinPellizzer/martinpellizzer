Cirsium × vancouverense

Cirsium × vancouverense, commonly known as Vancouver thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a robust root system and can form dense colonies, which helps it thrive in competitive environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
