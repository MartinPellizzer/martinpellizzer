Cleisostoma striatum

Cleisostoma striatum, commonly known as the striped hooded orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish with contrasting stripes, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleisostoma striatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
