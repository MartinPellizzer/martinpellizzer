Cupressus lindleyi subsp. benthamii

Cupressus lindleyi subsp.

benthamii, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a subspecies native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscaping.
