Corbichonia rubriviolacea

Corbichonia rubriviolacea, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins on pale green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.

Corbichonia rubriviolacea plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
