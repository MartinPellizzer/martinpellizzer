Cornus polygama

Cornus polygama, commonly known as the many-flowered dogwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish inflorescences that resemble umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cornus polygama is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and foliage.
