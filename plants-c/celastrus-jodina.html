Celastrus jodina

Celastrus jodina, commonly known as the Jodina creeper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest understories where it utilizes other plants for support.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
