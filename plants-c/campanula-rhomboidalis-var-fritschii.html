Campanula rhomboidalis var. fritschii

Campanula rhomboidalis var. fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped to rhomboid leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
