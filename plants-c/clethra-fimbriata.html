Clethra fimbriata

Clethra fimbriata, commonly known as fragrant clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Clethra fimbriata is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
