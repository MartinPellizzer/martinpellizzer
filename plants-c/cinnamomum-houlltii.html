Cinnamomum houlltii

Cinnamomum houlltii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum houlltii, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its flowers are small and fragrant.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
