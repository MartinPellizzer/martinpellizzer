Cinnamomum pauciflorum var. fischeri

Cinnamomum pauciflorum var. fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's cinnamon, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the cinnamon tree.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. This plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and unique floral structure, which differs from the more common cinnamon species. The bark of this variety is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically valuable species.
