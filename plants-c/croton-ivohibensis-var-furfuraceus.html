Croton ivohibensis var. furfuraceus

Croton ivohibensis var. furfuraceus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
