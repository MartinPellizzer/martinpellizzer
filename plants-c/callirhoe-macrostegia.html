Callirhoe macrostegia

Callirhoe macrostegia, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Callirhoe macrostegia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
