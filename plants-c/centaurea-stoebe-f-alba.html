Centaurea stoebe f. alba

Centaurea stoebe f. alba, commonly known as the white knapweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.

The inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
