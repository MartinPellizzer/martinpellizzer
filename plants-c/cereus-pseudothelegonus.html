Cereus pseudothelegonus

Cereus pseudothelegonus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, ribbed stem and ability to store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in arid environments and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are large, showy, and usually white or pale yellow, blooming at night.

Despite its common name, it is not a true cactus but belongs to the genus Cereus, which includes many night-blooming species.
