Cucumis melo var. goorgab

Cucumis melo var. goorgab, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, fragrant flesh. The plant typically grows as a vigorous trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its high water content and use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
