Cymbidium sikkimense

Cymbidium sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange-red, with prominent markings, and bloom during the winter months.

Cymbidium sikkimense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking appearance.
