Coleus scutellarioides var. blumei

Coleus scutellarioides var. blumei, commonly known as Blume's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green, often with contrasting patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often used in landscape design due to its striking visual appeal.
