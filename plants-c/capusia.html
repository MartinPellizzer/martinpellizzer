Capusia

Capusia, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are perennial herbs native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Capusia species are characterized by their smooth, often hairy stems and their ability to thrive in woodland or shaded environments.

They are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value, though they can be toxic to livestock and humans.
