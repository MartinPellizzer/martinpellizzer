Chlorocyperus glaber f. fasciculariforme

Chlorocyperus glaber f. fasciculariforme, commonly known as the fasciculate flat sedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and robust rhizomes. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, greenish-white flowers.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
