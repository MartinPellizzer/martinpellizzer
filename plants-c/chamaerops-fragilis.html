Chamaerops fragilis

Chamaerops fragilis, commonly known as the windmill palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves and slender, unbranched trunk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Chamaerops fragilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
