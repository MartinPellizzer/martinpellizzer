Crocus novicii

Crocus novicii, commonly known as the Italian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in woodland and rocky areas. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with six petals, which bloom in late autumn to early winter. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crocus novicii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
