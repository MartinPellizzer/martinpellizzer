Coryphantha villarensis

Coryphantha villarensis, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a rare ornamental plant.
