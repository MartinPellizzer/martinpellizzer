Calamagrostis onaei

Calamagrostis onaei, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicles.

Calamagrostis onaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
