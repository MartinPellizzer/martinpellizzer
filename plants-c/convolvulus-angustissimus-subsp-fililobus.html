Convolvulus angustissimus subsp. fililobus

Convolvulus angustissimus subsp.

fililobus, commonly known as narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

The plant is part of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
