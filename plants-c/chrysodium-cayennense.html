Chrysodium cayennense

Chrysodium cayennense, commonly known as the Cayenne chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a central disc of smaller florets. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical structure includes hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
