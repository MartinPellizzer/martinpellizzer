Crataegus chippewaensis

Crataegus chippewaensis, commonly known as Chippewa hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
