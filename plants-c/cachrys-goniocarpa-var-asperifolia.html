Cachrys goniocarpa var. asperifolia

Cachrys goniocarpa var. asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-seeded myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along the edges of forests and wetlands.
