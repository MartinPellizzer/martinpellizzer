Crotalaria shrirangiana

Crotalaria shrirangiana, commonly known as the Indian false flax, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with distinct spurred petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in various parts of Asia.
