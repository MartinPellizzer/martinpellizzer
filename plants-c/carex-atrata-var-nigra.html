Carex atrata var. nigra

Carex atrata var. nigra, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces spikelets that are typically black or dark brown in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Carex atrata var. nigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
