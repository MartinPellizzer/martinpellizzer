Cardamine yezoensis var. roseiflora

Cardamine yezoensis var. roseiflora, commonly known as the Yezo watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
