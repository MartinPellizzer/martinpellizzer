Coriaria microphylla

Coriaria microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf coriander or lesser coriander, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
