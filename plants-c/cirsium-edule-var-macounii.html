Cirsium edule var. macounii

Cirsium edule var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust root system and tall, spiny stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads, which are surrounded by numerous bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, covered in fine hairs, and have a serrated edge.

While it is not typically cultivated for food, it has historical use in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
