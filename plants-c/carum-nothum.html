Carum nothum

Carum nothum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called mericarps, which contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Carum nothum is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
