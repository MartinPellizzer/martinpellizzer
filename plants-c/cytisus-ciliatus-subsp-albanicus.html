Cytisus ciliatus subsp. albanicus

Cytisus ciliatus subsp.

albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are finely hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as hillsides and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
