Centaurea triumfettii subsp. lingulata

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
