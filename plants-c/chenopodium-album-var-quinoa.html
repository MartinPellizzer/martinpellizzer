Chenopodium album var. quinoa

Chenopodium album var. quinoa, commonly known as quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its leafy stems and small, green flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible seeds that are rich in protein, fiber, and essential amino acids. Quinoa thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its cultivation has gained global popularity due to its nutritional value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
