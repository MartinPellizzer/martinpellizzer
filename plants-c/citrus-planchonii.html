Citrus planchonii

Citrus planchonii, commonly known as the Madagascar lemon or citronella tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It produces small, yellow, lemon-like fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and a fragrant, aromatic scent. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.

The fruit is also used in some local cuisines and for its essential oils.
