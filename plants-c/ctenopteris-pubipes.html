Ctenopteris pubipes

Ctenopteris pubipes, commonly known as the hairy sea palm, is a species of cycad from the family Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, segmented stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. Native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant's unique morphology allows it to adapt to saline and sandy soils, making it a resilient species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various coastal organisms.
