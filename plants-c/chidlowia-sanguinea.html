Chidlowia sanguinea

Chidlowia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed chidlowia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge.

Chidlowia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
