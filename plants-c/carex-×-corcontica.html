Carex × corcontica

Carex × corcontica, commonly known as the hybrid marsh sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its tufted growth habit and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex × corcontica is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
