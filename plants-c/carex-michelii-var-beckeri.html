Carex michelii var. beckeri

Carex michelii var. beckeri, commonly known as Becker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
