Canarium acutifolium var. aemulans

Canarium acutifolium var. aemulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf candlenut, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
