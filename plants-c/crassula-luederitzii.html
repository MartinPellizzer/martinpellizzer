Crassula luederitzii

Crassula luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
