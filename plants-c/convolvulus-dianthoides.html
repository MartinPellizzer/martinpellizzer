Convolvulus dianthoides

Convolvulus dianthoides, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
