Campanula grisebachiana

Campanula grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray campanula, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, contributing to its common name.

Campanula grisebachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
