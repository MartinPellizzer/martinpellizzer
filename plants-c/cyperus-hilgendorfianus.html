Cyperus hilgendorfianus

Cyperus hilgendorfianus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering spikes are usually erect, bearing small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or brown in color.

Cyperus hilgendorfianus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
