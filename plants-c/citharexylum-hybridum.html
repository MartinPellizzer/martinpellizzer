Citharexylum hybridum

Citharexylum hybridum, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a vigorous, evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
