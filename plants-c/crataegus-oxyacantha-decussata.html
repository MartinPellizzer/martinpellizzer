Crataegus oxyacantha decussata

Crataegus oxyacantha decussata, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
