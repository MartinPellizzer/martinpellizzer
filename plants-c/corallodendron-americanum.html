Corallodendron americanum

Corallodendron americanum, commonly known as the American coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its dense, coral-like clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and often reddish in color.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
