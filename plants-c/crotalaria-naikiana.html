Crotalaria naikiana

Crotalaria naikiana, commonly known as the Indian helmet flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with compound leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Crotalaria naikiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
