Carex drymophila

Carex drymophila, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with high humidity and moderate temperatures. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into three-angled, capsule-like fruits.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
