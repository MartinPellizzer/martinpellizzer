Cornus acuminata

Cornus acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Cornus acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
