Crantzia carolinensis

Crantzia carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.
