Chrysanthemum indicum var. aromaticum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. aromaticum, commonly known as the Kashmiri chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
