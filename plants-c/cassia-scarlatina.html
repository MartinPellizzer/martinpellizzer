Cassia scarlatina

Cassia scarlatina, commonly known as the scarlet cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia scarlatina thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
