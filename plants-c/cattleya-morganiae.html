Cattleya morganiae

Cattleya morganiae, commonly known as the Morgan's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that was first introduced in the late 19th century.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. It produces pseudobulbs and has long, strap-like leaves that help in photosynthesis.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is a popular choice for both ornamental cultivation and botanical study.
