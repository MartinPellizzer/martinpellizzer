Carex abramsii

Carex abramsii, commonly known as Abrams' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex abramsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystem health.
