Calpurnia intrusa

Calpurnia intrusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calpurnia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give it a delicate appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, clustered in racemes, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
