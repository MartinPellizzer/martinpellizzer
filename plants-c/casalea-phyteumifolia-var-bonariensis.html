Casalea phyteumifolia var. bonariensis

Casalea phyteumifolia var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy and distinctive, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
