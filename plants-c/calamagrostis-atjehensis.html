Calamagrostis atjehensis

Calamagrostis atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Calamagrostis atjehensis is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its capacity to absorb heavy metals from contaminated soils.
