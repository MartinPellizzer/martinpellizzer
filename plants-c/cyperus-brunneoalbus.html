Cyperus brunneoalbus

Cyperus brunneoalbus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with yellow or brown. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands, rice fields, and along waterways.

Cyperus brunneoalbus is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and water levels.
