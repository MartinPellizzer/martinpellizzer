Coelogyne foerstermannii

Coelogyne foerstermannii, commonly known as the Foerstermann's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to orange-red blooms with dark markings, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne foerstermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
