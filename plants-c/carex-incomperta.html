Carex incomperta

Carex incomperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex incomperta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
