Capparis herbacea var. microphylla

Capparis herbacea var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible capers.
