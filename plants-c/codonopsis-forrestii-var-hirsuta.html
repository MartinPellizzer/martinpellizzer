Codonopsis forrestii var. hirsuta

Codonopsis forrestii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
