Cistus canadensis

Cistus canadensis, commonly known as Canadian rockrose, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Cistus canadensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
