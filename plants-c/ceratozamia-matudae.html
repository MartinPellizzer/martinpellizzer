Ceratozamia matudae

Ceratozamia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae cycad, is a rare and ancient plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
