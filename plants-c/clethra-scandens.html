Clethra scandens

Clethra scandens, commonly known as the climbing clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along supports. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Clethra scandens is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
