Cirsium hystrix

Cirsium hystrix, commonly known as the hispid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by a series of bracts. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cirsium hystrix is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
