Cucumis flexuosus var. rectoalbus

Cucumis flexuosus var. rectoalbus, commonly known as the white-flowered gourd, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green, elongated fruits that mature to a white or yellowish color. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with white markings, and it produces small, inedible fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
