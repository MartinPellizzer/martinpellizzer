Cochleanthes amazonica

Cochleanthes amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian cochleanthes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are striking, featuring vibrant colors and intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Botanically, it is characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves adapted to its humid, shaded habitat.
