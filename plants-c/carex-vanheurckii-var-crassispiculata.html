Carex vanheurckii var. crassispiculata

Carex vanheurckii var. crassispiculata, commonly known as the broad-spiked sedgeland sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
