Clematis mauritiana subsp. humilis

Clematis mauritiana subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the Mauritius clematis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
