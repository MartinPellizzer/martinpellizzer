Carex stellulata f. capillacea

Carex stellulata f. capillacea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with fine, silky hairs, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes and bog gardens.
