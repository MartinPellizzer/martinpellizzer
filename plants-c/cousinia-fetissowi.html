Cousinia fetissowi

Cousinia fetissowi, commonly known as the Persian cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cousinia fetissowi is adapted to high altitudes and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
