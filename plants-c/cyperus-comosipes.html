Cyperus comosipes

Cyperus comosipes, commonly known as the tufted flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wetland or marshy environments. Cyperus comosipes is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, tufted growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
