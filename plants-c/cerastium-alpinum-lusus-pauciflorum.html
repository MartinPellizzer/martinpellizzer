Cerastium alpinum lusus pauciflorum

Cerastium alpinum lusus pauciflorum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in horticulture.
