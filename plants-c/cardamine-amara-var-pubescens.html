Cardamine amara var. pubescens

Cardamine amara var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented pods. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, basal, and often have a distinctive heart-shaped form.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
