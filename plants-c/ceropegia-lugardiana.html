Ceropegia lugardiana

Ceropegia lugardiana, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia lugardiana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
