Cissus japonica var. dentata

Cissus japonica var. dentata, commonly known as the Japanese snow vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
