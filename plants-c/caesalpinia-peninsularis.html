Caesalpinia peninsularis

Caesalpinia peninsularis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and bright orange to red flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Caesalpinia peninsularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
