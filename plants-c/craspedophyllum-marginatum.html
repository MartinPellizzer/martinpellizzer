Craspedophyllum marginatum

Craspedophyllum marginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved craspedophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
