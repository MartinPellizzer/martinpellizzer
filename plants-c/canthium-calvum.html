Canthium calvum

Canthium calvum, commonly known as the smooth spurge or African spurge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its stems are generally smooth and can become woody with age, and it is known for its milky sap, which is a common feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium calvum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
