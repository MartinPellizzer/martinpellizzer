Cardamine hamulosa

Cardamine hamulosa, commonly known as the hooked watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is a perennial species that can be found in parts of Europe and Asia.
