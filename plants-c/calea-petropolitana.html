Calea petropolitana

Calea petropolitana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that open at night. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by nocturnal insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Calea petropolitana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
