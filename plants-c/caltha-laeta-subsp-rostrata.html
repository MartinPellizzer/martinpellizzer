Caltha laeta subsp. rostrata

Caltha laeta subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated golden cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, beak-like tip on the petal, giving it its common name.
