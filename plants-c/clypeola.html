Clypeola

Clypeola, commonly known as the golden cup, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray flowers that resemble a cup or bowl shape. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky soils and are native to regions of southern Africa. Clypeola species are often found in open grasslands and shrublands, where they thrive in full sun.

Their unique floral structure and vibrant color make them popular in horticultural gardens.
