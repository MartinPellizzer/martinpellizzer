Cryptocarya nova-anglica

Cryptocarya nova-anglica, commonly known as the New Zealand Christmas tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated for its decorative foliage during the holiday season.
