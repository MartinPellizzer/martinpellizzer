Cleome puberula

Cleome puberula, commonly known as the stinkweed or hairy cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Cleome puberula is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
