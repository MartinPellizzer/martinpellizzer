Cyrtandra cauliflora

Cyrtandra cauliflora, commonly known as the calypso flower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, often clustered in inflorescences at the base of the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.
