Ceropegia nana

Ceropegia nana, commonly known as the dwarf chain plant, is a small, trailing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thin, wiry stems that produce delicate, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia nana is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
