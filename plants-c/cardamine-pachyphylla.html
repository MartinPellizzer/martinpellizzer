Cardamine pachyphylla

Cardamine pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its unique leaf structure and growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
