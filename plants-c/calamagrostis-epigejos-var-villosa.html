Calamagrostis epigejos var. villosa

Calamagrostis epigejos var. villosa, commonly known as silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage and feathery panicles that add ornamental value to landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
