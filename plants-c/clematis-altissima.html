Clematis altissima

Clematis altissima, commonly known as the giant clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach lengths of up to 30 feet, making it an impressive sight in gardens. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms.

Clematis altissima thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
