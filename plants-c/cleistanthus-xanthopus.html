Cleistanthus xanthopus

Cleistanthus xanthopus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Cleistanthus xanthopus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
