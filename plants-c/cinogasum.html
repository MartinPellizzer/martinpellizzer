Cinogasum

Cinogasum, commonly known as the white sage, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers. Cinogasum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and ceremonial practices.
