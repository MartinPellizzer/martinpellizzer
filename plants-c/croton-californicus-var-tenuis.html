Croton californicus var. tenuis

Croton californicus var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a perennial shrub native to California.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a distinctive yellow-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
