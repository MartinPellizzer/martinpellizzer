Carex tetanica var. woodii

Carex tetanica var. woodii, commonly known as Wood's sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
