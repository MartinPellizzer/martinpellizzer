Cissus biformifolia

Cissus biformifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, succulent fruits. Its stems are flexible and can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Cissus biformifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.
