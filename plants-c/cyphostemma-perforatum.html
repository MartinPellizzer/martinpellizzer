Cyphostemma perforatum

Cyphostemma perforatum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with distinctive perforations along the nodes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
