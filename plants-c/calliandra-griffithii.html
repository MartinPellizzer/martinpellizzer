Calliandra griffithii

Calliandra griffithii, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
