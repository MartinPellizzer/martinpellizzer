Clusia pulcherrima

Clusia pulcherrima, commonly known as the glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening. Its adaptability and ornamental value have made it a favored plant in horticulture.

Clusia pulcherrima is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
