Chrysophyllum wagapense

Chrysophyllum wagapense, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Chrysophyllum wagapense is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local biodiversity.
