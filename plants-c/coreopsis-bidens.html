Coreopsis bidens

Coreopsis bidens, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Coreopsis bidens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
