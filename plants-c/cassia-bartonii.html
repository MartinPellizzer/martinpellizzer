Cassia bartonii

Cassia bartonii, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.

Cassia bartonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
