Claytonia discolor

Claytonia discolor, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, waxy flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Claytonia discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
