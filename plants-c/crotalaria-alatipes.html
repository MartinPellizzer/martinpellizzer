Crotalaria alatipes

Crotalaria alatipes, commonly known as the winged cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
