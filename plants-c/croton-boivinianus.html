Croton boivinianus

Croton boivinianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often showy flowers. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Croton boivinianus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
