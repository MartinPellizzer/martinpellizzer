Connarus duparquetianus

Connarus duparquetianus, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
