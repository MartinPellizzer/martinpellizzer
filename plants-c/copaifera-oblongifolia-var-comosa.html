Copaifera oblongifolia var. comosa

Copaifera oblongifolia var. comosa, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
