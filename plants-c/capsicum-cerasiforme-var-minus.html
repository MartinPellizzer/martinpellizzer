Capsicum cerasiforme var. minus

Capsicum cerasiforme var. minus, commonly known as the miniature sweet pepper, is a compact variety of chili pepper native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has small, lobed leaves and produces flowers that are usually yellow or white. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and mild flavor.
