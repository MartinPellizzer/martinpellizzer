Carpobrotus edulis var. rubescens

Carpobrotus edulis var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in sandy soils.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
