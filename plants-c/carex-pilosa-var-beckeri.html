Carex pilosa var. beckeri

Carex pilosa var. beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Native to North America, this plant thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
