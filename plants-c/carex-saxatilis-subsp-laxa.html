Carex saxatilis subsp. laxa

Carex saxatilis subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and rocky areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued in ecological landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
