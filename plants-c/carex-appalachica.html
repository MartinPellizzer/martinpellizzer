Carex appalachica

Carex appalachica, commonly known as Appalachian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex appalachica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
