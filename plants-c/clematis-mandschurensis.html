Clematis mandschurensis

Clematis mandschurensis, commonly known as the Manchurian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, such as trellises or fences, for optimal display.
