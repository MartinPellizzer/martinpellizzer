Cleomella jaliscensis

Cleomella jaliscensis, commonly known as the Jalisco cleom, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with serrated edges.

Cleomella jaliscensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native region.
