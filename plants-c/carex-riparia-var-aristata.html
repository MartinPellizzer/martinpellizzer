Carex riparia var. aristata

Carex riparia var. aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a distinctive arrowhead shape at the base. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Carex riparia var. aristata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
