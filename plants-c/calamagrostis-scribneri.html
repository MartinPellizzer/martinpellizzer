Calamagrostis scribneri

Calamagrostis scribneri, commonly known as Scribner's reed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
