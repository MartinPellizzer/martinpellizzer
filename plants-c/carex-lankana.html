Carex lankana

Carex lankana, commonly known as the Lankana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex lankana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
