Cynanchum fimbricorona

Cynanchum fimbricorona, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among members of its family. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a silvery or grayish underside.

Cynanchum fimbricorona is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
