Cornus macrophylla var. stracheyi

Cornus macrophylla var. stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
