Carex commutata

Carex commutata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex commutata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
