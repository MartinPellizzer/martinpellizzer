Convolvulus sanguineus

Convolvulus sanguineus, commonly known as the blood vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
