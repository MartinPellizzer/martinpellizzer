Celtis bungeana var. jessoensis

Celtis bungeana var. jessoensis, commonly known as the Korean hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
