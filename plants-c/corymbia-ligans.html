Corymbia ligans

Corymbia ligans, commonly known as the slender bloodwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical and sub-tropical regions of northern Australia, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, pale green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
