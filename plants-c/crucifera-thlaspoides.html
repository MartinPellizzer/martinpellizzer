Crucifera thlaspoides

Crucifera thlaspoides, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain numerous seeds. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
