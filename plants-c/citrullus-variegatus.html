Citrullus variegatus

Citrullus variegatus, commonly known as the spotted cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow patterns. The plant produces elongated, thin fruits with a mild, sweet flavor and a rough, hairy surface. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated both for gardening purposes and as a food crop.
