Coleus porphyranthus

Coleus porphyranthus, commonly known as the purple-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically grows as an upright shrub. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often in shades of violet or white.

It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
