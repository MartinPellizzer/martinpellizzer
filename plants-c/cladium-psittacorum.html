Cladium psittacorum

Cladium psittacorum, commonly known as the yellow papyrus, is a monocot native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spike is composed of small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
