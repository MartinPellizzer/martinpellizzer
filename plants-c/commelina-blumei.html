Commelina blumei

Commelina blumei, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to blue flowers that are often clustered in a spadix-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
