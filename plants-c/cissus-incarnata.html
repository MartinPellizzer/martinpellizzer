Cissus incarnata

Cissus incarnata, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, bright pink to red flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
