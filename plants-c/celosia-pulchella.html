Celosia pulchella

Celosia pulchella, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Celosia pulchella is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
