Cnicus rhizocephalus

Cnicus rhizocephalus, commonly known as the sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant contains compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
