Cuphea carthagenensis

Cuphea carthagenensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Cuphea carthagenensis is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping in suitable climates.
