Coriaria napalensis

Coriaria napalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan coriander, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often at high elevations. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while its fruits are small, dry, and schizocarpic.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
