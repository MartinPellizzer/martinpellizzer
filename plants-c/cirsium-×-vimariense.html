Cirsium × vimariense

Cirsium × vimariense, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × vimariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
