Canna indica var. edwardsii

Canna indica var. edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a tropical landscape plant.
