Caltha palustris var. dentata

Caltha palustris var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name "dentata.". It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
