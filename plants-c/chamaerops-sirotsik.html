Chamaerops sirotsik

Chamaerops sirotsik, commonly known as the Persian fan palm, is a species of palm native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a slender, upright plant with a smooth, grayish trunk and feather-like, fan-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight blue tint. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of black, berry-like fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Chamaerops sirotsik is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
