Carex oxycarpa

Carex oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, erect leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex oxycarpa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
