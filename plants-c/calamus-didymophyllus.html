Calamus didymophyllus

Calamus didymophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its morphological features and ecological role in wetland habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
