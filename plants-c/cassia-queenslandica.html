Cassia queenslandica

Cassia queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Cassia queenslandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
