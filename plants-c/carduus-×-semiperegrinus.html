Carduus × semiperegrinus

Carduus × semiperegrinus, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Carduus and Cirsium.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring robust, spiny stems and large, often woolly leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

The hybrid is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
