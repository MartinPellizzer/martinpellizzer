Cereus tweediei

Cereus tweediei, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit, with large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a columnar growth form and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Cereus tweediei is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
