Capsicum annuum var. baccatum

Capsicum annuum var. baccatum, commonly known as the "Aji Amarillo," is a variety of chili pepper native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow or orange fruits that turn red when mature. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruits are used both fresh and dried in various culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile, which is fruity and slightly sweet with a moderate heat level.
