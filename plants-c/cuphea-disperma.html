Cuphea disperma

Cuphea disperma, commonly known as the scattered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its unique flower structure attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
