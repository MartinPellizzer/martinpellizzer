Chirita pumila

Chirita pumila, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Chirita pumila is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
