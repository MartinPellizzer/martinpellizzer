Coleus grandis

Coleus grandis, commonly known as the scarlet begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange and pink, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically green with contrasting veins. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Coleus grandis prefers partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
