Croton fianarantsoae var. petiolaris

Croton fianarantsoae var. petiolaris, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage.

Native to Madagascar, it features broad, lance-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of green, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
