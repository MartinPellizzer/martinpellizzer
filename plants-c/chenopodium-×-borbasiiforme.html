Chenopodium × borbasiiforme

Chenopodium × borbasiiforme, commonly known as Indian spinach or New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in cooking. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its botanical features include small green flowers and a fibrous root system.
