Centaurea × adeana

Centaurea × adeana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea stoebe (devil's bit scabious), resulting in a hybrid with unique floral characteristics. The plant typically features showy, bright blue or purple composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in gardens and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.
