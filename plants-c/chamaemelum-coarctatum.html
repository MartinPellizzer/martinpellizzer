Chamaemelum coarctatum

Chamaemelum coarctatum, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, with a distinctive yellowish-green color, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
