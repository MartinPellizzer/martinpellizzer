Cyperus merrillii

Cyperus merrillii, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
