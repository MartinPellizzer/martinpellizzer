Cinnagrostis vicunarum

Cinnagrostis vicunarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf filaree, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescence composed of small yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cinnagrostis vicunarum is often found in the western United States and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
