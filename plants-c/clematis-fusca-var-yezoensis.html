Clematis fusca var. yezoensis

Clematis fusca var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of small tendrils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of alternate, palmate leaves that are often divided into three to five lobes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
