Campanula latifolia var. fimbriata

Campanula latifolia var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with white or pale edges. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
