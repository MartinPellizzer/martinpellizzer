Copaifera chodatiana var. fruticosa

Copaifera chodatiana var. fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the resin used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
