Carex salina f. reducta

Carex salina f. reducta, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge.

This plant is a variant of Carex salina, characterized by its reduced inflorescence and narrower leaves. It thrives in saline environments, such as coastal marshes and tidal flats, where it tolerates high salt concentrations. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of saltwater ecosystems.
