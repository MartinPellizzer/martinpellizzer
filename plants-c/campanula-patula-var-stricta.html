Campanula patula var. stricta

Campanula patula var. stricta, commonly known as the upright bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
