Cimicifuga simplex var. intermedia

Cimicifuga simplex var. intermedia, commonly known as the American bugbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring tall, unbranched stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers at the top of the stems, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with pinnately arranged leaflets that are lance-shaped and serrated.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
