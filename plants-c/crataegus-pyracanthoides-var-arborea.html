Crataegus pyracanthoides var. arborea

Crataegus pyracanthoides var. arborea, commonly known as the tree form of the firethorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive flowers and persistent fruits.
