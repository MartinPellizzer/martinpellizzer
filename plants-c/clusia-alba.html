Clusia alba

Clusia alba, commonly known as white glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its evergreen, thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, blooming throughout the year in warm climates. Clusia alba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
