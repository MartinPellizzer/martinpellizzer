Cyclodiscus

Cyclodiscus, commonly known as the wheel flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which resembles a wheel or a disc, hence its common name. The flowers are typically arranged in a circular pattern around a central stem, with each flower having five petals. Cyclodiscus species are native to Australia and are often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
