Caucanthus cinereus

Caucanthus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-gray bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Caucanthus cinereus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
