Campanula hohenackeri var. darialica

Campanula hohenackeri var. darialica, commonly known as the Darialian bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
