Cerastium × rigoi

Cerastium × rigoi, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
