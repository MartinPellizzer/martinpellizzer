Canarium harveyi var. nova-hebridiense

Canarium harveyi var. nova-hebridiense, commonly known as the New Hebrides candlenut, is a tropical tree native to the South Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing oily seeds. It is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have traditional uses in cooking and medicine.

The species is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
