Cheirostylis chalmersii

Cheirostylis chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, red-brown, torch-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Cheirostylis chalmersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
