Ctenopteris rhodocarpa

Ctenopteris rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The fronds are typically green with prominent red veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
