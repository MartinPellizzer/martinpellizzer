Commelina virginica var. australis

Commelina virginica var. australis, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Commelina genus in the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, typically from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
