Carex rigida var. glacialis

Carex rigida var. glacialis, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to cold and windy conditions.

This variety is often found in alpine tundras and rocky slopes, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
