Cyrtochilum detortum

Cyrtochilum detortum, commonly known as the twisted lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowering stems that bear small, lip-shaped flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

The orchid's unique floral structure and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
