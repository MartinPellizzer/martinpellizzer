Cupressus nevadensis

Cupressus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mountainous regions.

The Sierra Madre cypress is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in native landscapes.
