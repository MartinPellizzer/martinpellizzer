Carex davalliana var. sieberiana

Carex davalliana var. sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. Native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
