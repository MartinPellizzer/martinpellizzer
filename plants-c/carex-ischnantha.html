Carex ischnantha

Carex ischnantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curled at the edges, with a distinct central stem. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

Carex ischnantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
