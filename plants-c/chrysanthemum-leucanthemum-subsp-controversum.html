Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subsp. controversum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum subsp.

controversum, commonly known as the oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to naturalize in various environments.
