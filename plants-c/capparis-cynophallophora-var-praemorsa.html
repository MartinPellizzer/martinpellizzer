Capparis cynophallophora var. praemorsa

Capparis cynophallophora var. praemorsa, commonly known as the white caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
