Costus longifolius

Costus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands or along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Costus longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
