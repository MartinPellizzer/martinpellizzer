Centaurea subcana

Centaurea subcana, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Centaurea subcana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
