Cucumis melo var. alwarensis

Cucumis melo var. alwarensis, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic, honeyed flavor. The plant is a vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and consistent watering for optimal growth and fruit development.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible, juicy flesh and is used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
