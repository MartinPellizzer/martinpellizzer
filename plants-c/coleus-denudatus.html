Coleus denudatus

Coleus denudatus, commonly known as the naked-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that often overshadow the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with thin, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often reduced or absent in some varieties. Its foliage is usually vibrant in color, ranging from red to orange, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are usually tubular and clustered in spikes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
