Celastrus fournieri

Celastrus fournieri, commonly known as the Vietnamese staff vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
