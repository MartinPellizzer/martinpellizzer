Commelina pseudopurpurea

Commelina pseudopurpurea, commonly known as the false purple arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, violet-pink bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by showy, leaf-like structures.

Commelina pseudopurpurea is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
