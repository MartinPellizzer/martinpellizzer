Cynanchum macranthum var. matsudanum

Cynanchum macranthum var. matsudanum, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
