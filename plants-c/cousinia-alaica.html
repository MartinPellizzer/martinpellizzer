Cousinia alaica

Cousinia alaica, commonly known as the Alai mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia alaica is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
