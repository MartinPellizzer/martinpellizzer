Chamaecrista major

Chamaecrista major, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Chamaecrista major is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
