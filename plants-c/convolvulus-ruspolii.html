Convolvulus ruspolii

Convolvulus ruspolii, commonly known as the Ruspoli bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Convolvulus ruspolii is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
