Chrysothamnus dracunculoides

Chrysothamnus dracunculoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, papery bracts and compact growth habit contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
