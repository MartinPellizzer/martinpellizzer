It is characterized by its unique, cup-shaped fruiting body that resembles a bird's nest, often filled with tiny, gelatinous spore sacs. Its spores are dispersed by wind, and the structure of the fruiting body provides protection for the spores until they are released.

Cortiella is an important decomposer in forest ecosystems, contributing to the breakdown of organic matter.
