Croton glandulosus

Croton glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular croton, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Croton glandulosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
