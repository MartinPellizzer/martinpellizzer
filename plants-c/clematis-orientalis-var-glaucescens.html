Clematis orientalis var. glaucescens

Clematis orientalis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a bluish-gray underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its cascading floral display.
