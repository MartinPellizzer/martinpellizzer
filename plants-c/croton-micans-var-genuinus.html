Croton micans var. genuinus

Croton micans var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from dark green to deep purple.

The foliage often features a waxy texture and intricate patterns of veins, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Due to its aesthetic qualities, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
