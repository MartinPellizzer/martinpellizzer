Carex recta var. ostrobottnica

Carex recta var. ostrobottnica, commonly known as the straight sedge, is a variant of the Carex recta species native to northern Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
