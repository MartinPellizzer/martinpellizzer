Cuphea nivea

Cuphea nivea, commonly known as white whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea nivea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
