Clematis aristata var. glycinoides

Clematis aristata var. glycinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue underside, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for climbing structures or trellises.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and seasonal flowering habits.
