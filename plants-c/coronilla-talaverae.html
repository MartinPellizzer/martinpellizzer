Coronilla talaverae

Coronilla talaverae, commonly known as the Talavera vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
