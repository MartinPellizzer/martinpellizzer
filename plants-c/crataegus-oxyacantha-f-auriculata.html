Crataegus oxyacantha f. auriculata

Crataegus oxyacantha f. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is typically grown as a shrub or small tree and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This cultivar is well-suited to a variety of soil types and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
