Cucumis melo var. durrieni

Cucumis melo var. durrieni, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is an annual vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, arid climates. It requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its fruit is typically harvested when the rind turns from green to a pale yellow or orange hue.
