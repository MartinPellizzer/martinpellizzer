Chusquea quitensis

Chusquea quitensis, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a ground cover in shaded gardens.
