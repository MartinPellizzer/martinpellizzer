Cheilanthes bergiana

Cheilanthes bergiana, commonly known as the Berg's rockcap, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or crevice environments, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its rhizomes are thick and succulent, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
