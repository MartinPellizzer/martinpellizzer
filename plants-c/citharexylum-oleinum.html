Citharexylum oleinum

Citharexylum oleinum, commonly known as the fragrant cithara or sweet cithara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the summer months.

Citharexylum oleinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
