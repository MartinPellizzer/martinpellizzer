Cucurbita moschata var. brunnea

Cucurbita moschata var. brunnea, commonly known as the orange pumpkin, is a variant of the winter squash characterized by its distinctive orange to reddish-orange skin and sweet, nutty-flavored flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash. The plant is a trailing vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the genus Cucurbita. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary use and ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and versatility in cooking.
