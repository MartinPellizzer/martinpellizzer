Centaurea pallescens subsp. hyalolepis

Centaurea pallescens subsp.

hyalolepis, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
