Cucurbita pepo var. maxima

Cucurbita pepo var. maxima, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often deeply ribbed fruits, which can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. Its fruit is typically used for culinary purposes, though it is also cultivated for decorative and competitive displays.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
