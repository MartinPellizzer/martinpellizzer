Carex backii var. subrostrata

Carex backii var. subrostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This species typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates in its native environment.
