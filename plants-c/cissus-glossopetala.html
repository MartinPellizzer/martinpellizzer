Cissus glossopetala

Cissus glossopetala, commonly known as the silver leaf cissus, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus glossopetala is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for vertical gardens and hanging baskets.
