Callichilia basileis

Callichilia basileis, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly from infrequent rains.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
