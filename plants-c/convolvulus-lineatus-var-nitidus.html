Convolvulus lineatus var. nitidus

Convolvulus lineatus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
