Cleome macedonica

Cleome macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome macedonica typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
