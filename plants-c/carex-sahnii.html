Carex sahnii

Carex sahnii, commonly known as the Sahni's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly found in regions of China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries.

Carex sahnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
