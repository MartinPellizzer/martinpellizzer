Crocus sibthorpianus var. pulchricolor

Crocus sibthorpianus var. pulchricolor, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers with vivid purple petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical beauty and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
