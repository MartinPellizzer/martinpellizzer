Crepis gracilis

Crepis gracilis, commonly known as slender hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flower heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Crepis gracilis is often found in open habitats and is adapted to a wide range of soil conditions.
