Carex flava var. nevadensis

Carex flava var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada yellow sedge, is a perennial sedge species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bright yellow to golden green in color. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
