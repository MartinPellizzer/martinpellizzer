Carex tangiana

Carex tangiana, commonly known as the Tangian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex tangiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
