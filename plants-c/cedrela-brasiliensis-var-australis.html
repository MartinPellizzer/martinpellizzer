Cedrela brasiliensis var. australis

Cedrela brasiliensis var. australis, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.
