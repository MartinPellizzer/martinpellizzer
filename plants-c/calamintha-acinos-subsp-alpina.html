Calamintha acinos subsp. alpina

Calamintha acinos subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine pennyroyal, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces tiny, pale blue or violet flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
