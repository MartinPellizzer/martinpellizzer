Camissonia claviformis subsp. peeblesii

Camissonia claviformis subsp.

peeblesii, commonly known as Peebles' slenderleaf, is a flowering plant native to California. It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as slopes and chaparral.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
