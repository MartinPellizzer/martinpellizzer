Carum coloratum

Carum coloratum, commonly known as the blue carrot or purple carrot, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, slightly spicy aroma.
