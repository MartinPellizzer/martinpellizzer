Cornus × elwinortonii

Cornus × elwinortonii, commonly known as the Knapweed Dogwood, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink bract-like flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds.

Cornus × elwinortonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
