Cassytha brasiliensis

Cassytha brasiliensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that can grow along the ground or climb other plants. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slight pubescence.

Cassytha brasiliensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
