Cucumis melo var. conomon

Cucumis melo var. conomon, commonly known as the Caspian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and smooth, thin rind.

It typically has a pale green color with a slightly bitter taste, which is more pronounced in the outer layers. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are yellow and monoecious, meaning both male and female flowers are present on the same plant.

The fruit develops from the female flowers and is harvested when it reaches its mature size and color.
