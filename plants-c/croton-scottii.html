Croton scottii

Croton scottii, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with lobed margins, often displaying a vibrant green or variegated coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
