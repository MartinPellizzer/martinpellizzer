Cardamine californica

Cardamine californica, commonly known as the California watercress, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

Cardamine californica thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
