Cryptocarya peumus

Cryptocarya peumus, commonly known as the Chilean laurel, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
