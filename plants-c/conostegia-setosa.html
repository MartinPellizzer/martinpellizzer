Conostegia setosa

Conostegia setosa, commonly known as the hairy conostegia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
