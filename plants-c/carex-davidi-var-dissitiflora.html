Carex davidi var. dissitiflora

Carex davidi var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces erect, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded forest floors.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog gardens.
