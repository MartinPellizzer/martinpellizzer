Campanula asturica

Campanula asturica, commonly known as the Asturian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanula asturica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
