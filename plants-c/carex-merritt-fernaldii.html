Carex merritt-fernaldii

Carex merritt-fernaldii, commonly known as the Merritt-Fernald sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
