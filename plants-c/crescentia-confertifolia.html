Crescentia confertifolia

Crescentia confertifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant produces a fruit that is a fibrous, spiny capsule containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Crescentia confertifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
