Cytisus supinus subsp. pannonicus

Cytisus supinus subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Europe. It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for dry, sunny environments.
