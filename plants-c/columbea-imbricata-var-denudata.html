Columbea imbricata var. denudata

Columbea imbricata var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-leaved columbea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often reduced or absent, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a desirable addition to gardens and floral arrangements.
