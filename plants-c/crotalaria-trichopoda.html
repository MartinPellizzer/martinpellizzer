Crotalaria trichopoda

Crotalaria trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers. Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
