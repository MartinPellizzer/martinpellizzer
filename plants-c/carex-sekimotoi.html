Carex sekimotoi

Carex sekimotoi, commonly known as Sekimoto's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
