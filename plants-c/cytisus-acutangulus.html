Cytisus acutangulus

Cytisus acutangulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a hairy texture.

Cytisus acutangulus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.
