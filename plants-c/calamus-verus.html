Calamus verus

Calamus verus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in spike-like clusters. Calamus verus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomes are often harvested for use in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent.
