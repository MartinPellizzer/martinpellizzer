Cissus osaensis

Cissus osaensis, commonly known as the African nettle or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by pale yellow berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
