Crotalaria graminicola

Crotalaria graminicola, commonly known as the grassy crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in agricultural systems to improve soil fertility.
