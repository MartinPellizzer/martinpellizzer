Cestrum corymbosum var. grandifolium

Cestrum corymbosum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the grandifolium cestrum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and nocturnal fragrance.
