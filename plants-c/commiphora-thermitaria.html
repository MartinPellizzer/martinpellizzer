Commiphora thermitaria

Commiphora thermitaria, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.
