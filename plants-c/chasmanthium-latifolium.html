Chasmanthium latifolium

Chasmanthium latifolium, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are tall and cylindrical, with dense, bright red or orange spikelets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
