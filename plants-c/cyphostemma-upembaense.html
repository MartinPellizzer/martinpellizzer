Cyphostemma upembaense

Cyphostemma upembaense, commonly known as the Upemba moonvine, is a climbing plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
