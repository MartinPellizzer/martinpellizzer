Cattleya elongata var. elegans

Cattleya elongata var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flower spikes that bear showy, fragrant blossoms in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens for its beauty and long-lasting blooms.
