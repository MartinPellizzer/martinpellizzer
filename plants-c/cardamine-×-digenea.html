Cardamine × digenea

Cardamine × digenea, commonly known as the broad-leaved watercress, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or broadly ovate. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Its leaves are often used in salads and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
