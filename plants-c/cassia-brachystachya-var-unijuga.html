Cassia brachystachya var. unijuga

Cassia brachystachya var. unijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
