Cranichis maldonadoana

Cranichis maldonadoana, commonly known as the Maldonado's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rock surfaces or in cloud forests. Its flowers are small, with a distinctive coloration pattern that includes shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
