Coleus lancifolius

Coleus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other ornamental coleus species. Its foliage is typically arranged oppositely on square, hairy stems.

Coleus lancifolius is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
