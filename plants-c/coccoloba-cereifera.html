Coccoloba cereifera

Coccoloba cereifera, commonly known as waxleaf coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are dark green and often have a slight purple tinge. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Coccoloba cereifera is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
