Cymbalaria muelleri subsp. villosa

Cymbalaria muelleri subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats.

The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its flowers are usually tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.
