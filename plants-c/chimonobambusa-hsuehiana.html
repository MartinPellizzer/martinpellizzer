Chimonobambusa hsuehiana

Chimonobambusa hsuehiana, commonly known as the Hsuehiana bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of its monocot classification. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
