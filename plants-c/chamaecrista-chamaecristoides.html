Chamaecrista chamaecristoides

Chamaecrista chamaecristoides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree with compound leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its papery, pod-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
