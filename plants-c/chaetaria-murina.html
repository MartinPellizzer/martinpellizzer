Chaetaria murina

Chaetaria murina, commonly known as the mouse-tail grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
