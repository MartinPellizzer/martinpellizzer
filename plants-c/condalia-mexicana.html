Condalia mexicana

Condalia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican condalia or Mexican tree of heaven, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable species with potential uses in horticulture and ecological restoration.
