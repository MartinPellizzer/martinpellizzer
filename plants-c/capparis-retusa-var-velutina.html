Capparis retusa var. velutina

Capparis retusa var. velutina, commonly known as velvet caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, tangy fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.
