Chrysosplenium davidianum var. alpinum

Chrysosplenium davidianum var. alpinum, commonly known as Alpine goldcup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of East Asia.

It features rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain heat in cold climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in cup-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
