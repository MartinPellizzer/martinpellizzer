Cassia angolensis

Cassia angolensis, commonly known as Angolan cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia angolensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
