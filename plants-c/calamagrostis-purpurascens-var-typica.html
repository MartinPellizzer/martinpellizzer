Calamagrostis purpurascens var. typica

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. typica, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and along coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
