Cyperus olfersianus var. maximiliani

Cyperus olfersianus var. maximiliani, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
