Cyperus fuscus var. condensatus

Cyperus fuscus var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
