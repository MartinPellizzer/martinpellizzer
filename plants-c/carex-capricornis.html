Carex capricornis

Carex capricornis, commonly known as the capricorn sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in dense clusters. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex capricornis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
