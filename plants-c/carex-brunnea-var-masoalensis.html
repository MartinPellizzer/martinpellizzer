Carex brunnea var. masoalensis

Carex brunnea var. masoalensis, commonly known as the Masoalensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
