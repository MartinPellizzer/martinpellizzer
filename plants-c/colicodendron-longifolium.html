Colicodendron longifolium

Colicodendron longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved colicodendron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The long-leaved colicodendron is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.
