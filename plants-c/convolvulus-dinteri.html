Convolvulus dinteri

Convolvulus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
