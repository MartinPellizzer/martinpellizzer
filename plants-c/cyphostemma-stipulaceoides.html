Cyphostemma stipulaceoides

Cyphostemma stipulaceoides, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
