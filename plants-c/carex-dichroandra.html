Carex dichroandra

Carex dichroandra, commonly known as the two-colored sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, giving it its common name.

Carex dichroandra plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
