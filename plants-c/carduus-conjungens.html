Carduus conjungens

Carduus conjungens, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a dense cluster.

Carduus conjungens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
