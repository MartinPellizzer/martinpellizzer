Coryphantha indensis

Coryphantha indensis, commonly known as the Indian hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing, spiny cactus that typically forms dense mats or clusters. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spiny tubercles. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the axils of the areoles.

Coryphantha indensis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
