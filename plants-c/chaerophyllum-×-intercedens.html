Chaerophyllum × intercedens

Chaerophyllum × intercedens, commonly known as the hybrid meadow parsnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like clusters, producing yellowish-green petals.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
