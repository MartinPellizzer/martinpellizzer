Celtis tala f. obtusata

Celtis tala f. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf hackberry, is a variant of the hackberry tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves with rounded lobes. The leaves are typically thick and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
