Cyperus esphacelatus

Cyperus esphacelatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and triangular stems.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. Cyperus esphacelatus is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish in waterlogged soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
