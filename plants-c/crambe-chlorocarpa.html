Crambe chlorocarpa

Crambe chlorocarpa, commonly known as the yellow crambe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
