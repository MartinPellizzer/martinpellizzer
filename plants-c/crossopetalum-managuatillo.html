Crossopetalum managuatillo

Crossopetalum managuatillo, commonly known as the Managua bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
