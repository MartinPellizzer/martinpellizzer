Chrysanthoglossum

Chrysanthoglossum, commonly known as the golden tongue plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tongues, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Chrysanthoglossum species are often found in regions of southern Africa, particularly in South Africa.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
