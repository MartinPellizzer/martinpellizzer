Campanula crispa

Campanula crispa, commonly known as the bellflower or crisped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Campanula crispa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
