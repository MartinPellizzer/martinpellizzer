Cissus acida var. macilenta

Cissus acida var. macilenta, commonly known as sour fig or Indian sour fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
