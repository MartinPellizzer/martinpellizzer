Consolida hispanica

Consolida hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often resembling those of the common sweet pea. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and can also serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
