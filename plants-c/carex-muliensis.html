Carex muliensis

Carex muliensis, commonly known as the Muliensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Carex muliensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
