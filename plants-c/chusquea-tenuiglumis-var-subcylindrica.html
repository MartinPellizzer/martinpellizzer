Chusquea tenuiglumis var. subcylindrica

Chusquea tenuiglumis var. subcylindrica, commonly known as the slender-stemmed weeping bamboo, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale, often hairy stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique form and texture.
