Castalia candida

Castalia candida, commonly known as the white castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

Castalia candida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
