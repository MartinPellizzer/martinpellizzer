Conyza pubescens

Conyza pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields.

Conyza pubescens is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
