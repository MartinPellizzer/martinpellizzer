Cleome noeana

Cleome noeana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features showy, spider-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and pinnately lobed leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Cleome noeana is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and easy cultivation.
