Cyperus sesquiflorus f. denticulatus

Cyperus sesquiflorus f. denticulatus, commonly known as the denticulate flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough or toothed edge, giving it its distinguishing name. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
