Crataegus damei

Crataegus damei, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Crataegus damei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential cardiovascular benefits.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
