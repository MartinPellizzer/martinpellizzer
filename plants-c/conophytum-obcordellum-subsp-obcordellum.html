Conophytum obcordellum subsp. obcordellum

Conophytum obcordellum subsp.

obcordellum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The leaves are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
