Cunonia pseudoverticillata

Cunonia pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the false verticillate cunonia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems and potential use in traditional medicine.
