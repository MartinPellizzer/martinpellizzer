Cyanococcus tallapusae

Cyanococcus tallapusae, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, making it economically important in the region.
