Caprifolium acuminatum var. oxylepis

Caprifolium acuminatum var. oxylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
