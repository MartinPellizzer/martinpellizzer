Cirsium polyanthemon

Cirsium polyanthemon, commonly known as the showy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium polyanthemon thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
