Corallocarpus welwitschii

Corallocarpus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's corallocarpus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
