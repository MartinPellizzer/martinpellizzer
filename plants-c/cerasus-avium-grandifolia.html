Cerasus avium grandifolia

Cerasus avium grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, often preceding the emergence of its foliage. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
