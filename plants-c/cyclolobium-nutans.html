Cyclolobium nutans

Cyclolobium nutans, commonly known as the drooping burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that hang downward. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. Cyclolobium nutans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
