Columbea brasiliana var. ridolfiana

Columbea brasiliana var. ridolfiana, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
