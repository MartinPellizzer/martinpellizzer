Copaifera bracteata

Copaifera bracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark that exudes a valuable essential oil. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its large, palmate leaves and yellowish flowers. Its wood is durable and often used in construction, while the resin has traditional medicinal uses.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
