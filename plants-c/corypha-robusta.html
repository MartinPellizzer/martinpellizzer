Corypha robusta

Corypha robusta, commonly known as the giant palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow or green in color. Corypha robusta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
