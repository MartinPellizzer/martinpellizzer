Ciminalis longiflora

Ciminalis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ciminalis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically blooms during the spring and early summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
