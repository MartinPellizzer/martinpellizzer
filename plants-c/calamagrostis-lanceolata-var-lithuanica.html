Calamagrostis lanceolata var. lithuanica

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and delicate, feathery panicles that bloom in late summer. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
