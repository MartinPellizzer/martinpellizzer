Coccoloba ascendens

Coccoloba ascendens, commonly known as the climbing beautyberry, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Coccoloba ascendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
