Corylus avellana f. grandifolia

Corylus avellana f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf hazel, is a cultivar of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its notably larger leaves compared to the typical species, which can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. The plant produces edible nuts encased in a hairy husk, similar to other hazel species. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and potential for nut production.
