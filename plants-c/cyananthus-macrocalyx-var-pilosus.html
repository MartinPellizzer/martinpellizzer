Cyananthus macrocalyx var. pilosus

Cyananthus macrocalyx var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy large-flowered bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its varietal name "pilosus.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
