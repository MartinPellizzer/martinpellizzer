Campium samoense

Campium samoense, commonly known as the Samoan campion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.

Campium samoense is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats along the shoreline.
