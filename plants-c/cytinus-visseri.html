Cytinus visseri

Cytinus visseri, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a unique flowering plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of North Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its small, fleshy leaves are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Cytinus visseri is also notable for its short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
