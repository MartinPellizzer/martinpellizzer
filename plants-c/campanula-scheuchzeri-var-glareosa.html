Campanula scheuchzeri var. glareosa

Campanula scheuchzeri var. glareosa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
