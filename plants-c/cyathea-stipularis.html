Cyathea stipularis

Cyathea stipularis, commonly known as the stipe-armed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome that supports its rapid growth and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its fronds are typically bright green and can be used for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Cyathea stipularis plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
