Chaetocyperus albibracteatus

Chaetocyperus albibracteatus, commonly known as the white bract sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist to aquatic environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
