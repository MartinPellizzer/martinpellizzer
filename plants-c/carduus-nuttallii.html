Carduus nuttallii

Carduus nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a single, showy head.

The plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid environments.
