Chamaepentas hindsioides var. glabrescens

Chamaepentas hindsioides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-hairless flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, flame-colored inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a smooth texture and lacks the pubescence typical of many related species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
