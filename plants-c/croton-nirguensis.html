Croton nirguensis

Croton nirguensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse species with often milky sap. The plant is distinguished by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsule-like, containing numerous seeds.

Croton nirguensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
