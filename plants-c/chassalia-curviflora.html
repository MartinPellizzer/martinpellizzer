Chassalia curviflora

Chassalia curviflora, commonly known as the curly-flowered chassalia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chassalia curviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
