Cunila altissima

Cunila altissima, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic, licorice-scented leaves and seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Cunila altissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.
