Crepis rhoeadifolia

Crepis rhoeadifolia, commonly known as the plains thistle, is a flowering plant native to the central and western regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Crepis rhoeadifolia is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
