Croton ferrugineus

Croton ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that range in color from bright green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
