Cantinoa carpinifolia

Cantinoa carpinifolia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cantinoa carpinifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
