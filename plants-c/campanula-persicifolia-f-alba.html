Campanula persicifolia f. alba

Campanula persicifolia f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower plant characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are softly hairy.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
