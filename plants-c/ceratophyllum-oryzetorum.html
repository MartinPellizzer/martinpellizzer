Ceratophyllum oryzetorum

Ceratophyllum oryzetorum, commonly known as the rice spikegrass, is a small, aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it reproduces through both vegetative propagation and flowering.

Ceratophyllum oryzetorum is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
