Cymbidium ensifolium f. misericors

Cymbidium ensifolium f. misericors, commonly known as the Sword-leafed Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-like leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant is a pseudobulbous orchid, meaning it has thickened stems that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This unique orchid is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
