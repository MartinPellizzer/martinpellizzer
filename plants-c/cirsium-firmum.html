Cirsium firmum

Cirsium firmum, commonly known as the hardstem thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with coarse teeth. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cirsium firmum typically grows in dry, disturbed soils in open habitats like meadows and prairies.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
