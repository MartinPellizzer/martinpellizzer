Cordia discolor var. pallida

Cordia discolor var. pallida, commonly known as the pale cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
