Clutia glabrescens

Clutia glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a tropical shrub native to Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Clutia glabrescens is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
