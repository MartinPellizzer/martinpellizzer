Cucurbita maxima var. penebroma

Cucurbita maxima var. penebroma, commonly known as the White Hubbard squash, is a variety of winter squash characterized by its large, pale yellow to white, elongated fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, gourds, and pumpkins. The plant is typically grown as an annual, producing broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in soups, stews, and baked dishes.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires a long growing season to mature.
