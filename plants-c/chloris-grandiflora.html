Chloris grandiflora

Chloris grandiflora, commonly known as large-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Chloris grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
