Cestrum hartwegii

Cestrum hartwegii, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant is a deciduous shrub or vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cestrum hartwegii is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
