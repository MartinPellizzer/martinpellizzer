Cheiridopsis pusilla

Cheiridopsis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a small succulent native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for succulent enthusiasts.
