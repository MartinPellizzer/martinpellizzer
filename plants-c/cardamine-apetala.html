Cardamine apetala

Cardamine apetala, commonly known as the barren bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small herbaceous species characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in a raceme.

Cardamine apetala is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
