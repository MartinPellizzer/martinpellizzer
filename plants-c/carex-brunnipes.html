Carex brunnipes

Carex brunnipes, commonly known as the brown-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a brownish sheath at the base, and it produces spike-like inflorescences.

Carex brunnipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
