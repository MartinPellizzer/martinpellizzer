Calamagrostis phragmitoides

Calamagrostis phragmitoides, commonly known as the tall reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color, adding visual interest in the fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
