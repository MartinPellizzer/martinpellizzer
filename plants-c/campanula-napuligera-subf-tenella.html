Campanula napuligera subf. tenella

Campanula napuligera subf.

tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
