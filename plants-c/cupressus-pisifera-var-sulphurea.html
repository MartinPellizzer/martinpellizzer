Cupressus pisifera var. sulphurea

Cupressus pisifera var. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cypress native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green needles. The plant has a compact, pyramidal shape and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in crossed pairs, typical of the Cupressaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a specimen plant.
