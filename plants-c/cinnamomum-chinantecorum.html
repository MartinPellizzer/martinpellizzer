Cinnamomum chinantecorum

Cinnamomum chinantecorum, commonly known as the Chinantec cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-10 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces oval, dark purple berries.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
