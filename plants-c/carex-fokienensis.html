Carex fokienensis

Carex fokienensis, commonly known as the Fokien sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex fokienensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
