Campanula barbeyi

Campanula barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbey's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Campanula barbeyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
