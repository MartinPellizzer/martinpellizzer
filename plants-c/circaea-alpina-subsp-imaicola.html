Circaea alpina subsp. imaicola

Circaea alpina subsp.

imaicola, commonly known as the alpine woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and opposite, elliptic leaves.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine flora.
