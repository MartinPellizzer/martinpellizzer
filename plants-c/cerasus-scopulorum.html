Cerasus scopulorum

Cerasus scopulorum, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or rock cherry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
