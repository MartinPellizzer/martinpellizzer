Carex mitchelliana

Carex mitchelliana, commonly known as Mitchell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.
