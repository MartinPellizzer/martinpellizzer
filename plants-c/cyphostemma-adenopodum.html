Cyphostemma adenopodum

Cyphostemma adenopodum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Cyphostemma adenopodum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
