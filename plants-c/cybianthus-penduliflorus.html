Cybianthus penduliflorus

Cybianthus penduliflorus, commonly known as the hanging flower or pendulous-flowered plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish coating.

Cybianthus penduliflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
