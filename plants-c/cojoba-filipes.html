Cojoba filipes

Cojoba filipes, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in saline environments and is often found growing along beaches and mangrove forests. Its ability to tolerate salt spray and poor soil conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Cojoba filipes plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
