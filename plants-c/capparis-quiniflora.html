Capparis quiniflora

Capparis quiniflora, commonly known as the six-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, green buds that are used in culinary applications, similar to capers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in shrubland or coastal dunes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
