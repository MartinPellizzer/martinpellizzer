Claytonia perfoliata var. rubra

Claytonia perfoliata var. rubra, commonly known as red-stemmed spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, oval leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces delicate, nodding flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and early blooming habit make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
