Chimonobambusa microfloscula

Chimonobambusa microfloscula, commonly known as the small-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical culms that are typically green and smooth. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white florets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
