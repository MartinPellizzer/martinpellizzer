Centaurea paniculata var. castellana

Centaurea paniculata var. castellana, commonly known as the Castellana knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lanceolate, serrated, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.
