Carex praecox var. setifolia

Carex praecox var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender bog sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety is native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
