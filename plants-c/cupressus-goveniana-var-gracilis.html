Cupressus goveniana var. gracilis

Cupressus goveniana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender lacebark cedar, is a variant of the lacebark cedar native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen conifer with a narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green foliage. The bark of this plant is distinctive, featuring a flaky, peeling texture that reveals a reddish-brown inner layer. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
