Coccinia dinteri

Coccinia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Coccinia dinteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
