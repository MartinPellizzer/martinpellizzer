Cattleya guttata f. munda

Cattleya guttata f. munda, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with distinctive spots or markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
