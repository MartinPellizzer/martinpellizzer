Cnidium yakushimense

Cnidium yakushimense, commonly known as the Yakushimense Cnidium, is a perennial herb native to Japan, particularly the island of Yakushima.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. It is valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like cnidiosides and other bioactive substances.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is also of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
