Chondrophora virgata

Chondrophora virgata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silvery-blue leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

Chondrophora virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
