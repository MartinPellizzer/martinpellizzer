Carex congestiflora

Carex congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

Carex congestiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
