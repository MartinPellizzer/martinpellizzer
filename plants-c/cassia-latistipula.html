Cassia latistipula

Cassia latistipula, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia latistipula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
