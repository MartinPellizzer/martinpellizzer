Chesneya ferganensis var. villosa

Chesneya ferganensis var. villosa, commonly known as the Fergana hairy chesneya, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
