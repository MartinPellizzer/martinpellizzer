Caragana brevispina subsp. tenzingii

Caragana brevispina subsp.

tenzingii, commonly known as the Tenzing's shrub, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found at high altitudes. Its woody stems are covered in thorny spines, which provide protection against herbivores.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
