Chrysobalanus sublanatus

Chrysobalanus sublanatus, commonly known as the rough-leafed chrysobalanus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalanoideae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
