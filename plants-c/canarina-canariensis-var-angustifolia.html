Canarina canariensis var. angustifolia

Canarina canariensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf canary, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
