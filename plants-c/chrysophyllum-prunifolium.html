Chrysophyllum prunifolium

Chrysophyllum prunifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.

Chrysophyllum prunifolium is also cultivated for its ability to provide shade and its ecological benefits in natural habitats.
