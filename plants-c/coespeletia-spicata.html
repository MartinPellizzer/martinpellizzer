Coespeletia spicata

Coespeletia spicata, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, high-altitude climates. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and an upright stem bearing the flower heads.

Coespeletia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
