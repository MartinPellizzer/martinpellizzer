Conyza gracilis

Conyza gracilis, commonly known as slender weed, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its small, white to yellowish flowers are arranged in a cluster, and it produces copious amounts of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Conyza gracilis is considered a weed in many areas due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new habitats.
