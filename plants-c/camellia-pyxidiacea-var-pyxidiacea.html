Camellia pyxidiacea var. pyxidiacea

Camellia pyxidiacea var. pyxidiacea, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to eastern China.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. It produces elliptical, woody fruits known as capsules, which contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
