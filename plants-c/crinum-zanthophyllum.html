Crinum zanthophyllum

Crinum zanthophyllum, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with orange markings. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Crinum zanthophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
