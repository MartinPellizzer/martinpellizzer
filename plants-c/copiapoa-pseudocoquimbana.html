Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana

Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbo copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in collections of desert plants.
