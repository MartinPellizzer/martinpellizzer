Chorizanthe polygonoides var. longispina

Chorizanthe polygonoides var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by bead-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its long, stiff spines are a notable feature, providing protection against herbivores.
