Crotalaria vallicola

Crotalaria vallicola, commonly known as the mountain crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria vallicola is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
