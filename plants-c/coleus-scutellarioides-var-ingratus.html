Coleus scutellarioides var. ingratus

Coleus scutellarioides var. ingratus, commonly known as the hairy leaved coleus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often hairy foliage that comes in a variety of colors, including green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in spikes at the tips of the branches. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
