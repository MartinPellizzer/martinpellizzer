Ceanothus foliosus subsp. medius

Ceanothus foliosus subsp.

medius, commonly known as the California ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its bark is often fissured and peeling.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
