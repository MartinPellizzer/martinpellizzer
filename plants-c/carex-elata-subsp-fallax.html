Carex elata subsp. fallax

Carex elata subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically found in the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
