Commelina dianthifolia var. filiformis

Commelina dianthifolia var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the genus Commelina. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, shade-tolerant plant.
