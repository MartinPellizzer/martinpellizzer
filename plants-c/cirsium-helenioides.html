Cirsium helenioides

Cirsium helenioides, commonly known as the Helen's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish-blue flower heads that are composed of many tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cirsium helenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
