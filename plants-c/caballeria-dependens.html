Caballeria dependens

Caballeria dependens, commonly known as the hanging caballero, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, often at high altitudes. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Caballeria dependens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
