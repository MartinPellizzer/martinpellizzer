Carex albolutescens var. brunnea

Carex albolutescens var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown-stem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The varietal form, brunnea, is distinguished by its darker, more bronzed stem color compared to the typical variety.
