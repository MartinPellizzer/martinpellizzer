Crassula lycopodioides f. purpusii

Crassula lycopodioides f. purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's crassula, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The leaves are fleshy, typically green or reddish in color, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. This frost-tolerant plant thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
