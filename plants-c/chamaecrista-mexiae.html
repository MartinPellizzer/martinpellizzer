Chamaecrista mexiae

Chamaecrista mexiae, commonly known as Mexican ticktree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chamaecrista mexiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
