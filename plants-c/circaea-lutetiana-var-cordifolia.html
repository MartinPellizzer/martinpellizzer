Circaea lutetiana var. cordifolia

Circaea lutetiana var. cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
