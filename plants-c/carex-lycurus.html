Carex lycurus

Carex lycurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex lycurus has small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed, capsule-like fruits.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
