Centaurea koktebelica

Centaurea koktebelica, commonly known as the Koktebel thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and a robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the plant produces small, bract-like leaves around the flower heads.

Centaurea koktebelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
