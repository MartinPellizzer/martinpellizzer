Copaifera arnoldiana

Copaifera arnoldiana, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and long, pendulous inflorescences. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its aromatic, resinous sap. This sap has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae and is valued for its ecological and economic significance.
