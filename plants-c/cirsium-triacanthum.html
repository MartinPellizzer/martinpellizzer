Cirsium triacanthum

Cirsium triacanthum, commonly known as the thistle hedgehog, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its robust growth habit makes it a common sight in rocky and disturbed soils.

Cirsium triacanthum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
