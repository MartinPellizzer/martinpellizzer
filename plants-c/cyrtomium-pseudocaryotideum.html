Cyrtomium pseudocaryotideum

Cyrtomium pseudocaryotideum, commonly known as the false cycad fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a dense cluster of dark green, leathery fronds. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape.

Cyrtomium pseudocaryotideum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
