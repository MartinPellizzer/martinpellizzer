Campanula affinis

Campanula affinis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula affinis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
