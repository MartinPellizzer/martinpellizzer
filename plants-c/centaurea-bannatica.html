Centaurea bannatica

Centaurea bannatica, commonly known as the Bannat's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea bannatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
