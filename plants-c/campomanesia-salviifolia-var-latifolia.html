Campomanesia salviifolia var. latifolia

Campomanesia salviifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf satinash, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves, which are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
