Callianthe picta

Callianthe picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vivid, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns and a wide range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Callianthe picta is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
