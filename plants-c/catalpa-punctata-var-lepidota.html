Catalpa punctata var. lepidota

Catalpa punctata var. lepidota, commonly known as the eastern catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to provide shade.
