Cortusa matthioli f. moravica

Cortusa matthioli f. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a compact, perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a clumping habit with dark green, lobed leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
