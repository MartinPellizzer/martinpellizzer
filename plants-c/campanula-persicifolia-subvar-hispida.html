Campanula persicifolia subvar. hispida

Campanula persicifolia subvar. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, hairy hairs, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
