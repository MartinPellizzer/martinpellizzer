Clematis patens var. atropurpurea

Clematis patens var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with a violet throat. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or fences.
