Calandrinia grandiflora

Calandrinia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Calandrinia grandiflora is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
