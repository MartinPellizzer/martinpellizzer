Calytrix strigosa

Calytrix strigosa, commonly known as the rough leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Calytrix strigosa is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
