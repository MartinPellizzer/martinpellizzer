Chimonobambusa quadrangularis f. suow

Chimonobambusa quadrangularis f. suow, commonly known as the Suow bamboo, is a unique variant of the quadrangular bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinct four-sided stem and dense, upright growth habit. The plant is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, where it thrives in humid, mountainous environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The Suow bamboo is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
