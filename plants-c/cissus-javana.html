Cissus javana

Cissus javana, commonly known as the climbing hedge or glass-leafed ivy, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often have a translucent or glass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by green, edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus javana is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential medicinal properties.
