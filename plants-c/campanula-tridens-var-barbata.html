Campanula tridens var. barbata

Campanula tridens var. barbata, commonly known as the bearded bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
