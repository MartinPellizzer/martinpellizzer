Campanula tubulosa var. carpatha

Campanula tubulosa var. carpatha, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
