Catasetum carrenhianum

Catasetum carrenhianum, commonly known as the Carribean Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, lip-like petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

The species is noted for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized structures that facilitate pollination by certain insect species.
