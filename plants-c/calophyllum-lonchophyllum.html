Calophyllum lonchophyllum

Calophyllum lonchophyllum, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy green leaves. The tree produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit's interior contains white, fleshy segments that are sweet and aromatic, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
