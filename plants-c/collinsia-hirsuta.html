Collinsia hirsuta

Collinsia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that resemble tiny eyes. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Collinsia hirsuta is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
