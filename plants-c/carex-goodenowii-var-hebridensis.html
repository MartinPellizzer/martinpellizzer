Carex goodenowii var. hebridensis

Carex goodenowii var. hebridensis, commonly known as the Hebridian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
