Camellia japonica var. porrecta

Camellia japonica var. porrecta, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in winter and early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during cooler months.
