Cineraria angustata

Cineraria angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered rare in the wild.
