Clematis diebuensis

Clematis diebuensis, commonly known as the African clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically white or blue flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Clematis diebuensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
