Calanthe obcordata

Calanthe obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and features showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that form a dense rosette, and its inflorescence consists of several resupinate flowers with spurred petals. Calanthe obcordata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
