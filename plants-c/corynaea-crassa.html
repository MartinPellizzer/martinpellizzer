Corynaea crassa

Corynaea crassa, commonly known as the broad-leafed corynaea, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring. Corynaea crassa is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
