Chamaecrista kolabensis

Chamaecrista kolabensis, commonly known as the Kolab bean, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
