Carum alaicum

Carum alaicum, commonly known as alpine caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small white to greenish flowers. Carum alaicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic qualities.
