Chamaenerion angustifolium subsp. macrophyllum

Chamaenerion angustifolium subsp.

macrophyllum, commonly known as the Great Willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery underside. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is often found in wet or disturbed soils, such as along riverbanks and roadsides.
