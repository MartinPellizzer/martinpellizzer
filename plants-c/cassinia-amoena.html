Cassinia amoena

Cassinia amoena, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. Its leaves are typically narrow and grey-green, providing a striking contrast to the bright flowers.

Cassinia amoena is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
