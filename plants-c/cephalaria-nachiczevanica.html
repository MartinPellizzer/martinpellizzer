Cephalaria nachiczevanica

Cephalaria nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Armenian cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Cephalaria nachiczevanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
