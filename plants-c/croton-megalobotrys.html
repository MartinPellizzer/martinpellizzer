Croton megalobotrys

Croton megalobotrys, commonly known as the giant croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
