Cucumis sativus var. curtus

Cucumis sativus var. curtus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like spines along the ridges of its fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is generally smaller and more elongated compared to the standard cucumber.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or for its unique flavor in specialty cuisines.
