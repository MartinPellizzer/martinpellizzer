Callicarpa pentandra f. hexandra

Callicarpa pentandra f. hexandra, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This shrub is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
