Chenopodium striatifolium

Chenopodium striatifolium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its serrated, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits. It is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium striatifolium is sometimes considered a weed but can also serve as a food source for certain wildlife.
