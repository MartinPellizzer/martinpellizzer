Conophytum crateriforme

Conophytum crateriforme, commonly known as the "Cup-shaped Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, crinkled leaves that form a rosette shape, often resembling a cup or bowl. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum crateriforme is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to rocky, dry habitats.
