Convolvulus acanthocladus

Convolvulus acanthocladus, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny calyces that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
