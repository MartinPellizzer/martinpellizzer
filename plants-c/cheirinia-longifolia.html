Cheirinia longifolia

Cheirinia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cheirinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, open habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying shades of pink or white.

Cheirinia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
