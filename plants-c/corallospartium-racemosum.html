Corallospartium racemosum

Corallospartium racemosum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small, pink or white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
