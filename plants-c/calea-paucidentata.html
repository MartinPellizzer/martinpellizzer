Calea paucidentata

Calea paucidentata, commonly known as the Mexican ghost flower, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that resemble ghostly faces, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Calea paucidentata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
