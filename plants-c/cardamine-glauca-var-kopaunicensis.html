Cardamine glauca var. kopaunicensis

Cardamine glauca var. kopaunicensis, commonly known as the Kopaonik watercress, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in its alpine habitat. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky slopes.
