Cineraria gualtata

Cineraria gualtata, commonly known as the Gualtata cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silvery-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its flowers bloom in shades of blue, violet, and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria gualtata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
