Cyperus longiculmis

Cyperus longiculmis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wet or marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread quickly, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus longiculmis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
