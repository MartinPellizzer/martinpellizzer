Crotalaria micheliana

Crotalaria micheliana, commonly known as the Mexican burr medic, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open areas and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and hairy.

Crotalaria micheliana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
