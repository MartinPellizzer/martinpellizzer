Chaetostoma selagineum

Chaetostoma selagineum, commonly known as the golden ghost shrimp, is a species of freshwater shrimp native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, translucent body with a distinctive golden hue that becomes more pronounced under light. This shrimp thrives in slow-moving or still waters with dense vegetation, often hiding among plants. Its diet consists mainly of algae and detritus, making it a beneficial species for aquariums.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful maintenance to ensure optimal health and longevity.
