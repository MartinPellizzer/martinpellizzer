Calliandra angusta

Calliandra angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
