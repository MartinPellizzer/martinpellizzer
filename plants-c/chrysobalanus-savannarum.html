Chrysobalanus savannarum

Chrysobalanus savannarum, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
