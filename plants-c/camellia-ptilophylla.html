Camellia ptilophylla

Camellia ptilophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf camellia, is a species native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia ptilophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
