Cytisus purpureus var. minor

Cytisus purpureus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf purple broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub.
