Crambe orientalis var. sulphurea

Crambe orientalis var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur crampgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds used for oil extraction. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its bright yellow flowers make it a notable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
