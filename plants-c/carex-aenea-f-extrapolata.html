Carex aenea f. extrapolata

Carex aenea f. extrapolata, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a variant of the silver sedge species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that often exhibits a silvery or bluish-gray sheen due to the presence of dense pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
