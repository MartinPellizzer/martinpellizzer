Calamus subspathulatus

Calamus subspathulatus, commonly known as the narrow-sheathed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family plants.

Calamus subspathulatus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
