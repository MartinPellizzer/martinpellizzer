Crataegus dasyphylla

Crataegus dasyphylla, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Crataegus dasyphylla is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
