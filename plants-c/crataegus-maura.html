Crataegus maura

Crataegus maura, commonly known as the Maura hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
