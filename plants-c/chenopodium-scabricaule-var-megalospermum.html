Chenopodium scabricaule var. megalospermum

Chenopodium scabricaule var. megalospermum, commonly known as roughleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional herbal medicine.
