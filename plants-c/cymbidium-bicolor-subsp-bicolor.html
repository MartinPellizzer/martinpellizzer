Cymbidium bicolor subsp. bicolor

Cymbidium bicolor subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the white lady orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
