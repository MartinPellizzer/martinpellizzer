Chamaecytisus banaticus

Chamaecytisus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat buckthorn, is a shrub native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Chamaecytisus banaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in hedges or as a decorative plant in gardens.
