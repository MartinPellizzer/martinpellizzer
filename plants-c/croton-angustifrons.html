Croton angustifrons

Croton angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a distinctive, often serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
