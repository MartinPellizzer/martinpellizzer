Cordia subserrata

Cordia subserrata, commonly known as the serrated cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, and it produces yellowish or reddish fruits that contain several seeds.

Cordia subserrata is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
