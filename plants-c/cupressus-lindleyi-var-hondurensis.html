Cupressus lindleyi var. hondurensis

Cupressus lindleyi var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras lacebark cypress, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely textured foliage and aromatic, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a narrow, columnar form that makes it suitable for ornamental planting. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture that adds to its visual appeal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
