Cassipourea celastroides

Cassipourea celastroides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purplish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassipourea celastroides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
