Cantuffa lacerans

Cantuffa lacerans, commonly known as the jagged leaf plant, is a unique species native to the humid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, serrated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Cantuffa lacerans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage.
