Cyperus mundii var. glaucus

Cyperus mundii var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, blue or purple flower clusters. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its foliage is often covered with a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive silvery-blue appearance.
