Cardiopteris lobata

Cardiopteris lobata, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a compact evergreen fern native to Japan and Korea.

It features a dense, hairy rhizome that supports its rosette-like growth habit. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive shield-shaped appearance, and are typically dark green with a silvery underside. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shady gardens.
