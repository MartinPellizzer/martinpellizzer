Convolvulus macrospermus

Convolvulus macrospermus, commonly known as the large-seeded bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
