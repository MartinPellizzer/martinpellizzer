Chamaecrista olesiphylla

Chamaecrista olesiphylla, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
