Cephaelis peduncularis var. suaveolens

Cephaelis peduncularis var. suaveolens, commonly known as sweetflower cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
