Callistemon rugulosus var. scaber

Callistemon rugulosus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that emerge from a dense cluster of leaves. The leaves are typically narrow, rough to the touch, and have a leathery texture. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
