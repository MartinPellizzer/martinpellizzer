Commelina reticulata

Commelina reticulata, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by three leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture.

Commelina reticulata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
