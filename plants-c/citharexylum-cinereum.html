Citharexylum cinereum

Citharexylum cinereum, commonly known as the silver bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are fragrant, white or pale yellow, and are borne in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
