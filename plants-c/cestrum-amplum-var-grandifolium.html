Cestrum amplum var. grandifolium

Cestrum amplum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the grandifolium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and greenhouses.
