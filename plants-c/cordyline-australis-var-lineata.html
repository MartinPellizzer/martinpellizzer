Cordyline australis var. lineata

Cordyline australis var. lineata, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive white or silver midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
