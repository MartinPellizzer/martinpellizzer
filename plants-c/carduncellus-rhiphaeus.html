Carduncellus rhiphaeus

Carduncellus rhiphaeus, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Carduncellus rhiphaeus is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
