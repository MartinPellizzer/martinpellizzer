Coreopsis gigantea

Coreopsis gigantea, commonly known as the giant coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has feathery, silver-green foliage and can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
