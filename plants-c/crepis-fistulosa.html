Crepis fistulosa

Crepis fistulosa, commonly known as the hollow stem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a distinctive hollow stem that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Crepis fistulosa is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
