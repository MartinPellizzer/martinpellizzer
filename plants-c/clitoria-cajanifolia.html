Clitoria cajanifolia

Clitoria cajanifolia, commonly known as the Indian bean or butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, butterfly-like blue or violet flowers. The plant is a climbing legume that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses.

Clitoria cajanifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
