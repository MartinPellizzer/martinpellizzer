Centaurea gudrunensis

Centaurea gudrunensis, commonly known as Gudrun's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Centaurea gudrunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
