Cytisus teneriffae

Cytisus teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in similar climates.
