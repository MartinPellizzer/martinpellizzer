Coleus angolensis

Coleus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola coleus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, often surrounded by showy, brightly colored bracts that attract pollinators.

Coleus angolensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a garden plant.
