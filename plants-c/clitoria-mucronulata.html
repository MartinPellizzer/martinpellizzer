Clitoria mucronulata

Clitoria mucronulata, commonly known as the spurred orchid or Indian bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers have a distinctive spur at the base, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Clitoria.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
