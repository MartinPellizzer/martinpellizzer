Ceratoschoenus macrophyllus

Ceratoschoenus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant has a distinctive inflorescence with showy, white flowers that open at night. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, while its stems float on the water's surface.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
