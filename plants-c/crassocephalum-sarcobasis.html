Crassocephalum sarcobasis

Crassocephalum sarcobasis, commonly known as the roughhead morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
