Carex sicula

Carex sicula, commonly known as Sicilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex sicula is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
