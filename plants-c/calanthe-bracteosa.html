Calanthe bracteosa

Calanthe bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
