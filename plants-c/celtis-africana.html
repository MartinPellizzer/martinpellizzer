Celtis africana

Celtis africana, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It is often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks, thriving in a variety of soil types.

Celtis africana is valued for its hardy nature and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
