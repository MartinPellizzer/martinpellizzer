Croton puncticulatus

Croton puncticulatus, commonly known as the speckled croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive pattern of white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
