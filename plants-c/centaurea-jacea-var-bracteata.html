Centaurea jacea var. bracteata

Centaurea jacea var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with prominent bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
