Cyclobalanopsis augustinei

Cyclobalanopsis augustinei, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its contribution to the biodiversity of tropical forests.
