Carex limosa f. pauciflora

Carex limosa f. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Carex.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a clumping growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
