Curtia gentianoides

Curtia gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Curtia gentianoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vivid color and ecological significance.
