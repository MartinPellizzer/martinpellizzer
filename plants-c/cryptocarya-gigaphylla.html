Cryptocarya gigaphylla

Cryptocarya gigaphylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Cryptocarya gigaphylla is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
