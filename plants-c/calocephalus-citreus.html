Calocephalus citreus

Calocephalus citreus, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. The plant features strap-shaped, yellowish-green leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
