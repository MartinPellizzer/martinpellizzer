Crataegus champlainensis

Crataegus champlainensis, commonly known as Champlain hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its small, red berries are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

The species is valued for its ecological role and ornamental qualities in landscaping.
