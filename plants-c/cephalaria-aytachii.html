Cephalaria aytachii

Cephalaria aytachii, commonly known as the Aytachii thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
