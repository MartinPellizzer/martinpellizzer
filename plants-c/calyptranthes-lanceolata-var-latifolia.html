Calyptranthes lanceolata var. latifolia

Calyptranthes lanceolata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
