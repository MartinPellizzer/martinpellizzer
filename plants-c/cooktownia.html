Cooktownia

Cooktownia, commonly known as the Cooktown fig, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Cooktownia is known for its edible figs, which are typically small and have a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
