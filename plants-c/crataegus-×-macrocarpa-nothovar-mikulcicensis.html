Crataegus × macrocarpa nothovar. mikulcicensis

Crataegus × macrocarpa nothovar. mikulcicensis, commonly known as the Mikulcice hawthorn, is a hybrid plant characterized by its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It typically features thorny stems and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
