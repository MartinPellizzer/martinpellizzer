Croton elliottii

Croton elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton elliottii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
