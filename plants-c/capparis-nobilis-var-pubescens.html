Capparis nobilis var. pubescens

Capparis nobilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, green berries that mature to a reddish color. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its unique, tangy fruit.
