Cyperus albiflorus

Cyperus albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, pond margins, and seasonally flooded areas.

Cyperus albiflorus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
