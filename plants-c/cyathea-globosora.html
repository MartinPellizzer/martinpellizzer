Cyathea globosora

Cyathea globosora, commonly known as the globosora tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage in tropical rainforests.
