Corpuscularia perdiantha

Corpuscularia perdiantha, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a response to temperature changes. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Corpuscularia perdiantha is also known for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its ecosystem.
