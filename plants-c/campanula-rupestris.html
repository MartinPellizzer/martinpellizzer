Campanula rupestris

Campanula rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are typically opposite and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
