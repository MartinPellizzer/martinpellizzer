Cordia sprucei

Cordia sprucei, commonly known as the red cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
