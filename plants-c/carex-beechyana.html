Carex beechyana

Carex beechyana, commonly known as beechwood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Carex genus within the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive light green color and a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among the foliage, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
