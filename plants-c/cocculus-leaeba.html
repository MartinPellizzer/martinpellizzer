Cocculus leaeba

Cocculus leaeba, commonly known as the white cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces large, berry-like fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
