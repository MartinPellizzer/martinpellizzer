Calliandra brandegeei

Calliandra brandegeei, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is also used in ecological restoration projects.
