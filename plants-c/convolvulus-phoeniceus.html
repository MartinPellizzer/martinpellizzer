Convolvulus phoeniceus

Convolvulus phoeniceus, commonly known as the crimson bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bright red, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Convolvulus phoeniceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
