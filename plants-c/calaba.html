Calaba

Calaba, commonly known as the wild turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often harvested for their edible qualities. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are edible and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Calaba is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial companion plant in agricultural settings.
