Cheirostylis spathulata

Cheirostylis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spatulate lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the lip being the most prominent feature.

Cheirostylis spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
