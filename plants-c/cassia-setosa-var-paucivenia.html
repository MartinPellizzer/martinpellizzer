Cassia setosa var. paucivenia

Cassia setosa var. paucivenia, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodland or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
