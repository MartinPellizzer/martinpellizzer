Cynanchum samuelssonii

Cynanchum samuelssonii, commonly known as Samuelsson's milkweed, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is typical of the genus Cynanchum. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
