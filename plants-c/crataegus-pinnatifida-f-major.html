Crataegus pinnatifida f. major

Crataegus pinnatifida f. major, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible, red berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, with a serrated edge, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
