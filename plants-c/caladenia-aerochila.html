Caladenia aerochila

Caladenia aerochila, commonly known as the sky rocket orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in areas with low rainfall. Caladenia aerochila is known for its unique floral structure, with a single flower per stem that resembles a rocket due to its elongated shape.

It is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
