Centaurea aplolepa subsp. levantina

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

levantina, commonly known as the Levant centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
