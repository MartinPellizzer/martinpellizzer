Cowellocassia scleroxyla

Cowellocassia scleroxyla, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ecological role in soil stabilization.

The tree also has traditional medicinal uses in various African communities.
