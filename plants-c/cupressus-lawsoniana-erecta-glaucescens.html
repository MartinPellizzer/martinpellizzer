Cupressus lawsoniana erecta-glaucescens

Cupressus lawsoniana erecta-glaucescens, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a variant of the Lawson's cypress, a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dense foliage, and bluish-green needles that give it a striking appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches, and it produces small, spherical cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
