Cleidion coriaceum

Cleidion coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is often deeply fissured and has a distinctive grayish-white color.

Cleidion coriaceum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
