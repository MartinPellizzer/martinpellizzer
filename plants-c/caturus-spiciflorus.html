Caturus spiciflorus

Caturus spiciflorus, commonly known as the spiky-flowered caturus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
