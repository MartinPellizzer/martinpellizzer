Coix lacryma-jobi var. maxima

Coix lacryma-jobi var. maxima, commonly known as Job's tears or Chinese pearl millet, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, teardrop-shaped caryopsis that resembles small pearls. The plant produces tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its seeds are rich in starch and have been used in traditional medicine and as a food source for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in various parts of the world for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
