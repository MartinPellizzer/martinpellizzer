Calamagrostis villosa var. gracilescens

Calamagrostis villosa var. gracilescens, commonly known as silky bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its fine texture and color make it a popular choice for naturalistic landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
