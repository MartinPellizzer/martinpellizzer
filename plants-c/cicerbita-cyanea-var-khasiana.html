Cicerbita cyanea var. khasiana

Cicerbita cyanea var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit with a warty surface. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is used in traditional cuisines and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

The leaves are ovate and hairy, contributing to its overall ornamental and ecological significance.
