Ceropegia ensifolia

Ceropegia ensifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf ceropegia, is a climbing or trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, fragrant flower that resembles a small, elongated tube. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
