Carex hordeistichos var. secalina

Carex hordeistichos var. secalina, commonly known as the yellow sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green, and the flowering structures often display a yellowish or brownish hue.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
