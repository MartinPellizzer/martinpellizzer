Cyperus insularis

Cyperus insularis, commonly known as the marsh flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. Cyperus insularis is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
