Carex supina f. elatior

Carex supina f. elatior, commonly known as the upright sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikelets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
