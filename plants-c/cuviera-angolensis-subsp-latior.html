Cuviera angolensis subsp. latior

Cuviera angolensis subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved cuviera, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid to humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
