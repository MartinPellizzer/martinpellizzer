Cordia oliveri

Cordia oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.
