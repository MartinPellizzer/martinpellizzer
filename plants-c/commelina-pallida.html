Commelina pallida

Commelina pallida, commonly known as the pale commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive floral structures. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers with three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its growth habit is usually rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.
