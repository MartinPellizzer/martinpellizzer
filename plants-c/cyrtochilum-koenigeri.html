Cyrtochilum koenigeri

Cyrtochilum koenigeri, commonly known as the Koeniger's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved flower spike, which bears a single, showy orchid flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color, are adapted for specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees.

Cyrtochilum koenigeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
