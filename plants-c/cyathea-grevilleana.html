Cyathea grevilleana

Cyathea grevilleana, commonly known as the Greville's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that produce spores, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea grevilleana is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
