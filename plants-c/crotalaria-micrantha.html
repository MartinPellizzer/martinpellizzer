Crotalaria micrantha

Crotalaria micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a distinctive appearance. Crotalaria micrantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
