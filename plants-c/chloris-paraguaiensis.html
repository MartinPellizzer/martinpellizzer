Chloris paraguaiensis

Chloris paraguaiensis, commonly known as paraguayan grass or yerba mate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fiber.

The plant is also cultivated for its seeds, which are used in various local food products.
