Cucumis laciniosus

Cucumis laciniosus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is edible, though often used decoratively rather than for consumption.
