Cirsium salvadoris

Cirsium salvadoris, commonly known as the Salvador's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical features include a thick, woody stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Cirsium salvadoris is part of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
