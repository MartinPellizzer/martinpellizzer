Carex acuminata

Carex acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are grass-like, with a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex acuminata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
