Calamus pogonacanthus

Calamus pogonacanthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araucariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
