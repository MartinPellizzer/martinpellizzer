Chaetopappa asteroides var. asteroides

Chaetopappa asteroides var. asteroides, commonly known as the star daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid landscapes.
