Cinnamomum fouilloyi

Cinnamomum fouilloyi, commonly known as the Fouilloy's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
