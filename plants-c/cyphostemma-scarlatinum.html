Cyphostemma scarlatinum

Cyphostemma scarlatinum, commonly known as the red-stemmed moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
