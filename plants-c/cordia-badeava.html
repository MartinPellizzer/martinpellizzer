Cordia badeava

Cordia badeava, commonly known as the red cedar or false cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often forming dense thickets.

Cordia badeava is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
