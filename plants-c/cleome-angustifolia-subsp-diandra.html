Cleome angustifolia subsp. diandra

Cleome angustifolia subsp.

diandra, commonly known as narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose, branched inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Cleome angustifolia subsp.

diandra is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
