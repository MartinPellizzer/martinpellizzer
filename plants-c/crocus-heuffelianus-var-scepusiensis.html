Crocus heuffelianus var. scepusiensis

Crocus heuffelianus var. scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and a yellow center, typically appearing in March or April. The plant grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible bulbs that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
