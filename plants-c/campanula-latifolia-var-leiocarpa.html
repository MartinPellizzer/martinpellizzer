Campanula latifolia var. leiocarpa

Campanula latifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and the plant typically thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
