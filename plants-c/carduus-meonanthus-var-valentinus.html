Carduus meonanthus var. valentinus

Carduus meonanthus var. valentinus, commonly known as Valentine's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
