Centaurea nigerica

Centaurea nigerica, commonly known as the black-centred centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its dark purple to black flowers are surrounded by pale blue or white ray florets, creating a striking visual contrast.

Centaurea nigerica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
