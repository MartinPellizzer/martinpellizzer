Carpinus betulus f. purpurea

Carpinus betulus f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed hornbeam, is a unique cultivar of the common hornbeam.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which turns reddish in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are small, oval, and have a finely toothed margin, typical of the genus Carpinus. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

This form is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
