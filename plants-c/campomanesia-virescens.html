Campomanesia virescens

Campomanesia virescens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and fruit yield make it a popular choice for cultivation in warm climates.
