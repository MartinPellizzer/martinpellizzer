Clematis denticulata

Clematis denticulata, commonly known as the toothed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late summer. The plant has a climbing habit and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis denticulata is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
