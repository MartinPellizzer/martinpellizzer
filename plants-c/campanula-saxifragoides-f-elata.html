Campanula saxifragoides f. elata

Campanula saxifragoides f. elata, commonly known as the tall rock campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
