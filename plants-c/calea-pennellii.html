Calea pennellii

Calea pennellii, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a striking flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, candle-like inflorescence composed of bright yellow tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often dark green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, aromatic leaves that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Calea pennellii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance.
