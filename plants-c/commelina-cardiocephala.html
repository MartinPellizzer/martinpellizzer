Commelina cardiocephala

Commelina cardiocephala, commonly known as the heartleaf commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae, which includes a variety of grass-like and herbaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina cardiocephala is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
