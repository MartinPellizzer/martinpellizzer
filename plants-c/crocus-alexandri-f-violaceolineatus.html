Crocus alexandri f. violaceolineatus

Crocus alexandri f. violaceolineatus, commonly known as the violet-veined crocus, is a rare and striking variant of the Alexandrian crocus.

It is characterized by its deep purple petals with distinctive violet veins, which set it apart from other crocus species. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy, cup-shaped flowers that are highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
