Cylindropuntia acanthocarpa var. major

Cylindropuntia acanthocarpa var. major, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is notable for its size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in xeriscaped gardens.
