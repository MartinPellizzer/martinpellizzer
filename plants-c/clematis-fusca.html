Clematis fusca

Clematis fusca, commonly known as the western clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically small, white or pale blue, with five petals. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits called follicles that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Clematis fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
