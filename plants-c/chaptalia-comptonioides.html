Chaptalia comptonioides

Chaptalia comptonioides, commonly known as the Compton's chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Chaptalia comptonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
