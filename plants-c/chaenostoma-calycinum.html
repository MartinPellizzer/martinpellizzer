Chaenostoma calycinum

Chaenostoma calycinum, commonly known as the bluebell of the rock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
