Christella subarida

Christella subarida, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture.

Christella subarida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
