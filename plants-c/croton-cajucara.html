Croton cajucara

Croton cajucara, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a tropical appearance. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

Croton cajucara is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant foliage and unique growth habit.
