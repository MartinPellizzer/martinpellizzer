Cerasus fruticosa f. acuta

Cerasus fruticosa f. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features ovate to lanceolate leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
