Campanula hondoensis

Campanula hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hondo in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula hondoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
