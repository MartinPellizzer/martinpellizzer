Croton subdioecus

Croton subdioecus, commonly known as the male and female flowering croton, is a dioecious plant, meaning that individual plants are either male or female.

It is native to tropical Africa and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant is characterized by its colorful, showy bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and vary in shape, while its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Croton subdioecus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral displays.
