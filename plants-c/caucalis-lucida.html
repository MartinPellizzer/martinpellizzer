Caucalis lucida

Caucalis lucida, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside.

Caucalis lucida is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
