Cyclopogon pedicellatus

Cyclopogon pedicellatus, commonly known as the slender grass-panicle, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stature and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Cyclopogon pedicellatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
