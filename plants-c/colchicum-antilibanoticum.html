Colchicum antilibanoticum

Colchicum antilibanoticum, commonly known as the Cyprus colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, large, showy flower per stem, which is surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It has a corm-like underground structure that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
