Comarostaphylis mucronifera

Comarostaphylis mucronifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.
