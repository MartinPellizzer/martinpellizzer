Chaetaria bipartita

Chaetaria bipartita, commonly known as the two-part hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as wet meadows and marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
