Cleisostoma cryptochilum

Cleisostoma cryptochilum, commonly known as the hidden-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers have a unique structure with a hooded lip.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
