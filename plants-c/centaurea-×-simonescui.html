Centaurea × simonescui

Centaurea × simonescui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright blue to purple composite flowers that resemble those of its parent species, Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea stoebe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and has naturalized in various regions across Europe.
