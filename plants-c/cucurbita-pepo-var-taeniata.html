Cucurbita pepo var. taeniata

Cucurbita pepo var. taeniata, commonly known as the bird's beak gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow fruits that resemble a bird's beak, typically measuring up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
