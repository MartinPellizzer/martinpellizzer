Celtis sinensis var. japonica

Celtis sinensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
