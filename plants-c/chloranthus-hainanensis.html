Chloranthus hainanensis

Chloranthus hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of southern China, particularly Hainan Island. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
