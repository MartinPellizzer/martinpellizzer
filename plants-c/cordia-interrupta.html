Cordia interrupta

Cordia interrupta, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cordia interrupta produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or animals.
