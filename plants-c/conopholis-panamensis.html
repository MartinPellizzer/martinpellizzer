Conopholis panamensis

Conopholis panamensis, commonly known as the Panama bottlebrush, is a unique flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients. The plant produces small, red or orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it a bottlebrush-like appearance. Its leaves are small and scale-like, and it typically grows in shaded, moist environments.

Conopholis panamensis plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to forest biodiversity.
