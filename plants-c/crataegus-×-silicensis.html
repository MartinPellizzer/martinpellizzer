Crataegus × silicensis

Crataegus × silicensis, commonly known as the silvery hawthorn, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a red or orange-pink berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
