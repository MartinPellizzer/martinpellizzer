Cissus subtruncata

Cissus subtruncata, commonly known as the African Stonecrop, is a climbing or trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, star-shaped glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by reddish, fleshy berries. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
