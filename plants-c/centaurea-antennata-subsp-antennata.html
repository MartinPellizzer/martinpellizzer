Centaurea antennata subsp. antennata

Centaurea antennata subsp.

antennata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with long, slender bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
