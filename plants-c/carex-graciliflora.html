Carex graciliflora

Carex graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
