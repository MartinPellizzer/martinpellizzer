Cinnamomum yabunikkei f. pilosum

Cinnamomum yabunikkei f. pilosum, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon or Yabunikkei cinnamon, is a rare and unique plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, with a distinct cinnamon-like scent when crushed.
