Ceratocaryum argenteum

Ceratocaryum argenteum, commonly known as silver panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
