Carum platycarpum

Carum platycarpum, commonly known as flat-seeded caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

The fruit, or schizocarp, is distinctive for its flat, broad shape and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
