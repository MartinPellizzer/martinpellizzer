Carpodinus uniflora

Carpodinus uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces a single, inconspicuous flower at the tip of each stem, which is surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.
