Clerodendrum laxiflorum

Clerodendrum laxiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow throats. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
