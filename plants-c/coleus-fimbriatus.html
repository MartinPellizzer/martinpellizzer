Coleus fimbriatus

Coleus fimbriatus, commonly known as the flame bamboo or painted nettle, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus fimbriatus is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
