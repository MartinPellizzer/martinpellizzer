Coreopsis leavenworthii var. typica

Coreopsis leavenworthii var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
