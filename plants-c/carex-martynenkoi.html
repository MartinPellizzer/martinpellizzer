Carex martynenkoi

Carex martynenkoi, commonly known as Martynenko's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Eastern Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Carex martynenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
