Caragana sajaensis

Caragana sajaensis, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

Caragana sajaensis is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
