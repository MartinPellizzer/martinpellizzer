Carex remota subsp. alta

Carex remota subsp.

alta, commonly known as the high alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for harsh, high-altitude environments.
