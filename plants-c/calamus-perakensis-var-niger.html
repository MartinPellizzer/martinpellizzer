Calamus perakensis var. niger

Calamus perakensis var. niger, commonly known as black calamus, is a variant of the calamus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black-colored rhizomes, which are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several feet in length, and are used in various cultural practices.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
