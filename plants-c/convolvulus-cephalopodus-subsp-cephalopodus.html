Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp. cephalopodus

Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp.

cephalopodus, commonly known as the head-shaped bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain environments.
