Camellia nervosa

Camellia nervosa, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a prominent central vein. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in winter and early spring. Camellia nervosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and is also valued for its resilience to cold temperatures.
