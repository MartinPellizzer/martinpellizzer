Convolvulus coelesyriacus

Convolvulus coelesyriacus, commonly known as the Arabian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a sprawling annual vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory-like plants.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily in disturbed environments.
