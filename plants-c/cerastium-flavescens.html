Cerastium flavescens

Cerastium flavescens, commonly known as yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often hairy, adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Cerastium flavescens is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
