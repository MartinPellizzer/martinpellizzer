Calamagrostis minarovii

Calamagrostis minarovii, commonly known as the Minarovii reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

Calamagrostis minarovii is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
