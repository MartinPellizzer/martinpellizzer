Citrus × aurantiifolia

Citrus × aurantiifolia, commonly known as the acerola or yellow lime, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, smooth skin and a high concentration of vitamin C. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and the flowers are fragrant, white, and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches full size and ripens to a vibrant yellow, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
