Conyza × royana

Conyza × royana, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It is known for its rapid growth and seed dispersal, which contributes to its invasive potential in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
