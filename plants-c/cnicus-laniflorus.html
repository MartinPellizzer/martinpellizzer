Cnicus laniflorus

Cnicus laniflorus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Cnicus laniflorus is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
