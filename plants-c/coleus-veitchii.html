Coleus veitchii

Coleus veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple, often with contrasting veins. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and flowering plants. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Coleus veitchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
