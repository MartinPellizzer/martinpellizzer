Cajanus cajan

Cajanus cajan, commonly known as pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. Cajanus cajan is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
