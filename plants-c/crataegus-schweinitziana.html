Crataegus schweinitziana

Crataegus schweinitziana, commonly known as Schweinitz's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.
