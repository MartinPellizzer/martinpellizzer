Clerodendrum nudiflorum var. puberulentum

Clerodendrum nudiflorum var. puberulentum, commonly known as the velvet clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Clerodendrum genus, which are known for their medicinal and horticultural significance.
