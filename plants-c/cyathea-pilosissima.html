Cyathea pilosissima

Cyathea pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of fibrous roots and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance.

Cyathea pilosissima plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
