Cienfuegosia phlomidifolia

Cienfuegosia phlomidifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. Cienfuegosia phlomidifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
