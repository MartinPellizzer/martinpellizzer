Campanula rotundifolia var. glabra

Campanula rotundifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, broad leaves that are typically smooth to the touch, giving it its varietal name "glabra.". The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
