Caladium indicum

Caladium indicum, commonly known as the Indian shield leaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it popular in gardens and as a houseplant.

Caladium indicum is typically propagated through tuberous roots or division of the rhizomes.
