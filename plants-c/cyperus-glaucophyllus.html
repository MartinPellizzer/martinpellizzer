Cyperus glaucophyllus

Cyperus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus glaucophyllus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
