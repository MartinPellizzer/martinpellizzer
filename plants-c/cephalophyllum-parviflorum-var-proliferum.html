Cephalophyllum parviflorum var. proliferum

Cephalophyllum parviflorum var. proliferum, commonly known as the clustered crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
