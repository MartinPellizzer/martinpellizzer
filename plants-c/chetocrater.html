Chetocrater

Chetocrater, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. Chetocrater thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
