Cyperus clarkeanus

Cyperus clarkeanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ditches, and flooded meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus clarkeanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
