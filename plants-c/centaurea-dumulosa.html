Centaurea dumulosa

Centaurea dumulosa, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant has small, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Centaurea dumulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
