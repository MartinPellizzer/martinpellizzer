Carex glauca var. thuringiaca

Carex glauca var. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its blue-green foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, moisture-loving plants.
