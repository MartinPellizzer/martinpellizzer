Chlora hybrida

Chlora hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid chlorophyte, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms.

It belongs to the family Chlorophyceae and is known for its unique chloroplast structure that supports efficient photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Chlora hybrida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
