Carex xiphium

Carex xiphium, commonly known as the sword-leaved sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a sharp, pointed tip. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex xiphium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
