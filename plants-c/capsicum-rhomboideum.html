Capsicum rhomboideum

Capsicum rhomboideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili or wild chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and oval-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is usually a small, elongated chili pepper that can vary in color from green to red depending on maturity.
