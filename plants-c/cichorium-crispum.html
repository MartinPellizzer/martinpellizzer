Cichorium crispum

Cichorium crispum, commonly known as curly endive, is a leafy plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its curly, frilled leaves that range in color from dark green to pale yellow. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often grown for its edible leaves. It prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

The leaves can be used raw in salads or cooked, offering a slightly bitter and nutty flavor.
