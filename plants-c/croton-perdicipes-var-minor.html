Croton perdicipes var. minor

Croton perdicipes var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with vibrant green or variegated patterns, often adorned with red or orange veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
