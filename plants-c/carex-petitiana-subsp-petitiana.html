Carex petitiana subsp. petitiana

Carex petitiana subsp.

petitiana, commonly known as the Petit's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Carex petitiana subsp.

petitiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
