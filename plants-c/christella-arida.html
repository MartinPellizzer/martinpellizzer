Christella arida

Christella arida, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, shrub-like species characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Christella arida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
