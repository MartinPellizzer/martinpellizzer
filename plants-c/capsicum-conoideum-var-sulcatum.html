Capsicum conoideum var. sulcatum

Capsicum conoideum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the horned pepper or horned chili, is a unique variant of the capsicum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fruit shape, which resembles a horn. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by fruits that can range in color from green to red as they mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to spicy flavor, making it popular in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
