Coleus bishopianus

Coleus bishopianus, commonly known as the Bishop's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
