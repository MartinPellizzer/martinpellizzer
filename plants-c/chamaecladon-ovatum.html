Chamaecladon ovatum

Chamaecladon ovatum, commonly known as the ovalleaf chamaecladon, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
