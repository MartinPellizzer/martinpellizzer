Campanula dersimensis

Campanula dersimensis, commonly known as the Dersim bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Eastern Anatolia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and upright.

Campanula dersimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
