Carex lyngbyei subsp. phaenocarpa

Carex lyngbyei subsp.

phaenocarpa, commonly known as the Lyngby's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
