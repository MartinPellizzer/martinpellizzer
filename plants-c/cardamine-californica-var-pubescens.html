Cardamine californica var. pubescens

Cardamine californica var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent watercress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its small, white flowers are borne in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It has a delicate, peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
