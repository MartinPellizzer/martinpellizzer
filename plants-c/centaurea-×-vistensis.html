Centaurea × vistensis

Centaurea × vistensis, commonly known as the Vistula centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact inflorescences.

Centaurea × vistensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
