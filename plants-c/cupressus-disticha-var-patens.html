Cupressus disticha var. patens

Cupressus disticha var. patens, commonly known as the spreading lacey cypress, is a variant of the lacey cypress characterized by its unique, spreading growth habit.

It features scale-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is closely related to other cypress species.
