Cacalia yatabei var. occidentalis

Cacalia yatabei var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western yatabe's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coastal regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in coastal dunes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
