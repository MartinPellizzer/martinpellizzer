Cyperus oostachyus

Cyperus oostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
