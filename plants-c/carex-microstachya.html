Carex microstachya

Carex microstachya, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex microstachya is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
