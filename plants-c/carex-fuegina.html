Carex fuegina

Carex fuegina, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex fuegina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
