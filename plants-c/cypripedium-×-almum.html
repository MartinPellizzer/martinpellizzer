Cypripedium × almum

Cypripedium × almum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a captivating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium × mackayi, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white with a contrasting lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and protected in the wild.
