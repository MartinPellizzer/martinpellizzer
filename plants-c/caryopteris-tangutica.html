Caryopteris tangutica

Caryopteris tangutica, commonly known as the Chinese bluemouth, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Caryopteris tangutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
