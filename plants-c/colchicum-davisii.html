Colchicum davisii

Colchicum davisii, commonly known as Davis' colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of waxy, purple to reddish-purple flowers. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often remain green throughout the growing season.

Davis' colchicum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
