Crotalaria duvigneaudii

Crotalaria duvigneaudii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grassland habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in supporting pollinators and improving soil fertility.
