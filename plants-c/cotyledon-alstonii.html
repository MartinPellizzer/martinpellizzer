Cotyledon alstonii

Cotyledon alstonii, commonly known as the elephant's ear, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or wavy, with a distinctive pale green to blue-green coloration. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
