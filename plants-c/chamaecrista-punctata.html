Chamaecrista punctata

Chamaecrista punctata, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that are often tinged with red, and its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Chamaecrista punctata is known for its rapid growth and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its widespread distribution.
