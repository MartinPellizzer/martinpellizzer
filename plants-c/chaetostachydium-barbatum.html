Chaetostachydium barbatum

Chaetostachydium barbatum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems that are often covered with fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.
