Capparis mucronifolia subsp. rosanowiana

Capparis mucronifolia subsp.

rosanowiana, commonly known as the Rosanow's caper, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing seeds, and its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to the arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
