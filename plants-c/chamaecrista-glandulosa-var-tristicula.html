Chamaecrista glandulosa var. tristicula

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. tristicula, commonly known as the three-lobed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
