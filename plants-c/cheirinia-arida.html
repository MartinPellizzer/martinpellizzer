Cheirinia arida

Cheirinia arida, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cheirinia arida is often found in rocky soils and is considered an important component of local desert ecosystems.
