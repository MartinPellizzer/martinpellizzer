Clusia huberi

Clusia huberi, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Clusia huberi is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
