Carum panul

Carum panul, commonly known as the Persian cumin or Persian coriander, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, umbrella-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a sharp, peppery aroma, similar to cumin.

The seeds, which are harvested after flowering, are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.
