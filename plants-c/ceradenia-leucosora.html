Ceradenia leucosora

Ceradenia leucosora, commonly known as the white ceradenia, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically dark green and velvety, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Ceradenia leucosora is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
