Coix lacryma-jobi f. aquatica

Coix lacryma-jobi f. aquatica, commonly known as water job's tears, is a variant of the Coix genus native to Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, translucent, tear-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically floating on the water surface or submerged.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
