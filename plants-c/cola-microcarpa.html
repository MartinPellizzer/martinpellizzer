Cola microcarpa

Cola microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sterculiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces small, dark purple berries. The fruit contains seeds that are used in traditional medicine and have a mild stimulant effect.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
