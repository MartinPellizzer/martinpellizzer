Crotalaria glauca

Crotalaria glauca, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria glauca is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
