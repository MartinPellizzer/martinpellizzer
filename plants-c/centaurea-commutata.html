Centaurea commutata

Centaurea commutata, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble miniature globes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Centaurea commutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
