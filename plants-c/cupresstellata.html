Cupresstellata

Cupresstellata, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and striking blue-gray foliage. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in clusters of three, giving the tree its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
