Cirsium acantholepis

Cirsium acantholepis, commonly known as the prickly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Cirsium acantholepis typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
