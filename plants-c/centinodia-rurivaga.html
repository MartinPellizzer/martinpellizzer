Centinodia rurivaga

Centinodia rurivaga, commonly known as the wandering centinodia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-like arrangement of leaves, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Despite its ornamental appeal, Centinodia rurivaga is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
