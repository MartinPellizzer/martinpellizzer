Conophytum corculum

Conophytum corculum, commonly known as the "Crowned Stone" or "Coral Stone," is a member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowers, typically yellow or orange, are small and borne in clusters, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage.

Conophytum corculum is well adapted to arid environments and is often found growing in rocky outcrops or sandy soils.
