Cherleria laricifolia subsp. diomedis

Cherleria laricifolia subsp.

diomedis, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments. It features a pale gray to white thallus with a distinctive, leaf-like structure.

The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and cyanobacteria, which contribute to its photosynthetic capabilities. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and specialized habitat make it an indicator species for environmental health.
