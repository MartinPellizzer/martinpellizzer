Campylocentrum serpentilingua

Campylocentrum serpentilingua, commonly known as the serpent's tongue plant, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, serpentine-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically display vibrant shades of red or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
