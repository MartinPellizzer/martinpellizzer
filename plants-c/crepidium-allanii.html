Crepidium allanii

Crepidium allanii, commonly known as the Allan's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale green in color. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
