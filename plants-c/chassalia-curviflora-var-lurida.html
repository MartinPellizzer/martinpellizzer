Chassalia curviflora var. lurida

Chassalia curviflora var. lurida, commonly known as the hairy chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
