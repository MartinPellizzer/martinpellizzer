Callitris intratropica

Callitris intratropica, commonly known as the tropical cypress, is a member of the Cupressaceae family and is native to northern Australia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed on the shoots, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy plant in its native environment.
