Cistus alyssoides var. rugosum

Cistus alyssoides var. rugosum, commonly known as roughleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, grey-green leaves with a rough texture, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
