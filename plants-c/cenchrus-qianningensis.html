Cenchrus qianningensis

Cenchrus qianningensis, commonly known as the Qianning grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often hairy and contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
