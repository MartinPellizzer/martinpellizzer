Capsella patagonica

Capsella patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, hairy, and lobed. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The fruit is a dry, segmented pod that releases seeds when mature.
