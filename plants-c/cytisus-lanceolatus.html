Cytisus lanceolatus

Cytisus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
