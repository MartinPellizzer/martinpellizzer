Cerastium vulgatum f. vulgare

Cerastium vulgatum f. vulgare, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
