Cyrtochilum pastorellii

Cyrtochilum pastorellii, commonly known as the Pastorelli's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blooms with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. Cyrtochilum pastorellii prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
