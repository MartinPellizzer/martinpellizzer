Coccinia diversifolia var. glabrescens

Coccinia diversifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf false cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a distinct veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
