Cytisus austriacus subsp. virescens

Cytisus austriacus subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
