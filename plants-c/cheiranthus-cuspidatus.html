Cheiranthus cuspidatus

Cheiranthus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
