Crocus uschakensis

Crocus uschakensis, commonly known as the Uschakovsky crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or forested areas. It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Iris and Gladiolus.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
