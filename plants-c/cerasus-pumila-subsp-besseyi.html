Cerasus pumila subsp. besseyi

Cerasus pumila subsp.

besseyi, commonly known as the Bessey cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, red or black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring.

The Bessey cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
