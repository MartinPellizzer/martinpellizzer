Celtis sumatrana

Celtis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, pale drupes. Celtis sumatrana is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
