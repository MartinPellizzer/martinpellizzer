Cnemidaria suprastrigosa

Cnemidaria suprastrigosa, commonly known as the slender leaf blightgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its low-growing habit and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
