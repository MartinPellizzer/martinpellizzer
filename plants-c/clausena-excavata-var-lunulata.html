Clausena excavata var. lunulata

Clausena excavata var. lunulata, commonly known as the moon-leafed klipspringer, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with a waxy coating and a unique, crescent-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-red fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
