Carex schlechteri

Carex schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
