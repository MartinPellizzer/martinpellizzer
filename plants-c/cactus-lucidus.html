Cactus lucidus

Cactus lucidus, commonly known as the Clear Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy surface and vibrant green color, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the dry season, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its stems are adapted to store water efficiently, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Despite its resilience, it requires specific soil and temperature conditions to thrive, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
