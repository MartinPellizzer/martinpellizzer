Cassia goldmanii

Cassia goldmanii, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-filled pods.

Cassia goldmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
