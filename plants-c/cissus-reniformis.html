Cissus reniformis

Cissus reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Cissus reniformis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
