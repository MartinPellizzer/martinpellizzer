Campium fluviatile

Campium fluviatile, commonly known as the river marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshy areas along riverbanks and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, usually blooming in the summer months. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Campium fluviatile is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
