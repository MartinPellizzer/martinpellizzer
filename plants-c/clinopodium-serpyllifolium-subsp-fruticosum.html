Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp. fruticosum

Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp.

fruticosum, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces tiny pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems, and the plant has a spreading growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
