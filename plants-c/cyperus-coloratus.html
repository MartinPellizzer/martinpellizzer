Cyperus coloratus

Cyperus coloratus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along waterways.

Cyperus coloratus is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
