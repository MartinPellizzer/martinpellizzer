Campanula steveniana

Campanula steveniana, commonly known as the Steven's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula steveniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
