Campylotropis macrocarpa var. giraldii

Campylotropis macrocarpa var. giraldii, commonly known as the Giant Bitterroot, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces edible, tuberous roots that are historically used by indigenous peoples. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
