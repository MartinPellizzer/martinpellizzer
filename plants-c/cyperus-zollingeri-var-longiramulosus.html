Cyperus zollingeri var. longiramulosus

Cyperus zollingeri var. longiramulosus, commonly known as the long-branched flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, branching inflorescences, giving it a unique appearance among sedges.
