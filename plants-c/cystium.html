Cystium

Cystium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Cystium species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and hardy nature.
