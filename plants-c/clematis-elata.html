Clematis elata

Clematis elata, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental vine.

Clematis elata is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences effectively.
