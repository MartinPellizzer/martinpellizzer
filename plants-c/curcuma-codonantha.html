Curcuma codonantha

Curcuma codonantha, commonly known as the white turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, tuberous rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The rhizomes are harvested for their medicinal properties and are used in various herbal remedies.
