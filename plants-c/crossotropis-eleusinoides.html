Crossotropis eleusinoides

Crossotropis eleusinoides, commonly known as the African grass or elephant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, fibrous leaves and can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

It is often used for forage, erosion control, and in traditional medicine.
