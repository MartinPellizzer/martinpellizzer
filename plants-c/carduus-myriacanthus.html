Carduus myriacanthus

Carduus myriacanthus, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a robust perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, upright stem covered in stiff, spiny leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive rough texture, while its flowers are surrounded by a dense layer of bracts.

Carduus myriacanthus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
