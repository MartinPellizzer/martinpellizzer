Crotalaria nayaritensis

Crotalaria nayaritensis, commonly known as the Nayarit cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, tropical environments and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces pods that contain seeds used for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
