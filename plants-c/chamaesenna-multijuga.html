Chamaesenna multijuga

Chamaesenna multijuga, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
