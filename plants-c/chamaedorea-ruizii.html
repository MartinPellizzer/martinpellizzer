Chamaedorea ruizii

Chamaedorea ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and prefers bright, indirect light. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in moderate humidity.

Chamaedorea ruizii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
