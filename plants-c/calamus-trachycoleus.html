Calamus trachycoleus

Calamus trachycoleus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Calamus trachycoleus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
