Cassebeera inframarginalis

Cassebeera inframarginalis, commonly known as the Marginal Leaf Cabbage Tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to several feet in diameter, with a waxy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
