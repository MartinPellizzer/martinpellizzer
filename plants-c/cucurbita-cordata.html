Cucurbita cordata

Cucurbita cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they reach a mature size. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cucurbita cordata is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
