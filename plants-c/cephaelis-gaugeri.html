Cephaelis gaugeri

Cephaelis gaugeri, commonly known as the pink flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cephaelis gaugeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
