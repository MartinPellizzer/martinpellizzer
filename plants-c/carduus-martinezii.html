Carduus martinezii

Carduus martinezii, commonly known as Martinez thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, achene fruits.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.
