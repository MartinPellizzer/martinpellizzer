Chusua takasago-montana

Chusua takasago-montana, commonly known as Japanese mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes, exhibiting a compact, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters of purple or pinkish-white petals.

Chusua takasago-montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal display.
