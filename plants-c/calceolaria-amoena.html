Calceolaria amoena

Calceolaria amoena, commonly known as the "pretty hooded orchid," is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped petals that resemble small cups. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting darker veins. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calceolaria amoena is a herbaceous perennial that prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.
