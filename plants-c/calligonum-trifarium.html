Calligonum trifarium

Calligonum trifarium, commonly known as the three-leaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert steppe and coastal dunes.

Calligonum trifarium plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
