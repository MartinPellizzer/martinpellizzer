Chamaecyparis boursieri var. erecta

Chamaecyparis boursieri var. erecta, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress, is a variety of the genus Chamaecyparis native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dense foliage. The plant features scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
