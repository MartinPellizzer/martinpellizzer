Cissus hibiscina

Cissus hibiscina, commonly known as the coral vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cissus hibiscina is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
