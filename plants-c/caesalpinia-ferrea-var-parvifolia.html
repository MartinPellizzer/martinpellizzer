Caesalpinia ferrea var. parvifolia

Caesalpinia ferrea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the parvifolia ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
