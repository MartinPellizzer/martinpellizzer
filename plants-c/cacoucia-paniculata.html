Cacoucia paniculata

Cacoucia paniculata, commonly known as the African turnip, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain round, white seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a valuable crop in arid environments.
