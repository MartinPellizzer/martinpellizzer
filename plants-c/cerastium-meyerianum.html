Cerastium meyerianum

Cerastium meyerianum, commonly known as meyer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium meyerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
