Chadsia grandidieri

Chadsia grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble stars, with a central white or pale yellow spathulate lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in the orchid world.
