Cerastium lanigerum subsp. nikolovii

Cerastium lanigerum subsp.

nikolovii, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are hairy and opposite, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
