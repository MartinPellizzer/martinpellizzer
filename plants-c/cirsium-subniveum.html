Cirsium subniveum

Cirsium subniveum, commonly known as the snowdrop thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture and reflect sunlight. Its flowers are purple to violet in color, with a distinctive tubular shape and a pale yellow throat.

Cirsium subniveum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
