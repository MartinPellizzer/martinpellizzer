Coryphantha vorwerkii

Coryphantha vorwerkii, commonly known as the Vorwerk's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a fleshy pear-shaped structure, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

Coryphantha vorwerkii is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
