Carex albowii

Carex albowii, commonly known as the albow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex albowii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
