Carex sikkimensis

Carex sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
