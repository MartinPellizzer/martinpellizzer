Crataegus orientobaltica f. glabra

Crataegus orientobaltica f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus characterized by its smooth, glabrous fruits.

It is native to parts of Eastern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
