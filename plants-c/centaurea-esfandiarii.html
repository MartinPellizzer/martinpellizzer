Centaurea esfandiarii

Centaurea esfandiarii, commonly known as the Esfandiari's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea esfandiarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
