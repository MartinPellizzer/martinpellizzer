Cheiloclinium neglectum

Cheiloclinium neglectum, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Cheiloclinium neglectum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils.
