Coursetia astragalina

Coursetia astragalina, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, drought-tolerant shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss.

Coursetia astragalina plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
