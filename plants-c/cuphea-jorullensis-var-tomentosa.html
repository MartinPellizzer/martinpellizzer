Cuphea jorullensis var. tomentosa

Cuphea jorullensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
