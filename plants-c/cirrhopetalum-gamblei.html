Cirrhopetalum gamblei

Cirrhopetalum gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's bell, is a rare and striking orchid native to the highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a deep purple or violet throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cirrhopetalum gamblei is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
