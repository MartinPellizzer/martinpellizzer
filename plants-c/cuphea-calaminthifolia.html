Cuphea calaminthifolia

Cuphea calaminthifolia, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a flowering species native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small honeycombs, often in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight hairy texture.

Cuphea calaminthifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
