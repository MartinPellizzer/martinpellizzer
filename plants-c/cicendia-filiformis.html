Cicendia filiformis

Cicendia filiformis, commonly known as the slender cicendia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its linear, grass-like leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Cicendia filiformis is known for its resilience to drought and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
