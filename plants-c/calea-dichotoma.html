Calea dichotoma

Calea dichotoma, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a distinctive, branched stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Calea dichotoma is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
