Chrysanthemum tingitanum

Chrysanthemum tingitanum, commonly known as the Moroccan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chrysanthemum tingitanum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
