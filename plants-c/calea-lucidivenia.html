Calea lucidivenia

Calea lucidivenia, commonly known as the "mirror plant," is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, petal-like bracts that resemble a mirror, giving it its common name. The plant features a central flowering stem with showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Calea lucidivenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
