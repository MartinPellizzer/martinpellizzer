Carduus defloratus subsp. sumanus

Carduus defloratus subsp.

sumanus, commonly known as the Sumatran thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with coarse, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
