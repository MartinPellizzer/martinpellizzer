Croton pseudoadipatus

Croton pseudoadipatus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that display a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
