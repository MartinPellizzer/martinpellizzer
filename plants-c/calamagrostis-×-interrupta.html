Calamagrostis × interrupta

Calamagrostis × interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically silver-green to golden in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
