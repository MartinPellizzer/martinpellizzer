Cerastium viscosum var. alsinoides

Cerastium viscosum var. alsinoides, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with numerous stems that spread along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in a sticky, glandular coating.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of streams.
