Cordia tacarcunensis

Cordia tacarcunensis, commonly known as the Tacaragua cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and arid conditions.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal properties.
