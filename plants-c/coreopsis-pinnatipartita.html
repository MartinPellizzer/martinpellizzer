Coreopsis pinnatipartita

Coreopsis pinnatipartita, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant collections.
