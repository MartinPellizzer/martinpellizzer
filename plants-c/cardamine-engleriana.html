Cardamine engleriana

Cardamine engleriana, commonly known as Engler's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, allowing it to colonize new areas.

The plant is noted for its peppery flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use in certain regions.
