Corydalis blanda subsp. oxelmanii

Corydalis blanda subsp.

oxelmanii, commonly known as the Oregon fawn lily, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and clusters of small, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces distinctive, spurred petals and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valued species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
