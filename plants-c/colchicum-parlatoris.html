Colchicum parlatoris

Colchicum parlatoris, commonly known as the Spanish autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has a distinctive, narrow, grass-like foliage that emerges in the spring. Its bulbs contain toxic compounds, including colchicine, which has been used in medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.
