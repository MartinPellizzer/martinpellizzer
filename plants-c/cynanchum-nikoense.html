Cynanchum nikoense

Cynanchum nikoense, commonly known as Niko's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common trait among members of its family. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a lanceolate shape with hairy surfaces.

Cynanchum nikoense is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.
