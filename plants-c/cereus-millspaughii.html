Cereus millspaughii

Cereus millspaughii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong scent to attract pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to thrive in desert climates with minimal rainfall.
