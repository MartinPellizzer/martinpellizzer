Casearia urbaniana

Casearia urbaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered, violet-blue berries that are highly ornamental. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is edible for some bird species, contributing to its ecological importance in native habitats.
