Cistus monspeliensis var. affinis

Cistus monspeliensis var. affinis, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, resinous fragrance.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, supporting local biodiversity.
