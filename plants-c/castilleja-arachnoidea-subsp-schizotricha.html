Castilleja arachnoidea subsp. schizotricha

Castilleja arachnoidea subsp.

schizotricha, commonly known as the spiderholly castilleja, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, branched, and often hairy stems.

The plant forms symbiotic relationships with the roots of neighboring plants, drawing water and nutrients through its haustoria. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
