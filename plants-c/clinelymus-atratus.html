Clinelymus atratus

Clinelymus atratus, commonly known as the black-leafed brittlebrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep roots that help it access underground water. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Clinelymus atratus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant landscaping.
