Cepa esculenta var. rubra

Cepa esculenta var. rubra, commonly known as red onion, is a botanical variant of the onion species characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple skin and robust, fleshy bulb.

It belongs to the Allium genus and is native to Central Asia, though it is now widely cultivated globally. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem with broad, flat leaves that form a bulbous base. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in umbels, and it is a biennial plant that completes its life cycle over two growing seasons.

The red onion is valued for its strong flavor and is used extensively in culinary applications.
