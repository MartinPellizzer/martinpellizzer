Callitriche bengalensis

Callitriche bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal water starwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Callitrichaceae.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces tiny, floating fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
