Clausia ussuriensis

Clausia ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri clematis, is a perennial climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in temperate regions.
