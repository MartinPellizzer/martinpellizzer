Carpesium macrocephalum

Carpesium macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a composite flower head composed of numerous small florets, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
