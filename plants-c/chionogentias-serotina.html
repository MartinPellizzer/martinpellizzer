Chionogentias serotina

Chionogentias serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering snow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white down, giving them a silvery appearance.

Chionogentias serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
