Campnosperma zeylanicum

Campnosperma zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are woody pods containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
