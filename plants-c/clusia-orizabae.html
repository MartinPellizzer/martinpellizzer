Clusia orizabae

Clusia orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican gloryberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but have a sour taste. Clusia orizabae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
