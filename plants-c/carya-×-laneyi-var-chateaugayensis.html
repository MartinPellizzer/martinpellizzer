Carya × laneyi var. chateaugayensis

Carya × laneyi var. chateaugayensis, commonly known as the Chateaugay hickory, is a hybrid species within the walnut family, Juglandaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and a rough, fissured bark. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk, which ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in horticulture and forestry.
