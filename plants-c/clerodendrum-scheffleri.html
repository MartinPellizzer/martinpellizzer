Clerodendrum scheffleri

Clerodendrum scheffleri, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Clerodendrum scheffleri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking foliage and seasonal display.
