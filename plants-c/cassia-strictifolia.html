Cassia strictifolia

Cassia strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia strictifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
