Curcuma zerumbet

Curcuma zerumbet, commonly known as the flame grass or golden flame, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, ornamental inflorescences that resemble a flame. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green stripes. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from a spike that can reach several feet in height.

Curcuma zerumbet is valued both for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and for its potential medicinal properties.
