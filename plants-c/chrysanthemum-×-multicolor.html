Chrysanthemum × multicolor

Chrysanthemum × multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored chrysanthemum, is a hybrid plant that showcases a wide range of colors in its flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to East Asia, though it is now cultivated worldwide. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers come in various forms, including pompon, anemone, and spider types, with petals that can be solid or variegated.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
