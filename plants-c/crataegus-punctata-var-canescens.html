Crataegus punctata var. canescens

Crataegus punctata var. canescens, commonly known as the graythorn, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.
