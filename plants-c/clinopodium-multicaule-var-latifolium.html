Clinopodium multicaule var. latifolium

Clinopodium multicaule var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive mint-like fragrance when crushed.

The flowers, which are small and tubular, are usually pale blue or purple and appear in whorls along the stems.
