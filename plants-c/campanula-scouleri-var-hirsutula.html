Campanula scouleri var. hirsutula

Campanula scouleri var. hirsutula, commonly known as the Hirsute Bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
