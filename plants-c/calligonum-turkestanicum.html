Calligonum turkestanicum

Calligonum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan tamarisk, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in sandy or saline soils.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
