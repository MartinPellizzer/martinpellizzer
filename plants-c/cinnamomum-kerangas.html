Cinnamomum kerangas

Cinnamomum kerangas, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.
