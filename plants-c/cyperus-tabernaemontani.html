Cyperus tabernaemontani

Cyperus tabernaemontani, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, consisting of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its adaptability to wet soils makes it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
