Cichorium ambiguum

Cichorium ambiguum, commonly known as the bitter chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its lactucin content, giving it a bitter taste. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Cichorium ambiguum is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in traditional herbal medicine.
