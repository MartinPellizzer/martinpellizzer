Cuphea ternata

Cuphea ternata, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Cuphea ternata is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
