Callistachys acuta

Callistachys acuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callistachys acuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
