Cheilophyllum marginatum

Cheilophyllum marginatum, commonly known as the marginate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white margin. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cheilophyllum marginatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
