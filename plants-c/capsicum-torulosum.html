Capsicum torulosum

Capsicum torulosum, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its unique, horn-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is initially green and turns red when mature, containing numerous small, yellow seeds.
