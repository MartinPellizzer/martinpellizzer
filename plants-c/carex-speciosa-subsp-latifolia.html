Carex speciosa subsp. latifolia

Carex speciosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet or semi-wet habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
