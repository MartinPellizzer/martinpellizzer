Ceropegia punctifera

Ceropegia punctifera, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often variegated with green and white markings. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia punctifera is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
