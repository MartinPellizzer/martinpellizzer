Centaurea huber-morathii

Centaurea huber-morathii, commonly known as the Morath's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea huber-morathii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and resilience.
