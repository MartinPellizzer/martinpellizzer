Calamagrostis arundinacea var. latifolia

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. latifolia, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often silver-white underneath, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads in late summer, which are both ornamental and beneficial for wildlife. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and wetlands.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is also cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
