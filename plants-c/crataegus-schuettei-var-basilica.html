Crataegus schuettei var. basilica

Crataegus schuettei var. basilica, commonly known as the Basilica hawthorn, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and showy, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
