Carex graeffeana var. samoensis

Carex graeffeana var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as forest floors or marshy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

The Samoan sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
