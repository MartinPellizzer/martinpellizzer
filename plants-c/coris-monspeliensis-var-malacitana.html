Coris monspeliensis var. malacitana

Coris monspeliensis var. malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal contribute to its value in both horticulture and ecological landscapes.
