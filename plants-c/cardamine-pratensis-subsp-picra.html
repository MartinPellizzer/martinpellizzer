Cardamine pratensis subsp. picra

Cardamine pratensis subsp.

picra, commonly known as yellow water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet meadows and marshy areas in Europe. It features trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette and small, bright yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is characterized by its preference for moist, nutrient-rich soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
