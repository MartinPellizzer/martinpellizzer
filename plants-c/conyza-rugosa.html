Conyza rugosa

Conyza rugosa, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed soils. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Conyza rugosa is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to thrive in various soil types.
