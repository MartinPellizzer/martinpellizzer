Coriandrum sativum var. africanum

Coriandrum sativum var. africanum, commonly known as African cumin, is a variant of the coriander plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are used as a spice, similar to regular coriander seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is valued both for its aromatic seeds and its ornamental foliage.
