Carduus × leridanus

Carduus × leridanus, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carduus lappa and Carduus rigida, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in parts of Europe and Asia. It has a robust root system and can form dense stands, making it both a weed and a potential forage plant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
