Centaurella dichotoma

Centaurella dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue or violet flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurella dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
