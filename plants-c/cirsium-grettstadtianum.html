Cirsium grettstadtianum

Cirsium grettstadtianum, commonly known as the Grettstadt thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
