Conomorpha abdita

Conomorpha abdita, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its bright yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain bird species. Conomorpha abdita has a complex life cycle involving both vegetative and sexual reproduction.

Its presence can have significant ecological impacts on its host trees, influencing local biodiversity and forest dynamics.
