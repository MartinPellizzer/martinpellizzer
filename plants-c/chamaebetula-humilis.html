Chamaebetula humilis

Chamaebetula humilis, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in harsh, cold environments such as tundra and rocky slopes. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand freezing temperatures and strong winds. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it relies on wind pollination.

Chamaebetula humilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
