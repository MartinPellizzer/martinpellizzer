Carex supina subsp. korshinskyi

Carex supina subsp.

korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of the sedge genus Carex.

This subspecies is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and the Russian Far East, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, demonstrating a preference for humid environments.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
