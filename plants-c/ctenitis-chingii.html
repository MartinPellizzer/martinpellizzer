Ctenitis chingii

Ctenitis chingii, commonly known as the Chinese tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, tropical appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
