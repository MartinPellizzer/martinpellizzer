Cladium restioides var. effusa

Cladium restioides var. effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sawgrass, is a variant of the sawgrass species native to wetland ecosystems in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically longer and wider than those of the typical sawgrass, with a rough, serrated edge. The plant thrives in freshwater marshes and is often found in areas with standing water, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to the structure of wetland habitats.

The plant is also an important component of the food web, serving as a food source for herbivorous invertebrates and small vertebrates.
