Cirsium rengehydrophilum

Cirsium rengehydrophilum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
