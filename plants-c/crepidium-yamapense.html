Crepidium yamapense

Crepidium yamapense, commonly known as the Japanese marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.
