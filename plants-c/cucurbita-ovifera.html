Cucurbita ovifera

Cucurbita ovifera, commonly known as the squash or gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant typically grows as a vine with sprawling stems and palmate leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The fruit is often used for culinary purposes or as a decorative element in traditional crafts.
