Cyclosorus procurrens

Cyclosorus procurrens, commonly known as the creeping water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has a distinctive appearance with delicate, waxy leaves that form a rosette pattern, often floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds and wetlands.

Cyclosorus procurrens plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
