Chilita unihamata

Chilita unihamata, commonly known as the single-ribbed chilita, is a unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, Chilita unihamata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
