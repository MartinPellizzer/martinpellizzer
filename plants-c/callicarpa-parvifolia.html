Callicarpa parvifolia

Callicarpa parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces vibrant purple berries in the fall, which are a key feature for its ornamental value. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Callicarpa parvifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and showy fruit display.
