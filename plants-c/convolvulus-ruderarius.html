Convolvulus ruderarius

Convolvulus ruderarius, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural settings.
