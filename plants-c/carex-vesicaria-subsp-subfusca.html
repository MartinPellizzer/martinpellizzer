Carex vesicaria subsp. subfusca

Carex vesicaria subsp.

subfusca, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often greenish or brownish in color.

The species is characterized by its subterranean rhizomes and is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
