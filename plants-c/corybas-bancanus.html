Corybas bancanus

Corybas bancanus, commonly known as the Bank's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flower that resembles a small helmet. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a dark central spot, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.
