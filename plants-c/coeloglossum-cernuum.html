Coeloglossum cernuum

Coeloglossum cernuum, commonly known as the drooping hawksbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, elongated raceme. Its flowers have four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Coeloglossum cernuum is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, thriving in temperate climates.
