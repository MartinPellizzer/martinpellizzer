Croton glaber

Croton glaber, commonly known as the smooth croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
