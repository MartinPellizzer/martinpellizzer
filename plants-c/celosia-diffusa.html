Celosia diffusa

Celosia diffusa, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Celosia diffusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
