Cucumis melo var. bos-valdy

Cucumis melo var. bos-valdy, commonly known as the Bosnian melon, is a variant of the cucumber family native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, round fruit with a thick, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing yellow flowers and requiring support for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is prized for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
