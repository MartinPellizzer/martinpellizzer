Cissus peruviana

Cissus peruviana, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
