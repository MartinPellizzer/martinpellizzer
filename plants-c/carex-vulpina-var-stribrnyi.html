Carex vulpina var. stribrnyi

Carex vulpina var. stribrnyi, commonly known as the Striped Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive striped patterns on its stems. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
