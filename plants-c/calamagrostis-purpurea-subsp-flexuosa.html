Calamagrostis purpurea subsp. flexuosa

Calamagrostis purpurea subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of purple-tinged flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
