Crocus × asturicus

Crocus × asturicus, commonly known as the Asturian crocus, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive purple or violet throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
