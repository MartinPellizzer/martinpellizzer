Chimonobambusa naibunensis

Chimonobambusa naibunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
