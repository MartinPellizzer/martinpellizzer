Clethra panamensis

Clethra panamensis, commonly known as Panama clethra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Clethraceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Clethra panamensis is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
