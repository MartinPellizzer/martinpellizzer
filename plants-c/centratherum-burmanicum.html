Centratherum burmanicum

Centratherum burmanicum, commonly known as Burmese star grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that resemble stars, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
