Cyrtophyllum caudatum

Cyrtophyllum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
