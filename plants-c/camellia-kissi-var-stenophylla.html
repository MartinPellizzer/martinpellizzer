Camellia kissi var. stenophylla

Camellia kissi var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces striking, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
