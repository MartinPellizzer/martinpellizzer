Centaurea affinis subsp. denudata

Centaurea affinis subsp.

denudata, commonly known as the naked centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and a lack of leaf hairs, giving it its common name.

It is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.
