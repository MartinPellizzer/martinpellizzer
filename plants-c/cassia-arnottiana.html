Cassia arnottiana

Cassia arnottiana, commonly known as golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow globular flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, fluffy flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, narrow leaflets.

Cassia arnottiana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also the floral emblem of Australia.
