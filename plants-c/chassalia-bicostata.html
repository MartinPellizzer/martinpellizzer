Chassalia bicostata

Chassalia bicostata, commonly known as the two-costed chassalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Chassalia bicostata is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
