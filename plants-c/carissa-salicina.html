Carissa salicina

Carissa salicina, commonly known as the sweet honey tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
