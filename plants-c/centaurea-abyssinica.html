Centaurea abyssinica

Centaurea abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers in composite flower heads. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
