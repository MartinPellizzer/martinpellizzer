Centaurea leucomalla

Centaurea leucomalla, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Centaurea leucomalla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
