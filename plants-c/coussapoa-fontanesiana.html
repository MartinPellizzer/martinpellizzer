Coussapoa fontanesiana

Coussapoa fontanesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
