Crotalaria gnidioides

Crotalaria gnidioides, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if ingested in large quantities.
