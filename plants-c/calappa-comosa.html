Calappa comosa

Calappa comosa, commonly known as the hairy calappa, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Capparaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of southern Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent features.

Calappa comosa is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
