Cyperus subaequalis

Cyperus subaequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus subaequalis thrives in saturated soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
