Crataegus villiflora

Crataegus villiflora, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
