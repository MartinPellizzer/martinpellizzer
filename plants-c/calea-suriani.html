Calea suriani

Calea suriani, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that open only at night. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces long, slender stems that bear the striking yellow flowers. Calea suriani is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a unique and fascinating species in the botanical world.
