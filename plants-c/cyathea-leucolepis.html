Cyathea leucolepis

Cyathea leucolepis, commonly known as the white-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

Cyathea leucolepis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
