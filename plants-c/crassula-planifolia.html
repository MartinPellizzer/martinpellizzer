Crassula planifolia

Crassula planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Crassula planifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
