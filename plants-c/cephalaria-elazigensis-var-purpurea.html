Cephalaria elazigensis var. purpurea

Cephalaria elazigensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique coloration and potential ecological significance.
