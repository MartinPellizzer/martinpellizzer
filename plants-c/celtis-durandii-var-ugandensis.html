Celtis durandii var. ugandensis

Celtis durandii var. ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and riverine habitats.

The plant has limited commercial use but is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
