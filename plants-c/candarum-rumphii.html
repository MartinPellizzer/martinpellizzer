Candarum rumphii

Candarum rumphii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces intricate, lip-shaped petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Candarum rumphii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
