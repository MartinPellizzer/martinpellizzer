Cistus surrejanus

Cistus surrejanus, commonly known as the Southern rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cistus surrejanus is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
