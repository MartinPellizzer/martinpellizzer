Cordyline arborea

Cordyline arborea, commonly known as the tree cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a flowering plant that is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. Its leaves are typically bright green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cordyline arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
