Calothamnus quadrifidus var. hirsutus

Calothamnus quadrifidus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy stork's bill, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, four-lobed flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "hirsutus.". It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry inland areas.
