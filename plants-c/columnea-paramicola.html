Columnea paramicola

Columnea paramicola, commonly known as the Andean flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Columnea paramicola thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
