Cyperus trispicatus

Cyperus trispicatus, commonly known as the three-branched flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Cyperus trispicatus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
