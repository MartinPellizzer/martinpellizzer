Cerasus × kawasakii

Cerasus × kawasakii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, edible cherry.

It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
