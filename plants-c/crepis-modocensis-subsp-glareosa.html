Crepis modocensis subsp. glareosa

Crepis modocensis subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the Modoc glareous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid and mountainous regions.
