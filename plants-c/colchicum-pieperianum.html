Colchicum pieperianum

Colchicum pieperianum, commonly known as the Armenian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features showy, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has striking, waxy leaves that are basal and often persist through the winter. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, and its growth habit is characterized by a dormant corm that stores nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
