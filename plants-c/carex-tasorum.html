Carex tasorum

Carex tasorum, commonly known as the Tasmanian sedge, is a perennial plant native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers.

Carex tasorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
