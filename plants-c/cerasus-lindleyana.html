Cerasus lindleyana

Cerasus lindleyana, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, tart red or yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic flowers.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant colors in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.
