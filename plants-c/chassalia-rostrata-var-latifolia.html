Chassalia rostrata var. latifolia

Chassalia rostrata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved chassalia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
