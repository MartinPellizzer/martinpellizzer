Cissampelos pareira f. emarginatomucronata

Cissampelos pareira f. emarginatomucronata, commonly known as the marginate-crested climbing vine, is a variant of the species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive notched or wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by greenish or reddish berries.

This plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
