Cacalia talaumifolia

Cacalia talaumifolia, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-blue underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cacalia talaumifolia is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.
