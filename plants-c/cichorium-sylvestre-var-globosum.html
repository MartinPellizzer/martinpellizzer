Cichorium sylvestre var. globosum

Cichorium sylvestre var. globosum, commonly known as the globe artichoke, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces a distinctive, globe-like flower head composed of tightly packed bracts that resemble leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, though some varieties may have white or pink blooms.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and flower buds, which are used in various culinary applications.
