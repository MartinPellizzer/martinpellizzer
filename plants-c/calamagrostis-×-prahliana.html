Calamagrostis × prahliana

Calamagrostis × prahliana, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a hybrid grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
