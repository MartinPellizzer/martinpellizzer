Cactus ambiguus

Cactus ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

While it is not widely cultivated, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for desert wildlife.
