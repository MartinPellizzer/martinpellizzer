Calamus perakensis subsp. crassus

Calamus perakensis subsp.

crassus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed calamus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its robust, thick rhizomes and long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as marshes and swamps. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically found near the water's surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
