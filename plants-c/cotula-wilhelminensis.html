Cotula wilhelminensis

Cotula wilhelminensis, commonly known as the white cotula, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its clustered, small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Cotula wilhelminensis has narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, white-pubescent flower heads.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a decorative plant in gardens.
