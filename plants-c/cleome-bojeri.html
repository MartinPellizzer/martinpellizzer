Cleome bojeri

Cleome bojeri, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are borne on slender pedicels and are often accompanied by small, leaf-like bracts.

Cleome bojeri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
