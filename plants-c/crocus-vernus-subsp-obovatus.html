Crocus vernus subsp. obovatus

Crocus vernus subsp.

obovatus, commonly known as the early crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
