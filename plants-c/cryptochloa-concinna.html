Cryptochloa concinna

Cryptochloa concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf cryptochloa, is a small, grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grassy leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Cryptochloa concinna is a relatively uncommon species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
