Carex eymae

Carex eymae, commonly known as Eymae's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like inflorescences.

Carex eymae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
