Camissonia boothii subsp. inyoensis

Camissonia boothii subsp.

inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The species features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils along the edges of lakes and streams in California and Nevada.
