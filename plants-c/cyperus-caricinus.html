Cyperus caricinus

Cyperus caricinus, commonly known as the marsh flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wetland environments. Cyperus caricinus is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
