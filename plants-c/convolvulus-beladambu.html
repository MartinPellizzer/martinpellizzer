Convolvulus beladambu

Convolvulus beladambu, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
