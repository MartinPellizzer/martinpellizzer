Cyperus melanopus

Cyperus melanopus, commonly known as the black mudflat sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in wetland and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus melanopus thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ability to tolerate brackish water makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
