Chenopodium frutescens

Chenopodium frutescens, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Americas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Purslane is known for its high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants.
