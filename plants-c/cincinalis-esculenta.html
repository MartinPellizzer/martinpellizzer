Cincinalis esculenta

Cincinalis esculenta, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, green, heart-shaped leaves and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in carbohydrates. The fruit develops from a single flower and can weigh up to 20 kilograms when fully mature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries.
