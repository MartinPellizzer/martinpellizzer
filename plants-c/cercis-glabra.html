Cercis glabra

Cercis glabra, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a legume pod that matures in late summer, containing small, stony seeds.

Cercis glabra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
