Colchicum osmaniyense

Colchicum osmaniyense, commonly known as the Ottoman colchic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, large, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
