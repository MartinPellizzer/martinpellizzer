Celastrus kiusianus

Celastrus kiusianus, commonly known as the Japanese staffvetch, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant bears orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a horticultural interest and an invasive species in some regions.
