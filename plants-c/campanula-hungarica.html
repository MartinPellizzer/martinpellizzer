Campanula hungarica

Campanula hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
