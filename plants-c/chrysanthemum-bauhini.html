Chrysanthemum bauhini

Chrysanthemum bauhini, commonly known as the African chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically produces showy yellow or orange blooms, often with a central disc of smaller florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Chrysanthemum bauhini is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
