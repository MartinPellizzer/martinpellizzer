Ceropegia boonjarasii

Ceropegia boonjarasii, commonly known as the Moon Vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using its wiry stems and tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ceropegia boonjarasii is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
