Cirsium fleischeri

Cirsium fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silver-green leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Cirsium fleischeri is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
