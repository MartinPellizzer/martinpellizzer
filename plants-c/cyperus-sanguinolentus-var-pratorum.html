Cyperus sanguinolentus var. pratorum

Cyperus sanguinolentus var. pratorum, commonly known as the red sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
