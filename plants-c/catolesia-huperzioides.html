Catolesia huperzioides

Catolesia huperzioides, commonly known as the Huperzia-like clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, erect, evergreen foliage and distinctive, spore-producing structures called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a leathery texture.

Catolesia huperzioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
