Cirsium hachijoense

Cirsium hachijoense, commonly known as Hachijo thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its structure includes a tall, hairy stem with alternate, serrated leaves.

Cirsium hachijoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
