Corylus hallaisanensis

Corylus hallaisanensis, commonly known as the Hallaisan hazelnut, is a species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a cupule, which is a unique feature of the Corylus genus. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.
