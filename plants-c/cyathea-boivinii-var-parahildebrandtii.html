Cyathea boivinii var. parahildebrandtii

Cyathea boivinii var. parahildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive spiral arrangement of its fronds, which is a common feature in many ferns. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, contributing to its structural support and anchoring in the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
