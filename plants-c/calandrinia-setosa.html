Calandrinia setosa

Calandrinia setosa, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and small, bright red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calandrinia setosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
