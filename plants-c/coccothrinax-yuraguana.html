Coccothrinax yuraguana

Coccothrinax yuraguana, commonly known as the Yuraguan palm, is a species of palm native to Cuba.

It is a small, slender palm that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. Its trunk is covered with a fibrous sheath that persists for several years.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
