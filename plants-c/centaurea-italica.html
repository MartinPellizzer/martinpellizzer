Centaurea italica

Centaurea italica, commonly known as Italian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive bristly pappus. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea italica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
