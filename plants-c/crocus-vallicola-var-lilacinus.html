Crocus vallicola var. lilacinus

Crocus vallicola var. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of lilac and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, slightly alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and vibrant color.
