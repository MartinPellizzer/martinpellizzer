Carya sinensis

Carya sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by hard, spiny fruits that mature in the fall. Its wood is valued for its strength and is used in furniture and tool-making.

The plant also plays a role in traditional Chinese medicine.
