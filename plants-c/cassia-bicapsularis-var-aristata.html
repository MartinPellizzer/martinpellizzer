Cassia bicapsularis var. aristata

Cassia bicapsularis var. aristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
