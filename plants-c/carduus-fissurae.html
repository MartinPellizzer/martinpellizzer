Carduus fissurae

Carduus fissurae, commonly known as the fissured thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched flower head that produces purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
