Cyrtodeira chontalensis

Cyrtodeira chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal pineapple tree, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a striking red or orange coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
