Crocus cartwrightianus var. leucadensis

Crocus cartwrightianus var. leucadensis, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces delicate, white flowers with a pale yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn or early winter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
