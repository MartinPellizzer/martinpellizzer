Cassia droogmansiana

Cassia droogmansiana, commonly known as the African cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Cassia droogmansiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
