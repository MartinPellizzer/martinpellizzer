Cynara cardunculus

Cynara cardunculus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a species of thistle native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a robust, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in a dense, spherical cluster, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits. Its leaves and stems are edible when young and are used in various culinary traditions.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
