Carex acuta var. sparganioides

Carex acuta var. sparganioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial sedge native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually green to brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life makes it ecologically significant.
