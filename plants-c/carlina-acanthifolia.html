Carlina acanthifolia

Carlina acanthifolia, commonly known as the spiked hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Carlina acanthifolia is often found in rocky or dry habitats, where it thrives in full sunlight.
