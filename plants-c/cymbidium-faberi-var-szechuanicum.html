Cymbidium faberi var. szechuanicum

Cymbidium faberi var. szechuanicum, commonly known as the Sichuan orchid, is a species native to China, particularly in the Sichuan province.

It is a deciduous orchid characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive red lip, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in cool climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
