Cleghornia cymosa

Cleghornia cymosa, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark seeds in its fruiting structure.

Cleghornia cymosa is often found in the eastern and central regions of North America.
