Chaetochloa leucopila

Chaetochloa leucopila, commonly known as the white-haired grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass species. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
