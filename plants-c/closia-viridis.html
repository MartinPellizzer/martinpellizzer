Closia viridis

Closia viridis, commonly known as the green closia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Closia viridis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
