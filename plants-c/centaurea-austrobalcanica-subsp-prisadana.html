Centaurea austrobalcanica subsp. prisadana

Centaurea austrobalcanica subsp.

prisadana, commonly known as the Southern Balkan knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and purple-blue flower heads.

The plant produces slender, linear leaves and has a fibrous root system typical of many members of its genus. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
