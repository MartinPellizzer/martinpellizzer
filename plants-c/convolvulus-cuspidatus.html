Convolvulus cuspidatus

Convolvulus cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant blue or violet flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it both a useful ornamental and a challenging invasive species.
