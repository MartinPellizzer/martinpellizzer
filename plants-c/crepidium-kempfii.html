Crepidium kempfii

Crepidium kempfii, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-draining soil.

Crepidium kempfii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and compact size.
