Cephalophyllum diversifolium

Cephalophyllum diversifolium, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure and fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically varied in shape and size, contributing to its common name "diversifolium.".

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
