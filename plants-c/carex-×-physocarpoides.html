Carex × physocarpoides

Carex × physocarpoides, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.

Carex × physocarpoides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
