Colchicum autumnale subsp. algeriense

Colchicum autumnale subsp.

algeriense, commonly known as the Algerian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spathaceous flower clusters. Its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially hazardous plant to handle.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
