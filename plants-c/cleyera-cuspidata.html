Cleyera cuspidata

Cleyera cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Cleyera cuspidata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
