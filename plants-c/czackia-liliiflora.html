Czackia liliiflora

Czackia liliiflora, commonly known as the lily-flowered czackia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers resembling lilies. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
