Calceolaria flavovirens

Calceolaria flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically yellow to green in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
