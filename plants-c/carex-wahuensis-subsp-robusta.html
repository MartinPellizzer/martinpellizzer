Carex wahuensis subsp. robusta

Carex wahuensis subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its robust growth habit makes it a hardy and adaptable species in its natural habitat.
