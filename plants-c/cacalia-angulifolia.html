Cacalia angulifolia

Cacalia angulifolia, commonly known as the angular false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the dandelion-like appearance.

Cacalia angulifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
