Cyperus andrewsii

Cyperus andrewsii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Cyperus andrewsii is native to wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
