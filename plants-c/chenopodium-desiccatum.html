Chenopodium desiccatum

Chenopodium desiccatum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its leaves and stems. It often grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is commonly found in deserts and along dry riverbeds.

Chenopodium desiccatum is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
