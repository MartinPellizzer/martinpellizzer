Carex angustilepis

Carex angustilepis, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Carex angustilepis has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
