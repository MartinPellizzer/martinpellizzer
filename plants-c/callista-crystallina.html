Callista crystallina

Callista crystallina, commonly known as the crystal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences.

Callista crystallina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
