Cirsium eriophorum subsp. chatenieri

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

chatenieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are typically surrounded by a series of bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
