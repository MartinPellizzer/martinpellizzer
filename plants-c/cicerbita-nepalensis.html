Cicerbita nepalensis

Cicerbita nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cicerbita nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
