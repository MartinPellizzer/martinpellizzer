Cryptocarya grandifolia

Cryptocarya grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya grandifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
