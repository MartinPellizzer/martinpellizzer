Cosmos scabiosoides

Cosmos scabiosoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with a fluffy appearance that gives it its common name.

Cosmos scabiosoides is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
