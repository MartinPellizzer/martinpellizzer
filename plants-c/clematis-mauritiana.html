Clematis mauritiana

Clematis mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius clematis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
