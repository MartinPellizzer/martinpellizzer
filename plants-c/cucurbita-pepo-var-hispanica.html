Cucurbita pepo var. hispanica

Cucurbita pepo var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas.

It is a vining annual plant that produces both male and female flowers, with the latter developing into the characteristic orange or yellow fruits. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and a robust stem covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are typically round or elongated, with a thick, edible rind and a sweet, nutty flavor.

This variety is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
