Clematis austrogansuensis

Clematis austrogansuensis, commonly known as the Australian clematis, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a deciduous vine that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis austrogansuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
