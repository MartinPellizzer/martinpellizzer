Camellia parvilimba

Camellia parvilimba, commonly known as the small-leaf camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are relatively small compared to other camellia species. The plant produces striking, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia parvilimba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
