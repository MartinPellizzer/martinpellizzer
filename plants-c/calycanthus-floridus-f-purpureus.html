Calycanthus floridus f. purpureus

Calycanthus floridus f. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. Its flowering stems often bear clusters of red or purple flowers, which contrast strikingly with the foliage.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
