Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. decrescens

Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. decrescens, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, silvery-green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
