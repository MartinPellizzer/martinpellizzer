Condaminea tinctoria

Condaminea tinctoria, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, spider-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often emerging after the flowers have faded.

Condaminea tinctoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
