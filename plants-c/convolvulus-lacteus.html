Convolvulus lacteus

Convolvulus lacteus, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by distinctive seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Convolvulus lacteus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
