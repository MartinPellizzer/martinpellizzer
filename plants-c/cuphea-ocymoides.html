Cuphea ocymoides

Cuphea ocymoides, commonly known as the flame flower or whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and bees.

Cuphea ocymoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
