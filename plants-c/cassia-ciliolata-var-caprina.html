Cassia ciliolata var. caprina

Cassia ciliolata var. caprina, commonly known as the Kashmiri cassia, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with finely toothed margins and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is commonly found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
