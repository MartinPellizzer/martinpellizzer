Calceolaria aurea

Calceolaria aurea, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped flowers, which are typically golden yellow in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria aurea is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial shade.
