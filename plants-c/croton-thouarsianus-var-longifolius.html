Croton thouarsianus var. longifolius

Croton thouarsianus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often prominently veined and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable regions.
