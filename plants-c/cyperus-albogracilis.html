Cyperus albogracilis

Cyperus albogracilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that develop from the base of the plant.
