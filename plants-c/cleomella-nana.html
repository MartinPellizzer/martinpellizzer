Cleomella nana

Cleomella nana, commonly known as the dwarf cleome, is a small annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Cleomella nana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ease of growth.
