Commiphora cerasiformis

Commiphora cerasiformis, commonly known as the Persian gum or Indian myrrh, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, reddish fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
