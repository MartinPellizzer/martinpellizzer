Carumbium fastuosum

Carumbium fastuosum, commonly known as the grandiose carumbium, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble intricate lacework, composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to grow outside its native range.
