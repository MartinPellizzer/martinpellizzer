Cyperus fenzelianus

Cyperus fenzelianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Cyperus fenzelianus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
