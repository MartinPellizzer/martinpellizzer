Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. densiflora

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered reed, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
