Conophytum aequale

Conophytum aequale, commonly known as the "Twin-leafed Stone Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features two fleshy, paired leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
