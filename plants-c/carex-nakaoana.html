Carex nakaoana

Carex nakaoana, commonly known as the Nakaoana sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and somewhat stiff, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Carex nakaoana plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
