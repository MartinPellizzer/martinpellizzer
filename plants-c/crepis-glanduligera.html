Crepis glanduligera

Crepis glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and have a distinctive glandular surface, giving it its common name.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a yellow color that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
