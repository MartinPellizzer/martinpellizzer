Cedrus libani var. glauca

Cedrus libani var. glauca, commonly known as the dark cedar or blue cedar, is a variety of the Lebanon cedar.

It is characterized by its dark blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This evergreen conifer is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly in Lebanon and Syria. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient architecture and religious contexts.
