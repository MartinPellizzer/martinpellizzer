Cleomella taurocranos

Cleomella taurocranos, commonly known as the golden cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleomella taurocranos is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
