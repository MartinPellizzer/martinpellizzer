Crocus lageniflorus subvar. striatus

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. striatus, commonly known as the striated crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with a distinctive striped pattern on the petals, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears 3 to 5 flowers, each with a prominent, cup-shaped tepal. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of sheathing leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring.
