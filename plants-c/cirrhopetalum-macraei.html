Cirrhopetalum macraei

Cirrhopetalum macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are adapted for efficient nutrient absorption.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
