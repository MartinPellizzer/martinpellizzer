Clompanus striatiflora

Clompanus striatiflora, commonly known as the striped-flowered clompanus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with a distinct striped pattern along the midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Clompanus striatiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
