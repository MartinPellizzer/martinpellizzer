Clinopodium colchicum var. panjutinii

Clinopodium colchicum var. panjutinii, commonly known as the Panjutin's woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
