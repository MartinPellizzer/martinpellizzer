Crataegus oxyacantha var. deltoxyacantha

Crataegus oxyacantha var. deltoxyacantha, commonly known as the deltoid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate to deltoid-shaped leaves, which are typically serrated and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
