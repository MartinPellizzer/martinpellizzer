Carex pelocarpa

Carex pelocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pelocarpa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
