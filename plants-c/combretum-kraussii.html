Combretum kraussii

Combretum kraussii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a reddish tinge when young. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
