Cotyledon papillaris

Cotyledon papillaris, commonly known as the wrinkled cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a distinctive, raised, waxy texture. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring.

The leaves are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and unique appearance.
