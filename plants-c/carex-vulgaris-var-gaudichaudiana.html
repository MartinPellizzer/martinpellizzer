Carex vulgaris var. gaudichaudiana

Carex vulgaris var. gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually brown or reddish in color, emerging from the base of the plant. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
