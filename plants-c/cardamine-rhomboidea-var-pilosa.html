Cardamine rhomboidea var. pilosa

Cardamine rhomboidea var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is typically found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
