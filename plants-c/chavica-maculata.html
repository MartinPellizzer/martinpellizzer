Chavica maculata

Chavica maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, come in shades of pink, white, and purple.

Chavica maculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
