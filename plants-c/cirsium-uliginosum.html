Cirsium uliginosum

Cirsium uliginosum, commonly known as the bog thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It features a tall, upright stem covered with stiff, spiny leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and other wetland environments.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
