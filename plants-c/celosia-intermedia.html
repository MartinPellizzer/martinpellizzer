Celosia intermedia

Celosia intermedia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues.

Celosia intermedia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its bold, showy appearance.
