Calathea martinicensis

Calathea martinicensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
