Carex boryana

Carex boryana, commonly known as the Bory's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex boryana has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
