Crepis pallasii

Crepis pallasii, commonly known as Pallas' hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a hardy, frost-tolerant species.
