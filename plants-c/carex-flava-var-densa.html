Carex flava var. densa

Carex flava var. densa, commonly known as the dense yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
