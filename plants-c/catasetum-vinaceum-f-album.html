Catasetum vinaceum f. album

Catasetum vinaceum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a unique variant of the Catasetum genus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast with the usual vibrant colors of the species. This orchid typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces specialized pollinia that are transferred by specific pollinators, a feature common in the Catasetum genus.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
