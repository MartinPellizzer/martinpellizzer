Coleus habrophyllus

Coleus habrophyllus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from deep purples to bright reds and oranges. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and striking coloration.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
