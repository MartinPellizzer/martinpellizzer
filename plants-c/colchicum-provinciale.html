Colchicum provinciale

Colchicum provinciale, commonly known as the Provincial Colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces a bulbous corm, which serves as its storage organ and allows it to survive in harsh alpine conditions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring before the flowers.

Colchicum provinciale is also notable for containing colchicine, a compound with medicinal and toxic properties.
