Carex cilicica subsp. cilicica

Carex cilicica subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its preference for cool, temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
