Carex curvula subsp. rosae

Carex curvula subsp.

rosae, commonly known as the Rosy Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale pinkish hues on the leaf sheaths, giving it its common name.
