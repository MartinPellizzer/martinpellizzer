Clematis gentianoides var. normalis

Clematis gentianoides var. normalis, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
