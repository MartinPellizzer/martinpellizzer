Chlamydocarya staudtii

Chlamydocarya staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted for pollination by wind or insects.
