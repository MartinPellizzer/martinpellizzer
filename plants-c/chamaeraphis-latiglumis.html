Chamaeraphis latiglumis

Chamaeraphis latiglumis, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is often found in saline soils.

Due to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, it is considered an important species for stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
