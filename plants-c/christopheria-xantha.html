Christopheria xantha

Christopheria xantha, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water. It produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Christopheria xantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
