Cnidium coniifolium

Cnidium coniifolium, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often complex inflorescences. The plant features slender, hollow stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish hue. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the family.

The roots and rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
