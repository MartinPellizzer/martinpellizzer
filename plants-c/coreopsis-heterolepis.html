Coreopsis heterolepis

Coreopsis heterolepis, commonly known as the eastern coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its upright growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
