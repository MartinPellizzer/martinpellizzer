Centaurea tartesiana

Centaurea tartesiana, commonly known as the Tartessian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and slopes. Its flowering period occurs mainly in the summer months, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Centaurea tartesiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
