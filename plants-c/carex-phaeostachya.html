Carex phaeostachya

Carex phaeostachya, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces dense clusters of dark brown to black spikelets, which are its flowering structures.

Carex phaeostachya is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
