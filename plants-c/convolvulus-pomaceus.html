Convolvulus pomaceus

Convolvulus pomaceus, commonly known as the apple vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
