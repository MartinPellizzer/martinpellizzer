Circaea alpina subsp. micrantha

Circaea alpina subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the alpine woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems, small white flowers, and opposite, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
