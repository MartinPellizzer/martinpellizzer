Canscora grandiflora

Canscora grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered censcoring, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
