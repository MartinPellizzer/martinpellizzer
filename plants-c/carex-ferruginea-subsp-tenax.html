Carex ferruginea subsp. tenax

Carex ferruginea subsp.

tenax, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

Its subspecies, tenax, is distinguished by its reddish-brown bracts and robust growth habit.
