Crataegus pruinosa f. angulata

Crataegus pruinosa f. angulata, commonly known as the angular hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive angular, often thorned branches and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
