Chloris truncata f. abbreviata

Chloris truncata f. abbreviata, commonly known as the shortleaf chloris, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, sturdy leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color, with a distinctive truncate (cut-off) tip. This variety is often found in sandy or well-drained soils, thriving in open, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
