Cerinthe macrophylla

Cerinthe macrophylla, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cerinthe macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
