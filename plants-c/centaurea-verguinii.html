Centaurea verguinii

Centaurea verguinii, commonly known as the Sicilian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea verguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
