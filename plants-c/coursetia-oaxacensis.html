Coursetia oaxacensis

Coursetia oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxaca coursetia, is a shrub native to the Mexican states of Oaxaca and Chiapas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through deep root systems. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coursetia oaxacensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with minimal water availability.
