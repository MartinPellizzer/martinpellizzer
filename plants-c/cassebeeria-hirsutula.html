Cassebeeria hirsutula

Cassebeeria hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil enrichment.
