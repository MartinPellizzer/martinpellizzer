Caladenia porphyrea

Caladenia porphyrea, commonly known as the Purple-veined Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its distinctive, spider-like flowers with a deep purple and white coloration. The plant typically blooms during the autumn season, producing intricate, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Caladenia porphyrea prefers sandy or loamy soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes threatened species.
