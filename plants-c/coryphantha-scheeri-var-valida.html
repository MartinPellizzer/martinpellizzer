Coryphantha scheeri var. valida

Coryphantha scheeri var. valida, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
