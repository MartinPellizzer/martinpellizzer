Campylotheca rutifolia

Campylotheca rutifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved campylotheca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually rough or hairy to the touch. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicle-like inflorescences, and are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.
