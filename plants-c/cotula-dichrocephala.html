Cotula dichrocephala

Cotula dichrocephala, commonly known as the two-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Cotula dichrocephala is known for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
