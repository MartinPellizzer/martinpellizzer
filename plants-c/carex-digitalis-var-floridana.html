Carex digitalis var. floridana

Carex digitalis var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
