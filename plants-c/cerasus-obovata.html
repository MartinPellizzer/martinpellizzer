Cerasus obovata

Cerasus obovata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Obovate cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, obovate-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible but often tart. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cerasus obovata thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
