Croton chinensis

Croton chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and can range from ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf colors and unique growth habit.
