Centaurea gnaphalioides

Centaurea gnaphalioides, commonly known as the slender knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features slender, branching stems and small, purple to pinkish flowers arranged in a composite head.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
