Cotula goughensis

Cotula goughensis, commonly known as Gough's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Cotula goughensis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to tolerate salt spray.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal dunes.
