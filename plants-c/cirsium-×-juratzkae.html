Cirsium × juratzkae

Cirsium × juratzkae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium palustre, resulting in a plant with distinctive morphological features. The leaves are typically spiny and lanceolate, while the flowers are purple or pink, arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It is native to wetland habitats in Europe and is often found in marshy areas along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
