Carex pairae f. chlorocarpa

Carex pairae f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-stem sedge, is a variant of the Carex pairae species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are generally narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.
