Crataegus apiomorpha

Crataegus apiomorpha, commonly known as the apple serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature, providing food for wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crataegus apiomorpha is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
