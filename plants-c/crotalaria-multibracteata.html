Crotalaria multibracteata

Crotalaria multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria multibracteata is also known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
