Campylia laciniata

Campylia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf campylia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Campylia laciniata is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with partial shade.
