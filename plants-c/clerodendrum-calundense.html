Clerodendrum calundense

Clerodendrum calundense, commonly known as the Philippine flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Clerodendrum is part of the Verbenaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowering plants.
