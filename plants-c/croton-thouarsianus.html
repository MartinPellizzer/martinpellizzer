Croton thouarsianus

Croton thouarsianus, commonly known as the Tahitian croton, is a tropical plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Croton, which includes over 1,000 species of flowering plants.
