Calamintha clinopodium subsp. villosa

Calamintha clinopodium subsp.

villosa, commonly known as woolly mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and mild scent.
