Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. macrolepis

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the broadleaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming clumps or tussocks.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common species in wetlands and along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
