Carex erythrorrhiza

Carex erythrorrhiza, commonly known as red-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks. Its rhizomes are reddish in color, giving it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex erythrorrhiza plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
