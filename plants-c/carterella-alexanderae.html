Carterella alexanderae

Carterella alexanderae, commonly known as the Alexander's cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a globular, spiny stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Carterella alexanderae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
