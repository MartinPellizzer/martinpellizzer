Cupania lanuginosa

Cupania lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky cupania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cupania lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
