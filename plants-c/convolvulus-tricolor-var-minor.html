Convolvulus tricolor var. minor

Convolvulus tricolor var. minor, commonly known as the lesser three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
