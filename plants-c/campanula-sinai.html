Campanula sinai

Campanula sinai, commonly known as the Sinai bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula sinai is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
