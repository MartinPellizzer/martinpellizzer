Chenopodium chilense

Chenopodium chilense, commonly known as Chilean goosefoot, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are edible and have been used traditionally in some South American cuisines.

Chenopodium chilense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
