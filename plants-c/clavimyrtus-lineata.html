Clavimyrtus lineata

Clavimyrtus lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Clavimyrtus lineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
