Calanthe furcata f. albomarginata

Calanthe furcata f. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin calanthe, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive white-veined, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, feature a combination of purple and white petals.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
