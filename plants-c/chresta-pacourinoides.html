Chresta pacourinoides

Chresta pacourinoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
