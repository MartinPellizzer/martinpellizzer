Corrigiola litoralis subsp. imbricata

Corrigiola litoralis subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the coastal sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal regions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shores, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a tight rosette. Its flowers are pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
