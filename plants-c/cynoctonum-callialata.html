Cynoctonum callialata

Cynoctonum callialata, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a thick, rough bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, alternate, and ovate, often with a waxy texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
