Cinnamomum pachyphyllum

Cinnamomum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces aromatic bark and leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
