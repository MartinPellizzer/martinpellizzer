Cynanchum formosanum var. ovalifolium

Cynanchum formosanum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming in late summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it a member of the milkweed family with defensive chemical compounds.
