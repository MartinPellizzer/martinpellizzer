Cajanus argenteus

Cajanus argenteus, commonly known as silver gram, is a leguminous plant native to India.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Silver gram is valued for its nutritional content and is used in both human consumption and animal feed.
