Carex pseudohirta

Carex pseudohirta, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it relies on wind pollination.

Carex pseudohirta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
