Chamaecrista stillifera

Chamaecrista stillifera, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
