Cassia nanodes

Cassia nanodes, commonly known as dwarf senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia nanodes thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
