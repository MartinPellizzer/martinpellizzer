Cassia fenarolii

Cassia fenarolii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cassia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to dry environments. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are dispersed by wind.

Cassia fenarolii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
