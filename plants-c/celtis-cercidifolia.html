Celtis cercidifolia

Celtis cercidifolia, commonly known as the Chinese Hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, green berries that ripen to dark purple.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat.
