Cressa australis

Cressa australis, commonly known as the Australian cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It features small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, demonstrating adaptability to arid conditions.

Cressa australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
