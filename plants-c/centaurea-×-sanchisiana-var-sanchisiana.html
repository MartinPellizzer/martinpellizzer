Centaurea × sanchisiana var. sanchisiana

Centaurea × sanchisiana var. sanchisiana, commonly known as the Sanchis' centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bract-like leaves beneath the flower heads.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
