Cochlearia officinalis var. arctica

Cochlearia officinalis var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in alpine and tundra environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
