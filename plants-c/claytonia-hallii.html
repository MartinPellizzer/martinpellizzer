Claytonia hallii

Claytonia hallii, commonly known as the Sierra glacierweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high elevations of California and Oregon.

It belongs to the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its unique morphology allows it to conserve moisture and withstand harsh weather conditions.

Claytonia hallii is often found in meadows and open slopes above the tree line.
