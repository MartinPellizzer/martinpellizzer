Convolvulus platypeltis

Convolvulus platypeltis, commonly known as the broad-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
