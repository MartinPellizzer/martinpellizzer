Centaurea bella subsp. nathadzeae

Centaurea bella subsp.

nathadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian gladiola, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and upright, branching stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
