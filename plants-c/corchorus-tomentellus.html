Corchorus tomentellus

Corchorus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, where it can thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
