Cyphostemma michelii

Cyphostemma michelii, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Cyphostemma michelii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
