Conyza sinensis

Conyza sinensis, commonly known as hoary weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Conyza sinensis is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
