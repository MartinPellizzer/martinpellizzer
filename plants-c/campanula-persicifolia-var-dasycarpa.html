Campanula persicifolia var. dasycarpa

Campanula persicifolia var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the Wooly Bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
