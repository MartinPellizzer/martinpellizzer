Crataegus punctata f. canescens

Crataegus punctata f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the typical form. This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to parts of Europe and Asia. It produces small, white flowers in spring followed by red berries in autumn.

The hairy hawthorn is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
