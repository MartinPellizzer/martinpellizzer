Cissus verrucosa

Cissus verrucosa, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or velvet leaf, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves that often have a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically eaten raw. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cissus verrucosa is also valued for its ability to climb and its ornamental appeal.
