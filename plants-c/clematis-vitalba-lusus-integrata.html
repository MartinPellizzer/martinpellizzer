Clematis vitalba lusus integrata

Clematis vitalba lusus integrata, commonly known as the European Clematis or Virgin's Bower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed carefully.
