Chaenactis achilleifolia

Chaenactis achilleifolia, commonly known as the Achillea-like dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into white, fluffy seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed environments.
