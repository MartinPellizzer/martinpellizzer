Ceropegia candelabrum

Ceropegia candelabrum, commonly known as the candleplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a unique, branching structure with fleshy, tubular stems that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale green, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water. It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.
