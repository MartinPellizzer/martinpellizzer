Citharexylum crassifolium

Citharexylum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf citharexylum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale yellow, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in coastal forests or scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
