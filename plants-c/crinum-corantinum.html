Crinum corantinum

Crinum corantinum, commonly known as the Colombian lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Crinum corantinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
