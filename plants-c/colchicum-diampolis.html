Colchicum diampolis

Colchicum diampolis, commonly known as the Armenian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Colchicum diampolis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and medicinal plant research.
