Chamaesyce chamaesyce

Chamaesyce chamaesyce, commonly known as the smallleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its compact growth form and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in various ecological settings.
