Cyperus gatesii

Cyperus gatesii, commonly known as the Gates' flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus gatesii is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
