Commiphora sessiliflora

Commiphora sessiliflora, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the leaf axils. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in dry, tropical, or subtropical climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and its historical use in traditional medicine.
