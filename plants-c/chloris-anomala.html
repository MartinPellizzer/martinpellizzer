Chloris anomala

Chloris anomala, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, upright blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
