Culcitium adscendens var. angustifolium

Culcitium adscendens var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf climbing fig, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a houseplant.
