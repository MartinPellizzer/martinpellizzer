Choripetalum aurantiacum

Choripetalum aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Choripetalum aurantiacum is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping.
