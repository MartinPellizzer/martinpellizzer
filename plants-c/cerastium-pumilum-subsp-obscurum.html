Cerastium pumilum subsp. obscurum

Cerastium pumilum subsp.

obscurum, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand mild winters and wet summers, making it a resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
