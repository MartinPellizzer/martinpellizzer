Chamaesyce fosbergii

Chamaesyce fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and grayish-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
