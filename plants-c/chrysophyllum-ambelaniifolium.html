Chrysophyllum ambelaniifolium

Chrysophyllum ambelaniifolium, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits with a crisp, juicy pulp. The fruit is often eaten fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
