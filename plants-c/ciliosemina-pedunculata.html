Ciliosemina pedunculata

Ciliosemina pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered ciliosemina, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
