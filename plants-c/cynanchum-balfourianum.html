Cynanchum balfourianum

Cynanchum balfourianum, commonly known as the Balfour's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
