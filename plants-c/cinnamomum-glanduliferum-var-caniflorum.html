Cinnamomum glanduliferum var. caniflorum

Cinnamomum glanduliferum var. caniflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent midribs and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic bark and leaves that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
