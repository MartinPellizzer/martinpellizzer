Cistus × fernandesiae

Cistus × fernandesiae, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Cistus × fernandesiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
