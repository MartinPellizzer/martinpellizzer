Cyperus macrorrhizus

Cyperus macrorrhizus, commonly known as the broadroot flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus macrorrhizus is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, with its extensive root system helping stabilize soil in these areas.
