Cichorium casnia

Cichorium casnia, commonly known as the Caspian chicory, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lobed and hairy, while the flowers are blue or purple in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
