Calamagrostis × filipes

Calamagrostis × filipes, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silvery-blue foliage that creates a striking visual contrast. The plant produces feathery plumes in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Calamagrostis × filipes is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
