Cyphia bechuanensis

Cyphia bechuanensis, commonly known as the Bechuanan cycad, is a unique cycad species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions.

Due to its ecological importance and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species.
