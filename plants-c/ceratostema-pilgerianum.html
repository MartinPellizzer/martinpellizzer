Ceratostema pilgerianum

Ceratostema pilgerianum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are borne on short, erect stalks.

Ceratostema pilgerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
