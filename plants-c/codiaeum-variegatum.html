Codiaeum variegatum

Codiaeum variegatum, commonly known as the croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a variety of patterns, including red, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in landscaped gardens.
