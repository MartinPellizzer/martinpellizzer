Caprifolium pallidum

Caprifolium pallidum, commonly known as the pale honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Caprifolium pallidum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
