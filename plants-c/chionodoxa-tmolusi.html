Chionodoxa tmolusi

Chionodoxa tmolusi, commonly known as the Persian glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
