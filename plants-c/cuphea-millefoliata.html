Cuphea millefoliata

Cuphea millefoliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea millefoliata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
