Coleocephalocereus flavisetus

Coleocephalocereus flavisetus, commonly known as the yellow-bristled cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
