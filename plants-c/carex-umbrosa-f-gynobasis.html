Carex umbrosa f. gynobasis

Carex umbrosa f. gynobasis, commonly known as the gloomy sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or purplish in color.

The subspecies gynobasis is distinguished by its unique floral structure, featuring female flowers at the base of the spikelets.
