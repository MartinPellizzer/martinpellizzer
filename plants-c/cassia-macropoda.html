Cassia macropoda

Cassia macropoda, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, cotton-like material. Cassia macropoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
