Centaurium hermannii

Centaurium hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's centaury, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hermann's centaury is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
