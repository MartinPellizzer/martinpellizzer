Clematis chrysocoma var. glabrescens

Clematis chrysocoma var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth clematis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
