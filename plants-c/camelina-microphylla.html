Camelina microphylla

Camelina microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed camelina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in drought-prone areas.

Camelina microphylla is also valued for its potential as a source of oilseed and its ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
