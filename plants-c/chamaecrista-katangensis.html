Chamaecrista katangensis

Chamaecrista katangensis, commonly known as Katanga burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or sandy soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and researchers.
