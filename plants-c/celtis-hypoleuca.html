Celtis hypoleuca

Celtis hypoleuca, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark blue color.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
