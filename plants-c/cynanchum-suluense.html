Cynanchum suluense

Cynanchum suluense, commonly known as the Sulu milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Cynanchum suluense is known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
