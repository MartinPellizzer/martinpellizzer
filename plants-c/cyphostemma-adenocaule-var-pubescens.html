Cyphostemma adenocaule var. pubescens

Cyphostemma adenocaule var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cyphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
