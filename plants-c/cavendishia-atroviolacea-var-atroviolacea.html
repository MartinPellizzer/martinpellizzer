Cavendishia atroviolacea var. atroviolacea

Cavendishia atroviolacea var. atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violet orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking dark purple to black petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique coloration and beauty, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
