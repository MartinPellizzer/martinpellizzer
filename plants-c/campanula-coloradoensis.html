Campanula coloradoensis

Campanula coloradoensis, commonly known as Colorado bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
