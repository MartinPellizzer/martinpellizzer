Capnoides aurea var. occidentale

Capnoides aurea var. occidentale, commonly known as the western golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow blooms that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Capnoides aurea var. occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
