Cathartolinum gallicum

Cathartolinum gallicum, commonly known as the French cathartic plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas. Its botanical significance lies in its historical use as a cathartic agent, though it is now primarily of interest in botanical studies.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
