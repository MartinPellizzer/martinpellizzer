Cruciata coronata var. mesopotamica

Cruciata coronata var. mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, branched clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for its unique appearance.
