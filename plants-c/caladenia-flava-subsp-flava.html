Caladenia flava subsp. flava

Caladenia flava subsp.

flava, commonly known as the Yellow Spider-Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forests and heathlands.

The plant features a single, slender stem with a single or occasionally two showy, yellowish-orange flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers have a distinctive, intricate structure with a spurred lip.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
