Calceolaria filiformis

Calceolaria filiformis, commonly known as the slender hooded orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small cups. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive hooded structure that shelters the reproductive organs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
