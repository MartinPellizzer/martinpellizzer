Carex purpureotincta

Carex purpureotincta, commonly known as the purple-tinted sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex purpureotincta is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
