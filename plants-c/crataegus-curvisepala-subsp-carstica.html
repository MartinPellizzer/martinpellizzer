Crataegus curvisepala subsp. carstica

Crataegus curvisepala subsp.

carstica, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that appear in spring, followed by red berries in autumn.

The plant has thorned branches and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
