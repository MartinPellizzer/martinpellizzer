Cinnamomum chemungianum

Cinnamomum chemungianum, commonly known as Chemung cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and bay leaf. The tree produces small, fragrant leaves and has a distinctive, cinnamon-like scent in its bark and leaves. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
