Crucifera lamarckii

Crucifera lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed or pinnately divided. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its historical significance in botanical studies, it remains a subject of interest in both taxonomy and ecological research.
