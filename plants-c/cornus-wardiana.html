Cornus wardiana

Cornus wardiana, commonly known as the mountain cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly serrated edge.

Cornus wardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
