Cussonia jatrophoides

Cussonia jatrophoides, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking yellowish-green spadices, which are typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

Cussonia jatrophoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
