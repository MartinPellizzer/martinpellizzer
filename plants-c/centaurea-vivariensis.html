Centaurea vivariensis

Centaurea vivariensis, commonly known as the Vivarion thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

Centaurea vivariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
