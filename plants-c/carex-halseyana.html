Carex halseyana

Carex halseyana, commonly known as Halsey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Carex, which is part of the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Carex halseyana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
