Conchocarpus obovatus

Conchocarpus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sea grape, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often found growing near coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or saline soils.
