Carex diclina

Carex diclina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive three-ranked leaves. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces triangular, three-sided berries.

Carex diclina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
