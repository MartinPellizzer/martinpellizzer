Carthamus daumasianus

Carthamus daumasianus, commonly known as the Daumasian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a type of saffron-like stamen, which is often harvested for its vivid color and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing, it remains a valued plant in horticulture.
