Cienfuegosia integrifolia

Cienfuegosia integrifolia, commonly known as the Cuban beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through the winter, making it visually striking. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
