Cinchona cordifolia var. vera

Cinchona cordifolia var. vera, commonly known as the Amazonian quinine tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds, and it is renowned for its historical use in the extraction of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.
