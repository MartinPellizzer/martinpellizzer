Clematis aristata var. breviappendiculata

Clematis aristata var. breviappendiculata, commonly known as the short-appendiculate clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
