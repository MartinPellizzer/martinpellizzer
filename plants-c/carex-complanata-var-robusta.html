Carex complanata var. robusta

Carex complanata var. robusta, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
