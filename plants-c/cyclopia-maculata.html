Cyclopia maculata

Cyclopia maculata, commonly known as the spotted cyclopia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in small, raised spots, giving it its common name.

Cyclopia maculata is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in South African herbal medicine.
