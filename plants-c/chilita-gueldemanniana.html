Chilita gueldemanniana

Chilita gueldemanniana, commonly known as the Gueldemann's chilita, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the cloud forests of western Ecuador.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that collect and store water. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Due to its restricted habitat and threats from deforestation, it is highly vulnerable to extinction.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical species.
