Chloris luetzelburgii

Chloris luetzelburgii, commonly known as Luetzelburg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
