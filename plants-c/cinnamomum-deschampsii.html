Cinnamomum deschampsii

Cinnamomum deschampsii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon or deschampsii cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Cinnamomum deschampsii thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
