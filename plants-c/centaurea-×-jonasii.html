Centaurea × jonasii

Centaurea × jonasii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Centaurea × jonasii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
