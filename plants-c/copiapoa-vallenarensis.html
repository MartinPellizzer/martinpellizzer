Copiapoa vallenarensis

Copiapoa vallenarensis, commonly known as the Vallenar cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to extreme drought, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
