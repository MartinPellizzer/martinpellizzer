Carapa slateri

Carapa slateri, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil. Its bark is thick and often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
