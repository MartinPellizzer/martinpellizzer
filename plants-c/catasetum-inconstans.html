Catasetum inconstans

Catasetum inconstans, commonly known as the variable catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific bees. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers with prominent lip structures. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum inconstans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
