Colubrina cubensis subsp. floridana

Colubrina cubensis subsp.

floridana, commonly known as Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces bright purple berries that are a notable feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
