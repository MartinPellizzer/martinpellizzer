Convolvulus xanthopotamicus

Convolvulus xanthopotamicus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
