Convolvulus obtusilobus

Convolvulus obtusilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that produce trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are heart-shaped with rounded lobes, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It blooms throughout the summer months and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
