Celosia pseudovirgata

Celosia pseudovirgata, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the showy bracts.

Celosia pseudovirgata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
