Calamus collinus

Calamus collinus, commonly known as the mountain calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features long, slender rhizomes that can reach several meters in length, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Calamus collinus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
