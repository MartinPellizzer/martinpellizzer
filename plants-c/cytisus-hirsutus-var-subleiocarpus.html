Cytisus hirsutus var. subleiocarpus

Cytisus hirsutus var. subleiocarpus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
