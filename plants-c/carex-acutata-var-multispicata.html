Carex acutata var. multispicata

Carex acutata var. multispicata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a fibrous root system. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often arranged in three to five vertical rows along the stem.

This variety is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
