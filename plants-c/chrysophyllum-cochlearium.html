Chrysophyllum cochlearium

Chrysophyllum cochlearium, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, green fruits with a white, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is native to tropical Africa and parts of Southeast Asia. Its fruit is edible, often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
