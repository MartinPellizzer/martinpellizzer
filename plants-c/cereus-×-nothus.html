Cereus × nothus

Cereus × nothus, commonly known as the hybrid night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Cereus × nothus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and unique flowering behavior.
