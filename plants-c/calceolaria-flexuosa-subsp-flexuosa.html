Calceolaria flexuosa subsp. flexuosa

Calceolaria flexuosa subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as the curlleaf calceolaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy coating, and exhibit a distinctive curling at the tips.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
