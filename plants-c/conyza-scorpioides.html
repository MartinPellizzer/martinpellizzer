Conyza scorpioides

Conyza scorpioides, commonly known as the horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and pastures. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Conyza scorpioides is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
