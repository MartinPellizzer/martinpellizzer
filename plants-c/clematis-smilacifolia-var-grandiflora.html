Clematis smilacifolia var. grandiflora

Clematis smilacifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant smilacifolia clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, ornamental vine.
