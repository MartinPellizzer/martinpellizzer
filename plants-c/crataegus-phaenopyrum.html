Crataegus phaenopyrum

Crataegus phaenopyrum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
