Canavalia obtusifolia var. versicolor

Canavalia obtusifolia var. versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its strikingly varied leaf colors, ranging from green to red, purple, and even white, which make it visually distinctive. The plant is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it a beneficial plant in agricultural systems.
