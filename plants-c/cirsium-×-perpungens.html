Cirsium × perpungens

Cirsium × perpungens, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Cirsium × perpungens is known for its robust growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
