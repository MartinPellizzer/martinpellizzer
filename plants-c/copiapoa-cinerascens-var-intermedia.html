Copiapoa cinerascens var. intermedia

Copiapoa cinerascens var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Copiapoa, is a columnar cactus native to northern Chile.

It features a tall, slender stem with a grayish-blue coloration and a rough, spiny texture. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
