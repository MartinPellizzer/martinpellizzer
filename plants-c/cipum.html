Cipum

Cipum, commonly known as the Indian cypress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conical shape. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a distinctive reddish-brown bark. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Cipum also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
