Cimicifuga ramosa

Cimicifuga ramosa, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Black cohosh is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
