Cucumis melo var. daree

Cucumis melo var. daree, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivar of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, slender fruit.

The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit develops from the female flowers and is usually harvested when it is still green and firm.

This variety is prized for its crisp texture and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for fresh consumption or use in salads.
