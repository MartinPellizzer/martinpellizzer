Capparis cinerea

Capparis cinerea, commonly known as the pale caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Capparis cinerea is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
