Clematis venosa

Clematis venosa, commonly known as the Veined Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often veined leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its cascading foliage and floral display.
