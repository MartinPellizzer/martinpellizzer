Cyperus blodgettii

Cyperus blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Cyperus blodgettii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
