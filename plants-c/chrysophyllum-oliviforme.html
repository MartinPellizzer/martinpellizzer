Chrysophyllum oliviforme

Chrysophyllum oliviforme, commonly known as the Natal plum or African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
