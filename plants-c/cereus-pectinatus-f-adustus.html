Cereus pectinatus f. adustus

Cereus pectinatus f. adustus, commonly known as the dark red cactus, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dark red to almost black spines, which distinguish it from other varieties. The plant has a tall, columnar stem with ribbed structures that aid in water storage. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing at the apex of the stem during the night.

This species is native to the arid regions of South America and is well adapted to hot, dry climates.
