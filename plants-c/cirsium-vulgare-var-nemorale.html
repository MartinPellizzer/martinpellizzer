Cirsium vulgare var. nemorale

Cirsium vulgare var. nemorale, commonly known as the wood thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in stiff, prickly hairs, and its leaves are deeply lobed with rough, serrated edges. Its flowers are purple to pink, forming dense, spherical clusters at the top of the plant.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities.
