Cassia angulata

Cassia angulata, commonly known as the sicklepod or angular cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its angular, ribbed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia angulata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
