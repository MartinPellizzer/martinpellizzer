Chamaecrista aiarana

Chamaecrista aiarana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its unique seed pods, which resemble burrs, aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.
