Cynosurus magellanicus

Cynosurus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellan's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds.

Cynosurus magellanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
