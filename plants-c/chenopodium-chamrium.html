Chenopodium chamrium

Chenopodium chamrium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential forage crop.
