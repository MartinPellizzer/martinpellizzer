Caprifolium aucheri

Caprifolium aucheri, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Caprifolium aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
