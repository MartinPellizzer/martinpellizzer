Chonemorpha macrophylla var. grandis

Chonemorpha macrophylla var. grandis, commonly known as the giant red hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or pink hues. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
