Chamaecyparis obtusa var. crippsii

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. crippsii, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or golden cypress, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-green foliage, which provides a striking contrast to its dark, fibrous bark. This evergreen conifer is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as a compact, conical shrub with a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small spaces.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
