Carex richii

Carex richii, commonly known as the rich's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in hue.

Carex richii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
