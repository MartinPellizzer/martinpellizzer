Coronilla vaginalis

Coronilla vaginalis, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Coronilla vaginalis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
