Convolvulus hispaniolae

Convolvulus hispaniolae, commonly known as the Spanish bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain environments.
