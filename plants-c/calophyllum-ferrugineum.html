Calophyllum ferrugineum

Calophyllum ferrugineum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
