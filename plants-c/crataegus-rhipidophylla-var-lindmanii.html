Crataegus rhipidophylla var. lindmanii

Crataegus rhipidophylla var. lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along streambanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
