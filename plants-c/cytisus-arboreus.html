Cytisus arboreus

Cytisus arboreus, commonly known as the tree broom, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Cytisus arboreus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
