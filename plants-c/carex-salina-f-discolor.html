Carex salina f. discolor

Carex salina f. discolor, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a variant of the salt marsh sedge native to coastal regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. Its foliage is typically bluish-gray, which helps it tolerate high salt concentrations.

The inflorescence consists of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of many sedges.
