Cassia mucronata

Cassia mucronata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in tropical landscapes.
