Conamomum spiceum

Conamomum spiceum, commonly known as the fragrant spice plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. Its leaves are often harvested for their essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
