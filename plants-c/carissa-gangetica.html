Carissa gangetica

Carissa gangetica, commonly known as the Natal plum or Indian hawthorn, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry regions.

Carissa gangetica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
