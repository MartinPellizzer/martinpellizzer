Chenopodium petiolare f. hastatum

Chenopodium petiolare f. hastatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its broad, lobed leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in moist environments. Its leaves are succulent and can be eaten raw or cooked, though they have a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
