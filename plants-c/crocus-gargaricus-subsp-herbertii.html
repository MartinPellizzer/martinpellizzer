Crocus gargaricus subsp. herbertii

Crocus gargaricus subsp.

herbertii, commonly known as the Herbert's crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in early spring.

The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically with six to eight petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
