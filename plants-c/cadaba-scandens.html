Cadaba scandens

Cadaba scandens, commonly known as the climbing cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly hairy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Cadaba scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
