Capparis leptophylla

Capparis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds.

Capparis leptophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
