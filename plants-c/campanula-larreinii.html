Campanula larreinii

Campanula larreinii, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Campanula larreinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
