Capsicum annuum var. nigrum

Capsicum annuum var. nigrum, commonly known as black pepper.

This variant is not actually a pepper but a type of Capsicum species that produces small, dark-colored fruits. It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions.
