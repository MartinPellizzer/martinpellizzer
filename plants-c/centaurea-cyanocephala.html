Centaurea cyanocephala

Centaurea cyanocephala, commonly known as the bluehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinguishing feature is the blue to violet-blue flower head, which consists of many tiny florets.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
