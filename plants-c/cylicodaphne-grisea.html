Cylicodaphne grisea

Cylicodaphne grisea, commonly known as the gray sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
