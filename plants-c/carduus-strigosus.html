Carduus strigosus

Carduus strigosus, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a robust, branching stem covered in stiff, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Carduus strigosus is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
