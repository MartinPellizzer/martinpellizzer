Cardamine pratensis f. alpina

Cardamine pratensis f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and in wet meadows. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
