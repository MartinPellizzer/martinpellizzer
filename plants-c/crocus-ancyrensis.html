Crocus ancyrensis

Crocus ancyrensis, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with six petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The Persian crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
