Crepis occidentalis var. glauca

Crepis occidentalis var. glauca, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them a bluish appearance.

The species is adapted to arid environments and has a low-growing habit with multiple stems.
