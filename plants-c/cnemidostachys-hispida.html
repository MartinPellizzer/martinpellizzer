Cnemidostachys hispida

Cnemidostachys hispida, commonly known as the woolly spike grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit, often forming low, mat-like clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its textured appearance and ability to provide ground cover in dry environments.
