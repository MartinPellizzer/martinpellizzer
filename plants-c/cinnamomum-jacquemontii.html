Cinnamomum jacquemontii

Cinnamomum jacquemontii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum jacquemontii, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries. The plant is valued for its aromatic bark, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural settings.
