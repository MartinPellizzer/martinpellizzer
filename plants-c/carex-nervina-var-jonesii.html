Carex nervina var. jonesii

Carex nervina var. jonesii, commonly known as Jones' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are followed by three-angled, spiky seed heads.
