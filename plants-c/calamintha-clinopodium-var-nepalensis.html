Calamintha clinopodium var. nepalensis

Calamintha clinopodium var. nepalensis, commonly known as Nepal mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The species is closely related to other members of the Calamintha genus, which are known for their medicinal and aromatic uses.
