Cassia fieldii

Cassia fieldii, commonly known as the field's cassia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Cassia fieldii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
