Crocus vernus var. andersonii

Crocus vernus var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the British Isles.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

This variety is noted for its reliability and ability to naturalize over time.
