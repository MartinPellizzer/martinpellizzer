Crassula macrantha

Crassula macrantha, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" or "Coral Bells," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple edges, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
