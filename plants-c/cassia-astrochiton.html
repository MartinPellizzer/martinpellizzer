Cassia astrochiton

Cassia astrochiton, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cassia astrochiton is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping or as a shade tree in suitable environments.
