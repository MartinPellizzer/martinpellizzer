Campanula rotundifolia subsp. velebitica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

velebitica, commonly known as the Velebit bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its rounded, heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cooler, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
