Chelidonium majus subsp. majus

Chelidonium majus subsp.

majus, commonly known as greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and its distinctive, branched, ribbed stems.

The plant contains alkaloids, including chelidonine, which have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, deeply lobed, and often covered in a sticky, milky sap.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
