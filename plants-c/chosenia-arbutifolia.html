Chosenia arbutifolia

Chosenia arbutifolia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Chosenia arbutifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
