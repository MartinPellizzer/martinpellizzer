Crassula spiciformis

Crassula spiciformis, commonly known as the Spiky Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces upright, spike-like clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
