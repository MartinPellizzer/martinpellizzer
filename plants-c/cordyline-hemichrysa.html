Cordyline hemichrysa

Cordyline hemichrysa, commonly known as the golden cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that range from bright green to golden yellow. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its vibrant foliage and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
