Critonia macropoda

Critonia macropoda, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its roots are known to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
