Crataegus crus-galli f. vulgaris

Crataegus crus-galli f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common hawthorn or maytree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and oval, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
