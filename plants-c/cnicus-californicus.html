Cnicus californicus

Cnicus californicus, commonly known as the California sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
