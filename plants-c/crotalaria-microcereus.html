Crotalaria microcereus

Crotalaria microcereus, commonly known as the small-headed crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can be toxic to livestock due to the presence of alkaloids in its foliage.
