Cocops rivalis

Cocops rivalis, commonly known as the Rival's coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces coconuts, which are encased in a fibrous husk and contain both water and milk. Its roots are adapted to thrive in sandy or coastal soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and providing resources for human use.
