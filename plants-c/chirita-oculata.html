Chirita oculata

Chirita oculata, commonly known as the eyeleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves that often have a prominent, eye-like marking on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Chirita oculata is valued for its unique foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
