Cassia pulchella

Cassia pulchella, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pulchella is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
