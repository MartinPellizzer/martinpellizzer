Clerodendrum robecchii

Clerodendrum robecchii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum robecchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
