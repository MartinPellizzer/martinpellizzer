Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. controversum

Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. controversum, commonly known as the Algerian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or wildflower meadows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
