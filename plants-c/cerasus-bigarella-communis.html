Cerasus bigarella communis

Cerasus bigarella communis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
