Cordia obliqua var. wallichii

Cordia obliqua var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan white cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
