Carex hieronymi

Carex hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikes, typical of the genus Carex. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
