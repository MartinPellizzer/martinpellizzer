Croton salvifolius

Croton salvifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
