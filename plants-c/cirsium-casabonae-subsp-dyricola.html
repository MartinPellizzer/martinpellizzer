Cirsium casabonae subsp. dyricola

Cirsium casabonae subsp.

dyricola, commonly known as the Dyricola thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with rough textures.

The plant produces showy purple or pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
