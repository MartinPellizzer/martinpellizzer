Cnicus kamtschaticus

Cnicus kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Cnicus kamtschaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
