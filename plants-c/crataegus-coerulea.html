Crataegus coerulea

Crataegus coerulea, commonly known as the blue hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Crataegus coerulea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
