Chrysanthellum indicum subsp. indicum

Chrysanthellum indicum subsp.

indicum, commonly known as the Indian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by petal-like rays.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
