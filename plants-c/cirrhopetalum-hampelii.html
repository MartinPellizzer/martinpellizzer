Cirrhopetalum hampelii

Cirrhopetalum hampelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, it is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, petal-like bracts that resemble petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cirrhopetalum hampelii is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
