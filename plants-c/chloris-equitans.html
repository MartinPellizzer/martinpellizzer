Chloris equitans

Chloris equitans, commonly known as the common grass or horse grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Chloris equitans is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant for livestock.
