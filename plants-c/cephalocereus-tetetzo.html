Cephalocereus tetetzo

Cephalocereus tetetzo, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive crown of spines and flowers. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
