Crepis armena subsp. armena

Crepis armena subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey, this plant thrives in rocky and arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the structure of its leaves and the arrangement of its florets.
