Campanula sibthorpiana

Campanula sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula sibthorpiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
