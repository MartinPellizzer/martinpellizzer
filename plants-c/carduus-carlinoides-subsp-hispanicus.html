Carduus carlinoides subsp. hispanicus

Carduus carlinoides subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features a robust taproot and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in coarse, hairy foliage.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a rough, serrated edge, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed soils and arid conditions.
