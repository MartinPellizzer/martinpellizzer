Crataegus berlandieri

Crataegus berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
