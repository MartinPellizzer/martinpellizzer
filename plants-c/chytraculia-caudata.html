Chytraculia caudata

Chytraculia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed chytrac, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers with showy, violet petals. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments with high humidity. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
