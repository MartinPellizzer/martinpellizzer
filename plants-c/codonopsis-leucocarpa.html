Codonopsis leucocarpa

Codonopsis leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, round, white fruits that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Codonopsis leucocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
