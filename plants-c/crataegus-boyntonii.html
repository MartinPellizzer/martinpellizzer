Crataegus boyntonii

Crataegus boyntonii, commonly known as the Boynton hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its simple, ovate leaves and persistent, spiny twigs.
