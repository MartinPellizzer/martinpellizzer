Cyathea nigrescens

Cyathea nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically dark green and may turn a darker shade as they mature.

Cyathea nigrescens plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various animals.
