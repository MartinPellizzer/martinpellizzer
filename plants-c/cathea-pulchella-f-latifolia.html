Cathea pulchella f. latifolia

Cathea pulchella f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cathea, is a cultivar of the cathea plant, which belongs to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often a striking silver-white with green margins. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the summer months. It is a compact, bushy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
