Ceropegia radians

Ceropegia radians, commonly known as the moon vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, often adorned with small, waxy leaves. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, spurred shape. These flowers are pollinated by specific species of moths, which are attracted to the plant's scent and nectar.

Ceropegia radians is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unusual floral display.
