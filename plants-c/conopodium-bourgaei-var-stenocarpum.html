Conopodium bourgaei var. stenocarpum

Conopodium bourgaei var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structures that house its seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
