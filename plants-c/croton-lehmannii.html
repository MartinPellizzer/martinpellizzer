Croton lehmannii

Croton lehmannii, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright yellow or green with contrasting patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is not recommended for indoor cultivation without proper precautions.
