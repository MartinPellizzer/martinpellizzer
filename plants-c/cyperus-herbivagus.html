Cyperus herbivagus

Cyperus herbivagus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
