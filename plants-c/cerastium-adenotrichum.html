Cerastium adenotrichum

Cerastium adenotrichum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cerastium adenotrichum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas.
