Cytisus supinus var. frivaldszkyanus

Cytisus supinus var. frivaldszkyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of bright yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and forest edges. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
