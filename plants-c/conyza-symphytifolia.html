Conyza symphytifolia

Conyza symphytifolia, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters.

Conyza symphytifolia is an annual herb that can spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common weed in many regions.
