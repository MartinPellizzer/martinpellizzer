Carex maximowiczii var. kinashii

Carex maximowiczii var. kinashii, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.
