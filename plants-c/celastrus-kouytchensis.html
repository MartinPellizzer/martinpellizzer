Celastrus kouytchensis

Celastrus kouytchensis, commonly known as the Kouytch or Kouytch vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated outside its natural range.
