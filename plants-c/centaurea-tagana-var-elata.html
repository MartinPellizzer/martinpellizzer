Centaurea tagana var. elata

Centaurea tagana var. elata, commonly known as the tall centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Centaurea tagana var. elata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
