Cousinia vvedenskyi

Cousinia vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Armenian spoonweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Cousinia vvedenskyi is often found in alpine and steppe regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
