Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. longipetiolata

Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled cyclopogon, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and distinctive, elongated petioles that give it its specific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or limestone substrates. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
