Connarus merizaldinua

Connarus merizaldinua, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
