Cirsium chlorocomos

Cirsium chlorocomos, commonly known as the greenstem thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers appear in late summer to early autumn.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
