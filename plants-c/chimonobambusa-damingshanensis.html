Chimonobambusa damingshanensis

Chimonobambusa damingshanensis, commonly known as the Daming Mountain bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Daming Mountains region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
