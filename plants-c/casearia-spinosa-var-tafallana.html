Casearia spinosa var. tafallana

Casearia spinosa var. tafallana, commonly known as the Tafallana casearia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
