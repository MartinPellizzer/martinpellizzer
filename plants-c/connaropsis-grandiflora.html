Connaropsis grandiflora

Connaropsis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered connar, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
