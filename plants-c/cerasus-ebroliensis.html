Cerasus ebroliensis

Cerasus ebroliensis, commonly known as the Ebro cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces berries that are used in local cuisines.

The Ebro cherry is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature.
