Crepis acuminata var. intermedia

Crepis acuminata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate prickly lettuce, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, tall, elongated inflorescence. Its fruit is a small, cylindrical achene with a pappus for wind dispersal.

It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
