Cyperus aureospicatus

Cyperus aureospicatus, commonly known as the golden spike sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spiky inflorescences that are often adorned with golden or reddish hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus aureospicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
