Commiphora edulis subsp. edulis

Commiphora edulis subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of East Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers and is known for its ability to exude a gum resin, which has been used for centuries. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has cultural significance in various regions.
