Commiphora zanzibarica var. elongata

Commiphora zanzibarica var. elongata, commonly known as the Zanzibar myrrh, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is exuded from the bark and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in tropical climates. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is valued for its unique fragrance and historical use in traditional medicine and incense.
