Clerodendrum thyrsoideum

Clerodendrum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy inflorescences.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slightly serrated margin.
