Carex banksii var. distans

Carex banksii var. distans, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-ranked spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
