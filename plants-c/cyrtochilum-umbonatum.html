Cyrtochilum umbonatum

Cyrtochilum umbonatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, and it produces a pleasant, subtle fragrance.

Cyrtochilum umbonatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
