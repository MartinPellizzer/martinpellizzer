Chisocheton frutescens

Chisocheton frutescens, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a rough, bark-like texture on its trunk and branches, and its leaves are typically opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
