Calamintha ferreri

Calamintha ferreri, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Calamintha ferreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
