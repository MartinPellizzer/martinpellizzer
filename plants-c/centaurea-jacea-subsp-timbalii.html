Centaurea jacea subsp. timbalii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalus knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
