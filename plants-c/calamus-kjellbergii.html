Calamus kjellbergii

Calamus kjellbergii, commonly known as the Kjellberg's arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus kjellbergii is native to tropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
