Cyperus poeoides

Cyperus poeoides, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Cyperus poeoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland areas.
