Cornulaca aucheri

Cornulaca aucheri, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure adapted to harsh desert environments. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

Cornulaca aucheri is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in sandy areas.
