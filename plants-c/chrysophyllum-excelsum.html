Chrysophyllum excelsum

Chrysophyllum excelsum, commonly known as the Natal plum or sweet-scented plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Chrysophyllum excelsum is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in traditional medicine.
