Carex hebecarpa var. lachnosperma

Carex hebecarpa var. lachnosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
