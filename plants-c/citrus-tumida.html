Citrus tumida

Citrus tumida, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, leathery rind. It is known for its high acidity and is often used in the production of marmalade and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
