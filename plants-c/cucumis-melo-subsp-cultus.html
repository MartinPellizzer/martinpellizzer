Cucumis melo subsp. cultus

Cucumis melo subsp.

cultus, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant is a climbing vine that produces yellow flowers and develops into a round or oval fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright orange color and develops a sweet, honey-like flavor.
