Cornus mas f. polonica

Cornus mas f. polonica, commonly known as the Polish dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus mas species, which is native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, orange, or yellow fruits that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardens.

The fruits are edible but are often used in jams and preserves due to their tart flavor.
