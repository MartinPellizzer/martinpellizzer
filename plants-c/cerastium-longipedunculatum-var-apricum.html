Cerastium longipedunculatum var. apricum

Cerastium longipedunculatum var. apricum, commonly known as the spring whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often appearing before the leaves are fully developed. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and garden use in wetter climates.
