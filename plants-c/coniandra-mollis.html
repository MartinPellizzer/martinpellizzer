Coniandra mollis

Coniandra mollis, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flower heads that resemble miniature daisies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, growing in a basal rosette.

Coniandra mollis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
