Crataegus bissellii

Crataegus bissellii, commonly known as the Bissell hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique floral and fruit morphology.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, contributing to local biodiversity.
