Carex stipata var. subsecuta

Carex stipata var. subsecuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads known as spikes, which are usually green to brown in color and are often found in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
