Cyperus longi-involucratus

Cyperus longi-involucratus, commonly known as the long-involucrate flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often subtended by long, narrow bracts.

Cyperus longi-involucratus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
