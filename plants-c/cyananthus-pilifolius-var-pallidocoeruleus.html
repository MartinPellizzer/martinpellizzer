Cyananthus pilifolius var. pallidocoeruleus

Cyananthus pilifolius var. pallidocoeruleus, commonly known as the pale sky-blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers with a pale sky-blue hue. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.

The species is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.
