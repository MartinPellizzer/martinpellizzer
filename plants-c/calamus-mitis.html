Calamus mitis

Calamus mitis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its aesthetic qualities.

Calamus mitis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
