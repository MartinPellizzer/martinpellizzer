Chamaecytisus supinus subsp. pannonicus

Chamaecytisus supinus subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a shrub native to the Pannonian region of Central Europe. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
