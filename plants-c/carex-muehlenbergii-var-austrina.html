Carex muehlenbergii var. austrina

Carex muehlenbergii var. austrina, commonly known as the Australian marsh sedge, is a perennial sedge native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swampy areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are followed by spikelets that mature into seeds.
