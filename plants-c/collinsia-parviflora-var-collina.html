Collinsia parviflora var. collina

Collinsia parviflora var. collina, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic value in native gardens.
