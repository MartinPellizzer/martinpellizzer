Copernicia hospita

Copernicia hospita, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, paddle-shaped leaves and produces large, woody pods that contain the edible Brazil nuts. The tree has a shallow root system and thrives in humid, tropical climates with high rainfall. It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also economically significant due to the valuable nuts it produces.
