Centaurea debeauxii var. nemoralis

Centaurea debeauxii var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood centaurea, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
