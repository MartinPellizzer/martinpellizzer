Camellia thea var. assamica

Camellia thea var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam tea plant, is a variant of the tea plant native to the eastern Himalayas and Assam region of India.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are harvested for the production of black and green tea.

This variety is well adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.
