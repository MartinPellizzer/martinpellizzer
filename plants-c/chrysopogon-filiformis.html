Chrysopogon filiformis

Chrysopogon filiformis, commonly known as slender grass or filiform grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along water bodies. It has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Chrysopogon filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
