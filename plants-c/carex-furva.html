Carex furva

Carex furva, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
