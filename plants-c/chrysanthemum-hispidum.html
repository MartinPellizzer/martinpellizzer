Chrysanthemum hispidum

Chrysanthemum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Chrysanthemum hispidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
