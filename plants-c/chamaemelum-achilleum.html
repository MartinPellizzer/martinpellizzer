Chamaemelum achilleum

Chamaemelum achilleum, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that consist of numerous small white or pink florets. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Chamaemelum achilleum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
