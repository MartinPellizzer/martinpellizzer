Calamagrostis diemii

Calamagrostis diemii, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tan foliage that turns a striking coppery hue in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its flowering stems are tall and erect, producing panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Calamagrostis diemii is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
