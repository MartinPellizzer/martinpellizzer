Callicarpa albida

Callicarpa albida, commonly known as the white beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are particularly noticeable in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Callicarpa albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
