Coffea montekupensis

Coffea montekupensis, commonly known as the Montekupensis coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and white, tubular flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.
