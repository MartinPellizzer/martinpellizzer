Claytonia peirsonii subsp. peirsonii

Claytonia peirsonii subsp.

peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features rosettes of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
