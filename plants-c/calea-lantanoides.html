Calea lantanoides

Calea lantanoides, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Calea lantanoides is also known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
