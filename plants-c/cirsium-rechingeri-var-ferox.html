Cirsium rechingeri var. ferox

Cirsium rechingeri var. ferox, commonly known as the ferocious thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its robust structure and defensive spines are adaptations to its harsh, cold climate.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
