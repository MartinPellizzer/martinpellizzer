Campanopsis angustifolia

Campanopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Campanopsis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
