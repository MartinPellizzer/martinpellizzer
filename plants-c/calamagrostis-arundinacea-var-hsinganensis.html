Calamagrostis arundinacea var. hsinganensis

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. hsinganensis, commonly known as the Hsingan reed, is a perennial grass native to northern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and helping to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
