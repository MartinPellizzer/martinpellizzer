Coryphantha panarottoi

Coryphantha panarottoi, commonly known as the Panarotto's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically features white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has spines that are often clustered at the base of the ribs, providing protection from herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
