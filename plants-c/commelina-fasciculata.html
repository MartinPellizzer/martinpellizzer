Commelina fasciculata

Commelina fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking floral structures. The plant typically features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a common weed and a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
