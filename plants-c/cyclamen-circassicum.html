Cyclamen circassicum

Cyclamen circassicum, commonly known as the Armenian cyclamen, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces vibrant, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in harsh conditions, making it resilient to winter dormancy.

Cyclamen circassicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
