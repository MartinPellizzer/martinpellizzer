Celsia australis

Celsia australis, commonly known as the Southern Celsia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
