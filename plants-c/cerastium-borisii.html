Cerastium borisii

Cerastium borisii, commonly known as Boris's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Cerastium borisii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
