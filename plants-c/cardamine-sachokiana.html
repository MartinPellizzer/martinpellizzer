Cardamine sachokiana

Cardamine sachokiana, commonly known as the Himalayan watercress, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinct, peppery aroma, and its small white flowers are arranged in racemes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional Himalayan cultures.
