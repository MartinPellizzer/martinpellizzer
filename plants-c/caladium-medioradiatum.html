Caladium medioradiatum

Caladium medioradiatum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is its most striking feature, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is typically grown as an annual in regions with colder winters.
