Cardamine maritima

Cardamine maritima, commonly known as sea hart's tongue, is a small, perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along shorelines, tolerating saline and wind-swept environments. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a single umbel, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Cardamine maritima plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
