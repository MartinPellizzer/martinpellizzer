Cyathea aculeata

Cyathea aculeata, commonly known as the spiny tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with some bearing small spines along the margins.

Cyathea aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
