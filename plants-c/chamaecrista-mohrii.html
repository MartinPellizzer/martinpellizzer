Chamaecrista mohrii

Chamaecrista mohrii, commonly known as the Mohri's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny seed pods that mature into woody capsules. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or savannas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
