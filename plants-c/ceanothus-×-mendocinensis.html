Ceanothus × mendocinensis

Ceanothus × mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and inland chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
