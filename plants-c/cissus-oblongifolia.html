Cissus oblongifolia

Cissus oblongifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or Indian nettle, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or trees.

Its ability to climb makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
