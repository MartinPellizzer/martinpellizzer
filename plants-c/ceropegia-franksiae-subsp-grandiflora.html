Ceropegia franksiae subsp. grandiflora

Ceropegia franksiae subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the giant African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
