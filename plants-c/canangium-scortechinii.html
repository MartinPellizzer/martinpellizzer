Canangium scortechinii

Canangium scortechinii, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and have a prominent central pistil. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Canangium scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
