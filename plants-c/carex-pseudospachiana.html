Carex pseudospachiana

Carex pseudospachiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
