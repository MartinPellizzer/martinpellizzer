Callista lobata

Callista lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Callista lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
