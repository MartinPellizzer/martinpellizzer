Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. petiolariforme

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

petiolariforme, commonly known as the slender goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically covered in a white, powdery coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
