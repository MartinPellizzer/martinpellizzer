Crataegus monogyna var. praecox

Crataegus monogyna var. praecox, commonly known as the early hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open woodlands.

The early hawthorn is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
