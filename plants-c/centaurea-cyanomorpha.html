Centaurea cyanomorpha

Centaurea cyanomorpha, commonly known as the bluehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea cyanomorpha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
