Campanula versicolor subsp. tenorei

Campanula versicolor subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Variable Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
