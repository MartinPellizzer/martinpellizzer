Cunonia simplicia

Cunonia simplicia, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, brown pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cunonia simplicia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
