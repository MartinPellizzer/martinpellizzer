Centaurea glaucescens

Centaurea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Centaurea glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
