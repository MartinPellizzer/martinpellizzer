Cardia praecox

Cardia praecox, commonly known as the early cardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cardia praecox is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
