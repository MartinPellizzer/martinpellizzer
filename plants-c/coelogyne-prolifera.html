Coelogyne prolifera

Coelogyne prolifera, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often in clusters. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne prolifera is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in humid, well-drained conditions.
