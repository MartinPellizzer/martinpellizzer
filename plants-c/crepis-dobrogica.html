Crepis dobrogica

Crepis dobrogica, commonly known as the Dobrogic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
