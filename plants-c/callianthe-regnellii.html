Callianthe regnellii

Callianthe regnellii, commonly known as the Regnell's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, which helps attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Callianthe regnellii is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
