Caucaea alticola

Caucaea alticola, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Caucaea alticola is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
