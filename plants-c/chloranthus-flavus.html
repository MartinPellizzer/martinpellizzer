Chloranthus flavus

Chloranthus flavus, commonly known as the yellow chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
