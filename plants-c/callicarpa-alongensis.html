Callicarpa alongensis

Callicarpa alongensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple to black berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
