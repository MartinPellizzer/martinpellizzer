Centropogon albolimbatus var. serratus

Centropogon albolimbatus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are ovate with serrated margins, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
