Convolvulus sagittatus var. aschersonii

Convolvulus sagittatus var. aschersonii, commonly known as the arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain regions.
