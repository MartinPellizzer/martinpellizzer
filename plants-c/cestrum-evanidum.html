Cestrum evanidum

Cestrum evanidum, commonly known as the evening glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum evanidum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
