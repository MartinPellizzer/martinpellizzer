Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. latilobum

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lipped chrysanthemum, is a variant of the chrysanthemum species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
