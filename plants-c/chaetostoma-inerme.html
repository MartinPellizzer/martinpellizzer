Chaetostoma inerme

Chaetostoma inerme, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It has a short life cycle and is typically annual or biennial.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
