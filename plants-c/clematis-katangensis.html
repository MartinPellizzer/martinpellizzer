Clematis katangensis

Clematis katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga Clematis, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces blue to purple flowers with five petals, and its foliage is usually trifoliate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
