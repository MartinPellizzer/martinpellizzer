Cynorkis gibbosa f. aurea

Cynorkis gibbosa f. aurea, commonly known as the golden cushion plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with fleshy, cylindrical stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water and thrive in arid conditions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form attractive, low-growing mounds in well-drained soil.
