Calamus pseudoulur

Calamus pseudoulur, commonly known as the false ulu reed, is a member of the Aroid family, Araceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus pseudoulur is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
