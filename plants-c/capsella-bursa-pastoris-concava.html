Capsella bursa-pastoris concava

Capsella bursa-pastoris concava, commonly known as the common weed or sheep's purse, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens. Its fruit is a short, inflated silique that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
