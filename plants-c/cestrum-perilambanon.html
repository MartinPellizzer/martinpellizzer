Cestrum perilambanon

Cestrum perilambanon, commonly known as the red cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Cestrum perilambanon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance, which attracts pollinators like moths.
