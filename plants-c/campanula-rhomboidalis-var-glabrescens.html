Campanula rhomboidalis var. glabrescens

Campanula rhomboidalis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which become less prominent as the plant matures.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
