Chrysanthemum × stipulaceum

Chrysanthemum × stipulaceum, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including white, yellow, red, and purple, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also have historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
