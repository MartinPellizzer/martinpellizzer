Cerasus officinalis

Cerasus officinalis, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. These fruits are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high acidity and antioxidant properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
