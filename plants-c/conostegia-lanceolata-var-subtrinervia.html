Conostegia lanceolata var. subtrinervia

Conostegia lanceolata var. subtrinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined corymbia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
