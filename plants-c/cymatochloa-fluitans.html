Cymatochloa fluitans

Cymatochloa fluitans, commonly known as the floating grass, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the water surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis.

Cymatochloa fluitans plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing water flow and providing habitat for microorganisms.
