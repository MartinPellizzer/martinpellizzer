Centaurea uniflora subsp. ferdinandi

Centaurea uniflora subsp.

ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and smaller, more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
