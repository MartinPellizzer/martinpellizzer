Coleus prittwitzii

Coleus prittwitzii, commonly known as the Pritchard's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white.

Coleus prittwitzii thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
