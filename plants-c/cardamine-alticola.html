Cardamine alticola

Cardamine alticola, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream sides. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a single umbel, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Cardamine alticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
