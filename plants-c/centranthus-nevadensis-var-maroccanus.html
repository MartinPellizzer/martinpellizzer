Centranthus nevadensis var. maroccanus

Centranthus nevadensis var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan red campion, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It features showy, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
