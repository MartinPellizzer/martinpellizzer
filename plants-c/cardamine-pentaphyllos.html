Cardamine pentaphyllos

Cardamine pentaphyllos, commonly known as the five-leafed water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into five distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a raceme, often emerging in early spring.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
