Chalybea corymbosa

Chalybea corymbosa, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are small and clustered in corymbs, giving the plant its scientific name.
