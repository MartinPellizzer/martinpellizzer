Ceratocephalus anactinus

Ceratocephalus anactinus, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution along coastal areas.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various insects and birds.
