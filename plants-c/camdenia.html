Camdenia

Camdenia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Camdenia has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

It is an important species for pollinators and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and North America.
