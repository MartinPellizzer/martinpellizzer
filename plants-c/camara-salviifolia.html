Camara salviifolia

Camara salviifolia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic life and improving water quality.

Additionally, it holds cultural and religious significance in many Asian countries.
