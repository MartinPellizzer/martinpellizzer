Cucurbita pepo var. icterina

Cucurbita pepo var. icterina, commonly known as the yellow squash or yellow crookneck squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, elongated fruits that typically measure between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a matte green surface and prominent veins. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in culinary applications.
