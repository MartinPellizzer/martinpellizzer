Carex echinus

Carex echinus, commonly known as the spiny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically grass-like in appearance, with a smooth or slightly rough surface.

Carex echinus often thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
