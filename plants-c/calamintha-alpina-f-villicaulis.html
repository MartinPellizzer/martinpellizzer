Calamintha alpina f. villicaulis

Calamintha alpina f. villicaulis, commonly known as the alpine calamint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
