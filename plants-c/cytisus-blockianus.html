Cytisus blockianus

Cytisus blockianus, commonly known as the Balkan broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cytisus blockianus is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
