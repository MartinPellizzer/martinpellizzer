Cyperus guadalajaranus

Cyperus guadalajaranus, commonly known as the Guadalajara flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus guadalajaranus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
