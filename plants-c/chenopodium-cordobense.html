Chenopodium cordobense

Chenopodium cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as utricles.

Chenopodium cordobense is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
