Ceropegia lodarensis var. foetida

Ceropegia lodarensis var. foetida, commonly known as the stinking ceropegia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing plant with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and scent, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
