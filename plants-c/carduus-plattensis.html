Carduus plattensis

Carduus plattensis, commonly known as the flatweed thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, preferring rich, loamy soils. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's robust root system helps prevent soil erosion in wetland environments.
