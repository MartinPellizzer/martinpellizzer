Carex albolutescens var. ferruginea

Carex albolutescens var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are often tinged with rust-colored hues, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
