Calceolaria fruticosa subsp. attenuata

Calceolaria fruticosa subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender calceolaria, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with distinctive calyx-like structures.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
