Chrysocephalum sericeum

Chrysocephalum sericeum, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silver everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and silvery, finely hairy leaves. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in compact clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Chrysocephalum sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
