Cissus rhamnoidea

Cissus rhamnoidea, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cissus rhamnoidea is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
