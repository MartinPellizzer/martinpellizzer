Campanula simplex

Campanula simplex, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Campanula simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
