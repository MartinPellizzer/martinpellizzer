Cissampelos testudinum

Cissampelos testudinum, commonly known as the turtle vine, is a climbing shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a cover plant.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
