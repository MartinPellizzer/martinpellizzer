Convolvulus hystrix f. inermis

Convolvulus hystrix f. inermis, commonly known as the softleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is less prickly than the typical Convolvulus hystrix, making it more suitable for ornamental gardens.
