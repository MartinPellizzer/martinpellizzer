Cyathostemma excelsum

Cyathostemma excelsum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting darker throat. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit includes trailing or climbing stems, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.
