Cookia anisodora

Cookia anisodora, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Cookia anisodora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
