Carex oreocharis var. enantiomorpha

Carex oreocharis var. enantiomorpha, commonly known as the twisted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its unique morphological features, including the twisted arrangement of its stems, make it a distinctive and interesting species for botanical study.
