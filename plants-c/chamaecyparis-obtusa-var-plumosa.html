Chamaecyparis obtusa var. plumosa

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. plumosa, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or creeping cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cypress.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive feathery, pendulous foliage that gives it a soft, cascading appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous cones. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.

This variety is native to Japan and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in garden landscapes.
