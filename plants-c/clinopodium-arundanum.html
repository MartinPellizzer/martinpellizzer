Clinopodium arundanum

Clinopodium arundanum, commonly known as meadow betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Clinopodium arundanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
