Coursetia cajamarcana

Coursetia cajamarcana, commonly known as the Peruvian thornbush, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
