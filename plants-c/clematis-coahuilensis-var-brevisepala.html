Clematis coahuilensis var. brevisepala

Clematis coahuilensis var. brevisepala, commonly known as the Coahuila clematis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and hairy. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a short corolla and five sepals.

This variety is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
