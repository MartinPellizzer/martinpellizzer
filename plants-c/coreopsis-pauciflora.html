Coreopsis pauciflora

Coreopsis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Coreopsis pauciflora is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
