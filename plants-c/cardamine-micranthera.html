Cardamine micranthera

Cardamine micranthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in shady, wet environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a peppery aroma, similar to that of other members of its family.

The small white flowers are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
