Carex anomoea

Carex anomoea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex anomoea plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
