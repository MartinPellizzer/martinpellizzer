Clusia cassinioides

Clusia cassinioides, commonly known as the broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Clusia cassinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
