Carex vulpinoidea var. annectens

Carex vulpinoidea var. annectens, commonly known as the fox sedge.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is typically found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in other wet habitats.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
