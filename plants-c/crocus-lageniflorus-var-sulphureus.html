Crocus lageniflorus var. sulphureus

Crocus lageniflorus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur yellow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces striking, saffron-yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped bloom that opens in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sunny locations. Its bulbs are adapted to cold climates and can be cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value.

The flowers are often associated with the symbolism of renewal and the arrival of spring.
