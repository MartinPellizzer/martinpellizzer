Ceradenia itatiaiensis

Ceradenia itatiaiensis, commonly known as the Itatiaia orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex structure with a spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are often pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss, Ceradenia itatiaiensis is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
