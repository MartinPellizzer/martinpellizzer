Cereus × flemingii

Cereus × flemingii, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and striking, nocturnal flowers that bloom only during the night. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners interested in desert plants.
