Clematis acapulcensis var. puberula

Clematis acapulcensis var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent clematis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically white or pale blue flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
