Catunaregam pentandra

Catunaregam pentandra, commonly known as the Indian nettle or *Urena lobata*, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring erect stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
