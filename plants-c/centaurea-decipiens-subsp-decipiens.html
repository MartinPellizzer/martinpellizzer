Centaurea decipiens subsp. decipiens

Centaurea decipiens subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its foliage is generally gray-green with hairy leaves, and it produces bright blue or purple flowers.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
