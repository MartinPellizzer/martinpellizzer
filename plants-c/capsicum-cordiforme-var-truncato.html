Capsicum cordiforme var. truncato

Capsicum cordiforme var. truncato, commonly known as the heart-shaped pepper, is a unique variety of Capsicum species characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, glandular stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and funnel-shaped, typical of the Solanaceae family. The fruits are usually small, red or yellow when mature, and have a mild to moderately spicy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
