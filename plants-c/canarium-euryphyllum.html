Canarium euryphyllum

Canarium euryphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
