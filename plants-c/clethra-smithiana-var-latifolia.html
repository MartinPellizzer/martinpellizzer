Clethra smithiana var. latifolia

Clethra smithiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
