Carex aristata

Carex aristata, commonly known as the arrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Carex aristata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
