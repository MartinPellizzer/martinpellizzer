Cassia gossweileri

Cassia gossweileri, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Cassia gossweileri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
