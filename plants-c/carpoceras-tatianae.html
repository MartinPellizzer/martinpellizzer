Carpoceras tatianae

Carpoceras tatianae, commonly known as the Tatiana's tamarisk, is a flowering plant belonging to the Tamaricaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
