Convolvulus ifniensis

Convolvulus ifniensis, commonly known as the Ifni morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
