Cirsium inuloides

Cirsium inuloides, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem with gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a globular shape, giving it its common name.

Cirsium inuloides is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
