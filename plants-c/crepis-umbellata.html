Crepis umbellata

Crepis umbellata, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Crepis umbellata is a perennial plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
