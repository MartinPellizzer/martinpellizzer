Cassia ferruginea

Cassia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia ferruginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
