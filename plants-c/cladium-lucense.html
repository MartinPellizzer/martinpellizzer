Cladium lucense

Cladium lucense, commonly known as the marsh pennywort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
