Clematis glaucescens

Clematis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and has a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis glaucescens thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
