Capparis commutata

Capparis commutata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits that enclose a cluster of seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
