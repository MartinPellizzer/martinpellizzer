Crataegus jenningsii

Crataegus jenningsii, commonly known as the Jennings hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
