Campanula giesekiana var. arctica

Campanula giesekiana var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in polar ecosystems.
