Carex oklahomensis

Carex oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. Its grass-like appearance features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex oklahomensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
