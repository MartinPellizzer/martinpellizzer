Chamaecrista bauhiniifolia

Chamaecrista bauhiniifolia, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a woody base and soft, branching stems.
