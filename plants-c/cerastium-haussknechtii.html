Cerastium haussknechtii

Cerastium haussknechtii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
