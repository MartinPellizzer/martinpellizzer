Clematis erectisepala

Clematis erectisepala, commonly known as the upright-sepaled clematis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pale blue flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its sepals are distinctly erect, giving it its specific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
