Campanula lanceolata subsp. arcuata

Campanula lanceolata subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the curved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow environments. It produces small, capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
