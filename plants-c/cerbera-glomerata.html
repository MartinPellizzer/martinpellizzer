Cerbera glomerata

Cerbera glomerata, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by bright orange-red berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Cerbera glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
