Clematis alpina var. kraettliana

Clematis alpina var. kraettliana, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that can climb to heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
