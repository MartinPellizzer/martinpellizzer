Cordia trichotoma f. tomentosa

Cordia trichotoma f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, white tomentum. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries.

It is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
