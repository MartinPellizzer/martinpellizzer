Capsicum inaequale

Capsicum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces yellow, orange, or red fruits that are often irregularly shaped, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
