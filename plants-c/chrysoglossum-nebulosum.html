Chrysoglossum nebulosum

Chrysoglossum nebulosum, commonly known as the cloud-tongued daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, fluffy white to pale blue flowers that resemble clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
