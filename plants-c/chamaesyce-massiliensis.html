Chamaesyce massiliensis

Chamaesyce massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its botanical features include a prostrate stem and small, clustered inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.
