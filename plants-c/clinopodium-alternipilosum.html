Clinopodium alternipilosum

Clinopodium alternipilosum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy, and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue and are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Clinopodium alternipilosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
