Cuphea calcarata

Cuphea calcarata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Cuphea calcarata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
