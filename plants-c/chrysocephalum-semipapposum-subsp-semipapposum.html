Chrysocephalum semipapposum subsp. semipapposum

Chrysocephalum semipapposum subsp.

semipapposum, commonly known as the slender golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, bract-like structures called pappus at the base of its flowers, which aid in seed dispersal.
