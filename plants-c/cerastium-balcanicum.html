Cerastium balcanicum

Cerastium balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Cerastium balcanicum is adapted to cool, moist environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
