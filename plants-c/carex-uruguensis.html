Carex uruguensis

Carex uruguensis, commonly known as the Uruguayan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex uruguensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
