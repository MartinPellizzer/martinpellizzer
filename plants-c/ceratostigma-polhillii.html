Ceratostigma polhillii

Ceratostigma polhillii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a compact growth habit with dark green foliage that turns reddish in the winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
