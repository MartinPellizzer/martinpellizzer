Chamaeanthus laxus

Chamaeanthus laxus, commonly known as the slender rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or open mountainous habitats. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically pink or purple in color.

Chamaeanthus laxus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
