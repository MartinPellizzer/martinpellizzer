Crotalaria andringitrensis

Crotalaria andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria andringitrensis is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
