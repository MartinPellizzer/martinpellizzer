Cirsium carminans

Cirsium carminans, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Cirsium carminans typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
