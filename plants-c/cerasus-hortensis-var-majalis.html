Cerasus hortensis var. majalis

Cerasus hortensis var. majalis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in late summer.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
