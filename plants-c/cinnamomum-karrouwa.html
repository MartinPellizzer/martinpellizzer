Cinnamomum karrouwa

Cinnamomum karrouwa, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is harvested for its essential oils and medicinal properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval, leathery leaves that are rich in essential oils. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and economic significance.
