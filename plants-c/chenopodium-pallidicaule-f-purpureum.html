Chenopodium pallidicaule f. purpureum

Chenopodium pallidicaule f. purpureum, commonly known as purple-leafed goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves, which give it a striking appearance. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas or along roadsides. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its edible leaves, which can be used in cooking.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
