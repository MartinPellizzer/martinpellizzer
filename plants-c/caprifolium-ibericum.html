Caprifolium ibericum

Caprifolium ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are red berries that attract various bird species.

Caprifolium ibericum plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is often found in Mediterranean ecosystems.
