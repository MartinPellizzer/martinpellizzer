Chamaecyparis obtusa f. gracilis

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. gracilis, commonly known as the Japanese lacewood or slender Japanese cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a unique, weeping habit.

It features scale-like leaves that are dense and soft to the touch, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and has a shallow root system that makes it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, exhibiting good tolerance to urban conditions.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and graceful form, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
