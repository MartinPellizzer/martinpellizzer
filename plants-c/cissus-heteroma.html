Cissus heteroma

Cissus heteroma, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that aid in climbing. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
