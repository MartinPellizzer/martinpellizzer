Cnicus taquetii

Cnicus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elongated, ribbed fruits.
