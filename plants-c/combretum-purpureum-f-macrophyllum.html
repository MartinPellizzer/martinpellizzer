Combretum purpureum f. macrophyllum

Combretum purpureum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
