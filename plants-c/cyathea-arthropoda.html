Cyathea arthropoda

Cyathea arthropoda, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer of dead leaves. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea arthropoda reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This species plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
