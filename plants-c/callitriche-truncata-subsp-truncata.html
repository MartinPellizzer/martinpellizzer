Callitriche truncata subsp. truncata

Callitriche truncata subsp.

truncata, commonly known as narrowleaf water-starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and marshes.

The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are tiny, with four petals, and it produces small, bladder-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in shallow waters and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich substrates.
