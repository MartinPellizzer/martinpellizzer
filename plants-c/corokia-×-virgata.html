Corokia × virgata

Corokia × virgata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which contrast with its dark green upper surfaces. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Corokia × virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
