Caladium glycyrrhizum

Caladium glycyrrhizum, commonly known as the licorice plant, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that often display striking variegation. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

The roots of Caladium glycyrrhizum are the primary part used, containing compounds that give it its distinctive licorice flavor.
