Cheilanthes bolborrhiza

Cheilanthes bolborrhiza, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are covered by a protective indusium.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
