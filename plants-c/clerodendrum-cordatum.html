Clerodendrum cordatum

Clerodendrum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
