Caryotaxus myristica

Caryotaxus myristica, commonly known as the African nutmeg, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to nutmeg.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique, fragrant properties.
