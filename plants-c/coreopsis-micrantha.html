Coreopsis micrantha

Coreopsis micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf coreopsis, is a compact perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a dark center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has fine, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to garden spaces.
