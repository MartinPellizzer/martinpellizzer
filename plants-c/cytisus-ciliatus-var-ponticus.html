Cytisus ciliatus var. ponticus

Cytisus ciliatus var. ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic broom, is a shrub native to the Black Sea region.

It features slender, woody stems with small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is also characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
