Capsicum salicifolium

Capsicum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf chili, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on maturity. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
