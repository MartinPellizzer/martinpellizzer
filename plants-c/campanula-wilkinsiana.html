Campanula wilkinsiana

Campanula wilkinsiana, commonly known as the Wilkins' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula wilkinsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
