Calamus clemensianus

Calamus clemensianus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Calamus clemensianus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
