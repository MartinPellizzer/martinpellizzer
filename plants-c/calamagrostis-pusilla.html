Calamagrostis pusilla

Calamagrostis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf reed or small reed, is a low-growing grass native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
