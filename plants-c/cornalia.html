Cornalia

Cornalia, commonly known as the cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cornalia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
