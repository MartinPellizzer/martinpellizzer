Calderonia

Calderonia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Calderonia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
