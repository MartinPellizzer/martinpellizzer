Clinopodium omuranum

Clinopodium omuranum, commonly known as Japanese mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
