Cattleya perrinii

Cattleya perrinii, commonly known as the Perin's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting lip. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cattleya perrinii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to cultivation.
