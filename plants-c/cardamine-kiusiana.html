Cardamine kiusiana

Cardamine kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusi watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in stream banks and forest floors.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
