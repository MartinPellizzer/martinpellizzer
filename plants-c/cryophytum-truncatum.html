Cryophytum truncatum

Cryophytum truncatum, commonly known as the snowdrop plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water in harsh, cold environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand freezing temperatures.

Cryophytum truncatum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing early-season nectar for pollinators.
