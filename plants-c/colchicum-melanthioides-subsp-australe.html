Colchicum melanthioides subsp. australe

Colchicum melanthioides subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian meadow saffron, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb that produces striking, showy flowers with reflexed petals and a yellow-orange stamen.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids, similar to other members of the Colchicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
