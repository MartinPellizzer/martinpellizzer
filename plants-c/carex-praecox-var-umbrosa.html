Carex praecox var. umbrosa

Carex praecox var. umbrosa, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
