Cyathea petiolulata var. pastoensis

Cyathea petiolulata var. pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.

The Pasto fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
