Chamaecytisus drepanolobus subsp. gibbsii

Chamaecytisus drepanolobus subsp.

gibbsii, commonly known as the Gibbs' broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has wiry, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in landscaping.
