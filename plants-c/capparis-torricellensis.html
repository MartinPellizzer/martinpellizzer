Capparis torricellensis

Capparis torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricelli's caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by edible, acidic berries. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis torricellensis is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in shaded undergrowth.
