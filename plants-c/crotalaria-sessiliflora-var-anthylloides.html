Crotalaria sessiliflora var. anthylloides

Crotalaria sessiliflora var. anthylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its specific epithet. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
