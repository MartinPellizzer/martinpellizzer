Centaurea funkii var. melanolepis

Centaurea funkii var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its floral structure consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
