Catasetum oerstedii

Catasetum oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous showy flowers.

Catasetum oerstedii is noted for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized structures that facilitate the transfer of pollen to specific pollinators.
