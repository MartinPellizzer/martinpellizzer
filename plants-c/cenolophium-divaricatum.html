Cenolophium divaricatum

Cenolophium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
