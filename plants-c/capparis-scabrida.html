Capparis scabrida

Capparis scabrida, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and hairy, often thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis scabrida is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
