Clutia myricoides

Clutia myricoides, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clutia myricoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
