Cissus gigantea

Cissus gigantea, commonly known as the giant grape ivy, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus gigantea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and rapid growth.
