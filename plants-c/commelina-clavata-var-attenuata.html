Commelina clavata var. attenuata

Commelina clavata var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are clustered in umbels.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
