Cyphostemma rubroglandulosum

Cyphostemma rubroglandulosum, commonly known as the red glandular cyphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with reddish glandular dots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
