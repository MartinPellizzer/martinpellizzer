Campanula graminifolia var. linearifolia

Campanula graminifolia var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are linear and often slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
