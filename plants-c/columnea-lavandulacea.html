Columnea lavandulacea

Columnea lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavenderleaf gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Columnea lavandulacea is popular in hanging baskets and as a decorative indoor plant due to its vibrant blooms and elegant foliage.
