Clinogyne hensii

Clinogyne hensii, commonly known as the Hensii's mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, such as wetlands and streambanks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Clinogyne hensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
