Canavalia hirsuta

Canavalia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy jackbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its long, winding stems and bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Canavalia hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
