Crotalaria longifoliolata var. latior

Crotalaria longifoliolata var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a distinctive feature of producing bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans. Its growth habit includes spreading through both seeds and rhizomes, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The botanical interest in this species lies in its chemical composition and potential use in traditional medicine.
