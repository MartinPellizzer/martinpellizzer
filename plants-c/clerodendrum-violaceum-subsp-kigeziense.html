Clerodendrum violaceum subsp. kigeziense

Clerodendrum violaceum subsp.

kigeziense, commonly known as the Kigezi bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
