Carex japonica var. naipiangensis

Carex japonica var. naipiangensis, commonly known as the Naipiang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ability to thrive in moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
