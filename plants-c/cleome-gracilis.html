Cleome gracilis

Cleome gracilis, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

Cleome gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
