Chamaerhodos corymbosa var. brevifolia

Chamaerhodos corymbosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved redroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red stems that stand out against its grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking stem color and ability to add texture to shaded landscapes.
