Croton acapulcensis

Croton acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with lobes that can range from bright green to deep red, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton acapulcensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
