Cleome parvipetala

Cleome parvipetala, commonly known as the small-flowered cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Cleome parvipetala is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plants.
