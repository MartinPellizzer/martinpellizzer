Crepidium asperum

Crepidium asperum, commonly known as the rough orchid, is a species of orchid native to various regions of the Americas.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stout rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears several leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinctive, rough texture on the petals and sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
