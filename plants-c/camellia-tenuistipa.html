Camellia tenuistipa

Camellia tenuistipa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.

Camellia tenuistipa is also valued for its ability to withstand mild frost, making it adaptable to a range of temperate climates.
