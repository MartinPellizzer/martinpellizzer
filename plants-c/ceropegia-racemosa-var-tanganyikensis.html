Ceropegia racemosa var. tanganyikensis

Ceropegia racemosa var. tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyikan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern African region of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes.
