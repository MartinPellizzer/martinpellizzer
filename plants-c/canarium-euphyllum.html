Canarium euphyllum

Canarium euphyllum, commonly known as the white beach nut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by hard, woody fruits containing edible nuts. The tree is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
