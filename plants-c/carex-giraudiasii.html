Carex giraudiasii

Carex giraudiasii, commonly known as the Giraud's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its preference for wet habitats, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
