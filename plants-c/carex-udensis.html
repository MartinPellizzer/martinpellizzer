Carex udensis

Carex udensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in wet environments.

Carex udensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
