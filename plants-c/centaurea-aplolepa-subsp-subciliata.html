Centaurea aplolepa subsp. subciliata

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

subciliata, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems with sparsely distributed leaves that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of ray florets surrounded by a series of tubular flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to dry, rocky habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
