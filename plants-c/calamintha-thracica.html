Calamintha thracica

Calamintha thracica, commonly known as Thrace calamint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antispasmodic properties.

Calamintha thracica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
