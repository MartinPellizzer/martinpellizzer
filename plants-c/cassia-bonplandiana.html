Cassia bonplandiana

Cassia bonplandiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Cassia bonplandiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
