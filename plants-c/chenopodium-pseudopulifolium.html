Chenopodium pseudopulifolium

Chenopodium pseudopulifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Chenopodium pseudopulifolium is an annual herb that is sometimes considered a weed but can also serve as a food source for certain wildlife.
