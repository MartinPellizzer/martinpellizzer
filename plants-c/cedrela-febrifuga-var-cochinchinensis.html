Cedrela febrifuga var. cochinchinensis

Cedrela febrifuga var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese cedar or Cochinchina cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for furniture and construction. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
