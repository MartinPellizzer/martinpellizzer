Carex × bogstadensis

Carex × bogstadensis, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that form dense, cylindrical clusters. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils, often found in bogs and marshes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
