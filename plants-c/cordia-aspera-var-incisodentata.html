Cordia aspera var. incisodentata

Cordia aspera var. incisodentata, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
