Croton occidentalis var. oblongifolius

Croton occidentalis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that vary in color from deep green to reddish-brown.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
