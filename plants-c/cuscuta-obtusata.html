Cuscuta obtusata

Cuscuta obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that involves germinating on the surface of host plants and then developing specialized structures to attach to them. The plant is often found parasitizing various species of grasses and shrubs in arid and semi-arid environments. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a challenging weed in agricultural settings.

Despite its parasitic nature, Cuscuta obtusata plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant populations and providing food for certain insects and animals.
