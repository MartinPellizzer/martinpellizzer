Convolvulus commutatus

Convolvulus commutatus, commonly known as the fringed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscaping applications.
