Cyclamen repandum subsp. creticum

Cyclamen repandum subsp.

creticum, commonly known as the Cretan cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Crete. It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive silver-green mottling and produces clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded rock crevices or woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the colder months. Cyclamen repandum subsp.

creticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
