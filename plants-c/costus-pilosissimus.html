Costus pilosissimus

Costus pilosissimus, commonly known as the velvet costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance and fragrance.
