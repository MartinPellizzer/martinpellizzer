Croton fluminensis

Croton fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazonian croton, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Croton fluminensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the rich, nutrient-dense soils of the Amazon rainforest.
