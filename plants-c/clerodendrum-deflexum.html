Clerodendrum deflexum

Clerodendrum deflexum, commonly known as the hanging clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Clerodendrum deflexum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
