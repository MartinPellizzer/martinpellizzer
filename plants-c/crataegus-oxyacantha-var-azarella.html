Crataegus oxyacantha var. azarella

Crataegus oxyacantha var. azarella, commonly known as the Azarella hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and they serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
