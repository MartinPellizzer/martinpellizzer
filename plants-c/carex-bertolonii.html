Carex bertolonii

Carex bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex bertolonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
