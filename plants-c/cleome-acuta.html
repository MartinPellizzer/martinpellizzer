Cleome acuta

Cleome acuta, commonly known as sharp cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome acuta thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
