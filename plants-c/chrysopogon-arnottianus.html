Chrysopogon arnottianus

Chrysopogon arnottianus, commonly known as the Australian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland margins.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
