Cavendishia tenella

Cavendishia tenella, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cavendishia tenella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to add visual interest to shaded gardens.
