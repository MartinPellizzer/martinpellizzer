Cratoxylum petiolatum

Cratoxylum petiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cratoxylum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a petiole. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
