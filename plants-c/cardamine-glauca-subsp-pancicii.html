Cardamine glauca subsp. pancicii

Cardamine glauca subsp.

pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's watercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and in damp woodlands.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are usually short and sprawling, with the leaves emerging from the base.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
