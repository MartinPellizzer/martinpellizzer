Colchicum cruciatum

Colchicum cruciatum, commonly known as the crossbloom or crucifix colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall, slender scape with a single, spurred flower that resembles a cross. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
