Cardamine mexicana

Cardamine mexicana, commonly known as Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to watercress.
