Cynanchum umtalense

Cynanchum umtalense, commonly known as African snake root, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corolla that features a hood-like structure.

Cynanchum umtalense is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
