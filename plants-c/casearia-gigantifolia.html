Casearia gigantifolia

Casearia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
