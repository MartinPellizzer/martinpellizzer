Copiapoa columna-alba var. nuda

Copiapoa columna-alba var. nuda, commonly known as the White Column Cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, white to pale green stem and lack of spines. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
