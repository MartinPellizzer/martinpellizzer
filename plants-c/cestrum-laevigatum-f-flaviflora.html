Cestrum laevigatum f. flaviflora

Cestrum laevigatum f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its most notable botanical feature is its clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The flowers are followed by small, round berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.
