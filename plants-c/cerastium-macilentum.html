Cerastium macilentum

Cerastium macilentum, commonly known as slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
