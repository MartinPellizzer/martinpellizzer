Calamagrostis cuzcoensis

Calamagrostis cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco reed, is a perennial grass native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are typically panicle-shaped and produce small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis cuzcoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
