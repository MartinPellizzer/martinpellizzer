Caragana tragacanthoides var. himalaica

Caragana tragacanthoides var. himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan caragana, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to harsh mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and alpine habitats.
