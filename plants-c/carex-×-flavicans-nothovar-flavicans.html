Carex × flavicans nothovar. flavicans

Carex × flavicans nothovar. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a hybrid plant species within the Carex genus.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
