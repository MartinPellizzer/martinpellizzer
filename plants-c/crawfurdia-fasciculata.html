Crawfurdia fasciculata

Crawfurdia fasciculata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy surface, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

Crawfurdia fasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
