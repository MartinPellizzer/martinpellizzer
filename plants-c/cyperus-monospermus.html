Cyperus monospermus

Cyperus monospermus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus monospermus thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
