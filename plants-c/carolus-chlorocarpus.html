Carolus chlorocarpus

Carolus chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem with smooth bark and a dense canopy of foliage.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.
