Cassidocarpus chilensis

Cassidocarpus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean holly, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, glossy berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in late summer.
