Carex simpliciuscula

Carex simpliciuscula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
