Centaurea meskhetica

Centaurea meskhetica, commonly known as the Caucasian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called achenes. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Centaurea meskhetica is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
