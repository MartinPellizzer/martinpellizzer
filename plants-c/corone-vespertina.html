Corone vespertina

Corone vespertina, commonly known as the evening crown, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to gardens and natural habitats.
