Crocus sulphureus var. isabellinus

Crocus sulphureus var. isabellinus, commonly known as the Isabelline crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a subtle, sweet fragrance during early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms clumps that spread over time. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
