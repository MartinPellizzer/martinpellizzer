Carpha filifolia

Carpha filifolia, commonly known as the silky grass or silky flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Carpha filifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
