Coleus penzigii

Coleus penzigii, commonly known as the Penzig's coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other coleus species. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Coleus penzigii thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance.
