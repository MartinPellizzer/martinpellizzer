Chenopodium album subsp. viride

Chenopodium album subsp.

viride, commonly known as green lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine.
