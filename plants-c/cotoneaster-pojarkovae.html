Cotoneaster pojarkovae

Cotoneaster pojarkovae, commonly known as the Pojarkov's cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Cotoneaster pojarkovae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
