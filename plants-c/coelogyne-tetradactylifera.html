Coelogyne tetradactylifera

Coelogyne tetradactylifera, commonly known as the four-fingered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne tetradactylifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
