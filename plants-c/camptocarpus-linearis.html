Camptocarpus linearis

Camptocarpus linearis, commonly known as the slender camptocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
