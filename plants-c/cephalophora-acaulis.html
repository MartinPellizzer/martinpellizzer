Cephalophora acaulis

Cephalophora acaulis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs.

Cephalophora acaulis is often found in coastal and inland grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
