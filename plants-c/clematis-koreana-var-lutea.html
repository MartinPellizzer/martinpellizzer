Clematis koreana var. lutea

Clematis koreana var. lutea, commonly known as the Korean yellow clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features slender, climbing stems with compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include palmate leaflets and a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
