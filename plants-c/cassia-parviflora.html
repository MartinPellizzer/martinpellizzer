Cassia parviflora

Cassia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and summer. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia parviflora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
