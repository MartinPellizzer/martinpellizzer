Calyptranthes polyantha

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, winged seeds when mature.

Calyptranthes polyantha is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
