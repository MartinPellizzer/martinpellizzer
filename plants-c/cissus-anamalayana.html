Cissus anamalayana

Cissus anamalayana, commonly known as the Malabar Devil's Ivy, is a climbing vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with a fine layer of white pubescence, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Cissus anamalayana is adapted to humid, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade.
