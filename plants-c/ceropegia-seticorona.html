Ceropegia seticorona

Ceropegia seticorona, commonly known as the stiff crown ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often spiny and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
