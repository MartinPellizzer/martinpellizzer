Convolvulus papiru

Convolvulus papiru, commonly known as paperleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
