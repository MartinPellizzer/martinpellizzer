Clusia arrudea

Clusia arrudea, commonly known as the Brazilian shieldleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or shield-shaped, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Clusia arrudea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
