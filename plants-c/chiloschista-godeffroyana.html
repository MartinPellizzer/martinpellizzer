Chiloschista godeffroyana

Chiloschista godeffroyana, commonly known as the Godeffroy's rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
