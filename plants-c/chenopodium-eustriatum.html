Chenopodium eustriatum

Chenopodium eustriatum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Silverleaf goosefoot is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also considered a potential invasive species in certain regions.
