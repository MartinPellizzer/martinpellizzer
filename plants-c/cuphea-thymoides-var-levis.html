Cuphea thymoides var. levis

Cuphea thymoides var. levis, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into fall.
