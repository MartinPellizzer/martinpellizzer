Chondrilla purpurea

Chondrilla purpurea, commonly known as purple burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny seed pods. The plant typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters tall and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Chondrilla purpurea is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
