Chrysanthemum heterotomum

Chrysanthemum heterotomum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its blooming period occurs in the fall, adding vibrant color to gardens during the autumn season.
