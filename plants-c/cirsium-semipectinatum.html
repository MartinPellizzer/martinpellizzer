Cirsium semipectinatum

Cirsium semipectinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in rocky or dry soils. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.
