Centaurea carratracensis

Centaurea carratracensis, commonly known as the Carratracense knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking purple to pinkish-red flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bristly seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
