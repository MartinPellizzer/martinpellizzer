Capsicum luteum

Capsicum luteum, commonly known as the yellow pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in traditional dishes.
