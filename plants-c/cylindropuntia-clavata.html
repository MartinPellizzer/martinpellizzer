Cylindropuntia clavata

Cylindropuntia clavata, commonly known as the birdcage cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Cylindropuntia clavata is a slow-growing plant that is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.
