Cirsium serratuloides

Cirsium serratuloides, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
