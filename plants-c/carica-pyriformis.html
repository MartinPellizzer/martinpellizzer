Carica pyriformis

Carica pyriformis, commonly known as the horned papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped fruit, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains seeds that are often removed before consumption.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caricaceae family and is closely related to the common papaya.
