Calliandra macrocephala var. foliolosa

Calliandra macrocephala var. foliolosa, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bracteate inflorescences, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, delicate leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
