Conophytum rugosum subsp. sanguineum

Conophytum rugosum subsp.

sanguineum, commonly known as the blood-flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often red or purple in color, which help it store water and protect against intense sunlight.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
