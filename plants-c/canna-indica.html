Canna indica

Canna indica, commonly known as the Indian shot or yellow canna, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, colorful leaves that can reach up to two meters in height, with broad, lance-shaped blades that are often variegated in green and yellow. The plant produces striking inflorescences composed of bright orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Canna indica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
