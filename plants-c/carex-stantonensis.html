Carex stantonensis

Carex stantonensis, commonly known as Stanton's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex stantonensis is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
