Chenopodium berlandieri subsp. pseudopetiolare

Chenopodium berlandieri subsp.

pseudopetiolare, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is native to the central and eastern United States, where it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, prickly texture along the margins. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, rounded berries that contain seeds.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source.
