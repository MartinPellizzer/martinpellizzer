Crocus nevadensis subsp. marcetii

Crocus nevadensis subsp.

marcetii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada crocus, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. It features delicate, nodding flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring, showcasing shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
