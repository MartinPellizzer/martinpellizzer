Carex paludosa proles spadicea

Carex paludosa proles spadicea, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex paludosa proles spadicea plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
