Carduus thessalus

Carduus thessalus, commonly known as the Thessalian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
