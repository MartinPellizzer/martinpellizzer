Ceanothus glomeratus

Ceanothus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen, providing year-round interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ceanothus glomeratus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
