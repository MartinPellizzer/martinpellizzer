Conyza burkartii

Conyza burkartii, commonly known as Burkart's fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Conyza burkartii is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in regions with mild temperatures.
