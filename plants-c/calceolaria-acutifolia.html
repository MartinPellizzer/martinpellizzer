Calceolaria acutifolia

Calceolaria acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved slipper orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and acute, giving it its common name, while its flowers bloom in spring and early summer.

Calceolaria acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, exotic appearance.
