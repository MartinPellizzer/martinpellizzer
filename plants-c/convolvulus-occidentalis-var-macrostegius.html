Convolvulus occidentalis var. macrostegius

Convolvulus occidentalis var. macrostegius, commonly known as the western bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
