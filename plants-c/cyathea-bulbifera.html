Cyathea bulbifera

Cyathea bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbiferous tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant reproduces through spores, which are produced on the undersides of its fronds in specialized structures called sporangia. One distinctive feature of Cyathea bulbifera is its ability to produce bulbils, small plantlets that develop at the base of its fronds and can grow into new plants.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
