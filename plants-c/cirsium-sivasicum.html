Cirsium sivasicum

Cirsium sivasicum, commonly known as the Sivas thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually purple or blue in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
