Capparis nobilis var. laurina

Capparis nobilis var. laurina, commonly known as the laurel caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and aromatic, evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
