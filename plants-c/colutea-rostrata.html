Colutea rostrata

Colutea rostrata, commonly known as the shieldflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Colutea rostrata is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
