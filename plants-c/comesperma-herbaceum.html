Comesperma herbaceum

Comesperma herbaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comesperma, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
