Chrysopogon asper

Chrysopogon asper, commonly known as rough grassvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Chrysopogon asper is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
