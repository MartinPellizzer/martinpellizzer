Celtis australis var. panormitana

Celtis australis var. panormitana, commonly known as the Mediterranean hackberry, is a variant of the common hackberry native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and urban environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
