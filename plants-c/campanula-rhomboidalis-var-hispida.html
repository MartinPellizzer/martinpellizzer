Campanula rhomboidalis var. hispida

Campanula rhomboidalis var. hispida, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are ovate to rhomboid in shape, with a hairy texture that gives the variety its name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
