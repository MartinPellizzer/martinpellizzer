Carex platyrhina

Carex platyrhina, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Carex platyrhina plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
