Crotalaria ankaizinensis

Crotalaria ankaizinensis, commonly known as the African bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, inflated pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
