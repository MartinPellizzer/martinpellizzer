Cassia geniculata

Cassia geniculata, commonly known as the sicklepod or Indian laburnum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with 8 to 16 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Cassia geniculata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
