Canna sulphurea

Canna sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Canna sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
