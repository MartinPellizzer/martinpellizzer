Chionanthus macrophyllus var. attenuatus

Chionanthus macrophyllus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whiteflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
