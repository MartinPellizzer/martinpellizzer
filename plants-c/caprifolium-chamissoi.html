Caprifolium chamissoi

Caprifolium chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.
