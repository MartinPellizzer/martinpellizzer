Cotula sarawaketensis

Cotula sarawaketensis, commonly known as the Sarawak cotula, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Cotula sarawaketensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
