Cotula acaenoides

Cotula acaenoides, commonly known as the common buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the warmer months. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cotula acaenoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
