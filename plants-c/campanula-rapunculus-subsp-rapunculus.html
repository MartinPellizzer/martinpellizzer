Campanula rapunculus subsp. rapunculus

Campanula rapunculus subsp.

rapunculus, commonly known as the bellflower or mouse-ear bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
