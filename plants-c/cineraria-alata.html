Cineraria alata

Cineraria alata, commonly known as the winged cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long-lasting blooms.
