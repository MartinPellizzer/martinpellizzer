Clarkeasia parviflora var. albescens

Clarkeasia parviflora var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered Clarkeasia, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant aroma. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
