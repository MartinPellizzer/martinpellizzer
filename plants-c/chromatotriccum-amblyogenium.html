Chromatotriccum amblyogenium

Chromatotriccum amblyogenium, commonly known as the sticky bryum, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, green to yellow-green foliage and the presence of small, sticky structures on its stems. The plant exhibits a simple morphology with leaf-like structures that are usually arranged in two ranks. It reproduces through spores, which are released from capsules that develop on specialized shoots.

Chromatotriccum amblyogenium is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of forest floor ecosystems.
