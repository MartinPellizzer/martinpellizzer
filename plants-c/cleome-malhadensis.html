Cleome malhadensis

Cleome malhadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spindly stems and showy, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome malhadensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and ease of care.
