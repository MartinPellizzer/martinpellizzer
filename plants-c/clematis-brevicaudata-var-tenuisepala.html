Clematis brevicaudata var. tenuisepala

Clematis brevicaudata var. tenuisepala, commonly known as the slender-sepal clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The flowers are showy, with five sepals that are narrow and elongated, giving the plant its common name. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, white to pale blue flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
