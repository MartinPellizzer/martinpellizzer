Cissus grisebachii

Cissus grisebachii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
