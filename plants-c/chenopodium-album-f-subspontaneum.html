Chenopodium album f. subspontaneum

Chenopodium album f. subspontaneum, commonly known as wild spinach or fat hen, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seed capsules are oblong and contain numerous tiny seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world, including North America.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
