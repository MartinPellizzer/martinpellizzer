Chionanthus caribaeus

Chionanthus caribaeus, commonly known as the Caribbean chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and seasonal flowering.
