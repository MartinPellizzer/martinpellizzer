Cymbidium ensifolium var. koran

Cymbidium ensifolium var. koran, commonly known as the Korean orchid, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
