Conophytum parvipetalum

Conophytum parvipetalum, commonly known as the dwarf flowered conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Conophytum parvipetalum is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
