Chloris alberti

Chloris alberti, commonly known as the Albert's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to blue-green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Chloris alberti is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
