Cyathea pseudogigantea

Cyathea pseudogigantea, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Cyathea pseudogigantea plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
