Coreopsis notha var. typica

Coreopsis notha var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to its habitat.
