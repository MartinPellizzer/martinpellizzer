Carex jamesonii

Carex jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex jamesonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
