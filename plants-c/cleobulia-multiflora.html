Cleobulia multiflora

Cleobulia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered cleobulia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cleobulia multiflora is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
