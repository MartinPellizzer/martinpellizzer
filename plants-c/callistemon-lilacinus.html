Callistemon lilacinus

Callistemon lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to lilac. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
