Cordyline forsteri

Cordyline forsteri, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, unbranched stems with large, sword-shaped leaves that are often lined with white or pink veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
