Cepa ventricosa

Cepa ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble balloons, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

The botanical features of Cepa ventricosa make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens.
