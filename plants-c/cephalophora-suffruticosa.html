Cephalophora suffruticosa

Cephalophora suffruticosa, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure with woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Cephalophora suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
