Coleus helenae

Coleus helenae, commonly known as Helen's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and come in shades of red, orange, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring moderate moisture and regular fertilization.

This plant is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
