Cabralea pilosa

Cabralea pilosa, commonly known as the silky cabralea, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
