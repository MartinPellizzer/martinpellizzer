Crepis rubicaulis

Crepis rubicaulis, commonly known as the redstem mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Crepis rubicaulis is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
