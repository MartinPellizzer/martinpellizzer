Convolvulus cantabrica f. hirsutus

Convolvulus cantabrica f. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy rock bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Its fibrous roots make it a hardy species that can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
