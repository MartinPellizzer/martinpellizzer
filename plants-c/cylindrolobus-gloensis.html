Cylindrolobus gloensis

Cylindrolobus gloensis, commonly known as the gloe's cylinderleaf, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
