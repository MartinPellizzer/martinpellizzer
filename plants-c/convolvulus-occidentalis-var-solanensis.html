Convolvulus occidentalis var. solanensis

Convolvulus occidentalis var. solanensis, commonly known as the western bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
