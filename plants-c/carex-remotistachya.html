Carex remotistachya

Carex remotistachya, commonly known as the distant-stamen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically brown or greenish in color.

Carex remotistachya is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
