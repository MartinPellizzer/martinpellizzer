Citrosma aspera

Citrosma aspera, commonly known as rough citrosma, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
