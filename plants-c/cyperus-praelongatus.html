Cyperus praelongatus

Cyperus praelongatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus praelongatus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
