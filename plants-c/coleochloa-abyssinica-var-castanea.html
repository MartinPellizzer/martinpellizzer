Coleochloa abyssinica var. castanea

Coleochloa abyssinica var. castanea, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass or Ethiopian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping or as a cover crop in dry regions.
