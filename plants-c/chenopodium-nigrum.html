Chenopodium nigrum

Chenopodium nigrum, commonly known as black goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium nigrum is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tart flavor and are used in various culinary traditions.
