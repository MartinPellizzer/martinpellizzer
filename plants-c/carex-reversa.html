Carex reversa

Carex reversa, commonly known as the reverse sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically found at the ends of the stems.

Carex reversa thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows.
