Combretum sylvicola

Combretum sylvicola, commonly known as the forest combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are usually greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.
