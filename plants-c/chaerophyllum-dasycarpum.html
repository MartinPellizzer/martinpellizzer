Chaerophyllum dasycarpum

Chaerophyllum dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves and a flowering stem that bears small, white to greenish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
