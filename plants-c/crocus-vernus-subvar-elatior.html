Crocus vernus subvar. elatior

Crocus vernus subvar. elatior, commonly known as the greater crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet to lilac flowers with a distinctive, showy crested style. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season floral display.
