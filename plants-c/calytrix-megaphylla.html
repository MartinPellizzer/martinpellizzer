Calytrix megaphylla

Calytrix megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaf calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

Calytrix megaphylla is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
