Cyperus javanicus

Cyperus javanicus, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus javanicus is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
