Cattleya tenebrosa var. pittiana

Cattleya tenebrosa var. pittiana, commonly known as the Pitta's dark cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with deep purple to violet petals and a contrasting white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
