Cordia radula

Cordia radula, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Cordia, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
