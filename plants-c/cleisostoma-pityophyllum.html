Cleisostoma pityophyllum

Cleisostoma pityophyllum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, torch-like shape. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Cleisostoma pityophyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
