Carex acuta var. amblylepis

Carex acuta var. amblylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the plant relying on wind pollination for reproduction.
