Cestrum langeanum

Cestrum langeanum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring thick, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant is renowned for its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by moths and bats.

Cestrum langeanum thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
