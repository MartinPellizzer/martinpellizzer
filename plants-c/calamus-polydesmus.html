Calamus polydesmus

Calamus polydesmus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in wet, tropical environments, typically found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
