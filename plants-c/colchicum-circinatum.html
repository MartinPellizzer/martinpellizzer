Colchicum circinatum

Colchicum circinatum, commonly known as the Alpine meadow saffron, is a species of flowering plant in the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, particularly in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces striking purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

The plant is characterized by its basal leaves and its ability to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments.
