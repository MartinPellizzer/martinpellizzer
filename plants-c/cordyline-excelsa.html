Cordyline excelsa

Cordyline excelsa, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer called the rhyzome. The plant produces large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Cordyline excelsa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique form.
