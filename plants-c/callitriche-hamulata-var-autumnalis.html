Callitriche hamulata var. autumnalis

Callitriche hamulata var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn waterstar, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water, often in still or slow-moving streams, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, floating or submerged leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and its distinctive, star-like appearance.
