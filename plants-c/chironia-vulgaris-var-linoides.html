Chironia vulgaris var. linoides

Chironia vulgaris var. linoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Chironia species, leading to variability in its morphological features.
