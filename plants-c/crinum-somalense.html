Crinum somalense

Crinum somalense, commonly known as the Somali rain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Somalia.

It features long, strap-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in wet, open areas and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes many bulbous plants.

Crinum somalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
