Cactus intortus f. desiradensis

Cactus intortus f. desiradensis, commonly known as the Desert Cactus, is a unique variant of the intortus cactus species.

It is characterized by its twisted, spiral-shaped stems that provide structural support and aid in water retention. The plant features spiny areoles with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its succulent leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This variety is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
