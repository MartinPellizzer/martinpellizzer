Cuspidaria erubescens var. glabrescens

Cuspidaria erubescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth red spikegrass, is a variant of the red spikegrass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its smooth, hairless appearance compared to other variants of the species.
