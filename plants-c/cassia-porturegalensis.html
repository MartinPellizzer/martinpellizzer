Cassia porturegalensis

Cassia porturegalensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, seed-filled seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
