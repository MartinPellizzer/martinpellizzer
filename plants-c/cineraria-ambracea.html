Cineraria ambracea

Cineraria ambracea, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Cineraria ambracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
