Calamus yuangchunensis

Calamus yuangchunensis, commonly known as the Yuangchun bamboo palm, is a rare species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
