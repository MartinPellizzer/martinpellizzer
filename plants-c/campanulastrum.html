Campanulastrum

Campanulastrum, commonly known as the bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped flowers, which range in color from blue to purple. They typically grow in temperate regions and are often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. Campanulastrum species are herbaceous, with some varieties being perennial and others annual.

The genus includes several species, such as Campanulastrum quadrifidum, which is native to North America.
