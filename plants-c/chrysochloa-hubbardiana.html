Chrysochloa hubbardiana

Chrysochloa hubbardiana, commonly known as the Hubbard's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a decorative appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowering stems produce small, pale green to yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chrysochloa hubbardiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in arid landscaping.
