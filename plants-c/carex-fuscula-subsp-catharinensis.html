Carex fuscula subsp. catharinensis

Carex fuscula subsp.

catharinensis, commonly known as the Catharinense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Brazil. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
