Cryptochloa dressleri

Cryptochloa dressleri, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which are followed by tiny, cylindrical seed pods. Cryptochloa dressleri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
