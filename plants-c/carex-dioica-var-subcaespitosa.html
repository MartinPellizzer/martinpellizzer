Carex dioica var. subcaespitosa

Carex dioica var. subcaespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and alternate leaves. The plant typically forms dense, clump-forming growths, with male and female flowers occurring on separate inflorescences, a feature typical of dioecious species. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
