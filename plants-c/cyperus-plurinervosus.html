Cyperus plurinervosus

Cyperus plurinervosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a tall, flowering spike. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in suitable habitats.

The flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.
