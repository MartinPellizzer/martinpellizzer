Cyathea sagittifolia

Cyathea sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive arrowhead-shaped base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea sagittifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
