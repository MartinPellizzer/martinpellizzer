Corydalis govaniana var. uniyali

Corydalis govaniana var. uniyali, commonly known as the pink corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
