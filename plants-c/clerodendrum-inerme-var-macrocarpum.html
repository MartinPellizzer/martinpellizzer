Clerodendrum inerme var. macrocarpum

Clerodendrum inerme var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and attractive foliage.
