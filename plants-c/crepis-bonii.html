Crepis bonii

Crepis bonii, commonly known as the Bonii's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis bonii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
