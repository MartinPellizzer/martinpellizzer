Calamagrostis haenkeana

Calamagrostis haenkeana, commonly known as the Haenke's reed, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, and its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis haenkeana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
