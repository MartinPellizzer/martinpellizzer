Colinil cinereum

Colinil cinereum, commonly known as the silver-haired cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Colinil cinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
