Calamus mattanensis

Calamus mattanensis, commonly known as the Mattan's arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically pollinated by wind.

The rhizomes of Calamus mattanensis are valued for their starch content and are used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
