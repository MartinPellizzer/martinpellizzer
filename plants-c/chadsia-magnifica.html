Chadsia magnifica

Chadsia magnifica, commonly known as the splendid chadsia, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem and produces fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
