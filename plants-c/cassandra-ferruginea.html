Cassandra ferruginea

Cassandra ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The bark and seeds contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
