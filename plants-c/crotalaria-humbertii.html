Crotalaria humbertii

Crotalaria humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria humbertii is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
