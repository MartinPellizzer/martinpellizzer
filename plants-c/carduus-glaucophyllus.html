Carduus glaucophyllus

Carduus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the glaucous thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in a whitish, powdery coating called bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually purple to pink in color and bloom in the summer months.
