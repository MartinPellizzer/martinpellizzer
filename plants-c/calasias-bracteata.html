Calasias bracteata

Calasias bracteata, commonly known as the bracted calasias, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, located beneath the vibrant bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and color.
