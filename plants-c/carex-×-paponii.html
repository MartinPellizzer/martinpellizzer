Carex × paponii

Carex × paponii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant within the Carex genus, native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and forest floors. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a rough texture. Its flowering spike is composed of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often grouped in clusters.

Carex × paponii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
