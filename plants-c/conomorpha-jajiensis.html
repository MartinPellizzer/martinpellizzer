Conomorpha jajiensis

Conomorpha jajiensis, commonly known as the Jaji orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring intricate petals and a specialized lip structure. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or on rocky outcrops. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
