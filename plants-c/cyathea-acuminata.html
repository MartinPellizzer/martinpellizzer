Cyathea acuminata

Cyathea acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

Cyathea acuminata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
