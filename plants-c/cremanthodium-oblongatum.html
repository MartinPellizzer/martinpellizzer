Cremanthodium oblongatum

Cremanthodium oblongatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, lantern-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
