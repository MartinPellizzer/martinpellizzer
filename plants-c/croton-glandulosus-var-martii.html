Croton glandulosus var. martii

Croton glandulosus var. martii, commonly known as the Martii Croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and glandular hairs on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.
