Carex alma

Carex alma, commonly known as the Alma sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of North America and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically brown or greenish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Carex alma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
