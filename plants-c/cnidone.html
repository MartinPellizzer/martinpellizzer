Cnidone

Cnidone, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually silvery-gray with a rough texture, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

Cnidone is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
