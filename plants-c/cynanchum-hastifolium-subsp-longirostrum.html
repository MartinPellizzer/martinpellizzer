Cynanchum hastifolium subsp. longirostrum

Cynanchum hastifolium subsp.

longirostrum, commonly known as the long-beaked milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically borne on long pedicels. Its milky sap, which is characteristic of the Asclepiadaceae family, is a notable botanical feature.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
