Celtis occidentalis var. audibertiana

Celtis occidentalis var. audibertiana, commonly known as the Audibertian Hackberry, is a variant of the common hackberry tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different environmental conditions.

The Audibertian Hackberry is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
