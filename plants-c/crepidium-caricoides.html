Crepidium caricoides

Crepidium caricoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a dense spike, and are pollinated by insects.

Crepidium caricoides is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
