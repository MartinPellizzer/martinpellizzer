Citrus × aurantium var. medica

Citrus × aurantium var. medica, commonly known as the citron, is a citrus fruit characterized by its thick, rough, and often fragrant rind.

It is native to Southeast Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years for both its fruit and its essential oils. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is generally less acidic than other citrus fruits and is used in culinary, medicinal, and perfumery applications.

The variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
