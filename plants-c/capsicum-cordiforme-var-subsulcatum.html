Capsicum cordiforme var. subsulcatum

Capsicum cordiforme var. subsulcatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili pepper, is a unique variety of Capsicum species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and elongated, narrow fruits that typically range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and small white flowers that develop into the characteristic chili fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to medium heat level in culinary applications.
