Coppensia varicosa var. insignis

Coppensia varicosa var. insignis, commonly known as the Variable Rhipsalid, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, scattered spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its foliage is minimal, with the stems serving as the primary photosynthetic structures.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
