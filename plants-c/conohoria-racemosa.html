Conohoria racemosa

Conohoria racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
