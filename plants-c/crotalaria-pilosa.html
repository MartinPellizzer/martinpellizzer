Crotalaria pilosa

Crotalaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties.

Crotalaria pilosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
