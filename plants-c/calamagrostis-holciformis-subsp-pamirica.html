Calamagrostis holciformis subsp. pamirica

Calamagrostis holciformis subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir reed, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
