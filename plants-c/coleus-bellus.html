Coleus bellus

Coleus bellus, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening for their striking appearance.

Coleus bellus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.
