Cotula traillii

Cotula traillii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Cotula traillii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
