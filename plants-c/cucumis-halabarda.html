Cucumis halabarda

Cucumis halabarda, commonly known as the Halabarda cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces elongated, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a trailing growth habit.

The species is valued for its mild flavor and versatility in cooking.
