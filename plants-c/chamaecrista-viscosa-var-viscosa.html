Chamaecrista viscosa var. viscosa

Chamaecrista viscosa var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers and compound leaves with small, sticky glands. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its sticky foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
