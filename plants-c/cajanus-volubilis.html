Cajanus volubilis

Cajanus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its twining stems that climb along supports, and its trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing several seeds. Cajanus volubilis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.

It is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
