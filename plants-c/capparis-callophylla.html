Capparis callophylla

Capparis callophylla, commonly known as the yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that are followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Capparis callophylla is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their ornamental and culinary uses.
