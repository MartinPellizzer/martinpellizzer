Clusia ovalis

Clusia ovalis, commonly known as the fatshedder or broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Clusia ovalis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
