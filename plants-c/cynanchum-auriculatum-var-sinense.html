Cynanchum auriculatum var. sinense

Cynanchum auriculatum var. sinense, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or Sinense milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health purposes.
