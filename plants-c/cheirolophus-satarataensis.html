Cheirolophus satarataensis

Cheirolophus satarataensis, commonly known as the Satarata Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
