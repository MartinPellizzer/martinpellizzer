Chamaetaxus nigra

Chamaetaxus nigra, commonly known as the black-china tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
