Cactus notesteinii

Cactus notesteinii, commonly known as the notestein's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its root system is shallow and adapted to quickly absorb water after rare rainfall events.

Cactus notesteinii is well-suited to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water and nutrients to survive.
