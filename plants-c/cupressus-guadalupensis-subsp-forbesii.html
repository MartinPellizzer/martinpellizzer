Cupressus guadalupensis subsp. forbesii

Cupressus guadalupensis subsp.

forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' cedar, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage and aromatic resinous scent.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscaping.
