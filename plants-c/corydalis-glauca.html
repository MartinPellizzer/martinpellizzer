Corydalis glauca

Corydalis glauca, commonly known as blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often with two upper petals that are more prominent than the lower ones. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Corydalis glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
