Cacalia longifolia

Cacalia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf cacalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cacalia longifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
