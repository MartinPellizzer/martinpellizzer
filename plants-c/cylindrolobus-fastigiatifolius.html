Cylindrolobus fastigiatifolius

Cylindrolobus fastigiatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cylinder-lobed plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
