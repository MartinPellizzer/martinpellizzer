Cyperus clarkei

Cyperus clarkei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense vegetation.

Cyperus clarkei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
