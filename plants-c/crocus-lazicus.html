Crocus lazicus

Crocus lazicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking violet or blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, making it a notable early-flowering species. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in arid conditions, storing moisture and nutrients for extended periods.

The Persian crocus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
