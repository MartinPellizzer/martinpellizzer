Cuphea nitidula var. strigosa

Cuphea nitidula var. strigosa, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
