Cucurbita maxima var. laevis

Cucurbita maxima var. laevis, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, warty, and often pale green to white skin, which becomes sponge-like as it matures. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It is typically cultivated for its edible, tender fruit, which is used in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.
