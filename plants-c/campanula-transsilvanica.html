Campanula transsilvanica

Campanula transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
