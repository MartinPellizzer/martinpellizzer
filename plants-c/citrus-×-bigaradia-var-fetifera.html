Citrus × bigaradia var. fetifera

Citrus × bigaradia var. fetifera, commonly known as the common mandarin.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its small, rounded fruits that are typically orange in color and have a thin, loose rind. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a slightly serrated margin. The flowers are fragrant and white, often clustered in small inflorescences.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
