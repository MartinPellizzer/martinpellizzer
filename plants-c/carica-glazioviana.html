Carica glazioviana

Carica glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian papaya, is a species of flowering plant in the Caricaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to the common papaya, though it is less widely cultivated. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
