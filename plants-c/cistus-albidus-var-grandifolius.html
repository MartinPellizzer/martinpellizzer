Cistus albidus var. grandifolius

Cistus albidus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, grey-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
