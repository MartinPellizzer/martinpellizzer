Clematis subtriloba

Clematis subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three distinct leaflets, each typically lobed at the base. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis subtriloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
