Catasetum thylaciochilum

Catasetum thylaciochilum, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, lip-like structure that is highly specialized for pollination.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
