Cactus neryi

Cactus neryi, commonly known as the Mexican fishhook cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of spines that resemble fishhooks, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
