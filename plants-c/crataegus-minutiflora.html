Crataegus minutiflora

Crataegus minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 feet and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it has a compact, rounded form.

Crataegus minutiflora is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
