Crepis pusilla

Crepis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-ear chickweed, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
