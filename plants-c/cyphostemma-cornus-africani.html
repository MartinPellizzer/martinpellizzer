Cyphostemma cornus-africani

Cyphostemma cornus-africani, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often ribbed stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Cyphostemma cornus-africani is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
