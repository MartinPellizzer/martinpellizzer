Cousinia margiana

Cousinia margiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky and arid soils. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia margiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
