Chryseis odorata

Chryseis odorata, commonly known as the golden sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and is often found growing in sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
