Cacalia gerberiformis

Cacalia gerberiformis, commonly known as the Gerber's cacalia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia gerberiformis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
