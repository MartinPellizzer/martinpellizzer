Carex tricostata

Carex tricostata, commonly known as the three-costate sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex tricostata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
