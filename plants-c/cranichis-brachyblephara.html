Cranichis brachyblephara

Cranichis brachyblephara, commonly known as the narrow-eyed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories in its native range.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
