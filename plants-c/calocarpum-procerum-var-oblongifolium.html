Calocarpum procerum var. oblongifolium

Calocarpum procerum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calocarpum, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its botanical characteristics make it notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

However, it is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
