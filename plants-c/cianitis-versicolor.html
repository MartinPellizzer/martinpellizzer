Cianitis versicolor

Cianitis versicolor, commonly known as the variable bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Cianitis versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
