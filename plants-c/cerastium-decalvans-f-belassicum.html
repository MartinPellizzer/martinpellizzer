Cerastium decalvans f. belassicum

Cerastium decalvans f. belassicum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has hairy, linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptation to harsh, high-altitude environments.
