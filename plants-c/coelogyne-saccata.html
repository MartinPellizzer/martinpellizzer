Coelogyne saccata

Coelogyne saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its unique, sac-like structures on the pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne saccata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
