Caesalpinia kwangtungensis

Caesalpinia kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent veins and its striking red or orange floral spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its vibrant flowers and adaptability contribute to its use in landscaping and horticulture.
