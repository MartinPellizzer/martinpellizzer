Cyphostemma braunii

Cyphostemma braunii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with thorn-like projections and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in dry woodlands.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
