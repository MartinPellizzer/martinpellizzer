Cavendishia fusiformis

Cavendishia fusiformis, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and a central stem that produces clusters of bananas. The plant typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its flowers are usually hidden within the fruit clusters, and it is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in potassium and other essential nutrients.
