Convolvulus purpureus

Convolvulus purpureus, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often opening in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been naturalized in various parts of the world.
