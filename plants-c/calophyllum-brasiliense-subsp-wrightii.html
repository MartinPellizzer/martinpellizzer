Calophyllum brasiliense subsp. wrightii

Calophyllum brasiliense subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's torchwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
