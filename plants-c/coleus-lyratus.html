Coleus lyratus

Coleus lyratus, commonly known as the lyrated coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often features intricate patterns of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant colors.

This plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
