Chrysophyllum ealaense

Chrysophyllum ealaense, commonly known as the Ealaense starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
