Caesalpinia portoricensis

Caesalpinia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
