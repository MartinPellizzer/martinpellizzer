Cyathea mutica var. grandis

Cyathea mutica var. grandis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer of dead fronds. The plant produces large, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
