Curcuma bhatii

Curcuma bhatii, commonly known as the Bhati turmeric, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other tropical plants. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous underground stem, which is the primary part used for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while the flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

Curcuma bhatii is valued for its bioactive compounds, particularly curcuminoids, which contribute to its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine.
