Clematis clitorioides

Clematis clitorioides, commonly known as the creeping Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly serrated edge.

Clematis clitorioides thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ground cover or as a decorative element in woodland gardens.
