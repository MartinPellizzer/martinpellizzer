Caesalpinia smilacea

Caesalpinia smilacea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feather-like inflorescences that resemble flames, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
