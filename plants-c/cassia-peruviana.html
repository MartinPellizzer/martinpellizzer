Cassia peruviana

Cassia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian cassia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cassia peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
