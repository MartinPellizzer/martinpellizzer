Crataegus rubribracteolata

Crataegus rubribracteolata, commonly known as the red-bract hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that appear beneath the flowers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Crataegus rubribracteolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
