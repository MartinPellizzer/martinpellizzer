Carex accedens

Carex accedens, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex accedens plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
