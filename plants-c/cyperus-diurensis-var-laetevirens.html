Cyperus diurensis var. laetevirens

Cyperus diurensis var. laetevirens, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
