Cerastium glutinosum var. laxum

Cerastium glutinosum var. laxum, commonly known as sticky chickweed or sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, spreading stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
