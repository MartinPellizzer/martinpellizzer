Commiphora missionis

Commiphora missionis, commonly known as the Missionary's incense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by unique, resinous fruits that contain a fragrant, aromatic resin.

This resin has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes by indigenous communities.
