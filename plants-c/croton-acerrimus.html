Croton acerrimus

Croton acerrimus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with red or purple veins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia.

Croton acerrimus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
