Chloris propinqua

Chloris propinqua, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Chloris propinqua is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
