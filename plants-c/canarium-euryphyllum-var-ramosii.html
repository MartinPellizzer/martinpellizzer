Canarium euryphyllum var. ramosii

Canarium euryphyllum var. ramosii, commonly known as the ramose candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
