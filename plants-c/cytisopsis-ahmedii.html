Cytisopsis ahmedii

Cytisopsis ahmedii, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in reclamation projects in degraded landscapes.
