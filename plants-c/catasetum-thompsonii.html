Catasetum thompsonii

Catasetum thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, elongated lip petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts specific pollinators like bees and wasps.

Catasetum thompsonii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
