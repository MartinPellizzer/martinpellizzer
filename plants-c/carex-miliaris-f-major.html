Carex miliaris f. major

Carex miliaris f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered sedge, is a variant of the small sedge, characterized by its larger, more prominent inflorescences.

It is a perennial plant native to North America, typically found in wetland or moist soil environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

Carex miliaris f. major is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
