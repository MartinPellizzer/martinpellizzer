Cissus veitchii

Cissus veitchii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, silver-green leaves that have a distinctive veined pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor areas.

Cissus veitchii is also noted for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
