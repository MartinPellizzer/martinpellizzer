Carlina canariensis var. xeranthemiformis

Carlina canariensis var. xeranthemiformis, commonly known as the Canary Island carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to a Mediterranean climate.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
