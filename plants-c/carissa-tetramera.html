Carissa tetramera

Carissa tetramera, commonly known as the four-angled carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating.

Carissa tetramera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
