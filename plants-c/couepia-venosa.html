Couepia venosa

Couepia venosa, commonly known as the veiny couepia, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
