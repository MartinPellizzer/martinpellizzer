Camelina sativa var. caucasica

Camelina sativa var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian camelina, is a variant of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.

This variety is also noted for its adaptability to marginal lands and potential as a sustainable crop.
