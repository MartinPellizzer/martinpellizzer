Commelina cymosa

Commelina cymosa, commonly known as the spreading dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil.

Commelina cymosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
