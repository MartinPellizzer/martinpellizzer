Chenopodium flabellifolium

Chenopodium flabellifolium, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its edible leaves are often used in traditional cuisines and are rich in nutrients such as vitamins and minerals.

Chenopodium flabellifolium is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing sandy environments.
