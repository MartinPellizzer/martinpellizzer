Carex pruinosa var. picta

Carex pruinosa var. picta, commonly known as the Variegated Wood Sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The leaves are typically bluish-gray and have a distinctive variegation pattern, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
