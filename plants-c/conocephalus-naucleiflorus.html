Conocephalus naucleiflorus

Conocephalus naucleiflorus, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
