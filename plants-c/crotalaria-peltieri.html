Crotalaria peltieri

Crotalaria peltieri, commonly known as the goldenball cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spherical flower clusters that resemble small balls. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas.

Crotalaria peltieri is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
