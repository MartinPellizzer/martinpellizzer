Chrysocoma hirsuta

Chrysocoma hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
