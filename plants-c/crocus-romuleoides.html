Crocus romuleoides

Crocus romuleoides, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces delicate, nodding flowers with bright yellow petals and a prominent, showy stamen, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.

The species is closely related to other members of the Crocus genus, sharing similar floral structures and growth patterns.
