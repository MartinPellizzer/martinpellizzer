Chamaecrista philippii

Chamaecrista philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's burrlegume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds.

Chamaecrista philippii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
