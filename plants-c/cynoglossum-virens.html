Cynoglossum virens

Cynoglossum virens, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum virens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
