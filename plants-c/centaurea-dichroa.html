Centaurea dichroa

Centaurea dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and a distinctive yellow center, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements.

Centaurea dichroa is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
