Cryptandra anomala

Cryptandra anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, red or pink fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
