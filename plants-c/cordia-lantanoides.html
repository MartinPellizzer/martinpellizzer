Cordia lantanoides

Cordia lantanoides, commonly known as the red cedar or yellow cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cordia lantanoides thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
