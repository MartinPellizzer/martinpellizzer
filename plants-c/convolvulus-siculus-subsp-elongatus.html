Convolvulus siculus subsp. elongatus

Convolvulus siculus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers typically in shades of pink or purple.

The plant features slender, twining stems that can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb or trail along surfaces. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
