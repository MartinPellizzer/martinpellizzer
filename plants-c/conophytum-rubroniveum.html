Conophytum rubroniveum

Conophytum rubroniveum, commonly known as the red-and-silver conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paired leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically covered in a white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water to thrive.
