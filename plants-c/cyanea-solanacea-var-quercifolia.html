Cyanea solanacea var. quercifolia

Cyanea solanacea var. quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids. The plant features broad, lobed leaves resembling oak leaves, giving it its common name. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple, followed by berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
