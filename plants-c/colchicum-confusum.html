Colchicum confusum

Colchicum confusum, commonly known as the confused autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, often waxy appearance. The plant features a corm as its underground storage organ, which allows it to survive in harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and often with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
