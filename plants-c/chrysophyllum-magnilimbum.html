Chrysophyllum magnilimbum

Chrysophyllum magnilimbum, commonly known as the large-leaved chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, creamy pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is also cultivated in some regions for its fruit and timber.
