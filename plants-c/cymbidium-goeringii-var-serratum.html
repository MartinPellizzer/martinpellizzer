Cymbidium goeringii var. serratum

Cymbidium goeringii var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, lance-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically broad and elliptic, with a waxy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers cool, humid conditions and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
