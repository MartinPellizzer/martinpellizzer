Crispiloba disperma

Crispiloba disperma, commonly known as the scattered fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
