Chenopodium amboanum

Chenopodium amboanum, commonly known as the Madagascar goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its seeds can be processed into a flour-like substance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
