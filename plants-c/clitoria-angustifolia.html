Clitoria angustifolia

Clitoria angustifolia, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-shaped flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also popular in culinary applications, particularly in beverages and desserts.
