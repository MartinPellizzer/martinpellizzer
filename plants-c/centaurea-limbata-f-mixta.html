Centaurea limbata f. mixta

Centaurea limbata f. mixta, commonly known as the mixed form of the slender knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that range in color from pink to purple, with bracts that are often reddish or purplish. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture.

This form is notable for its variability in flower color and morphology, making it a popular choice in ornamental gardening.
