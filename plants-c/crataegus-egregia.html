Crataegus egregia

Crataegus egregia, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
