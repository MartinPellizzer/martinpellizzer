Carex oederi var. cataractae

Carex oederi var. cataractae, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

This species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its aesthetic appeal in water garden settings.
