Cyrtandra × perlmanii

Cyrtandra × perlmanii, commonly known as the hybrid beauty flower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra sericea and Cyrtandra pedata, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are attractive to pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its foliage consists of glossy, heart-shaped leaves that contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
