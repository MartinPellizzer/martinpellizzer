Coelorachis foveolata

Coelorachis foveolata, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white blooms with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its specialized habitat, Coelorachis foveolata is considered a rare and endangered species.
