Centaurea spinosa var. tragacanthoides

Centaurea spinosa var. tragacanthoides, commonly known as the thorny centaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, spiny stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seed heads. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Asteraceae family.
