Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-aurata

Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-aurata, commonly known as the pomelo or sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus species characterized by its large, fragrant fruits.

The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and thorned branches. Its flowers are white and fragrant, typically blooming in the spring, and the fruit develops over several months. The fruit's skin, or zest, is thick and contains a high concentration of essential oils, while the pulp is juicy and segmented.

This variety is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as for its ornamental value.
