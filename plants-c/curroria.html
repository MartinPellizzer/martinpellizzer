Curroria

Curroria, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Curroria is often found in moist, shady areas and is native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
