Cystorchis javanica

Cystorchis javanica, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Cystorchis javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
