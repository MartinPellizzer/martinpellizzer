Clinopodium oaxacanum

Clinopodium oaxacanum, commonly known as Oaxacan mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are aromatic, with a mint-like scent, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Clinopodium oaxacanum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
