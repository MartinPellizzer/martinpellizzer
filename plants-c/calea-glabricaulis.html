Calea glabricaulis

Calea glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed calea, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Calea glabricaulis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
