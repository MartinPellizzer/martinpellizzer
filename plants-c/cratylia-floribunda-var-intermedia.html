Cratylia floribunda var. intermedia

Cratylia floribunda var. intermedia, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems that produce clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
