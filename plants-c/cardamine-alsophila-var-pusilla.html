Cardamine alsophila var. pusilla

Cardamine alsophila var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf water cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
