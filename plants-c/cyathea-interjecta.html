Cyathea interjecta

Cyathea interjecta, commonly known as the intermediate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Cyathea interjecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
