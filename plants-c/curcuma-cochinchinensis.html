Curcuma cochinchinensis

Curcuma cochinchinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish rhizomes that contain curcuminoids, giving it its distinctive color and medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

This plant is widely cultivated for both culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
