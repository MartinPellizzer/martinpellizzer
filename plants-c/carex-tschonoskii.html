Carex tschonoskii

Carex tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex tschonoskii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
