Cainito

Cainito, commonly known as *Pouteria paniculata*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Cainito's fruits are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
