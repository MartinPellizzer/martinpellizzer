Chamaesyce paucipila

Chamaesyce paucipila, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, often blooming in spring.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert or coastal dune habitats.
