Chamaedorea whitelockiana

Chamaedorea whitelockiana, commonly known as the White Lock's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery fronds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. Its leaves are usually a deep green and have a smooth, glossy texture.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
