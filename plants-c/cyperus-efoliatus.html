Cyperus efoliatus

Cyperus efoliatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus efoliatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
