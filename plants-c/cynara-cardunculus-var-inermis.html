Cynara cardunculus var. inermis

Cynara cardunculus var. inermis, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple, thistle-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust growth habit. Its leaves are often used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, while the plant also has historical uses in medicine and as a decorative plant.

The giant cardoon is well adapted to dry, sunny climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.
