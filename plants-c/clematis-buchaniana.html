Clematis buchaniana

Clematis buchaniana, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Clematis buchaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
