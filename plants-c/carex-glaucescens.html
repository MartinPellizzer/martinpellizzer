Carex glaucescens

Carex glaucescens, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
