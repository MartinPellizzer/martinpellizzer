Celastrus ruber

Celastrus ruber, commonly known as red bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Celastraceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Celastrus ruber is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, where it thrives in partial shade.
