Cyperus hochstetteri var. tenuis

Cyperus hochstetteri var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in damp soils near water sources.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
