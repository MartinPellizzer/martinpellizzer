Cymbidium × maronii

Cymbidium × maronii, commonly known as the maroon orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for winter floral displays. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium × maronii prefers cool, bright conditions and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
