Catasetum aripuanense

Catasetum aripuanense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Catasetum aripuanense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
