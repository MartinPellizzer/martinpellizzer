Centranthus calcitrapae subvar. albiflorus

Centranthus calcitrapae subvar. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower centranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
