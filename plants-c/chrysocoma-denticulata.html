Chrysocoma denticulata

Chrysocoma denticulata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.
