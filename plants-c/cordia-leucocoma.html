Cordia leucocoma

Cordia leucocoma, commonly known as white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Cordia leucocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
