Carduus nigrescens var. tetuanensis

Carduus nigrescens var. tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
