Centrolepis pusilla

Centrolepis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf centrolepis, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
