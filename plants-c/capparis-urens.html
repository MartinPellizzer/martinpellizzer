Capparis urens

Capparis urens, commonly known as the stinging nettle or small nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, which release irritants when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruits are capsules containing tiny seeds. It is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Capparis urens is adapted to thrive in temperate climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
