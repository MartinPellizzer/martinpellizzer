Cestrum amictum var. latifolium

Cestrum amictum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are broad and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries it produces are toxic and should be handled with care.
