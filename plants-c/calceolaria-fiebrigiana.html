Calceolaria fiebrigiana

Calceolaria fiebrigiana, commonly known as the "Fiebrig's slipper" or "slipper orchid," is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette.

Calceolaria fiebrigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique and colorful blooms.
