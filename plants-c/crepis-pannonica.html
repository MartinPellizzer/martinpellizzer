Crepis pannonica

Crepis pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis pannonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
