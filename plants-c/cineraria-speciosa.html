Cineraria speciosa

Cineraria speciosa, commonly known as the showy cineraria, is a striking flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features velvety, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cineraria speciosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate appearance and resilience in mild climates.
