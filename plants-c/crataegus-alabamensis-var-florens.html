Crataegus alabamensis var. florens

Crataegus alabamensis var. florens, commonly known as the Alabama hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
