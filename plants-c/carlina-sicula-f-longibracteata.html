Carlina sicula f. longibracteata

Carlina sicula f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a showy composite flower head that typically consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pink ray florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its bracts are elongated and often persist through flowering, giving the plant its distinguishing feature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
