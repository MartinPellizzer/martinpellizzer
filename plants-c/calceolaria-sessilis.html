Calceolaria sessilis

Calceolaria sessilis, commonly known as the sessile slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers, which are often violet or purple, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.
