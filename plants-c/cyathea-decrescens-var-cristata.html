Cyathea decrescens var. cristata

Cyathea decrescens var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a large, evergreen fern that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick, fibrous layer of old fronds. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, wavy or crisped appearance along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
