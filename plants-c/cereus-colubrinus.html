Cereus colubrinus

Cereus colubrinus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming at night. Its stems are covered in areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and sometimes branches.

Cereus colubrinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
