Chionanthus triflorus

Chionanthus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in trichas, giving it the species name *triflorus*, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.
