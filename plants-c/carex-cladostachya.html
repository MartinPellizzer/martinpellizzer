Carex cladostachya

Carex cladostachya, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex cladostachya is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
