Colea alata

Colea alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features prominently veined, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. Colea alata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
