Cyperus squarrosulus

Cyperus squarrosulus, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves that often have a woolly texture. Its flowering spikes are typically pale green to reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus squarrosulus is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways.
