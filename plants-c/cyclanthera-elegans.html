Cyclanthera elegans

Cyclanthera elegans, commonly known as the elegant cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, edible fruits resembling cucumbers. Its botanical features include a unique combination of tendrils for climbing and a distinct, waxy coating on its leaves.

Cyclanthera elegans is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its use in local cuisines.
