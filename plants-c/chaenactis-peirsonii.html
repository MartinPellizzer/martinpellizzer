Chaenactis peirsonii

Chaenactis peirsonii, commonly known as Peirson's brittleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, adding a subtle beauty to its habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
