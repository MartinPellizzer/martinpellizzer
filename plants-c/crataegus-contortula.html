Crataegus contortula

Crataegus contortula, commonly known as the twisted hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and small mammals, and it is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
