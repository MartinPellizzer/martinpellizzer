Cymbidium pendulum var. purpureum

Cymbidium pendulum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in spring and are known for their vibrant purple and white hues. This orchid thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
