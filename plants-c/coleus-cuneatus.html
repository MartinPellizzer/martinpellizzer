Coleus cuneatus

Coleus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Coleus cuneatus is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
