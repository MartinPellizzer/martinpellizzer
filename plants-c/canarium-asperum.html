Canarium asperum

Canarium asperum, commonly known as the rough canarium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a hard, spiny capsule that contains a single, oily seed.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also used in traditional practices.
