Cestrum cuneatum

Cestrum cuneatum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with smooth, often thornless stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and glossy, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Cestrum cuneatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its nocturnal blooms.
