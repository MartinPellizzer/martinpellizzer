Convolvulus × turcicus nothosubsp. peshmenii

Convolvulus × turcicus nothosubsp.

peshmenii, commonly known as the Peshmenian morning glory, is a hybrid species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species. It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical interest lies in its hybrid origin and ornamental value.
