Crupina vulgaris proles alpestris

Crupina vulgaris proles alpestris, commonly known as the alpine crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
