Cyrtopodium cristatum

Cyrtopodium cristatum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy, and often crested appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are arranged in a raceme, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Cyrtopodium cristatum is appreciated by botanists and gardeners for its distinctive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
