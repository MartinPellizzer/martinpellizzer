Cyperus isabellinus

Cyperus isabellinus, commonly known as the Isabelline flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among sedges.

Cyperus isabellinus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
