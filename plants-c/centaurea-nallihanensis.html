Centaurea nallihanensis

Centaurea nallihanensis, commonly known as the Nallihan's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurea nallihanensis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
