Citrus × aurantium var. grassensis

Citrus × aurantium var. grassensis, commonly known as the grassensis citron, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, smooth rind. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of citron fruits used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is well adapted to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
