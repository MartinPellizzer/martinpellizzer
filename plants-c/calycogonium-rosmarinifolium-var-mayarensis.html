Calycogonium rosmarinifolium var. mayarensis

Calycogonium rosmarinifolium var. mayarensis, commonly known as the Mayarensis variant, is a lesser-known species within the Calycogonium genus.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which resembles that of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant scent when crushed.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued in some local horticultural practices for its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties.
