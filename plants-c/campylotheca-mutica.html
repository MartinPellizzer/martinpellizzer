Campylotheca mutica

Campylotheca mutica, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Campylotheca mutica is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
