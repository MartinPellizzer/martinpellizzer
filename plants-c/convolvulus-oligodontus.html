Convolvulus oligodontus

Convolvulus oligodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus oligodontus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its twining growth habit and showy blooms.
