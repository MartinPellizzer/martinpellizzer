Chlorophytum sphacelatum subsp. hockii

Chlorophytum sphacelatum subsp.

hockii, commonly known as the Hock's spider plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa. It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings.

The plant produces long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
