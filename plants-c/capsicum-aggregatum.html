Capsicum aggregatum

Capsicum aggregatum, commonly known as the bird pepper or chili pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, often brightly colored fruits that range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and the fruits are used both as a spice and in culinary applications.

Capsicum aggregatum is valued for its heat-producing compounds, such as capsaicin, which contribute to its flavor and medicinal properties.
