Calamintha × cadevallii

Calamintha × cadevallii, commonly known as sweet germander, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

Calamintha × cadevallii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
