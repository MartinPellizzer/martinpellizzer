Cousinia zagrica

Cousinia zagrica, commonly known as the Zagrica cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Alps.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and silver-haired, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Cousinia zagrica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
