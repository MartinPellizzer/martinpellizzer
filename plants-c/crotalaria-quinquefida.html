Crotalaria quinquefida

Crotalaria quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with five leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in agricultural settings.
