Ceropegia kachinensis

Ceropegia kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
