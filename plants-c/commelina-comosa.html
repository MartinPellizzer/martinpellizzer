Commelina comosa

Commelina comosa, commonly known as the slender dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers with white markings, typically appearing in clusters at the base of its leaves. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Commelina comosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
