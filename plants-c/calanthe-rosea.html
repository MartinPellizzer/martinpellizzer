Calanthe rosea

Calanthe rosea, commonly known as the pink calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety pink petals and a showy, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant flowers.

Calanthe rosea prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
