Cuphea glutinosa

Cuphea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Cuphea glutinosa is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
