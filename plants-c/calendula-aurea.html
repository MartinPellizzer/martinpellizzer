Calendula aurea

Calendula aurea, commonly known as the pot marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, slightly rough texture. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The flowers are often used in cosmetics, medicine, and as a natural dye due to their high content of flavonoids and carotenoids.
