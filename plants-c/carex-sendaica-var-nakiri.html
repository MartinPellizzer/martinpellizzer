Carex sendaica var. nakiri

Carex sendaica var. nakiri, commonly known as the Nakiri sedge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
