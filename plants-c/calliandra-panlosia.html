Calliandra panlosia

Calliandra panlosia, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

Calliandra panlosia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
