Conobea alata

Conobea alata, commonly known as the winged conobea, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Conobea alata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique climbing plant.
