Crabbea cirsioides

Crabbea cirsioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Crabbea cirsioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
