Conophytum crassum

Conophytum crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaved stone plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
