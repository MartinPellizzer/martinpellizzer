Carex michauxiana f. asiatica

Carex michauxiana f. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
