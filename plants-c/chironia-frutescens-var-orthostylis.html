Chironia frutescens var. orthostylis

Chironia frutescens var. orthostylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chironia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
