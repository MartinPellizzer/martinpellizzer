Coreopsis auriculata

Coreopsis auriculata, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive brown center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
