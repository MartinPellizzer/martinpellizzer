Carex leporina var. conferta

Carex leporina var. conferta, commonly known as the dense sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
