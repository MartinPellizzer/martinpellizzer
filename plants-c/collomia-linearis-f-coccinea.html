Collomia linearis f. coccinea

Collomia linearis f. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered phlox, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers. The inflorescence consists of clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
