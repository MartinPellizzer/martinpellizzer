Carex lyallii

Carex lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided seeds.

Carex lyallii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
