Ceropegia perforata

Ceropegia perforata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features cascading stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with small, perforated holes. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
