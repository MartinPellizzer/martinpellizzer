Corydalis bulbosa subsp. fabacea

Corydalis bulbosa subsp.

fabacea, commonly known as the yellow corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces bulbous roots, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Corydalis bulbosa subsp.

fabacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
