Carum trachycarpum

Carum trachycarpum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that are used as a spice in various cuisines. Its seeds contain essential oils that contribute to its distinct flavor and aroma.
