Cimicifuga dahurica f. mascula

Cimicifuga dahurica f. mascula, commonly known as the male form of the Dahurian bugbane, is a botanical variant of the species Cimicifuga dahurica.

It is characterized by its distinct morphological features, including more robust stems and smaller, clustered flowers compared to the female form. This plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a dioecious species, where male and female plants are separate, contributing to its unique reproductive biology.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
