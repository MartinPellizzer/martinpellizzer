Cleome flexuosa

Cleome flexuosa, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome flexuosa is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
