Cestrum pulverulentum

Cestrum pulverulentum, commonly known as the fragrant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, spicy scent.
