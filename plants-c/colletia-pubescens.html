Colletia pubescens

Colletia pubescens, commonly known as the woolly candelabra cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are densely covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
