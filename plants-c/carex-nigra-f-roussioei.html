Carex nigra f. roussioei

Carex nigra f. roussioei, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its rusty-colored bracts, which give it its common name.
