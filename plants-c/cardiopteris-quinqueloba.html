Cardiopteris quinqueloba

Cardiopteris quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures are borne on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

The plant's ability to absorb nutrients from water makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
