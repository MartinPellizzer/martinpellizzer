Crataegus delecta

Crataegus delecta, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a species of hawthorn native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and its bark is often fissured and scaly.

Crataegus delecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
