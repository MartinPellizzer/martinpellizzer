Cyrtochilum deburghgraeveanum

Cyrtochilum deburghgraeveanum, commonly known as the Burghgraeve's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Cyrtochilum deburghgraeveanum is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
