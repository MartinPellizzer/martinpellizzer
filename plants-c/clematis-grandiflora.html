Clematis grandiflora

Clematis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or trellises. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Clematis grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
