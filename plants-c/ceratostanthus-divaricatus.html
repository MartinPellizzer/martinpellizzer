Ceratostanthus divaricatus

Ceratostanthus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable horned plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
