Convolvulus suendermannii

Convolvulus suendermannii, commonly known as the Suendermann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
