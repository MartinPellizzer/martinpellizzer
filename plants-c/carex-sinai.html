Carex sinai

Carex sinai, commonly known as the Sinai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sinai is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
