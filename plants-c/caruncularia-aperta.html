Caruncularia aperta

Caruncularia aperta, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower genus. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in full sun and prefers well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas.

Caruncularia aperta is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
