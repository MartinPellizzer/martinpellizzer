Cotoneaster grammontii

Cotoneaster grammontii, commonly known as the Grammont's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Cotoneaster grammontii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
