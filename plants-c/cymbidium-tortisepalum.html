Cymbidium tortisepalum

Cymbidium tortisepalum, commonly known as the twisted-sepal orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive twisted sepal structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium tortisepalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its elegant floral display.
