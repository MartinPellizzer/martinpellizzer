Carex lucida var. buchananii

Carex lucida var. buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its bright green, shiny foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex lucida var. buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
