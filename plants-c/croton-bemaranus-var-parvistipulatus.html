Croton bemaranus var. parvistipulatus

Croton bemaranus var. parvistipulatus, commonly known as the small-stipule croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
