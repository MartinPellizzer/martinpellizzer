Crataegus pinnatifida var. garanica

Crataegus pinnatifida var. garanica, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
