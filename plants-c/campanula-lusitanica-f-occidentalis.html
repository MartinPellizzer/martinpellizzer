Campanula lusitanica f. occidentalis

Campanula lusitanica f. occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
