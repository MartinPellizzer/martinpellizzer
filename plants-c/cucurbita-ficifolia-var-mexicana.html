Cucurbita ficifolia var. mexicana

Cucurbita ficifolia var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a tropical vine native to Central America and Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and elongated, spiny fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices due to their unique flavor and nutritional content.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
