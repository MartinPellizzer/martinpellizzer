Cyperus radicans

Cyperus radicans, commonly known as spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often produce erect spikes of small, greenish or brownish flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, commonly found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in water retention.

Cyperus radicans plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
