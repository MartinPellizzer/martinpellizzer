Calendula decumbens

Calendula decumbens, commonly known as creeping calendula, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features sprawling, low-growing stems that often lie along the ground, producing bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically tubular at the base and expand into a radiate shape, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Calendula decumbens is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
