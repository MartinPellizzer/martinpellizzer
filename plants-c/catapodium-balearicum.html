Catapodium balearicum

Catapodium balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for erosion control.
