Cyathea quadrata

Cyathea quadrata, commonly known as the quadrat fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

The fronds are evergreen and can remain on the plant for several years, adding to its ornamental value.
