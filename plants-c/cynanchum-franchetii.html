Cynanchum franchetii

Cynanchum franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
