Convolvulus aristolochiifolius

Convolvulus aristolochiifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
