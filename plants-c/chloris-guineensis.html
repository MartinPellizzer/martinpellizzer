Chloris guineensis

Chloris guineensis, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
