Chamaecrista fulgida

Chamaecrista fulgida, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
