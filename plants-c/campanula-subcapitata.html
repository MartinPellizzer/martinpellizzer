Campanula subcapitata

Campanula subcapitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula subcapitata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
