Campylospermum engama

Campylospermum engama, commonly known as the Engama bush, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
