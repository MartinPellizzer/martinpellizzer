Cereus speciosissimus var. hitchensii

Cereus speciosissimus var. hitchensii, commonly known as the Hitchens' night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and unique blooming habit make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
