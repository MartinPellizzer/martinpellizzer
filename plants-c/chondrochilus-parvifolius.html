Chondrochilus parvifolius

Chondrochilus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leafed rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with small, elliptical leaves that are densely packed along its length. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is characteristic of the genus.

Chondrochilus parvifolius is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized collections.
