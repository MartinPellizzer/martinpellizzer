Caesalpinia fischeri

Caesalpinia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. Its flowers are typically bisexual, with showy petals that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Caesalpinia fischeri is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
