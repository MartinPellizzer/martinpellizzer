Campanula garciniae

Campanula garciniae, commonly known as the Madagascar bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula garciniae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
