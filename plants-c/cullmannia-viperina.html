Cullmannia viperina

Cullmannia viperina, commonly known as the viper's grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, creeping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of grassland ecosystems.
