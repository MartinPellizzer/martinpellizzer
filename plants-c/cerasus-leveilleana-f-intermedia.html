Cerasus leveilleana f. intermedia

Cerasus leveilleana f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate cherry, is a botanical variant of the cherry species.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield in gardens and orchards.
