Cinnamomum lawang

Cinnamomum lawang, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon-like fragrance.

The bark is often harvested for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
