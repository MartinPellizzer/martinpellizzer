Cyanus nigrofimbrius

Cyanus nigrofimbrius, commonly known as the black-fringed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers with dark purple margins, which give it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Cyanus nigrofimbrius is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
