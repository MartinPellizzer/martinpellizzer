Cassia oreades

Cassia oreades, commonly known as the mountain cassia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open woodland areas.

Cassia oreades is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
