Clematis patens var. amaliae

Clematis patens var. amaliae, commonly known as the Amaliae Clematis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
