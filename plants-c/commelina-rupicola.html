Commelina rupicola

Commelina rupicola, commonly known as the rock commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Commelina rupicola is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.
