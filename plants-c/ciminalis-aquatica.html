Ciminalis aquatica

Ciminalis aquatica, commonly known as water cumin, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinct, spicy scent.

Ciminalis aquatica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
