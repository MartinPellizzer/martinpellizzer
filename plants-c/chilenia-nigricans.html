Chilenia nigricans

Chilenia nigricans, commonly known as the black Chilean tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spring, followed by the production of woody, elongated fruits.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a useful species in ecological restoration projects.
