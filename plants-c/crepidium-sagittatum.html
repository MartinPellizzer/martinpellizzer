Crepidium sagittatum

Crepidium sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy coating. The plant blooms with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
