Cineraria seminuda

Cineraria seminuda, commonly known as the naked cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
