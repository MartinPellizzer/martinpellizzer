Cyperus microcephalus subsp. microcephalus

Cyperus microcephalus subsp.

microcephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
