Cynophalla laeta

Cynophalla laeta, commonly known as the sweetbriar, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cynophalla laeta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
