Campanula mollis subsp. mollis

Campanula mollis subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. Campanula mollis subsp.

mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
