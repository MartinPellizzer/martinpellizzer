Chamaecrista neesiana var. goyazensis

Chamaecrista neesiana var. goyazensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
