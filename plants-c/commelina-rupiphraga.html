Commelina rupiphraga

Commelina rupiphraga, commonly known as the purple arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet bracts that enclose the small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
