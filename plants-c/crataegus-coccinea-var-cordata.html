Crataegus coccinea var. cordata

Crataegus coccinea var. cordata, commonly known as the red-flowering dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its fruits are small, red drupes that mature in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
