Corydalis persica var. hyrcana

Corydalis persica var. hyrcana, commonly known as Persian corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant features slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
