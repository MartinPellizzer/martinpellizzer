Cuphea zimapani

Cuphea zimapani, commonly known as the Zimapan firecracker, is a striking flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow tubular flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea zimapani is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
