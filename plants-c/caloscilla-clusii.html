Caloscilla clusii

Caloscilla clusii, commonly known as the African orchid or Clusius's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. Caloscilla clusii typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
