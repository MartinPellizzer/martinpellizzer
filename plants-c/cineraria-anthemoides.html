Cineraria anthemoides

Cineraria anthemoides, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria anthemoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
