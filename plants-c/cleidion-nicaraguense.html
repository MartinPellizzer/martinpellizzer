Cleidion nicaraguense

Cleidion nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan soap tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional remedies.
