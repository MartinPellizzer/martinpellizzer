Callirhoe digitata f. alba

Callirhoe digitata f. alba, commonly known as the white prairie coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink ray flowers and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The white variant of this species is distinguished by its lighter flower color compared to the typical red or orange forms.
