Cyperus luerssenii

Cyperus luerssenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Cyperus luerssenii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
