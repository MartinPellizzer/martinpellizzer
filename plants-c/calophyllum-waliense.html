Calophyllum waliense

Calophyllum waliense, commonly known as the Waliense torchwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often glossy bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
