Cacalia arenaria

Cacalia arenaria, commonly known as the sand daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the spring. It has hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cacalia arenaria is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
