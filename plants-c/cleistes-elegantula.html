Cleistes elegantula

Cleistes elegantula, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are pale green to white with a distinctive, hood-like structure.

Cleistes elegantula is a rare and endangered species, often requiring specific habitat conditions to thrive.
