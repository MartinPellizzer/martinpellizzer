Carex straminea var. crawei

Carex straminea var. crawei, commonly known as the Crawe's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
