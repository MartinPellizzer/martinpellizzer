Calamagrostis lanceolata var. angustifolia

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluejoint, is a tall, grass-like perennial native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like blades that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive blue-gray sheen. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with purple or pink tints, especially in the fall. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
