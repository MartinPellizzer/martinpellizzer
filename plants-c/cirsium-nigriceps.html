Cirsium nigriceps

Cirsium nigriceps, commonly known as black-headed thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bracts that form a spherical shape, with the central flowers being the most prominent.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
