Camptocarpus orientalis

Camptocarpus orientalis, commonly known as the Persian rose or oriental camptocarpus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Camptocarpus orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
