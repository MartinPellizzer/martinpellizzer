Chimonobambusa montigena

Chimonobambusa montigena, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
