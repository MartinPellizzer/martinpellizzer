Convolvulus malacophyllus subsp. collinus

Convolvulus malacophyllus subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
