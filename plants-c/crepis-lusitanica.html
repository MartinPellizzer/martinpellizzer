Crepis lusitanica

Crepis lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Crepis lusitanica is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
