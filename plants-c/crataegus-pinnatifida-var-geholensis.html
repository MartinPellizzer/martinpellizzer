Crataegus pinnatifida var. geholensis

Crataegus pinnatifida var. geholensis, commonly known as the Gehol's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
