Clinopodium suborbiculare

Clinopodium suborbiculare, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale blue. Its leaves are narrow and slightly toothed, with a fragrant, minty scent when crushed.

Clinopodium suborbiculare is often used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.
