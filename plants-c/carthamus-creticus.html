Carthamus creticus

Carthamus creticus, commonly known as Cretan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Carthamus creticus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
