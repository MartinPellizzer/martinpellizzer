Chamaecerasus standishii

Chamaecerasus standishii, commonly known as the Standish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Chamaecerasus standishii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
