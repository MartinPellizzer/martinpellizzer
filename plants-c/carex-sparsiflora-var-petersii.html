Carex sparsiflora var. petersii

Carex sparsiflora var. petersii, commonly known as the Peters' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky cluster of small, wind-pollinated flowers, and it produces capsule fruits that contain seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
