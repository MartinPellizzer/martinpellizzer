Chisocheton polyanthus

Chisocheton polyanthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The golden flame tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
