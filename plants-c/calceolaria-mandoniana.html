Calceolaria mandoniana

Calceolaria mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows in moist, cool environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude meadows. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Calceolaria mandoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
