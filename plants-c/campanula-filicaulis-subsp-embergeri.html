Campanula filicaulis subsp. embergeri

Campanula filicaulis subsp.

embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue to violet.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
