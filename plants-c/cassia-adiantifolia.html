Cassia adiantifolia

Cassia adiantifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Cassia adiantifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
