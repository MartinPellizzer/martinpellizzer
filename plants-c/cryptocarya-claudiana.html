Cryptocarya claudiana

Cryptocarya claudiana, commonly known as the New Zealand punga, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya claudiana is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
