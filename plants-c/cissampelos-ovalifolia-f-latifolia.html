Cissampelos ovalifolia f. latifolia

Cissampelos ovalifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed moon vine, is a variant of the moon vine species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The foliage is broad and glossy, providing a lush appearance, while the vines can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
