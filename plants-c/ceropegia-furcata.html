Ceropegia furcata

Ceropegia furcata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
