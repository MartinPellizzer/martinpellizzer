Chondrorhyncha antonii

Chondrorhyncha antonii, commonly known as the Antonii's coralroot, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a mycoheterotroph that relies entirely on fungal associations for nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its roots form intricate symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which provide essential nutrients in exchange for carbohydrates.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Chondrorhyncha antonii is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a conservation concern.
