Chuquiraga ferox

Chuquiraga ferox, commonly known as the Chilean burr or chuchundra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. Its fruit is a burr-like structure that contains seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Chuquiraga ferox is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
