Canarium palawanense

Canarium palawanense, commonly known as the Palawan canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
