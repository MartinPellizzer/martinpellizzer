Campanula ficarioides subsp. orhyi

Campanula ficarioides subsp.

orhyi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico. It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
