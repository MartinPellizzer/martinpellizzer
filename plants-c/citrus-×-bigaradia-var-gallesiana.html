Citrus × bigaradia var. gallesiana

Citrus × bigaradia var. gallesiana, commonly known as the Gallesian lemon.

This hybrid citrus plant is a cross between the bergamot and the mandarin, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, aromatic peel. The leaves are typically oval and dark green, with a slightly textured surface.

The plant is valued for its fragrant zest and is often used in culinary and aromatic applications.
