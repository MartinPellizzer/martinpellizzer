Cytisus candicans

Cytisus candicans, commonly known as the golden broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often thorned stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
