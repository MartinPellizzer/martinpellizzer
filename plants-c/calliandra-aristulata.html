Calliandra aristulata

Calliandra aristulata, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Calliandra aristulata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
