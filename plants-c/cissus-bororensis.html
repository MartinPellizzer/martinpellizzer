Cissus bororensis

Cissus bororensis, commonly known as the Bororensis Devil's Ivy, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Cissus bororensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
