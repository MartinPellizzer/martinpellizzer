Casselia davurica

Casselia davurica, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Casselia davurica is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
