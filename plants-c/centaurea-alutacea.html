Centaurea alutacea

Centaurea alutacea, commonly known as mouse-ear cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
