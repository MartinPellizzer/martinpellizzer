Cirsium jaliscoense

Cirsium jaliscoense, commonly known as the Jalisco thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mexican state of Jalisco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open grasslands or along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium jaliscoense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential ecological roles in native habitats.
