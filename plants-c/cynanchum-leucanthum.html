Cynanchum leucanthum

Cynanchum leucanthum, commonly known as white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic, containing various cardiac glycosides. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a lanceolate shape with hairy surfaces.

Cynanchum leucanthum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
