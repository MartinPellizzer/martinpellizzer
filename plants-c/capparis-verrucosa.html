Capparis verrucosa

Capparis verrucosa, commonly known as the rough caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces spherical, greenish-yellow fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Capparis verrucosa is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
