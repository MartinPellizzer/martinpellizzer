Calophanes multicaulis

Calophanes multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered calophane, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact manner along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in tropical botany.
