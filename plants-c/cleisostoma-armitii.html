Cleisostoma armitii

Cleisostoma armitii, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

Cleisostoma armitii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique fragrance.
