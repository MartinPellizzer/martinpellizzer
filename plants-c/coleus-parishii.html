Coleus parishii

Coleus parishii, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a pleasant mint-like fragrance.

Coleus parishii thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
