Carex sellowiana var. tenuis

Carex sellowiana var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetland environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
