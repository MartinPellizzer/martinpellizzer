Capparis trichocarpa

Capparis trichocarpa, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while the fruits are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in coastal areas.
