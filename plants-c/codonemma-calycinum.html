Codonemma calycinum

Codonemma calycinum, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
