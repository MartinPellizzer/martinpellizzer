Crassocephalum cernuum

Crassocephalum cernuum, commonly known as the hanging head sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers, and its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
