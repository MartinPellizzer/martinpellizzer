Calamintha vardarensis

Calamintha vardarensis, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calamintha vardarensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
