Cymbidium formosanum var. gracillimum

Cymbidium formosanum var. gracillimum, commonly known as the Taiwan orchid, is a species native to Taiwan and belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves that are often dark green in color. The flowers are typically pale yellow with red or orange markings, and they bloom in late winter to early spring. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.

It requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
