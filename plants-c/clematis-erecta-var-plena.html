Clematis erecta var. plena

Clematis erecta var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strong, twining stems that can climb up to 10 feet or more, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the showy blooms.

The flowers are typically shades of white, pink, or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.
