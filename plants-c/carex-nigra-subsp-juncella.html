Carex nigra subsp. juncella

Carex nigra subsp.

juncella, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
