Centaurea dissecta f. montaltensis

Centaurea dissecta f. montaltensis, commonly known as the Montaltensian star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower structures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in certain areas.
