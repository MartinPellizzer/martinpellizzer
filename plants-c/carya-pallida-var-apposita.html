Carya pallida var. apposita

Carya pallida var. apposita, commonly known as the white hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and a tendency to form dense, thorny thickets.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
