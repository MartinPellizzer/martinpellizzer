Croton klotzschii var. oblongifolius

Croton klotzschii var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct veining pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense, compact form.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
