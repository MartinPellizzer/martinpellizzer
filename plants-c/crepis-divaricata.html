Crepis divaricata

Crepis divaricata, commonly known as the bent-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis divaricata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
