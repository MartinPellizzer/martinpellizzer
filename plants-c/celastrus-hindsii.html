Celastrus hindsii

Celastrus hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' staff-vine, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural research.
