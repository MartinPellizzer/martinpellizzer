Coelachyrum oligobrachiatum var. villiglume

Coelachyrum oligobrachiatum var. villiglume, commonly known as the narrow-bracted coelachyrum, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that often grow in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, and are typically clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release the seeds.
