Cyperus trisulcus

Cyperus trisulcus, commonly known as the three-angled sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Cyperus trisulcus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water purifier.
