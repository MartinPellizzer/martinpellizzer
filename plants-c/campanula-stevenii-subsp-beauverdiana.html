Campanula stevenii subsp. beauverdiana

Campanula stevenii subsp.

beauverdiana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Campanula stevenii subsp.

beauverdiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
