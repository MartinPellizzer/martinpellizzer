Carex hirtiglumis var. arcuata

Carex hirtiglumis var. arcuata, commonly known as the curly flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet to mesic environments such as marshes, swales, and the margins of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
