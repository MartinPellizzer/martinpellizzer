Centaurea cuspidata subsp. elegantissima

Centaurea cuspidata subsp.

elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the Centaurea genus.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
