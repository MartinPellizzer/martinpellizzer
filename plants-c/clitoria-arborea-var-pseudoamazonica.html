Clitoria arborea var. pseudoamazonica

Clitoria arborea var. pseudoamazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian blue pea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, bright blue or violet flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for gardens and reforestation projects.
