Commiphora iringensis

Commiphora iringensis, commonly known as the Somali myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in incense. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers and has leathery, oval-shaped leaves. Its habitat includes dry, rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

The botanical features of Commiphora iringensis make it well-adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
