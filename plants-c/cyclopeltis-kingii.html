Cyclopeltis kingii

Cyclopeltis kingii, commonly known as the King's cyclopeltis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged fronds that form a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant's leaves are typically leathery and have a deep green color, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture. Cyclopeltis kingii thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense rainforests.
