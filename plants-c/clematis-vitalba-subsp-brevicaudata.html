Clematis vitalba subsp. brevicaudata

Clematis vitalba subsp.

brevicaudata, commonly known as the short-tailed honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
