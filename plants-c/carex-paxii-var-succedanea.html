Carex paxii var. succedanea

Carex paxii var. succedanea, commonly known as the succedanea sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The botanical features of Carex paxii var. succedanea make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
