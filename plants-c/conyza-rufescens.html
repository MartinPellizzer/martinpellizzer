Conyza rufescens

Conyza rufescens, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers range from white to pale yellow.

Conyza rufescens is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
