Crataegus chrysocarpa var. faxonii

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. faxonii, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange fruits that mature in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are often followed by thorny branches. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
