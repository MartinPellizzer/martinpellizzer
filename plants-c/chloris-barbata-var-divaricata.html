Chloris barbata var. divaricata

Chloris barbata var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its distinctive, hair-like appearance and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing sandy or disturbed soils.
