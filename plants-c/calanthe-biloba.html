Calanthe biloba

Calanthe biloba, commonly known as the bilobed orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetric flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and the flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive bilobed lip.

Calanthe biloba is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
