Clematis fruticosa var. canescens

Clematis fruticosa var. canescens, commonly known as the silver Clematis, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-gray hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
