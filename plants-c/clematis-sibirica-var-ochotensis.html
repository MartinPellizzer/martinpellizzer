Clematis sibirica var. ochotensis

Clematis sibirica var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Siberia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cold climates.
