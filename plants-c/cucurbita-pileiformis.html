Cucurbita pileiformis

Cucurbita pileiformis, commonly known as the inflated pumpkin, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, balloon-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant is native to Mexico and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy surface.

The flowers are typically yellow and unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.
