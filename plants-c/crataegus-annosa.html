Crataegus annosa

Crataegus annosa, commonly known as the rough-chinned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
