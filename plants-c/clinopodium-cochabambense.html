Clinopodium cochabambense

Clinopodium cochabambense, commonly known as Andean oregano, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Clinopodium cochabambense is valued both for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems and its potential applications in herbal remedies.
