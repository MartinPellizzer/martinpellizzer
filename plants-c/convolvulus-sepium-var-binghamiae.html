Convolvulus sepium var. binghamiae

Convolvulus sepium var. binghamiae, commonly known as Bingham's morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory native to the southwestern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a notable species in botanical collections and gardens.
