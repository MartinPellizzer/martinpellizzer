Corylus rostrata var. californica

Corylus rostrata var. californica, commonly known as the California hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Corylaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and alternate, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into edible, hazelnut-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
