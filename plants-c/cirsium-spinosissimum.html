Cirsium spinosissimum

Cirsium spinosissimum, commonly known as the prickly centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in stiff bristles. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
