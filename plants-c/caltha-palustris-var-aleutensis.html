Caltha palustris var. aleutensis

Caltha palustris var. aleutensis, commonly known as the Aleutian marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

This variant is distinguished by its adaptability to colder climates and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
