Crinum scaberrimum

Crinum scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are strap-like, broad, and often have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Crinum scaberrimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
