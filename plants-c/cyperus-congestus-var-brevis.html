Cyperus congestus var. brevis

Cyperus congestus var. brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, giving it a distinct appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
