Cyperus aethiops

Cyperus aethiops, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Cyperus aethiops is widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions, often serving as an indicator of wetland ecosystems.
