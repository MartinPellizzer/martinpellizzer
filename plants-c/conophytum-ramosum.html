Conophytum ramosum

Conophytum ramosum, commonly known as the branched conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, basal rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves. The leaves are typically green with a bluish tinge and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. During the flowering season, it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
