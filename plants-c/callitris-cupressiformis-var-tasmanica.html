Callitris cupressiformis var. tasmanica

Callitris cupressiformis var. tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian white cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Tasmanian ecosystems.
