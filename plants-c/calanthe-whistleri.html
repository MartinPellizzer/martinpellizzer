Calanthe whistleri

Calanthe whistleri, commonly known as Whistler's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture.

Calanthe whistleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
