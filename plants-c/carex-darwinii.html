Carex darwinii

Carex darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex darwinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
