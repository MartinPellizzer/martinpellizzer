Cuprespinnata disticha var. pendula

Cuprespinnata disticha var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping holly, is a variant of the holly species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that hang in cascading clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds but toxic to humans and pets.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for hedges or specimen plants.
