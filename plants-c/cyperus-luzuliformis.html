Cyperus luzuliformis

Cyperus luzuliformis, commonly known as the silver flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus luzuliformis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
