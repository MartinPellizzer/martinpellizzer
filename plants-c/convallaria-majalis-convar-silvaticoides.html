Convallaria majalis convar. silvaticoides

Convallaria majalis convar. silvaticoides, commonly known as the wild lily of the valley, is a variant of the traditional lily of the valley species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread and survival in natural habitats.
