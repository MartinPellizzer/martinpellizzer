Cyperus eximius

Cyperus eximius, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or pale yellow, arranged in spikelets. Cyperus eximius thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
