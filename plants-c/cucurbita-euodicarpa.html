Cucurbita euodicarpa

Cucurbita euodicarpa, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its long, ridged, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves. Its fruits are typically harvested when mature and are used in various culinary traditions.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
