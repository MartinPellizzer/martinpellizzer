Capsicum caatingae

Capsicum caatingae, commonly known as the caatinga chili or wild pepper, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in dry, semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually yellow or white.

Capsicum caatingae is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional remedies.
