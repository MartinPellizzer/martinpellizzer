Calanthe nicolae

Calanthe nicolae, commonly known as the Nicolae's Calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe nicolae is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
