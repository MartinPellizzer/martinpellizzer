Calamagrostis curvula

Calamagrostis curvula, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silver-gray foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pink to purplish in color, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Calamagrostis curvula plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
