Crepinella huberi

Crepinella huberi, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with clusters of pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
