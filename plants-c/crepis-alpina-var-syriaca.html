Crepis alpina var. syriaca

Crepis alpina var. syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian alpine hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
