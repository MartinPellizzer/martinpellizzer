Cinnamomum dulce var. sieboldii

Cinnamomum dulce var. sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain dark, shiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its leaves are used in traditional Japanese medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.
