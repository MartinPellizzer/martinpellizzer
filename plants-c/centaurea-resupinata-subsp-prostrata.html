Centaurea resupinata subsp. prostrata

Centaurea resupinata subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as creeping centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers with dark purple or black centers, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has prostrate or decumbent stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
