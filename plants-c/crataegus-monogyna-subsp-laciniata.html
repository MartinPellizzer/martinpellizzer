Crataegus monogyna subsp. laciniata

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its finely lobed, often serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and other sunny, well-drained habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
