Cortusa amurensis

Cortusa amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cortusa amurensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
