Callicarpa fulva

Callicarpa fulva, commonly known as the red persimmon, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The berries are not only ornamental but also edible, though they are typically consumed when fully mature.
