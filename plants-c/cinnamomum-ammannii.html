Cinnamomum ammannii

Cinnamomum ammannii, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is not as commonly used for cinnamon as other species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has some medicinal uses and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
