Crassula quadrifida

Crassula quadrifida, commonly known as the chicken's foot plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, four-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Crassula quadrifida is also valued for its ease of propagation through leaf cuttings.
