Cestichis cespitosa

Cestichis cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, forming dense clumps with its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Cestichis cespitosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native environment.
