Curio articulatus f. variegatus

Curio articulatus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated string of pearls, is a unique succulent plant characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, bead-like leaves.

The leaves are typically green with white or yellow variegation, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is native to South Africa and thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil. The plant is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and occasional pruning to encourage bushier growth.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a decorative indoor plant.
