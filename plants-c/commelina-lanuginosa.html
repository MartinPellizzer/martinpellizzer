Commelina lanuginosa

Commelina lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that are surrounded by three white or pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, downy hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

Commelina lanuginosa is part of the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with similar ornamental and ecological features.
