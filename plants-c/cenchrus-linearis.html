Cenchrus linearis

Cenchrus linearis, commonly known as the rigid grass or linear grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Cenchrus linearis is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant in some regions.
