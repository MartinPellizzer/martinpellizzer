Chamaerhodos erecta var. stricta

Chamaerhodos erecta var. stricta, commonly known as the upright redroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red stems that become more prominent in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
