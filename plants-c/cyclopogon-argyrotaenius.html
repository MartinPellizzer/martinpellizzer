Cyclopogon argyrotaenius

Cyclopogon argyrotaenius, commonly known as the silver-stripped grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and wetlands. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to silver-colored glumes.

Cyclopogon argyrotaenius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
