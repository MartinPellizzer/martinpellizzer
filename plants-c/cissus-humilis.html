Cissus humilis

Cissus humilis, commonly known as the creeping grape or stonecrop, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, grape-like berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus humilis thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
