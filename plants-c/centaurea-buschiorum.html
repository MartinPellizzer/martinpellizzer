Centaurea buschiorum

Centaurea buschiorum, commonly known as the Buschiorum knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that are typically sparsely hairy, with lanceolate to oblanceolate leaves that are often serrated. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, composite flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
