Carex muricata f. argyrolepis

Carex muricata f. argyrolepis, commonly known as the silver-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex muricata.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish to brown in color.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
