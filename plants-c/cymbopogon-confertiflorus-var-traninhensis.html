Cymbopogon confertiflorus var. traninhensis

Cymbopogon confertiflorus var. traninhensis, commonly known as Traninh's citron grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences, and its rhizomes are typically fibrous and robust. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
