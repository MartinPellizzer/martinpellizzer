Clematicissus simsiana

Clematicissus simsiana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for vertical gardens and trellis coverage.
