Croton adamantinus

Croton adamantinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep reds to bright yellows and greens. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. Its leaves are typically large and have a distinctive, often asymmetrical shape.

Croton adamantinus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
