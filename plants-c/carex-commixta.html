Carex commixta

Carex commixta, commonly known as the mixed sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
