Cassia surattensis var. suffruticosa

Cassia surattensis var. suffruticosa, commonly known as Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to seven elliptical leaflets, and its most striking characteristic is its bright yellow, pendulous racemes of flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
