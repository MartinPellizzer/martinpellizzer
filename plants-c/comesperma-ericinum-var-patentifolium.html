Comesperma ericinum var. patentifolium

Comesperma ericinum var. patentifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from red to pink. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
