Capsella hispida

Capsella hispida, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a small annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Capsella hispida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and gardens.
