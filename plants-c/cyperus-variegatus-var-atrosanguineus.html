Cyperus variegatus var. atrosanguineus

Cyperus variegatus var. atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black-swan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark purple to black seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its striking dark-colored foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
