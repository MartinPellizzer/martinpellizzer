Carex gaudichaudiana var. thunbergii

Carex gaudichaudiana var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control and wetland restoration.
