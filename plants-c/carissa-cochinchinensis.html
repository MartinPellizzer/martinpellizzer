Carissa cochinchinensis

Carissa cochinchinensis, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
