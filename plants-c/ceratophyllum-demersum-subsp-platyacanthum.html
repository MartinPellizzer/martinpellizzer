Ceratophyllum demersum subsp. platyacanthum

Ceratophyllum demersum subsp.

platyacanthum, commonly known as the broad-awned watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, whorled leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often found near the water surface. It thrives in still to slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can become invasive in non-native regions.
