Carex scopulorum var. chimaphila

Carex scopulorum var. chimaphila, commonly known as the Chimaphila sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of pale yellow or greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
