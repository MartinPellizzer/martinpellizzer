Copaifera canime

Copaifera canime, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated leaves. The plant is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in medicine and perfumery. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Copaifera canime plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
