Corynaea

Corynaea, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Oleaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs native to North America. The plants are characterized by their aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, yellowish or white flowers. Corynaea species typically thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are often found in woodland areas.

They are valued for their ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
