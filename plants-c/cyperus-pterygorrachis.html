Cyperus pterygorrachis

Cyperus pterygorrachis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear small, three-lobed flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Cyperus pterygorrachis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
