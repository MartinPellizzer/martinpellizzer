Chamaecrista pilosa var. pilosa

Chamaecrista pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the partridge pea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. It has been used traditionally for its edible seeds and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
