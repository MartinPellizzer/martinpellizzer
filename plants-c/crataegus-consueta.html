Crataegus consueta

Crataegus consueta, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Crataegus consueta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
