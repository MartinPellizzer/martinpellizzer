Cyperus ornithopodioides

Cyperus ornithopodioides, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Cyperus ornithopodioides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
