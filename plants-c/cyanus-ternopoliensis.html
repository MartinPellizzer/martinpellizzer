Cyanus ternopoliensis

Cyanus ternopoliensis, commonly known as the Ternopil blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Ukraine.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
