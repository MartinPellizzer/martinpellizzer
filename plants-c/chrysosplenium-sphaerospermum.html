Chrysosplenium sphaerospermum

Chrysosplenium sphaerospermum, commonly known as the globular sphaerosperm, is a low-growing evergreen herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest floors.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
