Carex × brueggeri

Carex × brueggeri, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically bright green to golden in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex × brueggeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
