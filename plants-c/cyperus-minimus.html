Cyperus minimus

Cyperus minimus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. The plant is often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus minimus has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and it reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

It is widely distributed across temperate and tropical regions around the world.
