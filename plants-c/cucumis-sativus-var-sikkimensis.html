Cucumis sativus var. sikkimensis

Cucumis sativus var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim cucumber, is a lesser-known variant of the common cucumber.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India such as Sikkim. This variety is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thin, crisp skin and a mild, refreshing taste. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is cultivated for its edible, nutrient-rich fruits.

The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing both male and female flowers on separate plants.
