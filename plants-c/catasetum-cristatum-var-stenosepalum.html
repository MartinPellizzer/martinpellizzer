Catasetum cristatum var. stenosepalum

Catasetum cristatum var. stenosepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. The plant produces a single, erect stem with several showy blooms, each featuring a unique, twisted lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
