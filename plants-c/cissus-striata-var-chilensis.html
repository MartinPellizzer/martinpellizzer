Cissus striata var. chilensis

Cissus striata var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean grape ivy, is a climbing perennial vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or trained on trellises.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
