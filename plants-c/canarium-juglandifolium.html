Canarium juglandifolium

Canarium juglandifolium, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and consist of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark is thick and cork-like, and it is often cultivated for its valuable nutmeg and mace spices.

Canarium juglandifolium plays an important role in both ecological systems and traditional medicine.
