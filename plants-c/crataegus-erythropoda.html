Crataegus erythropoda

Crataegus erythropoda, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus erythropoda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
