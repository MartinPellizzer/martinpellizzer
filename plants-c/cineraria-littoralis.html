Cineraria littoralis

Cineraria littoralis, commonly known as the coastal cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. This species thrives in coastal environments, often growing on rocky shores or cliffs. It is well adapted to saline and windy conditions, making it a hardy plant.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
