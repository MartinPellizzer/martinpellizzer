Campanula giesekiana subsp. groenlandica

Campanula giesekiana subsp.

groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, helping to conserve moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
