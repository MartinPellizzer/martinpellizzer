Cytisanthus acanthocladus

Cytisanthus acanthocladus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral displays.
