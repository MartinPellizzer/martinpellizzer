Chloranthus erectus

Chloranthus erectus, commonly known as the upright chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually arranged in terminal clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Chloranthus erectus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
