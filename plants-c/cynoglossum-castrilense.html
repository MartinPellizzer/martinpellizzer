Cynoglossum castrilense

Cynoglossum castrilense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Cynoglossum castrilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
