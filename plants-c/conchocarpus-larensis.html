Conchocarpus larensis

Conchocarpus larensis, commonly known as the Larensis sea bean, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements. Its seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous capsule, which is adapted for dispersal by water.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
