Chenopodium ferulatum

Chenopodium ferulatum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of minerals such as iron and magnesium.
