Cassia splendida var. angustifolia

Cassia splendida var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf golden shower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The seeds are encased in slender pods and can be used for propagation.
