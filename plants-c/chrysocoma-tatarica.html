Chrysocoma tatarica

Chrysocoma tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Chrysocoma tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
