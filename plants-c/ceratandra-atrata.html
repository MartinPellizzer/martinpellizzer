Ceratandra atrata

Ceratandra atrata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are unique in appearance and often resemble small, intricate sculptures. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, Ceratandra atrata is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
