Carex sempervirens

Carex sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodland areas.

Carex sempervirens is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
