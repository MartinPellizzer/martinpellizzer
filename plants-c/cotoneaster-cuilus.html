Cotoneaster cuilus

Cotoneaster cuilus, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Cotoneaster cuilus typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
