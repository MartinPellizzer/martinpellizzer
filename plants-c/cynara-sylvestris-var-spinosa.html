Cynara sylvestris var. spinosa

Cynara sylvestris var. spinosa, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, upright growth habit. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in sharp, stiff spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
