Cypripedium × williamsianum

Cypripedium × williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams' lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a pouch-like lip that is typically pink or white. The plant has a pseudobulb structure and produces a single, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coniferous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
