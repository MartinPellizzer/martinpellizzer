Carex oligandra

Carex oligandra, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, spike-like fruits.

Carex oligandra plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
