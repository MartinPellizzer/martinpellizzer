Carex canescens var. sphaerostachya

Carex canescens var. sphaerostachya, commonly known as the spherical-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer. Its inflorescence is distinctive, with the upper spike being spherical or club-shaped, giving it its specific epithet.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
