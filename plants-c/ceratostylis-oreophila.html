Ceratostylis oreophila

Ceratostylis oreophila, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soil.

Ceratostylis oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
