Carex illegitima

Carex illegitima, commonly known as the false sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex illegitima is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
