Crataegus monogyna var. heterodonta

Crataegus monogyna var. heterodonta, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is noted for its compound leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
