Crataegus monogyna f. pinnatifida

Crataegus monogyna f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its lanceolate, pinnatifid leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
