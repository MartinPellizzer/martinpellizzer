Cucurbita pepo var. pyrula

Cucurbita pepo var. pyrula, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can vary in color from pale green to yellow or orange. The plant is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, while the vines can grow up to several meters in length.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and can be cooked in various ways.
