Cryptanthus lyman-smithii

Cryptanthus lyman-smithii, commonly known as the Lyman-Smith's candy cane plant, is a rare and striking member of the amaranth family, native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped foliage that resembles a candy cane, with deep red and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among collectors and botanists.
