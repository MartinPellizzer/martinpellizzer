Commiphora tenuipetiolata

Commiphora tenuipetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
