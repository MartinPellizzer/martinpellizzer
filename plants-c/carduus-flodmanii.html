Carduus flodmanii

Carduus flodmanii, commonly known as the Flodman's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is considered a rare and threatened species due to habitat loss and overgrazing.
