Cyrtophlebium decurrens

Cyrtophlebium decurrens, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly fern, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyrtophlebium decurrens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
