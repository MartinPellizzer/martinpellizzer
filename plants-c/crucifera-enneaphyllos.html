Crucifera enneaphyllos

Crucifera enneaphyllos, commonly known as the nine-leaf crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically has pinnately compound leaves with nine leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, round pods containing seeds.
