Cytisus hirsutissimus

Cytisus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry, and often hairy stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cytisus hirsutissimus is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.
