Crotalaria teixeirae

Crotalaria teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeira's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features distinctive yellow flowers with prominent spurred petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate. Crotalaria teixeirae is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
