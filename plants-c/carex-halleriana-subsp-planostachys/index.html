Carex Halleriana Subsp. Planostachys

Carex halleriana subsp. planostachys is a lesser-known member of the sedge family known for its unique botanical features. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and forest floors. Its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence make it easily identifiable. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated in horticulture and conservation efforts. Understanding this species is key to preserving biodiversity in its native habitats.
