Coniogramme intermedia f. striata

Coniogramme intermedia f. striata, commonly known as the variegated ivy or variegated climbing fern, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, with leaves displaying a combination of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant is a climbing fern that thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
