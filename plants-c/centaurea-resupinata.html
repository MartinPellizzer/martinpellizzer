Centaurea resupinata

Centaurea resupinata, commonly known as the prostrate cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
