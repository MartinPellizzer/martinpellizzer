Carex curvula f. elongata

Carex curvula f. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex curvula.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled at the tips. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to wetland ecosystems and its role in stabilizing soil in such habitats.
