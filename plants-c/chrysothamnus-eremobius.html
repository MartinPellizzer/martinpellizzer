Chrysothamnus eremobius

Chrysothamnus eremobius, commonly known as the desert goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
