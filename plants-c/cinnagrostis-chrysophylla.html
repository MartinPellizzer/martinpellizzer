Cinnagrostis chrysophylla

Cinnagrostis chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. It is a perennial species with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Cinnagrostis chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
