Cyathea tuyamae

Cyathea tuyamae, commonly known as the Tuyama's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the trunk and roots.

Cyathea tuyamae plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
