Cordia kingstoniana

Cordia kingstoniana, commonly known as the Kingston's cordia, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
