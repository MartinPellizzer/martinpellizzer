Cyrtogonium acuminatum

Cyrtogonium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyrtogonium acuminatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
