Cissus pseudomanikensis

Cissus pseudomanikensis, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive tendrils used for climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Cissus pseudomanikensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
