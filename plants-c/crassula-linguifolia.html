Crassula linguifolia

Crassula linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
