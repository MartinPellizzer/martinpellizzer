Ceratostylis linearifolia

Ceratostylis linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Ceratostylis is known for its unique floral structures and is closely related to other orchid genera in the subfamily Orchidoideae.
