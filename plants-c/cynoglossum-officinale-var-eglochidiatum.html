Cynoglossum officinale var. eglochidiatum

Cynoglossum officinale var. eglochidiatum, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple, and it grows in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
