Centaurea aplolepa subsp. maremmana

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

maremmana, commonly known as the Maremma thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers.

The plant has a robust stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
