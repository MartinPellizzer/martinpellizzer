Chuquiraga glabra var. rectispina

Chuquiraga glabra var. rectispina, commonly known as the smooth chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and perennial growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
