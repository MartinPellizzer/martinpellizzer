Centratherum punctatum

Centratherum punctatum, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a low-growing habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits resembling tiny tomatoes. These fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its unique fruit structure and hairy foliage.
