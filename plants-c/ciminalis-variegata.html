Ciminalis variegata

Ciminalis variegata, commonly known as the variegated ciminalis, is a unique plant characterized by its striking foliage patterns.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental value in horticulture. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinct variegation, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
