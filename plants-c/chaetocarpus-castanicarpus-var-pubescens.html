Chaetocarpus castanicarpus var. pubescens

Chaetocarpus castanicarpus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
