Commelina martyrum

Commelina martyrum, commonly known as the sacred blue flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue petals and distinctive white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Commelina martyrum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional practices.
