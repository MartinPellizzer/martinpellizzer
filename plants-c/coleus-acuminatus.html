Coleus acuminatus

Coleus acuminatus, commonly known as the spiked coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus acuminatus prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest in their landscapes.
