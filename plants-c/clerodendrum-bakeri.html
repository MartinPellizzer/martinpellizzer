Clerodendrum bakeri

Clerodendrum bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum bakeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
