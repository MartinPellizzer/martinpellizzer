Cremosperma auriculatum

Cremosperma auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate cremosperma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often found in coastal regions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
