Carex communis var. amplisquama

Carex communis var. amplisquama, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. This variety thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, leafy stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
