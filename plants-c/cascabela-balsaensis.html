Cascabela balsaensis

Cascabela balsaensis, commonly known as the Balsam Tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
