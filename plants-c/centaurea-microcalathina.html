Centaurea microcalathina

Centaurea microcalathina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Centaurea microcalathina is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
