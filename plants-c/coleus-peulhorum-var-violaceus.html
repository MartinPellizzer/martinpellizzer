Coleus peulhorum var. violaceus

Coleus peulhorum var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-leaved coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
