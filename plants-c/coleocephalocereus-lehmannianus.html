Coleocephalocereus lehmannianus

Coleocephalocereus lehmannianus, commonly known as the Lehmann's candelabra cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its multiple stems that branch out to form a candelabra-like structure. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its spines are relatively short and often covered in a fine white bloom.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
