Carex peiktusani

Carex peiktusani, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex peiktusani is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
