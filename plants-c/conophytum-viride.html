Conophytum viride

Conophytum viride, commonly known as the green conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Conophytum viride thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.
