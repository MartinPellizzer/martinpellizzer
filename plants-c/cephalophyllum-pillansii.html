Cephalophyllum pillansii

Cephalophyllum pillansii, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot's hat, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Cephalophyllum pillansii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
