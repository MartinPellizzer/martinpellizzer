Cyathea myosuroides

Cyathea myosuroides, commonly known as the Myosotis tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea myosuroides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
