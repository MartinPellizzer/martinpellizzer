Carex myosurus var. gracilior

Carex myosurus var. gracilior, commonly known as slender bog sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This variety is typically found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
