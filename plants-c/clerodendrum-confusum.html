Clerodendrum confusum

Clerodendrum confusum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its decorative foliage and unique floral display.

Clerodendrum confusum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
