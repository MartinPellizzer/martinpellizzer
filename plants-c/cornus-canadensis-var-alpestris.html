Cornus canadensis var. alpestris

Cornus canadensis var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine cornel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
