Cerasus vulgaris f. plena

Cerasus vulgaris f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree characterized by its dense, double blooms.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that are highly ornamental and bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits in the summer.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
