Collaea velutina

Collaea velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a flowering plant native to Australia, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in dry, sandy soils. Its small, white or pinkish flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
