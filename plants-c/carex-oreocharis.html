Carex oreocharis

Carex oreocharis, commonly known as the blackroot sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by distinctive three-sided seed capsules.

Carex oreocharis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
