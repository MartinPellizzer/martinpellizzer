Cieca fuscescens

Cieca fuscescens, commonly known as the black velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its dark, velvety foliage.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is known for its striking appearance. Its leaves are dense and mat-like, forming a compact rosette.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively rare and requires careful cultivation to maintain its health.
