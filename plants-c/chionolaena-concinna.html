Chionolaena concinna

Chionolaena concinna, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chionolaena concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
