Cyrilla racemiflora var. parvifolia

Cyrilla racemiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal areas.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
