Carex heleonastes f. sendtneriana

Carex heleonastes f. sendtneriana, commonly known as the Sendtner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found in the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
