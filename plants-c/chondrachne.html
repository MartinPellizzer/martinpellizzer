Chondrachne

Chondrachne, commonly known as the brittlegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Chondrachne species are known for their ability to thrive in harsh environments, making them resilient to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its flowers are usually small and clustered, contributing to its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
