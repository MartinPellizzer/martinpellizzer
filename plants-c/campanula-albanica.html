Campanula albanica

Campanula albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
