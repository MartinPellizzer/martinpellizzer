Campanula langsdorffiana

Campanula langsdorffiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slight hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and prolonged blooming period.
