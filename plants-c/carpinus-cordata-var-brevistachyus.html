Carpinus cordata var. brevistachyus

Carpinus cordata var. brevistachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stalked hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are a characteristic feature of the Betulaceae family. Its fruit is a small, winged nut enclosed in a cupule, which splits open to release the seed.
