Centaurea centriflora

Centaurea centriflora, commonly known as the blueweed or common centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a radiate pattern. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it is often found in disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually unbranched.

Centaurea centriflora is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
