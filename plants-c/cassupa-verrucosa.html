Cassupa verrucosa

Cassupa verrucosa, commonly known as the rough bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, inflated, bladder-like fruits that are covered in a rough, warty texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Cassupa verrucosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
