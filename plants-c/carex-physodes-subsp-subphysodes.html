Carex physodes subsp. subphysodes

Carex physodes subsp.

subphysodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
