Centropogon lagotis

Centropogon lagotis, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Centropogon lagotis is adapted to warm climates and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
