Cybianthus magnoliifolius

Cybianthus magnoliifolius, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of magnolia trees, hence its common name. The plant has broad, glossy leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It blooms in spring, producing fragrant, white or pale pink petals.

Cybianthus magnoliifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
