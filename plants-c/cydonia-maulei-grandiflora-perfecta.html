Cydonia maulei grandiflora-perfecta

Cydonia maulei grandiflora-perfecta, commonly known as the Maule quince, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit that resembles a pear in shape but has a thick, leathery skin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
