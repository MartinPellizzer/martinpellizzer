Cyperus foliosissimus

Cyperus foliosissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green or brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Cyperus foliosissimus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
