Calanthe furcata

Calanthe furcata, commonly known as the forked calanthe, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocks or tree trunks. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Calanthe furcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
