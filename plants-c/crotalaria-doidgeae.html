Crotalaria doidgeae

Crotalaria doidgeae, commonly known as the Doidge's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its striking yellow flowers with spurred petals make it a distinctive species within the genus Crotalaria.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
