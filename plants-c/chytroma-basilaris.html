Chytroma basilaris

Chytroma basilaris, commonly known as the basal chytromycete, is a unique fungal species that exhibits specific botanical characteristics.

It is primarily found in soil and decaying organic matter, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling. The organism has a distinct basidial structure, which is essential for its reproductive process. Its morphology includes a basal hyphal network that supports its growth and sporulation.

Due to its specialized habitat and structure, it is of interest in mycological studies for its ecological and potential biotechnological applications.
