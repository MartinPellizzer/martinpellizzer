Clinopodium cimbricum

Clinopodium cimbricum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Clinopodium cimbricum is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
