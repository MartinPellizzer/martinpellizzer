Cirsium brevicaule

Cirsium brevicaule, commonly known as the short-hairy thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in dense, short hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom from late summer to early autumn.
