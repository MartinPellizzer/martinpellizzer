Cucurbita maxima var. naudinii

Cucurbita maxima var. naudinii, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivated variant of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits that can weigh several kilograms, often reaching weights of over 100 pounds. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. Its vines are vigorous growers, producing large leaves and yellow flowers that give way to the edible fruit.

The fruit is typically used in various culinary applications, including pies, soups, and roasted dishes.
