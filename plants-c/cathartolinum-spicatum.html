Cathartolinum spicatum

Cathartolinum spicatum, commonly known as the spiky cathartolinum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spiky flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open meadows.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
