Ceratostylis pinguis

Ceratostylis pinguis, commonly known as the fat-stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
