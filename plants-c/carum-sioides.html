Carum sioides

Carum sioides, commonly known as black cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, dark purple to black seeds that are highly valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, while the seeds are used in both culinary and pharmaceutical applications.

Carum sioides is known for its distinctive licorice-like scent and is cultivated for its essential oils and health benefits.
