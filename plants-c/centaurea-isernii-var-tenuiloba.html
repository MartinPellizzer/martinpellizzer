Centaurea isernii var. tenuiloba

Centaurea isernii var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
