Campanula rapunculus var. elatior

Campanula rapunculus var. elatior, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Campanula rapunculus var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
