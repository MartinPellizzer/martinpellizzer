Centaurea phrygia subsp. pseudophrygia

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

pseudophrygia, commonly known as the Anatolian cornflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
