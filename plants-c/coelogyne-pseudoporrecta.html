Coelogyne pseudoporrecta

Coelogyne pseudoporrecta, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral lips, which are adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow, green, and white, making it a visually appealing species.

Coelogyne pseudoporrecta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
