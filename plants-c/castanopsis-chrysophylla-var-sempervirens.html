Castanopsis chrysophylla var. sempervirens

Castanopsis chrysophylla var. sempervirens, commonly known as the golden-leafed tanoak, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to the temperate rainforests of southern Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, golden-yellow foliage that remains throughout the year, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a cupule, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
