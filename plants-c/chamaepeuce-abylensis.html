Chamaepeuce abylensis

Chamaepeuce abylensis, commonly known as the Algerian juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
