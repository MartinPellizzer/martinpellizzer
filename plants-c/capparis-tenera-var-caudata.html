Capparis tenera var. caudata

Capparis tenera var. caudata, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name.
