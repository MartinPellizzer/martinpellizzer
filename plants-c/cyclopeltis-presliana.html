Cyclopeltis presliana

Cyclopeltis presliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cyclopeltis presliana is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
