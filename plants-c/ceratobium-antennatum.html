Ceratobium antennatum

Ceratobium antennatum, commonly known as the skyrocket, is a small, mat-forming flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and tiny, clustered flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular plant among gardeners in suitable climates.
