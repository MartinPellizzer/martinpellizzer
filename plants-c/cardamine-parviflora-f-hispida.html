Cardamine parviflora f. hispida

Cardamine parviflora f. hispida, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the species name "hispida," and its flowers are usually borne in clusters.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
