Cedronella coccinea

Cedronella coccinea, commonly known as the red cedronella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cedronella coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
