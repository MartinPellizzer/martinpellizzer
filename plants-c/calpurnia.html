Calpurnia

Calpurnia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Calpurnia species are typically shrubs or small trees, with compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
