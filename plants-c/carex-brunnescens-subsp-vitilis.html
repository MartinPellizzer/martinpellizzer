Carex brunnescens subsp. vitilis

Carex brunnescens subsp.

vitilis, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, with a robust rhizomatous root system. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its pale or whitish coloration on the lower parts of the leaf blades, giving it a distinctive appearance.
