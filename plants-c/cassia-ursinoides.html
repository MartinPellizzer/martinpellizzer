Cassia ursinoides

Cassia ursinoides, commonly known as the white cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Cassia ursinoides thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
