Cerinthe minor var. ciliaris

Cerinthe minor var. ciliaris, commonly known as the lesser cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower borders.
