Chlorophytum immaculatum

Chlorophytum immaculatum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a popular ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that are typically variegated with white and green stripes. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Chlorophytum immaculatum is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be easily propagated to grow new plants.
