Cyphostemma macropus

Cyphostemma macropus, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with recurved, spiny tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Cyphostemma macropus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
