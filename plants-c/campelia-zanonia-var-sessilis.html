Campelia zanonia var. sessilis

Campelia zanonia var. sessilis, commonly known as the Zanzibar dahlia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the island of Zanzibar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

The variety *sessilis* is distinguished by its sessile (short-stemmed) flowers, which give it a unique appearance compared to other cultivars of *Campelia zanonia*.
