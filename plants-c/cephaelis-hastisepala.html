Cephaelis hastisepala

Cephaelis hastisepala, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
