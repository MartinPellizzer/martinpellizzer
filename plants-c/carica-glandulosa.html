Carica glandulosa

Carica glandulosa, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree that produces small, yellow to orange fruits with a waxy coating. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with glandular dots. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
