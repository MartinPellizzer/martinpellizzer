Centaurea × soproniensis

Centaurea × soproniensis, commonly known as the Sopron hybrid cornflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea stoebe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Centaurea × soproniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.
