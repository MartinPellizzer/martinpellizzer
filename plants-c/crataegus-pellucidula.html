Crataegus pellucidula

Crataegus pellucidula, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Crataegus pellucidula is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
