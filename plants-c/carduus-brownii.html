Carduus brownii

Carduus brownii, commonly known as the brown thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
