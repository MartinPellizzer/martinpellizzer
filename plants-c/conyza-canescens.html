Conyza canescens

Conyza canescens, commonly known as white cocklebur, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that are often covered in bristly structures. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
