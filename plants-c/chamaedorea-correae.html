Chamaedorea correae

Chamaedorea correae, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces long, feather-like leaves that are typically green and can grow up to 2 meters in length. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Chamaedorea correae is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
