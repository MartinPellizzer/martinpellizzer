Centaurea montana var. pyrenaica

Centaurea montana var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region.

It features slender, branched stems and produces striking blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
