Chrysanthemum indicum var. coreanum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. coreanum, commonly known as the Korean chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and sturdy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
