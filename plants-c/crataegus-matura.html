Crataegus matura

Crataegus matura, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
