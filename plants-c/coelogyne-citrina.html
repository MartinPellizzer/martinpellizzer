Coelogyne citrina

Coelogyne citrina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking yellow and green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has long, narrow leaves and grows on tree trunks or branches, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from rainwater and debris.

Coelogyne citrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
