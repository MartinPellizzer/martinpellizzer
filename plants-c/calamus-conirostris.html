Calamus conirostris

Calamus conirostris, commonly known as the broad-beaked calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
