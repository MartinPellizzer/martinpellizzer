Carex stilbophaea

Carex stilbophaea, commonly known as the silverstem sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, particularly found in wetland and marshy environments. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically silver-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex stilbophaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
