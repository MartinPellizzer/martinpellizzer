Clematis maximowicziana

Clematis maximowicziana, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are alternate along the stems.

Clematis maximowicziana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
