Commelina linearifolia

Commelina linearifolia, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its roots are often fibrous and help stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Commelina linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
