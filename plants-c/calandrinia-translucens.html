Calandrinia translucens

Calandrinia translucens, commonly known as the translucent purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it well-adapted to dry environments.

Calandrinia translucens is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
