Canavalia rupicola

Canavalia rupicola, commonly known as rock bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid or rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Canavalia rupicola is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
