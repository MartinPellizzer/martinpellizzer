Cicer cuneatum

Cicer cuneatum, commonly known as the Armenian pea or mountain pea, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces elongated pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Cicer cuneatum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
