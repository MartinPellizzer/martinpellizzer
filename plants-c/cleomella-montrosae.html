Cleomella montrosae

Cleomella montrosae, commonly known as the Montrose cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, papery flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lobed and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Cleomella montrosae is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
