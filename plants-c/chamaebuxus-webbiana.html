Chamaebuxus webbiana

Chamaebuxus webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that attract birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Chamaebuxus webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
