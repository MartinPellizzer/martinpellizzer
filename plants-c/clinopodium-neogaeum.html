Clinopodium neogaeum

Clinopodium neogaeum, commonly known as New Zealand mint, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Clinopodium neogaeum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
