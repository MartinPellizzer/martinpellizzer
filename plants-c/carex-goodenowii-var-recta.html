Carex goodenowii var. recta

Carex goodenowii var. recta, commonly known as the straight-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown or yellowish flowers in the summer, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
