Coreopsis ulugurica

Coreopsis ulugurica, commonly known as the Uluguru coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within forested areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure. It is adapted to the humid and shaded conditions of its native habitat.

Coreopsis ulugurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
