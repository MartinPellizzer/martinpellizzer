Caesalpinia ouenensis

Caesalpinia ouenensis, commonly known as the Ouenense flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for its ecological benefits.
