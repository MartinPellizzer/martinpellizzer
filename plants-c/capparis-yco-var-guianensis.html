Capparis yco var. guianensis

Capparis yco var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan caper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
