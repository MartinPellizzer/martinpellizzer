Chilocarpus globosus

Chilocarpus globosus, commonly known as the globular hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
