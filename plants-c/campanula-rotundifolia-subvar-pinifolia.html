Campanula rotundifolia subvar. pinifolia

Campanula rotundifolia subvar. pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. This subvariety is typically found in moist, acidic soils near coniferous forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
