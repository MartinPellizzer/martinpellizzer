Chenopodium polyspermum var. rubrum

Chenopodium polyspermum var. rubrum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, especially under stress conditions. The plant produces small, green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, triangular seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically by Indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
