Carex minor

Carex minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually found at the tips of the stems.
