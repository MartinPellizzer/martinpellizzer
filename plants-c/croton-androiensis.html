Croton androiensis

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique sap and diverse leaf structures. The plant typically features alternate, palmate leaves that vary in shape and size. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia.
