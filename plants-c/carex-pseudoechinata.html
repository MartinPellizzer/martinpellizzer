Carex pseudoechinata

Carex pseudoechinata, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex pseudoechinata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
