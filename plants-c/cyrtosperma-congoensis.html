Cyrtosperma congoensis

Cyrtosperma congoensis, commonly known as the Congo arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of many aroid species. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
