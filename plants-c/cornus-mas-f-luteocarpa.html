Cornus mas f. luteocarpa

Cornus mas f. luteocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited cornel.

It is a variant of the cornel dogwood, characterized by its distinctive yellow berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in woodland areas and along riverbanks. The fruits are edible but typically tart, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It has a compact growth habit and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
