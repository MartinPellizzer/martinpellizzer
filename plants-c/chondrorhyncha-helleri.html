Chondrorhyncha helleri

Chondrorhyncha helleri, commonly known as the Heller's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, submerged or floating leaves that often have bladders for trapping prey. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its specialized structures for nutrient absorption. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
