Cynanchum acutum subsp. sibiricum

Cynanchum acutum subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, adapted to survive in poor, sandy soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
