Coronilla perieri

Coronilla perieri, commonly known as the Periera Crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration projects.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in various habitats.
