Christella × intermedia

Christella × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid Christella, is a species resulting from the cross between Christella monanthica and Christella quadrifida.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. It has fibrous roots and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
