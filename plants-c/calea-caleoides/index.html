Calea Caleoides

Calea caleoides is a striking plant native to Mexico known for its unique bioluminescent properties. Its leaves glow faintly in the dark, creating a mesmerizing natural display. This phenomenon is due to specialized cells containing luciferin and luciferase. Often called the "ghost flower," it captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. The plant's beauty and mystery make it a subject of ongoing scientific study and admiration.
