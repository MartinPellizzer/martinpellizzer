Crepis czuensis

Crepis czuensis, commonly known as the Chinese mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Crepis czuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
