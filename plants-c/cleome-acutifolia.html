Cleome acutifolia

Cleome acutifolia, commonly known as the cutleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with serrated edges, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cleome acutifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
