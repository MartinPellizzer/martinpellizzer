Chrysobotrya lindleyana

Chrysobotrya lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads.

Chrysobotrya lindleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
