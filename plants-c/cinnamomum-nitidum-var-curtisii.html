Cinnamomum nitidum var. curtisii

Cinnamomum nitidum var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's camphor tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous forest areas.
