Cerasus trichantha

Cerasus trichantha, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, sweet drupes that ripen in summer.

The hairy cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
