Cochemiea pondii subsp. setispina

Cochemiea pondii subsp.

setispina, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a low-growing, mat-like structure.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small and are shed as the plant matures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.
