Cola laurentii

Cola laurentii, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that eventually develop into woody pods containing oval seeds. Cola laurentii is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
