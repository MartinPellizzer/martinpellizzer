Cucumis melo f. dulcis

Cucumis melo f. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet melon or honeydew melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, round, green fruit with a smooth, waxy skin. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy, pale green flesh that is typically seedless in most commercial varieties. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely grown for its culinary use in salads, beverages, and as a fresh fruit.
