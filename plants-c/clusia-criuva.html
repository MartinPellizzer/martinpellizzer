Clusia criuva

Clusia criuva, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is evergreen and thrives in humid, warm climates with partial shade.

Clusia criuva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate low light conditions.
