Chamaesyce remyi

Chamaesyce remyi, commonly known as the Florida marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in wetland environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
