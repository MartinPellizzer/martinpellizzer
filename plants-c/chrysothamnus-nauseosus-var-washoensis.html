Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. washoensis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. washoensis, commonly known as the Wasco goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and gray-green foliage, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive, sometimes unpleasant odor.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
