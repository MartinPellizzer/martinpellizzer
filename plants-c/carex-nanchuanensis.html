Carex nanchuanensis

Carex nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex nanchuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
