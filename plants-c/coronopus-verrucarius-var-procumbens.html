Coronopus verrucarius var. procumbens

Coronopus verrucarius var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear.

This variant is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit. It features small, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in dry, open habitats.
