Cordiopsis mirabiloides

Cordiopsis mirabiloides, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that range from deep purples to bright yellows, creating a visually stunning display. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a miniature rainbow. Its roots are adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often growing epiphytically on tree trunks.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, Cordiopsis mirabiloides is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
