Carex × crinitoides

Carex × crinitoides, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, silky-hairy leaves that give it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × crinitoides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
