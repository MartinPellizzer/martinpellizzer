Ceropegia richardiana

Ceropegia richardiana, commonly known as the Cape violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers that are often borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
