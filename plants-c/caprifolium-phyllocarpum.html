Caprifolium phyllocarpum

Caprifolium phyllocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence.

Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
