Cacalia lanceolata var. elliottii

Cacalia lanceolata var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
