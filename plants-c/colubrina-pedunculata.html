Colubrina pedunculata

Colubrina pedunculata, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Colubrina pedunculata is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
