Canna heliconiifolia

Canna heliconiifolia, commonly known as the red bamboo canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Canna heliconiifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage and flowers add vibrant color and texture to tropical landscapes.
