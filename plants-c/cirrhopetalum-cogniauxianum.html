Cirrhopetalum cogniauxianum

Cirrhopetalum cogniauxianum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with elongated petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
