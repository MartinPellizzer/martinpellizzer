Capsella curvisiliqua

Capsella curvisiliqua, commonly known as the bent-silicle weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, curved silique fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
