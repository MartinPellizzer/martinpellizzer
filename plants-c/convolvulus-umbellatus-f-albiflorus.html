Convolvulus umbellatus f. albiflorus

Convolvulus umbellatus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with a slight pinkish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens for its delicate blooms.
