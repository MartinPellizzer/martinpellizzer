Campanula linifolia var. humilis

Campanula linifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the low-growing bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
