Commicarpus verticillatus

Commicarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered false indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in spring.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release the seeds.
