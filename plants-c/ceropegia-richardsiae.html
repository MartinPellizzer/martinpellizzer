Ceropegia richardsiae

Ceropegia richardsiae, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
