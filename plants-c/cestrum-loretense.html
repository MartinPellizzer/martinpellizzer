Cestrum loretense

Cestrum loretense, commonly known as the Loretto nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum loretense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates.
