Cactus heyderi var. hemisphaericus

Cactus heyderi var. hemisphaericus, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a striking visual element to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
