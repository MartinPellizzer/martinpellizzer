Clerodendrum buxifolium

Clerodendrum buxifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often mistaken for those of boxwood plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a dense, bushy growth habit.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
