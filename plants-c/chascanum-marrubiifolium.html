Chascanum marrubiifolium

Chascanum marrubiifolium, commonly known as the Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage.

Chascanum marrubiifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
