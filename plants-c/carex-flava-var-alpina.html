Carex flava var. alpina

Carex flava var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-yellow flowers, typical of the genus Carex. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
