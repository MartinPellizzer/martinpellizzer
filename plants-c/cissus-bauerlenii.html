Cissus bauerlenii

Cissus bauerlenii, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a member of the family Vitaceae and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves that have a textured surface. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus bauerlenii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
