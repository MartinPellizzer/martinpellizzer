Carex mielichhoferi

Carex mielichhoferi, commonly known as the Mielichhofer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Carex mielichhoferi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
