Cereus aethiops var. landbeckii

Cereus aethiops var. landbeckii, commonly known as the Landbeck's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
