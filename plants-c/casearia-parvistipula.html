Casearia parvistipula

Casearia parvistipula, commonly known as the small-tipped casearia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates with well-drained soil.
