Chlorophytum blepharophyllum

Chlorophytum blepharophyllum, commonly known as the "earthy spider plant," is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are dark green with a metallic sheen, and it produces delicate, grass-like flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It has a compact growth habit and is often used as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its foliage is also known to be slightly toxic to pets if ingested.
