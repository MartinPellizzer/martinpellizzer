Cardamine schinziana f. lasiocarpa

Cardamine schinziana f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of short, hairy glands. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its hirsute foliage and unique floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Cardamine genus.
