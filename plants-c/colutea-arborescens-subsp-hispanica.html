Colutea arborescens subsp. hispanica

Colutea arborescens subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish broom. It is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
