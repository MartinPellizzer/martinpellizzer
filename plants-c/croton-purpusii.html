Croton purpusii

Croton purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
