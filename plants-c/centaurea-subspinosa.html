Centaurea subspinosa

Centaurea subspinosa, commonly known as the thorny centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Centaurea subspinosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
