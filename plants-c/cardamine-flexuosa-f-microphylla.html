Cardamine flexuosa f. microphylla

Cardamine flexuosa f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf water crowfoot, is a delicate aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a basal rosette structure. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in a single umbel, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value.
