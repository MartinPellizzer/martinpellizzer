Cicuta ninci

Cicuta ninci, commonly known as the lesser water hemlock, is a toxic plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant contains potent toxic compounds, primarily cicutoxin, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is often considered one of the most dangerous plants in its habitat.
