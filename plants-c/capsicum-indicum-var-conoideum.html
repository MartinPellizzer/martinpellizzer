Capsicum indicum var. conoideum

Capsicum indicum var. conoideum, commonly known as the horn pepper, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature, though they can also be red. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and spicy fruits.

The species is closely related to other chili peppers and is part of the Solanaceae family.
