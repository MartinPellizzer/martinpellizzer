Cerastium peruvianum

Cerastium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian chickweed, is a low-growing flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium peruvianum is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions, and is often found in moist, rocky or sandy soils.
