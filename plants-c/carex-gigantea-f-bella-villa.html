Carex gigantea f. bella-villa

Carex gigantea f. bella-villa, commonly known as the Bella Villa Giant Sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, broad leaves with a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to reddish-brown, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for wetland gardens or water features.

Its dense growth habit also makes it an effective erosion control plant in riparian zones.
