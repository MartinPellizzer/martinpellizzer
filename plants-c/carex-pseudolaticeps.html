Carex pseudolaticeps

Carex pseudolaticeps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable environments.

Carex pseudolaticeps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
