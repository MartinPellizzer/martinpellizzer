Chamaerhodiola asiatica

Chamaerhodiola asiatica, commonly known as the Asian sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Siberia. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Chamaerhodiola asiatica is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
