Cytisus ambiguus

Cytisus ambiguus, commonly known as the creeping yellow broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, sprawling growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus ambiguus is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
