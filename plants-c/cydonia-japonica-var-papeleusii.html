Cydonia japonica var. papeleusii

Cydonia japonica var. papeleusii, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces yellowish-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. The plant features oval, dark green leaves and has a compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.
