Conyza canadensis var. robusta

Conyza canadensis var. robusta, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread easily.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions contributes to its widespread distribution.
