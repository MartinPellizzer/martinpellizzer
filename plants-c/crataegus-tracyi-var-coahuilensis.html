Crataegus tracyi var. coahuilensis

Crataegus tracyi var. coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuiltecan hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico and southern Texas.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
