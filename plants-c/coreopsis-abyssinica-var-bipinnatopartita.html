Coreopsis abyssinica var. bipinnatopartita

Coreopsis abyssinica var. bipinnatopartita, commonly known as the Abyssinian coreopsis, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow upright, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and a yellow disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with a bipinnate structure that gives it a feathery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
