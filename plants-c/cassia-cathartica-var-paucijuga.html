Cassia cathartica var. paucijuga

Cassia cathartica var. paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for its potential health benefits.
