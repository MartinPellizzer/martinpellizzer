Citrus salicifolia

Citrus salicifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage, which is used in teas and culinary applications. Its leaves contain high levels of volatile oils, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Citrus salicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
