Clematis fruticosa var. tomentella

Clematis fruticosa var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with soft, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for rock gardens, borders, and containers.
