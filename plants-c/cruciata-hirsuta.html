Cruciata hirsuta

Cruciata hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cruciata, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
