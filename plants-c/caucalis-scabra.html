Caucalis scabra

Caucalis scabra, commonly known as the rough caucalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. The plant has hairy or rough-textured foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is found in temperate regions.

Caucalis scabra is often used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
