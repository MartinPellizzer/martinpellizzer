Capparis kollimalayana

Capparis kollimalayana, commonly known as the Kollimalayana Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forests. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
