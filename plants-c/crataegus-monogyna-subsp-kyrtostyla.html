Crataegus monogyna subsp. kyrtostyla

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

kyrtostyla, commonly known as the Scottish hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
