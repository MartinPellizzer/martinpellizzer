Convolvulus serotinus

Convolvulus serotinus, commonly known as late bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer and autumn. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a tubular structure at the base.

Convolvulus serotinus is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
