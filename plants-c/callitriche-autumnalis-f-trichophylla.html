Callitriche autumnalis f. trichophylla

Callitriche autumnalis f. trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant's leaves are covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, which are followed by small, rounded fruits.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
