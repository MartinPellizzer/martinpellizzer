Callicarpa loureiroi var. laxiflora

Callicarpa loureiroi var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces vibrant purple berries in the fall, which are a key feature for its ornamental value. These berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
