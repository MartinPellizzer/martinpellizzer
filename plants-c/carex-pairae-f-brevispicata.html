Carex pairae f. brevispicata

Carex pairae f. brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spiked sedges, is a variant of the Carex pairae species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of short, dense spikes that are usually reddish-brown in color.

The botanical features of this sedge make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
