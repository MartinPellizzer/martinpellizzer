Calea heteropappa

Calea heteropappa, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are often white or purple.

Calea heteropappa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
