Crocus sieberi var. atticus

Crocus sieberi var. atticus, commonly known as the Attic crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn to early winter. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and dry soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in ancient Greek culture.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
