Chrysothamnus asper

Chrysothamnus asper, commonly known as the rough tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dense, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rough, hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, while its aromatic foliage emits a strong, resinous scent.

Chrysothamnus asper is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
