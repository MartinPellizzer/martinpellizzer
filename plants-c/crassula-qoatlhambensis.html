Crassula qoatlhambensis

Crassula qoatlhambensis, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it has become a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
