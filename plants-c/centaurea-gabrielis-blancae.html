Centaurea gabrielis-blancae

Centaurea gabrielis-blancae, commonly known as the White Gabriel's Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, pale blue to white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
