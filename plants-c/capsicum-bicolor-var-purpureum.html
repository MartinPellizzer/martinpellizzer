Capsicum bicolor var. purpureum

Capsicum bicolor var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple horn pepper, is a unique variety of chili pepper native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-purple coloration, which develops as the fruit matures. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, and the fruit is elongated with a pointed tip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to medium heat level, making it popular in both gardens and culinary use.
