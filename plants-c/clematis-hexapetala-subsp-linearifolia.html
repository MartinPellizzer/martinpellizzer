Clematis hexapetala subsp. linearifolia

Clematis hexapetala subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved clematis, is a perennial vine native to East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
