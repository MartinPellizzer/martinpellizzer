Centaurea phrygia subsp. carpatica

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
