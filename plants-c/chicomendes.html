Chicomendes

Chicomendes, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry containing several seeds.

Chicomendes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
