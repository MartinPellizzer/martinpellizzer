Cyathea tinganensis

Cyathea tinganensis, commonly known as the Tinganensis tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of dense tropical forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
