Cerasus pygmaea

Cerasus pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Cerasus pygmaea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to container gardening due to its size.
