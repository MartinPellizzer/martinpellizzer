Chimaphila taiwaniana

Chimaphila taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan Christmas fern, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in late winter to early spring, typically in shades of white or pink. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, shade-tolerant plant.
