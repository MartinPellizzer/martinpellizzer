Campanula linifolia var. albanica

Campanula linifolia var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive silvery-blue underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
