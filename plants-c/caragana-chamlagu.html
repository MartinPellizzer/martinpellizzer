Caragana chamlagu

Caragana chamlagu, commonly known as the Afghan pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used for both food and traditional medicine.

Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
