Cardamine × fringsii

Cardamine × fringsii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cardamine pratensis and Cardamine bulbifera, characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
