Cynanchum multinerve var. kiyohikoanum

Cynanchum multinerve var. kiyohikoanum, commonly known as the Kiyohiko's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
