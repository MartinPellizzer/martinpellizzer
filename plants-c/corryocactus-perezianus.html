Corryocactus perezianus

Corryocactus perezianus, commonly known as the Perezianus cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water for survival in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
