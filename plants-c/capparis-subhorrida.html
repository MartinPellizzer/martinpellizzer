Capparis subhorrida

Capparis subhorrida, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels, and it is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
