Carex praecox f. umbrosiformis

Carex praecox f. umbrosiformis, commonly known as the early sedge in shaded form, is a variant of the early sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. This form thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to wetland restoration and ecological landscaping projects.
