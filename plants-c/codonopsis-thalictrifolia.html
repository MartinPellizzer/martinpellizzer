Codonopsis thalictrifolia

Codonopsis thalictrifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
