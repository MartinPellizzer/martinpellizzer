Cyperus bicolor

Cyperus bicolor, commonly known as the pink flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the pinkish or reddish bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus bicolor is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
