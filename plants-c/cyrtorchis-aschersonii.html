Cyrtorchis aschersonii

Cyrtorchis aschersonii, commonly known as the aschersonii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces a single spike with several showy, violet or purple flowers, each with a distinct lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or forest margins.

Cyrtorchis aschersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
