Cassia flavicoma

Cassia flavicoma, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Cassia flavicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
