Cerinthe major var. macrosiphonia

Cerinthe major var. macrosiphonia, commonly known as the large-horned cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, with prominent, elongated lower lips. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
