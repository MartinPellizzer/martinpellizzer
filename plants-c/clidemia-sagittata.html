Clidemia sagittata

Clidemia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf mitegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color.
