Chenopodium quercifolium

Chenopodium quercifolium, commonly known as bear's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, maple-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
