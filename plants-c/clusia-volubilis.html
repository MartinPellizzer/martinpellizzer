Clusia volubilis

Clusia volubilis, commonly known as the rubber plant or wax plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It is a member of the Clusiaceae family, also known as the guttiferae family, which includes other plants with similar adaptive features.

Clusia volubilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
