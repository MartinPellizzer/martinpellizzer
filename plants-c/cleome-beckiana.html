Cleome beckiana

Cleome beckiana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Cleome beckiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
