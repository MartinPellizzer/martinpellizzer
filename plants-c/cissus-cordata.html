Cissus cordata

Cissus cordata, commonly known as heartleaf cissus, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cordata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
