Campanula cochleariifolia var. bellardii

Campanula cochleariifolia var. bellardii, commonly known as the bell-shaped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. This variety is closely related to other campanula species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, blooming from late spring to early summer.
