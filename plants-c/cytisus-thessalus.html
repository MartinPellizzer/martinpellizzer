Cytisus thessalus

Cytisus thessalus, commonly known as the Thessalian broom, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cytisus thessalus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
