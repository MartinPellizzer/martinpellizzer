Capsicum microcarpum f. herbaceum

Capsicum microcarpum f. herbaceum, commonly known as the dwarf chili pepper, is a compact, herbaceous variant of the species.

It features small, rounded fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. The plant has a bushy growth habit with numerous small leaves and short stems. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild flavor.

This form is well-suited for container gardening and small spaces.
