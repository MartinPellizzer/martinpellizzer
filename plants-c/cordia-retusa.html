Cordia retusa

Cordia retusa, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Cordia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
