Crataegus pedicellata var. ellwangeriana

Crataegus pedicellata var. ellwangeriana, commonly known as the Ellwangeriana hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in native habitats.
