Chenopodium album var. corymbosopaniculatum

Chenopodium album var. corymbosopaniculatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters plant.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variant is known for its edible leaves, though they can be bitter and require thorough washing before consumption.
