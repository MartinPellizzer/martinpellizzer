Calceolaria ferruginea

Calceolaria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, pouch-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that help it adapt to its high-altitude environment. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
