Centaurea semilunaris

Centaurea semilunaris, commonly known as the moonflower centaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white central disk, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies and garden cultivation.
