Callicarpa japonica var. taquetii

Callicarpa japonica var. taquetii, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
