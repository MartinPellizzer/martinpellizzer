Ceropegia kerrii

Ceropegia kerrii, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces striking, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia kerrii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
