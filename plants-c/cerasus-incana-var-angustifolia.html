Cerasus incana var. angustifolia

Cerasus incana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
