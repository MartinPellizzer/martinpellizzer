Cymbidium ochroleucum

Cymbidium ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
