Cornus comosa

Cornus comosa, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink bract-like flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cornus comosa thrives in partial shade to full sun and is commonly used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
