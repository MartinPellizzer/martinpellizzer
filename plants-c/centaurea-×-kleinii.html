Centaurea × kleinii

Centaurea × kleinii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple radial flowers with prominent yellow centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.

Centaurea × kleinii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
