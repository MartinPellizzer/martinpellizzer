Coccinia subspicata

Coccinia subspicata, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that are typically unisexual. Its fruit is a small, green, oval berry that turns orange when mature.

Coccinia subspicata is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.
