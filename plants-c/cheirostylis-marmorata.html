Cheirostylis marmorata

Cheirostylis marmorata, commonly known as the marbled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, marbled-patterned leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a distinctive yellow throat.

Cheirostylis marmorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
