Convolvulus meisneri f. coeruleus

Convolvulus meisneri f. coeruleus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail, adding visual interest to gardens and containers.
