Campanula tridens var. ciliata

Campanula tridens var. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, and the plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
