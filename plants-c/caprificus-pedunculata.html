Caprificus pedunculata

Caprificus pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

Caprificus pedunculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
