Cyperus erythrorhizus

Cyperus erythrorhizus, commonly known as red-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wet or marshy environments. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name, and it often grows in dense colonies.

Cyperus erythrorhizus is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
