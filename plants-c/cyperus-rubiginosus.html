Cyperus rubiginosus

Cyperus rubiginosus, commonly known as the red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus rubiginosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water filtration plant.
