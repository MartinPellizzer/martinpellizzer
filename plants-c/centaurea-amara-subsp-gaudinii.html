Centaurea amara subsp. gaudinii

Centaurea amara subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically pink to purple in color.

The species is noted for its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of various secondary metabolites.
