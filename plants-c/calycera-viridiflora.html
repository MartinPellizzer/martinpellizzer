Calycera viridiflora

Calycera viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered calycera, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which is unusual for many flowering plants. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Calycera viridiflora is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
