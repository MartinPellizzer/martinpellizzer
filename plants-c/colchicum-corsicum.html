Colchicum corsicum

Colchicum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often withering before flowering.

Colchicum corsicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
