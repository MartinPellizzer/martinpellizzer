Calanthe mexicana var. retusa

Calanthe mexicana var. retusa, commonly known as the Mexican calanthe, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, upright spike bearing several white or pale pink flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
