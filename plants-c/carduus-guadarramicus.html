Carduus guadarramicus

Carduus guadarramicus, commonly known as the Guadarrama thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a solitary, purple flower head that blooms in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowering stems can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a notable presence in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
