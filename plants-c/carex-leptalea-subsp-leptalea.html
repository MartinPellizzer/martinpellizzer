Carex leptalea subsp. leptalea

Carex leptalea subsp.

leptalea, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
