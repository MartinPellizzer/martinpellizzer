Campanula hacerae

Campanula hacerae, commonly known as the Hacera bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture.

Campanula hacerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
