Cakile maritima subsp. aegyptia

Cakile maritima subsp.

aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
