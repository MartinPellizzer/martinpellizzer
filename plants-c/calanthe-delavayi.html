Calanthe delavayi

Calanthe delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Calanthe delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
