Coleocephalocereus elongatus

Coleocephalocereus elongatus, commonly known as the Long-spined Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and spiny, tuberculate structure. The plant produces white, nocturnal flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and xeriscaping.
