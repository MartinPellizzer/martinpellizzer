Campium argutum

Campium argutum, commonly known as the roughleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carnations and snapdragons. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are characterized by their rough, serrated edges. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Campium argutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
