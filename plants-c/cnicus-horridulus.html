Cnicus horridulus

Cnicus horridulus, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Cnicus horridulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
