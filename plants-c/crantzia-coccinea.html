Crantzia coccinea

Crantzia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet crantzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, lance-shaped leaves.

Crantzia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking color and compact growth habit.
