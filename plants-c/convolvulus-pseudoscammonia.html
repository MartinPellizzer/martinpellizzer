Convolvulus pseudoscammonia

Convolvulus pseudoscammonia, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
