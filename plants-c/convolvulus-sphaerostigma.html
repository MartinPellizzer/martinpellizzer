Convolvulus sphaerostigma

Convolvulus sphaerostigma, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and alternate along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
