Cucumis umbilicatus

Cucumis umbilicatus, commonly known as the Mexican burr cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically covered in a spiny, hairy coating. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is often cultivated for its unique appearance and can be used in traditional cuisines.
