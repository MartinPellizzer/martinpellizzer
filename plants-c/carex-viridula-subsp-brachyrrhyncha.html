Carex viridula subsp. brachyrrhyncha

Carex viridula subsp.

brachyrrhyncha, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
