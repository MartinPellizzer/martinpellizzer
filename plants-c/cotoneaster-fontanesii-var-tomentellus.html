Cotoneaster fontanesii var. tomentellus

Cotoneaster fontanesii var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, red berries that are clustered in groups of three. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy underside, giving the plant its "tomentellus" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its winter interest and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
