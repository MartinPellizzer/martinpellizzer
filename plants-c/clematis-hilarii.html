Clematis hilarii

Clematis hilarii, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China and Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Clematis hilarii is valued for its showy blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
