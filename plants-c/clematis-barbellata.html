Clematis barbellata

Clematis barbellata, commonly known as the bellflower clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful growth habit and long flowering period.
