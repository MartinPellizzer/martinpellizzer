Cerastium × brueggerianum

Cerastium × brueggerianum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Cerastium × brueggerianum is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
