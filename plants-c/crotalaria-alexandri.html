Crotalaria alexandri

Crotalaria alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
