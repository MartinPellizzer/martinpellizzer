Chionanthus ramiflorus var. peninsularis

Chionanthus ramiflorus var. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright purple to violet berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and persistent berries, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
