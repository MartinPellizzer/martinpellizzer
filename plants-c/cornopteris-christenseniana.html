Cornopteris christenseniana

Cornopteris christenseniana, commonly known as the Christensen's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its spores are dispersed by wind, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
