Calamus inopinatus

Calamus inopinatus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Calamus inopinatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
