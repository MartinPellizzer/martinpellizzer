Crataegus steubenensis

Crataegus steubenensis, commonly known as Steuben's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
