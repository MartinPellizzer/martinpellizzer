Colona discolor

Colona discolor, commonly known as the variegated colona, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
