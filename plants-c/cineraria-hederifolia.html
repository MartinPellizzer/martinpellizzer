Cineraria hederifolia

Cineraria hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
