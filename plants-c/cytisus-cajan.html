Cytisus cajan

Cytisus cajan, commonly known as the Indian broom, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cytisus cajan is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
