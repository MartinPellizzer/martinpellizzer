Croton magdalenensis var. leucoxanthus

Croton magdalenensis var. leucoxanthus, commonly known as the Magdalena croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
