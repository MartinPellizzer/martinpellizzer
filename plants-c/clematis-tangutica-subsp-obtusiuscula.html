Clematis tangutica subsp. obtusiuscula

Clematis tangutica subsp.

obtusiuscula, commonly known as the Tangut Clematis, is a perennial climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of China. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
