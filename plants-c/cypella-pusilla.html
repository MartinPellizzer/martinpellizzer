Cypella pusilla

Cypella pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf cypella, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Cypella pusilla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
