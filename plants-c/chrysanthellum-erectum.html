Chrysanthellum erectum

Chrysanthellum erectum, commonly known as the upright chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color and bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
