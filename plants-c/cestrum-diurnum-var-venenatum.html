Cestrum diurnum var. venenatum

Cestrum diurnum var. venenatum, commonly known as the nightshade cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
