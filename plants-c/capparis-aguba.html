Capparis aguba

Capparis aguba, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a small fig, is edible and has a sour taste.

Capparis aguba is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
