Campanula carnica var. angustifolia

Campanula carnica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
