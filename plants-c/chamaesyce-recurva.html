Chamaesyce recurva

Chamaesyce recurva, commonly known as the curlyleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their curly, rolled appearance, which gives the species its common name.

Chamaesyce recurva is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
