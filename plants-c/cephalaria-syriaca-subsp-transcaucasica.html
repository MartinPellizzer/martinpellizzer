Cephalaria syriaca subsp. transcaucasica

Cephalaria syriaca subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale yellow to white in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
