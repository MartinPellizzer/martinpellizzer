Capparis oliveriana

Capparis oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Capparis oliveriana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
