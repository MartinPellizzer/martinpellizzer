Carex echinochloe subsp. nyasensis

Carex echinochloe subsp.

nyasensis, commonly known as the Nyasai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
