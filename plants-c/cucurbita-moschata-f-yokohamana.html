Cucurbita moschata f. yokohamana

Cucurbita moschata f. yokohamana, commonly known as the Yokohama pumpkin, is a unique horticultural form of the squash species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often striped fruit, which can vary in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine, producing large, palmately lobed leaves. It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

This variety is particularly notable for its adaptability to different climates and its resistance to certain diseases.
