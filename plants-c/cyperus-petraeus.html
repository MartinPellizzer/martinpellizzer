Cyperus petraeus

Cyperus petraeus, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. It is well adapted to survive in dry, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky crevices or coastal areas.

Cyperus petraeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
