Claoxylon mercurialis

Claoxylon mercurialis, commonly known as the mercury tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
