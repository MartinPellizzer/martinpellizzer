Chamaecrista serpens

Chamaecrista serpens, commonly known as the serpentine burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Chamaecrista serpens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
