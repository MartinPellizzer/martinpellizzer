Catalpa bignonioides var. variegata

Catalpa bignonioides var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation of white, yellow, and green. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its winged seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and floral display.
