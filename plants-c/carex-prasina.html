Carex prasina

Carex prasina, commonly known as green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters known as spikes. Carex prasina is a hardy plant that thrives in wet or damp soil conditions.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
