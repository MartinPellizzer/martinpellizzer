Chaetospora × intermedia

Chaetospora × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the cultivar. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Chaetospora × intermedia is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and hardiness.
