Calliandra filipes

Calliandra filipes, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
