Ceropegia immaculata

Ceropegia immaculata, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces specialized structures called "ceropegia" that resemble bird beaks, which are adapted to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia immaculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
