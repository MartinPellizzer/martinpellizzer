Commelina macrosperma

Commelina macrosperma, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a spike, often with a distinctive blue bract beneath the inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies or in wetlands.

Commelina macrosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
