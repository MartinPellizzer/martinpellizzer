Cyperus alvesii

Cyperus alvesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces spikelets that are clustered at the top of the stem.

Cyperus alvesii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
