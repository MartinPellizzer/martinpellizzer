Chaenactis glabriuscula var. orcuttiana

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. orcuttiana, commonly known as Orcutt's frostweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
