Chromolaena calamocephala

Chromolaena calamocephala, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability and rapid growth rate.
