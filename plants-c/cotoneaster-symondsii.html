Cotoneaster symondsii

Cotoneaster symondsii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky areas and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are generally elliptical with a leathery texture, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Cotoneaster symondsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
