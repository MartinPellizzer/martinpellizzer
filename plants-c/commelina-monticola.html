Commelina monticola

Commelina monticola, commonly known as the mountain commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Commelina monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for small insects and birds.
