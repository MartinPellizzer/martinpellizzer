Cyanothamnus bipinnatus

Cyanothamnus bipinnatus, commonly known as the blue wrens or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are alternate, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
