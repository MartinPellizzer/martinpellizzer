Carex vulgaris var. bructeri

Carex vulgaris var. bructeri, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has rhizomatous growth and can form dense mats, often serving as a habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

The plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
