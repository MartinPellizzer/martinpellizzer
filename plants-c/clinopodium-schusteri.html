Clinopodium schusteri

Clinopodium schusteri, commonly known as Schuster's betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, and they bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium schusteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
