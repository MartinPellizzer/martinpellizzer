Chamaedrys polium

Chamaedrys polium, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or violet, and it produces small, rounded fruits called schizocarps.

Chamaedrys polium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
