Carduus tenuiflorus var. stenolepis

Carduus tenuiflorus var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-flowered thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
