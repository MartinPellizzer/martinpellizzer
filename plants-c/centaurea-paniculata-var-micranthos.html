Centaurea paniculata var. micranthos

Centaurea paniculata var. micranthos, commonly known as the narrow-flowered cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
