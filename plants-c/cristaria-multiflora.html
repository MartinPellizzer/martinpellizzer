Cristaria multiflora

Cristaria multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces multiple flowering stems that rise from the center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cristaria multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
