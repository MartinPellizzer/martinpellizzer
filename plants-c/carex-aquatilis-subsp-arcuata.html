Carex aquatilis subsp. arcuata

Carex aquatilis subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the curved sedge, is a wetland plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to aquatic environments such as marshes and pond margins.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved at the tips, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
