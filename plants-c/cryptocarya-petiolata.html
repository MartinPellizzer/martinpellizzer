Cryptocarya petiolata

Cryptocarya petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along streamlines.

Cryptocarya petiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
