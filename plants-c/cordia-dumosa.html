Cordia dumosa

Cordia dumosa, commonly known as the African leatherwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Cordia dumosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
