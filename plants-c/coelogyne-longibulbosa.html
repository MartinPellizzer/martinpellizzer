Coelogyne longibulbosa

Coelogyne longibulbosa, commonly known as the long-bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The plant requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, and it is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
