Chloranthus blumeanus

Chloranthus blumeanus, commonly known as the Blume's angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling lanterns. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
