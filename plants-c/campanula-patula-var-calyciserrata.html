Campanula patula var. calyciserrata

Campanula patula var. calyciserrata, commonly known as the serrated calyx bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lanceolate and often have serrated edges, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
