Carex interimus

Carex interimus, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers.

Carex interimus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
