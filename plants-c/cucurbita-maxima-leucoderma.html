Cucurbita maxima leucoderma

Cucurbita maxima leucoderma, commonly known as the white-fleshed pumpkin, is a variety of the common squash species native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale, creamy-colored flesh and elongated, oblong shape. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
