Cuspidaria trifoliata

Cuspidaria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its woody stems are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Cuspidaria trifoliata is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in desert scrublands.
