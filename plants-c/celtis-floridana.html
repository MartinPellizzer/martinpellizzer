Celtis floridana

Celtis floridana, commonly known as the Florida hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by yellowish-green berries. Celtis floridana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a common sight in urban and suburban landscapes.
