Crotalaria katangensis

Crotalaria katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with distinctive spurred petals, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Crotalaria katangensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various environmental conditions.
