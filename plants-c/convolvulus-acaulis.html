Convolvulus acaulis

Convolvulus acaulis, commonly known as the barrenstick, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
