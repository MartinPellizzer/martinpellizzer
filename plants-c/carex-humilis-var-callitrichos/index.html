Carex Humilis Var. Callitrichos

Carex humilis var. callitrichos is a low-growing sedge native to wetland and marsh environments in eastern North America. This variant is distinguished by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in swampy areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in late spring and early summer. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
