Cerastium obtusifolium

Cerastium obtusifolium, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has fuzzy, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Cerastium obtusifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
