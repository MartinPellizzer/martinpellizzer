Cacalia toluccana

Cacalia toluccana, commonly known as the Toluca bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Cacalia toluccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
