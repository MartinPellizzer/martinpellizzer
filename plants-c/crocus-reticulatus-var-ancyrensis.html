Crocus reticulatus var. ancyrensis

Crocus reticulatus var. ancyrensis, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive reticulate pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
