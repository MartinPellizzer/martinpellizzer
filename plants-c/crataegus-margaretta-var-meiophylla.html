Crataegus margaretta var. meiophylla

Crataegus margaretta var. meiophylla, commonly known as the Margaretta hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter green or white on the underside, giving it its varietal name "meiophylla," which refers to the pale lower surface. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
