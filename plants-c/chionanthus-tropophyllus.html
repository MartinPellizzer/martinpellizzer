Chionanthus tropophyllus

Chionanthus tropophyllus, commonly known as the tropical chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
