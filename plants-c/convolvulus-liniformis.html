Convolvulus liniformis

Convolvulus liniformis, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
