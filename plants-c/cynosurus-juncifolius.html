Cynosurus juncifolius

Cynosurus juncifolius, commonly known as the rush grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tuft of flowering heads at the tip of each stem.

Cynosurus juncifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
