Carex paniculata var. secta

Carex paniculata var. secta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
