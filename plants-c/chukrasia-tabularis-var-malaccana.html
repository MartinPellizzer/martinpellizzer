Chukrasia tabularis var. malaccana

Chukrasia tabularis var. malaccana, commonly known as the Malacca flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture.
