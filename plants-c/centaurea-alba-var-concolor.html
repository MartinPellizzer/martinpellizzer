Centaurea alba var. concolor

Centaurea alba var. concolor, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
