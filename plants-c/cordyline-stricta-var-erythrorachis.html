Cordyline stricta var. erythrorachis

Cordyline stricta var. erythrorachis, commonly known as the red-flowered cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with red or purple veins. The plant produces showy, red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
