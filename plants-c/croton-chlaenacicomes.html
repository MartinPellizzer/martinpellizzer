Croton chlaenacicomes

Croton chlaenacicomes, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and diverse leaf structures. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored, with a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive orange or red seed capsules.

The leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle the plant with care.
