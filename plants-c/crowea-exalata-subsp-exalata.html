Crowea exalata subsp. exalata

Crowea exalata subsp.

exalata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
