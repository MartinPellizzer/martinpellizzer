Cytisus supinus subsp. absinthioides

Cytisus supinus subsp.

absinthioides, commonly known as the dwarf Scotch broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles. It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, elliptical leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.
