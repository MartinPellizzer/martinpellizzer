Carex aenea f. flumini-regalis

Carex aenea f. flumini-regalis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a wetland plant characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes and along the edges of rivers and lakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and densely packed. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, clustered flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
