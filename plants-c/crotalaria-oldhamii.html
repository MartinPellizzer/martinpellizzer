Crotalaria oldhamii

Crotalaria oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria oldhamii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
