Crassocephalum bauchiense

Crassocephalum bauchiense, commonly known as the Bauchi morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly Nigeria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers range in color from white to pink. It is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Crassocephalum bauchiense is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
