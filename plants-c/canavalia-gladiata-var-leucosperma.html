Canavalia gladiata var. leucosperma

Canavalia gladiata var. leucosperma, commonly known as the white-flowered cowpea, is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, white or pale yellow seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional value.
