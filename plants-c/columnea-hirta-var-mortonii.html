Columnea hirta var. mortonii

Columnea hirta var. mortonii, commonly known as the hairy columnea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding insects.
