Calathea oppenheimiana

Calathea oppenheimiana, commonly known as the "princess fern," is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a deep green base accented with silver or pink veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
