Cyathea crinita

Cyathea crinita, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive hairy appearance on its trunk, which gives it its common name. Cyathea crinita thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
