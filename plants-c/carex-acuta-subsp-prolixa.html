Carex acuta subsp. prolixa

Carex acuta subsp.

prolixa, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
