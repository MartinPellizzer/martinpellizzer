Cascarilla calyptrata

Cascarilla calyptrata, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind or tamarindo, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces yellowish-orange fruits that are roughly spherical and contain a single seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
