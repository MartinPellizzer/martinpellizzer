Campanula bicolor

Campanula bicolor, commonly known as the two-color bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula bicolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
