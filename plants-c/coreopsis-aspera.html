Coreopsis aspera

Coreopsis aspera, commonly known as rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
