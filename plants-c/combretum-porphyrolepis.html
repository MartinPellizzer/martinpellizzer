Combretum porphyrolepis

Combretum porphyrolepis, commonly known as the purple-leaved combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark purple to reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
