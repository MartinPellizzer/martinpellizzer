Chamaeraphis magna

Chamaeraphis magna, commonly known as the glassworts, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and coastal areas. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its small, green flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in muddy or wet soils and can tolerate high levels of salt and waterlogging.

Chamaeraphis magna plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for various organisms.
