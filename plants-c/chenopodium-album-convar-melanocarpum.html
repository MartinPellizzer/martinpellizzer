Chenopodium album convar. melanocarpum

Chenopodium album convar. melanocarpum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically covered in a sticky substance. It is a common weed found in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
