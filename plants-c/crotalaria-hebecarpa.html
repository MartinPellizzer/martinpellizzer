Crotalaria hebecarpa

Crotalaria hebecarpa, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria hebecarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
