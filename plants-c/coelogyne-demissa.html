Coelogyne demissa

Coelogyne demissa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, and it produces a single inflorescence with multiple flowers.

Coelogyne demissa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its pleasant, sweet fragrance.
