Campanula burgalensis

Campanula burgalensis, commonly known as the Burgas bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanula burgalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
