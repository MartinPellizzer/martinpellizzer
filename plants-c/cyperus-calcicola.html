Cyperus calcicola

Cyperus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone sedge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in well-drained soils, often on limestone or rocky substrates.

Cyperus calcicola is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
