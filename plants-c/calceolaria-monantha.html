Calceolaria monantha

Calceolaria monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered slipper orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Calceolaria monantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral structure and vibrant colors.

It is also notable for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season under proper care.
