Chloris ctenoides

Chloris ctenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass or slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in landscaping and as a forage plant for livestock.
