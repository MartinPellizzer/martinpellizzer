Crataegus grossidentata

Crataegus grossidentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
