Croton campestris var. laetifolius

Croton campestris var. laetifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
