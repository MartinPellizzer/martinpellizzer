Canarium pilosum subsp. borneensis

Canarium pilosum subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Borneo. It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, simple leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its oil-rich seeds used in traditional practices and industry.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy young shoots and broader leaves compared to other Canarium species.
