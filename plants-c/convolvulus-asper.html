Convolvulus asper

Convolvulus asper, commonly known as rough convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

Its rapid growth and persistent nature make it both a weed and a valued ornamental plant in some regions.
