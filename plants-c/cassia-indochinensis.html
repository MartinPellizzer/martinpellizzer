Cassia indochinensis

Cassia indochinensis, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. Cassia indochinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are used in various local applications.
