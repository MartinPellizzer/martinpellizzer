Chloranthus indicus

Chloranthus indicus, commonly known as the Indian chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
