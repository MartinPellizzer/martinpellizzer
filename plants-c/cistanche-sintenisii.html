Cistanche sintenisii

Cistanche sintenisii, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to arid regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and has a unique structure with a tubular stem that coils around its host. It is often found growing on the roots of various shrubs and grasses in desert and semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature and medicinal uses, it is of interest in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
