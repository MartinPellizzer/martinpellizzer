Cypripedium × arnoldianum

Cypripedium × arnoldianum, commonly known as the Arnold's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white with a yellow throat. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce a single leaf per growth season. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and are often visited by various insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
