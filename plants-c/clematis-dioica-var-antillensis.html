Clematis dioica var. antillensis

Clematis dioica var. antillensis, commonly known as the Antillean Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five sepals and five petals, and are pollinated primarily by insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
