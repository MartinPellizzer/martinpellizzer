Cola rubra

Cola rubra, commonly known as the red cola tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods that contain stimulant-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
