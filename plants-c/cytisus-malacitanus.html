Cytisus malacitanus

Cytisus malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its woody stems are covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Cytisus malacitanus is adapted to the Mediterranean climate, showing resistance to drought and salt.
