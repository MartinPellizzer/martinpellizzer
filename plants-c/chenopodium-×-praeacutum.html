Chenopodium × praeacutum

Chenopodium × praeacutum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is commonly found in disturbed soils.

Chenopodium × praeacutum is known for its nutritional value and has been used as a food source in various cultures.
