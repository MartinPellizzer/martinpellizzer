Codiaeum pictum

Codiaeum pictum, commonly known as the croton plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can display shades of red, yellow, orange, and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant is typically shrub-like, with smooth, waxy leaves that are often lobed or notched. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its striking foliage.

Codiaeum pictum is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
