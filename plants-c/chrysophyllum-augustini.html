Chrysophyllum augustini

Chrysophyllum augustini, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
