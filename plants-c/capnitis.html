Capnitis

Capnitis, commonly known as the common caper.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn purple when mature. Capnitis is often cultivated for its edible flower buds, which are used in various culinary dishes.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
