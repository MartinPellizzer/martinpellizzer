Cryptocentrum dunstervilleorum

Cryptocentrum dunstervilleorum, commonly known as the Dunsterville's cryptocrystal, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that exhibit a unique crystalline structure under microscopic examination. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. Cryptocentrum dunstervilleorum thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
