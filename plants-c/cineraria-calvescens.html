Cineraria calvescens

Cineraria calvescens, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silvery-gray foliage with a woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cineraria calvescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
