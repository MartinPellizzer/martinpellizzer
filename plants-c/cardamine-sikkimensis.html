Cardamine sikkimensis

Cardamine sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan regions, particularly in Nepal, India, and Bhutan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in rocky stream banks and forest floors, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
