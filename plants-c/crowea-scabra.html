Crowea scabra

Crowea scabra, commonly known as the rough crowea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Crowea scabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
