Carex × deserta

Carex × deserta, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert regions.

The plant produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds effectively in its native habitat.
