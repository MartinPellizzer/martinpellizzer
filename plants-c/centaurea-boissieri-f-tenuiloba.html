Centaurea boissieri f. tenuiloba

Centaurea boissieri f. tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
