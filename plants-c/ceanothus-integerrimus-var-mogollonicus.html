Ceanothus integerrimus var. mogollonicus

Ceanothus integerrimus var. mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing drought tolerance.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
