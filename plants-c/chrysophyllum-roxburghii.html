Chrysophyllum roxburghii

Chrysophyllum roxburghii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed, often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
