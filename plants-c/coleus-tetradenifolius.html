Coleus tetradenifolius

Coleus tetradenifolius, commonly known as the four-veined coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that display shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with four veins on each leaf, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
