Cyperus clarus

Cyperus clarus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems that are typically green or reddish in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often subtended by bracts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus clarus is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
