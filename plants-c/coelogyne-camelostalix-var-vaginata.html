Coelogyne camelostalix var. vaginata

Coelogyne camelostalix var. vaginata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and elongated, strap-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and store water, helping it survive in seasonal dry periods.

The variety 'vaginata' is distinguished by its unique floral structure and foliage, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
