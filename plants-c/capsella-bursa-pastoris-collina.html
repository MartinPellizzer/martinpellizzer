Capsella bursa-pastoris collina

Capsella bursa-pastoris collina, commonly known as shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or heart-shaped seed pods, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its small white or purple flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant completes its life cycle within a single growing season.

The leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
