Canarium merrillii var. originarium

Canarium merrillii var. originarium, commonly known as the Philippine candlenut, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing a single seed. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
