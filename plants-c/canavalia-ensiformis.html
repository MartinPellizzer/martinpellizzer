Canavalia ensiformis

Canavalia ensiformis, commonly known as Jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and large, flat pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in height, with a strong, fibrous stem. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces a significant amount of nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots.

Canavalia ensiformis is also known for its high protein content in its seeds, making it an important crop in some parts of the world.
