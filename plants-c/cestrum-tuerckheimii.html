Cestrum tuerckheimii

Cestrum tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Cestrum tuerckheimii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
