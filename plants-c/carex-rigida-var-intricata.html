Carex rigida var. intricata

Carex rigida var. intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in garden settings for its unique foliage structure.
