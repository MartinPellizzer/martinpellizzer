Calanthe forbesii

Calanthe forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often white or pinkish, floral spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Calanthe forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
