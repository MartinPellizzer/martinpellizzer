Clausena dentata var. longipes

Clausena dentata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled sour orange, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-colored, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, though the pulp is often sour, and it contains seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
