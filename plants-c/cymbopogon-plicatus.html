Cymbopogon plicatus

Cymbopogon plicatus, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, clustered flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
