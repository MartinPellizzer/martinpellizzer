Colutea arborescens

Colutea arborescens, commonly known as the tree clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a bushy growth habit with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy areas.

Colutea arborescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
