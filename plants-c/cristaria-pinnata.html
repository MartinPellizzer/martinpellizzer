Cristaria pinnata

Cristaria pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

Cristaria pinnata is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
