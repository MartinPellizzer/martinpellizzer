Crataegus micrantha

Crataegus micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus micrantha is often found in moist, woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
