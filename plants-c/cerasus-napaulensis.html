Cerasus napaulensis

Cerasus napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal cherry, is a species of Prunus native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its bark is gray and fissured.

The Nepal cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
