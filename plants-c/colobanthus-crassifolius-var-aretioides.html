Colobanthus crassifolius var. aretioides

Colobanthus crassifolius var. aretioides, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the Antarctic region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in harsh, cold environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, round fruits.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is an important component of Antarctic vegetation.
