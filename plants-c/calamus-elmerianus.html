Calamus elmerianus

Calamus elmerianus, commonly known as Elmer's canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fruits that are consumed by local communities.

Due to its ornamental value and sustainable harvesting practices, it is sometimes cultivated for both decorative and practical purposes.
