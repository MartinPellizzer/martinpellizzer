Clibadium propinquum

Clibadium propinquum, commonly known as the Brazilian burr or the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant is known for its hairy, rough texture, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Clibadium propinquum is also noted for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.
