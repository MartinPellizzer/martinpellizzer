Cyclamen aegineticum

Cyclamen aegineticum, commonly known as the Greek cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, upward-facing flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Cyclamen aegineticum is also valued for its historical significance in traditional medicine.
