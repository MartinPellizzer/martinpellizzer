Crossandra nilotica subsp. massaica

Crossandra nilotica subsp.

massaica, commonly known as the Massaica flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, often with yellow throats.

The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.
