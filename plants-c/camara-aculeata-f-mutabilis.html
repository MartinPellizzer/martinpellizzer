Camara aculeata f. mutabilis

Camara aculeata f. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to certain regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is notable for its striking, often colorful bracts that can change in hue depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
