Callicarpa longifolia var. subglabrata

Callicarpa longifolia var. subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-hair Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple to pink berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
