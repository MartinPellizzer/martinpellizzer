Calophyllum glabrum

Calophyllum glabrum, commonly known as the smooth calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glossy bark and broad, palmate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
