Cyathea nothofagorum

Cyathea nothofagorum, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
