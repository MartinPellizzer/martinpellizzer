Chamaecyparis pisifera f. plumosa

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. plumosa, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or Japanese lacewood, is a cultivar of the chamaecyparis genus.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery appearance due to its finely dissected, needle-like foliage. The plant has a slow growth rate and is typically evergreen, maintaining its ornamental form throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its delicate, plume-like foliage gives it a graceful, airy appearance that makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
