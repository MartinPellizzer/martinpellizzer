Cerastium schmidtianum var. dentatum

Cerastium schmidtianum var. dentatum, commonly known as the serrated mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves that have serrated edges. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are pale yellow or white and attract various pollinators.
