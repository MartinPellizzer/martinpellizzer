Cassia kirkii var. guineensis

Cassia kirkii var. guineensis, commonly known as African candle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
