Cocculus pahni

Cocculus pahni, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds. Its botanical features include the presence of a unique, spiral-shaped stem that aids in climbing.
