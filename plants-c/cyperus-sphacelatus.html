Cyperus sphacelatus

Cyperus sphacelatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus sphacelatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
