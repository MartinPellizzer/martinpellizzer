Cypripedium × marmoratum

Cypripedium × marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive marbled pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the stem.

Cypripedium × marmoratum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
