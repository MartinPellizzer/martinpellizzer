Colutea floribunda

Colutea floribunda, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, black seeds that are often used as a spice in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Colutea floribunda is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal medicine for various purposes.
