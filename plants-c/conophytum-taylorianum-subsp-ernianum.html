Conophytum taylorianum subsp. ernianum

Conophytum taylorianum subsp.

ernianum, commonly known as the Taylor's conophytum, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa. It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It has a unique rosette growth form, with leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
