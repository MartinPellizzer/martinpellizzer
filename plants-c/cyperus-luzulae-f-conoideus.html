Cyperus luzulae f. conoideus

Cyperus luzulae f. conoideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, three-lobed flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
