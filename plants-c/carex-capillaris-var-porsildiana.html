Carex capillaris var. porsildiana

Carex capillaris var. porsildiana, commonly known as the Porsild's sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging wetland conditions.
