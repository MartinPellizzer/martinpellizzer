Conoclinium betonicifolium

Conoclinium betonicifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped to ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
