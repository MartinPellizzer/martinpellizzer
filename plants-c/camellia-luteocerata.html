Camellia luteocerata

Camellia luteocerata, commonly known as the yellow-scented camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Camellia luteocerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
