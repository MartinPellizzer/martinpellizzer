Caucaea cucullata

Caucaea cucullata, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cool, humid climate.
