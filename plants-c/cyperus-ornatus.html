Cyperus ornatus

Cyperus ornatus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive purple or reddish bracts that surround its flowering spikes. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and can often be found in wetlands or along water edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
