Cochlospermum parkeri

Cochlospermum parkeri, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a distinctive, rough, and fissured bark. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
