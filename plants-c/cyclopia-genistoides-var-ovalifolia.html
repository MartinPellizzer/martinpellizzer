Cyclopia genistoides var. ovalifolia

Cyclopia genistoides var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldcup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
