Clematis heracleifolia var. tubulosa

Clematis heracleifolia var. tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in natural and cultivated landscapes.
