Cladium bidwellii

Cladium bidwellii, commonly known as the Florida marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Florida and parts of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and swamps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cladium bidwellii is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in its environment.
