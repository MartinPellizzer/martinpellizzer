Cremocephalum cernuum

Cremocephalum cernuum, commonly known as the nodding goldenhead, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy.

It is often found in the eastern and central United States.
