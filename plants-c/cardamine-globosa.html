Cardamine globosa

Cardamine globosa, commonly known as the globe buttercup, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a globular inflorescence, giving it its common name.

Cardamine globosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
