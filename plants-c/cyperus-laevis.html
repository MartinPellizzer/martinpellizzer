Cyperus laevis

Cyperus laevis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus laevis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
