Centaurea jacea var. ruscinonensis

Centaurea jacea var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis Cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
