Centaurea toletana subsp. argecillensis

Centaurea toletana subsp.

argecillensis, commonly known as the Toleto centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural reserves.
