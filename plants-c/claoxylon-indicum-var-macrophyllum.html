Claoxylon indicum var. macrophyllum

Claoxylon indicum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf claoxylon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
