Cedrus libani f. glauca

Cedrus libani f. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Atlas Cedar, is a unique horticultural form of the Atlas Cedar.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping needles that reflect light. This cultivar is native to the mountains of North Africa and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance.

The plant is also noted for its slow growth rate and longevity, making it a desirable specimen for landscape design.
