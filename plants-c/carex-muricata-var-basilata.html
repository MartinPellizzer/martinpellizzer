Carex muricata var. basilata

Carex muricata var. basilata, commonly known as the Basilate Sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with light green and yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insect species.
