Cistus × pascalis

Cistus × pascalis, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, while its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy ornamental choice.

Cistus × pascalis is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
