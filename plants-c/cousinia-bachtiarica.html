Cousinia bachtiarica

Cousinia bachtiarica, commonly known as the Afghan cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia bachtiarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
