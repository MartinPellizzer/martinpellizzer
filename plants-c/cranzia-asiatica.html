Cranzia asiatica

Cranzia asiatica, commonly known as the Asian cranzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Cranzia asiatica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
