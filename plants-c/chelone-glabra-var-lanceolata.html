Chelone glabra var. lanceolata

Chelone glabra var. lanceolata, commonly known as white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
