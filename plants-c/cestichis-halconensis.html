Cestichis halconensis

Cestichis halconensis, commonly known as the goldenrod hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

The goldenrod hawkweed is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in roadside areas.
