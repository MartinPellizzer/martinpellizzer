Comocladia dentata var. propinqua

Comocladia dentata var. propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its specific epithet "dentata.". This variety is typically found in coastal areas and moist habitats, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing nectar for pollinators.
