Campanula angustifolia

Campanula angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
