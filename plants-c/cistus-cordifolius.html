Cistus cordifolius

Cistus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to rocky or coastal environments.

Cistus cordifolius is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
