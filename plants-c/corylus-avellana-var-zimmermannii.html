Corylus avellana var. zimmermannii

Corylus avellana var. zimmermannii, commonly known as the Hungarian hazel, is a variety of the hazelnut tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional agriculture.
