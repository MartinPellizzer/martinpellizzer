Caltha glacialis

Caltha glacialis, commonly known as the glacier buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Caltha glacialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in early season pollination.
