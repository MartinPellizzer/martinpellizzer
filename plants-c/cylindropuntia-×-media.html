Cylindropuntia × media

Cylindropuntia × media, commonly known as the Indian fig cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless or sparsely spined cactus with cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range from yellow to red, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
