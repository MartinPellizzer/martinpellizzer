Cycnoches amparoanum

Cycnoches amparoanum, commonly known as the Amparoan moonflower, is a striking night-blooming plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at dusk and close by dawn. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. Its unique flowering pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticultural displays.

Cycnoches amparoanum is also notable for its ability to thrive in high humidity and partial shade, making it a sought-after addition to tropical gardens.
