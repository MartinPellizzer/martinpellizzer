Corylus avellana var. cuneata

Corylus avellana var. cuneata, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a variant of the common hazel that is native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and oval, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by edible, oval nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nuts, which are used in cooking and baking.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
