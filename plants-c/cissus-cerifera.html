Cissus cerifera

Cissus cerifera, commonly known as the wax plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive waxy, translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant.

Cissus cerifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify indoor air.
