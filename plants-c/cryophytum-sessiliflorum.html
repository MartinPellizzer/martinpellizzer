Cryophytum sessiliflorum

Cryophytum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the frost plant, is a rare and unique species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and its ability to survive in cold, harsh environments. The plant produces small, sessile flowers that bloom in the early spring, often in response to brief periods of warmer weather. Its leaves are densely packed, waxy, and adapted to minimize water loss in arid conditions.

Cryophytum sessiliflorum is of particular interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations to extreme climates.
