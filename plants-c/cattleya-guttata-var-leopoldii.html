Cattleya guttata var. leopoldii

Cattleya guttata var. leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's spotted cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

This hybrid variety is characterized by its vibrant pink and white petals with distinctive dark spots, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. It typically blooms during the late summer and early fall, producing fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators. The plant prefers cool, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
