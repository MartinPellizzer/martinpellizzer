Chrysophyllum acreanum

Chrysophyllum acreanum, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages, while the tree also has traditional medicinal uses.

Chrysophyllum acreanum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
