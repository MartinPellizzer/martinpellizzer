Caesalpinia benthamiana

Caesalpinia benthamiana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia benthamiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
