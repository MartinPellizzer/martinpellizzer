Centaurea zaferii

Centaurea zaferii, commonly known as Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Balkans.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and upright, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea zaferii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
