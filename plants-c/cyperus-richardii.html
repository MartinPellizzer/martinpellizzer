Cyperus richardii

Cyperus richardii, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus richardii is also known for its ability to tolerate saline environments, making it adaptable to various wetland ecosystems.
