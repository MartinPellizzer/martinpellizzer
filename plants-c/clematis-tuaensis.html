Clematis tuaensis

Clematis tuaensis, commonly known as the Tuaensis Clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the vine family, Rutaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Clematis tuaensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
