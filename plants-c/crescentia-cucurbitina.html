Crescentia cucurbitina

Crescentia cucurbitina, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces large, orange-yellow fruits that resemble gourds, which contain numerous seeds. Crescentia cucurbitina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and fruiting pattern make it a distinctive species within the nightshade family.
