Callicarpa plumosa

Callicarpa plumosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, vivid purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Callicarpa plumosa thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
