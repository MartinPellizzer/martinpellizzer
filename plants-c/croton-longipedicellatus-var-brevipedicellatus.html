Croton longipedicellatus var. brevipedicellatus

Croton longipedicellatus var. brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the short-stalked croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
