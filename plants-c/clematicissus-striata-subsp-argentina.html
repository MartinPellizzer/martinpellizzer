Clematicissus striata subsp. argentina

Clematicissus striata subsp.

argentina, commonly known as the Argentine climbing clematis, is a vine native to South America. It features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive silver-white underside.

The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.
