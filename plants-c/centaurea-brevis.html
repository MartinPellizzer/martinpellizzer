Centaurea brevis

Centaurea brevis, commonly known as the shortstalk bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea brevis is often found in open areas and is adapted to thrive in regions with low moisture availability.
