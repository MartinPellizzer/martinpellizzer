Caesalpinia glenieii

Caesalpinia glenieii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Caesalpinia glenieii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
