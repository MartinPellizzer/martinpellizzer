Coleus blumei var. pectinatus

Coleus blumei var. pectinatus, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a waxy texture and a variety of colors including red, purple, and green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

The species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a bedding plant.
