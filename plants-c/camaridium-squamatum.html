Camaridium squamatum

Camaridium squamatum, commonly known as the silver scale plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white to grayish foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in heads. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Camaridium squamatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
