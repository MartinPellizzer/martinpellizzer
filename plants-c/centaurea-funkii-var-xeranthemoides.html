Centaurea funkii var. xeranthemoides

Centaurea funkii var. xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert starflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, fluffy clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
