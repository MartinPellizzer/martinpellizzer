Centaurea wagenitzii

Centaurea wagenitzii, commonly known as the Wagenitz's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
