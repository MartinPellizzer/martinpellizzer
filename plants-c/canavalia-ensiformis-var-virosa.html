Canavalia ensiformis var. virosa

Canavalia ensiformis var. virosa, commonly known as the sickle bean or horse bean, is a variant of the common bean plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its slender, sickle-shaped pods and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to grow in poor soils and can thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are followed by the characteristic elongated pods.

The seeds are typically used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value, though the plant can be toxic in certain forms.
