Carex lanceata

Carex lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex lanceata is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
