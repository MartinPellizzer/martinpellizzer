Campelia fastigiata

Campelia fastigiata, commonly known as the silverleaf campelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Campelia fastigiata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
