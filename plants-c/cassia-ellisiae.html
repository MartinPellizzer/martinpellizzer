Cassia ellisiae

Cassia ellisiae, commonly known as the Elicea cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia ellisiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
