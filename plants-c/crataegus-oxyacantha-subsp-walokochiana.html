Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. walokochiana

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

walokochiana, commonly known as the African hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny branches, small elliptical leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
