Carex festucacea var. echinodes

Carex festucacea var. echinodes, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its rough, bristly texture on the leaf surfaces, giving it its common name.
