Crassula malladrae

Crassula malladrae, commonly known as the Kariakoo crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula malladrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
