Celtis laevigata

Celtis laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by berry-like fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. Celtis laevigata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

Its leaves and fruits are used by various wildlife species, contributing to its ecological significance.
