Cassia cowanii

Cassia cowanii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

Cassia cowanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also significant in Australian culture as a symbol of the country.
