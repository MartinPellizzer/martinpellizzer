Crucihimalaya axillaris

Crucihimalaya axillaris, commonly known as the Himalayan crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its flowers are often pale or white, with a distinctive cruciform shape, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
