Crotalaria pseudoalexanderi

Crotalaria pseudoalexanderi, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
