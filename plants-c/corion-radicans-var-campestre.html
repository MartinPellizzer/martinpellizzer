Corion radicans var. campestre

Corion radicans var. campestre, commonly known as creeping corion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, soft pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
