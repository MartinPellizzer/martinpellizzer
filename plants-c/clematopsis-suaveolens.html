Clematopsis suaveolens

Clematopsis suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-scented clematopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Native to specific regions in Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticulture.
