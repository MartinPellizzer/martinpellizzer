Crassula obvallaris

Crassula obvallaris, commonly known as the "Laceplant" or "Beaded Window," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the tips. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the spring.

It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves, and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
