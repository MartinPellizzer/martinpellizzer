Carex helleri

Carex helleri, commonly known as Heller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex helleri is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
