Cordiera tuberculata

Cordiera tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cordiera tuberculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
