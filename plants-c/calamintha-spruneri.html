Calamintha spruneri

Calamintha spruneri, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha spruneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
