Cocos inajai

Cocos inajai, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive fruit known as the coconut, which contains both water and a white, fleshy interior. The coconut palm is widely cultivated for its fruit, oil, and fiber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
