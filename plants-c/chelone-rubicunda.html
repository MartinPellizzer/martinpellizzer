Chelone rubicunda

Chelone rubicunda, commonly known as the redstem turtlehead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone rubicunda is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
