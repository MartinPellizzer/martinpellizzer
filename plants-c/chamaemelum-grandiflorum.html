Chamaemelum grandiflorum

Chamaemelum grandiflorum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces numerous small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by feathery, seed-producing bracts. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
