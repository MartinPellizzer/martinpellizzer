Colchicum autumnale f. radei

Colchicum autumnale f. radei, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a botanical variant of the autumn crocus, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology and coloration.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant features striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and emerge from a bulbous corm, which serves as its storage organ.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
