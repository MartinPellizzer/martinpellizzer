Chamula

Chamula, commonly known as the Chamula pepper, is a variety of Capsicum species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elongated shape and a vibrant red or orange color when ripe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Chamula peppers are often cultivated in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

They are valued for their spicy flavor and are used extensively in traditional Mexican cuisine.
