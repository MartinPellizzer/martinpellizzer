Cyclanthera explodens var. intermedia

Cyclanthera explodens var. intermedia, commonly known as the exploding cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often inflated fruits that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit, which is edible when young, turns yellow or orange when mature and can be used in local cuisines.
