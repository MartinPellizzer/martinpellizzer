Calyptromyrcia eugenioides var. puberula

Calyptromyrcia eugenioides var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, pubescent appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
