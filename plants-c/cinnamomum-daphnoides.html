Cinnamomum daphnoides

Cinnamomum daphnoides, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that contain a single seed, and its bark is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Cinnamomum daphnoides is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
