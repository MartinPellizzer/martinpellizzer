Croton asperifolius

Croton asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
