Chenopodium incisum var. mexicanum

Chenopodium incisum var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible leaves.
