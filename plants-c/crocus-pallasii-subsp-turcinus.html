Crocus pallasii subsp. turcinus

Crocus pallasii subsp.

turcinus, commonly known as the Pallas' crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that are adapted to cold climates. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.
