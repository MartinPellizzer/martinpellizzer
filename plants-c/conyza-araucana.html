Conyza araucana

Conyza araucana, commonly known as the "blueweed," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in heads, which are often mistaken for dandelions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Conyza araucana is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
