Chrysopsis trichophylla

Chrysopsis trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique texture.
