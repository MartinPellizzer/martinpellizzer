Citrus × limodulcis

Citrus × limodulcis, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The fruit has a thin, edible peel that is rich in vitamin C and essential oils. The flesh inside is sweet and juicy, often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
