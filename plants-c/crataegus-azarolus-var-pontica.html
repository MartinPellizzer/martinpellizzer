Crataegus azarolus var. pontica

Crataegus azarolus var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
