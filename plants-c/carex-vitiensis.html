Carex vitiensis

Carex vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Fiji.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex vitiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
