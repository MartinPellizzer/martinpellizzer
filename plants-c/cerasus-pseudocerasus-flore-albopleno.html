Cerasus pseudocerasus flore-albopleno

Cerasus pseudocerasus flore-albopleno, commonly known as the white-flowered sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching structure and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically sour and used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy, fragrant blossoms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
