Caragana wenhsienensis var. inermis

Caragana wenhsienensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with leaflets that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species for rocky or sandy landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife in its native environment.
