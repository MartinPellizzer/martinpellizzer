Campanula rotundifolia subvar. re

Campanula rotundifolia subvar. re, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
