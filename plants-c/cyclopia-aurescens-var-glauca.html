Cyclopia aurescens var. glauca

Cyclopia aurescens var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered cyclopia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue or violet flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
