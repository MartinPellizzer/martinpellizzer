Crotalaria mokoroensis

Crotalaria mokoroensis, commonly known as the Mokoro shrub, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Crotalaria mokoroensis is also known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
