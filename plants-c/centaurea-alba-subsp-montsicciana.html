Centaurea alba subsp. montsicciana

Centaurea alba subsp.

montsicciana, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
