Cassia glandulosa

Cassia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are compound with small, glandular structures. Cassia glandulosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The seeds of this plant are encased in pods and can be used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
