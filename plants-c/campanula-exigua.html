Campanula exigua

Campanula exigua, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula exigua is known for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
