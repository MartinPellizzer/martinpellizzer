Cryptolepis arbuscula

Cryptolepis arbuscula, commonly known as the African arrowroot or black root, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forest understories.

Cryptolepis arbuscula is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine.
