Cirsium transsilvanicum

Cirsium transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands, rocky soils, and open woodlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, spiky fruits.

Cirsium transsilvanicum is often considered a characteristic species of its habitat and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
