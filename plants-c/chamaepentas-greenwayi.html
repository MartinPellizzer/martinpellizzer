Chamaepentas greenwayi

Chamaepentas greenwayi, commonly known as the Greenway's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
