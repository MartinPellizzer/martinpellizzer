Coryphopteris sylva-nipponica

Coryphopteris sylva-nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish outer layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
