Cynanchum longipes

Cynanchum longipes, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
