Coelogyne wilhelmii var. wilhelmii

Coelogyne wilhelmii var. wilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or red lip. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
