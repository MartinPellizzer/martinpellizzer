Cornus eydeana

Cornus eydeana, commonly known as the Eyde's dogwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae.

It is native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, often turning red in the autumn.

Cornus eydeana is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
