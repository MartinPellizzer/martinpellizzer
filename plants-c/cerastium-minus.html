Cerastium minus

Cerastium minus, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically forms dense mats on the ground, often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in racemes, while the leaves are oval and covered in fine hairs.

Cerastium minus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
