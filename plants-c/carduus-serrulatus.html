Carduus serrulatus

Carduus serrulatus, commonly known as the sickle thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, spiny leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, upright head, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, disturbed soils.

It is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas where it can compete with other vegetation.
