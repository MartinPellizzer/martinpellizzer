Cytisus lithuanicus

Cytisus lithuanicus, commonly known as the Lithuanian broom, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Lithuania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
