Capparis mollis

Capparis mollis, commonly known as the sweet-smelling caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and has small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, spherical fruit known as capers, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Capparis mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
