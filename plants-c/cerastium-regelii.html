Cerastium regelii

Cerastium regelii, commonly known as the Regel's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. Cerastium regelii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate appearance.
