Centaurea paniculata subsp. hoffmannseggii

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

hoffmannseggii, commonly known as Hoffmann's plume thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its most distinctive feature is its tall, panicle-like inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink bracts and small yellow flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
