Cymbopogon divaricatus

Cymbopogon divaricatus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, silver-blue blades with a distinctive fluffy appearance, often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows in clumps, preferring well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage emits a subtle minty aroma, which can be utilized in aromatherapy or as a natural insect repellent.
