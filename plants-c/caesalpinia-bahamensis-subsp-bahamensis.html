Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp. bahamensis

Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp.

bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian burrbean, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces long, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
