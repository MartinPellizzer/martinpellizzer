Cereus pentlandii var. carmineus

Cereus pentlandii var. carmineus, commonly known as the Crimson Cereus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming at night. These flowers are often fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
