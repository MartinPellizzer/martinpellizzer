Chromolaena kleinii

Chromolaena kleinii, commonly known as the African daisies, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Chromolaena kleinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
