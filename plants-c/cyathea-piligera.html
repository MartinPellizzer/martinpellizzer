Cyathea piligera

Cyathea piligera, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea piligera thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
