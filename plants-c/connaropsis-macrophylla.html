Connaropsis macrophylla

Connaropsis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved connaraceae, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
