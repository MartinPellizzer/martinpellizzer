Capparis speciosa var. normalis

Capparis speciosa var. normalis, commonly known as the sweet-scented caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form, with thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly succulent texture, while the flowers emit a fragrant, sweet aroma.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
