Chosenia urbaniana

Chosenia urbaniana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
