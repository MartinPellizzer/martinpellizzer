Chaenactis tanacetifolia var. gracilenta

Chaenactis tanacetifolia var. gracilenta, commonly known as slenderleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are pale yellow to white.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
