Chamaecyparis obtusa f. pendula

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a unique form of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, umbrella-like shape. This ornamental conifer is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and remain throughout the year.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
