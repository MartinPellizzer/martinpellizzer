Calamagrostis obscura

Calamagrostis obscura, commonly known as the dark reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along streams, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically brown to purple in color.

Calamagrostis obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
