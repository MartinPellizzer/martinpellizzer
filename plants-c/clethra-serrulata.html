Clethra serrulata

Clethra serrulata, commonly known as the serrated clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Clethra serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
