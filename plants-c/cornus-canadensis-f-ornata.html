Cornus canadensis f. ornata

Cornus canadensis f. ornata, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood or red-twig dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems that provide visual interest throughout the winter. The plant features small white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its seasonal color changes and ecological benefits to wildlife.
