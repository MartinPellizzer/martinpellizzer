Carex helvetica

Carex helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Carex helvetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
