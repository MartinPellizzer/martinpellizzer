Cirsium viridifolium

Cirsium viridifolium, commonly known as the greenleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a robust, spiny stem that supports its foliage. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, often with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
