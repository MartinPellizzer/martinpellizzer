Cyperus solmsii

Cyperus solmsii, commonly known as the red swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Cyperus solmsii thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to waterlogged soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
