Carlina × clemenceaui

Carlina × clemenceaui, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carlina acaulis and Carlina balsamifera, characterized by its robust growth and distinctive foliage. The plant features large, spiny leaves and produces showy purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
