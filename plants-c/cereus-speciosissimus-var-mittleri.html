Cereus speciosissimus var. mittleri

Cereus speciosissimus var. mittleri, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem and striking, colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, spiny stem and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
