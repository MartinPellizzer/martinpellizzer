Croton pittieri

Croton pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Croton pittieri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its striking foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
