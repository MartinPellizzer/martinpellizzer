Castanola agamae

Castanola agamae, commonly known as the desert agama, is a small, diurnal lizard native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender body, long tail, and distinctive coloration that varies with temperature and mood. The species is well adapted to hot, dry environments, with a specialized physiology for water conservation. Its diet primarily consists of insects, small invertebrates, and occasionally plant matter.

Castanola agamae plays an important role in its ecosystem as both a predator and prey species.
