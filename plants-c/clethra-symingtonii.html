Clethra symingtonii

Clethra symingtonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet pepper.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Clethra symingtonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
