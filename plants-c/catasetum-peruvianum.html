Catasetum peruvianum

Catasetum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated stem bearing several showy flowers.

The botanical features of Catasetum peruvianum make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
