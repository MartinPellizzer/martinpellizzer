Centaurea handelii

Centaurea handelii, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange-red composite flowers. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea handelii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
