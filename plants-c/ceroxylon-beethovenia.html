Ceroxylon beethovenia

Ceroxylon beethovenia, commonly known as the Beethoven's wax palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense crown of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant is notable for its slow growth and can reach heights of up to 15 meters in optimal conditions. Its leaves are waxy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ceroxylon beethovenia is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and shelter for various wildlife species.
