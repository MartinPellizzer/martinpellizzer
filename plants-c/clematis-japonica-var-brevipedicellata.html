Clematis japonica var. brevipedicellata

Clematis japonica var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can climb using twining growth habits.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
