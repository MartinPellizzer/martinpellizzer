Cucurbita siceraria

Cucurbita siceraria, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along fences.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and adaptability to various environments.
