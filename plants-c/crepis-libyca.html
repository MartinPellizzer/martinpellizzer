Crepis libyca

Crepis libyca, commonly known as the Libyan hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flower heads consist of ray and disk florets.

Crepis libyca is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
