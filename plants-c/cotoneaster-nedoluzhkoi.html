Cotoneaster nedoluzhkoi

Cotoneaster nedoluzhkoi, commonly known as the Korean cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Korean Peninsula, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually white or pink and appear in clusters.

Cotoneaster nedoluzhkoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
