Croton hibiscifolius

Croton hibiscifolius, commonly known as the Hibiscifolius Croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of hibiscus plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
