Clematis courtoisii

Clematis courtoisii, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or other structures.

Clematis courtoisii is relatively low maintenance but requires support for optimal growth.
