Calystegia catesbeiana subsp. sericata

Calystegia catesbeiana subsp.

sericata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a vigorous vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and attractive blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some areas.
