Cyperus corymbosus var. pangorei

Cyperus corymbosus var. pangorei, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale brown spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
