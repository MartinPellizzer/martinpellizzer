Cuminum hispanicum

Cuminum hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, dill, and carrots, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with finely divided leaves and umbels of small white to yellow flowers. Its seeds, which are harvested when mature, are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
