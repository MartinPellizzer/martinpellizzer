Cupressus articulata

Cupressus articulata, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and scale-like foliage that gives it a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value.

The Arizona cypress is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
