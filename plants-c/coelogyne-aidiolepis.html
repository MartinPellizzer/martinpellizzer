Coelogyne aidiolepis

Coelogyne aidiolepis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coelogyne aidiolepis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
