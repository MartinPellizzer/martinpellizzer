Cirsium decoloratum

Cirsium decoloratum, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The pale thistle is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
