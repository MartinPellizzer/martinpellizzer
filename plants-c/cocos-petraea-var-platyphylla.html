Cocos petraea var. platyphylla

Cocos petraea var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf rock coconut, is a rare and hardy plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits with a fibrous husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic landscapes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
