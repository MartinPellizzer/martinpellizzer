Chassalia euchlora

Chassalia euchlora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Chassalia euchlora is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
