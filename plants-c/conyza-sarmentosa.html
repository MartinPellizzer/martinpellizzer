Conyza sarmentosa

Conyza sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, sprawling stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to regions of Africa, South America, and parts of Asia, and it often thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Conyza sarmentosa is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.
