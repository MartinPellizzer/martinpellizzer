Clinopodium nepeta

Clinopodium nepeta, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain high concentrations of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effects on cats.

Clinopodium nepeta is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
