Critonia Microdon

Littleleaf critonia, scientifically known as Critonia microdon, is a rare and unique plant species native to the coastal regions of southern California. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing the ecosystem. Despite its small size, the plant is notable for its delicate, waxy leaves and subtle, fragrant blossoms that appear in late spring. Due to habitat loss and climate change, Critonia microdon faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are now underway to protect and preserve this endangered species for future generations.
