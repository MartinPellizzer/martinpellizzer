Calyptranthes rotundata

Calyptranthes rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Calyptranthes rotundata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
