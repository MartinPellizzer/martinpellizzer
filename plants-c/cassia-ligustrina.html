Cassia ligustrina

Cassia ligustrina, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or small-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

Cassia ligustrina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
