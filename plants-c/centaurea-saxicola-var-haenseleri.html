Centaurea saxicola var. haenseleri

Centaurea saxicola var. haenseleri, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, spiny leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers. This variety thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
