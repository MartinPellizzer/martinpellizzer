Clambus araneosus

Clambus araneosus, commonly known as the spider lichen, is a type of lichen that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a composite organism formed by a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its thallus is usually crust-like, with a dark, leathery texture.

Clambus araneosus plays an important role in soil stabilization and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
