Cuphea corniculata

Cuphea corniculata, commonly known as the flame beak plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble a bird's beak, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms.

Cuphea corniculata is a perennial shrub that prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant.
