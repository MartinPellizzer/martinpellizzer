Carex sutchuensis

Carex sutchuensis, commonly known as the Sutchuan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and streambanks, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex sutchuensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
