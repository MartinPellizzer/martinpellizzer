Celastrus lucidus

Celastrus lucidus, commonly known as the shining staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its rapid growth and woody stems make it an effective ground cover in shaded environments.
