Cissus paullinifolia

Cissus paullinifolia, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, rocky areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
