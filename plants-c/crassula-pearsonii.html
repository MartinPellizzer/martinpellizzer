Crassula pearsonii

Crassula pearsonii, commonly known as the Rock Pineapple, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to store water. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
