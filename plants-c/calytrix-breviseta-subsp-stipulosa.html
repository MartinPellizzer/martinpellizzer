Calytrix breviseta subsp. stipulosa

Calytrix breviseta subsp.

stipulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers.

The subspecies stipulosa is distinguished by its narrow leaves and the presence of stipules at the base of the leaves. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

This plant is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
