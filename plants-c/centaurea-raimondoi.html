Centaurea raimondoi

Centaurea raimondoi, commonly known as Raimond's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea raimondoi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
