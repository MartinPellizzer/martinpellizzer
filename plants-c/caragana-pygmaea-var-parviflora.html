Caragana pygmaea var. parviflora

Caragana pygmaea var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf shrub or parviflora shrub, is a low-growing, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, compound leaves and produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
