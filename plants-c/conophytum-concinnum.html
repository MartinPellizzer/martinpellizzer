Conophytum concinnum

Conophytum concinnum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a dense, rounded rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum concinnum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
