Crataegus tianschanica

Crataegus tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky or arid soils.

Crataegus tianschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
