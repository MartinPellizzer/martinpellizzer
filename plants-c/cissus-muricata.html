Cissus muricata

Cissus muricata, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive bumpy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw.

Cissus muricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
