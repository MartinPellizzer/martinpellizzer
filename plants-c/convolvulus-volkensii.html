Convolvulus volkensii

Convolvulus volkensii, commonly known as the red sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
