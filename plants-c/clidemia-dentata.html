Clidemia dentata

Clidemia dentata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Clidemia dentata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
