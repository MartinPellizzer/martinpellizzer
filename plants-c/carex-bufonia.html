Carex bufonia

Carex bufonia, commonly known as the brown-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, brownish flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or brownish tinge at the base.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
