Cyrtandra × nutans

Cyrtandra × nutans, commonly known as the hanging heliconia, is a hybrid plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional Hawaiian basket weaving. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that attracts birds and insects for pollination.

Cyrtandra × nutans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in Polynesian societies.
