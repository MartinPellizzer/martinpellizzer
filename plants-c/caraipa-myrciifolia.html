Caraipa myrciifolia

Caraipa myrciifolia, commonly known as the Caraipa myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, glossy leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
