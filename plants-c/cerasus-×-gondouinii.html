Cerasus × gondouinii

Cerasus × gondouinii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × gondouinii, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, edible cherry that is often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

Cerasus × gondouinii is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is a symbol of renewal and the arrival of spring.
