Crepis linguifolia

Crepis linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue-shaped hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, tongue-like leaves and yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds in a disk-shaped flower head.

It is often found in moist or sandy soils and is considered a perennial species.
