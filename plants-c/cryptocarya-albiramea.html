Cryptocarya albiramea

Cryptocarya albiramea, commonly known as the white-flowered cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, typical of many members of the Lauraceae family.
