Cirsium poluninii

Cirsium poluninii, commonly known as the Polunin's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough, and covered with stiff hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head.

Cirsium poluninii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
