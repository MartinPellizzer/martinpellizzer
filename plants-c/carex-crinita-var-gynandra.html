Carex crinita var. gynandra

Carex crinita var. gynandra, commonly known as the silky hair sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
