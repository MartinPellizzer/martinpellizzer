Campanula barrelieri

Campanula barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Campanula barrelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
