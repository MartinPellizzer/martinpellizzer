Chamaecostus fragilis

Chamaecostus fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant chamaecostus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful handling and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
