Carex laxa f. refracta

Carex laxa f. refracta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Carex.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
