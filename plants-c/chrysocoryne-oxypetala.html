Chrysocoryne oxypetala

Chrysocoryne oxypetala, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is typically found growing as a climbing vine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
