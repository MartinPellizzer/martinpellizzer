Chrysocoma alterna

Chrysocoma alterna, commonly known as the alternate chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters, making it visually striking.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
