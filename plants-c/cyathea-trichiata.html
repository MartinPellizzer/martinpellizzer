Cyathea trichiata

Cyathea trichiata, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer that resembles bark. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea trichiata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
