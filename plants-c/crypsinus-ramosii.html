Crypsinus ramosii

Crypsinus ramosii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural settings.
