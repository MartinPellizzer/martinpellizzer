Crataegus macrosperma var. matura

Crataegus macrosperma var. matura, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open areas.
