Cybianthus minutiflorus

Cybianthus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf flaxflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tiny, delicate flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to conserve moisture.

Cybianthus minutiflorus is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and slopes.
