Campanula persicifolia var. angustiflora

Campanula persicifolia var. angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
