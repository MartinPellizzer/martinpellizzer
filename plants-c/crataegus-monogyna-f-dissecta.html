Crataegus monogyna f. dissecta

Crataegus monogyna f. dissecta, commonly known as the shredded rosehip or dissected hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and attract wildlife. It is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

This form is notable for its unique leaf structure, which sets it apart from the typical form of Crataegus monogyna.
