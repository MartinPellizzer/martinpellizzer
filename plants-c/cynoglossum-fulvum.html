Cynoglossum fulvum

Cynoglossum fulvum, commonly known as the greater hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, tongue-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Cynoglossum fulvum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
