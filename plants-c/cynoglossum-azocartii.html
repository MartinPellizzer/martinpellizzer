Cynoglossum azocartii

Cynoglossum azocartii, commonly known as the Azocartrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to the unique Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
