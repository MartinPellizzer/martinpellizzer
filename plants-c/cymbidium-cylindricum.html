Cymbidium cylindricum

Cymbidium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of elongated, cylindrical flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium cylindricum is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
