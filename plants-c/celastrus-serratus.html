Celastrus serratus

Celastrus serratus, commonly known as the roughleaf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its stems are often woody and can have a rough, ridged texture.

Celastrus serratus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
