Clerodendrum nipense var. pubescens

Clerodendrum nipense var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or orange calyx. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the base of the plant, adding to its visual appeal.
