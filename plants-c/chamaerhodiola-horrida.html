Chamaerhodiola horrida

Chamaerhodiola horrida, commonly known as the rough fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in harsh environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in tundra regions.

Chamaerhodiola horrida is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
