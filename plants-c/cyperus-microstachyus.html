Cyperus microstachyus

Cyperus microstachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems.

Cyperus microstachyus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
