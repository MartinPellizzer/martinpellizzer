Callipteris malabarica

Callipteris malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar umbrella fern, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like fronds that are divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Callipteris malabarica is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
