Crataegus debilis

Crataegus debilis, commonly known as the weak thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features thorny branches, simple leaves with serrated edges, and small white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
