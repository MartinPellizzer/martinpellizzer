Campanula lancifolia

Campanula lancifolia, commonly known as the lancolate bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanula lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
