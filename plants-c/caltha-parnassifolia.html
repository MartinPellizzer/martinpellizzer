Caltha parnassifolia

Caltha parnassifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and dark green, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Caltha parnassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
