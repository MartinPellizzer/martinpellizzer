Canthium griseum

Canthium griseum, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.
