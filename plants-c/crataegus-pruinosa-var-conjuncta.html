Crataegus pruinosa var. conjuncta

Crataegus pruinosa var. conjuncta, commonly known as the clustered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
