Caprifolium segreziense

Caprifolium segreziense, commonly known as the Segreziense honeysuckle, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
