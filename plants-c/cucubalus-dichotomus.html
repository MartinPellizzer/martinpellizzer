Cucubalus dichotomus

Cucubalus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems that twine around supports and produce small, yellow flowers. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
