Carex shichiseitensis

Carex shichiseitensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex shichiseitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
