Citronella sarmentosa var. lucidula

Citronella sarmentosa var. lucidula, commonly known as the shining citronella, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its citronella oil, which is used in natural insect repellents. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its varietal name "lucidula" refers to its shiny, waxy leaf surface.
