Calathea singularis

Calathea singularis, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and variegation. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and exhibit a unique ability to fold upwards at night, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
