Carex laevis f. balcana

Carex laevis f. balcana, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a variant of the smooth sedge species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is usually dark green and smooth, with narrow, strap-like leaves that are densely packed.

The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
