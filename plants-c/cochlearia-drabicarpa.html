Cochlearia drabicarpa

Cochlearia drabicarpa, commonly known as the Drabcarpa cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold climates.

Cochlearia drabicarpa is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to frost and low nutrient conditions.
