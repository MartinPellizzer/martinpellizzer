Campanula calycantherrea

Campanula calycantherrea, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Campanula calycantherrea prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
