Cassipourea verticillata

Cassipourea verticillata, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Cassipourea verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
