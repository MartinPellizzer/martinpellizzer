Calamus macrochlamys

Calamus macrochlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, narrow rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Calamus macrochlamys is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
