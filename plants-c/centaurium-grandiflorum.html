Centaurium grandiflorum

Centaurium grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Centaurium grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
