Carex kukkoneniana

Carex kukkoneniana, commonly known as the Kukkonen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
