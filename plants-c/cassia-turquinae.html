Cassia turquinae

Cassia turquinae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its surroundings. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in appearance. Cassia turquinae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
