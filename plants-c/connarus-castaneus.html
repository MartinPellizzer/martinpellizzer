Connarus castaneus

Connarus castaneus, commonly known as the chestnut tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by woody, chestnut-like fruits containing one or two seeds. Its roots are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
