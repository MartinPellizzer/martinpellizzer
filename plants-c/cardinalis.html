Cardinalis

Cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a striking plant native to North America.

It is part of the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant features showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Cardinalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.
