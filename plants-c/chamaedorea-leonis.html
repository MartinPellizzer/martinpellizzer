Chamaedorea leonis

Chamaedorea leonis, commonly known as the golden palm or the lemon tree palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elegant, feather-like leaves that are often a vibrant green or golden hue. The plant typically grows as a solitary stem with a crown of arching fronds, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental use. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it relatively easy to care for.

The plant is also valued for its ability to purify the air and thrive in low-light conditions.
