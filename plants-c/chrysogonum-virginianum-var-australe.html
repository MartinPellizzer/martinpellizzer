Chrysogonum virginianum var. australe

Chrysogonum virginianum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
