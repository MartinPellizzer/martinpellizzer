Campanula lusitanica subsp. specularioides

Campanula lusitanica subsp.

specularioides, commonly known as the Portuguese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. It is well adapted to sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
