Corylus avellana var. lambertii

Corylus avellana var. lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's hazel, is a variety of the common hazel native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, edible nuts that are typically harvested in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and greyish.

This variety is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
