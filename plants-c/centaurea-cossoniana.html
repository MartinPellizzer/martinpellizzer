Centaurea cossoniana

Centaurea cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
