Chloidia vernalis

Chloidia vernalis, commonly known as the spring chlorella, is a unique plant species characterized by its chlorophyll-rich cells that give it a vibrant green appearance.

It thrives in temperate regions during the early spring months, often emerging from moist, nutrient-rich soil. The plant is notable for its rapid growth cycle, completing its life stage within a few weeks. Its cellular structure is specialized for efficient photosynthesis, making it an important component of early-season ecosystems.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
