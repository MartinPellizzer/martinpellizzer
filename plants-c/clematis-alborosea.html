Clematis alborosea

Clematis alborosea, commonly known as the white rose clematis, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, woody stems that climb by means of small tendrils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis alborosea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
