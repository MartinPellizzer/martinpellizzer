Combretum petersii

Combretum petersii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Combretum petersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
