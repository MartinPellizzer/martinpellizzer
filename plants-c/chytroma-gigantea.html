Chytroma gigantea

Chytroma gigantea, commonly known as the giant chytroma, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in length, providing ample shade under the forest canopy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Despite its size, it is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in humid environments with indirect sunlight.
