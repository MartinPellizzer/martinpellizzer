Cousinia anisoptera

Cousinia anisoptera, commonly known as the broad-leaved cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and hairy, often with a bluish or grayish hue. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Cousinia anisoptera is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
