Cassia brasiliana

Cassia brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are bipinnately compound. Cassia brasiliana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral displays.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
