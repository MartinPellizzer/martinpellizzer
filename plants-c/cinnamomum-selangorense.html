Cinnamomum selangorense

Cinnamomum selangorense, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and oval-shaped berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinct fragrance and medicinal properties.

Cinnamomum selangorense is valued for its potential health benefits and is sometimes used as a substitute for true cinnamon in culinary applications.
