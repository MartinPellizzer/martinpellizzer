Canavalia virosa

Canavalia virosa, commonly known as the virus bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor nutrient conditions. It has a climbing habit and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Canavalia virosa is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems.
