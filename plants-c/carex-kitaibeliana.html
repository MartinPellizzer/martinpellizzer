Carex kitaibeliana

Carex kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
