Carlina tristis

Carlina tristis, commonly known as the lesser ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered heads. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Carlina tristis is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
