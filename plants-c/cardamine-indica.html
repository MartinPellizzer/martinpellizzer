Cardamine indica

Cardamine indica, commonly known as Indian watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It has a shallow root system and is often found in temperate regions of Asia.

Its edible leaves are sometimes used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
