Cardamine bellidifolia var. petiolaris

Cardamine bellidifolia var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

The plant is noted for its peppery flavor, making it a popular choice for culinary use in certain regions.
