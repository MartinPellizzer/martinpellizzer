Cornus flava

Cornus flava, commonly known as the yellow dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Cornus flava is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
