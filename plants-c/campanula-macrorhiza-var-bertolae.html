Campanula macrorhiza var. bertolae

Campanula macrorhiza var. bertolae, commonly known as the large-rooted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often hairy, while the roots are thick and fleshy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
