Celastrus obtusifolius

Celastrus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive, ridged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
