Chaetogastra galeottiana

Chaetogastra galeottiana, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central golden stamen that resembles a star. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
