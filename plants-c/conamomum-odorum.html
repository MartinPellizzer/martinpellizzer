Conamomum odorum

Conamomum odorum, commonly known as the fragrant cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that exudes a sweet, spicy scent when harvested. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, leathery fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, food flavoring, and traditional medicine.
