Cynoglossum divaricatum

Cynoglossum divaricatum, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cynoglossum divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
