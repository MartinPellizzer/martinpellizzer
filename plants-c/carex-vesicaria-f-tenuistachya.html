Carex vesicaria f. tenuistachya

Carex vesicaria f. tenuistachya, commonly known as the narrow-stamen bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale to greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a compact, rounded head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
