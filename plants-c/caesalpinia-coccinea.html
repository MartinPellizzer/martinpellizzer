Caesalpinia coccinea

Caesalpinia coccinea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
