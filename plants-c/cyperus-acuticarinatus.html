Cyperus acuticarinatus

Cyperus acuticarinatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus acuticarinatus is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.
