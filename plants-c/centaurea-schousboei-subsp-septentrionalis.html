Centaurea schousboei subsp. septentrionalis

Centaurea schousboei subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flower heads.

The species belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower structures. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
