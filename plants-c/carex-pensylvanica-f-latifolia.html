Carex pensylvanica f. latifolia

Carex pensylvanica f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are broader than those of typical sedges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
