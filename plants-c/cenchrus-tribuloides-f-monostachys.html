Cenchrus tribuloides f. monostachys

Cenchrus tribuloides f. monostachys, commonly known as the single-stemmed burrgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed heads.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats. Its morphology includes narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The unique structure of its inflorescence contributes to its role in seed dispersal and ecological interactions.
