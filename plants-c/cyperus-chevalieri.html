Cyperus chevalieri

Cyperus chevalieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cyperus chevalieri produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous, with the true flowers being tiny and wind-pollinated.
