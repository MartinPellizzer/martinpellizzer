Cuprespinnata disticha

Cuprespinnata disticha, commonly known as the clustered spiky vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
