Curima colophylla

Curima colophylla, commonly known as the yellow curima, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Curima colophylla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
