Crataegus infera

Crataegus infera, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh climates.

Crataegus infera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
