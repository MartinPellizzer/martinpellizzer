Cirsium rubricaule

Cirsium rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its stems are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Cirsium rubricaule is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
