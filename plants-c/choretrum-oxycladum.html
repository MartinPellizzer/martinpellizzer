Choretrum oxycladum

Choretrum oxycladum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
