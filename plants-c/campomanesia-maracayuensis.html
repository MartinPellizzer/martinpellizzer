Campomanesia maracayuensis

Campomanesia maracayuensis, commonly known as the yellow maracayu, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits. The fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species within its native habitat.
