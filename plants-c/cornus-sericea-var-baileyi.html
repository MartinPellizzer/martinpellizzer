Cornus sericea var. baileyi

Cornus sericea var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
