Campanula athoa

Campanula athoa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Campanula athoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
