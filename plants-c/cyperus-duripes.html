Cyperus duripes

Cyperus duripes, commonly known as the hard-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous. Cyperus duripes thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and cultivated settings.
