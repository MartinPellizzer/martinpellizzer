Calamagrostis pseudophragmites subvar. exserta

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites subvar. exserta, commonly known as the tall millet grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical leaves and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. This subspecies is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
