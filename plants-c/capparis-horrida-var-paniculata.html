Capparis horrida var. paniculata

Capparis horrida var. paniculata, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green berries that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species, adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
