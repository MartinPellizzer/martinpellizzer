Crotalaria cylindrocarpa

Crotalaria cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the cylindrical cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
