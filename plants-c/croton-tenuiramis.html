Croton tenuiramis

Croton tenuiramis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
