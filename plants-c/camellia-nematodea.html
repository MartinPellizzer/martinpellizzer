Camellia nematodea

Camellia nematodea, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia nematodea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
