Carex involucrata

Carex involucrata, commonly known as the purple tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex involucrata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
