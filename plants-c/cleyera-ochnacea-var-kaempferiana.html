Cleyera ochnacea var. kaempferiana

Cleyera ochnacea var. kaempferiana, commonly known as the Kaempfer's cleyera, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
