Convolvulus besseri

Convolvulus besseri, commonly known as the Armenian bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
