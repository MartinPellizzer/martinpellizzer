Carpinus betulus var. pendula

Carpinus betulus var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique visual appearance. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, spiny fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
