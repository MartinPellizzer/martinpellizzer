Cleome fimbriata subsp. brachystyla

Cleome fimbriata subsp.

brachystyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
