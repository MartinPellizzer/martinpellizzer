Crepis corymbosa var. baetica

Crepis corymbosa var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a corymb, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
