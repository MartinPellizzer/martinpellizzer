Callicarpa japonica

Callicarpa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
