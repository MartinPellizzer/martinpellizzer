Cirsium californicum var. bernardinum

Cirsium californicum var. bernardinum, commonly known as the California thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple or pink flowers. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
