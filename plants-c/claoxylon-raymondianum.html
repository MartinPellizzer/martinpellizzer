Claoxylon raymondianum

Claoxylon raymondianum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark red heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
