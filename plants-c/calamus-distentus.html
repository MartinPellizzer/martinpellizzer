Calamus distentus

Calamus distentus, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its long, rhizomatous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The botanical features of Calamus distentus make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
