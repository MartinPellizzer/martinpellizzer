Cyrtopodium bradei

Cyrtopodium bradei, commonly known as the Brade's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces linear leaves and has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or streaked.

Cyrtopodium bradei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
