Capsella cornigera

Capsella cornigera, commonly known as the mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
