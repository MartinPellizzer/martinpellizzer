Cicer subaphyllum

Cicer subaphyllum, commonly known as the small-flowered vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
