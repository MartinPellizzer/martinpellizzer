Citrus macroptera var. combara

Citrus macroptera var. combara, commonly known as the Natal orange, is a citrus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, round, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
