Carex formosensis

Carex formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan sedge, is a flowering plant species native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-stamened flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex formosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
