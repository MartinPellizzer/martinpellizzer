Ceanothus maritimus

Ceanothus maritimus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall. Its leaves are often gray-green and have a fragrant scent when crushed.

Ceanothus maritimus plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
