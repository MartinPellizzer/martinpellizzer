Callista aquea

Callista aquea, commonly known as water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water or mud. Its leaves are typically green and waxy, while the flowers come in various colors, including white, pink, and yellow.

Callista aquea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water bodies.
