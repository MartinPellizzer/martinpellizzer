Chorizanthe obovata

Chorizanthe obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically broad and obovate, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.
