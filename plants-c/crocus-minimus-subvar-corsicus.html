Crocus minimus subvar. corsicus

Crocus minimus subvar. corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and coastal areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
