Cycnoches sanderiana

Cycnoches sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's cycnoches, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cycnoches sanderiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful management to ensure optimal growth and flowering.
