Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. parviflorum

Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
