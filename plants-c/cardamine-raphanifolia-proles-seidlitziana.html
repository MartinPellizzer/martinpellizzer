Cardamine raphanifolia proles seidlitziana

Cardamine raphanifolia proles seidlitziana, commonly known as the Seidlitz's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in aquatic or wetland soils.

This species is often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and other freshwater environments where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich conditions.
