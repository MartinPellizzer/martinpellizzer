Convolvulus quadriflorus

Convolvulus quadriflorus, commonly known as the four-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-petaled blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a trailing habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.
