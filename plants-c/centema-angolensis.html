Centema angolensis

Centema angolensis, commonly known as the Angola centella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Centema angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
