Cenia expansa

Cenia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf cenia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cenia expansa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
