Celastrus salicifolius

Celastrus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leafed staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Celastrus salicifolius thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
