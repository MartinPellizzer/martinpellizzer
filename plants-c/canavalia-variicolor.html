Canavalia variicolor

Canavalia variicolor, commonly known as the colorful morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from red to purple and yellow, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Canavalia variicolor is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
