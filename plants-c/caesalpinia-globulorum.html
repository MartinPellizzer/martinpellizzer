Caesalpinia globulorum

Caesalpinia globulorum, commonly known as the globular thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical seed pods that turn red when mature, often containing several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and thorn-like structures at the base of its branches. It produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Caesalpinia globulorum is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
