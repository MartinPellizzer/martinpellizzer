Cordyla

Cordyla, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It comprises several species, most notably Cordyla africana, which is native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces fleshy, starchy fruits that are edible. These fruits are often used as a food source in traditional diets, similar to breadfruit.

The genus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
