Curcuma alismatifolia

Curcuma alismatifolia, commonly known as the water ginger or alisma turmeric, is a rhizomatous perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Curcuma alismatifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
