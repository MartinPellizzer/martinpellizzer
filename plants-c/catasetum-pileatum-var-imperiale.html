Catasetum pileatum var. imperiale

Catasetum pileatum var. imperiale, commonly known as the imperial catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each bloom featuring a specialized structure for pollination by bees.

The leaves are elongated and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant floral display.
