Cybianthus dubius

Cybianthus dubius, commonly known as the slender leek, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important subject for botanical study.
