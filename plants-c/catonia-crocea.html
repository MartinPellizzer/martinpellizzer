Catonia crocea

Catonia crocea, commonly known as the red-flowered catonia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in small, blue-green leaves that help it store water. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central disk.

Catonia crocea thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
