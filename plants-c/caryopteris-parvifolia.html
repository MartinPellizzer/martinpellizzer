Caryopteris parvifolia

Caryopteris parvifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
