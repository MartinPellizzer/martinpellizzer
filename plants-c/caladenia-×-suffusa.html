Caladenia × suffusa

Caladenia × suffusa, commonly known as the hybrid spider orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Caladenia flava and Caladenia trapeziformis.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, hairy stems and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, spider-like flowers with a distinctive, elongated labellum and vividly colored sepals and petals. It typically blooms in spring, with flowers ranging in color from yellow to orange and red.

Native to southern Australia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.
