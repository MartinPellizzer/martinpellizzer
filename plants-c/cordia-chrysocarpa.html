Cordia chrysocarpa

Cordia chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellow-orange fruits that are fleshy and contain several seeds. The bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish color, and the tree is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
