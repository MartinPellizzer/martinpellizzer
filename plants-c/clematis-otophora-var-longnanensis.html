Clematis otophora var. longnanensis

Clematis otophora var. longnanensis, commonly known as the Longnan clematis, is a species native to China, specifically the Longnan region.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, compound leaves, and a long flowering period.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
