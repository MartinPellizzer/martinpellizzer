Calliandra haematocephala var. haematocephala

Calliandra haematocephala var. haematocephala, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-pink flower clusters that appear in late summer and early autumn. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
