Chlorophytum bukobense

Chlorophytum bukobense, commonly known as the Bukobense spider plant, is a species native to East Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences bearing small white flowers, which are followed by clusters of green, fleshy berries. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
