Campanula hispanica f. minor

Campanula hispanica f. minor, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower or dwarf bellflower, is a compact, flowering perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
