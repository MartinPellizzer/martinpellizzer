Cyrtanthus angustifolius

Cyrtanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bell orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cyrtanthus angustifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
