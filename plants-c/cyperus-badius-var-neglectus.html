Cyperus badius var. neglectus

Cyperus badius var. neglectus, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

This species is adapted to tolerate saline and waterlogged conditions, making it a common component of tidal wetlands.
