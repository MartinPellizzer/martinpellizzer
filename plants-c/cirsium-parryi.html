Cirsium parryi

Cirsium parryi, commonly known as Parry's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium parryi is often found in open areas with well-drained soils and can tolerate arid conditions.
