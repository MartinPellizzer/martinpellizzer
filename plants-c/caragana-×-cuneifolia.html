Caragana × cuneifolia

Caragana × cuneifolia, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a hybrid shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, compound leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pea-like pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought, poor soils, and extreme temperatures.

Caragana × cuneifolia is often used for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in colder regions.
