Chenopodium ruderale

Chenopodium ruderale, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, rough, hairy stems, and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in dense, branched inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in various habitats.

Chenopodium ruderale is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.
