Cosmos scabiosoides f. typicus

Cosmos scabiosoides f. typicus, commonly known as the typical form of the scarlet globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bright red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and stems that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid landscapes.
