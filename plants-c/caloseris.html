Caloseris

Caloseris, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flower heads that resemble small cups, hence its common name. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky soils and are native to regions in southern Africa. They produce small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to their spread in arid environments.

Caloseris species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant color and unique floral structure.
