Coccinia fernandesiana

Coccinia fernandesiana, commonly known as the Madagascar nightshade, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, melon-like flavor.

The species is notable for its unique adaptation to the island's diverse ecosystems.
