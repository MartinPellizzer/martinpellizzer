Canavalia gladiata f. alba

Canavalia gladiata f. alba, commonly known as the white cowpea, is a variant of the common cowpea species characterized by its white flowers and pods.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which benefits agricultural productivity. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

The pods are cylindrical, containing several seeds that are used as a food source in various cuisines.
