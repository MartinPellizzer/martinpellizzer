Calamintha bonanovae

Calamintha bonanovae, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Calamintha bonanovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
