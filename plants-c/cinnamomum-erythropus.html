Cinnamomum erythropus

Cinnamomum erythropus, commonly known as red cinnamon or red camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional remedies.
