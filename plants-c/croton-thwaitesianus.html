Croton thwaitesianus

Croton thwaitesianus, commonly known as the Wawai tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
