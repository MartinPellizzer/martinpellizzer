Cytisus albus subsp. microphyllus

Cytisus albus subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved whitethorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
