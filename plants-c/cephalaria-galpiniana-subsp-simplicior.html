Cephalaria galpiniana subsp. simplicior

Cephalaria galpiniana subsp.

simplicior, commonly known as the smooth cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its adaptability in dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.
