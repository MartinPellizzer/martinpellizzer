Centaurea gueryi var. genuina

Centaurea gueryi var. genuina, commonly known as the Guery's knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pinkish-red flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets.

Centaurea gueryi var. genuina is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
