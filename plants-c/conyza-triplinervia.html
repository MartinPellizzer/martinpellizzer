Conyza triplinervia

Conyza triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined cudweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
