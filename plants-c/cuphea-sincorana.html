Cuphea sincorana

Cuphea sincorana, commonly known as the flame flower or sacred candle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cuphea sincorana is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 feet tall, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
