Cerasus sylvestris var. dulcis

Cerasus sylvestris var. dulcis, commonly known as the wild cherry or sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. The fruits are typically round, firm, and have a red or dark red skin with a juicy, flavorful pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
