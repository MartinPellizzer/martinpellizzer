Cousinia diezii

Cousinia diezii, commonly known as the Desert Cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Cousinia diezii is often found in rocky soils and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
