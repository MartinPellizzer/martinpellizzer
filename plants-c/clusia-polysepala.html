Clusia polysepala

Clusia polysepala, commonly known as the wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by bright red or orange berries. Clusia polysepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its resilience to drought and air pollution.
