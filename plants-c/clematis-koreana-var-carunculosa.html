Clematis koreana var. carunculosa

Clematis koreana var. carunculosa, commonly known as the Korean clematis with a tufted stigma, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple, often with a distinctive tuft of hair at the base of the stigma. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
