Cleome kalachariensis

Cleome kalachariensis, commonly known as the red cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cleome kalachariensis is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
