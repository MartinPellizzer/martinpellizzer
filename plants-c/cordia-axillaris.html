Cordia axillaris

Cordia axillaris, commonly known as the wax tree or white candlewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and opposite, simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
