Chamaecytisus palmensis

Chamaecytisus palmensis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, arching stems covered in small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
