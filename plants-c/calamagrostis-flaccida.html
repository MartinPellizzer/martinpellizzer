Calamagrostis flaccida

Calamagrostis flaccida, commonly known as the drooping reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, arching stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to golden in color.
