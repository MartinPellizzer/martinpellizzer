Cyathea malegassica

Cyathea malegassica, commonly known as the Madagascar tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Cyathea malegassica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
