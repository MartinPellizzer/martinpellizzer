Calliandra brevicaulis f. roseiflora

Calliandra brevicaulis f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
