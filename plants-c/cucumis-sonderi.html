Cucumis sonderi

Cucumis sonderi, commonly known as the Sonder's cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by cucumbers that are typically thin and elongated. Its fruit is edible and has a mild flavor, making it suitable for consumption.

It thrives in warm, arid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
