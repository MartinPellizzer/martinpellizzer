Cuscuta corymbosa

Cuscuta corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to North America and can be found growing on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, allowing it to spread widely.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
