Carpobrotus rubrocinctus

Carpobrotus rubrocinctus, commonly known as the red-banded purslane, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves and vibrant red markings along the leaf margins. The plant produces bright orange flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often forming dense mats that help prevent erosion.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered both a valuable ground cover and an invasive species in some regions.
