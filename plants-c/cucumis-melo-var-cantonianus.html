Cucumis melo var. cantonianus

Cucumis melo var. cantonianus, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Asia.

It is a trailing vine that produces large, round fruits with a netted rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and typically flowers in the early summer. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns from green to a golden-orange color.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
