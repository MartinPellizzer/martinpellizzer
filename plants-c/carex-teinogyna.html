Carex teinogyna

Carex teinogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex teinogyna is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife habitats.
