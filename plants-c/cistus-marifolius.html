Cistus marifolius

Cistus marifolius, commonly known as the marjoram cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been used historically in various traditional applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
