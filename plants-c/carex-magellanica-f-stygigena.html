Carex magellanica f. stygigena

Carex magellanica f. stygigena, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This subspecies is typically found in wetland or bog environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
