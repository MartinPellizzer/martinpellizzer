Cota

Cota, commonly known as the blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Cota species are annual or biennial herbs with hairy stems and alternate leaves.

They are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
