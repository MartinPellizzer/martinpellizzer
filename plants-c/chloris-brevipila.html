Chloris brevipila

Chloris brevipila, commonly known as the short-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, tufted foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets.

Chloris brevipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and landscaping.
