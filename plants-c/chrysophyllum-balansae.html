Chrysophyllum balansae

Chrysophyllum balansae, commonly known as the African cherry, is a tropical tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
