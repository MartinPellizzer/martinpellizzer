Croton tranomarensis var. rosmarinifolius

Croton tranomarensis var. rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and can range in color from yellow to orange.
