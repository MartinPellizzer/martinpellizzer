Cuscuta syrtorum

Cuscuta syrtorum, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, often attaching itself to species like grasses and shrubs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a rapid growth pattern, allowing it to spread quickly in suitable environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or mechanical means, contributing to its invasive potential in new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly weaken or even kill its host plants.
