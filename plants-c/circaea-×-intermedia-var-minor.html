Circaea × intermedia var. minor

Circaea × intermedia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser whitebeard, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically forms dense mats of hairy, rounded leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
