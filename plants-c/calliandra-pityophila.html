Calliandra pityophila

Calliandra pityophila, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra pityophila is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
