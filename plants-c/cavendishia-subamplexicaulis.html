Cavendishia subamplexicaulis

Cavendishia subamplexicaulis, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist garden settings.
