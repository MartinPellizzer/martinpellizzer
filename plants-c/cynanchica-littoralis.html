Cynanchica littoralis

Cynanchica littoralis, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

Cynanchica littoralis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting coastal biodiversity.
