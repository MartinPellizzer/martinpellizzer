Carex omeyica

Carex omeyica, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ability to thrive in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in marshy areas, stream banks, and other wetlands.

Carex omeyica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
