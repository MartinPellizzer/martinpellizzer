Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. nervosum

Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-veined cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that have a distinctively narrow venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, edible berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This variant is valued for its unique fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
