Cirsium palousense

Cirsium palousense, commonly known as Palous thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its growth habit is upright, with a height ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters.

Cirsium palousense plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
