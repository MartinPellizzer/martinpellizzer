Camellia haaniensis

Camellia haaniensis, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia haaniensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
