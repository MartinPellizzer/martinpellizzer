Corchorus hirtus var. martii

Corchorus hirtus var. martii, commonly known as the Egyptian burr plant, is a variant of the common burr plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into woody, spiky fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used for making ropes and textiles.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
