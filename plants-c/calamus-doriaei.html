Calamus doriaei

Calamus doriaei, commonly known as Doria's rattan, is a climbing palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by feather-like fruits that contain seeds. Calamus doriaei is often found in humid lowland forests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
