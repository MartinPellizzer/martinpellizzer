Colocasia esculenta var. rupicola

Colocasia esculenta var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock colocasia, is a variant of the elephant ear plant characterized by its robust, arrowhead-shaped leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves can grow up to 3 feet in length, making it a striking addition to tropical landscapes.

While not typically cultivated for its edible tubers, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
