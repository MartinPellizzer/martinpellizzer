Carpesium nepalense var. nilagiricum

Carpesium nepalense var. nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri thistle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is often found in mountainous areas with cool climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
