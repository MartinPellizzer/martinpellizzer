Cleome amblyocarpa

Cleome amblyocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cleome amblyocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
