Caltha minor subsp. arctica

Caltha minor subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic marsh marigold, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It produces bright yellow flowers with showy petals that bloom in early summer.

The plant has smooth, succulent stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and wet meadow habitats.

Its botanical features are adapted to withstand cold climates and short growing seasons.
