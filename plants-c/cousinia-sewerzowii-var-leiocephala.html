Cousinia sewerzowii var. leiocephala

Cousinia sewerzowii var. leiocephala, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
