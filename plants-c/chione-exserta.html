Chione exserta

Chione exserta, commonly known as the rock lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Chione exserta is often found in mountainous regions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
