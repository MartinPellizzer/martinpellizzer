Crataegus × maligna

Crataegus × maligna, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha. This plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small white flowers, and red berries that ripen in the fall. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

The botanical features of Crataegus × maligna make it a common ornamental plant in gardens and hedgerows.
