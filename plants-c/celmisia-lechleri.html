Celmisia lechleri

Celmisia lechleri, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Celmisia lechleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been traditionally used in Andean cultures for various purposes.
