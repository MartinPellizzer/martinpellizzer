Cakile edentula subsp. californica

Cakile edentula subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California rockcress, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and bluff habitats. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

The species is also known for its edible, peppery leaves, which have been used in traditional culinary practices.
