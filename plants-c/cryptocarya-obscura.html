Cryptocarya obscura

Cryptocarya obscura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mohua, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinct midrib, while its fruits are small, woody drupes.

Cryptocarya obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
