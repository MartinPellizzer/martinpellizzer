Calamagrostis stricta subsp. groenlandica

Calamagrostis stricta subsp.

groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland sedge, is a perennial grass native to Arctic and alpine regions of Greenland and northern Canada. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
