Campanula gummifera

Campanula gummifera, commonly known as the gummed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems and leaves.

Campanula gummifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
