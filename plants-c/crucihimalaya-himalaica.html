Crucihimalaya himalaica

Crucihimalaya himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers that resemble a cross, hence its common name. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous regions.
