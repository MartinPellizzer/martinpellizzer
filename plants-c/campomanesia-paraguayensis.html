Campomanesia paraguayensis

Campomanesia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, yellow or orange berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
