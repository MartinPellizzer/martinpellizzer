Cassia ursina

Cassia ursina, commonly known as wild cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain small, flat seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cassia ursina is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
