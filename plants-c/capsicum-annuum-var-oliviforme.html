Capsicum annuum var. oliviforme

Capsicum annuum var. oliviforme, commonly known as the olive-shaped pepper, is a unique variety of chili pepper characterized by its elongated, oval fruit shape and a distinctive olive-green coloration.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to the Americas, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit develops from a single ovary and matures through a series of color changes, starting from green to red or yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to medium heat level, making it popular in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
