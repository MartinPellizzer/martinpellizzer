Cavalcantia

Cavalcantia, commonly known as the golden cup, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small cups. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Cavalcantia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
