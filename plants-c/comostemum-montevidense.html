Comostemum montevidense

Comostemum montevidense, commonly known as the Montevideo thistle, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny stems. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with rough textures and produces purple to pink flower heads that attract pollinators. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
