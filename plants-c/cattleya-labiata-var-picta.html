Cattleya labiata var. picta

Cattleya labiata var. picta, commonly known as the painted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a wide range of hues, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
