Cassia abbreviata

Cassia abbreviata, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia abbreviata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
