Caelestina hastata

Caelestina hastata, commonly known as the skyblue aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-violet flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Caelestina hastata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
