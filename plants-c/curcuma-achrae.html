Curcuma achrae

Curcuma achrae, commonly known as the African turmeric, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric species. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three petals and three sepals.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for their potential antioxidant properties.
