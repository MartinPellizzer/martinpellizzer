Cytisus spinescens

Cytisus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus spinescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
