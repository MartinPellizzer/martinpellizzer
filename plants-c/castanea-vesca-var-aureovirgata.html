Castanea vesca var. aureovirgata

Castanea vesca var. aureovirgata, commonly known as the golden-haired horse chestnut, is a variant of the horse chestnut tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to golden flowers that appear in panicles during late spring. The leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a velvety texture. The fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two chestnuts, which are edible when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
