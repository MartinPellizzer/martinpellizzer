Cucurbita pepo var. xanthapion

Cucurbita pepo var. xanthapion, commonly known as the yellow cushaw, is a variant of the common gourd belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant is an annual vine that produces both male and female flowers, with the latter developing into the edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The yellow cushaw is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds and squash.
