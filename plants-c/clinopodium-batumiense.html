Clinopodium batumiense

Clinopodium batumiense, commonly known as Batumi mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Clinopodium batumiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
