Cacalia linaria

Cacalia linaria, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Cacalia linaria is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
