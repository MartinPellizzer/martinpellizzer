Coleus recurvata

Coleus recurvata, commonly known as the curly leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a wavy, curling edge, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
