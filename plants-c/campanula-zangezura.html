Campanula zangezura

Campanula zangezura, commonly known as the Zangezur bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula zangezura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
