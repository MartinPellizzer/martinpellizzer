Centaurea jacea subsp. decipiens

Centaurea jacea subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright red or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and are accompanied by distinctive bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
