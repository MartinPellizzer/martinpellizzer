Caprifolium rotundifolium

Caprifolium rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Caprifolium rotundifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
