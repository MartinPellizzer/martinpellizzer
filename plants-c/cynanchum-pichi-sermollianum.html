Cynanchum pichi-sermollianum

Cynanchum pichi-sermollianum, commonly known as the Pichi-sermollian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and specialized floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and display a mix of yellow and green hues.

The plant's latex contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
