Cineraria pulchra

Cineraria pulchra, commonly known as the "Blue Cineraria," is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in its native arid environment. It typically grows as a low shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Cineraria pulchra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
