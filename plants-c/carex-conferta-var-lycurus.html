Carex conferta var. lycurus

Carex conferta var. lycurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
