Clematis alpina subsp. occidentalis

Clematis alpina subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
