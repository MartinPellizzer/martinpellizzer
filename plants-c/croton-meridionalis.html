Croton meridionalis

Croton meridionalis, commonly known as the Canary Islands croton, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
