Cerastium lazicum

Cerastium lazicum, commonly known as the Lazic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
