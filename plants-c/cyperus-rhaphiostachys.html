Cyperus rhaphiostachys

Cyperus rhaphiostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed or aquatic habitats.

Cyperus rhaphiostachys is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
