Conospermum longifolium subsp. angustifolium

Conospermum longifolium subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved conospermum, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal floral display.
