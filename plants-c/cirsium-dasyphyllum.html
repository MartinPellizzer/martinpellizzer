Cirsium dasyphyllum

Cirsium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its fuzzy, bristly foliage serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Cirsium dasyphyllum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
