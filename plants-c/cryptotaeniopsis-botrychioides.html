Cryptotaeniopsis botrychioides

Cryptotaeniopsis botrychioides, commonly known as the woolly mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
