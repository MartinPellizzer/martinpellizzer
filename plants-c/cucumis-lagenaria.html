Cucumis lagenaria

Cucumis lagenaria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant produces large, hollow fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length, often used as containers or for culinary purposes. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
