Cirsium creticum subsp. dictaeum

Cirsium creticum subsp.

dictaeum, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal areas or mountainous regions. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
