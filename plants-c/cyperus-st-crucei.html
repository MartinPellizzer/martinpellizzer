Cyperus st-crucei

Cyperus st-crucei, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, with each spikelet containing several tiny flowers. Cyperus st-crucei is often found in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
