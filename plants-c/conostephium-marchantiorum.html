Conostephium marchantiorum

Conostephium marchantiorum, commonly known as the desert thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny pods that protect them until favorable conditions for germination arise.

Conostephium marchantiorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
