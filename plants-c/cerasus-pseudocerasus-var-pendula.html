Cerasus pseudocerasus var. pendula

Cerasus pseudocerasus var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus species native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with a spreading, weeping habit that makes it visually striking. Its leaves are oval-shaped, dark green, and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces due to its aesthetic appeal.
