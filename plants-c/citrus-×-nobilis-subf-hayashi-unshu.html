Citrus × nobilis subf. hayashi-unshu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

hayashi-unshu, commonly known as the Unshu mandarin, is a citrus hybrid native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a sweet, juicy pulp.

The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The flowers are fragrant and white, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
