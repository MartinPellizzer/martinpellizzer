Crepis muhlisii

Crepis muhlisii, commonly known as Muhl's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive lobed shape, while its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Crepis muhlisii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, arid climates.
