Caltha introloba var. obtusa

Caltha introloba var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and glossy, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
