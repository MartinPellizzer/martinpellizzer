Cyphia sylvatica var. graminea

Cyphia sylvatica var. graminea, commonly known as the grassy Cyphia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
