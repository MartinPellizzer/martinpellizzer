Cyperus mwinilungensis

Cyperus mwinilungensis, commonly known as the Mwinilungensis flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering structures, called spikelets, are small and clustered, producing tiny flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Cyperus mwinilungensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
