Corydalis angustifolia var. ramosa

Corydalis angustifolia var. ramosa, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
