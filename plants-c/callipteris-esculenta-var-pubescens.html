Callipteris esculenta var. pubescens

Callipteris esculenta var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dwarf palm, is a compact, cold-hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, feather-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further enhance its appeal as a landscape plant.
