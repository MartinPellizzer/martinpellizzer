Chromolaena perglabra

Chromolaena perglabra, commonly known as roughleaf tung, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Chromolaena perglabra is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
