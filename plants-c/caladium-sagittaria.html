Caladium sagittaria

Caladium sagittaria, commonly known as arrowhead plant, is a tropical aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns, making it visually striking. The plant thrives in shallow water and moist soil, often found in wetlands and along stream edges. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, contributing to its ecological role.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by oval-shaped berries.
