Ceropegia lawii var. panchganiensis

Ceropegia lawii var. panchganiensis, commonly known as the Panchgani ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its roots are succulent, adapted for water storage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
