Crotalaria perbracteolata

Crotalaria perbracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and its pods are slender, cylindrical, and contain several seeds.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
