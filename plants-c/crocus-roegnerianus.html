Crocus roegnerianus

Crocus roegnerianus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn to early winter. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
