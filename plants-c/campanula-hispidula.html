Campanula hispidula

Campanula hispidula, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a similar fuzzy texture.

Campanula hispidula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.
