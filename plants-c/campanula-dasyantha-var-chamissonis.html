Campanula dasyantha var. chamissonis

Campanula dasyantha var. chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
