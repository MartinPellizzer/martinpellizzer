Clonodia verrucosa

Clonodia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-hair fern, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with erect, branched stems covered in dense, velvety hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
