Cyathea kermadecensis

Cyathea kermadecensis, commonly known as the Kermadec tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance in its natural habitat.

Cyathea kermadecensis plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem, providing shelter and habitat for various native species.
