Cereus pentlandii var. janthinus

Cereus pentlandii var. janthinus, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear clusters of bright pink or red flowers during the holiday season. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it produces showy, tubular flowers that open at night.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and unique appearance.
