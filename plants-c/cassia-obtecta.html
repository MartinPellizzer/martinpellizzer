Cassia obtecta

Cassia obtecta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous yellow, fragrant flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of elliptical, glossy leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light brown, while the seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature.

Cassia obtecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
