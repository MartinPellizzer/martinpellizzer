Croton rubricapitirupis var. macrophyllus

Croton rubricapitirupis var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the red-headed croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking red stems and large, glossy leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variant is distinguished by its larger foliage compared to other croton species, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
