Ciminalis ochroleuca

Ciminalis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can reach up to 15 cm in length.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
