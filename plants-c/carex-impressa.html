Carex impressa

Carex impressa, commonly known as the impressed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-angled seed heads.

Carex impressa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
