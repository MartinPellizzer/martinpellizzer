Chrysanthemum maritimum var. inodorum

Chrysanthemum maritimum var. inodorum, commonly known as the scentless sea chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. This variety is notable for its lack of a strong fragrance, distinguishing it from other chrysanthemum species. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in maritime climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
