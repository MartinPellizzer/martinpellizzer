Caesalpinia murifructa

Caesalpinia murifructa, commonly known as the African flame bean, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright red or orange pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in panicles, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid conditions.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
