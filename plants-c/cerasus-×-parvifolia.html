Cerasus × parvifolia

Cerasus × parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
