Colutea persica

Colutea persica, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, including parts of Iran and Turkey. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
