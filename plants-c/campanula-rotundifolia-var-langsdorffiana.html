Campanula rotundifolia var. langsdorffiana

Campanula rotundifolia var. langsdorffiana, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in woodland or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

The species is also noted for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
