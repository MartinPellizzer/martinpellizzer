Cleome tenella var. madagascariensis

Cleome tenella var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with five petals that are often adorned with a central dark spot.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
