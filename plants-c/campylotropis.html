Campylotropis

Campylotropis, commonly known as the bottlebrush acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, which give the genus its common name. Campylotropis species are typically shrubs or small trees native to regions of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas. They have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
