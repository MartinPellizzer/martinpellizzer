Convolvulus holosericeus subsp. macrocalycinus

Convolvulus holosericeus subsp.

macrocalycinus, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
