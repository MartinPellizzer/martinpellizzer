Carex lectissima

Carex lectissima, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex lectissima is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
