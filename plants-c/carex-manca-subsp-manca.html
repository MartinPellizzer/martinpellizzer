Carex manca subsp. manca

Carex manca subsp.

manca, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
