Chamaefistula quadrifoliolata

Chamaefistula quadrifoliolata, commonly known as the four-leafed boxwood, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically four-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it suitable for hedges and topiary.
