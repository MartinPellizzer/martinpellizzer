Campanula saonissia

Campanula saonissia, commonly known as the Saonissia bellflower, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in countries like Italy and France. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, giving them a soft texture.

Campanula saonissia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
