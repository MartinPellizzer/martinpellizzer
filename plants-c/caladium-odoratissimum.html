Caladium odoratissimum

Caladium odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet-scented arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Caladium odoratissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
