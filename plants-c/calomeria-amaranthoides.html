Calomeria amaranthoides

Calomeria amaranthoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures similar to dandelion seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Calomeria amaranthoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
