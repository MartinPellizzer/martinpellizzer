Carex ventosa

Carex ventosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex ventosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
