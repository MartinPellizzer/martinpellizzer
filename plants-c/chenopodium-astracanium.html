Chenopodium astracanium

Chenopodium astracanium, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Chenopodium astracanium is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
