Cerasus semperflorens var. pendula-aurea

Cerasus semperflorens var. pendula-aurea, commonly known as the golden weeping cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree characterized by its weeping growth habit and golden-yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of small, fragrant blossoms in early spring. The leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the bright flowers. It is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6-8.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
