Cucumis melo subsp. flexuosus

Cucumis melo subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, ribbed fruits with a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant has palmate leaves and yellow flowers typical of the genus Cucumis. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
