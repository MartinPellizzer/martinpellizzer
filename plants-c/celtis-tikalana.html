Celtis tikalana

Celtis tikalana, commonly known as the Tikalana hackberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, round berries. Celtis tikalana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
