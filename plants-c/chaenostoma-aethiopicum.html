Chaenostoma aethiopicum

Chaenostoma aethiopicum, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its ability to thrive in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wetlands effectively.

Chaenostoma aethiopicum is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
