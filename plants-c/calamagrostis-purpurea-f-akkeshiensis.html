Calamagrostis purpurea f. akkeshiensis

Calamagrostis purpurea f. akkeshiensis, commonly known as the Akkeshi purple reed, is a cold-hardy grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and striking purple-tinted foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy or boggy areas, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing fluffy, silver-green panicles that attract pollinators.

This ornamental grass is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife in wetland environments.
