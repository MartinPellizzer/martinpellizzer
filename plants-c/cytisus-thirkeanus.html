Cytisus thirkeanus

Cytisus thirkeanus, commonly known as Thirke's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Thirke's broom is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
