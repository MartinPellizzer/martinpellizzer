Cerasus padifolia

Cerasus padifolia, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or padifolia cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Cerasus padifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in certain regions of East Asia.
