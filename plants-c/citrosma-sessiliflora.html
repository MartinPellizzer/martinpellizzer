Citrosma sessiliflora

Citrosma sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower citrosma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

The botanical characteristics of Citrosma sessiliflora make it an interesting subject for study in plant taxonomy and ecology.
