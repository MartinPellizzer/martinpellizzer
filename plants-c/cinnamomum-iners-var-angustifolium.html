Cinnamomum iners var. angustifolium

Cinnamomum iners var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cinnamon, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly cultivated than other cinnamon species.
