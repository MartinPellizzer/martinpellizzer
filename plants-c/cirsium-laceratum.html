Cirsium laceratum

Cirsium laceratum, commonly known as the bristly thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and lobed, serrated leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Cirsium laceratum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
