Campanula asperifolia

Campanula asperifolia, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
