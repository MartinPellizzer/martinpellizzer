Crocus sylvester

Crocus sylvester, commonly known as the wood crocus, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm.

The wood crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
