Crassula venezuelensis

Crassula venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Venezuela.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or mountainous regions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and succulent enthusiasts alike.
