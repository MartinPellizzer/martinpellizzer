Clematis chrysantha var. paucidentata

Clematis chrysantha var. paucidentata, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
