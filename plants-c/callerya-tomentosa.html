Callerya tomentosa

Callerya tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly callerya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features dense, woolly hair covering its stems and young leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white.

Callerya tomentosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and floral display.
