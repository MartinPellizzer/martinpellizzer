Chlorocyathus welwitschii

Chlorocyathus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's green cup, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, fleshy stems that are typically green in color, which help it store water in its arid environment. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
