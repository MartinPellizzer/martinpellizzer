Convolvulus equitans

Convolvulus equitans, commonly known as the upright bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows in a sprawling, vine-like manner, often climbing or trailing over other plants. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white, with five lobes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Convolvulus equitans is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
