Catasetum × splendens var. album

Catasetum × splendens var. album, commonly known as the white catasetum, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers, which often have a unique, intricate pattern resembling a crown or halo. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several showy flowers.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its elegance and the complexity of its floral structure.
