Calathea truncata

Calathea truncata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves with contrasting dark and light green patterns. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and vibrant coloration.

Calathea truncata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
