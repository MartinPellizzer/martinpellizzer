Citrus × otachibana

Citrus × otachibana, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or kinkan, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, fragrant peel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its high vitamin C content and aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
