Carex hindsii

Carex hindsii, commonly known as Hinds' sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. This perennial herb typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
