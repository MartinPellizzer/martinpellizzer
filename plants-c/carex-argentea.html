Carex argentea

Carex argentea, commonly known as silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs on the leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex argentea produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its flowering stems.
