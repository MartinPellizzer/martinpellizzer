Carex siciformis

Carex siciformis, commonly known as the Sicilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

Carex siciformis produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by three-sided, spike-like seed heads.
