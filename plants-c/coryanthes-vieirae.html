Coryanthes vieirae

Coryanthes vieirae, commonly known as the Vieira's hooded orchid, is a rare and striking tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that resemble a hooded structure, with a distinctive spadix and a modified lip. The plant belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is known for its complex pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species. Its vibrant colors and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
