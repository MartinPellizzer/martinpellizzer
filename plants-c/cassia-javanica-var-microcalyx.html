Cassia javanica var. microcalyx

Cassia javanica var. microcalyx, commonly known as dwarf cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, flowering shrub.
