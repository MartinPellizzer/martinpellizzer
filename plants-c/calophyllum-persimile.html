Calophyllum persimile

Calophyllum persimile, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
