Callicarpa rhynchophylla

Callicarpa rhynchophylla, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
