Crataegus punctata var. nitidula

Crataegus punctata var. nitidula, commonly known as the smooth thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and small, thorned branches. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely serrated margins and a glossy green upper surface. It produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
