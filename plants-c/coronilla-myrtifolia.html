Coronilla myrtifolia

Coronilla myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive myrtle-like fragrance, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is often used in landscaping and soil restoration projects.
