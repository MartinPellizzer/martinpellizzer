Cyperus esculentus f. macrostachyus

Cyperus esculentus f. macrostachyus, commonly known as the giant sedges or swamp cyperus, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, triangular stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to waterlogged soils.
