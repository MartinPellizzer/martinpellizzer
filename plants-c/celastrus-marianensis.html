Celastrus marianensis

Celastrus marianensis, commonly known as the Marian's staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its botanical features include smooth, green bark that becomes fissured with age and small, scale-like leaves.

Celastrus marianensis is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
