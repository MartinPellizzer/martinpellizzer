Cuphea strigulosa subsp. opaca

Cuphea strigulosa subsp.

opaca, commonly known as the opaca cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
