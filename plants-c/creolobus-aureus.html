Creolobus aureus

Creolobus aureus, commonly known as the golden creolobus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
