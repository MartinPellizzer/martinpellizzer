Carex popovii subsp. popovii

Carex popovii subsp.

popovii, commonly known as Popov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along water bodies. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Popov's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
