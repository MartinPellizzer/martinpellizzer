Chimonobambusa baviensis

Chimonobambusa baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae, native to Vietnam.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clumps, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by small, three-angled fruits.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in its native habitat.
