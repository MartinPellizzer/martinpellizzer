Colletia paradoxa

Colletia paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that forms dense mats of fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its thick, succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Colletia paradoxa is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
