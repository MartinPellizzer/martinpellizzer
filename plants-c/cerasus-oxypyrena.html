Cerasus oxypyrena

Cerasus oxypyrena, commonly known as the Pyrenean cherry, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
