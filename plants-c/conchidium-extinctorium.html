Conchidium extinctorium

Conchidium extinctorium, commonly known as the flame-dampening fern, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its specialized leaf structures.

Its fronds are covered in a dense layer of fine, waxy hairs that help absorb and retain moisture, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by nocturnal insects, a trait that sets it apart from many other ferns. Its seeds are encased in a hard, protective shell that prevents desiccation until the right conditions for germination are met.

Due to its limited range and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
