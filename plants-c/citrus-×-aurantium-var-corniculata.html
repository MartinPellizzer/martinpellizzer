Citrus × aurantium var. corniculata

Citrus × aurantium var. corniculata, commonly known as the pomelo or sometimes called the Chinese pomelo, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit that can weigh up to 10 pounds, with a pale yellow to greenish color. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The fruit is used for both culinary and medicinal purposes, and its peel is often used in traditional remedies.
