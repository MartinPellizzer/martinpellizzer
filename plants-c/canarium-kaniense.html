Canarium kaniense

Canarium kaniense, commonly known as the Kani Island candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are used in traditional practices and have economic value in local communities.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in forest ecosystems.
