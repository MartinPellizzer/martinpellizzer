Camaridium suaveolens

Camaridium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented camaridium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique fragrance and visual appeal, it is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
