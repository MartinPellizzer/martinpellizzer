Camellia bugiamapensis

Camellia bugiamapensis, commonly known as the Bugian camellia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant belongs to the Theaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
