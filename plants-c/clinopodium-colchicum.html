Clinopodium colchicum

Clinopodium colchicum, commonly known as wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers that are pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

Clinopodium colchicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
