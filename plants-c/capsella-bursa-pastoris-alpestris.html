Capsella bursa-pastoris alpestris

Capsella bursa-pastoris alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It has a low-growing habit with hairy leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in mountainous areas where it thrives in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
