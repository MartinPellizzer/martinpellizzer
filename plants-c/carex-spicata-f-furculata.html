Carex spicata f. furculata

Carex spicata f. furculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a distinct midrib, and the plant produces spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
