Citrus khasya

Citrus khasya, commonly known as the wild lemon or khasya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western Himalayas.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a sour, aromatic pulp with a high concentration of citric acid. It is often used in traditional medicine for its digestive and antiseptic properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
