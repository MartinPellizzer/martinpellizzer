Caragana tibetica

Caragana tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan shrub vetch, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and can thrive in poor soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Caragana tibetica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
