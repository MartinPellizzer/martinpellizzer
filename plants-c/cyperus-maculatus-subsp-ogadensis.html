Cyperus maculatus subsp. ogadensis

Cyperus maculatus subsp.

ogadensis, commonly known as the spotted flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonal pools. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, a common feature in the Cyperaceae family.
