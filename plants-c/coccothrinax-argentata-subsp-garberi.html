Coccothrinax argentata subsp. garberi

Coccothrinax argentata subsp.

garberi, commonly known as the Garber's silver palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean. It is a small, slender palm characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and wind-prone environments. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that provide a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical landscapes.
