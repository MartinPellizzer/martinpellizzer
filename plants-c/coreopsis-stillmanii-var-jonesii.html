Coreopsis stillmanii var. jonesii

Coreopsis stillmanii var. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' coreopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 feet tall, adorned with bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
