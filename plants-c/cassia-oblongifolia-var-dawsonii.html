Cassia oblongifolia var. dawsonii

Cassia oblongifolia var. dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's cassia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in elongated racemes. These flowers are followed by oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
