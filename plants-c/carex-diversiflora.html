Carex diversiflora

Carex diversiflora, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense spikes.

Carex diversiflora plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
