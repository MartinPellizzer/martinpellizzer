Carex praecox var. dumetorum

Carex praecox var. dumetorum, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often dark green and arranged in dense clusters. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
