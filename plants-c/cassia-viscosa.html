Cassia viscosa

Cassia viscosa, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with silky, hairy surfaces and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Cassia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
