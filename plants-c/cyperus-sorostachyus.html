Cyperus sorostachyus

Cyperus sorostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus sorostachyus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
