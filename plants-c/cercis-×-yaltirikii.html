Cercis × yaltirikii

Cercis × yaltirikii, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that combines traits from Cercis orientalis and Cercis siliquastrum.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically appearing before the leaves in early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more textured with age.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
