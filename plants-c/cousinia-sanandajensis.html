Cousinia sanandajensis

Cousinia sanandajensis, commonly known as the Sanandaj cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia sanandajensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
