Chamaecyparis decussata

Chamaecyparis decussata, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Hinoki cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that is dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Chamaecyparis decussata is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional Japanese craftsmanship.
