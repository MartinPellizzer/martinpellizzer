Cryptocarya guianensis

Cryptocarya guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cryptocarya guianensis is often found in humid lowland forests and plays a role in local ecosystems.
