Calystegia rosea

Calystegia rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored bindweed, is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a nuisance in agricultural and natural areas.
