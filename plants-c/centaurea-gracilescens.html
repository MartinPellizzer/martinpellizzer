Centaurea gracilescens

Centaurea gracilescens, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.

Centaurea gracilescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
