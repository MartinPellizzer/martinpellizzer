Campanula spatulata f. hirsuta

Campanula spatulata f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "hirsuta," meaning hairy in Latin. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
