Croton macrocarpus

Croton macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are a key feature in its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage.

Due to its size and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for large gardens and landscapes.
