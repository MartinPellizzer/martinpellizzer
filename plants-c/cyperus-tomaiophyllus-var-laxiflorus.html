Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. laxiflorus

Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowers are characteristic of the sedge family.

The variety laxiflorus is distinguished by its loose, open inflorescences compared to the more compact ones of the typical form.
