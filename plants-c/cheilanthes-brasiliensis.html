Cheilanthes brasiliensis

Cheilanthes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in rocky or shaded environments. It has a short, underground rhizome and produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

This fern is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices.
