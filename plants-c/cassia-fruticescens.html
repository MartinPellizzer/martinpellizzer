Cassia fruticescens

Cassia fruticescens, commonly known as flame tree or torchwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia fruticescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
