Castilleja angustifolia var. abbreviata

Castilleja angustifolia var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrowleaf Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its bright red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble paintbrushes. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its roots to absorb nutrients from host plants, making it a partial parasite. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
