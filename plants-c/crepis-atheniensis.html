Crepis atheniensis

Crepis atheniensis, commonly known as the Athens thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in late summer.

Crepis atheniensis is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
