Cosmos montanus

Cosmos montanus, commonly known as the mountain cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces white to pink flowers with prominent yellow centers, which bloom from summer to autumn. Cosmos montanus is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its upright growth habit and delicate appearance make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
