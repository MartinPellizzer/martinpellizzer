Cineraria integrifolia var. minor

Cineraria integrifolia var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue or violet-colored flowers. The leaves are typically broad, lobed, and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often hairy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
