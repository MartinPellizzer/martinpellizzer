Coreopsis senifolia

Coreopsis senifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Coreopsis senifolia is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
