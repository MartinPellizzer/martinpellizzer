Coddia rudis

Coddia rudis, commonly known as the slender coddia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically in shades of pink or purple.

Coddia rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
