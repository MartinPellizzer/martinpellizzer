Cestrum obscurum

Cestrum obscurum, commonly known as dark trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. These flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.
