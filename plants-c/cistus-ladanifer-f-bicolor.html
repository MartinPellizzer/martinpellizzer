Cistus ladanifer f. bicolor

Cistus ladanifer f. bicolor, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its fragrant, resinous leaves and flowers contribute to its ecological and horticultural value.

The variety bicolor is distinguished by its two-toned flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal.
