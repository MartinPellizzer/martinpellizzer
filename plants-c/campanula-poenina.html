Campanula poenina

Campanula poenina, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody at the base.

Campanula poenina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
