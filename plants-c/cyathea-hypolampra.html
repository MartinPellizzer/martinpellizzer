Cyathea hypolampra

Cyathea hypolampra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fibrous root system, which helps stabilize the plant in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea hypolampra is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
