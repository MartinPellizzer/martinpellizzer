Crocanthemum rosmarinifolium

Crocanthemum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
