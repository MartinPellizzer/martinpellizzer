Chlorocardium rodiei

Chlorocardium rodiei, commonly known as the red-veined daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with deep red veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent-like stems and leaves.
