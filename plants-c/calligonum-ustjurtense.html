Calligonum ustjurtense

Calligonum ustjurtense, commonly known as the Central Asian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Calligonum ustjurtense is known for its resilience to drought and extreme temperatures, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
