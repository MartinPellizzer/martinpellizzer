Campanula rotundifolia var. pennina

Campanula rotundifolia var. pennina, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
