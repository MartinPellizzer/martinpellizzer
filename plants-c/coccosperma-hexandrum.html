Coccosperma hexandrum

Coccosperma hexandrum, commonly known as the dwarf fan palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, fan-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and deeply divided. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Coccosperma hexandrum is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
