Chlorogalum pomeridianum var. divaricatum

Chlorogalum pomeridianum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the desert lily or variegated milkplant, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking, waxy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of gray-green, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its stems are fibrous and often branched, supporting the flowering stalks.

This variety is distinguished by its variegated leaf patterns and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in xeriscapic gardens.
