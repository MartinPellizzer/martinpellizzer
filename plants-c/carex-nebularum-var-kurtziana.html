Carex nebularum var. kurtziana

Carex nebularum var. kurtziana, commonly known as the Kurtz's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
