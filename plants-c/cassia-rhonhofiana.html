Cassia rhonhofiana

Cassia rhonhofiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically followed by long, slender pods. Cassia rhonhofiana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
