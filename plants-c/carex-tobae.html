Carex tobae

Carex tobae, commonly known as Toba sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

Carex tobae produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in clusters along the stem.
