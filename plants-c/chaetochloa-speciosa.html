Chaetochloa speciosa

Chaetochloa speciosa, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its silky texture.

Chaetochloa speciosa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
