Cyathea atrox

Cyathea atrox, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and robust, supporting its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands.

Cyathea atrox plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native ecosystems.
