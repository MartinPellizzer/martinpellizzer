Coursetia insomniifolia

Coursetia insomniifolia, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
