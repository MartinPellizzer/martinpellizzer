Codonoboea kompsoboea

Codonoboea kompsoboea, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
