Centropogon calochlamys

Centropogon calochlamys, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with elongated, tubular flowers that are often adorned with contrasting markings. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
