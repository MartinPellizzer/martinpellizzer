Citrus × dimorphocarpa

Citrus × dimorphocarpa, commonly known as the rough lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originates from a cross between the citron and the mandarin. The plant is characterized by its rough, thornless bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is typically small, yellow, and has a thick, bitter rind.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of citric acid.
