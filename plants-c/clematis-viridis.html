Clematis viridis

Clematis viridis, commonly known as green clematis, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
