Clypeola jonthlaspi subsp. macrocarpa

Clypeola jonthlaspi subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited clypeola, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its robust, herbaceous growth habit and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a glandular underside.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more inflated fruits compared to other members of the species.
