Calceolaria glauca

Calceolaria glauca, commonly known as the blue calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet spurred petals, which are highly specialized for attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria glauca is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
