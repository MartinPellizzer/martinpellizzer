Croton lombardianus

Croton lombardianus, commonly known as the Lombardian croton, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Italy.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Croton lombardianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
