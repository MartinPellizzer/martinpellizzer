Copiapoa pepiniana var. fiedleriana

Copiapoa pepiniana var. fiedleriana, commonly known as the Fiedler's cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a ribbed stem with prominent areoles and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its spines are typically white or pale in color, providing some shade and protection from intense sunlight.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
