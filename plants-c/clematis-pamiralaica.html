Clematis pamiralaica

Clematis pamiralaica, commonly known as the Pamir Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
