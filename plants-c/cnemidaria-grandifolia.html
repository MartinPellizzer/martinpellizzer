Cnemidaria grandifolia

Cnemidaria grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fronds that are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cnemidaria grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
