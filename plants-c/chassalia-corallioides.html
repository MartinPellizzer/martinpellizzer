Chassalia corallioides

Chassalia corallioides, commonly known as the coral-like chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, branching inflorescence that resembles coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Chassalia corallioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
