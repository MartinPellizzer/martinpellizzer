Carex pseudoatrata

Carex pseudoatrata, commonly known as the dark fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces berries that are important for wildlife.

Carex pseudoatrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
