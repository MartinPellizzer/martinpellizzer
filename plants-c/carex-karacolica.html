Carex karacolica

Carex karacolica, commonly known as the Karacolica sedge, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

Carex karacolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
