Cytisus georgievii

Cytisus georgievii, commonly known as the Georgian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, pinnate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
