Cordula × frankeana

Cordula × frankeana, commonly known as the hybrid fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is a hybrid between Cordula lutea and Cordula quinquangularis. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
