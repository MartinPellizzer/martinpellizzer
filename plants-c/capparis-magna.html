Capparis magna

Capparis magna, commonly known as the giant caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis magna is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
