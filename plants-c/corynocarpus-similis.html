Corynocarpus similis

Corynocarpus similis, commonly known as the New Zealand honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
