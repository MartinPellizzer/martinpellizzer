Cyperus melanostachyus var. caespitosus

Cyperus melanostachyus var. caespitosus, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark, almost black, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
