Condalia parryi var. microphylla

Condalia parryi var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
