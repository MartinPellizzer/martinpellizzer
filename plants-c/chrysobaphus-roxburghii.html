Chrysobaphus roxburghii

Chrysobaphus roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its habitat. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysobaphus roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
