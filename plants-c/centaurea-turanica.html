Centaurea turanica

Centaurea turanica, commonly known as the Turanian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae.

Centaurea turanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
