Cyanus reuterianus

Cyanus reuterianus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Cyanus reuterianus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
