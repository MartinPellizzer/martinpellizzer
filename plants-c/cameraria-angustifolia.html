Cameraria angustifolia

Cameraria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed leafminer, is a species of moth whose larvae are known for feeding on the leaves of various plants.

The plant itself is not a flowering plant but rather a type of leafminer, meaning its larvae cause characteristic mines within the leaves of host plants. It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid environments. The botanical significance of Cameraria angustifolia lies in its role as a pest in agricultural and horticultural settings.

Its life cycle and feeding habits have been studied for their impact on plant health and ecosystem dynamics.
