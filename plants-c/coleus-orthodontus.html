Coleus orthodontus

Coleus orthodontus, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, velvety leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Coleus orthodontus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
