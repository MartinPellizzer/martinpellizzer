Centaurea cineraria subsp. cinerea

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and resilience to coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The subspecies cinerea is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other subspecies of Centaurea cineraria.
