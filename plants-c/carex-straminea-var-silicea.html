Carex straminea var. silicea

Carex straminea var. silicea, commonly known as the silicea sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
