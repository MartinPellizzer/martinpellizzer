Chenopodium dubium

Chenopodium dubium, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or rhombic in shape, and small, greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, due to its adaptability and rapid growth. It produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution.

Chenopodium dubium is also known for its nutritional value, as its leaves can be consumed as a leafy green vegetable.
