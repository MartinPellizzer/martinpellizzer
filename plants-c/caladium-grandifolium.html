Caladium grandifolium

Caladium grandifolium, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, often with striking variegation in shades of white, red, and green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near waterways. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, giving it the common name, and it is popular as an ornamental plant in warm climates.

Caladium grandifolium is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like philodendrons and peace lilies.
