Cladium japonicum

Cladium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height, with a dense root system that helps stabilize soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Cladium japonicum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
