Centaurea paniculata proles leucophaea

Centaurea paniculata proles leucophaea, commonly known as the white-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems covered in coarse, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy, fluffy flower heads composed of pale blue to white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and a distinctive whitish underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.
