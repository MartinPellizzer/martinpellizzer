Cavendishia longiflora

Cavendishia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stems and distinctive, elongated flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
