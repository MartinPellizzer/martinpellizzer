Crataegus oxyacantha var. apiifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. apiifolia, commonly known as the leafy hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed, lobed leaves that resemble those of the carrot plant, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically pinkish-white and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
