Campanula rotundifolia subsp. pedemontana

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pedemontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
