Cineraria tenella

Cineraria tenella, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Cineraria tenella prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
