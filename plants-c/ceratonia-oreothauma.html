Ceratonia oreothauma

Ceratonia oreothauma, commonly known as the mountain carob, is a species of leguminous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It plays a role in preventing soil erosion due to its extensive root system.

The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, contributing to its ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
