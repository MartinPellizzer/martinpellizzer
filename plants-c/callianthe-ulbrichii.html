Callianthe ulbrichii

Callianthe ulbrichii, commonly known as the Ulbrich's sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray from the ocean. It is often found growing in dune environments, contributing to the stabilization of coastal ecosystems.

Callianthe ulbrichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
