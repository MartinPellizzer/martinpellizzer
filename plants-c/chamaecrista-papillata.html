Chamaecrista papillata

Chamaecrista papillata, commonly known as the hairy crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, papillate (bump-like) structures on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chamaecrista papillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
