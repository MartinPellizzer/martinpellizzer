Cynodon dactylon var. arcuatus

Cynodon dactylon var. arcuatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or African carpet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is highly adaptable to various soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form a dense cover make it both a useful forage crop and a persistent weed.
