Calandrinia potentilloides var. minor

Calandrinia potentilloides var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent structure. Its small, clustered flowers bloom throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive with minimal water.
