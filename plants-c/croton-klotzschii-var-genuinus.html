Croton klotzschii var. genuinus

Croton klotzschii var. genuinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage that displays shades of yellow, green, and red, making it highly sought after for indoor and outdoor gardens. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often striking members. It typically features small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the genus.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, thriving in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
