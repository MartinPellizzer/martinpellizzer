Cacoucia longispicata

Cacoucia longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces elongated, spiny pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Cacoucia longispicata is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive flowers and unique spines.
