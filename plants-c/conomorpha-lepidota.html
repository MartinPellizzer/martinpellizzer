Conomorpha lepidota

Conomorpha lepidota, commonly known as the velvet leaf tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
