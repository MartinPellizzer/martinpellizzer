Calliandra tehuantepecensis

Calliandra tehuantepecensis, commonly known as the Mexican peacock flower, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Calliandra tehuantepecensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
