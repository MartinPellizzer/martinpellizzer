Cyathea bilineata

Cyathea bilineata, commonly known as the two-lined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a smooth, grayish-brown trunk and fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea bilineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
