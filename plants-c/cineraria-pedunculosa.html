Cineraria pedunculosa

Cineraria pedunculosa, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has basal rosettes of silver-gray leaves and tall, spindly stems that support its showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Cineraria pedunculosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
