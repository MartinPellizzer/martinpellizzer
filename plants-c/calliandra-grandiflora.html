Calliandra grandiflora

Calliandra grandiflora, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bract-like flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra grandiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
