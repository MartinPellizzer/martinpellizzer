Colona poilanei

Colona poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the dense forests of its native habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.
