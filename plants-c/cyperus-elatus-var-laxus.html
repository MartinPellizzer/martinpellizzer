Cyperus elatus var. laxus

Cyperus elatus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to submerged soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity, making it a common species in aquatic environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted for waterlogged conditions, with rhizomes that help in spreading and stabilizing the soil.
