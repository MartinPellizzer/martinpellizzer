Crataegus glandulosa var. rotundifolia

Crataegus glandulosa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its ovate to round leaves with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
