Chondrosea rosella

Chondrosea rosella, commonly known as the Rosella Sea Mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and broad, fleshy leaves adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

Chondrosea rosella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
