Cordia fawcettii

Cordia fawcettii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Cordia fawcettii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
