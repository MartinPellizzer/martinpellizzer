Cyperus suaveolens

Cyperus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet grass or sweet sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often pale green or brown. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus suaveolens is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has a pleasant, aromatic scent.
