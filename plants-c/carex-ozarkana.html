Carex ozarkana

Carex ozarkana, commonly known as Ozark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring.

Ozark sedge is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats.
