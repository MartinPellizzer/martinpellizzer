Cestrum potaliifolium

Cestrum potaliifolium, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

Cestrum potaliifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
