Conostegia rhodopetala

Conostegia rhodopetala, commonly known as the scarlet beauty or red-leaved acanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Conostegia rhodopetala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
