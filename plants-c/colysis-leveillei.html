Colysis leveillei

Colysis leveillei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the dry season, producing clusters of pale yellow to white flowers with intricate patterns. Colysis leveillei thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
