Cottendorfia brachyphylla

Cottendorfia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cottendorfia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its short, upright stems. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of small, greenish-yellow blooms that appear in late spring.

Cottendorfia brachyphylla is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
