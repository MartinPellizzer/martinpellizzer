Carex bulgarica

Carex bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, usually brown or greenish flowers. Native to regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
