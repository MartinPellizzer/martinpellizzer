Campanula malatyaensis

Campanula malatyaensis, commonly known as Malatya bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
