Corypha microclada

Corypha microclada, commonly known as the small-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, spiny fruits. Corypha microclada is typically found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
