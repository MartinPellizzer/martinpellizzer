Crataegus khatamsazae

Crataegus khatamsazae, commonly known as Persian hawthorn, is a species of shrub native to Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
