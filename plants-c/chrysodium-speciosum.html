Chrysodium speciosum

Chrysodium speciosum, commonly known as the showy chrysodium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chrysodium speciosum is often found in moist, shaded environments within forest undergrowth.
