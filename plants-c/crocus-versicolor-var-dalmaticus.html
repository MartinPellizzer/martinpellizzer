Crocus versicolor var. dalmaticus

Crocus versicolor var. dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with petals that range from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
