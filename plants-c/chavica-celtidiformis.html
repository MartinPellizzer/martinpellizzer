Chavica celtidiformis

Chavica celtidiformis, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow up to 12 inches in length, adding to its ornamental value.

Chavica celtidiformis is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
