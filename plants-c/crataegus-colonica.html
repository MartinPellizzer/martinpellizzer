Crataegus colonica

Crataegus colonica, commonly known as the Russian hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges.

Crataegus colonica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
