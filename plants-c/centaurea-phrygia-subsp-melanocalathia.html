Centaurea phrygia subsp. melanocalathia

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

melanocalathia, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy foliage.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymb-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, serrated edge.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
