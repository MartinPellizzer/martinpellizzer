Cyperus elegans subsp. rubiginosus

Cyperus elegans subsp.

rubiginosus, commonly known as the red-stem sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often reddish-stemmed stalks.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in swamps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Cyperus elegans subsp.

rubiginosus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
