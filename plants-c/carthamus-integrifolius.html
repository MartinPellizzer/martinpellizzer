Carthamus integrifolius

Carthamus integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are entire and lack teeth. The plant produces striking yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pink to red ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant species.
