Commelina boissieriana

Commelina boissieriana, commonly known as the slender dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

The flowers bloom during the day and close at night, a trait common among many dayflower species.
