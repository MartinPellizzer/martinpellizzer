Cardamine californica var. integrifolia

Cardamine californica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the California watercress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with smooth, lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, though it is less common in modern cuisine compared to other watercress varieties.
