Centaurea isaurica

Centaurea isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Centaurea isaurica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
