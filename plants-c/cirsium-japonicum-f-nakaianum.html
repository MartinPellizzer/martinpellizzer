Cirsium japonicum f. nakaianum

Cirsium japonicum f. nakaianum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle or Nakaianum thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or grassy areas.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies and garden cultivation.
