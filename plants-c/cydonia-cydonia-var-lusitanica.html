Cydonia cydonia var. lusitanica

Cydonia cydonia var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Spanish quince, is a variety of the common quince native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellow flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically oval, with a rough, pale-yellow skin that turns orange when ripe. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is used in culinary applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
