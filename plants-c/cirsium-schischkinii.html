Cirsium schischkinii

Cirsium schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
