Cynanchica capitellata

Cynanchica capitellata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Cynanchica capitellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
