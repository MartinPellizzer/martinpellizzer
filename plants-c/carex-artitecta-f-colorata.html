Carex artitecta f. colorata

Carex artitecta f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that displays vibrant, colorful inflorescences, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for water gardens or boggy areas in landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and upright, forming a compact, decorative mound that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
