Cardamine californica f. microphylla

Cardamine californica f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf canadian waterleaf, is a variant of the Canadian waterleaf species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its foliage is often densely packed, forming a low, mat-like growth habit.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical form of Cardamine californica.
