Crotalaria formosana

Crotalaria formosana, commonly known as the Formosa cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets. Crotalaria formosana is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
