Coronilla repanda subsp. dura

Coronilla repanda subsp.

dura, commonly known as the hard or robust coronilla, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
