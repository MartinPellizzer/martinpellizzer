Cydonia vulgaris var. algeriensis

Cydonia vulgaris var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian quince, is a variant of the quince species native to North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and oval, dark green leaves. Its fruit, which matures in late summer, is typically small, yellow, and aromatic.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
