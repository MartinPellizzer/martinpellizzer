Cinnamomum morrenii

Cinnamomum morrenii, commonly known as the Morren's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark, aromatic fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum morrenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its contribution to local biodiversity.
