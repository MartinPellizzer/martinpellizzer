Cotyliscus

Cotyliscus, commonly known as the "cottonseed plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and palmately lobed leaves. Cotyliscus produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in the production of cottonseed oil and animal feed.
