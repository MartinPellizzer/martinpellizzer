Cattleya × floribunda

Cattleya × floribunda, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors including white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya × floribunda is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
