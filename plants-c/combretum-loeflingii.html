Combretum loeflingii

Combretum loeflingii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, producing clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a notable species in the Combretaceae family.
