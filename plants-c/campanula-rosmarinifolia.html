Campanula rosmarinifolia

Campanula rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant features aromatic leaves that resemble rosemary, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
