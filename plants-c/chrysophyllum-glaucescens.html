Chrysophyllum glaucescens

Chrysophyllum glaucescens, commonly known as the glossy apple or white sapote, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
