Carex jungenda

Carex jungenda, commonly known as the Jungend's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist or wet environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Carex jungenda is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
