Chaenactis alpina var. leucopsis

Chaenactis alpina var. leucopsis, commonly known as the white ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant is adapted to alpine environments, featuring a low-growing, mat-forming structure to withstand harsh weather conditions. Its white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety leucopsis is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other varieties of Chaenactis alpina.
