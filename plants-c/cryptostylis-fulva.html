Cryptostylis fulva

Cryptostylis fulva, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing multiple flowers, each with a distinctive yellow-green lip.

Cryptostylis fulva is known for its ephemeral blooming period, often appearing after rainfall and lasting only a few weeks.
