Cheiranthus × cheiri var. ferrugineus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive rust-colored stems and foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found growing in rocky or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
