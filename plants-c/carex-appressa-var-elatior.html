Carex appressa var. elatior

Carex appressa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
