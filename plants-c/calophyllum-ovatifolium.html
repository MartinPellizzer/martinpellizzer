Calophyllum ovatifolium

Calophyllum ovatifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
