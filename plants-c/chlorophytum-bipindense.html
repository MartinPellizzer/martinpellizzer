Chlorophytum bipindense

Chlorophytum bipindense, commonly known as the African spear plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces long, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Chlorophytum bipindense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
