Cyperus viviparus

Cyperus viviparus, commonly known as the sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. Cyperus viviparus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed fruits.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
