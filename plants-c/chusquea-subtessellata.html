Chusquea subtessellata

Chusquea subtessellata, commonly known as the serrate common bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and dense, sword-shaped leaves. The plant features a thick, rhizomatous root system that allows it to form extensive colonies. Its culms are typically slender and have a distinctive, serrated appearance along the edges.

Chusquea subtessellata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
