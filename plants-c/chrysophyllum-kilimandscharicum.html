Chrysophyllum kilimandscharicum

Chrysophyllum kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional value of its fruit.
