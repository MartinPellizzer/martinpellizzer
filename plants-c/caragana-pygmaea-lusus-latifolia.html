Caragana pygmaea lusus latifolia

Caragana pygmaea lusus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dwarf shrub, is a low-growing, deciduous shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, compound leaves that are broad and oval in shape, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental gardening in dry climates.
