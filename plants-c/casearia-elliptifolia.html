Casearia elliptifolia

Casearia elliptifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
