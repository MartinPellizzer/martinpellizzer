Cuscuta trichostyla

Cuscuta trichostyla, commonly known as the slender dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrition.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that involves germinating on the surface of host plants and then establishing a connection through specialized haustoria. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found on various species of grasses and shrubs. Its rapid growth and lack of roots make it a challenging weed in agricultural and natural settings.

Despite its parasitic nature, Cuscuta trichostyla plays a role in ecological interactions and has been studied for its unique biological adaptations.
