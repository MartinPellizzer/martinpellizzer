Clematis stricta

Clematis stricta, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded location. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb support structures with its strong, twining stems.

Clematis stricta is relatively hardy and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
