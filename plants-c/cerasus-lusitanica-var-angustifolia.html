Cerasus lusitanica var. angustifolia

Cerasus lusitanica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
