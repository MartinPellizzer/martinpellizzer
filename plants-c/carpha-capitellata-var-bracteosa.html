Carpha capitellata var. bracteosa

Carpha capitellata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soil conditions. It has fibrous roots and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive bracts that enclose the flower clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
