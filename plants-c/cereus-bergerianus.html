Cereus bergerianus

Cereus bergerianus, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and clusters of bright, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
