Cnidoscolus tridentifer

Cnidoscolus tridentifer, commonly known as the thorned arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and spines along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its unique morphology and potential culinary uses, it is of interest in both botanical studies and agricultural research.
