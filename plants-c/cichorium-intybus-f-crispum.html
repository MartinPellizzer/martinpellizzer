Cichorium intybus f. crispum

Cichorium intybus f. crispum, commonly known as curly endive, is a cultivar of the chicory plant characterized by its curly, frilly leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste. The leaves are often used in salads or as a cooked vegetable, and the plant can be harvested throughout the growing season.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
