Cayaponia racemosa var. plumieri

Cayaponia racemosa var. plumieri, commonly known as the plumier's cayaponia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
