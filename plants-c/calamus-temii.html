Calamus temii

Calamus temii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking inflorescence. Native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines, it thrives in wet, swampy environments. The plant is valued for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
