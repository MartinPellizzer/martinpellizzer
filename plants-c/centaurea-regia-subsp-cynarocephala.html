Centaurea regia subsp. cynarocephala

Centaurea regia subsp.

cynarocephala, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which resemble a crown, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
