Capsella brittonii

Capsella brittonii, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of four-petaled white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found throughout much of North America. It produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds.

Capsella brittonii is often considered a weed but has ecological and historical significance.
