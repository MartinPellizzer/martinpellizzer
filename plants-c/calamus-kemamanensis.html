Calamus kemamanensis

Calamus kemamanensis, commonly known as the Kemaman cane, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used for weaving. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its rhizome is sometimes utilized in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is often cultivated for both commercial and horticultural purposes.
