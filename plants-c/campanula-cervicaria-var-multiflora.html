Campanula cervicaria var. multiflora

Campanula cervicaria var. multiflora, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in woodland or shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
