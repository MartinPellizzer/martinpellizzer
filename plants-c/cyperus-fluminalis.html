Cyperus fluminalis

Cyperus fluminalis, commonly known as the river flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually erect, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus fluminalis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
