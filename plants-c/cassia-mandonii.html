Cassia mandonii

Cassia mandonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow inflorescences that bloom during the warmer months. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a woody structure with a spreading habit. Cassia mandonii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
