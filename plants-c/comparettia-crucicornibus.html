Comparettia crucicornibus

Comparettia crucicornibus, commonly known as the cross-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that feature a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the lip, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange, making it a visually appealing species for botanical enthusiasts.
