Cirrhopetalum pseudopicturatum

Cirrhopetalum pseudopicturatum, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
