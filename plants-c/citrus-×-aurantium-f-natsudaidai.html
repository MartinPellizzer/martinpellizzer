Citrus × aurantium f. natsudaidai

Citrus × aurantium f. natsudaidai, commonly known as the Japanese bitter orange, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, leathery rind, often used in traditional Japanese medicine. The plant is native to southern China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of various citrus hybrids.
