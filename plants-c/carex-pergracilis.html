Carex pergracilis

Carex pergracilis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to wet or moist environments, it thrives in marshes, swales, and other waterlogged habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters and has a rhizomatous root system.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
