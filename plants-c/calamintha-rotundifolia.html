Calamintha rotundifolia

Calamintha rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like scent.

Calamintha rotundifolia is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
