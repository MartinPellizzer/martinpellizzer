Centaurea sterilis subsp. vankovii

Centaurea sterilis subsp.

vankovii, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces bright blue or purple flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other centaurea species.
