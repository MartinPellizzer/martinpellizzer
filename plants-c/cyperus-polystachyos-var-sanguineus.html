Cyperus polystachyos var. sanguineus

Cyperus polystachyos var. sanguineus, commonly known as the red flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers.

This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
