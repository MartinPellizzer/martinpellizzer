Callista templeana

Callista templeana, commonly known as the Temple's callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Callista templeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
