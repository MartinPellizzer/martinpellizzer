Cyathea underwoodii

Cyathea underwoodii, commonly known as the Underwood's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's upright growth.

Cyathea underwoodii plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
