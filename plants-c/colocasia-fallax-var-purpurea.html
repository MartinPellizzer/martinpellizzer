Colocasia fallax var. purpurea

Colocasia fallax var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that display a vibrant purple hue.

Native to Southeast Asia, this variant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires rich, well-draining soil. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and purple, adding dramatic visual interest to gardens. The plant is a cultivar of Colocasia fallax, which is part of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure.

It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its lush, dramatic foliage rather than for its flowers.
