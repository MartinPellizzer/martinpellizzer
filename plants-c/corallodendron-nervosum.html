Corallodendron nervosum

Corallodendron nervosum, commonly known as the veiny coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red or orange. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its flowers are typically five-petaled. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Corallodendron nervosum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
