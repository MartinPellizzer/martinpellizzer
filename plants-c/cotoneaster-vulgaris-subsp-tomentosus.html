Cotoneaster vulgaris subsp. tomentosus

Cotoneaster vulgaris subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions. Cotoneaster vulgaris subsp.

tomentosus is also valued for its role in providing food and shelter for wildlife.
