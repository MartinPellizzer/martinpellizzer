Canephora madagascariensis

Canephora madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar coffee plant, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely on the stems.

This species is valued for its aromatic and flavorful coffee beans, which are used in both commercial and specialty coffee markets.
