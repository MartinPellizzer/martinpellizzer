Carex pullei

Carex pullei, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along streambanks. Its slender, triangular stems are covered with small, grass-like leaves, and it produces spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex pullei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
