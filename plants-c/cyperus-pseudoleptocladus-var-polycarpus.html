Cyperus pseudoleptocladus var. polycarpus

Cyperus pseudoleptocladus var. polycarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
