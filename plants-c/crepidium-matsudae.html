Crepidium matsudae

Crepidium matsudae, commonly known as the Matsuda's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, arranged in a spiral along a slender stem. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic, forest-floor environment.

Crepidium matsudae is often found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
