Carex atroviridis

Carex atroviridis, commonly known as black-veined sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves with prominent black veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex atroviridis is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
