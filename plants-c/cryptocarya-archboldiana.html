Cryptocarya archboldiana

Cryptocarya archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the wet tropical forests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Its unique morphology and habitat requirements make it a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
