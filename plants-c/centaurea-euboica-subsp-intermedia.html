Centaurea euboica subsp. intermedia

Centaurea euboica subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Euboean knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive purple or red central disk, forming dense, composite inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
