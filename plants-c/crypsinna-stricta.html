Crypsinna stricta

Crypsinna stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf crypton, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Crypsinna stricta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
