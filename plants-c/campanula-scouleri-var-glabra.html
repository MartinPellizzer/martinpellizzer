Campanula scouleri var. glabra

Campanula scouleri var. glabra, commonly known as the Pacific bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
