Codiaeum nevilliae

Codiaeum nevilliae, commonly known as the velvet leaf or neville's croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it highly sought after for horticultural purposes. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant or in landscaped gardens.
