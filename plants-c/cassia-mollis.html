Cassia mollis

Cassia mollis, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Cassia mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
