Carex mochomuensis

Carex mochomuensis, commonly known as the Mochomuensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex mochomuensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
