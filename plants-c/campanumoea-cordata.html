Campanumoea cordata

Campanumoea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bell-like, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
