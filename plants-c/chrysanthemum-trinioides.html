Chrysanthemum trinioides

Chrysanthemum trinioides, commonly known as the Trinidad chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant colors and fragrance.
