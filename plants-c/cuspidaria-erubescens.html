Cuspidaria erubescens

Cuspidaria erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered cuspidaria, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinct reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
