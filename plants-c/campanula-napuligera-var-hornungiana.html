Campanula napuligera var. hornungiana

Campanula napuligera var. hornungiana, commonly known as the Hornung's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine downy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
