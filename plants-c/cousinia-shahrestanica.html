Cousinia shahrestanica

Cousinia shahrestanica, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia shahrestanica is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
