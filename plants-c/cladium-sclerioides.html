Cladium sclerioides

Cladium sclerioides, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland ecosystems in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in basket weaving. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and swamps. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cladium sclerioides plays a vital role in wetland habitats by providing shelter for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
