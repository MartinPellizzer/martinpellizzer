Cicerbita sikkimensis

Cicerbita sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often in rocky or moist soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape typical of the family.

The leaves are usually alternate and may have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
