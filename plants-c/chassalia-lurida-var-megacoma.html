Chassalia lurida var. megacoma

Chassalia lurida var. megacoma, commonly known as the large-flowered chassalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods. It has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces achenes as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
