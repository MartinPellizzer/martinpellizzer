Calendula aegyptiaca subsp. tripterocarpa

Calendula aegyptiaca subsp.

tripterocarpa, commonly known as Egyptian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The subspecies tripterocarpa is distinguished by its three-lobed petals and robust growth habit compared to other calendula varieties.
