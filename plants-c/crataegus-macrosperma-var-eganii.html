Crataegus macrosperma var. eganii

Crataegus macrosperma var. eganii, commonly known as the Egan's hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
