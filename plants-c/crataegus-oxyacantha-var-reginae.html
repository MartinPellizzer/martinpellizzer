Crataegus oxyacantha var. reginae

Crataegus oxyacantha var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's hawthorn, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
