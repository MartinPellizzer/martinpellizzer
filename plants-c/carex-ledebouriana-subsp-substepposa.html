Carex ledebouriana subsp. substepposa

Carex ledebouriana subsp.

substepposa, commonly known as the steppe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps, contributing to its ability to stabilize soil in its native habitat.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and shrubland ecosystems, where it plays a role in maintaining ecological balance.
