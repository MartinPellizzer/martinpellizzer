Centaurea × melanomacroptilon

Centaurea × melanomacroptilon, commonly known as the purple gladiolus, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, daisy-like inflorescences that range in color from purple to pink and white. The plant produces both ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late autumn.
