Convolvulus capensis var. malvifolius

Convolvulus capensis var. malvifolius, commonly known as the Cape morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to grow along supports or other vegetation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
