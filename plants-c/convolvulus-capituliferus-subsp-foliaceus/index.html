Convolvulus Capituliferus Subsp. Foliaceus

Convolvulus capituliferus subsp. foliaceus is a lesser-known yet fascinating member of the bindweed family. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its distinctive foliage features broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble small leaves, hence its subspecific name. The plant produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated in botanical circles and is often overlooked in horticultural discussions.
