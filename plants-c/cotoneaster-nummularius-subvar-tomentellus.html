Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. tomentellus

Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive woolly appearance. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant produces orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This subspecies is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types.
