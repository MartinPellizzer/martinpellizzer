Calophyllum williamsianum

Calophyllum williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams's candlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces yellow-orange flowers followed by woody, ornamental fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
