Coleus phulangkaensis

Coleus phulangkaensis, commonly known as the Phulangkaensis coleus, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, purple, and green. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Coleus phulangkaensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
