Carica rochefortii

Carica rochefortii, commonly known as the Mexican papaya or Rochefort's papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a large, fleshy fruit that is similar in appearance to the common papaya but has a distinct flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
