Cleome densiflora var. pallens

Cleome densiflora var. pallens, commonly known as the pale cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features branching stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow or white flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental for its delicate appearance and is also used in native plant gardens.
