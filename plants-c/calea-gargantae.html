Calea gargantae

Calea gargantae, commonly known as the "Mexican night bloomer," is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. Its flowers are composed of two large, overlapping petals and a central, tubular structure.

Calea gargantae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
