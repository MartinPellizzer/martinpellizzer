Chaptalia monticola

Chaptalia monticola, commonly known as the mountain chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cooler temperatures and lower oxygen levels. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.

Chaptalia monticola is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
