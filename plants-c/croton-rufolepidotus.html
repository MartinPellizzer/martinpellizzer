Croton rufolepidotus

Croton rufolepidotus, commonly known as the red-leaved croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are often variegated with green and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
