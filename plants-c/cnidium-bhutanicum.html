Cnidium bhutanicum

Cnidium bhutanicum, commonly known as Bhutanese birthwort, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and northern India.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. However, due to its toxic components, it is not recommended for consumption without proper guidance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
