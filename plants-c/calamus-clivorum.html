Calamus clivorum

Calamus clivorum, commonly known as the cliff reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, grass-like plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus clivorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
