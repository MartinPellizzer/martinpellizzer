Calamus meyenianus

Calamus meyenianus, commonly known as the giant reed or giant arrowroot, is a member of the Araucariaceae family.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and stout, cylindrical stems. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant also plays a role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
