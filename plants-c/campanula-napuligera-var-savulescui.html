Campanula napuligera var. savulescui

Campanula napuligera var. savulescui, commonly known as the Savulescu's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Romania.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. This variant is part of the Campanulaceae family and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-altitude flora.
