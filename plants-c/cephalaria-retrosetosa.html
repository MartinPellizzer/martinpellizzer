Cephalaria retrosetosa

Cephalaria retrosetosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria retrosetosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
