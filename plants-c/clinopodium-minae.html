Clinopodium minae

Clinopodium minae, commonly known as Minae's woodruff, is a small perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge and emit a subtle, mint-like fragrance when crushed.

Clinopodium minae is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
