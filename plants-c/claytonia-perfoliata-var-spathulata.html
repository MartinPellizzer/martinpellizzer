Claytonia perfoliata var. spathulata

Claytonia perfoliata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf springbeauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
