Cupressus mas

Cupressus mas, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender form and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, round cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its foliage is dense and gives the tree a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
