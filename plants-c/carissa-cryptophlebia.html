Carissa cryptophlebia

Carissa cryptophlebia, commonly known as the sweet honeycomb or yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive honeycomb-like pattern on the petals. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
