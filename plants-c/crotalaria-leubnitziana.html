Crotalaria leubnitziana

Crotalaria leubnitziana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
