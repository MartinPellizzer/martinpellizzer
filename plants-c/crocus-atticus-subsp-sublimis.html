Crocus atticus subsp. sublimis

Crocus atticus subsp.

sublimis, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and western Asia. It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional horticulture.
