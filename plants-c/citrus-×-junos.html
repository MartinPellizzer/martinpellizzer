Citrus × junos

Citrus × junos, commonly known as the kumquat.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is unique in that it is edible both inside and outside, with a thin, sweet skin and a segmented, juicy interior. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is a hybrid between the mandarin and the pomelo, showcasing a blend of traits from both parent species.
