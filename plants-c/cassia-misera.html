Cassia misera

Cassia misera, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
