Cyperus marlothii var. seineri

Cyperus marlothii var. seineri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and along waterways. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually pale green to brownish in color.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
