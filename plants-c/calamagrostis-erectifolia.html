Calamagrostis erectifolia

Calamagrostis erectifolia, commonly known as the upright reed grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
