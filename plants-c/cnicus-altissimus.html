Cnicus altissimus

Cnicus altissimus, commonly known as the tall knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand arid conditions and grazing.
