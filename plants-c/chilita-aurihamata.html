Chilita aurihamata

Chilita aurihamata, commonly known as the golden-hamata chilita, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is distinguished by its vibrant yellow-orange petals, which are arranged in a daisy-like pattern, and its sturdy, hairy stems that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. Chilita aurihamata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts worldwide.
