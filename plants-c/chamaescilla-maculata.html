Chamaescilla maculata

Chamaescilla maculata, commonly known as the spotted rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance on the upper surface. Its flowers are white to pale pink and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
