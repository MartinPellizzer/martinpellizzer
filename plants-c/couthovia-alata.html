Couthovia alata

Couthovia alata, commonly known as the winged sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's most distinctive feature is its feathery, winged bracts that surround the flower head, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Couthovia alata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
