Carex marina subsp. pseudolagopina

Carex marina subsp.

pseudolagopina, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal areas. It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in tidal zones and estuaries.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its adaptability to saltwater environments makes it a key component of salt marsh ecosystems.
