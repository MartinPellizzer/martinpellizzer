Campanula bravensis

Campanula bravensis, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Campanula bravensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
