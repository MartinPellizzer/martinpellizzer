Centaurea debeauxii

Centaurea debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

Centaurea debeauxii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
