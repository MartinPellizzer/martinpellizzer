Carex splendens

Carex splendens, commonly known as shining sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces showy, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Carex splendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
