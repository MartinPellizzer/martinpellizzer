Callirhoe papaver var. bushii

Callirhoe papaver var. bushii, commonly known as the Bush's poppy, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
