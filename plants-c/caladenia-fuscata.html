Caladenia fuscata

Caladenia fuscata, commonly known as the black-veined spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as New South Wales and Victoria. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color. Its flowers have a distinctive spider-like appearance, with elongated petals and a narrow, twisted labellum.

Caladenia fuscata is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
