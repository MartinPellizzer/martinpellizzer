Cosbaea coccinea

Cosbaea coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and wasps.

Cosbaea coccinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
