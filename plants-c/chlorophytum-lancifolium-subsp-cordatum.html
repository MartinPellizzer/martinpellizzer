Chlorophytum lancifolium subsp. cordatum

Chlorophytum lancifolium subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the curly snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family. It is characterized by its stiff, upright leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by dark blue berries. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

It is a popular indoor plant due to its air-purifying properties and low maintenance requirements.
