Camellia polyodonta f. alba

Camellia polyodonta f. alba, commonly known as the white camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blossoms. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticultural breeding programs due to its unique floral traits.
