Calylophus serrulata

Calylophus serrulata, commonly known as the serrated calylophus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Calylophus serrulata is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
