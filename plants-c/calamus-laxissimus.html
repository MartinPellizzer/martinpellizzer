Calamus laxissimus

Calamus laxissimus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Calamus laxissimus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies where it thrives in moist, tropical environments.
