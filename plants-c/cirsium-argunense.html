Cirsium argunense

Cirsium argunense, commonly known as the Argun thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Cirsium argunense is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
