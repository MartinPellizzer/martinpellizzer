Colchicum cupanii var. valeryi

Colchicum cupanii var. valeryi, commonly known as Valery's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It features striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm, typical of the Colchicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its potential therapeutic applications.
