Carlina macrocephala var. toscanensis

Carlina macrocephala var. toscanensis, commonly known as the Tuscan crownflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive, spherical head of yellow florets surrounded by white to pale blue bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
