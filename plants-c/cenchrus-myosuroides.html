Cenchrus myosuroides

Cenchrus myosuroides, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Cenchrus myosuroides is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
