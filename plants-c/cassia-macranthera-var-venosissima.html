Cassia macranthera var. venosissima

Cassia macranthera var. venosissima, commonly known as the veiny cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent venation, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
