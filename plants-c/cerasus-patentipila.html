Cerasus patentipila

Cerasus patentipila, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is known for its ornamental value due to its showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its fruits are small, sweet, and edible.

Cerasus patentipila is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
