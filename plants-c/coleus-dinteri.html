Coleus dinteri

Coleus dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, often displaying shades of red, purple, and green. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative and low-maintenance plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
