Cyphostemma ledermannii

Cyphostemma ledermannii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and long, slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
