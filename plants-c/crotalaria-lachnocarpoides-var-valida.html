Crotalaria lachnocarpoides var. valida

Crotalaria lachnocarpoides var. valida, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is a common component of grassland ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
