Cantinoa villicaulis

Cantinoa villicaulis, commonly known as the velvet-stemmed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Cantinoa villicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
