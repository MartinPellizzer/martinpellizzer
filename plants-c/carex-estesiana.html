Carex estesiana

Carex estesiana, commonly known as Estes' sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America, particularly found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swales.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typical of the sedge family. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Carex estesiana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
