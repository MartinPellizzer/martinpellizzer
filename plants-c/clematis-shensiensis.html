Clematis shensiensis

Clematis shensiensis, commonly known as the Szechuan Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability.

Clematis shensiensis is also noted for its ability to climb using its tendrils, often reaching heights of several meters.
