Cruciata pedemontana var. reflexa

Cruciata pedemontana var. reflexa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive five-lobed structure. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and open slopes at high elevations.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
