Chenopodium rigidum

Chenopodium rigidum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and have a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, tangy berries.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
