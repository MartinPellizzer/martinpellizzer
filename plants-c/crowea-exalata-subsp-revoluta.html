Crowea exalata subsp. revoluta

Crowea exalata subsp.

revoluta, commonly known as the pink crowea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of pink or red tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is noted for its long blooming period, which can extend from spring through early autumn.
