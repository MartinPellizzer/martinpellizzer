Centaurea cineraria subsp. busambarensis

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

busambarensis, commonly known as the Busambarensis cineraria, is a subspecies native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
