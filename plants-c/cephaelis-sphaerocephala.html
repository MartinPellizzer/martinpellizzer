Cephaelis sphaerocephala

Cephaelis sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed cephaleis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
