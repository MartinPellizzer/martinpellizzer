Cynorkis discolor

Cynorkis discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential therapeutic properties.
