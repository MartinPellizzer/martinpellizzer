Clematis heracleifolia var. ichangensis

Clematis heracleifolia var. ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb supports naturally.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
