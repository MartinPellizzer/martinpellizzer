Campanula aurea var. angustifolia

Campanula aurea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
