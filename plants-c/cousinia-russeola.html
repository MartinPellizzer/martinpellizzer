Cousinia russeola

Cousinia russeola, commonly known as the Russian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Cousinia russeola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
