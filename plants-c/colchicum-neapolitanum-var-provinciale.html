Colchicum neapolitanum var. provinciale

Colchicum neapolitanum var. provinciale, commonly known as the Neapolitan colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a waxy coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
