Cerastium arvense f. canescens

Cerastium arvense f. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along fences.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
