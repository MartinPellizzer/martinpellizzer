Convolvulus congestus

Convolvulus congestus, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus congestus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
