Cyanotis zebrina

Cyanotis zebrina, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its striking blue and white striped leaves, which give it its common name. It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Cyanotis zebrina is a hardy, evergreen perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
