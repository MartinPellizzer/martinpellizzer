Camellia philippinensis

Camellia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine camellia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, and bloom in the winter months.

Camellia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
