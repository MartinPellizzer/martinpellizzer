Crocus hadriaticus var. saundersianus

Crocus hadriaticus var. saundersianus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
