Carex canescens subsp. disjuncta

Carex canescens subsp.

disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are often surrounded by bracts.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
