Capparis trisonthiae

Capparis trisonthiae, commonly known as the Socotra caper, is a flowering plant native to the Socotra Archipelago.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and thick, waxy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, sour fruits.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a significant species in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
