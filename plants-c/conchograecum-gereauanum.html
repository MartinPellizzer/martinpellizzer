Conchograecum gereauanum

Conchograecum gereauanum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique and rare succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its desert environment. The plant has a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny stems that help it store water and withstand harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
