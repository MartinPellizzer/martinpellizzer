Ctenopsis cynosuroides

Ctenopsis cynosuroides, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, translucent, and waxy leaves that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unusual appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
