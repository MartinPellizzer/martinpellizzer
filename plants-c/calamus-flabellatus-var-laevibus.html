Calamus flabellatus var. laevibus

Calamus flabellatus var. laevibus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed flag bamboo, is a variant of the flag bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a dark green coloration. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
