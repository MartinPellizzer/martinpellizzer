Condalia globosa var. pubescens

Condalia globosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy globosa.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and spherical, woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
