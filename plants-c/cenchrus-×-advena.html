Cenchrus × advena

Cenchrus × advena, commonly known as the hybrid sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cenchrus × advena is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
