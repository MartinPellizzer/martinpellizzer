Cuphea hirtella

Cuphea hirtella, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular red or pink flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
