Coelogyne beyrodtiana

Coelogyne beyrodtiana, commonly known as the Beyer's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting to its often dry habitat. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its inflorescence is a raceme of several flowers, each with three petals and a distinctive lip.

Coelogyne beyrodtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
