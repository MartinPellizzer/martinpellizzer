Carex artitecta var. subtilirostris

Carex artitecta var. subtilirostris, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
