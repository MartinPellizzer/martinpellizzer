Cyathea pittieri

Cyathea pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value. Cyathea pittieri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy in tropical and subtropical climates.
