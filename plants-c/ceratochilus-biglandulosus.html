Ceratochilus biglandulosus

Ceratochilus biglandulosus, commonly known as the large-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
