Chilianthus dysophyllus

Chilianthus dysophyllus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a sturdy, upright growth habit with compound leaves consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowering period makes it a popular ornamental species in gardens and public spaces.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
