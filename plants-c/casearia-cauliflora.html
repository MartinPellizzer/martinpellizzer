Casearia cauliflora

Casearia cauliflora, commonly known as the Christmas casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the trunk and branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Casearia cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
