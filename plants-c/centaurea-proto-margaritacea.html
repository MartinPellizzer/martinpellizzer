Centaurea proto-margaritacea

Centaurea proto-margaritacea, commonly known as the dwarf margarita centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers with purple or red centers. Its flowers are typically arranged in compact, spike-like clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
