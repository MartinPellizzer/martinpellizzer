Cryptandra pimelioides

Cryptandra pimelioides, commonly known as the slender cryptandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as sandplains and heathlands.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a priority for conservation.
