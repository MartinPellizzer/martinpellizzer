Cuphea lophostoma

Cuphea lophostoma, commonly known as the flame beak cuphea, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Cuphea lophostoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
