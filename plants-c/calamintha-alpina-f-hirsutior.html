Calamintha alpina f. hirsutior

Calamintha alpina f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy alpine germander, is a subspecies of the alpine germander native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive hirsute appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming properties.

The subspecies is adapted to cold, rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh alpine conditions.
