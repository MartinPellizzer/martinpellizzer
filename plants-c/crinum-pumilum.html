Crinum pumilum

Crinum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a bulbous base with strap-like, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
