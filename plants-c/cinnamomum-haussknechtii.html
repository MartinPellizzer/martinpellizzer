Cinnamomum haussknechtii

Cinnamomum haussknechtii, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinnamon or Phong van, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark brown, rough bark that exudes a strong cinnamon scent when peeled. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while its bark is the primary source of its aromatic compounds.

This species is valued for its unique fragrance and is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
