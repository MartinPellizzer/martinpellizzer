Coelogyne johannis-winkleri

Coelogyne johannis-winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's coelogyne, is a flowering orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant orchid blooms that typically appear in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, and long, strap-like leaves. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
