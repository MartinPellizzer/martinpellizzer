Ceratolobus micholitzianus

Ceratolobus micholitzianus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, narrow leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine.
