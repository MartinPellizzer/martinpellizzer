Cypripedium × amandum

Cypripedium × amandum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its large, showy, pouch-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a single flower, which is often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
