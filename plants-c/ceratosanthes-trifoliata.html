Ceratosanthes trifoliata

Ceratosanthes trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
