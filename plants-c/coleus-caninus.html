Coleus caninus

Coleus caninus, commonly known as the dog's mouth plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
