Celtis vesiculosa

Celtis vesiculosa, commonly known as the balloon tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and its distinctive, inflated, orange fruit that resembles small balloons. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
