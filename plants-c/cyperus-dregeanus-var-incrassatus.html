Cyperus dregeanus var. incrassatus

Cyperus dregeanus var. incrassatus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and compact, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
