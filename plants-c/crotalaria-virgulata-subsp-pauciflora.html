Crotalaria virgulata subsp. pauciflora

Crotalaria virgulata subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the slender crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
