Coleus ascendens

Coleus ascendens, commonly known as the climbing coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant has a trailing habit and can grow up to several meters in length when supported. Its stems are square in cross-section, a common feature among the mint family.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.
