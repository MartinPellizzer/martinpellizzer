Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-alba-plena

Crataegus oxyacantha var. flore-alba-plena, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in jams and wines. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and its role in supporting pollinators.

This variety is hardy and adaptable, thriving in a range of soil types and climates.
