Camaridium affine

Camaridium affine, commonly known as the small-flowered camaridium, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and finely divided leaves. The flowers are small and typically arranged in umbels, giving it a delicate appearance. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
