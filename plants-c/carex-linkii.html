Carex linkii

Carex linkii, commonly known as the Link's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges. Carex linkii produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
