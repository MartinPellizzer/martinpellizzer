Clematis finetiana

Clematis finetiana, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with hooked thorns. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
