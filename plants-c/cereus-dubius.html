Cereus dubius

Cereus dubius, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit adds a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
