Cornus kousa var. leucotricha

Cornus kousa var. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-flowered dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, which appear in early spring. The plant produces clusters of red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge and turn yellow in the autumn.

It thrives in partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
