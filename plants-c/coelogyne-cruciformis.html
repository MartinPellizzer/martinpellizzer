Coelogyne cruciformis

Coelogyne cruciformis, commonly known as the Cruciform Coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and red, often arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne cruciformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
