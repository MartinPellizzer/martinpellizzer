Catesbaea

Catesbaea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Catesbaea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
