Crepis segetum

Crepis segetum, commonly known as the field mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a rosette, while the stem is erect and covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
