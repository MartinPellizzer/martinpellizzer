Crotalaria bondii

Crotalaria bondii, commonly known as the African cowpea or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Crotalaria bondii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
