Calea cordifolia

Calea cordifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and its unique, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Calea cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for both botanists and gardeners.
