Ceropegia cufodontii

Ceropegia cufodontii, commonly known as the African violet creeper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
