Castanea japonica var. obtexta

Castanea japonica var. obtexta, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

It is a variety of the chestnut tree native to Japan, characterized by its dense, obtexta (obstinate) bracts that remain attached to the fruit even after maturity. The plant produces oval, spiny nuts enclosed in a thick, hairy husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible nuts.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.
