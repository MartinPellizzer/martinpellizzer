Campanula trichocalycina

Campanula trichocalycina, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Campanula trichocalycina is a perennial herb that produces small, showy flowers from late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
