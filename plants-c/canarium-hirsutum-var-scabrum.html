Canarium hirsutum var. scabrum

Canarium hirsutum var. scabrum, commonly known as the rough canarium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Burseraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on its leaves and bark, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its potential medicinal and economic uses.
