Cirrhopetalum mirum

Cirrhopetalum mirum, commonly known as the miraculous curl-petals, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, curling petals that resemble spirals, giving it a distinctive and almost otherworldly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cirrhopetalum mirum is considered an endangered species.
