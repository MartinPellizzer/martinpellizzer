Chuquiraga hassleriana

Chuquiraga hassleriana, commonly known as the Andean burflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It has a robust root system and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Chuquiraga hassleriana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
