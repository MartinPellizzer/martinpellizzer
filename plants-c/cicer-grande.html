Cicer grande

Cicer grande, commonly known as the large vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in soil remediation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its flowers are typically yellow or violet, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Cicer grande is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
