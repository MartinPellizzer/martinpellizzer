Copernicia longiglossa

Copernicia longiglossa, commonly known as the long-tongued copernicia, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense, waxy layer. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in fibrous husks, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
