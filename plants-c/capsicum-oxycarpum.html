Capsicum oxycarpum

Capsicum oxycarpum, commonly known as the bird pepper or wild chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
