Chromolaena baccharidifolia

Chromolaena baccharidifolia, commonly known as the tropical wild hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in sunny, moist environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
