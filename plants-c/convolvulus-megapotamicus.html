Convolvulus megapotamicus

Convolvulus megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andes morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
