Colocasia adiana

Colocasia adiana, commonly known as the elephant ear or Indian taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rhizomes are edible when properly cooked and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure.
