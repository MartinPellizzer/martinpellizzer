Carex caduca

Carex caduca, commonly known as the ephemeral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant is known for its short growing season, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
