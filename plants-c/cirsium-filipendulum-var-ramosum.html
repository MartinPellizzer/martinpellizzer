Cirsium filipendulum var. ramosum

Cirsium filipendulum var. ramosum, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry meadows and rocky soils. Its hairy stems and leaves provide a distinctive appearance, while its flowers attract various pollinators.

It is typically herbaceous, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.
