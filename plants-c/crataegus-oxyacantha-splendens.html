Crataegus oxyacantha splendens

Crataegus oxyacantha splendens, commonly known as the shining hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
