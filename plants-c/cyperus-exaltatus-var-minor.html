Cyperus exaltatus var. minor

Cyperus exaltatus var. minor, commonly known as the narrow leaf flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense mats, which help in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats.
