Campanula rimarum

Campanula rimarum, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Campanula rimarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
