Cassia curvifolia

Cassia curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag-shaped branches and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Cassia curvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
