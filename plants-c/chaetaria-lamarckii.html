Chaetaria lamarckii

Chaetaria lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's hairgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, preferring acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. Its small, greenish-brown spikelets are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Chaetaria lamarckii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
