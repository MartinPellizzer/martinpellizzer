Carex penalpina

Carex penalpina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in summer.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
