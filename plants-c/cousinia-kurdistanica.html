Cousinia kurdistanica

Cousinia kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia kurdistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
