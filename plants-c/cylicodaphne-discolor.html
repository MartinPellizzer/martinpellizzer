Cylicodaphne discolor

Cylicodaphne discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Cane Plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cylicodaphne discolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
