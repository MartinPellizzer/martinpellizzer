Centranthus ruber var. velenovskyi

Centranthus ruber var. velenovskyi, commonly known as the red monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped, crimson flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
