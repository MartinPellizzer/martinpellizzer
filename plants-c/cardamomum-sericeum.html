Cardamomum sericeum

Cardamomum sericeum, commonly known as silky cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by aromatic, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
