Clematis akebioides

Clematis akebioides, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its strong, flexible stems that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis akebioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
