Chenopodium tonkinense

Chenopodium tonkinense, commonly known as Vietnamese goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued as a food source in some regions due to its edible leaves.
