Campanula uniflora var. giesekiana

Campanula uniflora var. giesekiana, commonly known as the Giesekian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
