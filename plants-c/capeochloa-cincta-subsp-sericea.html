Capeochloa cincta subsp. sericea

Capeochloa cincta subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.
