Cucumis anguria

Cucumis anguria, commonly known as the bitter gourd or karela, is a vine plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces elongated, typically green fruits with a bitter taste, which can be used in various culinary preparations. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

Cucumis anguria is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
