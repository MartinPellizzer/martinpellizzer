Cephalocroton polygynus

Cephalocroton polygynus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
