Cousinia rubrarenosa

Cousinia rubrarenosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and vibrant red stems that contrast with its pale green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months, adding visual interest to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia rubrarenosa is often found in rocky slopes and desert margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
