Cactus wrightii

Cactus wrightii, commonly known as the chainlink cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristly spines and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Cactus wrightii plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
