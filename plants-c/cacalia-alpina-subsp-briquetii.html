Cacalia alpina subsp. briquetii

Cacalia alpina subsp.

briquetii, commonly known as the Alpine cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are small, with a yellow center surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
