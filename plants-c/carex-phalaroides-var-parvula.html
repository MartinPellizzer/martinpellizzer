Carex phalaroides var. parvula

Carex phalaroides var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it serves as a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
