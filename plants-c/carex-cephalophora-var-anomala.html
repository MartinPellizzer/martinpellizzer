Carex cephalophora var. anomala

Carex cephalophora var. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Carex cephalophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
