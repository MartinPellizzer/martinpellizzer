Centaurium umbellatum var. majus

Centaurium umbellatum var. majus, commonly known as the greater mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, bright green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Gentianaceae family.
