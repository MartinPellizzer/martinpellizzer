Centaurea teydis

Centaurea teydis, commonly known as the Teydis knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea teydis is often found in open areas such as grasslands and along coastal cliffs.
