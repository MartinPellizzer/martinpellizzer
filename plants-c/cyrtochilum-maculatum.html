Cyrtochilum maculatum

Cyrtochilum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pink or purple, with a distinctive pattern of spots on the petals.

Cyrtochilum maculatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
