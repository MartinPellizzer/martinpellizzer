Cissus dinklagei var. pilosa

Cissus dinklagei var. pilosa, commonly known as the velvety cissus, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
