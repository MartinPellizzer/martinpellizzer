Cornus alba var. gouchaultii

Cornus alba var. gouchaultii, commonly known as the Gouchalt's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often display a reddish or purple hue, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its foliage is typically dark green with a reddish tinge, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is valued for its seasonal color changes and hardiness.
