Chesniella macrantha

Chesniella macrantha, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it a standout in any garden. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, helping it retain moisture in its native habitat. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
