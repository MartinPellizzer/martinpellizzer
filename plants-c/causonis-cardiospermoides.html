Causonis cardiospermoides

Causonis cardiospermoides, commonly known as the heartseed tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a distinctive, heart-shaped capsule that contains a single, large seed.

The plant is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its endemic status.
