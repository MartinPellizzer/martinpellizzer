Casuarina cristata

Casuarina cristata, commonly known as the mountain casuarina, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Casuarina cristata is valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
