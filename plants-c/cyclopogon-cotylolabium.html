Cyclopogon cotylolabium

Cyclopogon cotylolabium, commonly known as the white-flowered cyclopogon, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical orchids.
