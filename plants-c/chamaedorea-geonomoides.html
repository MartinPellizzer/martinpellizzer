Chamaedorea geonomoides

Chamaedorea geonomoides, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen bamboo, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters and prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It is known for its ability to thrive in indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.

Its foliage remains lush and vibrant throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
