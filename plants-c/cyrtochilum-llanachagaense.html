Cyrtochilum llanachagaense

Cyrtochilum llanachagaense, commonly known as the Llanachaga orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces greenish-white or pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on rocky substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
