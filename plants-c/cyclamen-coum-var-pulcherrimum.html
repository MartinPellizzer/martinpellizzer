Cyclamen coum var. pulcherrimum

Cyclamen coum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive violet or purple underside, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and unique foliage, which make it a popular choice for winter flowering displays.

Cyclamen coum var. pulcherrimum is also valued for its ability to bloom during the winter months when few other plants are in flower.
