Clerodendrum rotundifolium var. stuhlmannii

Clerodendrum rotundifolium var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's spindlewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
