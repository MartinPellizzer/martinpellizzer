Campanula frutetorum

Campanula frutetorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula frutetorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
