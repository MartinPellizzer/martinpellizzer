Cirsium lanceolatum var. stenocephalum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. stenocephalum, commonly known as narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

The variety stenocephalum is distinguished by its narrower flower heads compared to other varieties of Cirsium lanceolatum.
