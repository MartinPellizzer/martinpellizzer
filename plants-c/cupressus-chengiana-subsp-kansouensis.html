Cupressus chengiana subsp. kansouensis

Cupressus chengiana subsp.

kansouensis, commonly known as the Kansouensis Chinese Fir, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to China. It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a specimen plant.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical conservation efforts.
