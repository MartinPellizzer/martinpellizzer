Carica dolichaula

Carica dolichaula, commonly known as the long-stalked papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, fleshy, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth surface and a distinct veins pattern.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, which eventually develop into the edible fruits.
