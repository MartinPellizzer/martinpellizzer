Cucumella aspera

Cucumella aspera, commonly known as the rough cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, elongated cucumber-like berry with a rough, textured surface.
