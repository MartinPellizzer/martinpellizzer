Calamagrostis neglecta var. pallida

Calamagrostis neglecta var. pallida, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are dense and form clumps, while its flowering stems produce panicles of small, pale flowers in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
