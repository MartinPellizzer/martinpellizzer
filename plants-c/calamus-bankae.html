Calamus bankae

Calamus bankae, commonly known as the Bornean calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tall, rhizomatous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. The plant features long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length, often with a distinctive waxy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid species.

Calamus bankae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
