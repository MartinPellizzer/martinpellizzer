Capparis sikkimensis

Capparis sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim caper, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features thorny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sour fruits. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Capparis sikkimensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude climates.
