Campanula sibirica f. abortiva

Campanula sibirica f. abortiva, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
