Chamguava

Chamguava, commonly known as the hybrid of the cherry and guava, is a unique fruit plant that combines characteristics of both parent species.

It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with dense foliage and thorny branches. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops from these blossoms.

The fruit is generally small, with a firm skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp that contains numerous seeds.
