Crateva capparoides

Crateva capparoides, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
