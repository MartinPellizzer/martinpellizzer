Cousinia oxytoma

Cousinia oxytoma, commonly known as the woolly frostweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Cousinia oxytoma is adapted to cold climates and is known for its resilience in harsh, arid conditions.
