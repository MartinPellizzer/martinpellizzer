Cryptodiscus cachroides

Cryptodiscus cachroides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed pincushion, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble tiny pincushions. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it blooms during the summer months.

Cryptodiscus cachroides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
