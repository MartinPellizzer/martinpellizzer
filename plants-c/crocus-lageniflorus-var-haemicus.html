Crocus lageniflorus var. haemicus

Crocus lageniflorus var. haemicus, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that are typically orange to reddish in color, with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces a showy display of blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that appear after flowering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming characteristics.
