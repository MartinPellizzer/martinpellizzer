Chamaecrista anamariae

Chamaecrista anamariae, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Chamaecrista anamariae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
