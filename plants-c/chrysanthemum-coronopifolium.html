Chrysanthemum coronopifolium

Chrysanthemum coronopifolium, commonly known as the laceleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
