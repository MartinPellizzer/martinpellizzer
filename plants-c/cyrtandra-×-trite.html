Cyrtandra × trite

Cyrtandra × trite, commonly known as the hybrid Cyrtandra, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive, often showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in horticulture, though its hybrid status makes it less common in the wild.
