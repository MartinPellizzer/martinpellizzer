Chaetanthera peruviana

Chaetanthera peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
