Cytisus albidus var. ifnianus

Cytisus albidus var. ifnianus, commonly known as the white broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and clusters of small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval, glossy green leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
