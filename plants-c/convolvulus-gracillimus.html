Convolvulus gracillimus

Convolvulus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
