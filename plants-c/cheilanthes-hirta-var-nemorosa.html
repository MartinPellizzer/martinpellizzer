Cheilanthes hirta var. nemorosa

Cheilanthes hirta var. nemorosa, commonly known as the hairy rock fern, is a perennial fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds covered in fine, short hairs. The plant thrives in rocky crevices and dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
