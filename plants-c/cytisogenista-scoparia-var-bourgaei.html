Cytisogenista scoparia var. bourgaei

Cytisogenista scoparia var. bourgaei, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
