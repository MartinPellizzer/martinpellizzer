Cotoneaster nohelii

Cotoneaster nohelii, commonly known as the Hawaiian cotoneaster, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various coastal and upland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
