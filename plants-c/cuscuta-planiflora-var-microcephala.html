Cuscuta planiflora var. microcephala

Cuscuta planiflora var. microcephala, commonly known as dwarf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found growing on grasses and other low-growing vegetation. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host plants and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
