Cymbidium biflorens

Cymbidium biflorens, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing pale yellow to greenish-white petals with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cymbidium biflorens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
