Centaurea affinis

Centaurea affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a gray-green appearance.

Centaurea affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
