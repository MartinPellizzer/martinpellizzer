Cryptocarya sellowiana

Cryptocarya sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel or Sellow's cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya sellowiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
