Clematis pauciflora

Clematis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower clematis, is a woody vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
