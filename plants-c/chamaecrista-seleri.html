Chamaecrista seleri

Chamaecrista seleri, commonly known as Seler's crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered together, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Chamaecrista seleri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
