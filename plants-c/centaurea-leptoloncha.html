Centaurea leptoloncha

Centaurea leptoloncha, commonly known as the slenderhead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its slender, spiny stems and small, blue to purple flowers are notable botanical features.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
