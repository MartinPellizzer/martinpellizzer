Cirsium scapanolepis

Cirsium scapanolepis, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cirsium scapanolepis is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
