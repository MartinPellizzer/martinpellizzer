Coronidium scorpioides

Coronidium scorpioides, commonly known as the spiny false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland or scrub habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Coronidium scorpioides is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
