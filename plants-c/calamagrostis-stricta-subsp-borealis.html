Calamagrostis stricta subsp. borealis

Calamagrostis stricta subsp.

borealis, commonly known as northern tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

This species thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets that bloom in late summer.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
