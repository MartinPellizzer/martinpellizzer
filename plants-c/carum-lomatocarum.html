Carum lomatocarum

Carum lomatocarum, commonly known as the mountain carrot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and edible plants. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
