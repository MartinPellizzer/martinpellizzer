Crepidium multiflorum

Crepidium multiflorum, commonly known as the multiflorous orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features strap-like leaves and has a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
