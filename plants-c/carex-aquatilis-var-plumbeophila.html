Carex aquatilis var. plumbeophila

Carex aquatilis var. plumbeophila, commonly known as the bluish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life, it plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
