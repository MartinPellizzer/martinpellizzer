Carex haenkeana

Carex haenkeana, commonly known as the Andes sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
