Cnicus suffultus var. incomptus

Cnicus suffultus var. incomptus, commonly known as the creeping sowthistle, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a prostrate or ascending stem that is often hairy and covered with glandular dots. The leaves are spoon-shaped, gradually narrowing toward the base, and are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
