Cimicifuga foetida var. tschonoskii

Cimicifuga foetida var. tschonoskii, commonly known as the Siberian black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate deciduous forests.
