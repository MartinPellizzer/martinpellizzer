Chrysanthemum × kuwashimae

Chrysanthemum × kuwashimae, commonly known as the Kuwashimae chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in the fall, producing a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
