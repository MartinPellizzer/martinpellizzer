Cytisus supinus subsp. austriacus

Cytisus supinus subsp.

austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Alps. It features slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
