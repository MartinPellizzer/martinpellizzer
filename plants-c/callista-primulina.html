Callista primulina

Callista primulina, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and pink flowers that resemble the structure of a passionfruit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, fragrant scent. Its flowers are pollinated by both insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Callista primulina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
