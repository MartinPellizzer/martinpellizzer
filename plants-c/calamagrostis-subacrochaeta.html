Calamagrostis subacrochaeta

Calamagrostis subacrochaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.
