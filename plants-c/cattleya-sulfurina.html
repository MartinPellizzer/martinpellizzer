Cattleya sulfurina

Cattleya sulfurina, commonly known as the Yellow Lady's Slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, producing a strong, sweet fragrance.

Cattleya sulfurina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
