Conophallus giganteus

Conophallus giganteus, commonly known as the giant conophallus, is a rare and striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, palm-like structure with a thick, segmented trunk and broad, pinnate leaves that can reach impressive sizes. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically borne in a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
