Coutarea hexandra var. amazonica

Coutarea hexandra var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
