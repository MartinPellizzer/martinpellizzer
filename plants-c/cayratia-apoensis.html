Cayratia apoensis

Cayratia apoensis, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
