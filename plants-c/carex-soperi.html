Carex soperi

Carex soperi, commonly known as Soper's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Carex soperi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
