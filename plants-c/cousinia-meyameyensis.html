Cousinia meyameyensis

Cousinia meyameyensis, commonly known as the Meyameyensis globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cousinia meyameyensis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
