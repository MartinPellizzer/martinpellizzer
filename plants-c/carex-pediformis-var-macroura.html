Carex pediformis var. macroura

Carex pediformis var. macroura, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
