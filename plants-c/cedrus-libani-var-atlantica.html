Cedrus libani var. atlantica

Cedrus libani var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cedar, is a variety of the Lebanon cedar native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its conical shape, thick trunk, and dense foliage of scale-like leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in ancient architecture.
