Chukrasia nimmonii

Chukrasia nimmonii, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in the spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with elongated leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
