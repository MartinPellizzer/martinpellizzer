Crupina vulgaris subsp. morisii

Crupina vulgaris subsp.

morisii, commonly known as Moris' crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Moris' crupina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
