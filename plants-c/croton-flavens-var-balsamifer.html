Croton flavens var. balsamifer

Croton flavens var. balsamifer, commonly known as the balsamiferous croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange, often displaying intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also emits a mild, balsamic scent, contributing to its unique aromatic profile.
