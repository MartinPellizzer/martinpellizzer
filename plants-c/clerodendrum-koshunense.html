Clerodendrum koshunense

Clerodendrum koshunense, commonly known as the Korean flame bush, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
