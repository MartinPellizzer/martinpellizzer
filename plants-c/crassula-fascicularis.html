Crassula fascicularis

Crassula fascicularis, commonly known as the Rosette Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
