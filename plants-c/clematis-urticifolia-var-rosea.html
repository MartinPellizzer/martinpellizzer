Clematis urticifolia var. rosea

Clematis urticifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
