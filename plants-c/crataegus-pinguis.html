Crataegus pinguis

Crataegus pinguis, commonly known as the fat hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus pinguis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing food for various animals.
