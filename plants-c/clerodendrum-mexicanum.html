Clerodendrum mexicanum

Clerodendrum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
