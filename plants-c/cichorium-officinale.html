Cichorium officinale

Cichorium officinale, commonly known as chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue or purple leaves and a flowering head that resembles a dandelion. The plant is native to Europe and parts of North Africa but is now cultivated worldwide. Chicory contains compounds such as inulin, which is used in food and medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
