Caesalpinia bonducella

Caesalpinia bonducella, commonly known as the flame bean or red bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright red, flame-like flower clusters that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both gardens and ecological landscapes.
