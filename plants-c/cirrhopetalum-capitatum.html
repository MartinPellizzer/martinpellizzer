Cirrhopetalum capitatum

Cirrhopetalum capitatum, commonly known as the crown petal orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers that resemble petals with frayed edges. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a unique combination of sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
