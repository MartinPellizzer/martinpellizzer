Cucurbita pepo var. marginalis

Cucurbita pepo var. marginalis, commonly known as the creeping zucchini, is a variant of the common squash species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and elongated, cylindrical fruits that typically measure around 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The leaves are broad, lobed, and often have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces yellow flowers, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, nutty flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
