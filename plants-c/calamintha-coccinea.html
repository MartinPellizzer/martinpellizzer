Calamintha coccinea

Calamintha coccinea, commonly known as red germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its leaves are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct scent.

Calamintha coccinea is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
