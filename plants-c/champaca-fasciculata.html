Champaca fasciculata

Champaca fasciculata, commonly known as the golden champa, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly pubescent underside.

The flowers are often used in religious ceremonies and are also valued for their aromatic properties in perfumery and traditional medicine.
