Centaurea × mairei

Centaurea × mairei, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea × mairei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
