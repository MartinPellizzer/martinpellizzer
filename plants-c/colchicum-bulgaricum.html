Colchicum bulgaricum

Colchicum bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive corms that serve as its storage organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
