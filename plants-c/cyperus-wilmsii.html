Cyperus wilmsii

Cyperus wilmsii, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its grass-like appearance features narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus wilmsii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
