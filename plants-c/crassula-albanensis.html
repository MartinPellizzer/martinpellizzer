Crassula albanensis

Crassula albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula albanensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal.
