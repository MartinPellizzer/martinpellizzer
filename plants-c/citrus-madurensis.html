Citrus madurensis

Citrus madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits. The fruit has a thin, edible peel and a sweet, segmented pulp, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic oils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
