Ceanothus × pallidus var. roseus

Ceanothus × pallidus var. roseus, commonly known as the roseleaf ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Ceanothus, which is part of the Rutaceae family. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
