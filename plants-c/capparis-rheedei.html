Capparis rheedei

Capparis rheedei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tangy, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
