Calanthe coodei

Calanthe coodei, commonly known as the Coode's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates.

Calanthe coodei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
