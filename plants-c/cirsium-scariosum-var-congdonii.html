Cirsium scariosum var. congdonii

Cirsium scariosum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough, scaly texture, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in arid environments.
