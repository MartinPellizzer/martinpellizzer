Cryptanthus leuzingerae

Cryptanthus leuzingerae, commonly known as the Zamioculcas zamiifolia, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Zamiaceae family, which includes cycads, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow streaks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is a slow-growing, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
