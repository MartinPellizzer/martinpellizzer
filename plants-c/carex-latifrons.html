Carex latifrons

Carex latifrons, commonly known as the broad-frond sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex latifrons plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
