Caladenia carnea var. gigantea

Caladenia carnea var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a single, showy flower with a long, slender spur and intricate petal structures that mimic the appearance of a spider. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open forest or heathland environments. Its flowering period occurs during the late winter to early spring, producing vibrant pink and white blooms.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance within its native habitat.
