Chasea prolifera

Chasea prolifera, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often produce multiple branches. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Chasea prolifera plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland habitats.
