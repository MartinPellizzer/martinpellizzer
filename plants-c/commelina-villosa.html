Commelina villosa

Commelina villosa, commonly known as the hairy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in a whorl at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Commelina villosa is a member of the Commelinales order and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
