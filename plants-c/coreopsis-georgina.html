Coreopsis georgina

Coreopsis georgina, commonly known as the Georgia tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow to orange blooms. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis georgina is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
