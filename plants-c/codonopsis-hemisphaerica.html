Codonopsis hemisphaerica

Codonopsis hemisphaerica, commonly known as the spherical bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spherical-shaped flower buds that resemble small, closed bells. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that open gradually as the plant matures. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
