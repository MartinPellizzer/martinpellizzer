Cirsium oreophilum

Cirsium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its bracts are often swollen and spiny.

Cirsium oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
