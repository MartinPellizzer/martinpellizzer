Carum flaccidum

Carum flaccidum, commonly known as the soft caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a subtle, spicy aroma when crushed.

Carum flaccidum is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
