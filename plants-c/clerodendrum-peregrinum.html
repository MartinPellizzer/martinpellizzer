Clerodendrum peregrinum

Clerodendrum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
