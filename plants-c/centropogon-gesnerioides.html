Centropogon gesnerioides

Centropogon gesnerioides, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.
