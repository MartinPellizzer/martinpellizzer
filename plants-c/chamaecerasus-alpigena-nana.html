Chamaecerasus alpigena nana

Chamaecerasus alpigena nana, commonly known as the dwarf snowberry, is a compact shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its low-growing form makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
