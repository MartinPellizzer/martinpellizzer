Camellia hiemalis

Camellia hiemalis, commonly known as the winter camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia hiemalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal floral display.
