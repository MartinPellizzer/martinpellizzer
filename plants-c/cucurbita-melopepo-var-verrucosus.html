Cucurbita melopepo var. verrucosus

Cucurbita melopepo var. verrucosus, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a variant of the species Cucurbita melopepo.

It is characterized by its thick, warty, and often hairy rind, which gives it its common name. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is used in traditional cuisines and crafts.
