Chlorocyperus glaber

Chlorocyperus glaber, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Chlorocyperus glaber is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
