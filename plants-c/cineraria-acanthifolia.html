Cineraria acanthifolia

Cineraria acanthifolia, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
