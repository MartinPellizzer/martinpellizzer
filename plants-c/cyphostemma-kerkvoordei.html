Cyphostemma kerkvoordei

Cyphostemma kerkvoordei, commonly known as the Kerkvoorde's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, slender stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cyphostemma kerkvoordei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
