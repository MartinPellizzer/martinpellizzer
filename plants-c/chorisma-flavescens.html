Chorisma flavescens

Chorisma flavescens, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water. The plant belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats.

Chorisma flavescens is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
