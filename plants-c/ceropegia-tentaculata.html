Ceropegia tentaculata

Ceropegia tentaculata, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, often silver-velvet leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that resembles a flower with a hood. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia tentaculata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
