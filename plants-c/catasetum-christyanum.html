Catasetum christyanum

Catasetum christyanum, commonly known as the Christian's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip used for pollination. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Catasetum christyanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
