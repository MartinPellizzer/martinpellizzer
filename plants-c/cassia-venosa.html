Cassia venosa

Cassia venosa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia venosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
