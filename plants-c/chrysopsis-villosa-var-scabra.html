Chrysopsis villosa var. scabra

Chrysopsis villosa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing drought tolerance. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
