Carex glauca f. subrotunda

Carex glauca f. subrotunda, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, pond edges, and shaded woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and naturalistic landscapes.
