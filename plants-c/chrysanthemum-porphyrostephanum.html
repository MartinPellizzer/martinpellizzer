Chrysanthemum porphyrostephanum

Chrysanthemum porphyrostephanum, commonly known as the Persian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to red flowers. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes in its native regions.
