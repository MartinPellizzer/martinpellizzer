Cerasus anglica

Cerasus anglica, commonly known as the sweet cherry or English cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or dark red when ripe, with a sweet, juicy flesh. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Cerasus anglica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental plants.
