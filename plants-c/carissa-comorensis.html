Carissa comorensis

Carissa comorensis, commonly known as the sweet shrub or yellow carissa, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, spiny branches. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Carissa comorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
