Cirsium killiasii

Cirsium killiasii, commonly known as the Killias' thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its scientific name honors the botanist who first described it, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium killiasii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
