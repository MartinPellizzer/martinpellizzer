Callicarpa simondii

Callicarpa simondii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of violet-pink berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Callicarpa simondii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn color.
