Carpinus betulus f. albovariegata

Carpinus betulus f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-leaf hornbeam, is a cultivar of the European hornbeam.

It is characterized by its striking white and variegated leaves that provide visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a compact, slow-growing shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
