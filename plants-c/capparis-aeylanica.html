Capparis aeylanica

Capparis aeylanica, commonly known as the Armenian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
