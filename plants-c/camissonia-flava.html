Camissonia flava

Camissonia flava, commonly known as the yellow camissonia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the family Camissoniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Camissonia flava is often found in coastal and inland regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
