Comarella

Comarella, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its low-growing habit and fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale green blooms that appear in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense, compact rosette.

Comarella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and delicate appearance.
