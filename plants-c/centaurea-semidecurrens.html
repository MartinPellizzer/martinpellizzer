Centaurea semidecurrens

Centaurea semidecurrens, commonly known as the creeping centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are typically red or brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea semidecurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
