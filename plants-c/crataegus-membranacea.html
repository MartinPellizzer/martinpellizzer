Crataegus membranacea

Crataegus membranacea, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, red-tinged branches that become more pronounced in the winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
