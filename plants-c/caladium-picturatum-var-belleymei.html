Caladium picturatum var. belleymei

Caladium picturatum var. belleymei, commonly known as the Painted Leaf Caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which features intricate patterns of red, orange, and white on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its leaves are often used in floral arrangements due to their bold, eye-catching appearance.

The plant is typically propagated through tuberous roots, which can be divided and planted in well-draining soil for optimal growth.
