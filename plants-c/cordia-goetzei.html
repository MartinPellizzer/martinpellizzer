Cordia goetzei

Cordia goetzei, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
