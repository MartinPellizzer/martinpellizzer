Calopogonium sericeum var. villicalyx

Calopogonium sericeum var. villicalyx, commonly known as silky calopogonium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a nitrogen-fixing plant, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions contributes to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
