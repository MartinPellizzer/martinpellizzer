Calea nelsonii

Calea nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's candleplant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calea nelsonii is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory ailments.
