Carex durangensis

Carex durangensis, commonly known as Durango sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex durangensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
