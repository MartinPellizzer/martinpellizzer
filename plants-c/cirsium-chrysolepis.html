Cirsium chrysolepis

Cirsium chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of yellow disk flowers.

Cirsium chrysolepis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
