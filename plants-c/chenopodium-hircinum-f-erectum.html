Chenopodium hircinum f. erectum

Chenopodium hircinum f. erectum, commonly known as the upright goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as a forage plant for livestock.
