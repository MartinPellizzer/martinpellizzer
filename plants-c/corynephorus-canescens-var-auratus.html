Corynephorus canescens var. auratus

Corynephorus canescens var. auratus, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray to golden-colored foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
