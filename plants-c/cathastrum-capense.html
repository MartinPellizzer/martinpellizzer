Cathastrum capense

Cathastrum capense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are encased in a prickly husk. These berries are edible when ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in gardens.
