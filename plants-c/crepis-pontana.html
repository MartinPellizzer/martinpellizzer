Crepis pontana

Crepis pontana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats at high altitudes.

Crepis pontana is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine plant communities.
