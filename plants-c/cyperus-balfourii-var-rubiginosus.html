Cyperus balfourii var. rubiginosus

Cyperus balfourii var. rubiginosus, commonly known as the red-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

The varietal name "rubiginosus" refers to the reddish coloration often present in its stems and inflorescences.
