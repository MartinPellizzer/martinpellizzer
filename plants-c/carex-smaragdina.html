Carex smaragdina

Carex smaragdina, commonly known as Emerald Sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, with narrow, dark green leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Carex smaragdina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
