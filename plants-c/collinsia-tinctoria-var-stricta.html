Collinsia tinctoria var. stricta

Collinsia tinctoria var. stricta, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit compared to other forms of Collinsia tinctoria.
