Citrus undulata

Citrus undulata, commonly known as the wavy citrus or undulating lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves that have a distinctive undulating or wavy edge. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for their aromatic zest and juice. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Citrus undulata is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions for ornamental purposes and as a source of essential oils.
