Cyperus niger var. capitatus

Cyperus niger var. capitatus, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus niger var. capitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
