Convolvulus capensis var. natalensis

Convolvulus capensis var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a creeping perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in gardens and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

The flowers bloom throughout the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
