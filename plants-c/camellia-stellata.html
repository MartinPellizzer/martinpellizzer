Camellia stellata

Camellia stellata, commonly known as the star camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Camellia stellata blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9.
