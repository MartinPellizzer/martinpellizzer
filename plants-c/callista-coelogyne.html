Callista coelogyne

Callista coelogyne, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers. The plant features a slender, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Callista coelogyne is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in suitable conditions.
