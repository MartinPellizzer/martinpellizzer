Calotropis persica

Calotropis persica, commonly known as Persian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and thick leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its latex sap is milky and toxic, containing compounds like cardiac glycosides.

Calotropis persica is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
