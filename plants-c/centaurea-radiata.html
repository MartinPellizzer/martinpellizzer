Centaurea radiata

Centaurea radiata, commonly known as the radiate centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea radiata is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
