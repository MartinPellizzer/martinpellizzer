Clematis hexasepala

Clematis hexasepala, commonly known as the six-parted clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, usually blue or purple flowers. The plant features a woody vine with trifoliate leaves and is known for its distinct six sepals that give it its scientific name. Clematis hexasepala typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
