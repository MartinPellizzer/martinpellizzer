Carex ovata

Carex ovata, commonly known as the oval sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex ovata prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
