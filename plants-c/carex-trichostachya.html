Carex trichostachya

Carex trichostachya, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex trichostachya is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
