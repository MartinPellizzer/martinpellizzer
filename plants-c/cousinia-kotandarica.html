Cousinia kotandarica

Cousinia kotandarica, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and arid environments. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia kotandarica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
