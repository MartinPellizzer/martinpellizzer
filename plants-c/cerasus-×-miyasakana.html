Cerasus × miyasakana

Cerasus × miyasakana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters and has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but less commonly cultivated for consumption compared to other cherry species.

Cerasus × miyasakana is widely appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its seasonal beauty.
