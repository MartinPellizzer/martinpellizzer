Convolvulus tricolor subsp. tricolor

Convolvulus tricolor subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trifoliate flowers that display red, white, and blue petals.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
