Cineraria solidaginifolia

Cineraria solidaginifolia, commonly known as the yellow cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, while the flowers are small and clustered in a head.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
