Cirsium × cenisium

Cirsium × cenisium, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period that occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium × cenisium is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
