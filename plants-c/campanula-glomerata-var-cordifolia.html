Campanula glomerata var. cordifolia

Campanula glomerata var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
