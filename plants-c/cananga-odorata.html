Cananga odorata

Cananga odorata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are widely used in traditional perfumes and religious ceremonies due to their pleasant scent.
