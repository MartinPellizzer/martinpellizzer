Cattleya laurae

Cattleya laurae, commonly known as the Laura's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange with darker markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-like and thick, adapted to withstand the high humidity and frequent rainfall.

Cattleya laurae is valued in horticulture for its striking blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
