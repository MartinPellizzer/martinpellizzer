Cistus mexicanus

Cistus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican rockrose, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Cistus mexicanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
