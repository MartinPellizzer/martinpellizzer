Carex boryana var. simplicissima

Carex boryana var. simplicissima, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
