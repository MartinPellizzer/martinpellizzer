Carduus × nutantiformis subsp. puechii

Carduus × nutantiformis subsp.

puechii, commonly known as the Puech's thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers, typical of the Carduus genus.

The subspecies puechii is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
