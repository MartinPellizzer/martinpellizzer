Cocculus leptostachyus

Cocculus leptostachyus, commonly known as the slender-stalked cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
