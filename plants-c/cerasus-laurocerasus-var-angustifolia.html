Cerasus laurocerasus var. angustifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry or Prunus laurocerasus 'Angustifolia', is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to gardens and urban landscapes.
