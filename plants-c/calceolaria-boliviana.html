Calceolaria boliviana

Calceolaria boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian hooded orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers that resemble small cups, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria boliviana is valued both for its aesthetic beauty and its ecological role in its native habitats.
