Calliandra pubens

Calliandra pubens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble small, clustered umbels. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets. Calliandra pubens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
