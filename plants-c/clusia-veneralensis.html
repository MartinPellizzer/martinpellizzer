Clusia veneralensis

Clusia veneralensis, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia veneralensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
