Cuphea stygialis

Cuphea stygialis, commonly known as the zigzag cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems and clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamental plants.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
