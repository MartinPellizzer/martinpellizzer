Carex simulans

Carex simulans, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Carex simulans produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts.
