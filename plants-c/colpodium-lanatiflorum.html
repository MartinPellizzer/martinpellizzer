Colpodium lanatiflorum

Colpodium lanatiflorum, commonly known as the woolly-flowered colpodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of soft, hairy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four lanceolate petals, typically in shades of white to pale pink. Its inflorescence is a terminal cyme, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and meadows.
