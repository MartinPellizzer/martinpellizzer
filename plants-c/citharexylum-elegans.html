Citharexylum elegans

Citharexylum elegans, commonly known as the fragrant beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slight waxy coating.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
