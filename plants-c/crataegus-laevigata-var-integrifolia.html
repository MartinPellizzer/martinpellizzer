Crataegus laevigata var. integrifolia

Crataegus laevigata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features simple, ovate leaves that are entire or slightly lobed, with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red or yellow berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
