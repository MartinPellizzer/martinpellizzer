Chorizanthe watsonii

Chorizanthe watsonii, commonly known as Watson's galleta, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Watson's galleta is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
