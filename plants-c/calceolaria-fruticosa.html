Calceolaria fruticosa

Calceolaria fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub calceolaria, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth habit and vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calceolaria fruticosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
