Copaifera cearensis

Copaifera cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense Copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant resin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its resin, known as copaiba, is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
