Cyathea pseudobrunonis

Cyathea pseudobrunonis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the trunk and roots.

Cyathea pseudobrunonis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
