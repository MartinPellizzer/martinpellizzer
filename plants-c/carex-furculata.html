Carex furculata

Carex furculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex furculata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
