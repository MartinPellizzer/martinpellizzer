Centaurea × munkacsensis

Centaurea × munkacsensis, commonly known as the Hungarian knapweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped to linear leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are usually erect and branching.

Centaurea × munkacsensis is native to Hungary and parts of Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates.
