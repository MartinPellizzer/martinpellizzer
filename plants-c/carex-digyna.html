Carex digyna

Carex digyna, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar grass-like plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, three-ranked spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it often grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Carex digyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
