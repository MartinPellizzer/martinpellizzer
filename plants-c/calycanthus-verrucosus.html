Calycanthus verrucosus

Calycanthus verrucosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Calycanthus verrucosus is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland or shaded garden settings.
