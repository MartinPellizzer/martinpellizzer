Chimaphila umbellata subsp. cisatlantica

Chimaphila umbellata subsp.

cisatlantica, commonly known as the eastern flameflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States. It features dense clusters of showy, red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
