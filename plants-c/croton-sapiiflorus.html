Croton sapiiflorus

Croton sapiiflorus, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. Its sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.

Despite its potential hazards, it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
