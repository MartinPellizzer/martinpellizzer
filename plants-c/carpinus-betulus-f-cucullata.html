Carpinus betulus f. cucullata

Carpinus betulus f. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded hornbeam, is a distinctive variant of the hornbeam tree characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its slow growth rate and longevity, often living for several decades.

The hooded hornbeam is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
