Calepina cochlearioides

Calepina cochlearioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed kidneyvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
