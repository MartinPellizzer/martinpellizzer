Croton asper

Croton asper, commonly known as the rough croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, rough projections, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple branches and a tendency to form dense foliage.

Croton asper is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
