Cissampelos nympheaefolia

Cissampelos nympheaefolia, commonly known as the waterleaf or water spinach, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it both a useful medicinal plant and a common ornamental in humid climates.
