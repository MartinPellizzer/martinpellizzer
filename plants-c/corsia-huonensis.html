Corsia huonensis

Corsia huonensis, commonly known as the Huon Island thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Huon in Tasmania, Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Corsia huonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators.
