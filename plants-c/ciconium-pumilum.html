Ciconium pumilum

Ciconium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf ciconium, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Ciconium pumilum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
