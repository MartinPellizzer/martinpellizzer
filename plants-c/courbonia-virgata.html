Courbonia virgata

Courbonia virgata, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small white flowers. The tree produces edible, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its glossy leaves and fragrant blooms.

Courbonia virgata is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in agroforestry systems.
