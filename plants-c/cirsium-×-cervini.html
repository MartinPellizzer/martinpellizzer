Cirsium × cervini

Cirsium × cervini, commonly known as the Everlasting or Field Thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that remain intact even after the flowers have faded, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Cirsium × cervini is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and long-lasting flowers.
