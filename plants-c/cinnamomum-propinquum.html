Cinnamomum propinquum

Cinnamomum propinquum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a distinct, glossy texture. Its bark contains essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties and therapeutic uses.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
