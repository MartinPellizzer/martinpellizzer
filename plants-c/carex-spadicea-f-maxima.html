Carex spadicea f. maxima

Carex spadicea f. maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color, distinguishing it from other sedges. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
