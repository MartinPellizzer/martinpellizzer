Collaea scarlatina var. latifolia

Collaea scarlatina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved collaea, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its alpine habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
