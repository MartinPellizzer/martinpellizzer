Coniogramme falcata

Coniogramme falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright cluster. The fronds are usually dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance, with a finely serrated margin. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
