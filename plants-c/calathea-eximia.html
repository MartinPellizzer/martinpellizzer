Calathea eximia

Calathea eximia, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with patterns resembling prayer hands, hence its common name. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture and vibrant coloration that changes throughout the day. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.

Calathea eximia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
