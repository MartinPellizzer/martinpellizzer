Carex jackiana f. oxyphylla

Carex jackiana f. oxyphylla, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that often exhibit a striking golden hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
