Cinnamomum longicarpum

Cinnamomum longicarpum, commonly known as wild cinnamon or Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and oblong, dark purple berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a strong, camphor-like scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, aromatherapy, and as a natural insect repellent.
