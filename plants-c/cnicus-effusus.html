Cnicus effusus

Cnicus effusus, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cnicus effusus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
