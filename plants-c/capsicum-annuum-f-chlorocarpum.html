Capsicum annuum f. chlorocarpum

Capsicum annuum f. chlorocarpum, commonly known as the green bell pepper, is a botanical variant of the common pepper plant.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and stems that are typically green, though they may turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit is usually harvested while still green, though it can ripen to various colors depending on the variety. This form is widely cultivated for its use in cooking and as a decorative plant.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.
