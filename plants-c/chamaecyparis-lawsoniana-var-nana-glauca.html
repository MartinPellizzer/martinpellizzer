Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. nana-glauca

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. nana-glauca, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress dwarf variety, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer.

It features a rounded, bushy shape with dense, scale-like foliage that has a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
