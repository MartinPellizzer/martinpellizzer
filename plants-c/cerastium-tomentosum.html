Cerastium tomentosum

Cerastium tomentosum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along garden paths.

Cerastium tomentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
