Caesalpiniodes monospermum

Caesalpiniodes monospermum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough to the touch. It produces long, slender pods that contain a single, large, smooth seed, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

This tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
