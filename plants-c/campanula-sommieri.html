Campanula sommieri

Campanula sommieri, commonly known as the summer bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula sommieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
