Cirsium virgatum

Cirsium virgatum, commonly known as the Canada thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prickly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to spread rapidly and become invasive in many regions. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in agricultural and natural settings.
