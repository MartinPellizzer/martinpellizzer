Cerastium alpinum lusus ciliatum

Cerastium alpinum lusus ciliatum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suited to harsh climates.
