Celtis luzonica

Celtis luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon chestnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that are encased in a thin, woody husk.

Celtis luzonica is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological importance.
