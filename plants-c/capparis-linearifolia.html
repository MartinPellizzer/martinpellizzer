Capparis linearifolia

Capparis linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often fragrant, and its leaves are narrow and linear in shape. It is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their edible flowers and ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
