Campanula phrygia

Campanula phrygia, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
