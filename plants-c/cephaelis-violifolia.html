Cephaelis violifolia

Cephaelis violifolia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often found in alpine meadows.

Cephaelis violifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
