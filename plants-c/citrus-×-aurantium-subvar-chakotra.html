Citrus × aurantium subvar. chakotra

Citrus × aurantium subvar. chakotra, commonly known as Chakotra kumquat, is a citrus hybrid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The fruit's peel is rich in essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring.
