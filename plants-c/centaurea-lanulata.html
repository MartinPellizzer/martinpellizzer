Centaurea lanulata

Centaurea lanulata, commonly known as the downy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea lanulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
