Campanula urbionensis

Campanula urbionensis, commonly known as the urban bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Campanula urbionensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
