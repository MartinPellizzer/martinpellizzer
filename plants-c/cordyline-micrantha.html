Cordyline micrantha

Cordyline micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in subtropical and tropical regions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure for optimal growth.
