Capparis spinosa subsp. himalayensis

Capparis spinosa subsp.

himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan caper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible, citrus-like fruits that are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Capparaceae family.
