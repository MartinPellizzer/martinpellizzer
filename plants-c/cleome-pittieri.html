Cleome pittieri

Cleome pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant is typically found in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and prairies. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds.

Cleome pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of growth in well-drained soils.
