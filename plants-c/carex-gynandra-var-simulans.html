Carex gynandra var. simulans

Carex gynandra var. simulans, commonly known as false arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
