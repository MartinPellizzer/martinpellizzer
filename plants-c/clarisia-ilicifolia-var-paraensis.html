Clarisia ilicifolia var. paraensis

Clarisia ilicifolia var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
