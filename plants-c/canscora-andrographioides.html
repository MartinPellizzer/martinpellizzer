Canscora andrographioides

Canscora andrographioides, commonly known as the Indian bittersweet, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shady areas such as forest floors and stream banks.

Canscora andrographioides is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.
