Chirita brachytricha

Chirita brachytricha, commonly known as the short-haired chirita, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
