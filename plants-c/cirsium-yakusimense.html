Cirsium yakusimense

Cirsium yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusim thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in coarse hairs, while the flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

Cirsium yakusimense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
