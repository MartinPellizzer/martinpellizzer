Cerasiocarpum bennettii

Cerasiocarpum bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's cherry.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in alpine regions and is found in the high mountains of Central Asia. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
