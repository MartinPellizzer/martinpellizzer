Campanula ruthenica

Campanula ruthenica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula ruthenica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
