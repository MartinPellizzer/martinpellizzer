Centaurea kasakorum

Centaurea kasakorum, commonly known as the Karakorum star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a hardy adaptation to harsh climatic conditions. Its bright blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
