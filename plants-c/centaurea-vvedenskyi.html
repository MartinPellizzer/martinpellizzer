Centaurea vvedenskyi

Centaurea vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Vvedensky's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its slender stems and upright growth habit make it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

Centaurea vvedenskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
