Cissus erosa var. linearifolia

Cissus erosa var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elongated, thin leaves that are often waxy and slightly serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its rapid growth and attractive foliage.

Its ability to climb and adapt to various substrates makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
