Critoniopsis quinqueflora

Critoniopsis quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems that provide protection against herbivores and water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
