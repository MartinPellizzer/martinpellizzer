Centaurea ilvensis

Centaurea ilvensis, commonly known as the Ilven's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea ilvensis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
