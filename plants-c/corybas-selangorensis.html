Corybas selangorensis

Corybas selangorensis, commonly known as the Selangor hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flower that resembles a cup, typically ranging in color from green to brown. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a specialized structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky areas.
