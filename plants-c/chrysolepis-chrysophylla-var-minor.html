Chrysolepis chrysophylla var. minor

Chrysolepis chrysophylla var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf golden alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
