Coreopsis delphiniifolia var. chlooidea

Coreopsis delphiniifolia var. chlooidea, commonly known as the silverleaf coreopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal in native plant landscapes.
