Carduus ammophilus f. bourgeanus

Carduus ammophilus f. bourgeanus, commonly known as the coastal thistle, is a variant of the common thistle that thrives in sandy or coastal environments.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species found along shores and dunes. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with multiple stems arising from the base.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
