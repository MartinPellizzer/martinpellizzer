Choretrum glomeratum var. chrysanthum

Choretrum glomeratum var. chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden clustered choretrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and delicate floral display.
