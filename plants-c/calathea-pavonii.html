Calathea pavonii

Calathea pavonii, commonly known as the peacock plant, is a tropical foliage plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaf patterns that resemble the plumage of a peacock. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with intricate veins and a vibrant mix of green, red, and purple hues. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea pavonii is prized for its ornamental value and is often used as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
