Crocus lageniflorus subvar. albus

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. albus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
