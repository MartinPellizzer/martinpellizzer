Callisia hintoniorum

Callisia hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Callisia hintoniorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
