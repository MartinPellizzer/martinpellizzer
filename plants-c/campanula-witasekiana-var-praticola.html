Campanula witasekiana var. praticola

Campanula witasekiana var. praticola, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
