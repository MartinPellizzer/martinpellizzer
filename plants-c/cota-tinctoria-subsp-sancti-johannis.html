Cota tinctoria subsp. sancti-johannis

Cota tinctoria subsp.

sancti-johannis, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in midsummer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is well-documented, particularly for its purported antidepressant properties.
