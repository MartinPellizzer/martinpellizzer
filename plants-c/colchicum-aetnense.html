Colchicum aetnense

Colchicum aetnense, commonly known as the Aetna colchicum, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that are often surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, sunny conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
