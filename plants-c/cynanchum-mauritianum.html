Cynanchum mauritianum

Cynanchum mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius milkweed, is a climbing plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic, containing cardiac glycosides. It thrives in coastal and disturbed habitats, often found in dry, sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
