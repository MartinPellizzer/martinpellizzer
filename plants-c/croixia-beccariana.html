Croixia beccariana

Croixia beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Croixia beccariana is valued in horticulture for its striking blooms and adaptability to humid climates.
