Cereus tenuatus

Cereus tenuatus, commonly known as the slender candle cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, a feature typical of many desert cacti. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
