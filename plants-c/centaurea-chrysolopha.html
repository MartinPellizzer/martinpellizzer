Centaurea chrysolopha

Centaurea chrysolopha, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.
