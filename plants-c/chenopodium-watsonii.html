Chenopodium watsonii

Chenopodium watsonii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional Native American cuisine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Chenopodium watsonii is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
