Costularia pantopoda

Costularia pantopoda, commonly known as the mountain balsam, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its reproductive strategy involves cross-pollination by insects, and it relies on specific ecological niches for survival.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
