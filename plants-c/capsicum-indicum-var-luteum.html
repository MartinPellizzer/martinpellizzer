Capsicum indicum var. luteum

Capsicum indicum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper, is a variety of Capsicum indicum native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped fruits that mature to a bright orange or red color. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use in various cuisines.
