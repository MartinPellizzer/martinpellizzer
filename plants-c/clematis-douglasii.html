Clematis douglasii

Clematis douglasii, commonly known as Douglas clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures like trellises or fences.

Clematis douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its beauty and hardiness.
