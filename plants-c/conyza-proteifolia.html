Conyza proteifolia

Conyza proteifolia, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Conyza proteifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
