Centaurea pseudophrygia subsp. abnormis

Centaurea pseudophrygia subsp.

abnormis, commonly known as the abnormal centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often strikingly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue or purple in color.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
