Clematis alpina var. chinensis

Clematis alpina var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
