Centaurea hybrida

Centaurea hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head with ray florets. The plant has a compact growth habit, with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Centaurea hybrida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
