Centaurea halkensis

Centaurea halkensis, commonly known as the Halken's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurea halkensis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
