Crassula ankaratrensis

Crassula ankaratrensis, commonly known as the Ankaratra crassula, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
