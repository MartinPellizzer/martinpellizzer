Crossopetalum microphyllum

Crossopetalum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved crossopetalum, is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Crossopetalum microphyllum is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates.
