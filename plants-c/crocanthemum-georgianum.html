Crocanthemum georgianum

Crocanthemum georgianum, commonly known as the Georgian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open habitats such as sand dunes or disturbed areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
