Cattleya forbesii

Cattleya forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with white and yellow accents. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cattleya forbesii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
