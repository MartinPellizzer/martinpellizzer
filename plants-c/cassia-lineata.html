Cassia lineata

Cassia lineata, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces long, drooping clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Cassia lineata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod and are sometimes used as a natural dye or in herbal remedies.
