Cymbidium × holfordianum

Cymbidium × holfordianum, commonly known as the Holford's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking floral display.
