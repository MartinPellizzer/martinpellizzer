Cyathea herzogii

Cyathea herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forests or along riverbanks. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the tall, unbranched trunk.

Cyathea herzogii plays a role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
