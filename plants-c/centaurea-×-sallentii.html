Centaurea × sallentii

Centaurea × sallentii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads, which consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-blue hue due to dense pubescence.

Centaurea × sallentii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
