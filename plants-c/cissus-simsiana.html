Cissus simsiana

Cissus simsiana, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, succulent leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ornamental value.
