Coptis japonica f. viridiflora

Coptis japonica f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and striking greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes contain berberine, a compound with medicinal properties used in traditional medicine.

The varietal form 'viridiflora' is distinguished by its uniquely colored flowers compared to the typical yellow blooms of the species.
