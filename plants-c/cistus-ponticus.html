Cistus ponticus

Cistus ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic rock rose, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grey-green, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cistus ponticus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
