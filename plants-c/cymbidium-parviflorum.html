Cymbidium parviflorum

Cymbidium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with upright, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to winter gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Cymbidium parviflorum is appreciated for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
