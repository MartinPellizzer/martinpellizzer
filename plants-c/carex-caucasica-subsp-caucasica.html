Carex caucasica subsp. caucasica

Carex caucasica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
