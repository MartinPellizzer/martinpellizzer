Cucurbita pepo var. thelomela

Cucurbita pepo var. thelomela, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and smooth, pale yellow to orange skin. The plant is a vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of many cucurbits. Its fruits are typically harvested when mature, and they are used in various culinary and traditional applications.

The botanical features of this variety include its Cucurbitaceae family affiliation and its ability to thrive in warm, temperate climates.
