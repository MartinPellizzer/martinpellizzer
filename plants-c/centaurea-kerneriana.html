Centaurea kerneriana

Centaurea kerneriana, commonly known as Kerner's star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, but it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
