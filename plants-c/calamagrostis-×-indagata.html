Calamagrostis × indagata

Calamagrostis × indagata, commonly known as the fountain grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fountain-like growth habit, with upright, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add visual interest to gardens and landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Calamagrostis × indagata is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
