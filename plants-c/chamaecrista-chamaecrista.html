Chamaecrista chamaecrista

Chamaecrista chamaecrista, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and distinctive, spiny pods that turn reddish as they mature. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
