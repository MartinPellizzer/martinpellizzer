Caesalpinia ferrea

Caesalpinia ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it is often found in dry to semi-deciduous forests.

Caesalpinia ferrea is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
