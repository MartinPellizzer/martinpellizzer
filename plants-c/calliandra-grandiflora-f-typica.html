Calliandra grandiflora f. typica

Calliandra grandiflora f. typica, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
