Crepis thompsonii

Crepis thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Crepis thompsonii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
