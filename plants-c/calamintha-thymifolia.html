Calamintha thymifolia

Calamintha thymifolia, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and thyme. The plant features slender, square stems and small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in whorls. Its leaves are fragrant, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, and are often used in herbal teas.

Calamintha thymifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, including its potential antispasmodic and calming effects.
