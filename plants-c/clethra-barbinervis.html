Clethra barbinervis

Clethra barbinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Clethra barbinervis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
