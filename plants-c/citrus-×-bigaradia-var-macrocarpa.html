Citrus × bigaradia var. macrocarpa

Citrus × bigaradia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the grapefruit.

It is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy pulp that is often segmented into numerous small chambers. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and used in juices, desserts, and as a source of essential oils.

It thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.
