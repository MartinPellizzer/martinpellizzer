Ceropegia chrysochroma

Ceropegia chrysochroma, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique corona, which consists of specialized structures called trichomes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Ceropegia chrysochroma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
