Claoxylon membranaceum

Claoxylon membranaceum, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds.

This species is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
