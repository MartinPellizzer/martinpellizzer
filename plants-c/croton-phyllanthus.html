Croton phyllanthus

Croton phyllanthus, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can vary in shape and hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
