Cyperus meyenii

Cyperus meyenii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and dense, umbrella-shaped clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Cyperus meyenii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often near water bodies or in wetlands.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
