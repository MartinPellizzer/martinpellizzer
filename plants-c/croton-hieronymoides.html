Croton hieronymoides

Croton hieronymoides, commonly known as the red-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, green, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and sometimes toxic species. Croton hieronymoides produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in gardens, though it should be handled with care due to its potential toxicity.
