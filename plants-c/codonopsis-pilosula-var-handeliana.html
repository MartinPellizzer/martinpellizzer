Codonopsis pilosula var. handeliana

Codonopsis pilosula var. handeliana, commonly known as the hairy ginseng, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, similar to true ginseng.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
