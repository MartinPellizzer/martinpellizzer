Calochortus watsonii var. ambiguus

Calochortus watsonii var. ambiguus, commonly known as the Watson's mariposa lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Calochortus, which includes a variety of flowering plants commonly referred to as mariposa lilies. This particular variety is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant is known for its resilience and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
