Cracca leptostachya

Cracca leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cracca, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink to red in color, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
