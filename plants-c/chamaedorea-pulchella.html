Chamaedorea pulchella

Chamaedorea pulchella, commonly known as the silver palm or Mexican evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Chamaedorea pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
