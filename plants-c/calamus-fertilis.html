Calamus fertilis

Calamus fertilis, commonly known as the fertile calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus fertilis is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
