Crataegus × palliata

Crataegus × palliata, commonly known as the common hawthorn or European hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in jams and jellies. Crataegus × palliata is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
