Costus chinensis

Costus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese costus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which are often brightly colored, are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
