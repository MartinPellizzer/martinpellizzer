Castanea vulgaris var. japonica

Castanea vulgaris var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which mature in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

The Japanese chestnut is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary use.
