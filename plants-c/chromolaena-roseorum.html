Chromolaena roseorum

Chromolaena roseorum, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and woody. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Chromolaena roseorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
