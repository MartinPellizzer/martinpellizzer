Clematis kockiana

Clematis kockiana, commonly known as the Kock's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twisting stems that climb by means of small hooked tendrils. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Clematis kockiana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
