Cyperus felipponei

Cyperus felipponei, commonly known as the Felippone's flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to parts of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its reproductive structures are usually found in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Cyperus felipponei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
