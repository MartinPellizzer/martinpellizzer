Cytisus biflorus

Cytisus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus biflorus is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
