Cosmos scabiosoides var. oblongus

Cosmos scabiosoides var. oblongus, commonly known as the oblongleaf cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, providing a long season of visual interest in the garden.
