Cyperus cunninghamii subsp. uniflorus

Cyperus cunninghamii subsp.

uniflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
