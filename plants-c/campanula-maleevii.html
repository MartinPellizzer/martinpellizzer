Campanula maleevii

Campanula maleevii, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Campanula maleevii is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade.
