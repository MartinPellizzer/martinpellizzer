Ceropegia adscendens var. attenuata

Ceropegia adscendens var. attenuata, commonly known as the climbing ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to climb or trail.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
