Carum minutum

Carum minutum, commonly known as lesser cumin, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its umbellate inflorescences. Carum minutum is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of its family.
