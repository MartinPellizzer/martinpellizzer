Camellia japonica var. spontanea

Camellia japonica var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild Japanese camellia, is a species native to southern Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
