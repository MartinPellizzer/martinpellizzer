Cassia cuthbertsonii

Cassia cuthbertsonii, commonly known as the Cuthbertson's Cassia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in elongated, fibrous pods that split open when mature.

Cassia cuthbertsonii is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
