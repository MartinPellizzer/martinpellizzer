Carex stenolepis

Carex stenolepis, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex stenolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
