Cynanchum scoparium

Cynanchum scoparium, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its woody, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Cynanchum scoparium is adapted to various soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
