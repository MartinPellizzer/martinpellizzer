Clinopodium × conillii

Clinopodium × conillii, commonly known as the hybrid mint or creeping mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Clinopodium × conillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties.
