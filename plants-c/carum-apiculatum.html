Carum apiculatum

Carum apiculatum, commonly known as mountain caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal remedies highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
