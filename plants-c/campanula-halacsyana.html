Campanula halacsyana

Campanula halacsyana, commonly known as the Halácsy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula halacsyana is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
