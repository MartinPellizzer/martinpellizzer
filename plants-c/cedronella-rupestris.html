Cedronella rupestris

Cedronella rupestris, commonly known as the rock cedronella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cedronella rupestris is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
