Coryphantha cornuta

Coryphantha cornuta, commonly known as the horned cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Coryphantha cornuta is adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water and reduce transpiration.
