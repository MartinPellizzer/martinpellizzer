Carex cryptolepis

Carex cryptolepis, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex cryptolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
