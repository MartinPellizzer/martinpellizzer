Cerasus leveilleana f. pendula

Cerasus leveilleana f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a cascading effect. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and ornamental horticulture.
