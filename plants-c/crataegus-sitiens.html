Crataegus sitiens

Crataegus sitiens, commonly known as the Sitien hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows in dry, rocky soils.

Crataegus sitiens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
