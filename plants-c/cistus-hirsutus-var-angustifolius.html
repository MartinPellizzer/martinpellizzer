Cistus hirsutus var. angustifolius

Cistus hirsutus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rocky or sandy soils.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
