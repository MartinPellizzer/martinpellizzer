Cynoglossum heterocarpum var. mariolense

Cynoglossum heterocarpum var. mariolense, commonly known as the Mariolense bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a tongue-shaped lip, hence its genus name "Cynoglossum.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
