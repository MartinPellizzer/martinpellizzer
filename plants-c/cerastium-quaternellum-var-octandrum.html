Cerastium quaternellum var. octandrum

Cerastium quaternellum var. octandrum, commonly known as the eight-stamen chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often opposite and lobed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
