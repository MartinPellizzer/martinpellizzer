Cirsium sichuanense

Cirsium sichuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan Province in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine meadows and forest clearings.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
