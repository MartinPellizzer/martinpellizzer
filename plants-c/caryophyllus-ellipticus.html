Caryophyllus ellipticus

Caryophyllus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
