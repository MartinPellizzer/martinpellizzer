Callicarpa cathayana

Callicarpa cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
