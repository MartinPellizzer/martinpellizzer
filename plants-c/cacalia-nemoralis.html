Cacalia nemoralis

Cacalia nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cacalia nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
