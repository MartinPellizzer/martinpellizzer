Crucianella colchica

Crucianella colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian sea bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.

The species is adapted to harsh coastal environments and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.
