Clerodendrum squamatum var. rumphianum

Clerodendrum squamatum var. rumphianum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
