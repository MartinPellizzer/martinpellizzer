Campanula bolosii

Campanula bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula bolosii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
