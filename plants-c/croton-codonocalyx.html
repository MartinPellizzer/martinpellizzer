Croton codonocalyx

Croton codonocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
