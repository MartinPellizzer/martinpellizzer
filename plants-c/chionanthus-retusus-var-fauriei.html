Chionanthus retusus var. fauriei

Chionanthus retusus var. fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
