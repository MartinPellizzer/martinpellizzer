Crataegus monogyna var. rosea

Crataegus monogyna var. rosea, commonly known as the common hawthorn rosea form, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wildlife, providing food and habitat for birds and insects.
