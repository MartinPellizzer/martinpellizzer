Capparis sicula

Capparis sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Capparis sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
