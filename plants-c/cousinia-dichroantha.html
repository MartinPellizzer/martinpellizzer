Cousinia dichroantha

Cousinia dichroantha, commonly known as the two-colored fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers are pale yellow to white, often appearing in clusters.

Cousinia dichroantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
