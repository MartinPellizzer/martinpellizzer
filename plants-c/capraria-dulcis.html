Capraria dulcis

Capraria dulcis, commonly known as sweetcapra or capra flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Capraria dulcis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
