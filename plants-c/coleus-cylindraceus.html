Coleus cylindraceus

Coleus cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and vary in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are typically purple or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
