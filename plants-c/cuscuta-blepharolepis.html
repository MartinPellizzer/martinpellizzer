Cuscuta blepharolepis

Cuscuta blepharolepis, commonly known as the silverleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nourishment.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, thread-like stem that wraps around its host. The plant is native to North America and is often found on a variety of herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
