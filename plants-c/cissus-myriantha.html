Cissus myriantha

Cissus myriantha, commonly known as the climbing hedge or rock fig, is a vigorous trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cissus myriantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.
