Coccinia grandis var. quinqueangularis

Coccinia grandis var. quinqueangularis, commonly known as the five-angled bitterball, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow, cucurbit-like flowers followed by round, green, and often spiny fruits. The fruit's skin is thick and bitter, containing a sweet, edible pulp with numerous seeds.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
