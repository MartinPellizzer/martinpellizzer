Chromolaena laevigata var. submembranacea

Chromolaena laevigata var. submembranacea, commonly known as the hairy false chaff tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
