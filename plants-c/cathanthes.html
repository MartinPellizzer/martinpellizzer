Cathanthes

Cathanthes, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves.

Native to various regions in Africa and the Mediterranean, Cathanthes species are valued for their ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting night-active wildlife.
