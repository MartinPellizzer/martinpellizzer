Cynodon maximus

Cynodon maximus, commonly known as Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is widely used for lawns, sports fields, and erosion control due to its durability and rapid growth.

Cynodon maximus is also known for its high tolerance to heat, drought, and grazing.
