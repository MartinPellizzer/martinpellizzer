Coccothrinax ekmanii

Coccothrinax ekmanii, commonly known as the Key West palm, is a flowering plant native to the Florida Keys and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small, slender palm that typically grows to a height of about 6 to 10 feet. The plant has a single trunk with smooth, grayish bark and produces feathery, compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are yellow when mature.

This species is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shore.
