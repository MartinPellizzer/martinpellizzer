Caesalpinia jayabo

Caesalpinia jayabo, commonly known as the Jayabo tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to red, flame-like flowers that appear in clusters. The tree has bipinnate leaves, and its pods are elongated, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
