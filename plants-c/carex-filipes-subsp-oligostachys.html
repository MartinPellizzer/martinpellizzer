Carex filipes subsp. oligostachys

Carex filipes subsp.

oligostachys, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shady, damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and forest floors.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
