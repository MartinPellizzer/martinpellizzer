Capsella bursa-pastoris oviculata

Capsella bursa-pastoris oviculata, commonly known as the common weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. It has small, white or purple flowers and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
