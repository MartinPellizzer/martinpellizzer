Chamaedorea pavoniana

Chamaedorea pavoniana, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elegant, feather-like fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and prefers bright, indirect light. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Chamaedorea pavoniana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and air-purifying qualities.
