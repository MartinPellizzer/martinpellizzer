Cyperus pennatiformis var. bryanii

Cyperus pennatiformis var. bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in disturbed or saturated soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
