Carpinus kawakamii

Carpinus kawakamii, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
