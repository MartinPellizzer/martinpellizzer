Campanula pallasiana

Campanula pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula pallasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
