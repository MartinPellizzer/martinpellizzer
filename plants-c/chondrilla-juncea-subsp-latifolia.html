Chondrilla juncea subsp. latifolia

Chondrilla juncea subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf chondrilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
