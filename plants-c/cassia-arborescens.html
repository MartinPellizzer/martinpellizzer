Cassia arborescens

Cassia arborescens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
