Centella cordifolia

Centella cordifolia, commonly known as Asiatic pennywort, is a creeping herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Centella cordifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
