Calanthe pauciverrucosa

Calanthe pauciverrucosa, commonly known as the few-warted calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

Calanthe pauciverrucosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
