Cineraria helenitis

Cineraria helenitis, commonly known as the Greek cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms in the spring.

Cineraria helenitis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
