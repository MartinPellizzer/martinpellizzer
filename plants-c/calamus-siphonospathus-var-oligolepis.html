Calamus siphonospathus var. oligolepis

Calamus siphonospathus var. oligolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and long, narrow leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are typically followed by elongated, slender fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
