Crocus heuffelianus subsp. heuffelianus

Crocus heuffelianus subsp.

heuffelianus, commonly known as the Heuffelianus crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps.

This perennial herb produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
