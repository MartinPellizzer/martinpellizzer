Camellia atuberculata

Camellia atuberculata, commonly known as the smooth-lobed camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces striking, double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia atuberculata is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
