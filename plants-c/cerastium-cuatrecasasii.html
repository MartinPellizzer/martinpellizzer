Cerastium cuatrecasasii

Cerastium cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
