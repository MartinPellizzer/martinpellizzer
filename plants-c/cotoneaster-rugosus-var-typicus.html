Cotoneaster rugosus var. typicus

Cotoneaster rugosus var. typicus, commonly known as the rough cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Cotoneaster rugosus var. typicus is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
