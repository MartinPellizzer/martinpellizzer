Cryptocarya vidalii

Cryptocarya vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cryptocarya vidalii is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
