Canthium ankotekonense

Canthium ankotekonense, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Madagascar. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathium structures, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
