Coriandrum cynapium

Coriandrum cynapium, commonly known as cilantro or coriander, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant produces small, round seeds that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
