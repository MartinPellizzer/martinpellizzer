Cereus fouachianus

Cereus fouachianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. These flowers are typically followed by a red, fleshy fruit.

Cereus fouachianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
