Cirsium × okamotoi

Cirsium × okamotoi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
