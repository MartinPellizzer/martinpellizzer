Carya × brownii

Carya × brownii, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Carya cathayensis and Carya oleifera.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant exhibits a range of morphological variations, including differences in leaf shape and fruit size. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The botanical study of Carya × brownii provides insights into hybridization patterns in hickory species.
