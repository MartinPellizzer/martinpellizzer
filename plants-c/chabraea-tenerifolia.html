Chabraea tenerifolia

Chabraea tenerifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
