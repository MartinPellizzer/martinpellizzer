Calliandra matisiana

Calliandra matisiana, commonly known as the Matisiana legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Calliandra matisiana is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a source of forage for livestock.
