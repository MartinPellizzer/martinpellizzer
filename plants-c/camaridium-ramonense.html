Camaridium ramonense

Camaridium ramonense, commonly known as the Ramon's camara, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Camaridium ramonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical significance also lies in its role within local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
