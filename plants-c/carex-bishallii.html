Carex bishallii

Carex bishallii, commonly known as the Bishallii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Carex bishallii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
