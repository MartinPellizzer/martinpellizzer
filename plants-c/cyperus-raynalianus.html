Cyperus raynalianus

Cyperus raynalianus, commonly known as the marsh clubmoss, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, three-lobed fruits.

Cyperus raynalianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
