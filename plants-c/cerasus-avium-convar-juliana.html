Cerasus avium convar. juliana

Cerasus avium convar. juliana, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant colors in the fall.
