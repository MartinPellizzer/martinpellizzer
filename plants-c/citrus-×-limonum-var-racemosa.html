Citrus × limonum var. racemosa

Citrus × limonum var. racemosa, commonly known as the lemon tree variety racemosa, is a botanical variant of the lemon plant characterized by its clustered fruiting pattern.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is native to regions with warm climates. The plant features broad, glossy leaves and produces small, yellow citrus fruits that are typically used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its botanical structure includes a strong, aromatic scent and a high concentration of essential oils in its peel.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often cultivated in subtropical regions for its fruit and ornamental qualities.
