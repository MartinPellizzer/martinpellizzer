Centaurea thasia

Centaurea thasia, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads.

Centaurea thasia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
