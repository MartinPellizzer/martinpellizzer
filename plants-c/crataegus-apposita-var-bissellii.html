Crataegus apposita var. bissellii

Crataegus apposita var. bissellii, commonly known as the Bissell's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins, and turn yellow in the fall.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or along stream banks.
