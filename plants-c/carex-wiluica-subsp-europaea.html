Carex wiluica subsp. europaea

Carex wiluica subsp.

europaea, commonly known as European sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
