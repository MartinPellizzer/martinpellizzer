Camara sellowiana

Camara sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. It produces berries that start green and ripen to a deep red, containing toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
