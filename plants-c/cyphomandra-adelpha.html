Cyphomandra adelpha

Cyphomandra adelpha, commonly known as the golden apple guava.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in culinary applications.
