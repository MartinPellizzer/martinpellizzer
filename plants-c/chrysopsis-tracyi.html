Chrysopsis tracyi

Chrysopsis tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
