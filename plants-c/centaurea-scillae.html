Centaurea scillae

Centaurea scillae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny leaves, which are adaptations to its coastal habitat. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea scillae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
