Centaurea paniculata var. olympica

Centaurea paniculata var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
