Crucifera danica

Crucifera danica, commonly known as the Danish crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe, it often grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
