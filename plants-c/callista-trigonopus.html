Callista trigonopus

Callista trigonopus, commonly known as the three-lobed rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
