Crotalaria poeppigii

Crotalaria poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive pod-like seed capsules. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge.

Crotalaria poeppigii is known for its role in ecological systems, particularly in supporting pollinators and aiding in soil stabilization.
