Carex senanensis

Carex senanensis, commonly known as the Senanensis sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex senanensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
