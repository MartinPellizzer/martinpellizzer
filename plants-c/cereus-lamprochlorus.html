Cereus lamprochlorus

Cereus lamprochlorus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a type of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant produces white or yellow flowers that bloom at night, a feature that aids in pollination by nocturnal insects. Its structure includes areoles, which are small, raised areas on the stem where spines, flowers, and branches develop.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
