Coleus scruposus

Coleus scruposus, commonly known as the mossy coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to maroon and purple hues. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured, slightly fuzzy surface. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add visual interest to garden landscapes.
