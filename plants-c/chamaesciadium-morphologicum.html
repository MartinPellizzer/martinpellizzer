Chamaesciadium morphologicum

Chamaesciadium morphologicum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, providing a striking visual contrast to its floral display. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
