Clematis gentianoides var. procumbens

Clematis gentianoides var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It features attractive blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a trailing growth habit, with slender stems that can spread along the ground or climb low structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
