Cerinthe glabra var. pyrenaica

Cerinthe glabra var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region.

It features striking, papery orange-red flowers with prominent, horn-like seed pods. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
