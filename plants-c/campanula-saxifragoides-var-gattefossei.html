Campanula saxifragoides var. gattefossei

Campanula saxifragoides var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Gattefosse's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to its rocky and alpine habitats. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
