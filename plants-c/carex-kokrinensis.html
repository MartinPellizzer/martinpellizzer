Carex kokrinensis

Carex kokrinensis, commonly known as the Kokrin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or marshy habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex kokrinensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
