Cavendishia leucantha

Cavendishia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and green.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
