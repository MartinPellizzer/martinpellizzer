Colchicum laetum

Colchicum laetum, commonly known as the meadow colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, waxy, purple or pinkish flowers that are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, meadow-like environments.

Colchicum laetum is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
