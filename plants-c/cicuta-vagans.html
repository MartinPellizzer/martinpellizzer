Cicuta vagans

Cicuta vagans, commonly known as water hemlock, is a toxic flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers. The plant contains potent toxins, primarily cicutoxin, which can be lethal if ingested. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a distinctive musty odor.

It typically grows in wet, disturbed soils along stream banks and in marshy areas.
