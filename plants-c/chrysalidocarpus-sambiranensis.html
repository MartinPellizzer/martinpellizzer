Chrysalidocarpus sambiranensis

Chrysalidocarpus sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
