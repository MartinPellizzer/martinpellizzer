Ceropegia intracolor

Ceropegia intracolor, commonly known as the rainbow vine, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, purple, and even red. The plant produces distinctive, waxy flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as flies and moths. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ceropegia intracolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and unique appearance.
