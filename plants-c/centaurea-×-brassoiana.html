Centaurea × brassoiana

Centaurea × brassoiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × brassoiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
