Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. graveolens

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the bitterleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-gold foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a strong, often unpleasant odor, which is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The subspecies graveolens is notable for its distinct chemical composition, including compounds that contribute to its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
