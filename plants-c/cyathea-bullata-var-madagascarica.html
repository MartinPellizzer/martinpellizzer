Cyathea bullata var. madagascarica

Cyathea bullata var. madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar tree fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
