Casearia spinosa

Casearia spinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits resembling small cherries. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its adaptability to different climates.
