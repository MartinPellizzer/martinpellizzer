Colysis adfine

Colysis adfine, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Colysis adfine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
