Ceranthera ilicifolia

Ceranthera ilicifolia, commonly known as the holly-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant orchid flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a resemblance to holly leaves, giving it its common name.

Ceranthera ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
