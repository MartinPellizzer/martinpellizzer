Carex foenea var. xerantica

Carex foenea var. xerantica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is adapted to wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
