Cyperus griffithianus

Cyperus griffithianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a distinctive silver-gray sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets that are often reddish in color.

Cyperus griffithianus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
