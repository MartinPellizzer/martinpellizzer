Calyptrocoryne wightii

Calyptrocoryne wightii, commonly known as the Wight's cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being ovoid and woody. Native to the Western Ghats of India, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
