Carex × firmior

Carex × firmior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
