Carex alligata

Carex alligata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Carex alligata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
