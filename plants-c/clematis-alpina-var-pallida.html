Clematis alpina var. pallida

Clematis alpina var. pallida, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with trifoliate leaves that have a velvety texture.

Clematis alpina var. pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
