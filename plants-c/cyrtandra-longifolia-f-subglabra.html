Cyrtandra longifolia f. subglabra

Cyrtandra longifolia f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth cyrtandra, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green to white in color, often with a slight reddish tinge. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
