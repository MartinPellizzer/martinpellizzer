Cissus elliptica

Cissus elliptica, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or Indian nettle, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Cissus elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
