Carex nemostachys

Carex nemostachys, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. Native to moist, shaded environments, it thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
