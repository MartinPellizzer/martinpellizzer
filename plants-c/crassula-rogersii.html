Crassula rogersii

Crassula rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring or summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Crassula rogersii is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
