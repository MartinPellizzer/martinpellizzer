Crassocephalum teysmannianum

Crassocephalum teysmannianum, commonly known as the African blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
