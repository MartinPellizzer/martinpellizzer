Calamagrostis longiflora

Calamagrostis longiflora, commonly known as the silvergrass or giant reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more silvery as the plant matures.

Calamagrostis longiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
