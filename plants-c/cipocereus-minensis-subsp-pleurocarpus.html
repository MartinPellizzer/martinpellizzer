Cipocereus minensis subsp. pleurocarpus

Cipocereus minensis subsp.

pleurocarpus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a succulent plant characterized by its segmented, jointed stems that form a branching structure.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
