Centaurea carthamus

Centaurea carthamus, commonly known as safflower, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flower heads composed of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Safflower is known for its medicinal and agricultural uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
