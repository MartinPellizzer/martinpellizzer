Crucifera montana

Crucifera montana, commonly known as mountain crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a dry, segmented pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
