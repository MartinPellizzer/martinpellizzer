Convallaria odorata

Convallaria odorata, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagales order and the Convallariaceae family, characterized by its fragrant, small, greenish-white flowers. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is known for its pleasant, hay-like aroma.

Convallaria odorata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
