Carex rafflesiana

Carex rafflesiana, commonly known as the Raffles' sedge, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex rafflesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
