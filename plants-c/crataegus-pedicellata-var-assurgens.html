Crataegus pedicellata var. assurgens

Crataegus pedicellata var. assurgens, commonly known as the rose dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with spines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
