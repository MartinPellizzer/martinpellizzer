Campanula tenerrima

Campanula tenerrima, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
