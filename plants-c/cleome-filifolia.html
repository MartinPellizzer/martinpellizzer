Cleome filifolia

Cleome filifolia, commonly known as the filamentous cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome filifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
