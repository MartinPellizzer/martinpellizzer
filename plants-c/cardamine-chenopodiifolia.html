Cardamine chenopodiifolia

Cardamine chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the sandstone watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or sandy substrates.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
