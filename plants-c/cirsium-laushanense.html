Cirsium laushanense

Cirsium laushanense, commonly known as the Laushan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, Cirsium laushanense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
