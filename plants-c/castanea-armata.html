Castanea armata

Castanea armata, commonly known as the Mexican chestnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fagaceae.

It is native to central Mexico and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, and it relies on wind pollination.

The nuts are an important food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
