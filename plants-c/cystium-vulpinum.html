Cystium vulpinum

Cystium vulpinum, commonly known as red sanders, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Cistaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, hard, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
