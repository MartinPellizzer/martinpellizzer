Cyphostemma paucidentatum subsp. paucidentatum

Cyphostemma paucidentatum subsp.

paucidentatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated, yellow fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
