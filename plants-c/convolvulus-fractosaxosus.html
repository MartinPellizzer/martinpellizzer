Convolvulus fractosaxosus

Convolvulus fractosaxosus, commonly known as the rock convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to arid and rocky regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on xerophytic adaptations and ecological resilience.
