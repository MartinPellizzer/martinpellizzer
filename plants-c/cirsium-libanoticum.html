Cirsium libanoticum

Cirsium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. Its scientific name reflects its geographic origin, with "libanoticum" referring to Lebanon.

Cirsium libanoticum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
