Chrysanthemum setabense

Chrysanthemum setabense, commonly known as the Setabense chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
