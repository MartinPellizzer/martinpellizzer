Convolvulus pileatus

Convolvulus pileatus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Convolvulus pileatus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
