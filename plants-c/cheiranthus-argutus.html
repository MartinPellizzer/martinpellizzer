Cheiranthus argutus

Cheiranthus argutus, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cheiranthus argutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
