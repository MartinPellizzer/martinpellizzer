Cirsium buchwaldii

Cirsium buchwaldii, commonly known as the Buchwald's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, rough, gray-green leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cirsium buchwaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
