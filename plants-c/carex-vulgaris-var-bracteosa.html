Carex vulgaris var. bracteosa

Carex vulgaris var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
