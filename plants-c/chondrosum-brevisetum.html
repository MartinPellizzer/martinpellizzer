Chondrosum brevisetum

Chondrosum brevisetum, commonly known as the short-stalked cushion moss, is a small, mat-forming moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and dark green to reddish-brown stems. The leaves are short and pointed, with a smooth or slightly rough surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like sporangia for reproduction. It is typically found in rocky crevices, under logs, or in damp forest soils.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
