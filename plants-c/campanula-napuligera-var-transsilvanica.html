Campanula napuligera var. transsilvanica

Campanula napuligera var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
