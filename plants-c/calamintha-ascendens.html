Calamintha ascendens

Calamintha ascendens, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme. Calamintha ascendens is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
