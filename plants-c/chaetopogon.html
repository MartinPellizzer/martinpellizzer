Chaetopogon

Chaetopogon, commonly known as the grassveld grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their tufted growth habit and hairy leaf surfaces. Chaetopogon species typically have slender, cylindrical stems and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as forage for livestock.
