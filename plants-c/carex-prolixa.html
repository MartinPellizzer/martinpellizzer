Carex prolixa

Carex prolixa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex prolixa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
