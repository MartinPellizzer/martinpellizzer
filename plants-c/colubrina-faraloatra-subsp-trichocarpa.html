Colubrina faraloatra subsp. trichocarpa

Colubrina faraloatra subsp.

trichocarpa, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
