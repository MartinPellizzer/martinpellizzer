Calandrinia subverticillata

Calandrinia subverticillata, commonly known as the mountain sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
