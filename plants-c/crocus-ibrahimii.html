Crocus ibrahimii

Crocus ibrahimii, commonly known as the Ibrahim's crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its corm is small and serves as the storage organ for nutrients.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
