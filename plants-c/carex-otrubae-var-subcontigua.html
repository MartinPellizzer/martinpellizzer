Carex otrubae var. subcontigua

Carex otrubae var. subcontigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often green or brown in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in temperate regions.

This sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
