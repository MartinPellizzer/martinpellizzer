Crocus moesiacus

Crocus moesiacus, commonly known as the Moesia crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often emerging in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are borne on short, hairy stems. Its bulbs are perennial and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

The Moesia crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
