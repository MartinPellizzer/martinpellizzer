Cissus burchellii

Cissus burchellii, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a vigorous trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cissus burchellii is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal.
