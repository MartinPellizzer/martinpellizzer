Chionanthus pyriformis

Chionanthus pyriformis, commonly known as the fragrant chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Chionanthus pyriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
