Crocus karsianus

Crocus karsianus, commonly known as the Karsianus crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crocus karsianus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
