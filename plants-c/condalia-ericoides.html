Condalia ericoides

Condalia ericoides, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Condalia ericoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
