Ciminalis grandiflora

Ciminalis grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora ciminalis, is a flowering plant characterized by its large, showy blooms.

It belongs to the genus Ciminalis, which is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant floral displays.

Its flowers can range in color from white to purple, adding a striking visual element to any landscape.
