Crotalaria trichophora

Crotalaria trichophora, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
