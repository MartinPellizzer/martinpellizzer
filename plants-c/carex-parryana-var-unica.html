Carex parryana var. unica

Carex parryana var. unica, commonly known as the Parry's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
