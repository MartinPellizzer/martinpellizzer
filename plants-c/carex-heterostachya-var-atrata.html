Carex heterostachya var. atrata

Carex heterostachya var. atrata, commonly known as black-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, grass-like foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
