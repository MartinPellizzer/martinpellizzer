Clematis pinchuanensis var. pinchuanensis

Clematis pinchuanensis var. pinchuanensis, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are characterized by a strong, woody structure with thorn-like projections.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
