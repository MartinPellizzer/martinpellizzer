Colutea porphyrogramma

Colutea porphyrogramma, commonly known as the purple-horned vervain, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
