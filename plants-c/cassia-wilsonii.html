Cassia wilsonii

Cassia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, flat pods that split open when mature.

Cassia wilsonii is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
