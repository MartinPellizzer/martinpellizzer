Campanula rotundifolia f. pygmaea

Campanula rotundifolia f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically smaller than those of the species. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its delicate appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
