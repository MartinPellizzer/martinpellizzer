Carex borbonica

Carex borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Carex borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
