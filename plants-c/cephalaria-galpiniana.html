Cephalaria galpiniana

Cephalaria galpiniana, commonly known as the Galpin's cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
