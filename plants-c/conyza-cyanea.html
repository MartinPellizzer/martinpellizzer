Conyza cyanea

Conyza cyanea, commonly known as the blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Conyza cyanea is also known for its wind-dispersed seeds, which are enclosed in a pappus, allowing them to travel long distances.
