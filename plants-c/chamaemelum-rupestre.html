Chamaemelum rupestre

Chamaemelum rupestre, commonly known as rock chamomile, is a perennial herb native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with white ray florets. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in limestone areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The essential oils derived from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
