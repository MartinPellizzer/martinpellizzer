Coronilla speciosa

Coronilla speciosa, commonly known as the showy crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Coronilla speciosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
