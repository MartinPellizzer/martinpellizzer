Cereus vagans

Cereus vagans, commonly known as the wandering candle, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers, which bloom at night, are large, white, and emit a strong fragrance.

Cereus vagans is often cultivated for its striking appearance and nocturnal blooms.
