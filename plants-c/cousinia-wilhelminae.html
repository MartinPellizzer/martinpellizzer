Cousinia wilhelminae

Cousinia wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmina's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia wilhelminae produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
