Crataegus × fretalis

Crataegus × fretalis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, thorny branches and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it is often used in hedgerows for its ornamental value.

Crataegus × fretalis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
