Conyza saligna

Conyza saligna, commonly known as woodland fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in moist to mesic habitats.

Conyza saligna is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
