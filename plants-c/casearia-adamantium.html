Casearia adamantium

Casearia adamantium, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.
