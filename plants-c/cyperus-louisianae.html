Cyperus louisianae

Cyperus louisianae, commonly known as the Louisiana flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Cyperus louisianae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
