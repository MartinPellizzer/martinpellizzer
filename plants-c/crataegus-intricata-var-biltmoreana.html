Crataegus intricata var. biltmoreana

Crataegus intricata var. biltmoreana, commonly known as the Biltmore hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it often has thorny branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
