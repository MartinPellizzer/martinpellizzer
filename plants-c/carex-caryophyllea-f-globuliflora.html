Carex caryophyllea f. globuliflora

Carex caryophyllea f. globuliflora, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes or along water edges. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique appearance and is often used in water garden settings.
