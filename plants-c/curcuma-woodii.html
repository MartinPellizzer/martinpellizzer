Curcuma woodii

Curcuma woodii, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, aromatic rhizomes that are rich in curcuminoids, giving it its distinctive yellow color. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching up to 1 meter in length.

The flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, are borne on tall, slender inflorescences and are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
