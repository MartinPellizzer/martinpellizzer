Cardamine legionensis

Cardamine legionensis, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Cardamine legionensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
