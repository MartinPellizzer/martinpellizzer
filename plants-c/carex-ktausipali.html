Carex ktausipali

Carex ktausipali, commonly known as the Ktausipali sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex ktausipali is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
