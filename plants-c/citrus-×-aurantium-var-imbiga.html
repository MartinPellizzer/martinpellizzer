Citrus × aurantium var. imbiga

Citrus × aurantium var. imbiga, commonly known as the pomelo, is a large citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, juicy pulp. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
