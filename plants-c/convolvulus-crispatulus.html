Convolvulus crispatulus

Convolvulus crispatulus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Convolvulus crispatulus is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
