Citharexylum surrectum

Citharexylum surrectum, commonly known as the honeywood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual beauty.
