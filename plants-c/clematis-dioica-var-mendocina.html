Clematis dioica var. mendocina

Clematis dioica var. mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino clematis, is a flowering vine native to California.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive, two-part fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal climates.
