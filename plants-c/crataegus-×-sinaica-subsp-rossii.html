Crataegus × sinaica subsp. rossii

Crataegus × sinaica subsp.

rossii, commonly known as the Sinai hawthorn, is a hybrid plant species native to the Middle East, particularly found in the Sinai Peninsula. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
