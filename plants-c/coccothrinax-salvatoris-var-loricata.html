Coccothrinax salvatoris var. loricata

Coccothrinax salvatoris var. loricata, commonly known as the Cuban palm or Loricata palm, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
