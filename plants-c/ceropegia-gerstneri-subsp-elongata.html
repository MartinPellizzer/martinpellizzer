Ceropegia gerstneri subsp. elongata

Ceropegia gerstneri subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink or white.

The plant has thick, trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
