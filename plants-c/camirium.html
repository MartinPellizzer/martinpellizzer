Camirium

Camirium, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on tall, slender stalks rising from broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in still water, often found in ponds and marshes. Its roots are used in traditional medicine, while its seeds are a source of edible oil.

The camirium holds cultural and spiritual significance in many Asian societies.
