Caprifolium californicum

Caprifolium californicum, commonly known as the California honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and along streambanks. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Caprifolium californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
