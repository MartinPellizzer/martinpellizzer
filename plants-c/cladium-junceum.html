Cladium junceum

Cladium junceum, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like blades that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas. Its narrow, linear leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cladium junceum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
