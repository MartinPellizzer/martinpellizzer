Cyphostemma bidgoodiae

Cyphostemma bidgoodiae, commonly known as the Bidgood's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
