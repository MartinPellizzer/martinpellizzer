Carex ericetorum proles approximata

Carex ericetorum proles approximata, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, triangular spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are usually arranged in dense, tufted clusters.
