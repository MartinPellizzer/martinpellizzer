Capparis spinosa

Capparis spinosa, commonly known as the flowering shrub or common caper, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems, thorny branches, and small, green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers followed by spherical, green fruit that turns red when mature. The edible flower buds and young leaves are used in various cuisines, particularly in Mediterranean cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Capparaceae family and is known for its unique, pungent aroma.
