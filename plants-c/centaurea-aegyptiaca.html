Centaurea aegyptiaca

Centaurea aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea aegyptiaca is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, arid environments.
