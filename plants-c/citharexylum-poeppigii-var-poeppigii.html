Citharexylum poeppigii var. poeppigii

Citharexylum poeppigii var. poeppigii, commonly known as the fragrant citharexylum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and horticultural research.
