Cinnamomum ehrenbergii

Cinnamomum ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark green leaves. Its bark is harvested for its aromatic properties, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
