Cordia chamaedrifolia

Cordia chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
