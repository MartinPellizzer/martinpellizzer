Cassia senna var. obtusata

Cassia senna var. obtusata, commonly known as sickle senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
