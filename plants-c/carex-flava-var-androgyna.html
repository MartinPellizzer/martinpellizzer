Carex flava var. androgyna

Carex flava var. androgyna, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
