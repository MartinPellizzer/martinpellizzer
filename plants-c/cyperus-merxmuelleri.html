Cyperus merxmuelleri

Cyperus merxmuelleri, commonly known as the silver grass or Mexican flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Cyperus merxmuelleri is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
