Ceratopetalum apetalum var. microphyllum

Ceratopetalum apetalum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf mountain heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical structure is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions.

The variety microphyllum is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to the typical form of Ceratopetalum apetalum.
