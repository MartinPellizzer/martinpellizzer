Clematis pogonandra var. pogonandra

Clematis pogonandra var. pogonandra, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support, such as trellises or fences, to climb and flourish.
