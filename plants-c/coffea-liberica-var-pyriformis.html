Coffea liberica var. pyriformis

Coffea liberica var. pyriformis, commonly known as the African coffee or the West African coffee, is a variant of the Liberica coffee species.

It is native to West Africa and is characterized by its large, elongated, and often pyriform (pear-shaped) berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and dark green, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

This variety is known for its unique flavor profile, which includes notes of spice and fruit, making it a distinctive choice for specialty coffee enthusiasts.
