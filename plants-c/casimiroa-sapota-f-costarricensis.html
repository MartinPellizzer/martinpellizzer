Casimiroa sapota f. costarricensis

Casimiroa sapota f. costarricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sapote, is a variant of the sapote tree native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically round, with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy pulp that contains a single large seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth.
