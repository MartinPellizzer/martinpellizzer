Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp. robustum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often resembling those of oregano, hence part of its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
