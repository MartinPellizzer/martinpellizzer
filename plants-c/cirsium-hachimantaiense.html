Cirsium hachimantaiense

Cirsium hachimantaiense, commonly known as the Hachiman Island thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves.

Cirsium hachimantaiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
