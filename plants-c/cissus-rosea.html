Cissus rosea

Cissus rosea, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a species of climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that resemble silver coins, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus rosea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
