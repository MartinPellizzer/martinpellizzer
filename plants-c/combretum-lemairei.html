Combretum lemairei

Combretum lemairei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds.

Combretum lemairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
