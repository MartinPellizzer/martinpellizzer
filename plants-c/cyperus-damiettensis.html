Cyperus damiettensis

Cyperus damiettensis, commonly known as the Damietta flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowering spikes are usually pale to brown in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Cyperus damiettensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
