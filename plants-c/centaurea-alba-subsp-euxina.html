Centaurea alba subsp. euxina

Centaurea alba subsp.

euxina, commonly known as the Crimean cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and bright yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
