Cineraria tussilaginea

Cineraria tussilaginea, commonly known as the hoary cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive addition to Mediterranean landscapes.
