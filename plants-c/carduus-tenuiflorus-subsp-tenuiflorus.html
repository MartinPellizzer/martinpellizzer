Carduus tenuiflorus subsp. tenuiflorus

Carduus tenuiflorus subsp.

tenuiflorus, commonly known as slenderflower thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

The plant features a robust, spiny stem with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster called a head, which is surrounded by bracts.

The species is characterized by its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
