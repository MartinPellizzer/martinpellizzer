Campanula patula var. platyphylla

Campanula patula var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are broad and flat, distinguishing it from other campanula varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
