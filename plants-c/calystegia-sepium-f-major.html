Calystegia sepium f. major

Calystegia sepium f. major, commonly known as the greater bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed species.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
