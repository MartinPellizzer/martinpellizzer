Cleome parviflora subsp. psoraleifolia

Cleome parviflora subsp.

psoraleifolia, commonly known as the smallflower cleome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
