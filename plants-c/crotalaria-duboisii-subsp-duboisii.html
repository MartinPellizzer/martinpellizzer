Crotalaria duboisii subsp. duboisii

Crotalaria duboisii subsp.

duboisii, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces elongated seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
