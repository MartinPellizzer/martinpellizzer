Corysanthes despectans

Corysanthes despectans, commonly known as the weeping corysanthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that hang in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to cream in color and bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.
