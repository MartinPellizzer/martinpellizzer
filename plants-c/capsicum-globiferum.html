Capsicum globiferum

Capsicum globiferum, commonly known as the giant capsicum or the Brazilian bell pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often brightly colored fruits that can range from yellow to red. The plant is a herbaceous shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. Its fruit is typically used in local cuisines and has a mild to spicy flavor depending on the variety.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
