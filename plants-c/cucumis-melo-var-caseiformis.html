Cucumis melo var. caseiformis

Cucumis melo var. caseiformis, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique variety of melon characterized by its distinctive spiky, green exterior.

The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and typically grows as a climbing vine with broad, lobed leaves. Its fruit develops from a single flower and matures to a smooth, yellowish skin with a thick, edible pulp. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
