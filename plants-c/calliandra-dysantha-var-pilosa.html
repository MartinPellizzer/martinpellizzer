Calliandra dysantha var. pilosa

Calliandra dysantha var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant features pinnate leaves with hairy textures, which contribute to its common name. It produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it an ornamental plant in gardens.

The hairy calliandra is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
