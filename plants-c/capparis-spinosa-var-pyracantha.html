Capparis spinosa var. pyracantha

Capparis spinosa var. pyracantha, commonly known as the pyracantha caper, is a thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often tart. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
