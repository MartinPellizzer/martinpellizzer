Calandrinia cachinalensis

Calandrinia cachinalensis, commonly known as the Chilean portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and coastal environments. The plant produces small, fleshy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its succulent leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve water.

Calandrinia cachinalensis is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
