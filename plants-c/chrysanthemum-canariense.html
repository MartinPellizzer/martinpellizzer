Chrysanthemum canariense

Chrysanthemum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale purple petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with greyish-green, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
