Croton cueroensis

Croton cueroensis, commonly known as the Cuero croton, is a shrub native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by three-lobed, orange to red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Croton cueroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
