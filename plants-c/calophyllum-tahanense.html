Calophyllum tahanense

Calophyllum tahanense, commonly known as the Tahanense Mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its potential medicinal properties.
