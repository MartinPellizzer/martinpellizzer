Cassia pulchra

Cassia pulchra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish color.

Cassia pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
