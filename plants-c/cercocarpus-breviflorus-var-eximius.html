Cercocarpus breviflorus var. eximius

Cercocarpus breviflorus var. eximius, commonly known as the Sierra foothill curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and grayish bark that peels in thin strips. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its foliage is aromatic and may have some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
