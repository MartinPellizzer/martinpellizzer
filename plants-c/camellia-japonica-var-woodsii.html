Camellia japonica var. woodsii

Camellia japonica var. woodsii, commonly known as the Woodsii Camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
