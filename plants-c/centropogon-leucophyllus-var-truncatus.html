Centropogon leucophyllus var. truncatus

Centropogon leucophyllus var. truncatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.
