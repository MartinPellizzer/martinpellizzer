Celmisia praestans

Celmisia praestans, commonly known as the silver tufted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-white foliage and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Celmisia praestans is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is an important component of New Zealand's alpine flora.
