Coleus insignis

Coleus insignis, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep purples to rich reds and greens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
