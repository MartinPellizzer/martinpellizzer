Centaurea sphaerocephala subsp. lusitanica

Centaurea sphaerocephala subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a grayish-green hue.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
