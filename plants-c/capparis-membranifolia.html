Capparis membranifolia

Capparis membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
