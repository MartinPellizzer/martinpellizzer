Carex bicknellii

Carex bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by three-sided, spike-like fruits.

Carex bicknellii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
