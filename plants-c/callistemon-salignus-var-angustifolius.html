Callistemon salignus var. angustifolius

Callistemon salignus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking red or pink cylindrical flower spikes, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
