Crataegus chrysocarpa var. praecox

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. praecox, commonly known as the early yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange fruits, which are typically harvested in early summer, giving it its common name. The plant features simple, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and its flowers are small, white, and appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
