Combretum cacoucia

Combretum cacoucia, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Combretum cacoucia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
