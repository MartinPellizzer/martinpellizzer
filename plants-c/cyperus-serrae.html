Cyperus serrae

Cyperus serrae, commonly known as the serrated flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically inconspicuous and lack showy petals. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and often grows in wet or marshy habitats.

Cyperus serrae is adapted to moist environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
