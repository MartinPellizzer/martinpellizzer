Cipura paludosa subsp. caatingensis

Cipura paludosa subsp.

caatingensis, commonly known as the caatinga cipura, is a flowering plant native to northeastern Brazil. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wetland and swampy habitats.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
