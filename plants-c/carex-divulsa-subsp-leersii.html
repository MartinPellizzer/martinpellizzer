Carex divulsa subsp. leersii

Carex divulsa subsp.

leersii, commonly known as the Leers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
