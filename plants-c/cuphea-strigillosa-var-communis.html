Cuphea strigillosa var. communis

Cuphea strigillosa var. communis, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
