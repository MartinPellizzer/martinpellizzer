Cirsium sieversii

Cirsium sieversii, commonly known as Sievers' thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium sieversii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
