Combretum aculeatum f. nudiflorum

Combretum aculeatum f. nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower combretum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that dehisce to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
