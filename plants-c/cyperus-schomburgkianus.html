Cyperus schomburgkianus

Cyperus schomburgkianus, commonly known as the Schomburgk flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
