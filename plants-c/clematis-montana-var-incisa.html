Clematis montana var. incisa

Clematis montana var. incisa, commonly known as the Sierra Valley clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
