Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. sikkimense

Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. sikkimense, commonly known as Sikkim cinnamon or Sikkim camphor, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, including regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at moderate altitudes.

The leaves are broad and ovate, with a leathery texture, and the flowers are small and white, often clustered in panicles.
