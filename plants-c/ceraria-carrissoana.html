Ceraria carrissoana

Ceraria carrissoana, commonly known as the Canary Islands sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to salt spray and arid conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Ceraria carrissoana is often found in dune environments, contributing to coastal stabilization.
