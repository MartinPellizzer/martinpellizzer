Ceradenia marginalis

Ceradenia marginalis, commonly known as the Marginal Ceradenia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Ceradenia marginalis thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forest habitats.
