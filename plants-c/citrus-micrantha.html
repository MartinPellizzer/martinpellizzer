Citrus micrantha

Citrus micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf lemon or wild lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, oval-shaped citrus fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Citrus micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions.
