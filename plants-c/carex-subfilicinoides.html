Carex subfilicinoides

Carex subfilicinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Carex subfilicinoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
