Coleus gibbosus

Coleus gibbosus, commonly known as the knobbed coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus gibbosus is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
