Cleome silvatica

Cleome silvatica, commonly known as the swamp cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, wetland environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Cleome silvatica is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
