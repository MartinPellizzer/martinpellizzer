Carphochaete

Carphochaete, commonly known as the ironweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the Americas and are characterized by their tall, erect stems and clusters of small, often purple or pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Carphochaete species are often found in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and marshes.
