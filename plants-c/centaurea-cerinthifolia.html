Centaurea cerinthifolia

Centaurea cerinthifolia, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its slender, upright stems are adorned with narrow, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea cerinthifolia is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
