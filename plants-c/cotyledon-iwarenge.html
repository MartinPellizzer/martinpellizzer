Cotyledon iwarenge

Cotyledon iwarenge, commonly known as the "Lamb's Ear" plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
