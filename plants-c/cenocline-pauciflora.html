Cenocline pauciflora

Cenocline pauciflora, commonly known as the rare desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has adapted to extreme drought conditions through its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its reproductive strategy involves producing a large number of small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
