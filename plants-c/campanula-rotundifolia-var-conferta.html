Campanula rotundifolia var. conferta

Campanula rotundifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compact growth with dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded and hairy, typically arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
