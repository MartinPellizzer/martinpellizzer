Calcitrapa pratensis

Calcitrapa pratensis, commonly known as the meadow calcitrapa, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Calcitrapa pratensis is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
