Cucumis melo var. chito

Cucumis melo var. chito, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic, yellow to orange-colored flesh. The plant is a climbing vine that produces monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, often after the rind has hardened and turned a deep green.
