Crataegus inopinata

Crataegus inopinata, commonly known as the eastern hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus inopinata plays a significant role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for numerous species.
