Cinnamomum wightii

Cinnamomum wightii, commonly known as the Wight's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain one or two seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum wightii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
