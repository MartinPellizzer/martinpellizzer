Calchas scutellarioides var. crispipilus

Calchas scutellarioides var. crispipilus, commonly known as the curly-hair false mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface and a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
