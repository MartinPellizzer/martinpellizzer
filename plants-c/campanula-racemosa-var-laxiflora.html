Campanula racemosa var. laxiflora

Campanula racemosa var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in open woodland or rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
