Campanopsis peduncularis

Campanopsis peduncularis, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Campanopsis peduncularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
