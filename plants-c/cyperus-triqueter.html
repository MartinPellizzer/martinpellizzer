Cyperus triqueter

Cyperus triqueter, commonly known as the three-angled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Cyperus triqueter is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
