Calceolaria polifolia

Calceolaria polifolia, commonly known as the fringed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small slippers, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy texture.

Calceolaria polifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
