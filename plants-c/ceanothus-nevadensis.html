Ceanothus nevadensis

Ceanothus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Ceanothus nevadensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
