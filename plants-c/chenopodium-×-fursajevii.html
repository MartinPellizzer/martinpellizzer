Chenopodium × fursajevii

Chenopodium × fursajevii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are typically small and wind-dispersed. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Chenopodium × fursajevii is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
