Cyperus vaginatus var. pseudotextilis

Cyperus vaginatus var. pseudotextilis, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
