Cinnamomum japonicum var. tenuifolium

Cinnamomum japonicum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or narrow-leaf cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
