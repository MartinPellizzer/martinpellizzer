Cupressus lusitanica subsp. genuina

Cupressus lusitanica subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
