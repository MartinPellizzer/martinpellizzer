Crataegus orientobaltica f. laciniata

Crataegus orientobaltica f. laciniata, commonly known as the lobed hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
