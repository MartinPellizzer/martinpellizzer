Cereus smaragdiflorus

Cereus smaragdiflorus, commonly known as the emerald flower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a smooth, green stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming at night. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
