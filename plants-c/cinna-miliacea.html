Cinna miliacea

Cinna miliacea, commonly known as the millet grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains, which are often used as a food source. Cinna miliacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for marginal soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small insects and contributing to soil stabilization in degraded areas.
