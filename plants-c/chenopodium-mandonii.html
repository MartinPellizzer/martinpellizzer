Chenopodium mandonii

Chenopodium mandonii, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Chenopodium mandonii is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
