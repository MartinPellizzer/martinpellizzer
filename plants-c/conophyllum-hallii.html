Conophyllum hallii

Conophyllum hallii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Conophyllum hallii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
