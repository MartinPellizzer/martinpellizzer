Cyperus adami

Cyperus adami, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Cyperus adami is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
