Centaurea Rutifolia Subsp. Jurineifolia

Centaurea rutifolia subsp. jurineifolia is a lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance. This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers make it a striking addition to wildflower gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked in botanical discussions. Understanding and preserving this plant is crucial for maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
