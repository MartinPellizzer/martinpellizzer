Carex leporina f. longibracteata

Carex leporina f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which consists of small, three-lobed flowers. The inflorescence is often adorned with long, narrow bracts that give the plant its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
