Calceolaria plantaginea

Calceolaria plantaginea, commonly known as the velvetleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are velvety and broad, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
