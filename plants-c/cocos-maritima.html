Cocos maritima

Cocos maritima, commonly known as the sea coconut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical coastal regions and is characterized by its robust, trunk-like stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its roots are adapted to tolerate saline conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
