Calanthe brevicornu var. wattii

Calanthe brevicornu var. wattii, commonly known as the Watt's narrow-horned calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its delicate floral display and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
