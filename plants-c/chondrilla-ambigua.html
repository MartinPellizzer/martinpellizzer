Chondrilla ambigua

Chondrilla ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with woolly, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Chondrilla ambigua is often found in desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
