Campomanesia cambessedesiana

Campomanesia cambessedesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature, with a tangy, citrus-like flavor.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
