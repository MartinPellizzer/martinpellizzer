Cupressus karwinskiana

Cupressus karwinskiana, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage that forms a pyramidal shape. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durability and resistance to pests and diseases.
