Corylus avellana var. praefracta

Corylus avellana var. praefracta, commonly known as the European hazel, is a variant of the hazelnut tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of edible nuts that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for pollination.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its nuts, which are used in cooking and baking.
