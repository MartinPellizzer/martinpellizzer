Caprifolium proliferum

Caprifolium proliferum, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive aromatic fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its berries, which are orange-red when mature, are edible and often used in jams and wines.

Caprifolium proliferum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
