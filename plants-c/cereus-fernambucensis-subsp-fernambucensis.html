Cereus fernambucensis subsp. fernambucensis

Cereus fernambucensis subsp.

fernambucensis, commonly known as the Fernambucan Cereus, is a cactus species native to Argentina. It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, typical of the Cactaceae family.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
