Calopsis adpressa

Calopsis adpressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf calopsis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are usually yellow and produce small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
