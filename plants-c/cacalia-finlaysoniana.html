Cacalia finlaysoniana

Cacalia finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Finlayson's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Cacalia finlaysoniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny woodland environments.
