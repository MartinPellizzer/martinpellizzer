Crataegus eriantha

Crataegus eriantha, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and thorny branches, which provide structural support for its dense foliage. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus eriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
