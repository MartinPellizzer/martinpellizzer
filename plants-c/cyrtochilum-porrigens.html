Cyrtochilum porrigens

Cyrtochilum porrigens, commonly known as the Porry's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, erect rhizome with a cluster of fleshy, green leaves. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are arranged in a spike, often with a distinctive, hood-like structure.

Cyrtochilum porrigens is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
