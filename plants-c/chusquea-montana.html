Chusquea montana

Chusquea montana, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its structural strength and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
