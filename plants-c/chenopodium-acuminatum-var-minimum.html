Chenopodium acuminatum var. minimum

Chenopodium acuminatum var. minimum, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, with small, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also has some ornamental value.
