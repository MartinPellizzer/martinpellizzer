Centropogon barbatellus

Centropogon barbatellus, commonly known as the hairy centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Centropogon barbatellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
