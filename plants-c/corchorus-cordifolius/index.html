Corchorus Cordifolius

Jute, scientifically known as Corchorus cordifolius, is a versatile and ancient fiber plant native to South and Southeast Asia. This hardy annual plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its strong, long fibers used in textiles, packaging, and paper. Its natural biodegradability makes it an eco-friendly alternative to synthetic materials. Jute has been used for thousands of years in traditional crafts and modern industries alike. Today, it remains a vital crop for millions of farmers worldwide.
