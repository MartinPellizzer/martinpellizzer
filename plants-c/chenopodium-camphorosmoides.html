Chenopodium camphorosmoides

Chenopodium camphorosmoides, commonly known as camphor weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium camphorosmoides is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many regions.
