Clerodendrum trichotomum f. ferrugineum

Clerodendrum trichotomum f. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaved clerodendrum, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.

The species is also noted for its tolerance to urban environments and air pollution.
