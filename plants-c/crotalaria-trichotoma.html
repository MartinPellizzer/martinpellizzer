Crotalaria trichotoma

Crotalaria trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy crownflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Crotalaria trichotoma is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
