Cynanchum rubricoronae

Cynanchum rubricoronae, commonly known as the red-crowned milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flower clusters. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbels and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
