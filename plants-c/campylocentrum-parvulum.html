Campylocentrum parvulum

Campylocentrum parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

The flowers are pale yellow and are followed by the characteristic star-shaped fruits that are often used in culinary applications.
