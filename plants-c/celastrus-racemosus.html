Celastrus racemosus

Celastrus racemosus, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous climbing shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

The bark and roots of Celastrus racemosus are often used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
