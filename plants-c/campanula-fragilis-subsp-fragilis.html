Campanula fragilis subsp. fragilis

Campanula fragilis subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the brittle bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Campanula fragilis subsp.

fragilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and easy maintenance.
