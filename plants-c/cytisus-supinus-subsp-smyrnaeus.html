Cytisus supinus subsp. smyrnaeus

Cytisus supinus subsp.

smyrnaeus, commonly known as the Smyrna broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, often prostrate stems that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.
