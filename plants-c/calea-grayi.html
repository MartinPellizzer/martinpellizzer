Calea grayi

Calea grayi, commonly known as the gray's candleplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

The plant is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence that consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea grayi is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
