Corylus fargesii

Corylus fargesii, commonly known as the Yunnan hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Corylaceae family and is characterized by its bushy growth habit and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Corylus fargesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in horticulture and traditional medicine.
