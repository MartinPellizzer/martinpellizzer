Corylus intermedia

Corylus intermedia, commonly known as the hazel nut or intermediate hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the autumn. Corylus intermedia is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Its ornamental value and edible yield make it a valued plant in both horticulture and agriculture.
