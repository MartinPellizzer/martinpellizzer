Cheiri

Cheiri, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Cheiri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
