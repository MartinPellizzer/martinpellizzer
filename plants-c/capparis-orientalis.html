Capparis orientalis

Capparis orientalis, commonly known as the Persian cucumber or oriental caper, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible berries. These berries, which resemble small cucumbers, are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
