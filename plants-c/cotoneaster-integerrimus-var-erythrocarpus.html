Cotoneaster integerrimus var. erythrocarpus

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy berries that appear in the fall.

These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
