Convolvulus abruptus

Convolvulus abruptus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy.

Convolvulus abruptus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
