Campe praecox

Campe praecox, commonly known as the early campanula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Campe praecox is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
