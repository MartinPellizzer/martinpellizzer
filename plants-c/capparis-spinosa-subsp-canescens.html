Capparis spinosa subsp. canescens

Capparis spinosa subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the white caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.
