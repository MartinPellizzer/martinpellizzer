Cucumis melo var. argenteus

Cucumis melo var. argenteus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variant of the melon species characterized by its elongated, thin fruit with a pale, waxy skin.

The plant is a vigorous vine that produces small, pointed fruits with a mild, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply veined, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and culinary use in various regional cuisines.
