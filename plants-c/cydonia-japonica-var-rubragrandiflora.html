Cydonia japonica var. rubragrandiflora

Cydonia japonica var. rubragrandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese quince with red large flowers, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of showy, red-pink flowers in spring, followed by yellow, fuzzy fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
