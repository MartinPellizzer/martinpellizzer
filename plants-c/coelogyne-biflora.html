Coelogyne biflora

Coelogyne biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with narrow, leathery leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant blooms once or twice a year, typically during the dry season. Its flowers have a distinctive two-lipped structure with prominent yellow calli.

Coelogyne biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
