Calliandra chamaedrys

Calliandra chamaedrys, commonly known as the silky tree or velvetwood, is a small, deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its unique, feathery foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra chamaedrys is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
