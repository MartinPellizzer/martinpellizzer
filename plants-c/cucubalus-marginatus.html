Cucubalus marginatus

Cucubalus marginatus, commonly known as the marginated cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. Its fruit is edible when ripe and has a mild, cucumber-like flavor.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often grown for its ornamental and culinary value.
