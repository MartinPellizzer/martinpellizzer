Centaurea paniculata subsp. rigidula

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

rigidula, commonly known as rigid thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
