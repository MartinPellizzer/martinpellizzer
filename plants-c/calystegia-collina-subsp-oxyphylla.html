Calystegia collina subsp. oxyphylla

Calystegia collina subsp.

oxyphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a perennial vine native to the western United States. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to violet, blooming in summer. Its stems are hairy and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along fences or shrubs.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
