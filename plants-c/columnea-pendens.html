Columnea pendens

Columnea pendens, commonly known as the hanging flame or weeping beauty, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, with a contrasting yellow throat, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and slightly hairy. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires support for its vine-like growth habit.

Columnea pendens is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
