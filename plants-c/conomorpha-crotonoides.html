Conomorpha crotonoides

Conomorpha crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the genus. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
