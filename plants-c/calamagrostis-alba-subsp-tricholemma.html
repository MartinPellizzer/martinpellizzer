Calamagrostis alba subsp. tricholemma

Calamagrostis alba subsp.

tricholemma, commonly known as the white sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It features slender, silver-green blades with a fine, silky texture along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tall, slender panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
