Campomanesia psidioides

Campomanesia psidioides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a bright red or orange skin, containing a single large seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
