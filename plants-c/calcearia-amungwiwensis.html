Calcearia amungwiwensis

Calcearia amungwiwensis, commonly known as the Amungwiwensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Calcearia amungwiwensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
