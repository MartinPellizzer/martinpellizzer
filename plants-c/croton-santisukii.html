Croton santisukii

Croton santisukii, commonly known as the Thai croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Croton. Its leaves are usually alternate and can vary in shape, sometimes with serrated edges.

Croton santisukii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
