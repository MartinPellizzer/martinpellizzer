Cupania xylocarpa

Cupania xylocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cupania, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

Cupania xylocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
