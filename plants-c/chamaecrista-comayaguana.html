Chamaecrista comayaguana

Chamaecrista comayaguana, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental use and ecological restoration.
