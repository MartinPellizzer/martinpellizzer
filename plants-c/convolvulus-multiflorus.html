Convolvulus multiflorus

Convolvulus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with stems that can twine around supports. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus multiflorus is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
