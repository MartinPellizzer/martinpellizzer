Carduus ramosissimus

Carduus ramosissimus, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with prominent veins and a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.
