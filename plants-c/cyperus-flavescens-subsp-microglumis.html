Cyperus flavescens subsp. microglumis

Cyperus flavescens subsp.

microglumis, commonly known as the dwarf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to brown flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
