Correa speciosa var. normalis

Correa speciosa var. normalis, commonly known as the pink correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
