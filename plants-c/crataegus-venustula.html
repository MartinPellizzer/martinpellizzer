Crataegus venustula

Crataegus venustula, commonly known as the beautiful hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Crataegus venustula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
