Cercidium peninsulare

Cercidium peninsulare, commonly known as the desert shrub or palo verde, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.

Cercidium peninsulare plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native range.
