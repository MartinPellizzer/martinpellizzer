Cassia pumila

Cassia pumila, commonly known as African candle bush, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Cassia pumila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
