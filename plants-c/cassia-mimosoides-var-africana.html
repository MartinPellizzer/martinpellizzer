Cassia mimosoides var. africana

Cassia mimosoides var. africana, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves that are often mistaken for those of the acacia genus due to their similar appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, seed-filled pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
