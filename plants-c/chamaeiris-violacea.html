Chamaeiris violacea

Chamaeiris violacea, commonly known as the violet wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Chamaeiris violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
