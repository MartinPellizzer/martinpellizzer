Cirsium × castriferrei

Cirsium × castriferrei, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, wastelands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × castriferrei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
