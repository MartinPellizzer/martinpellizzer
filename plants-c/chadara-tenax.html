Chadara tenax

Chadara tenax, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in crevices and on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

Chadara tenax is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
