Capsella bursa-pastoris var. auriculata

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate shepherd's purse, is a variant of the common shepherd's purse.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods. The leaves are lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and yellow. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding.
