Chrysochlamys clusiifolia subsp. panamaensis

Chrysochlamys clusiifolia subsp.

panamaensis, commonly known as Panama flame tree, is a member of the Clusiaceae family. It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, elliptical leaves. Its bark is smooth and often reddish in color, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
