Cyperus laetus var. cephalanthus

Cyperus laetus var. cephalanthus, commonly known as the pink flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale pink to white spikelets that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in floodplains and seasonally flooded areas.

Its botanical structure is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.
