Calea zacatechichi var. calyculata

Calea zacatechichi var. calyculata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that open at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance in the evening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
