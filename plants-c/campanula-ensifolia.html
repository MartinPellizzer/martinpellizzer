Campanula ensifolia

Campanula ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Campanula ensifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
