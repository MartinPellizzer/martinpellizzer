Carex lachnosperma

Carex lachnosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to Australia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
