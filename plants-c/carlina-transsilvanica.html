Carlina transsilvanica

Carlina transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cudweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, forming a composite head that resembles a daisy.

Carlina transsilvanica is often found in meadows, forest edges, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.
