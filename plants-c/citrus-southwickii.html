Citrus southwickii

Citrus southwickii, commonly known as the Southwick's orange.

It is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Socotra, located in the Arabian Sea. This plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often spiny leaves. The fruit is typically small, with a thick rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
