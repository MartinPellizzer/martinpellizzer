Cucumis cubensis

Cucumis cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, tropical climates. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, crisp flavor. Its botanical features include palmately lobed leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Cucumis cubensis is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
