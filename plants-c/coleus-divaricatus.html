Coleus divaricatus

Coleus divaricatus, commonly known as the spreading coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its trailing growth habit. Its flowers are small and tubular, typically appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.
