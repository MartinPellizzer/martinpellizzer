Cleome arabica subsp. amblyocarpa

Cleome arabica subsp.

amblyocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that are typically narrow and cylindrical, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
