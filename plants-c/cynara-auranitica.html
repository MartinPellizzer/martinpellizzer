Cynara auranitica

Cynara auranitica, commonly known as the golden globe artichoke, is a species of thistle native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, purple-bracted heads that enclose the edible flower buds. The plant produces a unique, globe-shaped inflorescence that is often cultivated for its tender, edible parts. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The botanical features of Cynara auranitica make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and botany.
