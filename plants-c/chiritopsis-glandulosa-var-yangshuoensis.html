Chiritopsis glandulosa var. yangshuoensis

Chiritopsis glandulosa var. yangshuoensis, commonly known as the Yangshuo chiritopsis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance.
