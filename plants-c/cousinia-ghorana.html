Cousinia ghorana

Cousinia ghorana, commonly known as the Afghan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Cousinia ghorana is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitats.
