Cotoneaster coreanus

Cotoneaster coreanus, commonly known as the Korean cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster coreanus is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
