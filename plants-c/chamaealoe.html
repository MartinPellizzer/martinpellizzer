Chamaealoe

Chamaealoe, commonly known as the rock agave, is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting them to arid environments. Chamaealoe species typically produce tall flower stalks that bear clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The genus includes several species, such as Chamaealoe valida, which is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
