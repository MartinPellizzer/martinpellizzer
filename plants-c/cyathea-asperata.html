Cyathea asperata

Cyathea asperata, commonly known as the rough tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive rough, scaly texture on its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea asperata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
