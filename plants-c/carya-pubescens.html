Carya pubescens

Carya pubescens, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and lobed leaves. The tree produces distinctive, spiky burrs that enclose its edible acorns, which are a food source for various wildlife. Carya pubescens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for numerous species.
