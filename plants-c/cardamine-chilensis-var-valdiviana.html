Cardamine chilensis var. valdiviana

Cardamine chilensis var. valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian watercress, is a perennial herb native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, alternate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a peppery flavor similar to watercress. It has small white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisines.
