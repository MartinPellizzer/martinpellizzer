Carpodinus trichanthera

Carpodinus trichanthera, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some regions.
