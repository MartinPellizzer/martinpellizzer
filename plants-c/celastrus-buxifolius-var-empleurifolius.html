Celastrus buxifolius var. empleurifolius

Celastrus buxifolius var. empleurifolius, commonly known as the Mexican staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in axillary umbels. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The plant is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
