Cynanchum forrestii var. balfourianum

Cynanchum forrestii var. balfourianum, commonly known as the Balfour's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive crown of hooked filaments, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
