Clinopodium alpinum subsp. meridionale

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as alpine oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a pungent, peppery flavor to dishes.

The subspecies meridionale is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth compared to other members of the species.
