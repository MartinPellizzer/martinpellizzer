Clypeola jonthlaspi subsp. microcarpa

Clypeola jonthlaspi subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited clypeola, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically yellow and consist of four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the mustard family. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.
