Cirsium occidentale var. coulteri

Cirsium occidentale var. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowering heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive basal rosette structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
