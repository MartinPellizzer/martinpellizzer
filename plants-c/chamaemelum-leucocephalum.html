Chamaemelum leucocephalum

Chamaemelum leucocephalum, commonly known as white sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The roots of this plant have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
