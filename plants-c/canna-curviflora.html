Canna curviflora

Canna curviflora, commonly known as the curlyflower cannas, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often colorful leaves that can reach up to three feet in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Canna curviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage and floral display.
