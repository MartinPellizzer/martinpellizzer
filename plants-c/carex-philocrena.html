Carex philocrena

Carex philocrena, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are usually narrow and sword-shaped, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Carex philocrena produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.
