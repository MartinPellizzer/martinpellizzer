Ctenopsis pectinella subvar. pubescens

Ctenopsis pectinella subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ctenopsis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
