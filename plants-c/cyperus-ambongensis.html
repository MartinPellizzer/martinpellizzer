Cyperus ambongensis

Cyperus ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambong grass, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinct inflorescence and is often found in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
