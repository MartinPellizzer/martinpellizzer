Carex stipata

Carex stipata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland, is a species of sedge native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex stipata is a perennial plant that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
