Cupania discolor

Cupania discolor, commonly known as the red sandcherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Cupania discolor is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
