Carex stipata var. maxima

Carex stipata var. maxima, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a robust perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and restoration projects.
