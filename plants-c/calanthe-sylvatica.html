Calanthe sylvatica

Calanthe sylvatica, commonly known as the mountain calanthe, is a perennial orchid native to the forests of East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Calanthe sylvatica has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
