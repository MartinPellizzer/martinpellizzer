Cactus neomexicanus

Cactus neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features flattened, fleshy stems that store water, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, succulent texture.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
