Camissonia benitensis

Camissonia benitensis, commonly known as the Benito's camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and low-growing habit. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are generally narrow and sometimes hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Camissonia benitensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
