Coelogyne papillosa

Coelogyne papillosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive veined pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces racemes of showy, colorful blooms.

Coelogyne papillosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
