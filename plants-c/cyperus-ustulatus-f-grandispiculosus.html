Cyperus ustulatus f. grandispiculosus

Cyperus ustulatus f. grandispiculosus, commonly known as the broad-bracted flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that often grows close to the ground.

This subspecies is often found in wetland habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
