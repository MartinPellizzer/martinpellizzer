Cuphea glareosa

Cuphea glareosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cuphea glareosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
