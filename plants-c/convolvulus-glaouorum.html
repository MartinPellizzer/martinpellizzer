Convolvulus glaouorum

Convolvulus glaouorum, commonly known as the Moroccan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
