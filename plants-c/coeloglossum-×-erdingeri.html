Coeloglossum × erdingeri

Coeloglossum × erdingeri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between Coeloglossum viride and Coeloglossum turgidum.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, narrow leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate species that is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid example of natural genetic variation in orchids.
