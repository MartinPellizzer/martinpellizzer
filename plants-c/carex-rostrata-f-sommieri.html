Carex rostrata f. sommieri

Carex rostrata f. sommieri, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive elongated bracts and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
