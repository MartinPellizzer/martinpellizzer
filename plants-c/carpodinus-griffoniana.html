Carpodinus griffoniana

Carpodinus griffoniana, commonly known as the Griffon's carpetweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
