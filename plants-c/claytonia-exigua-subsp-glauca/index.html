Claytonia Exigua Subsp. Glauca

Claytonia exigua subsp. glauca is a delicate flowering plant native to the western united states. it is known for its small, pale blue or white flowers and its ability to thrive in shady, moist environments. this subspecies is often found in forested areas and along stream banks. its leaves are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them a bluish appearance. it is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders due to its beauty and ease of care.
