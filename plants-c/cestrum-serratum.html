Cestrum serratum

Cestrum serratum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular white flowers that open at night. The plant has woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum serratum is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
