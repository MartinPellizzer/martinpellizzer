Citrus × racemosa

Citrus × racemosa, commonly known as the trifoliate orange, is a hybrid citrus plant native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are typically not eaten but are used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a rootstock in citrus cultivation due to its resistance to certain diseases.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and botanical research.
