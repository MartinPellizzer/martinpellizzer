Colchicum kotschyi

Colchicum kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian meadow saffron, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces striking, violet-blue flowers with prominent yellow stamens, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant features a short, narrow leaf sheath and a tunicate bulb that stores nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
