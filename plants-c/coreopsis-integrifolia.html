Coreopsis integrifolia

Coreopsis integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Coreopsis integrifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
