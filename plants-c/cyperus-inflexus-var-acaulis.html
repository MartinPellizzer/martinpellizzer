Cyperus inflexus var. acaulis

Cyperus inflexus var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often hairless. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in wet or moist environments.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced stem structure, giving it a more compact appearance compared to other Cyperus species.
