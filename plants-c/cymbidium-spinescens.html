Cymbidium spinescens

Cymbidium spinescens, commonly known as the spiny orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, making it a prized addition to indoor gardens. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Cymbidium spinescens is relatively hardy and can be grown outdoors in mild climates or as a potted plant in colder regions.
