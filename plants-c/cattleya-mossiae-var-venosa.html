Cattleya mossiae var. venosa

Cattleya mossiae var. venosa, commonly known as the Venosa Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, velvety petals and vibrant coloration.

It originates from the tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with ample sunlight. The plant typically produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its varietal name. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal manner with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its beauty and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
