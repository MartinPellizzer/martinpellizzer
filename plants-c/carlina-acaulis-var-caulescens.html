Carlina acaulis var. caulescens

Carlina acaulis var. caulescens, commonly known as the tall stemless cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stem and dense clusters of blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The species is often found in meadows and open slopes at higher elevations.
