Cyperus aurantiacus

Cyperus aurantiacus, commonly known as the red flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowers that often appear in shades of red or orange. Cyperus aurantiacus typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
