Cyanea angustifolia var. racemosa

Cyanea angustifolia var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose cyanea, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.
