Centaurea cineraria subsp. circae

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

circae, commonly known as the Spanish blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
