Carex mollis

Carex mollis, commonly known as soft sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are soft and slightly hairy, with a distinctive rosette growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

Carex mollis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
