Cicerbita zeravschanica

Cicerbita zeravschanica, commonly known as the Pamir bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its small, white or yellow flowers are followed by elongated, narrow pods that contain the seeds.

Cicerbita zeravschanica is notable for its bitter taste and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
