Candollea imbricata

Candollea imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a compact, panicle-like structure. Native to Mexico and parts of Central America, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
