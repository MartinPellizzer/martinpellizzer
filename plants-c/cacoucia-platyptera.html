Cacoucia platyptera

Cacoucia platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
