Calliandra elegans

Calliandra elegans, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and profuse, showy inflorescences that resemble small showers of pink or red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for warm climates.

Calliandra elegans is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits surrounding vegetation.
