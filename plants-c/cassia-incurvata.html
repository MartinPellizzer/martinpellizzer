Cassia incurvata

Cassia incurvata, commonly known as the sickle cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia incurvata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
