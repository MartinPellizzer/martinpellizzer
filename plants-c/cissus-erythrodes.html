Cissus erythrodes

Cissus erythrodes, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are often covered in small, translucent tubercles.

It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
