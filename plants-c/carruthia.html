Carruthia

Carruthia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a unique, intricate floral structure. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, Carruthia thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
