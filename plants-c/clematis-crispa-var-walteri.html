Clematis crispa var. walteri

Clematis crispa var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
