Camissonia multijuga

Camissonia multijuga, commonly known as the narrowleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Camissonia multijuga is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
