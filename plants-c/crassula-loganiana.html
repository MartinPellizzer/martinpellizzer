Crassula loganiana

Crassula loganiana, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" or "Fairy Fingers," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique foliage and colorful blooms make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
