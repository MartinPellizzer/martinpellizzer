Caricina curvula

Caricina curvula, commonly known as the slender bristly grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often curved, giving it its scientific name.

Caricina curvula is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
