Carex gravida var. lunelliana

Carex gravida var. lunelliana, commonly known as the moonlight sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly recurved, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or tidal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
