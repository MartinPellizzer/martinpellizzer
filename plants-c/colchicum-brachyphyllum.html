Colchicum brachyphyllum

Colchicum brachyphyllum, commonly known as the short-leaved autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ and is essential for its survival during dormant periods. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, emerging from the base of the plant after flowering.

Colchicum brachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
