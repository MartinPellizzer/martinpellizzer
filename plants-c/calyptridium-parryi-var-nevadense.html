Calyptridium parryi var. nevadense

Calyptridium parryi var. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada calyptridium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-blue leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions, particularly in Nevada and adjacent states.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
