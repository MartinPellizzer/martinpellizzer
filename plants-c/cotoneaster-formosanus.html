Cotoneaster formosanus

Cotoneaster formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Cotoneaster formosanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
