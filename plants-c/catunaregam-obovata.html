Catunaregam obovata

Catunaregam obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
