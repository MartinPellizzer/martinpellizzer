Carex teretiuscula var. ehrhartiana

Carex teretiuscula var. ehrhartiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
