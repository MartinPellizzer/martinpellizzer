Chilochloa dentata

Chilochloa dentata, commonly known as the serrated grass, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in forest margins or wetlands.

Chilochloa dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
