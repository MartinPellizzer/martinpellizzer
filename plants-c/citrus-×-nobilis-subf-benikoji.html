Citrus × nobilis subf. benikoji

Citrus × nobilis subf.

benikoji, commonly known as the Benikoji mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with a thin, smooth peel.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
