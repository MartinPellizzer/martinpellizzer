Carex acuta f. diluta

Carex acuta f. diluta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the broad-leaved sedge, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its texture and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Carex acuta f. diluta is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
