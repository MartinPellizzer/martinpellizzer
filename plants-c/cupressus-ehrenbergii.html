Cupressus ehrenbergii

Cupressus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber adds to its ecological and cultural significance.
