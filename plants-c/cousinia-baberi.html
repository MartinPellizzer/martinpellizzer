Cousinia baberi

Cousinia baberi, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia baberi is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
