Cephalopodum hissaricum

Cephalopodum hissaricum, commonly known as the Caucasian horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cephalopodum hissaricum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
