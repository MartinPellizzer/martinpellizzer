Capsella bursa-pastoris densa

Capsella bursa-pastoris densa, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a short, hairy capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along roadsides.
