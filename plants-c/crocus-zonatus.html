Crocus zonatus

Crocus zonatus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow stripe down the center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is part of the Iris family.

Crocus zonatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
