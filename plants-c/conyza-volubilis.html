Conyza volubilis

Conyza volubilis, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza volubilis is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
