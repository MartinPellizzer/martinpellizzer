Crimaea

Crimaea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Crimaea typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty, windy environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
