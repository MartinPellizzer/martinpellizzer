Crassula hirsuta

Crassula hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
