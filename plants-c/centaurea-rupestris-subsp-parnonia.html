Centaurea rupestris subsp. parnonia

Centaurea rupestris subsp.

parnonia, commonly known as the Parnon's centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features slender, erect stems and produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow centers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
