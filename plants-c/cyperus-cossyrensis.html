Cyperus cossyrensis

Cyperus cossyrensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cyperus cossyrensis is a hardy plant that can tolerate a range of moisture conditions, making it adaptable to various habitats.
