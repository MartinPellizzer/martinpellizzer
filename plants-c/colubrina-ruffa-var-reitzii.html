Colubrina ruffa var. reitzii

Colubrina ruffa var. reitzii, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
