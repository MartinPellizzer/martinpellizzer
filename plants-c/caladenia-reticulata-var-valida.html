Caladenia reticulata var. valida

Caladenia reticulata var. valida, commonly known as the Valid Wattle-lily, is a striking orchid species native to south-western Australia.

It features a single, erect stem that bears a single, showy pink or red flower with a distinctive, intricate pattern on its dorsal sepal. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its floral structure includes a narrow, elongated labellum that is adorned with intricate veining.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique appearance.
