Calamus karnatakensis

Calamus karnatakensis, commonly known as the Karnataka calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus karnatakensis is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental and possibly medicinal properties.
