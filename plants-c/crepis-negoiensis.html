Crepis negoiensis

Crepis negoiensis, commonly known as the Negoi's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its small, daisy-like flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer.

Crepis negoiensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
