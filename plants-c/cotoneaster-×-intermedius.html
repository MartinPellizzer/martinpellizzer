Cotoneaster × intermedius

Cotoneaster × intermedius, commonly known as the hybrid cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Cotoneaster × intermedius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
