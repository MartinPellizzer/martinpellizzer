Crocus variegatus lusus striatus

Crocus variegatus lusus striatus, commonly known as the Striped Crown Crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, early spring blooms with purple and white petals. The plant features a short, tuberous corm and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically borne on erect stems and are often adorned with distinctive striped patterns on the petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early blooming display.
