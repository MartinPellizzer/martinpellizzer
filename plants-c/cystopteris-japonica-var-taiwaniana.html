Cystopteris japonica var. taiwaniana

Cystopteris japonica var. taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan water fern, is a variant of the Japanese water fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features delicate, finely divided fronds that are often described as having a lace-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Dennstaedtiaceae family.

This variety is native to Taiwan and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
