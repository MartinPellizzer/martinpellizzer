Carex magellanica

Carex magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Carex magellanica is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
