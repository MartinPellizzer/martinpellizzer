Convolvulus boerhavioides

Convolvulus boerhavioides, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped, funnel-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
