Carex goodenowii f. basigyna

Carex goodenowii f. basigyna, commonly known as the basigyna sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
