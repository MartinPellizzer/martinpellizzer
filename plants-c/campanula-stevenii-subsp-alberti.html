Campanula stevenii subsp. alberti

Campanula stevenii subsp.

alberti, commonly known as the Albert's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
