Crambe gigantea

Crambe gigantea, commonly known as giant crambe, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for oil production, it has gained interest in horticulture and agricultural research.
