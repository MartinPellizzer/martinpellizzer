Cattleya × motae

Cattleya × motae, commonly known as the Motae Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid originating from the cross between Cattleya mossiae and Cattleya bicolor.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often displaying a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. Its pseudobulbs are robust, and it produces long, pendant inflorescences that bear multiple blooms.

Cattleya × motae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.
