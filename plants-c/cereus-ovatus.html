Cereus ovatus

Cereus ovatus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
