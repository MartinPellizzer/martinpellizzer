Crataegus macracantha var. occidentalis

Crataegus macracantha var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
