Cladanthus mixtus var. mixtus

Cladanthus mixtus var. mixtus, commonly known as the mixed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small florets surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its robust root system and adaptability to various soil conditions.
