Camellia mairei f. alba

Camellia mairei f. alba, commonly known as the white camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a variant of the Camellia mairei species, characterized by its large, white, semi-double flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is evergreen, with broad, dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia mairei f. alba is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to add winter interest to gardens.
