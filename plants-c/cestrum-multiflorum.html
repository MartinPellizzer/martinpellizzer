Cestrum multiflorum

Cestrum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by night-flying insects such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
