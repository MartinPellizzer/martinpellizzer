Cucurbita maxima var. chiloensis

Cucurbita maxima var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean squash, is a variety of pumpkin native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit with a smooth, dark green skin. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal.
