Conyza albida

Conyza albida, commonly known as white cockle, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically hairy and alternate along the stem, and it often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Conyza albida is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
