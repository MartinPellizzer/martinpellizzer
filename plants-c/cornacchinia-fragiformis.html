Cornacchinia fragiformis

Cornacchinia fragiformis, commonly known as the slender frond fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Cornacchinia fragiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
