Cinnamomum argenteum

Cinnamomum argenteum, commonly known as silver camphor, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by round, orange-colored berries. It is valued for its aromatic oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of timber.
