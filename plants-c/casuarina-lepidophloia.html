Casuarina lepidophloia

Casuarina lepidophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a member of the Casuarinaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its needle-like leaves and unique, cone-like structures. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its small, pale yellow flowers are followed by woody, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Casuarina lepidophloia is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
