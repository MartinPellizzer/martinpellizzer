Crabbea pedunculata

Crabbea pedunculata, commonly known as the bluebell of the Cape, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a clump-like form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Crabbea pedunculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
