Cryptocoryne × purpurea nothovar. borneoensis

Cryptocoryne × purpurea nothovar. borneoensis, commonly known as the Bornean Cryptocoryne, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Borneo.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tint, especially under strong lighting. The plant thrives in low to moderate light conditions and is often found in slow-moving or still waters with soft, nutrient-rich substrates. It is popular among aquarists for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and resilience in both natural and artificial aquatic environments.
