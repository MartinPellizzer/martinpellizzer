Calycothrix creswellii

Calycothrix creswellii, commonly known as the Creswell's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense, tangled masses on its host, which can affect the host's health.

Calycothrix creswellii is an interesting example of plant parasitism in forest ecosystems.
