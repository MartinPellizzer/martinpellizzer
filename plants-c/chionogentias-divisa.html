Chionogentias divisa

Chionogentias divisa, commonly known as the divided snow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often divided, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
