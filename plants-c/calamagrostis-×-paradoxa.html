Calamagrostis × paradoxa

Calamagrostis × paradoxa, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pinkish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Calamagrostis × paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
