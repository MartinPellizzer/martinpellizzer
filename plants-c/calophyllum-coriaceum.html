Calophyllum coriaceum

Calophyllum coriaceum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a dense, dark green canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green, clustered in panicles. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
