Chlorophytum saxicola

Chlorophytum saxicola, commonly known as the rock spider plant, is a small, perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. The plant produces delicate, spider-like inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are followed by blue or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental appeal make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
