Camellia rusticana f. lancifolia

Camellia rusticana f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and slightly serrated. The plant produces striking, double pink flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanic gardens.
