Crepis Paludosa

Crepis paludosa is a delicate flowering plant commonly found in wetland environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its small, daisy-like blooms. This species thrives in moist soils and is often seen along the edges of ponds and marshes. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important part of wetland ecosystems. Despite its modest size, Crepis paludosa plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity.
