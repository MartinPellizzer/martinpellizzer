Cyperus dietrichiae var. brevibracteatus

Cyperus dietrichiae var. brevibracteatus, commonly known as the narrow-bract flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This variety is distinguished by its short, narrow bracts that give it its specific name.
