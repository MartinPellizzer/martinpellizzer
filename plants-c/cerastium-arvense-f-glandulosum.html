Cerastium arvense f. glandulosum

Cerastium arvense f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is notable for its dense growth habit and ability to form mats, which help prevent soil erosion.
