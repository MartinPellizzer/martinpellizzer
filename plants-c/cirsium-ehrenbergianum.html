Cirsium ehrenbergianum

Cirsium ehrenbergianum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds in a distinctive cone-shaped structure at the top of its flowering head. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cirsium ehrenbergianum is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides.
