Conicosia coruscans

Conicosia coruscans, commonly known as the shimmering conicosia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, iridescent blue and purple petals that reflect light in a dazzling manner. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Conicosia coruscans is valued for its unique beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
