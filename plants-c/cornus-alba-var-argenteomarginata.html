Cornus alba var. argenteomarginata

Cornus alba var. argenteomarginata, commonly known as the silver-margin dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features striking white to cream-colored margins on its lance-shaped leaves, which contrast beautifully with the dark green central portion. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage.

This variety is prized for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various soil conditions.
