Carex goodenowii var. juncella

Carex goodenowii var. juncella, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This variety is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, three-lobed spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
