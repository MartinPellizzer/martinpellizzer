Chamaeangis sarcophylla

Chamaeangis sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecrop orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
