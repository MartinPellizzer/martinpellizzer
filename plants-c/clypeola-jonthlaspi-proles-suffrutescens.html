Clypeola jonthlaspi proles suffrutescens

Clypeola jonthlaspi proles suffrutescens, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It typically features succulent, fleshy stems that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a slightly lobed or toothed edge.

This species is well adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in rocky soils or along coastal areas.
