Crantzia villosa

Crantzia villosa, commonly known as the hairy crantzia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crantzia villosa thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
