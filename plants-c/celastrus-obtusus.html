Celastrus obtusus

Celastrus obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf staff vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant's leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rounded or obtuse tip, giving it its scientific name. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
