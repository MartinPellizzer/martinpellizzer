Crassula globulifolia

Crassula globulifolia, commonly known as the globe-shaped crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its low maintenance and compact size.

Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
