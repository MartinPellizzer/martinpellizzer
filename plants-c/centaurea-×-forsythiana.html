Centaurea × forsythiana

Centaurea × forsythiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, often with a distinctive central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it grows up to 60 cm in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
