Conophytum pium

Conophytum pium, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum pium is prized for its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves.
