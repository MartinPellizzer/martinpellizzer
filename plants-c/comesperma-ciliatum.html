Comesperma ciliatum

Comesperma ciliatum, commonly known as the silky comesperma, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and have a distinctive silvery appearance due to fine cilia along the edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
