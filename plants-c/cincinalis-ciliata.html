Cincinalis ciliata

Cincinalis ciliata, commonly known as the hairy cincinalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
