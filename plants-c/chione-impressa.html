Chione impressa

Chione impressa, commonly known as the silverleaf snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chione impressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
