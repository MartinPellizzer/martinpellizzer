Carex capillipes

Carex capillipes, commonly known as the slender filmy sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Carex capillipes typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
