Chloris bournei

Chloris bournei, commonly known as Bornean grass or Borneo grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color.

Chloris bournei plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
