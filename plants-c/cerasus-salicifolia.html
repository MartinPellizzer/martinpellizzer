Cerasus salicifolia

Cerasus salicifolia, commonly known as the willow leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are typically pink or white. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in late summer.

Cerasus salicifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
