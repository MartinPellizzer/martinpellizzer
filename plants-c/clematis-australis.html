Clematis australis

Clematis australis, commonly known as the native clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis australis is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
