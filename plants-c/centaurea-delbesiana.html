Centaurea delbesiana

Centaurea delbesiana, commonly known as the Delbes' knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Centaurea delbesiana is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
