Crataegus vegrandis

Crataegus vegrandis, commonly known as the large-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus vegrandis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and open fields.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
