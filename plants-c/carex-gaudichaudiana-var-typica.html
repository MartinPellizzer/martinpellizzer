Carex gaudichaudiana var. typica

Carex gaudichaudiana var. typica, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
