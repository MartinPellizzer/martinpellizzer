Cissus hexangularis

Cissus hexangularis, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, diamond-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cissus hexangularis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
