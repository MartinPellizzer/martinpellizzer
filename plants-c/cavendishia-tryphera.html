Cavendishia tryphera

Cavendishia tryphera, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are ribbed and covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
