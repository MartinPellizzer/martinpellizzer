Cleistopholis pynaertii

Cleistopholis pynaertii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers that are often borne in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
