Cuphea strigulosa

Cuphea strigulosa, commonly known as the rough cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea strigulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
