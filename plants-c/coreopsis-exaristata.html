Coreopsis exaristata

Coreopsis exaristata, commonly known as the rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive, rough-textured central disk. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.

Coreopsis exaristata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
