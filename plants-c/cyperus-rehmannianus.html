Cyperus rehmannianus

Cyperus rehmannianus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves, often forming clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically pale green to brownish in color.

Cyperus rehmannianus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
