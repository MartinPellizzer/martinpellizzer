Cyrtopera amazonica

Cyrtopera amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in vibrant shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, adapted to capture sunlight in the dense forest canopy.

Cyrtopera amazonica plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for various pollinators.
