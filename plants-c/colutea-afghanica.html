Colutea afghanica

Colutea afghanica, commonly known as Afghan Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Colutea afghanica is often used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological value in soil stabilization.
