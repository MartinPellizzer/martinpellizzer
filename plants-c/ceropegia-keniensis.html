Ceropegia keniensis

Ceropegia keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan moonvine, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a warm, sunny environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
