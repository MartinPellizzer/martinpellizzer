Cedrela kingii var. birmanica

Cedrela kingii var. birmanica, commonly known as Burmese teak, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight grain, fine texture, and high density, making it highly valued for furniture and construction. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while the flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.
