Clematis taeguensis

Clematis taeguensis, commonly known as the Korean clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis taeguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
