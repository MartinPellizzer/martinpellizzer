Campanopsis spinulosa

Campanopsis spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Campanopsis spinulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
