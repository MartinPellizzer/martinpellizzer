Centaurea josiae

Centaurea josiae, commonly known as the Josiah's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea josiae is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
