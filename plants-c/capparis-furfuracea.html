Capparis furfuracea

Capparis furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis furfuracea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in some regions.
