Cirrhopetalum plumatum

Cirrhopetalum plumatum, commonly known as the plumose orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and intricate, plumose (feathery) petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, Cirrhopetalum plumatum is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
