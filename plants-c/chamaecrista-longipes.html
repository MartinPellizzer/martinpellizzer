Chamaecrista longipes

Chamaecrista longipes, commonly known as the longipes burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
