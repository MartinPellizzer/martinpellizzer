Chenopodium album f. viridescens

Chenopodium album f. viridescens, commonly known as blite, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a raceme, and its seeds are typically wind-dispersed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
