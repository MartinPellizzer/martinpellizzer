Cynorkis galeata

Cynorkis galeata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cynorkis galeata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
