Combretum squamosum var. dissitum

Combretum squamosum var. dissitum, commonly known as the rough-leaved flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish, scaly bark and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
