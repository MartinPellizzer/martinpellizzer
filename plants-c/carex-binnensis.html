Carex binnensis

Carex binnensis, commonly known as the Binn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex binnensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
