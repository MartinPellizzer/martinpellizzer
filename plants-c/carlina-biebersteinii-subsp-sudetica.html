Carlina biebersteinii subsp. sudetica

Carlina biebersteinii subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Carpathian cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and rocky slopes, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of ray florets and disk florets.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution.
