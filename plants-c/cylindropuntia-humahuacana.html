Cylindropuntia humahuacana

Cylindropuntia humahuacana, commonly known as the desert nightshade cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots and the ability to store water in its stems.
