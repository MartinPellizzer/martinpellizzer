Crocus rubens

Crocus rubens, commonly known as the red crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late autumn to early winter, making it one of the first flowers to appear in the colder months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Crocus rubens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
