Carex varia var. minor

Carex varia var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
