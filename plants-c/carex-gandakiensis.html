Carex gandakiensis

Carex gandakiensis, commonly known as the Gandaki sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Carex gandakiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
