Croton cerroazulensis

Croton cerroazulensis, commonly known as the Blue Mountain croton, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
