Cephalophyllum validum

Cephalophyllum validum, commonly known as the helmet cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a distinctive, helmet-like stem that is often covered in dense, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which provide additional protection from herbivores.

Cephalophyllum validum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
