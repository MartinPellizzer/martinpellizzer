Calea verticillata

Calea verticillata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use. Its leaves are opposite and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Calea verticillata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
