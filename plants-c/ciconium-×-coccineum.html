Ciconium × coccineum

Ciconium × coccineum, commonly known as the red ciconium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae, characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The red bracts are not true flowers but serve as modified leaves, attracting pollinators to the small, inconspicuous actual flowers.
