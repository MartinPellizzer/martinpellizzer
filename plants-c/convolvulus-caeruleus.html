Convolvulus caeruleus

Convolvulus caeruleus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it suitable for various garden settings.
