Convolvulus trianthus

Convolvulus trianthus, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus trianthus is a perennial species that blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
