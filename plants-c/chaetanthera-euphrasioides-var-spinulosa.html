Chaetanthera euphrasioides var. spinulosa

Chaetanthera euphrasioides var. spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked chaetanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
