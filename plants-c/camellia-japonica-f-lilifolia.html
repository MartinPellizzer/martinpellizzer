Camellia japonica f. lilifolia

Camellia japonica f. lilifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with lily-like flowers.

This plant is a cultivar of the Camellia japonica species, characterized by its unique, lily-shaped blooms that are typically white or pale pink. It is a evergreen shrub native to Japan, prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often more delicate and showy than those of the typical Camellia japonica.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
