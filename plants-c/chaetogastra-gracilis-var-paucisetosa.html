Chaetogastra gracilis var. paucisetosa

Chaetogastra gracilis var. paucisetosa, commonly known as the slender hairy sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along streams. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety paucisetosa is distinguished by its reduced number of hairs on the stems and leaves compared to the typical form.
