Cacalia tarchonanthifolia

Cacalia tarchonanthifolia, commonly known as the tarchonanthifolium cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
