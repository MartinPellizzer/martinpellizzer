Carex paniculata subsp. szovitsii

Carex paniculata subsp.

szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its morphological features, including the structure of its inflorescence and leaf blade.
