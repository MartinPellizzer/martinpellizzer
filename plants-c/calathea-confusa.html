Calathea confusa

Calathea confusa, commonly known as the "confused calathea," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate leaf patterns that change color in response to light conditions. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy, glossy surface. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
