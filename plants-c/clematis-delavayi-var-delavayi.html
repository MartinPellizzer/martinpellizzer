Clematis delavayi var. delavayi

Clematis delavayi var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It features climbing stems with thornless, ridged, and often hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of alternate, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
