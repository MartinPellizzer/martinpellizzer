Chionographis japonica subsp. minoensis

Chionographis japonica subsp.

minoensis, commonly known as the Mino snowbell, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
