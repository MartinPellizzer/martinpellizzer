Cuphea miniata

Cuphea miniata, commonly known as the velvet flower or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is noted for its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea miniata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
