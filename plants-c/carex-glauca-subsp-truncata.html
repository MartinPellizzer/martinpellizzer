Carex glauca subsp. truncata

Carex glauca subsp.

truncata, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are usually green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
