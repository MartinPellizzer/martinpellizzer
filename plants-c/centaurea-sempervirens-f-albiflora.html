Centaurea sempervirens f. albiflora

Centaurea sempervirens f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and long-lasting flowers, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
