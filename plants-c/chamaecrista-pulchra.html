Chamaecrista pulchra

Chamaecrista pulchra, commonly known as the pretty crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chamaecrista pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
