Ceropegia hirtella

Ceropegia hirtella, commonly known as the velvet leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia hirtella is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.
