Casearia silvana var. oubatchensis

Casearia silvana var. oubatchensis, commonly known as the Oubatchensis casearia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
