Ceropegia pulvinata var. glabricaulis

Ceropegia pulvinata var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and waxy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
