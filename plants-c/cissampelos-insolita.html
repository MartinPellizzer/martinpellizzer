Cissampelos insolita

Cissampelos insolita, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The botanical features of Cissampelos insolita make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
