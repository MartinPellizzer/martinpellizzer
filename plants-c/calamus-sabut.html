Calamus sabut

Calamus sabut, commonly known as the fragrant cane or sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy, with a pleasant, aromatic scent. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
