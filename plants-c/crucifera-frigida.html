Crucifera frigida

Crucifera frigida, commonly known as the frostweed, is a perennial herb native to cold climates.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas in harsh environments.
