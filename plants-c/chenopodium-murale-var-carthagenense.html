Chenopodium murale var. carthagenense

Chenopodium murale var. carthagenense, commonly known as Purslane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making it a nutritious addition to diets.

It is also known for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.
