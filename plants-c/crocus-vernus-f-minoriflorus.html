Crocus vernus f. minoriflorus

Crocus vernus f. minoriflorus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a botanical variant of the common crocus.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical crocus species. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple during early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
