Cornus subumbellata

Cornus subumbellata, commonly known as the white cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Cornus subumbellata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive fall foliage and ecological benefits.
