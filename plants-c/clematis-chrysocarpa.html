Clematis chrysocarpa

Clematis chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique fruiting structure.
