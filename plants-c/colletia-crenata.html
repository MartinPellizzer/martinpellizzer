Colletia crenata

Colletia crenata, commonly known as the mountain candelabra cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that often form a candelabra-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its unique structure and adaptive features make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
