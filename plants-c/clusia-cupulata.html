Clusia cupulata

Clusia cupulata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-yellow berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Clusia cupulata is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for various horticultural applications.
