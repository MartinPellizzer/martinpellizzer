Cyathea hemichlamydea

Cyathea hemichlamydea, commonly known as the hemichlamydic tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its fronds.
