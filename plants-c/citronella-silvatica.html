Citronella silvatica

Citronella silvatica, commonly known as the mountain citronella, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its strong citrus scent, which is derived from the essential oils present in its leaves and stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
