Calophyllum exiticostatum

Calophyllum exiticostatum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting various forms of wildlife in its native habitat.
