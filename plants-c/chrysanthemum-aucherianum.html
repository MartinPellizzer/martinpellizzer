Chrysanthemum aucherianum

Chrysanthemum aucherianum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
