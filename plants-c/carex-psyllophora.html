Carex psyllophora

Carex psyllophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
