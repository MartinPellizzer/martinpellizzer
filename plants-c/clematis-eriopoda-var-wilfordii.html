Clematis eriopoda var. wilfordii

Clematis eriopoda var. wilfordii, commonly known as the Wilford's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can climb by means of twining.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.
