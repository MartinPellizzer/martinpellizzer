Cyanospermum javanicum

Cyanospermum javanicum, commonly known as the Java blue spiraling plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its spiral-shaped, blue-green filaments.

It belongs to the order Oscillatoriales and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich freshwater environments. The plant's pigmentation is due to the presence of phycocyanin, a blue-green pigment common in cyanobacteria. It plays a role in water clarification by consuming excess nutrients and can be found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats.

Cyanospermum javanicum is often used in bioremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb pollutants.
