Calluna vulgaris subf. torta

Calluna vulgaris subf.

torta, commonly known as the twisted heather, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and moorlands across the northern hemisphere.
