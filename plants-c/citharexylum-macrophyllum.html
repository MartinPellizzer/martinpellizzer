Citharexylum macrophyllum

Citharexylum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
