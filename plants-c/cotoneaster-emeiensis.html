Cotoneaster emeiensis

Cotoneaster emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sparsely branched structure. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

Cotoneaster emeiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
