Chlorophytum bharuchae

Chlorophytum bharuchae, commonly known as the Indian spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in regions like Gujarat and Maharashtra. The plant features long, grass-like leaves that are often striped with white or silver markings. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

Chlorophytum bharuchae is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
