Calamagrostis imberbis

Calamagrostis imberbis, commonly known as the slender reed, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis imberbis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
