Courtoisia cyperoides

Courtoisia cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish hue.

Courtoisia cyperoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
