Cirsium erisithales subsp. ochroleucum

Cirsium erisithales subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head, and the subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow to white coloration.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
