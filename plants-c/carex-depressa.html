Carex depressa

Carex depressa, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are often submerged or nearly so.

Carex depressa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
