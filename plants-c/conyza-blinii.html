Conyza blinii

Conyza blinii, commonly known as the blinii weed, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Conyza blinii is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
