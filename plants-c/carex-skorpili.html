Carex skorpili

Carex skorpili, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense, compact growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex skorpili is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
