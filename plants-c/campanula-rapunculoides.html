Campanula rapunculoides

Campanula rapunculoides, commonly known as the bellflower or hare's bell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campanula rapunculoides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
