Celtis laevigata var. texana

Celtis laevigata var. texana, commonly known as the Texas hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for some wildlife but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and along riverbanks.

The Texas hackberry is also valued for its adaptability and ability to provide habitat and food for various animals.
