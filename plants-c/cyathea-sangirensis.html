Cyathea sangirensis

Cyathea sangirensis, commonly known as the Sangirese tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of the upright stem.

Cyathea sangirensis plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
