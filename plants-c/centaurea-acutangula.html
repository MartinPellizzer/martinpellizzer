Centaurea acutangula

Centaurea acutangula, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea acutangula is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
