Carex feddeana

Carex feddeana, commonly known as the Fedde's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Carex feddeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
