Cestrum newellii

Cestrum newellii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are typically tubular with a five-lobed corolla, and it is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
