Canarium littorale f. rufum

Canarium littorale f. rufum, commonly known as the red-fruited candlenut.

It is a variant of the candlenut tree, native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, reddish-brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The tree thrives in sandy soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
