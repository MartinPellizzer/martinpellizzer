Cyrtopodium grisebachii

Cyrtopodium grisebachii, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, narrow leaf and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers with white petals and a deep violet lip. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in humid tropical forests. Its flowers bloom in spring and are characterized by their intricate, delicate structure.

Cyrtopodium grisebachii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
