Crepis conyzifolia subsp. djimilensis

Crepis conyzifolia subsp.

djimilensis, commonly known as the Djimilensis hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to parts of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
