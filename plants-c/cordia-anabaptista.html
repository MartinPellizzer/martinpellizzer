Cordia anabaptista

Cordia anabaptista, commonly known as the red cedar or red gum tree, is a member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, white to pink flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
