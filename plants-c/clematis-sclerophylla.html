Clematis sclerophylla

Clematis sclerophylla, commonly known as the rock clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Clematis sclerophylla is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
