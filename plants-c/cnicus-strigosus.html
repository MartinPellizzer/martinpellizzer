Cnicus strigosus

Cnicus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cnicus strigosus is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.
