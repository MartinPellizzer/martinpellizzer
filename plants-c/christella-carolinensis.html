Christella carolinensis

Christella carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native species supporting local ecosystems and its ornamental value in gardens.
