Cotula bicolor

Cotula bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Cotula bicolor has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
