Crataegus sheila-phippsiae var. saskatchewanensis

Crataegus sheila-phippsiae var. saskatchewanensis, commonly known as Saskatchewan hawthorn, is a rare and endemic shrub native to the Canadian province of Saskatchewan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
