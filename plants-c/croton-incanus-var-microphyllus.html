Croton incanus var. microphyllus

Croton incanus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often a vibrant green with prominent veins.

It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into three-winged, capsule-like fruits. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
