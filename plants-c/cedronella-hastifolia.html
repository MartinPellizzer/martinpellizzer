Cedronella hastifolia

Cedronella hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cedronella, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of alpine flora.
