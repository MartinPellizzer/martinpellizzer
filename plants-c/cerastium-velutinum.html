Cerastium velutinum

Cerastium velutinum, commonly known as downy chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, white flowers with five petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, often woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium velutinum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
