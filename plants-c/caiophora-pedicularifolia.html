Caiophora pedicularifolia

Caiophora pedicularifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers bloom throughout the year in warm climates, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
