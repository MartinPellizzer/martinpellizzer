Campanula kladniana subsp. mentiens

Campanula kladniana subsp.

mentiens, commonly known as the Kladnica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Czech Republic. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
