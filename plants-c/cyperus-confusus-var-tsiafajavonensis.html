Cyperus confusus var. tsiafajavonensis

Cyperus confusus var. tsiafajavonensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
