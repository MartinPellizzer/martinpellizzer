Chrysanthemum kotschyi

Chrysanthemum kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
