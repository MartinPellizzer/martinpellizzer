Cornus sericea f. californica

Cornus sericea f. californica, commonly known as the California dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly wavy texture.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
