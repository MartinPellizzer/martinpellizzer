Cucubalus paniculatus

Cucubalus paniculatus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody vine structure.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
