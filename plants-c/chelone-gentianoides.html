Chelone gentianoides

Chelone gentianoides, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, rich soils and partial to full shade.

Chelone gentianoides is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
