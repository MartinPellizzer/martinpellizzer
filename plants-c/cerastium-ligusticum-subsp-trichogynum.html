Cerastium ligusticum subsp. trichogynum

Cerastium ligusticum subsp.

trichogynum, commonly known as the hairy female cerastium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

This species is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
