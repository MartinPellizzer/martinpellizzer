Cornus controversa var. angustifolia

Cornus controversa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red or orange berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and often has a mottled appearance, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil.
