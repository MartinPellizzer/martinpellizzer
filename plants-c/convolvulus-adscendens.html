Convolvulus adscendens

Convolvulus adscendens, commonly known as the ascending bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Convolvulus adscendens is a perennial species that can spread aggressively through its extensive root system.
