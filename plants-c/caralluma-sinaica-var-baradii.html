Caralluma sinaica var. baradii

Caralluma sinaica var. baradii, commonly known as the Sinai Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
