Crataegus orbicularis

Crataegus orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its round, orbicular-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

Crataegus orbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
