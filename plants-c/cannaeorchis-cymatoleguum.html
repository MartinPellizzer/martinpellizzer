Cannaeorchis cymatoleguum

Cannaeorchis cymatoleguum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
