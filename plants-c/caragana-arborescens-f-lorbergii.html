Caragana arborescens f. lorbergii

Caragana arborescens f. lorbergii, commonly known as the silverleaf caragana, is a shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
