Chrysanthemum vulgare

Chrysanthemum vulgare, commonly known as the common chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including yellow, white, red, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 150 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysanthemum vulgare is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
