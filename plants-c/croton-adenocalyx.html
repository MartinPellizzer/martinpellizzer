Croton adenocalyx

Croton adenocalyx, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
