Crepis vesicaria var. willkommii

Crepis vesicaria var. willkommii, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features slender, hairy stems that produce small, clustered flowers with yellow stamens and white petals. The leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped, with a hairy texture and a slightly toothed edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
