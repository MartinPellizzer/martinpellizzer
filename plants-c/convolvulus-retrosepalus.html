Convolvulus retrosepalus

Convolvulus retrosepalus, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a cultivated and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
