Cereus candicans var. gladiatus

Cereus candicans var. gladiatus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Cereus peruvianus and Schlumbergera truncata.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that form a bushy structure, often with pointed, waxy leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This popular houseplant is well-suited for indoor cultivation and is relatively easy to care for.
