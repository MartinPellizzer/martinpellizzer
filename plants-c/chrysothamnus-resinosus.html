Chrysothamnus resinosus

Chrysothamnus resinosus, commonly known as sticky goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and resinous, sticky secretions on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
