Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. linearifolius

Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctively linear appearance. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
