Cuphea ferrisiae var. ferrisiae

Cuphea ferrisiae var. ferrisiae, commonly known as the red flame cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that resemble flames, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers within the bract clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant color and is suitable for container gardening or as a border plant.
