Cardamine purpurascens

Cardamine purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks, woodland floors, and wet meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish hue on the stems and undersides, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme, often appearing in early spring.

Cardamine purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and aquatic ecosystems.
