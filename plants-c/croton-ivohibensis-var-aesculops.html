Croton ivohibensis var. aesculops

Croton ivohibensis var. aesculops, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
