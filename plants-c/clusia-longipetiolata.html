Clusia longipetiolata

Clusia longipetiolata, commonly known as the broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces vibrant red bracts during the holiday season, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Clusia longipetiolata is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
