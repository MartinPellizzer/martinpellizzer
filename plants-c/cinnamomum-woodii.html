Cinnamomum woodii

Cinnamomum woodii, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
