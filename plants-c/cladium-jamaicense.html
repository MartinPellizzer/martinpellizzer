Cladium jamaicense

Cladium jamaicense, commonly known as sawgrass, is a perennial marsh plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves with serrated edges that resemble a saw, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems. Sawgrass forms dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes and roots help stabilize the soil, preventing erosion in marshy areas.
