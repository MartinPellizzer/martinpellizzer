Campanula rotundifolia f. micranthoides

Campanula rotundifolia f. micranthoides, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
