Cotula pumila

Cotula pumila, commonly known as dwarf dandelion, is a small annual plant native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow and arranged in clusters. Cotula pumila is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
