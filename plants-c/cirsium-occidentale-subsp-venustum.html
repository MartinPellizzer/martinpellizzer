Cirsium occidentale subsp. venustum

Cirsium occidentale subsp.

venustum, commonly known as the showy milkweed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and a distinct central vein. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single terminal head, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
