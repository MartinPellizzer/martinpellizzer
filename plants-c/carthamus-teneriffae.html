Carthamus teneriffae

Carthamus teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Islands safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and woody.

Carthamus teneriffae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
