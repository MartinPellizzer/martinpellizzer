Conospermum capitatum subsp. velutinum

Conospermum capitatum subsp.

velutinum, commonly known as the silky bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silky coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
