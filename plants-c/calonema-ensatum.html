Calonema ensatum

Calonema ensatum, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a perennial flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Calonema ensatum is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and adaptability to indoor environments.
