Cassia beareana

Cassia beareana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, winged pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Cassia beareana thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
