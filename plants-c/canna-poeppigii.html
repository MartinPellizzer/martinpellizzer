Canna poeppigii

Canna poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna poeppigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
