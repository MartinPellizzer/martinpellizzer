Crassula connata

Crassula connata, commonly known as the rock rose or stone plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Crassula connata is also prized for its ornamental value and ease of care.
