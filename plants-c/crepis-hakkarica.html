Crepis hakkarica

Crepis hakkarica, commonly known as the Karakorum hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis hakkarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
