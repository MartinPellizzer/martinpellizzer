Couma utilis

Couma utilis, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
