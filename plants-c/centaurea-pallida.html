Centaurea pallida

Centaurea pallida, commonly known as the pale centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale blue to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Centaurea pallida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
