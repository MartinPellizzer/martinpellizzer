Ceramia serpyllifolia

Ceramia serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and hardy species.
