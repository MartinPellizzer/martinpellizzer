Catapodium halleri var. pauciflorum

Catapodium halleri var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf catapodium, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical structure is characterized by a robust rhizome system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
