Crotalaria hislopii

Crotalaria hislopii, commonly known as the desert crown flower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and along stream banks.
