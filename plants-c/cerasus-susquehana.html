Cerasus susquehana

Cerasus susquehana, commonly known as the Susquehanna cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
