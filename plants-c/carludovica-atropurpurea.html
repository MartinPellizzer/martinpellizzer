Carludovica atropurpurea

Carludovica atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a striking member of the Passifloraceae family native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and red flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and are highly attractive to pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
