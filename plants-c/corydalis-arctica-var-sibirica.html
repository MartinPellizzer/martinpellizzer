Corydalis arctica var. sibirica

Corydalis arctica var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in cold climates.
