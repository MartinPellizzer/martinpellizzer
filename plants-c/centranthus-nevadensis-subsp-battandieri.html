Centranthus nevadensis subsp. battandieri

Centranthus nevadensis subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the Sierra needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of ecological study.
