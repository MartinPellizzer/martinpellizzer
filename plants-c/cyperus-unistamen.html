Cyperus unistamen

Cyperus unistamen, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a tall, cylindrical stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the top of the stem. Cyperus unistamen is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
