Cyperus speciosus

Cyperus speciosus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Cyperus speciosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
