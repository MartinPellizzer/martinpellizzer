Crepis aurea var. glabrescens

Crepis aurea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the golden hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow, adding visual interest to the landscape.

This variety is notable for its glabrous (smooth) appearance compared to other forms of Crepis aurea.
