Convolvulus barbiger

Convolvulus barbiger, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in gardens.

Convolvulus barbiger is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.
