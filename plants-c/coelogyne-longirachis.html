Coelogyne longirachis

Coelogyne longirachis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, arching inflorescence that bears clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Coelogyne longirachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
