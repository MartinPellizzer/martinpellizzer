Crucifera turritis

Crucifera turritis, commonly known as the Turritous crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often spirally arranged inflorescence and small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually pinnately lobed, and the fruit is a dry, dehiscent silique.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
