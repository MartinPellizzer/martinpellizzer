Chionanthus axilliflorus subsp. axilliflorus

Chionanthus axilliflorus subsp.

axilliflorus, commonly known as the silverbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the spring.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The subspecies axilliflorus is distinguished by its flowers that are borne in the leaf axils, giving it its specific epithet.
