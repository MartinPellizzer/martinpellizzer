Cucurbita maxima subsp. andreana

Cucurbita maxima subsp.

andreana, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a cultivated variant of the common pumpkin. It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The plant is a vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
