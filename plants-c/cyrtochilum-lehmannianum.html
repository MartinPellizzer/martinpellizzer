Cyrtochilum lehmannianum

Cyrtochilum lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a pale yellow or greenish hue, and are adorned with intricate patterns.

Cyrtochilum lehmannianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
