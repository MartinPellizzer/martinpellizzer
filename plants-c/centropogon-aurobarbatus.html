Centropogon aurobarbatus

Centropogon aurobarbatus, commonly known as the golden-barbed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
