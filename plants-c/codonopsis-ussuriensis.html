Codonopsis ussuriensis

Codonopsis ussuriensis, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng or Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, while its roots are often harvested for medicinal use.

Codonopsis ussuriensis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and immune-boosting properties.
