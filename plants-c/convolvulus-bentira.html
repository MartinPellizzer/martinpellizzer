Convolvulus bentira

Convolvulus bentira, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus bentira is an annual or biennial plant, blooming from spring to early summer.
