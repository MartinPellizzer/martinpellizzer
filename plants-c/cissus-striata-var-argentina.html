Cissus striata var. argentina

Cissus striata var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine cutleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive striated pattern along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its rapid growth makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid environments.
