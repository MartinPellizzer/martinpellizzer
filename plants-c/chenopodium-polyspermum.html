Chenopodium polyspermum

Chenopodium polyspermum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are tender and edible. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as it is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Its growth habit includes upright stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into seeds.
