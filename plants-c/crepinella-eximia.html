Crepinella eximia

Crepinella eximia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
