Chamaedorea formosa

Chamaedorea formosa, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the velvet palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gracefully arching fronds that give it a lush, tropical appearance. Chamaedorea formosa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
