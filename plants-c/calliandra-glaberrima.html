Calliandra glaberrima

Calliandra glaberrima, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Additionally, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
