Chenopodium canihua

Chenopodium canihua, commonly known as canihua, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are rich in nutrients, including protein and minerals. Canihua thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

It has been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities for both food and medicinal purposes.
