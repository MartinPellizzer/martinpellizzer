Convolvulus armatus

Convolvulus armatus, commonly known as the armed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Convolvulus armatus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing and trailing plants.
