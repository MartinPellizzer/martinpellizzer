Chaerophyllum brachycarpum

Chaerophyllum brachycarpum, commonly known as small-seeded chaerophyllum, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, dry, and schizocarpic, splitting into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
