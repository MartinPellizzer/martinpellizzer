Chenopodium densifoliatum

Chenopodium densifoliatum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, inflated seed pods. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

Chenopodium densifoliatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
