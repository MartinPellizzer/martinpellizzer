Cleome confusa

Cleome confusa, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often clustered in groups of three, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance.

Cleome confusa is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.
