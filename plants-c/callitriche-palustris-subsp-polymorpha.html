Callitriche palustris subsp. polymorpha

Callitriche palustris subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as swamp water-star, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetland environments. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in ponds, ditches, and marshes.

The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
