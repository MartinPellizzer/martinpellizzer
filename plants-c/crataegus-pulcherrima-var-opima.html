Crataegus pulcherrima var. opima

Crataegus pulcherrima var. opima, commonly known as the Opima hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, gray-brown bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a resilient ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitat.
