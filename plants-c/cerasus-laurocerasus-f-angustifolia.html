Cerasus laurocerasus f. angustifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark glossy green leaves that are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark red berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic if ingested.
