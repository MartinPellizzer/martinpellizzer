Chrysopsis resinolens

Chrysopsis resinolens, commonly known as the resinous goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often reddish in color.

Chrysopsis resinolens is known for its ability to produce a resinous substance, which has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
