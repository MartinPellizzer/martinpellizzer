Crocus abracteolus

Crocus abracteolus, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant, scent. It typically blooms in early spring and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Crocus abracteolus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
