Cereus dybowskii

Cereus dybowskii, commonly known as the Dybowskii Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, spindly stem and produces large, showy white flowers at night. The plant is characterized by its sharp, cylindrical spines and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cereus dybowskii is also known for its ability to bloom during the Christmas season, earning it the nickname "Christmas Cactus.".
