Coccinia diversifolia

Coccinia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Coccinia diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
