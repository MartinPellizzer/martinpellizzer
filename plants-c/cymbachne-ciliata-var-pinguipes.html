Cymbachne ciliata var. pinguipes

Cymbachne ciliata var. pinguipes, commonly known as the woolly cymbachne, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping or as a source of forage.
