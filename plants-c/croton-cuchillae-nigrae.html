Croton cuchillae-nigrae

Croton cuchillae-nigrae, commonly known as black croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic members. The plant is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking, colorful inflorescences that range from yellow to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
