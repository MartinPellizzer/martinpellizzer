Clematis psilandra

Clematis psilandra, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky coating. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clematis psilandra requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
