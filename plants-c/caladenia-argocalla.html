Caladenia argocalla

Caladenia argocalla, commonly known as the crimson spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are predominantly red or orange with intricate patterns, attracting specific pollinators like thynnid wasps.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
