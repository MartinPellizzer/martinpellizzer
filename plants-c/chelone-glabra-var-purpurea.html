Chelone glabra var. purpurea

Chelone glabra var. purpurea, commonly known as the white wood betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental value contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
