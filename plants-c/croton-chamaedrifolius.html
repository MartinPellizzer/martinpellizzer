Croton chamaedrifolius

Croton chamaedrifolius, commonly known as the creeping croton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
