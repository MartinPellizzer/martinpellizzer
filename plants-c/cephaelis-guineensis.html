Cephaelis guineensis

Cephaelis guineensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cephaelis guineensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
