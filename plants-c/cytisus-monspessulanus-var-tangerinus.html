Cytisus monspessulanus var. tangerinus

Cytisus monspessulanus var. tangerinus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically thorny. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

The variety tangerinus is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other cytisus species.
