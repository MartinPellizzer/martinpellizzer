Cytisus decumbens var. procumbens

Cytisus decumbens var. procumbens, commonly known as creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
