Cacalia guineensis

Cacalia guineensis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a sprawling or climbing habit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the flowers consist of yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pink ray florets.

Cacalia guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
