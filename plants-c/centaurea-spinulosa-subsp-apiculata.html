Centaurea spinulosa subsp. apiculata

Centaurea spinulosa subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are surrounded by pale blue or white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
