Calamus longipinna

Calamus longipinna, commonly known as the long-pinnate calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine.

Calamus longipinna is an important component of wetland and swamp ecosystems, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
