Chrysanthemum schwarzianum

Chrysanthemum schwarzianum, commonly known as the German chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features bright yellow or orange flowers with a central disc, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
