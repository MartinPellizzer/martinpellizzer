Croton squamulosus

Croton squamulosus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
