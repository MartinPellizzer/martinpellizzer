Carduus medius subsp. martrinii

Carduus medius subsp.

martrinii, commonly known as the Mediterranean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
