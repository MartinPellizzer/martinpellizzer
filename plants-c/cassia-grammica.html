Cassia grammica

Cassia grammica, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Cassia grammica is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and street landscaping.
