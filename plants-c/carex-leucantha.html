Carex leucantha

Carex leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex leucantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
