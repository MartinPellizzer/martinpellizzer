Carlina vulgaris proles malinvaudii

Carlina vulgaris proles malinvaudii, commonly known as the Malinvaud's common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
