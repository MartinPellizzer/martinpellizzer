Catasetum vinaceum var. splendidum

Catasetum vinaceum var. splendidum, commonly known as the Splendid Catasetum, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange-colored flowers, which are highly specialized for pollination by specific bees. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers, and it possesses a unique structure called a "column" that houses the reproductive organs.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
