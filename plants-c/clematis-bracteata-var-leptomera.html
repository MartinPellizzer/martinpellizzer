Clematis bracteata var. leptomera

Clematis bracteata var. leptomera, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
