Chorisepalum ovatum var. sipapoanum

Chorisepalum ovatum var. sipapoanum, commonly known as the Sipapoan star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
