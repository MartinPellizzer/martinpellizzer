Cerasiocarpum zeylanicum

Cerasiocarpum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian cherry or Ceylon cherry, is a tropical plant native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
