Callitriche platycarpa var. leptophylla

Callitriche platycarpa var. leptophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often found in nutrient-rich environments and is an important part of the aquatic ecosystem.
