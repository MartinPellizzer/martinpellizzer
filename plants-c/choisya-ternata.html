Choisya ternata

Choisya ternata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright purple berries in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Choisya ternata is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other wildlife due to its edible berries.
