Cinnagrostis violacea var. violacea

Cinnagrostis violacea var. violacea, commonly known as the violet sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, succulent stems and grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
