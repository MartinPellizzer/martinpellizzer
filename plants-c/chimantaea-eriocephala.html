Chimantaea eriocephala

Chimantaea eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
