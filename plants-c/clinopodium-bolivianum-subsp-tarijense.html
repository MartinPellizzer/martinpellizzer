Clinopodium bolivianum subsp. tarijense

Clinopodium bolivianum subsp.

tarijense, commonly known as Tarijense mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and possibly medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the broader species.
