Cymbopogon validus var. lysocladus

Cymbopogon validus var. lysocladus, commonly known as lemon grass or citronella grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes, aromatherapy, and natural insect repellents. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
