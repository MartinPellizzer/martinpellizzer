Calyptranthes ramosissima

Calyptranthes ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Calyptranthes ramosissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
