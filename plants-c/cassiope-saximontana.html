Cassiope saximontana

Cassiope saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra snowbells, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, mat-forming foliage that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and open slopes.

Cassiope saximontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
