Chomelia truncatocalyx

Chomelia truncatocalyx, commonly known as the roughleaf chomelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Chomelia truncatocalyx is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
