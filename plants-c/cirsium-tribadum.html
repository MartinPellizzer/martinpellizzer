Cirsium tribadum

Cirsium tribadum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are spiny and lanceolate, with a grayish-green color, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium tribadum is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
