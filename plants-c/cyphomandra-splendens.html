Cyphomandra splendens

Cyphomandra splendens, commonly known as the golden apple guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruits, which are covered in a rough, hairy skin. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.
