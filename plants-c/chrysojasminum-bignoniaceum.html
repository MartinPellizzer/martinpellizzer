Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum

Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum, commonly known as the golden jasmine vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bignoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring opposite leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
