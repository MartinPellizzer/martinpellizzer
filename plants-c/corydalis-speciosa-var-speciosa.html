Corydalis speciosa var. speciosa

Corydalis speciosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple. The flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
