Carex umbrosa var. coreana

Carex umbrosa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean shadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually dark green and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
