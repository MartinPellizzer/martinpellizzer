Cracca benensis

Cracca benensis, commonly known as the Benin cracca, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or red in color.

Cracca benensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
