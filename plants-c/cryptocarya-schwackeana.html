Cryptocarya schwackeana

Cryptocarya schwackeana, commonly known as the New Zealand white mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and typically grows on the branches of host trees such as rimu and kahikatea. The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white waxy coating. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers followed by round, fleshy berries.

Cryptocarya schwackeana plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects.
