Carex pairae var. leersii

Carex pairae var. leersii, commonly known as the Leers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the sedge family.
