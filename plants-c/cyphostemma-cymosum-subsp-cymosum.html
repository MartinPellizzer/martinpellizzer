Cyphostemma cymosum subsp. cymosum

Cyphostemma cymosum subsp.

cymosum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in cymes, followed by yellow, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
