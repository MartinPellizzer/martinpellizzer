Campanula tridentata var. bithynica

Campanula tridentata var. bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Turkey and the Balkans.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, giving the plant its species name "tridentata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
