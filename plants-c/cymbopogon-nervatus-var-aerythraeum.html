Cymbopogon nervatus var. aerythraeum

Cymbopogon nervatus var. aerythraeum, commonly known as the red-stemmed lemon grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish-brown stems and aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing tiny, pale green flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and natural medicine.
