Cirsium × wankelii

Cirsium × wankelii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × wankelii is often found in rocky or grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
