Cerastium arvense f. capitatum

Cerastium arvense f. capitatum, commonly known as the clustered mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and delicate appearance.
