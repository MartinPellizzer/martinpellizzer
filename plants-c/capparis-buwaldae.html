Capparis buwaldae

Capparis buwaldae, commonly known as the Balochistan Caper, is a flowering plant native to Pakistan and parts of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, while its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
