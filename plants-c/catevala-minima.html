Catevala minima

Catevala minima, commonly known as the small green orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a subtle fragrance, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Catevala minima is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
