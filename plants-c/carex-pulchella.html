Carex pulchella

Carex pulchella, commonly known as the elegant sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
