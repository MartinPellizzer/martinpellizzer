Cypripedium fasciculatum f. viride

Cypripedium fasciculatum f. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, greenish-yellow petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest floors, thriving in cool and humid environments. Its flowers are highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to their unique coloration and rarity.

This orchid plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
