Cyperus recurvus

Cyperus recurvus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowering spikes are typically clustered at the top of the plant, forming a distinctive umbrella-like shape.

Cyperus recurvus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
