Chesneya cuneata

Chesneya cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chesneya, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, conical inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to red.

Chesneya cuneata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
