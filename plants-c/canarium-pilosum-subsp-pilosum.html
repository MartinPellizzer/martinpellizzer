Canarium pilosum subsp. pilosum

Canarium pilosum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its lanceolate, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The tree produces woody, oval fruits containing a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has economic and ecological significance due to its oil-rich seeds and role in local ecosystems.
