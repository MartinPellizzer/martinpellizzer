Carex macloviana subsp. subfusca

Carex macloviana subsp.

subfusca, commonly known as the dark-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
