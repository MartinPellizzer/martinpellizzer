Calamagrostis deschampsioides var. subapicalis

Calamagrostis deschampsioides var. subapicalis, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and feathery plumes that emerge in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland restoration and water garden settings. Its narrow leaves are often used in ornamental landscaping for their elegant, upright growth habit.

The variety subapicalis is distinguished by its unique flowering structure, with inflorescences that are more compact and clustered at the apex of the stems.
