Cucumis melo convar. adana

Cucumis melo convar. adana, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, slender fruits.

The plant typically grows as a vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves, thriving in warm climates. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in Cucurbitaceae family plants. The fruit develops from the female flower and is typically harvested when it reaches a mature green stage.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mild flavor and is cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
