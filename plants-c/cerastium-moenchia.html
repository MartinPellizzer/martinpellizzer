Cerastium moenchia

Cerastium moenchia, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and typically forms a mat-like growth habit. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium moenchia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural habitats.
