Calla humilis

Calla humilis, commonly known as the dwarf calla, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces elegant, white spatulate flowers with a yellow spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Calla humilis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
