Commelina rufipes

Commelina rufipes, commonly known as the redfoot spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with red veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.
