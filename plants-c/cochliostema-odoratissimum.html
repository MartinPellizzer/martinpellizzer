Cochliostema odoratissimum

Cochliostema odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
