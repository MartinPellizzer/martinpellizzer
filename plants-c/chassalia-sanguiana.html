Chassalia sanguiana

Chassalia sanguiana, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that resemble blood, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Chassalia sanguiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
