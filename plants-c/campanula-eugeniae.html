Campanula eugeniae

Campanula eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Campanula eugeniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
