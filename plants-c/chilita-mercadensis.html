Chilita mercadensis

Chilita mercadensis, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright red or orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chilita mercadensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
