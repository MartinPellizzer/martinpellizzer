Crocus clusianus

Crocus clusianus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus clusianus is a hardy perennial that blooms in early spring.
