Cynodon dactylon var. hirsutissimus

Cynodon dactylon var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as hairy Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It is often used for lawns, erosion control, and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

Its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth allows it to spread aggressively, making it both useful and potentially invasive.
