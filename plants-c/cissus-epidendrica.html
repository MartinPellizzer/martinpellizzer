Cissus epidendrica

Cissus epidendrica, commonly known as the climbing fig-leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus epidendrica is known for its vigorous climbing habit and is popular for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
