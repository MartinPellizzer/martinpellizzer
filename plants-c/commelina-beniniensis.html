Commelina beniniensis

Commelina beniniensis, commonly known as the Benin spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Commelina family (Commelinaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface, while its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves.

Commelina beniniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
