Calliandra grisebachiana var. carolae

Calliandra grisebachiana var. carolae, commonly known as the Carolae calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
