Carlea

Carlea, commonly known as the "buttercup flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and includes several species, such as Carlea aurea and Carlea tetragona. The plants are characterized by their showy, often yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. They typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.

Carlea species are popular in gardens for their vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
