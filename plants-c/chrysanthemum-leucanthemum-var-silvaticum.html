Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. silvaticum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. silvaticum, commonly known as the wood chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy yellow to orange flower heads with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
