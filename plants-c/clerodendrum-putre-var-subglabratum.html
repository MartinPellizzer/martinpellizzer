Clerodendrum putre var. subglabratum

Clerodendrum putre var. subglabratum, commonly known as the silver-leaf clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it popular in horticulture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
