Cassia barbata

Cassia barbata, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, spindle-shaped pods containing seeds. Cassia barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
