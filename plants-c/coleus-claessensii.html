Coleus claessensii

Coleus claessensii, commonly known as the African coleus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that vary in hue from green to red, purple, and yellow. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters at the leaf axils.

Coleus claessensii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
