Campanula spatulata subsp. spruneriana

Campanula spatulata subsp.

spruneriana, commonly known as the spruner's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
