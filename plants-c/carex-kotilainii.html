Carex kotilainii

Carex kotilainii, commonly known as the Kamchatka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, boggy environments and is often found in tundra and subarctic regions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Carex kotilainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
