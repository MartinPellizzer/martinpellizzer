Cistus serpillifolius

Cistus serpillifolius, commonly known as rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing shrub form, with evergreen leaves that are narrow and leathery. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Cistus serpillifolius is also known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
