Camara lucida

Camara lucida, commonly known as the nightshade or deadly nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic if ingested.

The plant contains alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine, which have both medicinal and poisonous properties.
