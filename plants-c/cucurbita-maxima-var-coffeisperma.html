Cucurbita maxima var. coffeisperma

Cucurbita maxima var. coffeisperma, commonly known as the coffee-seed gourd, is a variant of the common squash species.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green, ribbed, and ridged fruit that resembles a coffee bean in appearance. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds within the fruit are typically black and can be used for propagation or culinary purposes.
