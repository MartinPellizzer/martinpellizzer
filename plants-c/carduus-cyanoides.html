Carduus cyanoides

Carduus cyanoides, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a bitter taste, while the flowers are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
