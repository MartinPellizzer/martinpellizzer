Crepis corniculata

Crepis corniculata, commonly known as the horned hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a single, showy head, making it a notable wildflower in its native range.
