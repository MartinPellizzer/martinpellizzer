Crepidium stenostachys

Crepidium stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a slight purple tinge, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Crepidium stenostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
