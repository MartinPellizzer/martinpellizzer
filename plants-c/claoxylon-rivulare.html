Claoxylon rivulare

Claoxylon rivulare, commonly known as the river celtuce, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found near water sources.
