Cryptocarya wilsonii

Cryptocarya wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya wilsonii is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
