Coleonema dregeanum

Coleonema dregeanum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Coleonema dregeanum is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient plant in Mediterranean climates.
