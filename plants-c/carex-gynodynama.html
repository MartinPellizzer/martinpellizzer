Carex gynodynama

Carex gynodynama, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Carex gynodynama plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
