Carex binata

Carex binata, commonly known as the two-branched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often subtended by bracts.

Carex binata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
