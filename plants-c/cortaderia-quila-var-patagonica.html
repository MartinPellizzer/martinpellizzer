Cortaderia quila var. patagonica

Cortaderia quila var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian feather grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that create a dramatic, architectural appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping, making it a popular choice in arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense clumps that provide structure and texture in garden settings.
