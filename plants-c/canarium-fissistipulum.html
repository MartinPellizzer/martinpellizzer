Canarium fissistipulum

Canarium fissistipulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candlenut.

It is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a narrow, pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and the seeds are valued for their oil content.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland rainforests.
