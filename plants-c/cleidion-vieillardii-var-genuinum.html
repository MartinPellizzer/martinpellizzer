Cleidion vieillardii var. genuinum

Cleidion vieillardii var. genuinum, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fruit that resembles a mango in appearance, though it is not related to the true mango. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
