Celtis mindanaensis

Celtis mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling berries.

Celtis mindanaensis is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its hard wood and ecological importance in its native habitat.
