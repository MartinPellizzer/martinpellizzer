Crassula latibracteata

Crassula latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
