Coleus argentatus

Coleus argentatus, commonly known as silverleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a velvety texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
