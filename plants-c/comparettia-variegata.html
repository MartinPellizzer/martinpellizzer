Comparettia variegata

Comparettia variegata, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with green and white patterns. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
