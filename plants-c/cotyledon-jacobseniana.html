Cotyledon jacobseniana

Cotyledon jacobseniana, commonly known as the Jacob's cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Aizoaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
