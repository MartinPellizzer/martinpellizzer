Clematis viticella subsp. crispa

Clematis viticella subsp.

crispa, commonly known as the curly clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant produces a distinctive, curled appearance in its foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for trellises or fences.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental vine.
