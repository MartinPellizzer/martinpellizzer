Chaetaria hystrix

Chaetaria hystrix, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in temperate regions with consistent moisture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Chaetaria hystrix plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
