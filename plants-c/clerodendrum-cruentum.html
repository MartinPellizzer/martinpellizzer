Clerodendrum cruentum

Clerodendrum cruentum, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
