Capsicum havanense

Capsicum havanense, commonly known as the Havana chili or the Mexican yellow chili, is a species of Capsicum native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow to orange-red fruits that mature to a deep red color. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is used in various culinary applications, particularly in traditional Mexican and Caribbean cuisines.
