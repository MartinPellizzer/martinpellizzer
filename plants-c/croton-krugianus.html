Croton krugianus

Croton krugianus, commonly known as the Krug's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
