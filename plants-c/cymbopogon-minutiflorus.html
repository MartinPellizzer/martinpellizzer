Cymbopogon minutiflorus

Cymbopogon minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves contain essential oils, particularly citral, which contribute to its distinctive lemony fragrance.

Cymbopogon minutiflorus is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
