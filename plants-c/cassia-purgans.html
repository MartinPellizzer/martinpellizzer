Cassia purgans

Cassia purgans, commonly known as yellow flame or Indian cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Cassia purgans is known for its medicinal properties, with its bark and leaves used in traditional medicine for their laxative effects.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
