Cyperus semitrifidus var. sanguinolentus

Cyperus semitrifidus var. sanguinolentus, commonly known as the red-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish or purple in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
