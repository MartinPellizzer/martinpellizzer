Cotula sericea var. concolor

Cotula sericea var. concolor, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pale green to silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming from late spring to early autumn. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
