Cereus limensis

Cereus limensis, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble the arms of a human, giving it a unique and striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the Easter season, hence its common name. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cereus limensis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
