Conocephalus diffusus var. obtusus

Conocephalus diffusus var. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, with the leaves at the base being broader than those higher up. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This variety is characterized by its rounded leaf tips, distinguishing it from other varieties of Conocephalus.
