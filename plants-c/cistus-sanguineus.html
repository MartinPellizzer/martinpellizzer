Cistus sanguineus

Cistus sanguineus, commonly known as red rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus sanguineus is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
