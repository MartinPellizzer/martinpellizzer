Calyptranthes brasiliensis var. laxa

Calyptranthes brasiliensis var. laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
