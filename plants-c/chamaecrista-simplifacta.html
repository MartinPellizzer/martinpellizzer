Chamaecrista simplifacta

Chamaecrista simplifacta, commonly known as the partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas like fields and roadsides.

Chamaecrista simplifacta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
