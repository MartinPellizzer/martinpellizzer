Cylindropuntia ganderi

Cylindropuntia ganderi, commonly known as Gander's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, known as prickles, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines. Its adaptations to arid environments include succulent stems for water storage and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Cylindropuntia ganderi plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
