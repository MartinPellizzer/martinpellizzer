Calea abelioides

Calea abelioides, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea abelioides is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
