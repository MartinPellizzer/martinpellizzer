Crataegus oxyacantha f. plena

Crataegus oxyacantha f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white to pink flowers, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red berries in the fall. It is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The flowers are particularly showy, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
