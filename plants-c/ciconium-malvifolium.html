Ciconium malvifolium

Ciconium malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and can form a dense mat, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Ciconium malvifolium is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
