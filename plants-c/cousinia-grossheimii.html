Cousinia grossheimii

Cousinia grossheimii, commonly known as the Armenian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia grossheimii is well adapted to harsh climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
