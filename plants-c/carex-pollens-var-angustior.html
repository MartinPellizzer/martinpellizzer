Carex pollens var. angustior

Carex pollens var. angustior, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
