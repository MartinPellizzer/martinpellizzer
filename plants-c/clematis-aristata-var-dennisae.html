Clematis aristata var. dennisae

Clematis aristata var. dennisae, commonly known as the Dennis's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
