Codonopsis minima

Codonopsis minima, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five lobes, and bloom in late summer.

Codonopsis minima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
