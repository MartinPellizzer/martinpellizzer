Caperonia senegalensis

Caperonia senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese caper, is a flowering plant native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and leathery. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Caperonia senegalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
