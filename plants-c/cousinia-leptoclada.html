Cousinia leptoclada

Cousinia leptoclada, commonly known as the slender leaf cottonthistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia leptoclada produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators.
