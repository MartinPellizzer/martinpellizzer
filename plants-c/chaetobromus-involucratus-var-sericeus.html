Chaetobromus involucratus var. sericeus

Chaetobromus involucratus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with fleshy, hairy stems that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
