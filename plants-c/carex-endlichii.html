Carex endlichii

Carex endlichii, commonly known as the Endlich's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Carex endlichii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
