Cyperus excisus

Cyperus excisus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers wet or marshy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.

Cyperus excisus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
