Cissus barteri

Cissus barteri, commonly known as the climbing fig or barter's cissus, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Cissus barteri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in landscaping.
