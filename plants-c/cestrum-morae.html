Cestrum morae

Cestrum morae, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum morae is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
