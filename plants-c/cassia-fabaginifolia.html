Cassia fabaginifolia

Cassia fabaginifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia fabaginifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
