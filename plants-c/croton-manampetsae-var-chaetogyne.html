Croton manampetsae var. chaetogyne

Croton manampetsae var. chaetogyne, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
