Cimicifuga racemosa var. dissecta

Cimicifuga racemosa var. dissecta, commonly known as the laceleaf black cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with finely cut, fern-like foliage that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
