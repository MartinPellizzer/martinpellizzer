Cyclophorus acrocarpus

Cyclophorus acrocarpus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within montane forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Cyclophorus acrocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
