Copiapoa cinerascens var. grandiflora

Copiapoa cinerascens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant copiapoa, is a columnar cactus native to northern Chile.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
