Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. major

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. major, commonly known as the tall bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
