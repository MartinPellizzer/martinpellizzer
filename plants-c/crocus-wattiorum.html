Crocus wattiorum

Crocus wattiorum, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The Persian crocus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
