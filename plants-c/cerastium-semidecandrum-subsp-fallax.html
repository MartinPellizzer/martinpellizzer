Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. fallax

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, white flowers that typically bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and its ability to form dense mats, making it a common ground cover in suitable habitats.
