Chrysanthemum komarovii

Chrysanthemum komarovii, commonly known as the Siberian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers with a distinct, aromatic scent. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
