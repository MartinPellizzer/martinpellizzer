Cynanchum contiguum

Cynanchum contiguum, commonly known as the creeping whipsticker, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds with tufts of silky hair for wind dispersal.

Cynanchum contiguum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
