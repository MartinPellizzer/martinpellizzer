Chaetocyperus

Chaetocyperus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where they thrive in moist to water-saturated soils. Chaetocyperus species are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with narrow leaves and spike-like flower clusters. They often form dense tussocks, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to colonize disturbed wetland areas.
