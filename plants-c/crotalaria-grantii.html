Crotalaria grantii

Crotalaria grantii, commonly known as the African spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy texture, while its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Crotalaria grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
