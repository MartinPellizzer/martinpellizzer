Cyathea glandulosa

Cyathea glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters known as sori.

Cyathea glandulosa is often found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests.
