Centranthus angustifolius subsp. longicalcaratus

Centranthus angustifolius subsp.

longicalcaratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming in summer. Its flowers are five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
