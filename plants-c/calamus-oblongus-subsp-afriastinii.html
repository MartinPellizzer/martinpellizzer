Calamus oblongus subsp. afriastinii

Calamus oblongus subsp.

afriastinii, commonly known as the African calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. This subspecies is native to tropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit, with long, arching leaves that can reach several meters in length.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
