Cassia diphylla

Cassia diphylla, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia diphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
