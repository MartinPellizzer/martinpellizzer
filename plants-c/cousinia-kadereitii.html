Cousinia kadereitii

Cousinia kadereitii, commonly known as the Karakoram fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps to reduce water loss.

Cousinia kadereitii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
