Cheiranthus semperflorens

Cheiranthus semperflorens, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its clustered flowers, which can range in color from yellow to red and orange. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hardy nature.
