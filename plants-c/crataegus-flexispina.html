Crataegus flexispina

Crataegus flexispina, commonly known as the flexible thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Crataegus flexispina typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas.

Its leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a smooth upper surface.
