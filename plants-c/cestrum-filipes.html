Cestrum filipes

Cestrum filipes, commonly known as the silver bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum filipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a decorative vine in gardens and greenhouses.
