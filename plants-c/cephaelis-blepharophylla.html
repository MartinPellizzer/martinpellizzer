Cephaelis blepharophylla

Cephaelis blepharophylla, commonly known as the eye-leaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
