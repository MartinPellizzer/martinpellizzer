Campylanthus incanus

Campylanthus incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Campylanthus incanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
