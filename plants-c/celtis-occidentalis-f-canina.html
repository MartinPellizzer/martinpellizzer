Celtis occidentalis f. canina

Celtis occidentalis f. canina, commonly known as the dogwood maple or American dogwood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and showy white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red drupes that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
