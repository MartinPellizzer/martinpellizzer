Caucalis elata

Caucalis elata, commonly known as the tall caucalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a finely toothed margin, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Caucalis elata is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.
