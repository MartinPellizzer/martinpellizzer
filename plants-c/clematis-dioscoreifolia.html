Clematis dioscoreifolia

Clematis dioscoreifolia, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can become woody with age, and it prefers well-drained soil in partial to full sun.

Clematis dioscoreifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
