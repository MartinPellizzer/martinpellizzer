Calamintha nepeta var. acinifolia

Calamintha nepeta var. acinifolia, commonly known as caraway thyme or sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, aromatic leaves that are often described as having a licorice-like scent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is used in culinary applications, particularly in flavoring dishes and beverages.

The plant is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and calming properties.
