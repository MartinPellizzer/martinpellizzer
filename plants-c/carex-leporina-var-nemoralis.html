Carex leporina var. nemoralis

Carex leporina var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
