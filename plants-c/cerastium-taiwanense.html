Cerastium taiwanense

Cerastium taiwanense, commonly known as Taiwan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
