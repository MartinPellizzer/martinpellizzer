Chilita multihamata

Chilita multihamata, commonly known as the multi-horned chilita, is a unique plant species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, multi-branched inflorescence that resembles a cluster of horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and they bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Chilita multihamata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
