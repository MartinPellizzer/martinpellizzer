Croton botryocarpus

Croton botryocarpus, commonly known as the "Coral tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble coral, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive, often reddish or purple hue.
