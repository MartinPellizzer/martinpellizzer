Canavalia macrobotrys

Canavalia macrobotrys, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, and the pods are elongated, containing edible seeds.

Canavalia macrobotrys is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in sustainable agriculture.
