Calamus renukae

Calamus renukae, commonly known as the Renuka canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the regions of India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by dark blue-black berries.

Calamus renukae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional practices.
