Carex tatjanae

Carex tatjanae, commonly known as Tatjana's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Carex tatjanae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
