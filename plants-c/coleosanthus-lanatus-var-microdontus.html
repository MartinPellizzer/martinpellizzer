Coleosanthus lanatus var. microdontus

Coleosanthus lanatus var. microdontus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed papyrus, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often submerged in shallow waters. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with tiny, clustered blooms that are pollinated by insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
