Carthamus albus

Carthamus albus, commonly known as white safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and relatively shallow.

Carthamus albus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in dyeing and herbal remedies.
