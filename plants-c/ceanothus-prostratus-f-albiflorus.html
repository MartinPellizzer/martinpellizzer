Ceanothus prostratus f. albiflorus

Ceanothus prostratus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered creeping ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to California.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers during the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
