Cinnamomum kruseanum

Cinnamomum kruseanum, commonly known as wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The bark, while not as commercially valuable as true cinnamon, is also used in some local remedies.
