Cirsium tauschii

Cirsium tauschii, commonly known as Tausch's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with coarse teeth, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium tauschii is also considered an important species in the study of plant genetics due to its role in hybridization with other thistle species.
