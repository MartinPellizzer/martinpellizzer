Caladenia × triangularis

Caladenia × triangularis, commonly known as the Triangular Spider-orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flowers that resemble spiders, with a narrow, triangular labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It produces a single, upright stem bearing several flowers, which are usually pink or purple in color.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
