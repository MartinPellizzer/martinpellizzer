Campanula rapunculus f. hirta

Campanula rapunculus f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.
