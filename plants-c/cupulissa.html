Cupulissa

Cupulissa, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Cupulissa has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and stream banks, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
