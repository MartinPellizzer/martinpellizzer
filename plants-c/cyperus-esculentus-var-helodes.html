Cyperus esculentus var. helodes

Cyperus esculentus var. helodes, commonly known as water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a thick, edible rhizome that grows beneath the water surface, often forming dense colonies in shallow wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and are used as a vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
