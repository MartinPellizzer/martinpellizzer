Crataegus heterophylla

Crataegus heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which can vary in shape and size depending on the plant's environment, a trait that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus heterophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
