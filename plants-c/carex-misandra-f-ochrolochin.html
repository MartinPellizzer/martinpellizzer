Carex misandra f. ochrolochin

Carex misandra f. ochrolochin, commonly known as the ochrolochin sedge, is a variant of the Carex misandra species, characterized by its distinct foliage coloration.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a striking yellow-green in color, setting it apart from other sedges. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer months, which are wind-pollinated.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
