Campylotropis smithii

Campylotropis smithii, commonly known as Smith's climbing brier, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, round seeds.

Campylotropis smithii is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
