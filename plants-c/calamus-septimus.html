Calamus septimus

Calamus septimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a member of the Acoraceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinct, pleasant fragrance. Its rhizomes are commonly used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus septimus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting biodiversity.
