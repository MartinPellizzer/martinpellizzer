Coreopsis taylorii

Coreopsis taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent central disks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Taylor's coreopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
