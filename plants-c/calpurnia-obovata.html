Calpurnia obovata

Calpurnia obovata, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Calpurnia obovata is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
