Crotalaria retusa var. tunguensis

Crotalaria retusa var. tunguensis, commonly known as the Tunguens spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like structures and are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
