Chamaecrista pedicellaris var. holguinensis

Chamaecrista pedicellaris var. holguinensis, commonly known as the Holguin's burr legume, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by oblong, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or limestone soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
