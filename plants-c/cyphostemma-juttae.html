Cyphostemma juttae

Cyphostemma juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, horn-like fruits. Cyphostemma juttae is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
