Conyza retusa

Conyza retusa, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are borne on slender peduncles. Conyza retusa thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
