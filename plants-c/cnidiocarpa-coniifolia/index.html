Cnidiocarpa Coniifolia

Cape ivy, scientifically known as Cnidiocarpa coniifolia, is a fast-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, trailing foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Often used for erosion control and as a decorative plant, it has become popular in horticulture worldwide. Despite its beauty, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems. Its unique appearance and resilience make it both a valuable and sometimes problematic plant.
