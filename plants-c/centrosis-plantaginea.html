Centrosis plantaginea

Centrosis plantaginea, commonly known as the silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Centrosis plantaginea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
