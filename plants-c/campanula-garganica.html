Campanula garganica

Campanula garganica, commonly known as the Garganica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Campanula garganica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
