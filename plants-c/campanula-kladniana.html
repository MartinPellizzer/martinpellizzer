Campanula kladniana

Campanula kladniana, commonly known as the Kladnica bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Czech Republic.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula kladniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
