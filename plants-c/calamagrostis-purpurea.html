Calamagrostis purpurea

Calamagrostis purpurea, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually brown or purplish in color.

Calamagrostis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
