Chamaemelum austriacum

Chamaemelum austriacum, commonly known as Austrian tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green, fern-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine.
