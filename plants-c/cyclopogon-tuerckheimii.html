Cyclopogon tuerckheimii

Cyclopogon tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Cyclopogon tuerckheimii is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
