Chamaecrista centiflora

Chamaecrista centiflora, commonly known as the hundred-leaf or coastal senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing adaptability to saline environments. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems and a spreading form.

The botanical features of Chamaecrista centiflora make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
