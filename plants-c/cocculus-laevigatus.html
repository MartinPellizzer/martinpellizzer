Cocculus laevigatus

Cocculus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its fruits are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds that should be avoided.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
