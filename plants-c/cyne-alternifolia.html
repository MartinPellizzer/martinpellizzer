Cyne alternifolia

Cyne alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically has slender, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. Cyne alternifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.
