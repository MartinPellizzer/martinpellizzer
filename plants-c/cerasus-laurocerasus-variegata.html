Cerasus laurocerasus variegata

Cerasus laurocerasus variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that often have striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, cherry-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
