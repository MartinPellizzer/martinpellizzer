Campium subcrenatum

Campium subcrenatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

Campium subcrenatum is perennial and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
