Cyclopogon olivaceus

Cyclopogon olivaceus, commonly known as the olive cyclopogon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of bromeliads. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange.

Cyclopogon olivaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
