Calamagrostis groenlandica

Calamagrostis groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small Arctic animals.

Due to its cold tolerance, it is a key component of the vegetation in high-latitude ecosystems.
