Caesalpinia pauciflora

Caesalpinia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower legume, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
