Cyathea macrosora

Cyathea macrosora, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, segmented structure.

Cyathea macrosora plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
