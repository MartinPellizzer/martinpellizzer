Clethra alcoceri

Clethra alcoceri, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered cymes, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Clethra alcoceri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
