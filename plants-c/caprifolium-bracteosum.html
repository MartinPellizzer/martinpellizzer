Caprifolium bracteosum

Caprifolium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteated honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
