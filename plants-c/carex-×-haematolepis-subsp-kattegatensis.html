Carex × haematolepis subsp. kattegatensis

Carex × haematolepis subsp.

kattegatensis, commonly known as the Kattegat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow-green flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
