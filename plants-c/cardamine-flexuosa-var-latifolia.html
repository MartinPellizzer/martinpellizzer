Cardamine flexuosa var. latifolia

Cardamine flexuosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant features delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are broad and ovate, with a waxy surface. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its role in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
