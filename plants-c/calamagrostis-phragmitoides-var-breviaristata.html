Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. breviaristata

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. breviaristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
