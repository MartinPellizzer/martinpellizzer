Crinum ornatum subvar. acutior

Crinum ornatum subvar. acutior, commonly known as the sharp-pointed crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, fragrant scent. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow in dense clumps, and it produces bulbous underground storage organs called bulbs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
