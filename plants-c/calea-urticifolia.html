Calea urticifolia

Calea urticifolia, commonly known as the "Mexican widow's tears," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea urticifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
