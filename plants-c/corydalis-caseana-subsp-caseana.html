Corydalis caseana subsp. caseana

Corydalis caseana subsp.

caseana, commonly known as the Pacific larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, ranging from pink to purple, and are borne in clusters at the leaf axils. Corydalis caseana subsp.

caseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
