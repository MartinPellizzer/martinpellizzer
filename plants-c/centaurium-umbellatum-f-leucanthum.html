Centaurium umbellatum f. leucanthum

Centaurium umbellatum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered mouse-ear chickweed, is a botanical variant of the centaurium genus.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the species. The plant is a low-growing herb with small, ovate leaves and is often found in moist, grassy habitats. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
