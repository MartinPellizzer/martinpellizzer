Cissus apoensis

Cissus apoensis, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in small, translucent tubercles. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Cissus apoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
