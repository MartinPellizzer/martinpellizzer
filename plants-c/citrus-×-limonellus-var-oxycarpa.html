Citrus × limonellus var. oxycarpa

Citrus × limonellus var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the Mexican lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and acidic. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. The fruit's high acidity and distinct flavor make it a popular choice in cooking and beverages.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species within the citrus genus, characterized by its unique genetic makeup and adaptability to various climates.
