Cyperus bracheilema

Cyperus bracheilema, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters called spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded environments, making it common in marshy areas and along waterways.

Cyperus bracheilema is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
