Cadetia homochroma

Cadetia homochroma, commonly known as the white sand catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered flowers with four pink to white petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
