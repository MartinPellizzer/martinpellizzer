Commelina lutea

Commelina lutea, commonly known as the yellow commelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and grows in moist, shaded environments.

Commelina lutea is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.
