Coleus petraeus

Coleus petraeus, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, crinkled appearance.

Coleus petraeus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
