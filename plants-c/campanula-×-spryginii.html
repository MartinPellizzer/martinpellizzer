Campanula × spryginii

Campanula × spryginii, commonly known as the Spryginii bellflower, is a hybrid species belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
