Cinnamomum riedelii

Cinnamomum riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces dark reddish-brown bark, which is highly valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Cinnamomum riedelii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
