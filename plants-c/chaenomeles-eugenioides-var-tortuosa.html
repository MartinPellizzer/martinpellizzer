Chaenomeles eugenioides var. tortuosa

Chaenomeles eugenioides var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thick, woody stems with a distinctive twisted growth pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn reddish in autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
