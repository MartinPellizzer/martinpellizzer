Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. asper

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

asper, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that give it its subspecies name, "asper.". It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
