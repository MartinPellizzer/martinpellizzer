Comocladia grandidentata

Comocladia grandidentata, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five lobes and a prominent throat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
