Centropogon ferruginoides

Centropogon ferruginoides, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features hairy, succulent stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and dune environments.
