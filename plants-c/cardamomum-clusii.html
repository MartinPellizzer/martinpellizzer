Cardamomum clusii

Cardamomum clusii, commonly known as the Indian cardamom or black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped seed pods. Cardamomum clusii is valued for its strong, spicy flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and traditional uses.
