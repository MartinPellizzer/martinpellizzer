Cassia itambana

Cassia itambana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia itambana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
