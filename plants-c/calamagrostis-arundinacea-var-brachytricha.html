Calamagrostis arundinacea var. brachytricha

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. brachytricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, silver-green leaves that have a distinctive silky texture. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that add ornamental value in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
