Cyclamen ambiguum

Cyclamen ambiguum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Cyclamen ambiguum has a tuberous root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring.
