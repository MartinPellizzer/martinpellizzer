Cousinia ceratophora

Cousinia ceratophora, commonly known as the horned cudweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia ceratophora is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
