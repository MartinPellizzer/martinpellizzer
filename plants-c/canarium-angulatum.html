Canarium angulatum

Canarium angulatum, commonly known as the angular canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its angular, ridged bark and smooth, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, oily nuts encased in a hard shell. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
