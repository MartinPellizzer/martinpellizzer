Cirsium joschii

Cirsium joschii, commonly known as the Joschii thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Cirsium joschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
