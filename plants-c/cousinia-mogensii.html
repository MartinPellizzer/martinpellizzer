Cousinia mogensii

Cousinia mogensii, commonly known as the Mongolian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Cousinia. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Cousinia mogensii is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
