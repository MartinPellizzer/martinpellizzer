Casuarina pauper

Casuarina pauper, commonly known as the poor casuarina, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage that resembles the appearance of a pine tree. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its woody, cylindrical cones are a distinctive feature, containing the seeds.

Casuarina pauper thrives in coastal and sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal stabilization and dune ecosystems.
