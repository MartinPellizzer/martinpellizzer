Cassia swartzioides var. scarlatina

Cassia swartzioides var. scarlatina, commonly known as the red-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking, bright red or orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant floral display makes it a popular choice for adding color to tropical landscapes.
