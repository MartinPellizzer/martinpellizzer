Campanula napuligera var. umbrosa

Campanula napuligera var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, adapting to its shady, moist habitats. This variety thrives in woodland environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for shaded garden landscapes.
