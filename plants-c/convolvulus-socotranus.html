Convolvulus socotranus

Convolvulus socotranus, commonly known as Socotra's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves and trumpet-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red berry that attracts birds and small mammals for seed dispersal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and evolutionary history.
