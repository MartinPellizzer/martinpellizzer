Centaurea karschiana

Centaurea karschiana, commonly known as Karsch's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and the stems are usually erect.

Centaurea karschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
