Calamus anceps

Calamus anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic feature of the Aroid family.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
