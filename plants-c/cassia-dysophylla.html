Cassia dysophylla

Cassia dysophylla, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain flat, elongated seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia dysophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
