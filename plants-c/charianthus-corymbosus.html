Charianthus corymbosus

Charianthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
