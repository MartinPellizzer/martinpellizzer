Crossandra spinosa

Crossandra spinosa, commonly known as the spiny crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has succulent, spiny stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Crossandra spinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
