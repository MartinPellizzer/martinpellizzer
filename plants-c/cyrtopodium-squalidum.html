Cyrtopodium squalidum

Cyrtopodium squalidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single or few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Cyrtopodium squalidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
