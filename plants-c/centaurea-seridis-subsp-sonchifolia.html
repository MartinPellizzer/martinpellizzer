Centaurea seridis subsp. sonchifolia

Centaurea seridis subsp.

sonchifolia, commonly known as the desert knapweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its height ranges from 20 to 60 centimeters, with a sprawling growth habit.

It is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal dunes.
