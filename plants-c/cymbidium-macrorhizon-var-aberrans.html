Cymbidium macrorhizon var. aberrans

Cymbidium macrorhizon var. aberrans, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features long, arching inflorescences with numerous blooms, each adorned with vibrant, often contrasting colors. Its pseudobulbs are robust, and the leaves are broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to produce multiple flower spikes over a long blooming season.
