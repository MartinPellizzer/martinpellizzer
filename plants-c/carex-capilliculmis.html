Carex capilliculmis

Carex capilliculmis, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex capilliculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
