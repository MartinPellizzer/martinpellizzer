Cuphea ericoides var. laxa

Cuphea ericoides var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa cuphea, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal or dry environments.
