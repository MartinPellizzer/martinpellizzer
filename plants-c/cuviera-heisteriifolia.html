Cuviera heisteriifolia

Cuviera heisteriifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues, which bloom in the summer months. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking foliage and floral display.
