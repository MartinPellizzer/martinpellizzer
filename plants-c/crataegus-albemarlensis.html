Crataegus albemarlensis

Crataegus albemarlensis, commonly known as the White Crataegus, is a species of hawthorn native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often found in moist woodland habitats.
