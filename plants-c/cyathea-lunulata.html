Cyathea lunulata

Cyathea lunulata, commonly known as the moon-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically light green and have a distinctive, wavy margin.

Cyathea lunulata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
