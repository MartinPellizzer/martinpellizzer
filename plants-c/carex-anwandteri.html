Carex anwandteri

Carex anwandteri, commonly known as Anwandter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and habitat restoration.
