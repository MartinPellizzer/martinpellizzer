Crepidium polyphyllum

Crepidium polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
