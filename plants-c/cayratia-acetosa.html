Cayratia acetosa

Cayratia acetosa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cayratia acetosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
