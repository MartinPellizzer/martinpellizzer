Conchium sphaeroideum

Conchium sphaeroideum, commonly known as the globe lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive rounded, cushion-like thallus with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic partnership between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the environment. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important indicator species for environmental health.
