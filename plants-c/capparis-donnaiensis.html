Capparis donnaiensis

Capparis donnaiensis, commonly known as the Donnai's Caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, green berries. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Capparis donnaiensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
