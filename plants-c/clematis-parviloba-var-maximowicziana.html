Clematis parviloba var. maximowicziana

Clematis parviloba var. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its spring blossoms.
