Castanopsis semiserrata

Castanopsis semiserrata, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The tree produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
