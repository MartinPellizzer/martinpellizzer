Convolvulus mazatlanensis

Convolvulus mazatlanensis, commonly known as the Mazatlan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
