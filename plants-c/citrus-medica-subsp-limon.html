Citrus medica subsp. limon

Citrus medica subsp.

limon, commonly known as the lemon. This subspecies is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange fruit.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a strong citrus scent. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, leathery rind and a high concentration of citric acid.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.
