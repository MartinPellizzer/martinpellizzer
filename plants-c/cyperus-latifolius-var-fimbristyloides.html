Cyperus latifolius var. fimbristyloides

Cyperus latifolius var. fimbristyloides, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland areas in the Americas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually hairless and have a linear to lance-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, ditches, and other aquatic environments.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
