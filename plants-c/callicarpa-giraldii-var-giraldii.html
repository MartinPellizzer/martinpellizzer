Callicarpa giraldii var. giraldii

Callicarpa giraldii var. giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
