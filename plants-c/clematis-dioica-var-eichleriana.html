Clematis dioica var. eichleriana

Clematis dioica var. eichleriana, commonly known as the Eichler's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Central and South America.

It features slender, climbing stems with compound leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a distinctive two-colored flower structure, with sepals that are usually a different color from the petals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
