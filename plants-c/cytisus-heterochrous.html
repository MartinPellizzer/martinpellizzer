Cytisus heterochrous

Cytisus heterochrous, commonly known as the variable broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the spring and early summer.
