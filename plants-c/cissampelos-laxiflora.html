Cissampelos laxiflora

Cissampelos laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved milk vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Cissampelos laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
