Crassula engleri

Crassula engleri, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive, compact appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
