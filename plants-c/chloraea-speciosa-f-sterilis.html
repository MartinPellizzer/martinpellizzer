Chloraea speciosa f. sterilis

Chloraea speciosa f. sterilis, commonly known as the sterile form of the showy chloraea, is a rare botanical variant of the native Australian plant Chloraea speciosa.

This form is characterized by its lack of fertile flowers, resulting in the absence of seed production. It typically exhibits the same vibrant, showy floral structures as the typical form but without the reproductive organs. The plant is often found in similar habitats, such as moist, shaded areas in woodland or heathland.

Its botanical interest lies in its genetic and morphological differences, making it a subject of study in plant genetics and evolution.
