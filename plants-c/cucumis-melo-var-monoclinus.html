Cucumis melo var. monoclinus

Cucumis melo var. monoclinus, commonly known as the Casablanca melon, is a variety of melon characterized by its thick, green rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive melon fruit. It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.

The fruit is known for its high water content and mild, honey-like flavor.
