Cercodia alternifolia

Cercodia alternifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

Cercodia alternifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial shade for optimal growth.
