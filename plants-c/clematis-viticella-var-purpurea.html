Clematis viticella var. purpurea

Clematis viticella var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a vigorous climbing plant native to Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens.
