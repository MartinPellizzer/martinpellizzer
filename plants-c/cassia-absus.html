Cassia absus

Cassia absus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Cassia absus is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.
