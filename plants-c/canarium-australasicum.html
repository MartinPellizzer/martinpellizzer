Canarium australasicum

Canarium australasicum, commonly known as the Australian nut or white nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a hard, woody shell. It is valued for its edible seed, which is rich in oils and has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
