Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. parviflorum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Its delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
