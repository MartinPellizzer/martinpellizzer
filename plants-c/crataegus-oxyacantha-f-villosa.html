Crataegus oxyacantha f. villosa

Crataegus oxyacantha f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The flowers are small and white, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds and mammals.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
