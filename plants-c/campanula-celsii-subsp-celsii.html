Campanula celsii subsp. celsii

Campanula celsii subsp.

celsii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Alps of Europe. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
