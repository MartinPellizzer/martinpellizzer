Cyathea mexiae

Cyathea mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth as a perennial plant.

Cyathea mexiae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
