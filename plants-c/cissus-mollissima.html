Cissus mollissima

Cissus mollissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus mollissima is also valued for its ability to climb and its adaptability to various soil types.
