Chilocarpus obovatus

Chilocarpus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern regions of South America.

It has a unique morphology characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to derive some of its nutrients from the host tree, while still performing photosynthesis.

Chilocarpus obovatus plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats by supporting biodiversity and contributing to forest dynamics.
