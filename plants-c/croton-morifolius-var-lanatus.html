Croton morifolius var. lanatus

Croton morifolius var. lanatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a striking reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it requires careful management in landscaping settings.
