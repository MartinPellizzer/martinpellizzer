Crataegus texana var. texana

Crataegus texana var. texana, commonly known as the Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and has thorned branches, which are characteristic of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
