Cardamine thalictroides

Cardamine thalictroides, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
