Copaiva guyanensis

Copaiva guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a yellowish sap that is tapped for its therapeutic properties. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth margin.

Copaiva guyanensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
