Crepis vesicaria var. taraxacifolia

Crepis vesicaria var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This variety is distinguished by its leaf morphology, which resembles that of dandelions, hence its common name.
