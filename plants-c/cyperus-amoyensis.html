Cyperus amoyensis

Cyperus amoyensis, commonly known as the Amoy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Cyperus amoyensis is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
