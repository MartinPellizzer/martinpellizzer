Centaurea cuneifolia subsp. cuneifolia

Centaurea cuneifolia subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
