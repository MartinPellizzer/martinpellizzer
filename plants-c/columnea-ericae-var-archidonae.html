Columnea ericae var. archidonae

Columnea ericae var. archidonae, commonly known as the Archidona columnea, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
