Crotalaria elliptica

Crotalaria elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Crotalaria elliptica is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
