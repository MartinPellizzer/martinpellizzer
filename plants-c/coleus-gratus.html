Coleus gratus

Coleus gratus, commonly known as the flame nectar or red flame plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale purple or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Coleus gratus thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil with partial to full sunlight.
