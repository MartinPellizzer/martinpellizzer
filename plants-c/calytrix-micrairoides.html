Calytrix micrairoides

Calytrix micrairoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Calytrix micrairoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.
