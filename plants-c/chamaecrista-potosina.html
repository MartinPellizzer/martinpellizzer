Chamaecrista potosina

Chamaecrista potosina, commonly known as Potosi burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange floral clusters that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chamaecrista potosina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
