Cereus chiloensis var. funkianus

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, while the actual flowers are small and clustered at the base of the inflorescence. Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of scientific study.
