Calamus parvulus

Calamus parvulus, commonly known as the small calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, marshy environments. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and appear in clusters.
