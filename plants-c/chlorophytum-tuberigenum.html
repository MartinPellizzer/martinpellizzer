Chlorophytum tuberigenum

Chlorophytum tuberigenum, commonly known as the Tiger Tooth Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green with white or yellow markings, resembling tiger teeth. The plant produces long, arching stems that bear small, white, tubular flowers. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
