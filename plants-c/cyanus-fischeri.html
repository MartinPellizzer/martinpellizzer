Cyanus fischeri

Cyanus fischeri, commonly known as the alpine sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, ligulate florets surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its height can range from 30 to 120 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Cyanus fischeri is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
