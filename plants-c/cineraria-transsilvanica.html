Cineraria transsilvanica

Cineraria transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cineraria, is a flowering plant native to Romania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
