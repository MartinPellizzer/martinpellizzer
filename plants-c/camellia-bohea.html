Camellia bohea

Camellia bohea, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces small, reddish-brown seeds encased in a hard, woody capsule. Camellia bohea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in tea production.

It thrives in humid, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
