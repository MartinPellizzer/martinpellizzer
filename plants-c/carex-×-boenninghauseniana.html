Carex × boenninghauseniana

Carex × boenninghauseniana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a hybrid between Carex plantaginea and Carex echinata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers in the summer. It is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
