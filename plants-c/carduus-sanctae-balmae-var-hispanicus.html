Carduus sanctae-balmae var. hispanicus

Carduus sanctae-balmae var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish holy thistle, is a thistle species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
