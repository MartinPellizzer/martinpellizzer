It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically pinnately divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of China. Its reproductive structures include sori that are arranged in a single row along the undersides of the fronds.

Cystopteris tangutica is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
