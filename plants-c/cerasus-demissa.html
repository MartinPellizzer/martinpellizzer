Cerasus demissa

Cerasus demissa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, shady environments.

Cerasus demissa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
