Cestrum stuebelii

Cestrum stuebelii, commonly known as the night blooming trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

Cestrum stuebelii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
