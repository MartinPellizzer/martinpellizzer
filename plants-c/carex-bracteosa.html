Carex bracteosa

Carex bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with the inflorescence consisting of a spikelike cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex bracteosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
