Crataegus pruinosa var. parvula

Crataegus pruinosa var. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with finely toothed margins, and it often grows in moist, woodland habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
