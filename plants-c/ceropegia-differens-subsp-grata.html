Ceropegia differens subsp. grata

Ceropegia differens subsp.

grata, commonly known as the "string of hearts," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is popular as a houseplant and is relatively easy to care for.
