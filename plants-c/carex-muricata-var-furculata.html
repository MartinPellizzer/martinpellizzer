Carex muricata var. furculata

Carex muricata var. furculata, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
