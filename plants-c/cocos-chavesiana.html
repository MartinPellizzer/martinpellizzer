Cocos chavesiana

Cocos chavesiana, commonly known as the Chaves palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fibrous fruits containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
