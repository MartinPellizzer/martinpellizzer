Citrosma dentata

Citrosma dentata, commonly known as the toothed citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when mature, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
