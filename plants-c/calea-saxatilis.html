Calea saxatilis

Calea saxatilis, commonly known as the rock calea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Calea saxatilis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in South American herbal medicine.
