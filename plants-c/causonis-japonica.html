Causonis japonica

Causonis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese glassworts, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in saline or brackish environments, often found in tidal zones and along shorelines. Its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Causonis japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
