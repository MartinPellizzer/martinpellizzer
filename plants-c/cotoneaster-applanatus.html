Cotoneaster applanatus

Cotoneaster applanatus, commonly known as the flatleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster applanatus is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
