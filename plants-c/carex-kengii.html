Carex kengii

Carex kengii, commonly known as the Keng's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, often arranged in spikes. Carex kengii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
