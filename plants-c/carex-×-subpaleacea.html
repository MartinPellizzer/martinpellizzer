Carex × subpaleacea

Carex × subpaleacea, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex paleacea and Carex subulata, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
