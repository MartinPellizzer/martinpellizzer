Cassia glabra

Cassia glabra, commonly known as sickle pod or small-leaved cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Cassia glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dye.
