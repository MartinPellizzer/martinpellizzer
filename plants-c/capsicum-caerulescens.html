Capsicum caerulescens

Capsicum caerulescens, commonly known as the blueberry pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its branching structure and small, oval-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops into a small, round, blue or purple berry when mature. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
