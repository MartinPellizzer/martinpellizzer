Clusia plukenetii

Clusia plukenetii, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia plukenetii is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
