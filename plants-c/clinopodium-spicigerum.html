Clinopodium spicigerum

Clinopodium spicigerum, commonly known as spike caraway, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive minty aroma.

Clinopodium spicigerum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
