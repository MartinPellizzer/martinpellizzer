Columnea calotricha

Columnea calotricha, commonly known as the golden flame or flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant produces opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Columnea calotricha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
