Convolvulus decumbens

Convolvulus decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus decumbens is an annual plant that blooms from spring to early summer.
