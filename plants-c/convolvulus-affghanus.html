Convolvulus affghanus

Convolvulus affghanus, commonly known as Afghan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Convolvulus affghanus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
