Cassia sclerocarpa

Cassia sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-seeded cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
