Carissa kopilii

Carissa kopilii, commonly known as the Natal yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Carissa kopilii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
