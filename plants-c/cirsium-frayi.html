Cirsium frayi

Cirsium frayi, commonly known as the Fray's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium frayi is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
