Calamus alberti

Calamus alberti, commonly known as Albert's reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus alberti is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
