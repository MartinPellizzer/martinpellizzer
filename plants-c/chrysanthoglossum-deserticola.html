Chrysanthoglossum deserticola

Chrysanthoglossum deserticola, commonly known as the desert chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
