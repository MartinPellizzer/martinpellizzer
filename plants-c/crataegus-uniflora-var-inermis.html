Crataegus uniflora var. inermis

Crataegus uniflora var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth thornless hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
