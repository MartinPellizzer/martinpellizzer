Cleome spinosa

Cleome spinosa, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, spidery flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cleome spinosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
