Croton septemnervius

Croton septemnervius, commonly known as the seven-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant colors ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
