Carex macloviana var. haydeniana

Carex macloviana var. haydeniana, commonly known as Hayden's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This variety is native to North America and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces wind-pollinated flowers, which are adapted for dispersal in open environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
