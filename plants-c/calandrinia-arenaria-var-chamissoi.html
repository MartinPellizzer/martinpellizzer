Calandrinia arenaria var. chamissoi

Calandrinia arenaria var. chamissoi, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is often found in coastal and arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
