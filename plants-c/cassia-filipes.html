Cassia filipes

Cassia filipes, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers, which are typical of the legume family. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia filipes is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
