Carex pachystoma

Carex pachystoma, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.
