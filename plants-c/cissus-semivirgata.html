Cissus semivirgata

Cissus semivirgata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
