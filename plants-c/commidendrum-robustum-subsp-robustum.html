Commidendrum robustum subsp. robustum

Commidendrum robustum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the Natal fan palm, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by black, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in many regions.
