Campanula rupestris var. virescens

Campanula rupestris var. virescens, commonly known as the green bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, greenish-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
