Croton goudotii var. tsaratananae

Croton goudotii var. tsaratananae, commonly known as the Tsaratanana croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
