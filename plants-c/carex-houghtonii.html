Carex houghtonii

Carex houghtonii, commonly known as Houghton's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex houghtonii is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
