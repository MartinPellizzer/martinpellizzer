Copaifera venezuelana

Copaifera venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and large, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its resin, known as copaiba balsam, is highly valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Copaifera venezuelana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
