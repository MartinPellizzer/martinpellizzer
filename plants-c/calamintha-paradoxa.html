Calamintha paradoxa

Calamintha paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often emitting a mint-like fragrance when crushed.

Calamintha paradoxa is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
