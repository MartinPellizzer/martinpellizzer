Capsicum indicum var. ribesium

Capsicum indicum var. ribesium, commonly known as the red bell pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often brightly colored fruits, which can range from red to yellow or orange. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications worldwide.
