Clematis patens f. alba

Clematis patens f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered Clematis, is a cultivar of the Clematis patens species, which is native to eastern Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant features trifoliate leaves and has a long flowering season, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, lace-like foliage and persistent fruiting structures.
