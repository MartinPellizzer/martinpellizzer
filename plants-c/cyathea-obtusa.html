Cyathea obtusa

Cyathea obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, tree-like structure, and it plays a role in tropical forest ecosystems.

Cyathea obtusa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
