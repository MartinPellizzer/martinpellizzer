Campanula rapunculus f. montana

Campanula rapunculus f. montana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
