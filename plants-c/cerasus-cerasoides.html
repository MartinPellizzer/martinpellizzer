Cerasus cerasoides

Cerasus cerasoides, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
