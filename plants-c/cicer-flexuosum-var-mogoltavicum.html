Cicer flexuosum var. mogoltavicum

Cicer flexuosum var. mogoltavicum, commonly known as the Mogoltavica vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant is characterized by its flexible, sprawling growth habit and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its seeds are rich in protein and can be used as a food source in certain regions.
