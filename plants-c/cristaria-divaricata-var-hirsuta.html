Cristaria divaricata var. hirsuta

Cristaria divaricata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cristaria, is a variant of the cristaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, branching growth habit. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually elongated and may exhibit a variegated coloration.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique texture.
