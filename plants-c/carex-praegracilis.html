Carex praegracilis

Carex praegracilis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Carex praegracilis is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
