Chassalia princei var. puberula

Chassalia princei var. puberula, commonly known as the silky chassalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
