Centropogon uncialis

Centropogon uncialis, commonly known as the small-flowered centropogon, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Centropogon uncialis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
