Chulusium fluitans

Chulusium fluitans, commonly known as the floating bellflower, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often floating just above the water's surface. The plant's leaves are broad and floating, adapted to absorb sunlight for photosynthesis. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of water bodies, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving waters.

Chulusium fluitans plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic life.
