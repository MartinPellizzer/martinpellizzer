Colchicum vernum

Colchicum vernum, commonly known as the spring colchicum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and appear after the flowers, often in a rosette formation.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which make it both a hazardous and ecologically significant species.
