Corydalis glaucescens subsp. glaucescens

Corydalis glaucescens subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas. It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its slender stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet hardy ornamental plant.
