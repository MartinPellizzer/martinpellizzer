Carpinus paxii

Carpinus paxii, commonly known as the Italian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
