Colubrina triquetra

Colubrina triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Colubrina triquetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
