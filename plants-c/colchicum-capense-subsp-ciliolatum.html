Colchicum capense subsp. ciliolatum

Colchicum capense subsp.

ciliolatum, commonly known as the Cape colchicum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely ciliate leaves and its habitat preferences.
