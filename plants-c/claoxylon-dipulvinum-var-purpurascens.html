Claoxylon dipulvinum var. purpurascens

Claoxylon dipulvinum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed tallowwood, is a tropical tree species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with its dark, rough bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
