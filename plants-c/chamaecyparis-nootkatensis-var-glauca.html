Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. glauca

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. glauca, commonly known as the Alaska cedar or glauca cedar, is a variety of the western red cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its blue-gray foliage and aromatic, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions of Alaska and western Canada.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to pests and diseases.
