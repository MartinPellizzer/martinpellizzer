Corynopuntia aggeria

Corynopuntia aggeria, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

Corynopuntia aggeria is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
