Caprifolium occidentale var. plantierensis

Caprifolium occidentale var. plantierensis, commonly known as the Western honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in riparian zones and along streambanks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
