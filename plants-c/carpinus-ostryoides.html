Carpinus ostryoides

Carpinus ostryoides, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments.
