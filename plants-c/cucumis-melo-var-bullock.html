Cucumis melo var. bullock

Cucumis melo var. bullock, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivated variety of melon belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruit with a smooth, light green skin and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
