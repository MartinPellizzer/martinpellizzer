Capparis oxysepala

Capparis oxysepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepaled caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Capparis oxysepala is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
