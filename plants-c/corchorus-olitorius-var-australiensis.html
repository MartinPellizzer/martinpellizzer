Corchorus olitorius var. australiensis

Corchorus olitorius var. australiensis, commonly known as Australian spinach, is a variant of the common plantain species native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in traditional Australian cuisine. The plant thrives in warm climates and is typically found in coastal regions and sandy soils. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are valued for their mild, slightly nutty flavor.

The plant also produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
