Chimonanthus praecox var. reflexus

Chimonanthus praecox var. reflexus, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
