Cereus hexangularis

Cereus hexangularis, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or yellow, blooming at night. These flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
