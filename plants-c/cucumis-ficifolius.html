Cucumis ficifolius

Cucumis ficifolius, commonly known as the fig-leaf gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble fig leaves, giving it its common name. Its fruit is typically a green, elongated gourd that can be harvested when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
