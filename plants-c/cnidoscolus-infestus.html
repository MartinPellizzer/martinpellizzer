Cnidoscolus infestus

Cnidoscolus infestus, commonly known as the arrow leaf or arrowhead plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are usually deeply lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
