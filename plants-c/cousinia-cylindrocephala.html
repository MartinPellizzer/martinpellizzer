Cousinia cylindrocephala

Cousinia cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical-headed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color, adding visual interest to its habitat. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia cylindrocephala is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
