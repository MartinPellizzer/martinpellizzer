Crataegus × macrocarpa nothosubsp. austromoravica

Crataegus × macrocarpa nothosubsp.

austromoravica, commonly known as the Austrian hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its ornamental value and adaptability. It typically exhibits small, sharp leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn.

The plant is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various horticultural settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
