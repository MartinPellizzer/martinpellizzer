Carex gracilis var. chlorotica

Carex gracilis var. chlorotica, commonly known as the green-stem sedges, is a variety of the slender sedge, characterized by its distinctive greenish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions and typically grows in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its foliage provides habitat for various invertebrates and is a valuable component of aquatic ecosystems.
