Cryptocarya ceramica

Cryptocarya ceramica, commonly known as the Ceramica laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Cryptocarya ceramica is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its wood is also used in local crafts and furniture making.
