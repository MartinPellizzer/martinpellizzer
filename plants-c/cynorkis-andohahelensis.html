Cynorkis andohahelensis

Cynorkis andohahelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the dense rainforests of its native habitat. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered threatened and is the subject of conservation efforts.
