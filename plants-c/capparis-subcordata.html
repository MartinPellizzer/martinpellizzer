Capparis subcordata

Capparis subcordata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange berries that enclose a cluster of seeds. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Capparis subcordata make it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.
