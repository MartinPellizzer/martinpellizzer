Citharexylum spinosum

Citharexylum spinosum, commonly known as the spiked citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
