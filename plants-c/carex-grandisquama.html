Carex grandisquama

Carex grandisquama, commonly known as the broad-scaled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex grandisquama is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
