Caltha palustris var. laeta

Caltha palustris var. laeta, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
