Chrysanthemum arvense

Chrysanthemum arvense, commonly known as field chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Chrysanthemum arvense is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
