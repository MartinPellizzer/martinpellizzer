Carex paddoensis

Carex paddoensis, commonly known as the Paddo sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Carex paddoensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
