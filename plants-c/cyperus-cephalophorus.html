Cyperus cephalophorus

Cyperus cephalophorus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge plants, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus cephalophorus is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
