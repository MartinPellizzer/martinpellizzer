Carica citriformis

Carica citriformis, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, spiky fruit that contains a single large seed. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmate leaves, and a unique fruit structure that distinguishes it from other members of the genus.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
