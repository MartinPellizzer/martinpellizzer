Cyperus macrocarpus

Cyperus macrocarpus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, and can form dense stands. Its distinctive feature is its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, hence its common name.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color.
