Caryochloa trichotoma

Caryochloa trichotoma, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaf blades, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Caryochloa trichotoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
