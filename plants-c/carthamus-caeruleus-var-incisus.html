Carthamus caeruleus var. incisus

Carthamus caeruleus var. incisus, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and showy flower structure.
