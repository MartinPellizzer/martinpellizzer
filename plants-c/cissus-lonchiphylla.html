Cissus lonchiphylla

Cissus lonchiphylla, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to trail or climb make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
