Carex gynocrates var. substaminata

Carex gynocrates var. substaminata, commonly known as the slender female sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, marshes, and forest floors. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces three-nucleate pollen grains.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wetland biodiversity.
