Cirsium cassium

Cirsium cassium, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Cirsium cassium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
