Critamus ammoides

Critamus ammoides, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and has deep roots to access groundwater.

Critamus ammoides is often found in sandy soils and is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
