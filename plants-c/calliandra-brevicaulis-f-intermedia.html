Calliandra brevicaulis f. intermedia

Calliandra brevicaulis f. intermedia, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, fluffy flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
