Cordia linearis

Cordia linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale yellow, producing a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
