Cantinoa rubicunda

Cantinoa rubicunda, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vivid red petals and yellow-orange sepals, which resemble a fiery flame. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Cantinoa rubicunda is primarily propagated through seed and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
