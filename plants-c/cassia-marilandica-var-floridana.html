Cassia marilandica var. floridana

Cassia marilandica var. floridana, commonly known as Florida cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that mature in the fall.

Florida cassia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
