Corispermum hyssopifolium var. microcarpum

Corispermum hyssopifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a crusty layer, aiding in seed dispersal in arid conditions. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of salt-tolerant vegetation in such ecosystems.
