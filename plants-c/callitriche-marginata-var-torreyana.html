Callitriche marginata var. torreyana

Callitriche marginata var. torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's water-starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, floating or submerged leaves that are often oval or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish or white in color.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
