Centropogon densiflorus var. lugens

Centropogon densiflorus var. lugens, commonly known as the denseflower centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or reddish blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
