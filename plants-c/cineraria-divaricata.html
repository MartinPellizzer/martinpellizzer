Cineraria divaricata

Cineraria divaricata, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Cineraria divaricata prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
