Cymbidium hirsutum

Cymbidium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, each featuring a distinctive, hairy lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium hirsutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden or greenhouse setting.
