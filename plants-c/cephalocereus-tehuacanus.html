Cephalocereus tehuacanus

Cephalocereus tehuacanus, commonly known as the Huariaca cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, spiny stem and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to maximize water absorption. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
