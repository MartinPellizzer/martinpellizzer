Crusea glaucescens

Crusea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue crusea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Crusea glaucescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
