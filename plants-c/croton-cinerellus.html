Croton cinerellus

Croton cinerellus, commonly known as the ash-gray croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.
