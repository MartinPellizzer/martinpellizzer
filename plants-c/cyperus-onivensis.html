Cyperus onivensis

Cyperus onivensis, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in South America. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with grass-like foliage. Its flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate inflorescences.

Cyperus onivensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
