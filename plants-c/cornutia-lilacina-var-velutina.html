Cornutia lilacina var. velutina

Cornutia lilacina var. velutina, commonly known as the velvety cornutia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers. The leaves are typically velvety to the touch, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
