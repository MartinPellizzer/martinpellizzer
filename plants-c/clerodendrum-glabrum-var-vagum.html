Clerodendrum glabrum var. vagum

Clerodendrum glabrum var. vagum, commonly known as the hairy clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
