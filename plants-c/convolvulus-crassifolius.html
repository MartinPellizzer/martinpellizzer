Convolvulus crassifolius

Convolvulus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
