Crawfurdia lobatilimba

Crawfurdia lobatilimba, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
