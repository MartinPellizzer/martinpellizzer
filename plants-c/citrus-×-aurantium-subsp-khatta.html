Citrus × aurantium subsp. khatta

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

khatta, commonly known as khatta orange, is a citrus hybrid native to South Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, yellowish rind and a high concentration of essential oils.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is primarily used for its aromatic peel, which is dried and used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The subspecies is valued for its unique flavor profile and medicinal properties.
