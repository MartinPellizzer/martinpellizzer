Crataegus varians

Crataegus varians, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Crataegus varians is often found in woodlands, along streambanks, and in open fields, showcasing its adaptability to different habitats.
