Crocus ancyrensis subsp. guneri

Crocus ancyrensis subsp.

guneri, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early bloom.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Crocus genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
