Campanula cochleariifolia subf. tyrolensis

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

tyrolensis, commonly known as the Tyrol bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
