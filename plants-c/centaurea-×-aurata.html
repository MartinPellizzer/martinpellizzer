Centaurea × aurata

Centaurea × aurata, commonly known as the hybrid gladiola or golden coneflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea stoebe, resulting in a plant with striking, showy flowers. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bright yellow to orange-red ray florets surrounding a central cone of tubular flowers. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
