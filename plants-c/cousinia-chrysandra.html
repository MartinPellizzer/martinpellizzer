Cousinia chrysandra

Cousinia chrysandra, commonly known as the golden cousinia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cousinia chrysandra is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
