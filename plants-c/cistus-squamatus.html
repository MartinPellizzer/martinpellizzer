Cistus squamatus

Cistus squamatus, commonly known as the silver cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland habitats.

Cistus squamatus is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
