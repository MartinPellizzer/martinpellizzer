Chrysopsis cryptocephala

Chrysopsis cryptocephala, commonly known as the cryptic-headed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
