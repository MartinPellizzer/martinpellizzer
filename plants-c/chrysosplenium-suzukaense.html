Chrysosplenium suzukaense

Chrysosplenium suzukaense, commonly known as Suzuka's golden spleenwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for study in botany and conservation efforts.
