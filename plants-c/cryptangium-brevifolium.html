Cryptangium brevifolium

Cryptangium brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptangium, is a small shrub native to the western regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Cryptangium brevifolium is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its succulent-like leaves and deep root system.

It is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of local flora in its native habitat.
