Cryptocarya cinnamomifolia

Cryptocarya cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or tamanu tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

Cryptocarya cinnamomifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
