Cerasus vulgaris var. caproniana

Cerasus vulgaris var. caproniana, commonly known as the Capronian cherry, is a botanical variant of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits and is native to Europe. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
