Cynanchum arizonicum

Cynanchum arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a slender, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, blooming from summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
