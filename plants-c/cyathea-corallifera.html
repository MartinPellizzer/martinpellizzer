Cyathea corallifera

Cyathea corallifera, commonly known as the coral fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds are typically green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea corallifera plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
