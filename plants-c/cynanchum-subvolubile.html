Cynanchum subvolubile

Cynanchum subvolubile, commonly known as the creeping trumpet vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cynanchum subvolubile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
