Calycanthus × raulstonii

Calycanthus × raulstonii, commonly known as the Raulston's sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically reddish-orange or pink, with a distinctive, almost spicy scent. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
