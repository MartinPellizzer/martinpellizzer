Carelia adscendens

Carelia adscendens, commonly known as the ascending carelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
