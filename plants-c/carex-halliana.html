Carex halliana

Carex halliana, commonly known as Hall's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex halliana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
