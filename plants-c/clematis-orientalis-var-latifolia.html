Clematis orientalis var. latifolia

Clematis orientalis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are broad and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or trellises.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
