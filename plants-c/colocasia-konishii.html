Colocasia konishii

Colocasia konishii, commonly known as the Japanese elephant's ear, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of yellow or red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
