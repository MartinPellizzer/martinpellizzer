Ceropegia bhatii

Ceropegia bhatii, commonly known as the Bhatii moonvine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Ceropegia bhatii is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
