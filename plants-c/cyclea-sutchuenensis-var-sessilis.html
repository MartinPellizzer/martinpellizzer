Cyclea sutchuenensis var. sessilis

Cyclea sutchuenensis var. sessilis, commonly known as the sessile leaf cycloe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a sessile (stemless) attachment. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that are typically orange-red in color. Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and relatively understudied species.
