Chamaecrista decora

Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spikelike arrangements.

Chamaecrista decora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
