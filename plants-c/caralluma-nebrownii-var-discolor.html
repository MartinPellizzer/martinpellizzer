Caralluma nebrownii var. discolor

Caralluma nebrownii var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated horned cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching stems covered in spines and its unique, often variegated foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
