Calamagrostis purpurascens var. ophitidis

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. ophitidis, commonly known as the purple reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking purple-tinted foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, feathery flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. ophitidis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
