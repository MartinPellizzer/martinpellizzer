Campanula hastifolia

Campanula hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, which gives the plant its specific epithet "hastifolia," meaning "arrow-leaved.". It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
