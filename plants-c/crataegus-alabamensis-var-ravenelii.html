Crataegus alabamensis var. ravenelii

Crataegus alabamensis var. ravenelii, commonly known as the Ravenel's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorned branches, which are a characteristic feature of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
