Cosmanthus viscidus

Cosmanthus viscidus, commonly known as the sticky cosmanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky resin, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
