Chenopodium quinoa

Chenopodium quinoa, commonly known as the "mother grain," is an annual plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, nutrient-rich seeds that are harvested before the plant matures. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are edible and often used in traditional Andean cuisine.

Quinoa is known for its high protein content and is considered a pseudocereal due to its nutritional profile and cultivation methods.
