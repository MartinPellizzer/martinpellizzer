Centrosema kermesi

Centrosema kermesi, commonly known as Kermes centrosema, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
