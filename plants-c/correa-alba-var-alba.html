Correa alba var. alba

Correa alba var. alba, commonly known as the white correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
