Centrosema unifoliatum

Centrosema unifoliatum, commonly known as the smallleaf centrosema, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.

Its seeds are often used as forage for livestock.
