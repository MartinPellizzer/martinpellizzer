Cicuta ampla

Cicuta ampla, commonly known as water hemlock, is a highly toxic member of the parsley family (Apiaceae).

It is native to North America and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers. The plant contains potent toxic compounds, including cicutoxin, which can cause severe poisoning in humans and animals. It thrives in wet, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is considered one of the most dangerous plants in North America.
