Chamaecrista confusa

Chamaecrista confusa, commonly known as the Florida burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Chamaecrista confusa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
