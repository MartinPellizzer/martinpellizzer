Cardamine uintahensis

Cardamine uintahensis, commonly known as Uinta watercress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are white or pale green, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round seeds.

Uinta watercress is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
