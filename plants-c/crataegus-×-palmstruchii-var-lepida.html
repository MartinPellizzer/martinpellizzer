Crataegus × palmstruchii var. lepida

Crataegus × palmstruchii var. lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowers.

Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
