Crucifera erucago

Crucifera erucago, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers.

This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
