Croton argutus

Croton argutus, commonly known as the roughleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Croton argutus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
