Cactus seidelii

Cactus seidelii, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its spines are modified leaves, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Cactus seidelii is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
