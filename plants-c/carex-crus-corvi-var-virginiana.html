Carex crus-corvi var. virginiana

Carex crus-corvi var. virginiana, commonly known as the black-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomatous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in swampy areas or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
