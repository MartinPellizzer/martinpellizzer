Crotalaria cylindroclados

Crotalaria cylindroclados, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a tropical annual legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and linear. Its flowers are yellow with distinctive black markings, and it produces a strong, unpleasant odor when in bloom.

This species is often cultivated for its edible seeds and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
