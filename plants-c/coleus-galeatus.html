Coleus galeatus

Coleus galeatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and striking variegation. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
