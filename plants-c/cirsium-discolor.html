Cirsium discolor

Cirsium discolor, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The pale coneflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
