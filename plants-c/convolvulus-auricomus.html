Convolvulus auricomus

Convolvulus auricomus, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive ear-like leaves and its affinity for sunny environments.
