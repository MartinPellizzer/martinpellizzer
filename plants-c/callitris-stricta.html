Callitris stricta

Callitris stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed she-oak, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Callitris stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
