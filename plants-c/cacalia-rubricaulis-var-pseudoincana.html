Cacalia rubricaulis var. pseudoincana

Cacalia rubricaulis var. pseudoincana, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Cacalia genus, which are part of the Boraginaceae family.
