Coreopsis polyactis

Coreopsis polyactis, commonly known as the stiffleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright, branching stems. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coreopsis polyactis is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
