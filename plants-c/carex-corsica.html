Carex corsica

Carex corsica, commonly known as the Corsican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Carex corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
