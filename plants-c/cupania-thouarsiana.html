Cupania thouarsiana

Cupania thouarsiana, commonly known as the Tahitian passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local populations. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cupania thouarsiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
