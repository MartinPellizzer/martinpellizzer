Campanula scabrida

Campanula scabrida, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. Campanula scabrida prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
