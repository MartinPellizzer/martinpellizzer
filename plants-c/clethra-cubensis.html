Clethra cubensis

Clethra cubensis, commonly known as Cuban sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the plant family Ericaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Clethra cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
