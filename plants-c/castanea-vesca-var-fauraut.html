Castanea vesca var. fauraut

Castanea vesca var. fauraut, commonly known as the Fauraut horse chestnut, is a botanical variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. The fruit is a spiny capsule that matures in autumn, containing one or two chestnuts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
