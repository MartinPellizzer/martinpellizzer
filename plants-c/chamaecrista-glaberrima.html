Chamaecrista glaberrima

Chamaecrista glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous crownflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional uses.
