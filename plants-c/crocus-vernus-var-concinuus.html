Crocus vernus var. concinuus

Crocus vernus var. concinuus, commonly known as the early crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its blooming period makes it one of the earliest flowering plants in the spring season.
