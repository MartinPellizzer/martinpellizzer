Condylocarpus humilis

Condylocarpus humilis, commonly known as the lowland fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous fronds that are usually less than 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Condylocarpus humilis is often found in humid climates and requires consistent moisture and partial shade to thrive.
