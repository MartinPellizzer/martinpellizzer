Calea jamaicensis

Calea jamaicensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, with its flowers opening at night and closing in the morning. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Calea jamaicensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
