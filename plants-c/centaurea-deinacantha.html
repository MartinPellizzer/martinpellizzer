Centaurea deinacantha

Centaurea deinacantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Centaurea deinacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
