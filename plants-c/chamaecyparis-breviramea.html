Chamaecyparis breviramea

Chamaecyparis breviramea, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or short-branch cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture.
