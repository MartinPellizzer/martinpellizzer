Cocos sancona

Cocos sancona, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
