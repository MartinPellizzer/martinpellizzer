Clematis nukiangensis

Clematis nukiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pale blue flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
