Cyanus luteus

Cyanus luteus, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cyanus luteus is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
