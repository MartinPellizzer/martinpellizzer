Colysis wui

Colysis wui, commonly known as the silverleaf cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy, silver-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, Colysis wui is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
