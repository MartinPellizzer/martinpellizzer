Capsicum villosum var. latifolium

Capsicum villosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wild pepper, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that range in color from green to red, depending on maturity. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Capsicum species and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
