Cytisus hirsutus subsp. ratisbonensis

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

ratisbonensis, commonly known as the Ratisbon bush clover, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
