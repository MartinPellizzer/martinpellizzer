Clerodendrum mandrarense

Clerodendrum mandrarense, commonly known as the white-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, round seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
