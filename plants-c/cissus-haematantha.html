Cissus haematantha

Cissus haematantha, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and hanging baskets.
