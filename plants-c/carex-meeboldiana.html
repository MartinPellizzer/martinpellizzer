Carex meeboldiana

Carex meeboldiana, commonly known as the Meebold sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex meeboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
