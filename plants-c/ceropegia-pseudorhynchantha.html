Ceropegia pseudorhynchantha

Ceropegia pseudorhynchantha, commonly known as the African violet or Cape ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces distinctive, hooded flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as moths.

Ceropegia pseudorhynchantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
