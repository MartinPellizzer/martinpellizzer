Croton grandivelum var. neuwiedii

Croton grandivelum var. neuwiedii, commonly known as the Neuwied croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with simple, alternate leaves that are often palmately lobed. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant leaves and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
