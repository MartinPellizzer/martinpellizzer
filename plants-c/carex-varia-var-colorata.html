Carex varia var. colorata

Carex varia var. colorata, commonly known as the colorado sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, which features a distinctive color variation, ranging from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden planting.
