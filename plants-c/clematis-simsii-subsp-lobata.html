Clematis simsii subsp. lobata

Clematis simsii subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
