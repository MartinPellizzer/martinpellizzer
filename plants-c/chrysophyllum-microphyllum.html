Chrysophyllum microphyllum

Chrysophyllum microphyllum, commonly known as the little-leaved satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
