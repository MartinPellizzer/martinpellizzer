Culcitium dentatum

Culcitium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed culcitium, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, serrated leaves that are often a deep green color with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, Culcitium dentatum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
