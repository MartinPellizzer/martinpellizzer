Ceramocarpium maximilianum

Ceramocarpium maximilianum, commonly known as the Mexican rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

It is native to Mexico and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
