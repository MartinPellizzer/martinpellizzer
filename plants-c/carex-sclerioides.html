Carex sclerioides

Carex sclerioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or moist soil environments, often in marshes or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the summer months.

Carex sclerioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
