Cieca dictamo

Cieca dictamo, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a delicate anise-like flavor.

Cieca dictamo thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile garden plant.
