Cynanchum macraei

Cynanchum macraei, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles that contain elongated, flattened seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health purposes.
