Carex bhutanica

Carex bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region, including Bhutan, India, and Nepal.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain streams. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color.

Carex bhutanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
