Cardamine pratensis subsp. matthioli

Cardamine pratensis subsp.

matthioli, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist meadows and wetlands in Europe. It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
