Cucumis melo convar. elongatus

Cucumis melo convar. elongatus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, slender fruits.

It typically features a smooth, pale green skin with a mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for fresh consumption. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms, and it relies on pollination for fruit development.

This variety is valued for its versatility in culinary uses and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
