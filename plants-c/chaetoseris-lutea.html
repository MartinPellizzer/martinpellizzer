Chaetoseris lutea

Chaetoseris lutea, commonly known as the yellow starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dense, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Chaetoseris lutea is often found in open habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
