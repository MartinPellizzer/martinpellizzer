Clianthus formosus

Clianthus formosus, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or the punga, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it often grows in dense stands along the shoreline.

Clianthus formosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
