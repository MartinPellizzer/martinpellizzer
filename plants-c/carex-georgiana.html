Carex georgiana

Carex georgiana, commonly known as the Georgiana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically green, with a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in coastal areas.

Carex georgiana produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
