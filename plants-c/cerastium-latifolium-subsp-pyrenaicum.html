Cerastium latifolium subsp. pyrenaicum

Cerastium latifolium subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May.

The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and limited sunlight, making it well-suited to its alpine and subalpine habitats.
