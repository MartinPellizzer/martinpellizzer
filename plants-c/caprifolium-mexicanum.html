Caprifolium mexicanum

Caprifolium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
