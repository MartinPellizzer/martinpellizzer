Cynara cardunculus var. ferocissima

Cynara cardunculus var. ferocissima, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a striking Mediterranean plant characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Europe and North Africa. The plant can grow up to 3 meters tall, with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in coarse hairs and have a distinctive, almost prehistoric appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in cuisine and medicine.
