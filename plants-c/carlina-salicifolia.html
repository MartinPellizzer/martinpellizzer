Carlina salicifolia

Carlina salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, fuzzy leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, creating a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Carlina salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
