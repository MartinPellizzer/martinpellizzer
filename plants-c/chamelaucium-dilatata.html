Chamelaucium dilatata

Chamelaucium dilatata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paper bush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathland or coastal dunes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.

Chamelaucium dilatata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
