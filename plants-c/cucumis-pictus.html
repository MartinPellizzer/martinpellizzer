Cucumis pictus

Cucumis pictus, commonly known as the painted cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny fruits that range in color from green to yellow and often display a mottled or speckled appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat bitter aroma.

Cucumis pictus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
