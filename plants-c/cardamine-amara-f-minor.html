Cardamine amara f. minor

Cardamine amara f. minor, commonly known as the lesser bittercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate basal leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in a siliqua, a dry, dehiscent fruit characteristic of the Brassicaceae family.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
