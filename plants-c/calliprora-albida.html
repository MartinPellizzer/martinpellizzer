Calliprora albida

Calliprora albida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Calliprora albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
