Cajanus helvolus

Cajanus helvolus, commonly known as the wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used for forage or as a protein source. Cajanus helvolus is adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
