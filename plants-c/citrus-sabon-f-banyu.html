Citrus sabon f. banyu

Citrus sabon f. banyu, commonly known as the Banyu citrus or water citrus, is a unique hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant fruits that have a high water content and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its aromatic properties.
