Corallodendron bracteatum

Corallodendron bracteatum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are usually pink, red, or orange, and they bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant.
