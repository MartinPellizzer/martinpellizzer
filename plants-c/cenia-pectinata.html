Cenia pectinata

Cenia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in three-winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Cenia pectinata is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in some regions.
