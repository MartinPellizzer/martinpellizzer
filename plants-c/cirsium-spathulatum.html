Cirsium spathulatum

Cirsium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Cirsium spathulatum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
