Coleus rugosus

Coleus rugosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, textured leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Coleus rugosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
