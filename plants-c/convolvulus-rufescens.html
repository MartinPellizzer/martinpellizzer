Convolvulus rufescens

Convolvulus rufescens, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It blooms throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
