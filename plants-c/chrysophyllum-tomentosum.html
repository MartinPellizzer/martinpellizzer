Chrysophyllum tomentosum

Chrysophyllum tomentosum, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry with a waxy, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
