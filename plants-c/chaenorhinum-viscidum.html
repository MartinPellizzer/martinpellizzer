Chaenorhinum viscidum

Chaenorhinum viscidum, commonly known as the sticky horned poppy, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, branched stems covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, radially symmetrical flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red or orange. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
