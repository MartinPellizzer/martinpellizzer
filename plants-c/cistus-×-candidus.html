Cistus × candidus

Cistus × candidus, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Cistus × candidus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
