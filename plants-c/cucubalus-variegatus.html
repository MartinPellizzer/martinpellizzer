Cucubalus variegatus

Cucubalus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow patterns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical fruits resembling cucumbers. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
