Cousinia dubia

Cousinia dubia, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
