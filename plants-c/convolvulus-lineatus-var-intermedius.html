Convolvulus lineatus var. intermedius

Convolvulus lineatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to several meters, using its tendrils to attach to supports.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
