Chrysopsis pinifolia

Chrysopsis pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, pine-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a branched stem, linear leaves, and a flowering head composed of numerous small florets.

Chrysopsis pinifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
