Carex berteroana

Carex berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
