Cephalocereus quadricentralis

Cephalocereus quadricentralis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its flowers are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
