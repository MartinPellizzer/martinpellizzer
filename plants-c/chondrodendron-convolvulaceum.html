Chondrodendron convolvulaceum

Chondrodendron convolvulaceum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes morning glories and other climbing plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pale greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name.

It is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
