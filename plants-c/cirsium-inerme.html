Cirsium inerme

Cirsium inerme, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it has been introduced to various parts of the world. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its prickly foliage serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
