Cogswellia platycarpa

Cogswellia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by spherical, green fruits that mature to a reddish color. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Cogswellia platycarpa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
