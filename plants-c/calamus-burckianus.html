Calamus burckianus

Calamus burckianus, commonly known as the Burckian's canes, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
