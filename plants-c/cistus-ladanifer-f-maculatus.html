Cistus ladanifer f. maculatus

Cistus ladanifer f. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive spots, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.
