Croton antisyphiliticus var. perdicipes

Croton antisyphiliticus var. perdicipes, commonly known as the red-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often display shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
