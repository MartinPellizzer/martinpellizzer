Cistus × incanus subsp. villosus

Cistus × incanus subsp.

villosus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its greyish, woolly foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is known for its aromatic properties.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to other Cistus species.
