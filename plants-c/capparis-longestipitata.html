Capparis longestipitata

Capparis longestipitata, commonly known as the long-styled caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Capparis longestipitata is adapted to arid environments and exhibits typical xerophytic features.
