Cephalotaxus griffithii

Cephalotaxus griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan nutmeg or the golden cypress, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its unique, scale-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
