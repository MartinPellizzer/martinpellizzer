Carex divulsa var. polycarpa

Carex divulsa var. polycarpa, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant is distinguished by its dense, clustered growth habit and the production of multiple fruiting spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
