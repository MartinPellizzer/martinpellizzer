Cerasus mahaleb var. variegata

Cerasus mahaleb var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry or variegated Mahaleb cherry, is a variant of the Mahaleb cherry native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its unique foliage, it is also valued for its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
