Convolvulus longiflorus

Convolvulus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus longiflorus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
