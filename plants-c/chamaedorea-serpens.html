Chamaedorea serpens

Chamaedorea serpens, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Aroid family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that features tall, arching leaves that are often striped with green and silver. The plant is well-suited for indoor environments due to its low light requirements and minimal watering needs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Chamaedorea serpens is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
