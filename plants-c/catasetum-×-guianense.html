Catasetum × guianense

Catasetum × guianense, commonly known as the Guianese Catasetum, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple showy flowers that bloom sequentially, attracting specific pollinators.

The botanical interest of Catasetum × guianense lies in its hybrid origin and its role in orchid hybridization studies.
