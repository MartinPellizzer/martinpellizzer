Carex sexspicata

Carex sexspicata, commonly known as the six-spurred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Carex sexspicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
