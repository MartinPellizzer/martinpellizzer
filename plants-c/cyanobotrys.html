Cyanobotrys

Cyanobotrys, commonly known as the blue-green algae, is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its ability to perform photosynthesis, similar to plants, and it contains chlorophyll a, which gives it its characteristic blue-green color. Cyanobotrys can form dense blooms in freshwater systems, sometimes leading to algal blooms that impact water quality. It plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems.

However, some species can produce toxins that are harmful to aquatic life and humans.
