Crotalaria ceciliae

Crotalaria ceciliae, commonly known as Cecilia's crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a crown-like structure. The plant typically grows in open areas and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria ceciliae is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
