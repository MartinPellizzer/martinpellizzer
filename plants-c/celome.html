Celome

Celome, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Celome is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in herbal remedies.
