Cucurbita maxima var. castanina

Cucurbita maxima var. castanina, commonly known as the Turk's Turban pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted fruit shape, which can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The skin of the fruit is thick and typically ranges in color from deep orange to dark red, with a rough, bumpy texture. This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
