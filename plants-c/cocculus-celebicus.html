Cocculus celebicus

Cocculus celebicus, commonly known as the Java morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive berries that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, and they open in the morning, giving it its common name.

Cocculus celebicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
