Capsicum ovatum

Capsicum ovatum, commonly known as the "Ovate Pepper" or "Mexican Pepper," is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, tropical climates. The plant produces small, round fruits that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on maturity. Its leaves are ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it has showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Capsicum ovatum is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cooking.
