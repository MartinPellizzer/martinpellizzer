Chenopodium ficifolium var. rubescens

Chenopodium ficifolium var. rubescens, commonly known as red-leaf goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its reddish foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or bronze color in response to environmental stress or during the autumn season. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are edible, though they are often consumed by birds and small mammals.

This plant is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry fields.
