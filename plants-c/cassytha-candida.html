Cassytha candida

Cassytha candida, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Cassytha candida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
