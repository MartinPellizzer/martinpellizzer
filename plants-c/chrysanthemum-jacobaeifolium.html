Chrysanthemum jacobaeifolium

Chrysanthemum jacobaeifolium, commonly known as the Jacob's bloodleaf, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, slightly hairy leaves with reddish veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Chrysanthemum jacobaeifolium is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
