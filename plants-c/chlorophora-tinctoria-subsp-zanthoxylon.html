Chlorophora tinctoria subsp. zanthoxylon

Chlorophora tinctoria subsp.

zanthoxylon, commonly known as the yellow-heart wood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that ranges from yellow to reddish-brown in color.

The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its high tannin content, which contributes to its durability and resistance to decay. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat and food source for various wildlife.
