Chrysalidocarpus baronii

Chrysalidocarpus baronii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
