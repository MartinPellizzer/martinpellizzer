Citrus × aurantium subsp. amara

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

amara, commonly known as bitter orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is typically small, bitter, and not suitable for eating fresh but is used in traditional medicine. It contains high levels of essential oils and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of aromatic compounds.
