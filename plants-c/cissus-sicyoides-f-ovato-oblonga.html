Cissus sicyoides f. ovato-oblonga

Cissus sicyoides f. ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the broad-ovate cissus, is a variant of the genus Cissus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often used in traditional medicine. The leaves are typically ovate-oblong, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
