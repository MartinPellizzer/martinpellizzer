Cupania americana

Cupania americana, commonly known as the American sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
