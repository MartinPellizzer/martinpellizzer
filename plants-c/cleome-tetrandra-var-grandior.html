Cleome tetrandra var. grandior

Cleome tetrandra var. grandior, commonly known as the grandiflora cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.
