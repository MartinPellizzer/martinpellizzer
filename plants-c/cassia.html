Cassia

Cassia, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Indian cinnamon, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

These shrubs or small trees are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Cassia species are often cultivated for their aromatic bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The leaves and flowers are also used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.
