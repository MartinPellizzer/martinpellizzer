Calamagrostis brachyathera

Calamagrostis brachyathera, commonly known as the short-haired reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
