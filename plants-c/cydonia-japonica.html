Cydonia japonica

Cydonia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces fragrant, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other quince species.
