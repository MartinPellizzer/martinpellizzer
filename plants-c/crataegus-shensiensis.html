Crataegus shensiensis

Crataegus shensiensis, commonly known as the Sichuan hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
