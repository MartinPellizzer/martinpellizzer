Cyathea × wilsonii

Cyathea × wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyathea cooperi and Cyathea trapezophylla.

It is a large evergreen tree fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

Cyathea × wilsonii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to add a tropical ambiance to gardens.
