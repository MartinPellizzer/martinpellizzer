Camelina sativa subsp. macrocarpa

Camelina sativa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded camelina, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear, hairy leaves.

The plant produces large, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which have both agricultural and industrial applications.
