Cineraria montevidensis

Cineraria montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo cineraria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, dandelion-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in its survival in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the spring and summer months.

Cineraria montevidensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
