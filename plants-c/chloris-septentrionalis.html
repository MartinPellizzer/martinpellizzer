Chloris septentrionalis

Chloris septentrionalis, commonly known as northern grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and prairies. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Chloris septentrionalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
