Convolvulus angustissimus subsp. peninsularum

Convolvulus angustissimus subsp.

peninsularum, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, twining stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Convolvulaceae family.
