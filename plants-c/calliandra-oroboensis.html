Calliandra oroboensis

Calliandra oroboensis, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to pink. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The leaves are bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green appearance.
