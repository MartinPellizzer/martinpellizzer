Carduus × carniolicus

Carduus × carniolicus, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
