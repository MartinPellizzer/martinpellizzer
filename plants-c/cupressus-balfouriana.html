Cupressus balfouriana

Cupressus balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar shape and scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Cupressus balfouriana is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and distinctive appearance make it a popular specimen tree in horticulture.
