Copiapoa coquimbana var. fiedleriana

Copiapoa coquimbana var. fiedleriana, commonly known as the Fiedler's copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
