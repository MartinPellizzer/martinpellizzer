Corchorus africanus

Corchorus africanus, commonly known as African marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
