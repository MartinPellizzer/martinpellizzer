Cryptocarya multicostata

Cryptocarya multicostata, commonly known as the mountain ash or Australian mountain ash, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
