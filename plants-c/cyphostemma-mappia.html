Cyphostemma mappia

Cyphostemma mappia, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnately divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
