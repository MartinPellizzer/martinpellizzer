Crataegus mexicana

Crataegus mexicana, commonly known as Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Crataegus mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
