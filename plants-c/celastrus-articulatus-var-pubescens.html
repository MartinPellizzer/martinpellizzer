Celastrus articulatus var. pubescens

Celastrus articulatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
