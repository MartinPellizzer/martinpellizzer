Chusquea pallida

Chusquea pallida, commonly known as the pale grass tree, is a species of bamboo-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in a whitish, powdery bloom. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests at moderate elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, fibrous texture.

Chusquea pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
