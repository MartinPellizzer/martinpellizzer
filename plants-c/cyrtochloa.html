Cyrtochloa

Cyrtochloa, commonly known as the button grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Cyrtochloa species are often found in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
