Clematis alpina var. ochotensis

Clematis alpina var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and the Far East.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems that climb by means of tendrils, and its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
