Ceanothus Megacarpus Var. Insularis

Island ceanothus, scientifically known as Ceanothus megacarpus var. insularis, is a rare and unique plant endemic to the Hawaiian Islands. This shrub thrives in the rugged, coastal environments of Maui and Molokai, where it has adapted to harsh conditions. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in late winter, providing a vivid contrast to the surrounding landscape. Despite its beauty, the species faces threats from invasive species and habitat loss, making conservation efforts critical. As a symbol of Hawaii's unique biodiversity, Island ceanothus holds both ecological and cultural significance.
