Cardamine peteroana

Cardamine peteroana, commonly known as the Peter's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Cardamine peteroana thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
