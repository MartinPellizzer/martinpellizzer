Clinopodium megalanthum

Clinopodium megalanthum, commonly known as giant betony, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a square stem typical of the Lamiaceae family.

Clinopodium megalanthum is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
