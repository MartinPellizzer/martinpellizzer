Caroxylon glabrescens var. apiculatum

Caroxylon glabrescens var. apiculatum, commonly known as the Apiculate Bristlecone, is a rare and ancient coniferous species native to the high-altitude regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, long lifespan, and distinctive, rugged bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant exhibits a conical shape with dense, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls. It thrives in harsh, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
