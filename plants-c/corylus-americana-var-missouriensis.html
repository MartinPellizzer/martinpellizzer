Corylus americana var. missouriensis

Corylus americana var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri hickory, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
