Citrus × funadoko

Citrus × funadoko, commonly known as the Funadoko mandarin.

It is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan, characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel. The fruit has a sweet and slightly acidic flavor, making it popular in both fresh consumption and culinary uses. Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is a result of crossbreeding between Poncirus trifoliata and Citrus reticulata.

Its ornamental value is also notable, with attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
