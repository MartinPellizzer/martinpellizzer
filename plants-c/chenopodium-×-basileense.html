Chenopodium × basileense

Chenopodium × basileense, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils such as fields and roadsides.

Chenopodium × basileense is known for its nutritional value and has been used historically as a food source.
