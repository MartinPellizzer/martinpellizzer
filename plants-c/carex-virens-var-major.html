Carex virens var. major

Carex virens var. major, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

Carex virens var. major is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
