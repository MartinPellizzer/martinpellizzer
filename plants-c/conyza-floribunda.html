Conyza floribunda

Conyza floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bluemink, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow with white ray florets, blooming from late winter to early summer.

Conyza floribunda is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and agricultural fields.
