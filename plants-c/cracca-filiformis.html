Cracca filiformis

Cracca filiformis, commonly known as the slender cracca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a cluster, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Cracca filiformis is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
