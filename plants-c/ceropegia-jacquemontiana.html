Ceropegia jacquemontiana

Ceropegia jacquemontiana, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, making it adapted to arid environments. It produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as moths and birds.

Ceropegia jacquemontiana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
