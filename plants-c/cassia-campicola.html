Cassia campicola

Cassia campicola, commonly known as the African cassia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia campicola is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
