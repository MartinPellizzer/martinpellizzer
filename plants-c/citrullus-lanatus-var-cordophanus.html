Citrullus lanatus var. cordophanus

Citrullus lanatus var. cordophanus, commonly known as the watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and a sprawling, trailing growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that give way to the familiar green rind and red or yellow flesh. Its fruit is rich in water, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it a popular fruit worldwide.

The variety is well-adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
