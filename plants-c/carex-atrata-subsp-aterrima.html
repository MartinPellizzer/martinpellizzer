Carex atrata subsp. aterrima

Carex atrata subsp.

aterrima, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescence structure.

The plant features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and use in horticultural settings for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
