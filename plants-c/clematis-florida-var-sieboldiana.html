Clematis florida var. sieboldiana

Clematis florida var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This variety is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to climb various structures with its twining stems.
