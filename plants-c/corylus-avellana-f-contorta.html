Corylus avellana f. contorta

Corylus avellana f. contorta, commonly known as the twisted hazel, is a unique horticultural form of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted branches that create an ornamental and often whimsical appearance. The plant produces small, edible nuts similar to those of its parent species, though they may be less abundant. It is typically grown as a decorative shrub in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The foliage is usually dark green, turning yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the garden.
