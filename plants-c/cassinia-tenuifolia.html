Cassinia tenuifolia

Cassinia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in spherical or conical heads.

Cassinia tenuifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
