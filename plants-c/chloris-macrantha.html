Chloris macrantha

Chloris macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which are borne on spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
