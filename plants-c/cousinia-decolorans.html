Cousinia decolorans

Cousinia decolorans, commonly known as the pale cousinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia decolorans is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
