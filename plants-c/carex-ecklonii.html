Carex ecklonii

Carex ecklonii, commonly known as the Ecklon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex ecklonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
