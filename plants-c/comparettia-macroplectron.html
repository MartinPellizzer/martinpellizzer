Comparettia macroplectron

Comparettia macroplectron, commonly known as the large-veined comparettia, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or yellow, have a complex structure with prominent, pleated petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
