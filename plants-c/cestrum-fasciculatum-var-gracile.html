Cestrum fasciculatum var. gracile

Cestrum fasciculatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.
