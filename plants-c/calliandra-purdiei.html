Calliandra purdiei

Calliandra purdiei, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Calliandra purdiei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
