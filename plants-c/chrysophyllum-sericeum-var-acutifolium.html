Chrysophyllum sericeum var. acutifolium

Chrysophyllum sericeum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the silky apple or silverleaf cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical and often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, creamy fruit with a unique, sweet-and-sour flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice for both landscaping and cultivation in suitable regions.
