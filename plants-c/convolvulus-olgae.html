Convolvulus olgae

Convolvulus olgae, commonly known as the Olgas bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, smooth seeds.
