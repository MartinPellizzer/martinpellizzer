Cupania falcata

Cupania falcata, commonly known as the Florida persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. Cupania falcata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various animals.
