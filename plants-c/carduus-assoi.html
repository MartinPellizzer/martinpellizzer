Carduus assoi

Carduus assoi, commonly known as the Asso's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowering heads contain both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carduus assoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
