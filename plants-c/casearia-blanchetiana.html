Casearia blanchetiana

Casearia blanchetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright blue berries in the fall. These berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds, making the plant a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.

Its adaptability to various soil types and tolerance to partial shade make it a versatile choice for landscaping in warm climates.
