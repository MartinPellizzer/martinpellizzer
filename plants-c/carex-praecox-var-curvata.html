Carex praecox var. curvata

Carex praecox var. curvata, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
