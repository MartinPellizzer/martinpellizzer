Ceropegia cynanchoides

Ceropegia cynanchoides, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a unique, spurred shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a potted plant.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
