Carex teretiuscula var. prairea

Carex teretiuscula var. prairea, commonly known as prairie sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
