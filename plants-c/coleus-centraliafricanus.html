Coleus centraliafricanus

Coleus centraliafricanus, commonly known as African coleus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

Native to Central Africa, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often colorful, displaying shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. The flowers are small and clustered in whorls, usually appearing in shades of blue or violet.

It is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
