Caryophyllus adenolepis

Caryophyllus adenolepis, commonly known as the desert zinnia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Caryophyllus adenolepis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
