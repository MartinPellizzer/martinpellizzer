Campanula rotundifolia subsp. sancta

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

sancta, commonly known as the sacred bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
