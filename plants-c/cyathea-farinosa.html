Cyathea farinosa

Cyathea farinosa, commonly known as the farinose tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the undersides of its fronds rather than flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea farinosa plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
