Carex trichocarpa var. imberbis

Carex trichocarpa var. imberbis, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, unbranched stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
