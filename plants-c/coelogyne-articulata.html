Coelogyne articulata

Coelogyne articulata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive yellow and white coloration. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne articulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique fragrance.
