Coreopsis foeniculacea

Coreopsis foeniculacea, commonly known as the sweet goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Coreopsis foeniculacea is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and low-maintenance garden plant.
