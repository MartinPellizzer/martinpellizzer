Crepis occidentalis subsp. costata

Crepis occidentalis subsp.

costata, commonly known as the western roughdock, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a robust stem with distinctive, lobed leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite inflorescence, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptability to arid environments.
