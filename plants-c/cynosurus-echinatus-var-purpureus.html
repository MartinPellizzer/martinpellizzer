Cynosurus echinatus var. purpureus

Cynosurus echinatus var. purpureus, commonly known as purple tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
