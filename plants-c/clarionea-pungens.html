Clarionea pungens

Clarionea pungens, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that cause a rash upon contact. Clarionea pungens is native to temperate regions and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
