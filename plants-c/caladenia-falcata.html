Caladenia falcata

Caladenia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender, spider-like flower spike. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing delicate, pale yellow to greenish flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. Caladenia falcata is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
