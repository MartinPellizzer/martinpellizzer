Coffea perrieri

Coffea perrieri, commonly known as the Perrieri coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia, where it grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,000 meters. The plant produces small, red berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. It is known for its unique flavor profile, characterized by floral and citrus notes.

Due to its limited cultivation and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
