Cypripedium × nandi

Cypripedium × nandi, commonly known as the Nandi's lily or the yellow lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
