Cyclamen officinarum

Cyclamen officinarum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia and the Caucasus.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinct waxy coating, and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It prefers cool, bright conditions and well-draining soil to thrive.

Cyclamen officinarum is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
