Chionanthus mannii

Chionanthus mannii, commonly known as the whiteflower chionanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval in shape, with a glossy green appearance.

Chionanthus mannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
