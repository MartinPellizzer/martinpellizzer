Cirsium aristatum

Cirsium aristatum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth and often have a grayish-green hue.

Cirsium aristatum is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
