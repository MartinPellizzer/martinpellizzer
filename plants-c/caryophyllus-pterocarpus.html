Caryophyllus pterocarpus

Caryophyllus pterocarpus, commonly known as the Java cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and thick, aromatic leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
