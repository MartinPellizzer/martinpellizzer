Chamaecrista semaphora

Chamaecrista semaphora, commonly known as the signal bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. Its seeds are encased in pods that turn from green to brown as they mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chamaecrista semaphora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
