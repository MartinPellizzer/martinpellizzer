Cupressus lawsoniana coerulea

Cupressus lawsoniana coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Atlas Cedar, is a striking coniferous tree native to the mountains of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage that gives it a unique and striking appearance. The tree has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its cold hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
