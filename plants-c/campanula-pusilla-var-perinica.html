Campanula pusilla var. perinica

Campanula pusilla var. perinica, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens with limited space.
