Cyperus benghalensis

Cyperus benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian papyrus or narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus benghalensis produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
