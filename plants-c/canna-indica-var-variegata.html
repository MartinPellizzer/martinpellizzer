Canna indica var. variegata

Canna indica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cannonball plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant produces showy, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in full sun and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to grow as a herbaceous perennial or shrub, depending on climate conditions.
