Carex kirinensis

Carex kirinensis, commonly known as the Kirin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex kirinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
