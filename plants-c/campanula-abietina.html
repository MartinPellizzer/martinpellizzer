Campanula abietina

Campanula abietina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its hardy nature.

Campanula abietina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
