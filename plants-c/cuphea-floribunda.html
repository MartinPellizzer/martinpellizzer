Cuphea floribunda

Cuphea floribunda, commonly known as the flame flower or red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea floribunda is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
