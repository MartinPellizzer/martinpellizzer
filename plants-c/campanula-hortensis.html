Campanula hortensis

Campanula hortensis, commonly known as the garden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Campanula hortensis is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
