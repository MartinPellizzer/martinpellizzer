Carthamus lanatus

Carthamus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Carthamus lanatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
