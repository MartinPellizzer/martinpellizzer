Canarium pilicarpum

Canarium pilicarpum, commonly known as the wax apple or sugar apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, waxy fruits that turn red when mature. The fruit's pulp is sweet and aromatic, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
