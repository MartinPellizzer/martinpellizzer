Cestrum amictum f. elongatum

Cestrum amictum f. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stalked nightshade, is a variant of the Cestrum amictum species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

This form is a popular choice for gardeners interested in night-blooming plants with unique aesthetic appeal.
