Coreopsis parvifolia

Coreopsis parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring finely cut, fern-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically beneficial plant for pollinators.
