Convolvulus penangianus

Convolvulus penangianus, commonly known as the Penang morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
