Centaurea lactiflora

Centaurea lactiflora, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and bright purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains a milky sap, which is responsible for its common name. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea lactiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
