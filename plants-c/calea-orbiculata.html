Calea orbiculata

Calea orbiculata, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that emerge from a rosette of velvety, waxy leaves. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which resembles a candle with a central spike of flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
