Callicarpa bodinieri var. lyi

Callicarpa bodinieri var. lyi, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to violet berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to ecological diversity.
