Chenolea diffusa

Chenolea diffusa, commonly known as the hairy chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by bracts. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Chenolea diffusa is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
