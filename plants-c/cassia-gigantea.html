Cassia gigantea

Cassia gigantea, commonly known as the giant senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds. Cassia gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
