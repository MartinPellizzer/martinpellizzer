Cissus glauca

Cissus glauca, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Cissus glauca is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
