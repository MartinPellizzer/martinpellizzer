Cestrum oliganthum var. latifolium

Cestrum oliganthum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically broad and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
