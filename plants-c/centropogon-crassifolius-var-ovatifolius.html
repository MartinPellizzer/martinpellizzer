Centropogon crassifolius var. ovatifolius

Centropogon crassifolius var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting various pollinators. Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
