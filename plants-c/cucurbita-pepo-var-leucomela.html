Cucurbita pepo var. leucomela

Cucurbita pepo var. leucomela, commonly known as the white-fruited gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its pale green to white, spherical or elongated fruits that are typically smooth and warty. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm climates. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and pumpkin.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a vegetable in traditional cuisines.
