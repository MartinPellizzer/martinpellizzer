Cupressus macrocarpa variegata

Cupressus macrocarpa variegata, commonly known as the Australian lacebark or variegated western arborvitae, is a cultivar of the western arborvitae.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and yellow markings on its needle-like leaves. The plant has a dense, conical shape with a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique coloration and evergreen nature, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
