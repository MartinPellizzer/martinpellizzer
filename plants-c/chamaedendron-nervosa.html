Chamaedendron nervosa

Chamaedendron nervosa, commonly known as the veined flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaped areas in warm regions.
