Callerya

Callerya, commonly known as the glorybower, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of about 100 species, primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and yellow. They typically have compound leaves and produce legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Callerya species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
