Crataegus rubescens

Crataegus rubescens, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
