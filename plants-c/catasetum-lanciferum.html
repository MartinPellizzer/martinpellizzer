Catasetum lanciferum

Catasetum lanciferum, commonly known as the lanciferum orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive inflorescence that bears showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are characterized by their elongated, spurred petals and a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

The orchid's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
