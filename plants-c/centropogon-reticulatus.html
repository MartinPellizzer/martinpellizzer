Centropogon reticulatus

Centropogon reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a distinct reticulate venation pattern, giving it its common name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Centropogon reticulatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
