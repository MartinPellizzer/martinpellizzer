Chondropetalum paniculatum

Chondropetalum paniculatum, commonly known as the common lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found on tree trunks, rocks, or soil surfaces. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the mutualistic relationship between the fungal and algal components. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on the alga for photosynthesis and the fungus for water and nutrient absorption.

Chondropetalum paniculatum plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
