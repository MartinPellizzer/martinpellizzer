Cleome nummularia

Cleome nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome nummularia is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
