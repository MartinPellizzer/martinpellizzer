Caesulia axillaris

Caesulia axillaris, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Caesulia axillaris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
