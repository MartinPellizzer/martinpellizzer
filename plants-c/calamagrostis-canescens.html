Calamagrostis canescens

Calamagrostis canescens, commonly known as gray beard grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn.

Calamagrostis canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
