Chrysogonum virginianum var. dentatum

Chrysogonum virginianum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a fibrous root system and produces basal leaves that are lanceolate with serrated margins. The plant bears yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cymes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, including swamps and stream banks, and is often found in the eastern United States.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
