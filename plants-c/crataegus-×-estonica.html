Crataegus × estonica

Crataegus × estonica, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna (common hawthorn) and Crataegus laevigata (smooth hawthorn). The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. It produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

Crataegus × estonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
