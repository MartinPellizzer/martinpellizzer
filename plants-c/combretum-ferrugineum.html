Combretum ferrugineum

Combretum ferrugineum, commonly known as red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Combretum ferrugineum is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
