Carex filifolia var. filifolia

Carex filifolia var. filifolia, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
