Ceropegia sheilae

Ceropegia sheilae, commonly known as the Sheilae African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia sheilae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
