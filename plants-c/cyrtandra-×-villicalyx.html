Cyrtandra × villicalyx

Cyrtandra × villicalyx, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cyrtandra villosa and Cyrtandra calycosa, characterized by its striking violet to purple bracts that resemble berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
