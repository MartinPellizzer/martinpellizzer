Carex tapintzensis var. lamprosandra

Carex tapintzensis var. lamprosandra, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct silver or whitish stripe running along the midrib.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in a loose, cylindrical arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
