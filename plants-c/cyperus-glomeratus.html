Cyperus glomeratus

Cyperus glomeratus, commonly known as umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus glomeratus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
