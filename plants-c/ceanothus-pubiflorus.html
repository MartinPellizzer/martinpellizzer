Ceanothus pubiflorus

Ceanothus pubiflorus, commonly known as the softleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
