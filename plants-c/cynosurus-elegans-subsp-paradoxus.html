Cynosurus elegans subsp. paradoxus

Cynosurus elegans subsp.

paradoxus, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
