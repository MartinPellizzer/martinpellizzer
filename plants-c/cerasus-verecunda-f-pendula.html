Cerasus verecunda f. pendula

Cerasus verecunda f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its cascading branches.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cultivar of the cherry species Cerasus verecunda. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically used in jams and preserves. Its unique pendulous growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy green color.
