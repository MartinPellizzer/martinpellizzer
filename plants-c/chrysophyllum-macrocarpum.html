Chrysophyllum macrocarpum

Chrysophyllum macrocarpum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces large, edible fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
