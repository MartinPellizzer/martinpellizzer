Cassebeeria obliqua

Cassebeeria obliqua, commonly known as the oblique cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Cassebeeria obliqua is also valued for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
