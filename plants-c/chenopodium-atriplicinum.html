Chenopodium atriplicinum

Chenopodium atriplicinum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-green, broadly ovate leaves with a pubescent surface, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain areas. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
