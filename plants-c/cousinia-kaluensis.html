Cousinia kaluensis

Cousinia kaluensis, commonly known as the Karakoram fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Cousinia kaluensis produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
