Cornus alba var. kesselringii

Cornus alba var. kesselringii, commonly known as the Kesselringii dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that emerge in early spring, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters before the bracts. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant seasonal display.
