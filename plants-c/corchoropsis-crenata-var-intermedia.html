Corchoropsis crenata var. intermedia

Corchoropsis crenata var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
