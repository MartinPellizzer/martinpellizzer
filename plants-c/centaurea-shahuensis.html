Centaurea shahuensis

Centaurea shahuensis, commonly known as Shahu's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
