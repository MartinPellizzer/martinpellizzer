Christisonia grandiflora

Christisonia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Christisonia, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that provides a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Christisonia grandiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in well-drained soils.
