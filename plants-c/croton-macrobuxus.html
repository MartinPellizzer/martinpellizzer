Croton macrobuxus

Croton macrobuxus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in some areas.
