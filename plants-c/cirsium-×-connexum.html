Cirsium × connexum

Cirsium × connexum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in other open areas.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
