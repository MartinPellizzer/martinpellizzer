Cryosophila macrocarpa

Cryosophila macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, helping to retain moisture in the cold climate.

Cryosophila macrocarpa is a perennial herb that plays a role in the alpine ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
