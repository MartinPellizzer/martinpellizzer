Crossandra longehirsuta

Crossandra longehirsuta, commonly known as the hairy crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Crossandra longehirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
