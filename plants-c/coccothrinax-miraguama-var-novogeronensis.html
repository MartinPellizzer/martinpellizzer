Coccothrinax miraguama var. novogeronensis

Coccothrinax miraguama var. novogeronensis, commonly known as the Geroné Island palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Geroné in the Dominican Republic.

It is a small, slender palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the humid tropical climate of its native habitat.
