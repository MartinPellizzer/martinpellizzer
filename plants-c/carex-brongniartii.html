Carex brongniartii

Carex brongniartii, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Carex brongniartii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ecological value in wetland restoration projects.
