Carex ledebourii

Carex ledebourii, commonly known as the ledebour's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or salt marshes. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on long, slender stems and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex ledebourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
