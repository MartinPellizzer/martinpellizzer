Cirrhopetalum caudatum

Cirrhopetalum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Cirrhopetalum caudatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
