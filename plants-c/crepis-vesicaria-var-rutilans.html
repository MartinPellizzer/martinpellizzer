Crepis vesicaria var. rutilans

Crepis vesicaria var. rutilans, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
