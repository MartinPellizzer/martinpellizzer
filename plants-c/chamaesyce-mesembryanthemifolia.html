Chamaesyce mesembryanthemifolia

Chamaesyce mesembryanthemifolia, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy habitats. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun with minimal water.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
