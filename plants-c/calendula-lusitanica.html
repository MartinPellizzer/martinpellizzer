Calendula lusitanica

Calendula lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Calendula lusitanica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
