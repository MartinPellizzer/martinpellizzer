Crinum mackenii

Crinum mackenii, commonly known as the Mackenzie crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are often broad and pointed, growing from a bulbous stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Crinum mackenii is also known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which is a common trait among plants in its family.
