Chaenomeles

Chaenomeles, commonly known as Japanese quince, is a genus of deciduous shrubs native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that often precede leaf emergence. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Chaenomeles is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
