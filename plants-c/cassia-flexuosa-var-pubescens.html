Cassia flexuosa var. pubescens

Cassia flexuosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
