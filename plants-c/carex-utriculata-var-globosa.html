Carex utriculata var. globosa

Carex utriculata var. globosa, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it can grow up to 3 feet tall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
