Convolvulus sepincola

Convolvulus sepincola, commonly known as the coastal bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy environments. It is often found in dunes and along the edges of salt marshes.

Convolvulus sepincola is known for its ability to tolerate saline conditions and is sometimes used in coastal restoration projects.
