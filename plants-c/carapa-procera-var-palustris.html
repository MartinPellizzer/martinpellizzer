Carapa procera var. palustris

Carapa procera var. palustris, commonly known as the swamp ceiba, is a tropical tree native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is adapted to wetland environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
