Cassia belemii

Cassia belemii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia belemii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
