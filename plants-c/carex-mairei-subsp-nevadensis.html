Carex mairei subsp. nevadensis

Carex mairei subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
