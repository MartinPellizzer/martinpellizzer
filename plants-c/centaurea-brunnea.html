Centaurea brunnea

Centaurea brunnea, commonly known as the brown centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea brunnea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
