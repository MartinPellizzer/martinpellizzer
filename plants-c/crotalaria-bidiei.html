Crotalaria bidiei

Crotalaria bidiei, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
