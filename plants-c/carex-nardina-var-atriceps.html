Carex nardina var. atriceps

Carex nardina var. atriceps, commonly known as blackstem sedges, is a perennial sedge native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
