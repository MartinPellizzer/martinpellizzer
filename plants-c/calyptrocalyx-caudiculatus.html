Calyptrocalyx caudiculatus

Calyptrocalyx caudiculatus, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like calyx that encases the corolla, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Calyptrocalyx caudiculatus is a perennial shrub that often forms dense, compact growth, making it a notable addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
