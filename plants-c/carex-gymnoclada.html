Carex gymnoclada

Carex gymnoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex gymnoclada plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
