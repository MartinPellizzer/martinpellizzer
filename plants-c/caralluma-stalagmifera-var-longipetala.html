Caralluma stalagmifera var. longipetala

Caralluma stalagmifera var. longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled rock fig, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that help store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and appetite-suppressing properties.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated petals, which set it apart from other Caralluma species.
