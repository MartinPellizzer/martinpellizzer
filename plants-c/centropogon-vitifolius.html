Centropogon vitifolius

Centropogon vitifolius, commonly known as the grapeleaf panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Centropogon vitifolius plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
