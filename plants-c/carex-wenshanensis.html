Carex wenshanensis

Carex wenshanensis, commonly known as the Wenshan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, and is found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.

Carex wenshanensis is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic contribution to garden landscapes.
