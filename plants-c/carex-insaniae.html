Carex insaniae

Carex insaniae, commonly known as the crazy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex insaniae thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
