Carex pittieri

Carex pittieri, commonly known as the dwarf basket sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems.
