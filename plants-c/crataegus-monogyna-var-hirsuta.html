Crataegus monogyna var. hirsuta

Crataegus monogyna var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, thorned branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

It is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
