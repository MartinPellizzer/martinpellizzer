Caladenia denticulata subsp. denticulata

Caladenia denticulata subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the toothed spider-orchid, is an endemic species found in the south-western region of Western Australia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, hairy stem and a single, erect flower that typically blooms in spring.

The flower has a distinctive, elongated labellum with a toothed edge, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed habitats.

The plant is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique and striking floral morphology.
