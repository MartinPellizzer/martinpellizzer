Croton erythroxyloides var. lanceolatus

Croton erythroxyloides var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lance-leaf croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a prominent venation pattern. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often yellow or greenish in color, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
