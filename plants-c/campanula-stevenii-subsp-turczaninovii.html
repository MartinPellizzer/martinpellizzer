Campanula stevenii subsp. turczaninovii

Campanula stevenii subsp.

turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a hairy texture, while the stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
