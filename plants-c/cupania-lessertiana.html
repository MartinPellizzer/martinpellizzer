Cupania lessertiana

Cupania lessertiana, commonly known as the Chilean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Cupania lessertiana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
