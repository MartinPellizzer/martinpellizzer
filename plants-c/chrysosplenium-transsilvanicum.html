Chrysosplenium transsilvanicum

Chrysosplenium transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian chrysosplenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark green, slightly hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chrysosplenium transsilvanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
