Capsicum baccatum var. baccatum

Capsicum baccatum var. baccatum, commonly known as the Andean or Peruvian chili, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, often curved fruit shape and a range of colors including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by the distinctive, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is used in traditional cuisines across South America.
