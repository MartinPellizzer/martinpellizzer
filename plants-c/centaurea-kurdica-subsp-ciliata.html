Centaurea kurdica subsp. ciliata

Centaurea kurdica subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the Kurdish cornflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and distinctive bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely ciliated leaves and narrow, linear foliage. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
