Centaurea cabardensis

Centaurea cabardensis, commonly known as the Kabardian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Centaurea cabardensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
