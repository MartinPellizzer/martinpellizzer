Cyathea subspathulata

Cyathea subspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and storage for nutrients.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
