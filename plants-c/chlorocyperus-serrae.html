Chlorocyperus serrae

Chlorocyperus serrae, commonly known as the serrated flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it a common sight in marshy environments. Its leaves are often green to bluish-green, and the plant can be found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
