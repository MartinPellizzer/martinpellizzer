Cucumis aromaticus

Cucumis aromaticus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and southwestern Asia. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for cultivation in temperate climates.
