Calamus pseudorivalis

Calamus pseudorivalis, commonly known as the false rival's reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is a climbing palm that produces long, slender leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its inflorescences are typically terminal and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Calamus pseudorivalis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
