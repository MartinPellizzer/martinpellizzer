Cyphomandra sibundoyensis

Cyphomandra sibundoyensis, commonly known as the Sibundoy passionfruit, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and showy, colorful flowers.
