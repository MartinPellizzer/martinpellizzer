Crataegus azarolus subsp. triloba

Crataegus azarolus subsp.

triloba, commonly known as the Turkish hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
