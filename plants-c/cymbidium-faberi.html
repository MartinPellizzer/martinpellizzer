Cymbidium faberi

Cymbidium faberi, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a species native to China and is part of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, with flowers that can range from white to yellow, often with red or purple markings. Cymbidium faberi is a hardy perennial that prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and indoor planting.

Its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
