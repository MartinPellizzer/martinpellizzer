Chenopodium hircinum var. quinoa

Chenopodium hircinum var. quinoa, commonly known as quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its edible, seed-rich heads that form in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with feather-like leaves and small, green flowers. Quinoa is known for its adaptability to diverse climates and soils, making it a resilient crop.

Its seeds are rich in protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, contributing to its status as a valuable ancient grain.
