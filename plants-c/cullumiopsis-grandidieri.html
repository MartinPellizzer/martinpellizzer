Cullumiopsis grandidieri

Cullumiopsis grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically small and star-shaped, often in shades of pink or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its restricted habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
