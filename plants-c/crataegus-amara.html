Crataegus amara

Crataegus amara, commonly known as the bitter hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically bitter and not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Crataegus amara is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to wildlife.
