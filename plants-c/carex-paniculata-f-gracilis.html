Carex paniculata f. gracilis

Carex paniculata f. gracilis, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
