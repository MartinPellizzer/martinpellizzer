Callista luteola

Callista luteola, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Callista luteola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
