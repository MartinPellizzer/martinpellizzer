Copaifera laxa

Copaifera laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its bark and sap contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Copaifera laxa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
