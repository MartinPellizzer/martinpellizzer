Campanula bocconei

Campanula bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula bocconei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
