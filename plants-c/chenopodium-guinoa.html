Chenopodium guinoa

Chenopodium guinoa, commonly known as quinoa, is an annual plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae) and is characterized by its leafy stems and small, green flowers. The plant produces tiny, dry fruits called siliques, which contain the edible seeds. Quinoa is known for its high nutritional value, containing protein, fiber, and essential minerals.

It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both its seeds and as a forage crop.
