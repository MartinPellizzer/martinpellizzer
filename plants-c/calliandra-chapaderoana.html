Calliandra chapaderoana

Calliandra chapaderoana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance.
