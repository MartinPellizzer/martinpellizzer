Casearia auriculata

Casearia auriculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and eye-catching appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape with slightly toothed edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Casearia auriculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
