Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. australis

Convolvulus sagittatus subvar. australis, commonly known as the Australian arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a sprawling perennial with heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.

The species is characterized by its ability to climb or trail, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
