Cailliea dichrostachys

Cailliea dichrostachys, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique floral display and adaptability to various climates contribute to its widespread cultivation.
