Crumenaria polygaloides var. paraguariensis

Crumenaria polygaloides var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, often near rivers or in humid understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
