Catasetum caucanum

Catasetum caucanum, commonly known as the Caucanum Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.

The botanical features of Catasetum caucanum make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
