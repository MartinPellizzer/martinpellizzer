Chlorophytum ledermannii

Chlorophytum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's spider plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it easy to cultivate and maintain.
