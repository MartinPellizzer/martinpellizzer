Cordyline alborosea

Cordyline alborosea, commonly known as the white-veined cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by blue or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and gardeners.
