Cyclanthera quadrifida

Cyclanthera quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with four to five leaflets, giving it its common name.

Cyclanthera quadrifida is also valued for its medicinal properties in some cultures.
