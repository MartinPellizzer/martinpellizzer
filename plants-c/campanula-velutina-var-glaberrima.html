Campanula velutina var. glaberrima

Campanula velutina var. glaberrima, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
