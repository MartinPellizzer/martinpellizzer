Calanthe arcuata subsp. arcuata

Calanthe arcuata subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the curved calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers arranged in a raceme along the stem. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
