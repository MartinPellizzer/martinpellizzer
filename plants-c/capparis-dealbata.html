Capparis dealbata

Capparis dealbata, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and thorny stems, which are typical of the Capparidaceae family. The plant produces small, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in coastal and sandy soils.

Capparis dealbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
