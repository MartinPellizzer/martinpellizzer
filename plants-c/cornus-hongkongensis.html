Cornus hongkongensis

Cornus hongkongensis, commonly known as Hong Kong cornel.

It is a flowering plant native to southern China and is part of the Cornaceae family. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and is an important component of its ecological role in its native habitat.
