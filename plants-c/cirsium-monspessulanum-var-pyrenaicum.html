Cirsium monspessulanum var. pyrenaicum

Cirsium monspessulanum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at higher elevations. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective pioneer species in disturbed or alpine environments.
