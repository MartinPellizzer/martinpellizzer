Clematitis viticella

Clematitis viticella, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven ovate leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences quickly. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a quick coverage plant.

However, its invasive potential in some regions requires careful management.
