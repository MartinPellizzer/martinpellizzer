Cyperus bisumbellatus

Cyperus bisumbellatus, commonly known as the narrow-umbel sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads known as umbels, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Cyperus bisumbellatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
