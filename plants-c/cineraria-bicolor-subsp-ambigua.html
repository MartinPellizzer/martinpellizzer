Cineraria bicolor subsp. ambigua

Cineraria bicolor subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the blue cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
