Chasechloa egregia

Chasechloa egregia, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features fine, silver-green blades that are densely packed, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its delicate foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, especially in bog gardens or water features.

Chasechloa egregia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
