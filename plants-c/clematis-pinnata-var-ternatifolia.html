Clematis pinnata var. ternatifolia

Clematis pinnata var. ternatifolia, commonly known as the three-parted clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
