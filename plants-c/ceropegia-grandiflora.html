Ceropegia grandiflora

Ceropegia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant candleplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators such as moths and flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
