Chrysophyllum gonocarpum

Chrysophyllum gonocarpum, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and yellowish-orange fruits. The fruits are typically small, oblong, and have a sweet, aromatic flavor when ripe. It produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, making it monoecious.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
