Cnicus virginianus

Cnicus virginianus, commonly known as the American burnet, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Cnicus virginianus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
