Caladenia nortonii

Caladenia nortonii, commonly known as Norton's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, intricately patterned orchid flower that resembles a spider. The plant typically blooms during late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it grows in sandy or gravelly soils.
