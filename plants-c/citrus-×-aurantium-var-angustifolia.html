Citrus × aurantium var. angustifolia

Citrus × aurantium var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lemon, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, while the fruit has a thin, peely rind and a high acidity level.

This variety is valued for its culinary and aromatic uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
