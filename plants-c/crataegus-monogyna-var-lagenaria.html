Crataegus monogyna var. lagenaria

Crataegus monogyna var. lagenaria, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous fruits that are typically reddish or orange in color. The plant has pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
