Carduus nutans subsp. subacaulis

Carduus nutans subsp.

subacaulis, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, pale purple to blue flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture and prominent spines along the edges. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable member of the Asteraceae family.
