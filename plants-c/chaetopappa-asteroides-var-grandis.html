Chaetopappa asteroides var. grandis

Chaetopappa asteroides var. grandis, commonly known as the giant dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that aids in wind pollination. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and herbal medicine.
