Chamaecrista tocantinensis

Chamaecrista tocantinensis, commonly known as the Tocantins burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
