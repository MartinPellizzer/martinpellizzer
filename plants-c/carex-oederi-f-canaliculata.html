Carex oederi f. canaliculata

Carex oederi f. canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in a dense, cylindrical structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
