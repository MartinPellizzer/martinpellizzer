Ceropegia aristolochioides subsp. albertina

Ceropegia aristolochioides subsp.

albertina, commonly known as the Albertina ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers that resemble small horns, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators, such as certain moths. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.
