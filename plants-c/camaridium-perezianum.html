Camaridium perezianum

Camaridium perezianum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Camaridium perezianum is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
