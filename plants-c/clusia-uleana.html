Clusia uleana

Clusia uleana, commonly known as the "false arum," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Clusia uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
