Celtis roxburghii

Celtis roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that attract various wildlife. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Celtis roxburghii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health properties.
