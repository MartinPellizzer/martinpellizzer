Cirsium virginianum var. filipendulum

Cirsium virginianum var. filipendulum, commonly known as the Virgin's plume, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely hairy stems and large, showy purple to pink flower heads that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
