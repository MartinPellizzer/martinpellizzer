Campanula hemschinica var. dasycarpa

Campanula hemschinica var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the Hemschich campanula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
