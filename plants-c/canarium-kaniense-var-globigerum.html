Canarium kaniense var. globigerum

Canarium kaniense var. globigerum, commonly known as the globigerous candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are valued for their high oil content, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
