Clematis tibetana var. pamiralaica

Clematis tibetana var. pamiralaica, commonly known as the Pamir Clematis, is a rare and woody vine native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in cool, mountainous environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a hairy underside.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
