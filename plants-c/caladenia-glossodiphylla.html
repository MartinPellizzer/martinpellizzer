Caladenia glossodiphylla

Caladenia glossodiphylla, commonly known as the glossy-lip spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and two to three hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive glossy, pinkish-red lip and a pale green or yellowish upper sepal. It blooms during the autumn season, typically between April and June.

This species is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its intricate and often colorful floral structures adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.
