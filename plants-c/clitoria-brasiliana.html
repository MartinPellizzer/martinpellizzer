Clitoria brasiliana

Clitoria brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian winged bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, violet-blue flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated as both an ornamental and a food plant.
