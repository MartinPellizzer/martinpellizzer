Carex paradoxa subsp. teretiuscula

Carex paradoxa subsp.

teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized flowering structures called spikes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
