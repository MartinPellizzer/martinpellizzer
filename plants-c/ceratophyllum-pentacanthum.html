Ceratophyllum pentacanthum

Ceratophyllum pentacanthum, commonly known as the five-armed horned water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has whorled, strap-like structures that perform photosynthesis. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Ceratophyllum pentacanthum is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
