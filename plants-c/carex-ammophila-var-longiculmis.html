Carex ammophila var. longiculmis

Carex ammophila var. longiculmis, commonly known as the long-beak sedge, is a perennial plant native to coastal and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sand dunes through its extensive root system.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration in coastal areas.
