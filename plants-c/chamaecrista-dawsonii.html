Chamaecrista dawsonii

Chamaecrista dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring, followed by the development of hairy, spiny seed pods.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization.
