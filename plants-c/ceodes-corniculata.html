Ceodes corniculata

Ceodes corniculata, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive seed pods that resemble horns, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ceodes corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beautiful blooms.
