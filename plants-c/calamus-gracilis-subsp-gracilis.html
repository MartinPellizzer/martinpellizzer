Calamus gracilis subsp. gracilis

Calamus gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its slender, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, followed by dark, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
