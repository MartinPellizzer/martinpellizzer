Calea fluviatilis

Calea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river calea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are often white or pale blue, arranged in a rounded cluster.

Calea fluviatilis is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contributes to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
