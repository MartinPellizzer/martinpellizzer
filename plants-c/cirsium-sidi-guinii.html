Cirsium sidi-guinii

Cirsium sidi-guinii, commonly known as the Moroccan thistle, is a perennial plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptation to harsh climates. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
