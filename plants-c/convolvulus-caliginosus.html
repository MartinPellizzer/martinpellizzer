Convolvulus caliginosus

Convolvulus caliginosus, commonly known as the cloud convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
