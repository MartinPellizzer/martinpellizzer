Cestrum guaraniticum f. latifolia

Cestrum guaraniticum f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and the plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree.
