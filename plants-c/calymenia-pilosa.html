Calymenia pilosa

Calymenia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy calymenia, is a unique plant belonging to the family Calymeniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, club-shaped sporophyte with a rough, hairy surface. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the sporangia located on the upper surface of the sporophyte.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Calymenia pilosa is considered an uncommon and ecologically significant species.
