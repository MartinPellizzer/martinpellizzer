Calandrinia gayana

Calandrinia gayana, commonly known as the pink fairy fanflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small fans. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh climates.

Calandrinia gayana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
