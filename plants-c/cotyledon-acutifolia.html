Cotyledon acutifolia

Cotyledon acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leafed cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, pointed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Cotyledon acutifolia is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
