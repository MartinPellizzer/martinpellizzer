Cheiranthus × marshallii

Cheiranthus × marshallii, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cheiranthus cheiri and Cheiranthus gardneri, characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Cheiranthus × marshallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
