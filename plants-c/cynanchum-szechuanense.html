Cynanchum szechuanense

Cynanchum szechuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
