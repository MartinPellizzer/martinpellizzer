Cyperus baronii

Cyperus baronii, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its ability to thrive in wet or aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters.

Cyperus baronii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to waterlogged soils.
