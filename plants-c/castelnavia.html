Castelnavia

Castelnavia, commonly known as the "Mexican morning glory," is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Castelnavia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
