Cienfuegosia subprostrata var. vera

Cienfuegosia subprostrata var. vera, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically yellow or orange, providing a striking visual display during the flowering season.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
