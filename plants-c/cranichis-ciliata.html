Cranichis ciliata

Cranichis ciliata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine, hair-like structures, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cranichis ciliata is a relatively rare species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
