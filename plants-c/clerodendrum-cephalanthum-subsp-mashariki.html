Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp. mashariki

Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp.

mashariki, commonly known as the East African glorybower, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators like birds and bees.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable regions.
