Calcearia ecarinata

Calcearia ecarinata, commonly known as the horned toad plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small horns. The plant produces showy, violet to purple blossoms that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Calcearia ecarinata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
