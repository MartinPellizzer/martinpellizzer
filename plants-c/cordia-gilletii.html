Cordia gilletii

Cordia gilletii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Cordia gilletii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
