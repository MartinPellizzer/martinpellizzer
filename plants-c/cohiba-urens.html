Cohiba urens

Cohiba urens, commonly known as the "smoke plant," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers. The plant produces a resinous substance that has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cohiba urens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
