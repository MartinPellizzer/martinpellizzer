Carex brasiliensis f. robusta

Carex brasiliensis f. robusta, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
