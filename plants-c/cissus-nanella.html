Cissus nanella

Cissus nanella, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus nanella is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
