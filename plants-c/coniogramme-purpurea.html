Coniogramme purpurea

Coniogramme purpurea, commonly known as the purple fern, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, lacy fronds that turn a vibrant purple in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it ideal for woodland gardens. It is a perennial that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense clumps over time.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for adding texture and color to shaded landscapes.
