Campanula podanthoides

Campanula podanthoides, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula podanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
