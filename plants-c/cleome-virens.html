Cleome virens

Cleome virens, commonly known as the green spiderflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that resemble spider legs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome virens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
