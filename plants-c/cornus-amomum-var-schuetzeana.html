Cornus amomum var. schuetzeana

Cornus amomum var. schuetzeana, commonly known as Schuetze's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and whitish beneath, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
