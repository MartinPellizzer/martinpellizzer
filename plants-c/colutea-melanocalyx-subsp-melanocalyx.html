Colutea melanocalyx subsp. melanocalyx

Colutea melanocalyx subsp.

melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leafed broom, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with soft, glandular hairs and trifoliate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that matures to a dark purple or black color, giving the species its common name.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
