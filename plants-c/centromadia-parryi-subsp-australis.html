Centromadia parryi subsp. australis

Centromadia parryi subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian centromadia, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands.

The plant features small, linear leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
