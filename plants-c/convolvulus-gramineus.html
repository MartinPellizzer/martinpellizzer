Convolvulus gramineus

Convolvulus gramineus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
