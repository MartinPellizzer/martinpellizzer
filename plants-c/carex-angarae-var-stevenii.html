Carex angarae var. stevenii

Carex angarae var. stevenii, commonly known as the Angara sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex angarae var. stevenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
