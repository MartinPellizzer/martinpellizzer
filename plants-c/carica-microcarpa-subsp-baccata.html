Carica microcarpa subsp. baccata

Carica microcarpa subsp.

baccata, commonly known as the dwarf papaya, is a small tropical shrub native to Central America. It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically less than 5 centimeters in diameter.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It flowers with unisexual, greenish-yellow flowers, and the fruit contains a single large seed.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
