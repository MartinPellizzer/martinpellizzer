Cynometra plurijuga

Cynometra plurijuga, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its seeds are used to produce shea butter, a valuable natural product in cosmetics and food industries.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and soil stabilization in its native range.
