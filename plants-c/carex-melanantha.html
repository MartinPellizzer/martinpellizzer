Carex melanantha

Carex melanantha, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green color that turns reddish-brown in the fall.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.
