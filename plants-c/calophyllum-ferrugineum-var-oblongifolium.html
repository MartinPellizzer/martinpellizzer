Calophyllum ferrugineum var. oblongifolium

Calophyllum ferrugineum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the rusty calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lanceolate to oblong leaves that are often slightly hairy beneath. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
