Coelocarpum africanum

Coelocarpum africanum, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight aromatic scent.

Coelocarpum africanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
