Chusquea tomentosa

Chusquea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly weeping bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its distinctive, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by feathery bracts, which are often more showy than the actual blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its unique texture and form.

Chusquea tomentosa is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various small animals and insects.
