Cerasus sinensis

Cerasus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cerasus sinensis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and seasonal blooming.
