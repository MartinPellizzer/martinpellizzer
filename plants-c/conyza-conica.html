Conyza conica

Conyza conica, commonly known as the Common Ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features a tall, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in a spherical head, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine pubescence.

Conyza conica is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
