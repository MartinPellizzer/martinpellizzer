Crataegus ruthiana

Crataegus ruthiana, commonly known as the Ruth's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. This deciduous shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a red or orange-pink berry, which is edible but often has a tart flavor.
