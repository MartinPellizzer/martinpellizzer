Crataegus uniflora var. florida

Crataegus uniflora var. florida, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The Florida hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
