Cheilanthes argyrophylla

Cheilanthes argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf rock fern, is a perennial fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its silvery, scale-like leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant thrives in rocky soils and can often be found growing on cliffs or in crevices. It is a drought-tolerant species, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
