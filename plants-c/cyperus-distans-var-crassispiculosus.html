Cyperus distans var. crassispiculosus

Cyperus distans var. crassispiculosus, commonly known as the broad-bracted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
