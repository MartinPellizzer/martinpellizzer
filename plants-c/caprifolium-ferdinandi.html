Caprifolium ferdinandi

Caprifolium ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and wildlife support purposes.
