Coffea magnistipula

Coffea magnistipula, commonly known as the large-stippled coffee plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with prominent stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. It prefers tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and coffee enthusiasts alike.
