Cissus elongata var. linearifolia

Cissus elongata var. linearifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf grape ivy, is a trailing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are often described as thread-like in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens.
