Critonia sexangularis

Critonia sexangularis, commonly known as the six-sided critonia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rare occurrence and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
