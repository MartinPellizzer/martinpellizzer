Crataegus claytoniana

Crataegus claytoniana, commonly known as Clayton's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorny branches with oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

Crataegus claytoniana is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
