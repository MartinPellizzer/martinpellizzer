Convolvulus zargarianus

Convolvulus zargarianus, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, climbing stems that produce tendrils for support. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
