Carduus assoi subsp. hispanica

Carduus assoi subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
