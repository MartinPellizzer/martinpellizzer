Callicarpa brevipes var. glabrescens

Callicarpa brevipes var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by clusters of bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically smooth and ovate, with a glossy appearance. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a host plant for several butterfly species.
