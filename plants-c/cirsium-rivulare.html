Cirsium rivulare

Cirsium rivulare, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and it has a robust root system.

Cirsium rivulare is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
