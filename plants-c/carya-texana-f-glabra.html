Carya texana f. glabra

Carya texana f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by hard, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

The smooth hickory is valued for its durable wood and is also a food source for various wildlife species.
