Chironia suffruticosa

Chironia suffruticosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree with woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Chironia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
