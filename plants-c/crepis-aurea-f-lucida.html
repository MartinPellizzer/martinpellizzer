Crepis aurea f. lucida

Crepis aurea f. lucida, commonly known as the shining yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, flat clusters. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and stems that are often reddish in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
