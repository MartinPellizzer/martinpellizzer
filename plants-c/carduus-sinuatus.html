Carduus sinuatus

Carduus sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuate Thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The Sinuate Thistle is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
