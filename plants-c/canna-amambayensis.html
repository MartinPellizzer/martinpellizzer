Canna amambayensis

Canna amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay canna, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, and showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by striking inflorescences.

Canna amambayensis is adapted to wetland and marshy environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and abundant water.
