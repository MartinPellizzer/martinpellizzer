Chamaecrista arduinervis

Chamaecrista arduinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
