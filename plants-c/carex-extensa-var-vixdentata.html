Carex extensa var. vixdentata

Carex extensa var. vixdentata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in wetland or moist soil environments, thriving in partial to full shade.
