Cardamine litoralis

Cardamine litoralis, commonly known as seaside watercress, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and tidal zones. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. It is often found in brackish or saltwater environments, showcasing its adaptability to saline conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for coastal ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
