Cynanchum henryi

Cynanchum henryi, commonly known as Chinese milkweed or Henry's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its latex sap is milky and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
