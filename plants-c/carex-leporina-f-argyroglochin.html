Carex leporina f. argyroglochin

Carex leporina f. argyroglochin, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its distinctive silver-gray sheaths on the stems make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
