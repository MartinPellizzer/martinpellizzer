Chenopodium ambrosioides

Chenopodium ambrosioides, commonly known as wormseed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a fleshy, seed-coated structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium ambrosioides is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural compounds.
