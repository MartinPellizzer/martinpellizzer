Carex × hageri

Carex × hageri, commonly known as the Hager's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it flowers in early summer. Carex × hageri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
