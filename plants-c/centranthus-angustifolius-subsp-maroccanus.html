Centranthus angustifolius subsp. maroccanus

Centranthus angustifolius subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan narrow-leaved gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
