Calamintha hungarica var. cuneata

Calamintha hungarica var. cuneata, commonly known as Hungarian calamint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features square stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
