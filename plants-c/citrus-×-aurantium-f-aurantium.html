Citrus × aurantium f. aurantium

Citrus × aurantium f. aurantium, commonly known as the common orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the intersection of citrus sinensis and citrus reticulata.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented, orange-colored rind and juicy, segmented fruit filled with aromatic pulp. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.

The fruit's essential oils and zest are also valued in the fragrance and food industries.
