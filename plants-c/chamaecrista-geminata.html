Chamaecrista geminata

Chamaecrista geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamaecrista geminata is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
