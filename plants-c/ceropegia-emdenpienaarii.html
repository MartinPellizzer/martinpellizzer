Ceropegia emdenpienaarii

Ceropegia emdenpienaarii, commonly known as the Emdenpie naarii moonflower, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
