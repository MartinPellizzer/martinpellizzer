Centaurea greuteri

Centaurea greuteri, commonly known as the Greuter's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Centaurea greuteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
