Cerasus serrulata f. hisakura

Cerasus serrulata f. hisakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or yaezakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, contributing to its ornamental value.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color.
