Caryophyllus pimenta

Caryophyllus pimenta, commonly known as the pimento pepper or allspice pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are harvested and dried to yield the spice known as allspice. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Caryophyllus pimenta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
