Carex turczaninowiana

Carex turczaninowiana, commonly known as the Turczaninow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Carex turczaninowiana is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
