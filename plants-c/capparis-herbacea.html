Capparis herbacea

Capparis herbacea, commonly known as the slender caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a slightly succulent texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
