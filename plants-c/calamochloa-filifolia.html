Calamochloa filifolia

Calamochloa filifolia, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

Calamochloa filifolia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
