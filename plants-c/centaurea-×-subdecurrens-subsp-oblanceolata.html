Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp. oblanceolata

Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp.

oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cornflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bright blue to purple composite flowers.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
