Chrysophyllum cinereum

Chrysophyllum cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the edible, creamy fruit with a complex flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritious fruit.
