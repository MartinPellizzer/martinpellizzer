Casearia tardieuae

Casearia tardieuae, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright blue, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Casearia tardieuae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
