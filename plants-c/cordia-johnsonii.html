Cordia johnsonii

Cordia johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It typically grows to a height of about 6 meters and has smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Cordia johnsonii is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and wind.
