Commelina bambusifolia

Commelina bambusifolia, commonly known as bamboo leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and resemble bamboo leaves, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
