Chusquea liebmannii

Chusquea liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
