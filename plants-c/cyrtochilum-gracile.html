Cyrtochilum gracile

Cyrtochilum gracile, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often found in cloud forests. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky soil.

Cyrtochilum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
