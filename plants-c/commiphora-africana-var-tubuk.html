Commiphora africana var. tubuk

Commiphora africana var. tubuk, commonly known as African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are opposite and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
