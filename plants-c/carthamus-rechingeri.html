Carthamus rechingeri

Carthamus rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with linear, slightly hairy leaves. Its flowers consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets, which are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant produces seeds that are similar in appearance to those of other safflower species, making it identifiable through its botanical features.
