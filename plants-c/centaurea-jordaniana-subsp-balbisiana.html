Centaurea jordaniana subsp. balbisiana

Centaurea jordaniana subsp.

balbisiana, commonly known as Jordanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
