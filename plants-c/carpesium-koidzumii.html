Carpesium koidzumii

Carpesium koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features spiny, hairy stems and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering heads are surrounded by bracts that resemble thistle-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carpesium koidzumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
