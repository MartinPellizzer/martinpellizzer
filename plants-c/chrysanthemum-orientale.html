Chrysanthemum orientale

Chrysanthemum orientale, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 150 centimeters, with sturdy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional medicine.
