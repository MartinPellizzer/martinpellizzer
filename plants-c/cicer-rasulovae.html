Cicer rasulovae

Cicer rasulovae, commonly known as the Rasulov's vetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for biodiversity conservation.
