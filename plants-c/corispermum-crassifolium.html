Corispermum crassifolium

Corispermum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pale flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry environments. Its leaves are thick and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Corispermum crassifolium is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
