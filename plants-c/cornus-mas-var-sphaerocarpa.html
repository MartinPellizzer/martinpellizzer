Cornus mas var. sphaerocarpa

Cornus mas var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical fruit clusters, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, followed by the showy, round berries that attract birds. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
