Cheiranthus pygmaeus

Cheiranthus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf crownflower, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, often in regions with mild, Mediterranean climates.
