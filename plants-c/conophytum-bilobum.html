Conophytum bilobum

Conophytum bilobum, commonly known as the two-lobed rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Conophytum bilobum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
