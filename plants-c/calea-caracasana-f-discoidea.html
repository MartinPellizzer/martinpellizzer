Calea caracasana f. discoidea

Calea caracasana f. discoidea, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a faint, pleasant fragrance. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its unique floral display. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance and is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
