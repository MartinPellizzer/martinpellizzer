Cassia chrysotingens var. obtusata

Cassia chrysotingens var. obtusata, commonly known as golden flame cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, glossy leaflets and clusters of bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscaping efforts.
