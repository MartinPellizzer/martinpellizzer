Clematis villosa subsp. kirkii

Clematis villosa subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
