Campanula nakaoi

Campanula nakaoi, commonly known as the Nakao's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula nakaoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
