Clavija lancifolia subsp. lancifolia

Clavija lancifolia subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clavija, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
