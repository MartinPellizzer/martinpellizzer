Cordylanthus bicolor

Cordylanthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking two-toned flowers, which range from white to pink, and its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.

Cordylanthus bicolor is often found in coastal and inland habitats, including grasslands and chaparral.
