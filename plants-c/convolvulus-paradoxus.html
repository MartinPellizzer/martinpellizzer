Convolvulus paradoxus

Convolvulus paradoxus, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a sprawling annual herb with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its seeds are encased in a tough, spiny capsule that aids in dispersal by attachment to animal fur or clothing.

Convolvulus paradoxus is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water.
