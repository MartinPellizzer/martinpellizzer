Cora weddelliana

Cora weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's cora, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange. The plant produces a rosette of narrow, strap-like leaves that help collect moisture and nutrients from the air. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of the canopy.

Cora weddelliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
