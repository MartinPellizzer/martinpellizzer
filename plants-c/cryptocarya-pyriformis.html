Cryptocarya pyriformis

Cryptocarya pyriformis, commonly known as the oval-leaved cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It produces dark blue, waxy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cryptocarya pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
