Cymbidium ramosissimum

Cymbidium ramosissimum, commonly known as the Chinese orchid or the ramose cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces linear, leathery leaves that are often dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cymbidium ramosissimum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor and garden displays.
