Carduus macrocephalus subsp. scabrisquamus

Carduus macrocephalus subsp.

scabrisquamus, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, globe-shaped composite flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are generally rough and hairy, with a distinctive scabrous texture. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
