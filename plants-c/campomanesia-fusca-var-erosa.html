Campomanesia fusca var. erosa

Campomanesia fusca var. erosa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or erosa campomanesia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, white flowers are followed by edible, orange-red berries that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and musical instruments.
