Calliandra perrieri

Calliandra perrieri, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow into a shrub or small tree.

Calliandra perrieri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
