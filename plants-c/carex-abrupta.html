Carex abrupta

Carex abrupta, commonly known as the abrupt sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex abrupta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
