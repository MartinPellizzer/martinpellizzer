Cardamine flexuosa subsp. fallax

Cardamine flexuosa subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in terminal racemes, and it produces slender, ribbed fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
