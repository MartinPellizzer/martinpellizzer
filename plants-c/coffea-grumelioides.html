Coffea grumelioides

Coffea grumelioides, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee tree, is a species native to Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 4 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing the seeds used for coffee. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and coffee enthusiasts alike.
