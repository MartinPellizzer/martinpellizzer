Centaurea boissieri subsp. willkommii

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

willkommii, commonly known as the Willkommi's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It has lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and produces composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
