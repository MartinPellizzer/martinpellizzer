Chaenomeles japonica var. fastigiata

Chaenomeles japonica var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its ornamental value lies in its showy blooms and attractive, peeling bark that becomes more colorful with age.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
