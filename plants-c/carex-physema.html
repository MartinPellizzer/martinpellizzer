Carex physema

Carex physema, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Carex physema is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
