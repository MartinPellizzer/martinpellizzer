Cyperus pachyrhizus

Cyperus pachyrhizus, commonly known as the broad-rooted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus pachyrhizus is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has also been studied for its potential ecological roles in wetland systems.
