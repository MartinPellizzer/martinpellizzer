Cordia guineensis subsp. mutica

Cordia guineensis subsp.

mutica, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, acidic flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments.
