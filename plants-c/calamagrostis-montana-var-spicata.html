Calamagrostis montana var. spicata

Calamagrostis montana var. spicata, commonly known as the mountain reed or spicata reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.

Its resilience and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat.
