Cousinia fragillima

Cousinia fragillima, commonly known as the fragile cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Cousinia fragillima is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
