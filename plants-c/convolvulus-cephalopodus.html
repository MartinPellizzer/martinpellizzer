Convolvulus cephalopodus

Convolvulus cephalopodus, commonly known as the helmeted bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which are typical features of many species in its genus. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Convolvulus cephalopodus is often found in rocky areas and along coastal regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
