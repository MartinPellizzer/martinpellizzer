Clerodendrum glabrum var. ovale

Clerodendrum glabrum var. ovale, commonly known as the oval-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
