Calycothrix squarrosa

Calycothrix squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and leaves that are covered in dense, white, cotton-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Calycothrix squarrosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
