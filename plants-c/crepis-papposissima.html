Crepis papposissima

Crepis papposissima, commonly known as the hairy thistle or slender goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a basal rosette structure, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a head.

The plant produces achenes with pappus hairs, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.
