Carex salina var. flavicans

Carex salina var. flavicans, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically green to yellowish in color. This plant thrives in saline environments, such as tidal flats and salt marshes, and is adapted to withstand periodic flooding. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, green to yellowish flowers.

Carex salina var. flavicans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
