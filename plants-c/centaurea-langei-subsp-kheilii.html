Centaurea langei subsp. kheilii

Centaurea langei subsp.

kheilii, commonly known as the Kheil's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple to pinkish flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
