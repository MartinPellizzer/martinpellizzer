Chalcas paniculata var. omphalocarpa

Chalcas paniculata var. omphalocarpa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
