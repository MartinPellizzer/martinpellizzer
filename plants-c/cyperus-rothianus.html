Cyperus rothianus

Cyperus rothianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies.

Cyperus rothianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
