Campnosperma coriaceum

Campnosperma coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in landscaping and for its ornamental appeal.
