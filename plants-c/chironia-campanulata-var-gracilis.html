Chironia campanulata var. gracilis

Chironia campanulata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
