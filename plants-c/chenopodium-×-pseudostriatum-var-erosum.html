Chenopodium × pseudostriatum var. erosum

Chenopodium × pseudostriatum var. erosum, commonly known as red sandreed, is a hybrid plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and vibrant red to purple stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant in dryland environments.
