Cyperus thomasii

Cyperus thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus thomasii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
