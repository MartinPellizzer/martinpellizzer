Cleomella

Cleomella, commonly known as the spider flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cleomaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. They are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers that resemble spiders, hence their common name. Cleomella species typically have alternate, simple leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
