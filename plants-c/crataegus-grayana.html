Crataegus grayana

Crataegus grayana, commonly known as Gray's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Crataegus grayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
