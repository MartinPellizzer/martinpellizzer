Cattleya × parthenia

Cattleya × parthenia, commonly known as the Parthenia Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that results from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossieri.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a vibrant mix of pink, white, and purple hues. It produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat.

Cattleya × parthenia is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
