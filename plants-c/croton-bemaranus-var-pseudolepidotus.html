Croton bemaranus var. pseudolepidotus

Croton bemaranus var. pseudolepidotus, commonly known as the Bemaranus croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
