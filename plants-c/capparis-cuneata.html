Capparis cuneata

Capparis cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by green, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Capparis cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
