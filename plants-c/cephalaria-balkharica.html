Cephalaria balkharica

Cephalaria balkharica, commonly known as the Balkh harebell, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head at the tip of each stem.

Cephalaria balkharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
