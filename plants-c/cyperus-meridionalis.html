Cyperus meridionalis

Cyperus meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern papyrus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown. The plant is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soil conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid environments.

Cyperus meridionalis is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of light conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
