Coriaria duthiei

Coriaria duthiei, commonly known as the dwarf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, woolly leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
