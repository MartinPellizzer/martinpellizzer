Cirsium hookerianum var. scariosum

Cirsium hookerianum var. scariosum, commonly known as Hooker's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, covered in fine hairs, and have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. This variety is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
