Colobandra lanata

Colobandra lanata, commonly known as the woolly colobandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Colobandra lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
