Crocus biflorus subsp. pseudonubigena

Crocus biflorus subsp.

pseudonubigena, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
