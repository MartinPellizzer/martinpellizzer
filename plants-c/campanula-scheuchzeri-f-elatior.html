Campanula scheuchzeri f. elatior

Campanula scheuchzeri f. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a striking plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
