Coleus lebrunii

Coleus lebrunii, commonly known as the Lebrun's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture and a wide range of hues including red, purple, and green. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
