Cousinia sven-hediniana

Cousinia sven-hediniana, commonly known as the Tibetan hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a robust, hairy stem and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Cousinia sven-hediniana is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
