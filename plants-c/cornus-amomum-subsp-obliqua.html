Cornus amomum subsp. obliqua

Cornus amomum subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking red stems that become prominent in the winter, providing seasonal interest.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
