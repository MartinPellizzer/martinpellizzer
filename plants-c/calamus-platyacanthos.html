Calamus platyacanthos

Calamus platyacanthos, commonly known as the flat-spined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
