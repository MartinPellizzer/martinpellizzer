Calamagrostis americana

Calamagrostis americana, commonly known as American switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis americana is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
