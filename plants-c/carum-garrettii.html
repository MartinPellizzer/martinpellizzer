Carum garrettii

Carum garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent when crushed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
