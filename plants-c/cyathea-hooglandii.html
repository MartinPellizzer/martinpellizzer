Cyathea hooglandii

Cyathea hooglandii, commonly known as the Hochland's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can reach several meters in length.

Cyathea hooglandii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
