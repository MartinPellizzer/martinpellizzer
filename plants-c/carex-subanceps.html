Carex subanceps

Carex subanceps, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Carex subanceps is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
