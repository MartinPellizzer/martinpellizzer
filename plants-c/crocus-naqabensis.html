Crocus naqabensis

Crocus naqabensis, commonly known as the Naqab crocus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant, often yellow or orange, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. It typically blooms in early spring, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
