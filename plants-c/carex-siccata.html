Carex siccata

Carex siccata, commonly known as the dry sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Carex siccata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
