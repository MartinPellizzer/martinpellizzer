Cassipourea caesia

Cassipourea caesia, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Cassipourea caesia is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
