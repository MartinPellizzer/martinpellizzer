Crucifera crepidifolia

Crucifera crepidifolia, commonly known as the leafy crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the cruciferous family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
