Coriaria myrtifolia

Coriaria myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved coriaria, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
