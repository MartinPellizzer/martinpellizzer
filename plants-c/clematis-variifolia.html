Clematis variifolia

Clematis variifolia, commonly known as the variable clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
