Celtis guangxiensis

Celtis guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi hawthorn, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Celtis guangxiensis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
