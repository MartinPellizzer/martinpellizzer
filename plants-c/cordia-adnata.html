Cordia adnata

Cordia adnata, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
