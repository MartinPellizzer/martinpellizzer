Convolvulus roseus

Convolvulus roseus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it both ornamental and somewhat invasive in certain regions.
