Carex tetanica f. woodii

Carex tetanica f. woodii, commonly known as the Wood's tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are generally narrow and have a distinct, fibrous texture.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.
