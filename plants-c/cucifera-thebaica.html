Cucifera thebaica

Cucifera thebaica, commonly known as the Egyptian cucumber, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its tendrils, heart-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers that develop into elongated, edible fruits. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are typically harvested when they reach a mature green color, though some varieties may turn orange or red.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.
