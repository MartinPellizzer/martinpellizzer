Clematis thunbergii

Clematis thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to ascend supports, and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
