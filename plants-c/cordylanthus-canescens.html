Cordylanthus canescens

Cordylanthus canescens, commonly known as the hairy cordylanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cordylanthus canescens is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
