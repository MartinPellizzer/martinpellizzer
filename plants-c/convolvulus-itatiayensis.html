Convolvulus itatiayensis

Convolvulus itatiayensis, commonly known as the Itatiaya bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant has climbing stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
