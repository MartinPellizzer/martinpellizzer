Campanula omeiensis

Campanula omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces showy clusters of flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula omeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
