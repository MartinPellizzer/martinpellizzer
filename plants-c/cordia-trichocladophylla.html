Cordia trichocladophylla

Cordia trichocladophylla, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
