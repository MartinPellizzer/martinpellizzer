Citrus sabon f. soyu

Citrus sabon f. soyu, commonly known as the soyu citrus or soyu lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between the kumquat and the mandarin.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a thin, slightly bitter peel. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the unique flavor of its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The fruit's high vitamin C content makes it a popular choice in traditional medicine and health foods.
