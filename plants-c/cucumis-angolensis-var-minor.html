Cucumis angolensis var. minor

Cucumis angolensis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf African cucumber, is a herbaceous vine native to southern Africa.

It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, cucumber-like flavor. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and potential use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
