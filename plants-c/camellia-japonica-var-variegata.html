Camellia japonica var. variegata

Camellia japonica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese camellia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are broad and glossy, often with a distinctive variegation pattern of white or cream markings. This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its winter blooming habit, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
