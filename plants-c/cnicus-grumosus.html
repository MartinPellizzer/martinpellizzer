Cnicus grumosus

Cnicus grumosus, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy, or gravelly soils. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with numerous branches and small, yellowish-green leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Cnicus grumosus is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
