Cupressus gigantea

Cupressus gigantea, commonly known as the giant cypress, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, scale-like leaves, and distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated environments.
