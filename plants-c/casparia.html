Casparia

Casparia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of approximately 100 species, predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plants typically have basal leaves that are alternate or opposite, with lanceolate or ovate shapes. Their flowers are composite, forming a head of small florets surrounded by bracts.

Casparia species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
