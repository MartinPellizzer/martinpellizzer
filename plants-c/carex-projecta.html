Carex projecta

Carex projecta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a spike-like structure, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Carex projecta plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
