Carex tosaensis

Carex tosaensis, commonly known as Tosa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green color.

Carex tosaensis produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
