Cucumis cognata

Cucumis cognata, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
