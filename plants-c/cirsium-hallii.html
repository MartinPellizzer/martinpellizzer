Cirsium hallii

Cirsium hallii, commonly known as Hall's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its distinctive, prickly leaves.

Cirsium hallii is a hardy species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
