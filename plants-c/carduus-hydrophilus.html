Carduus hydrophilus

Carduus hydrophilus, commonly known as water thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
