Campanopsis patula

Campanopsis patula, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Campanopsis patula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
