Cotoneaster kaganensis

Cotoneaster kaganensis, commonly known as the Kagan cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance. Cotoneaster kaganensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
