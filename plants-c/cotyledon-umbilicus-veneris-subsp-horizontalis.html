Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris subsp. horizontalis

Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris subsp.

horizontalis, commonly known as the "Cotyledon of Venus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy texture.

The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water in arid environments. It produces small, pale pink or white flowers during the spring or summer.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
