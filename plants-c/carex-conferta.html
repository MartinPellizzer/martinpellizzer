Carex conferta

Carex conferta, commonly known as the dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which contains small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it often grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
