Celtis soyauxii

Celtis soyauxii, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by green, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. Celtis soyauxii is adapted to various soil types and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

It has ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
