Convolvulus arvensis var. erectus

Convolvulus arvensis var. erectus, commonly known as the upright field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
