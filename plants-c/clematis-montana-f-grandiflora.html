Clematis montana f. grandiflora

Clematis montana f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered montana clematis, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, palmately arranged leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shrub borders.
