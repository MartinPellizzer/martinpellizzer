Carduus moschatus

Carduus moschatus, commonly known as the musk thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and tall, purple to reddish flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white to grayish seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

The plant has a strong, musky scent, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
