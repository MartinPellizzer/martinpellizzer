Croton mocquerysii subsp. meridionalis

Croton mocquerysii subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant green or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
