Colobanthus saginoides

Colobanthus saginoides, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the high Arctic and Antarctic regions.

It is a small, mat-forming plant with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is adapted to survive in extreme cold and has a shallow root system to anchor itself in rocky or soil-covered terrain.

Colobanthus saginoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in polar ecosystems.
