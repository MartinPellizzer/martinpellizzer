Crocodilium aureum

Crocodilium aureum, commonly known as the golden crocodile plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that resemble the scales of a crocodile, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a splash of color to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
