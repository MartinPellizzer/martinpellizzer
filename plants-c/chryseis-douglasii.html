Chryseis douglasii

Chryseis douglasii, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Chryseis douglasii is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to bloom in early summer.
