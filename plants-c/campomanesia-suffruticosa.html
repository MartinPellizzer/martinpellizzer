Campomanesia suffruticosa

Campomanesia suffruticosa, commonly known as pitaya doce or sweet pitaya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a sweet, tangy flavor. These berries are rich in nutrients, including vitamin C and antioxidants, making them valuable both nutritionally and commercially.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit in parts of South America.
