Carex rosea var. retroflexa

Carex rosea var. retroflexa, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of pale pink to white flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
