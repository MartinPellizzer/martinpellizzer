Clematis smilacifolia subsp. andamanica

Clematis smilacifolia subsp.

andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands. It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, wiry stems and large, showy flowers.

The flowers are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The leaves are compound, consisting of three leaflets, and are alternate along the stem.

This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
