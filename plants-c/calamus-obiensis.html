Calamus obiensis

Calamus obiensis, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnate leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Its rhizomes are harvested for their aromatic compounds, which contribute to its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Calamus obiensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing soil and providing shelter for various wildlife species.
