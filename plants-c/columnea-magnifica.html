Columnea magnifica

Columnea magnifica, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a striking yellow throat. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Columnea magnifica is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb supports in garden settings.
