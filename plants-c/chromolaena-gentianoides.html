Chromolaena gentianoides

Chromolaena gentianoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Chromolaena gentianoides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
