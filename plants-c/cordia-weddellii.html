Cordia weddellii

Cordia weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's cordia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
