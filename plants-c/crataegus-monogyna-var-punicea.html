Crataegus monogyna var. punicea

Crataegus monogyna var. punicea, commonly known as the red thornapple or red hawthorn, is a variety of hawthorn native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and hedgerows.
