Conanthera alba

Conanthera alba, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinct conical shape with reflexed petals and a prominent central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Conanthera alba is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
