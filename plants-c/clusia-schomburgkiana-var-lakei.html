Clusia schomburgkiana var. lakei

Clusia schomburgkiana var. lakei, commonly known as the Lakei glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its resilience to urban environments.
