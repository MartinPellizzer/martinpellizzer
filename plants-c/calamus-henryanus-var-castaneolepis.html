Calamus henryanus var. castaneolepis

Calamus henryanus var. castaneolepis, commonly known as the red-scaled calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented rhizomes and long, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a reddish-brown or chestnut-colored sheath. The plant produces small, fragrant inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by triangular, pale brown fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
