Campanula × racemosa

Campanula × racemosa, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Campanula × racemosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
