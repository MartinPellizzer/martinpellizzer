Calamagrostis vicunarum var. humilior

Calamagrostis vicunarum var. humilior, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
