Campylotropis capillipes subsp. capillipes

Campylotropis capillipes subsp.

capillipes, commonly known as the slender cowpea or narrow-leaved cowpea, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Asia. It features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with finely serrated margins.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
