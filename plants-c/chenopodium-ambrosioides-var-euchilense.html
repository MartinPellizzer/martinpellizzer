Chenopodium ambrosioides var. euchilense

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. euchilense, commonly known as Mexican goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, smooth shell. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its adaptability, it can be both a weed and a forage plant in certain regions.
