Carallia

Carallia, commonly known as the "Indian currant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Violaceae.

It consists of about 15 species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants are shrubs or small trees, characterized by their opposite, simple leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The fruits are small, fleshy berries that often contain several seeds.

Botanically, Carallia species are notable for their medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.
