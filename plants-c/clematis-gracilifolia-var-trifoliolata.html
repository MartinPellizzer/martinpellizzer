Clematis gracilifolia var. trifoliolata

Clematis gracilifolia var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to significant heights, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
