Cissus rhodesiae

Cissus rhodesiae, commonly known as the Rhodesian devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often spiny stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus rhodesiae is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
