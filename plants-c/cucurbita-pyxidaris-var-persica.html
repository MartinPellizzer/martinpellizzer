Cucurbita pyxidaris var. persica

Cucurbita pyxidaris var. persica, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a vine species characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

Its leaves are broadly ovate with a serrated margin, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruiting ones. The fruit is a small, elongated cucumber with a slightly ribbed surface and a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is relatively easy to grow in home gardens.
