Cornus mas f. macrocarpa

Cornus mas f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus mas species, which is native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring, followed by large, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and jellies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
