Carex ustulata var. coriophora

Carex ustulata var. coriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet to moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
