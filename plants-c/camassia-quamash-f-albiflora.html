Camassia quamash f. albiflora

Camassia quamash f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered camas, is a variant of the camas plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features grass-like, bluish-green leaves and produces bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
