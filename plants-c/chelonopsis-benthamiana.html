Chelonopsis benthamiana

Chelonopsis benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
