Carex polygyna

Carex polygyna, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. This plant is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Carex polygyna has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
