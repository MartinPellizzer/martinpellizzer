Campomanesia rabeniana

Campomanesia rabeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a yellow-orange berry with a waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
