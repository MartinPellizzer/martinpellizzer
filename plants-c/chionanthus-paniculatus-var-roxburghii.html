Chionanthus paniculatus var. roxburghii

Chionanthus paniculatus var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
