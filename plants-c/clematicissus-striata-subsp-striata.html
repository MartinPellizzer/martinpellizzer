Clematicissus striata subsp. striata

Clematicissus striata subsp.

striata, commonly known as the striated clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of East Asia. It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often twining around supports.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive striated pattern along the veins.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.
