Chenopodium album var. leptophyllum

Chenopodium album var. leptophyllum, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, fleshy stems, and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are succulent, often with a slight tangy flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

This plant is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of omega-3 fatty acids and vitamins.
