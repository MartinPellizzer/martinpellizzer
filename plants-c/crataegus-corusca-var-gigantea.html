Crataegus corusca var. gigantea

Crataegus corusca var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, thorned branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
