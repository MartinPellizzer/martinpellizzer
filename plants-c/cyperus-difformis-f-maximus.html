Cyperus difformis f. maximus

Cyperus difformis f. maximus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems and broad, flat leaves that are often ribbed and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
