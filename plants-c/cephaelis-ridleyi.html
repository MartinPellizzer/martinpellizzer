Cephaelis ridleyi

Cephaelis ridleyi, commonly known as the pink-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

Cephaelis ridleyi is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
