Convolvulus namaquensis

Convolvulus namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Its fibrous roots help it survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
