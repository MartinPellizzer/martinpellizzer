Centaurea malinvaldiana

Centaurea malinvaldiana, commonly known as the Malinvaldian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its slender, branched stems and narrow leaves are adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
