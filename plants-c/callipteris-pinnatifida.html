Callipteris pinnatifida

Callipteris pinnatifida, commonly known as the Florida panicum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a feather-like pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Callipteris pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
