Camellia japonica var. haylockii

Camellia japonica var. haylockii, commonly known as the Haylock's Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from deep red to pale pink, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the yellow stamens. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
