Cnemidaria cruciata

Cnemidaria cruciata, commonly known as the cross-veined grasshopper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped venation on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cnemidaria cruciata is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in environments with well-drained soil.
