Cytisus burgalensis

Cytisus burgalensis, commonly known as the Burgalensis broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus burgalensis is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
