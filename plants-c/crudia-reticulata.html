Crudia reticulata

Crudia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct net-like venation pattern, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
