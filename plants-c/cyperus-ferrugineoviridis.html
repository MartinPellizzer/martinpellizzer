Cyperus ferrugineoviridis

Cyperus ferrugineoviridis, commonly known as the rusty-green flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often tinged with rust-colored markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Cyperus ferrugineoviridis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
