Cereus maelenii

Cereus maelenii, commonly known as the Easter Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, branching stems and clusters of vibrant, pink or red flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and bloom in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens or indoor spaces.

Cereus maelenii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
