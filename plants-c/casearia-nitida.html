Casearia nitida

Casearia nitida, commonly known as the white casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
