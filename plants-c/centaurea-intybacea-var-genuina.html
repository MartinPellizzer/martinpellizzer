Centaurea intybacea var. genuina

Centaurea intybacea var. genuina, commonly known as the Armenian crownflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bright blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
