Calliandra haematomma var. tortuensis

Calliandra haematomma var. tortuensis, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like, trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright red, pea-like flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires regular watering during the growing season.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
