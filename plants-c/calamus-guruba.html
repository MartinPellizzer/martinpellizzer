Calamus guruba

Calamus guruba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus guruba produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in various traditional applications.
