Clematis fusca var. mandshurica

Clematis fusca var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for climbing structures or trellises.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
