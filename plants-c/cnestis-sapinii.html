Cnestis sapinii

Cnestis sapinii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to various regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the rainy season. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Cnestis sapinii plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for certain insects and animals.
