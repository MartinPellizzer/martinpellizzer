Catasetum lehmannii

Catasetum lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with specialized pollination mechanisms, including a pollinia that adheres to the body of pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
