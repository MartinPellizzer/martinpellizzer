Croton pluriglandulosus

Croton pluriglandulosus, commonly known as the many-gland croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its growth habit includes both woody and herbaceous parts, and it is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
