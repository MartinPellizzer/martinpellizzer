Croton stenotrichus

Croton stenotrichus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green veins and bright yellow or orange margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
