Cajanus cinereus

Cajanus cinereus, commonly known as the black gram or urad, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is well-adapted to warm climates and requires a long growing season. Cajanus cinereus is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and mineral content.

It plays an important role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
