Casuarina verticillata

Casuarina verticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf she-oak, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with dense, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. Casuarina verticillata is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
