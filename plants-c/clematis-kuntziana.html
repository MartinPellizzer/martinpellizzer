Clematis kuntziana

Clematis kuntziana, commonly known as the Kuntziana Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, allowing it to climb trees or trellises. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Clematis kuntziana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
