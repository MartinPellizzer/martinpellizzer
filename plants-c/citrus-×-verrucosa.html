Citrus × verrucosa

Citrus × verrucosa, commonly known as the kumquat.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits. The fruit has a thin, edible peel and a sweet, segmented pulp, making it popular in both culinary and ornamental contexts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with showy white flowers that appear in the spring.
