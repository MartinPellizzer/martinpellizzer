Cyrilla spinosa

Cyrilla spinosa, commonly known as the prickly cyrilla, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of swamps. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Cyrilla spinosa is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas.
