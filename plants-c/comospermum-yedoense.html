Comospermum yedoense

Comospermum yedoense, commonly known as the Japanese starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant's fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
