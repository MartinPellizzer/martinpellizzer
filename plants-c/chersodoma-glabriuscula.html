Chersodoma glabriuscula

Chersodoma glabriuscula, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Chersodoma glabriuscula is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
