Carduus × nutantiformis subsp. lesurinus

Carduus × nutantiformis subsp.

lesurinus, commonly known as the Lesurin thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and can be found in various regions across Europe.
