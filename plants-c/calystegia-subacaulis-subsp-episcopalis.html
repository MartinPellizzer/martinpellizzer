Calystegia subacaulis subsp. episcopalis

Calystegia subacaulis subsp.

episcopalis, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and ditches.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
