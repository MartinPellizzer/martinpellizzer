Cyperus conjunctus

Cyperus conjunctus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, with a distinctive flat, ribbon-like appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Cyperus conjunctus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
