Calceolaria incarum subsp. incarum

Calceolaria incarum subsp.

incarum, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, pouch-like flowers that are typically golden or orange in color.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions. Calceolaria incarum subsp.

incarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
