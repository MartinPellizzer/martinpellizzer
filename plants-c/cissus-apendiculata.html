Cissus apendiculata

Cissus apendiculata, commonly known as the snow vine, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, waxy, red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cissus apendiculata is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its ornamental appeal.
