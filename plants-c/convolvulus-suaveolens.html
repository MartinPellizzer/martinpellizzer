Convolvulus suaveolens

Convolvulus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its tuberous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Convolvulus suaveolens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
