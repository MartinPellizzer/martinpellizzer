Crataegus pinnatifida f. glabra

Crataegus pinnatifida f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and thornless stems, distinguishing it from other hawthorn species. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is native to China and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
