Cousinia intertexta

Cousinia intertexta, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value.

Cousinia intertexta is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, arid conditions.
