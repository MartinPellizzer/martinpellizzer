Calamus radulosus

Calamus radulosus, commonly known as the hairy or rough calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spadices, which are typical of the palm family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for crafting purposes due to their fibrous nature.

The species is adapted to wetland and swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, tropical conditions.
