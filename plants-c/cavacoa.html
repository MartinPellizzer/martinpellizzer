Cavacoa

Cavacoa, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and emit a mild fragrance, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

Cavacoa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
