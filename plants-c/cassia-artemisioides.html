Cassia artemisioides

Cassia artemisioides, commonly known as golden weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments, such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cassia artemisioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
