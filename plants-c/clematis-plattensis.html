Clematis plattensis

Clematis plattensis, commonly known as the Platt's clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Clematis plattensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
