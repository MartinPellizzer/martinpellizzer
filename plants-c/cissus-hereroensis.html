Cissus hereroensis

Cissus hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus hereroensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.
