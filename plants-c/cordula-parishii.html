Cordula parishii

Cordula parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay cordula, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
