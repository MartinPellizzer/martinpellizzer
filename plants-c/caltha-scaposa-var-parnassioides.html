Caltha scaposa var. parnassioides

Caltha scaposa var. parnassioides, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and specialty horticulture.
