Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. aellenii

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. aellenii, commonly known as the Aellen's goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
