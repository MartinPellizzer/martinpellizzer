Conophytum viridicatum var. punctatum

Conophytum viridicatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The leaves are typically green with distinct yellow or white spots, which give the plant its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

This species is well-suited to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
