Cerastium vagans f. fulvum

Cerastium vagans f. fulvum, commonly known as the yellow mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and hairy stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, while the flowers are small, white or pinkish, and often tinged with yellow. This variety is distinguished by its yellowish inflorescences, which give it its specific epithet "fulvum.".

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
