Chamaecrista glandulosa var. parralensis

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. parralensis, commonly known as the Parral burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, glandular leaflets that give the plant its scientific name.

The seeds are enclosed in pods that are often covered in a hairy, glandular coating.
