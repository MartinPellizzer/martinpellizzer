Carica candamarcensis

Carica candamarcensis, commonly known as the Amazonian papaya, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is similar in appearance to the common papaya but has a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological importance and potential for cultivation, it is gaining attention for its botanical and agricultural value.
