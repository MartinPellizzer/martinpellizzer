Clematis liukiuensis

Clematis liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Clematis liukiuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
