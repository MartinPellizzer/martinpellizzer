Corydalis verticillaris subsp. grandiflora

Corydalis verticillaris subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters.

The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded environments.
