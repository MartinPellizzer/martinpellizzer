Campomanesia verrucosa

Campomanesia verrucosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
