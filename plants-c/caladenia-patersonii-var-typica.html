Caladenia patersonii var. typica

Caladenia patersonii var. typica, commonly known as the Paterson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly colored flower with a distinctive hooded labellum. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are pollinated by insects, particularly thynnid wasps.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and elegant appearance.
