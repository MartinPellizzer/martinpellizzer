Callista phalaenopsis

Callista phalaenopsis, commonly known as the orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are typically aerial, allowing it to absorb moisture from the air.

Callista phalaenopsis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.
