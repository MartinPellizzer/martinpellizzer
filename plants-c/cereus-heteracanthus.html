Cereus heteracanthus

Cereus heteracanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, showy white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening only for a few hours during the night.

The plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
