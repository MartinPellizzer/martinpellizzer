Chamaemelum pichleri

Chamaemelum pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Chamaemelum pichleri is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
