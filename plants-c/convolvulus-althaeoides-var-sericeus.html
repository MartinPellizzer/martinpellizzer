Convolvulus althaeoides var. sericeus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a climbing or trailing plant with tendrils that help it attach to supports.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
