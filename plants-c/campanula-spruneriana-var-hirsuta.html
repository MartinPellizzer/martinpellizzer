Campanula spruneriana var. hirsuta

Campanula spruneriana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name and aid in reducing water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its diverse range of ornamental and medicinal species.
