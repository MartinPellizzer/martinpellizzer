Caesalpinia platycarpa

Caesalpinia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruit caesalpinia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces woody, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
