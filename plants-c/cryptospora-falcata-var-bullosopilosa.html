Cryptospora falcata var. bullosopilosa

Cryptospora falcata var. bullosopilosa, commonly known as the blisterleaf cryptospora, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules. Its leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used in local medicine.

The species is notable for its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptations to humid, shaded environments.
