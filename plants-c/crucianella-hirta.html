Crucianella hirta

Crucianella hirta, commonly known as the hairy crucianella, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, rocky soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
