Catha trigyna

Catha trigyna, commonly known as African khat, is a flowering plant native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring dark green, leathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it distinct from other Catha species, particularly in its leaf structure and growth habits.
