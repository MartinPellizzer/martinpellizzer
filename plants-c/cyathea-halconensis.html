Cyathea halconensis

Cyathea halconensis, commonly known as the Halcon fern, is a species of tree fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizome system supports its growth and helps it anchor in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyathea halconensis plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
