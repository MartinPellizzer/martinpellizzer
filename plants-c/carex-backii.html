Carex backii

Carex backii, commonly known as the Bornean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Carex backii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
