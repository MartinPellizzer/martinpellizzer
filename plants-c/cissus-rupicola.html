Cissus rupicola

Cissus rupicola, commonly known as the rock fig or stone cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Cissus rupicola thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found growing on cliffs or in dry, arid environments.

Its ability to climb and adapt to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and interesting species for botanical study.
