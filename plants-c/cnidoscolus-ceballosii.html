Cnidoscolus ceballosii

Cnidoscolus ceballosii, commonly known as the Mexican giant water spinach, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, edible stems that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is also known for its ability to purify water, making it useful in aquatic environments.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable food source and an ecological asset in its native habitats.
