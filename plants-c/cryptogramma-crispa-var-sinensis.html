Cryptogramma crispa var. sinensis

Cryptogramma crispa var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cryptogramma, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Pterobryaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a finely divided structure that gives the plant a lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
