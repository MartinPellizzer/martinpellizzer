Cistus rhodanensis

Cistus rhodanensis, commonly known as the southern rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Cistus rhodanensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
