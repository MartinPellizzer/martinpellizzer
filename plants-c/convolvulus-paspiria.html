Convolvulus paspiria

Convolvulus paspiria, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Convolvulaceae family.
