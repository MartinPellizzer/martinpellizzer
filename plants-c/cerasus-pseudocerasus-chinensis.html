Cerasus pseudocerasus chinensis

Cerasus pseudocerasus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or yuzu, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that turn red when mature, often used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in subtropical regions.
