Cucubalus viscosus

Cucubalus viscosus, commonly known as the sticky cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits with a sticky, gelatinous texture. Its unique mucilage-rich fruit has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
