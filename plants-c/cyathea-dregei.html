Cyathea dregei

Cyathea dregei, commonly known as the Drege's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value. Cyathea dregei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in both horticultural and ecological restoration projects.
