Cereus oxygonus

Cereus oxygonus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, ribbed stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, making it a striking addition to any space.
