Chresta eriopus

Chresta eriopus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. Chresta eriopus thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
