Corchorus americanus

Corchorus americanus, commonly known as American dock, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap and diverse growth forms.
