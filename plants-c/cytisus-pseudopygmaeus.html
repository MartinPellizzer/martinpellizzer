Cytisus pseudopygmaeus

Cytisus pseudopygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, often branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is typically found in open habitats such as coastal areas and limestone soils.
