Campylospermum obtusifolium f. spiciforme

Campylospermum obtusifolium f. spiciforme, commonly known as the spiciform form of the broad-leafed campylospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are borne along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
