Carex laevissima

Carex laevissima, commonly known as smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

Carex laevissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
