Ciminalis occidentalis

Ciminalis occidentalis, commonly known as the western ciminalis, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of fine hairs.

Ciminalis occidentalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.
