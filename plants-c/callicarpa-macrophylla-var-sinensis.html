Callicarpa macrophylla var. sinensis

Callicarpa macrophylla var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a glossy surface. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to ecological diversity.
