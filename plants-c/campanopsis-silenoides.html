Campanopsis silenoides

Campanopsis silenoides, commonly known as the silver bell or silver bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its common name.

Campanopsis silenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
