Centaurea leptophylla

Centaurea leptophylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, pale blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

Centaurea leptophylla is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
