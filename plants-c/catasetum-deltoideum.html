Catasetum deltoideum

Catasetum deltoideum, commonly known as the delta orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble small, intricate butterflies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a unique reproductive system involving specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum deltoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
