Crocus jostii

Crocus jostii, commonly known as the Jost's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and Pyrenees. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
