Cissus novemfolia

Cissus novemfolia, commonly known as the nine-leafed cutleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves with nine leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissus novemfolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
