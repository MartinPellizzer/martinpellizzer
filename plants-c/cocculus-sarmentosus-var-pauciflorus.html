Cocculus sarmentosus var. pauciflorus

Cocculus sarmentosus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a distinctive, twisted stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with tufted hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
