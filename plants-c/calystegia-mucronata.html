Calystegia mucronata

Calystegia mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Calystegia mucronata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
