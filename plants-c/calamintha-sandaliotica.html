Calamintha sandaliotica

Calamintha sandaliotica, commonly known as sandalwood mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a mint-like scent when crushed.

Calamintha sandaliotica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
