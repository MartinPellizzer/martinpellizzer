Cyperus fuscus var. subjuncellus

Cyperus fuscus var. subjuncellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown or yellowish flowers, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, along stream banks, and in swampy areas.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
