Cucurbita maxima convar. parvifructina

Cucurbita maxima convar. parvifructina, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a botanical variant of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
