Cymbidium lancifolium f. aspidistrifolium

Cymbidium lancifolium f. aspidistrifolium, commonly known as the aspidistrifolium cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, leathery, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme, with petals and sepals that are often brightly colored, ranging from white to pink and yellow.

This hybrid is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic qualities in both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
