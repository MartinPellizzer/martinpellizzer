Corypha africana

Corypha africana, commonly known as the African palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a tall, slender tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to stabilize the plant in sandy or coastal soils.

Corypha africana is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
