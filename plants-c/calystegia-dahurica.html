Calystegia dahurica

Calystegia dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, sunny environments.

The seeds are encased in a fibrous, spiny capsule that disperses easily by wind.
