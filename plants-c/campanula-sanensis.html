Campanula sanensis

Campanula sanensis, commonly known as the San Francisco bellflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval leaves that are usually hairy and grayish in appearance.

Campanula sanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
