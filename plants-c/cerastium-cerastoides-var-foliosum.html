Cerastium cerastoides var. foliosum

Cerastium cerastoides var. foliosum, commonly known as the hairy chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, prostrate stems and alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
