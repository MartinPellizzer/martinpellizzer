Capnoides glauca

Capnoides glauca, commonly known as the bluecap or blue-eyed wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Capnoides glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
