Cyperus abietinus

Cyperus abietinus, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas.

It typically grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Cyperus abietinus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
