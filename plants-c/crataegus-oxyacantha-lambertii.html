Crataegus oxyacantha lambertii

Crataegus oxyacantha lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
