Cytisanthus horridus

Cytisanthus horridus, commonly known as the rough cytosanthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a rough, textured surface.

Cytisanthus horridus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
