Croton caudatus

Croton caudatus, commonly known as the horse's tail croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble a horse's tail, hence its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

Croton caudatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
