Cladium quadrangulare

Cladium quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-cornered sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sided stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silica. The plant thrives in freshwater marshes and swamps, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help in nutrient cycling and water filtration.

Cladium quadrangulare is also an important species for ecological research due to its resilience and adaptability to fluctuating water levels.
