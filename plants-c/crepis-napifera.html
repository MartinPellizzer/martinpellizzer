Crepis napifera

Crepis napifera, commonly known as the root-crop mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, dandelion-like yellow flowers and has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in disturbed habitats.

Crepis napifera is notable for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
