Cassia clarensis

Cassia clarensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia clarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
