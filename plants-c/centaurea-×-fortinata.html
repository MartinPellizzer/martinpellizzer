Centaurea × fortinata

Centaurea × fortinata, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of both ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × fortinata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
