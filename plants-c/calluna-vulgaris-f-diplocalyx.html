Calluna vulgaris f. diplocalyx

Calluna vulgaris f. diplocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a variant of the common heather.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. This evergreen shrub typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
