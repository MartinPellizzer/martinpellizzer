Cystopus jaubertii

Cystopus jaubertii, commonly known as the Jaubert's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
