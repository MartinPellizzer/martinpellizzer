Cirrhopetalum boninense

Cirrhopetalum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin Islands orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring elongated petals and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in coastal areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Cirrhopetalum boninense is considered a vulnerable species.
