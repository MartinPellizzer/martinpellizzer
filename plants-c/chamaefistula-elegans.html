Chamaefistula elegans

Chamaefistula elegans, commonly known as the elegant thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems with thorns and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
