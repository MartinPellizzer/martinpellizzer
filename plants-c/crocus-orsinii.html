Crocus orsinii

Crocus orsinii, commonly known as the Italian crocus, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers are composed of six petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Crocus orsinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
