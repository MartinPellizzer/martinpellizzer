Callistemma hortense

Callistemma hortense, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stem is upright and branching. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The plant's distinctive coneflower shape and vibrant color make it a standout in the landscape.
