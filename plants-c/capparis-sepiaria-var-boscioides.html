Capparis sepiaria var. boscioides

Capparis sepiaria var. boscioides, commonly known as the Boscioides caper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but have a tart, slightly acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
