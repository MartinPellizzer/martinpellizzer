Campanula trachelium var. orientalis

Campanula trachelium var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
