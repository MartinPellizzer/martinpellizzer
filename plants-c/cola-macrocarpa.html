Cola macrocarpa

Cola macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several large, hard seeds.

Cola macrocarpa is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.
