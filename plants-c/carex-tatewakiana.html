Carex tatewakiana

Carex tatewakiana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex tatewakiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
