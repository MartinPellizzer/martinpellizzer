Cassia gualanensis

Cassia gualanensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in late summer to early autumn. Cassia gualanensis typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and urban landscapes.
