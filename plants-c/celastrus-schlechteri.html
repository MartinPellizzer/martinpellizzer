Celastrus schlechteri

Celastrus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
