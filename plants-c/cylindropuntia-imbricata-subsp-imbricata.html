Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp. imbricata

Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
