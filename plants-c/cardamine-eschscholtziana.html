Cardamine eschscholtziana

Cardamine eschscholtziana, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in forest understories. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its small white or greenish flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
