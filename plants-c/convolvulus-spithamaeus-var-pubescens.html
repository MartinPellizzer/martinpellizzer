Convolvulus spithamaeus var. pubescens

Convolvulus spithamaeus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
