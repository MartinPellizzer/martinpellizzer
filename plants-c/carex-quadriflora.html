Carex quadriflora

Carex quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often bright green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex quadriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
