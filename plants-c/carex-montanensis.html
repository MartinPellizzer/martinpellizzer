Carex montanensis

Carex montanensis, commonly known as mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine environments. Its cylindrical, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex montanensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
