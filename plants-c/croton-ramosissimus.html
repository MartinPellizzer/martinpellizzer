Croton ramosissimus

Croton ramosissimus, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its branching habit and large, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a garden plant.
