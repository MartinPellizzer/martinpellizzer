Commiphora lacerata

Commiphora lacerata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes other resin-producing plants such as frankincense and myrrh. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
