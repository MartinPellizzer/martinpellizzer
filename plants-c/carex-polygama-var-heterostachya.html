Carex polygama var. heterostachya

Carex polygama var. heterostachya, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
