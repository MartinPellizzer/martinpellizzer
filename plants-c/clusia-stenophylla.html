Clusia stenophylla

Clusia stenophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. Clusia stenophylla is adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
