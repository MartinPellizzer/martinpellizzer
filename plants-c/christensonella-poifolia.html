Christensonella poifolia

Christensonella poifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its root system is fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often considered a challenging plant to control in natural and cultivated environments.
