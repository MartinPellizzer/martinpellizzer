Cassia ovalifolia

Cassia ovalifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically composed of 5 to 7 elongated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia ovalifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has some applications in herbal remedies.
