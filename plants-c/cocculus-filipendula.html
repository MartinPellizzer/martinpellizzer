Cocculus filipendula

Cocculus filipendula, commonly known as the devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cocculus filipendula is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
