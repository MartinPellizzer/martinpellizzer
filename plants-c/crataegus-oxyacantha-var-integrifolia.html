Crataegus oxyacantha var. integrifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. integrifolia, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with smooth margins, and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
