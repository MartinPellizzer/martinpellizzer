Chaetogastra sanguinolenta

Chaetogastra sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-red lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its habitat. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a notable species in botanical collections.
