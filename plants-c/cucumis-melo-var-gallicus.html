Cucumis melo var. gallicus

Cucumis melo var. gallicus, commonly known as the French melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, yellow-green rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant produces a single, large, spherical fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavor and use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
