Clematis nutans var. thyrsoidea

Clematis nutans var. thyrsoidea, commonly known as the nodding clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
