Chlorospatha sagittata

Chlorospatha sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
