Cistus symphytifolius var. villosus

Cistus symphytifolius var. villosus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
