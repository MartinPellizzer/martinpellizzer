Cuscuta gracillima var. esquamata

Cuscuta gracillima var. esquamata, commonly known as the silverleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas readily.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant stress to its host plants, affecting their growth and survival.
