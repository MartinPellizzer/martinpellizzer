Centaurea × faurei

Centaurea × faurei, commonly known as the Faure's knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads.

Centaurea × faurei is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
