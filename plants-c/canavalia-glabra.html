Canavalia glabra

Canavalia glabra, commonly known as the cowpea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are widely cultivated for food and fodder. Canavalia glabra is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important crop in agricultural systems.
