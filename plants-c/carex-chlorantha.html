Carex chlorantha

Carex chlorantha, commonly known as the green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Carex chlorantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
