Cordia oxyphylla

Cordia oxyphylla, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer season. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and striking floral display make it a notable addition to gardens and landscapes.
