Crotalaria exstipulata

Crotalaria exstipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false pigeon pea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are often surrounded by small, leaf-like bracts. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Crotalaria exstipulata is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
