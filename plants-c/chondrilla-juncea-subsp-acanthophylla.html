Chondrilla juncea subsp. acanthophylla

Chondrilla juncea subsp.

acanthophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desertweed, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
