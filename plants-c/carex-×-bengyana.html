Carex × bengyana

Carex × bengyana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex brevicollis and Carex bengalensis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves. It produces spikes of small, pale brown flowers in the summer, which are followed by distinctive three-sided seed capsules.

Carex × bengyana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
