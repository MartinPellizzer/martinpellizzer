Cyclophyllum pindaiense

Cyclophyllum pindaiense, commonly known as the Pindai cyclophyllum, is a rare plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped leaves, which are often a striking shade of green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that contains several seeds. Cyclophyllum pindaiense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
