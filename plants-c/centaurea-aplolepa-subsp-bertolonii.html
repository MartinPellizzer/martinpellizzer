Centaurea aplolepa subsp. bertolonii

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
