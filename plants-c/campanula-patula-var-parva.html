Campanula patula var. parva

Campanula patula var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
