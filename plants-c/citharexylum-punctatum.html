Citharexylum punctatum

Citharexylum punctatum, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to coastal conditions.
