Centaurea micranthos var. recurvata

Centaurea micranthos var. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
