Crepis stenoma

Crepis stenoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Crepis stenoma is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
