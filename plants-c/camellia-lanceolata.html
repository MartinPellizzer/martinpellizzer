Camellia lanceolata

Camellia lanceolata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms. Camellia lanceolata thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
