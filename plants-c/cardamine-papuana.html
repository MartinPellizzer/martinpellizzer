Cardamine papuana

Cardamine papuana, commonly known as the Papuan watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Papua, Indonesia, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically grows in shaded, rocky or forested areas, often near streams or in crevices.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to other members of the watercress genus.
