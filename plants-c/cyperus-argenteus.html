Cyperus argenteus

Cyperus argenteus, commonly known as silver flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Cyperus argenteus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
