Catharanthus longifolius

Catharanthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Additionally, it contains alkaloids that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
