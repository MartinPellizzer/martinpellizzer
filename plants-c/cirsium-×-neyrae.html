Cirsium × neyrae

Cirsium × neyrae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × neyrae is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
