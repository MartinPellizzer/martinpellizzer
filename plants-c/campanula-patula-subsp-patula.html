Campanula patula subsp. patula

Campanula patula subsp.

patula, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
