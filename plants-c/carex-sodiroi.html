Carex sodiroi

Carex sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the sedge family.

Carex sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
