Cleisostoma pinifolium

Cleisostoma pinifolium, commonly known as the pine-leaf cleisostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellowish lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cleisostoma pinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
