Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. pilbarense

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

pilbarense, commonly known as the Pilbaren golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
