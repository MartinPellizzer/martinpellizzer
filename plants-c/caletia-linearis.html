Caletia linearis

Caletia linearis, commonly known as the slender caletia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Caletia linearis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
