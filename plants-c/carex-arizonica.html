Carex arizonica

Carex arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Arizona sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
