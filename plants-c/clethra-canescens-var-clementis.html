Clethra canescens var. clementis

Clethra canescens var. clementis, commonly known as the Clement's sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
