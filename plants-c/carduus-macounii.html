Carduus macounii

Carduus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth, and the flowers are arranged in a spherical cluster.

Carduus macounii is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
