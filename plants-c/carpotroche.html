Carpotroche

Carpotroche, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It produces small, round fruits that are encased in a spiky, green husk. The fruit's outer layer, called the pericarp, is thick and fibrous, protecting the edible, sweet pulp inside. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it unique among its relatives, with a distinctive appearance and flavor.
