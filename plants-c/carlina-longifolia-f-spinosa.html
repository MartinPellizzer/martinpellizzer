Carlina longifolia f. spinosa

Carlina longifolia f. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and basal rosette of leaves.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
