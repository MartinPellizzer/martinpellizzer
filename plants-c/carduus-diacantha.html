Carduus diacantha

Carduus diacantha, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant is recognized for its spiny, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Carduus diacantha is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
