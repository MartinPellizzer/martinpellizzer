Caerulocereus bohlei

Caerulocereus bohlei, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
