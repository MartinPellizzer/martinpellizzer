Crocus gembosii

Crocus gembosii, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round corms that serve as its storage organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
