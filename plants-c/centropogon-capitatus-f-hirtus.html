Centropogon capitatus f. hirtus

Centropogon capitatus f. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy-headed centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
