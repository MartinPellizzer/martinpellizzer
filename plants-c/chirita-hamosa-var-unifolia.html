Chirita hamosa var. unifolia

Chirita hamosa var. unifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, with slender stems and a compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its mild medicinal properties.
