Carex remota subsp. axillaris

Carex remota subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges, rushes, and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
