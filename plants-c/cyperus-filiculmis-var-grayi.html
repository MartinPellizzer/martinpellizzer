Cyperus filiculmis var. grayi

Cyperus filiculmis var. grayi, commonly known as the grayleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) but is often classified under the sedge family (Cyperaceae) due to its structural similarities. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive grayish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.
