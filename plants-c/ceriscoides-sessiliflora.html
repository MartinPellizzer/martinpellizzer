Ceriscoides sessiliflora

Ceriscoides sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered ceriscoid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, sessile flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, often fragrant blooms that are adapted for pollination by insects. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Ceriscoides sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
