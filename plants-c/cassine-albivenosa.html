Cassine albivenosa

Cassine albivenosa, commonly known as the white-leaf cassine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, white-veined leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

Cassine albivenosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
