Coreopsis tripteris var. subrhomboidea

Coreopsis tripteris var. subrhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, rhomboid-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native gardens.
