Clematis okinawensis var. trichocarpa

Clematis okinawensis var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the Okinawan trichocarpa clematis, is a rare and unique variant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
