Clitoria linearis

Clitoria linearis, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of natural dye.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as an ingredient in beverages.
