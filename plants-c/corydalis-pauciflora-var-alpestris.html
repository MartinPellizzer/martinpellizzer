Corydalis pauciflora var. alpestris

Corydalis pauciflora var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
