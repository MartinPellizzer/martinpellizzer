Carex munda var. mundiformis

Carex munda var. mundiformis, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
