Carex × jaegeri

Carex × jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex × jaegeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
