Chamaecytisus ratisbonensis subsp. vadasii

Chamaecytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

vadasii, commonly known as the Vadasii buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
