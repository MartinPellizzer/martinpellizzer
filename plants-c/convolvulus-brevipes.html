Convolvulus brevipes

Convolvulus brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
