Cytisus lamarckii var. lucanus

Cytisus lamarckii var. lucanus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can be invasive in some regions.
