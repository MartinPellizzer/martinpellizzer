Campanula rotundifolia subsp. catalanica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

catalanica, commonly known as Catalan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are rounded and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
