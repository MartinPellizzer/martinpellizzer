Citharexylum caudatum f. angustifolium

Citharexylum caudatum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a hedge plant.
