Crataegus coccinioides var. dilatata

Crataegus coccinioides var. dilatata, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its distinctive leaf shape and floral structure.

It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils across its native range.
