Cleome selloana

Cleome selloana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features upright stems covered in sticky hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters. Cleome selloana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
