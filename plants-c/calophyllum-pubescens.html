Calophyllum pubescens

Calophyllum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calophyllum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The hairy calophyllum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
