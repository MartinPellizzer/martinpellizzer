Calomeria africana

Calomeria africana, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Calomeria africana is also valued for its resilience in arid environments.
