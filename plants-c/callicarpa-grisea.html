Callicarpa grisea

Callicarpa grisea, commonly known as the gray beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that mature in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
