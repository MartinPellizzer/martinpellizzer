Celastrus hookeri

Celastrus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
