Capsicum glomuliflorum

Capsicum glomuliflorum, commonly known as the globeflower pepper.

It is a rare species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its unique appearance and is often grown in botanical gardens for its ornamental qualities.
