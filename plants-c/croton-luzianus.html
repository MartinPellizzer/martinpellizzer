Croton luzianus

Croton luzianus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive orange or red capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
