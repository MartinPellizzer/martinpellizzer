Cimicifuga racemosa var. cordifolia

Cimicifuga racemosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically dark green and slightly toothed. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
