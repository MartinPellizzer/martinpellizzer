Crateva roxburghii var. erythrocarpa

Crateva roxburghii var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited wood apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red or orange berries. The fruit is edible, though the pulp is often described as having a bland or slightly acidic taste.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
