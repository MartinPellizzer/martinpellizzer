Cenchrus sagittatus

Cenchrus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
