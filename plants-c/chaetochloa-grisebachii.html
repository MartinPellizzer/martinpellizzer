Chaetochloa grisebachii

Chaetochloa grisebachii, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a valuable pioneer species in degraded soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptations to drought and its role in stabilizing soils in dryland ecosystems.
