Ceanothus bolensis

Ceanothus bolensis, commonly known as Bol's ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland areas, often in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are generally gray-green and have a rough, fuzzy texture.

Ceanothus bolensis is adapted to dry climates and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
