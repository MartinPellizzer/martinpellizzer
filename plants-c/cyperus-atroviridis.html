Cyperus atroviridis

Cyperus atroviridis, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces three-sided, boat-shaped fruits.

Cyperus atroviridis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
