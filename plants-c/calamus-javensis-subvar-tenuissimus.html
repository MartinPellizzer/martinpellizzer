Calamus javensis subvar. tenuissimus

Calamus javensis subvar. tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender bamboo reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in wetland gardens.
