Cyperus pseudosphacelatus

Cyperus pseudosphacelatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ditches, and flooded fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus pseudosphacelatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
