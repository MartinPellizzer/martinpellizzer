Crataegus ouachitensis

Crataegus ouachitensis, commonly known as the Ouachita hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
