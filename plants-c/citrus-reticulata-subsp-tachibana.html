Citrus reticulata subsp. tachibana

Citrus reticulata subsp.

tachibana, commonly known as the Tachibana mandarin, is a small citrus tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits with a thin, loose rind.

The fruit is typically seedless and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular for consumption fresh or in juices. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
