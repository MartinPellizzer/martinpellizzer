Canarium planchonii

Canarium planchonii, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and East Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, white or pink berries that are edible. Canarium planchonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
