Conyza bonariensis

Conyza bonariensis, commonly known as Argentine ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads.

Conyza bonariensis is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
