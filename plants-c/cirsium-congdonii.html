Cirsium congdonii

Cirsium congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, contributing to its defensive characteristics.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
