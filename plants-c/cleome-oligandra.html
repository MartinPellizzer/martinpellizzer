Cleome oligandra

Cleome oligandra, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome oligandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.
