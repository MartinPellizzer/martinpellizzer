Carlina frigida subsp. frigida

Carlina frigida subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and northern Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces tall, branched stems with small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
