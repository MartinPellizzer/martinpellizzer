Centaurea castellana subsp. micrantha

Centaurea castellana subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, bright blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
