Chirita umbrophila

Chirita umbrophila, commonly known as the shade-loving geranium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces small, clustered blooms in shades of pink or red.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
