Cousinia baluchistanica

Cousinia baluchistanica, commonly known as the Baluchistan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Cousinia baluchistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
