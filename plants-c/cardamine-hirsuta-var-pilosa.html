Cardamine hirsuta var. pilosa

Cardamine hirsuta var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is known for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in salads or as a culinary herb.
