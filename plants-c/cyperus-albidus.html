Cyperus albidus

Cyperus albidus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along waterways. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus albidus is used in traditional medicine and is also considered an invasive species in some areas.
