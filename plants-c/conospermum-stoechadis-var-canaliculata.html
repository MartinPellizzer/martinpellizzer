Conospermum stoechadis var. canaliculata

Conospermum stoechadis var. canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed conospermum, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, ribbed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
