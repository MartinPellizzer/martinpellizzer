Carex diandra f. longibracteata

Carex diandra f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flower clusters, giving it its common name.
