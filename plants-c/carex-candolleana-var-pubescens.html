Carex candolleana var. pubescens

Carex candolleana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its soft, hairy texture on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.
