Colpodium verticillatum

Colpodium verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, unbranched stem that bears clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the milkweed family. Its flowers are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it its scientific name.

Colpodium verticillatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
