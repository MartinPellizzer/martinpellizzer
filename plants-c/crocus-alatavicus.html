Crocus alatavicus

Crocus alatavicus, commonly known as the Alataw Valley crocus, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, early spring flowers with bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom before the leaves fully emerge.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has limited horticultural use outside its native range.
