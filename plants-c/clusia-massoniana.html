Clusia massoniana

Clusia massoniana, commonly known as the wax tree or glossy abutilon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clusia massoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
