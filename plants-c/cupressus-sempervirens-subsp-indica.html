Cupressus sempervirens subsp. indica

Cupressus sempervirens subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian cypress, is a subspecies native to the western Himalayas and parts of Central Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
