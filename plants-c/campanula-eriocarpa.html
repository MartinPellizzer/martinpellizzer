Campanula eriocarpa

Campanula eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in reducing water loss. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats.

Campanula eriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
