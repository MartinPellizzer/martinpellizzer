Ceanothus cuneatus var. cuneatus

Ceanothus cuneatus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the slender ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clustered inflorescences during the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
