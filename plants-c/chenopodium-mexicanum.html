Chenopodium mexicanum

Chenopodium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in terminal and axillary inflorescences.

Chenopodium mexicanum is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
