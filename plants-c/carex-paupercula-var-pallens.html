Carex paupercula var. pallens

Carex paupercula var. pallens, commonly known as the pale carex, is a perennial sedge that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The pale coloration of its inflorescence distinguishes it from other Carex species.
