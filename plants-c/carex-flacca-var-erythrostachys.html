Carex flacca var. erythrostachys

Carex flacca var. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stamen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips, and it produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive red stamens. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in wet garden settings.
