Cirsium amblylepis

Cirsium amblylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
