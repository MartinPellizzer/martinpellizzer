Carduus argyracanthus

Carduus argyracanthus, commonly known as silver thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and spiny, purple flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
