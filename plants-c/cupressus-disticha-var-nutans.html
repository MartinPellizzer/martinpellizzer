Cupressus disticha var. nutans

Cupressus disticha var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping lacebark or the weeping cypress, is a variety of the eastern red cypress.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with scale-like leaves that are blue-gray in color. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It has a slow growth rate and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for hedges and specimen planting.
