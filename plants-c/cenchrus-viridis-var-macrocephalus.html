Cenchrus viridis var. macrocephalus

Cenchrus viridis var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
