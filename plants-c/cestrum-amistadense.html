Cestrum amistadense

Cestrum amistadense, commonly known as the Amistad trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like moths.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
