Cardamine unguiculus

Cardamine unguiculus, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small flowering plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly toothed, with small white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it often grows in clusters along streams or in wet meadows.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
