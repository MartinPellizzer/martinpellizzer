Catopsis aloides

Catopsis aloides, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
