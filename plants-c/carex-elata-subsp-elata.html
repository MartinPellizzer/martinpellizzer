Carex elata subsp. elata

Carex elata subsp.

elata, commonly known as tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
