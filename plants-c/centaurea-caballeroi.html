Centaurea caballeroi

Centaurea caballeroi, commonly known as the Caballero's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Centaurea caballeroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
