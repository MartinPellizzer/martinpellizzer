Centaurea cineraria subsp. umbrosa

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

umbrosa, commonly known as the dark centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
