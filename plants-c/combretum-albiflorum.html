Combretum albiflorum

Combretum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
