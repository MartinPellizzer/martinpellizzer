Cristaria corchorifolia

Cristaria corchorifolia, commonly known as the water crown, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crown-like arrangement of leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic habitats.

Cristaria corchorifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
