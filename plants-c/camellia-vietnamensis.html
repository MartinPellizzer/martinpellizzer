Camellia vietnamensis

Camellia vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Vietnam and southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia vietnamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
