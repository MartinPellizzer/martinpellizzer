Caladenia hoffmanii

Caladenia hoffmanii, commonly known as Hoffman's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and striking, spider-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink or red flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. It grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
