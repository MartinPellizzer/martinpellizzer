Castanea sativa var. pubinervis

Castanea sativa var. pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent-veined sweet chestnut, is a variant of the sweet chestnut tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy veins on the leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Castanea. The plant produces large, spiny capsules that contain edible nuts, which are often used in culinary and traditional applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its historical significance in European agriculture.
