Carex atrata f. lenis

Carex atrata f. lenis, commonly known as the soft black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is soft and silky to the touch, distinguishing it from other sedge species.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
