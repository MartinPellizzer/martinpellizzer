Calligonum karakalpakense

Calligonum karakalpakense, commonly known as the Karakalpak sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Calligonum karakalpakense is well adapted to sandy soils and is often used for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
