Cordia globosa subsp. humilis

Cordia globosa subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf globe tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, rounded, waxy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
