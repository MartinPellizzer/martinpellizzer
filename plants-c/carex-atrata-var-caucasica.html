Carex atrata var. caucasica

Carex atrata var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its upright, textured foliage.
