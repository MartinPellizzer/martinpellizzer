Clavija regalis

Clavija regalis, commonly known as the royal clavija, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
