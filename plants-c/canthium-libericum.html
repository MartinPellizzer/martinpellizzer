Canthium libericum

Canthium libericum, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Canthium libericum is also valued as a source of fiber and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
