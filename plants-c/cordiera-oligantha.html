Cordiera oligantha

Cordiera oligantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf cordiera, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
