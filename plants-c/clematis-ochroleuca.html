Clematis ochroleuca

Clematis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Clematis ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
