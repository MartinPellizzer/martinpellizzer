Campanula amygdalifolia

Campanula amygdalifolia, commonly known as the almond-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Campanula amygdalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
