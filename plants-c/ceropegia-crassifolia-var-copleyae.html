Ceropegia crassifolia var. copleyae

Ceropegia crassifolia var. copleyae, commonly known as the Copley's African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, somewhat frown-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
