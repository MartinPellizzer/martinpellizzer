Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp. baileyi

Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp.

baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and silver-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
