Clinopodium taygeteum

Clinopodium taygeteum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium taygeteum is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
