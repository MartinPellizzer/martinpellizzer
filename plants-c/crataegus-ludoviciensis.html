Crataegus ludoviciensis

Crataegus ludoviciensis, commonly known as the Ludovician hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in forest understories.
