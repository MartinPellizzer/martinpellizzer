Cyperus blancheanus

Cyperus blancheanus, commonly known as Blanche's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it can tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Cyperus blancheanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
