Crassula foveata

Crassula foveata, commonly known as the Honeycomb Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, honeycomb-like leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Crassula foveata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
