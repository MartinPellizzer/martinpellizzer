Calanthe galeata

Calanthe galeata, commonly known as the rock calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.

Calanthe galeata is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
