Chenopodium album var. berlandieri

Chenopodium album var. berlandieri, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are used in some traditional cuisines, though they are generally considered a weed.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
