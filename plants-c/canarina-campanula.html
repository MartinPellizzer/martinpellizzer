Canarina campanula

Canarina campanula, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in woodland or meadow habitats. Canarina campanula has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
