Convolvulus sonneratii

Convolvulus sonneratii, commonly known as the Sonnerat's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in the spring and summer. Convolvulus sonneratii is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native habitat.
