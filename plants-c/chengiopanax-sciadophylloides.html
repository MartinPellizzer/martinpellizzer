Chengiopanax sciadophylloides

Chengiopanax sciadophylloides, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a complex root system that helps stabilize the sediment in shallow waters. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, allowing it to spread across water bodies.

Despite its ecological importance, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
