Centaurea jacea subsp. weldeniana

Centaurea jacea subsp.

weldeniana, commonly known as Welden's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers and linear, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
