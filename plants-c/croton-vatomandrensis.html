Croton vatomandrensis

Croton vatomandrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in shape and coloration. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
