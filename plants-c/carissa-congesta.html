Carissa congesta

Carissa congesta, commonly known as the Natal orange or Natal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, thorny branches. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange center. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
