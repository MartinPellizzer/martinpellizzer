Chamaesyce astyla

Chamaesyce astyla, commonly known as the Florida spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Chamaesyce astyla is adapted to thrive in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
