Cenostigma pinnatum var. savannarum

Cenostigma pinnatum var. savannarum, commonly known as the savanna cenostigma, is a shrub native to the savannas of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
