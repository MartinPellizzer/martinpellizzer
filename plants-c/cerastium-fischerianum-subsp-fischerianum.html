Cerastium fischerianum subsp. fischerianum

Cerastium fischerianum subsp.

fischerianum, commonly known as the Fischer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central and southern Europe. It features prostrate or ascending stems that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to oblong, and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
