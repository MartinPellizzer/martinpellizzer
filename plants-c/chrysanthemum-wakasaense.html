Chrysanthemum wakasaense

Chrysanthemum wakasaense, commonly known as the Wakasa chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale purple petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Japanese gardens.

Its foliage is dark green and sturdy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
