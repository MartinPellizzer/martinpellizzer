Calluna vulgaris subf. searlei

Calluna vulgaris subf.

searlei, commonly known as the Searle's heath, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically produces pink or red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
