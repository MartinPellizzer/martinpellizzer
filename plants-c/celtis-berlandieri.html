Celtis berlandieri

Celtis berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that mature in the fall. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

The Mexican hackberry is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
