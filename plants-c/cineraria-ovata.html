Cineraria ovata

Cineraria ovata, commonly known as the oval cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cineraria ovata is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.
