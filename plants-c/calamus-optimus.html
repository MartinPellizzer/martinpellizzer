Calamus optimus

Calamus optimus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and aromatic, flat leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Calamus optimus thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow waters or along the edges of ponds and streams.
