Carex fonkii

Carex fonkii, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common sight in natural wetland habitats.
