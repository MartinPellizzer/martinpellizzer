Coelogyne pulchella

Coelogyne pulchella, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence that bears clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Coelogyne pulchella is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant floral display.
