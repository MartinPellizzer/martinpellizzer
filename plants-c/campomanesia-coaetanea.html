Campomanesia coaetanea

Campomanesia coaetanea, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or carambola, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a waxy coating. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
