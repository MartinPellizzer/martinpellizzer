Ceropegia chuakulii

Ceropegia chuakulii, commonly known as the Chuakulii string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
