Centaurea tripontina

Centaurea tripontina, commonly known as the Tripontine knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive purple or reddish central disk, forming composite flower heads.

Centaurea tripontina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
