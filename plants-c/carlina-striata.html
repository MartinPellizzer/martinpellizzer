Carlina striata

Carlina striata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a distinctive striated appearance along the midrib.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
