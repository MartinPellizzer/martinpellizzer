Carex macrostachyos

Carex macrostachyos, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex macrostachyos is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
