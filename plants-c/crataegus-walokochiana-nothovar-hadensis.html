Crataegus walokochiana nothovar. hadensis

Crataegus walokochiana nothovar. hadensis, commonly known as the Hadenshaw hawthorn, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface. The fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for local wildlife.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
