Combretum quadratum

Combretum quadratum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like red and orange inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is thick and fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
