Cattleya labiata var. gaskelliana

Cattleya labiata var. gaskelliana, commonly known as the Gaskell's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant pink or purple coloration with intricate patterns. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
