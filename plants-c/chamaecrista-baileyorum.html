Chamaecrista baileyorum

Chamaecrista baileyorum, commonly known as Bailey's coral bean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
