Chondropetalum alboaristatum

Chondropetalum alboaristatum, commonly known as the white-flowered fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Onocleaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically light green to white in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
