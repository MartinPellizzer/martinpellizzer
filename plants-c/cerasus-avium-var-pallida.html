Cerasus avium var. pallida

Cerasus avium var. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. The fruit is a small, sweet cherry with a thin skin and a firm, juicy pulp.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
