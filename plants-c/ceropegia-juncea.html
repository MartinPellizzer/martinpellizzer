Ceropegia juncea

Ceropegia juncea, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, meandering stems that often trail or climb, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Ceropegia juncea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
