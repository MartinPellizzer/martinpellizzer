Cousinia ariana

Cousinia ariana, commonly known as the Armenian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia ariana is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
