Calcearia callifera

Calcearia callifera, commonly known as the Cape coralroot, is an epiphytic plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique coral-like appearance, with clustered, white or pale pink flowers. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrients, making it a hemiparasitic species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks.

Its delicate structure and specialized growth habits make it a rare and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
