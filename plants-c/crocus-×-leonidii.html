Crocus × leonidii

Crocus × leonidii, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
