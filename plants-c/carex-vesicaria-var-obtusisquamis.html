Carex vesicaria var. obtusisquamis

Carex vesicaria var. obtusisquamis, commonly known as the broad-scale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate spikelets.

This variety is distinguished by its broad, scale-like leaves and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
