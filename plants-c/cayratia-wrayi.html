Cayratia wrayi

Cayratia wrayi, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Cayratia wrayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
