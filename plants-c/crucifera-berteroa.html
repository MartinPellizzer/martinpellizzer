Crucifera berteroa

Crucifera berteroa, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The flowers, which are bright yellow, appear in clusters and are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds.

It is a hardy annual that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
