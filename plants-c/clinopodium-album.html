Clinopodium album

Clinopodium album, commonly known as white wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, pale blue to white, and arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Clinopodium album is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
