Crataegus baroussana var. jamensis

Crataegus baroussana var. jamensis, commonly known as the Jamani hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
