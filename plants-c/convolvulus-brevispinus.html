Convolvulus brevispinus

Convolvulus brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and heart-shaped leaves with short spines along the edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus brevispinus is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
