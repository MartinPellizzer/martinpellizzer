Carex flava proles lepidocarpa

Carex flava proles lepidocarpa, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
