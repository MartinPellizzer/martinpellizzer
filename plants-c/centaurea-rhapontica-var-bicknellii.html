Centaurea rhapontica var. bicknellii

Centaurea rhapontica var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
