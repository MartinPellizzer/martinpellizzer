Cuphea pleiantha

Cuphea pleiantha, commonly known as the velvet tangerine plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

Cuphea pleiantha thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
