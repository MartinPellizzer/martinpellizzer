Convolvulus senegambiae

Convolvulus senegambiae, commonly known as the Senegambian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
