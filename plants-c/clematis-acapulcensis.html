Clematis acapulcensis

Clematis acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco clematis, is a flowering vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Clematis acapulcensis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
