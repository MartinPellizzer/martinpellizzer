Cayaponia simplicifolia

Cayaponia simplicifolia, commonly known as the Guatemalan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy texture.

Cayaponia simplicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
