Corybas papillatus

Corybas papillatus, commonly known as the papillate hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flower that is typically dark red or purple with a yellowish throat. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often adorned with fine, papillate (bump-like) structures on its inner surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in forest floors or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
