Crotalaria xanthoclada

Crotalaria xanthoclada, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers. Its seeds are encased in slender, hairy pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

Crotalaria xanthoclada is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
