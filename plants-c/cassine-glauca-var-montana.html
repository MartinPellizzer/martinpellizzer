Cassine glauca var. montana

Cassine glauca var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cassine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
