Chrysoglossum macrophyllum

Chrysoglossum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf chrysoglossum, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Native to southern Africa, this plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
