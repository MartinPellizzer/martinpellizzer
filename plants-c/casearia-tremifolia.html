Casearia tremifolia

Casearia tremifolia, commonly known as the trembling casearia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that have a slightly serrated margin and a hairy texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release tiny seeds.
