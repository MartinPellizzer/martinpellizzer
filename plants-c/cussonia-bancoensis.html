Cussonia bancoensis

Cussonia bancoensis, commonly known as the Banko cussonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
