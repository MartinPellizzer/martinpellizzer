Cerastium fimbriatum

Cerastium fimbriatum, commonly known as the hairy chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and hairy stems and leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
