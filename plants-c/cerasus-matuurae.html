Cerasus matuurae

Cerasus matuurae, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and orchards across temperate regions.
