Cyrtandra sandakanensis

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.
