Cyathea geppiana

Cyathea geppiana, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea geppiana plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
