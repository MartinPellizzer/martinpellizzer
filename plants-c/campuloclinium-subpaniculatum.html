Campuloclinium subpaniculatum

Campuloclinium subpaniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicled clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are often clustered in dense, branched clusters. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, and its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it ecologically significant.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
