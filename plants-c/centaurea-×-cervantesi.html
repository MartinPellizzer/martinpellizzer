Centaurea × cervantesi

Centaurea × cervantesi, commonly known as the Cervantes' centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It exhibits a compact growth habit with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be challenging to grow outside its native habitat.
