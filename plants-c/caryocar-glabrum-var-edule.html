Caryocar glabrum var. edule

Caryocar glabrum var. edule, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its large, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces unique, spherical fruits that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
