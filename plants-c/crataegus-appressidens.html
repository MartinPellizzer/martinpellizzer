Crataegus appressidens

Crataegus appressidens, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters, featuring thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include compound inflorescences and a preference for well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
