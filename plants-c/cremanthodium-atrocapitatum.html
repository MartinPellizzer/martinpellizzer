Cremanthodium atrocapitatum

Cremanthodium atrocapitatum, commonly known as the black-headed cushion flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically dark purple to black in color, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
