Centaurea rutifolia subsp. pseudobovina

Centaurea rutifolia subsp.

pseudobovina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
