Callista japonica

Callista japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Callista japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
