Cattleya intermedia

Cattleya intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cattleya intermedia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
