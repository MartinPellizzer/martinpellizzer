Cuscuta spectabilis

Cuscuta spectabilis, commonly known as the yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrition.

It produces small, yellow or orange flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on grasses, shrubs, and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to spread across various habitats.

Despite its parasitic nature, Cuscuta spectabilis plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant growth and serving as a food source for certain insects and birds.
