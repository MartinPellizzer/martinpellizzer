Castanea formosana

Castanea formosana, commonly known as the Formosan chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves and male and female flowers on separate plants. The tree produces spiny, cup-like fruits called husks, which enclose one or two edible nuts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
