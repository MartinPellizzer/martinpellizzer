Crataegus orientalis var. aronia

Crataegus orientalis var. aronia, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in culinary and medicinal applications, including the production of sloe gin.
