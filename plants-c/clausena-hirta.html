Clausena hirta

Clausena hirta, commonly known as wild mango or hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, acidic fruits that resemble mangoes in appearance. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves.

Clausena hirta is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and for its medicinal properties.
