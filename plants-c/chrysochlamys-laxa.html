Chrysochlamys laxa

Chrysochlamys laxa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, giving it a bell-like appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
