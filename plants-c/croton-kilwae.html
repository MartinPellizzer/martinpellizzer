Croton kilwae

Croton kilwae, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which can vary in shape from ovate to elliptic. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton kilwae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
