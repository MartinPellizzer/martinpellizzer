Cerastium lanigerum var. alpicola

Cerastium lanigerum var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and scree slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its compact growth and frost resistance, it is a characteristic species of alpine vegetation.
