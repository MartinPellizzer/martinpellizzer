Clusia sessiliflora

Clusia sessiliflora, commonly known as the wax plant or broadleaf waxleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Clusia sessiliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
