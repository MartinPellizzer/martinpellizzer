Chamaecrista aspidiifolia

Chamaecrista aspidiifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and medicinal research.
