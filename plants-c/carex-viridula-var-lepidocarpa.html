Carex viridula var. lepidocarpa

Carex viridula var. lepidocarpa, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

The leaves are usually narrow, smooth, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
