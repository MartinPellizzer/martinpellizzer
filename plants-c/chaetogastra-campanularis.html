Chaetogastra campanularis

Chaetogastra campanularis, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Chaetogastra, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and contribute to its distinctive appearance. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticulture.
