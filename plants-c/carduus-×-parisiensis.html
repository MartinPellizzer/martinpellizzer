Carduus × parisiensis

Carduus × parisiensis, commonly known as the French thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus benedictus and Carduus marianus.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple or pink flower heads that resemble thistles. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a composite flower structure typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
