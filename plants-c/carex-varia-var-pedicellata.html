Carex varia var. pedicellata

Carex varia var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a single pistil.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
