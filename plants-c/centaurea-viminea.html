Centaurea viminea

Centaurea viminea, commonly known as slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered inflorescences.

Centaurea viminea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
