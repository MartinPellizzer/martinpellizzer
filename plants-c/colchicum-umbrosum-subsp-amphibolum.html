Colchicum umbrosum subsp. amphibolum

Colchicum umbrosum subsp.

amphibolum, commonly known as the amphibole colchicum, is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is native to parts of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with reflexed petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing colchicine, a compound used in the treatment of gout.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
