Calamus macrosphaerion

Calamus macrosphaerion, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, robust plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, typical of the arum family traits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens and as a source of sustainable materials.
