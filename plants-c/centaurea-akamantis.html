Centaurea akamantis

Centaurea akamantis, commonly known as the Akamantis thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, ray-like florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Centaurea akamantis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
