Crataegus schuettei var. gigantea

Crataegus schuettei var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, thornless stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, orange, or yellow berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
