Cichorium intybus var. divaricatum

Cichorium intybus var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, sometimes branched stems and basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots.

This variety is closely related to other chicory species and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
