Coniogramme fraxinea var. serrulata

Coniogramme fraxinea var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated coniogramme, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded gardens.
