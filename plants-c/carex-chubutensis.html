Carex chubutensis

Carex chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Carex chubutensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
