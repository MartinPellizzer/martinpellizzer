Cinnamomum porosum

Cinnamomum porosum, commonly known as the porous cinnamon or porose cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant resin. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery, medicine, and as a flavoring agent.
