Corylus rostrata var. mandshurica

Corylus rostrata var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian hazelnut, is a variant of the hazel genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed nuts and hairy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are catkins, and it produces edible nuts that are harvested in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
