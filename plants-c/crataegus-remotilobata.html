Crataegus remotilobata

Crataegus remotilobata, commonly known as the remote lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely toothed margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
