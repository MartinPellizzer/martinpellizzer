Clematis scandens

Clematis scandens, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Clematis scandens is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
