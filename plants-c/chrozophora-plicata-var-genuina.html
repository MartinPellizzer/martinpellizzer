Chrozophora plicata var. genuina

Chrozophora plicata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
