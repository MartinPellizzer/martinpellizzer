Chimonobambusa puberula

Chimonobambusa puberula, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
