Cynodon ciliatus

Cynodon ciliatus, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, silky-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient and widely distributed species.
