Carpinus betulus var. carpinizza

Carpinus betulus var. carpinizza, commonly known as the Hungarian hornbeam, is a variant of the common hornbeam.

It is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe, characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red-brown berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
