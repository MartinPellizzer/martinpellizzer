Croton hilarii

Croton hilarii, commonly known as the horned croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and bright, colorful bracts that range from red to yellow. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a garden plant.
