Cotoneaster boulayi

Cotoneaster boulayi, commonly known as the Boulay's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster boulayi is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of food for birds.
