Calamus bicolor

Calamus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive dark green leaves with a lighter midrib, forming a dense, fan-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spadices, which are typical of many palm-like plants. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
