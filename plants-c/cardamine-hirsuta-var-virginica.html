Cardamine hirsuta var. virginica

Cardamine hirsuta var. virginica, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams, in wet meadows, and in woodland areas. The plant features alternate, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The species is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
