Carex nambuensis

Carex nambuensis, commonly known as the Nambu sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Carex nambuensis is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
