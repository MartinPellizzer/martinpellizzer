Carex angolensis

Carex angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is native to southern Africa, particularly Angola and Namibia.

Carex angolensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
