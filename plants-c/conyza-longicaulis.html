Conyza longicaulis

Conyza longicaulis, commonly known as slenderleaf blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a composite head.

Conyza longicaulis is often considered a weed but plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small insects.
