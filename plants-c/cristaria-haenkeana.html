Cristaria haenkeana

Cristaria haenkeana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, humid environments. Cristaria haenkeana is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
