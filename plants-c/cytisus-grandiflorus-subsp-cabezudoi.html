Cytisus grandiflorus subsp. cabezudoi

Cytisus grandiflorus subsp.

cabezudoi, commonly known as the Cabezudo broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
