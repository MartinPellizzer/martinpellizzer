Clompanus gregorii

Clompanus gregorii, commonly known as the Gregor's ghost flower, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
