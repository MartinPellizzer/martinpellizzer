Crocus olbanus

Crocus olbanus, commonly known as the Alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Crocus olbanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
