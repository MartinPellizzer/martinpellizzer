Colchicum × byzantinum

Colchicum × byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine colchic, is a hybrid plant in the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Turkey, where it grows in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous corm.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
