Cassine comorensis

Cassine comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros cassine, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Cassine comorensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
