Citrus × notissima

Citrus × notissima, commonly known as the trifoliate orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant native to China and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant is often used as an ornamental due to its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage. It is also valued in horticulture for its cold hardiness and ability to serve as a rootstock for other citrus species.

The fruit, though not typically eaten raw, is used in the production of essential oils and flavorings.
