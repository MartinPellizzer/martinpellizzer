Cynotoxicum

Cynotoxicum, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a toxic plant that contains tropane alkaloids such as atropine, scopolamine, and hyoscyamine. The plant produces dark purple berries that are highly poisonous if ingested. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxicity, it has been historically used in medicine and as a poison.
