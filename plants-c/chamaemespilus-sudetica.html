Chamaemespilus sudetica

Chamaemespilus sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally not palatable to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, clearings, and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
