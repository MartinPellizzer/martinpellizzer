Carduncellus battandieri

Carduncellus battandieri, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
