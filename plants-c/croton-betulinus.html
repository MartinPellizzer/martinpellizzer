Croton betulinus

Croton betulinus, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Croton betulinus thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
