Crataegus peramoena

Crataegus peramoena, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, a small red to orange pome, matures in late summer and is often used in jams and wines.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
