Caesalpinia rubra

Caesalpinia rubra, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Caesalpinia rubra thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
