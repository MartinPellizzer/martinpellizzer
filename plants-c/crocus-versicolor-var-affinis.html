Crocus versicolor var. affinis

Crocus versicolor var. affinis, commonly known as the variable crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
