Calceolaria ledifolia

Calceolaria ledifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to bloom in late summer.

Calceolaria ledifolia prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
