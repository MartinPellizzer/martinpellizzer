Chaenostoma denudatum

Chaenostoma denudatum, commonly known as the naked stem water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, delicate leaves that float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, emerging from the water surface on slender stalks.

Chaenostoma denudatum plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and is sometimes used in ornamental water gardens.
