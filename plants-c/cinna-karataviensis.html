Cinna karataviensis

Cinna karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavi cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain the seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their essential oils and traditional uses in herbal remedies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
