Carex flacca subsp. claviformis

Carex flacca subsp.

claviformis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The subspecies claviformis is distinguished by its elongated, club-shaped inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance among other Carex species.
