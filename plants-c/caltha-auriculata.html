Caltha auriculata

Caltha auriculata, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Caltha auriculata is often found in moist soils and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
