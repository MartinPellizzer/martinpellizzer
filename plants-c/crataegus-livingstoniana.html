Crataegus livingstoniana

Crataegus livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
