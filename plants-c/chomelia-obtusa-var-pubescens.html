Chomelia obtusa var. pubescens

Chomelia obtusa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy chomelia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
