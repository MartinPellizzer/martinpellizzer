Claytonia rubra subsp. rubra

Claytonia rubra subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed forest ecosystems.
