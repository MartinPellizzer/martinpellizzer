Chelone cristata

Chelone cristata, commonly known as the zigzagged bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly toothed margin.

Chelone cristata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
