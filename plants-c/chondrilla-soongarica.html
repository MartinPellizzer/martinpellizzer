Chondrilla soongarica

Chondrilla soongarica, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Chondrilla soongarica is often found in steppe and desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
