Cecropia teleargentea

Cecropia teleargentea, commonly known as silver ceiba, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a fast-growing, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, smooth, grayish bark. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.

The species is also notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, which provide protection in exchange for shelter and food.
