Calophyllum lankaensis

Calophyllum lankaensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan torchwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
