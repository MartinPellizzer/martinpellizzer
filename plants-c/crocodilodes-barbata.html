Crocodilodes barbata

Crocodilodes barbata, commonly known as the hairy dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry conditions.
