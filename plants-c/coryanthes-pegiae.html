Coryanthes pegiae

Coryanthes pegiae, commonly known as the red-tailed coryanthe, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flower that resembles a large, intricate cup. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its flowers are predominantly red or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur that extends from the base of the flower.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
