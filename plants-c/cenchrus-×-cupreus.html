Cenchrus × cupreus

Cenchrus × cupreus, commonly known as the coppergrass, is a hybrid grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive copper-colored glumes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Cenchrus × cupreus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
