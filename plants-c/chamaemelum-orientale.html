Chamaemelum orientale

Chamaemelum orientale, commonly known as the oriental chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves and is often grown for its medicinal properties. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oil, extracted from the flowers, is widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
