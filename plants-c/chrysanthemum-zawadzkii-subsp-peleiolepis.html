Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp. peleiolepis

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp.

peleiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the chrysanthemum genus.
