Cyrtococcum capitis-york

Cyrtococcum capitis-york, commonly known as the scalp lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in humid environments.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form and is typically found on the bark of trees or rocks. The thallus is usually yellowish to orange in color, and it contains a mixture of algae and fungi. It plays a role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem and is often used as an indicator of environmental conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized organism.
